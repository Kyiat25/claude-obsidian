# Half-Orc
## Lineage

> *"Born of two paths, but forged in one. Blood is not enough. Scar is not enough. Only choice is enough."*  
> — Spoken at the **Trial of Line-Claiming**

You are Half-Orc. Born between worlds, claimed by neither until you **prove** yourself. You carry Orcish blood, but that alone means nothing—not until your will demonstrates that you are worthy of the Line. You are **unclaimed**—a truth that is neither shame nor weakness, but challenge.

At age 14, you will face the **Trial of Line-Claiming**, a sacred test that determines whether you join the Scarred Line as full Orc, or walk into the world as honored witness. Either path carries dignity. Either path requires strength. But the choice—and the trial—is **yours alone**.

---

## Between the Scar and the Vein

In Orcish tradition, **mixed blood is not dishonor**. But it demands **clarity of purpose**. You must choose whether the scars you bear are yours or borrowed, whether the weight you carry is inherited or earned, whether your name will be written in the Ledger or carried in your heart alone.

You were not born lesser. You were born **untested**—and among Orcs, the untested carry potential that may surpass even pure bloodlines. Some of the greatest line-founders were once Name-Hungry, walking without claim until their deeds forced the gods themselves to remember.

---

## The Trial of Line-Claiming

At age 14, all Half-Orcs undergo the **Trial of Line-Claiming**, also called the **Scarfire Choice**. This is not punishment—it is **recognition** that you exist between worlds and must choose which weight to carry.

### **The Trial Process:**
- Overseen by two **Line-Keepers** and one **Scar-Warden**
- You must complete a trial of **endurance, wit, and resolve** tied to your ancestral line's defining value
- You speak aloud the name you seek to claim and **recite a story of its weight**
- You accept one **sacred wound**, symbolizing your acceptance of legacy

### **If You Pass:**
- Your name is carved into the clan's Ledger
- From that moment forward, you are **Orc in every way**
- Your Half-Orc heritage is never mentioned again unless **you** choose to acknowledge it
- You may choose to keep a dual-epithet (e.g., "Bridge-Born," "Two-Blooded") as a mark of pride

### **If You Fail:**
- You are **not shamed**—failure of the first trial is common and expected
- You receive a **soft-scar** across the ribs—a mark that the line is not yet earned
- You are escorted with full dignity and honor to the nearest major settlement
- You are provided weapon, armor, rations, and protection
- This is called **The Farewell of Fire**, and the whole clan attends

> *"Your blood burns, but your name must be tempered. Come back when you're ready."*

### **Important Truth:**
Many Half-Orcs never take the trial. This is **not failure**—it is simply choosing a different path. You can live your entire life as honored witness, welcomed in any Orcish territory, treated with respect and dignity. The trial is an **invitation, not an obligation**.

---

## Your Place Among Orcs

### **Before the Trial (or if never taken):**
- You are **Children of the Scar's Shadow**—not full members, but not lesser
- You may observe all rites, attend teachings, and train
- You cannot speak during scar-testimony unless invited
- You are treated as **Honored Watchers**—guests of all clans

### **After Passing the Trial:**
- You are **Orc**. Full stop.
- Your heritage is your business alone
- All rights and honors of the clan are yours
- Some choose the epithet "-Claimed" or "Bridge-Born" to honor their journey

### **After Failing or Declining the Trial:**
- You are welcome in any Orcish land as guest
- You may witness rites and funerals
- You may **fight in a Warden's name** if called
- Scar-Keepers send messages reminding you **the Line waits**
- You are protected by guest-right

### **Critical Note on Dishonor:**
If you **dishonor the clan, lie, or defile sacred rites**, judgment is **far harsher** than for full-blooded Orcs. Your dishonor scars not just your soul but both bloodlines—Orcish and whatever other heritage you carry. This is considered **blasphemy**, and your soft-scar will be struck through with black ash, marking you as **"Split-Blood Failed"**—one of the gravest entries in Orcish record-keeping.

## Physical Characteristics

**Age:** You mature slightly faster than humans, reaching adulthood by 14 (the traditional age of Line-Claiming). You can live to about 75 years—shorter than humans but longer than full Orcs, as if your mixed heritage moderates both lifespans.

**Size:** You typically stand between 5½ to 6½ feet tall, smaller than full Orcs but larger and more powerfully built than most humans. Your size is Medium.

**Speed:** Your base walking speed is 30 feet.

**Build:** You combine human adaptability with Orcish power—muscular and broad-shouldered, but with more varied body types than pure Orcs. Your features clearly show your mixed heritage: prominent lower canines (though smaller than full Orcs), grayish skin tones ranging from green-tinged to earthen brown, and strong bone structure.

**Appearance Notes:** 
- Your scars heal visibly like Orcs, creating permanent marks
- Your eyes may carry Orcish intensity (solid colors) or human variation
- Your build suggests strength without the intimidating mass of full Orcs
- You may file or cap your tusks, or display them with pride—your choice

## Half-Orc Traits

As a Half-Orc, you possess the following lineage traits:

### **Darkvision**
You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

Inherited from your Orcish bloodline, though not as developed as full Orcs.

### **Relentless Endurance**
When you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, you can drop to 1 hit point instead. You can't use this feature again until you finish a long rest.

This is perhaps the strongest evidence of your Orcish blood—**you refuse to fall**. Even without the full clan training, your body remembers what it means to endure.

### **Savage Attacks**
When you score a critical hit with a melee weapon attack, you can roll one of the weapon's damage dice one additional time and add it to the extra damage of the critical hit.

Your Orcish heritage manifests in devastating strikes when you find an opening—brief moments of overwhelming force.

### **Bridge-Walker**
You have proficiency in two skills of your choice from: Intimidation, Insight, Persuasion, or Survival.

This represents your unique position between cultures. You've learned to navigate both worlds, reading social situations that pure-blooded members of either culture might miss.

### **Dual Heritage**
You have advantage on saving throws against being frightened. Your existence between two worlds has taught you that belonging is earned, not given—making external threats less psychologically devastating.

## Mental Attributes & Psychology

**Focus Modifier:** +0  
**Sanity Modifier:** +0

Your mental baseline is balanced between human adaptability and Orcish resilience. While you don't receive the full Orcish +1 Sanity bonus, your Dual Heritage trait provides specific advantages against fear.

### **Psychological Identity**
Your mental landscape is defined by **liminality**—existing between states:

- **Cultural flexibility:** You can code-switch between Orcish honor-culture and human social structures
- **Identity pressure:** The question of "where do I belong?" creates both stress and adaptability  
- **Earned worth:** You've internalized that value comes from deeds, not birthright
- **Observation skills:** Standing outside both cultures makes you excellent at reading social dynamics

### **Common Mental Patterns:**
- **Imposter syndrome** when among full Orcs (even after Claiming)
- **Overcompensation** to prove worth to either culture
- **Hyper-awareness** of how others perceive you
- **Independence** born of never fully belonging anywhere
- **Deep loyalty** when finally accepted, because you know its value

### **Corruption Vulnerability: The Split Thread**

As a Half-Orc, you have a **different** relationship with Corruption than full Orcs. Your mixed heritage creates a unique vulnerability to corruption that enters through **identity crisis, belonging denial, or self-hatred**.

**When you gain Corruption Score 10+ (Tier 2), you suffer:**
- Identity Fracture: Difficulty remembering which culture's values to follow
- Belonging Void: Feeling permanently alienated from both heritages
- Worth Erosion: Questioning whether your deeds truly matter

**Split Thread Table (Corruption Tier 2+)**

When you complete a long rest at Corruption Score 10+, roll 1d6:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **False Belonging:** You become convinced you fully belong to one heritage, forgetting the other exists. Make a DC 15 Wisdom save or suffer disadvantage on Bridge-Walker skills for 24 hours. |
| 2 | **Heritage Rejection:** You feel intense self-loathing about your mixed blood. You cannot use Relentless Endurance until your next long rest. |
| 3 | **Dual Silence:** Neither Orcish nor Common feels like your true language. You have disadvantage on Charisma checks for 24 hours. |
| 4 | **Identity Void:** You cannot remember why you chose the path you're on. Make a DC 12 Sanity save or lose 1d4 Sanity. |
| 5 | **Whisper of Unbelonging:** Something whispers that you will never be enough. Gain 1 Whisper Point but your next use of Savage Attacks fails (no extra die). |
| 6 | **Bridge Holds:** Your unique perspective resists corruption. Reduce your Corruption Score by 1 (minimum 10). |

## Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Integration

### **Evasion Profile: Adaptive Warrior**
- **Dodge Evasion:** +1 (more agile than full Orcs)
- **Endure Evasion:** +1 (tougher than humans)
- **Deflect Evasion:** +0 (balanced approach)

**Tactical Note:** You're more **versatile** than either parent lineage—you can take hits (Relentless Endurance) but also avoid them better than full Orcs. This makes you excellent for "flexible bruiser" builds.

### **Natural Damage Reduction:** +0
You lack the thick hide of full Orcs, but your toughness manifests through Relentless Endurance instead.

### **Combat Philosophy**
Half-Orcs fight with **calculated aggression**—balancing Orcish relentlessness with human tactical flexibility. You can:
- Charge in like an Orc when needed (Savage Attacks rewards aggression)
- Adapt tactics mid-combat (Bridge-Walker social reading applies to combat tells)
- Refuse to stay down (Relentless Endurance)
- Exploit critical moments (Savage Attacks on crits)

### **Unique Combat Identity**
While full Orcs are unstoppable juggernauts, you're more like a **surge fighter**—waiting for the right moment to deliver devastating blows (Savage Attacks) while having the backup plan of Relentless Endurance if things go wrong.

## Languages

**Languages:** You can speak, read, and write Common and Orcish.

You learned Orcish either:
- From Orcish parent/clan before Trial of Line-Claiming
- Through cultural exposure if raised among Orcs
- Self-taught to honor your heritage
- During Trial preparation

Many Half-Orcs speak Orcish with a slight accent that full Orcs recognize as "two-tongued"—not mockery, but acknowledgment that you learned it as well as lived it.

**Orcish Fluency:** Despite any accent, you can speak the **language of scars and honor** perfectly. Challenge-Tongue may not come as naturally as to full Orcs, but your words still carry weight when you invoke ancestral names.

## Half-Orc Backgrounds

Unlike full Orcs who are typically raised within clan structure, Half-Orcs come from **diverse backgrounds**:

### **Clan-Raised**
Raised among Orcs, prepared for Line-Claiming since birth:
- Deep understanding of Scar-Law
- Pressure to prove worth constantly
- Well-versed in Orcish tradition
- May feel like an outsider despite proximity

### **Human-Raised**
Raised primarily in human settlements:
- Orcish heritage as abstract concept
- Learned Orcish culture later or academically
- May face prejudice from both sides
- Seeks connection to heritage

### **Wanderer**
Raised by neither culture fully:
- Self-taught in both traditions
- Deep independence
- Forges own path
- May romanticize or misunderstand both cultures

### **Dual Household**
Raised by both parents actively:
- Best of both worlds (or worst, depending on family dynamics)
- Natural cultural translator
- May feel pressure to "choose sides"
- Understands both perspectives deeply

## Playing a Half-Orc

### **Core Fantasy:**
You are the bridge between worlds, the proof that strength and adaptability can coexist, the warrior who refuses to fall because you've spent your life proving you belong **somewhere**. Your mixed heritage is not weakness—it's the source of your versatility and resilience.

### **Mechanical Identity:**
- **Versatile Bruiser:** Balanced Dodge/Endure Evasion creates flexibility
- **Critical Striker:** Savage Attacks rewards aggressive play
- **Survivor:** Relentless Endurance creates dramatic clutch moments
- **Social Chameleon:** Bridge-Walker gives skill flexibility
- **Fear-Resistant:** Dual Heritage advantage on frightened saves

### **Roleplaying Hooks:**
- **The Claimant:** Seeking to pass the Trial of Line-Claiming
- **The Declined:** Chose not to take the trial, forging own path
- **The Failed:** Failed trial, seeking redemption or new purpose  
- **The Bridge:** Actively working to unite Orcish and human communities
- **The Searcher:** Looking for Orcish parent/clan/heritage
- **The Rejected:** Bitter about neither culture fully accepting them
- **The Proud:** Wearing mixed heritage as badge of honor
- **The Proven:** Already Claimed, but still processing the journey

### **Party Dynamics:**
Your unique position creates interesting interactions:

**With Full Orcs:**
- Respect if Claimed; watchful acceptance if not
- Potential mentor/student dynamics
- Cultural guide for party navigating Orcish lands
- May face questions about why you chose this path

**With Humans:**
- May face suspicion in some communities
- Can translate Orcish honor for confused allies
- Proof that "savage" Orcs are complex people
- Sometimes seen as "one of us who got bigger"

**With Other Races:**
- Often assumed to be warrior (can subvert or embrace)
- Useful for intimidation when needed
- Surprising diplomacy from Bridge-Walker skills
- May bond with other "between worlds" characters (Half-Elves, etc.)

### **Common Character Arcs:**
- From seeking acceptance to accepting self
- From hiding heritage to proud display
- From believing blood defines you to valuing deeds
- From cultural confusion to bridge-builder
- From ashamed half-breed to honored warrior
- From obsessed with Claiming to finding worth elsewhere

## Mechanical Considerations

### **Optimal Classes:**
Your abilities synergize well with:

- **Barbarian:** Savage Attacks + Brutal Critical = massive damage; Relentless Endurance + Rage = hard to kill
- **Fighter:** Crit-fishing builds love Savage Attacks; Relentless Endurance extends battlefield presence
- **Paladin:** Savage Attacks + Divine Smite on crits is devastating; Dual Heritage + paladin auras stack
- **Ranger:** Bridge-Walker survival + Savage Attacks creates effective skirmisher
- **Warlock (Hexblade):** Crit-focused builds benefit hugely from Savage Attacks

### **Strong But Unconventional:**
- **Bard:** Bridge-Walker social skills + Savage Attacks in melee creates "warrior poet"
- **Cleric (War Domain):** Savage Attacks + spiritual weapons + Relentless Endurance
- **Rogue:** Savage Attacks on crit Sneak Attack is **brutal**; Bridge-Walker covers social skills
- **Monk:** Relentless Endurance means you stay conscious to use ki; Savage Attacks on unarmed crits

### **Multiclass Synergies:**
- **Barbarian/Fighter:** Crit-fishing with advantage (Reckless) + Champion archetype + Savage Attacks
- **Paladin/Barbarian:** Smite crits + Savage Attacks (DM discretion on Rage/smite interaction)
- **Fighter/Rogue:** Savage Attacks + Sneak Attack devastates on crits
- **Ranger/Rogue:** Scout builds with survival + stealth + crit damage

- Dual Heritage gives specific resistance to fear effects

## The Truth About Half-Orcs in FEARS

### **Not "Half" But "Bridge"**
Modern understanding (especially in areas touched by the Faith of the Bound Pattern) recognizes that Half-Orcs aren't diluted versions of either parent race—they're **unique beings** with their own strengths:

- Orcish endurance + human adaptability
- Traditional honor + tactical flexibility  
- Cultural translator naturally
- Living proof that worth transcends blood

### **Claiming vs. Not Claiming:**
Both paths have equal dignity:

**If you Claim:**
- You are Orc, with all rights and responsibilities
- Your choice shows strength of will
- You carry dual perspective even within Orcish culture
- Some of the greatest Orcish heroes were once Half-Orcs

**If you don't Claim:**
- You forge your own path with honor
- You represent integration over assimilation
- You keep connections to both worlds
- Your children may choose differently, and that's sacred too

### **Modern Acceptance:**
In cities following the Faith of the Bound Pattern:
- Half-Orcs often become law-keepers and judges
- Your Bridge-Walker abilities are valued in diplomacy
- Relentless Endurance makes you reliable guardian
- Cultural translation skills prevent misunderstandings

In Orcish lands:
- Honored guests if un-Claimed
- Full clan members if Claimed
- Respected for trials overcome
- Welcomed to attempt Claiming at any age

### **Mechanical Balance:**
Half-Orcs are intentionally strong:
- Relentless Endurance is one of the best racial features
- Savage Attacks scales excellently with crits
- Bridge-Walker provides skill flexibility
- Balanced Evasion profile creates versatility

This reflects that being "between" isn't weakness—it's **adaptability**, one of the most powerful traits in FEARS.

## Final Notes

### **The Weight of Choice:**
Unlike full Orcs who inherit their path, you **choose** yours. This is not burden—it's **freedom**. Your worth is not determined by bloodline but by deeds. Your name, if you claim one, is earned twice: once at birth, once through trial.

### **The Name-Hungry:**
If you haven't Claimed a name, you are **Name-Hungry**—not shame, but acknowledgment of potential. Many of the greatest line-founders were once without names. Wandering as witness and story-keeper until worthy is **honorable path**.

### **To Other Half-Orcs:**
You may never meet another Half-Orc. You may meet hundreds. Either way, understand:
- Your experience is valid
- Your choice (to Claim or not) is yours alone
- Blood does not define worth—deeds do
- Being "between" means you can see both sides
- You are not broken pieces of two things; you are whole as yourself

### **The Bridge Stands:**
In FEARS, Half-Orcs represent **integration without erasure**—you can honor both heritages without diminishing either. You can be Claimed as Orc while remembering your human parent. You can walk among humans while carrying Orcish honor in your heart.

You are the proof that **the Line adapts**, that **the Weave grows**, and that **strength comes in forms the gods didn't plan for**.
