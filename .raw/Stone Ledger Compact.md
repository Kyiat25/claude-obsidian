# The Stone Ledger Compact
## The World-Bank of Dór-o Estel

> *"Gold may pass. Crowns may fall.  
> Stone remembers what blood forgets."*  
> — Inscription above the Third Vault Gate

---

## Classification

**Type:** Trans-Loom Financial & Legal Authority  
**Primary Seat:** Thar Vornhal  
**Founded:** Late First Age (Pre-Faith Formalization)  
**Jurisdiction:** Recognized across all Loom-Cities and Imperial States  
**Status:** Active, Untouchable, System-Critical  

---

## Executive Summary

The **Stone Ledger Compact** is the single most powerful non-divine institution in Dór-o Estel.

It does not rule territory.  
It does not command armies.  
It does not speak prophecy.

It **decides which civilizations are allowed to survive collapse**.

All Loom-Cities, empires, and major trade coalitions are bound—directly or indirectly—by contracts held within the Compact's vaults. Some debts are denominated in gold. Others in land, labor, memory, or *time*.

The Compact exists to ensure **continuity**, even when justice, truth, or mercy must fail.

---

# PART I: INSTITUTIONAL STRUCTURE

## I. Purpose & Doctrine (Why the Bank Exists)

### The Foundational Principle
> *"Stability precedes morality."*

The Stone Ledger Compact was founded after multiple early-age collapses revealed a fatal truth:

Civilizations do not fall because they are evil.  
They fall because **too many systems fail at once**.

The Compact exists to:
- Prevent synchronized collapse
- Delay catastrophic failure
- Distribute suffering across time instead of space

It does not prevent collapse.  
It **paces** it.

---

### What the Compact Explicitly Does

- Holds sovereign and Loom-City reserves
- Issues inter-city credit
- Arbitrates debt disputes beyond national law
- Funds post-catastrophe reconstruction
- Enforces indenture and labor bonds
- Maintains Anchor-stability contingency funds

### What It Refuses to Do

- Cancel debt without replacement burden
- Act on moral or religious grounds
- Intervene militarily
- Recognize revolutions without collateral proof of stability

---

## II. The Vaultward Deeps

### Location

The **Vaultward Deeps** lie beneath the Upper City of Thar Vornhal, carved directly into load-bearing strata tied to the Earth Thread.

They are not merely underground.  
They are **structural**.

If the Vaultward Deeps collapse, so does part of the city above.

---

### Structure

The Deeps are divided into **Seven Vault Rings**, each deeper and more restricted than the last:

| Ring | Contents |
|---|---|
| First Ring | Active state treasuries, trade bonds |
| Second Ring | Guild compacts, fleet insurance |
| Third Ring | Loom-City emergency reserves |
| Fourth Ring | Generational debt contracts |
| Fifth Ring | Anchor-adjacent funding & fail-safes |
| Sixth Ring | Pre-Faith financial records |
| Seventh Ring | **Sealed Obligations** (unknown total) |

No single individual has access to more than **two rings**.

---

### Access Control

- Entry requires a **Stone Writ** issued by the Tribunal
- Vault doors respond to **geomantic resonance**, not keys
- Forced entry risks localized Earth-Thread destabilization
- Some vaults are deliberately *unmapped*

---

### DM Truth

At least one Vault Ring predates the current Earth Thread alignment and contains contracts written **before the Loom existed**.

---

## III. The Stone Ledger

### Not a Book

The **Stone Ledger** is a geomantic record system etched into layered stone strata.

Each contract is:
- Inscribed physically
- Reinforced magically
- Echoed metaphysically through the Earth Thread

Debt is not *written*.  
It is **embedded**.

---

### Properties of the Ledger

- Cannot be fully erased without geological disruption
- Partial alteration causes ledger-instability events
- Records persist even if the Compact collapses
- Attempts to falsify entries produce resonance backlash

The Ledger is considered a **World-Scale Artifact**, though it is not worshipped.

---

### What Is Recorded

- Sovereign debt
- City-wide obligations
- Indenture contracts
- Anchor-maintenance liabilities
- Reconstruction loans
- Resource extraction rights
- Penal labor quotas

### What Is Not Recorded

- Moral justification
- Political intent
- Casualties

---

## IV. The Debtwrights

### Role

**Debtwrights** are the Compact's operators, analysts, and enforcers.

They are not bankers.  
They are **burden-engineers**.

---

### Training

Debtwrights are trained in:
- Advanced geomancy
- Inter-city law
- Demographic collapse modeling
- Anchor-stress forecasting
- Memory-resistant cognition techniques

They are deliberately selected for:
- Emotional dampening
- Long-term thinking
- Resistance to charisma and prophecy

---

### Authority

A Debtwright may:
- Freeze city credit
- Reassign labor obligations
- Trigger emergency rationing clauses
- Call in generational debt
- Recommend "managed default"

They may not:
- Forgive debt outright
- Act independently of the Ledger
- Override Anchor-priority rulings

---

### Cultural Perception

- Feared by rulers
- Hated by populists
- Respected by guilds
- Avoided by priests

---

## V. Slavery, Indenture & Human Cost

### Legal Framework

**Chattel slavery**
- Illegal in most Loom-Cities
- Publicly condemned

**Indentured servitude**
- Universal
- Enforced by the Compact
- Often permanent in practice

---

### Indenture Mechanics

- Debt can be inherited
- Labor can be transferred
- Contracts can be bound to Anchors
- Escape voids legal personhood

Most indentures are signed:
- During famine
- After disaster
- As part of reconstruction aid

Consent is *technically* present.  
Alternatives rarely are.

---

### Anchor Labor Clauses

Some contracts bind workers directly to:
- Anchor maintenance
- Hazardous Thread zones
- Sacrificial shifts during instability

Deaths are considered **acceptable loss** if collapse is delayed.

---

### The Quiet Truth

Entire districts of some Loom-Cities are:
- Legally free
- Economically owned
- Structurally unescapable

The Compact does not deny this.

It classifies it as **stability expenditure**.

---

## VI. Relationship to the Loom-Cities

| City | Relationship |
|---|---|
| Kel Varyan | Financial legitimacy & debt law |
| Veltharyn | Reconstruction & penal labor |
| Aeravae | Memory-indexed obligations |
| Selveran | Ecological stabilization bonds |
| Thar Vornhal | Host, enforcer, last resort |

Every Loom-City believes it can outmaneuver the Compact.

None have succeeded.

---

## VII. Why No One Destroys the Bank

To destroy the Stone Ledger Compact would:
- Freeze all inter-city credit
- Collapse trade routes
- Trigger Anchor failure cascades
- Cause asynchronous wars

The Compact does not threaten.

It simply **stops intervening**.

# PART II: THE WEIGHT OF SEVEN

## Governing Body

The Stone Ledger Compact is ruled by a conclave known as **The Weight of Seven**—the highest financial authority in Dór-o Estel.

They do not rule territory.  
They do not command armies.  
They do not interpret prophecy.

They determine **how collapse is distributed**.

Their chamber lies deep beneath **Thar Vornhal**, directly above the deepest Vault Ring, where Earth Thread resonance is strongest and escape is impossible.

No two members are ever chosen from the same city.  
Each represents a *category of systemic failure*, not a nation.

---

## The Seven Debtwrights

### 1. Arkhavel Stone-Limit

**Title:** Warden of Thresholds  
**Domain:** Collapse forecasting, brink modeling  

Arkhavel determines how far a city may fall before intervention becomes mandatory. He is infamous for delaying aid until the final viable moment.

- Never recorded a miscalculation  
- Believes panic is a stabilizing force  
- Treats death curves as moral-neutral data  

**Secret:**  
Arkhavel believes **one Loom-City must fall completely** to reset the system. He is waiting for the correct candidate.

**Player Leverage:**  
Convince him that the collapse he anticipates is *too early*, not unjust.

---

### 2. Maerith of the Silent Column

**Title:** Keeper of Indenture  
**Domain:** Labor bonds, inherited debt  

Maerith oversees all long-term servitude contracts recognized by the Compact.

- Soft-spoken, precise, relentless  
- Ends uprisings by forgiving minimal debt  
- Views freedom as a destabilizing luxury  

**Secret:**  
Her own family has been indentured for **six generations**, upheld by her own rulings.

**Player Leverage:**  
Expose her neutrality as self-imposed punishment rather than objectivity.

---

### 3. Kelros Vant, the Grain Hand

**Title:** Arbiter of Sustenance  
**Domain:** Food routes, ration law, famine pacing  

Kelros controls when food shortages become real.

- Redirects grain to force political compliance  
- Has started wars without issuing a command  
- Believes hunger teaches obedience better than fear  

**Secret:**  
Once prevented a famine by violating Compact doctrine—and erased the evidence.

**Player Leverage:**  
Reveal the precedent and force him to repeat the act publicly.

---

### 4. Sister Dhalienne of the Measured Veil

**Title:** Liaison to Faith & Anchors  
**Domain:** Anchor collateral, reconstruction law  

A former priestess, Dhalienne authored the first Anchor-backed debt frameworks.

- Speaks of mercy while enforcing sacrifice  
- Maintains Compact legitimacy with the Faith  
- Treats Anchors as financial instruments  

**Secret:**  
One World Anchor is already **fully mortgaged** and nearing failure.

**Player Leverage:**  
Threaten disclosure to rival Loom-Cities or the Faith of the Bound Pattern.

---

### 5. Borrik Deep-Writ

**Title:** Keeper of the Ledger Strata  
**Domain:** Geomantic inscription, permanence  

Borrik physically inscribes debt into stone.

- Missing one hand, replaced with rune-scarred iron  
- Treats the Ledger as a living structure  
- Speaks to stone more than people  

**Secret:**  
He knows of **false Ledger entries**—and maintains balance instead of correction.

**Player Leverage:**  
Force him to choose which lie survives.

---

### 6. Tessera Quill-No-More

**Title:** Arbiter of Defaults  
**Domain:** Managed collapse, legal death of cities  

Tessera signs the documents that end civilizations.

- Burns seals into stone instead of writing  
- Believes mercy compounds suffering  
- Cold, exact, terrifyingly calm  

**Secret:**  
She is running out of cities that can collapse **without chain reaction**.

**Player Leverage:**  
Prove that the next default will not remain isolated.

---

### 7. The Seat of Burden

**Title:** Final Balance  
**Occupant:** Unknown / Rotational / Possibly Non-Mortal  

The Seat of Burden breaks ties and authorizes Seal-Level actions.

- Rarely speaks  
- Overrides all other rulings  
- May be bound directly to the Earth Thread  

**Rumor:**  
The Seat is not a person, but an **institutional consciousness** embedded in stone.

**Player Leverage:**  
None directly. Influence the other six.

# PART III: CAMPAIGN INTEGRATION

## How Players Confront the Bank (Critical)

The Stone Ledger Compact **cannot** be defeated by:
- Assassination  
- Theft  
- Arson  
- Moral exposure alone  

It is defeated by **forcing contradictions**.

---

## Valid Player Victory Conditions

### 1. Collapse the Assumption

Prove that stability does **not** require this level of suffering.

- A city survives without Compact pacing  
- An Anchor stabilizes outside debt law  

This threatens the Compact more than rebellion.

---

### 2. Break Synchronization

Trigger:
- Two collapses at once  
- Or a collapse that unexpectedly stabilizes  

The Compact is designed to manage **one failure at a time**.

---

### 3. Steal Legitimacy

If a Loom-City rejects Compact arbitration and survives one full Thread cycle, Compact authority fractures globally.

---

### 4. Force an Anchor Choice

Reveal that two Anchors cannot both be saved—and that the Compact already chose one.

Make the choice public.

---

## Player Interaction Paths

- Expose a sealed obligation
- Destroy or alter a single contract
- Free an indentured population
- Cause a city default intentionally
- Blackmail a Debtwright
- Discover Anchor collateralization

---

## Consequences

### Partial Player Victory

- Debt law weakens  
- Indenture destabilizes  
- Guilds panic  
- Faith fractures  

### Total Player Victory

- The Loom survives  
- The Compact collapses  
- A more dangerous uncertainty replaces it  

---

## Moral Axis

Every victory costs something else.

The Compact ensures that players must choose:
- Who suffers now
- Who suffers later
- And whether stability is worth the price

---

## Final Truth (DM-Only)

The Stone Ledger Compact is not evil.

It is afraid.

Afraid that without it:
- People will choose hope too early  
- The world will tear itself apart trying to deserve it  

The campaign's real question is not:

**"Is the bank wrong?"**

But:

**"Is it still necessary?"**
