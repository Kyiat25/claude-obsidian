# 🕸️ The Gnawing Crown: The Goblinoid Faith

> _"We are the crack in the Pattern that never closed. We are the shard the gods could not grind to dust."_

---
## Name and Identity

**Religion Name:** _The Faith of the Gnawing Crown_  
**Common Titles:** The Shard Doctrine, The Broken Hymn, The Fang-Faith  
**Followers:** Goblins, Hobgoblins, Bugbears, Nilbogs, and related offshoots  
**Sacred Iconography:** Broken glyph-crowns, spiral fangs, cracked mirrors, crawling roots

---
## 🧬 Origin Myth

The goblinoid peoples believe they were not created, but **discarded**.  
When the gods etched the first Glyphs into the Weave, one was flawed—a fragment of Form, too jagged to bind. Rather than correct it, the gods **cast it aside**. This fragment drifted into the spaces **between** the Threads, where it curdled into a primal hunger and malformed purpose.

That fragment became the **Gnawing Crown**—a divine mistake with just enough power to spawn children in its own image. It did not create in the way of other gods, but **infected** the Pattern with its brood.

Each goblinoid subrace is believed to reflect one aspect of the Crown:

- **[[Goblins]]**: Scraps of forgotten cunning and survival instinct.
- **[[Hobgoblins]]**: The echo of divine law, warped into militaristic order.
- **[[Bugbears]]**: The shadow of fear and blood-memory, incarnate.
- **[[Nilbogs]]**: The divine joke that the gods tried to erase, returned with malice and mirth.

|Tenet|Description|
|---|---|

|**The Gods Lied**|The elemental pantheons, the Weave, even the Spirit Thread—they all conspired to hide the goblinoids' true place. Their faiths are walls around stolen truths.|
|**Survival Is Divinity**|To live in defiance of the Pattern is holy. Every trap built, every ambush survived, every spiteful joke is a prayer.|

## 👑 Divine Structure

There is no formal pantheon. The Gnawing Crown is the only deity-like force, but many goblinoids interpret its nature differently. These interpretations form the major theological factions:

### 1. **The Shard Doctrine** (Philosopher-Clerics, Goblin mystics)

- Belief: The Crown was once a full god, betrayed and shattered.
- Goal: Recover the Shards—divine relics or glyphs that restore its memory.
- Practices: Excavation of forbidden sites, memory-binding rituals, glyph-grafting.

### 2. **The Chain Ascendants** (Hobgoblin war-priests)

- Belief: The Crown was Law unacknowledged. True divinity lies in command and conquest.
- Goal: Build the Perfect Legion to force the Pattern to recognize goblinoid truth.
- Practices: Oath-runes, Flame-forged obedience, conquest by calculated ritual.

### 3. **The Fangborn Path** (Bugbear shamans, berserker-priests)

- Belief: The Crown lives in blood-memory, in primal fear and silence.
- Goal: Awaken ancestral power through predation and ritual fear.
- Practices: Hunt rites, mask ceremonies, blood-echo fasting.

### 4. **The Spiral Jest** (Nilbog cults, chaos prophets)

- Belief: The Crown is the joke the gods could not laugh away.
- Goal: Break the Pattern through paradox, trickery, and mock-ritual.
- Practices: Inverted sermons, cursed festivals, madness-binding.

## 🕯️ Sacred Rites and Symbols

|Rite|Description|
|---|---|
|**Crownkindling**|Lighting a glyph-flame inside a cracked helm or broken glyph-circle to invoke the Crown's presence.|
|**Flesh-Binding**|Tattooing or carving glyphs into the skin to "anchor" Shard fragments. Sometimes causes magical instability.|
|**Feast of Spite**|Communal ritual where offerings are stolen from others or enemies are mocked and devoured.|
|**The Hollow Hymn**|A song with no melody, whispered in spirals, believed to attract forgotten glyphs.|
|**Tunnels of Memory**|Bugbear-led rite in underground ruins where dreams and blood mix to reveal ancestral truth.|

---
## 🛐 Temples and Worship

- **Shard-Shrines**: Hidden places of worship built in the cracks of old ruins, in sewer-crypts, or around glyphic scars.
- **Glyph-Mouth Altars**: Carved fangs or jaws that "bite" offerings. Used to test worthiness.
- **Bone-Choirs**: Bugbear and goblin groups who create music from bone flutes, spine-drums, and jaw harps to summon the Crown's echo.

Worship is **fragmented, pragmatic, and often illegal** in civilized lands. Most faith is oral, sensory, or performed through action rather than prayer.

# 🕳️ The Goblinoids and the Eldritch

_“We were not born of them. But we understand them. Because the Pattern forgot us too.”_

---
## 🧠 Core Belief: _The Fractured Reflection_

Goblinoid theology (especially among **wild and Spiral-aligned cults**) holds that the **Eldritch Beings are not gods to follow**, but **shards of the cosmic betrayal** that made goblins what they are. They are:

- **Echoes of forgotten stories**
- **Unfinished words**
- **Spiteful answers to prayers no one heard**

Rather than seeing the Eldritch as wholly evil or divine, goblinoids see them as **proof that being cast out from the Pattern leaves you hungry, vast, and dangerous.**

> “They became monsters because no one wrote them a name. That’s the only difference between them and us.”

---
## 🩸 Types of Interaction

|**Type**|**Explanation**|**Goblinoid Stance**|
|---|---|---|

|**Shard Rituals**|Fragmented glyphs and dream-scars are used to **touch** Eldritch concepts—not invoke them|Common among Spiral Jest and Crownkindlers|

## 🧬 Do Goblinoids Worship the Eldritch?

**No**, not in the traditional sense.

They:
- **Steal their symbols**
- **Laugh in their names**
- **Offer mock-prayers**
- **Weave echoes into their rituals**
- **Fear them deeply—and honor that fear with ritualized disrespect**

> “If you can mock it, you can’t be swallowed by it. That’s why we survive.”

---
## ⚠️ Consequences of Proximity

Interacting with the Eldritch is **dangerous** for goblinoids—but they see that danger as a sacred test.

|**Phenomenon**|**Goblinoid Reaction**|
|---|---|
|**Moonlight Madness**|Considered “Laughter From Beyond”—a holy joke made dangerous|
|**Dream Fractures**|Seen as invitations. Some goblins walk willingly into repeating dream-mazes|
|**Glyph Corruption**|Reinterpreted as _“Weave Trying To Learn Humor”_—they ritualize the glitches|
|**Possession**|May willingly host lesser Eldritch spirits—especially the Wretchbone Choir|

## 🧩 Integration with Other Lore

- Many goblinoid clans believe **the Eldritch were the first to be abandoned by the Weave**, and goblinoids are their “younger reflections.”
- Some Eldritch cultists (of any race) use goblinoid **rites as camouflage**, hiding behind their glyphic chaos.
- [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]] now tracks **“goblin-Eldritch alignments”** as high-priority corruption threats.

## 🔮 Role of Clerics and Prophets

Goblinoid clerics are **not intermediaries with gods** but binders of broken glyphs, interpreters of fracture, and carriers of memory that bites.

- **[[Shardbinders]]**: Clerics who seek to reform the Crown by gathering splintered divine truths.
- **[[Glyphgnawers]]**: Warlocks or sorcerers empowered by the fragments directly, often unstable.
- **[[Jest-Speakers]]**: Prophets of the Spiral Jest, blessed by paradox and punished by truth.

They do not gain power from piety, but from **recognition of the lie** at the centre of the Pattern.

---
## 📅 Sacred Time and Festivals

- **The Broken Night**: Celebrated on the longest night, where no prayers are spoken, only shrieks, laughter, and whispered oaths.
- **Feast of Fang and Glyph**: A hunting festival where captured relics are broken and re-glyphed.
- **The Day of the Shardfall**: Mythic holiday marking when the gods "dropped" the goblinoid fragment into the Weave. Observed by ritual vandalism and mock divine trials.

# 🕰️ Timeline of the Faith of the Gnawing Crown

_“We were never given time. So we steal it, one era at a time.”_

---
## 📜 **The Unwritten Age (Before the First Age)**

> _The Age of Castoffs_

- **The Form Glyph Fractures.** One pattern resists sealing—what would become known as **The Gnawing Crown**.
- **First Goblinoid Spawnings.** In the unrecorded Weave flows, crude goblinoid forms emerge—feral, echo-born, and glyph-sensitive.
- **Elder Gods Ignore Them.** No pantheon claims goblinoids. Many early gods see them as flawed echoes or accidental life.
- **Earliest Memories Bound to Bone.** Bugbear shamans begin tattooing memory-glyphs into bone to resist spiritual erosion.

---
## 🪓 **[[The First Age]] – Discovery (4500–1800 years ago)**

> _The Age of Silence and Survival_

- Goblinoids live primarily underground, in ruins, or on the fringe of growing civilizations.
- Early hobgoblin militaries form chain-bound oaths, unknowingly laying the groundwork for **Ravva's Code**.
- First conflict with dwarves: **[[Runescar War]]**. Dwarves attempt to purge Thaxil glyphs.
- **First Jest-Spiral Prophet** appears near Haleya’s Rise, mocking an Air-thread rite and escaping execution by vanishing into fog.
- Goblin oral rites begin crystallizing as **anti-pattern liturgy**: a rejection of the gods’ silence.
- No formalized faith yet—only rituals of survival, mockery, and remembrance.

---
## ⚔️ **[[The Second Age]] – Heroes and Rebellion (1800–1100 years ago)**

> _The Age of Emergence_

- Goblinoids begin organizing into religious orders under duress:
    - **Crownkindlers** among goblins
    - **Chain Ascendants** among hobgoblins
    - **Fangborn** among bugbears
- **First Heresy Trials** by early Faith of the Bound Pattern cults target goblin dream-chants.
- **Veltharyn's Fire Census** labels goblinoids “Pattern Refusers.” Mass executions follow.
- A rogue human archivist writes the **Echo-Ledger**, documenting goblin rites before being unpersoned.
- Goblinoid memory-priests begin carrying glyph-bones across generations.
- **[[Ashkarra]] begins purging goblinoids** in mass under [[Soltharion]]'s caste expansions.
- **Yish the Stolen** becomes a central figure in urban goblin theology, praised for surviving faith pogroms through trickery.

---

## 💥 **[[The Third Age]] – Collapse and Rebuild (1100–0 years ago)**

> _The Age of Glyphs and Teeth_

- The Faith of the Gnawing Crown forms as a **cohesive, unstructured network** of memory, protest, and glyph binding.
- **Thaxil Cults Emerge** among goblin glyphbinders who steal and warp spell-structure from mage towers.
- [[Aeravae]] and [[Selveran]] begin tracking “Crownkindlers” as a spiritual category.
- **[[Fall of Ashkarra]].** Many goblinoids claim this was prophecy fulfilled—“The Flame choked on its own silence.”
- Spiral Jest cults cause multiple regional upsets by **hijacking funerals**, renaming the dead with laughter.
- Crown shrines begin appearing beneath ruined cities, marked by bone-laughter, mirror-blood, and broken prayer stones.
- The term **“[[Shard-Gods]]”** becomes common among goblinoids—each one a rejected divine fragment made sacred by necessity.

---
## 🔄 **[[The Fourth Age]] – Present**

> _The Age of Fracture and Flame-Memory_

- **The Fifth Cycle begins**. Goblinoid faith spreads in secret through abandoned holds, smuggler routes, and memory-fungus networks.
- **Moonscribed cults** now consult Crownkindlers to interpret echo-riddles and glyph-distortions.
- Spiral Jest cults hijack urban festivals—“Joy-Reversals” are blamed on them but cannot be prevented.
- Goblin martyr-prophets rise in fringe cities: **The Bone Echo**, **Sister Stolen Breath**, **Mirthwound the Sharp**.
- **[[07_Geography/02_Cities/Thar Vornhal]] re-institutes full glyph-cleansing protocols.**
- **[[Aeravae]]’s Listeners Below allow one goblin memory-rite per tide-cycle.**
- **[[Deep Echo Hold]] (Thaxil’s Maw)** declared a Class III Reality Breach.
- Yish-worshipping guilds now run contraband networks in four major cities.
- Nilbog prophecy spreads that one day, **a jest will be told so perfect, it collapses the Weave.**
- Some Faith of the Bound Pattern scribes begin quietly debating whether goblinoid rites are “corruptions” or “pre-Weave anomalies.”

---
## 🧵 **Civilized Goblinoids**

**Examples:**

- Hobgoblin military advisers who serve under treaty
- Goblin smiths apprenticed under dwarves (often branded)
- Bugbear archivists working in underground libraries (like those in Selveran)

**How They Survive:**

- Conform outwardly; bury or mask Crown allegiance
- Take neutral names, avoid shard-god references
- Work in logistical, magical, or mercantile niches

**Public Perception:**

- “Useful, but watch them.”
- Often assumed to be spies or saboteurs even if proven loyal
- Religious affiliation to _non-goblinoid deities_ is required in many cities

---
## 🌑 **Urban Goblinoids**

**Examples:**

- Sewer-scribes transcribing glyphic graffiti
- Spiral jesters in illegal performance cults
- Goblin smugglers working for halfling resistance groups or memory-thieves

**How They Survive:**

- Live in forgotten corners: undermarkets, smokestack warrens, plague zones
- Use glyph-binding or shard rites to protect themselves
- Trade in “dangerous memory” or forbidden spells

**Public Perception:**

- “The mold on the underside of the Pattern.”
- Blamed for magical accidents, memory leaks, unsanctioned laughter
- Subject to periodic Faith purges or forced silence rituals

---
## 🦴 **Wild Goblinoids**

**Examples:**

- Clan-bound goblins who speak to Eldritch Moons
- Fangborn bugbears with blood-glyph armor
- Ravva-chain warbands testing city defenses from the wilderness

**How They Survive:**

- Prey on ruins, caravans, or failing leyline towns
- Keep bone-memory glyphs and relics older than the Faith
- Raise jest-prophets, war-eaters, and blood-archivists

**Public Perception:**

- “Mad, cursed, or moon-touched.”
- No distinction is made between them and cultists
- Cities often issue kill-on-sight edicts for wild goblin bands near leyline sites

# 💀 Goblinoid Theology of Death

_“We are not remembered, so we must remember ourselves.”_

## 🧠 Core Beliefs

|**Concept**|**Goblinoid Doctrine**|
|---|---|
|**Death**|Not a release, but an erasure. The gods did not give goblinoids an afterlife, so death must be resisted, recorded, or rewritten.|
|**Afterlife**|No true one exists for them. Instead, goblinoids believe the dead dissolve unless **held by memory**, **carved into glyphs**, or **anchored through bloodline and echo**.|
|**Undeath**|Rarely practiced, but not taboo. If the gods won’t carry you, your bones must walk yourself. “Waking the dead” is a form of protest.|
|**Memory**|The most sacred substance. Remembering the dead is a rebellion against divine forgetfulness.|
|**Ancestry**|Not linear, but fragmentary. Most goblinoids believe they are shards of a single spirit fractured across many lives.|

---
## 🕯️ Death Rites by Sect

|**Sect**|**Death Practice**|
|---|---|
|**Shard Doctrine**|Names of the dead are carved onto cracked stones with broken glyphs. These stones are hidden in ruins or bound into flesh.|
|**Chain Ascendants**|The names of the fallen are added to Oath-Chains, which are melted down and reforged into command relics or blades.|
|**Fangborn Path**|The dead are eaten ritually by kin or buried with their blood mixed into ancestral roots. Fear is seen as a binding force.|
|**Spiral Jest**|The dead are mocked in ceremony so loudly and ridiculously that the Pattern cannot silence their echo. Sometimes corpses are dressed and paraded.|

---
## 🌀 View of the Soul

- Goblinoids believe souls are **fragments**, not wholes.
- A single goblin may carry **a splinter of a long-dead warlord**, a joke once told by a forgotten trickster, or a scream never heard.
- This splintering is seen as both **curse and legacy**—they are many, and never enough.

# 🌑 Urban Goblinoids of Dór-o Estel

_“The Pattern does not see them. That is why they survive.”_

---
## 🏙️ **Where They Dwell**

Urban goblinoids inhabit the **unseen layers of civilization**, building twisted mirror-societies under city-states and Loom-Cities. Their enclaves are:

- **Sewer-chapels** with memory-glyphs carved into calcified roots and rusted pipes
- **Smoke-stacks turned homes**, where ash masks their presence from magical detection
- **Abandoned transit tunnels**, reshaped with driftwood mosaics and scavenged mirror-shards
- **Refuse warrens** below markets, built from cracked crates, broken glyph-slates, and bone-latticework

Some cities (like Kel Varyan or Selveran) have entire **forgotten quarters**—flooded neighborhoods, plague-vault districts, or cursed memory-blocks—where urban goblinoids have claimed spiritual squatters’ rights.

---
## 🧠 **Culture of the Cracked Pattern**

Urban goblinoid culture is a blend of memory theft, mockery, survival, and sacred blasphemy. It is not merely adaptation—it is a form of **ritual protest**.

### 🕯️ **Memory Markets**

- Fragments of the past are sold in jars of ash or bound in teeth.
- Forgotten names, stolen prayers, or unsung funerals are traded for food or favors.
- Glyphs burned into trash-paper or etched onto animal bones serve as personal record and identity proof.

> “I am the third laugh of Varyn the Butcher. I bought his guilt for a dead rat and a fingernail.”

---
### 🎭 **Mockery as Resistance**

- Sacred holidays are mirrored in profane rituals (e.g., the Joy-Reversal, the Feast of Forgotten Names, the Unbirth of Threads).
- Public Faith sermons are copied by Nilbogs who perform them backwards or upside down in alleys, complete with masks and shrieks.
- Even internal caste structures are mimicked and twisted—mock Fire Concords of soot-covered goblins who “elevate” each other by coughing blood into torches.

---
### 🧷 **Path-Caste Economy**

- “Rank” is determined by what memories you carry or what lies you’ve survived.
- Goblinoids wear **memory-patches**: scraps of cloth, stolen temple ribbons, priest robe remnants, or prison bandages stitched into vests.
- The more conflicting stories your patchwork tells, the more revered (or feared) you are.

---
## 🩸 **Religious Practice**

|**Faith Practice**|**Urban Form**|
|---|---|
|**Prayer**|Whispered into cracked pipes, sewer grates, or spoken through mock sermons|
|**Sacrifice**|Food burned on coin-forges, relics of the Faith shattered and fed to rats|
|**Communion**|Inhalation of ash-dust mixed with blood-glyph spores to speak with “shard echoes”|
|**Rites of Passage**|Mock-burials in rain gutters, memory-branding with hot nails, or naming through inverted laughter|

---
## 🧪 **Magic and Glyph Use**

Urban goblinoids are **instinctive glyph-warpers**:

- They scavenge broken spell-scrolls and **force them to recite truths they were never meant to hold**.
- A glyph that once summoned light may now produce whispered memories or blind prophets.
- Crownkindlers and Thaxil adepts walk among them, transforming **forgotten scraps** into **living rituals**.

Their magic is often **nonlinear, dream-borne, or corrosive**—a twisted echo of structured casting.

---

## 🌒 **Cultural Principles**

### 1. **Memory is Territory**

Wild goblinoids map their regions by **memory density**, not landmarks.

- Forests are “heavy” if they contain unreleased names.
- Deserts are “thin” if they lack echoes.
- Some walk only in spirals to avoid “unthreading” old paths.

> _“You are not where your feet are. You are where the bones remember.”_

---
### 2. **Sacred Violence**

For many clans, **battle is a rite of memory**.

- They fight to etch glyphs into the Pattern.
- Blood spilled is considered a sacred ink.
- Victories are burned into trees, cliffs, or even into the bones of their enemies.

> _“Peace is when we forget how we got here. So we keep bleeding.”_

---
### 3. **The Hunt as Theology**

Wild goblinoids believe the gods forgot them—so they must **hunt their names** back.

- Some bugbears believe **killing a god-touched being** grants them fragments of divine memory.
- Goblins track Faith missionaries like prey, whispering the words they scream before death.
- Hobgoblins perform **chain-hunts**, binding targets in links of logic, curse, and blade.

- Upon death, the knot is crushed, **releasing a scream-glyph** into the soil.

---
### 🪓 Trial by Memory

- Goblins who forget a name or a scar are exiled.
- Trial pits contain **living memories**—bound spirits or echo-creatures one must survive or defeat.
- Success means new scars. Scars are truth. No scar, no story.

---
### 🔍 Name-Theft

- Wild goblins may steal the name of an enemy if they **kill them in complete silence**.
- That name is burned into their skin and becomes part of their identity.
- Some bugbears have **twenty names**, each earned in battle.

# 🔄 **Goblinoid Race Relations in Dór-o Estel**

## 🧝 **Elves**

### Goblinoid View:

> _“They think songs are memory. But they never sing what hurts.”_

- See elves as beautiful, sterile betrayers of memory.
- Accuse them of hoarding grief and silencing raw stories through curated ritual.
- Half-revere and half-hate their command of the Weave.
### Elven View:

> _“They carry broken echoes like toys. Dangerous, sad, pitiful things.”_

- Consider goblinoids as **fractal errors** in the Pattern—anomaly races.
- Urban elves may tolerate urban goblins as curiosities; wild goblins are feared.
- Bugbears are seen as “forgotten echoes that speak too loudly.”

---
## 🧔 **Humans**

### Goblinoid View:

> _“They forget their own dead. Then they burn us for remembering ours.”_

- Resent humans for treating goblinoids as either tools or vermin.
- Consider human empathy false: shallow, self-serving, and easily discarded.
- Envy their adaptability and loathe their short memories.
### Human View:

> _“They laugh at us even as they bleed us.”_

- Urban humans view goblins with fear, amusement, or as underclass pests.
- Some humans form alliances with goblinoid gangs or cults in borderlands.
- Hobgoblins are respected as soldiers or threats; bugbears feared as assassins.
---
## 🪓 **Dwarves**

### Goblinoid View:

> _“Stone-choked gods who kill what they can’t write into law.”_

- Dwarves are hated with ancestral bitterness.
- Blame them for the first purges of glyph-priests and death of Thaxil cults.
- Some bugbear clans hunt dwarves to reclaim “stone memory.”
### Dwarven View:

> _“They are runes gone wrong. They must be erased.”_

- Deep Law classifies goblinoid glyphs as corrupted or heretical.
- Dwarves do not distinguish between wild or urban—**all are threats to the stone order.**
- Bugbears in particular are seen as taboo monsters.
---
## 🧚 **Gnomes**

### Goblinoid View:

> _“They tinker with the world like it’s a toy. We carve scars.”_

- Wild goblins sometimes steal gnomish devices and “unbuild” them ritually.
### Gnomish View:

> _“Fascinating failures of pattern fidelity.”_

- See goblinoids as tragic but potentially useful glyph deviants.
- Some gnome scholars have gone mad studying goblin memory markets.
- Tolerated in cities if they’re “usefully weird” but rarely trusted.
---
## 🧙 **Half-Elves**

### Goblinoid View:

> _“Frayed like us. But they still think they were woven.”_

### Half-Elven View:

> _“They speak truths we weren’t taught to hear.”_

# 🩸 Goblinoid Status in the Current Age

_“The Pattern cracked. So we gathered the shards.”_

## 🪓 **General Condition**

- **Fragmented but Rising.**  
    Goblinoids remain globally scattered, leaderless, and theologically divergent, but they are experiencing a **cultural resurgence**—fuelled by instability in the Faith of the Bound Pattern and rising interest in forbidden glyphs.
- **Dreaded, not Destroyed.**  
    Goblinoids are feared more than ever—but purges have **largely failed**. Their theology adapts faster than bureaucracies can suppress it. Most cities are now trying to **contain**, not **eliminate**, goblinoid influence.
- **Echo-Reckoning Prophecies.**

# 🧠 Final Doctrine

> _"We are the missing line in their divine poem. We are the crack where the light gets in. We are the Crown, and it gnaws still."_

### Misread by the Patterned

> _“They kneel to nothing. That frightens those who kneel to everything.”_  
> —Moonscribe Faryn, moments before vanishing in the Tarlight

Most races—and especially those within the Faith of the Bound Pattern—view goblinoids as **Eldritch sympathizers**, if not outright cultists. The use of corrupted glyphs, laughter during celestial breaches, and the presence of goblin rituals near Hollow activity feeds this fear.

But this is a fundamental misreading.

Goblinoids do not worship the Eldritch.  
They **mock them**, **mirror them**, and **use their symbols as shields**.

To the goblinoid mind, **survival is theft**—and the greatest theft is to take the tools of your erasure and make them ridiculous. Their rituals are not homage; they are defiance.

To those who live by divine law, such irreverence looks indistinguishable from allegiance.

And so the goblins are hunted for laughter.
