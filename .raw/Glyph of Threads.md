# Glyph of Threads – Aeloria’s Loom, the Hand of Fate
> _"Nothing is without pattern, no moment is without consequence. The Loom spins on."_

---
## 🧱 Layer
**Layer:** Primal
**Tier Ranking**: 1 (First Tier, foundational)  
**Domain(s):** Fate, Guidance, Causality, Connection  
**Opposing Glyph(s):** Possibly [[The Glyph of Severance]] (by antithesis)  
**Symbol:** A woven knot inscribed with concentric circles  

---
## 🔷 Core Meaning

- The Glyph of Threads represents the **interconnectivity of all things**—events, choices, and beings.
- It is the **framework by which destiny manifests**—not as a lawgiver, but as a weaver.
- The glyph does not control—it **observes, maps, and reflects the consequence** of action and inaction.
---
### **The Nature of the Loom**

The **Glyph of Threads does not choose paths—it records them.**

- It is **why time moves forward and not backward.**
- It is **why history repeats, yet never in quite the same way.**
- It is **why prophecy is both a blessing and a curse.**

> “To some, destiny. To others, a chain.” But to the Glyph of Threads, it is simply **the pattern—the eternal unfolding of cause and effect.**

🛠️ **GM Tip:**  
When a character touches fate—through prophecy, irreversible choice, or binding oath—Threads may respond. Visions may echo before or after the act, and players may feel deja vu, hear whispers of their own voice, or glimpse woven patterns in dust or flame.

---
## 🌀 Narrative Role

- Threads is the **anchor of prophecy** and the logic behind the **[[Faith of the Bound Pattern]]**.
- It forms the **moral architecture** of divine justice, reincarnation, and karmic law.
- Aeloria spins the Threads not to enforce, but to **reveal what already is**, aligning her mythic role with inevitable consequence.

---
## ✨ Magical Influence

- Used in divination, retrocognition, fate-binding rituals, and glyph-weaving magic.
- Powerful rituals involving **patterned outcomes or cyclic repetition** must invoke this glyph.
- Thread-magic often anchors **contingency spells, oaths, soul-forging, or magical contracts**.

---
## 🧩 Divine Associations

- **[[Aeloria]]** is the origin and guide of this glyph. She is known as the **Weaver Eternal** and spins the Loom to reveal—not shape—destiny.
- **[[Nyzara]]**, guardian of memory, threads echoes through the past and reverent remembrance.
- **[[Gaeldros]]** invokes Threads in divine rulings, as justice must follow the cause-and-effect woven into law.
- **[[The Spirit Pantheon]]** honours this glyph as the metaphysical root of all fate echoes and soul transitions.
---

## ⚠️ Eldritch Corruption Risk

- When broken, Threads can tangle or **fracture destiny**:  
  - **Fates unravel** and become self-conflicting.  
  - Prophets go mad trying to read **impossible futures**.  
  - The **past begins to rewrite itself**—causing paradoxes, memory collapse, and self-erasing phenomena.

- Known corrupted forms include:
  - **Thread-Eaters** – beings who consume destiny.
  - **Snarlbinds** – knots of self-cancelling lives.
  - **Woven Nulls** – mortals who never existed but still influence others.

---
## 🧠 Player Tie-ins

- **Clerics, Bards, and Warlocks** who manipulate fate or prophecy.
- Sorcerers born during overlapping Threads cycles may be marked by **patterned power** or foreseen destinies.
- Possible subclass ideas:
  - *Weaver of Threads* (Wizard) – cast spells by tethering future outcomes.
  - *Loomspeaker* (Bard) – narrates destiny into action.

---
## 📜 Associated Myths & Fragments

- *The Seven Threads of Regret* – a tale of a goddess who tried to undo fate and was bound in her own past.
- *The Shroud That Spoke Itself* – a divine prophecy cloth that updated its verses as decisions were made.
- *The Third Thread of Ashkarra* – the deadly omen of repeating alignment that led to the city’s doom.

---
## 🏛️ Cultural or Religious Significance

- Central to the **Faith of the Bound Pattern**, where it is symbolised not by a deity, but by the **“Silent Hand”**— presumed to be the pantheons of gods that guides the weave of fate.
- In Loom-Cities and sanctums, **Loom-Chambers** are kept sacred—silent halls where fate is traced in string and ink, but no god is named.
- In many cultures, **birth cords, weaving rites, and thread-cutting ceremonies** mark the passage of destiny—but the one who watches the Loom is never mentioned, only honoured in absence.

> _“We do not speak of the hand. We only follow the thread.”_  
> — Proverb of the Faith of the Bound Pattern
---
## 🏛️ Places of Worship & Legacy

- The **[[Loomspire of Nyzara]]** in [[Aeravae]] contains a **living tapestry**—a magical weave that subtly shifts with the actions of those who gaze upon it.
- **Threadwells** beneath [[Kel Varyan]] allow priests to draw long cords of fate for the recently born, a rite called **The First Patterning**.
- The ruins of **[[Ashkarra]]** still echo with Thread residue—scholars say the glyph resisted the unraveling even as the [[World Anchor]] collapsed.


---
## 🧵 Fate & Glyphic Mechanics

- All **[[Fate-bound magic]]**, including **divination**, **memory invocation**, and **prophetic dreaming**, anchors through this glyph.
- The **Echoed Weaving** not only carries Will, but also walks upon Threads—visions of their path ripple across the Loom.
- When Threads are invoked, **glyphic magic** becomes *cyclical*, *responsive*, or *self-evolving*, triggering latent echoes of past uses or consequences.
- **Spirit-linked Glyphs** (e.g. Memory, Oath, Reflection) all inherit part of Threads as a conceptual framework.
