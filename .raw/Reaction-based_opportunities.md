# Reaction-Based Opportunities

> *"The moment between an attack and its consequence — that is where battles are truly won."*

---

## 1. What Are Reactions?

Reactions are split-second responses to incoming attacks, giving defenders the ability to retaliate, reposition, or seize tactical advantage rather than passively absorbing damage. Every character has at least one reaction per round — the DCS expands how and when that reaction can be spent.

There are two categories of reaction in FEARS:

- **Standard Reactions** — used at any point in the round when a valid trigger occurs (opportunity attacks, readied actions, class features).
- **Tactical Reactions** — gained by deliberately forgoing Evasion. You take the hit in exchange for an immediate response.

---

## 2. Tactical Reactions — Forgoing Evasion

### How It Works

Rather than rolling Evasion, you may choose to **deliberately take the hit** to gain a **free Tactical Reaction**. This is a high-risk, high-reward exchange: you absorb the blow but immediately seize an opening.

**Rules:**
- You may forego Evasion **once per attack, per attacker**, per round. Each enemy's separate attack creates its own opportunity. You cannot forego Evasion against the same attacker's second hit in a multi-attack sequence unless it comes from a new action (such as Action Surge, which creates a new window).
- Forgoing Evasion does **not** consume your normal reaction for the round — both remain available.
- When you forego Evasion, **immediately declare which Tactical Reaction you are using** — before damage is rolled. This allows reactions like Shield Block to modify the incoming damage. You then take the full hit, and execute your declared reaction.
- You lose **−1 Momentum** for taking the hit.
- You take the **full damage** of the attack after DR. All additional effects (poison, knockback, conditions) also apply.

### Tactical Reaction Options

| Reaction | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Counterattack** | Make a melee or ranged weapon attack against the attacker with **advantage**. |
| **Magic Attack** | Make a touch spell attack against the attacker with advantage, or a ranged spell attack without advantage. If you have already used a levelled spell this turn, the spell must be a cantrip. |
| **Trap Weapon** | Contest the attacker's Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) to lock their weapon. On success, the attacker cannot use the weapon until they succeed on a Strength check (DC = 8 + your PB + STR or DEX mod) as an action. |
| **Disarm** | Contest the attacker's Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics). On success, knock their weapon to a space within 5 feet of them. |
| **Shield Reaction** | Use one Shield Reaction (Block, Bash, or Deflect) as a free action. When using Shield Block, declare it before damage is rolled — see the timing note below. |
| **Grapple Back** | Immediately initiate a grapple (Athletics vs Acrobatics/Athletics). |
| **Trip / Sweep** | Attempt to knock the attacker prone — they make a Dexterity saving throw (DC = 8 + your PB + STR mod). |
| **Tactical Retreat** | Move up to 5 feet in any direction without provoking opportunity attacks. Requires open space. |
| **Weapon Bind** | Lock the attacker's weapon with yours. Both you and the attacker suffer −2 to attacks until one breaks the bind (opposed Strength check as an action). |
| **Intimidating Glare** | Force the attacker to make a **Wisdom save (DC = 8 + PB + CHA mod)** or suffer disadvantage on their next attack roll and lose 1 Momentum. Once per encounter per target. |

### Momentum and Tactical Reactions

| Outcome | Momentum Change |
|---|---|
| Forego Evasion (take the hit) | −1 MS |
| Successful Counterattack or Disarm via Forgoing Evasion | +2 MS |

> **Design Intent:** The net Momentum gain from a successful Counterattack (+2 MS) after forgoing Evasion (−1 MS) is a **deliberate +1 net reward for risky play**. A character who takes a hit to deliver one is making a bold tactical choice, and the system rewards that aggression. High-DR characters — Barbarians, armoured Fighters, Paladins — benefit most from this exchange, as the damage they absorb is mitigated by their soak.

## 3. Shield Reactions

Shield Reactions are available to any character wielding a shield. They do **not** require forgoing Evasion — they can be used as a standard reaction at any valid trigger point. However, they may also be used as a Tactical Reaction option when forgoing Evasion.

| Reaction | Trigger | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Shield Block** | After an attack hits, before damage is rolled | Add **+3 DR** against that attack at all locations covered by the shield. |
| **Shield Deflect** | After an attack hits | Contest the attacker's attack roll with **Strength (Athletics)**. On success, the attack is completely negated. On failure, take full damage. |
| **Shield Bash** | In response to a melee attack or opportunity attack | Deal **1d6 + Strength modifier** bludgeoning damage and attempt to push the attacker **5 feet** (Strength vs Strength or Dexterity contest). |

> **Great Shield Note:** Great shield wielders use Brace only and cannot Dodge. Their Shield Block applies the +3 DR bonus to **all locations** when interposed — representing the shield being actively thrown across the body. The great shield's base +5 DR applies at the arm location at all times while wielded.

---

## 4. Standard Reactions

Standard reactions are spent in response to events that don't require forgoing Evasion. They follow the normal rules from the core 5E reaction framework, extended by FEARS mechanics:

| Trigger | Reaction Available |
|---|---|
| Enemy moves through your reach | Opportunity Attack |
| Ally is attacked within reach | Intercept (class features only, e.g., Paladin Interpose) |
| Enemy attempts to grapple or shove you | Contested response (as part of the grapple/shove resolution) |
| Defender rolls a Nat 20 on Evasion | Free attack or action (no reaction cost) |
| Class feature trigger (Riposte, Primal Defiance, etc.) | As specified by the feature |

Standard reactions **do** consume your normal reaction for the round unless a feature explicitly states otherwise.

---

## 5. Reaction Economy

Understanding the full reaction economy per round is important for tactical play:

| Reaction Type | Uses Per Round | Costs Normal Reaction? |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard Reaction** (OA, class features) | 1 | Yes |
| **Tactical Reaction** (forgoing Evasion) | Once per attack per enemy | No |
| **Shield Reaction** (used as standard reaction) | 1 (shares with Standard) | Yes |
| **Shield Reaction** (used as Tactical Reaction) | Once per attack per enemy | No |
| **Nat 20 Defender** (free attack on perfect defence) | 1 per occurrence | No |

> **Example:** A Fighter is attacked twice in a round — once by an orc, once by a hobgoblin. The Fighter can forego Evasion against the **orc's** attack (gaining a free Tactical Reaction) and still have their normal reaction available for the hobgoblin's attack or for an opportunity attack later.

> **Note:** At **+4 Momentum (Level 17+)**, PCs gain 1 extra reaction per round. This additional reaction can be used for any Standard or Shield Reaction.

## 6. Class Features and Reactions

Certain classes gain enhanced reaction abilities. These interact with both Standard and Tactical Reactions depending on the feature:

| Class / Subclass | Feature | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Fighter (Battle Master)** | Precision Reaction | Spend a Superiority Die to add the result to a Counterattack or Disarm attempt. |
| **Fighter (Champion)** | Indomitable Defence | Advantage on contested rolls for Shield Deflect, Bash, and Disarm. |
| **Barbarian (Path of the Bear)** | Totemic Resistance | Gain resistance to the triggering attack's damage type when performing a Tactical Reaction. |
| **Barbarian (Path of the Berserker)** | Reckless Counter | While Raging, Counterattacks via forgoing Evasion do not gain advantage but use Reckless Attack rules instead. |
| **Barbarian (Level 7)** | Primal Defiance | When you would lose Momentum from taking damage, use your reaction to reduce the loss by 1 (minimum 0) and gain +1 MS. Uses = PB per short rest. |
| **Rogue** | Opportunistic Retaliation | When using Uncanny Dodge alongside a Counterattack, halve incoming damage before retaliating. |
| **Paladin (Oath of the Crown)** | Shield of Resolve | Shield Block provides +5 DR instead of +3 DR. |
| **Paladin (Oath of Conquest)** | Frightening Bash | Shield Bash forces a Wisdom save (DC = 8 + PB + CHA mod) or the attacker is Frightened until the end of your next turn. |
| **Ranger** | Hunter's Precision | Counterattacks against Hunter's Mark targets deal bonus damage equal to Wisdom modifier. |
| **Ranger (Favoured Enemy)** | Favoured Disarm | Disarm attempts against favoured enemies automatically succeed on a contested roll of 15 or higher. |

## 8. Combat Flow with Reactions

### Tactical reaction (forgoing Evasion)

| Step | Details |
|---|---|
| **1. Attack lands** | Attacker's roll beats the defender's Evasion, or defender deliberately forgoes rolling. |
| **2. Declare Tactical Reaction** | Immediately declare which Tactical Reaction you are using — before damage is rolled. This allows Shield Block to apply its +3 DR to the incoming damage. All other reactions are declared here and executed in Step 4 after damage. |
| **3. Damage resolves** | Roll and apply damage (after DR, including Shield Block if declared). All conditions and effects from the attack apply. |
| **4. Resolve the reaction** | Execute the declared reaction — contested rolls, attacks, or effects as stated. |
| **5. Continue the round** | Defender's normal reaction remains available. |

> **Shield Block timing:** Because you commit to forgoing Evasion before the damage roll, you may declare Shield Block as your Tactical Reaction at the same moment — before dice are rolled. This is the one Tactical Reaction that must be declared before damage, because it modifies the damage roll itself.

### Shield reaction (as standard reaction)

| Step | Details |
|---|---|
| **1. Attack declared against you** | Attacker rolls. |
| **2. Evasion Roll** | Defender rolls Evasion normally. |
| **3. Attack hits** | If the attack would land, declare Shield Block or Deflect before damage is rolled. |
| **4. Resolve** | Apply Block's +3 DR, or resolve Deflect's Athletics contest. |
| **5. Reaction spent** | Normal reaction consumed. |

## 9. Examples

### Scenario 1: Counterattack (Tactical Reaction)

A Fighter (medium armour, Torso DR 4) is attacked by a hobgoblin (attack roll 18, damage 1d12+3).

1. **Fighter forgoes Evasion** and immediately declares **Counterattack** as their Tactical Reaction.
2. **Damage resolves:** Hobgoblin rolls 10 + 3 = 13. DR 4 → **9 damage taken**. Fighter loses −1 MS.
3. **Fighter executes Counterattack:** `1d20 + 6` with advantage → hits → deals 9 damage. Fighter gains +2 MS.
4. **Net result:** Fighter took 9 damage, dealt 9, and gained +1 net MS. Normal reaction still available.

---

### Scenario 2: Disarm (Tactical Reaction)

A Rogue (light armour, Torso DR 2) is attacked by a bandit (attack roll 17, damage 1d8+2).

1. **Rogue forgoes Evasion** and immediately declares **Disarm** as their Tactical Reaction.
2. **Damage resolves:** Bandit rolls 7 + 2 = 9. DR 2 → **7 damage taken**. Rogue loses −1 MS.
3. **Rogue executes Disarm — Contested roll:** Rogue rolls `1d20 + 5 (DEX)` = 18. Bandit rolls `1d20 + 3 (STR)` = 15. Rogue wins → bandit's sword lands 5 feet away.
4. **Rogue gains +2 MS.** Bandit is now unarmed.

---

### Scenario 3: Shield Deflect (Standard Reaction)

A Paladin (standard shield) is attacked by an ogre (attack roll 19, damage 2d8+4).

1. **Paladin rolls Evasion** — fails (Brace roll 14 vs attack roll 19).
2. **Attack would land.** Paladin declares Shield Deflect before damage is rolled.
3. **Athletics contest:** Paladin rolls `1d20 + 4 (STR)` = 21 vs ogre's attack roll 19. Paladin wins → attack completely negated. No damage.
4. **Normal reaction consumed.** Paladin gains +1 MS from the successful Deflect.

---

### Scenario 4: Forgoing Evasion Twice in One Round

A Barbarian (heavy armour while Raging) is attacked by both an orc and a hobgoblin in the same round.

1. **Orc attacks.** Barbarian forgoes Evasion → takes 8 damage (after DR). Declares Counterattack → hits for 12 damage. −1 MS + 2 MS = **+1 net MS**.
2. **Hobgoblin attacks.** Barbarian can forego Evasion again — it's a different attacker's attack. Takes 6 damage (after DR). Declares Grapple Back → succeeds. −1 MS + 1 MS (successful action) = **net neutral**.
3. **Normal reaction** still available for an opportunity attack if something moves through reach.

---

### Scenario 5: Shield Block as a Tactical Reaction

A Fighter (great shield, Brace only) is attacked by a warhammer-wielding knight (attack roll 20, damage 2d8+4).

1. **Fighter forgoes Evasion** — commits to taking the hit.
2. **Fighter immediately declares Shield Block** as their Tactical Reaction. Because Shield Block modifies the incoming damage, it must be declared before damage is rolled.
3. **Damage resolves with Block active:** Knight rolls 2d8+4 = 16. Shield Block adds +3 DR to all locations → effective DR for Torso becomes 6 + 3 = 9. 16 − 9 = **7 damage taken**. Fighter loses −1 MS.
4. **Fighter's normal reaction remains available** — the Tactical Reaction did not consume it.

---

## 10. Design Summary

- **Tactical Reactions** reward bold, aggressive play — taking a hit to gain an edge is a real tactical option, not a fallback.
- **Declare your Tactical Reaction immediately when forgoing Evasion** — before damage is rolled. This is what allows Shield Block to modify incoming damage. All other reactions are declared now and executed after damage resolves.
- **Forgoing Evasion** is once per attack per enemy — each attacker's separate attack is its own opportunity window.
- **Shield Reactions** do not require forgoing Evasion and can be stacked alongside Tactical Reactions in the same round (one as free via forgoing, one as standard reaction).
- **Momentum** makes the risk/reward explicit: −1 for taking the hit, +2 for a successful response.
- **Class features** extend and modify the reaction system at higher levels — the base system rewards all martial builds, the class features reward specific ones.
