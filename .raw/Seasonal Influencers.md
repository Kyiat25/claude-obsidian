# 🌱 Verdant Rise
**Months:** *First Bloom* → *Emerald Tithe*  
**Pantheon Trends:** Strongest with [[The Earth Pantheon]] and [[The Water Pantheon]]  
**Tone:** Renewal, planting, cautious optimism

---

### 🌿 Lore Overview
Verdant Rise marks the **awakening of the land**. Meltwater surges through river veins, seeds quicken beneath fertile soils, and traders emerge from winter stores with goods to sell.  
The gods of earth and water are most present, but their favour is not guaranteed—too much rain drowns the fields, too little withers them before they can sprout.  
Across [[Dór-o Estel]], planting festivals bless the soil, offerings are given to river spirits, and the first caravans set out to re-link cities cut off during [[Deepveil]].

---
### 📜 Historic Precedents

- **Year of the Hollow Flood (Cycle 281)** – Excessive Water Pantheon influence drowned crops for three consecutive years, leading to the Bread Riots of Aeravae.  
- **Ashkarra's Last Spring (Cycle 364)** – During a triple Spirit Thread alignment, omens of renewal turned to madness as an Eldritch Moon rose during planting season.  
- **The Shifting Furrows (Cycle 229)** – Air Pantheon dominance moved soil patterns so drastically that planting maps became useless overnight.

# 🔥 Scorchpeak
**Months:** *Suncrest* → *High Ember*  
**Pantheon Trends:** Strongest with [[The Fire Pantheon]] and [[The Air Pantheon]]  
**Tone:** Heat, storms, passion, and conflict

---
### 🌋 Lore Overview
Scorchpeak is the crucible of the year.  
The sun burns longest and hottest, roads shimmer in waves of heat, and tempers flare as resources grow scarce. Trade is risky but lucrative — caravans braving the desert winds and high seas can earn fortunes, but failure is often fatal. The Fire Pantheon burns brightest now, but other gods seize the moment to twist the heat to their own designs: Air to drive storms, Earth to crack the land, Water to flood the scorched coasts, Spirit to ignite passion or unrest, and the Eldritch… to make the sky itself seem alien.

---

### 📜 Historic Precedents

- **The Red Gale (Cycle 197)** – A Fire–Air Pantheon alignment birthed week-long burning sandstorms across the western marches, burying three cities in ash.  
- **The Flooded Ember (Cycle 233)** – Water Pantheon dominance melted glacial caps too quickly, sending catastrophic floods into southern plains.  
- **The Great Iron March (Cycle 342)** – Earth Pantheon influence allowed massive military mobilization; war campaigns were supplied without drought hindrance.  
- **The Burning Procession (Cycle 308)** – Spirit Pantheon rituals lit entire forests as offerings; these “sacrificial burns” devastated trade for decades.  
- **Ashfall Eclipse (Cycle 367)** – Eldritch Pantheon sway combined with an Eldritch Moon to blacken the summer sun; crops blistered overnight.

# 🌾 Twilight Tithe
**Months:** *Harvestcall* → *Amberwane*  
**Pantheon Trends:** Balanced sway between [[The Earth Pantheon]], [[The Water Pantheon]], and [[The Spirit Pantheon]], with frequent swings to others.  
**Tone:** Harvest, trade, and the looming shadow of winter.

---

### 🌱 Lore Overview
Twilight Tithe is the hinge of the Verdant Loom year — the moment between the bright pulse of Verdant Rise and the cold clasp of Deepveil. This is when fields either deliver their promise or betray it.  

Markets swell with grain, fruit, ore, and crafted goods; guilds tally profits; and temples prepare their year’s grand offerings. But every community feels the risk — one storm, one blight, or one divine whim could undo a year’s work.  

It is here that the gods turn most political: Earth seeks to secure its bounty, Water to cleanse or drown, Spirit to inspire or unsettle, Air to carry storms or winds of fortune, Fire to burn away the weak or drive celebration, and the Eldritch to rot harvests in the field.  

Because the Threads Turn often brushes this season, **multiple pantheons may act at once**, amplifying fortune or disaster.  

---

### 📜 Historic Precedents

- **The Seven-Granary Pact (Cycle 189)** – Earth Pantheon dominance ensured perfect harvests, creating surplus so vast that granaries fed five cycles of winters.  
- **The Rotten March (Cycle 242)** – Eldritch Pantheon influence brought black rot to stored crops; famine spread in less than a month.  
- **The Skyshare Festival (Cycle 311)** – Air Pantheon winds carried trade ships in record time, flooding markets with exotic goods before Deepveil.  
- **Ash-Harvest Rite (Cycle 334)** – Fire Pantheon celebrants burned plague fields, halting blight but causing a trade collapse in the south.  
- **The Spirit Census (Cycle 359)** – Spirit Pantheon prophets declared each soul’s worth in offerings; cities bled wealth to temples, triggering revolt.

# ❄ Deepveil
**Months:** *Frostwane* → *Ashfade*  
**Pantheon Trends:** Winter sway often leans toward [[The Water Pantheon]], [[The Spirit Pantheon]], and [[The Air Pantheon]], but any can seize the season.  
**Tone:** Survival, scarcity, and the testing of all that was built before.

---

### 🧊 Lore Overview
Deepveil is the locked door of the year. Trade slows to a crawl, harvests lie buried, and even the most gifted mages work harder to draw on the Weave through the muffling cold.  

It is not merely a time of hunger — it is a time when the gods and the Threads seem to ask: *“What will endure?”* The season rewards preparation, bold ventures, and trust in the strength of bonds. It also punishes those who misjudged Twilight Tithe’s markets or wasted Verdant Rise’s plenty.  

In a gentle Deepveil, winter roads may hold, sea-ice can form safe crossings, and trade may flow between hearth-lit cities. In a chaotic one, the world becomes a labyrinth of frost storms, frozen rivers, and icebound silence — where even the gods seem quieter, save for those who feast on ruin.  

Deepveil is infamous for its **Threads Turn volatility** — multiple pantheons often vie for influence, making miracles and calamities arrive side-by-side.

---
### 📜 Historic Precedents

- **The Frostbond Accord (Cycle 211)** – Air and Water Pantheons jointly froze a warfront, allowing peace talks to take place on the ice.  
- **The Black Winter (Cycle 243)** – Eldritch Pantheon turned snow into ash; famine claimed half the northern valleys.  
- **The Frozen Bridge (Cycle 277)** – Earth Pantheon’s icebound stonework opened a winter caravan route; trade boomed.  
- **The Howling Hunt (Cycle 308)** – Spirit Pantheon sent spectral wolves to cull a growing undead horde in the north.  
- **The Ember Lanterns (Cycle 362)** – Fire Pantheon’s eternal braziers kept a city warm through the coldest year on record.

# 🌌 Auroratide 
"The Threads Turn"
**Months:** *Aurotide* (single month season)  
**Pantheon Trends:** None dominant; two Pantheons always influence the season.  
**Tone:** Instability, opportunity, and divine crosscurrents.

---

### ✨ Lore Overview
Auroratide is not simply a season — it is the breath between years, the instant when the Weave shifts and the Threads take new paths. In the skies, the aurora flares with colors unseen at any other time, rippling like the god-hand of Aelys herself across the heavens.

For mortals, Auroratide is unsettling. The natural order frays: rivers reverse, day and night blur, and the domains of the Pantheons overlap in ways both wondrous and catastrophic. It is the only time of year when **two Pantheons** hold sway at once, each weaving their will into the same month.

Some cities celebrate Auroratide as a festival of change, bargaining for the blessings of both Threads. Others dread it, fearing the sudden loss of stability, market collapse, or the risk that both Pantheons might be Eldritch — a herald of disaster known as the **Black Aurora**.

---

### 📜 Historic Precedents

- **The Twin Harvest (Cycle 191)** – Spirit and Earth Pantheons doubled crop yield overnight; markets overflowed.  
- **The Black Aurora (Cycle 244)** – Both Pantheon rolls were Eldritch; three Moons rose at once, and the city of Thryss was erased.  
- **The Steel Gale (Cycle 276)** – Air and Fire combined, forging a storm that hardened into metal shards mid-flight.  
- **The Frozen Song (Cycle 301)** – Water and Spirit froze a city in stasis for 10 days; when thawed, no one had aged.  
- **The Ash-Bloom (Cycle 355)** – Earth and Fire burned cropland, then regrew it overnight in flowering stone.
