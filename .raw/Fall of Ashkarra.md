# **The Lead-Up to Catastrophe**

## **100 Years Before: The Seeds of Arrogance**

- [[Ashkarra]] at peak power as mightiest [[Loom-City]]
- Imperial leaders began dismissing Faith of the Bound Pattern warnings
- Religious supremacism - Burning Concord declared itself superior to other faiths
- Aggressive expansion campaigns straining magical resources
- Capital city of the [[Dragonborn Empire]]

## **50 Years Before: First Spirit Thread - The Fatal Decision**

- **First Spirit Thread alignment** brought unprecedented prosperity and power
- **Critical Error:** Imperial authorities declared Spirit Thread "divinely approved" for extended use
- This directly violated the fundamental law that Threads must rotate
- Enhanced prophetic abilities and magical power convinced them they were exempt from cosmic laws

## **30 Years Before: Second Spirit Thread - Mounting Problems**

- **Unprecedented decision** to maintain Spirit alignment for second consecutive cycle
- **Early warning signs appeared:**
    - Prophetic contradictions and paradoxical visions
    - Glyph instability - sacred inscriptions changing meaning spontaneously
    - Temporal hiccups - brief moments of backward time flow
    - Identity confusion among citizens
    - Magical feedback effects

### **International Opposition Ignored:**

- Other Loom-Cities demanded Thread rotation (dismissed as jealousy)
- Faith of the Bound Pattern declared Ashkarra heretical (ignored)
- Internal dissent from some Embermarked priests (suppressed)
- Military reports of strange phenomena (covered up)

## **10 Years Before: Third Spirit Thread - The Point of No Return**

- **The Third Thread** - never before attempted in history
- **Immediate spiritual crisis:**
    - Weave stagnation - energy accumulating instead of flowing
    - Echo redundancy - every action creating multiple reflections
    - Memory overflow - past/present/future overlapping
    - Reality strain - physical laws becoming unreliable

### **The Ember Crucible Begins Failing:**

- Sacred flame burning in reverse (consuming heat/light instead of fuel)
- Spiral of Flame and Form inverting its rotation
- Obsidian mirrors reflecting spiritual rather than physical forms
- Temporal loops in judgments and ceremonies

## **Final 3 Years: Reality Breakdown**

- **Names became fluid** - citizens' identities shifting unpredictably
- **Caste lines blurred** - sacred hierarchy becoming meaningless
- **Time disobedience** - past and future bleeding into present
- **Reflections gained independence** - mirrors showing different realities
- **Memory became unreliable** - historical records changing themselves

### **Last Warning Ignored:**

Prophet **Tyraen of the Pool-Sky** gave final prophecy:

> _"When the Thread weaves no longer, When echoes outnumber breath, When the moonlight comes in chorus, Then shall flame forget its name."_

**Imperial Response:** Tyraen was mocked, imprisoned, then disappeared. Prophecy declared heretical.

# **THE COLLAPSE: The Day of Twelve Shadows**

### **Morning: The Sky Cracks**

During the Spirit Festival (now meaningless ceremony), **twelve Eldritch Moons** appeared glyphically in the sky:

- Soundless lightning cracks appeared
- All reflections stopped working properly
- Saphyran Lake began boiling into crystal glass
- Oasis of Threads spoke in reversed ancient languages
- Citizens saw infinite versions of themselves in the sky

### **Noon: The Ember Crucible's Final Crisis**

The World Anchor reached critical failure:

1. **Flame Reversal** - eternal flame turned inside out, consuming reality
2. **Glyph Collapse** - Spiral of Flame and Form completed inversion
3. **Anchor Implosion** - World Anchor imploded, pulling reality into Null Reflection
4. **Final Glyph** - last inscription was "Ashkarra" written backward in bleeding script

### **Afternoon: The Unmaking**

Not destruction but **"unwriting"** from reality:

1. **Reflections stopped reflecting**, shadows gained independence
2. **Names became fluid**, identity markers lost meaning
3. **Time flowed in multiple directions** simultaneously
4. **Physical space folded** into impossible geometries
5. **The city ceased to exist** in any comprehensible way

### **Population Fate:**

- **Most citizens simply ceased to exist** - erased from reality
- **Some became Eldritch echoes** - twisted reflections in temporal loops
- **Others absorbed into the breach** between realities
- **A few survivors escaped** but were erased from all records and memories

## **Immediate Aftermath:**

- **Global magical catastrophe** - leylines snapped across continents
- **Dragonborn Empire immediately collapsed** into warring fragments
- **Reality breach created the Shard Wastes** - permanent zone of instability
- **Beginning of the Age of Shadows** - 150+ years of magical chaos

**The Key Point:** Ashkarra fell not from external attack or natural disaster, but from **spiritual pride and violation of cosmic law**. They believed their power made them exempt from the fundamental rules governing reality - and reality itself "corrected" this error by unmaking them entirely.
