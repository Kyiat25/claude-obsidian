 ## *The Short Memory and the Forged Faith*

> *"We do not remember what the elves remember. We were not there when the world was young. But the gods spoke to us, and we listened. They told us truth, and we believed. They gave us purpose, and we accepted. Were we wrong? Were we deceived? Or were we simply... human?"*  
> — **Excerpt from "The Penitent's Question," banned theological text, author unknown**

## The Cosmological Lie (Player-Facing - What Humans/Dragonborn Believe)

**What We Were Taught:**
- The gods created all mortal races during the First Age
- Each pantheon shaped their chosen peoples
- **Soltharion the Sun Tyrant** forged the dragonborn in sacred fire
- The **Five Pantheons** gave humanity the Threads of fate to follow
- The elves rejected divine grace and clung to primitive nature worship
- The Drow fell into darkness through their own ambition
- The Faith of the Bound Pattern is the culmination of divine will

**What We Know:**
- Gods speak to us in visions and miracles
- Divine magic works - prayers are answered
- Following the Threads brings order and prosperity
- Those who reject divine authority bring chaos
- We are young, but we are faithful
- Our purpose is clear: serve the Pattern, maintain the Threads

**What We Don't Question:**
- Why elves live so much longer than us
- Why they refuse simple truths we accept
- Why their "sacred groves" feel older than our oldest temples
- Why the sea elves won't speak to us
- Why half-elves seem caught between two truths
- Why some of our own ancestors helped elves hide

## The Truth (DM Only - What Actually Happened)

**The Human Tragedy:**
- Humans were created by **Astralor** like all races
- They emerged **later** than elves (~8,000 years ago, vs elves' 20,000+)
- Their **short lifespans** (80-100 years) meant no cultural continuity
- **Divine Spark gods** found them **easy to claim** - no pre-divine memory
- By the time humans developed civilization, gods had already organized
- Humans **never knew a world without organized divine worship**

**The Dragonborn's Compact:**
- Dragonborn emerged ~10,000 years ago during the Loomless Time
- **Soltharion** found them first - before they developed culture
- He offered them **dominion** in exchange for **absolute loyalty**
- They accepted, becoming the **first race to truly worship a god**
- This made them the **model** for what gods wanted from all races
- They became enforcers of divine will - willingly, genuinely, faithfully

**Why This Matters:**
- Humans and dragonborn don't remember a world without gods
- They have no Pattern-sense like elves
- Their faith is **genuine** - they truly believe
- They were manipulated, but they don't know it
- They became **weapons against truth** without realizing it
- The tragedy is that they're **both victims and perpetrators**

## The First Humans: Born Into a World Already Claimed

### **Timeline:** ~8,000 years ago (Late Loomless Time)

**When Humans Emerged:**

Unlike elves, who walked the world when it was still forming, humans arrived to find:
- **Elemental Embodiments** already established (Vatharun, Nereus, Soltharion, etc.)
- **Divine Spark gods** already organizing into pantheons
- **Elves** with 12,000+ years of unbroken tradition
- **Dragonborn** already in service to Soltharion
- **The world already mapped, claimed, and explained**

**The First Contact:**

When proto-human tribes first encountered the divine, they had no context:

> *"The sky spoke. The earth answered. Fire walked among us wearing flesh. We were small, weak, short-lived. What else could we do but kneel?"*  
> — **The First Testament**, oldest human religious text

**What Gods Offered:**
- **Protection** from natural dangers
- **Purpose** in a bewildering world
- **Explanation** for forces they couldn't understand
- **Power** through divine magic
- **Identity** as chosen peoples

**What Gods Demanded:**
- **Worship** as creators
- **Obedience** to divine law
- **Service** to divine will
- **Rejection** of alternative truths
- **Enforcement** of divine authority

**The Critical Difference:**

**Elves** encountered the Weave first, then gods claimed authority over it.  
**Humans** encountered gods first, and never knew anything else.

To humans, the idea that gods **didn't** create them was as incomprehensible as the idea that the sun doesn't rise. They had no memory of anything different.

## The Dragonborn Compact: Soltharion's First Servants

### **Timeline:** ~10,000 years ago (Mid-Loomless Time)

**The First Meeting:**

Dragonborn emerged during the chaos of the Loomless Time, when reality was still stabilizing. They were:
- Naturally fire-resistant
- Physically powerful
- Instinctively hierarchical
- Without developed culture or religion

**Soltharion found them before they found themselves.**

> *"The Sun Tyrant descended from the sky, wreathed in flames that did not harm us. He spoke not as a stranger, but as a father finding his lost children. He said: 'I forged you in fire. You are mine. And I will make you great.'"*  
> — **The Compact Testament**, sacred dragonborn text

**The Offer:**

Soltharion offered the dragonborn:

**Glory:**
- They would be the **first among mortals**
- The **mightiest warriors** of the age
- The **champions of divine will**
- The **example** for all other races

**Purpose:**
- To serve as **enforcers of divine order**
- To **spread the faith** to other races
- To **punish** those who rejected divine authority
- To **prove** that mortals could be perfected through service

**Power:**
- Enhanced strength through divine blessing
- Fire breath as a gift from their "creator"
- Extended lifespans (300+ years, vs human 80)
- Direct divine communication with Soltharion

**What He Demanded:**

**Absolute loyalty:**
- No questioning of divine authority
- No compromise with "heretics" (those who rejected divine claims)
- No mercy for those who defied the Pattern
- No tolerance for alternative truths

**The Dragonborn Accepted.**

Not through coercion. Not through threat. But through **genuine belief**.

They truly thought Soltharion had created them. Why wouldn't they? They had no memory of anything before him. No elven Pattern-sense. No trance-memory of ancient truths.

**He was there when they emerged. To them, this was proof enough.**

### **The Dragonborn Empire: Divine Authority Made Manifest**

**Timeline:** ~9,000-7,000 years ago (Late Loomless Time into Early First Age)

**What They Built:**

The dragonborn, empowered by Soltharion, built the **First Divine Empire**:

- Cities dedicated to the Fire Pantheon
- Temples where Soltharion's word was law
- Hierarchies based on divine favor
- Military might enforcing "divine order"
- The first systematic theology of divine creation

**Key Doctrine:**
> *"The gods shaped mortals from the elements. We are Fire's chosen. All others must acknowledge their creators or face correction."*

**What This Meant:**

When dragonborn encountered elves, they found:
- A race that **rejected divine creation claims**
- Traditions that **predated divine organization**
- Magic that **worked without divine intermediation**
- A people who **refused to kneel**

To the dragonborn, this was **heresy**. Not from malice, but from **genuine belief** that elves were denying obvious truth.

**The First Persecutions:**

The dragonborn, serving Soltharion's will, began:

- **"Consecrating" elven sacred groves** (claiming them for the Fire Pantheon)
- **Burning "heretical" texts** (anything suggesting pre-divine reality)
- **Forcing conversion** (through pressure, not yet violence)
- **Destroying evidence** (anything contradicting divine creation myth)

They didn't see this as evil. They saw it as **saving souls from delusion**.

**The Tragedy:**

The dragonborn were **genuinely trying to help**. They believed elves were trapped in dangerous falsehood. They thought they were **liberating** elves from primitive ignorance.

The road to hell is paved with good intentions.

### **The Dragonborn Today: Faithful Warriors in Crisis**

**Fourth Age Population:** ~800,000 (declined from empire's peak of ~2 million)

**Current State:**

Modern dragonborn are:
- Still **predominantly faithful** to the Fire Pantheon
- Often **Paladins and Clerics** of Soltharion
- **Military leaders** in Faith of Bound Pattern societies
- **Conflicted** about their empire's fall

**The Crisis of Faith:**

Newer generations are questioning:
- Why did their empire fall if Soltharion favored them?
- Why do gods allow Pattern instability?
- Why do escaped Drow (who worship a false god) sometimes speak truth?
- Why do some elven prophecies come true despite being "heretical"?
- Why does their own Compact Testament contradict newer divine revelations?

**The Conservative Response:**

Most dragonborn double down:
- **Increased devotion** to prove worthiness
- **Stricter adherence** to divine law
- **Harsher judgment** of heretics
- **Rejection** of doubt as weakness

**The Reformist Minority:**

Some dragonborn are beginning to ask:
> *"What if Soltharion isn't our creator, but still worthy of worship? What if service and truth aren't the same thing?"*

These are considered **dangerous heretics** by their own people.

## Humanity's Emergence: The Short Memory

### **Timeline:** ~8,000-6,000 years ago (Late Loomless Time)

**When Humans Entered the World:**

Humans were **the last major race** to emerge (except for halflings, gnomes, and some others who came even later). By the time they developed consciousness and culture:

- **Gods had already organized** into pantheons
- **Dragonborn Empire** already dominated much of the known world
- **Elves** had been resisting divine claims for ~2,000 years
- **The "god-created-mortals" myth** was already established dogma

**The Critical Weakness:**

Human lifespans: **80-100 years**

This meant:
- **No cultural continuity** beyond ~4 generations
- **No living memory** of events 200+ years ago
- **No ability** to verify ancient claims
- **No resistance** to being told "this has always been true"

**The Manipulation:**

When gods approached early human tribes, they found:

**Perfect Believers:**
- No pre-divine traditions to contradict
- No Pattern-sense to feel wrongness
- No long memory to question claims
- No cultural infrastructure to resist

**Willing Servants:**
- Desperately needed divine protection
- Craved purpose and meaning
- Wanted power against hostile world
- Sought explanation for existence

**Ideal Enforcers:**
- Reproduced quickly (new generation every 20 years)
- Died before questioning too deeply
- Could be molded within a single lifetime
- Would forget inconvenient truths within centuries

**The Gods' Strategy:**

Different pantheons approached different human tribes:

**Fire Pantheon:**
- Offered **strength and conquest**
- Promised **glory through service**
- Taught that **hierarchy is natural**
- Created **warrior cultures**

**Water Pantheon:**
- Offered **safety from the sea**
- Promised **prosperity through trade**
- Taught that **the tide never lies**
- Created **seafaring civilizations**

**Earth Pantheon:**
- Offered **stability and harvest**
- Promised **endurance through obedience**
- Taught that **the mountain stands eternal**
- Created **agricultural kingdoms**

**Air Pantheon:**
- Offered **freedom and knowledge**
- Promised **wisdom through revelation**
- Taught that **the wind carries truth**
- Created **nomadic and scholarly peoples**

**Spirit Pantheon:**
- Offered **fate and meaning**
- Promised **purpose through destiny**
- Taught that **the Pattern guides all**
- Created **mystics and prophets**

**The Result:**

Within 2,000 years, humanity was **completely integrated** into divine worship systems. They had no concept of existence without gods. The idea that gods might not have created them was literally unthinkable.

## The Faith of the Bound Pattern: Humanity's Gift to the Gods

### **Timeline:** ~3,000-2,000 years ago (Early-Mid First Age)

**The Synthesis:**

As human civilizations grew, they did something elves never did: they **organized divine worship into systematic theology**.

**Why Humans Did This:**
- Short memory required **written doctrine**
- Competing divine claims needed **hierarchy**
- Multiple pantheons needed **coordination**
- Social order required **clear rules**

**What They Created:**

The **Faith of the Bound Pattern** synthesized all divine claims into one coherent theology:

**Core Doctrine:**
1. The **Five Pantheons** created all races from the elements
2. The **Pattern** (Weave) is divine will made manifest
3. The **Threads** connect all mortals to divine purpose
4. Following one's **Thread** brings harmony and reward
5. Deviating from the **Pattern** brings chaos and punishment

**The Cycles:**
- Year divided into **five divine cycles** (one per pantheon)
- Each cycle emphasizes different divine aspects
- **Threadbearers** (priests) interpret divine will
- Society organized around these cycles

**The Genius:**

The Faith of the Bound Pattern made divine worship:
- **Comprehensible** (clear rules, clear rewards)
- **Practical** (applicable to daily life)
- **Universal** (applies to all races, all situations)
- **Enforceable** (Threadbearers have authority)

**The Poison:**

It also made divine lies:
- **Systematic** (organized falsehood is harder to challenge)
- **Self-reinforcing** (questioning one part undermines all)
- **Totalizing** (applies to everything, allows no alternatives)
- **Hereditary** (taught to children before they can question)

**The Elven Problem:**

Elves **rejected** the Faith of the Bound Pattern because:
- They remembered a world before gods organized
- They had traditions predating divine claims
- They could sense the Pattern wasn't "divine will"
- They knew the Weave existed before gods claimed it

To humans, this was **inexplicable**:
> *"Why would anyone reject such obvious truth? Why would they choose chaos over order? Why would they deny their own creators?"*

It never occurred to humans that elves might be **right**.

## The Great Persecution: Humanity's Holy War

### **Timeline:** ~2,000-1,100 years ago (Late First Age into Second Age)

**The Theological Crisis:**

As the Faith of the Bound Pattern spread, elven resistance became increasingly **intolerable**:

**The Problem:**
- Elves had **evidence** (World Trees, sacred groves, Current-Songs)
- Elves had **credibility** (longest-lived, most magical race)
- Elves had **influence** (some humans listened to them)
- Elves threatened the **entire theological foundation**

**If Elves Were Right:**
- Gods didn't create mortals
- Divine authority was fabricated
- The Faith of the Bound Pattern was built on lies
- Human purpose and meaning evaporated
- Social order collapsed

**The Solution:**

The **Threadbearers**, backed by the **Fire Pantheon** and **dragonborn military**, declared:

> *"The elves are trapped in ancient error. They cling to primitive superstition. They reject divine grace. They threaten the Pattern itself. For the good of all mortals, for the stability of civilization, for the will of the gods themselves—they must be corrected or removed."*

**Phase One: Forced Conversion (Late First Age):**

Human and dragonborn armies **"liberated" elven lands**:

- Sacred groves were **"consecrated"** to proper gods
- World Trees were **cut down** "for the good of the Pattern"
- Elven texts were **burned** "to prevent corruption"
- Elven scholars were **"reeducated"** or executed
- Elven children were **taken** and raised in the Faith

**The Justification:**
> *"We are not conquerors. We are saviors. We free them from delusion."*

**Phase Two: The Grove Burnings (Early Second Age):**

When conversion failed, **systematic destruction** began:

**Targets:**
- **Sacred groves** that predated temples
- **World Trees** whose rings contradicted divine claims
- **Root-sanctums** maintaining pre-divine traditions
- **Coral archives** preserving pre-divine history
- **Current-Songs** recording elven memory

**Methods:**
- Fire (cleansing through Soltharion's gift)
- Logging (resources for divine temples)
- Consecration (claiming for "proper" worship)
- Desecration (making sites unusable for "heresy")

**The Enforcers:**

Who carried out the persecution?

**Dragonborn Paladins:**
- Elite warriors of Soltharion
- Genuinely believed they served righteousness
- Saw elves as **deluded and dangerous**
- Fought with **holy conviction**

**Human Crusaders:**
- Common soldiers fighting "holy war"
- Told they **saved souls** by destroying groves
- Rewarded with **land and status**
- Believed elves threatened **their children's souls**

**Threadbearers:**
- Priests directing the persecution
- Deciding what was "heretical"
- Ordering destruction of evidence
- **Truly believed** they protected civilization

**The Tragedy:**

Almost none of them thought they were evil. They thought they were:
- Protecting truth from falsehood
- Saving souls from damnation
- Defending civilization from chaos
- Serving divine will faithfully

**And they were so, so wrong.**

## The Dissidents: Those Who Questioned

### **Timeline:** Throughout the persecution

**Not All Humans Agreed:**

Even during the worst persecution, some humans questioned:

**The Doubters:**

**Scholars who noticed:**
- Elven architecture was impossibly old
- Tree rings didn't match theological timeline
- Elven magic worked differently than divine magic
- Current-Songs contained impossible knowledge

**Soldiers who witnessed:**
- The grief in elven eyes when groves burned
- The sincerity in elven pleas
- The beauty in what they destroyed
- The wrongness of "righteous" violence

**Lovers who listened:**
- Humans who fell for elves (creating half-elves)
- Who heard elven truths in intimate moments
- Who saw Pattern-sense wasn't delusion
- Who chose love over orthodoxy

**The Consequences:**

Those who questioned faced:
- **Ostracism** from their communities
- **Heresy charges** from Threadbearers
- **Execution** in extreme cases
- **Exile** if they were lucky

**But Some Persisted:**

**The Underground:**
- Humans who helped elves escape
- Scholars who hid "heretical" texts
- Priests who secretly questioned doctrine
- Soldiers who refused orders
- Families who hid half-elven children

**The Price:**

Many paid with their lives. But they preserved:
- **Friendships** across racial divides
- **Knowledge** that would otherwise be lost
- **Doubt** that theology wasn't complete
- **Hope** that truth might someday matter

**The Half-Elven Question:**

Half-elves are **living proof** that not all humans embraced persecution:

- Someone **loved** across the divide
- Someone **chose** relationship over orthodoxy  
- Someone **risked** everything for connection
- Someone **believed** elves might be right

Every half-elf is a testament to **human capacity for doubt, love, and courage**.

But they're also proof of **how dangerous questioning is**:
- Half-elves are often **mistrusted** by both races
- Seen as **corrupted** by the faithful
- Seen as **tainted** by traditional elves
- Caught between **two incompatible truths**

## The Dragonborn Empire Falls: Divine Will Proven False

### **Timeline:** ~1,500-1,200 years ago (Mid-Second Age)

**The Problem:**

If dragonborn were **Soltharion's chosen**, created to **rule in his name**, blessed with **divine favor**—why did their empire **collapse**?

**What Happened:**

The Dragonborn Empire, at its height, controlled:
- Most major trade routes
- Richest agricultural lands
- Strongest military in the world
- Unquestioned religious authority

**Then It Fell:**

Not to conquest. Not to plague. But to:
- **Internal corruption** (power corrupted divine purpose)
- **Environmental disaster** (over-harvesting sacred lands)
- **Economic collapse** (trade routes failed)
- **Social fragmentation** (subject peoples rebelled)
- **Divine silence** (Soltharion didn't intervene)

**The Theological Crisis:**

Dragonborn had preached:
> *"We are Soltharion's chosen. Our empire proves divine favor. Our power demonstrates divine truth."*

**If the empire fell**, what did that prove?

**Official Explanation:**
> *"Soltharion tests us. Our faith was corrupted by comfort. We must prove ourselves worthy again through sacrifice and devotion."*

**Unofficial Doubts:**
- What if Soltharion isn't omnipotent?
- What if he doesn't control everything?
- What if divine favor isn't proven by empire?
- What if... we were never his "chosen" at all?

**The Cover-Up:**

Threadbearers worked to explain:
- Empire fell due to **mortal sin**, not divine weakness
- This proved **need for stricter faith**, not questioning
- Dragonborn must **atone**, not doubt
- The Pattern **demands** humility, not pride

**The Problem:**

If empire through divine favor was false, what else might be?

Most dragonborn refused to ask. But **some** couldn't stop wondering.

## The Faith Today: Cracks in the Foundation

### **Fourth Age (Year 487, current day)**

**Official Doctrine:**

The Faith of the Bound Pattern remains **dominant**:

**Population Breakdown:**
- **~60% of humans** (deeply faithful)
- **~80% of dragonborn** (cultural identity)
- **~40% of halflings** (with modifications)
- **~30% of dwarves** (where it doesn't conflict with Deep Law)
- **~10% of elves** (surface elves who compromised)
- **~5% of gnomes** (who study it academically)
- **~0% of drow** (absolute rejection)
- **~0% of pure wood elves** (absolute rejection)

**What They Teach:**
- Gods created all races
- Pattern guides all existence
- Threads connect mortals to divine will
- Five Cycles structure reality
- Heresy threatens civilization

**What They Don't Discuss:**
- Pre-divine elven evidence
- Empire collapse theological implications
- Pattern instability's meaning
- Escaped Drow testimony
- Half-elven insights
- Why elves live 10x longer if humans are "equal creations"

**The Cracks:**

**Pattern Instability:**
- Divine glyphs **failing** without explanation
- Thread-connections **weakening** unpredictably
- Prophecies **contradicting** each other
- Threadbearers **losing authority**

**Eldritch Incursions:**
- Things older than gods appearing
- Forces beyond divine control manifesting
- Cosmic truth bleeding through
- Reality itself questioning theology

**Escaped Drow Testimony:**
- They say Vel'Zyren **transformed** them, not created them
- They remember being surface elves
- Some reclaim pre-transformation memories
- This suggests **divine creation is transformation**, not origin

**Half-Elven Questions:**
- They have **elven Pattern-sense** (hereditary, not granted)
- They live **longer than humans** (gradual, not divine gift)
- They feel the **wrongness** when gods claim creation
- They're **living proof** races aren't separate divine works

**The Dragonborn Crisis:**
- Younger generation **questions** Compact
- Empire's fall still **unexplained**
- Soltharion's **silence** during crises
- Other pantheons' **contradictory claims**

**The Scholar's Problem:**
- Tree rings **still** predate theology
- Elven languages **still** more complex/ancient
- Coral archives **still** exist (in protected seas)
- Mathematics **doesn't support** theological timeline

**The Response:**

**Traditionalists (majority):**
- Double down on faith
- Persecute doubters harder
- Explain crises as **tests of faith**
- Demand **stricter orthodoxy**

**Reformists (growing minority):**
- Seek **theological evolution**
- Allow **symbolic interpretation**
- Focus on **divine guidance** over creation claims
- Open to **compromise with elven traditions**

**Heretics (dangerous fringe):**
- Question **fundamental doctrines**
- Study **elven alternatives**
- Seek **pre-divine truth**
- Risk **execution** for answers

Humans and dragonborn are **both**:

**Victims:**
- Manipulated by gods they trusted
- Born into lies they couldn't question
- Used as weapons without consent
- Robbed of true history
- Denied access to deeper truths

**Perpetrators:**
- Destroyed irreplaceable evidence
- Murdered innocents for "heresy"

**The Complexity:**

Most didn't know they were wrong:
- Genuinely believed they served righteousness
- Truly thought they saved souls

The sacred groves are still burned.

**And the lies are still told.**

## What This Means for Your Game

### **For Players:**

**If You Play a Human:**

You're part of a race that:
- Never knew a world without organized gods
- Built the dominant religion on divine lies
- Enforced those lies through persecution
- Still mostly believes them today
- Is **starting to question** in the Fourth Age

**Your Options:**
- **True Believer**: The Faith is truth, elves are deluded
- **Questioning Faithful**: Love the gods, but doubt details
- **Secret Doubter**: Outwardly faithful, privately uncertain
- **Open Heretic**: Actively seeking truth, risking persecution
- **Defender**: Protecting the Faith by any means
- **Reformist**: Seeking theological evolution

**Your Burden:**
- Your ancestors destroyed irreplaceable truth
- Your religion might be built on lies
- Your short life means you'll never know for sure
- Your children will inherit your choices

**If You Play a Dragonborn:**

You're part of a race that:
- Was found by Soltharion before having culture
- Built identity on being "chosen"
- Served as divine enforcers for millennia
- Watched your empire collapse despite divine favor
- Still clings to the Compact despite evidence

**Your Options:**
- **Compact Loyalist**: Soltharion is creator, empire's fall was test
- **Conflicted Warrior**: Serve faithfully, question privately
- **Crisis of Faith**: Empire's fall proves something wrong
- **Heretic**: Reject creation claim, keep service
- **Apostate**: Abandon Fire Pantheon entirely
- **Seeker**: Study elven alternatives despite taboo

**Your Burden:**
- You were primary enforcers of persecution
- Your "chosen" status is increasingly questionable
- Your longer life (300+ years) lets you see patterns
- Your identity is tied to claims you're questioning

**If You Play a Half-Elf:**

You're **living proof** of:
- Love across theological divide
- Human capacity to question orthodoxy
- Courage to choose truth over comfort
- Sacrifice ancestors made for connection

**Your Experience:**
- **Dual nature**: Both human and elven
- **Pattern-sense**: You feel the wrongness of divine claims
- **Extended life**: Long enough to question human theology
- **Mistrust**: Both races see you as tainted/corrupted
- **Insight**: You understand both perspectives
- **Bridge**: You could help heal the divide—if anyone lets you

**Your Burden:**
- Neither race fully accepts you
- You carry truth humans resist and elves resent
- Your existence proves theology is incomplete
- You must choose which truth to follow

## The Penitent's Archive (Banned Text Fragment)

> *We are told the elves reject truth out of pride.*
> *We are told they cling to primitive superstition.*
> *We are told they deny their creators in arrogance.*
> 
> *But I have seen their sacred groves—fifteen thousand years old.*
> *I have read their Current-Songs—recording events before our gods organized.*
> *I have witnessed their Pattern-sense—feeling truths we cannot perceive.*
> *I have loved one of them—and learned what she knew I could never fully grasp.*
> 
> *We are told we saved them from delusion.*
> *We are told we freed them from error.*
> *We are told we served divine righteousness.*
> 
> *But I burned those groves.*
> *I destroyed those songs.*
> *I killed those who wouldn't convert.*
> 
> *And I wonder:*
> *What if we are the deluded?*
> *What if we are the prisoners?*
> *What if we served not righteousness, but lies?*
> 
> *I have only eighty years to live.*
> *She will live eight hundred.*
> 
> *She will remember what we did long after my name is forgotten.*
> *She will carry the truth long after my lies are dust.*
> 
> *And I wonder:*
> *Is this the true difference between us?*
> *Not divine favor or primitive superstition—*
> *But simply that she remembers, and I forget?*
> 
> *What truths did my grandfather destroy that I'll never recover?*
> *What lies did he build that I'm still living?*
> *What will my grandchildren believe that isn't true?*
> 
> *We are told questioning is heresy.*
> *We are told doubt is weakness.*
> *We are told the Faith protects us.*
> 
> *But from what?*
> *From truth?*
> *From memory?*
> *From the uncomfortable realization that we might be wrong?*
> 
> *I write this knowing I will be executed for it.*
> *I write this knowing it will be burned.*
> *I write this knowing my name will be erased.*
> 
> *But perhaps someone will find these words.*
> *Perhaps someone will remember.*
> *Perhaps someone will ask the question I couldn't escape:*
> 
> *Were we the faithful...*
> *Or the deceived?*

**— Author Unknown, Executed for Heresy, ~400 Years Ago**  
**Text preserved in hidden elven archive, now slowly circulating among reformist scholars**
