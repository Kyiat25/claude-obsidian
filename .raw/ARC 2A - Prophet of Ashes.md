## *Let It Burn - Canonical Version with Eldritch Conspiracy*

> *"I watched 850,000 souls unmade. Ceased to exist. Not killed—erased from reality itself. My city. My people. My faith. For five centuries I searched for WHY. ## **CAMPAIGN SUMMARY**

**Category:** Revealer (Truth-at-Any-Cost) / Unknowing Eldritch Pawn

**BBEG:** **Kethyr Flameheart the Truthbringer**, Ancient Dragonborn Warlock & Ashkarra Survivor

**Core Premise:**

**Kethyr Flameheart** survived Ashkarra's unmaking during the Third Age, approximately **500-600 years ago** (exact date lost to temporal corruption from the Day of Twelve Shadows). He wasn't in Ashkarra when it was unmade—he was abroad leading a persecution campaign against truth-tellers in coastal Vashkora, burning sacred oases where wood elves maintained evidence of pre-divine civilizations. When news reached him that the catastrophic Third Spirit Thread had caused 850,000 dragonborn souls to simply cease to exist—not killed but unmade, erased from reality—he returned to find only the Wyrmglass Flats where his civilization had stood.

As a high-ranking Embermarked priest (~100 years old at Ashkarra's fall), he had personally led religious persecution for decades. He taught that Soltharion granted dragonborn divine exemption from cosmic law. He assured parents their children were safe in Ashkarra's divine protection. He executed scholars who questioned Thread stagnation or divine creation myths. His wife Sylara, his children Korvus and Velithra, his extended family—all gone. Unmade. Twenty relatives who believed everything he taught them.

In his grief and guilt, he made a **warlock pact with Xey'Tazun, The Time Unwritten**—an Eldritch entity of temporal collapse and chrono-curses. The pact granted him centuries of extended life to "seek truth and prevent future Ashkarras." For 500+ years, he has investigated WHY, discovering two devastating truths:

**First Truth (Thread Violation):** Ashkarra fell because the empire violated cosmic law by maintaining Spirit Thread for three consecutive cycles. The warnings were ignored. Divine favor couldn't override natural cosmic forces. They died from spiritual pride, not divine punishment.

**Second Truth (Divine Fraud):** The gods had no authority to grant exemptions anyway. Soltharion didn't create dragonborn—he stole credit for Astralor's primordial work. The Divine Spark gods are powerful entities who claimed creator status but lack actual cosmogonic authority. Millennia of warnings about divine fraud were TRUE.

**The Hidden Truth (Unknown to Kethyr):** Xey'Tazun doesn't care about truth or justice. The Eldritch entity seeks to **unlock the Nameless One Below**—the unknowable and ancient entity that the Earth Pantheon's primary purpose is to contain. Kethyr's truth-revelation campaign weakens divine authority, destabilizes Faith institutions, and—most critically—**breaks the seals and oaths that keep the Nameless One imprisoned**. Every theological crisis he triggers, every divine authority he undermines, every ancient certainty he shatters... brings the Earth Pantheon's containment closer to failure.

Now the ancient dragonborn has become what he once persecuted: an apocalyptic truth-revealer willing to burn civilization to expose divine fraud. His **Ashen Prophets** systematically destroy Faith institutions, leaving undeniable evidence. The revelations cause riots, theological civil wars, and civilizational chaos. And Kethyr says: **good. 850,000 souls were unmade because we murdered truth-tellers. Let the lies burn.**

But every burned temple, every shattered certainty, every theological crisis weakens the Lock That Holds the World. And something ancient stirs beneath the mountains, forgetting its chains.

## **THE ASHKARRA CONTEXT**

### **What Actually Happened: The Unmaking (Third Age)**

**When:** Third Age, Cycle 3 (approximately 500-600 years before campaign start)
- Exact date lost to temporal corruption from Day of Twelve Shadows
- Some records say 523 years ago, others 547, others 612
- Temporal bleeding from catastrophe makes precise dating impossible
- Xey'Tazun's influence ensures timeline remains "unstable"

**Population Before:** ~850,000 (90% dragonborn, 8% human slaves, 2% others)

**Survivors:** ~200,000 (mostly those outside the city during collapse, plus scattered military forces abroad)

**The Third Spirit Thread Crisis:**

Ashkarra was at peak power, mightiest Loom-City in history. When the First Spirit Thread brought unprecedented prosperity, Imperial authorities declared it "divinely approved" for extended use—directly violating fundamental cosmic law that Threads must rotate. They maintained Spirit alignment for the Second Thread cycle despite international warnings. By the Third Thread (unprecedented in all history), reality began breaking down:

- Prophetic contradictions and paradoxical visions
- Glyph instability—sacred inscriptions changing spontaneously
- Temporal hiccups—time flowing backward briefly
- Identity confusion among citizens
- The Ember Crucible World Anchor beginning to fail

**The Day of Twelve Shadows:**

During the Spirit Festival, twelve Eldritch Moons appeared glyphically aligned. The Ember Crucible reached critical failure:

1. **Flame Reversal:** Eternal flame turned inside out, consuming reality instead of fuel
2. **Glyph Collapse:** The Spiral of Flame and Form completed inversion
3. **Anchor Implosion:** World Anchor imploded, pulling reality into Null Reflection
4. **The Unmaking:** Ashkarra wasn't destroyed—it was "unwritten" from reality
5. **Temporal Corruption:** Xey'Tazun manifested, creating permanent timeline instability

**Population Fate:**
- Most citizens simply **ceased to exist**—erased from causality
- Some became **Eldritch echoes**—temporal loop fragments
- Others **absorbed into dimensional breach**
- Timeline corruption means some records claim different death tolls
- Xey'Tazun's influence ensures exact truth remains unknowable

**The Wyrmglass Flats:** Permanent zone of instability where Ashkarra stood. Temporal anomalies, mirror storms, fused glass desert, phantom architecture, echo populations. Called "Ashkarra's Grave" despite containing no actual graves—because the unmade left no corpses to bury.

### **Why This Matters to Kethyr**

**The Double Lie:**

1. **Thread Violation:** Ashkarra's leadership believed divine favor exempted them from cosmic law. Warnings for centuries that three Spirit Thread cycles would cause catastrophe. Every warning dismissed as "jealousy" or "heresy."

2. **Divine Fraud:** But Kethyr's 500+ year investigation revealed deeper horror—Soltharion had no authority to grant exemptions anyway. The gods didn't create mortals. They claimed credit for Astralor's work. Divine protection was always illusion.

**His Personal Guilt:**

The Last Persecution Campaign (5 days before Ashkarra's unmaking):
- Led raid on coastal Vashkora sacred oases where wood elves maintained records
- These isolated communities (Islands of Eternal Green) were in Burning Concord territory
- Burned ancient trees fed by deep aquifers, some 12,000+ years old
- Executed Cycle-Singer Aeloren Rootspeaker who maintained evidence
- Destroyed archaeological sites showing pre-divine civilizations
- The Cycle-Singer's last words: *"You're killing evidence that could save your city. The alignment approaches. The mathematics are absolute. Your gods cannot override cosmic law."*

Five days later, news reached him that Ashkarra had ceased to exist. His wife, children, extended family—all unmade because they believed what he taught them.

**The Warlock Pact:**

In the Wyrmglass Flats, standing in temporal echo zones where his city flickered in and out of existence, grief-mad Kethyr called out to anything that would answer. **Xey'Tazun responded**—not with words but with temporal visions showing him two paths:

1. Die naturally within 200 years, Ashkarra's truth dying with him
2. Accept the pact: centuries of life to "prevent future Ashkarras through truth"

Kethyr accepted. The entity granted him:
- Extended lifespan (now ~600-700 years old, appears elderly but vigorous)
- Resistance to temporal effects (can navigate Wyrmglass Flats safely)
- Visions of "what must be revealed" (actually Eldritch manipulation)
- Power to survive persecution and testimony

**What Kethyr Doesn't Know:**

Xey'Tazun showed him "truth" but concealed its ultimate purpose. Every theological crisis Kethyr triggers weakens something deeper than divine authority—it weakens the **ancient oaths, seals, and certainties that the Earth Pantheon uses to contain the Nameless One Below**. 

The Faith of the Bound Pattern isn't just about spiritual guidance—it's part of the **Lock That Holds the World**. Gaeldros's divine law, Thamur's sealed gates, Vatharun's leyline structure—all require widespread belief, maintained oaths, and theological certainty to function. Kethyr's truth-revelation campaign doesn't just expose divine fraud—it **unravels the containment infrastructure**.

And beneath the mountains, something ancient begins to remember it was imprisoned. Something that should never be free starts forgetting its chains.

## **FINAL CAMPAIGN THEMES**

The campaign forces players to grapple with impossible questions across multiple layers:

**Truth vs. Suffering:**
- Can truth justify the suffering it causes?
- Is comfortable stability built on lies preferable to painful honesty?
- Who decides what suffering is acceptable for truth?
- At what casualty count does revelation become murder?
- **Deeper:** What if truth serves cosmic horror?

**Guilt vs. Redemption:**
- Can murderers become prophets?
- Does confession absolve historical crimes?
- Can good ends justify evil means?
- Is Kethyr redeemed or just adding to body count?
- **Deeper:** Can redemption matter when serving unknowable agenda?

**Civilization vs. Honesty:**
- Does society require founding myths?
- Can governance function without divine authority?
- Is stability more important than accuracy?
- What foundation should civilization rest on?
- **Deeper:** Do some lies serve necessary cosmic functions?

**Individual vs. Systemic:**
- Is Kethyr responsible for riot deaths?
- Or is system that created him responsible?
- Can individuals trigger necessary change?
- Or do systems only change through violence?
- **Deeper:** Can individuals resist cosmic manipulation?

**Revolution vs. Reform:**
- Is radical change ever justified?
- Can gradual reform address fundamental lies?
- Is compromise always cowardice?
- Or is it sometimes wisdom?
- **Deeper:** How balance truth with cosmic safety?

**Knowledge vs. Ignorance:**
- Are some truths too dangerous to know?
- Does noble lie beat dangerous truth?
- Can ignorance be justified by outcomes?
- Is pursuit of truth always righteous?
- **Deeper:** What about truth that serves cosmic horror?

**Cosmic Horror Layer:**
- What if truth-revealer is Eldritch pawn?
- Can evidence be real yet weaponized for worse purposes?
- Should containment of cosmic horror justify lying?
- Do mortals deserve to know what lies beneath?
- **Ultimate:** Can you serve truth without serving what truth serves?

**No Easy Answers:**

Every ending is bittersweet. Every path has costs. Every decision creates new problems. The campaign doesn't provide "correct" solution—it forces players to make impossible choices with cosmic implications and live with consequences.

Kethyr represents the "enlightened terrorist / unknowing cosmic pawn" archetype—genuinely believes he's saving civilization, makes compelling arguments, demonstrates his methods work for preventing future deception. But his revelations cause genuine suffering and death. And his patron serves unknowable cosmic agenda. Opposing him requires refuting not just a person but a philosophy while potentially serving different hidden agenda. Supporting him requires accepting responsibility for casualties AND cosmic risk.

The Ashen Prophets embody campaign's moral complexity—genuine truth-seekers using terroristic methods while unknowingly serving Eldritch conspiracy. Evidence is real. Persecution was actual atrocity. Divine fraud is provable. Kethyr's guilt is genuine. But forced revelation destroys lives AND weakens cosmic containment. Can you save people by traumatizing them with truth? Is liberation through education different from oppression through indoctrination? And does it matter if both serve hidden Eldritch agenda?

The Earth Pantheon represents impossible position—must maintain containment of cosmic horror while appearing to defend comfortable lies. Can't reveal true stakes without causing worse panic. Must suppress certain truths to serve greater good. But suppression looks exactly like authoritarian control. How do mortals distinguish necessary secrecy from power-serving deception?

Whether players stop him, join him, reform his movement, expose his patron, maintain containment, or build alternative—they'll live with consequences long after campaign ends. The theological crisis transforms civilization permanently. The evidence can't be suppressed once distributed. The cosmic horror doesn't go away. The question lingers: 

**Was revelation necessary truth, tragic manipulation, or both simultaneously? Can truth serve justice even when weaponized for cosmic horror? Should Lock That Holds the World be maintained through noble lies or reformed through dangerous honesty?**

**Kethyr's Final Legacy:**

Regardless of ending, Kethyr ensures one thing: no future civilization will die from the same lies Ashkarra did. Whether that justifies 50,000+ casualties depends on your moral framework. Whether his methods were necessary or merely destructive remains debated. Whether he was prophet, terrorist, or pawn remains unclear. Whether truth matters more than consequences defines civilization's future. Whether cosmic horror can be contained while accepting truth determines reality's stability.

But 850,000 unmade souls got their truth-teller, even if he came 500 years too late and served forces he didn't understand. The evidence is real. The divine fraud is exposed. The persecution is acknowledged. The cosmic horror is contained (or isn't). And civilization must decide: what comes next?
