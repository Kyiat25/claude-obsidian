# The Apothecary

**Masters of Desperate Science**

> *"In a world where gods lie dead and magic itself corrupts, you turn to older truths — the reactions of acid and base, the potency of rare herbs, the terrible beauty of controlled explosions."*

---

## Science in the Shadow of Dead Gods

The healer in the party prays. You measure. Where they wait for divine intervention, you have already prepared fourteen vials, cross-referenced the patient's symptoms against three field manuals, and begun cauterising the wound with a precision that no prayer would achieve. You are not faithless. You simply found something more reliable than faith.

You are the Apothecary. Your **Reagent System** transforms raw materials into miracles and nightmares. Your **Field Alchemy** means you can heal without magic, treat wounds without spells, and create chemical weapons while your allies are still reading their spell lists. Your **Concoction Slots** are your daily resource — each morning you prepare vials of precisely measured death and deliverance.

In FEARS, your Momentum engine is your healing. **Chemical Rush** at 1st level: whenever you successfully heal a bloodied ally or apply a beneficial concoction in combat, gain +1 Momentum. The more desperate the situation, the more chemistry you bring to it.

---

## The Apothecary's Essence

You are the party's field medic, combat chemist, and engineering mind. You are the class best equipped to treat Injuries mechanically — the Field Medic discipline gives you tools no magical healer has. **Unstable Concoctions** at 5th level creates high-risk, high-reward explosives that can end fights or end you.

You struggle in fights that don't allow time to use your kit. You excel in extended encounter sequences where pre-prepared concoctions pay dividends.

---

## Complexity Assessment: MODERATE (6/10)

The Apothecary tracks:                                       
• Reagent Points (long rest recovery, short rest forage) 
• Concoction Slots (prepared daily, varies by formula) 
• Formulas Known (growing list of alchemical options) 
• Class Reaction pool (PB uses, reaction-heavy play)    
• Corruption Score interactions (Chemical Burn, Emergency)   

Recommended for players who enjoy preparation, tactical resource use, and supporting allies in non-magical ways.

## Class Features

### Hit Points

- **Hit Dice:** 1d8 per Apothecary level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 8 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per Apothecary level after 1st

### Proficiencies

- **Armour:** Light armour
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons, blowgun, hand crossbow
- **Tools:** Alchemist's supplies, Herbalism kit, Poisoner's kit
- **Saving Throws:** Intelligence, Constitution
- **Skills:** Medicine, plus choose two from Arcana, Investigation, Nature, Perception, Sleight of Hand, and Survival

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) any simple weapon
- (a) leather armour or (b) scale mail
- (a) a scholar's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
- Alchemist's supplies, herbalism kit, and 10 empty vials
- A plague doctor's mask (counts as your arcane focus for Apothecary features)

---

## Quick Build

To quickly create an Apothecary, follow these steps:

1. **Ability Scores:** Make Intelligence your highest score, followed by Constitution.
2. **Lineage and Heritage:** Any combination works; Gnome, Human, and Dwarf are thematic.
3. **Background:** Sage, Guild Artisan, or any background with Medicine or Nature.
4. **Combat Strategy:** Prepare a mix of healing and offensive concoctions each morning. Use Emergency Injection reactively; deploy offensive concoctions offensively with your action. Use Called Shots with Anatomical Precision for tactical disruption.

## Core Class Features

### Field Alchemy (1st Level)

Your study of chemical reactions and biological processes grants you the following benefits:

- Use Intelligence instead of Wisdom for Medicine checks.
- Gain proficiency in Medicine if you don't already have it; if you do, gain Expertise.
- During a short rest, spend 1 Reagent Point to grant one creature an additional 1d4 HP per Hit Die spent. Increases to 1d6 at 5th level, 1d8 at 11th level, and 1d10 at 17th level.
- Identify poisons, diseases, and chemical substances with an INT (Medicine) or INT (Nature) check.

**DCS Integration — Injury Treatment:** Each discipline interacts with the Injury System differently (see Discipline Feature). The Apothecary is the class best equipped to treat, prevent, and downgrade Injuries mechanically.

**Momentum Interaction — Chemical Rush:** When you successfully heal a bloodied ally or apply a beneficial concoction in combat, gain +1 Momentum.

---

### Reagent System (1st Level)

You maintain a pool of **Reagent Points** representing your prepared chemical supplies. Maximum shown in the table. Regain all Reagent Points on a long rest. During a short rest, spend 10 minutes gathering materials to regain 1d4 Reagent Points (once per short rest).

**Concoction Slots** represent your prepared formulas. Each morning, prepare a number of concoctions equal to your Concoction Slots from your known formulas. Using a concoction requires an action (unless specified) and may cost Reagent Points. Some concoctions are single-use; others can be used multiple times by spending Reagent Points.

You know a number of **Formulas** as shown in the table. When you gain a level, you may replace one known formula with another.

---

### Medical Expertise (2nd Level)

When you use a healer's kit to stabilise a dying creature, that creature also regains 1 HP. As an action and 1 Reagent Point, end one disease or neutralise one poison affecting a creature within 5 feet. Advantage on saving throws against being poisoned and diseases.

---

### Toxin Resistance (2nd Level)

Resistance to poison damage. Immunity to the poisoned condition. At 10th level, resistance to acid damage. At 15th level, immunity to poison damage.

---

### Alchemical Disciplines (3rd Level, and 6th, 10th, 14th, 18th Level)

Choose your specialised field of study: **Plague Doctor** (diseases, toxins, and the line between life and death), **Mad Scientist** (self-experimentation and forced evolution), or **Field Medic** (battlefield triage and emergency medicine). Your discipline grants features at 3rd, 6th, 10th, 14th, and 18th levels.

**Plague Doctor — 3rd Level:** Toxic concoctions ignore resistance to poison damage; creatures with poison immunity take half damage instead. Diagnose any disease or poison by examining a creature for 1 minute.

**Mad Scientist — 3rd Level:** During a long rest, create a mutagen granting one of: +2 to one ability score (max 20), Darkvision 60 feet, +10 feet movement, or natural armour (+2 DR). One mutagen at a time.

**Field Medic — 3rd Level:** As a bonus action (instead of action), administer concoctions to adjacent allies. When you heal a creature at 0 HP, they gain temporary HP equal to your INT modifier.

**Field Medic — 6th Level — Trauma Specialist:** Spend 1 minute and 2 Reagent Points to remove a Minor Injury; 10 minutes and 4 RP for a Major Injury; 1 hour and 8 RP for a Critical Injury.

---

### Anatomical Precision (4th Level)

Gain proficiency in Called Shots. When you make a Called Shot with a thrown concoction, target a specific body part:

- **Head:** Target has disadvantage on mental saves for 1 round.
- **Torso:** Target takes +1d6 damage from the concoction.
- **Arms:** Target has disadvantage on attack rolls for 1 round.
- **Legs:** Target's speed is reduced by 10 feet for 1 round.

Once per turn when you hit with a concoction, the target's DR is reduced by 1 against your next concoction until end of your next turn.

---

### Unstable Concoctions (5th Level)

Your formulas become more powerful but dangerous. When you use a concoction, you can make it "unstable" for enhanced effect. Roll a d20. On a 1–2, a mishap occurs (roll on the Mishap Table). On all other results, the concoction's numerical effects increase by 50%.

**Mishap Table (d6):**
1. The concoction affects you instead of the target.
2. Explodes: 2d6 fire damage to all within 5 feet.
3. You gain 1 Corruption Score.
4. Toxic fumes: all within 10 feet make a CON save or be poisoned.
5. Works but you take 1d6 psychic damage and lose 1 Focus.
6. Wild magic surge.

---

### Enhanced Reagents (6th Level)

Reroll any 1s on concoction effect dice (keep the new result). When a concoction costs 2+ Reagent Points, reduce the cost by 1 (minimum 1).

---

### Emergency Protocol (7th Level)

At the start of combat, if you have at least half your Reagent Points, gain temporary HP equal to your Apothecary level.

Once per short rest, when you would be reduced to 0 HP, use your reaction to consume an emergency stimulant: drop to 1 HP instead and gain resistance to all damage until start of your next turn. Gain 1 Corruption Score.

---

### Advanced Formulas (9th Level)

Learn two additional formulas that don't count against your known formulas limit. You can maintain concentration on two concoction effects simultaneously.

---

### Heroic Boon (10th Level)

Choose one:

**Boon of Endless Experimentation:** Regain 1d4 Reagent Points at the start of each combat. Prepare 2 additional concoctions each day. Learn 3 additional formulas.

**Boon of Chemical Immunity:** Become immune to acid and poison damage. Cannot be diseased, petrified, or paralysed. Your concoctions can affect creatures normally immune to their effects.

---

### Chemical Burn (11th Level)

As a bonus action, inject yourself with combat drugs. For 1 minute: +2 to attack rolls and saving throws; +10 feet movement speed; extra bonus action each turn (concoctions only); immunity to frightened and charmed.

When it ends: gain 1 level of exhaustion and 1d4 Corruption Score. Once per long rest.

---

### Metamorphic Infusion (13th Level)

During a long rest, permanently alter a willing creature by spending 8 Reagent Points and rare materials (100 gp). Grant one of: +1 to an ability score (max 22), a new sense (darkvision, tremorsense 10 ft), resistance to one damage type, or a minor physical adaptation. Each creature benefits once per month. Each infusion risks 1d4 Corruption for the subject.

---

### Master Alchemist (15th Level)

Concoction ranges are doubled. Area effects increase by 5 feet radius. Create two concoctions with a single action (paying full costs for each). On a critical success attack roll with a thrown concoction, it doesn't consume the concoction.

---

### Volatile Mastery (17th Level)

When using Unstable Concoctions, mishaps only occur on a 1 (not 1–2). You can choose to automatically trigger a "controlled mishap" for double effect (you choose the mishap result). Immunity to your own area effect concoctions.

---

### Epic Boon (20th Level)

Choose one:

**Boon of the Philosopher's Stone:** Once per day, transmute up to 1 cubic foot of material into another material. Your healing concoctions also remove all conditions, diseases, and poisons. You no longer age and are immune to magical aging. Reduce all Corruption gains by half. Once per long rest, create 1d6 vials of Universal Solvent (each melts 1 cubic foot of material except glass).

**Boon of the Living Laboratory:** At the start of each turn, choose a damage type — gain immunity to it until your next turn. Regain 5 HP at start of each turn if you have at least 1 HP. Regain 1 Reagent Point at start of each turn in combat. When you take piercing or slashing damage, all creatures within 5 feet take 2d6 acid damage (no save).

---

## Last Stand Options

When reduced to 0 HP, the Apothecary chooses between destruction, transformation, or sacrifice.

**Rules for Last Stand:** Invoked when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, roll a death saving throw, or die outright. A free action requiring no reaction or consciousness. Once resolved, the character permanently dies.

---

### 1. Chemical Martyrdom

*You shatter every vial on your person in a final desperate act.*

**Effect:**
- All remaining concoctions detonate simultaneously.
- 30-foot radius explosion centred on you.
- Deal 2d6 damage per Reagent Point remaining (mixed types) to all creatures.
- CON save (DC = 10 + PB + INT): failure = full damage + poisoned + one random condition for 1 minute; success = half damage, no conditions.
- Area becomes toxic wasteland for 1 hour (difficult terrain, 1d6 poison per turn).

---

### 2. Emergency Metamorphosis

*You inject yourself with every mutagen at once, becoming something monstrous.*

**Effect:**
- Transform into an Aberrant Hulk for 5 rounds: size Large, 50 temp HP, +4 to all physical stats, −4 to mental stats.
- 3 attacks per turn (2d6 + STR each). Regenerate 10 HP at start of each turn. Resistance to all physical damage.
- When transformation ends: die instantly, body dissolves into toxic sludge.

---

### 3. Panacea Sacrifice

*You transform your life essence into pure healing energy.*

**Effect:**
- All allies within 60 feet heal 4d8 + INT modifier HP.
- End all diseases and poisons on each ally.
- Each ally removes one condition of their choice.
- Allies gain immunity to poison and disease for 24 hours.
- Allies gain advantage on all saves for 1 hour.
- Your body crystallises into a Philosopher's Stone (legendary item) — can resurrect you once, or be used for legendary crafting.

| Last Stand Option | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Chemical Martyrdom** | Detonate all reagents; 30-ft toxic explosion; wasteland for 1 hour. | Maximum enemy damage. |
| **Emergency Metamorphosis** | Become Aberrant Hulk for 5 rounds; devastating physical attacks. | Final offensive push. |
| **Panacea Sacrifice** | Full party heal, condition removal, 24-hour immunities; body becomes Philosopher's Stone. | Team support and legacy. |

## Class Reactions

Apothecaries have access to powerful reactions powered by their Proficiency Bonus pool, refreshing on short or long rest.

### Emergency Injection (1st Level)

**Trigger:** An ally within 5 feet takes damage.
**Effect:** Spend 1 PB use and 1 Reagent Point. The ally gains temp HP equal to 1d6 + your INT modifier. If they're bloodied, they also gain resistance to the triggering damage type until start of your next turn.

---

### Volatile Defence (2nd Level)

**Trigger:** A creature moves into a space within 5 feet of you.
**Effect:** Spend 1 PB use. Release a defensive chemical cloud. The triggering creature makes a CON save (DC = 8 + PB + INT) or be poisoned until end of your next turn and have their movement reduced to 0.

---

### Caustic Retaliation (3rd Level)

**Trigger:** You're hit by a melee attack.
**Effect:** Spend 1 PB use and 1 Reagent Point. The attacker takes 2d6 acid damage. If they used a weapon, it loses 1 DR penetration (cumulative, max −3) until repaired.

---

### Adrenaline Surge (5th Level)

**Trigger:** An ally within 30 feet fails a saving throw.
**Effect:** Spend 2 PB uses and 2 Reagent Points. Throw a stimulant allowing them to reroll the save with advantage. If they still fail, they gain +2 Momentum.

---

### Smoke Bomb Escape (7th Level)

**Trigger:** You or an ally within 30 feet is targeted by an attack or spell.
**Effect:** Spend 2 PB uses. Create a 10-foot radius smoke cloud centred on the target (heavily obscured until start of your next turn). The target can immediately move up to half their speed without provoking opportunity attacks.

---

### Panacea Protocol (10th Level)

**Trigger:** An ally within 30 feet drops to 0 HP.
**Effect:** Spend 3 PB uses and 4 Reagent Points. They immediately regain 3d8 + INT modifier HP and end one condition affecting them. You gain +2 Momentum from the dramatic save.

---

### Philosopher's Reversal (15th Level)

**Trigger:** You or an ally within 30 feet would gain a condition (except exhaustion).
**Effect:** Spend 3 PB uses and 3 Reagent Points. Negate the condition and reflect it back on the source (if within 60 feet). They make a CON save (DC = 8 + PB + INT) or suffer the condition themselves.
