# The Sangromancer

**Masters of Blood and Bone**

> *"You are not a reckless monster. You are a calculator. Every spell is a negotiation — between what you want and what your body can afford."*

---

## The Cost Made Power

You don't have spell slots. You have hit points, and the calculation is simple: how much are you willing to spend? Where a wizard draws from the Weave and a cleric borrows from their god, you draw from something that cannot be borrowed — your own life. Every incantation is paid in blood. Every moment of power is a moment closer to the edge.

You are the Sangromancer. Your **Sanguine Casting** means magic is always available — until your body runs out. Your **Vitae Harvest** feeds you from the wounds of your enemies, making you stronger the more the battle escalates. Your **Blood Resonance** reduces your costs against bloodied targets, turning enemy suffering into efficiency. You are the only caster who gets stronger as the fight goes on, because the fight going on means enemies are bleeding.

Your **Vitae Strain** marks the price of pushing too far. It accumulates slowly, transforms you at each threshold, and at its highest reaches grants power that would be impossible any other way. The boons are real. So are the curses.

**Note:** Vitae Strain is distinct from the world's Corruption Score. Vitae Strain measures only what the sanguine arts have done to *you*, not what eldritch exposure has done to your soul.

---

## The Sangromancer's Essence

You are a sustained caster who scales with desperation — yours and your enemies'. You excel in long fights against multiple bloodied targets where your Vitae Harvest keeps your HP afloat. You struggle in fights that end quickly before your resource feedback loops can engage.

**Warning:** You can die from your own abilities. Casting below 30% HP accumulates Vitae Strain. Certain subclass features have permanent HP costs. Learn your thresholds before you need them.

---

## Complexity Assessment: HIGH (8/10) 

The Sangromancer tracks:                                     
• HP as spell fuel (no spell slots — HP is the resource) 
• Vitae Strain (permanent accumulation with thresholds)
• Blood Sense awareness (passive, range-scaling)           
• Vitae Harvest and Vitae Depletion per creature            
• Blood Resonance cost reduction vs. bloodied targets  
• Bone Ward effects (active/inactive)                   

Recommended for experienced players who want maximum mechanical depth and high-risk, high-reward gameplay.

## Class Features

### Hit Points

- **Hit Dice:** 1d6 per Sangromancer level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 6 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d6 (or 4) + your Constitution modifier per Sangromancer level after 1st

### Proficiencies

- **Armour:** None *(Blood magic requires exposed skin; wearing armour interferes with vitae flow. Sangromancers who multiclass into armoured classes must choose: armour or casting, not both.)*
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons, scimitars, shortswords
- **Tools:** None
- **Saving Throws:** Constitution, Intelligence
- **Skills:** Choose two from Arcana, History, Insight, Intimidation, Investigation, Medicine, Nature, Religion

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) a scimitar or (b) any simple weapon
- (a) a component pouch or (b) an arcane focus
- (a) a scholar's pack or (b) a dungeoneer's pack
- A dagger and a bone harvesting kit

---

## Quick Build

To quickly create a Sangromancer, follow these steps:

1. **Ability Scores:** Make Intelligence your highest score, followed by Constitution (your survival depends on it).
2. **Lineage:** Any combination works; Shadar-kai, Tiefling, and Drow are thematic.
3. **Background:** Sage, Hermit, or any background with Medicine or Arcana.
4. **Combat Strategy:** Target bloodied enemies first — Blood Resonance reduces your casting cost. Use Vitae Harvest feedback to stay afloat. Never cast below 30% HP unless the fight is ending.

## Core Class Features

### Sanguine Casting (1st Level)

You fuel magic with life itself. **No Spell Slots.** Instead, you cast spells by spending hit points.

**Casting Cost:** Spend HP equal to **4 × the spell's level** (minimum 2 HP for cantrips). You can cast any spell you know at any level, provided you have sufficient HP.

**Heightened Casting:** Cast at higher levels by paying additional HP: +4 HP per level above base (e.g., casting a 2nd-level spell at 3rd level costs 8 + 4 = 12 HP).

**HP Casting Restrictions:** Cannot spend temporary HP to fuel Sanguine Casting. Cannot reduce yourself below 10% of your maximum HP via Sanguine Casting alone.

**Bloodied Empowerment:** When below 50% HP, spells that deal damage deal an additional **1d6 damage**; spells that restore HP restore an additional **1d6 HP**.

**Critical State:** When below 25% HP, spells deal an additional **2d6 damage** (replaces Bloodied Empowerment); spells restore 2d6 additional HP; disadvantage on CON saving throws.

**Hemorrhagic Vulnerability:** When Bloodied (below 50% HP), you have **vulnerability to piercing and slashing damage**.

**Vitae Strain Trigger:** Gain 1 Vitae Strain when casting while below **30% HP**.

**Spellcasting Ability:** Intelligence.
- **Spell Save DC** = 8 + PB + INT modifier
- **Spell Attack Modifier** = PB + INT modifier

---

### Blood Sense (1st Level)

You can sense living creatures within 30 feet, even through walls (but not lead or 3+ feet of stone). You know each creature's HP category: Healthy (75–100%), Wounded (50–74%), Bloodied (25–49%), or Critical (1–24%).

This sense is passive and requires no action.

---

### Vitae Harvest (1st Level)

When you deal damage to a Bloodied creature (below 50% HP) with a spell, automatically regain HP equal to the **spell's level** (cantrips = 1 HP). Occurs in addition to any other effects.

| Level | Healing |
|---|---|
| 1st–9th | Spell level |
| 10th–16th | 1.5 × spell level (rounded down) |
| 17th+ | 2 × spell level |

**Vitae Depletion:** After targeting the same Bloodied creature 3 times with spells, that creature's blood is depleted — no more Vitae Harvest healing from it and no Blood Resonance cost reduction. Resets when the creature is no longer Bloodied or when you finish a long rest.

---

### Bone Harvest (1st Level)

Spend 1 minute harvesting a fresh corpse (dead less than 24 hours, Small or larger, must have a skeleton) to create a **Bone Ward**. Choose one effect when harvesting:

- **Osseous Aegis:** Gain temp HP equal to 5 × your PB (lasting until your next long rest).
- **Skeletal Barrier:** Gain DR equal to your PB against bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage for 1 hour.

Only one Bone Ward at a time. Harvesting a bloodied corpse grants +5 temp HP and doubles the ward's duration.

At 9th level: Osseous Aegis grants 10 × PB temp HP; Skeletal Barrier grants DR equal to PB + INT modifier.

---

### Life Tap (2nd Level)

As a bonus action, touch a willing creature within 5 feet (30 feet at 7th level). They take 1d6 necrotic damage (cannot be reduced or prevented). You regain HP equal to 1d6 + your CON modifier.

Against Bloodied targets: damage increases to 2d6; you regain 2d6 + CON modifier HP; if target is below 25% HP, regain 2d6 + CON + PB.

Uses equal to your INT modifier per long rest.

---

### Vitae Strain Track (2nd Level)

Your use of blood magic accumulates **Vitae Strain** — the physical and spiritual toll of repeatedly drawing on your own life force. This is distinct from Corruption Score.

**Gaining Vitae Strain:** You gain 1 Vitae Strain when you cast while below 30% HP, when you harvest a sentient humanoid corpse, or when you critically fail a CON save while casting.

**Corruption Feast:** You do NOT gain Vitae Strain from casting while Bloodied if your spell targets a Bloodied enemy. Feeding on their weakness rather than burning your own desperation spares you the toll.

**Thresholds — Boon and Curse at Each Level:**

**First Threshold (5 Vitae Strain):**
- Boon — Sanguine Surge: Once per short rest, cast a spell using half the normal HP cost (rounded up). Against Bloodied targets, cost is reduced to one quarter instead.
- Curse — Skeletal Hands: Disadvantage on CHA (Deception) checks to hide your nature. Healing spells you cast on yourself heal 1d6 fewer HP (minimum 1).

**Second Threshold (10 Vitae Strain):**
- Boon — Bone Armour: Gain bonus AC equal to your Vitae Strain ÷ 5 (rounded down, minimum +1).
- Curse — Blood Thirst: You must spend HP on at least one spell per day or suffer 1 level of exhaustion at the next dawn. Consume raw meat (1 lb) each day or suffer the same penalty.

**Third Threshold (15 Vitae Strain):**
- Boon — Crimson Extraction: When you reduce a Bloodied creature to 0 HP with a spell, regain HP equal to spell level × 5, and gain temp HP equal to spell level × 2.
- Curse — Ashen Flesh: Disadvantage on all CHA checks with non-corrupted creatures. Cannot benefit from magical disguises.

**Fourth Threshold (20 Vitae Strain):**
- Boon — Necrotic Infusion: Your spells ignore resistance to necrotic damage and treat immunity as resistance. Against Bloodied creatures, necrotic damage also reduces their maximum HP by the same amount dealt until they finish a long rest.
- Curse — Haemophilia: When you take slashing or piercing damage, take an additional 2d6 damage and continue bleeding for 2d6 at the start of each of your turns. Bleeding stopped with a DC 18 Medicine check (action) or any magical healing. Stacks with Hemorrhagic Vulnerability.

**Fifth Threshold (25 Vitae Strain):**
- Boon — Bone Sovereignty: Maintain concentration on two spells simultaneously if at least one involves blood, bone, or necrotic damage.
- Curse — Hollow Bones: Vulnerability to bludgeoning damage. Maximum HP reduced by 25. Movement speed reduced by 10 feet. Disadvantage on STR and DEX saving throws.

**Maximum (30 Vitae Strain):**
- Gain access to the Avatar of the Vitae Epic Boon option.
- **Reducing Vitae Strain:** Only through specific magical rituals (*Greater Restoration* at 7th level or higher), divine intervention, or GM-approved atonement quests. Does not refresh on rest.

---

### Sangromancer Pact (3rd Level)

Choose your approach to blood magic: **Pact of the Bloodreaver** (melee, direct vitae feeding), **Pact of the Crimson Sage** (ranged manipulation, blood as puppet strings), or **Pact of the Marrow King** (bone constructs, undead legion command). Grants features at 3rd, 6th, 10th, 14th, and 18th levels.

---

### Blood Resilience (3rd Level)

When you sacrifice HP to power a blood magic ability, spell, or class feature, you have advantage on the next Injury saving throw before the end of your next turn. Your intimate connection with your own blood allows you to channel sacrifice into resilience.

---

### Crimson Surge (5th Level)

When you cast a spell using Sanguine Casting, use a bonus action to make one weapon attack. If the attack hits, regain HP equal to your INT modifier.

---

### Blood Resonance (5th Level)

When you cast a spell targeting a Bloodied creature, the HP cost is reduced by **1 HP per spell level** (minimum 1 HP).

| Level | Cost Reduction |
|---|---|
| 5th–10th | −1 HP per spell level |
| 11th–16th | −2 HP per spell level |
| 17th+ | −3 HP per spell level |

---

### Controlled Hemorrhage (5th Level)

Once per short rest, when you would sacrifice HP to power a blood magic ability, spell, or class feature, halve the HP cost (rounded down, minimum 1 HP).

---

### Blood Sense Enhancement (7th Level)

When a creature within range becomes Bloodied, you immediately sense it — an automatic alert requiring no action. Track up to 3 Bloodied creatures simultaneously within your Blood Sense range.

Blood Sense range: 30 feet at 7th level, 60 feet at 11th, 90 feet at 15th.

---

### Hemorrhage Enhancement (9th Level)

When you deal damage with a spell to a creature with blood, force a CON save (DC = Spell Save DC). Failure: creature begins bleeding (1d6 necrotic at start of each turn; stopped with a DC 15 Medicine check or any magical healing). Success: no effect.

Against Bloodied creatures: save DC +2; bleeding damage increases to 2d6; cannot be stopped by mundane Medicine — only magical healing. You can sense the location of all bleeding creatures within Blood Sense range even through walls.

Uses equal to your PB per long rest.

---

### Crimson Armour (9th Level)

As a bonus action, sacrifice any amount of HP. Until the start of your next turn, gain **DR 1 for every 5 HP sacrificed** (maximum DR 3). Your spilled blood crystallises into a protective barrier.

---

### Heroic Boon (10th Level)

Choose one:

**Boon of the Vitae Font:** Maximum HP increases by 20. Maintain a Blood Bank (up to 20 HP stored during rests; draw from it as a bonus action to regain HP or fuel spells). Bank from bloodied enemy damage: can store up to half damage dealt; lasts 3 days. Maximum Blood Bank increases to 40 HP. Once per long rest, transfer up to 20 HP from Blood Bank to a willing creature within 30 feet.

**Boon of the Ossuary Master:** Bone Ward effects doubled (temp HP ×2, DR ×2). Bloodied corpse harvest effects tripled instead of doubled. Harvest a corpse as an action instead of 1 minute. Maintain two Bone Wards simultaneously (same or different effects).

---

### Vitae Mastery (11th Level)

**Life Surge:** As a bonus action, spend 10 HP to gain temp HP equal to 2d8 + your INT modifier. Uses equal to your PB per short rest.

**Sacrificial Ward:** When you cast a spell while Bloodied (below 50% HP), gain DR equal to the spell's level (minimum 1) until start of your next turn. Stacks with Bone Ward DR.

---

### Bone Lord (13th Level)

As an action, sacrifice 10% of your maximum HP to create a **Bone Construct** from a harvested corpse or gathered bones. This HP loss cannot be healed while the construct is active. Use the Animated Armour stat block with: bone material; necrotic damage instead of bludgeoning; HP increased by your Sangromancer level; add your PB to attack rolls and damage; resistance to necrotic damage. Lasts until destroyed or dismissed.

**Bloodied Instant Summon:** When you kill a Bloodied creature, instantly animate its skeleton as a Bone Construct at no HP cost and no action required. This bonus construct lasts for 1 hour. You can only maintain one Bone Construct at a time unless your subclass specifies otherwise.

---

### Exsanguinating Strike (13th Level)

When you deal damage with a spell or weapon attack, you can force the target to make a CON saving throw (DC = Spell Save DC). On failure, the target loses **5 HP at the start of each of their turns for 1 minute**. This HP loss bypasses DR and counts as HP loss not damage. Only one creature can be affected at a time. Uses equal to your PB per long rest.

---

### Safe Bloodletting (13th Level)

Hit point loss you inflict on yourself through your class features — blood magic, vitae costs, sanguine casting — cannot cause you to suffer a Critical Injury. Your controlled self-harm is too precise and ritualised to cause the worst trauma.

You may still suffer Critical Injuries from external sources (enemy attacks, environmental hazards). This feature prevents only self-inflicted Critical Injuries. Minor and Major Injuries from self-inflicted damage remain possible.

---

### Sanguine Resilience (15th Level)

You no longer gain Vitae Strain from casting while Bloodied if your current Vitae Strain total is at or below 10. Your growing mastery of blood magic creates a buffer against the worst of its toll.

---

### Sanguine Momentum (15th Level)

When you cast a spell using Sanguine Casting that costs 8 or more HP, gain **+1 Momentum**. Your willingness to spend your own life to empower your magic creates a powerful surge of combat confidence.

---

### Vitae Strain's Gift (17th Level)

Your Vitae Strain has transformed you into something beyond mortal limits. Choose one permanent benefit based on your current Vitae Strain total:

- **10+ Vitae Strain:** Your spell save DC increases by 2 permanently.
- **15+ Vitae Strain:** You no longer need food, water, or sleep — you are sustained by blood magic. You require 1 HP expenditure per day (Sanguine Sustenance) instead.
- **20+ Vitae Strain:** Once per long rest, cast any spell you know without spending HP (the blood magic flows freely for one moment).
- **25+ Vitae Strain:** You are immune to death saving throw failures from HP expenditure — only taking damage can kill you, never your own casting.

---

### Epic Boon (20th Level)

Choose one:

**Avatar of the Vitae (Requires 30 Vitae Strain):** Once per long rest, transform for 1 minute. In this form: spells cost no HP to cast; you regain HP equal to twice your Vitae Strain total immediately; you are immune to all damage from non-magical sources; all your bloodied threshold features apply to every creature you can see; when you reduce a creature to 0 HP, all other creatures within 30 feet take necrotic damage equal to your Sangromancer level.

**Crimson Apotheosis:** Your blood magic reaches perfect efficiency. Spells targeting Bloodied creatures cost 1 HP regardless of level. When you use Vitae Harvest, regain HP equal to your full Sangromancer level (instead of the spell's level). Your Bone Constructs are permanent and do not require HP sacrifice to maintain — they are reanimated by the accumulated power of your Vitae Strain.

---

## Last Stand Options

When a Sangromancer faces their final breath, their art refuses to fade quietly. Blood ignites, bones animate, and the body becomes a last instrument of will.

**Rules for Last Stand:** Invoked when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, roll a death saving throw, or die outright. A free action requiring no reaction or consciousness. Once resolved, the character permanently dies.

---

### 1. Crimson Cataclysm

*Your blood ignites with all the Vitae Strain you have accumulated, exploding outward in a wave of sanguine destruction.*

**Effect:**
- Your body erupts in a 60-foot radius explosion of blood and bone.
- All creatures make a CON save (DC = 10 + PB + Vitae Strain ÷ 3):
  - Failure: 6d10 damage + 1d10 per 5 Vitae Strain (20 Vitae Strain = +4d10); stunned until end of their next turn.
  - Success: half damage, not stunned.
- All allies within 60 feet regain HP equal to your Sangromancer level × 2.
- You leave behind a Crimson Relic — one ally can consume it to regain 50 HP or gain +2 to spell attack rolls and save DC for 1 hour.

---

### 2. Ossuary Immortal

*You sacrifice your flesh to become pure bone, fighting on as an undead servant.*

**Effect:**
- Your flesh burns away, leaving an animated skeleton fighting for 1 minute.
- **Skeletal Form:** Resistance to all damage except radiant and bludgeoning; immunity to poison, disease, and exhaustion; all spells deal maximum damage; you can move through spaces as narrow as 1 inch.
- Each spell you cast in Skeletal Form extends its duration by 1 round.
- When the duration ends, you collapse to dust permanently.

---

### 3. Blood Legacy

*You transform all your remaining vitality into a blessing for your allies.*

**Effect:**
- All allies within 60 feet gain the following for 10 minutes:
  - Temp HP equal to your remaining HP + your Sangromancer level.
  - When they hit with an attack: +2d6 necrotic damage; regain HP equal to the necrotic dealt.
  - Advantage on saves against frightened or charmed.
  - +2 AC.
- Your body transforms into a Blood Font (mystical pool) for 10 minutes:
  - Allies draw from it as an action, regaining 4d8 HP (once per ally).
  - Enemies starting their turn within 10 feet take 3d6 necrotic.
  - The Font has AC 10, 50 HP — if destroyed, all benefits end.

| Last Stand Option | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Crimson Cataclysm** | 60-ft explosion scaling with Vitae Strain; allies heal. | Surrounded by enemies. |
| **Ossuary Immortal** | Become bone skeleton for 1 minute; max damage on all spells. | Final minute of devastating output. |
| **Blood Legacy** | Full party buff, temp HP, necrotic lifesteal, Blood Font. | Ensuring party survival and victory. |

## Class Reactions

Sangromancers have access to powerful reactions that leverage their blood magic. These reactions use your **Proficiency Bonus pool**, refreshing on short or long rest.

### Sanguine Shield (2nd Level)

**Trigger:** You take damage from any source.
**Effect:** Spend 1 PB use and 5 HP. Until start of your next turn, gain DR equal to your INT modifier against all damage. Your own blood coagulates into a protective barrier.

---

### Haemorrhagic Retaliation (7th Level)

**Trigger:** A creature within 30 feet casts a spell or makes a weapon attack.
**Effect:** Spend 1 PB use. The triggering creature makes a CON save (DC = Spell Save DC) or takes 2d8 necrotic damage and loses 1 use of a daily ability (GM determines which one). Against bloodied creatures, the save is made with disadvantage.

---

### Blood Transfusion (11th Level)

**Trigger:** An ally within 30 feet drops to 0 HP.
**Effect:** Spend 2 PB uses and any amount of HP (minimum 8). The ally regains HP equal to the HP you spent. You may also transfer one condition from the ally to yourself. Your vitae flows into them, sustaining them with your life force.
