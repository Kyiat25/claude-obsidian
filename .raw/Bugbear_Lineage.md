# Bugbear
## Lineage

> *"The gods cast us into shadow. So we became the thing that hunts in darkness—and taught fear itself to pray."*  
> — **Murruth's First Howl**, Fangborn Scripture

You are Bugbear. Not monster, not beast—**sanctified predator**. Your people do not kneel before gods but hunt alongside them as equals who understand that survival requires teeth. Where others see horror, you see **the sacred hunt that defines all existence**. Where they see savage violence, you see the ritual that separates living from merely breathing.

You were not born to be understood. You were born to **make fear holy**, to demonstrate that predation is prayer, to show that the rejected can become the thing others fear encountering in darkness. Your ancestors survived not through discipline or cunning but through **patient stalking, ritual violence, and the absolute certainty that fear is the oldest truth**. Now you carry that legacy in your blood, your silence, your certainty that **the hunt makes all things sacred**.

---

## The Shadow of the Blood-Memory

According to the **Faith of the Gnawing Crown**, bugbears represent **the shadow of fear and blood-memory, incarnate**. When the Fragment was cast out, part of it remembered the hunt—predator and prey, silence and scream, the moment between stalk and strike. That memory became you—**the proof that fear itself can be divine**.

Bugbears believe you are not mindless monsters but **priests of an older truth**. Before the gods named things, there was the hunt. Before the Pattern ordered reality, there was predator and prey. You remember this. You embody this. Every hunt you conduct is **theology in motion**.

The Fangborn Path teaches that Murruth—whether goddess, ancestral collective, or the Hunt itself—lives in **blood-memory**. The gods tried to erase this truth. You carry it in your bones.

---

## Life Among the Shadows

You were raised knowing that **silence is sacred**, that every successful hunt is prayer, that your worth is measured not in kills but in **how perfectly you embody the predator's way**. Bugbear culture treats existence as endless hunt, every breath a step in the stalk.

Your lifespan is roughly **80 years**—similar to hobgoblins but measured differently. You count not in years but in **hunts completed and fears inflicted**, measuring worth not in age but in **how many remember your shadow**.

Every significant hunt is recorded in **Fang-Marks**—scars, trophies, or ritual masks that preserve the memory of worthy prey. These aren't mere souvenirs; they're **proof that you honored the hunt** and gave your prey a death worth remembering.

**Fear** is sacred among your people. Not cruelty, but **the honest terror of prey facing something that can't be escaped**. To hunt is to create this fear. To create this fear is to touch something older than gods.

## Physical Characteristics

**Age:** You mature by age 12, reaching full adulthood by 16. Most bugbears don't die of old age—you die in the hunt, defending tribal grounds, or by walking into the final darkness when your stalking days end.

**Size:** You stand between 6½ to 8 feet tall, with builds designed for silent power. Your size is Medium (barely—among the largest Medium creatures).

**Speed:** Your base walking speed is 30 feet. You move with deliberate silence, each step placed with predatory precision.

**Build:** Massively built with long arms, thick fur, and powerful frames. Your skin is hidden beneath fur ranging from yellow-brown to brown-black, sometimes with reddish tinges. Your features are bestial—broad nose, large ears, prominent teeth—designed for intimidation. Your body is built for patient stalking, silent approaches, and overwhelming force when the moment arrives.

## Bugbear Traits

As a Bugbear, you possess the following lineage traits:

### **Darkvision**
You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

Your ancestors perfected the art of hunting in absolute darkness—not just seeing, but **becoming** the dark.

### **Long-Limbed**
When you make a melee attack on your turn, your reach for that attack increases by 5 feet.

Your arms are unnaturally long, allowing you to strike from distances that surprise prey. This extra reach turns expected safe zones into kill zones.

### **Powerful Build**
You count as one size larger when determining your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag, or lift.

Your strength isn't merely physical—it represents the predator's ability to drag prey to the den, to carry the weight of the hunt itself.

### **Sneaky**
You are proficient in the Stealth skill. Despite your size, you move with uncanny silence—something that unnerves those who witness it.

This isn't natural for a creature your size. This is **spiritual stealth**, the hunter's gift.

### **Surprise Attack**
If you surprise a creature and hit it with an attack during the first round of combat, the attack deals an extra 2d6 damage. This increases to 3d6 at 5th level, 4d6 at 11th level, and 5d6 at 17th level.

The moment between stalk and strike is when you're most dangerous. The first blow often ends the hunt.

> *"I waited three hours in the rafters. Motionless. Silent. Breathing once every five minutes. When he finally relaxed, I dropped. He never even screamed. That's respect. That's the sacred hunt."*

### **Fang-Memory**
You have advantage on Intimidation checks. Additionally, once per long rest, you can lock eyes with a creature within 30 feet and force it to make a Wisdom saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier) or become frightened of you for 1 minute. On each of its turns, the frightened creature can repeat the save, ending the effect on a success.

When you use this ability, the target senses something ancient and predatory in your gaze—**not just you, but the Hunt itself staring through you**.

## Mental Attributes & Psychology

**Focus Modifier:** +0  
**Sanity Modifier:** +1

Your biology grants you **exceptional psychological resilience**. Your Sanity bonus represents generations of living in darkness, witnessing horror, and embodying fear without becoming consumed by it. You face terror as **familiar rather than foreign**.

### **Cultural Mental Conditioning**
Beyond your biological advantage, Bugbear upbringing creates:

- **Patience incarnate:** You can wait motionless for hours without discomfort
- **Comfort in darkness:** Lightless spaces feel like home, not threat
- **Fear as tool:** You understand terror as craft, not trauma
- **Ritual violence:** You process killing as sacred act, not moral crisis

### **Corruption Vulnerability: The Hunting Madness**

As a Bugbear, you have a unique relationship with Corruption. Your comfort with darkness and violence provides strong defenses, but corruption that enters through **sadistic cruelty, dishonored hunts, or killing without ritual** affects you catastrophically.

**When you gain Corruption Score 10+ (Tier 2), you suffer:**
- Stalk-Sickness: You struggle to stop hunting, even when it's unwise
- Prey-Confusion: You begin seeing allies as potential prey
- Ritual-Loss: Killing loses sacred meaning, becoming merely violence

**Maddened Fang Table (Corruption Tier 2+)**

When you complete a long rest at Corruption Score 10+, roll 1d6:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **False Hunt:** You remember stalking prey you never hunted. Make a DC 15 Wisdom save or believe it's real and feel compelled to finish the hunt. |
| 2 | **Shadow-Blindness:** You can no longer move silently. You lose your proficiency in Stealth until your next long rest. |
| 3 | **Fang Rejection:** Your predatory instincts fail. Take 1d4 psychic damage and lose the benefits of Long-Limbed until your next long rest. |
| 4 | **Weight of Bloodlust:** You feel overwhelming urge to kill without ritual. Make a DC 12 Sanity save or lose 1d4 Sanity. |
| 5 | **Eldritch Prey:** Something marks you as prey instead of predator. Gain 1 Whisper Point but your next use of Surprise Attack fails automatically. |
| 6 | **Hunt Holds:** Your ritual discipline resists corruption this night. Reduce your Corruption Score by 1 (minimum 10). |

## Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Integration

### **Evasion Profile: Stalking Striker**
- **Dodge Evasion:** +1 (you avoid attacks through positioning)
- **Endure Evasion:** +1 (your bulk absorbs punishment)
- **Deflect Evasion:** +0 (you don't rely on deflection)

**Tactical Note:** You are designed for **ambush tactics and first-strike advantage**. Surprise Attack deals devastating damage to unaware targets, while Long-Limbed gives you reach advantage. Sneaky + your size creates unsettling juxtaposition.

### **Natural Damage Reduction:** +1
Your thick fur and dense muscle provide inherent protection. This stacks with armor.

### **Combat Philosophy**
Bugbears fight with **patient stalking and overwhelming first strikes**. You leverage Sneaky for positioning, use Long-Limbed to strike before enemies expect, and rely on Surprise Attack to end fights fast. You fight like you hunt: **patience, positioning, and perfectly timed violence**.

### **Breaking Point Mechanics**
Due to your predatory psychology and comfort with violence, you have **advantage on Rally checks** during combat. However, you have **disadvantage on Rally checks** in social situations where violence isn't appropriate. You're resilient in your element, struggle outside it.

## Languages

**Languages:** You can speak, read, and write Common and Goblin.

**Goblin** in your mouth sounds different from how goblins or hobgoblins speak it. Where goblins cackle and hobgoblins command, bugbears **growl**—every word carrying weight, threat, and primal certainty. You use Goblin as a hunting language, stripping it of unnecessary sound and reforging it into something that carries through darkness.

When Bugbears speak of sacred matters, they use **Fang-Tongue**—a whispered dialect of Goblin filled with metaphors of hunt, prey, blood, and darkness. Many words have no translation except through demonstration.

## Bugbear Subraces

While all Bugbears share the Fangborn Path, different tribes have specialized over generations through their hunting grounds and prey. Choose one of the following subraces:

### **Shadowstalk Lineage** *(Patient Hunter)*
You descend from tribes who perfected the art of **stalking in absolute darkness**, who can wait days for the perfect moment to strike.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 2, and your Dexterity score increases by 1.

**Perfect Patience:** You can remain perfectly still for extended periods without discomfort. You have advantage on saving throws against exhaustion caused by waiting or lack of movement. Additionally, when you remain motionless for at least 1 minute, you gain advantage on your next Stealth check.

**Shadowblend:** When you're in dim light or darkness, you can use a bonus action to become nearly invisible until you move or take an action. Creatures have disadvantage on Perception checks to see you while this effect lasts.

**Silent Strike:** When you hit a surprised creature with your Surprise Attack, it must make a Constitution saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength modifier) or be unable to speak or make noise until the end of your next turn. The hunt should be silent.

### **Bloodhowl Lineage** *(Ritual Predator)*
You descend from tribes who worship **Murruth** most directly, who perform elaborate hunt-rites and wear the faces of their prey.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 2, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Murruth's Mask:** You can spend 1 hour crafting a ritual mask from the remains of a creature you've killed. While wearing this mask, you gain advantage on Intimidation checks and can use your Fang-Memory ability twice per long rest instead of once. You can only have one mask active at a time.

**Blood Rite:** When you reduce a creature to 0 hit points with a melee attack, you can use your bonus action to perform a brief ritual (takes 1 round). If you do, you gain temporary hit points equal to twice your proficiency bonus and advantage on your next attack roll. This is sacred violence, not cruelty.

**Howl of the Hunt:** Once per short rest, you can unleash a terrifying howl as a bonus action. All enemies within 30 feet that can hear you must make a Wisdom saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier) or become frightened of you until the end of your next turn.

### **Nightmaw Lineage** *(Urban Stalker)*
You descend from tribes who adapted to hunting in **cities and settlements**, who learned to stalk through crowds and strike from rooftops.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 2, and your Intelligence score increases by 1.

**Urban Predator:** You have proficiency in Investigation or Insight (your choice). Additionally, you can move through crowds without penalty and have advantage on Stealth checks made in urban environments (cities, towns, settlements).

**Vertical Hunter:** You have a climbing speed equal to your walking speed. Additionally, when you make an attack against a creature while you're above them (on a roof, in rafters, etc.), you deal an extra 1d6 damage.

**Civilized Terror:** You understand how to use civilization against its inhabitants. When you use Fang-Memory in an urban setting, the DC increases by 2 (making it harder to resist). Additionally, you can read architectural blueprints and building layouts innately.

### **Bonegnaw Lineage** *(Death-Keeper)*
You descend from tribes who honor **the boundary between hunt and death**, who keep vigil over the fallen and ensure prey's memory survives.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 2, and your Wisdom score increases by 1.

**Death-Speech:** You can speak with the recently dead (within 24 hours). This works like the *Speak with Dead* spell but is a natural ability you can use once per long rest. The dead recognize you as one who honors their passing.

**Bone-Sense:** You can detect the presence of corpses or undead within 60 feet as an action. Additionally, you have advantage on saving throws against effects caused by undead.

**Honored Prey:** When you kill a creature, you can perform a 10-minute ritual to honor their death. If you do, the next time you rest, you gain insight into their life—learning one useful piece of information about them, their allies, or their goals (GM's choice). This is how you remember those you've hunted.

## Naming Conventions

Bugbear names are **guttural, primal, and often invoke predators or darkness**—designed to be growled rather than spoken.

### **Structure:**
- **Given Name:** Your birth name, often animal or darkness-related
- **Hunt-Name:** A name earned after your first significant hunt
- **Shadow-Name:** A whispered name used only in sacred contexts

### **Examples:**

**Given Names:**

*Common:* Grag, Hruuk, Krog, Murk, Skarn, Thak, Vrok, Zarg

*Fangborn:* Graaknok, Hurrash, Krakka, Murgoth, Thakkor, Vrashk

**Hunt-Names (Earned):**
- Silentstalker (perfected stealth)
- Bonebreaker (powerful strikes)
- Shadowfang (hunts in darkness)
- Fleshrender (brutal efficiency)
- Nightclaw (nocturnal specialist)
- Bloodhowl (ritual hunter)
- Preyseeker (tactical tracker)

**Shadow-Names (Sacred):**
These are rarely spoken aloud and often known only to tribal elders:
- Murruth's-Hand
- The-Thing-That-Waits
- Silence-Before-Scream
- Death's-Patience
- The-Hunt-Made-Flesh

**Full Name Examples:**
- **Hruuk Silentstalker** (male who perfected silent approach)
- **Krog Bonebreaker, called Murruth's-Hand** (female ritual hunter with shadow-name)
- **Thak Nightclaw** (non-binary urban predator)
- **Murk Shadowfang, the Thing-That-Waits** (elder death-keeper)

### **Name Practices:**
- Bugbears earn hunt-names through first major kill
- Shadow-names are given by elders during ritual ceremonies
- Some bugbears collect multiple hunt-names (Thak Nightclaw Preyseeker)
- Speaking someone's shadow-name without permission is grave taboo

## Cultural Touchstones

### **The Faith of the Gnawing Crown - Fangborn Path**
All Bugbears are raised within this framework:

- The Crown lives in blood-memory, in primal fear and silence
- Awaken ancestral power through predation and ritual fear
- The hunt is prayer; fear is the oldest truth
- Every kill must be honored or become meaningless violence
- Darkness is not absence of light but presence of truth

### **The Seven Fangs of the Hunt**
These foundational teachings guide Bugbear life:

1. **Patience Before Strike** - The stalk matters more than the kill
2. **Honor Your Prey** - Give them death worth remembering
3. **Silence is Sacred** - The hunt requires no words
4. **Fear is Gift** - To feel terror is to be truly alive
5. **Darkness is Home** - Light reveals; shadow protects
6. **Memory Through Bone** - The dead speak if you listen
7. **The Hunt Never Ends** - Even death is merely transition

### **Spiritual Roles**
Bugbear spiritual leaders are shamanic hunters:

- **Fangbound Shamans:** Ritual leaders who maintain hunt traditions
- **Bone-Keepers:** Those who preserve memory of honored prey
- **Mask-Wearers:** Priests who embody Murruth through ritual costume
- **Death-Watchers:** Those who guard boundary between hunt and murder

### **Rituals**
- **Hunt Rites:** Elaborate pre-hunt ceremonies invoking Murruth
- **Mask Ceremonies:** Crafting faces from prey remains
- **Blood-Echo Fasting:** Ritual hunger to sharpen predatory senses
- **Tunnels of Memory:** Underground vigils where dreams mix with blood to reveal truth

## Playing a Bugbear

### **Core Fantasy:**
You are the patient predator, the sacred stalker, the living proof that fear itself can be holy. You don't survive through chaos or order—you **become the thing others fear in darkness**, the patient shadow that turns stalking into prayer.

### **Mechanical Identity:**
- **Ambush Specialist:** Surprise Attack + Sneaky + Long-Limbed for devastating first strikes
- **Intimidation Master:** Fang-Memory creates fear effects
- **Patient Striker:** High Stealth despite large size creates unsettling juxtaposition
- **Psychological Fortress:** +1 Sanity makes you resistant to horror
- **Reach Advantage:** Long-Limbed provides tactical positioning benefit

### **Roleplaying Hooks:**
- **The Sacred Hunter:** Treating every combat as ritual hunt
- **The Urban Shadow:** Adapting ancient hunt-ways to city stalking
- **The Death-Keeper:** Honoring those you've killed, preserving their memory
- **The Exile:** Cast out for breaking hunt-rites, seeking redemption
- **The Teacher:** Showing others that predation can be sacred
- **The Questioner:** Starting to doubt if all hunts are holy
- **The Last Witness:** Carrying the masks of an extinct tribe

### **Party Dynamics:**
Other races often misunderstand Bugbears as mindless brutes or sadists. This creates opportunities for:

- **Scouting excellence** - Sneaky + size makes you unexpected infiltrator
- **Intimidation support** - Fang-Memory helps in social encounters
- **Protective instinct** - You hunt threats to the pack
- **Unexpected wisdom** - Death-Keepers and Bone-Keepers carry deep knowledge
- **Cultural education** - Show that fear and violence can be sacred, not evil

### **Common Character Arcs:**
- From viewing party as prey to viewing them as pack
- From ritualized violence to understanding gentleness
- From patient predator to impatient protector
- From solitary hunter to pack leader
- From sacred violence to questioning what makes killing holy
- From fear-maker to fear-breaker

## Integration with Mental Systems

### **Focus & Sanity:**
Your **+1 Sanity** is significant:
- Advantage on fear effects stronger
- Breaking Points harder to trigger
- Horror and darkness processed as familiar
- BUT your focus is standard—you're patient, not necessarily disciplined

**Typical Bugbear Mental Strategy:**
- Use high Sanity to resist horror and fear
- Frame violence as ritual rather than trauma
- Rally during combat (advantage) but struggle in peaceful social situations (disadvantage)
- Leverage comfort with darkness as tactical advantage

### **Corruption Concerns:**
Your unique corruption vulnerability centers on **dishonored violence**:
- Killing without ritual accelerates corruption
- Sadistic cruelty (vs. sacred violence) creates corruption vulnerability
- Breaking hunt-rites deals corruption damage
- At Tier 2+, the Maddened Fang table reflects loss of sacred purpose

### **Rally & Morale:**
Bugbears have combat-focused morale:
- **Advantage on Rally during combat** (comfortable with violence)
- **Disadvantage on Rally in social situations** (struggle with non-violent contexts)
- Seeing a bugbear use Surprise Attack to devastate an enemy **demoralizes enemies**
- Allies feel protected by having a predator watching their backs

## Design Philosophy

The Bugbear lineage is designed to support:

1. **The Ambush Specialist** (Surprise Attack + Sneaky + positioning)
2. **The Patient Striker** (stealth at large size creates memorable encounters)
3. **The Fear-Maker** (Fang-Memory + intimidation + size)
4. **The Sacred Predator** (rich ritual framework for RP)
5. **The Reach Controller** (Long-Limbed provides tactical positioning)

Your mechanical abilities (Surprise Attack, Long-Limbed, Sneaky, Fang-Memory) create an **ambush predator who strikes from unexpected positions with devastating force**. Unlike goblins who survive through mobility or hobgoblins who fight through coordination, you **hunt through patience and overwhelming first strikes**.

The subrace options allow you to lean into different hunting specializations—from shadow-stalkers to ritual predators to urban hunters to death-keepers—while maintaining the core Bugbear identity of sacred predation.

## Final Notes

### **The Weight of the Hunt:**
Every Bugbear carries the burden of ritualized violence. Your character likely knows:
- Your tribe's complete hunting traditions
- The names and stories of prey honored in bone-songs
- Multiple hunt-rites by heart
- The feeling of waiting hours in absolute stillness

This isn't burden—it's **calling**. You hunt knowing each kill is prayer.

### **Fear Across Cultures:**
Modern Dór-o Estel fears bugbears viscerally:
- Cities post guards specifically against bugbear incursion
- Children are told bugbear stories to frighten them into obedience
- Even hardened warriors feel unease facing bugbear ambush
- The Faith of the Bound Pattern considers bugbears proof of Pattern corruption

But some recognize deeper truth:
- Rangers acknowledge bugbear tracking skills
- Druids sometimes consult bugbear death-keepers
- Desperate people hire bugbear hunters when nothing else works
- Wise scholars study bugbear ritual violence vs mindless cruelty

### **The Hunt That Never Ends:**
Your people believe the Hunt is eternal—predator becomes prey becomes predator in endless cycle. When you die, you become prey for Death itself. If you hunted well, Death will honor you with proper respect.

You adventure knowing this truth. Some hunt to perfect their craft before Death comes. Some hunt to understand what makes killing sacred. Some hunt to prove bugbears are more than monsters.

But all bugbears carry the same certainty: **We were cast into shadow. So we became the thing that dwells there—patient, sacred, and undeniable.**
