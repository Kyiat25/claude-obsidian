# 🌍 **Geographical Zones of Dór-o Estel**

## _How Reputation, Rumor, and Law Flow Across the World_

The world of _[[Dór-o Estel]]_ is not merely a landscape—it's a network of perception, memory, and response. Every village, every alleyway, every crowded port reacts to who you are and what you've done. Whether you're a celebrated hero or a hunted criminal, your actions ripple outward, their reach shaped not just by what you did—but where you did it.

The **Geographical Zones** system formalises this spread of notoriety, helping players and DMs determine how far news travels, how NPCs react, and how risk scales based on a character's **[[Wanted Score]]**, **[[Disposition]]**, and **[[Reputation]]**.

---

## 🧭 **Zone Tiers and Spread of Influence**

|Zone|Description|Disposition|Notoriety Spread|Info Speed|Law Level|Example Distance|
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|**Hamlet**|Isolated clusters of families or kin. Suspicious of strangers.|Suspicious|Score 1–2|Very Slow (word of mouth)|Local elders or informal custom|~10–15 miles|
|**Village**|Small, trade-linked settlements. Slightly more open.|Indifferent (or Suspicious)|Score 1–2|Slow (messengers, merchants)|Reeve or local sheriff|~20–40 miles|
|**Town**|Regional hubs with commerce and governance.|Indifferent|Score 3–4|Moderate (notices, criers)|Captain, Watch, Council|~60–80 miles|
|**City**|National centers of law, trade, and culture.|Indifferent (by default)|Score 5–6|Fast (print, networks, magic)|Guard Bureau, Courts|~150–200 miles|
|**Metropolis**|Seats of empires or global trade. Vast, impersonal, influential.|Indifferent|Score 7+|Instant (international relays, spell, elite informants)|Secret Police, State Bureaus|Variable (weeks of travel)|

---
## 🔁 **How News Travels**

Each Zone defines how quickly your deeds become public knowledge:

- **Word of Mouth**: Slowest, often distorted. Hamlet-to-Village gossip.
- **Merchant Trails**: Slightly faster. Travels with caravans or barge captains.
- **Public Postings & Town Criers**: Used in Towns and up. Often reliable.
- **Printed Notices, Magical Relays**: Fastest. Cities and Metropolises may use enchanted ink, scry-nets, or divine couriers.

A crime or hero deed performed in a **Village** may reach nearby **Towns** in a week. One done in a **City** could reach a **Metropolis** in three days or less.

---
## ⚖️ **Wanted Score Integration**

The higher your **[[Wanted Score]]**, the farther and faster your reputation spreads:

|Score|Title|Notoriety Reach|Risk in Larger Zones|
|---|---|---|---|
|1–2|Known Troublemaker|Hamlet, Village|None unless persistent|
|3–4|Lawbreaker / Wanted Alive|Town, Region|Regional bounty hunters begin pursuit|
|5–6|Criminal / Dead or Alive|City, Nation|Faces recognized by guards, posted rewards|
|7+|Enemy of the State|Metropolis, International|Instant detention, elite response units|

---
## 🗺️ **Disposition by Settlement Type**

Your **default [[Disposition]]** in a zone depends on how open that community is to outsiders:

- **[[Hamlet]]**: _Suspicious_ – Outsiders viewed as threats or trouble.
- **[[Village]]**: _Indifferent or Suspicious_ depending on recent events.
- **[[Town]]**: _Indifferent_ – Expect scrutiny if known.
- **[[City]]**: _Indifferent_, but Districts may vary (_Friendly_ in trade quarters, _Suspicious_ in elite districts).
- **[[Metropolis]]**: _Indifferent_, with local modifiers by subculture, faction, or class.

Disposition can shift based on your **Reputation Title**, **Notoriety**, or **Visible [[Corruption]]/[[Fame (or Infamy)]]**.

---
## 📉 **Information Decay: Escaping Your Reputation**

Fame fades. Notoriety can be buried.

If a character avoids attention and commits no further crimes, their [[Wanted Score]] **decays over time**. DMs can use the following baseline:

|Zone of Crime|Decay Interval (per Score step)|
|---|---|
|Hamlet|1 week|
|Village|2 weeks|
|Town|1 month|
|City|3 months|
|Metropolis|6 months to 1 year|

> **[[Streetwise]] Checks** may reduce this interval if players actively suppress rumours or bribe couriers.  
> **[[Memory]] Checks** may be used by DMs to test if an NPC recalls the PC's name or face after decay begins.

---

## 🎲 **Optional Rule: Streetwise & Memory in Zone Navigation**

- **Streetwise** lets a character:
    - Know where their face is still dangerous
    - Find safe paths between zones
    - Identify or intercept rumor routes
- **Memory** allows:
    - NPCs to recall past events involving the PC
    - Players to reconstruct where and when they were seen
    - Insight into how deep their infamy is in a given zone

---
## 🧠 **Using Zones in Play**

- Treat each Zone as a **circle of reputation**. When a deed occurs, imagine it _radiating_ outward, zone by zone.
- [[Reputation]], [[Disposition]], and [[Law Enforcement]] all scale with **Zone awareness**.
- Players can use zones to plan movement, disguise, black market access, or escape from pursuit.
- NPCs use zones to judge risk, reward, and reaction. Not every guard in a Metropolis knows your face—but someone **always does**.

---
## 🏁 Conclusion

Zones offer a scalable, immersive way to **make the world react realistically** to player actions. Whether building fame or dodging infamy, understanding this system lets players navigate _Dór-o Estel_ with strategic precision—and keeps the consequences of their legend woven deep into the world.

> You are never just a name. You are a presence in the weave of rumor, fear, and hope. And every step you take is remembered—somewhere.
