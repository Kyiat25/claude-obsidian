# Goliath
## Lineage

> *"We were not shaped by gods. We were shaped by the climb."*  
> — **Valkaan, the Wind-Shouldered**, First Strider of the Horizon-Soul

You are Goliath. Not blessed, not chosen—**forged**. Your people do not kneel before gods but walk beside them as equals earned through ascent. Where others see mountains as obstacles, you see **the only direction worth living**. Where they see hardship, you see the sacred test that separates those who endure from those who merely exist.

You were not born to conquer summits. You were born **climbing**. Your ancestors carved their names into the high places not through divine favor but through breath held against thin air, through fingers frozen to stone, through the refusal to descend when every fiber screamed to retreat. Now you carry that legacy in your blood, your bones, your absolute certainty that **forward is the only direction that matters**.

---

## The Climb That Broke the Sky

According to the **Path of the Horizon-Soul**, Goliaths were not created by gods—they were **born from memory of ascent**. Before there were names, there was only stone and wind. From this silence rose **Valkaan**, the Wind-Shouldered, who simply stood up from stone and began walking toward a summit no one could see.

Behind him followed others:
- **Orenya**, who laughed when she fell and sang louder with every scar
- **Koraak**, who never spoke, but whose gaze judged kings and cowards alike
- **Uldra**, who stayed behind to teach memory, stacking stone upon stone until she became the first cairn

When they reached the point where even the wind could not breathe, the sky broke. Stars poured out like dust from a long-sealed tomb. These stars became the **Horizon-Soul**, scattered echoes of their climb. From these echoes came the Goliaths: born not of gods, but of **the memory of ascent**.

Your people believe the soul is a horizon—**always ahead, never reached**—and that the purpose of life is to move toward it with strength, grace, and honor. You do not seek divine favor. You seek **divine likeness**: to embody the echoes of those who climbed before you.

## Life Among the Heights

You were raised knowing that **the climb never ends**, that every breath is a test of resolve, that your worth is measured not in victories but in **endurance**. Goliath culture treats existence as a mountain where every step is sacred struggle.

Your lifespan is roughly **80 years**—identical to humans, but you measure time differently. You count not in years but in **ascents completed and cairns witnessed**, measuring worth not in age but in **heights reached and trials endured**.

Every significant achievement is recorded in **Cairn-Words**—stones stacked in memory of the dead, each carved with a word describing a truth of their life. These cairns mark the high places where your people proved themselves.

**Competition** is sacred among your people. Not petty rivalry, but the **Sky-Witness Trial**—constant measuring of one against another to determine who climbs highest, who endures longest, who deserves to lead. Losing such contests is not shame; **refusing to compete** is the only disgrace.

## Physical Characteristics

**Age:** You mature quickly, reaching adulthood by age 18. Few Goliaths die of old age—most die on the climb, in defense of their clan, or by walking into the final storm when they can no longer carry their name's weight.

**Size:** You stand between 7 to 8 feet tall, with powerful builds designed for endurance at altitude. Your size is Medium (barely—you're among the tallest Medium creatures).

**Speed:** Your base walking speed is 30 feet. Your body is built for mountain climbing and high-altitude endurance, not sprinting.

**Build:** Heavily muscled but lean, with mottled gray-white skin marked with dark patches that some say resemble mountain patterns. Your body is designed to withstand extreme cold, thin air, and brutal environmental conditions.

## Goliath Traits

As a Goliath, you possess the following lineage traits:

### **Natural Athlete**
You have proficiency in the Athletics skill. Your people's entire existence centers on climbing, pushing physical limits, and moving through hostile terrain.

### **Stone's Endurance**
You can focus yourself to occasionally shrug off injury. When you take damage, you can use your reaction to roll a d12. Add your Constitution modifier to the number rolled and reduce the damage by that total.

You can use this trait a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, regaining all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

This is your defining trait—**you endure**. While Orcs refuse to fall, you absorb punishment that should cripple and keep climbing. This isn't stubbornness; it's biological adaptation to environments that kill weaker peoples.

> *"She took an arrow through the shoulder at dawn. By noon she'd climbed another thousand feet. The wound? She called it 'wind's kiss' and never mentioned it again."*

### **Powerful Build**
You count as one size larger when determining your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag, or lift.

Your strength isn't merely impressive—it represents generations of ancestors who carried burdens up mountains where others would collapse.

### **Mountain Born**
You have resistance to cold damage. You're also naturally acclimated to high altitude, including elevations above 20,000 feet, and you ignore difficult terrain caused by ice or snow.

### **Little Giant**
You have proficiency with the Athletics skill (if you don't already have it from Natural Athlete, choose another skill from Acrobatics, Perception, or Survival). When you use your Stone's Endurance trait, you can also end one effect on yourself that is causing you to be frightened or charmed.

Your size and mental fortitude make certain threats simply **irrelevant**.

### **Horizon-Sense**
Once per long rest, when you're outdoors and can see the horizon or distant peaks, you can meditate for 10 minutes to gain perfect directional sense toward a location you've been to before. You instinctively know the direction and approximate distance (within 10%).

This ability reflects the Goliath spiritual connection to summits and pathways.

## Mental Attributes & Psychology

**Focus Modifier:** +1  
**Sanity Modifier:** +0

Your biology grants you **exceptional mental resilience**. While your Focus is standard, your Sanity receives a bonus because Goliath culture trains you from birth to process hardship, pain, and isolation as **necessary rather than traumatic**.

You are taught that struggle is sacred, that endurance defines worth, that the climb tests everything worth keeping. This isn't cultural conditioning alone—it's bred into your very bones through generations of surviving conditions that should have destroyed lesser peoples.

### **Cultural Mental Conditioning**
Beyond your biological advantage, Goliath upbringing creates:

- **Competitive resilience:** Constant measuring against others builds mental toughness
- **Environmental adaptation:** You process extreme conditions as normal
- **Death acceptance:** You expect to die climbing and prepare for it mentally
- **Failure as teacher:** Falling is part of learning; shame comes only from not rising

### **Corruption Vulnerability: The False Summit**

As a Goliath, you have a unique relationship with Corruption. Your connection to the Horizon-Soul and constant forward motion provides some protection, but corruption that enters through **acceptance of shortcuts, abandonment of ascent, or worship of power without effort** affects you catastrophically.

**When you gain Corruption Score 10+ (Tier 2), you suffer:**
- Directional Confusion: Your Horizon-Sense becomes unreliable
- Summit-Sickness: Standing still causes physical discomfort
- Echo-Loss: You struggle to remember the names of your cairns

**Fractured Path Table (Corruption Tier 2+)**

When you complete a long rest at Corruption Score 10+, roll 1d6:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **False Height:** You remember reaching a summit you never climbed. Make a DC 15 Wisdom save or believe it's real and feel unearned pride. |
| 2 | **Wind-Deafness:** You can no longer hear the whispers of the Horizon-Souled. You cannot use Stone's Endurance until your next long rest. |
| 3 | **Stone-Rejection:** Your body feels alien. Take 1d4 psychic damage and lose proficiency in Athletics until your next long rest. |
| 4 | **Weight of Stagnation:** You feel crushing certainty that climbing is meaningless. Make a DC 12 Sanity save or lose 1d4 Sanity. |
| 5 | **Eldritch Shortcut:** Something offers you power without ascent. Gain 1 Whisper Point but your next use of Stone's Endurance fails automatically. |
| 6 | **Path Holds:** Your connection to the climb resists corruption this night. Reduce your Corruption Score by 1 (minimum 10). |

## Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Integration

### **Evasion Profile: Adaptive Mountaineer**
- **Dodge Evasion:** +1 (you move with purpose, avoiding when needed)
- **Endure Evasion:** +1 (you withstand punishment naturally)
- **Deflect Evasion:** +0 (you don't rely on deflection)

**Tactical Note:** You are designed for **balanced durability with tactical flexibility**. Stone's Endurance provides reactive damage mitigation that stacks with armor and class features, creating "clutch save" moments.

### **Natural Damage Reduction:** +0
Unlike Orcs or Dwarves, you don't have thick hide or dense bone. Your protection comes from **adaptation and reactive endurance** (Stone's Endurance) rather than passive defense.

### **Combat Philosophy**
Goliaths fight with **measured aggression and calculated endurance**. You leverage Natural Athlete for grappling and tactical positioning, use Stone's Endurance to absorb big hits, and rely on Powerful Build to control space. You fight like you climb: **deliberately, relentlessly, adapting to terrain**.

### **Breaking Point Mechanics**
Due to your cultural conditioning and competitive nature, you treat **personal challenges differently than unit morale**. In personal combat, you're exceptionally difficult to intimidate. In group combat, you're more likely to **break from formation** to pursue individual glory or test yourself against the strongest enemy.

Rally checks when fighting **as a unit** are standard, but Rally checks when fighting **to prove worth** gain advantage.

## Languages

**Languages:** You can speak, read, and write Common and Giant.

**Giant** is a language of booming declarations and whispered secrets, well-suited to carrying across mountain valleys or through howling winds. Goliaths speak it with particular patterns—your people use the **Witness-Tongue**, a dialect that emphasizes describing deeds rather than claiming them.

When Goliaths speak of sacred matters, they often use **Cairn-Words**—ancient Giant phrases carved into stone, each word carrying layers of meaning about trials, memory, and honor.

## Goliath Subraces

While all Goliaths share the Path of the Horizon-Soul, different tribes have specialized over generations through their founding environments and challenges. Choose one of the following subraces:

### **Cloudreach Lineage** *(Sky-Walkers)*
You descend from tribes who live above the snowline, in places where even other Goliaths struggle to breathe. Your ancestors learned to walk on wind and sleep standing on ledges where falling means death.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 2, and your Wisdom score increases by 1.

**Sky-Step:** You ignore the first 10 feet of falling damage you would take during any fall. When you fall and take damage, you can immediately use your reaction to move up to 10 feet in any horizontal direction (representing controlled falling/jumping).

**Thin Air Adaptation:** You can hold your breath for a number of minutes equal to 1 + twice your Constitution modifier (minimum 2 minutes). You have advantage on Constitution saving throws caused by altitude, thin air, or breathing difficulties.

**Wind-Walker Heritage:** You have proficiency in Perception. When you're in high-altitude or windy environments, you can use the Wind-Walker's art of reading air currents—you have advantage on Perception checks to detect approaching weather, hidden creatures, or changes in air pressure.

### **Icepeak Lineage** *(Frost-Marked)*
You descend from **Tharuk Frostbone** and the tribes who dwell in the eternal glaciers, places where stone itself is wrapped in endless winter. Your people endure cold that would kill others in hours.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 2, and your Constitution score increases by 1.

**Deep Winter's Child:** Your resistance to cold damage becomes immunity to cold damage. Additionally, you ignore all negative effects of extreme cold weather.

**Frost-Warded:** When you use your Stone's Endurance trait, you can create a thin layer of frost over your skin. Until the start of your next turn, any creature that hits you with a melee attack takes cold damage equal to your Constitution modifier (minimum 1).

**Glacial Patience:** Once per long rest, when you fail a saving throw, you can choose to succeed instead. Your ancestors learned to outlast avalanches by simply refusing to break.

### **Stonefall Lineage** *(Earth-Bound)*
You descend from the tribes who refused to climb higher, instead digging into the living stone of the mountains themselves. Your people became half-dwarf in mindset, learning to read stone as language.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 2, and your Intelligence score increases by 1.

**Stone-Tongue:** You can communicate simple ideas with earth and stone. You know if a wall is hollow, a floor is trapped, or a tunnel is stable. You have advantage on Investigation checks related to stonework.

**Mountain's Memory:** You have proficiency in History. When you touch worked stone, you can sense the emotional resonance of its creation—whether it was made with pride, fear, haste, or love.

**Rockborn Resilience:** Your Stone's Endurance die increases from d12 to d12 + your Constitution modifier. When you use Stone's Endurance, you take root—you cannot be moved against your will until the start of your next turn.

### **Skyhorn Lineage** *(Storm-Called)*
You descend from **Orenya, the Laughing Scar**, and the tribes who climb **during** storms rather than avoiding them. Your ancestors learned to hear lightning as language and embrace thunder as song.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 2, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Storm-Born:** You have resistance to lightning and thunder damage. You ignore difficult terrain and visibility penalties caused by storms, high winds, or heavy precipitation.

**Thunder-Voice:** Once per short rest, when you use your Stone's Endurance trait, you can let out a thunderous shout. All creatures within 10 feet of you must succeed on a Constitution saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier) or be deafened until the end of your next turn.

**Laughing Scar Heritage:** When you take damage from a critical hit, you can use your reaction to laugh defiantly. The next attack you make before the end of your next turn has advantage. You embrace pain as Orenya did—turning it into strength.

## Naming Conventions

Goliath names carry **weight and witness**. They are not mere labels but **records of achievement**, **acknowledgments of nature**, and **promises of ascent**. Your name tells your story to those who understand the language of the heights.

### **Structure:**
- **Birth Name:** Given by family, often describing a feature or moment of birth
- **Clan Name:** Your tribe designation, usually referencing geography
- **Earned Name/Epithet:** A title earned after significant trials (often replaces birth name)
- **Witness Name:** Additional titles earned through exceptional deeds

### **Examples:**

**Birth Names (Given at Birth):**

*Male-pattern:* Aukan, Eglath, Gae-Al, Gauthak, Ilikan, Keothi, Kuori, Lo-Kag, Manneo, Maveith, Nalla, Orilo, Paavu, Pethani, Thalai, Thotham, Uthal, Vaunea, Vimak

*Female-pattern:* Gae-Al, Ilikan, Keothi, Kuori, Manneo, Nalla, Orilo, Paavu, Pethani, Uthal, Vaunea

*Note: Many Goliath names are gender-neutral; the distinction matters less than the deed.*

**Clan Names (Geographic or Ancestral):**
- Anakalathai (Endless Horizon)
- Elanithino (Sky-Seeker) 
- Gathakanathi (Summit-Caller)
- Kalagiano (Stone-Song)
- Kolae-Gileana (Frost-Walker)
- Ogolakanu (Storm-Path)
- Thuliaga (Wind-Shoulder)
- Thunukalathi (Ice-Cutter)
- Vaimei-Laga (Cloud-Touch)

**Earned Epithets (Achievements):**
- Cliff-Dancer (first to scale a specific face)
- Storm-Stander (endured a legendary blizzard)
- Wound-Singer (turned injury into triumph)
- Echo-Breaker (accomplished what others said impossible)
- Last-to-Fall (outlasted all rivals in contest)
- First-After-Frost (led through deadly winter)
- Bone-Carrier (retrieved fallen comrade from impossible place)

**Full Name Examples:**
- **Vimak Stormpath Storm-Stander** (male who survived the legendary Skyhorn Blizzard)
- **Keothi Thuliaga Cliff-Dancer** (female who first climbed the Wind-Shouldered Face)
- **Lo-Kag Gathakanathi Echo-Breaker** (non-binary who accomplished the Impossible Fifth Ascent)
- **Nalla Kolae-Gileana Last-to-Fall** (female who won the Trial of Endurance against seven rivals)

### **Name Earning:**
- Children receive a birth name and clan name
- The **first earned epithet** typically comes during late adolescence (age 15-20)
- Additional epithets accumulate throughout life based on achievements
- Using an epithet you haven't earned is considered **spiritual theft** and may result in exile or a forced trial
- Some Goliaths abandon their birth names entirely, going only by epithet and clan name

**Nickname Practice:** Goliaths often shorten formal names when among friends—Vimak Stormpath becomes "Vim" or "Stormpath." But during trials, challenges, or formal occasions, **full names must be spoken** to properly witness the achievement.

## Cultural Touchstones

### **The Path of the Horizon-Soul**
All Goliaths are raised within this faith. Even if you've left your clan or questioned tradition, its lessons shaped you:

- The soul is a horizon—always ahead, never reached
- The body is a cairn—you mark your journey with endurance
- The climb never ends—even in death, you ascend
- Worth is measured by trials endured, not victories claimed
- To refuse competition is to refuse growth

### **The Five Stones of Ascent**
These foundational truths guide Goliath life:

1. **The Soul Is a Horizon** - It moves with you; walk toward it
2. **The Body Is a Cairn** - Mark your journey with acts of endurance
3. **The Clan Is the Rope** - You do not climb alone
4. **The Wind Is the Test** - Without struggle, you are hollow
5. **The Summit Is Not the End** - Even in death, the climb continues

### **Spiritual Roles**
While not priests in the traditional sense, certain Goliaths take on spiritual responsibilities:

- **Stone-Speakers:** Keepers of cairns, oaths, and tribal memory
- **Wind-Walkers:** Interpreters of signs—clouds, avalanches, echoes
- **Climb-Bound:** Those on oath-bound wandering or trial
- **Echo-Bearers:** Those who carry the unfinished burden of the fallen

### **Rituals**
- **Sky-Witness Trial:** Solitary climb to sacred peak for coming of age
- **Cairn-Words:** Stone stacking ceremony for the dead
- **Bone-Mark:** Oath-binding through scarification or carving
- **Stone Song:** Communal remembrance through droning chants

## Playing a Goliath

### **Core Fantasy:**
You are the deliberate climber, the sacred competitor, the one who measures worth through endurance. You don't conquer mountains—you **become** them, ascending through trials that would break lesser beings. Your every step forward is spiritual practice.

### **Mechanical Identity:**
- **Reactive Tank:** Stone's Endurance provides flexible damage mitigation
- **Athletic Specialist:** Natural Athlete + Powerful Build creates grappling/climbing excellence
- **Environmental Master:** Mountain Born + cold resistance makes you dominant in harsh terrain
- **Mental Fortress:** +1 Sanity makes you resistant to fear and horror effects
- **Sustained Endurance:** Multiple uses of Stone's Endurance per day creates sustained durability

### **Roleplaying Hooks:**
- **The Exile Seeker:** Searching for clan members who vanished on a forbidden climb
- **The Unproven:** Seeking worthy challenges to earn your first real epithet
- **The Final Climb:** Elderly Goliath on one last journey before walking into the storm
- **The Bridge-Walker:** Teaching lowlanders about the sacred nature of competition
- **The Cairn-Builder:** Collecting stories to add to your clan's Stone Songs
- **The Lost Summit:** Tracking down a legendary peak from ancient tales
- **The Horizon-Chaser:** Following visions received during meditation

### **Party Dynamics:**
Other races often misunderstand Goliaths as competitive to a fault or recklessly brave. This creates opportunities for:

- **Education through deed** - Show that competition creates excellence, not enmity
- **Quiet witness** - Let your endurance speak when words fail
- **Cultural exchange** - Explain why you measure against others, why heights matter
- **Surprising wisdom** - Stonefall lineage can shock with tactical insight
- **Protective nature** - Use your size and strength to shield smaller allies

### **Common Character Arcs:**
- From solo competitor to understanding team dynamics
- From measuring only physical challenges to valuing mental/social trials
- From seeking summits to building communities
- From viewing death as endpoint to understanding legacy
- From rigid tradition to adapted honor
- From name-hungry to name-earned
- From competing against others to competing against self

### **Focus & Sanity:**
Your **+1 Sanity** is significant:
- Advantage on fear effects becomes even stronger
- Breaking Points are harder to trigger
- Mental attacks are less effective
- Environmental stress (isolation, extreme conditions) is processed differently

**Typical Goliath Mental Strategy:**
- Use high Sanity to resist battlefield panic
- Frame suffering as necessary trial rather than trauma

### **Corruption Concerns:**
Your unique corruption vulnerability centers on **shortcuts and false summits**:
- Seeking power without earning it accelerates corruption
- Abandoning competition or refusing challenges creates corruption vulnerability
- Worshiping gods rather than walking beside them as equals
- At Tier 2+, the Fractured Path table reflects abandonment of ascent

### **Rally & Morale:**
Goliaths have complex morale dynamics:
- **Advantage on personal Rally checks** (individual resilience)
- **Disadvantage on unit cohesion** if you break formation to pursue personal glory
- Witnessing a Goliath use Stone's Endurance to endure massive damage **inspires allies** (potential Rally bonus for others)
- Enemy forces become demoralized facing opponents who won't stop climbing/advancing

## Design Philosophy

The Goliath lineage is designed to support:

1. **The Enduring Tank** (Stone's Endurance + cold resistance + mental resilience)
2. **The Sacred Competitor** (constant measurement against others drives growth)
3. **The Mountain Master** (Mountain Born + Natural Athlete creates environmental dominance)
4. **The Spiritual Climber** (Path of Horizon-Soul provides rich RP framework)
5. **The Flexible Survivor** (balanced evasion + reactive mitigation allows adaptation)

Your mechanical abilities (Stone's Endurance, Powerful Build, Natural Athlete, Mountain Born) create a **adaptable survivor who absorbs punishment and keeps advancing**. Unlike Orcs who refuse to fall or Dwarves who anchor themselves, you **absorb, adapt, and continue ascending**.

The subrace options allow you to lean into different tribal traditions—from sky-walking Cloudreach to glacier-bound Icepeak to storm-embracing Skyhorn—while maintaining the core Goliath identity of measured, relentless ascent.

## Final Notes

### **The Weight of Names:**
Every Goliath carries the pressure of witness. Your character likely knows:
- At least 3-5 significant cairn-sites by location and story
- The names of legendary climbers from your tribe
- Multiple Stone Songs by heart
- The founding tale of your clan

This isn't burden—it's **context**. You climb knowing others watch, judge, and remember.

### **Respect Across Cultures:**
Modern Dór-o Estel recognizes Goliath strength:
- Dwarves respect the parallel to their own craft-endurance
- Orcs acknowledge the similar honor-through-trial philosophy
- Humans admire the clarity of measured competition
- Even the Faith of the Bound Pattern cites Goliath trials as spiritual examples

### **The Horizon Beyond Death:**
Your people believe death is **transition into longer climb**. The worthy dead become part of the Summit Echo, appearing in dreams and avalanche-songs to guide the living. Some even return as **Sky-Whispers**—wind-carried voices that warn of danger or encourage the struggling.

You adventure knowing your ancestors witness every step, that your cairn-words will be judged by those who come after, that climbing doesn't end with breath. This is not pressure to fear—it is **certainty** that endurance creates eternal echo.
