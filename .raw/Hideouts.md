# The Hidden Edge

When a name becomes too loud, it must learn to hide. Whether hunted by guards, rival gangs, or betrayed allies, a fugitive needs more than speed—they need a place the world forgets. **Hideouts and Safe Houses** offer that edge: a concealed sanctuary to rest, recover, and regroup. This system introduces rules for establishing and maintaining hidden shelters across [[Dór-o Estel]], including mechanical support for risk, discovery, and upgrades. More than just a bed behind a bookshelf or a cave beneath the ruins, a well-run hideout becomes a lifeline, a staging ground, and sometimes, the last place standing between survival and capture.

# Establishing a Hideout

- **Finding a Location:** Characters can find potential hideouts through exploration, investigation (DC 12 Intelligence (Investigation) check), or by contacting informants (requiring connections or a Charisma (Persuasion) check), or by knowing a "good place" Charisma ([[Streetwise]]) check. Suitable locations could include abandoned buildings, hidden caves, secret rooms in taverns, or even a secluded spot in the wilderness.

- **Setting Up:** Requires resources and time. The cost and time depend on the desired level of security and comfort. A basic hideout might take a few days and a modest sum of gold, while a fortified safe house could require weeks and a significant investment. Characters can use relevant skills like Dexterity (Thieves' Tools) for creating secret entrances, Intelligence (Investigation) for finding hidden compartments or weak points, and Wisdom (Survival) for camouflaging a wilderness hideout.

- **Security Measures:** Characters can invest in security measures such as traps, alarms, secret passages, or hired guards. Each measure has a cost and provides a bonus to the hideout's defence against intruders.
 
## **Maintaining a Hideout**

- **Ongoing Costs:** Hideouts require regular upkeep, including repairs, supplies, bribes (if necessary), and potentially paying off informants or guards.

- **Risks:**
    - **Discovery:** There's always a risk that the hideout will be discovered by rivals, law enforcement, or nosy neighbours. The risk increases with the character's Wanted Score and the frequency of their criminal activity. A discovered Hideout will be raided 2d6 days after it has been discovered.
    - **Betrayal:** Informants or hired help might betray the characters for a better offer or under duress.
    - **Raids:** [[Law enforcement]] or rival gangs could raid the hideout, leading to a potentially dangerous encounter.
## **Benefits**

- **Safe Haven:** Provides a secure place to rest, recover, and store illicit goods.
- **Strategic Advantage:** Can serve as a base of operations for planning criminal activities or launching ambushes.
- **Information Network:** Can be used to establish an information network by attracting informants and contacts.
- **[[Black Market]]**: Setting up a safe location for the trading of goods not usually available "over the counter" in the same manner.
