# The Reaper

**Harvesters of Souls and Dealers of Death**

> *"All things end. I have simply learned to harness that ending."*

---

## Death's Harvester

The battlefield falls silent as the last enemy draws their final breath. Around you, ethereal wisps rise from the fallen—departing souls visible only to your eyes. You reach out, and they flow into you like water into a vessel, filling you with cold power. Your next strike erupts with necrotic energy, the collected essence of those who fell before now fuelling the death of those who remain.

Listen well, seeker of death's power, for the path of the Reaper is one of profound certainty: **all things end, and you have learned to harness that ending.**

At the core of your power lies **Soul Harvest**—the ability to capture departing life force when creatures fall in battle. These harvested souls become Soul Tokens, a supernatural resource you channel through your weapons, creating devastating necrotic strikes and unnatural resilience. You are not a necromancer who enslaves the dead. You are a harvester who collects what they leave behind.

Your **Reaper's Strike** transforms ordinary attacks into conduits for captured souls. Each blow carries the weight of those who fell before, erupting with necrotic energy that withers flesh and breaks spirits. Your **Death Ward** allows you to coat yourself in protective necrotic energy, absorbing damage that would otherwise fell you.

As you grow in power, features like **Execution** let you guarantee critical hits on wounded enemies, **Soul Empowerment** grants temporary advantages fuelled by death itself, and **Reaper's Presence** makes you a walking nexus of mortality that affects everyone nearby. When enemies become bloodied and their courage wavers, you harvest not just their lives but their breaking morale—feeding on fear itself.

The **Reaper's Covenant** at 3rd level defines your relationship with death:
- **Grave Warden**: Protect the dying and ensure peaceful passage
- **Soul Drinker**: Consume life force to sustain yourself
- **Death Knight**: Execute enemies with supernatural precision

Your **Epic Boon** at 20th level represents ultimate mastery of your chosen path, transforming you into death's avatar.

---

## The Reaper's Essence

In FEARS, a Reaper is a **tactically nuanced martial class** that rewards smart positioning, resource management, and understanding combat flow. You track multiple resources—Soul Tokens, enemy HP thresholds, positioning ranges, and mental attributes—making moment-to-moment decisions about when to spend your limited power for maximum effect.

You excel when:
- **Enemies cluster together** (Soul Harvest range matters)
- **You contribute to kills** (harvest from bloodied thresholds and deaths)
- **Enemy morale breaks** (harvest tokens from failed Breaking Points)
- **You position carefully** (Death Throes require safe distance)

You struggle when:
- **Isolated from combat** (can't harvest souls)
- **Facing undead/constructs** (immune to morale, fewer harvest opportunities)
- **Your Focus degrades** (maximum Soul Token capacity shrinks)
- **Fighting solo** (need allies to create harvest opportunities)

## Quick Build

To quickly create a Reaper, follow these steps:

1. **Ability Scores**: Make Strength or Dexterity your highest ability score (depending on weapon choice), followed by Constitution, then Wisdom (for mental resilience and Soul Focus)
2. **Lineage and Heritage**: Any combination works; undead-touched or death-themed options are thematic
3. **Background**: Soldier, Mercenary, Gravedigger, or any background with Intimidation or Medicine
4. **Combat Strategy**: Position for Soul Harvest range, contribute damage to get kills or wounds, manage Soul Token spending carefully

## Core Class Features

### Soul Harvest (1st Level)

When a creature dies within **30 feet** of you, you harvest its departing soul, gaining **1 Soul Token**. You can have a maximum number of Soul Tokens based on your Soul Focus feature (see 3rd level).

Additionally, when you reduce a creature to half its hit points or lower for the first time (making them **bloodied**), you gain **1 Soul Token**. You can gain this token only once per creature.

**Morale Harvester**: When an enemy creature within 30 feet fails a Breaking Point save (from the Bloodied, Breaking Points & Rally system), you gain **1 Soul Token** as you harvest their breaking courage. This can occur multiple times per combat as different enemies break.

**Soul Token Uses**: You can spend Soul Tokens to fuel your Reaper abilities, including:
- Empowering your attacks with necrotic damage
- Protecting yourself with necrotic energy
- Gaining combat advantages
- Activating your Covenant features

**Carrying Souls**: Soul Tokens remain with you until you spend them or finish a long rest. At the end of a long rest, your Soul Tokens reset to 0.

**Multiple Deaths**: If multiple creatures die simultaneously (such as from an area effect), you gain 1 Soul Token per creature, up to your maximum.

**Creature Size**: You can harvest souls from creatures of Tiny size or larger, but Tiny creatures must have a CR of 1/4 or higher. Swarms, familiars, and conjured creatures of CR 0 provide no tokens.

**Reliable Harvest**: At the start of each of your turns in combat, if you currently have 0 Soul Tokens, you gain 1 Soul Token. This token disappears at the end of your turn if not spent.

---

### Reaper's Strike (1st Level)

Your attacks carry the weight of harvested souls. When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can spend Soul Tokens to deal extra **necrotic damage**.

**Cost and Damage**:
- **Levels 1-4**: 1 Soul Token = 1d6 necrotic damage
- **Levels 5-16**: 2 Soul Tokens = 2d6 necrotic damage
- **Levels 17-20**: 2 Soul Tokens = 3d6 necrotic damage

You can use this feature multiple times per turn if you have sufficient Soul Tokens, but you can only add Reaper's Strike damage **once per weapon attack**.

**Example**: At 5th level with Extra Attack and 4 Soul Tokens, you could spend 2 tokens on your first attack for 2d6 necrotic damage and 2 tokens on your second attack for another 2d6 necrotic damage (total: 4d6 necrotic across both attacks).

---

### Death Ward (2nd Level)

You can channel harvested souls into protective necrotic energy. As a **bonus action**, spend **2 Soul Tokens** to gain temporary hit points equal to **1d10 + your Reaper level**.

These temporary hit points last until depleted or until you finish a short or long rest.

**Scaling**: At 10th level, you gain **2d10 + your Reaper level** temporary hit points instead.

---

### Fighting Style (2nd Level)

You adopt a particular style of fighting as your specialty. Choose one of the following options:

**Great Weapon Fighting**: When you roll a 1 or 2 on a damage die for an attack you make with a melee weapon that you are wielding with two hands, you can reroll the die and must use the new roll. The weapon must have the two-handed or versatile property.

**Dueling**: When you are wielding a melee weapon in one hand and no other weapons, you gain a +2 bonus to damage rolls with that weapon.

**Defense**: While you are wearing armor, you gain a +1 bonus to AC.

**Two-Weapon Fighting**: When you engage in two-weapon fighting, you can add your ability modifier to the damage of the second attack.

---

### Reaper's Covenant (3rd Level)

Your approach to death and soul harvesting defines your path. Choose one of the following Covenants:

- **Grave Warden**: Protect the dying and ensure peaceful passage
- **Soul Drinker**: Consume life force to sustain and empower yourself  
- **Death Knight**: Execute enemies with supernatural precision

Your choice grants features at 3rd, 6th, 10th, 11th, 14th, and 17th level, and determines your Epic Boon at 20th level.

See "Reaper's Covenants" later in this class description for details on each Covenant.

---

### Soul Focus (3rd Level)

Your mental discipline determines how many souls you can hold simultaneously. Your maximum Soul Token capacity equals:

**Base Maximum (from Progression Table) + Focus Modifier**

**Example**: At 3rd level (base maximum 2) with Focus 14 (+2), your maximum is 2 + 2 = 4 tokens.

As your Focus degrades from magical strain or eldritch exposure, your maximum token capacity shrinks. This creates a direct mechanical consequence for mental degradation—you literally lose the ability to hold as many souls.

**Focus Recovery**: You can restore Focus through rest, meditation, or spending downtime in places of spiritual calm. Your DM determines specific recovery methods based on what caused the degradation.

---

### Ability Score Improvement (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th Level)

You can increase one ability score by 2, or two ability scores by 1 each. You can't increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

Alternatively, you can take a feat.

---

### Extra Attack (5th Level)

You can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.

---

### Execution (7th Level)

You've learned to deliver killing blows to wounded enemies. When you hit a creature that is below half its hit point maximum (bloodied) with a weapon attack, you can spend **3 Soul Tokens** to automatically score a **critical hit**.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your **proficiency bonus** per long rest.

**Morale Break**: If this attack kills an enemy leader, all enemies within 120 feet who witnessed the kill must immediately make a Breaking Point check at **+4 DC penalty** (instead of the normal +2) as they watch their leader executed with brutal precision.

**Rally Interrupt**: If you use Execution on an enemy leader who is attempting an Auto Rally (triggered by being bloodied), your attack interrupts their rally cry. If the attack kills them, the Rally automatically fails and all enemies within range lose **-2 Momentum** instead of the normal -1.

**Synergy**: Critical hits on creatures below half HP are very likely to kill them, which generates a Soul Token through Soul Harvest, partially refunding the cost.

---

### Soul Empowerment (9th Level)

You can spend harvested souls to empower yourself. As a **bonus action**, spend **2 Soul Tokens** to gain one of the following benefits until the end of your next turn:

- **Advantage** on all attack rolls
- **Advantage** on all saving throws
- **Advantage** on all ability checks
- **+10 feet** to your movement speed and your attacks score critical hits on 19-20

You can only have one Soul Empowerment benefit active at a time.

---

### Harvest the Broken (9th Level)

When enemies rout, flee, or surrender within 30 feet of you, you can harvest the despair of their broken courage.

**Broken Harvest**: When an enemy fails a Breaking Point save within 30 feet of you and routs, you may use your **reaction** to harvest their broken spirit, gaining **2 Soul Tokens** (instead of the normal 1 from Morale Harvester).

**Reaping Panic**: Until the end of your next turn after harvesting from a routing enemy, you gain the following benefits:
- +2 to attack rolls against frightened or routing creatures
- Your movement speed increases by 10 feet
- You can move through routing enemy spaces without provoking opportunity attacks

**Cascading Fear**: Once per turn, when you kill a routing enemy within 30 feet, all other routing enemies within 30 feet must make a Wisdom save (DC = 8 + proficiency + Constitution modifier) or be **frightened** of you for 1 minute.

---

### Heroic Boon (10th Level)

You gain a transformative ability that enhances your mastery over death. Choose one of the following Heroic Boons:

#### Death's Resilience

Your connection to death makes you incredibly difficult to kill.

**Benefits**:
- Your base Soul Token maximum increases by 2 (this stacks with your Focus modifier)
- When you would be reduced to 0 hit points, you can spend **4 Soul Tokens** as a reaction to instead drop to 1 hit point. You can use this ability once per long rest.
- You have advantage on death saving throws
- When you stabilize a dying creature (including yourself), you gain 1 Soul Token

#### Reaper's Harvest

You harvest souls more efficiently and powerfully.

**Benefits**:
- When a creature dies within 30 feet of you, you gain **1 additional Soul Token** (so 2 total instead of 1), still subject to your maximum
- When you score a critical hit that kills a creature, you gain **1 additional Soul Token** (so 3 total: 1 from death, 1 from Reaper's Harvest, 1 from critical kill)
- Once per turn, when you spend Soul Tokens on Reaper's Strike, you can spend **1 additional token** to force the target to make a Constitution saving throw (DC = 8 + proficiency + Constitution modifier) or have **vulnerability to necrotic damage** until the end of your next turn

#### Death's Momentum

You channel death's energy into superior combat flow.

**Benefits**:
- When you harvest a soul (gain a Soul Token from Soul Harvest), you gain +1 Momentum. This can occur only once per round.
- When you drop an enemy to 0 hit points, your movement speed increases by 10 feet until the end of your turn
- You can use Death Ward as a free action once per turn (still costs Soul Tokens)

---

### Reaper's Presence (13th Level)

Your aura of death becomes palpable. You gain the following benefits:

**Aura of Death**: Creatures of your choice within 10 feet of you have disadvantage on death saving throws. When a creature fails a death saving throw within this aura, you gain 1 Soul Token.

**Dread Presence**: Enemies within 30 feet of you have **disadvantage on Breaking Point saves**. This represents the psychological weight of death itself standing among them—they can feel your harvest coming.

**Death Sense**: You always know the current hit point total of creatures within 30 feet of you. You can sense when creatures within 60 feet are undead or have died within the past hour.

**Morale Sense**: You can sense the morale state of enemy groups within 60 feet. You know:
- Which enemies are at negative Momentum
- Which enemies have failed Breaking Points
- Which enemies are routing or considering flight
- The approximate location of enemy leaders

**Unnatural Vitality**: While you have at least 3 Soul Tokens, you don't need to eat, drink, or breathe. You can go without sleep for up to 3 days without suffering exhaustion (after 3 days, normal exhaustion rules apply).

---

### Death's Inevitability (15th Level)

Your strikes become inescapable. When you make a weapon attack, you can spend **4 Soul Tokens** to make the attack ignore:
- All damage reduction
- Resistance to necrotic damage
- Cover bonuses to AC
- The target's Dodge or Deflect Evasion

Additionally, if this attack reduces the target to 0 hit points, you gain **2 Soul Tokens** (in addition to the normal 1 from Soul Harvest).

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

---

### Soul Resonance (18th Level)

Your mastery over harvested souls reaches its peak.

**Resonant Power**: For each Soul Token you currently possess, you gain the following benefits:
- +1 to weapon attack rolls
- +1 to weapon damage rolls
- +5 feet to movement speed

**Example**: With 4 Soul Tokens, you have +4 to hit, +4 to damage, and +20 feet movement speed.

**Soul Overflow**: When you would gain Soul Tokens that would exceed your maximum, you can grant those excess tokens to allies within 30 feet. Allies can hold up to 3 Soul Tokens and can spend them on your Reaper's Strike ability (using your damage dice) until the end of your next long rest.

**Death's Embrace**: Once per long rest, when you drop to 0 hit points, you can immediately spend all your Soul Tokens (minimum 4) to return to life with hit points equal to **5 × Soul Tokens spent**. When you do this, all creatures within 15 feet must make a Wisdom saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier) or take **3d10 necrotic damage** and be frightened of you for 1 minute.

---

### Epic Boon (20th Level)

You achieve ultimate mastery over death itself. Choose one of the following Epic Boons:

#### Guardian of the Veil

You have become the ultimate protector, standing at the boundary between life and death.

**Benefits**:
- You cannot die while any ally is conscious within 60 feet of you. If you would die, you drop to 1 hit point instead
- All allies within 30 feet gain +2 to AC and saving throws
- When an ally within 30 feet drops to 0 hit points, you can spend **1 Soul Token** as a reaction (no additional action cost beyond the reaction) to have them drop to 1 instead
- Once per long rest, you can resurrect up to 6 creatures who died within the past hour within 60 feet of you as an action, without material components

#### Eternal Hunger

You transcend mortality, becoming a perpetual engine of life drain.

**Benefits**:
- At the start of each of your turns, regain hit points equal to **the number of Soul Tokens you have × 3**
- Your Vampiric Strike healing (if you have it) can exceed your maximum hit points, granting you temporary hit points for the excess (up to your Reaper level × 2)
- When you drop to 0 hit points, if you have at least 4 Soul Tokens, you can spend them all to instead drop to **10 × Soul Tokens spent** hit points. This happens automatically (no action required)
- You gain resistance to necrotic damage and immunity to poison damage and the poisoned condition

#### Inevitable End

You have become death incarnate, an unstoppable force that ends all things.

**Benefits**:
- You can spend **2 Soul Tokens** to make your next weapon attack within the next minute automatically critical hit against creatures below 50% of their maximum hit points
- When you kill a creature, you can make one additional weapon attack as a bonus action. This bonus action attack can trigger Death Sentence. You can do this once per round.
- Your critical hit range increases to 18-20 against all creatures
- Once per long rest, you can activate **Death's Decree**: For 1 minute, all creatures you can see within 60 feet are marked (as if by Mark of Death if you have it), you have advantage on all attack rolls, and all your weapon attacks deal an additional **2d8 necrotic damage**

## Class Reactions

Reapers have access to powerful reactions channeling death's power. These reactions use your **Proficiency Bonus pool** (shared across all class reactions) which refreshes on a short or long rest.

### Class Reaction Uses & Recovery

- **Shared Pool**: You have a shared pool of Class Reaction uses equal to your **Proficiency Bonus (PB)**
- **Refresh**: The pool refreshes on a short or long rest
- **One per round**: You can still take only 1 reaction each round
- **Opportunity Attacks**: Do not spend a class reaction use (unless a feature explicitly says otherwise)

---

### Soul Shield (2nd Level)

**Trigger**: You are hit by an attack  
**Cost**: 1 reaction use + 1 Soul Token  
**Effect**: Reduce the damage by **2d10 + your Constitution modifier**. If this reduces the damage to 0, you regain the spent Soul Token and gain +1 Momentum.

**Scaling**: 
- 10th level: **3d10 + Constitution modifier**
- 15th level: **4d10 + Constitution modifier**

**Tactical Use**: Soul Shield is your primary defensive reaction. The ability to potentially refund the Soul Token cost (if you negate all damage) and gain Momentum creates interesting risk-reward decisions. Use this to survive big hits that would otherwise break through your armor.

---

### Death Mark (7th Level)

**Trigger**: A creature within 30 feet drops to 0 hit points  
**Cost**: 1 reaction use  
**Effect**: Choose one creature within 30 feet of the dying creature. That creature takes necrotic damage equal to **your Reaper level + Constitution modifier** and you gain 1 Soul Token.

**Scaling**: Damage increases automatically as you level (Reaper level + CON mod).

**Tactical Use**: Death Mark lets you capitalize on ally kills, not just your own. When the wizard drops an enemy with a fireball, you harvest the death energy and redirect it into another enemy. This keeps you active and dangerous even when you're not landing killing blows.

**Synergy**: Combines beautifully with area effects and multi-target attacks from allies.

---

### Reaper's Retribution (13th Level)

**Trigger**: A creature within 30 feet reduces an ally to 0 hit points  
**Cost**: 1 reaction use  
**Effect**: You can immediately make a weapon attack against that creature. If this attack hits, it automatically becomes a **critical hit** and you gain **2 Soul Tokens**.

**Tactical Use**: Reaper's Retribution punishes enemies who drop your allies. The automatic critical hit ensures significant damage, and the 2 Soul Token gain helps you immediately respond with powerful follow-up strikes on your next turn.

**Positioning**: Stay within 30 feet of fragile allies to maximize opportunities to use this reaction.

---

### Deathblow Panic (15th Level)

**Trigger**: You kill an enemy leader or highest CR creature within 30 feet  
**Cost**: 1 reaction use  
**Effect**: 

All enemies within 60 feet who witnessed the kill must immediately make a Breaking Point save, even if they wouldn't normally trigger one yet (e.g., casualties aren't at 25% threshold).

The DC for this Breaking Point is:
- **Base DC 15** if no other casualties
- **Current casualty tier DC +2** if casualties already exist

Additionally, all enemies within 30 feet of the slain leader must make a Wisdom save (DC = 8 + proficiency + Constitution modifier) or be **frightened** of you until the end of your next turn.

**Tactical Use**: Use this after killing a leader to potentially trigger mass routs before normal Breaking Point thresholds. Combine with Execution for maximum morale impact. Position near enemy formations before killing their leader to create "shock and awe" moments that can end battles instantly.

**Scaling**: At 18th level (Soul Resonance), enemies frightened by this ability have their speed reduced to 0 until the end of your next turn—paralyzed by terror at witnessing their leader's execution.

## Last Stand Options

When death comes for you, you have one final opportunity to harvest souls and unleash devastation. You can invoke your Last Stand when:

1. You would be reduced to 0 hit points
2. You roll a death saving throw
3. You die outright

Choose one of the following Last Stand options:

---

### Final Harvest

You explode in a burst of necrotic energy, consuming all the souls you've harvested.

**Effect**: 
- You immediately die (no death saves)
- All Soul Tokens you have are consumed
- All creatures within **10 feet × Soul Tokens consumed** must make a Constitution saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier)
- On a failure, they take **1d10 necrotic damage per Soul Token consumed** (maximum 6d10) and have their maximum hit points reduced by the damage dealt (until long rest)
- On a success, they take half damage and no maximum HP reduction
- All allies within the area gain temporary hit points equal to **your Reaper level × 2**

**Thematic Note**: "My death feeds you one last time. Use this power—finish what I started."

---

### Soul Anchor

You bind your soul to the mortal plane, refusing death's call.

**Effect**:
- You immediately die (no death saves)
- Your body remains where it fell
- For the next **1 minute**, you can manifest as a spectral form by spending 1 Soul Token as a bonus action
- While manifested:
  - You appear as a ghostly version of yourself
  - You have a fly speed of 30 ft and can hover
  - You can move through creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain (you take 1d10 force damage if you end your turn inside an object)
  - You can make one weapon attack dealing necrotic damage equal to normal weapon damage
  - You can use Soul Harvest to collect tokens from dying creatures
  - You cannot be targeted or damaged
  - The manifestation lasts until the start of your next turn
- After 1 minute or when you run out of Soul Tokens, you vanish completely

**Thematic Note**: "I'm not done yet. One more minute. A few more kills. Then I'll rest."

---

### Death's Bargain

You offer death itself a trade: your life for theirs.

**Effect**:
- You immediately die (no death saves)
- Choose one creature within 60 feet that you can see
- That creature must make a Wisdom saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier)
- On a failure:
  - They drop to 0 hit points immediately
  - They are dying and cannot be stabilized for 1 minute (death saves continue normally)
  - All healing they receive is halved for 24 hours
- On a success:
  - They take **10d10 necrotic damage**
  - They are frightened of you for 1 minute (even though you're dead)
- One ally you choose within 60 feet immediately regains hit points equal to **your Reaper level × 2** and gains all your remaining Soul Tokens (they can use Reaper's Strike as if they were a Reaper for 1 minute, using your damage dice)

**Thematic Note**: "If I die, you're coming with me. But my allies? They'll survive to finish this."

## Reaper's Covenants

Your Covenant represents your philosophical relationship with death and soul harvesting. Each Covenant grants you features at **3rd, 6th, 10th, and 14th level**.

---

## Grave Warden

You serve as death's guardian, ensuring peaceful passage for the dying and protecting the living from untimely ends. Where other Reapers harvest souls for power, you harvest them to grant mercy.

### Covenant Features

#### Gentle Reaping (3rd Level)

Your soul harvesting can be turned to healing.

**Merciful Harvest**: When you gain a Soul Token from any source, you can immediately spend it as part of the same trigger to do one of the following:
- Stabilize one dying creature within 30 feet
- Grant one creature within 30 feet temporary hit points equal to your Reaper level + Wisdom modifier
- Remove the frightened or charmed condition from one creature within 30 feet
- **Offer Mercy**: End the routing condition on one enemy within 30 feet, stabilizing their morale. They become neutral toward you (not hostile, not friendly) for 1 minute or until attacked

**Warden's Presence**: Allies within 10 feet of you have advantage on death saving throws. This increases to 20 feet at 10th level.

**Spirit Speech**: You can communicate with the spirits of recently deceased creatures (died within the past hour). They can answer simple yes/no questions or provide brief (one sentence) responses. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1) per long rest.

#### Guardian's Shield (3rd Level, Covenant Reaction)

**Trigger**: An ally within 30 feet is hit by an attack  
**Cost**: 1 reaction use + 2 Soul Tokens  
**Effect**: You redirect the attack to yourself. Make a Constitution saving throw (DC = 10 + damage dealt). On a success, you take half damage. On a failure, you take full damage. The ally takes no damage. If you reduce the damage to 0 through DR or other means, you regain 1 of the spent Soul Tokens.

**Warden's Sacrifice**: If this reaction would reduce you to 0 hit points, the ally you protected gains temporary hit points equal to your Reaper level + your Wisdom modifier.

---

#### Guardian's Intervention (6th Level)

You can intercept death itself with greater power.

**Shield the Dying**: When an ally within 30 feet would be reduced to 0 hit points, you can use your **reaction** and spend **2 Soul Tokens** to prevent them from dropping. Instead, they remain at 1 hit point and gain temporary hit points equal to your Reaper level + Wisdom modifier.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

**Peaceful Rest**: When you stabilize a dying creature (through any means), they gain **1d8 + your Wisdom modifier** hit points instead of just stabilizing at 0.

**Warden's Reprieve**: When an enemy leader within 30 feet attempts an Auto Rally after becoming bloodied, you can spend **3 Soul Tokens** to grant them advantage on their Rally check. If they succeed, you gain 2 Soul Tokens back and the enemy leader becomes indebted to you (DM determines narrative consequences—they may withdraw honorably, offer information, or switch sides).

---

#### Death's Sanctuary (10th Level)

Your protection extends beyond individual allies to create zones of safety.

**Hallowed Ground**: As an action, spend **4 Soul Tokens** to create a 30-foot radius sanctuary centered on you that lasts for 1 minute. Within this area:
- Undead have disadvantage on attack rolls
- Allies cannot drop below 1 hit point (instead of 0)
- Dying creatures automatically stabilize at the start of their turn
- You gain 1 Soul Token at the start of each of your turns (up to your maximum)

You can use this feature once per long rest.

**Mercy's Blade**: When you hit a creature with a weapon attack enhanced by Reaper's Strike, you can choose to transfer up to half the necrotic damage dealt (rounded down) as healing to one ally within 30 feet.

**Split Harvest**: When you harvest a soul from a creature dying within 30 feet, you can choose to split it. You gain 1 Soul Token, and one ally within 30 feet also gains 1 Soul Token (they can hold up to 3 tokens and use them for your Reaper's Strike feature for 1 minute, using your damage dice).

---

#### Saint of the Grave (14th Level)

You have become death's chosen protector.

**Death Defiance**: Allies within 30 feet of you cannot have their maximum hit points reduced and have advantage on saving throws against effects that would kill them outright (like *power word kill*).

**Mass Resurrection**: Once per long rest, you can spend **8 Soul Tokens** and 1 minute in prayer to resurrect up to 6 creatures who died within the past hour within 60 feet of you. They return to life with half their maximum hit points and no levels of exhaustion.

**Deathless Vigil**: You cannot die while an ally within 30 feet is at 0 hit points. If you would die, you instead drop to 1 hit point. Once this feature prevents your death, you cannot use it again until you finish a long rest.

**Warden's Resurrection**: Once per long rest, when an ally dies within 30 feet of you, you can spend **5 Soul Tokens** as a reaction to resurrect them immediately. They return to life with hit points equal to their Constitution modifier + your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1) and are conscious. This must be used within 1 minute of their death.

## Soul Drinker

You are a vampire of essence, consuming the life force of your enemies to sustain and empower yourself. Where others fear death, you embrace it as nourishment.

### Covenant Features

#### Life Drain (3rd Level)

Your attacks steal life force directly.

**Vampiric Strike**: When you hit a creature with a weapon attack enhanced by Reaper's Strike, you regain hit points equal to **half the necrotic damage dealt** (rounded down).

**Feast on the Fallen**: When a creature dies within 10 feet of you, you can immediately regain hit points equal to your Reaper level. This doesn't cost Soul Tokens and happens automatically (no action required).

**Terror Feast**: When an enemy within 30 feet fails a Breaking Point save, you regain hit points equal to your Reaper level and gain 1 Soul Token. This happens automatically (no action required).

**Hunger**: You have advantage on saving throws against being poisoned, and you have resistance to poison damage.

#### Blood Shield (3rd Level, Covenant Reaction)

**Trigger**: You are hit by an attack  
**Cost**: 1 reaction use  
**Effect**: Instead of taking damage normally, you convert the incoming damage into life force. Make a Constitution saving throw (DC = 10 + half damage dealt, rounded down). On a success, you take half damage and regain hit points equal to your Reaper level. On a failure, you take full damage but still regain hit points equal to half your Reaper level (rounded down).

**Crimson Hunger**: If you are bloodied (below half HP) when you use this reaction and succeed on the save, you also gain 1 Soul Token.

---

#### Blood Price (6th Level)

You can sacrifice your own vitality to harvest more souls.

**Crimson Harvest**: When a creature within 30 feet dies, you can choose to take necrotic damage equal to your Reaper level to gain **1 additional Soul Token** (so 2 total instead of 1), still subject to your maximum.

**Sanguine Empowerment**: When you use Soul Empowerment, you can choose to pay the cost in hit points instead of Soul Tokens (**10 hit points per token**). If you do, the duration increases to 1 minute (concentration) instead of until the end of your next turn.

**Transfusion**: As an action, you can touch a willing creature and transfer hit points to them. You take necrotic damage equal to the amount healed (maximum = your Reaper level × 3 per use). You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Constitution modifier (minimum 1) per long rest.

**Soul Conversion**: As a bonus action, you can spend hit points to gain Soul Tokens. Every **10 HP spent** grants 1 Soul Token (maximum = your proficiency bonus tokens gained per use). You can use this feature once per short rest.

---

#### Eternal Thirst (10th Level)

Your hunger becomes insatiable and your rewards greater.

**Greater Life Drain**: Your Vampiric Strike now heals you for **75% of the necrotic damage dealt** (rounded down) instead of half.

**Soul Feast**: Once per short rest, as an action, you can consume 3 or more Soul Tokens. For each token consumed:
- Regain 1d10 hit points
- Gain 1d6 temporary hit points
- Your Strength or Dexterity (your choice) increases by +1 for 1 minute (stacks, maximum +4)

**Bloodied Hunger**: When you are bloodied (below half HP), your Reaper's Strike deals an additional **1d6 necrotic damage**. This stacks with your normal Reaper's Strike damage and triggers Vampiric Strike on the full damage.

**Draining Presence**: At the start of each of your turns, all hostile creatures within 10 feet of you take necrotic damage equal to your Constitution modifier, and you regain hit points equal to half the total damage dealt (minimum 1).

**Panic Aura**: At the start of each of your turns, all hostile creatures within 10 feet of you must make a Wisdom save (DC = 8 + proficiency + Constitution modifier) or have disadvantage on their next Breaking Point save. You regain hit points equal to half the total necrotic damage dealt to creatures within this aura.

**Devouring Rout**: When you kill a routing enemy within 10 feet, you regain hit points equal to **twice your Reaper level** and gain **2 Soul Tokens** (instead of the normal 1).

---

#### Master of Life and Death (14th Level)

You blur the line between living and undead.

**Lichborn**: You gain the following benefits:
- Immunity to poison damage and the poisoned condition
- Immunity to disease
- Resistance to necrotic damage
- You can choose to appear living or undead to detection magic (spells like *detect undead* show whichever you choose)
- You no longer age and can't die of old age. You don't need to eat or drink normal food—you sustain yourself entirely through Soul Harvest and Life Drain

**Critical Feast**: When you score a critical hit with a weapon attack, you regain hit points equal to your Reaper level and gain 1 Soul Token (in addition to normal Soul Harvest if the attack kills the target).

**Soul Consumption**: When you use Soul Feast, you can choose to consume souls for power instead of healing. You can spend tokens for the following effects:
- **2 tokens**: Your next weapon attack within the next minute is a critical hit
- **3 tokens**: You gain an additional action this turn (can be used only for the Attack action)
- **4 tokens**: You can cast *finger of death* (save DC = 8 + proficiency + Constitution modifier) without components

**Hunger's Call**: Once per long rest, when you drop below half your maximum hit points, you can activate Hunger's Call as a reaction (doesn't cost a class reaction use). For the next minute:
- All your weapon attacks gain Vampiric Strike (even without spending Soul Tokens), healing you for 25% of weapon damage dealt
- Your movement speed increases by 10 feet
- You have advantage on attack rolls against wounded creatures (below half HP)
- You gain 1 Soul Token at the start of each of your turns (up to your maximum)

**Eternal Regeneration**: At the start of your turn, if you have at least 1 Soul Token, you regain hit points equal to your Constitution modifier. This increases to twice your Constitution modifier if you're bloodied.

## Death Knight

You are death's executioner, a warrior who delivers killing blows with supernatural precision. Your purpose is singular: **end threats efficiently and permanently**.

### Covenant Features

#### Executioner's Precision (3rd Level)

Your strikes seek lethal openings.

**Killing Blow**: Your critical hit range increases to **19-20** when attacking creatures below half their maximum hit points.

**Death Sentence**: When you reduce a creature to 0 hit points with a weapon attack, you can immediately move up to 10 feet and make one additional weapon attack against a different creature. This movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks. You can use this feature a number of times per turn equal to half your proficiency bonus (rounded down, minimum 1).

**Mark of Death**: As a bonus action, mark one creature you can see within 60 feet. For the next minute, you score critical hits against that creature on rolls of **18-20**. You can have only one creature marked at a time, and marking a new creature ends the previous mark. You can use this a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per short rest.

**Mark of Doom**: When you mark an enemy leader, all enemies within 30 feet of the marked creature have disadvantage on Breaking Point saves while the mark persists. If you kill a marked enemy leader, the Breaking Point DC for their troops increases by **+4** (instead of the normal +2), and their Auto Rally attempt (if they were attempting one) automatically fails.

#### Reaper's Vengeance (3rd Level, Covenant Reaction)

**Trigger**: A creature within 30 feet reduces an ally to 0 hit points  
**Cost**: 1 reaction use + 1 Soul Token  
**Effect**: You can immediately make a weapon attack against that creature. If this attack hits, it becomes a **critical hit** and you gain **2 Soul Tokens**. If this attack kills the creature, you can immediately use Death Sentence to continue attacking (if you have uses remaining).

**Executioner's Fury**: If the creature that triggered this reaction is marked by your Mark of Death, your critical hit range for this attack is **17-20** instead of automatic.

---

#### Inevitable Strike (6th Level)

Your attacks bypass defenses.

**Armor Breaker**: When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can spend **2 Soul Tokens** to ignore their Damage Reduction for that attack.

**Perfect Execution**: When you use your Execution feature (7th level class feature) and kill the target, you regain **2 of the 3 Soul Tokens** spent.

**Relentless Assault**: When you hit a creature with both attacks from your Extra Attack, you can spend **2 Soul Tokens** to make a third attack against the same target as part of the same action.

**Sure Strike**: Once per turn, when you miss with a weapon attack, you can spend **3 Soul Tokens** to turn that miss into a hit.

---

#### Executioner's Wrath (10th Level)

Your killing blows cascade into unstoppable momentum.

**Chain Execution**: When you use Death Sentence to make an additional attack after killing a creature, if that attack also kills its target, you can continue this process. You can chain up to 3 kills maximum in this way per turn.

**Chain the Broken**: You can use Death Sentence on **routing enemies** without restriction. Each kill on a routing enemy:
- Grants you +1 Momentum (stacks)
- Allows you to continue the chain (no limit on chained kills against routing enemies)
- Spreads fear: Each kill forces other routing enemies within 30 feet to make a Wisdom save (DC = 8 + proficiency + Constitution modifier) or drop prone and become paralyzed by fear until the end of your next turn

**Enhanced Mark**: Your Mark of Death now lasts for 1 hour and you can mark up to 2 creatures simultaneously. When you kill a marked creature, you gain **1 additional Soul Token** (so 2 total from the death).

**Dreadful Presence**: Creatures you have marked with Mark of Death are frightened of you while they can see you. They can make a Wisdom saving throw (DC = 8 + proficiency + Constitution modifier) at the end of each of their turns to end the frightened condition, but the mark remains.

**Death's Approach**: You have advantage on initiative rolls, and creatures marked by your Mark of Death cannot take reactions when you move within 5 feet of them.

---

#### Avatar of Endings (14th Level)

You become a force of inevitable death.

**Instant Execution**: When you hit a creature with a weapon attack and that attack would reduce them to 50 hit points or less, you can spend **4 Soul Tokens** to immediately reduce them to 0 hit points instead. Creatures with legendary resistance can expend one use to avoid this effect.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

**Unstoppable Assault**: When you take the Attack action, you can spend **2 Soul Tokens** to gain an additional attack as part of that action. This stacks with Extra Attack (so you make 3 attacks total).

**Killing Intent**: When you roll initiative, you can immediately mark up to 2 creatures you can see with Mark of Death without spending any uses. These marks last for the entire combat.

**Final Strike**: Once per long rest, when you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can declare it a Final Strike. The target must make a Constitution saving throw (DC = 8 + proficiency + Constitution modifier) or be instantly reduced to 0 hit points. On a success, they instead take **8d10 necrotic damage** and have their maximum hit points reduced by the damage dealt until they complete a long rest. Creatures with legendary resistance automatically succeed this save but still take the damage and max HP reduction.

**Death's Army**: When you kill a creature of CR 3 or lower with a weapon attack, you can spend **3 Soul Tokens** to animate it as a skeletal minion. It rises immediately with hit points equal to half its maximum and acts on your turn. You can have a maximum of 3 minions at one time. Minions last until destroyed or until you finish a long rest.

**Supreme Executioner**: Your critical hit range against marked creatures increases to **17-20**. Against bloodied marked creatures, it increases to **16-20**.

## Integration with FEARS Systems

### Dynamic Combat System (DCS)

Reapers interact uniquely with the Dynamic Combat System:

**Evasion Profile**: Reapers are medium armor wearers with moderate Evasion scores:
- **Dodge Evasion**: +0 to +2 (depending on Dexterity)
- **Deflect Evasion**: +1 to +2 (shield use, medium armor)
- **Endure Evasion**: +2 to +3 (Constitution-based, death resistance)

**Damage Reduction**: Chain mail + medium armor proficiency gives 5-7 DR baseline.
- **Death's Resilience**: At 5th level, while you have at least 3 Soul Tokens, you gain +1 DR. This increases to +2 DR at 11th level and +3 DR at 17th level.

**Momentum Generation**: Reapers build Momentum naturally through:
- Consecutive attacks (Extra Attack)
- Killing blows that trigger Death Sentence movement and attacks
- Critical hits on bloodied enemies (Mark of Death)
- **Momentum of the Grave** (9th level): When you harvest a soul (gain a Soul Token from Soul Harvest), you gain +1 Momentum. This can occur only once per round.

**Called Shots**: Reapers can spend Soul Tokens to enhance Called Shots:
- Spend **2 Soul Tokens**: Ignore the Called Shot penalty entirely
- Spend **3 Soul Tokens**: The Called Shot automatically hits and is considered a critical hit if the target is bloodied

**Bloodied Synergy**: Many Reaper features key off enemies being bloodied (below 50% HP):
- Soul Harvest triggers when reducing enemies to half HP
- Execution works on bloodied enemies
- Mark of Death crits more against bloodied
- Killing Blow crits more against bloodied
- This creates tactical focus: damage enemies to bloodied threshold, then execute

**Injuries**: All classes suffer injuries when bloodied or from critical hits. Soul Drinker Reapers have advantage on saves against injuries while Hunger's Call is active.

---

### Mental Systems

**Focus Integration**:
- **Soul Focus** (3rd level): Your maximum Soul Token capacity = Base Maximum + Focus Modifier
- When your Focus degrades, your token capacity shrinks—you literally lose the ability to hold souls
- Harvesting 3+ souls in a single round requires a DC 10 Wisdom save or lose 1 Focus
- When your Focus drops below 5, you have disadvantage on death saving throws (the line between life and death blurs)

**Sanity Integration**:
- **Harvest Madness**: When you drop below half your maximum Sanity, you become Obsessed with harvesting souls:
  - You must spend all Soul Tokens before the end of each combat or make a DC 15 Wisdom save to resist the compulsion
  - You have disadvantage on Wisdom saves
  - You gain +2 to attack rolls against bloodied creatures (your obsession makes you efficient)
  
- **Harvest Frenzy**: When you reach 0 Sanity:
  - You must attack the nearest creature each turn (ally or enemy)
  - You automatically use Reaper's Strike on every attack
  - You cannot willingly end combat
  - When combat ends, roll on the Sanity Fracture table
  
- After combat where you harvested 10+ souls total, make a DC 12 Wisdom save or lose 1 Sanity
- Soul Drinkers who use Crimson Harvest repeatedly (taking damage to gain extra souls) make this save at disadvantage

**Corruption Interaction**:
- Harvesting souls from innocent creatures (non-hostile, non-threatening) gives 1 Corruption per 5 souls
- Using Soul Harvest on humanoids you've killed yourself gives 1 Corruption per 10 souls
- **Death's Taint**: Corruption affects you progressively:
  - **Tier 1 (1-4)**: Minor visual changes (pale skin, cold touch, faint death scent)
  - **Tier 2 (5-9)**: Living creatures instinctively fear you; animals are hostile; disadvantage on Animal Handling and Performance
  - **Tier 3 (10-14)**: You count as Undead for detection spells; healing spells have disadvantage to affect you; advantage on saves vs Turn Undead
  - **Tier 4 (15-19)**: You can speak with undead; radiant damage has advantage against you; you have resistance to necrotic damage
  - **Tier 5 (20+)**: You are partially undead; don't need to eat/sleep; heal from necrotic damage; radiant damage crits on 19-20 against you
- Soul Drinkers can spend **5 Soul Tokens** during a long rest to reduce Corruption by 1 (they "purge" the taint through vitae manipulation)

---

### Bloodied, Breaking Points & Rally System

The Reaper class has natural synergy with enemy morale systems:

**Bloodied Threshold Integration**:
- Your Soul Harvest feature already triggers when you reduce creatures to half HP—the exact bloodied threshold
- When enemies become bloodied, they trigger Auto Rally attempts—which you can harvest from if they fail
- Your Dread Presence (13th level) gives enemies disadvantage on Breaking Point saves
- This creates cascading opportunities for Soul Token generation

**Morale Harvesting**:
- **Morale Harvester** (1st level): Gain 1 Soul Token when enemies within 30 feet fail Breaking Point saves
- **Harvest the Broken** (9th level): Gain 2 Soul Tokens (reaction) when enemies rout within 30 feet
- **Deathblow Panic** (15th level): Force mass Breaking Point checks when you kill enemy leaders

**Enemy Leader Targeting**:
- **Execution** on enemy leaders interrupts their Auto Rally attempts
- Killing enemy leaders with Execution triggers **+4 DC** Breaking Point checks (instead of +2)
- Your **Mark of Death** sabotages enemy morale even before you kill them
- **Death Knight's Chain the Broken** lets you massacre routing enemies for unlimited tokens

**Tactical Flow**:
1. Reduce enemy leader to bloodied → they attempt Auto Rally
2. Your Dread Presence gives enemies disadvantage → many fail Breaking Points
3. You harvest tokens from failed saves → gain Momentum
4. Enemy Momentum locks at -2 → your party acts first next round
5. You execute leader with initiative advantage → Deathblow Panic triggers
6. Mass Breaking Point checks → enemy army collapses
7. Harvest tokens from routing enemies → sustain for next fight

### Weapon Mastery System

Reapers gain access to Weapon Mastery:

**Starting Mastery** (1st level): Choose one martial weapon and one simple weapon

**Additional Masteries**:
- 3rd level: +1 weapon
- 7th level: +1 weapon
- 11th level: +1 weapon
- 15th level: +1 weapon

**Gaining New Masteries Through Combat**:
After you score critical hits with a weapon you are proficient with, you may spend 1 week of downtime training to unlock mastery:
- **Simple Weapons**: 2 critical hits required
- **Martial Weapons**: 3 critical hits required

**Recommended Weapons for Reapers**:
- **Greatsword**: High damage, pairs well with Reaper's Strike
- **Longsword + Shield**: Balanced offense/defense, good DR
- **Scythe** (if available): Thematic reach weapon
- **Maul**: Brutal critical hits, synergy with Execution

## Multiclassing

### Prerequisites
To qualify for multiclassing into or out of the Reaper class, you must have a **Constitution score of 13 or higher**.

### Proficiencies Gained
When you multiclass into Reaper, you gain the following proficiencies:
- Light armor, medium armor
- Simple weapons, martial weapons
- One skill from the Reaper skill list

### Soul Tokens
Your Soul Token maximum is based on your Reaper levels only:
- Base Maximum = shown on Reaper progression table
- Actual Maximum = Base Maximum + Focus Modifier

Multiclassing does not grant additional Soul Tokens from other classes.

### Synergies
Reaper works well with:
- **Fighter**: Extra attacks = more chances to apply Reaper's Strike, Action Surge for burst rounds
- **Barbarian**: Rage damage helps secure kills, Reckless Attack enables Mark of Death crits
- **Paladin**: Divine Smite + Reaper's Strike = massive burst damage for finishing blows
- **Rogue**: Sneak Attack helps finish wounded enemies, Death Knight synergizes with Assassinate

**Anti-Synergies**:
- Full casters: Reapers want to be in melee getting kills; full casters want to stay back
- Monks: Different ability score priorities (WIS/DEX vs STR/CON)

## Roleplaying Your Reaper

### Character Concepts

**The Reluctant Harvester**: You didn't ask for this power. It manifested after a traumatic event (sole survivor of massacre, witnessed too much death). Now you carry souls whether you want to or not. You struggle with the moral weight of your abilities.

**The Death Cultist**: You worship death as the natural end of all things. Your soul harvesting is a sacred rite, and you see yourself as death's chosen priest, ushering souls to their proper rest.

**The Merciful Executioner**: You end suffering. Those dying in agony find peace in your blade. You harvest souls not for power but to ensure they don't linger in torment, and you use that power to protect the living.

**The War Veteran**: You've seen so much death on the battlefield that you've become attuned to it. Your scars glow faintly with necrotic energy when souls are near. You're numb to violence but fiercely protective of those you care about.

**The Bounty Hunter**: You hunt the living, and your payment is their souls. Every contract fulfilled adds to your power. You're professional, efficient, and utterly ruthless in pursuit of your marks.

---

### Personality Traits

1. I keep a tally of every soul I've harvested—I owe them remembrance
2. I speak quietly because I've heard enough screaming for ten lifetimes
3. I'm unnaturally calm in the presence of death
4. I collect small tokens from those I've killed (a button, a coin, a lock of hair)
5. I prefer the company of the dead to the living—they're more honest
6. I see death everywhere: in withering flowers, in sunset, in aging faces
7. I'm obsessed with ensuring people die "properly" (peacefully, honored, remembered)
8. I joke about death constantly—it's the only way I cope

---

### Ideals

1. **Mercy**: Death is a release from suffering (Good)
2. **Balance**: All must die eventually; I simply choose when (Neutral)
3. **Power**: The dead empower me; I'll ensure there are many (Evil)
4. **Duty**: I serve death itself as its faithful instrument (Lawful)
5. **Freedom**: Death is the ultimate freedom from chains (Chaotic)
6. **Purpose**: Without death, nothing has meaning (Any)

---

### Bonds

1. Someone I love died, and I harvest souls hoping to find theirs among the departed
2. I'm searching for the creature that killed my entire village—I'll harvest its soul personally
3. A necromancer taught me soul harvesting, then betrayed me; I hunt them still
4. I'm trying to harvest enough souls to fuel a resurrection ritual for someone precious
5. Death itself speaks to me in dreams, giving me commands and purpose
6. I protect a graveyard where victims of a massacre are buried; their souls guard me in return

---

### Flaws

1. I'm addicted to harvesting souls—I feel empty and weak without them
2. I sometimes forget people have names and see them only as "future souls"
3. I'm terrified of dying without having harvested enough souls to matter
4. I hear the voices of souls I've collected, and they're getting louder
5. I take unnecessary risks to ensure I land the killing blow instead of allies
6. I'm convinced I'm already dead and this is some kind of purgatory

## Narrative Role

Reapers excel in campaigns involving:

**Horror and Death**: Your class is built for FEARS's horror themes. You embody death's inevitability and harvest the souls of the fallen. Perfect for gothic horror, undead-heavy campaigns, or psychological horror.

**Dark Fantasy**: In grim, brutal settings, Reapers thrive. You're the character who makes hard choices and lives with the consequences. Your growing Corruption tells a story of moral degradation or resilience.

**Military Campaigns**: Battlefields are your feeding grounds. Large-scale combat provides abundant soul harvesting opportunities. You're the specialist brought in when things get desperate.

**Undead-Focused Campaigns**: Your death magic and soul manipulation make you perfect for campaigns against necromancers, liches, and undead armies. You understand death in ways others can't.

**Moral Ambiguity**: The Reaper forces questions about the ethics of power drawn from death. Are you a monster? A necessary evil? A force for mercy? Your Covenant choice and roleplaying answer these questions.

## Party Synergies

Reapers support their party through:

**Sustained Damage**: Your Reaper's Strike ensures consistent high damage throughout combat, not just nova rounds.

**Battlefield Control**: Death Sentence allows you to quickly eliminate multiple enemies, controlling action economy by reducing enemy numbers.

**Frontline Pressure**: As a medium armor warrior, you can hold the line while threatening devastating strikes that finish wounded enemies.

**Execution**: You excel at finishing wounded enemies, preventing enemy healers from bringing them back into the fight.

**Mental Resilience** (High Sanity): Your above-average Sanity makes you resistant to fear effects and psychological attacks, providing stability.

**Morale Warfare**: You can shatter enemy courage, creating routs and surrenders that end combat early.

**Work well with:**
- **Tanks**: They hold enemies in place while you execute them; Grave Warden can protect tanks
- **Controllers**: They damage enemies to bloodied, you finish them with Execution
- **Strikers**: Multiple damage dealers = more kills = more souls for everyone via Soul Overflow
- **Healers**: Grave Warden can assist healing; all Reapers benefit from staying alive to harvest more

**Potential Friction:**
- Kill-stealing can create tension if not discussed beforehand
- Death Knight's efficiency can overshadow other strikers
- Soul Drinker's self-sufficiency can make healers feel less needed

**Table Talk**: Discuss with your party how Soul Harvest interacts with shared kill credit. Most tables rule that whoever lands the killing blow gets the token, but contributing damage also generates tokens, reducing friction.

## Final Thoughts

The Reaper represents a **tactically nuanced martial class** that integrates deeply with FEARS's mental systems, combat mechanics, morale systems, and horror themes. With Soul Tokens as your primary resource—fluctuating based on kills, damage contribution, enemy morale breaks, and your Focus attribute—you must carefully manage when to spend power for maximum effect.

Your choice of Covenant fundamentally shapes your playstyle:
- **Grave Warden**: Protective support who uses death's power to shield allies and offer mercy
- **Soul Drinker**: Self-sustaining vampire who grows stronger through consumption and feeds on fear
- **Death Knight**: Ruthless executioner who chains kills with supernatural efficiency and shatters enemy morale

Whether you're a reluctant harbinger of death struggling with your power, a devoted servant of the natural cycle, a pragmatic warrior wielding death as a tool, or a psychological warfare specialist who breaks armies through morale collapse, the Reaper offers deep mechanical and narrative possibilities.

**Power comes from understanding that death is inevitable, and you have learned to harness it.** Every fallen foe makes you stronger. Every bloodied enemy creates opportunity. Every broken morale feeds your power. Every battlefield becomes your harvest. You don't fear the end—you are its instrument.

The souls are yours to harvest. The dead are yours to claim. The courage is yours to break. The battlefield is yours to reap.

**Welcome to the harvest.**
