# PART I: OVERVIEW

## At a Glance

| **Attribute** | **Details** |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Official Name** | The Greenwood Sovereignty of Talanthar |
| **Population** | ~2.8 million (difficult to count; integrated into forest) |
| **Capital** | Talanthar (Vael'Tharyn) |
| **Government** | Federation of Circles under the High Council of the Greenwood |
| **Head of State** | High Archdruid Liraen of the Nine Circles |
| **Territory** | ~35,000 square miles of contiguous woodland, ravine, and marsh |
| **Religion** | Veneration of Vaelthir; Temples to Ishara, Thamur, Sylthara, and Sarnock |
| **Economy** | Rare herbs, enchanted wood, griffin feathers, moonwood, spiritual services |
| **Military Doctrine** | Asymmetric forest warfare; specialist small-unit orders |
| **Primary Threat** | Corvessa's perpetual religious war (Prophecy of the Felled Root) |
| **Cultural Inspiration** | Celtic/Slavic forest cultures + Amazonian ecology |

Talanthar is an ancient forest kingdom built around the World Tree Vaelthir in the great gorge carved by converging rivers. Unconquered for centuries, it represents successful resistance to Corvessan crusades through asymmetric warfare, terrain mastery, and complete integration with the forest ecosystem.

The city itself is a **vertical labyrinth** of terraces, bridges, and caverns spanning gorge cliffs—less an urban construct than a living ecosystem where architecture is grown, not built, and people dwell in harmony with the ancient forest rather than above it.

---

## The Endless Song Philosophy

The core philosophical truth of Talanthar is expressed in what they call **The Endless Song**:

> *"Life is an endless song. Every leaf falls to feed the root; every death a verse that gives the next life its rhythm. To stop the cycle is the true heresy. To fix the Weave into a static design is to kill the song."*

**Key Tenets:**
- Life is a song that must never stop flowing
- Every death feeds the next life's rhythm
- Vaelthir is not a god but living memory of creation itself
- The Pattern (Corvessa's faith) is a cage that murders the living cycle
- To fix the Weave into static design kills the song

This philosophy stands in direct opposition to Corvessa's **Faith of the Bound Pattern**, which sees the Weave as a loom where life is a thread in divine design, and perfection comes through stillness and obedience. This theological incompatibility makes their conflict **eschatological, not territorial**—they fight over the nature of reality itself, not merely land.

# PART II: GEOGRAPHY AND ENVIRONMENT

## The Greenwood

The Greenwood Sovereignty occupies the north-eastern corridor of Elarion, bounded west by the Kingdom of Corvessa, east by the Skyspine Mountains, and south by the Ivora tributaries that vanish beneath the hills.

**Physical Characteristics:**
- ~35,000 square miles of contiguous woodland, ravine, and marsh
- Territory smaller than Corvessa yet infinitely harder to traverse
- Forest canopy averages 200 feet in height; in oldest sectors exceeds 300 feet
- Light filters in muted shafts, giving the region perpetual dusk
- Air heavy with spores, dew, and a low electrical hum sensitive augurs describe as "living resonance"

**Hydrography:**
Two rivers dominate the landscape:
- **The Vaelrun (or Aerendel)**: Descending from Skyspine glaciers, narrow and violently swift
- **The Thalaris (or Myrn)**: Broader, meandering through limestone before plunging underground

Their confluence carves the **Vaelthir Gorge**, a deep cleft whose vertical walls rise near 500 feet. The gorge varies in width—narrowing to a few hundred feet where swaying bridges span between cliff faces, widening to broader chambers where floating markets drift on the converging waters.

**Environmental Phenomena:**
- Seasonal change differs from the rest of Elarion
- Winters remain mild but bright, canopy reflecting snowlight like silver glass
- Summers plunge into emerald twilight as leaves thicken to near opacity
- Rainfall constant yet rarely violent—filtering through foliage in a whispering drizzle called "the forest's breath"
- Lightning never strikes the same tree twice; diverted along invisible wards
- Flora and fauna exhibit pronounced sympathetic magic
- Predators strike only when the forest "hungers"
- Disease seldom spreads beyond a single host before fading into spores

**The Forest's Awareness:**
The forest itself appears to possess will and intention. It protects its children but also tests them, demanding respect, blood, and balance. Those who ignore its will vanish, becoming part of the roots and legends that sustain it.

## The Spirit of the Greenwood

The Greenwood is three parts location and one part living entity. It is not merely a forest—it is an ancient presence with will, memory, and judgment. Those who enter feel it immediately: an unsettling sensation of being watched, a tingle at the nape of the neck, a certainty that something vast and incomprehensible observes from behind every tree.

### The Greenwood as Entity

**Personality and Will:**
The Greenwood has perceptions and powers beyond the understanding—and often beyond the notice—of those who walk beneath its canopy. It nourishes itself. It defends itself. And it remembers those who have challenged it in the past.

Most outsiders describe this presence as a dark, sinister force, but perhaps they simply lack understanding of the natural order of this ancient place. Deep-woods druids cast the Greenwood in a brighter—if wilder—light, describing the supernatural presence as *beautiful, terrible, and bestial*. They speak of "the Greenwood" as if it were a living creature with a personality and a will of its own. The religious reject the idea that the forest is "alive" in the conventional sense but acknowledge that some great spirit lives within. 

Perhaps all interpretations are correct. The Greenwood existed before such recent inventions as gods and spells and names and language.

**Attitude:**
The Greenwood is quick to anger, slow to forgive, and will hold a grudge that passes from generation to generation like a powerful curse. It dreams of ancient times, of the behaviors of long-dead creatures and the wild world before civilization. What the Empire calls "progress," the Greenwood considers pale shadows, intolerable deviations into the madness of civilization, and corruptions of the green and ancient state of life.

Those behaviors of old are considered correct; incorrect behavior must be *pruned*.

### The Greenwood's Senses

**Tremorsense:**
The Greenwood feels. Keenly. It enjoys forest-wide tremorsense, keen enough to pinpoint the location of any creature and identify it by species. Some believe the forest recognizes individuals by their unique footfalls. Inhabitants sometimes "step" like other creatures to protect themselves from the ever-vigilant trees. Secrets are often spoken in whispers while rapping loudly on a tree trunk.

**Bloodsense:**
The first time an individual bleeds on the forest floor, the Greenwood *knows* that creature. It tracks such individuals with its tremorsense until they leave the forest. If an individual re-enters and bleeds again, the forest "remembers" the uniqueness of that blood and connects the individual's current activity to past deeds it has witnessed.

Often the Greenwood connects newcomers to blood relatives who spilled their blood in the past, perhaps many generations or even centuries before. Those whose ancestors performed dark deeds in the Greenwood feel a great threatening presence all around them. The saying "the Greenwood punishes men for the sins of their fathers" is disturbingly accurate.

**Memory:**
What the Greenwood consumes, it remembers. In addition to recognizing creatures by blood, the Greenwood absorbs many of the memories and much of the knowledge of those who expire beneath its canopy. To something as old and alien as the Greenwood, this knowledge is rarely valuable on an individual level, but it helps refine the forest's viewpoint of each species.

### The Greenwood's Powers

**Movement:**
The Greenwood moves when creatures are not looking. Since it cannot see normally, it uses its tremorsense to determine when target creatures are still and their heartbeats are slowed. Its activity typically occurs when creatures are sleeping, but relaxed watchers sometimes hear or notice movement while sitting quietly at a campfire.

The Greenwood can move paths, add forks, or redirect game trails at will. Explorers who wake in the Greenwood might feel disoriented, as if their camp had rotated during the night. Trails that should lead deeper into the forest now lead out of it. Landmarks cannot be trusted. Cautious parties go around large, ancient trees on the same side, lest they risk being separated.

*"Don't make any appointments,"* the experienced say. *"A path may take three days, or it may take a fortnight. It just depends."*

**Light and Shadow:**
Night comes early in the Greenwood. The forest can thicken or thin its canopy at will. During the day, the Greenwood filters the sun's rays, creating areas of dim light centered on targets it dislikes. Vampires and undead travelers experience the opposite effect, as the canopy opens above them and subjects them to the full glare of the sun.

At night, the Greenwood typically hides the moon and stars, making navigation by celestial bodies impossible. The exception is for lycanthropes, whose wildness the Greenwood favors—from such it never conceals the moon.

**Starvation:**
One of the Greenwood's most insidious defenses against interlopers is to starve them. It moves its roots to redirect streams underground and withhold fresh water. It shakes its branches to spook game and withhold fresh food. An old saying—*"Deer and rabbits dance around the starving man"*—has been memorialized in wood sculptures whittled by emaciated men and found near their bones.

**Voice:**
The Greenwood has the power to listen to vibrations and repeat them anywhere within its borders. It does not understand languages or the meanings of sounds, but it experiments with repetition and has catalogued the reactions that species have to various noises. The Greenwood might repeat the roar of a mountain lion or the wail of a banshee to scare interlopers into perilous terrain.

These strange, disconnected sounds have prompted many rumors among neighboring folk. Most believe the Greenwood is haunted. It is, but deep-woods crones know that the sounds the forest makes are not true evidence of that fact. Some listeners have learned to gather imprecise information about distant areas of the woods by pressing ears to bark.

**Aura of Rust:**
Iron and iron alloys rust quickly in the Greenwood. Though the effect does not destroy thick blades overnight, the sharpening and replacement cost of rusted axes and saw blades makes large-scale logging unprofitable. Corvessan crusaders have learned to their dismay that armor and weapons corrode at an alarming rate the longer a campaign drags on.

**Aura of Wildness:**
A mild aura of wildness permeates the Greenwood. Bardic tales counsel travelers to abandon or kill animal companions that grow aggressive under the canopy. Tamed beasts regress to feral states over time. Occasionally, devolved creatures emerge from the forest—things that were once horses or hounds but are now something far more primal.

**Awakening and Control:**
The Greenwood's ability to move trees, roots, and branches is a minor version of its full ability to awaken and control forest-born flora and fauna. No one knows the limit of this power. The forest awakens animals to speak and help travelers, spawns green hussars as guardians, and creates permanent protectors like treants and leshy to ward certain springs, runestones, groves, and ley line sites.

The Greenwood's power over death enables it to spontaneously reincarnate fauna, sometimes in the bodies of dead humanoids. Disturbing stories of "the ferals" describe ordinary folk who wandered into the Greenwood as people and re-emerged with the disposition of a rabid animal. In truth, these unfortunates perished in the wood, and while their souls have passed on, their risen bodies now host the awakened soul of a badger, an owl, or a wolf.

### Standing with the Greenwood

The inhabitants of the Greenwood learn quickly that their every move, action, and word are witnessed by the nearly omniscient forest. Social niceties mean nothing to the Greenwood. The standing of visitors in other lands has no bearing. But within the forest, a creature's *status* with respect to the Greenwood is a critical consideration.

**Determining Standing:**
The Greenwood views certain races and classes more favorably than others:
- **Favored:** Elves (+2), druids (+2), rangers (+1), those who follow the Old Ways (+1)
- **Neutral:** Most humanoids (0)
- **Disfavored:** Those with ancestors who invaded (-1 to -4), undead (-4), constructs (-4), aberrations (-3)

Standing fluctuates based on actions:
- Protecting Greenwood creatures or sites: +1 to +2
- Defeating intelligent undead: +1
- Damaging the forest: -1 to -4
- Breaking a sworn oath: -2
- Casting blight or similar spells: -1 to -2

**Effects of Standing:**

| Status | Standing |
|--------|----------|
| Negative | **Invader:** The forest actively hinders you. Paths loop, water sours, predators stalk |
| 1-10 | **Uninvited Guest:** Largely ignored but monitored. The forest is indifferent |
| 11-20 | **Guest:** Proven harmless. The forest is friendly and may favor you in times of duress |
| 21-30 | **Friend:** Your actions have benefited the Greenwood. Once per week, you may call upon the forest for aid |
| 31+ | **Hero:** The Greenwood reciprocates your protection. Once per day, you may call upon it for aid |

**Friendly Greenwood Effects:**
- Ample trails and game sign; automatic success on hunting checks
- Fresh water always nearby
- Animals are curious rather than hostile
- Travel covers extra distance; difficult terrain does not slow you
- Stealth checks made with advantage
- Perception checks made with advantage

**Hostile Greenwood Effects:**
- Game vanishes; disadvantage on hunting checks
- Sinkholes, quicksand, and brambles appear in your path
- Beasts choose to attack rather than flee
- Difficult terrain deals damage from thorns
- Fresh water is rare or tainted
- Shadows and sounds disorient; disadvantage on Perception checks
- A root is always underfoot; risk of falling when running

### The Old Ways

To outsiders, the life of a Greenwood native is mysterious, filled with strange sayings, regimented rituals, and daily traditions that seem superstitious and backward. These are "the Old Ways," passed down through generations. They have spiritual connotations, habitually observed and rarely shared with outsiders. Characters who endeavor to learn the Old Ways quickly find themselves participating in the mood and traditions of the forest.

**Common Practices:**

*On Death:*
- Open the eyes of the dead, lest the deceased wander lost and angry through the wood
- A bloodless person cannot pass to the afterlife; thus the dead are never buried, lest the roots exsanguinate them before the souls depart
- Those buried dead or alive become revenants; every dawn they return to their graves, and the root-wicker cage that was once their circulatory system
- Sinners confess transgressions by cutting themselves and dripping blood upon the forest floor

*On Building:*
- Any manufactured structure built without sacrificing blood to the forest shakes itself down upon its owner
- In a year's time, treants grow from those dead who have commended themselves to the forest with sap, seed, and ritual

*On Fire and Light:*
- Torches are extinguished before midnight
- Never permit white moths to gather, lest a mora (witch) hide among their number

*On Nature:*
- Greenwood beasts must never leave the forest as tamed companions of outsiders, lest they become infatuated with "civilization" and become half-men—the abominations outsiders call lycanthropes
- Nail the skin or hair of a sick person to a tree; the tree will absorb part of the disease and lend its strength to healing
- Every plant has a theme, a power, and a purpose. Flowers tend to be beguilers; true power lies in leaf, stem, and root
- Salt cannot hold magic and makes an excellent ward against magical creatures, enchantments, and curses

*On Omens:*
- If you count butterflies one less than your group's number, one of you will die
- An evil creature is born every time a "civilized" man enters the woods
- Every year, kin, friends, and lovers celebrate the upcoming spring by giving each other blood-dyed pieces of thread. Each wears the thread until a tree signifying their relationship blossoms, whereupon the bearer drapes the thread across the branch. It is an ill omen if the tree never flowers

### Entering the Greenwood

Very little is known in the wider world about the Greenwood's interior. There is simply something *wrong* with the place, something that unsettles the nerves, plays upon fears, and discourages exploration. Too many stories of danger exit the wood, and too few travelers return. The few adventurers who dare the Greenwood's deep trails return with strange scars, stranger stories, and too few prizes to warrant either.

From the outside, the Greenwood looks like any ancient forest. In some places it is dark, tangled, and foreboding. In other places, it is sun-dappled and open, like an evergreen palace of towering tree pillars, ivy carpet, and wind-rustled canopy. But there is something *more* to it. Something hiding behind the wind and the leaves and the trees. Something living and vigilant. A presence that none who stand dwarfed among the trees can deny.

The Greenwood unsettles the nerves of interlopers, evoking fear and trembling, especially in first-time visitors. It conjures awe and intimidation as the intruder delves deeper. And nearest the heart of the forest, the unsettling feeling of being in proximity to a supernatural or divine "other" is intensified.

## The Vaelthir World Tree

At the center of all things Talanthari stands **Vaelthir**, the World Tree—not merely a landmark, but a **living axis of the Weave** itself. This is no ordinary tree.

**Physical Description:**
- Trunk wider than the greatest cathedrals of the Empire—perhaps two hundred feet or more in diameter
- Branches pierce the clouds, spreading a canopy that shadows the entire gorge
- Roots descend so deep they lace through the Underbloom and beyond, perhaps touching the very bones of the world
- Glows faintly at dusk
- Feeds both rivers from springs that emerge among its roots
- Said to be the first World Tree in Elarion

**Spiritual Significance:**
- Not worshipped as a god, but venerated as both spirit and guardian
- A being older than the gods, though not one of them
- Living memory of creation itself
- Every birth, death, and union within Talanthar is marked by blood offerings to the earth at Vaelthir's base
- During the Corvessan Crusades, the bark absorbed the blood of thousands—the scar still gleams silver under moonlight

**The Blood Covenant:**
The people of Talanthar maintain a **blood-bond** with the forest spirit through Vaelthir. This covenant means:
- The forest consciousness knows friend from foe
- Terrain shifts to aid inhabitants
- Weather patterns favor defenders
- Animals fight alongside warriors
- Invaders face constant supernatural opposition

*"You cannot conquer something that feeds on your defeat."*

## Major Regions and Sacred Sites

### The High Crown
Sacred terraces surrounding the World Tree's roots. Home to the Archdruidic conclave, sacred pools, and living monuments of bark and crystal. Access restricted; even most citizens enter only for ritual. The Circle of the Moonweave and the Council of Nine Groves meet here.

### The Silver Hollow
The underground district where trade, metalwork, and magic converge. Mid-level caverns housing forges and laboratories. Here Magi-smiths alloy imported metals with sapstone, using river-spirits' breath to temper enchanted alloys, producing weapons that hum with low vitality.

### The Bridge-Courts
Hanging walkways and suspended markets where griffon feathers, enchanted hides, and rare herbs are traded. Markets suspended between cliffs where traders exchange goods with dwarven or Imperial intermediaries.

### The Shadow Pools
Lower caverns of dark water and fungal canopy where witch-covens commune with reflected spirits under bioluminescent trees. The atmosphere is one of serene menace—beauty too still to be benign. Imperial observers are forbidden entry; none who disobeyed have returned whole.

### Skyperch Cliffs (Griffin Eyries)
Jagged limestone ridges overlooking the gorge, where griffons and their riders make their aeries. Home to the Skyguard Orders—aerial knights mounted on griffins and great eagles. Signal-fires and runestone perches allow instantaneous warnings across hundreds of miles. Defensive patrols can reach the forest edge within an hour.

### Moonveil Glades
Misty forest valleys where witch covens and moon-elves dwell. Here reality bends gently—paths repeat, moons double in reflection, and spirits dance in silver dew.

### The Thornward Flats
A marshy plain between Corvessa and the forest edge, where countless invasions have died in the mud. Once a fertile plain of trade and migration, now a **cursed frontier**. Blackened trees, vine-choked ruins, and shallow marshes filled with broken helms and rusting banners.

- Monuments of rusting helms mark where Corvessa's finest Lances fell
- Talanthar's druids allow no settlement here; the land is considered a living graveyard
- Ghost-lights drift over pools that once held the bodies of knights and raptors
- From the Corvessan side, the horizon appears misted—yet the fog never crosses into Corvessan lands. It waits, like a living sentinel.
- Talanthar's side is eerily alive: green, bright, yet oppressive. Flowers bloom from skulls; roots form bridges over forgotten roads.

### The Underbloom
Vast caverns beneath the city, filled with glowing lichens, fungal orchards, and subterranean groves. These chambers range from narrow crawlways to vaults vast enough to contain entire villages. Bioluminescent flora—glowmoss, lich-orchids, and silver-cap fungi—illuminate the depths in soft hues of blue and green. Mushroom forests, pallid willows, and silver-rooted birches form subterranean biomes of their own.

### The Halfling Joy-Wilds
Stretching southward of the main forest, a web of rolling meadows and tangled copses where wild halfling tribes roam. They live by hunt, feast, and song; their raptor-mounted warbands are beloved by Talanthar but feared by all others. A tavern may appear for a week on a certain glade and vanish the next, yet always seem to stand exactly where weary travelers most need it.

## The City of Talanthar (Vael'Tharyn)

Talanthar's capital is not a single city, but a **vertical labyrinth** of terraces, bridges, and caverns that span the cliffs where the twin rivers Aerendel and Myrn meet to carve their deep gorge.

**Structure:**
- Half above and half below the surface world
- Districts cling to living stone on both cliff faces
- Root-bridges and rope-spans swing across the gorge at dozens of levels, swaying gently in the perpetual mist
- Where the gorge narrows, buildings on opposite walls nearly touch, connected by covered walkways
- Terraces, bridges, and platforms spiral along the gorge walls
- Residences grow from living wood coaxed into shape by druidic art
- Below the waterline, **vast underwater caves** honeycomb the cliff bases—accessible by diving or submerged tunnels
- River tunnels lined with carved crannogs—half-stone, half-root—joined by bridges that sway yet never fall
- **Floating markets** drift on the converging waters, tethered by living vines, accessible by rope ladders and diving platforms
- Above, aerial walkways thread through upper canopy, linking cliff-perched towers to colossal trees whose trunks serve as halls, archives, and temples
- The World Tree Vaelthir rises from the gorge's heart, its roots forming natural bridges and its trunk housing sacred chambers

**Atmosphere:**
- Night glows with cultivated luminescence—glow-mushrooms, lich-lamps, and hanging gardens of light-berries
- Lanternlight and glowberry vines cast perpetual twilight hue upon the streets
- Waterfalls cascade from level to level
- No two streets remain the same shape for long: the rock shifts, guided by old runes and the will of Vaelthir itself
- At night, the entire gorge glows faintly—not by flame, but by the slow respiration of luminous vines. The effect is breathtaking and profoundly disquieting: the sense that the city itself is awake.

**Key Districts:**

| **District** | **Location** | **Function** |
|-------------|--------------|--------------|
| The High Crown | Upper terraces surrounding Vaelthir's roots | Sacred space, Archdruid's residence, Council chambers |
| The Silver Hollow | Mid-level underground caverns | Forges, trade, magical metalwork |
| The Bridge-Courts | Suspended between cliffs | Markets, commerce, external trade |
| The Shadow Pools | Lower caverns | Witch rituals, communion with spirits |
| Skyperch Aeries | Upper rim towers | Griffin landing platforms, defensive patrols |

**Population:**
Reliable counts are impossible due to the shifting nature of the forest and semi-nomadic habits of many communities. Estimates range from:
- 80,000-100,000 permanent residents within the gorge
- 320,000-400,000 dispersed through outlying hamlets and groves
- Total population: ~2.8 million across the sovereignty

## Magic in the Greenwood

Like any living thing, the Greenwood wants to thrive and grow. But unlike a normal forest, the Greenwood requires more than sun, rain, and soil. The Greenwood *hungers* for magical energy, which it acquires by absorbing some of the power of every spell cast within its borders.

### Spell Siphoning

The Greenwood's power to siphon magic affects all spells cast within its borders. Spells cast by outsiders suffer reduced effectiveness, while those who have gained the forest's favor may cast freely.

The dissipation of siphoned energy into the forest frequently manifests visually, in a fashion associated with the spell's nature:
- **Healing magic**: Causes grass to grow, weeds to sprout, and buds to open in a circle centered on the target. Days after blood has soaked into the forest floor, a perspicacious adventurer can still detect the site of a battle by the lush circles that dot the landscape.
- **Conjuration**: Transparent images of summoned creatures scream out of the caster's face and melt into the ground. The Greenwood usually *chooses* the creatures summoned—casters must include a tooth, claw, or body part from the desired creature to override the forest's selection.
- **Teleportation**: Ancient stone circles, sacred groves, and ley lines tug and distort the path to destinations. Teleportation is unreliable at best.
- **Evocation**: Effects linger—frost covers the ground, fire singes vegetation, sparks arc between trees.
- **Illusion**: The forest adds details to make illusions fit; moss grows on illusory trees, brambles curl around illusory walls, and birdsong accompanies phantom sounds.

### Native Magic

Every intelligent creature born in the Greenwood and raised in the Old Ways treasures an intimate magical connection with the forest. Even those with no talent for the magical arts cradle a tiny spark of Greenwood magic in their soul. This spark allows them to cast a single cantrip once daily—always the same spell for each individual, with unique components, gestures, or rhymes.

Through this magic, one native may have a knack for keeping blades sharp, another for locating game, and another for predicting storms or the arrival of foreigners. The spell represents both the character's relationship with the forest and the expenditure of cantrip magic that *feeds* the forest.

### Hearts of the Greenwood

Scattered throughout the Greenwood are regions of concentrated magical power where the forest's essence is most potent. These "Hearts" are places of great power and great danger.

**The Western Heart** (Shadow Heart):
Deep in the oldest sections of the Greenwood, taproots older than the modern gods draw power from the Shadow Plane. Shadows of trees and beasts are disproportionately long even at noon; sometimes they move independently or detach entirely.
- Undead and shadow creatures gain enhanced abilities
- Those who fall prone or lie down risk being drawn into the earth by erupting roots
- Creatures reincarnated here gain shadow abilities but cannot touch loved ones without harm

**The Central Heart** (Wild Heart):
A place of unbridled wildness surrounding the oldest groves near Vaelthir. Trails disappear behind those who blaze them. Structures shake themselves down. Poisons are more deadly, diseases more virulent.
- Aura of Rust and Aura of Wildness are twice as potent
- The forest may transmit lycanthropy through the needles of its conifers
- The forest haunts the dreams of sleeping creatures, inducing paranoia against companions
- Those with rage abilities find it nearly impossible to control

**The Forming Heart** (Dream Heart):
In the eastern reaches, a new Heart is slowly forming. The veil to the Astral Plane thins here, and unbidden dreams carry folk to its timeless expanse.
- Time flows strangely; travelers may enter young and emerge old, or vice versa
- Ghosts appear as spirits of loved ones pass through the Astral on their way to the afterlife
- Vast areas of utter silence where no birds sing and nothing moves but wind
- Divination spells have greatly extended range from high points

### Standing Stones

Scattered throughout the Greenwood are circles of ancient standing stones marking ley line conjunctions and mystical crossroads. These "dancing stones" are far more animated and dangerous than typical stone circles.

**When the Stones Dance:**
The stones are known for being active at solstices and equinoxes, and quiet most other times. They can be awakened by druids, ley line initiates, or those casting certain earth magic. Once awakened, the stones dance, and those who witness them may be compelled to dance as well, taking damage from the effort of continuous movement.

**Known Stone Circles:**
- **The Teller of Tales**: Three tall stones and one fallen, said to speak to visitors if addressed politely through ritual
- **The Hungry Bears**: Popular among bearfolk, who drive off "trespassers"
- **The Silver Spinners**: Four stones with veins of silvery material, site of moonweave coven rituals
- **The Antler Hill Stones**: Sacred to the deerfolk, each carved with antlers or ferns, marking graves of grand druids

# PART III: CULTURE AND SOCIETY

## The Federation of Circles

Talanthar is less a monarchy and more a **federation of circles**: druidic orders, witch covens, ranger brotherhoods, and halfling joy-tribes. Each governs its own grove or valley, sending a representative to the **High Council of the Greenwood**, presided over by the Archdruid of Vaelthir.

Society divides into **Circles** rather than classes:
- **Druids**: Nature magic practitioners, keepers of the Covenant
- **Rangers**: Scouts, trackers, and defenders of the borders
- **Magi**: Scholarly magic-users, archive keepers
- **Witches**: Moon-readers, curse-weavers, psychological warfare specialists
- **Shamanic lineages**: Spirit-speakers, intermediaries with forest entities

No monarch presides; authority rotates with the seasons, granted by ritual consensus rather than decree.

---

## The Races of Talanthar

### Moon Elves
The eldest voices of the forest, keepers of memory and song. They dwell in the highest terraces and deepest grottoes, moving between worlds with an ease that unnerves outsiders. Their songs are polyphonic whispers that echo through the trees—beautiful, mournful, and unnervingly unified.

### Wood Elves
Including both indigenous populations and descendants of the Islands of Eternal Green refugees who fled Vashkoran persecution ~500-600 years ago. The "Ash-Marked" identity represents fully integrated Talanthari citizens who maintain additional memorial traditions. Significant intermarriage with moon elves over generations has created blended bloodlines while preserving distinct cultural knowledge.

### Halflings
The Joy-Wild clans who hunt from giant raptors and elk. They are the laughter of the Greenwood, the ones who feast after battle and turn sorrow into song. To Corvessans, they are "forest vermin." To Talanthar, they are **the Joy of the Greenwood** itself. They reportedly slew knights at the "Feast of Horns" with nothing but dinner forks and wild abandon.

### Beastfolk
Descendants of those who swore the Oath of the Greenwood in ages past, their bloodlines mingled with the spirits of the forest. They serve as scouts, shamans, and bridge-builders between the human and the wild.

### Humans
Fewer in number but no less devoted. Many are descendants of refugees from Corvessa's crusades, or those who fled the rigid order of the Pattern for the freedom of the forest. They serve as rangers, blacksmiths, and the interface between Talanthar and the outside world.

---

## The Ash-Marked Communities

A distinct sub-cultural group within Talanthar, the Ash-Marked are descendants of wood elf refugees from the **Islands of Eternal Green** (destroyed ~500-600 years ago on coastal Vashkora by Ashkarra's Burning Concord). Approximately 2,000 refugees fled to Talanthar over the following year, taking the Oath of the Greenwood. Ashkarra itself unmade five days after the Islands' destruction.

**Population Breakdown:**
- Core Ash-Marked settlements: ~12,000-15,000 (18 communities)
- Total Ash-Marked heritage (anyone with Islands ancestry): ~45,000-55,000 (10-12% of Talanthar's population)
- Integration: Fully Talanthari citizens participating in all Circles

**Distinguishing Characteristics:**
- **Astronomical observatories**: Maintaining Cycle-Singer traditions from Islands heritage
- **Memorial ceremonies**: Annual Seven Oases Remembrance honoring destroyed homeland
- **Enhanced vigilance**: Disproportionate representation in border defense and intelligence roles
- **Craft specializations**: Lens-making, astronomical instruments, specialized glasswork
- **Cultural synthesis**: Blended moon elf and wood elf traditions over 500+ years

**Notable Ash-Marked Settlements:**

| **Settlement** | **Location** | **Population** | **Function** |

| Aeloren's Grove | Southern interior | ~650 | Cultural center, memorial site, hosts Seven Oases festival |

| Rootremembrance | Thornward frontier | ~600 | Training center for anti-persecution tactics |
| Glasswork Haven | Southern interior | ~500 | Lens-making and astronomical tool crafts |

**Contribution to Talanthar:**
The Ash-Marked contribute disproportionately to Talanthar's intelligence infrastructure. Their specialized knowledge includes:
- Pattern recognition for predicting when civilizations become dangerous
- Methods for hiding archives during book-burning campaigns
- Maintaining knowledge through oral tradition when writing is threatened
- Evacuation protocols for scholars and children
- Recognizing escalation patterns before violence begins
- Direct knowledge of dragonborn military tactics from refugee ancestors

## Religion and Beliefs

The people of Talanthar do not worship Vaelthir as a god, but **venerate** it as both spirit and guardian—a being older than the gods, though not one of them.

**Temple Worship:**
Temples to the following deities stand beside the sacred groves, representing balance between nature's fury and grace:
- **Ishara** (nature and magic)
- **Thamur** (storms and strength)
- **Sylthara** (wisdom and prophecy)
- **Sarnock** (death and cycles)

**Spiritual Intermediaries:**
Witches, druids, and shamans act as intermediaries with lesser forest spirits. Magic in Talanthar is not studied from books but **learned from the land itself**—a conversation with the Weave rather than a command.

**Core Belief Structure:**
- Every birth, death, and union is marked by blood offerings to the earth
- The cycle of life-death-rebirth is sacred and eternal
- To fix the Weave into a static pattern is heresy
- The forest is a living entity that demands respect and reciprocation

## Festivals and Celebrations

Festivals follow the lunar cycles rather than solar seasons:

### The Night of Still Waters
When all magic and song fall silent to let the forest rest. Even the witches do not cast spells, and the druids do not call the spirits. A night of profound silence and meditation.

### The Rising Veil
When druids call the spirits to awaken the new season. Music, dancing, and the blessing of new growth marks this celebration.

### The Feast of Memory
An annual commemoration of all those who have returned to the roots, celebrated with storytelling and blood-offerings.

### Seven Oases Remembrance (Celestialdawn 15-23)
Annual memorial honoring the Islands of Eternal Green, destroyed by Ashkarra's Burning Concord ~500-600 years ago. Primarily observed by Ash-Marked communities but increasingly adopted as general Talanthari commemoration of all victims of institutional knowledge suppression. Nine-day ceremony honors each destroyed oasis and reflects on Ashkarra's subsequent unmaking five days later.

### The Day of Laughing Shadows
Annual celebration of the Thornward victory (482 FA), with feasts and songs mocking Corvessan arrogance. Talanthari bards recite the Five Failures to laughter and applause in every tavern.

# PART IV: GOVERNMENT AND POLITICS

## The High Council of the Greenwood

Governance in Talanthar operates by **consensus and omen**, not decree.

**Structure:**
- Each grove or valley governs itself through its local circle
- Each sends a representative to the High Council
- The Archdruid of Vaelthir presides over the Council

**Decision-Making Process:**
1. Long councils convene where druids listen to whispers of the forest
2. Witches read portents in water and moon
3. Representatives of each grove speak for their people
4. When consensus cannot be reached, the Archdruid consults Vaelthir directly
5. This consultation can take days of meditation and blood-offering

**Circle Representation:**
The Council includes representatives from:
- Major druidic orders (Circle of Blood, Circle of the Deep Root, etc.)
- Witch covens (Moonweave Covens, etc.)
- Ranger brotherhoods (Greenwatch Rangers, Thornward Watch)
- Magi (Eldermantle Archives)
- Halfling Joy-tribes
- Skyguard Orders

---

## The Doctrine of Rooted Stillness

Talanthar does not wage wars of conquest. It does not seek to expand beyond its borders or impose its will on others.

**Core Principles:**
- Aggression is only sanctioned when the forest itself is threatened
- Defense is reactive, not preemptive
- The forest's will determines military action

**When Threatened:**
The Greenwood answers as one organism:
- Every ranger, beast, spirit, and the land itself moves in harmony
- Invasions are defeated not by superior numbers but by **superior adaptation**
- The forest learns, adjusts, and responds with terrifying efficiency

**Internal Debate:**
Warden Aelthir Ashborn and some Ash-Marked communities argue for expanded interpretation of "threatened"—suggesting preemptive action against Corvessan mobilizations rather than waiting for invasion. The High Council generally rejects this, maintaining that Rooted Stillness requires actual forest endangerment, not merely external hostility.

# PART V: MILITARY STRUCTURE

## The Forest as Fortress

Talanthar's military doctrine is simple: **Never fight where the enemy is strong; always fight where the forest is alive.**

**Key Principles:**
- The Greenwood itself is the first, last, and greatest weapon
- Every tree, root, and stream can become a tool of war
- Small-unit tactics over massed formations
- Asymmetric warfare over direct confrontation
- Cannot survive open plains warfare
- Cannot be defeated in own forest

**Why Conventional Armies Fail:**
- Terrain shifts to aid Talanthari, confuse invaders
- Weather patterns favor defenders
- Animals fight alongside warriors
- Paths close behind enemy forces
- Bridges collapse, crops fail, horses refuse to approach
- Invaders face constant supernatural opposition

---

## The Specialist Orders

Unlike Corvessa's massed cavalry charges, Talanthar employs **specialist small-unit orders** that excel at asymmetric warfare:

### Martial Orders

**Greenwatch Rangers**
Elite pathfinders and ambushers. Includes subclasses such as Gloom Stalkers, Wardens, and Spears of the Weald. Masters of camouflage, tracking, and ambush. They move through the forest like ghosts, appearing and disappearing at will.

**Verdant Guard**
Mage-Knight order of Bladesingers, Magi, and Elementalists. Elite spell-blade strike units combining martial prowess with arcane power.

**Skyguard**
Aerial cavalry mounted on griffins and great eagles. Based at the cliff aeries. They strike from above with devastating speed, dropping molten resin and burning sap upon enemy formations before vanishing into the clouds. Can scramble to the border within an hour.

**Halfling Joy-Wild Raiders**
Irregular cavalry mounted on giant raptors and dire hares. Famed for joyful ferocity and hunting rituals. They do not fight with discipline but with **wild abandon and laughter**. They hunt fleeing enemies like prey, turning retreat into massacre.

**Paladins of the Grove**
Religious-martial order of Oath of the Ancients paladins. Battlefield anchors and protectors of sacred sites.

### Magical and Religious Circles

**Circle of Blood**
Druidic order specializing in blood magic and sacrifice. Rumored to shape terrain through life-force exchanges.

**Circle of the Deep Root**
Druidic order guarding subterranean biomes and river caves.

**Moonweave Covens**
Witch/Warlock organizations under Witch-Mother Selune Maraith. Responsible for:
- Maintaining moon-roads
- Veiling Talanthar from scrying
- Inverting light in battle
- Cursing weapons to shatter
- Whispering madness into invaders' minds
- The mists that claimed Saint-General Virel Marrow and his army

### Combat Druids and Shamans
Command the very elements of the forest. Capabilities include:
- Summoning floods
- Growing thorn barriers mid-battle
- Causing roots to erupt and impale cavalry
- Bending light to create illusions that turn armies in circles
- Weather manipulation

# PART VII: RELATIONS WITH NEIGHBORS

## The Eternal Enemy: Corvessa

### The Nature of the Conflict

This is not political conflict—it is **eschatological rivalry**. Not a border dispute, not a trade war, but a conflict over the nature of reality itself.

**Corvessan Doctrine of the Bound Pattern:**
- The Weave is a loom, life a thread in divine design
- Perfection comes through stillness, obedience, completion
- Death is the final stitch; chaos the unraveling
- The forest's cycle denies finality and mocks divine architecture
- Vaelthir is a blasphemy of recursion, stealing divine authority

**Talanthari Doctrine of the Endless Song:**
- Life is a song that must never stop
- Every death feeds the next life's rhythm
- To fix the Weave into static design is to kill the song
- Vaelthir is memory of creation itself, not a god
- The Pattern is a cage that murders the living cycle

**Why Peace is Impossible:**
Each society defines virtue through the other's sins. Both believe the other's worldview must be destroyed for their own to survive. No treaty can bridge this divide.

### Corvessa's View of Talanthar
- "Heretic forest vermin"
- False idol worshipers
- Uncivilized, wild
- Enemy to be purged
- The Prophecy of the Felled Root commands them to destroy Vaelthir

### Talanthar's View of Corvessa
- "Blind zealots"
- Children playing with dead gods' bones
- Deaf to the song of life
- Predictable, arrogant fools who march in straight lines into forests that fight back
- The Pattern's slaves, caged souls

### The Border
- There is **no diplomacy**
- No standing embassy, trade treaties, or peace accords
- Corvessa maintains six active forts and dozens of ruined chapels along the border
- Primary fortress is **Rivenscar**, built of black stone quarried from the first Crusade's ruins
- Griffin towers perch upon cliffs overlooking these valleys, their riders watching in silence
- Neither side fires the first shot—both remember what happened when they did

### Ghost-Roads
Between the outer hamlets of Talanthar and Corvessa's reed villages lie the **ghost-roads**:
- Paths that alter course overnight
- Some lead to the same tavern every traveller swears is elsewhere
- Others end in pools reflecting stars not yet risen
- These liminal routes act as neutral ground: smuggling trails, meeting places, sometimes graveyards for the lost

## The Six Great Failures

Corvessa has launched **six major campaigns** into the Greenwood over the centuries, all ending in failure or stalemate:

### Pre-Imperial Age: The Vanishing
Corvessa's river-chiefs pushed eastward seeking timber and hunting grounds. Early expeditions vanished almost without trace. Only the Chronicle of Ser Tares the Younger survives:

> *"The woods do not yield their paths; by noon the road is new, by dusk our names unmade."*

### First Failure (312-314 FA): The First Ride of Purity
The First Crusade of the Pattern. Ten thousand horse and levy crossed the Thornward Flats under banners of white flame. **None returned save two heralds whose skin bore living vine scars that never healed.**

### Second Failure (333-334 FA): The Second Ride of Binding
A smaller, more disciplined force commanded by Saint-General Virel Marrow reached the Vaelrun ford before being **encircled in mists that lasted six days**. When the fog cleared, the army's standards hung from the trees upside-down, the men gone. Corvessa canonized Marrow as martyr; Talanthar remembers him as **"The Weeping Steel."**

### Third Failure (359-360 FA): The Third Ride of Order / Battle of Singing Thorns
Jointly sanctioned by the Kel Varyan Ecclesia before Corvessa became a client kingdom. The expedition was **annihilated at the Battle of Singing Thorns**: vines reportedly erupted mid-charge, impaling cavalry. Surviving accounts describe music—the forest humming in time with the slaughter.

### Fourth Failure (298 FA): The Breaking at Mossveil Gorge
Still seen in Talanthar as their greatest triumph, in Corvessa as their greatest martyrdom. An entire Corvessan army marched into the gorge and never emerged.

### Fifth Failure (421 FA): The Clearing Crusade
Attempted to burn the forest edge to create secure invasion corridors. The fire spread faster than expected, consuming Corvessan supply camps and roasting knights in their armor as wind patterns shifted impossibly.

### Sixth Failure (482 FA): The Thornward Flats Disaster
See detailed section below.

---

## The Thornward Flats Disaster (482 FA)

The culmination of centuries of zeal came in the so-called **Holy Campaign of the River Lances**, an attempt to permanently subdue Talanthar with Imperial blessing.

**The Force:**
- Nearly half of Corvessa's nobility
- Nine thousand lancers
- A thousand clerics
- Innumerable squires and servants

**The Disaster:**
- Rain turned the Flats to mire
- Arrows fell like sleet
- **The forest itself advanced**—roots splitting armour, mud swallowing horses whole
- Rangers harried supply lines
- Witches inverted light
- Druids drowned the flanks with summoned floods
- When retreat came, halfling joy-tribes hunted the survivors in **The Great Chase**
- Each slain knight became a trophy and offering of laughter to the forest
- **Within five days the campaign ceased to exist**
- Half of Corvessa's knightly youth died

**Legends:**
- The halfling tribes sang the **"Iron Feast"**—captured knights were roasted in their own armour as offerings to the Old Wood
- The moon elves tell tales of **"Silver Blood"**—the streams that ran red after the Lances fell

**Aftermath:**
- Corvessa's pride broke with its lances
- Its king aged ten years in mourning
- The Empire took note
- Corvessa calls it the **Martyrdom at Thornward**, framing it as a test of faith rather than defeat
- Talanthar celebrates it as **The Day of Laughing Shadows**

**Ash-Marked Contributions:**
While Talanthar would have won through the forest's inherent advantages, Ash-Marked communities contributed:
- Enhanced intelligence preparation
- Institutional pattern recognition predicted the campaign six months in advance
- Knowledge of Burning Concord military doctrine informed counter-tactics
- Reduced Talanthari casualties and accelerated campaign's collapse

---

## The Kel Varyan Empire

**The Imperial Approach:**
The Empire approached differently—with caution and curiosity rather than religious zeal.

### The Border Skirmish of 486 FA
Four years after Thornward, the Empire conducted a limited incursion under guise of mapping:
- Two cohorts of the 14th Legion entered the eastern marches
- Orders: reconnaissance and demonstration of strength
- They faced no open resistance—only ambush, misdirection, and weather turning hostile beyond natural measure
- Entire units wandered into ravines unseen on Imperial maps
- After 48 hours, command collapsed
- Fewer than half returned
- Analysis showed legionaries were not slain en masse but separated and neutralized piecemeal by small, expert teams

**The Lesson:**
Corvessa's generals had called the forest sorcery. Imperial tacticians now called it **superior adaptation**.

**The Empire learned what Corvessa refuses to accept: discipline fails where the forest itself fights back.**

### Imperial Policy
- Tolerates Talanthar's independence
- Prefers to avoid direct entanglement
- Sees Corvessa's zeal as keeping their focus eastward, leaving their southern flank compliant and dependent
- Talanthar's isolation ensures it will never become an aggressive regional power
- **The unending war that neither side can win has become the mechanism that preserves Imperial peace**
- The Empire keeps Corvessa as a client buffer state but views their fanaticism as dangerous

## The Crusades of the Pattern

The Crusades represent Corvessa's attempts to fulfill the **Prophecy of the Felled Root**, which commands the destruction of Vaelthir. Each failure was reinterpreted as a divine test rather than military defeat, ensuring the cycle continues.

**Common Pattern After Each Disaster:**
1. Initial shock and mourning
2. Theological reframing as "martyrdom" and "divine trial"
3. Intensification of zeal rather than tactical adaptation
4. Rebuilding for the next attempt
5. More knights, more chaplains, more consecrated weapons

**The Faith's Logic:**
- The Prophecy cannot be wrong
- Therefore, failure means insufficient faith
- Therefore, greater faith will bring success
- This creates a feedback loop ensuring future crusades

## Running the Greenwood

### Setting the Mood

The Greenwood should feel *alive* in a way that unsettles players. Use these techniques:

**Ambient Description:**
- Constant sounds: rustling leaves, distant bird calls, water dripping, creaking wood
- The feeling of being watched—always
- Movement glimpsed at the edge of vision that vanishes when looked at directly
- Familiar paths that seem different when traversed again
- Trees that appear to have moved overnight

**Sensory Details:**
- Air thick with spores, pollen, and the electric hum of living resonance
- The forest "breathing"—a low rhythmic pulse barely felt rather than heard
- Smells shift: sweet flowers, rot, ozone, wet earth, ancient moss
- Temperature varies dramatically between sun-dappled glades and shadow-choked hollows

**Environmental Storytelling:**
- Old weapons rusting into the earth
- Bones wound through with roots
- Abandoned camps with no sign of violence—just emptiness
- Messages carved into bark in languages no one speaks anymore
- Offerings at the base of ancient trees: fruit, blood, small tokens

### Forest Creatures

**Greenwood Guardians:**
- **Treants**: Ancient trees awakened to defend sacred sites
- **Leshy**: Small forest spirits that police the Greenwood's boundaries
- **Awakened Beasts**: Animals granted intelligence by the forest's will
- **Green Hussars**: Plant-creature guardians spawned by the Greenwood in times of need
- **Dryads**: Tree spirits that serve as diplomats and observers
- **Sprites and Pixies**: Mischievous fey that test travelers' patience and intentions

**Dangers of the Deep:**
- **Ferals**: Humanoid bodies hosting reincarnated animal souls—savage and unpredictable
- **Hollow Men**: Constructs of twisted wood and bone that serve dark powers
- **Vine Golems**: Animated plant matter that guards forbidden areas
- **Shadow Beasts**: Creatures of the Shadow Heart, more dangerous at night
- **Corrupted Treants**: Trees tainted by Drow magic, hostile to all life

**Corvessan Threats:**
- **Pattern-Sworn**: Crusaders who have sacrificed their capacity for doubt
- **Inquisitors**: Specialists in rooting out "corruption" and "heresy"
- **War-Priests of Veranis**: Divine casters empowered by geometric faith
- **Forge-Golems**: Mechanical constructs brought against the forest

### The Forest's Reactions

The Greenwood responds to player actions. Use these guidelines:

**Helpful Actions (Improve Standing):**
- Defending a forest creature from attack
- Making proper offerings at sacred sites
- Following the Old Ways (even if imperfectly understood)
- Punishing those who harm the forest
- Healing sick or wounded trees or animals

**Harmful Actions (Damage Standing):**
- Lighting large fires
- Cutting living wood
- Killing without necessity
- Showing disrespect at sacred sites
- Allying with Corvessa or bringing Corvessans into the forest

**The Forest's Rewards:**
- Safe passage through dangerous areas
- Healing springs revealed when needed
- Predators guided away from the party
- Shelter provided during storms
- Visions or prophecies granted in dreams

**The Forest's Punishments:**
- Paths that loop endlessly
- Water sources that dry up or become tainted
- Predators that stalk relentlessly
- Nightmares that deny rest
- Transformation into forest creatures (in extreme cases)
