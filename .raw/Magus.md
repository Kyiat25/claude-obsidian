# The Magus

**Where Blade and Spell Converge**

> *"The wizard memorises how magic speaks. The Magus speaks back — through a longsword."*

---

## Steel and Spell as One

The guards expected a mage. They had positioned archers in the gallery above — a reasonable countermeasure. What they got instead was something that closed the distance in two steps, channelled a *green-flame blade* through the first guard's parry without breaking stride, and was already drawing back the blade for a second strike before the fire had finished spreading. Not a mage with a sword. Not a swordsman with a cantrip. Something else entirely.

You are the Magus. Your **Spellstrike** fuses spell and attack into one movement, turning a weapon hit into a magical delivery mechanism. **Spell Combat** means you don't have to choose between casting defensively and attacking offensively — you do both simultaneously. **Arcane Enhancement** gives your blade an elemental edge you switch in real time to exploit vulnerabilities.

In FEARS, your Momentum comes through consistency. **Adaptive Mind** at 11th level lets you reduce Momentum loss from incoming hits as a reaction, preserving your combat rhythm under assault. Your Called Shot DCS integration through **Arcane Counterstrike** at 13th level means you can disrupt concentration spells with physical strikes.

---

## The Magus's Essence

You are a hybrid striker who escalates danger as the fight continues. You combine the Fighter's reliable weapon attacks with the Wizard's spell access, in a body that is neither and both simultaneously. Your **War Tradition** shapes whether you lean toward devastating offense, defensive magic, or arcane disruption.

You struggle when you cannot close the distance and when spell slots drain without payoff. You excel in sustained melee pressure that combines physical momentum with magical escalation.

---

## Complexity Assessment: HIGH (7/10)

The Magus tracks:
• Spell slots (half-caster, long rest recovery)   
• Arcane Reservoir (Arcane Guard resource)      
• Spellstrike and Spell Combat action economy each turn 
• Weapon Mastery interactions with Spellstrike    
• Momentum (Adaptive Mind preserves it under fire)  

Recommended for players comfortable with action economy management and hybrid martial-magical playstyles.

## Class Features

### Hit Points

- **Hit Dice:** 1d8 per Magus level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 8 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per Magus level after 1st

### Proficiencies

- **Armour:** Light armour and shields
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons and martial weapons
- **Tools:** None
- **Saving Throws:** Constitution, Intelligence
- **Skills:** Choose two from Arcana, Athletics, Crafting, History, Insight, Investigation, Perception, Religion, and Resolve

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) a longsword or (b) any martial weapon
- (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) any simple weapon
- (a) a scholar's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
- Leather armour and an arcane focus or component pouch

## Quick Build

To quickly create a Magus, follow these steps:

1. **Ability Scores:** Make Intelligence your highest score, followed by Strength or Dexterity (matching your weapon preference).
2. **Lineage and Heritage:** Any combination works; Half-Elf, Human, and Tiefling are thematic.
3. **Background:** Sage, Soldier, or any background with Arcana or Athletics.
4. **Combat Strategy:** Spellstrike when you need magical delivery; Spell Combat to protect yourself and still attack. Use Arcane Enhancement at the start of combat to add elemental damage matching enemy vulnerabilities.

## Core Class Features

### Spellcasting (1st Level)

You channel arcane power through martial discipline. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability.

- **Spell Save DC** = 8 + PB + INT modifier
- **Spell Attack Modifier** = PB + INT modifier

You know spells from the Magus spell list as shown. When you gain a level, you may replace one known spell with another of a circle you can cast.

---

### Spellstrike (1st Level)

When you cast a cantrip that deals damage or has a single target, you can deliver it through a melee weapon attack. Make the weapon attack roll — if it hits, apply both the weapon's damage and the cantrip's effects to the target. If the cantrip would normally require an attack roll, it hits automatically on a weapon hit. If it requires a saving throw, the target makes that save after taking weapon damage.

**Momentum Interaction:** Spellstrike counts as a weapon attack for all Momentum purposes. A successful Spellstrike generates +1 Momentum (or +2 on a critical hit).

---

### Spell Combat (1st Level)

When you cast a non-damaging cantrip (such as *blade ward* or *guidance*), you can make one melee weapon attack as a bonus action. This allows you to protect yourself magically and still apply martial pressure simultaneously.

---

### Weapon Mastery (1st Level)

At 1st level, gain Weapon Mastery with **one weapon** of your choice (simple or martial). When you hit with that weapon, apply one Weapon Skill once per turn.

**Spellstrike Synergy:** You can combine Weapon Skills with Spellstrike — deliver a spell and apply a Weapon Skill on the same hit for devastating magical-martial combinations.

---

### Fighting Style (2nd Level)

Choose one: Archery, Agile Defence, Dueling, Great Weapon Fighting, Interception, Shield Master, or Two-Weapon Fighting.

---

### War Tradition (2nd Level)

Choose your approach to arcane-martial fusion: **Consuming** (offence-focused, burning through resources for maximum damage), **Distorting** (battlefield control, warping positions and sight lines), or **Protecting** (defensive magic turned into impenetrable armour). Your War Tradition grants features at 2nd, 7th, 11th, and 15th levels.

---

### Arcane Enhancement (3rd Level)

As a bonus action, imbue your weapon with elemental damage. Choose fire, cold, or lightning — your weapon deals an additional 1d6 damage of the chosen type for 1 minute. Adapts to enemy elemental vulnerabilities in real time.

---

### Extra Attack (5th Level)

When you take the Attack action, you can make two attacks instead of one.

---

### Arcane Guard (5th Level)

As a bonus action, spend 1 point from your Arcane Reservoir to gain **DR 1** until the start of your next turn. Your spell-fuelled defences create temporary wards from the same energy that powers your Spellstrikes.

---

### Arcane Deflection (6th Level)

When an enemy attacks you or forces you to make a saving throw, use your reaction to gain +2 to your Defence Roll against the attack, or advantage on the saving throw. After using this feature, you cannot cast levelled spells on your next turn.

---

### Aether Step (7th Level)

As a bonus action, spend 1 spell slot to teleport up to 10 feet to an unoccupied space you can see. Does not provoke opportunity attacks and leaves a shimmer of arcane energy where you were standing. Does not count as a levelled spell.

---

### Empowered Weapon (9th Level)

Once per short rest, double the bonus elemental damage from Arcane Enhancement for 1 minute. If your weapon deals 1d6 elemental damage, it instead deals 2d6 for the duration.

---

### Battlemind (10th Level)

Your tactical acumen enhances your reaction time. Add your Intelligence modifier to initiative rolls.

---

### Heroic Boon (10th Level)

Choose one:

**Boon of Arcane Harmony:** After casting a spell of 1st level or higher, use a bonus action to make a melee weapon attack. If the spell was 3rd level or higher, the attack gains a momentum bonus die equal to your PB. When using Spell Strike, add force damage equal to your INT modifier to the attack.

**Boon of the Warding Aegis:** As a reaction when you or an ally within 30 feet is hit, expend a spell slot to reduce the incoming damage by 2d6 + spell slot level + INT modifier; your next attack deals extra force damage equal to damage prevented. At the start of your turn, while you have at least one spell slot remaining, gain temp DR equal to your PB against physical damage until start of your next turn.

---

### Arcane Momentum (11th Level)

When you cast a spell of 1st level or higher, your melee weapon attacks gain a surge of energy until the end of your next turn: add your INT modifier to weapon attack damage; gain +10 feet of movement speed.

---

### Arcane Counterstrike (13th Level)

When a creature misses you with a melee attack, use your reaction to cast a damaging cantrip against it. This cantrip does not require an attack roll and automatically hits the attacker if they are within 5 feet.

---

### Spell Warfare (14th Level)

Your Spell Combat feature is no longer limited to cantrips. You can now use a levelled spell as your action and still make a melee attack as a bonus action.

---

### Spellbreaker's Edge (15th Level)

When you strike a creature with a melee weapon attack:

**Disruptive Strike:** If the target is concentrating on a spell, it has disadvantage on its next Concentration check.

**Arcane Vulnerability:** If affected by a spell you cast, your melee weapon attacks deal an additional 1d6 force damage to the creature until the start of your next turn.

---

### Greater Spellstrike (17th Level)

You can now use the Spellstrike feature to channel a levelled damaging spell of 1st or 2nd level through your weapon. The weapon's attack roll determines if the spell hits, and the weapon's damage is added to the spell's effects.

---

### Counterstrike (18th Level)

When a creature within 30 feet casts a spell, use your reaction to disrupt it with a melee attack. If your attack hits, the caster must succeed on a Concentration check (DC = 10 + spell level). On failure, the spell is disrupted.

---

### Epic Boon (20th Level)

Choose one:

**Boon of Spellblade Mastery:** Apply any spell you cast of 3rd level or lower to a weapon attack when using Spell Strike — the spell's effects apply to each creature damaged by the attack. Once per long rest, make a weapon attack dealing 5d10 additional force damage and imposing the shaken condition until end of your next turn.

**Boon of Arcane Supremacy:** Once per long rest, when you cast a spell of 5th level or lower, deal maximum damage instead of rolling. Once per turn, when you use your action to cast a spell, immediately follow it with a melee weapon attack as part of the same action — this attack deals an additional 2d10 arcane damage.

---

## Last Stand Options

When the Magus meets their final hour, blade and spell fuse into one devastating final act.

**Rules for Last Stand:** Invoked when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, roll a death saving throw, or die outright. A free action requiring no reaction or consciousness. Once resolved, the character permanently dies.

---

### 1. Arcane Finality

*Your blade and magic combine into a devastating crescendo.*

**Effect:**
- Cast any damaging spell up to 5th level, channelling it through your melee weapon.
- The spell automatically hits and critically strikes all creatures in its area or chosen targets.
- If the spell has no area of effect, target up to 3 creatures within melee range.
- The weapon attack deals an additional 5d10 force damage, leaving an arcane mark that burns for 1 minute (2d10 force damage at the start of marked targets' turns).

---

### 2. Blade of Continuum

*Your blade vibrates with temporal energy, ensuring enemies feel your wrath across time.*

**Effect:**
- For the next 5 minutes, every enemy you strike with a melee weapon attack:
  - Takes an immediate critical hit.
  - At the start of their next turn, takes additional damage equal to the weapon's damage dice + your INT modifier.
- Enemies cannot regain HP while within 60 feet of you.
- When 5 minutes elapse, your blade shatters, sending a shockwave dealing 6d10 force damage to all enemies within 30 feet.

---

### 3. Arcane Aegis Unbroken

*As death claims you, you summon a shield of overwhelming magic.*

**Effect:**
- Create a 40-foot radius Arcane Aegis lasting 1 minute:
  - All allies within gain resistance to all damage.
  - Allies gain temporary HP equal to 4 × your Magus level when they enter for the first time.
- Any spells cast by enemies within 60 feet are reflected back at them if they target you or your allies. Lasts for the duration.

| Last Stand Option | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Arcane Finality** | Channel a 5th-level spell as a critical hit; 5d10 force + lingering mark damage. | Devastating strikes against bosses or clusters. |
| **Blade of Continuum** | Every strike crits + echoes; enemies cannot heal; final shockwave. | Disabling regenerating enemies. |
| **Arcane Aegis Unbroken** | Allies gain damage resistance + temp HP; reflect enemy spells for 1 minute. | Protecting allies against overwhelming odds. |

## Class Reactions

Magi have access to powerful reactions that blend martial prowess with arcane power. These reactions use your **Proficiency Bonus pool**, refreshing on short or long rest. Note: some reactions also expend spell slots.

### Arcane Deflection (2nd Level)

**Trigger:** You are hit by a weapon attack.
**Effect:** Expend 1 spell slot to reduce damage by 1d8 per slot level + INT modifier. You may immediately move 10 feet without provoking opportunity attacks.

*(Costs both 1 PB use AND a spell slot. Use strategically.)*

---

### Arcane Riposte (7th Level)

**Trigger:** A creature within 5 feet hits you with a melee attack.
**Effect:** Expend a spell slot. Deal slot level × d8 force damage back to the attacker. Force damage bypasses most resistances and is guaranteed (no attack roll, no save).

---

### Adaptive Mind (11th Level)

**Trigger:** You are hit by an attack.
**Effect:** Reduce any Momentum loss from that attack by 1 (to a minimum of 0). Your battle-mage training allows you to adapt to incoming strikes and maintain your combat rhythm.

Uses equal to your Proficiency Bonus per short rest.
