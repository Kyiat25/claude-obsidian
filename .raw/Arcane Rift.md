# Arcane Rift

An **Arcane Rift** is a tear in the magical fabric of reality that strengthens some types of spells while weakening others. These rifts are visible distortions in space—shimmering cracks glowing with energy.

As you approach the rift, the air shimmers and pulses with unseen energy. Colors twist at the edge of your vision, and the ground hums beneath your feet. You feel your magic responding—rising, resonating, eager. Spells come easier here, as if something deeper is guiding your will. But there’s danger in that ease. You’re not alone in controlling the flow. The rift amplifies—but it also tests.

#### ✨ Effects in Combat:

- **Empowered Magic**: Spells from the rift’s associated school are more powerful, often cast as if they were a higher level.
- **Disrupted Opposition**: Spells from the _opposing school_ are harder to control and less effective.
- **Concentration Advantage**: It’s easier to maintain concentration on spells linked to the rift’s school.
- **Unstable Feedback**: Overpowering a target with magic can trigger unexpected magical surges.

### 🎯 Mechanics Players Need to Know:
🧠 **Concept:** Empowered but unstable spellcasting tied to one school of magic.

- **Spell Amplification:**
    - Spells of the rift's school are cast **1 level higher** (e.g., a 3rd-level spell acts as 4th level).
- **Opposing School Penalty:**
    - Disadvantage on spell attacks.
    - Opposing school spells **cannot be upcast**.
- **Concentration:**
    - **Advantage** for concentration checks on rift’s school
    - **Disadvantage** for opposing school.
- **Overkill Sanity Check:**
    - If your spell deals **double or more** the target’s HP, roll a **Sanity Check** (or Wisdom/Int if Sanity isn’t used).
    - Failure = **Wild Magic Surge**.
- **Critical Range Changes:**
    - Rift School: Crit on **19–20**.
    - Opposing School: Crit only on **natural 20**.

#### 🎭 What You Experience:

- Spells feel **easier** to cast—but more **dangerous**.
- The air hums with energy, the environment shifts, and your magic may _overreact_ if pushed too far.
- You may be asked to make special saves if your spells overperform.
