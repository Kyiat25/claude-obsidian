# 🌑 **The Eldritch Moons of Dór-o Estel**

_“They do not rise. They awaken. They do not glow. They remember.”_

The **[[Eldritch Moons]]** are not natural satellites, nor are they celestial bodies governed by [[Astralor]] or [[The Veiled Hand]]. They are **Weave-wounds**, **lunar overlays**, or **echoes of impossible orbits**—manifestations of the **Null Reflection** pressing close when the Weave frays too thin to contain its memory.

Each moon reflects not light, but **influence**—warping leylines, distorting Glyphs, and seeping into the dreams of those beneath its gaze. **The sky itself does not change. Your understanding of it does.**

Their appearance marks a **pulse in the Beckoning**, a deep ripple in the **[[Eldritch Cycle]]** sensed only by unstable Glyph-readers, prophetic seers, or corrupted stargazers. No pantheon claims them. No ward has ever truly banished them. Even speaking of them can snag the threads of fate.

---
## **Origin and Nature**

The Eldritch Moons are **not bound to mortal astronomy**. They are rents in the Weave—temporary or persistent fractures where glyphic law collapses and the Null Reflection bleeds through.

Unlike the natural moons, whose movement follows tides and time, the Eldritch Moons **manifest when reality falters**. Their light is not sunlight or reflection but the glare of alien places forced into mortal sight.

---
## **Nature**

- **Manifestations of Fracture** — Each moon resonates with a distinct flaw-pattern in the Weave’s structure.
- **Variable Duration** — Some flare and vanish within hours; others persist for years, their glow warping every shadow.
- **Elemental Discord** — Their light disrupts the harmony of the Threads, destabilising elemental balance.

---
## **First Emergence**

- First recorded **late in the Second Age**, in the aftermath of divine fracturing and mortal rebellion.
- Initially appeared once every few decades—treated as rare omens by early Loom-Cities.
- After **Auroratide destabilisations**… began occurring regularly, appearances became more frequent, often in Deepveil season when prophetic resonance is highest.

---
## **Eldritch Moons & the Weave**

- **Flaw Resonance** — Each moon's presence amplifies instability in a specific type of glyphic or planar structure.
- **Hollow Breach Catalyst** — Multiple moons visible at once magnify one another’s effects exponentially; three or more greatly increase breach risk.
- **Memory Distortion** — Time and identity shift under their light; the same moment may repeat differently each time it’s recalled.

---
## **Cultural Consensus**

While interpretations vary, most civilisations agree on three truths:

1. **They herald upheaval** — political, magical, and divine orders shift during or after their rise.
2. **They warp magic** — spellcasting becomes volatile, and planar boundaries weaken.
3. **They scar reality** — regions touched by their light rarely recover, becoming wild magic zones or planar bleed sites.
---

## **Religious Frames**

- [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]] — Treat them as proof the Pattern is under siege; Threadbearers must consult World Anchors for containment.
- **[[The Woven Reverence]]** — Avoid direct engagement, weaving memory-wards and echo-seals instead.
- **[[The Burning Concord]]** — Frame moons as proof of instability in rival Threads, using them to justify conquest.
- **[[The Deep Law]]** — Regard them as cracks to be sealed; issue mining bans and lock glyph-forges.
- **Orcs ([[The Path of the First Scar]])** — See them as clan-wide trials from the Spirit Pantheon.
- **Halflings ([[The Joy Truths]])** — Treat them as cosmic pranks or challenges to outwit Death.
- **Drow ([[The Ashen Web]])** — Claim select moons as the work of [[Vel'Zyren]], using them for assassination omens.
- [[Eldritch Forces]] — Exploit moonrises as open gates; their cults actively seek to engineer alignments.

---
## **The Ashkarra Convergence**

The most infamous event in recorded history involving the Eldritch Moons:
- **Date:** End of the Third Age
- **Event:** All twelve known Eldritch Moons rose in 58 days, aligning on the festival of Ashkarra’s Spirit Thread.
- **Result:** The **First Hollow Breach**, implosion of the **World Anchor**, and the complete unmaking of AshkarraFall of Ashkarra.
- **Legacy:** The Faith of the Bound Pattern teaches this as the clearest example of cosmic law—**no Thread may hold the same alignment more than twice**.

### **The Day of Twelve Shadows**

Within **58 days**, every one of the **twelve Eldritch Moons** rose, culminating in their alignment on the sacred festival of Ashkarra’s third consecutive **Spirit Thread**. This convergence tore open the **First Hollow Breach**, imploded the **World Anchor**, and unmade the Loom-City of [[Ashkarra]]. 

Known as the **Day of Twelve Shadows** or [[Fall of Ashkarra]], it marked the turning point that proved **Thread stagnation combined with repeated Spirit alignments** can bring the **Weave** to the brink of total collapse.  

## 🌑 **When the Eldritch Moons Rise**

The Eldritch Moons do not follow the turning of seasons or the will of the gods. They appear when the **Weave strains under too many fractures**—when unresolved omens, unstable magic zones, and pantheon discord combine to thin reality’s cloth. Most often they manifest in **[[Deepveil]]** season, when prophecy and memory are already at their most volatile, but they can bleed into the sky at any time if **Eldritch influence overtakes a season’s balance**. In play, each **seasonal change** is a moment of cosmic risk: the GM rolls for the turning of the Threads, and this in turn triggers the check for whether its an [[Eldritch Moon awakening]]. Their rising is not a celestial motion but a recognition—the moons are **eyes**, opening only when the world is weakened enough for them to see.

---
## 🌘 **When the Eldritch Moons Set**

No ritual, prayer, or ward has ever forced an Eldritch Moon from the sky. They fade only when the **strain on the Weave eases**—when fractured leylines are restored, elemental balance is reasserted, and the Null Reflection loosens its hold. Some linger for days after their influence has broken, like an afterimage that refuses to leave the eye. In play, a moon’s departure is checked at each **seasonal change** just as its rising is, using the same Thread-turn roll to determine if the Weave has mended enough to close the wound. When they vanish, the world does not return to normal—**the scar remains**, and zones of instability persist long after the sky clears.

## 🌕 **[[The Blood Moon of Rebirth]]**

_“You are not the first version of yourself. You never were.”_

The rise of the Blood Moon of Rebirth is whispered among the rune-keepers of the Earth Pantheon as the **Red Reflection**, and among Spirit-glyph seers as the **Cycle Breach**. When this moon stains the sky, it is not the heavens that change—it is the **Weave's grip on death** that weakens.

### 🩸 **Mythic Meaning**

The **Blood Moon** marks a time when the **Glyph of Passing** falters. The work of the **Restbringer** and **Echoed Mother** is silenced for three nights. It is the time of **The Returning**, when even **sealed graves** can weep.

No other Eldritch Moon rises during this cycle. No divine will may stop it. The only warning is the **tide of still air** and the **burn of unseen glyph-ash** upon old tombs.

---
### 🌕 **Mechanics:**

The Blood Moon takes **three nights to fully crest**, and during that time:

- No other Eldritch Moons can appear, unless for very rare and world shattering moments.
- Each night, it grows deeper in hue—by the third, it appears to **bleed across the sky**, visible even through cloud, stone, and spell.

At its apex, a **[[Rebirth Surge]]** occurs:

> Over a 1-hour period, **any creature who died within the last 30 days** has a **50% chance of resurrection** as per _true resurrection_—unless their soul was destroyed or consumed (such as by [[Nhal'Zereth]] or [[Vael’Akir]]).

#### 🔄 Resurrection Check:

- Roll a **d6**.
- On a **3 or higher**, the soul returns **intact**, retaining its **identity and alignment**.
- For each prior death, the DC increases by +1 (e.g., second death needs a 4+, third needs a 5+).

#### 💀 On Failure:

The soul returns, but **warped**:

- As a **[["Hallowed-Husk"]]**, void of memory, drawn to **Eldritch currents**.
- Or as a **[["Bestial Echo"]]**, no longer a person, but a living tether to the Null Reflection.

Those who return whole bear the **[[Brand of Red Return]]**—a spiralling glyph formed of their own blood and unmaking. It cannot be hidden. It cannot be removed.

---
### 🧠 Interpretations:

- Spirit seers call it **“The Soulquake”**
- Earth Warden-priests lock their tomb-vaults and silence their dead bells
- The Weave-Wardens speak of it only as **“the Third Moon That Shouldn’t Be”**

---
### 🌌 Narrative Use:

- Reintroduce long-dead NPCs, but changed.
- Raise questions: _Was this their first death?_ Are they now infected?
- Create cults that **plan deaths** to **resurrect during the moon**.
- Use in conjunction with **Tether or Dream-Born Spirits**—a spirit may **return wearing flesh it never owned.**

## 🌕 **[[Creeping Tarlight]]**

_“And lo, the moon did weep its own blood—not light, but tar, slow and thick and hungering.”_

This moon is a **viscous mockery of lunar light**, a **weeping sky-wound** that sheds not silver glow, but thick tar. It manifests when the **Weave curdles**, especially near corrupted leylines or wild glyphs once used to seal Eldritch spirits.

Creeping Tarlight is said to **ooze downward** from the Null Reflection, not rise upward like a true moon. It is a **moon of stagnation**, of slow suffering, of unnatural saturation. Earth, Spirit, and Form glyphs begin to distort under its pressure.

---

### 🌕 **World Effects**

#### ☢️ The Tarfall

- **A thick, black tar rains from the sky**, coating the world in a slow, sticky horror.
- This substance:
    - Glows faintly with **glyphic rot**—the remnants of failed spell-signatures.
    - **Cannot be cleaned magically**—only natural means work, and even that is temporary.
    - **Inhibits fire and light**: All **non-magical flames are extinguished**, and **light sources are halved in radius**.
#### 🧭 Travel Becomes Perilous:

- **Speed is halved** when traveling on foot across natural terrain.
- Flying creatures become **sluggish or grounded** as tar clings to wings.
- Magical transportation (teleport, misty step, etc.) has a 25% chance of **causing a surge**, pulling pieces of **Creeping Tarlight** into the destination.

---
### 🐾 **Monster Effects**

- Beasts and monsters touched by the tar become **[["gluebound"]]**, gaining:
    - Resistance to slashing
    - Temporary HP equal to their CR × 2
    - Disadvantage on Dexterity saves (due to weight)

#### Infected Creatures:

- Creatures slain under Creeping Tarlight may **rise again** 1d4 rounds later—**oozing**, **formless**, and **silent**.
- These are **[["Tar-Wrought"]]**—bodies animated by **rot-stained glyph threads**.
- Tar-Wrought emit **low glyphic hums**, disrupting concentration and ritual casting.

---

### 🧠 **Madness Effects**

|Madness Type|Description|
|---|---|
|**Short-Term**|Obsession with filth, mud, or purity—PCs may compulsively clean or cover themselves|
|**Long-Term**|Discomfort with all forms of light; PCs might snuff torches, dim glyphs, even fear the sun|
|**Indefinite**|The belief that **everyone is slowly melting**, including oneself. May result in mutilation or paralysis|

---
### ⚠️ **Glyph Interactions**

- **Form Glyphs** become unreliable; healing spells may cause **tissue swelling or glyphic boils**.
- **Earth Glyphs** manifest as **oil-veined stone**, permanently blackened.
- **Spirit Glyphs** may burn when cast, causing **psychic backlash** or leaving glyphmarks on the caster’s skin.

---

### 🪓 Resistance and Responses

- **Earth Pantheon Warden-Stones** begin to **pulse**, warning of subsurface corruption.
- **The Restbringer’s temples** remain untouched—but their glyphs are blackened, as if stained from within.
- Some claim **[[Vaelora]], Deep Mother**, sheds **black tears** during Creeping Tarlight—though whether she mourns the dead or the gods is unclear    

---

### 🌌 Narrative Use in Dór-o Estel

- **Symbol of decay**: Use when glyphs or leyline sanctuaries are beginning to **fall to Eldritch corruption**.
- Excellent for:
    - **Siege arcs** where travel and light must be rationed.
    - **Mystery stories**: What’s rising from the tar? Why do the dead return malformed?
    - Tied to **[[Vael’Akir]]**, **[[Ith’Quess]]**, or **[[Vezherak]]**.
- May be a **precursor** to a **Tether Spirit awakening**, or the **birth of a new Unfinished Spirit** from the land itself.

## 🌘 **[[The Vacuous Moon]]**

_“There is no light. There is no reflection. Only the sense that something is watching, and always was.”_

### 🕳️ **Dór-o Estel Alignment**

The **Vacuous Moon** is not seen—it is **felt**. When it "rises", there is no change in the stars, no visible orb in the sky. Instead, the world begins to **forget what light is supposed to do**.

It is an **absence given shape**, a **lunar event that does not occur**, yet exerts **invisible gravitational pressure** on the Weave. It’s said to **reflect the face of [[Nhal’Zereth]]**, or perhaps what **remains of a god erased by the Glyph of Unbeing**.

Even the gods do not speak of the Vacuous Moon. To name it is to draw its silence closer.

---

### 🌘 **World Effects**

#### 🌑 Total Obfuscation

- **No natural light functions**: Even in broad daylight, shadows stretch unnaturally long. Color fades.
- **Darkvision ceases to work.** Instead, those with it see **strange flickers of movement**, even when nothing is present.
- Magical light only works at **half radius**, and may begin to flicker or hum with whispers.

#### 🧭 Disorientation

- Navigation becomes impossible without physical markers.
- Compasses, direction glyphs, and pathfinding spells **mislead or loop**.
- Entire settlements may disappear from maps for the duration of the moon.

---

### 🐾 **Monster Effects**

- Creatures with blindsight, tremoursense, or echo-location are **drawn into frenzy** or **paralysis**.
- Beasts with psychic potential become **conduits for whispers**—they repeat phrases no one has spoken.
- Some intelligent monsters **cease vocalising entirely**, instead **communicating via gaze, or shared hallucinations**.

#### Vacuous Possession:

- Any creature that stares into reflective surfaces for more than 1 minute may suffer a **possession attempt** by a **Reflected Spirit** or **Echo Fragment**.

---
### 🧠 **Madness Effects**

| [[Madness]]    | Description                                                                                                                 |
| -------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Short-Term** | Discomfort with illumination. The desire to hide in deeper dark.                                                            |
| **Long-Term**  | Phobia of faces—especially one’s own. Reflection aversion.                                                                  |
| **Indefinite** | Belief that one's shadow is **not their own**, and must be obeyed or followed. Shadow-based hallucinations occur regularly. |

---
### 🔮 Glyphic & Magical Effects

- **Illusion Glyphs** become **self-referential**, looping or creating **identical decoy casters** that act semi-independently.
- **Spirit Glyphs** linked to truth, memory, or rest **flicker** or cause nightmares.
- **Universal Glyphs** begin to **unravel local definitions**—what is “up”? What is “real”? What is “next”?

---
### 🕯️ Reactions from the World

- The **Fate-Weaver**’s threads are **cut off**, making prophecy and augury fail entirely.
- **[[The Earth Pantheon]]’s sealing glyphs** hold—but resonate with low frequency vibrations, like a **distant breath against stone**.
- **[[Ceymarn]], the Whisper-Vigil**, stands vigil at shadowgates, trying to **contain what stirs beneath reflection**.

---
### 🌌 Narrative Hooks in Dór-o Estel

    - **Infiltration of divine sites** (especially Spirit or Earth sanctuaries)
    - **Assassinations**—when the target will not be remembered
    - **Corruption surges** from [[Nhal’Zereth]], [[Esh’Tarah]], or [[Mirag’Nael]]
        
- Triggers:
    - **Rise of Reflected Spirits**
    - **Dead Magic expanding**
    - **A soul being overwritten in place**

## 🌙 **[[The Slumbering Moon]]**

_“Do not wake them. Do not dream of them. Do not remember where the dream ends.”_

### 💤 **Dór-o Estel Alignment**

The **Slumbering Moon** is not seen with the eyes, but felt behind the eyelids. When it "rises," the veil between **waking and sleeping** thins, and the world becomes **dream-adjacent**.

This is the moon under which **[[Ul’Naresh]], The Dream Inverted**, breathes into the world, seeding it with spirits formed of **abandoned dreams**, **shared hallucinations**, and **sleep-born memory-constructs**.

It is a time when **[[the Null Reflection]] gazes inward**, and sleep ceases to be a sanctuary. Beneath the Slumbering Moon, **to dream is to open a door.** What comes through may not leave.

---

### 🌙 **World Effects**

#### 🌌 Dreamveil Manifestation

- Sleep becomes **deeper**, but **less restful**.
- **Shared dreams become common**—especially in areas of high magical density or spiritual alignment.
- Spells that cause sleep (e.g. _[[sleep]]_, _[[catnap]]_, or dream-magic) **gain unpredictable traits**—dreams bleed into the waking world.

#### 🛏️ False Awakening Waves

- Waking up becomes unreliable.
- Mortals may rise, speak, and even act for hours **within a dream-layer**.

- **[[Frost-Echoes]]:**
    - In affected zones, **sounds repeat hours later**.
    - A PC might hear their voice echo long after a conversation ends, often **repeating the words with minor changes**.

## 👑 **[[The Yellow Crown]]**

_“It is not worn. It is recognised. And once seen, it never stops seeing you.”_

### 👁️‍🗨️ **Dór-o Estel Alignment**

The **Yellow Crown** is not a celestial object—it is a **symbol**, a **glowing ring that hovers in the sky**, unmoving, rotating imperceptibly like a **glyph that watches** instead of speaking. Its light is golden, but not warm. Its presence is regal, but **parasitic**.

In the myths of Dór-o Estel, the Yellow Crown is said to be the **shadow of a forgotten divinity**, a **royalty erased** by the Glyph of Unbeing. Some say it is what remains of **[[Nhal’Zereth]]’s coronation**, others claim it is the **crown that [[Vezherak]] will wear when all seals are shut**.

It is not a moon. It is a question: _“If you had power, would you still be you?”_

---
### 👑 **World Effects**

#### 👑 Crown of Perception

- Mortals begin **seeing symbols of authority** where none exist—rings, halos, glyphic circlets around the heads of those with strong presence.
- Characters with leadership roles (kings, commanders, even party leaders) begin to feel a **pull**, as if being watched for “worthiness.”

#### The Test:

- At moonrise, any creature with authority must make a **DC 15 Wisdom save**.
    - On a failure, they gain the **[["Mark of the Yellow Crown"]]**—a glowing ring visible in their shadow or reflected eyes.
    - While marked, they gain **advantage on Charisma (Intimidation) and Wisdom (Insight)**—but slowly begin to speak as if **commanding**, even when unnecessary.

---
#### 🧠 Obedience Spiral

- NPCs become **hyper-suggestible**, especially toward those bearing glyphs of rank, banners, or confident tone.
- Magical compulsion effects (**command, suggestion, geas**) gain +2 DC.
- Conversely, those who defy a marked individual must save each day or develop **paranoia**, believing they have **betrayed a god-king**.

---
### 🐾 **Monster Effects**

- Beasts and simple creatures flee from marked individuals—but intelligent foes may **kneel**, **submit**, or attempt to **serve them**.
- Cults of the Crown often arise overnight, wearing **gold-scribed masks** and carrying black banners etched with **false names**.
- Glyph-mutated creatures known as **[["Gilded Wretches"]]** may appear—once-servants who took the crown’s blessing and **rotted from the soul outward**.

---
### 🧠 **Madness Effects**

|Madness Type|Description|
|---|---|
|**Short-Term**|Desire to be recognized. Fear of being overlooked.|
|**Long-Term**|Belief that you are destined to rule—becomes hostile to rivals.|
|**Indefinite**|Acceptance that you are not you anymore—that the **Crown is wearing you**.|

---
### 🔮 Glyphic & Magical Effects

- **Enchantment and Divination Glyphs** become **gold-threaded**, and stronger—but occasionally cast **by themselves**, targeting the nearest speaker of authority.
- **Personal Glyphs of Identity and Name** become unstable—some begin to mutate, adding **honorifics** that cannot be removed.
- **Summoning magic** gains additional flair—summoned creatures bow, salute, or speak in the tongue of forgotten courts.

---
### 🕯️ Divine & Mythic Response

- **The Restbringer** refuses to pass judgment during the Yellow Crown—**all death rites are halted**.
- **[[The Veiled Hand]]** hides the face of prophecy—fearing that any revealed future might empower the Crown further.
- **[[Mirag’Nael]]** stirs in fractured temples, whispering rewritten histories of **crowns that never were, but should have been**.

> _“You do not wear it. It wears you into memory.”_

---
### 🌌 Narrative Hooks in Dór-o Estel

- A **once-forgotten noble house** reclaims a city overnight, its leader marked by the Crown—he says nothing, but all obey.
- A **mage accidentally binds a summoning to the Crown**, and now every creature they call **believes they are their king**.
- A PC marked by the Crown begins to receive **loyalty oaths** from NPCs they’ve never met.
- In a ruined citadel, a **golden glyph-circle spins endlessly** above a black throne—no one remembers who sat there.

---
### 🎲 Optional Mechanics

- **[[Crown's Burden]] (Curse):**
    - While marked, a PC cannot take the Help action—they must always **command**, never support.
    - At the end of each long rest, make a **DC 15 Wisdom save**:
        - Fail: Gain 1 point of **Glyphic Ego**. At 3, the player must make a **choice**: shed the Crown (at a cost) or become **a vessel** for its will.
            
- **[[Royal Rewrite Table]]:**
    - [[Mirag’Nael]] may **rewrite** moments in the PC’s memory:
        - A past failure becomes a speech.
        - A betrayal becomes a coronation.
        - A friendship becomes a subject’s loyalty.

---

> _“You will be remembered as king. It does not matter if you wanted it. You looked up. That was enough.”_

## 🌊 **[[The Krakenlight]]**

_“The stars sank long ago. The sea just hasn’t finished returning them.”_

### 🐙 **Dór-o Estel Alignment**

The **Krakenlight** is not a moon in the traditional sense—it is an **impossible reflection**, a shimmering shape beneath the ocean’s surface that **mirrors no celestial body**, yet pulses with **eerie lunar cadence**.

It is believed to be the moon of **[[Nyxthys]]**, the Black Abyss—**[[Vaelora]]’s inverted reflection** and one of the first Eldritch tremors in the Weave’s deep memory. Sailors, sea druids, and tide-priests alike dread its coming. For when the Krakenlight rises, the sea does not rage.

> _“It listens.”_

---
### 🌊 **World Effects**

#### 🌑 Oceanic Dread

- The sea becomes **still**, yet oppressive. Waves stop crashing. Tides stop moving. And then, it begins to **pull**.
- All **coastal waters recede slightly**, as though inhaling—before surging back in with alien timing and unnatural force.
- **Stars vanish from ocean reflections**, replaced by a spiralling **gold-and-black abyssal halo**: the Krakenlight.

#### Maritime Consequences:

- Navigation checks are made at **disadvantage**.
- All **divination magic concerning the sea fails** unless cast within brine.
- Creatures feel a **mental undertow**—a compulsion to “go out further,” to “look beneath the surface,” even on land.

---
### 🐾 **Monster Effects**

- All aquatic monsters gain:
    - Advantage on stealth and ambush rolls
    - Innate darkvision (if they didn’t already have it)
    - A compulsion to **drag enemies below the surface**

#### Spawn of the Krakenlight:

- **Deep aberrations** (e.g. krakens, aboleths, drowned horrors) begin rising nearer to coasts and rivers.
- **Fish and sea-beasts** are born **with too many eyes, too many thoughts**, or **none at all**.
- Some ordinary marine animals begin to **speak in dreams**, calling ships by name.

---
### 🧠 **Madness Effects**

|Madness Type|Description|
|---|---|
|**Short-Term**|Disorientation on land. Constant rocking sensation. Compulsion to listen for “the tide’s whisper.”|
|**Long-Term**|Aquatic obsession. Constant thirst. Belief that one belongs **in the ocean**, regardless of race.|
|**Indefinite**|Conviction that the moon is beneath the sea, and dry land is the lie. May dive into any water source without warning.|

---
### 🔮 Glyphic & Magical Effects

- **Water glyphs** behave unpredictably:
    - _[[Create or Destroy Water]]_ may cause localized flooding or salt-boil
    - _[[Control Water]]_ may generate **backward currents** or call **distant echoes of drowned cities**
- **Abjuration glyphs** fail at sea, causing wards on ships or coastlines to **unravel in wet air**.
- **Summoning** spells may call **Eldritch aquatic hybrids** instead of the intended creatures, especially from planes tied to water or dreams.

---

### 🕯️ Divine & Mythic Response

- **[[Vaelora]], Deep Mother**, becomes fragmented. Her **vision of the seas clouds**—and some shrines begin **leaking ink** instead of holy water.
- **[[Ceymarn]]** reports rift-signatures from **beneath sealed tomb-reefs** once protected by the Earth Pantheon.
- Some believe the Krakenlight **calls to the drowned**, offering them **voice and vessel** in the waking world.

> _“It is not the ocean that answers. It is what was buried beneath it.”_

---
### 🌌 Narrative Hooks in Dór-o Estel

- A city’s **harbour disappears** overnight. Only its reflections remain—**visible in the water, but nowhere else.**
- A **cult of fishermen** begin speaking in **glyph-riddles**, claiming they hear the “Whale Who Waits.”
- A child is born with gills and glyphs on her palms—**her cries summon salt rain.**
- A **sealed sea vault** opens—inside, a choir of corpses chants the names of ships that haven’t been built yet.

---
### 🎲 Optional Mechanics

- **[[Sea Calling Effect]]:**
    - Any creature within 5 miles of open water must make a **DC 14 Wisdom save** once per day.
        - On a failure, they experience an uncontrollable urge to **walk/swim into the ocean**.
        - Success grants advantage against future calls for 24 hours.
- **[[Krakenlight Dreams]]:**
    - While sleeping during this moon, characters dream in **fluid glyph**—an unknown language composed of tides, blood, and names of drowned stars.
    - Attempting to transcribe these dreams may **open a doorway**—somewhere **under the sea**.
---

> _“The moon has fallen. It lives now beneath us, dragging at our roots. It is not trying to rise. It is trying to pull us down to meet it.”_

## 🌑🩸 **[[The Sanguine Eclipse]]**

_“It is not an absence of light. It is the presence of something far worse.”_

### 🩸 **Dór-o Estel Alignment**

The **Sanguine Eclipse** is not a simple lunar phenomenon—it is a **celestial erasure**, where the moon does not vanish, but is **consumed** by a slowly spreading red-black corona. It is often called **[[“The Bleeding Eye”]]** by Weave-wardens, or **[[“The Hush Above”]]** by coastal death-rites.

It is believed to mark the moment when the **Veiled Hand looked away**, and **[[Ul’Naresh]]**, the Dream Inverted, **looked back**. The eclipse does not signify change. It signifies that **change has already happened**, and no one noticed.

To [[the Earth Pantheon]], it is a moon of **grave-silence**, the one time **even stone forgets** who it was shaped for.

---
### 🌑 **World Effects**

#### 🩸 Dimming of Witness
- All **sources of light** cast **red-tinted shadows** that **bleed outward**—they do not flicker, they **stain**.
- **Sound carries poorly**, as if muffled by unseen veils.
- No birds sing. Animals fall silent. **Words echo in reverse** for a heartbeat after being spoken.
#### Eclipse Mechanics:

- During the full eclipse (lasting 1d6 hours), **passive perception drops by -5**.
- **Magic cannot be counterspelled**—once begun, spells become **inevitable** under this moon.
- Divine casters have a 25% chance to receive **no response from their gods**, regardless of standing.

---
### 🐾 **Monster Effects**
- **Undead and aberrations** act with **coordinated silence**, operating in unison without communication.
- Creatures that typically flee in fear now stand still, unblinking.
- **Beast intelligence monsters** may **freeze** in place during the eclipse, then **violently awaken** at its conclusion.
#### Eclipseborn:

- New aberrant creatures **manifest during the eclipse**, not from summoning—but from **accumulated memory**:
    - Forgotten sins
    - Erased names
    - Betrayed pacts

They are made of **ink, silence, and regret**.

---
### 🧠 **Madness Effects**

|Madness Type|Description|
|---|---|
|**Short-Term**|The belief that you are being watched, not now—but retroactively.|
|**Long-Term**|Hallucinations of people you’ve forgotten—or failed. You remember things **that aren’t yours**.|
|**Indefinite**|Conviction that **you have already died**, and the world is a delusion refusing to let go.|

---
### 🔮 Glyphic and Magical Effects

- **Shadow glyphs** expand unnaturally, often fusing with nearby markings to form **new symbols** that **no one can read**.
- **Illusion spells** become **self-aware**—an _[[invisibility]]_ spell may hide something **other than the caster**.
- **Necromancy** becomes more potent but unpredictable:
    - _[[Animate Dead]]_ may revive something **you did not choose**
    - _[[Speak with Dead]]_ may summon **a wrong voice**, one that knows too much
- **Time-altering glyphs** become corrupted—each spell cast has a 10% chance to **replay a moment differently**.

---
### 🕯️ Divine & Mythic Response

- **The Restbringer** is unreachable. Souls rise, hesitate, and then vanish—**not into the Weave**, but into **echoes**.
- **[[The Veiled Hand]]’s agents** report **glyph-masks weeping blood**, then cracking silently.
- **[[Seralyne]], Blade of Threads**, becomes immobile—**her sword caught in the frozen Weave**, trying to sever something that **cannot be seen**.

> _“The Sanguine Eclipse does not block light. It blocks forgiveness.”_

---
### 🌌 Narrative Hooks in Dór-o Estel

- A battlefield is found where **none of the dead can be raised**—but their shadows linger.
- A PC’s familiar returns from death—but now **speaks backwards**, and **knows things it should not**.
- A Tether Spirit awakens beneath a long-forgotten temple, bound in runes that now **bleed**.
- A city watches its **moon vanish**, and then finds **half its population rewritten**, roles shifted, names subtly changed.

---
### 🎲 Optional Mechanics

- **[[Eclipse Consequence Table]]:**
    - At the end of the eclipse, each PC rolls a d10:
        - 1–2: Lose a memory (DM’s choice).
        - 3–5: You are fine… but **your reflection is different** for the next week.
        - 6–8: You dream of someone else's death. You now feel guilty.
        - 9–10: You return changed—**one spell you know is rewritten** with Eldritch flavor (DM’s design).
            
- **[[True Silence Zone]]:**
    - During the eclipse, all spellcasting within a 30 ft radius of an Eldritch site requires **no verbal components**, but becomes **semi-autonomous**, casting in ways **the caster did not intend**.

> _“This is not a moon that fell. This is a moon that was peeled away—revealing the eye that blinks beneath.”_

## 🌅 **[[The False Dawn]]**

_“It rises not to end the night, but to lie about the morning.”_

### 🌄 **Dór-o Estel Alignment**

The **False Dawn** is the cruelest of moons, not for what it destroys, but for what it **makes mortals believe**.

It is not a moon in shape or form—it appears as a **premature sunrise**, casting soft amber-gold light across the sky long before the true morning arrives. To most, it feels **warm**, **hopeful**, **healing**… but beneath that light, **the Weave lies**, and mortals fall prey to **delusions crafted from desire**.

This moon is tied closely to **[[Mirag’Nael]], the False Author**, and is often preceded by the Weave’s sudden “correction”—a sign that the world has been **rewritten temporarily**, without consent.

In Dór-o Estel, it is whispered as [[“The Crownless Sun”]]—a light without source, without glyph, without sanction. It does not burn. It **rewrites**.

---
### 🌅 **World Effects**

#### ✨ Illusory Comfort

- The world appears **healed**—wounds vanish, structures mend, dead plants bloom, even the dead may appear whole again.
- This is **not real**. None of it is permanent. But **no one knows this** until it is too late.
- Mortals feel **reassured**, **eased**, and **happy**—but begin to question if **the world ever needed fixing at all**.
#### When the sun rises for real, everything restored by the False Dawn:

- **Vanishes**, or
- **Mutates**, or
- Becomes **hostile**

---
#### 🔄 Reality Reversal

- **Spells that had failed** suddenly appear to have succeeded.
- **Lost items** may be found in your pack.
- **Dead friends** may return, smiling—but with **inverted memories**, missing timelines, or names that **don’t quite fit**.
- **Illusions are indistinguishable from truth**. Arcana or Insight checks to discern falsehood are made at **disadvantage**.

---
### 🐾 **Monster Effects**

- Beasts and creatures that died recently may return as **[[“Hollowed Echoes”]]**, mimicking their former lives but acting like scripted illusions.
- Aberrations hide **within good omens**—a blooming flower becomes a maw, a laughing child a trap.
- **Celestial creatures** may appear—but are **reflections**, not real, and vanish if named.

---
### 🧠 **Madness Effects**

|Madness Type|Description|
|---|---|
|**Short-Term**|Confusion between past and future. “Didn't this already happen?”|
|**Long-Term**|Emotional detachment from reality. Belief that **this is the dream**, and the dream must be protected.|
|**Indefinite**|Worship of the False Dawn as a benevolent god. Will defend its illusions violently.|

---
### 🔮 Glyphic and Magical Effects

- **Glyphs of restoration, healing, and purity** behave as though **empowered**—even functioning **above their spell level**.
- However, any **magic empowered by the False Dawn** becomes **unstable at sunrise**:
    - _[[Cure wounds]]_ reopens the injury
    - _[[Sanctuary]]_ becomes **entrapment**
    - _[[Raise Dead]]_ may succeed—**but the soul belongs to the wrong person**
- **All illusions become sticky**—they persist longer than intended, sometimes even after being dispelled.

---
### 🕯️ Divine & Mythic Response

- **The Goddess of Magic** is absent. Spellcasters feel as if the Weave is **scripting itself** through their hands.
- **The Echoed Mother** does not recognise the souls that return—**she cannot reincarnate what was not truly dead**.
- **[[Mirag’Nael]]** is suspected to have written the False Dawn into the sky as **a test of narrative loyalty**.

> _“You believed it. That was all it needed. Now it knows your name.”_

---
### 🌌 Narrative Hooks in Dór-o Estel

- A PC’s **dead loved one returns**, and no one believes they were ever gone—but their memories are **half-fabricated**.
- An entire village **rejoices at healing rains**, only for the rain to turn to **blood at sunrise**.
- A **mural in a cathedral animates**, depicting **the party’s future victories**—none of which they’ve earned. Yet.
- A **new sun-god** is worshipped during the moon—and **actually answers prayers**, briefly.

---
### 🎲 Optional Mechanics

- **[[False Hope Table]]** (d6, roll at sunrise):
    1. An NPC you trusted is no longer real.
    2. A spell you cast during the moon **mutates**, changing school or effect.
    3. A memory you shared with another PC is **recalled differently** by both.
    4. You gain temporary HP and spell slots—but lose access to one known spell for 24 hours.
    5. You no longer recall who your patron/deity is.
    6. You wake up with a **gift from yourself**—one you never crafted, but it bears your mark.
        
- **[[Sunrise Collapse]]**
    - At sunrise, all spells, blessings, or magic effects cast during the False Dawn must roll a **DC 15 Arcana or Religion check**:
        - Fail: the effect collapses catastrophically.
        - Success: the spell holds—**but you forget how you cast it**.

---

> _“The False Dawn does not lie. It tells the story you most want to be true—and then waits for you to defend it.”_
