# Transformation Systems - Grim Hollow Integration
## FEARS Adaptation

> *"Power demands a price. The question is whether you pay it in coin, corruption, or your very soul."*

---

## Overview

Transformations represent fundamental changes to your character's nature—becoming vampire, werewolf, angel, or something far stranger. Unlike Corruption (which is always a threat), transformations are **optional paths** that grant immense power at terrible cost.

**Key Principles:**
- Transformations are **permanent** once begun
- Each has **4 progressive stages** with increasing power and drawbacks
- Some transformations **replace** Terminal Corruption outcomes
- Others exist **independently** of the Corruption system
- All transformations fundamentally alter how your character interacts with society

---

## Integration with FEARS Systems

### Transformation vs. Corruption

**Three Models:**

1. **Corruption Replacement** (Fiend, Aberrant Horror, Spectre)
   - Triggers at CS 15-20 as alternative to character loss
   - Locks Corruption at current level
   - Prevents further CS gain but applies transformation drawbacks
   - "Deal with the devil" option

2. **Anti-Corruption Path** (Seraph)
   - Requires LOW Corruption (CS 0-9 max)
   - Each stage *reduces* Corruption permanently
   - Incompatible with corrupted characters
   - Divine intervention option

3. **Independent System** (Lycanthropy, Vampirism, Lich, Fey)
   - Can occur at any Corruption level
   - Has unique triggers (curse, disease, ritual)
   - Interacts with but doesn't replace Corruption
   - Creates complex dual-system characters

### Mechanical Impact

**All transformations affect:**
- **Mental Attributes**: Sanity/Focus modifiers change
- **DCS Integration**: Evasion profiles, DR, special combat abilities
- **Social Interaction**: NPCs react differently based on transformation
- **Equipment**: Some transformations restrict armor/weapons
- **Class Features**: May enhance or restrict class abilities

# 1. SERAPH (Angelic Ascension)

**Type:** Anti-Corruption Path  
**Trigger:** Divine intervention, celestial blessing, heroic sacrifice  
**Prerequisite:** CS 0-9 maximum (cannot be corrupted)  
**Irreversible:** Yes

## Philosophy

Seraph transformation represents **divine purification**—the Pattern itself reaching down to elevate a hero beyond mortal limits. This is the rarest transformation, granted only to those who resist Corruption while facing impossible odds.

Unlike other transformations that corrupt or curse, Seraph transformation **purifies**. Each stage strips away Corruption, strengthens mental resolve, and grants celestial power. The cost is freedom—you become bound to divine purpose.

## Corruption Relationship

**Inverse System:**
- Each stage *reduces* Corruption permanently
- Gain resistance (then immunity) to future Corruption
- **Critical Rule**: If you gain CS 5+ after transformation, you **lose all Seraph powers** until Corruption is cleansed
- Cannot pursue Seraph transformation if CS 10+ (too corrupted)

## Seraph Stages

---

### Stage 1: Touched by Grace

**Prerequisites:**
- Must have performed significant heroic act
- CS 0-9
- Divine entity chooses to bless you
- OR: Resist Terminal Corruption through epic quest

**Transformation Process:**
- Celestial vision or visitation
- 7-day ritual of fasting and prayer
- Permanent mark appears (halo, glowing eyes, wing-shaped birthmark)
- Immediate CS reduction

**Boons:**
- **Darkvision** 60 feet
- **Celestial Resistance**: Resistance to radiant and necrotic damage
- **Divine Clarity**: +2 to Sanity and Focus ability scores
- **Corruption Reduction**: Reduce CS by 5 (minimum 0)
- **Corruption Resistance**: Advantage on saves that would grant Corruption
- **Healing Hands** (if don't have it): Heal 2d6 HP, 1/long rest

**Flaws:**
- **Honorable Conduct**: Must act honorably; lying, breaking oaths, or abandoning innocents causes loss of all Seraph powers for 24 hours
- **Visible Mark**: Your celestial nature is obvious to those who look closely (Insight DC 10)
- **Fiend Attention**: Devils and demons can sense your nature within 60 feet

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity +2 (divine peace)
- Focus +2 (celestial clarity)

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +0
- Endure Evasion: +1 (divine resilience)
- Deflect Evasion: +0
- Natural DR: +0

**Progression Trigger:**
- Perform act of extraordinary heroism (save 100+ lives, defeat major evil)
- OR: Complete divine quest assigned by celestial patron
- AND: Maintain CS 0-4 for minimum 1 month

---

### Stage 2: Exalted Form

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 1 active for minimum 1 month
- Completed heroic deed or divine quest
- CS still 0-9

**Transformation Process:**
- Wings manifest (spectral or feathered)
- Eyes glow with inner light
- Voice carries celestial resonance
- Immediate additional CS reduction

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 1 Boons**
- **Flight**: 30 feet (spectral wings manifest at will)
- **Enhanced Clarity**: +4 to Sanity and Focus (replaces +2)
- **Greater Corruption Reduction**: Reduce CS by additional 5 (total -10 from start)
- **Mental Fortitude**: Advantage on ALL Sanity and Focus saves
- **Lesser Restoration**: Can cast 1/day
- **Radiant Strike**: Weapon attacks deal +1d6 radiant damage
- **Ability Increase**: +2 Charisma

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 1 Flaws**
- **Radiant Aura**: Shed dim light in 10-foot radius (cannot hide in darkness)
- **Fiend Hostility**: Fiends and undead become immediately hostile on sight
- **Divine Compulsion**: Must help those in need when encountered (Wisdom save DC 15 to resist)
- **Light Dependency**: Lose 1d4 temporary HP per hour in complete darkness

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity +4 (replaces +2)
- Focus +4 (replaces +2)

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +1 (flight provides mobility)
- Endure Evasion: +2 (divine toughness)
- Deflect Evasion: +1 (wing deflection)
- Natural DR: +1 (celestial resilience)

**Progression Trigger:**
- Defeat CR 15+ fiend or undead
- OR: Prevent apocalyptic event
- AND: Maintain CS 0 for minimum 3 months

---

### Stage 3: Ascendant Angel

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 2 active for minimum 3 months
- Completed world-changing heroic deed
- CS locked at 0

**Transformation Process:**
- Physical form becomes partially ethereal
- Wings become permanent (cannot be dismissed)
- Halo manifests visibly
- Divine language known instinctively
- Perfect mental state achieved

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 2 Boons**
- **Flight**: 60 feet (permanent wings)
- **Perfect Mind**: Sanity and Focus both locked at 20 (cannot be reduced)
- **Corruption Immunity**: Completely immune to gaining Corruption Score
- **Greater Restoration**: Can cast 1/day
- **Resurrection**: Can cast 1/week (requires 8-hour ritual)
- **Ability Increase**: +4 Charisma, +4 Wisdom (replaces +2 Cha)
- **Immunity**: Charm, fear, and disease effects
- **Regeneration**: 5 HP per round while CS remains 0
- **Truesight**: 30 feet

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 2 Flaws**
- **Cannot Lie**: Physically incapable of speaking falsehood
- **Compulsive Protection**: MUST protect innocents; witnessing harm without intervening causes 2d10 psychic damage
- **Ethereal Form**: Difficult to wear heavy armor (disadvantage on Stealth even in light armor)
- **Divine Binding**: If you commit evil act, transformation **reverses entirely** (lose all powers, become mortal, gain +10 CS immediately)
- **Mortal Rejection**: Some mortals fear/worship you; social interactions complicated

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: Locked at 20 (perfect divine peace)
- Focus: Locked at 20 (celestial perfection)

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +2 (perfect aerial mobility)
- Endure Evasion: +4 (near-invulnerable to harm)
- Deflect Evasion: +2 (wing defense)
- Natural DR: +3 (divine armor)
- Regeneration: 5 HP/round

**Progression Trigger:**
- N/A - This is final stage
- May ascend to true celestial (become NPC) if DM agrees

---

### Stage 4: Celestial Avatar (Optional NPC Transition)

**This is not a playable stage**—it represents the character becoming a true celestial being and leaving mortal concerns behind.

**Possible if:**
- Stage 3 maintained for 1+ year
- Prevented world-ending catastrophe
- Divine patron offers transcendence

**Effect:**
- Character becomes NPC under DM control
- Becomes recurring ally/patron for party
- May return for critical moments
- Player rolls new character

## Corruption Interaction Rules

**If CS increases while Seraph:**

| New CS | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| 1-4 | No effect; still pure |
| 5-9 | Lose all Seraph powers for 24 hours per CS point gained; must cleanse within 1 week or transformation ends |
| 10+ | Transformation **immediately ends**; lose all powers permanently; return to mortal form with full Corruption intact |

**Cleansing Corrupted Seraph:**
- Greater Restoration + 3-day ritual at holy site
- Removes 1d4 CS
- Can only attempt once per month
- If CS reaches 10+, transformation lost permanently

---

## Thematic Integration with Dór-o Estel

**Faith of the Bound Pattern:**
- Seraph transformation seen as **Pattern's Blessing**
- Extremely rare; only 3-4 known Seraphs alive
- Treated as living saints by faithful
- Hunted by Eldritch cults (your purified nature disrupts their work)

**Loom-Cities:**
- Most cities have laws protecting Seraphs
- Some require registration (feared as too powerful)
- Noble houses seek to recruit/marry Seraphs for prestige

**Aasimar Relationship:**
- Aasimar can become Seraph more easily (affinity with celestial)
- Fallen Aasimar CANNOT (Necrotic Shroud incompatible)
- Protector/Scourge Aasimar get advantage on progression triggers

---

## Risk/Reward Summary

**Rewards:**
- Escape Corruption entirely
- Massive mental resilience (perfect mind at Stage 3)
- Flight, healing, resurrection powers
- Immunity to charm/fear/disease
- Regeneration
- Social prestige among good factions

**Risks:**
- Behavioral restrictions (honor, truth, protection compulsions)
- Cannot hide or operate in shadows
- Fiends/undead immediately hostile
- Loss of all powers if corrupted
- May lose character to NPC status
- Difficult social interactions

**Balance Assessment:**
- **VERY HARD** to achieve (requires divine intervention)
- **POWERFUL** but restrictive
- Best for lawful good paladins/clerics
- Poor choice for rogues/warlocks
- Locks you into "hero" role permanently

# 2. VAMPIRISM (The Blood Curse)

**Type:** Independent System  
**Trigger:** Vampire bite + death, dark ritual, ancient curse  
**Prerequisite:** None (can occur at any CS)  
**Irreversible:** Yes (except Wish or divine intervention)

## Philosophy

Vampirism is **predatory immortality**—trading your humanity for eternal unlife, superhuman power, and an insatiable hunger. Unlike Corruption (which degrades), vampirism **replaces** your nature entirely.

Vampires in Dór-o Estel are not romantic figures—they are **apex predators** wearing humanoid masks, forever separated from Pattern-woven life by their need to consume it.

## Corruption Relationship

**Parallel System:**
- Vampirism does NOT reduce or increase Corruption
- Can be vampire at CS 0 (freshly turned) or CS 20 (corrupted vampire)
- **High CS vampires** are nightmare monsters (corruption + vampiric power)
- **Low CS vampires** retain more humanity

**Special Rule:** Vampires at CS 15+ suffer additional madness:
- Blood frenzy becomes uncontrollable
- May attack allies when hungry
- Eldritch corruption manifests in vampiric form (tentacles, extra mouths, etc.)

## Vampirism Stages

---

### Stage 1: Fledgling (The First Night)

**Prerequisites:**
- Killed by vampire while blood was drained
- OR: Willingly underwent vampiric ritual
- Body must be buried/hidden for 24 hours

**Transformation Process:**
- Awaken at sunset as undead
- All mortality scores (Sanity, Focus, HP) reset
- Hunger immediately present

**Boons:**
- **Undead Nature**: No longer need to breathe, eat (food), or sleep
- **Darkvision**: 60 feet
- **Enhanced Attributes**: +2 Dexterity
- **Vampiric Bite**: Natural weapon (1d6 piercing + 2d6 necrotic); heal HP equal to necrotic damage dealt
- **Spider Climb**: Climb walls/ceilings at walking speed
- **Blood Sense**: Detect bloodied creatures within 30 feet through walls
- **Undead Resilience**: Resistance to necrotic damage

**Flaws:**
- **Sunlight Sensitivity**: Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks in direct sunlight; take 1d6 radiant damage per round of exposure
- **Blood Dependency**: Must feed on humanoid blood 1/week or suffer 1 exhaustion level per day without
- **Forbidden Threshold**: Cannot enter residence without invitation
- **Running Water**: Take 2d6 acid damage if end turn in running water
- **Stake Vulnerability**: If staked through heart while unconscious, instantly destroyed
- **No Reflection**: Don't appear in mirrors; easily identified

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -2 (predatory instincts replace empathy)
- Focus: +0 (unchanged)
- **New**: Roll on Minor Madness table (blood hunger creates obsession)

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +1 (supernatural reflexes)
- Endure Evasion: +0
- Deflect Evasion: +0
- Natural DR: +0

**Feeding Mechanics:**
- Must drain blood from living humanoid
- Takes 1 minute of feeding (victim restrained)
- Victim loses 2d6 HP per minute
- If reduced to 0 HP while feeding: victim dies, make Sanity save DC 15 or gain +1 CS (murder)
- Can feed from willing victims without killing (10 HP drain satisfies 1 week)

**Progression Trigger:**
- Kill 10+ humanoids through feeding
- OR: Survive 6 months as fledgling
- OR: Complete task for vampire sire

---

### Stage 2: Vampire Spawn (True Undeath)

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 1 active for 6+ months
- Killed at least 10 humanoids through feeding (or equivalent evil acts)
- OR: Willingly embrace vampiric nature (dark ritual)

**Transformation Process:**
- Body becomes fully supernatural
- Heart stops beating entirely
- Eyes glow red in darkness
- Fangs become retractable

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 1 Boons**
- **Enhanced Attributes**: +2 Dexterity, +2 Charisma (replaces +2 Dex)
- **Vampiric Regeneration**: Regenerate 10 HP per round (stops if take radiant or fire damage)
- **Misty Escape**: When reduced to 0 HP, transform into mist (as *Gaseous Form* spell) instead of dying; must reach coffin/lair within 2 hours or destroyed
- **Charm**: Can cast *Charm Person* at will (DC 8 + prof + Cha)
- **Shapechanger**: Can transform into bat or wolf (as polymorph) 1/day
- **Blood Frenzy**: When bloodied creature within 30 feet, advantage on melee attacks against it

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 1 Flaws (Enhanced)**
- **Sunlight Vulnerability**: Take 20 radiant damage per round in direct sunlight; all saves auto-fail
- **Blood Dependency**: Must feed every 3 days (not weekly)
- **Lair Dependency**: Must rest in coffin/lair with soil from birthplace
- **Symbol Weakness**: Holy symbols wielded by faithful cause disadvantage on attacks within 10 feet

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -4 (humanity fading rapidly)
- Focus: +2 (predatory focus intensifies)
- Roll on Major Madness table (permanent personality change)

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +2 (supernaturally fast)
- Endure Evasion: +1 (regeneration)
- Deflect Evasion: +0
- Natural DR: +1 (undead resilience)
- Regeneration: 10 HP/round

**Social Impact:**
- Most mortals instinctively fear you (Intimidation advantage, Persuasion disadvantage)
- Clerics detect you as undead
- Banned from temples, many cities
- Hunted by vampire hunters

**Progression Trigger:**
- Kill 50+ humanoids through feeding
- OR: Create 5+ vampire spawn of your own
- OR: Complete century as vampire spawn
- OR: Kill your vampire sire and drink their blood

---

### Stage 3: True Vampire (Lordly Power)

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 2 active for 1+ year
- Killed 50+ humanoids
- OR: Destroyed your sire

**Transformation Process:**
- Become free-willed (no longer bound to sire)
- Establish domain/lair
- Physical form perfects (beauty or terror)

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 2 Boons**
- **Enhanced Attributes**: +4 Dexterity, +4 Charisma (replaces +2/+2)
- **Superior Regeneration**: 15 HP per round
- **Legendary Resistance** (3/day): When fail save, can choose to succeed instead
- **Lair Actions**: In your lair, can use lair action on initiative 20:
  - Summon 2d4 bats/rats/wolves
  - Create area of magical darkness (60ft radius)
  - Gaze causes charm (all within 30ft, Cha save DC 17)
- **Children of the Night**: Can summon 3d6 wolves, 5d6 bats, or 3d6 rats (arrive in 1d4 rounds)
- **Dominate**: Can cast *Dominate Person* 3/day (DC 17)
- **Spider Climb**: No concentration required

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 2 Flaws (Enhanced)**
- **Sunlight Destruction**: 30 radiant damage per round in sunlight
- **Blood Dependency**: Must feed nightly
- **True Death**: If destroyed outside lair, regenerate in coffin in 24 hours UNLESS staked or decapitated
- **Compulsive Invitation**: If invited into home, MUST enter (compulsion)

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -6 (barely recognizable as former self)
- Focus: +4 (apex predator)
- Alignment shifts to Evil (if not already)
- Roll on Critical Madness table

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +4 (nearly impossible to hit)
- Endure Evasion: +2 (legendary resilience)
- Deflect Evasion: +1
- Natural DR: +2
- Regeneration: 15 HP/round
- Legendary Actions: 3/round

**Social Impact:**
- Cannot function in normal society
- Nobles/criminals may negotiate with you (powerful but feared)
- Most cities have kill-on-sight orders
- May rule hidden vampire court

**Vampire Spawn Creation:**
- Can create vampire spawn from victims killed by feeding
- Spawn are bound to your will
- Each spawn created: roll on Corruption table (creating undead is corrupting)

**Progression Trigger:**
- Survive 5+ years as True Vampire
- AND achieve one:
  - Kill 500+ humanoids
  - Rule city/region in secret
  - Destroy rival vampire lord
  - Complete dark apotheosis ritual

---

### Stage 4: Vampire Lord (Undead Demigod)

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 3 active for 5+ years
- Ruled territory or achieved legendary status
- Performed apotheosis ritual

**Transformation Process:**
- Become near-mythical being
- Physical form customizable (appear young, old, beautiful, monstrous)
- Lair becomes supernaturally connected to you

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 3 Boons**
- **Enhanced Attributes**: +6 Dexterity, +6 Charisma, +4 Intelligence
- **Perfect Regeneration**: 20 HP per round; can regenerate from single drop of blood in lair
- **Legendary Resistance**: 5/day (replaces 3/day)
- **True Shapechanger**: Can assume any humanoid form (including specific people)
- **Mass Charm**: Can charm entire room (everyone within 60ft, Cha save DC 20)
- **Summon Greater Children**: Can summon vampire spawn (2d4), wights (1d6), or specters (1d4)
- **Immortality**: If destroyed, regenerates in lair after 1d10 days unless lair destroyed

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 3 Flaws**
- **Lair Binding**: If lair destroyed, cannot regenerate (true death)
- **Sunlight Annihilation**: 50 radiant damage per round in sunlight; instant destruction if exposed for 3 consecutive rounds
- **Blood Dependency**: Must feed from sapient creature nightly (animals no longer sustain)
- **Corruption Magnet**: Gain +1 CS per year automatically (dark power accumulates)

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -8 (inhuman)
- Focus: +6 (terrifying will)
- Personality unrecognizable from original
- May become NPC if DM determines too monstrous

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +6
- Endure Evasion: +4
- Deflect Evasion: +2
- Natural DR: +4
- Regeneration: 20 HP/round
- Legendary Actions: 5/round
- Lair Actions: Can use 3 simultaneously

**Campaign Impact:**
- Become major antagonist or dark patron
- Control vampire courts, criminal networks
- May achieve immortality goals (build empire, seek godhood)
- Relationship with party determines if NPC or playable

## Corruption Interaction Examples

**Example 1: Low-Corruption Vampire**
```
Stage 2 Vampire, CS 3
- Still has some humanity
- Feeds reluctantly (willing victims or criminals)
- Struggles with predatory urges
- Can pass for mortal in shadows
```

**Example 2: High-Corruption Vampire**
```
Stage 2 Vampire, CS 17 (Eldritch Corruption)
- All mental saves auto-fail
- Blood frenzy uncontrollable
- Physical mutations (tentacles from back, extra mouths)
- Cannot distinguish allies from prey when hungry
- Probably should become NPC (too dangerous)
```

**Example 3: Vampire Lord at Terminal Corruption**
```
Stage 4 Vampire, CS 25+
- Becoming Eldritch abomination
- Vampire powers + Eldritch abilities
- Lair becomes pocket dimension
- Definitively NPC territory
```

## Special Rules

**Feeding & Mortality:**
- Killing through feeding: Sanity save DC 15 or gain +1 CS
- Feeding from unwilling victims: -1 Sanity per victim
- Creating vampire spawn: +1d4 CS per spawn (creating undead corrupts)

**Vampire Hunters:**
- Many organizations hunt vampires
- Faith of the Bound Pattern considers vampires "Pattern-breakers" (executed on sight)
- Some cities offer bounties

**Cure/Reversal:**
- **Wish** spell can restore mortality (before Stage 3)
- Divine intervention (Seraph, powerful cleric)
- Destroying vampire sire MAY break curse (DM discretion, only works at Stage 1)
- After Stage 3: permanent

# 3. LYCANTHROPY (The Beast Curse)

**Type:** Independent System  
**Trigger:** Werewolf bite, curse, ancient bloodline  
**Prerequisite:** None  
**Irreversible:** Difficult (Remove Curse works at Stage 1 only)

## Philosophy

Lycanthropy is **uncontrolled duality**—a beast sleeping within you, emerging under the moon or in moments of rage. Unlike vampirism (which replaces humanity), lycanthropy **adds** a second nature that wars with the first.

In Dór-o Estel, lycanthropes are tragic figures—neither fully beast nor human, struggling to maintain control while society fears and rejects them.

## Corruption Relationship

**Parallel System:**
- Lycanthropy doesn't cause or reduce Corruption
- **High-CS lycanthropes** lose control more easily
- At CS 15+: beast form becomes dominant (Sanity saves to avoid transforming)

**Special Rule:**
- Transforming while CS 10+ has 50% chance of permanent beast form
- CS 20+ lycanthropes are basically feral monsters

## Lycanthrope Types

Choose one when cursed:

- **Werewolf** (most common): +Str/Con, aggressive, pack mentality
- **Werebear**: +Str/Con/Wis, protective, solitary
- **Wereboar**: +Str/Con, stubborn, charges enemies
- **Weretiger**: +Str/Dex, cunning, ambush predator
- **Wererat**: +Dex/Wis, sneaky, disease carrier

*Stats below use Werewolf as baseline; adjust for other types*

---

### Stage 1: Afflicted (The First Moon)

**Prerequisites:**
- Bitten by lycanthrope and survived
- OR: Born into lycanthrope bloodline
- OR: Cursed by druid/hag

**Transformation Process:**
- Curse takes hold during first full moon
- Transformation is involuntary and painful
- No memory of actions while transformed (first time)

**Boons:**
- **Keen Senses**: Advantage on Perception checks using smell/hearing
- **Natural Weapons** (humanoid form): Claws/bite (1d6 slashing/piercing)
- **Hybrid Form** (during full moon, involuntary):
  - +4 Strength, +2 Constitution (replaces normal scores)
  - AC = 10 + Dex + Con (minimum 12)
  - Natural weapons: claws (1d8), bite (1d10)
  - Speed increases by 10 feet
  - Darkvision 60 feet
  - Immunity to nonmagical weapon damage
  - Duration: Until dawn or knocked unconscious
- **Rapid Healing**: Heal 1 additional HP per hit die during short rest

**Flaws:**
- **Involuntary Transformation**: Every full moon, must transform (no save)
- **Loss of Control**: First transformation = no control (DM controls, beast is hostile)
- **Silver Vulnerability**: Attacks with silver weapons ignore immunity and deal +1d6 damage
- **Beast Instincts**: Disadvantage on Insight checks; advantage on Survival checks
- **Curse Mark**: Animals can sense your nature (may fear or attack you)

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -1 (beast whispers)
- Focus: -1 (instincts distract)
- When transformed: Sanity -4, Focus -4 (beast dominates)

**DCS Integration (Hybrid Form):**
- Dodge Evasion: +0
- Endure Evasion: +3 (beast toughness)
- Deflect Evasion: -1 (mindless aggression)
- Natural DR: +2 (thick hide)
- Immunity to nonmagical weapons

**Control Mechanics:**
- After transformation, make Wisdom save DC 15
- Success: Remember what happened; gain +1 control point
- Failure: No memory; lose 1d4 Sanity
- At 5 control points: advance to Stage 2

**Progression Trigger:**
- Survive 5 full moon transformations
- AND accumulate 5 control points
- OR: Embrace the curse willingly (instant Stage 2, but permanent)

---

### Stage 2: Embraced (Master of Two Forms)

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 1 active for 5+ months
- Either: accumulated control points OR willingly embraced curse

**Transformation Process:**
- Gain voluntary control over transformation
- Can shift at will (not just full moon)
- Memory retained while transformed

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 1 Boons**
- **Voluntary Shift**: Can assume hybrid form as action (lasts 1 hour, 1/short rest)
- **Extended Attributes**: +4 Str, +4 Con in hybrid form (replaces +4/+2)
- **Pack Tactics**: Advantage on attacks if ally within 5 feet
- **Scent Tracking**: Can track creatures by scent (Survival check)
- **Regeneration**: 5 HP per round while in hybrid form (stops if take silver or fire damage)
- **Natural Armor** (hybrid): AC = 12 + Dex + Con

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 1 Flaws**
- **Full Moon Compulsion**: Still MUST transform during full moon (but retain control)
- **Bloodlust**: When bloodied in hybrid form, make Wisdom save DC 12 or attack nearest creature (friend or foe) next turn
- **Social Stigma**: If transformation witnessed, NPCs react with fear/hostility
- **Alignment Drift**: Chaotic tendencies increase

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -2 (beast voice louder)
- Focus: -1 (instincts stronger)
- Hybrid form: Sanity -3, Focus -2 (more control than Stage 1)

**DCS Integration (Hybrid Form):**
- Dodge Evasion: +1 (beast agility)
- Endure Evasion: +4 (regeneration + toughness)
- Deflect Evasion: +0
- Natural DR: +3
- Regeneration: 5 HP/round
- Immunity to nonmagical weapons

**Bloodlust Mechanics:**
- When you drop enemy to 0 HP in hybrid form: Wisdom save DC 13 or spend next action attacking nearest creature
- When you kill creature in hybrid form: gain 1d4 temporary HP (bloodlust empowers)
- If you kill ally while bloodlusted: lose 1d6 Sanity, gain +1d4 CS

**Progression Trigger:**
- Spend 100+ hours in hybrid form
- OR: Kill 20+ creatures in hybrid form
- OR: Lead pack of lycanthropes (3+)

### Stage 3: Alpha (Beast Lord)

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 2 active for 1+ year
- Killed 20+ creatures in hybrid form
- OR: Defeated another alpha lycanthrope

**Transformation Process:**
- Become alpha of your kind
- Can maintain hybrid form indefinitely
- Physical changes even in humanoid form (more muscular, feral features)

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 2 Boons**
- **Persistent Hybrid**: Can maintain hybrid form indefinitely (no duration limit)
- **Alpha Attributes**: +6 Str, +6 Con in hybrid form (replaces +4/+4)
- **Pack Leader**: Can sense other lycanthropes within 1 mile; they recognize you as alpha
- **Regeneration**: 10 HP per round (replaces 5)
- **Infectious Bite**: Victims bitten must make Con save DC 15 or contract lycanthropy
- **Moon Call**: Once per full moon, can summon 2d6 wolves or 1d4 lesser lycanthropes (arrive in 10 minutes)
- **Partial Transformation**: Can manifest claws/enhanced senses in humanoid form without full shift

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 2 Flaws (Enhanced)**
- **Dominant Bloodlust**: DC for bloodlust saves increases to 15
- **Territory Instinct**: Compelled to defend "territory" (50-mile radius around lair)
- **Challenge Compulsion**: Cannot back down from direct challenge (Wisdom DC 18 to retreat)
- **Silver Weakness**: Silver damage increased to +2d6
- **Permanent Changes**: Even in humanoid form, clearly inhuman (glowing eyes, fangs, excessive hair)

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -4 (beast and human merge)
- Focus: -2
- Hybrid form: Sanity -4, Focus -3 (beast powerful but controlled)

**DCS Integration (Hybrid Form):**
- Dodge Evasion: +2
- Endure Evasion: +5
- Deflect Evasion: +1
- Natural DR: +4
- Regeneration: 10 HP/round
- Immunity to nonmagical weapons
- Pack Tactics (constant advantage if ally nearby)

**Pack Dynamics:**
- Lesser lycanthropes defer to you
- Can command pack in combat
- Pack members will die for you
- Creating new lycanthropes strengthens pack

**Corruption Interaction at High CS:**
- If CS 15+ while Alpha: Wisdom saves at disadvantage
- Risk of permanent beast form (50% chance per full moon)
- May lose character to feral state (DM discretion)

**Progression Trigger:**
- Survive 5+ years as Alpha
- AND: Rule pack of 10+ lycanthropes
- AND: Defeat legendary beast or rival alpha

**Transformation Process:**
- Become myth made flesh

- May grow to Large size

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 3 Boons**
- **Primal Attributes**: +8 Str, +8 Con, +4 Wis

- **Primal Roar**: Action - all enemies within 60ft make Wisdom save DC 18 or frightened for 1 minute
- **Perfect Tracking**: Cannot lose trail; always know direction to tracked creature

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 3 Flaws**

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -6 (barely human)
- Focus: -4
- Essentially a different creature from original character

# 4. LICH (The Undying Scholar)

**Type:** Independent System (but compatible with Corruption)  
**Trigger:** Willingly perform dark ritual  
**Prerequisite:** Can cast 6th-level spells, access to forbidden knowledge  
**Irreversible:** Yes (by design—immortality is the point)

## Philosophy

Lichdom is **intellectual immortality**—trading your soul, mortality, and humanity for eternal existence and limitless magical power. Unlike vampires (predators) or lycanthropes (cursed), liches **choose** undeath deliberately.

In Dór-o Estel, liches are the ultimate expression of magical ambition. They sacrifice everything mortal—love, warmth, sensation—to transcend death and achieve mastery over arcane forces.

## Corruption Relationship

**Synergistic System:**
- Becoming lich ADDS +10 CS immediately (ritual is massively corrupting)
- High-CS characters make BETTER liches (already corrupted)
- Lich powers + Eldritch corruption = terrifying combination
- Many liches intentionally cultivate Corruption for power

**Special Rule:**
- Liches at CS 20+ may undergo **Eldritch Apotheosis** (become demilich with Eldritch powers)
- CS 30+ liches are definitely NPCs (too alien to play)

## Phylactery Mechanics (Core to All Stages)

**Creating Phylactery:**
- Requires rare materials (10,000 gp worth)
- Evil ritual (murder sapient creature, trap soul)
- Takes 10 days
- Phylactery must be hidden in secure location

**Phylactery Function:**
- Soul contained in object (gem, amulet, book, etc.)
- If body destroyed, soul returns to phylactery
- Body regenerates near phylactery in 1d10 days
- **Only way to kill lich:** Destroy phylactery first, THEN kill body

**Phylactery Feeding:**
- Must feed phylactery soul of sapient creature every 30 days
- Failure: Lose 1d4 Sanity, +1 CS, physical body starts decaying
- If 90 days without feeding: Transform into uncontrolled demilich (NPC)

---

### Stage 1: Newly Risen (Fresh Undeath)

**Prerequisites:**
- Can cast 6th-level spells
- Completed ritual (murder + phylactery creation)
- Died while soul bound to phylactery

**Transformation Process:**
- Awaken as undead
- Flesh decays over 1d4 days (becomes skeletal)
- Feel cold, hunger disappears, emotions fade

**Boons:**
- **Undead Nature**: No need to breathe, eat, sleep
- **Darkvision**: 120 feet
- **Arcane Empowerment**: +2 Intelligence, +2 Constitution
- **Spell Resistance**: Advantage on saves vs spells
- **True Immortality**: If killed, regenerate at phylactery
- **Undead Resilience**:
  - Immunity: poison, disease, exhaustion
  - Resistance: necrotic damage
  - Immune to nonmagical weapons
- **Truesight**: 60 feet (see through illusions)
- **Paralyzing Touch**: Touch attack, target makes Con save DC (8 + prof + Con) or paralyzed 1 minute

**Flaws:**
- **Phylactery Dependency**: Must feed phylactery soul every 30 days
- **Corruption Gain**: Immediately gain +10 CS (ritual is evil)
- **Sunlight Sensitivity**: Disadvantage on all rolls in direct sunlight
- **Fire Vulnerability**: Take +50% damage from fire
- **Obvious Undead**: Rotting/skeletal appearance (Deception impossible)
- **Moral Decay**: Alignment shifts to Evil
- **Social Death**: Virtually all societies will attempt to destroy you

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -3 (undeath dulls emotions)
- Focus: +3 (undeath enhances magical focus)
- Gain +10 CS from transformation ritual

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: -1 (stiff, decaying body)
- Endure Evasion: +2 (undead resilience)
- Deflect Evasion: +0
- Natural DR: +1
- Immunity to nonmagical weapons

**Spell Changes:**
- Can cast spells as normal
- Necromancy spells cast at +1 spell level (free)
- Learn *Animate Dead* if don't know it
- No spell components required (undead don't need material components)

**Phylactery Feeding Mechanics:**
- Kill sapient creature (Int 6+)
- Perform 1-hour ritual within 24 hours
- Creature's soul trapped in phylactery
- Each soul lasts 30 days
- Missing feeding: -1d4 Sanity, +1 CS, body decay accelerates

**Progression Trigger:**
- Survive 1 year as lich
- Feed phylactery 12+ souls
- Master 7th-level spells

---

### Stage 2: Established Lich (Arcane Master)

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 1 active for 1+ year
- Fed phylactery 12+ souls
- Mastered 7th-level spells

**Transformation Process:**
- Flesh completely gone (pure skeleton)
- Eyes become pinpricks of light (green/red/blue)
- Voice becomes hollow, echoing

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 1 Boons**
- **Greater Arcane Power**: +4 Int, +4 Con (replaces +2/+2)
- **Enhanced Spellcasting**:
  - +1 spell slot for each level 1-5
  - Necromancy spells ignore resistance
  - Can concentrate on two spells simultaneously (both necromancy)
- **Frightful Presence**: Action - creatures within 60ft make Wisdom save DC 15 or frightened 1 minute
- **Undead Mastery**: Can control 4× normal undead with *Animate Dead*
- **Legendary Resistance**: 1/day (can choose to succeed on failed save)
- **Rejuvenation**: If destroyed, reform at phylactery in 1d10 days

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 1 Flaws**
- **Phylactery Feeding**: Now requires soul every 20 days (not 30)
- **Isolation**: Long-term exposure (1+ hour) causes living creatures to feel dread
- **Paranoia**: Must make Wisdom save DC 12 daily or become convinced someone is hunting phylactery
- **Magical Dependency**: If in antimagic field for 1+ minute, take 2d10 psychic damage

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -5 (humanity fading)
- Focus: +5 (pure magical will)
- Personality noticeably changed (colder, more logical)

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +0 (skeletal = flexible)
- Endure Evasion: +3
- Deflect Evasion: +1 (magical deflection)
- Natural DR: +2
- Legendary Resistance: 1/day
- Immunity to nonmagical weapons

**Lair Creation:**
- Can attune to location (1-week ritual)
- Lair gains lair actions:
  - Animate dead within lair
  - Create zone of magical darkness
  - Target must make save or take 2d6 necrotic
- Phylactery typically hidden in lair

**Undead Army:**
- At this stage, most liches command undead armies
- 20-50 skeletons/zombies
- 5-10 wights or mummies
- 1-2 vampire spawn

**Progression Trigger:**
- Survive 10+ years as lich
- Feed phylactery 100+ souls
- Master 9th-level spells
- Complete grand magical work (create artifact, write spell, etc.)

---

### Stage 3: Ancient Lich (Undying Archmage)

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 2 active for 10+ years
- Fed phylactery 100+ souls
- Mastered 9th-level spells

**Transformation Process:**
- Bones become crystal or metal (choice)
- Eyes burn with pure magical energy
- Voice resonates with arcane power
- May customize appearance (robes fused to body, etc.)

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 2 Boons**
- **Transcendent Intellect**: +6 Int, +6 Con, +4 Wis (replaces +4/+4)
- **Arcane Supremacy**:
  - +2 spell slots for each level 1-9
  - Can cast *Wish* 1/year without aging (undead don't age)
  - Necromancy spells ignore immunity
  - Can concentrate on three spells simultaneously
- **Legendary Resistance**: 3/day (replaces 1/day)
- **True Undeath**: Immune to Turn Undead
- **Soul Cage**: When kill creature, can trap soul (as *Soul Cage* spell) at will
- **Lair Mastery**: Lair actions become more powerful:
  - Can cast any spell learned through lair
  - Lair regenerates you (5 HP/round while inside)
  - Phylactery in lair = advantage on all rolls

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 2 Flaws**
- **Obsessive Research**: Must spend 4+ hours daily on arcane research or lose ability to cast highest-level spells
- **Megalomania**: Convinced of own superiority; disadvantage on Insight about threats
- **Detachment**: Cannot remember what emotions feel like
- **Typically NPC**: Most Ancient Liches become campaign villains

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -8 (barely recognizable as former person)
- Focus: +8 (pure arcane will)
- Original personality nearly gone
- Memories of mortal life fade

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +1
- Endure Evasion: +4
- Deflect Evasion: +2
- Natural DR: +3
- Legendary Resistance: 3/day
- Lair Regeneration: 5 HP/round
- Immunity to nonmagical weapons + Turn Undead

**Research & Knowledge:**
- Knows 50+ spells (all schools)
- Created unique spells
- May have library worth 50,000+ gp
- Crafted magic items
- Written magical treatises

**Corruption at This Stage:**
- Most Ancient Liches have CS 20-30
- Corruption manifests as Eldritch mutations:
  - Extra eye sockets with void inside
  - Bones covered in glowing glyphs
  - Aura causes madness in living
  - Phylactery becomes Eldritch artifact

**Progression Trigger:**
- Survive 100+ years
- Feed phylactery 1,000+ souls
- Achieve legendary arcane feat
- OR: Embrace Eldritch corruption (CS 30+)

---

### Stage 4: Demilich (Undead Demigod)

**This is NPC-only territory**

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 3 active for 100+ years
- Fed phylactery 1,000+ souls
- Achieved legendary status

**Transformation Process:**
- Physical form becomes unnecessary
- Most of body crumbles to dust
- Only skull (and maybe hands) remain
- Skull floats, glows with power

**Powers:**
- Near-godlike magical ability
- Can cast *Wish* at will
- Lair is pocket dimension
- Immune to almost everything
- Legendary Actions: 5/round
- Legendary Resistance: 5/day
- Howl = instant death (Con save DC 25 or die)

**At This Point:**
- Definitively campaign-ending boss
- Not playable
- Personality completely alien
- May ascend to lichgod (if that's a thing in your setting)

## Special: Eldritch Lich (CS 30+ Variant)

**What Happens:**
- Lich at CS 30+ undergoes transformation
- Becomes fusion of undead and Eldritch entity
- Phylactery becomes portal to Null Reflection
- Body manifests impossible geometry

**Powers:**
- All Ancient Lich powers
- Plus: Eldritch abilities (tentacles, void gaze, madness aura)
- Can corrupt living creatures with touch
- Phylactery feeds on Corruption instead of souls

**This is the "bad ending":**
- Character becomes NPC immediately
- Main villain material
- Possibly world-ending threat

- Lich guards phylactery obsessively
- Hidden in secure lair (often planar vault)
- Protected by traps, guardians, magical wards
- May be disguised as mundane object

**Destroying It:**
- Requires specific method unique to each phylactery
- Examples:
  - Bathed in holy fire from celestial forge
  - Shattered by weapon of pure good
  - Consumed by Tarrasque
  - Thrown into specific volcano
  - Unmade with *Wish*

**After Destruction:**

## Thematic Integration with Dór-o Estel

# 5. FIENDISH TRANSFORMATION (Demonic Corruption)

**Type:** Corruption Replacement Path  
**Trigger:** At CS 15-20, can embrace demonic pact instead of Terminal Corruption  
**Prerequisite:** Must have patron (devil, demon, or dark entity willing to sponsor transformation)  
**Irreversible:** Yes (soul-binding)

## Philosophy

Fiendish transformation is **corruption made flesh**—instead of resisting the darkness consuming you, you bargain with it. Trade your soul for power, become the monster you feared, but retain agency instead of losing yourself to madness.

In Dór-o Estel, this represents making a deal with entities from the outer planes or with the Null Reflection itself. You become fiend-kin: horns, wings, fire, and eternal damnation.

## Corruption Relationship

**Replacement System:**
- Available ONLY at CS 15-19 (Eldritch tier)
- Choosing this path LOCKS CS at current level
- Won't progress to CS 20+ (Terminal)
- But also can't reduce CS (permanent)
- Transformation uses Corruption as fuel for power

**Mechanics:**
- Each stage requires spending Corruption
- Higher CS = more powerful fiendish form
- CS 15 fiend < CS 19 fiend in raw power

---

### Stage 1: Tainted (The First Deal)

**Prerequisites:**
- CS 15-19 (Eldritch Corruption tier)
- Contact with fiend or dark entity
- Willingly sign pact (blood signature)

**Transformation Process:**
- Immediate physical changes
- Skin tone shifts (red, purple, ash-gray)
- Small horns/claws manifest
- Eyes glow with inner fire
- CS locks at current level

**Boons:**
- **Fiendish Resilience**: Resistance to fire and poison damage
- **Darkvision**: 120 feet
- **Natural Weapons**: Claws (1d6 slashing)
- **Infernal Blessing**: +2 Charisma, +2 Constitution
- **Corruption Lock**: CS cannot increase beyond current level
- **Dark Power**: Can cast *Hellish Rebuke* 1/day (2d10 fire damage)
- **Patron's Gift**: Choose one:
  - Devil: Silvered tongue (+2 to Deception/Persuasion)
  - Demon: Battle rage (+2 to attack when bloodied)
  - Yugoloth: Mercenary soul (advantage on one roll/day, must be selfish)

**Flaws:**
- **Soul Pact**: Soul belongs to patron upon death (no resurrection without patron's permission)
- **Corruption Permanent**: CS locked; cannot be reduced below current level
- **Obvious Changes**: Horns/skin color visible (Disguise Self required for society)
- **Holy Vulnerability**: Take +1d6 radiant damage from holy sources
- **Alignment Shift**: Drifts toward Evil
- **Fiendish Compulsions**: Must make Wisdom save DC 12 to resist temptation to commit sin (patron-specific)

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: Base -4 from CS 15+ PLUS transformation penalties don't stack further (locked state)
- Focus: Auto-fails from CS 15+ remain
- **Benefit**: Mental degradation stops here (won't get worse)

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +0
- Endure Evasion: +1 (fiendish toughness)
- Deflect Evasion: +0
- Natural DR: +1

**Current CS Effects Still Apply:**
- All Sanity/Focus saves still auto-fail (from CS 15+)
- Physical trauma saves still at disadvantage
- Recovery still impaired
- **But**: Won't get worse (CS locked)

**Patron Types & Compulsions:**

**Devil Pact** (Lawful Evil):
- Compulsion: Must honor contracts literally
- Sin: Breaking your word
- Temptation: Make binding deals with others

**Demon Pact** (Chaotic Evil):
- Compulsion: Must sate bloodlust weekly
- Sin: Showing mercy to enemies
- Temptation: Destroy instead of control

**Yugoloth Pact** (Neutral Evil):
- Compulsion: Must profit from every situation
- Sin: Helping others without payment
- Temptation: Betray allies for personal gain

**Progression Trigger:**
- Serve patron for 1 year
- Commit 10+ acts aligned with patron's nature
- Complete major task for patron

---

### Stage 2: Infernal (True Transformation)

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 1 active for 1+ year
- Completed patron's major task
- Committed to fiendish path

**Transformation Process:**
- Major physical changes
- Wings manifest (bat-like, 20ft span)
- Horns grow larger
- Tail appears
- Eyes become fully fiendish (no whites)

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 1 Boons**
- **Fiendish Wings**: Fly 40 feet
- **Enhanced Attributes**: +4 Cha, +4 Con (replaces +2/+2)
- **Immunity**: Fire damage (replaces resistance)
- **Infernal Magic**: Can cast *Fireball* and *Darkness* each 1/day
- **Fearful Presence**: Action - creatures within 30ft make Wisdom save DC (8 + prof + Cha) or frightened 1 minute
- **Damage Resistance**: Add bludgeoning/piercing/slashing from nonmagical weapons
- **Regeneration**: 5 HP per round (stops if take radiant or cold damage)

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 1 Flaws**
- **Obvious Fiend**: Cannot pass for human even with magic (major illusion required)
- **Holy Ground**: Take 1d10 radiant damage per round on consecrated ground
- **Running Water**: Cannot cross running water without invitation or bridge
- **Compulsion Intensifies**: Save DC increases to 15
- **Plane Tug**: Patron can summon you to their plane 1/month (no save; 24-hour duration)

**Mental Attributes:**
- Still locked from Stage 1
- Personality shifts more fiendish (calculating, cruel, or wrathful depending on patron)

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +2 (flight)
- Endure Evasion: +2
- Deflect Evasion: +1
- Natural DR: +2
- Regeneration: 5 HP/round
- Resistance to nonmagical weapons

**Patron Powers (Choose One Additional):**

**Devil:**
- **Chains of Hell**: Can cast *Hold Person* 3/day
- **Devil's Tongue**: Advantage on all Charisma checks

**Demon:**
- **Chaos Strike**: When you crit, roll all damage dice three times (not twice)
- **Abyssal Frenzy**: Reaction when ally drops to 0 HP - gain extra attack

**Yugoloth:**
- **Mercenary's Luck**: 3/day, reroll any d20 (must keep second roll)
- **Calculated Cruelty**: Add Intelligence modifier to damage 1/turn

**Progression Trigger:**
- Serve patron for 10+ years
- Commit 100+ evil acts
- Achieve legendary infamy
- Complete patron's grand design

---

### Stage 3: Archfiend (Lordly Power)

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 2 active for 10+ years
- Legendary service to patron
- Achieved infamy or completed grand task

**Transformation Process:**
- Become greater fiend
- Size may increase to Large (optional)
- Appearance fully monstrous
- Aura of dread surrounds you

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 2 Boons**
- **Legendary Attributes**: +6 Cha, +6 Con, +4 Str (replaces +4/+4)
- **Greater Wings**: Fly 60 feet
- **Legendary Resistance**: 3/day
- **Superior Regeneration**: 10 HP per round (replaces 5)
- **Archfiend Magic**: Can cast patron's signature spell 1/day:
  - Devil: *Dominate Person* (no concentration)
  - Demon: *Cloudkill* (necrotic damage instead of poison)
  - Yugoloth: *Modify Memory* (at will)
- **Summon Lesser Fiends**: Action - summon 2d4 imps or 1d4 lesser demons (serve for 1 hour)
- **Aura of Sin**: Creatures within 30ft have disadvantage on saves vs your spells/abilities

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 2 Flaws**
- **Bound Servant**: Patron can command you telepathically; refusing direct command = 4d10 psychic damage
- **Obvious Fiend Lord**: Cities send armies to kill you on sight
- **Cannot Enter Holy Sites**: Physically barred from temples, consecrated ground
- **Radiant Vulnerability**: Take double damage from radiant sources
- **Probably NPC**: At this stage, most players become villains

**Mental Attributes:**
- Personality completely aligned with patron type
- Original character barely recognizable
- May lose character to NPC status

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +3
- Endure Evasion: +4
- Deflect Evasion: +2
- Natural DR: +3
- Regeneration: 10 HP/round
- Legendary Resistance: 3/day
- Legendary Actions: 2/round

**Archfiend Powers:**
- Command lesser fiends
- Create contracts that bind souls
- Corrupt mortals with touch (inflict Corruption)
- May have personal lair with lair actions

**Progression:**
- Stage 4 exists (Demon Prince/Archdevil) but is pure NPC
- Character becomes campaign villain
- May achieve immortality in patron's plane

## Stage 4: Demon Prince / Archdevil (NPC Only)

**This is endgame villain territory:**
- CR 20+ equivalent
- Commands armies of fiends
- Has plane of existence
- Can grant warlock pacts
- Effectively immortal
- World-threatening power

---

## Unique Mechanics

**Soul Debt:**
- Every time you die, patron can claim your soul
- Can negotiate temporary "lease" (serve for X years = resurrection)
- Eventually patron claims soul permanently
- Resurrection by others requires patron's permission (they won't give it)

**Corruption as Fuel:**
- Can temporarily **spend** CS to power abilities
- Each CS spent = +1d6 to damage roll OR +1 to save DC
- CS spent this way is lost for 24 hours, then returns
- Example: CS 17 fiend can spend 2 CS to boost spell (becomes CS 15 temporarily)

**Infernal Hierarchy:**
- Higher-stage fiends command lower-stage
- Devil pacts respect hierarchy
- Demon pacts are constant power struggles
- Yugoloth pacts are mercenary contracts

---

## Thematic Integration with Dór-o Estel

**Planar Entities:**
- Devils/Demons/Yugoloths exist in outer planes
- Rarely manifest on Material Plane
- Seek souls and servants
- Fiendish transformation = way to extend influence

**Faith of the Bound Pattern:**
- Fiends are "unravelers" of the Pattern
- Fiendish PCs hunted relentlessly
- No redemption possible (soul already sold)

**Why Play This?**
- **Escape Terminal Corruption** (trade one doom for another)
- Get significant power immediately
- Retain player agency (unlike Terminal Corruption = NPC)
- Evil campaigns or morally gray settings
- **Warning**: Still leads to character loss eventually (just slower)

---

## Risk/Reward Summary

**Rewards:**
- Escape Terminal Corruption (CS locks)
- Immediate power boost (attributes, flight, magic)
- Regeneration, fire immunity, legendary resistance
- Can build fiend cult/army
- Patron provides resources/knowledge

**Risks:**
- Soul belongs to patron (no resurrection)
- CS permanently locked (can't cleanse)
- Society will kill you
- Must serve patron's whims
- Compulsions restrict behavior
- Holy vulnerability
- Will probably become NPC at Stage 3

**Balance Assessment:**
- **POWERFUL** but costly
- Better than Terminal Corruption (retain agency)
- Worse than cleansing (permanent evil)
- Best for: Warlocks, evil campaigns
- Requires: DM buy-in, party tolerance
- Expect: Social consequences, eventual NPC transition

# 6. FEY TRANSFORMATION (The Wyld Calling)

**Type:** Independent System  
**Trigger:** Fey bargain, ancient bloodline, crossing into Feywild permanently  
**Prerequisite:** None (can occur at any CS, but Low CS preferred)  
**Irreversible:** Extremely difficult (Fey don't release bargains easily)

## Philosophy

Fey transformation is **wild immortality**—trading linear time, mortal constraints, and human logic for eternal youth, capricious power, and existence bound to nature and whimsy. Unlike fiends (evil) or undead (death), Fey are **alien morality**—neither good nor evil, but strange.

In Dór-o Estel, Fey are creatures of the Verdant Loom's wildest threads—living contradictions who embody beauty and horror, generosity and cruelty, in equal measure.

## Corruption Relationship

**Inverse Incompatibility:**
- Fey transformation **reduces** Corruption (nature purifies Eldritch taint)
- Each stage reduces CS by 1d6
- **BUT**: If CS ever reaches 15+ after transformation, Fey magic **breaks** (transformation reverses violently)
- Fey nature and Eldritch corruption are fundamentally incompatible

**Ideal Candidate:**
- CS 0-9 (pure or lightly tainted)
- CS 10-14 can become Fey but risky
- CS 15+ cannot become Fey (too corrupted)

---

### Stage 1: Wyld-Touched (Mortal No Longer)

**Prerequisites:**
- Make bargain with Fey entity
- OR: Spend 1+ year in Feywild
- OR: Born to Fey bloodline (dormant, awakens in crisis)

**Transformation Process:**
- Hair/eyes change color (unnatural: silver, green, gold)
- Seasons affect mood/appearance
- Time feels different (sometimes hours pass like minutes)
- Dreams become vivid, prophetic

**Boons:**
- **Fey Ancestry**: Advantage on saves vs charm; can't be put to sleep by magic
- **Eternal Youth**: Age 10× slower (stop aging physically at peak)
- **Seasonal Attunement**: Choose one season; gain benefits:
  - **Spring**: +2 Charisma, can cast *Charm Person* 1/day
  - **Summer**: +2 Strength, resistance to fire damage
  - **Autumn**: +2 Wisdom, advantage on Survival checks
  - **Winter**: +2 Constitution, resistance to cold damage
- **Wyld Magic**: Learn *Druidcraft* and one 1st-level druid spell
- **Fey Step**: Bonus action teleport 30 feet to unoccupied space you can see (1/short rest)
- **Corruption Reduction**: Reduce CS by 1d6

**Flaws:**
- **Cold Iron Weakness**: Take +1d6 damage from cold iron weapons
- **Fey Bargain**: Owe service to Fey patron (must complete tasks when called)
- **Truth Binding**: Cannot tell direct lie (can mislead, but not false statements)
- **Threshold Invitation**: Cannot enter dwelling without invitation (like vampire)
- **Seasonal Mood**: Personality shifts with seasons (Spring = playful, Winter = melancholic)

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: +1 (Fey nature stabilizes mortal madness)
- Focus: -1 (chaotic, harder to maintain rigid concentration)
- Personality becomes more whimsical, mercurial

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +1 (Fey grace)
- Endure Evasion: +0
- Deflect Evasion: +0
- Natural DR: +0

**Fey Bargain Types:**
- **Service**: Perform tasks for Fey lord
- **Tithe**: Offer yearly gift (memories, emotions, first laugh of child, etc.)
- **Guardianship**: Protect specific grove/stream/creature
- **Story**: Live interesting life; Fey harvest stories from your experiences

**Progression Trigger:**
- Serve Fey patron for 7 years
- OR: Complete seven impossible tasks
- OR: Win Fey lord's favor through great deed

---

### Stage 2: Fey-Blooded (Half-Mortal)

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 1 active for 7+ years
- Completed Fey bargain OR earned favor
- Deep connection to Feywild

**Transformation Process:**
- Features become ethereally beautiful or eerily strange
- Skin may shimmer with faint light
- Plants respond to presence (flowers bloom, vines part)
- No longer need food (sustain on moonlight/emotions)

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 1 Boons**
- **Enhanced Seasonal Attunement**: +4 to chosen seasonal attribute (replaces +2)
- **Fey Resilience**: Resistance to psychic damage

- **Wyld Magic**: Can cast two 2nd-level druid spells 1/day each
- **Speak with Beasts and Plants**: At will
- **Fey Presence**: Action - charm or frighten creatures within 30ft (Wisdom save DC 8 + prof + Cha)

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 1 Flaws**
- **Cold Iron Vulnerability**: Take double damage from cold iron
- **Bound by Word**: If you give your word, magically compelled to keep it (Wisdom save DC 18 to break, take 4d10 psychic damage if succeed)
- **Seasonal Weakness**: Opposite season affects you:
  - Spring → Autumn: Disadvantage on Charisma checks
  - Summer → Winter: Vulnerability to cold
  - Autumn → Spring: Disadvantage on Wisdom saves

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: +2 (replaces +1; Fey nature soothing)

- Dodge Evasion: +2 (supernatural agility)

- Can call on Fey allies in emergencies
- Must attend yearly Fey gatherings

**Progression Trigger:**
- Serve 70 years as Fey-Blooded
- OR: Defeat rival Fey in formal duel
- OR: Complete "impossible" task (DM discretion)
- OR: Fully embrace Fey nature (abandon mortal identity)

---

### Stage 3: True Fey (Archfey Aspirant)

**Boons:**

**Flaws:**

# 7. ABERRANT HORROR (Eldritch Apotheosis)

**Type:** Corruption Replacement Path  
**Trigger:** At CS 15-20, embrace Eldritch corruption instead of resisting  
**Prerequisite:** Must have contacted Eldritch entity or Null Reflection  
**Irreversible:** Absolutely yes (you become the thing mortals fear)

## Philosophy

Aberrant Horror transformation is **embracing madness**—instead of fighting the Eldritch corruption consuming you, you surrender to it completely. Your body becomes living wrongness, your mind transcends (or breaks beyond) mortal thought, and you gain power by becoming the nightmare.

This is the "bad ending" made playable—for a time.

## Corruption Relationship

**Apotheosis System:**
- Requires CS 15+ to begin
- Each stage ADDS +5 CS (embracing corruption)
- CS becomes **power source** not penalty
- At CS 40+, transformation completes (become full aberration = NPC)

**Critical Rule:**
- Once begun, CANNOT reverse
- CS will only increase, never decrease
- Character has maybe 2-3 years before NPC transition
- This is "playing the villain origin story"

---

### Stage 1: Touched by the Void (Early Mutation)

**Prerequisites:**
- CS 15-19 (Eldritch Corruption tier)
- Failed Terminal Corruption save
- **Choose** to embrace transformation instead of fighting

**Transformation Process:**
- Physical mutations begin
- Eyes change (too many pupils, wrong color, void behind them)
- Skin texture shifts (scales, slime, chitinous patches)
- Whispers constant now (but comprehensible)
- Gain +5 CS immediately (now CS 20-24)

**Boons:**
- **Aberrant Resilience**: Resistance to psychic damage, advantage on saves vs madness
- **Eldritch Sight**: Truesight 30 feet (see through illusions, into ethereal)
- **Void Touched**: +2 Intelligence, +2 Wisdom (alien knowledge)
- **Tentacle Manifestation**: Can sprout 1d4 tentacles as bonus action (melee reach 10ft, 1d6 bludgeoning, last 1 minute, 1/short rest)
- **Madness Aura**: Creatures starting turn within 10ft must make Wisdom save DC (8 + prof + Int) or frightened until start of their next turn
- **CS Becomes Power**: For every 5 CS you have, gain +1 to spell save DCs and spell attack rolls

**Flaws:**
- **CS Increase**: Immediately gain +5 CS (now at 20-24)
- **Physical Horror**: Mutations obvious; disguise requires illusion magic
- **Sanity Damage to Allies**: Allies who can see you must make Sanity save DC 12 when you transform or lose 1d4 Sanity
- **Corruption Magnet**: Gain +1 CS per month automatically
- **Mental Degradation**: Personality shifts toward alien logic
- **Holy Anathema**: Take 2d6 radiant damage when entering consecrated ground

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -6 (embracing madness)
- Focus: +4 (Eldritch clarity)
- Thoughts become non-linear
- Mortal concerns fade

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +0
- Endure Evasion: +2 (aberrant flesh)
- Deflect Evasion: +0
- Natural DR: +1 (chitinous growths)

**CS Power Scaling:**
- At CS 20: +4 to spell DCs/attacks
- At CS 25: +5 to spell DCs/attacks
- This makes you TERRIFYINGLY powerful caster

**Aberrant Mutations (Choose 1):**
1. **Extra Eyes**: Gain blindsight 30ft
2. **Void Veins**: Blood is black ichor; bleed deals psychic damage to nearby creatures
3. **Impossible Joints**: Advantage on Escape Artist checks; can fit through smaller spaces
4. **Echo Voice**: Can speak from multiple points simultaneously within 30ft

**Progression Trigger:**
- Survive 1 year
- Gain CS 25+ (happens automatically through monthly gain)
- OR: Contact Null Reflection directly

---

### Stage 2: Voidspawn (Major Aberration)

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 1 active for 1+ year
- CS 25-29
- Contacted Eldritch entity

**Transformation Process:**
- Major mutations
- Body geometry becomes wrong (extra limbs, impossible angles)
- Size may increase
- Constantly surrounded by faint reality distortion

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 1 Boons**
- **Enhanced Aberrant Mind**: +4 Int, +4 Wis (replaces +2/+2)
- **Permanent Tentacles**: 1d6 tentacles always present (10ft reach, 1d8 bludgeoning each)
- **Immunity**: Psychic damage (replaces resistance)
- **Eldritch Resilience**: Resistance to all magic (advantage on saves vs spells)
- **Reality Warp**: 1/day, can cast *Dimension Door* or *Blink* without components
- **CS Power**: Now +5 or +6 to spell DCs/attacks (depending on exact CS)
- **Void Step**: Teleport 60ft as bonus action (through Ethereal Plane), 1/short rest
- **Madness Aura**: Increased to 20ft radius, DC increases to 13

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 1 Flaws (Enhanced)**
- **CS Increase**: Gain +5 CS immediately (now CS 30-34)
- **Obvious Aberration**: Cannot pass for humanoid; cities attack on sight
- **Sanity Drain to Allies**: Allies near you (30ft) lose 1 Sanity per hour
- **Monthly CS**: Gain +1d4 CS per month (replaces +1)
- **Alignment Shift**: Becomes Chaotic Neutral or Chaotic Evil
- **Whispers Demand**: Must complete one Eldritch task per month or take 4d10 psychic damage

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -8 (barely coherent by mortal standards)
- Focus: +6 (alien perfection)
- Memories fragmenting
- Original personality nearly gone

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +1
- Endure Evasion: +3
- Deflect Evasion: +2 (tentacles deflect)
- Natural DR: +2
- Multiple attacks (tentacles)

**Aberrant Abilities (Gain 2):**
1. **Phase Through Matter**: Can move through solid objects (difficult terrain)
2. **Mind Blast**: Action - 60ft cone, Int save DC 15 or 4d8 psychic + stunned
3. **Eldritch Regeneration**: 5 HP per round (stops if take radiant)
4. **Void Gaze**: Stare at creature - Wisdom save or charmed (see you as friend/master)
5. **Reality Tear**: Create 10ft portal to random location 1/day

**At This Stage:**
- Party may turn on you (too dangerous/horrifying)
- Most campaigns would have you become antagonist
- DM should discuss with player

**Progression Trigger:**
- CS reaches 35+ (automatic through monthly gain in 5-10 months)
- Transformation becomes inevitable

---

### Stage 3: Eldritch Abomination (Near-Complete)

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 2 active

# 8. SPECTRE (The Unfinished Business)

**Type:** Independent System (but often Corruption-adjacent)  
**Trigger:** Violent death with unfinished business, curse, failure to move on  
**Prerequisite:** Must die (obviously)  
**Irreversible:** Technically yes (already dead), but can be "completed" (move on)

## Philosophy

Spectre transformation is **death without peace**—your soul refuses to pass on, binding itself to the Material Plane through sheer force of will, unfinished purpose, or dark curse. Unlike vampires (who choose undeath) or liches (who plan it), spectres are **accidents of trauma**—ghosts that refuse the final rest.

In Dór-o Estel, spectres are seen as "broken threads"—souls that should have been rewoven into the Pattern but instead cling to mortality through obsession, vengeance, or love.

## Corruption Relationship

**Death-Adjacent System:**
- Becoming spectre can happen at any CS
- **Low CS spectres** retain humanity (tragic ghosts)
- **High CS spectres** become nightmare wraiths
- CS affects what KIND of spectre you become

---

### Stage 1: Lingering Spirit (Fresh Ghost)

**Prerequisites:**
- Died violently OR with powerful unfinished business
- Failed death save with natural 1 (DM may offer this option)
- OR: Deliberately choose to remain as ghost instead of dying

**Transformation Process:**
- Awaken as incorporeal spirit
- Tethered to location or object (anchor)
- Realize you're dead but refuse to move on

**Boons:**
- **Incorporeal**: Can move through solid objects (difficult terrain)
- **Flight**: 30 feet (hover)
- **Invisibility**: Can turn invisible as action (until attack or concentrate ends)
- **Possession** (limited): Can attempt to possess corpse or unconscious creature (1/long rest, Cha save DC 12, lasts 1 hour)
- **Immune**: Poison, disease, exhaustion
- **Resistant**: All damage except radiant and force
- **No Physical Needs**: Don't need to eat, sleep, breathe
- **Anchor Awareness**: Know exact location of your anchor object at all times

**Flaws:**
- **Incorporeal Weakness**: Cannot physically interact with world (can't pick up objects, open doors unless possess someone)
- **Sunlight Sensitivity**: Take 1d6 radiant damage per round in direct sunlight; disadvantage on all rolls
- **Anchor Binding**: Cannot move more than 1 mile from anchor object/location
- **Turn Vulnerability**: Can be turned by clerics (as undead)
- **Unfinished Business**: Compelled to pursue your purpose; cannot rest until complete
- **Fading**: Lose 1 point from random ability score per month (eventually fade to nothingness if business never finished)

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -2 (trauma of death)
- Focus: +0 (unchanged initially)
- Emotions intensified (especially tied to death/purpose)

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +3 (incorporeal = hard to hit)
- Endure Evasion: +0 (no physical body)
- Deflect Evasion: +0
- Natural DR: +0 (but resistant to most damage)

**Anchor Types:**
- **Location**: Site of death, important place
- **Object**: Weapon that killed you, treasured possession, wedding ring
- **Person**: Someone you love/hate/swore to protect
- **Purpose**: Revenge, protection, delivering message

**Unfinished Business Examples:**
- Revenge on killer
- Protect loved one
- Deliver crucial message
- Find lost treasure
- Clear your name
- Ensure child grows up safe

**Possession Mechanics:**
- Can possess corpse or unconscious humanoid
- Possessed creature uses your mental stats, its physical stats
- Can speak, interact physically
- Possession lasts 1 hour or until you end it
- Creature makes Cha save when you try to possess (DC 12)

**Progression Trigger:**
- Exist as spectre for 1+ year
- Make significant progress on unfinished business
- OR: Corruption/obsession deepens

---

### Stage 2: Wraith (Powerful Haunting)

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 1 active for 1+ year
- Unfinished business still incomplete
- Growing more desperate/angry/obsessed

**Transformation Process:**
- Become more powerful, more dangerous
- Form becomes darker, more frightening
- Voice becomes hollow, echo-like
- Temperature drops 10 degrees within 20ft

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 1 Boons**
- **Enhanced Flight**: 60 feet
- **Life Drain Touch**: Melee spell attack (+prof + Cha to hit), reach 5ft, 3d6 necrotic damage; you heal half damage dealt
- **Improved Possession**: 3/long rest, DC increases to 14, can possess willing creatures without save
- **Create Specter**: When humanoid dies within 30ft of you, can raise as specter under your control (1/day)
- **Horrifying Visage**: Action - creatures within 30ft that can see you make Wisdom save DC 14 or frightened for 1 minute; on failure by 5+, also age 1d4 × 10 years

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 1 Flaws (Enhanced)**
- **Sunlight Weakness**: 2d6 radiant damage per round (replaces 1d6)
- **Obsession Deepens**: Disadvantage on Wisdom saves related to your purpose
- **Fading Accelerates**: Lose 1d4 ability score points per month (not 1)
- **Hatred Growth**: Alignment shifts toward Evil as obsession consumes you
- **Turn Undead**: More vulnerable (turn DC decreases by 2)

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -4 (death and obsession eroding)
- Focus: +2 (singular purpose)
- Personality darkening

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +4
- Endure Evasion: +1
- Deflect Evasion: +0
- Natural DR: +0
- Life Drain healing

**Haunting Powers:**
- Can manipulate small objects (books fly, doors slam)
- Can manifest terrifying illusions
- Control temperature in area
- Speak without possessing anyone (ghostly voice)

**Corruption Interaction:**

**Low-CS Wraith (CS 0-9):**
- Retain more humanity
- Haunting is sad, tragic
- May help living allies
- Purpose likely noble

**High-CS Wraith (CS 15+):**
- Become malevolent
- Haunting is terrifying
- May attack innocents
- Purpose twisted by corruption

**Progression Trigger:**
- Exist 10+ years
- Unfinished business STILL incomplete (growing desperate)
- Killed 10+ living creatures
- OR: Complete unfinished business (ends transformation - see below)

### Stage 3: Death Knight / Banshee (Legendary Undead)

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 2 active for 10+ years
- Unfinished business still unresolved
- Deep obsession or corruption

**Transformation Type:**
Choose one path based on your nature:

#### **Path A: Death Knight** (Martial/Warrior ghosts)

**Transformation:**
- Bind soul to suit of armor
- Become armored undead warrior
- Physical form returns (sort of)

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 2 Boons**
- **Physical Form**: Regain ability to interact with physical world
- **Armor Integration**: Wear armor (it's part of you); AC = 18 + Dex mod
- **Weapon Mastery**: Proficiency with all weapons; attacks deal +2d6 necrotic
- **Undying Warrior**: If reduced to 0 HP, explode (20ft radius, 4d6 necrotic, Dex save DC 15 half), then reform at anchor in 1d4 days
- **Frightful Presence**: As action, creatures within 60ft make Wis save DC 16 or frightened
- **Legendary Resistance**: 1/day

**Flaws:**
- **Bound to Armor**: Armor is your anchor; if destroyed, you fade completely
- **Sunlight Banishment**: Direct sunlight forces you back to anchor (teleport away)
- **Holy Vulnerability**: Radiant damage bypasses resistances
- **Probably NPC**: Most Death Knights become antagonists

#### **Path B: Banshee** (Caster/Emotion ghosts)

**Transformation:**
- Become spirit of wailing sorrow/rage
- Pure emotional manifestation
- Horrifying to behold

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 2 Boons**
- **Wail of the Banshee**: Action (1/day) - all creatures within 60ft hear your wail; Con save DC 16 or drop to 0 HP; on success, take 4d10 psychic damage
- **Enhanced Incorporeality**: Can phase through magical barriers
- **Possession Mastery**: Can possess at will (no limit), DC 16
- **Emotional Resonance**: Sense strong emotions within 1 mile
- **Legendary Resistance**: 2/day

**Flaws:**
- **Emotional Instability**: Must make Wisdom save DC 15 hourly or act on strongest emotion (rage, sorrow, etc.)
- **Wail Exhaustion**: After using Wail, cannot speak for 24 hours
- **Fading**: Lose 2d4 ability score points per month
- **Probably NPC**: Banshees are typically tragic villains

---

**Both Paths Share:**

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -6 (centuries of undeath)
- Focus: +4 (or -4, depending on emotional state)
- Original personality nearly gone

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +5
- Endure Evasion: +2
- Deflect Evasion: +1
- Natural DR: +2 (Death Knight) or +0 (Banshee)
- Legendary Resistance

**At This Stage:**
- **Recommend**: Become campaign NPC
- Centuries of undeath have made you inhuman
- Unfinished business likely impossible to complete
- May need party to put you to rest

## Completing Unfinished Business

**What Happens:**
When you finally complete your purpose:

**Stage 1:**
- Soul can move on
- You get emotional farewell scene
- Pass into afterlife peacefully
- Character ends (but satisfied)

**Stage 2:**
- Harder to let go
- Must make Wisdom save DC 15 to choose to move on
- Failure = find NEW unfinished business (obsession too strong)
- Success = peaceful rest

**Stage 3:**
- Cannot move on naturally (too far gone)
- Requires *Resurrection* or *True Resurrection* to restore to life
- OR: Allies must help you find peace (ritual, quest, etc.)
- OR: Destroy anchor to force you to move on

**Alternative: Eternal Guardian:**
- Some spectres choose to remain as protectors
- Bind themselves to place/person eternally
- Become benevolent haunting
- Possible PC ending (rare)

---

## Special Mechanics

**Anchor Destruction:**
- If anchor destroyed, spectre begins fading
- Lose 1d6 from all ability scores per hour
- When any stat reaches 0, spectre dissipates (true death)
- Can attempt to bind to new anchor (Wisdom save DC 18, only 1 attempt)

**Spectre Society:**
- Some spectres form communities (haunted houses, ghost ships)
- Share information about living world
- May protect each other's anchors
- Spectres can see/interact with each other normally

**Possession and Class Features:**
- While possessing body, can use class features that make sense
- Martial classes: Full access
- Casters: Can cast if possessed body has mouth/hands
- But spell slots/resources limited to what you had when died

- **Eldritch Shade**: Fusion of ghost and Eldritch aberration

# Implementation Guidelines for DMs

## When to Offer Transformations

**Seraph:**
- Epic moment of heroism
- Divine intervention
- Resisting Terminal Corruption through virtue

**Vampirism:**
- Vampire encounter gone wrong
- Willing embrace (evil PC)
- Curse from dark ritual

**Lycanthropy:**
- Werewolf attack survival
- Ancient bloodline activation
- Druid curse

**Lich:**
- High-level wizard PC seeking immortality
- Discovery of ancient phylactery ritual
- Desperate bargain for power

**Fiendish:**
- CS reaches 15+ and player wants alternative to Terminal
- Warlock patron offers transformation
- Desperate deal to save world

**Fey:**
- Extended time in Feywild
- Archfey takes interest in PC
- Ancient Fey bargain fulfilled

**Aberrant:**
- CS reaches 15+ and player embraces corruption
- Contact with Null Reflection
- "Villain arc" campaign

**Spectre:**
- Character dies with unfinished business
- Natural 1 on death save + emotionally charged moment
- Player wants to continue after death

---

## Balancing Transformations

**Power Budget:**
- Transformations should be roughly equivalent to 2-3 levels
- Stage 1 = moderate power
- Stage 2 = significant power
- Stage 3 = epic power (possibly NPC)

**Drawbacks Matter:**
- Social consequences are REAL
- Weaknesses should be exploitable
- Time limits (fading, CS gain) create tension

**Party Dynamics:**
- Discuss with whole party before approving
- Some transformations (vampire, aberrant) may break party
- Consider parallel transformation (whole party transforms)

---

## Narrative Use

**Character Arcs:**
- Transformation = major character development
- Plan the arc WITH player
- Ending should be satisfying

**Campaign Themes:**
- Transformations work best in dark, Gothic, or horror campaigns
- High fantasy campaigns: Limit to Seraph, Fey, Lycanthropy
- Grimdark campaigns: All transformations on table

**Tragic Heroes:**
- Aberrant, Spectre, and Fiendish transformations are tragedy
- PC becomes villain
- Party must eventually face them
- Can be powerful storytelling

---

## Player Contracts

**Before Approving Transformation:**
1. Discuss endgame (will character become NPC?)
2. Agree on time limits
3. Clarify power level expectations
4. Confirm party buy-in
5. Establish narrative goals

**Red Flags:**
- Player wants power without drawbacks
- Player won't accept NPC transition
- Party not on board
- Transformation doesn't fit campaign

---

# Conclusion

Transformations in FEARS are **powerful, permanent, and dangerous**. They offer incredible power but at terrible cost—whether that cost is your soul (Fiend, Lich), your humanity (Vampire, Aberrant), your mortality (Spectre), or your freedom (Fey, Seraph).

**Use transformations to:**
- Create memorable character arcs
- Explore themes of power and sacrifice
- Generate tragic villains from former heroes
- Give players agency in their character's dark path

**Avoid using transformations:**
- As simple power-ups
- Without planning endgame
- In campaigns that can't support them
- When player doesn't understand consequences

**Remember:**
Most transformations lead to NPC status eventually. That's not a bug—it's a feature. These are stories about transcending (or losing) humanity. The question is whether the character achieves something meaningful before they pass beyond mortality.

May your transformations be epic, your sacrifices meaningful, and your final moments legendary.

# 9. GIANT TRANSFORMATION (Primordial Heritage)

**Type:** Independent System  
**Trigger:** Ancient ritual, primordial blessing, exposure to giant runes  
**Prerequisite:** None (but Goliaths have advantage on transformation)  
**Irreversible:** Yes (body fundamentally changes)

## Philosophy

Giant transformation is **primordial enlargement**—your body expands, your bones thicken, your blood becomes molten stone or frozen glacier. You don't just grow larger; you become a living embodiment of the world's first age, when giants ruled and mortals were barely conceived.

In Dór-o Estel, giants are remembered as the "First Weavers"—creatures who existed before the Pattern solidified, whose footsteps carved valleys and whose breath shaped weather.

## Lineage Interactions

**Goliaths:**
- **Affinity**: Goliaths have giant heritage already
- **Easier Transformation**: Goliath prerequisites reduced by 1 stage
- **Enhanced Power**: Goliath Giants gain +1 size category at each stage
- **Cultural Impact**: Seen as "returning to glory" by some clans, "abandoning balance" by others

**Other Lineages:**
- **Dwarves**: Painful transformation (mountain vs mountain-folk conflict)
- **Halflings/Gnomes**: Extremely rare, psychologically traumatic (identity erasure)
- **Dragonborn**: Incompatible with Wyrm transformation (can't be both)
- **Humans/Half-breeds**: Standard transformation

## Corruption Relationship

**Primordial Path:**
- Giant transformation is **neutral** to Corruption
- Can become Giant at any CS
- **BUT**: High-CS Giants become "Corrupted Titans" (see variants)
- CS 20+ Giants often serve Eldritch entities (against will)

## Giant Types

Choose one when transformation begins:

**Stone Giant** - Mountain, earth, endurance
**Frost Giant** - Ice, winter, conquest  
**Fire Giant** - Flame, forges, war  
**Storm Giant** - Thunder, seas, wisdom  
**Cloud Giant** - Air, nobility, magic

*Stats below use Stone Giant as baseline*

---

### Stage 1: Giant-Touched (First Growth)

**Prerequisites:**
- Exposure to giant magic or runes
- OR: Complete primordial ritual at sacred site
- OR: Born to giant/Goliath bloodline (dormant, awakens)
- **Goliaths**: Automatically qualify if they seek transformation

**Transformation Process:**
- Painful growth over 1d4 days
- Bones crack and reform
- Skin thickens, gains texture (stone-like, frost patterns, etc.)
- Size increases to Large (if Medium) or stays Large + becomes bulkier

**Boons:**
- **Size Increase**: Become Large (or stay Large but +50% bulk if already Large)
  - Reach increases by 5 feet
  - +1d4 damage on all melee attacks
  - Advantage on Strength checks
- **Primordial Resilience**: +2 Constitution, +2 Strength
- **Giant Endurance**: Resistance to your giant type's element:
  - Stone: Acid and poison resistance
  - Frost: Cold resistance
  - Fire: Fire resistance
  - Storm: Lightning and thunder resistance
  - Cloud: Psychic resistance
- **Powerful Build**: Count as Huge for carrying capacity
- **Natural Armor**: AC = 13 + Dex modifier (stone skin, icy hide, etc.)
- **Rock Throwing**: Can throw boulders (improvised weapon, 60/180 range, 2d6 + Str bludgeoning)

**Flaws:**
- **Size Problems**: Cannot fit through normal doors, ride normal mounts, wear normal armor
- **Heavy**: Disadvantage on Stealth; wooden floors may break
- **Increased Appetite**: Must eat 4× normal food (expensive)
- **Social Stigma**: Most people fear giants; cities may ban you
- **Equipment Costs**: All equipment costs 4× normal (Large size)
- **Lineage Conflict** (if not Goliath): Some of your culture may reject you

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -1 (body dysmorphia, alienation)
- Focus: +0 (unchanged initially)
- Personality becomes more direct, blunt

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: -1 (large target, less agile)
- Endure Evasion: +3 (massive hit points, tough skin)
- Deflect Evasion: +0
- Natural DR: +2 (thick skin)

**Giant Culture Access:**
- Learn Giant language instinctively
- Can read primordial runes
- Some giant clans may accept you
- Others see you as "pretender" (if not true giant blood)

**Goliath-Specific:**
- If Goliath: Seen as "Stone-Blessed" by traditional clans
- Gain Elder status in some communities
- Some clans exile you (fear of "returning to old ways")
- +1 additional AC (Goliath heritage + transformation stack)

**Progression Trigger:**
- Survive 1 year at Large size
- Defeat creature of equal or greater size in single combat
- OR: Complete giant rite of passage (varies by type)

---

### Stage 2: True Giant (Full Heritage)

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 1 active for 1+ year
- Defeated Large+ creature in combat
- OR: Accepted by giant clan

**Transformation Process:**
- Further growth spurt
- Features become distinctly giant-like
- Voice deepens to rumbling bass
- May develop giant mutations (see type)

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 1 Boons**
- **Size Increase**: Become Huge (if were Large)
  - Reach increases to 15 feet total
  - +2d6 damage on all melee attacks (replaces +1d4)
  - Weapons are now Huge-sized
- **Enhanced Attributes**: +4 Str, +4 Con (replaces +2/+2)
- **Giant Immunity**: Immunity to your element (replaces resistance)
- **Type-Specific Power**:
  - **Stone**: Can merge with stone (1/long rest, 8 hours)
  - **Frost**: Frost aura (10ft, creatures take 1d6 cold at start of turn)
  - **Fire**: Weapon attacks deal +1d6 fire damage
  - **Storm**: Can fly 40ft when thunderstorm present
  - **Cloud**: Can cast *Levitate* at will on self
- **Rock Catching**: Reaction to catch projectiles (AC +5 vs ranged)
- **Tremorsense**: 30 feet (Stone only)

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 1 Flaws (Enhanced)**
- **Cannot Enter Cities**: Too large for most buildings/gates
- **Increased Appetite**: Must eat 8× normal food
- **Equipment**: Costs 8× normal, very hard to find
- **Slow**: Speed reduced by 10 feet (massive weight)
- **Social Isolation**: Few mortals can relate to you

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -2 (world feels small, mortals seem fragile)
- Focus: +1 (primordial clarity)
- Thoughts slow down (patient, but less quick-witted)
- Mortal concerns seem trivial

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: -2 (huge target)
- Endure Evasion: +5 (nearly indestructible)
- Deflect Evasion: +1
- Natural DR: +4 (iron-hard skin)
- Reach advantage in combat

**Giant Society:**
- Accepted by giant clans (usually)
- Can trade with giants
- May establish own territory (valley, mountain peak)
- Other giants may challenge you for dominance

**Goliath-Specific:**
- Goliath clans either worship you or exile you
- Seen as "Titan Reborn"
- May become leader of Goliath tribe
- Or hunted as "corruption of heritage"

**Dragonborn Reaction:**
- Dragonborn see Giants as rivals (ancient enemy races)
- Expect hostility from Dragonborn NPCs
- "Dragon vs Giant" is archetypal conflict

**Progression Trigger:**
- Survive 10 years as Huge giant
- Defeat legendary creature (CR 15+)
- OR: Master primordial rune magic
- OR: Rule giant clan

---

### Stage 3: Primordial Titan (Legendary Giant)

**Prerequisites:**
- Stage 2 active for 10+ years
- Legendary deed (killed dragon, toppled castle, etc.)
- OR: Became giant chief/lord

**Transformation Process:**
- Final growth
- Become living geography (mountains tremble at approach)
- Element suffuses entire being
- Achieve near-immortality

**Boons:**
- **All Stage 2 Boons**
- **Size Increase**: Become Gargantuan (if were Huge)
  - Reach increases to 20 feet
  - +3d6 damage on melee attacks (replaces +2d6)
  - Can grapple/swallow Huge creatures
- **Legendary Attributes**: +6 Str, +6 Con, +4 Wis (replaces +4/+4)
- **Primordial Immortality**: Age 100× slower; can live 10,000+ years
- **Legendary Resistance**: 3/day
- **Type Mastery**:
  - **Stone**: Earth Glide (move through stone at half speed)
  - **Frost**: Ice Storm (call blizzard, 100ft radius, 1/day)
  - **Fire**: Summon fire elementals (3d6 magma mephits, 1/day)
  - **Storm**: Call Lightning (as spell, at will in storms)
  - **Cloud**: Wind Control (create/dismiss storms, 1/day)
- **Tremor Stomp**: Action - stomp creates 30ft radius earthquake; Dex save DC 18 or knocked prone + 4d6 bludgeoning
- **Lair Creation**: Can establish lair with lair actions (like dragon)

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 2 Flaws (Enhanced)**
- **Cannot Enter Civilization**: Too large for any humanoid structure
- **Increased Appetite**: Must eat 16× normal food or devour animals whole
- **Legendary Target**: Heroes will seek to slay you
- **Isolation**: Mortals are insects to you now
- **Probably NPC**: Most Titans become world-shaping NPCs

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -4 (time perception warped, mortals incomprehensible)
- Focus: +2 (timeless patience)
- Original personality distant memory
- Think in decades, not days

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: -3 (massive target)
- Endure Evasion: +7 (mountain made flesh)
- Deflect Evasion: +2
- Natural DR: +6 (granite skin)
- Legendary Resistance: 3/day
- Legendary Actions: 3/round
- Lair Actions if in domain

**Campaign Impact:**
- Reshape geography (footsteps create ravines)
- Weather changes when you're angry
- Entire armies required to challenge you
- May become campaign villain or ally
- Some worship you as god

**Goliath Apotheosis:**
- If Goliath: Achieve "First Goliath" status
- Legends say you're reborn progenitor
- Goliath tribes pilgrimage to see you
- You are effectively their deity

**VS Dragonborn:**
- Ancient enemy
- Dragons may come to challenge you
- Wyrm-transformed characters are natural rivals

### Stage 4: World Shaper (Mythic Titan)

**NPC Only:**
- CR 20-25 equivalent
- Kaiju-sized (can wrestle mountains)
- Elementals serve you
- Shape continents
- Immortal barring violence
- Campaign-defining entity

**Risks:**

# 10. WYRM TRANSFORMATION (Draconic Apotheosis)

**Type:** Independent System  
**Trigger:** Ancient draconic ritual, dragon blood infusion, centuries of study  
**Prerequisite:** Can cast 3rd-level spells OR Dragonborn lineage  
**Irreversible:** Yes (draconic nature is permanent)

## Philosophy

Wyrm transformation is **ascending to dragonhood**—your mortal flesh is slowly replaced by scales, claws, wings, and elemental fury. You don't just gain draconic power; you ARE becoming a dragon, with all the pride, greed, and terrible majesty that entails.

In Dór-o Estel, true dragons are ancient, rare, and revered or feared depending on their temperament. Mortals who undergo Wyrm transformation walk a path that may lead to true dragonhood—or monstrous corruption.

## Lineage Interactions

**Dragonborn:**
- **Strong Affinity**: Dragonborn ALWAYS have advantage on transformation
- **Faster Progression**: Dragonborn advance stages 2× faster
- **Enhanced Power**: Dragonborn Wyrms get +1 to breath weapon DCs
- **Cultural Pride**: Most Dragonborn clans celebrate this transformation
- **Clan Support**: Dragonborn Wyrms often sponsored by clans

**Other Lineages:**
- **Tieflings**: Conflicted (dragon vs devil heritage)
- **Aasimar**: Difficult (celestial vs draconic nature)
- **Kobolds**: If they existed, would worship you
- **Goliaths**: Incompatible with Giant transformation (choose one)

## Corruption Relationship

**Chromatic vs Metallic:**

**Metallic Dragons (Gold, Silver, Bronze, Copper, Brass):**
- Transformation REDUCES Corruption by 1d4 per stage
- Incompatible with CS 15+ (too corrupted to become metallic)
- Noble, wise, protective nature

**Chromatic Dragons (Red, Blue, Green, Black, White):**
- Transformation is NEUTRAL to Corruption
- Can become chromatic at any CS
- High-CS chromatics become nightmare dragons
- Cruel, greedy, destructive nature

**Gem Dragons (Amethyst, Crystal, Emerald, Sapphire, Topaz):**
- Transformation LOCKS Corruption at current level
- Psionic, alien, neutral nature

## Dragon Color Choice

**When transformation begins, choose:**

**Metallic (Good-aligned, reduces CS):**
- Gold - Fire breath, wisdom, nobility
- Silver - Cold breath, compassion, valor  
- Bronze - Lightning breath, coastal, martial
- Copper - Acid breath, trickery, wit
- Brass - Fire breath, sociable, desert

**Chromatic (Evil-aligned, neutral to CS):**
- Red - Fire breath, greed, pride, mountains
- Blue - Lightning breath, vanity, deserts  
- Green - Poison breath, deceit, forests
- Black - Acid breath, cruelty, swamps
- White - Cold breath, savagery, tundra

**Gem (Psionic, locks CS):**
- Amethyst - Force breath, psychic, regal
- Crystal - Radiant breath, reflective, arctic
- Emerald - Psychic breath, paranoid, underground
- Sapphire - Thunder breath, military, mountains
- Topaz - Necrotic breath, antisocial, coasts

*Stats below use Red (Chromatic) as baseline*

# 11. COVENBORN (Hag Transformation)

**Type:** Corruption-Adjacent Path  
**Trigger:** Dark bargain with hag coven, consumption of hag flesh, centuries of witchcraft  
**Prerequisite:** Female (traditionally), can cast 3rd-level spells  
**Irreversible:** Extremely difficult (requires true love's sacrifice)

## Philosophy

Covenborn transformation is **beauty becoming corruption**—your mortal form twists into something that embodies dark feminine power. Hags are not simply evil witches; they are living contradictions: wise yet cruel, beautiful yet hideous, nurturing yet devouring.

In Dór-o Estel, hags exist on the fringes—feared, consulted, and hated in equal measure. They make bargains, steal children, and grant wishes, always at terrible cost.

## Gender Notes

**Traditional**: Hags are female
**FEARS Adaptation**: Can affect any gender; transformation manifests differently:
- **Female**: Traditional hag (crone, witch, coven-mother)
- **Male**: "Hexer" or "Warlock-Hag" (twisted male equivalent)
- **Nonbinary**: "Changeling Hag" (shifting, ambiguous form)

*Stats below use traditional female hag; adjust as needed*

## Corruption Relationship

**Dark Bargain Path:**
- Requires CS 10+ to begin (must already be corrupted)
- Each stage ADDS CS (bargaining with darkness)
- **BUT**: Hag transformation gives CS benefits (can use CS as power)
- High-CS hags are most powerful (unlike most transformations)

### Stage 1: Hexed (The First Bargain)

**Prerequisites:**
- CS 10+ (must already be corrupted)
- Can cast 3rd-level spells (especially illusion/enchantment)
- Made bargain with hag coven OR consumed hag flesh
- OR: Born to hag mother (dormant, awakens)

**Transformation Process:**
- Subtle at first
- Nails grow sharp and black
- One eye changes (different color, cat-like pupil)
- Hair streaks with white/gray
- Can alternate between "beautiful" and "crone" appearance

**Boons:**
- **Dual Form**: Can appear beautiful (Deception to hide true nature) or reveal crone form
  - Beautiful form: +2 Charisma, advantage on Persuasion
  - Crone form: Frightful, advantage on Intimidation
- **Hag Magic**: Learn two enchantment/illusion spells (your choice, up to 2nd level)
- **Evil Eye**: Action - target within 30ft makes Wisdom save DC (8+prof+Cha) or cursed:
  - Disadvantage on saves vs your spells
  - You know their location within 1 mile
  - Lasts until you curse another or target succeeds on save (can retry at dawn)
- **Coven Sense**: Detect other hags within 1 mile
- **Nightmare Touch**: Your touch inflicts nightmares (target has disadvantage on long rest)

**Flaws:**
- **CS Increase**: Gain +2 CS immediately
- **Hag Bargain**: Owe service to hag coven (must complete tasks)
- **Iron Weakness**: Take +1d6 damage from cold iron weapons
- **Beauty Fades**: In true form, appear elderly and twisted
- **Alignment Shift**: Drift toward Evil
- **Compulsive Bargains**: Must make Wisdom save DC 12 to refuse bargain offer

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: -2 (embracing dark nature)
- Focus: +2 (witch's cunning)
- Personality becomes manipulative, cruel

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +0
- Endure Evasion: +1
- Deflect Evasion: +0
- Natural DR: +0

**Bargain Mechanics:**
- Can make binding contracts (like warlock patron)
- Typical bargains: beauty for years of life, love for firstborn, youth for soul
- Breaking bargain = curse (DM determines)

**Corruption as Power:**
- Can spend CS temporarily to boost spells
- Each CS spent = +1 to spell save DC or +1d6 damage
- CS returns after long rest

**Progression Trigger:**
- Survive 1 year hexed
- Make 13 bargains (traditional hag number)
- OR: Kill another hag and consume heart

  - Can cast 6th-level spells

  - Triple casting range

**Flaws:**
- **All Stage 1 Flaws (Enhanced)**
- **CS Increase**: Gain +3 CS (now CS 15+, possibly higher)

- **Bargain Compulsion**: DC increases to 15 to refuse bargain
- **Probably Evil**: Alignment shifts to NE, CE, or LE

- Sanity: -4 (dark magic corrupts)
- Focus: +4 (witch perfection)
- Original personality twisted, cruel

**DCS Integration:**

- Deflect Evasion: +1

**Green Hag:**

**Bheur Hag:**

- Can cast: *Identify*, *Ray of Enfeeblement*, *Hold Person*, *Locate Object*, *Bestow Curse*, *Scrying*, *Eyebite*

### Stage 4: Archag (Legendary Hag Queen)

**NPC Only:**

# 12. FLORASPAWN (Plant/Fungal Apotheosis)

**Type:** Independent System  
**Trigger:** Druid ritual, fungal infection, symbiosis with ancient plant  
**Prerequisite:** Druid levels OR exposure to primal nature magic  
**Irreversible:** Difficult (requires severing from root network)

## Philosophy

Floraspawn transformation is **green immortality**—your flesh becomes bark, your blood sap, your thoughts root-slow and patient. You don't die; you become part of the endless cycle of growth, decay, and rebirth.

Unlike other transformations (which corrupt or elevate), Floraspawn is LATERAL—you become different, alien, but not necessarily better or worse. You are no longer animal; you are vegetable.

## Corruption Relationship

**Nature's Cleansing:**
- Floraspawn transformation REDUCES CS by 1d6 per stage
- Incompatible with CS 20+ (Eldritch corruption kills plant life)
- Low-CS Floraspawn are wise guardians
- High-CS Floraspawn become "blighted" (corrupted plants)

## Flora Type

Choose when transformation begins:

**Tree-Kin** - Oak, willow, pine (wisdom, endurance)
**Fungal** - Mushroom, spore-bearer (decay, rebirth)
**Vine-Lord** - Ivy, strangler fig (growth, control)
**Flower-Heart** - Rose, lily, orchid (beauty, lure, poison)
**Moss-Mantle** - Lichen, moss (patience, erosion)

*Stats below use Tree-Kin (Oak) as baseline*

**Flaws:**

# 13. ELEMENTAL FUSION (Primordial Binding)

**Type:** Independent System  
**Trigger:** Binding with elemental spirit, exposure to elemental plane, primordial ritual  
**Prerequisite:** Can cast 3rd-level spells OR survived elemental catastrophe  
**Irreversible:** Yes (elemental essence fused to soul)

## Philosophy

Elemental Fusion is **primordial incarnation**—your mortal flesh becomes infused with raw elemental power. You don't just wield fire or command water; you ARE fire, you ARE water. Your body becomes a vessel for one of creation's fundamental forces.

In Dór-o Estel, elementals are echoes of the world's birth—pure expressions of fire, water, air, or earth before they were refined into Pattern-threads.

## Corruption Relationship

**Primordial Purity:**
- Elemental Fusion LOCKS CS at current level
- Cannot gain or lose CS while fused
- High-CS elementals become "Corrupted Primordials" (see variants)
- Low-CS elementals are pure manifestations

## Elemental Type

Choose when fusion begins:

**Fire** - Flame, heat, passion, destruction
**Water** - Flow, adaptation, healing, erosion  
**Air** - Wind, freedom, lightning, thought
**Earth** - Stone, endurance, growth, stability

*Stats below use Fire as baseline*

---

### Stage 1: Kindled (First Fusion)

**Prerequisites:**
- Can cast 3rd-level spells OR survived elemental catastrophe

**Transformation Process:**
- Painful elemental infusion over 1d4 days
- Eyes glow with elemental energy
- Skin temperature/texture changes
- Aura of elemental power

**Boons:**
- **Elemental Affinity**: Resistance to your element

- **Minor Manifestation**: Can create minor elemental effects (light flames, ripple water, gust wind, shape stone)
- **Elemental Speech**: Can communicate with elementals of your type
- **Primordial Sense**: Detect large concentrations of your element within 60ft

**Type-Specific:**
- **Fire**: Glow in darkness (dim light 10ft radius)
- **Water**: Water breathing, swim speed 30ft
- **Air**: Never fall (always gentle descent)
- **Earth**: Tremorsense 10ft

**Flaws:**
- **Opposing Vulnerability**: Vulnerable to opposing element:
  - Fire ↔ Cold/Water
  - Air ↔ Earth
  - Water ↔ Fire/Lightning
  - Earth ↔ Air/Thunder

- **Elemental Dependency**: Must be near your element 1/day or gain exhaustion
- **Temperature**: Uncomfortable in opposing environments
- **Obvious**: Elemental aura visible (glowing eyes, steaming skin, etc.)

**Mental Attributes:**
- Sanity: +0 (or -1 if Fire/Air, +1 if Water/Earth)
- Focus: +1 (elemental clarity)
- Personality becomes more elemental (passionate if Fire, calm if Water, etc.)

**DCS Integration:**
- Dodge Evasion: +1 (Air/Water) or +0 (Fire/Earth)
- Endure Evasion: +1 (Fire/Earth) or +0 (Air/Water)
- Deflect Evasion: +0
- Natural DR: +0

- Whatever CS you have now is permanent

# 16. CONSTRUCT TRANSFORMATION (Mechanical Apotheosis)

**Type:** Independent System (Technological)  
**Trigger:** Replace body parts with mechanical components, runic infusion, magitech surgery  
**Prerequisite:** Inventor levels OR access to master artificer/runesmith  
**Irreversible:** Extremely difficult (would need to regrow organic parts)

## Philosophy

Construct transformation is **mechanical transcendence**—you replace fallible flesh with perfect metal, unreliable biology with precise gears, mortality with immortal clockwork. You don't die; you become a machine that thinks, feels (maybe), and endures.

In Dór-o Estel, this is the Inventor's ultimate dream—proving that magic + mechanics can surpass nature. You are living proof that consciousness doesn't require biology.

## Corruption Relationship

**Mechanical Immunity:**
- Construct transformation makes you IMMUNE to Corruption (no longer biological)
- Whatever CS you had LOCKS permanently (cannot change)
- **BUT**: High-CS Constructs are "Corrupted Machines" (Eldritch-infused clockwork)
- CS 20+ Constructs may become portals to Null Reflection

## Construct Type

Choose your construction method when transformation begins:

**Clockwork** - Gears, springs, precision (steampunk)  
**Runic** - Magical runes carved into metal (arcane)  
**Magitech** - Magic crystals + technology (blend)  
**Living Iron** - Enchanted metal with pseudo-life (organic metal)  
**Bone-and-Steel** - Skeleton reinforced with metal (hybrid)

*Stats below use Clockwork as baseline*

## DM Implementation Guide

### Campaign Themes:

**High Fantasy Heroic:**
- Offer: Seraph, Wyrm (metallic), Primordial Beast
- Avoid: Aberrant, Fiend, Covenborn

**Dark Gothic Horror:**
- Offer: Vampire, Lich, Spectre, Aberrant
- Perfect for: Grim campaigns

**Nature/Primal:**
- Offer: Floraspawn, Primordial Beast, Fey
- Works well: Wilderness campaigns

**Magitech/Steampunk:**
- Offer: Construct, Living Spell
- Perfect for: Technology-heavy settings

**Corruption-Heavy:**
- Offer: Fiend, Aberrant, Covenborn
- Use: CS-focused campaigns

### Balance Considerations:

**Transformation Limits:**
- Max 1 transformation per PC
- Some are incompatible (Giant + Wyrm)
- Party should discuss before committing

**Power Scaling:**
- Stage 1: Equivalent to +1-2 character levels
- Stage 2: Equivalent to +3-4 character levels
- Stage 3: Legendary/NPC territory

**Time Investment:**
- Fast transformations (1-5 years) work for short campaigns
- Slow transformations (decades) work for long campaigns
- Plan accordingly
