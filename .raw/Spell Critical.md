# Disruptions of the Structured Weave

> A **Spell Critical** is not just an extreme success—it is a **rupture** in the Weave, a moment when magical forces surge beyond containment and mortal control. These events can awaken echoes, open rifts, or permanently alter reality itself.

---
## 📌 **When a Spell Critically Hits**

A Spell Critical is triggered under the following circumstances:

- A **natural 20** on a **spell attack roll**.
- A target rolls a **natural 1** on a **saving throw** against your spell.
- Some rare effects or magical zones may _force_ a Spell Critical under unique conditions (e.g., [[leyline]] pulse or corrupted glyph structures).

---

## **Spell Critical Effects** _(Resolve in this order)_

---
### 1. **Critical Hit Damage Tables**

- Roll on the **[[Critical Hit Damage Tables]]** tied to each damage type the spell uses.
- *Multiple Damage Types*: Roll separately on each table.

### 2. **Spell Upcast**

- The spell is treated as if cast at **1 spell level higher** than the level used.
- **Cantrips** scale as if the caster is **one level higher** than normal.

### 3. **Fear Effect**

- All **enemies within 60 feet** who witness the spell must make a **Sanity** or **Wisdom saving throw** (target's choice) against your **spell save DC**.
    - On failure, they are **Frightened** for **3 rounds**.
    - If already frightened, they must also roll a **Fracture Save** (DC 10 + your proficiency bonus) or suffer a **Fracture Effect** from the Sanity system.

### 4. **Focus Check**

- Roll a D6 - Even nothing happens, Odd use the rules below
- Perform a **Focus Check** (d20 + your Focus modifier) vs. your own **spell save DC**:

| **Focus Result** | **Effect**                                                                                                                                           |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Natural 20**   | An **[[Enhanced Arcane Rift]]** forms. It amplifies all spells of that school and surges unpredictably (use Enhanced Arcane Rift rules) .            |
| **Success**      | A standard **[[Arcane Rift]]** appears (see Arcane Rifts for school-based effects). It empowers similar magic and destabilizes oppositional schools. |
| **Failure**      | The spell causes a **[[Wild Magic Zone]]** to form. Roll on the **Wild Magic Surge Table** linked to the spell’s school .                            |
| **Natural 1**    | The area becomes a **[[Dead Magic Zone]]**. All concentration spells fail. Spellcasting here becomes impossible until the Weave stabilizes.          |

> 🌀 **Notes:**
> - A caster with a **Spirit Thread resonance** may roll this check **with advantage** in sacred or emotionally charged areas.
> - Certain classes or subclasses (e.g., Circle of Echoes Druids, Moonscribes) can alter or delay Rift formation.

---
### 5. **Wild Magic Surge Roll**

- If a **[[Wild Magic Zone]]** is formed, the caster must **immediately roll** on the **[[School-specific Wild Magic Table]]**.
- Echo Glyphs and class features used inside the zone may cause **glyph flares** or **Echo Fragmentation**, requiring additional rolls.

---
### 6. **Echo Glyph Surge**

- If the spell is **eligible** for an Echo Glyph (you know one or have space to learn):
    - Attempt a **Glyphcraft Check** to learn or enhance an Echo Glyph:
        > d20 + spellcasting ability + proficiency
        > **DC = 10 + spell level**
- On success, you may:
    - **Learn** a new Echo Glyph tied to the spell.
    - **Swap** your current Glyph if you already have one.
    - **Imprint** the Glyph into your Echo Journal (if permitted by subclass/culture)Echo Glyphs.

> [[Warlocks]], [[Witches]], and some [[Inventors]] have **modified learning rules** (see class entries).

---
##  **Consequences and Risk**

###  **Instability**

- **[[Arcane Rifts]]** can permanently change the area. Some **[[leylines]] record** these events, causing future spells to behave unpredictably.
- [[Wild Magic Zones]] are volatile and may spread if not contained. NPCs and creatures in the zone may become **[[Glyphburned]]**.

### **Concentration Stress**

- If maintaining concentration on the critical spell:
    - Roll **Concentration Checks at disadvantage**.
    - On failure: Spell remains active but reverts to **normal level**.
    - In a **[[Dead Magic Zone]]**: Concentration is **immediately lost** for all active spells.

---
# **Integration Notes**

- **Magical zones** (Wild, Dead, Rifted, Eldritch) directly interact with this system.
- **[[Sanity]]**, **Whispers**, and **Fractures** may be triggered as secondary effects—especially if a spell touches Spirit, Eldritch, or Memory Threads.
- **Glyph Class Synergy**: Classes with Echo Glyph features may trigger **Glyph Awakening** or **Reactive Learning** when a Spell Critical occurs.
