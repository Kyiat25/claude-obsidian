# Aasimar
## Lineage

> *"You are not the light. You are the shadow it casts when truth hides its face."*  
> — **The Oath of the Veiled Star**

You are Aasimar. Not angel, not chosen—**marked**. Your people do not walk in divine glory or bask in celestial radiance. You are **living omens**, prophecies made flesh, beings who exist because the Pattern needed someone to act when all else falls silent. Where others receive divine guidance clearly, you receive **fragments, whispers, and veiled truths** that you must piece together alone.

You were not made to shine. You were made to **watch, to wait, and to act when the moment demands it**. Your ancestors stood at the edge of catastrophe, each proving that sometimes mercy requires silence, that justice must be unseen, and that prophecy too clear becomes poison.

---

## The Star-Marked

According to the **Oath of the Veiled Star**—the faith followed by many Aasimar—your people were not born of divine blessing or celestial parentage. You are **Star-Marked**: beings carried into form by prophecy withheld, needed when memory fails and the Pattern frays at its edges.

> *"Before flame, glyph, or god—there was the Pattern unwoven. And between silence and shadow, something watched."*

The **Veiled Star** itself is older than the gods, a pre-cosmic force of restraint that refused to be forgotten but also refused to speak too soon. When the gods warred and the Threads formed, the Veiled Star remained hidden—never woven, never worshipped—watching for the moment when intervention would be necessary.

From its shrouded glow came the first Aasimar: not chosen, but **needed**. You are not divine champions but **divine restraint made flesh**. Your purpose is not to proclaim but to **intervene**—when prophecy screams too loud, when truth arrives too early, when the Eldritch breach and no one else can act.

This is not burden—it is **sacred necessity**. You exist in the space between divine will and mortal need, walking beneath stars that speak in silence.

---

## Life in the Veil

You were raised knowing that **you are different**, that others see signs in your very existence, that your dreams carry weight you don't always understand. Even in the most accepting communities, you feel the pressure of **unspoken purpose**.

**Calling (star'ven)** is not metaphorical. It is:
- Received through dreams, visions, and impossible certainty
- Interpreted through symbols, not clear instruction
- Acted upon through judgment, not blind obedience
- The burden of knowing you must act when others cannot

Your lifespan is roughly **160 years**—longer than humans but shorter than elves, as if the divine spark burns your life brighter and briefer. You measure worth not in years lived but in **moments of intervention, prophecies fulfilled, and truths preserved** through your actions.

Every significant action becomes part of your **Star-Path**—the pattern of choices that defines why you were needed, recorded not in books but in the echo you leave on the Pattern itself.

## Physical Characteristics

**Age:** You mature at the same rate as humans, reaching adulthood around 20. However, many Aasimar don't understand their true purpose until later in life—some receive their calling at 30, 50, or even older. You can live 160-180 years.

**Size:** You stand between 5 to 6½ feet tall, with builds that range from athletic to ethereal. Your size is Medium.

**Speed:** Your base walking speed is 30 feet. You move with purpose, neither rushed nor casual.

**Build:** You resemble humans in basic structure, but with subtle signs of your celestial nature: skin that catches light strangely, eyes that seem to glow faintly in darkness, hair with unusual luster or faint iridescence, features that seem almost too perfect or too intense.

**Appearance Notes:**
- Your appearance is **unsettling** rather than beautiful—people sense something otherworldly
- Some have faint patterns on their skin that shift with emotion or moonlight
- Your shadow sometimes moves wrong, or appears darker than it should
- In moments of strong emotion, you might emit faint radiance or cast no shadow at all
- Many bear **Star-Marks**—birthmarks shaped like constellations or sacred geometry

## Aasimar Traits

As an Aasimar, you possess the following lineage traits:

### **Darkvision**
You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

Your celestial nature grants you sight in darkness, though some say you see **too clearly**—perceiving things others miss.

### **Celestial Resistance**
You have resistance to necrotic damage and radiant damage.

Your nature exists between life and death, light and shadow, protected by the paradox of your existence.

### **Healing Hands**
As an action, you can touch a creature and cause it to regain a number of hit points equal to your level. Once you use this trait, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

This represents the gentle mercy of your calling—the quiet intervention that saves lives without acclaim.

### **Light Bearer**
You know the *light* cantrip. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for it.

You can call upon illumination when needed, though many Aasimar prefer to work in shadow.

### **Veiled Purpose** *(Cultural Trait)*
You have proficiency in Insight. Additionally, once per long rest when you make an Insight check, you can add double your proficiency bonus to the roll. You perceive truths others miss—not through divine revelation, but through patterns and silence.

### **Languages**
You can speak, read, and write Common and Celestial.

Celestial is the language of divine beings, filled with harmonics that carry meaning beyond words. Speaking it feels like remembering something you never learned.

## Mental Attributes & Psychology

**Focus Modifier:** +0  
**Sanity Modifier:** +1

Your mental baseline shows your celestial heritage clearly: your **Sanity receives a bonus** because Aasimar are psychologically conditioned by prophecy and purpose. You're trained from your first vision to process impossible truths, fragmentary revelations, and the weight of unspoken knowledge as **necessary burden** rather than madness.

This isn't cultural—it's woven into your very being. The divine spark that marks you also **shields your mind** from breaking under the weight of terrible knowledge.

### **Psychological Identity**
Your mental landscape is defined by **purpose and paradox**:

- **Prophetic weight:** You carry knowledge you don't fully understand
- **Restrained action:** You must judge when to act and when to remain silent
- **Burden of clarity:** You see patterns others miss, sometimes wishing you didn't
- **Sacred doubt:** Unlike paladins, your certainty comes through fragments, not commandments

### **Corruption Vulnerability: The Screaming Star**

As an Aasimar, you have a unique relationship with Corruption. Your celestial nature provides significant protection against chaos and darkness, but corruption that enters through **prophecy, impossible revelation, or forced clarity** affects you catastrophically.

The Eldritch Moons specifically target your kind—attempting to turn your gift of veiled truth into **truth too pure to survive**.

**When you gain Corruption Score 10+ (Tier 2), you suffer:**
- Vision-Bleed: Your dreams become others' nightmares
- Star-Burn: Your mark becomes painful, as if rejecting you
- Clarity-Madness: You begin seeing patterns that don't exist

**Veiled Fracture Table (Corruption Tier 2+)**

When you complete a long rest at Corruption Score 10+, roll 1d6:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **False Prophecy:** You receive a vision that feels true but is entirely fabricated. Make a DC 15 Wisdom save or believe it completely until proven wrong. |
| 2 | **Star-Wound:** Your celestial mark burns. You cannot use your Healing Hands ability until your next long rest, and touching others causes you pain. |
| 3 | **Purpose-Void:** Your sense of calling fractures. Take 1d4 psychic damage and lose proficiency in Insight until your next long rest. |
| 4 | **Weight of Clarity:** You see too much, too clearly. Make a DC 12 Sanity save or lose 1d4 Sanity as revelations overwhelm you. |
| 5 | **Eldritch Touch:** One of the Eldritch Moons speaks directly to you. Gain 1 Whisper Point but your next use of Veiled Purpose reveals something terrible instead of helpful. |
| 6 | **Star Holds:** Your celestial nature fights back. Reduce your Corruption Score by 1 (minimum 10). |

## Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Integration

### **Evasion Profile: Balanced Guardian**
- **Dodge Evasion:** +0 (you move with purpose, not panic)
- **Endure Evasion:** +1 (your celestial nature helps you withstand punishment)
- **Deflect Evasion:** +0 (you don't rely on parrying or deflection)

**Tactical Note:** You are designed to **support and intervene**. Your transformation abilities let you adapt to combat needs, while your healing provides crucial support.

### **Natural Damage Reduction:** +0
You have no natural armor. Your protection comes from your resistance to radiant and necrotic damage, not physical toughness.

### **Combat Philosophy**
Aasimar fight with **purposeful intervention and adaptive tactics**. You assess situations, wait for the crucial moment, then act decisively. Your transformation (described in subraces) provides burst capability when needed. You're not a frontline tank or glass cannon—you're the **tactical adapter** who does what the moment requires.

### **Breaking Point Mechanics**
Due to your prophetic conditioning, you have **advantage on saving throws against fear effects** and **+1 bonus to Breaking Point DCs** (harder for you to panic). However, when corruption reaches you through prophecy, you become **more vulnerable** to mental breaks.

## Languages

**Languages:** You can speak, read, and write Common and Celestial.

**Celestial** is the language of the divine, filled with harmonics and resonances that convey meaning on multiple levels simultaneously. Speaking it feels like pulling words from collective memory—every Aasimar knows Celestial instinctively, though none remember learning it.

When you speak Celestial, others sometimes hear echoes of their own forgotten prayers or half-remembered hymns their ancestors sang.

## Aasimar Subraces

While all Aasimar are Star-Marked, different manifestations of celestial purpose have created distinct forms. Choose one of the following subraces:

### **Protector Aasimar** *(Guardian of the Innocent)*
You are called to shield others, to stand between harm and those who cannot defend themselves.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Wisdom score increases by 2, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Radiant Soul:** Starting at 3rd level, you can use your action to unleash the divine energy within yourself, causing your eyes to glimmer and two luminous, spectral wings to sprout from your back temporarily.

Your transformation lasts for 1 minute or until you end it as a bonus action. During it, you have a flying speed of 30 feet, and once on each of your turns, you can deal extra radiant damage to one target when you deal damage to it with an attack or a spell. The extra radiant damage equals your level.

Once you use this trait, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

**Guardian's Insight:** You have advantage on Wisdom (Insight) checks to determine if someone is lying or in danger.

### **Scourge Aasimar** *(Burning Judgment)*
You are called to destroy evil, to burn away corruption even if it means burning yourself.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Constitution score increases by 2, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Radiant Consumption:** Starting at 3rd level, you can use your action to unleash the divine energy within yourself, causing searing light to radiate from you, pour out of your eyes and mouth, and threaten to char you.

Your transformation lasts for 1 minute or until you end it as a bonus action. During it, you shed bright light in a 10-foot radius and dim light for an additional 10 feet, and at the end of each of your turns, you and each creature within 10 feet of you take radiant damage equal to half your level (rounded up). In addition, once on each of your turns, you can deal extra radiant damage to one target when you deal damage to it with an attack or a spell. The extra radiant damage equals your level.

Once you use this trait, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

**Zealous Endurance:** You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened.

### **Fallen Aasimar** *(Shadow of Purpose)*
Your calling twisted, your purpose corrupted, or your judgment deemed necessary by forces that terrify others. You are not evil—you are **sharp mercy**, the hard choice others won't make.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 2, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Necrotic Shroud:** Starting at 3rd level, you can use your action to unleash the divine energy within yourself, causing your eyes to turn into pools of darkness and two skeletal, ghostly, flightless wings to sprout from your back temporarily. The instant you transform, other creatures within 10 feet of you that can see you must succeed on a Charisma saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier) or become frightened of you until the end of your next turn.

Your transformation lasts for 1 minute or until you end it as a bonus action. During it, once on each of your turns, you can deal extra necrotic damage to one target when you deal damage to it with an attack or a spell. The extra necrotic damage equals your level.

Once you use this trait, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

**Shadow's Mercy:** You have advantage on Intimidation checks and on saving throws against effects that would reveal your thoughts or intentions.

### **Veiled Aasimar** *(Silent Watcher)*
You are called not to act but to **observe and report**, to gather the pieces of prophecy others miss.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Wisdom score increases by 2, and your Intelligence score increases by 1.

**Shrouded Presence:** Starting at 3rd level, you can use your action to activate a veil of divine secrecy. For 1 minute, you gain the following benefits:

- You become invisible while in dim light or darkness
- You gain advantage on Stealth checks
- You can move through occupied spaces as if they were difficult terrain
- Once per turn, when you make an attack from hiding, you can add radiant damage equal to your level

Your transformation lasts for 1 minute or until you end it as a bonus action.

Once you use this trait, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

**Prophetic Warning:** When a creature you can see within 30 feet is about to be hit by an attack, you can use your reaction to grant them a bonus to AC equal to your proficiency bonus against that attack. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, regaining all uses when you finish a long rest.

### **Radiant Aasimar** *(Bearer of True Light)*
You are called to **illuminate**, to reveal truth and dispel deception—even when that truth is painful.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Charisma score increases by 2, and your Wisdom score increases by 1.

**Radiant Revelation:** Starting at 3rd level, you can use your action to channel pure celestial light through your being. For 1 minute, you gain the following benefits:

- You shed bright light in a 20-foot radius and dim light for an additional 20 feet
- Invisible creatures within the bright light are revealed
- Illusions within the bright light appear translucent
- Once per turn, when you deal radiant damage, you can force the target to make a Constitution saving throw (DC 8 + proficiency + Charisma modifier) or be blinded until the end of your next turn
- You can add your Charisma modifier to radiant damage you deal

Your transformation lasts for 1 minute or until you end it as a bonus action.

Once you use this trait, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

**Truth Sense:** You automatically detect when someone speaks a direct lie in your presence (though not what the truth is).

## Aasimar Names

Aasimar naming reflects their dual nature—born to mortal parents but marked by celestial purpose.

**Birth Names** (Given by parents):
- Often human names: Elara, Marcus, Aria, Tomas, Seraphina, Gabriel
- Sometimes celestial-influenced: Celestian, Lumina, Solara, Aethon, Radiance
- May be names of hope: Dawn, Grace, Justice, Mercy, Haven

**Celestial Names** (Revealed in visions):
- Single-word epithets: Vigilant, Merciful, Judging, Veiled, Burning
- Abstract concepts: Silence-Before-Dawn, Shadow-of-Truth, Light-That-Warns
- Sometimes just sensations or impossible sounds only other Aasimar can pronounce

**Oath Names** (Taken by those who follow the Veiled Star):
- Star-marked titles: Watcher-of-Signs, Bearer-of-Silence, Reader-of-Fragments
- Often kept secret except from other Oathbearers

**Typical Pattern:** Most Aasimar use their birth name in daily life but are known by a celestial name to other Aasimar or in moments of revelation:
- "Marcus" to friends
- "The Vigilant One" to other Aasimar
- "Watcher-of-the-Third-Sign" to Oathbearers

## Aasimar in Dór-o Estel

Aasimar occupy a strange position in society—revered by some, feared by others, and misunderstood by most. Your very existence is interpreted as omen, blessing, or curse depending on the observer.

**After the Collapse of Ashkarra**, many Aasimar believe the catastrophe proved the necessity of the Veiled Star's restraint. The **Day of Twelve Shadows** (when all Eldritch Moons aligned) is considered the Veiled Star's darkest failure—some say the Oath should have acted sooner, others that even silence couldn't prevent such catastrophe.

The **Faith of the Bound Pattern** has complex views on Aasimar. During **Spirit or Water Cycles**, you're often welcomed as prophetic interpreters or Dreamguards. During other cycles, especially times of celestial instability, you're viewed with suspicion.

When working with other races:

**Humans**: Awe mixed with fear. They see you as touched by divinity but aren't sure if that's blessing or burden.

**Elves**: Philosophical interest. Long-lived elves remember generations of Aasimar and track patterns in your appearance.

**Dwarves**: Pragmatic respect. If you do good work and keep your oaths, your celestial nature is just another tool.

**Halflings**: Surprising comfort. They treat you as just another person blessed with unique gifts, like their own joy-touch.

**Tieflings**: Profound kinship. Both marked by supernatural heritage, both judged for what you are rather than who you are. Many close friendships and romances occur between Aasimar and Tieflings.

**Dragonborn**: Conflicted. Orthodox Concord members see you as unstructured divine chaos. Some view you as proof that purpose can exist outside caste.

**Orcs**: Respectful distance. They understand trial and purpose, but find your prophetic burden strange compared to their ancestor-weight.

**Other Aasimar**: Immediate recognition. You can spot each other even in crowds, sensing the shared burden.

## Adventure Hooks

**The First Vision**: You've just received your first clear prophetic dream. It shows disaster coming, but the details are fragmentary. You must piece together the truth before it's too late.

**The Star-Call**: An older Aasimar finds you and explains you've been Called—chosen for a specific purpose. But as you investigate, you discover the Call might not be from the Veiled Star at all.

**The Fallen Choice**: You must make an impossible decision—save many innocents or prevent a greater catastrophe. Either choice will haunt you, but inaction is not an option.

**The Oath-Breaking**: Another Aasimar has revealed prophecy too soon, causing catastrophe. You must find them and decide whether to help, stop, or join them.

**The Moon-Touched**: You dream during an Eldritch Moon rise and receive impossible clarity. The Oath considers you contaminated. Can you prove the vision was true, not poisoned?

**The Silent War**: Eldritch cults are spreading. The Veiled Star calls its children to act, but you must fight in shadow—no glory, no recognition, only necessary violence.

## Integration with Mental Systems

### **Focus & Sanity:**
Your **+1 Sanity** is significant:
- You process prophetic visions without madness
- Fear affects you less than others
- You resist mental manipulation through purpose
- Terrible truths don't break you—they inform you

**Typical Aasimar Mental Strategy:**
- Frame horrors as information, not trauma
- Use prophetic certainty to maintain composure
- Trust in your calling when doubt would paralyze others
- Accept that sometimes you must know terrible things

### **Corruption Concerns:**
Your unique corruption vulnerability centers on **prophecy and revelation**:
- Eldritch Moons specifically target Aasimar
- Forced clarity can shatter your mind
- False visions corrupt your purpose
- At Tier 2+, the Veiled Fracture table reflects prophetic breakdown

### **Rally & Morale:**
Aasimar have unique morale advantages:
- Your presence inspires hope (literally—you emit faint radiance)
- Seeing an Aasimar stand firm reassures allies that providence is present
- Your transformations create dramatic rally moments
- However, if you fall or flee, allied morale suffers greatly

## Design Philosophy

The Aasimar lineage is designed to support:

1. **The Prophetic Guardian** (high Sanity + protective abilities create reliable defenders)
2. **The Burning Zealot** (Scourge transformation + resistance create aggressive purifiers)
3. **The Shadow Mercy** (Fallen variant creates morally complex judgment characters)
4. **The Silent Observer** (Veiled variant creates infiltrators and information gatherers)
5. **The Truth-Bearer** (Radiant variant creates social and support specialists)

Your mechanical abilities (transformations, Healing Hands, celestial resistance, high Sanity) create a **character who intervenes at crucial moments**. You're designed to adapt to situations, provide crucial support, and act when others cannot.

The subrace options allow you to emphasize different aspects of celestial purpose—from the protective Radiant Soul to the destructive Radiant Consumption to the terrifying Necrotic Shroud—while maintaining the core Aasimar identity of purposeful intervention.

## Final Notes

### **The Weight of Prophecy:**
Every Aasimar carries prophetic pressure. Your character likely:
- Has experienced at least one prophetic dream or vision
- Knows at least one other Aasimar's story
- Struggles with the question "Why was I marked?"
- Feels the weight of purpose even in quiet moments

This isn't burden—it's **clarity**. You know you matter, even if you don't fully understand how yet.

### **Respect Across Cultures:**
Modern Dór-o Estel has complex views on Aasimar:
- Spirit-aligned cities welcome you as dream-readers
- Fire-aligned regions distrust your restraint
- Tieflings see you as kindred souls marked by heritage
- Common folk alternate between reverence and fear

### **The Star That Watches:**
Your people believe that existence is shaped by moments of crucial intervention. The Veiled Star doesn't speak clearly because **prophecy too pure becomes poison**—truth must arrive at the right time, in the right way, through the right person.

You adventure knowing that your very existence is an omen, that your choices ripple through the Pattern, that you were needed even if you don't yet know why. This is not pressure to fear—it is **certainty** that when the moment comes, you will know what must be done.
