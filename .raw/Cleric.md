# The Cleric

**Divine Conduits of Transcendent Power**

> *"A god's will doesn't need steel to be felt. It needs someone willing to carry it."*

---

## Faith as Force

The battlefield was chaos — wounded allies, summoned horrors, a cleric who had spent her most powerful spells two rounds ago. She had nothing left, she thought, except her faith. Then the holy symbol in her hand grew warm and the undead at the edge of the fight scattered. Channel Divinity is not a resource you spend. It is a reminder of what you are.

You are the Cleric. Not because you heal — though you do that better than almost anyone. Because you are the link between the mortal struggle and the divine will behind it. **Channel Divinity** is that link made mechanical: a direct negotiation with your deity, bypassing the careful management of spell slots to invoke raw divine power at the moment it's needed.

In FEARS, your Momentum engine is the battlefield itself. **Divine Rhythm** at 6th level grants you +1 Momentum whenever your healing restores an ally past a major HP threshold. You generate combat advantage by keeping your party alive — the role you were born to play.

---

## The Cleric's Essence

You are the party's healer, battlefield support, and divine hammer against undead and fiends. Your prepared spell list gives you flexibility no known-spell class can match — switch your approach between long rests to match the day's challenges.

You struggle in fights where you need to be purely offensive. You excel in extended encounters where your healing, Channel Divinity, and sustained spellcasting keep the party ahead of attrition.

---

## Complexity Assessment: MODERATE (4/10)                    

The Cleric tracks:                                           
• Prepared spells (WIS modifier + level)       
• Spell slots (full caster)                                 
• Channel Divinity uses (1–4 per rest)                    
• Class Reaction pool (PB uses per short rest)             
• Momentum (Divine Rhythm on threshold-crossing heals)  

Recommended for players who enjoy adaptive strategy, supporting allies, and turning the tide of difficult fights.

## Class Features

### Hit Points

- **Hit Dice:** 1d8 per Cleric level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 8 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per Cleric level after 1st

### Proficiencies

- **Armour:** Light armour, medium armour, shields
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons
- **Tools:** None
- **Saving Throws:** Wisdom, Charisma
- **Skills:** Choose two from Crafting, History, Insight, Medicine, Persuasion, Religion, and Resolve

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) a mace or (b) a warhammer (if proficient)
- (a) scale mail, (b) leather armour, or (c) chain mail (if proficient)
- (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) any simple weapon
- (a) a priest's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
- A shield and a holy symbol

---

## Quick Build

To quickly create a Cleric, follow these steps:

1. **Ability Scores:** Make Wisdom your highest score, followed by Constitution.
2. **Lineage and Heritage:** Any combination works; Human, Dwarf, and Aasimar are thematic but not required.
3. **Background:** Acolyte, Hermit, or any background with Religion or Insight.
4. **Combat Strategy:** Prepare a mix of healing, control, and damage spells. Use Channel Divinity proactively before you need it — don't save it until enemies are already winning.

## Core Class Features

### Spellcasting (1st Level)

Clerics use Divine magic. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability.

- **Spell Save DC** = 8 + PB + Wisdom modifier
- **Spell Attack Modifier** = PB + Wisdom modifier

You prepare spells equal to your Wisdom modifier + Cleric level. Preparing new spells requires prayer (1 minute per spell circle). Ritual spells can be cast without spell slots.

---

### Divine Domain (1st Level)

Choose a **Divine Domain** (Life, War, Knowledge, Light, Death, etc.). At 1st level, gain two subclass features specific to your domain, plus Domain Spells always prepared that don't count against your prepared spell limit.

---

### Manifestation of Faith (1st Level)

Choose one:

**Manifest Might:** Gain proficiency with heavy armour and one martial weapon. When you hit with a weapon attack, add radiant or necrotic damage equal to your Proficiency Bonus.

**Manifest Miracles:** Learn one additional cantrip from any spell list (counts as Divine for you). Add your Proficiency Bonus to the damage of any Divine cantrip.

---

### Channel Divinity (2nd Level)

Channel divine power to create magical effects. Begin with **Turn the Profane** at 2nd level. Gain additional uses at 6th, 13th, and 18th levels as shown in the table.

**Turn the Profane:** Force undead and fiends within 30 feet to make a Wisdom saving throw. On failure, they are turned for 1 minute or until damaged.

**Ward of Faith (Reaction via Channel Divinity):** When an allied creature within 30 feet would lose Momentum, expend one use of Channel Divinity to reduce their Momentum loss by 1 (to a minimum of 0).

**Judgment (Bonus Action via Channel Divinity):** Your next weapon attack or spell attack this turn counts as a Called Shot with no attack roll penalty. Must attack before end of your turn or the use is wasted.

---

### Harness Divine Power (2nd Level)

As a bonus action, expend one use of Channel Divinity to regain one spell slot of a level equal to or less than half your Proficiency Bonus (rounded up). Example: at 5th level (PB +3), regain a 2nd-level slot.

---

### Cleric Subclass (3rd Level, and 7th, 11th, 15th Level)

Your Divine Domain provides subclass features at 3rd, 7th, 11th, and 15th levels.

---

### Destroy the Profane (5th Level and beyond)

When undead or fiends fail their save against Turn the Profane, destroy them if their CR is equal to or lower than the threshold shown:

| Cleric Level | CR Threshold |
|---|---|
| 5th | 1/2 |
| 8th | 1 |
| 11th | 2 |
| 14th | 3 |
| 17th | 4 |

---

### Sacred Guidance (6th Level)

As a bonus action, grant one creature within 30 feet a bonus to their next attack roll or saving throw equal to your Proficiency Bonus. Uses equal to your Wisdom modifier per long rest.

---

### Divine Rhythm (6th Level)

When you restore HP to an allied creature and that healing brings them above a HP threshold (from Critical to Bloodied, Bloodied to Wounded, or Wounded to Full), you gain **+1 Momentum**. Your divine magic flows more easily when it brings someone back from the brink.

---

### Blessed Strikes (8th Level)

When you hit with a weapon attack or a cantrip, deal an additional **1d8 radiant damage** once per turn.

---

### Divine Intervention (9th Level)

Once per long rest, call on your deity to intervene. Roll a d20 + Proficiency Bonus. On a 20+, the deity intervenes, casting a spell or performing an act of divine power.

---

### Heroic Boon (10th Level)

Choose one:

**Gift of Consecration:** Immune to disease and poison damage, and the poisoned condition. Once per short rest, if reduced to 0 HP, instead reduced to 1 HP and gain temporary HP equal to twice your Cleric level.

**Gift of Wrath:** Once per short rest, when you deal radiant or necrotic damage, double the damage dice. Enemies within 10 feet take radiant damage equal to your Wisdom modifier whenever they hit you with a melee attack (Aura of Retribution).

---

### Mercy of the Divine (10th Level)

*Reaction.* Once per long rest, when an allied creature you can see within 30 feet would suffer an Injury, use your reaction to downgrade that Injury by one tier: Major becomes Minor; Minor is negated (creature still takes the damage but suffers no Injury effects).

---

### Blessed Resilience (13th Level)

When you take damage from a spell or magical effect, reduce the damage by an amount equal to your Wisdom modifier. Uses equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest.

---

### Turn the Profane Expanded (14th Level)

Your Turn the Profane now affects any creature deeply corrupted or fundamentally opposed to your divine patron — including creatures with high Corruption scores (typically 8+) or entities antithetical to your deity's domain. The GM determines which creatures qualify for your specific deity.

---

### Epic Boon (20th Level)

Choose one:

**Divine Herald:** Automatically succeed on Divine Intervention rolls. Gain a second use of Divine Intervention, but only once per long rest.

**Avatar of Divinity:** Gain flight (60 feet, wings of radiant or necrotic energy). Radiate an aura of power: allies within 30 feet gain resistance to radiant and necrotic damage; heal 1d6 + WIS modifier HP at the start of your turns; enemies within 30 feet take radiant or necrotic damage equal to your Wisdom modifier at the start of their turns.

---

## Last Stand Options

When preparing to meet their deity, a Cleric draws upon their divine connection for one final act of devotion.

**Rules for Last Stand:** Invoked when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, roll a death saving throw, or die outright. A free action requiring no reaction or consciousness. Once resolved, the character permanently dies.

---

### 1. Heaven's Gate

*Their kingdom come — your deity accepts you through an open portal revealing their fury and splendour.*

**Effect:**
- All enemies within 1000 feet make a DEX saving throw (DC = 10 + PB + WIS modifier).
- Failure: radiant damage equal to twice your character level; blinded and deafened for 1 minute; knocked prone; drop all held items.
- Success: radiant damage equal to your character level; knocked prone.
- Ignores line of effect. Legendary Resistance cannot be used.

---

### 2. Acts of God

*You become a vessel for your deity's most fundamental act — creation or destruction in its purest form.*

**Effect:**
- Choose one option representing your deity's domain, which manifests over a 100-foot radius for 5 minutes.
- Examples: a Life cleric's death reverses (allies regain 2d8 HP at start of each turn); a War cleric's battlefield transforms (all attacks are critical hits for allies); a Death cleric's area becomes a necrotic wasteland (enemies take 2d10 necrotic at start of each turn).
- Work with your GM to define the appropriate manifestation for your deity.

---

### 3. Martyr's Blessing

*You pour your entire divine connection into your companions, leaving nothing of yourself.*

**Effect:**
- All allies within 100 feet gain the following for 5 minutes:
  - Cannot fail death saving throws.
  - Immune to necrotic, radiant, and psychic damage.
  - Deal maximum damage on all attack rolls and spell damage.
  - Any ally reduced to 0 HP immediately regains 1 HP and can continue acting.
- Your body dissolves into divine light.

| Last Stand Option | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Heaven's Gate** | 1000-foot area DEX save; radiant damage + blind/prone (no Legendary Resistance). | Devastating multiple enemies. |
| **Acts of God** | Domain manifestation for 5 minutes over 100 feet. | Thematically appropriate climax. |
| **Martyr's Blessing** | Allies cannot fail death saves; immune to multiple damage types; deal max damage for 5 minutes. | Ensuring party survives and wins. |

## Class Reactions

Clerics have access to reactions that protect allies and channel divine energy at critical moments. These reactions use your **Proficiency Bonus pool**, refreshing on short or long rest.

### Blessed Ward (2nd Level)

**Trigger:** An ally within 30 feet makes a saving throw.
**Effect:** Add 1d4 to their roll (after they see the result but before the outcome is determined).

---

### Sacred Interposition (6th Level)

**Trigger:** An ally within 30 feet would take damage from a spell or magical effect.
**Effect:** Halve the damage. Once per long rest; not limited by reaction pool.

---

*(Listed under Core Class Features above as a separate reaction feature.)*

### Divine Reprisal (13th Level)

**Trigger:** A creature within 30 feet hits you with a melee attack.
**Effect:** That creature takes radiant or necrotic damage (your choice) equal to **2d8 + WIS modifier**. Makes a WIS save (DC = 8 + PB + WIS) or be frightened of you until end of their next turn.
