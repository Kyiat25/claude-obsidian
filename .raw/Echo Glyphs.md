# Echo Glyphs

_“All glyphs speak. Some echo.”_ — Vaedoris, the Soulward Shield

Where **Primary Glyphs** shape reality and **Secondary Glyphs** structure it, **Echo Glyphs** represent a third, more personal layer—**reflections of magic shaped by memory, culture, and will**. They are not part of the original Weave. They are **variations**, shaped through experience and preserved in magical consciousness like a whispered refrain.

**Echo Glyphs** are the third and most personal tier of glyphic magic in the world of [[Dór-o Estel]]. Unlike the eternal truths of the **[[Primary Glyphs]]** or the structured forms of **[[Secondary Glyphs]]**, Echo Glyphs are mutable, memory-bound reflections of magic—a whisper of how a spell _wants_ to be cast, shaped by place, lineage, will, or trauma.

They are not taught in traditional magical academies (though some claim to teach them); they are **learned**, **remembered**, or **gifted**—and no two cultures interpret them the same way.

---

### 🧵 What Is an Echo Glyph?

An Echo Glyph is a **spell-specific glyphic modification**, typically expressed as a small rune, phrase, or mental resonance bound to a **single known spell**. Echo Glyphs change **how** a spell behaves without altering its core identity.

They may:
- Adjust casting time or range
- Change elemental type or effect flavour
- Modify targets, duration, or save mechanics
- Introduce new trade-offs (e.g., bonus damage but shortened range)
- Echo personal memories or regional traditions into spellcasting

They are sometimes called:

- **Thread Glyphs** by those aligned with the **Faith of the Bound Pattern**
- **Mnemonic Edits** by gnomes and scholars of spirit-lore
- **Glyph-Mutations** by those who study Eldritch taint or magical decay
- **Legacy Echoes** in ancestral or shamanic cultures
- **Void Whispers** if sourced from corrupted ley lines or forbidden sources

---

### 🔸 Function and Mechanics

- **One Echo Glyph per Spell**: You may bind one Echo Glyph to any spell you know, modifying how you cast it.
- **Limited Attunement**: You may have a number of active Echo Glyphs equal to level + your Proficiency bonus), with a minimum of one.
- **Switching Glyphs**: Echo Glyphs are bound during a long rest, spirit rite, or during ritual reflection at a glyph anchor, leyline nexus, or memory pool.
- **No Slot Cost**: Echo Glyphs do not take spell slots or cost glyphic energy unless stated.
- **Stacking**: Only one Echo Glyph may affect a given casting of a spell. They do not stack with other Echo Glyphs, though they may combine with [[Metamagic]] or other effects.

> 📘 _Example_:  
> A caster with the _[[Misty Step]]_ spell may bind an Echo Glyph of _[["Flickering Step"]]_ This variant allows them to teleport 10 ft more but leaves behind a faint illusory duplicate for 1 round, distracting foes (granting disadvantage to one attacker).

## ✴️ Learning Echo Glyphs on a Critical Spell Hit

_“The Weave notices when you leave a mark.”_

Whenever you score a **critical hit with a spell attack** ([[Critical spell hit]]) or force a creature to **fail a saving throw against a spell you cast on a natural 1**, you may attempt to **learn an Echo Glyph** tied to that spell.

### 🌀 Learning Roll

After the spell resolves, make a **Glyphcraft check**:

> **d20 + your spellcasting ability modifier + proficiency bonus**

- **DC = 10 + the spell’s level**
- If successful, you **immediately learn one Echo Glyph** for that spell.
- If you already know an Echo Glyph for that spell, you may **swap it out** or **store** the new one in your Echo Journal (if your class or culture permits).

> ✎ Glyphcraft is not a separate skill. The check reflects your intuitive or studied resonance with the Weave.

### 🎓 Additional Notes

- You may only attempt to learn **one Echo Glyph per turn** this way.
- If you critically hit with a spell cast via **scroll, item, or magical device**, you must succeed the check **with disadvantage** unless the spell is on your class list.
- GMs may allow certain locations (spirit wells, leyline nodes, Null scars) to **reduce the DC** or allow rerolls when casting from those sites.

---
### 🧙 Optional Racial or Class Variants

- **Witches**: Gain advantage if the target is **marked**.
- **Wizards**: May reroll once per long rest when using this method.
- **Gnomes/Elves**: Add +1 to the Glyphcraft check due to Weave-bonded heritage.
- **Warlocks**: Automatically learn one glyph per long rest via critical if their patron grants glyphic knowledge.

# ✴️ Echo Glyph Attunement

_“The more threads you follow, the more patterns you may weave.”_  
— High Threadbearer Marisyne, Keeper of Nine Echoes

### Base Echo Glyph Capacity

All spellcasters can attune to a number of Echo Glyphs equal to:

> **Character Level + Proficiency Bonus**

This determines how many Echo Glyphs you may bind at any one time. You may change your attuned glyphs during a **long rest**, a **memory rite**, or by **communing at a glyphic locus** (such as a leyline node, spirit well, or sacred mirror).

There are also feats that can increase the amount.

### 🧠 Narrative Meaning

- **Wizards**: Lifelong glyph theorists, they master **echo-binding architecture**. Their minds become **living codices**, able to carry and weave more echoes than any other class.
- **Theurges**: As spiritual conduits and pattern-binders, they **resonate** with both divine and arcane Weave structures, granting them a **broader glyphic capacity** than standard casters.
- **Others**: Clerics, Druids, Sorcerers, Warlocks, and Bards hold the standard glyphic bandwidth, shaped more by intuition or guidance than glyphic discipline.

# ⚠️ Limits & Optional Rules

- **Cap**: No character may **cast more than one Echo Glyph-modified spell per turn** unless a class feature or item states otherwise.
- **Nullglyph Threshold**: At attunement 20+, the Weave may become unstable—this is when **Null Glyphs** or **Eldritch glyphic echoes** begin to whisper.

---
## 🌀 Echo Glyph Sources

Echo Glyphs are shaped by magical **memory** and **intent**, not fixed formulae. Some of the known sources include:

- **[[Spirit Wells]]**: Locations where the Weave pools with deep memory, often near shrines to Aelys or Elythra.
- **[[Weave Fractures]]**: Wild zones where spells remember themselves differently.
- **[[Ancestral Inheritance]]**: Echoes passed down through family or cultural ritual.
- **Personal Trauma or Revelation**: Rare but potent—when a spell _changes_ in response to your own story.
- **Corrupted Origins**: Dangerous variants whispered through Eldritch moons or Null Reflections.
- **Masters**: Certain NPCs can teach Echo Glyphs

---
## 🔹 Cultural Attitudes

- **[[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]]** reveres Echo Glyphs as **Thread Glyphs**—proof that mortals, too, may shape the Pattern with intention and devotion. They are often regulated, taught, or licensed.
- **[[Elves]]** and **spirit-attuned [[gnomes]] treat them as **[[Echo Threads]]**, shaped through emotion and memory.
- **[[Dwarves]]** accept only vetted and documented Glyph Variants—most Echo Glyphs are viewed as dangerous improvisation unless sanctioned.
- **[[Dragonborn]] of [[The Burning Concord]]** view them as signs of divine flame favour—or heresy, depending on the caste.
- **[[Orcs]]** might interpret Echo Glyphs as _scar-marks of remembered trials_, binding spell to experience.

> “The Weave remembers what we do not. The glyph echoes it.” — High Threadbearer of Salt and Silence

---
# ⚠ Eldritch Variants

Beware: some Echo Glyphs are **not echoes**, but **distortions**—**[[Null Glyphs]]** shaped not by memory but by void. These often:

- Grant powerful benefits with steep, hidden costs
- Cause misfires or Weave fractures on natural 1s
- Mark the caster over time, inviting attention from the Eldritch pantheon

Such glyphs may be banned, burned, or whispered only in ruins like [[Ashkarra]].
