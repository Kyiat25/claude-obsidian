# Bloodied, Breaking Points & Rally 

> _"The roar of a bloodied warlord can still the trembling hands of an army - or shatter them if the cry falters."_

---

## Overview

In standard D&D, enemy morale is binary and often ignored. Either monsters fight to the death or they flee arbitrarily. A devastating loss of a leader has no mechanical weight. An orc warband watching their chieftain fall fights with the same resolve as one riding the tide of victory. The _psychology_ of combat - the moment enemy courage breaks, the desperate rally cry from their commander, the infectious panic of a rout - exists only in narrative flavor, never in the mechanics that drive the battle.

**The Bloodied, Breaking Points & Rally system** fundamentally changes this for your **enemies and monsters**.

**IMPORTANT: This is an ENEMY/MONSTER system.** These rules apply to NPCs, monsters, and adversaries the party faces. PCs do not use this system unless you choose to implement the optional variant at the end of this document. This asymmetry is intentional - it gives players tactical options to break enemy morale while maintaining PC agency and avoiding forced retreats.

This module integrates **morale and battlefield psychology** directly into enemy combat behavior, creating battles where the fight for courage becomes a tactical element players can exploit. When an enemy is pushed to their limits, they don't simply tick down HP - they reveal their true nature. When enemy allies witness their leader fall, they don't simply continue fighting - they face a moment of crisis that could shatter their resolve or steel their determination.

Every battle becomes a tactical puzzle. Players can identify enemy leaders. They can force bloodied states to trigger weak Rally attempts. They can target morale directly through spells and abilities. And when the enemy tide turns, players gain the satisfaction of watching their foes break and scatter - or face a terrifying rally that turns the battle back against them.

### The Three Pillars (Enemy Systems)

**Bloodied & Bruised** - At 50% HP, enemies reveal their desperation. Frailties expose weaknesses players can exploit. Strengths grant resilience that forces adaptation. Abilities trigger once-per-combat moves that change tactics. Death Throes promise explosive finales that reward careful positioning. This is the moment where NPC survival instinct overrides tactics.

**Breaking Points** - When enemy leaders die or their casualties mount, their allies face Sanity-based morale tests. Success means enemies fight on despite fear. Failure means rout, panic, or surrender. This is the moment where enemy courage is measured - and where players can shatter it.

**Rally** - Enemy leaders can attempt Focus-based checks to restore their troops' courage and hold the line. Successful enemy rallies reset their Momentum and inspire their forces. Failed enemy rallies demoralize further and create openings for players. This is the moment where enemy leadership quality becomes tactically relevant.

### Why This System Exists

Combat isn't just attrition. When the orc chieftain falls bloodied and screaming, do the orcs fight harder out of desperation, or does doubt creep in? When half the goblin squad lies dead, do they hold formation or break? When the bandit captain roars defiance despite grievous wounds, does it steel their spines or reveal weakness the PCs can exploit?

**This system makes those moments matter mechanically for enemies.** It transforms battles from HP attrition contests into psychological warfare where killing the right target at the right moment can shatter an entire enemy force. It gives players new tactical options beyond "deal damage until enemies die." It makes enemy Focus and Sanity scores combat-relevant. It ensures enemy leaders become high-value targets whose deaths create cascading consequences.

And it ensures that every wound on an enemy leader, every death in the enemy ranks, every desperate enemy cry to hold the line becomes a decision point that players can recognize and exploit.

## SYSTEM 1: Bloodied Thresholds (Enemies)

### When Enemies Become Bloodied

An enemy creature becomes **Bloodied** at **50% HP or below**.

This is not simply "injured" - this is the moment where enemy survival overrides their tactics, where desperation fuels both strength and weakness, where the creature's true nature is revealed under pressure. Players can see this threshold coming and plan around it.

### Bloodied & Bruised Effects (Enemies)

When an enemy becomes bloodied, they immediately gain **all four** of the following effects simultaneously:

| **Effect**       | **Description**                                                                                                             |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Frailty**      | A tactical weakness is exposed that players can exploit (e.g., -2 EV, disadvantage on saves, vulnerable to a damage type)   |
| **Strength**     | A desperation resilience emerges that players must adapt to (e.g., +2 to damage, resistance to a damage type, temporary HP) |
| **Ability**      | A once-per-combat triggered move activates that changes enemy tactics (special attack, buff, debuff, or escape ability)     |
| **Death Throes** | An explosive finale effect that triggers on death, rewarding careful positioning (area damage, curse, transformation, etc.) |


**Design Note:** Effects should match creature type and narrative context. A cornered wolf might gain Pack Tactics (Strength) but expose its wounded flank (-2 AC, Frailty). A dying demon might explode in hellfire (Death Throes). A bloodied knight might gain advantage on attacks (Strength) but suffer disadvantage on Evasion (Frailty).

**Player Tactical Options:**

- Force enemies bloodied early to trigger their Frailty
- Prepare for Death Throes with positioning and readied actions
- Exploit Frailty weaknesses once revealed
- Adapt tactics when enemy Strength manifests

---

### Auto Rally Attempt Rule (Enemy Leaders)

When an **enemy leader** (or, if no leader exists, the **highest CR enemy creature**) becomes bloodied and triggers Bloodied & Bruised effects:

1. They **must immediately** attempt a **Rally check** (no choice)
2. **Range:** 120 feet - all enemy allies within range are affected
3. This represents their cry of defiance or display of fury echoing across the battlefield

**Rally Check:** 1d20 + Focus modifier vs DC 15 (or current casualty tier DC if higher)

**Results:**

|**Roll Result**|**Outcome**|
|---|---|
|**Success**|Enemy allies hold; restore +1 Momentum to all enemy allies within range (max +3)|
|**Failure**|Enemy allies lose -1 Momentum; leader's cry rings hollow|
|**Natural 20**|All enemy allies reset to 0 Momentum and gain **advantage on their next attack**|
|**Natural 1**|All enemy allies lose -2 Momentum and leader cannot Rally again this combat|

**Legendary Enemy Leaders:** Once per combat, auto-succeed on any Rally attempt (including Auto Rally).

**Player Tactical Options:**

- Target enemy leaders early to force weak Rally attempts
- Kill enemy leaders when bloodied to prevent successful rallies
- Force enemy leaders bloodied when isolated from troops (>120 ft away)
- Disrupt enemy leader Focus scores before bloodied threshold

## SYSTEM 2: Breaking Points (Enemy Morale)

### What Triggers Enemy Breaking Points

Enemy Breaking Points occur **only** when death or mass casualties shake enemy resolve. This is not fear of injury - this is the existential crisis of enemy troops watching their cause crumble, their leader fall, their comrades die around them.

|**Trigger**|**Effect**|
|---|---|
|**Enemy Leader Dies**|All enemy allies roll Breaking Point at **+2 DC penalty**|
|**Highest CR Enemy Dies** (if no designated leader)|All enemy allies roll Breaking Point at **+2 DC penalty**|
|**25% Enemy Casualties**|All remaining enemy allies roll Breaking Point vs DC 15|
|**50% Enemy Casualties**|All remaining enemy allies roll Breaking Point vs DC 20|
|**75% Enemy Casualties**|All remaining enemy allies roll Breaking Point vs DC 25|

**Enemy Casualties:** Count dead, unconscious, routed, or fled enemy creatures as casualties when calculating percentages.

**Player Tactical Options:**

- Kill enemy leaders to trigger the hardest Breaking Point checks
- Push enemy casualties to 25%/50%/75% thresholds strategically
- Use area damage to cross multiple casualty thresholds rapidly
- Target weak Sanity enemies first to trigger cascading routs

---

### Enemy Breaking Point Check

**Check:** 1d20 + Sanity modifier vs DC (10 + 5 x casualty tier)

|**Situation**|**Base DC**|**Leader Death Modifier**|
|---|---|---|
|25% Enemy Casualties|**15**|+2 if leader dead = **DC 17**|
|50% Enemy Casualties|**20**|+2 if leader dead = **DC 22**|
|75% Enemy Casualties|**25**|+2 if leader dead = **DC 27**|
|Enemy Leader Bloodied|Current Casualty DC|+0|

---

### Enemy Breaking Point Results

|**Roll Result**|**Outcome**|
|---|---|
|**Success**|Enemy fights on, but loses **-1 Momentum**|
|**Failure**|**Enemy Routs:** Panics, flees, or surrenders (see Monster Type Behaviours)|
|**Natural 1**|**Immediate Enemy Rout** + nearby enemy allies (30 ft) roll next Breaking Point with **disadvantage**|
|**Natural 20**|**Enemy Inspired:** Immune to Breaking Points for rest of combat + gains **+1 Momentum**|

**Enemy Momentum Floor:** Failed Breaking Points lock enemy allies at **-2 Momentum minimum** until a successful enemy Rally resets them.

**Initiative Impact:** Routed enemies are removed from Side Initiative calculations (see Initiative & Combat Flow system).

**Player Tactical Options:**

- Force Natural 1s by targeting low-Sanity enemies near groups
- Prevent Natural 20s by attacking enemy morale systematically
- Exploit routed enemies for easy kills or let them flee
- Use routed enemies' -2 Momentum lock to dominate initiative

## SYSTEM 3: Enemy Rally (Focus-Based Leadership)

### Normal Enemy Rally (Deliberate Action)

An enemy leader may attempt Rally as a **free action** (once per round) when enemy allies fail Breaking Points or are wavering.

**Requirements:**

- Must be a designated enemy leader or highest CR enemy creature
- Enemy allies must be within **120 feet**
- Cannot Rally if enemy leader is **incapacitated**, **routing**, or at **0 Focus**

**Rally Check:** 1d20 + Focus modifier vs same DC as the failed Breaking Point

|**Roll Result**|**Outcome**|
|---|---|
|**Success**|Enemy allies reroll failed Breaking Point saves with **advantage**; those who succeed reset to **0 Momentum**|
|**Failure**|Enemy allies lose **-1 Momentum** instead (leader's words fall flat)|
|**Natural 20**|All enemy allies reset to **0 Momentum** + gain **advantage on their next attack** + immune to next Breaking Point|
|**Natural 1**|Enemy allies locked at **-2 Momentum**; leader cannot Rally again this combat|

**Player Tactical Options:**

- Interrupt enemy Rally attempts with stuns or silences
- Damage enemy leaders before Rally to lower Focus
- Force enemy leaders to Rally repeatedly by sustaining pressure
- Kill enemy leaders after failed Rallies when troops are demoralized

---

### Auto Rally Attempt (Enemy Bloodied Surge)

**Trigger:** Automatic when enemy leader/highest CR becomes bloodied (see System 1).

**Differences from Normal Rally:**

- **No choice** - automatically triggers
- Uses **current casualty tier DC** or **DC 15** if no casualties yet
- **Range:** 120 feet
- Follows same result table as Normal Rally

**Legendary Enemy Leaders:** Can use their auto-succeed ability on this check.

**Player Tactical Options:**

- Save burst damage for when enemy leader is near bloodied threshold
- Force enemy leaders bloodied when isolated from their troops
- Prepare countermeasures (silence, stun) for Auto Rally moment
- Accept that bloodied legendary enemies will Rally successfully once

---

### Enemy Rally Limits

- **Once per round** for Normal Rally (free action)
- **Once per combat** for Legendary auto-succeed
- **No Rally if at 0 Focus** or **below 0 Sanity**
- **Cannot Rally** if enemy leader is routing, unconscious, or dead

## Integration with DCS

### Momentum Synergy (Enemy Application)

|**System**|**Interaction**|
|---|---|
|**Failed Enemy Breaking Point**|Enemies locked at **-2 Momentum minimum** until Rally succeeds|
|**Successful Enemy Rally**|Resets enemy Momentum to **0** or restores **+1 Momentum**|
|**Natural 20 Enemy Rally**|All enemy allies reset to **0 Momentum** + advantage on next attack|
|**Routing Enemies**|Removed from Side Initiative Momentum calculations|

**Player Benefits:**

- Enemies at -2 Momentum are easier to hit and evade
- Momentum lock prevents enemy initiative advantage
- Breaking enemy morale directly impacts combat flow

### Injury System (Enemy Application)

- **Minor/Major/Critical Injuries** on enemies don't trigger Breaking Points by themselves
- **IF an injury kills an enemy leader** - triggers Breaking Point for their troops
- **Called Shots** on enemy leaders (head/heart/throat) may fast-track enemy morale collapse

**Player Tactical Options:**

- Use Called Shots to cripple enemy leaders before killing them
- Kill wounded enemy leaders to maximize Breaking Point impact
- Target enemy leader body parts that affect Focus/Sanity

### Called Shots & Evasion (Enemy Targets)

- **Called Shots against enemy leaders** can force early bloodied states
- **Bloodied enemy leaders** trigger Auto Rally attempts that may fail
- **Failed enemy Rally** - creates tactical advantage via Momentum loss

### Focus & Sanity Integration (Enemy Stats)

|**Attribute**|**Role in This System**|
|---|---|
|**Enemy Focus**|Powers Rally checks; enemy leader with high Focus stabilizes their army|
|**Enemy Sanity**|Determines Breaking Point resistance; low enemy Sanity = easier routs|
|**Enemy Focus 0**|Cannot Rally; enemy leader becomes tactical liability players can exploit|
|**Enemy Sanity 0**|Automatically fails Breaking Points; mentally broken enemy|

**Player Tactical Options:**

- Use spells that damage Focus/Sanity (Mind Sliver, Psychic Scream)
- Target low-Sanity enemies first to trigger cascading routs
- Disrupt enemy leader concentration to lower Focus
- Research enemy leader mental stats before combat

### Class Hooks (Player Options)

|**Class**|**Enemy Morale Manipulation**|
|---|---|
|**Bard**|Cutting Words reduces enemy Rally checks; Vicious Mockery targets enemy Sanity|
|**Paladin**|Divine Smite on enemy leaders to force bloodied; Channel Divinity to frighten|
|**Warlock**|Eldritch Blast + Repelling Blast to isolate enemy leaders >120 ft from troops|
|**Investigator**|Study enemy leader to identify low Focus/Sanity stats for targeting|
|**Cleric**|Bane on enemy Rally checks; Turn Undead forces automatic rout on undead|

## Monster-Type Behaviours (Enemy Routs)

Enemy morale outcomes vary by creature type:

|**Creature Type**|**Rout Behaviour**|**Rally Traits**|
|---|---|---|
|**Humanoids / Beasts**|Flee or surrender; highly reliant on leaders|Standard Rally rules; players can negotiate surrender|
|**Fiends**|Often turn on each other if leader dies; infighting escalates|Rarely Rally; chaos increases and creates openings|
|**Giants / Dragons**|Rarely rout; enter **rage states** or **death-throe phases** instead|High Focus; difficult to break but devastating when broken|
|**Celestials**|Almost always succeed Rally attempts; inspire nearby allies automatically|Legendary Rally traits; requires overwhelming force|
|**Undead / Constructs**|**Immune** to Breaking Points and Rally (no morale)|N/A - cannot be broken through morale|
|**Aberrations**|Unpredictable; may attack randomly, freeze, or teleport away|Sanity-based; erratic behavior creates chaos|
|**Fey**|May vanish, turn invisible, or shift allegiance if morale breaks|Trickster Rally; deception-based and unreliable|

**Player Tactical Notes:**

- Don't waste time trying to break undead/construct morale
- Fiend infighting can be exploited for free attacks
- Giant/Dragon rage states are predictable and can be prepared for
- Fey may become allies or neutral if morale breaks favorably

## Player Tactical Options

### How PCs Attack Enemy Morale

1. **Kill Enemy Leaders Early** - Trigger Breaking Points with +2 DC penalty
2. **Force Bloodied States on Leaders** - Make enemy leaders attempt Auto Rally (which can fail)
3. **Target Enemy Sanity** - Use spells like _Fear_, _Phantasmal Force_, _Mind Sliver_ to lower Sanity modifiers
4. **Isolate Enemy Leaders** - Keep them >120 ft from their troops so Rally attempts affect no one
5. **Use Focus Attacks** - Disrupt concentration, forcing Focus degradation in enemy leaders
6. **Kill Systematically** - Push enemy casualties to 25%/50%/75% thresholds strategically
7. **Create Cascading Routs** - Target low-Sanity enemies near groups to force Natural 1s
8. **Interrupt Enemy Rallies** - Stun, silence, or kill enemy leaders during their Rally attempts

### Reading Enemy Morale

Players can gain advantage by recognizing enemy morale states:

**Signs of Low Enemy Morale:**

- Enemies at -2 Momentum (easier to hit/evade)
- Enemy leader visibly bloodied (Rally attempt coming)
- Enemy casualties approaching thresholds (25%/50%/75%)
- Enemy leader isolated from troops (Rally ineffective)
- Previous failed Rally attempts

**Signs of High Enemy Morale:**

- Enemies at +2/+3 Momentum (harder to hit/evade)
- Recent successful enemy Rally
- Enemy leader healthy and central
- Low enemy casualties
- Legendary enemy leader present (auto-succeed available)

## OPTIONAL VARIANT: PC Breaking Points

**WARNING: This variant is OPTIONAL and changes the core asymmetry of the system.** By default, PCs do not use Breaking Points - this maintains player agency and prevents forced retreats. Only implement this if your table wants higher stakes and is comfortable with PCs potentially being forced to flee.

If you choose to implement this variant, PCs may also face Breaking Point checks when:

- **Party leader falls** to 0 HP
- **Half the party is incapacitated** (unconscious, dead, or fled)
- **Witnessing catastrophic events** (at DM discretion - e.g., ancient dragon appears, city destroyed)

**Modified DCs for PCs:**

- **Leader Down:** DC 12 (PCs are more resilient than monsters)
- **50% Party Down:** DC 15
- **Catastrophic Event:** DC 10-20 (scales with event severity)

**PC-Specific Results:**

- **Success:** No penalty (PCs are heroes)
- **Failure:** Lose -1 Momentum + must use next action to move away from threat OR stabilize fallen ally
- **Natural 1:** Frightened condition for 1 round + lose -2 Momentum
- **Natural 20:** Inspired; immune to Breaking Points this combat + gain +2 Momentum

**Important Differences from Enemy System:**

- PCs never automatically rout (maintain player agency)
- PCs get to choose between retreating or helping allies
- PCs can Rally each other without designated leader (highest Focus PC can attempt)
- Legendary PCs (level 17+) may auto-succeed Rally once per combat

**Recommendation:** Only use this variant if your table wants a grittier, more punishing experience where even heroes can break under pressure. The default system (enemies only) is recommended for most tables.

## Legendary & Epic Enemy Considerations

### Legendary Enemy Creatures

- **Auto-Rally Once Per Combat:** Can choose to auto-succeed any Rally check
- **Death-Defying Presence:** Enemy allies within 120 ft gain **+2 to Breaking Point saves**
- **Bloodied Rage:** Instead of standard Bloodied effects, enter **Legendary Phase 2** with new abilities

**Player Tactical Options:**

- Assume legendary enemy leaders will succeed first Rally attempt
- Force multiple Rallies to burn their auto-succeed
- Focus fire to kill legendary leader in single round if possible
- Prepare for Legendary Phase 2 when bloodied threshold approaches

### Epic-Tier Enemy Leaders (CR 17-20)

- **Expanded Momentum:** Can reach +4/+5 Momentum, making Breaking Points much rarer
- **Inspiring Presence:** High Focus (18+) enemy leaders Rally as **bonus action**
- **Mental Fortitude:** Sanity 20+ enemy leaders are **immune** to Breaking Points from casualties

**Player Tactical Options:**

- Epic enemy leaders require overwhelming force to break
- Target epic enemy troops instead of leader to trigger casualties
- Use reality-bending magic to bypass morale entirely (Banishment, Maze)
- Negotiate or flee if epic enemy leader's morale cannot be broken

## Example Scenarios

### Example 1: Orc Warlord Bloodied

**Situation:** Orc Warlord (CR 4, Leader) drops from 60 HP to 28 HP (below 50%) from PC critical hit.

**Bloodied & Bruised Effects Trigger:**

- **Frailty:** -2 AC (exposed wounds) - PCs can hit more easily now
- **Strength:** +2 to damage rolls (desperation fury) - PCs take more damage if hit
- **Ability:** Battle Cry - all orc allies gain advantage on next attack
- **Death Throes:** Explodes in 10ft radius for 2d6 damage on death - PCs note to stay back

**Auto Rally Attempt:**

- Warlord rolls 1d20 + 2 (Focus) = 14 vs DC 15
- **Fails** - All orc allies within 120 ft lose -1 Momentum
- Orc morale wavers; PCs sense weakness

**PC Tactical Response:**

- Rogue: "His troops are shaken! Focus fire on the Warlord!"
- Wizard: "I'll stay back - he explodes when he dies"
- Fighter: "Advantage on my attacks now with his -2 AC"

**Next Round:**

- PCs kill Warlord, triggering Death Throes (party positioned safely)
- Remaining orcs must make Breaking Point check DC 17 (leader death +2)
- Several orcs rout, battle becomes mop-up operation

---

### Example 2: Enemy Leader Death Triggers Rout

**Situation:** Party kills bandit captain. 8 bandits remain (no other high-CR enemies).

**Breaking Point Triggered:**

- All 8 bandits must roll Sanity saves
- **DC = 15 + 2 (leader death) = 17**
- Average bandit Sanity modifier: +0

**Results:**

- 3 bandits succeed: Fight on at -1 Momentum (defending desperately)
- 4 bandits fail: **Rout** - flee toward cave entrance (PCs can pursue or ignore)
- 1 bandit rolls Natural 1: **Immediate rout** + drops weapon, sprints away in panic

**PC Options:**

- Paladin: "Let the routing ones go - focus on the three still fighting"
- Ranger: "I'll hunt down the fleeing bandits while you finish here"
- Cleric: "Three at -1 Momentum? Easy - we act first next round"

**Battlefield Change:**

- Only 3 bandits remain engaged (demoralized)
- 4 routing bandits removed from initiative calculation
- Side Initiative now heavily favors PCs (enemy average Momentum = -1)
- Combat effectively over

---

### Example 3: Successful Enemy Rally Holds the Line

**Situation:** Goblins defending a fort. 10 goblins + 1 Hobgoblin Captain (leader, Focus +3). PCs kill 3 goblins (30% casualties).

**Breaking Point Triggered:**

- 25% threshold crossed
- All 7 remaining goblins roll Sanity vs DC 15
- 4 succeed (-1 Momentum, fight on)
- 3 fail (begin to rout - fleeing toward ladder)

**Captain Attempts Rally:**

- Uses free action to Rally
- Rolls 1d20 + 3 (Focus) = 18 vs DC 15
- **Success!**

**Rally Effect:**

- 3 routing goblins reroll with advantage
- All 3 succeed on reroll
- All goblins reset to 0 Momentum
- Captain's words: "Stand your ground! They'll tire before we do!"

**PC Response:**

- Wizard: "Damn, the captain rallied them. We need to kill him next"
- Fighter: "At least they're back to 0 Momentum - we're not losing initiative"
- Rogue: "I'll try to sneak behind the captain while he's focused on his troops"

**Tactical Impact:**

- All 7 goblins remain in fight (PCs can't exploit rout)
- Initiative recalculates with 0 average enemy Momentum
- PCs adapt plan to prioritize captain kill
- PCs recognize captain has high Focus (successful Rally = threat)

---

### Example 4: Players Exploit Enemy Isolation

**Situation:** Yuan-ti warband (1 Yuan-ti Abomination leader + 6 Yuan-ti Purebloods). PCs use _Repelling Blast_ to push leader 150 ft away.

**Bloodied Threshold:**

- PCs focus fire on isolated leader, bloodying them
- Leader triggers Auto Rally attempt but is >120 ft from troops
- **Rally affects zero allies** (no one in range)

**Breaking Point Triggered:**

- Leader bloodied triggers Breaking Point for troops
- 6 Purebloods roll Sanity vs DC 15
- Average rolls: 3 succeed, 3 fail
- 3 routing Purebloods flee toward leader

**PC Tactical Success:**

- Warlock: "Got him isolated - his Rally did nothing!"
- Ranger: "Three are routing - pick off the remaining three"
- Paladin: "Kill the stragglers, then we finish the leader"

**Result:**

- Enemy force effectively split
- Leader's Rally wasted due to positioning
- PCs win through superior tactics exploiting morale system

## Design Philosophy

**Why This Enemy-Only System Exists:**

1. **Player Agency Preserved:** PCs choose their actions; enemies are forced by morale
2. **Tactical Depth Added:** Players gain new win conditions beyond "kill everything"
3. **Asymmetric Design:** Heroes are brave by nature; enemies must test their courage
4. **Satisfying Victories:** Watching enemy armies break feels powerful and earned
5. **Narrative Richness:** Enemy routs create story moments and aftermath opportunities

**Balance Goals:**

- Enemy death spirals are **achievable but not guaranteed** (Rally provides counterplay)
- Legendary enemies remain **intimidating** (immunities + auto-Rally)
- Players can **target enemy morale strategically** without it feeling automatic
- System adds **depth without overwhelming bookkeeping** (DM tracks, players exploit)
- Maintains **PC heroic fantasy** (PCs don't flee unless players choose to)

## Design Summary

**Core Principle:** This is an **enemy/monster system** that gives players tactical options to break enemy morale.

**Round 1:** Traditional initiative; combat begins chaotically.

**Round 2+:** Side Initiative driven by Momentum; enemy Breaking Points and Rally swing initiative flow.

**50% HP:** Enemy leader bloodied - Auto Rally attempt - shapes enemy battlefield psychology.

**Enemy Leader Dies:** Breaking Point DC +2 - potential enemy rout cascade unless enemy Rally succeeds.

**Momentum as Core:** All enemy morale effects interact with enemy Momentum, giving PCs initiative advantage.

**Focus & Sanity Integration:** Enemy mental attributes finally matter in physical combat - players can target them.

This system ensures **enemy courage, leadership, and morale** become tactically exploitable by players. Every wound on an enemy leader creates opportunity. Every death in enemy ranks creates a crisis point. Every enemy rally cry can turn the tide - or fall hollow and break the enemy army.

Combat becomes not just a test of martial prowess, but a psychological battle where clever players can shatter enemy resolve and claim victory through superior tactics, not just superior damage.

**PCs are heroes. Enemies must test their courage. Players exploit the difference.**
