# 🌟 **Fame & Infamy**

### _The Spotlight Cuts Both Ways_

Fame is not merely recognition—it is gravity. In _[[Dór-o Estel]]_, to be known is to be pursued, feared, adored, or manipulated. Whether raised as a folk hero or whispered about as a walking curse, your fame becomes a force of consequence.

This optional module expands the core **[[Reputation]]** system to give structured benefits, risks, and social effects based on how widely you are known, and **why**.

---
## 🧭 **Fame vs. Infamy**

Fame is **positive recognition**. Infamy is **notorious recognition**. Both attract attention, open doors, and provoke reactions.

You may be:

- **Famous** to some (villagers you saved, bards you inspired)
- **Infamous** to others (temples you defiled, guards you escaped)
- **Both** at once in different regions or factions

---
## 🎖️ **Fame/Infamy Tiers** _(Optional Abstract Mechanic)_

|Tier|Public Response|Example Effects|
|---|---|---|
|0|Unknown|No benefits or penalties|
|1|Recognized (by name or deed)|Minor discounts, mild suspicion or awe|
|2|Talked About (rumor-level fame)|Offers of help or challenge, questions from strangers|
|3|Locally Iconic / Notorious|Easier access to courts or gangs; fame-based interruptions|
|4|Regionally Celebrated / Hated|Song, propaganda, wanted posters; followers or fanatics emerge|
|5|Globally Revered / Feared|Magical tracking, divine omens, impersonators, legacy trials|

DMs may assign or track **Fame Points** to escalate this scale. Fame decays slowly without dramatic acts or mythic witnesses.

---
## 🎁 **Benefits of Fame**

- **Public Favour**: Free rooms, food, or minor gifts from admirers
- **Audience Access**: Nobles, temple heads, or faction leaders may request your presence
- **Inspired Loyalty**: NPCs may fight harder, follow you, or protect your name
- **Bardic Echoes**: Your deeds become stories; new players may have heard of you
- **Divine Interest**: Fame may attract boons, visions, or trials from aligned gods

---
## ☠️ **Risks of Infamy**

- **Hunted**: Zealots, paladins, or bounty guilds may track you
- **Public Distrust**: Townsfolk refuse service or spread falsehoods
- **Impersonators**: Actors or criminals adopt your identity—or twist it
- **Magical Anchoring**: Scrying, geas, or soul-marking rituals may use your name
- **Reputation-based Traps**: Enemies bait you with symbols of your legend

---

## 🧠 **Roleplaying with Fame/Infamy**

Fame changes how the world reacts:

- Children stare or mimic your fighting style
- Priests invoke your name in sermons (positively or as a warning)
- Criminals curse you or try to trade on your name
- Street performers exaggerate your deeds (or invent new ones)

DMs are encouraged to use **Fame Tags**: short one-line rumours, chants, or gossip fragments attached to the character.

Examples:

- _"That's the one who tamed the storm!"_
- _"His eyes saw the Hollow Star and survived."_
- _"She burned the bindings of three judges."_

---
## 📜 **Sample Reactions by NPC Type**

|NPC Type|Reaction to Fame|Reaction to Infamy|
|---|---|---|
|**Commoner**|Offers help, praise, small tokens|Avoids you, alerts guard, spreads rumor|
|**Guard**|Shows respect or confusion|Demands papers, prepares for combat|
|**Clergy**|Invites you to speak, offers blessings|Demands confession, curses you, forbids entry|
|**Criminal**|Sells at discount, offers alliances|Tests you, imitates you, fears you|
|**Noble**|Requests escort, honors you publicly|Leverages or challenges your notoriety|
|**Child**|Follows, mimics, idealizes|Afraid or drawn to dark myths|

---
## 🔄 **Decay and Shift**

- **Fame fades** when ignored: no great deeds, no stories told, no presence seen.
- **Infamy lingers longer**, but can be buried through _redemption arcs_, _identity shifts_, or _divine rewriting_.
- Players can **embrace** or **reject** their fame: manipulate it, twist it, or burn it down.

> You may leave the spotlight. That does not mean it leaves you.

---
## 🏁 Final Note

Fame and Infamy are not moral judgments—they are **social weight**. They are the reflection of your myth, cast in the minds of strangers. Harness them. Fear them. Or disappear before they devour you.

> "The crowd cheered my name as I fled the pyre. I never looked back. Fame is just another kind of fire."
