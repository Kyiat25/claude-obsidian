# Momentum — Combat Rhythm

> *"Battle is not just blood and steel — it is rhythm. Those who ride the rhythm of momentum carve through their foes; those who lose it are swept away."*

---

## What Is Momentum?

Momentum represents the shifting flow of combat advantage. It is not a fixed statistic like HP, but a **living measure of rhythm, timing, and control**. It rises when you strike with precision, defend with skill, or dominate the field. It falls when you falter, stumble, or suffer punishing blows.

Momentum is not a spendable pool like spell slots. It is a **state bonus** — constantly rising and falling, shaping every attack roll, evasion roll, and tactical decision.

- **Momentum Score (MS):** Your current rhythm state.
- **Standard PC Range:** −2 to +3. Expands at epic levels.
- **Application:** MS modifies Attack rolls, Evasion rolls, and certain manoeuvres.

---

## Momentum Ranges

### Standard Range (All PCs, All Monsters up to +3)

| Score | Effects |
|---|---|
| **+3 (Combat Advantage)** | +3 to Attack & Evasion. First attack each turn has Advantage. Enemies have Disadvantage on Called Shots against you. |
| **+2** | +2 to Attack & Evasion. +5 ft movement speed. |
| **+1** | +1 to Attack & Evasion. |
| **0 (Neutral)** | No effect. |
| **−1** | −1 to Attack & Evasion. |
| **−2 (Struggling)** | −2 to Attack & Evasion. Critical threat range against you expands to 19–20. |

---

### Epic Expansion (Levels 17 and 20)

At high levels, PCs can exceed the standard cap to remain competitive against legendary monsters.

- **Level 17:** PCs may reach **+4 Momentum**.
- **Level 20:** PCs may reach **+5 Momentum**.

| Score | PC Effects (Level 17+) |
|---|---|
| **+4 (Overwhelming)** | +4 to Attack & Evasion. First hit each round adds +PB damage. Gain 1 extra Reaction each round. |
| **+5 (Dominating)** | +5 to Attack & Evasion. Unleash one **Heroic Surge** per combat (extra Action). |

> Crit expansion to 19–20 and aura suppression at +5 are **monster-only** effects. PCs at +5 gain the Heroic Surge but do not gain those monster properties.

---

## Gaining Momentum

| Event | Momentum Gained |
|---|---|
| Successful attack | +1 MS |
| Critical Hit | +2 MS |
| Called Shot (hit + target fails save) | +2 MS |
| Clean Dodge or Brace (any armour) | +1 MS |
| Clean Dodge in **light armour** | +2 MS |
| Successful Counterattack or Disarm via Forgoing Evasion | +2 MS |
| Successful Shield Bash or Deflect | +1 MS |
| Dropping a foe to 0 HP | +1 MS |
| Healing an ally | +1 MS |
| Tactical or environmental edge (GM discretion) | +1 MS |

> **Defensive Momentum:** Momentum is earned through both offence and defence. A clean dodge in light armour generates more Momentum than a standard one because it represents precise, fluid movement — the mark of a combatant at the peak of their rhythm.

---

## Losing Momentum

| Event | Momentum Lost |
|---|---|
| Missed attack | −1 MS |
| Suffer a Critical Hit | −2 MS |
| Forgoing Evasion (taking the hit) | −1 MS |
| Glancing Blow received (non-medium armour) | −1 MS |
| Critical fail on any roll (Nat 1) | −2 MS |
| Receiving a condition (Stunned, Blinded, etc.) | −1 MS |
| Failing a Saving Throw | −1 MS |
| Knocked Prone or Restrained | −1 MS |
| Minor Injury (75% HP threshold) | −1 MS |
| Major Injury (50% HP threshold) | Reset to 0 |
| Critical Injury (25% HP threshold) | −2 MS |
| Fatal (0 HP) | Reset to 0 |

> **Threshold Note:** Momentum losses from HP thresholds only trigger when damage causes a threshold to be **crossed** for the first time. Healing back past a threshold does not re-trigger gains.

---

### Class Exceptions

**Barbarian:** The Barbarian's Momentum resilience is tied to **Rage** and scales with level, rather than being a permanent baseline exception.

- **While Raging (Level 1 — Resilient Flow):** Major Injury at 50% HP drops Momentum to −1 instead of resetting to 0.
- **While Raging (Level 1 — Fury Threshold):** If you begin your turn Raging at −2 or lower, immediately gain +2 MS.
- **Level 7 (Primal Defiance):** As a reaction when you would lose Momentum due to taking damage, reduce the loss by 1 (minimum 0) and gain +1 MS. Usable a number of times equal to PB per short rest.
- **Level 11 (Enhanced Rage):** While Raging, your Momentum cannot be reduced below 0 by any source, including injury thresholds and failed saves.

Outside of Rage, Barbarians lose Momentum normally from all standard sources including being hit, missing, and failing saves.

**Monk:** Monks ignore the −1 Momentum penalty from **missed attacks** (but not critical misses — a Nat 1 still costs −2 MS as normal). All other Momentum losses apply normally, including being hit, taking conditions, failing saves, and crossing injury thresholds.

This reflects the Monk's *Balanced Rhythm* — their flowing style means an attack that doesn't connect is simply redirected, not a break in their rhythm. A blow that lands, however, still disrupts it.

## Optional Rule: Swing Guardrail

By default, Momentum can shift freely each turn based on events. For groups who find combat swings too volatile, this optional rule smooths the pace:

A creature cannot gain or lose more than **±2 Momentum per turn** before injury resets apply.

**Epic variant (Level 17+):** Expands to **±3 per turn**, allowing more explosive late-tier combat.

> This rule is recommended for groups new to DCS or running high-encounter-density sessions. Experienced tables may prefer the raw swing of unrestricted Momentum.

---

## Exhaustion and Momentum

Exhaustion degrades your ability to build rhythm — it reduces Momentum **gains**, not the cap.

- Each level of Exhaustion reduces all Momentum gains by **1** (minimum 0 gained per event).
- Exhaustion does **not** reduce your maximum Momentum cap or apply penalties to your current score.

| Exhaustion Level | Effect on Momentum Gains |
|---|---|
| 1 | All gains reduced by 1 (a successful attack grants +0 instead of +1) |
| 2 | All gains reduced by 2 (a critical hit grants +0 instead of +2) |
| 3+ | No Momentum can be gained until Exhaustion is reduced |

---

## Resetting Momentum

Momentum resets when the rhythm of combat breaks:

| Trigger | Reset |
|---|---|
| Short Rest or scene break | Reset to 0 |
| **Disengage or Withdraw action** | Reset to 0 |
| Knocked unconscious or stabilised | Reset to 0 |
| Reduced to 0 HP | Reset to 0 (before death saves) |
| GM discretion (parley, battlefield pause, magical disruption) | Reset to 0 |

> **Important:** The **Disengage or Withdraw action** resets Momentum — not the act of rolling Evasion. Defenders roll Evasion every time they are attacked, which is not a Disengage. Taking the Disengage or Withdraw action as your action on your turn is a deliberate choice to leave the fight's rhythm.

### Reset Prevention

A character can only prevent or soften a Momentum reset **once per combat** unless a class feature or ability explicitly states otherwise.

## Monster Scaling

Monster Momentum ranges reflect their nature and danger level.

| Monster Type | Momentum Range |
|---|---|
| **Beasts / Humanoids** | −2 to +2 — Instinct and grit. Predictable rhythm. |
| **Fiends, Aberrations, Monstrosities** | −3 to +3 — Erratic, dangerous, and unpredictable. |
| **Giants, Dragons, Celestials** | −5 to +5 — Build into overwhelming dominance. |
| **Undead / Constructs** | Rarely exceed +2 unless Legendary — limited but unnervingly steady. |

---

### Monster Effects at High Momentum

| Tier | Monster-Only Effects |
|---|---|
| **+4 (Overwhelming)** | Extra Reaction each round. **Aura:** Creatures in melee must make a **DC 15 Focus or Wisdom save** at the start of their turn or their first attack against this creature has Disadvantage. |
| **+5 (Dominating)** | Critical threat range expands to 19–20. First hit each round forces a **Strength or Dexterity save** (GM's choice based on attack type) or the target is knocked prone, pushed 10 ft, or disarmed. **Aura:** Creatures in melee must make a **DC 18 Focus or Wisdom save** at the start of their turn or their Momentum is capped at 0 for that turn. |

> **Aura Saves:** The +5 Momentum aura does not automatically strip Momentum. Creatures in melee make a Focus or Wisdom save at the start of each of their turns. On a failure, their Momentum is treated as 0 for that turn only — it does not permanently change their score. Success means they act normally at their current Momentum. This ensures the aura is threatening without being an automatic shutdown of the entire party.

---

### PC vs Monster Overcap Comparison

| Tier | PC Effects | Monster Effects |
|---|---|---|
| **+4** | +PB damage on first hit; extra Reaction | Extra Reaction; aura (Focus/Wis save or Disadvantage on first attack) |
| **+5** | Heroic Surge (extra Action once per combat) | Crit expansion 19–20; aura (save or Momentum capped at 0); forced STR/DEX saves on first hit |

## Caster Protection — Spell Save DC Floor

Casters never count below **−1 MS** when calculating Spell Save DCs. Even at −2 Momentum, spell DCs are calculated as if at −1. This ensures casters remain viable under sustained attrition without negating the system's pressure on them.

---

## System Interactions

**Evasion:** MS modifies both attack and evasion rolls. At +3 you move like flowing water; at −2 you stumble through every exchange.

**DR:** Momentum governs whether blows connect. DR only applies after the attack lands.

**Called Shots:** Harder to land Called Shot effects against high-MS defenders (advantage on saves at +3). Easier against low-MS targets (disadvantage on saves at −2).

**Injury System:** Major injuries reset Momentum; Critical injuries drain it. The Injury System and Momentum interact to create spiralling pressure when a character is losing — a wounded fighter becomes harder to defend and easier to hit.

**Reactions:** Some class features explicitly shift Momentum as part of their effect. See individual class entries.

**Weapon Mastery:** All Weapon Skill Momentum references use the standard +3 baseline cap. At levels 17 and 20 this cap expands, and Weapon Skills that reference Momentum interact with the expanded cap naturally.

## Example Scenarios

### Low Level — Fighter vs Owlbear (Level 5)

The Fighter builds Momentum with Called Shots, rising to +2. The Owlbear caps at +2, forcing a back-and-forth rhythm. A critical hit from the Owlbear resets the Fighter's flow — suddenly the fighter is at 0, the beast at +2. The Fighter has to rebuild, choosing between aggressive Called Shots and safer standard strikes.

### Mid Level — Rogue vs Beholder (Level 12)

The Rogue dances between cover, using positioning to avoid the eye rays and build Momentum through Sneak Attacks. The Beholder surges to +5, triggering its aura. The Rogue makes their Focus save and succeeds — acting at full +3 Momentum, landing a Called Shot that drains the Beholder's rhythm before it can use Legendary Actions to recover.

### High Level Martial — Barbarian vs Ancient Red Dragon (Level 20)

Both surge toward +5. The Barbarian's Rage insulates them from injury Momentum loss, letting them maintain rhythm even as the Dragon's breath weapon crits. The Dragon at +5 forces STR saves — the Barbarian passes, holds their ground, and unleashes a Heroic Surge. The Dragon triggers its Legendary Surge. Two titans at maximum rhythm, every exchange mythic.

### High Level Caster — Wizard vs Fiend (Level 15)

The Wizard falls to −2 after a failed save and a condition. Their Spell Save DC floor kicks in — they calculate DCs as if at −1, keeping them viable. They cast a control spell, the Fiend fails, and the Wizard climbs back to +1. The caster floor rule is the difference between a stalled caster and one who fights back.

---

## Summary

- **Standard range:** −2 to +3 for all PCs and most monsters.
- **Epic expansion:** +4 at level 17, +5 at level 20.
- **Defensive gains are canonical:** Clean dodge (light) = +2 MS; clean dodge/brace (other) = +1 MS.
- **Swing Guardrail:** Optional rule — ±2 per turn baseline, ±3 at level 17+.
- **Barbarian exception:** Rage-dependent. While Raging: Major Injury drops MS to −1 (not reset); Fury Threshold (+2 MS if starting turn at −2); Level 11: MS cannot drop below 0. Outside Rage: loses MS normally.
- **Monk exception:** Ignores −1 MS on missed attacks only (Nat 1 crits still cost −2 MS). All other losses apply normally.
- **Monster aura at +5:** Focus/Wisdom save to resist — not automatic shutdown.
- **Disengage/Withdraw resets Momentum** — rolling Evasion does not.
- **Exhaustion reduces gains** — not the cap.
- **Caster DC floor at −1 MS.**
- **Weapon Mastery cap: +3 baseline**, expanding at 17/20.
- Reset prevention limited to **once per combat**.
