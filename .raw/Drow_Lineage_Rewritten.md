# Drow
## Lineage

> *"They think they buried us. They gave us a kingdom."*  
> — **Vel'Zyren, The Web-Spinner**

You are Drow. Not fallen, not cursed—**chosen**. Where others saw exile, your ancestors saw liberation. Where the surface gods offered conditional love, the depths offered absolute clarity. You were not cast down into darkness; you descended into truth, shedding the false light of the surface world for the honesty that only shadow can provide.

Your people remember what others have forgotten: that beauty is a weapon, that cruelty is precision, that mercy is weakness disguised as virtue, and that the Pattern itself can be rewritten by those strong enough to hold the thread. You are not survivors—you are the spiders that dance upon the web of destiny, and you were made to hunt.

---

## The Descent into Truth

According to the **Doctrine of the Descent**, the Drow were once surface elves—but unlike your kin, you understood what the gods above would never acknowledge: **they had abandoned you**. While other elves worshiped peace, balance, and harmony, your ancestors sought power, purity, and dominion. They were warriors, conquerors, the strong who refused to share the world with the weak.

When the surface gods offered nothing but silence, **Vel'Zyren called to you**.

> *"You are not meant for them. You were never meant to share your place. Come below, and I will give you what they never could—purpose absolute, power earned, perfection refined in darkness."*

One by one, the greatest minds, strongest warriors, and most cunning souls descended through the rift into the Underdark. The surface world forgot them. The histories erased them. But in the deep places, they were **remade**.

This was not punishment—this was **purification**. Your ancestors shed the weakness of mercy, learned what it meant to truly hunt, and became something the gods above had always feared: **predators perfected**.

The transformation was not metaphorical. Generations in absolute darkness changed your biology—eyes that see in pitch black, skin that darkens to ashen tones, blood that carries shadow like silk. **You are not elves who live in darkness. You are darkness given form.**

---

## Life in the Endless Dark

You were born in the Underdark, where no natural light has ever reached. The darkness is not your enemy—it is your home, your weapon, your birthright. While surface dwellers stumble blind, you see perfectly, navigating by the faint bioluminescence of fungal forests, the glow of magical crystals, and the heat signatures your people have learned to read like others read stars.

**Hierarchy (keth'zzar)** is not cultural preference. It is:
- How you prove your worth through competition
- How you ensure only the strong shape your people's future
- How you maintain perfect discipline in a hostile environment
- How you honor Vel'Zyren's Pattern of dominance

Drow society is built on **ambition measured against capability**. Weakness is not tolerated because weakness threatens survival. Beauty is cultivated as a weapon, grace refined into predation, and obedience enforced through divine certainty. You were raised knowing that Vel'Zyren watches through every mirror, judges through every reflection, and rewards only those who prove themselves worthy of her Pattern.

Your lifespan matches that of surface elves—750 years—but you measure time differently. Surface elves count seasons and harvests. You count **trials survived, rivals eliminated, reflections perfected, and positions claimed**.

Every major achievement is recorded in the **Mirror-Scrolls**—enchanted reflective surfaces that capture moments of triumph, preserved in House vaults where your accomplishments literally shine in darkness.

## Physical Characteristics

**Age:** You mature physically by age 20, but Drow society doesn't consider someone truly adult until age 100—when you've survived long enough to prove you deserve to continue. Few Drow die of old age; most die in intrigue, assassination, or battle.

**Size:** You stand between 5 to 6 feet tall, with builds ranging from lithe and graceful to lean and predatory. Your size is Medium, though your presence often makes you seem larger.

**Speed:** Your base walking speed is 30 feet. Generations in the Underdark have not slowed you—instead, you've learned to move with lethal purpose through cramped tunnels and vast caverns alike.

**Build:** Most Drow possess elegant, almost delicate features that disguise surprising strength. Your movements are fluid and deliberate, carrying the grace of your elven heritage refined through predatory necessity. Every gesture is calculated, every stance defensible.

**Appearance:** Your skin ranges from obsidian black to ash gray to deep purple-black. Your hair is typically stark white, silver, or pale blonde—a striking contrast that makes you unmistakable. Your eyes glow faintly in darkness: red, violet, blue, or pale gold. You are **beautiful** in a way that makes others uncomfortable—too perfect, too sharp, too dangerous.

## Drow Traits

As a Drow, you possess the following lineage traits:

### **Superior Darkvision**
You can see in dim light within 120 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

Your eyes are adapted to absolute darkness—pupils that dilate to capture every photon, retinas that process heat and magical emanations. You see in conditions that would blind any surface dweller.

### **Sunlight Sensitivity**
You have disadvantage on attack rolls and Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight when you, the target of your attack, or whatever you are trying to perceive is in direct sunlight.

This is the cost of Superior Darkvision—your eyes no longer tolerate brightness. Smart Drow plan around this limitation: fighting at dusk, dawn, underground, or using magical darkness. This isn't a flaw to be ashamed of; it's **the price of perfection in shadow**.

> *"The sun is their advantage. Darkness is ours. Choose your battlefield wisely."*

### **Keen Senses**
You have proficiency in the Perception skill.

Centuries of hunting in darkness, where the slightest sound means life or death, have sharpened every sense. You notice details others miss—the faint scrape of boot on stone, the subtle shift in air pressure, the almost-invisible tripwire.

### **Fey Ancestry**
You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed, and magic can't put you to sleep.

Your elven blood still carries traces of the Feywild, even though your people rejected its light. Vel'Zyren's transformation preserved this resistance—she wanted her chosen protected from rival gods' manipulation.

### **Trance**
You don't need to sleep. Instead, you meditate deeply for 4 hours a day, remaining semiconscious.

After resting in this way, you gain the same benefit that other races do from 8 hours of sleep.

Unlike surface elves who use Trance to relive ancestral memories, your Trance is **different**. You reflect on your position, plan tomorrow's moves, visualize potential threats and opportunities. Some Drow report seeing Vel'Zyren's web during Trance, feeling the weight of her gaze judging their worth.

**Mechanical Note:** Trance still counts as a long rest. You remain aware of surroundings and can wake instantly if threatened. During Trance, you are not accessing ancestral memories—you are **planning your ascension**.

### **Shadow Step** *(Unique Drow Trait)*
As a bonus action, you can magically teleport up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space you can see that is in dim light or darkness.

You can use this trait a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

This represents your innate connection to shadow—not learned magic, but **biological adaptation** refined by Vel'Zyren's blessing. Your body understands that darkness is not empty space but a network of threads you can step between.

### **Drow Magic**
Your innate magical heritage manifests in the following way:

- You know the *dancing lights* cantrip.
- When you reach 3rd level, you can cast *faerie fire* once, and you regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest.
- When you reach 5th level, you can cast *darkness* once, and you regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest.

Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells.

**Tactical Analysis:** While surface elves use magic to illuminate and reveal, Drow magic specializes in **misdirection and concealment**—lights that distract, fire that outlines prey, and darkness that blinds enemies while you see perfectly.

### **Drow Weapon Training**
You have proficiency with rapiers, shortswords, and hand crossbows.

The elegant weapons favored by your people for their precision and lethality. These aren't ceremonial—they're the tools you were trained with from childhood, weapons designed for Underdark combat where space is limited and noise can be fatal.

### **Languages**
You speak, read, and write Common, Elvish, and Undercommon.

Undercommon is the trade language of the Underdark—harsh, efficient, designed for negotiations where both parties expect betrayal. You likely speak it better than Elvish, which most Drow consider a relic of weakness.

## Mental Attributes & Psychology

**Focus Modifier:** +0  
**Sanity Modifier:** +0

Your mental baseline is standard, but your psychology is distinctly shaped by Drow culture. You are not inherently more focused or more sane than other elves—but your relationship with control, obedience, cruelty, and ambition is fundamentally different.

### **Cultural Mental Conditioning**
While your biology doesn't grant mental modifiers, your upbringing typically results in:

- **Exceptional Focus discipline:** Trained from childhood to maintain concentration under pressure (failure often means death)
- **Controlled emotional responses:** Taught to process fear as tactical information rather than panic
- **Mirror Reckoning conditioning:** Regular exposure to divine judgment through reflection rituals
- **Ambition management:** Learning to balance advancement with survival

Your people understand that **power without control is suicide**. You were taught to want more, to climb higher, to eliminate rivals—but to do so **intelligently**. The dead cannot enjoy their victories.

### **Corruption Vulnerability: The Cracked Mirror**

As a Drow, you have a unique relationship with Corruption. Your divine connection to Vel'Zyren provides some protection against random Eldritch influence, but corruption that enters through **mirrors, reflections, divine doubt, or rejection of hierarchy** affects you catastrophically.

**Corruption Resistance:** You have advantage on saving throws against Corruption from **chaos and disorder** sources (entities that attack structured thinking, anarchic forces).

Your mind is trained to maintain perfect hierarchy even under assault. Chaos cannot easily fracture what has been so rigidly organized.

**Corruption Vulnerability:** You have disadvantage on saves against Corruption from **mirrors, reflections, and divine judgment** sources.

Your deepest connection—to Vel'Zyren's Pattern, to the Mirror Reckoning, to seeing yourself as she wishes—becomes a vulnerability when Eldritch forces target it. The same pathways that let you commune with your goddess can become conduits for corruption.

**When you gain Corruption Score 10+ (Tier 2), you suffer:**
- Disturbed Trance: Roll on Disturbed Reflection table after each long rest
- Mirror Distortion: Reflective surfaces sometimes show you as Vel'Zyren wishes you to be rather than as you are
- Pattern Strain: The web's threads pull at your choices, limiting free will
- Hierarchy Confusion: Difficulty determining proper social positioning

**Disturbed Reflection Table (Corruption Tier 2+)**

When you complete a long rest via Trance at Corruption Score 10+, roll 1d6:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **False Perfection**: Your reflection shows you flawless. Make a DC 15 Wisdom save or become unable to acknowledge personal faults for 24 hours, gaining disadvantage on Insight checks as your judgment becomes unreliable. |
| 2 | **Web-Strain**: You feel Vel'Zyren's threads pulling at your will. You have disadvantage on saving throws against divine magic until your next long rest, and must succeed on a DC 12 Wisdom save to act against what you perceive as her wishes. |
| 3 | **Watched and Judged**: Vel'Zyren's gaze weighs upon you. Gain advantage on Intimidation checks but disadvantage on Deception checks for 24 hours. Mirrors seem to follow you with their reflections. |
| 4 | **Reflected Truth**: Your reflection shows you a truth about yourself you've been avoiding—a weakness, a fear, a failure. Make a DC 12 Sanity save or lose 1d4 Sanity as the realization strikes. |
| 5 | **Shadow Communion**: Something else speaks to you through the darkness during Trance, using Vel'Zyren's voice. Gain 1 Whisper Point but learn one piece of forbidden knowledge that conflicts with your goddess's teachings. |
| 6 | **Web's Grace**: Vel'Zyren's Pattern shields you from corruption this night, reaffirming your place in her design. Reduce your Corruption Score by 1 (minimum 10). |

**When you reach Corruption Tier 3 (CS 15+):**
Your connection to Vel'Zyren begins to fray—or worse, **something else begins using that connection**. Mirrors no longer show consistent reflections. The web feels wrong, threads pulling in directions that make no sense. Other Drow instinctively sense something corrupted about you—you smell wrong, your shadow moves strangely, your presence feels like discord in the Pattern.

At this level, you may be hunted by your own people as **corrupted** rather than faithful.

## Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Integration

### **Evasion Profile: Precision Predator**
- **Dodge Evasion:** +1 (graceful elven heritage refined by predatory training)
- **Endure Evasion:** -1 (lighter elven frame, not built to tank)
- **Deflect Evasion:** +0 (you prefer not to be hit at all)

**Tactical Note:** Your biology is built for **avoiding damage entirely through superior positioning**. You excel at not getting hit rather than tanking punishment. Use terrain, darkness, Shadow Step, and superior senses to control engagement range and strike from advantage.

### **Natural Damage Reduction:** +0
Your light frame provides no inherent protection—you rely on not being struck.

### **Combat Philosophy**
Drow fight with **lethal efficiency**. You don't duel honorably—you **assassinate strategically**. High Dodge Evasion + Shadow Step + darkness manipulation makes you excellent at:
- Ambush attacks from absolute darkness
- Hit-and-run tactics using teleportation
- Creating tactical blind spots with *darkness*
- Eliminating high-value targets before they can respond

You win fights by **not fighting fairly**. Fairness is a luxury for those who can afford to lose.

### **Breaking Point Mechanics**
Due to your cultural conditioning and hierarchical training, you have **advantage on Rally checks** when fighting for clear position or under orders from recognized authority, but **disadvantage** when fighting for ideals, mercy, or causes that don't advance your position.

Drow fight best when they understand **what they gain** from victory.

## Subraces

Drow culture varies based on your relationship with Vel'Zyren, your house status, and your position in the web. Choose one of the following subraces:

---

### House Drow (Web-Loyal)

> *"I serve my house. My house serves Vel'Zyren. Therefore, I serve perfection."*

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Charisma score increases by 1.

You were born into one of the Great Houses—matriarchal dynasties that have ruled Underdark cities for millennia. You understand that society is a **carefully maintained hierarchy** where position is earned through capability and maintained through vigilance.

You are not a fanatic—you're a **professional**. Vel'Zyren's Pattern works because it creates strong rulers, eliminates weakness, and rewards excellence. Whether you truly believe or simply benefit from the system, you've learned to work within it masterfully.

**Noble Heritage:** You have proficiency in the History skill and one of the following: Deception, Insight, or Persuasion (your choice).

Your education was comprehensive—you know House lineages, political alliances, and how to navigate high society.

**House Resources:** When in a Drow settlement aligned with your house (or willing to honor house agreements), you have access to:
- Safe lodging and basic supplies
- Information networks (with appropriate compensation)
- Legal protection (within Drow law)
- Introduction to useful contacts

**Web-Blessed:** You have advantage on saving throws against being frightened by creatures serving Vel'Zyren or aligned with her Pattern.

Your faith (genuine or performative) provides protection against divine fear.

**Mirror Reckoning:** Once per long rest, you can spend 10 minutes in ritual before a reflective surface to ask Vel'Zyren one yes/no question about your current position or immediate future. The answer comes as a feeling: certainty (yes), doubt (no), or silence (she will not answer).

This is not guaranteed truth—it's divine *guidance*, filtered through your interpretation and her agenda.

**DCS Integration:**
- **Evasion Modifier:** Enhanced profile (Dodge +2, Endure -1, Deflect +0)
- **Combat Style:** Tactical duelist who fights with precision and grace
- **Momentum Generation:** Calculated fighter—generates Momentum through advantageous positioning
- **Unique Role:** The Drow who treats combat like high-stakes politics

---

### Escaped Drow (Web-Breaker)

> *"They sent assassins. I sent them back in pieces. Eventually, they'll learn."*

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Constitution score increases by 1, and your Wisdom score increases by 1.

You are **anathema**. You fled the Underdark, rejected Vel'Zyren's Pattern, and somehow survived. Your house sent Retrieval Squads—elite assassins whose sole purpose is dragging you back for public execution. You killed them. They sent more. You're still here.

This makes you either the luckiest Drow alive or the most dangerous. You represent the one thing Drow society cannot tolerate: **proof that you can leave**.

**Hunted Reflexes:** You have advantage on initiative rolls.

Constant vigilance against assassination has made you impossibly alert. You sleep light, eat carefully, and trust no one completely.

**Survivor's Instinct:** When you are reduced to half your hit points or fewer, you gain a +2 bonus to AC until you regain hit points above half maximum.

Desperation has taught you to fight best when wounded.

**Severed Thread:** You have advantage on saving throws against divination magic and effects that would magically track or locate you.

Vel'Zyren's connection to you is **damaged**—not fully severed, but frayed enough that her sight doesn't follow as easily. This also means you no longer benefit from her protection.

**Shadow Refuge:** You've learned to hide in places most can't reach. During a short rest in dim light or darkness, you can spend one Hit Die to scout 120 feet in all directions, sensing large creatures and general terrain layout.

Paranoia has practical applications.

**Corruption Caveat:** You have disadvantage on saves against Corruption from **guilt, isolation, and purposelessness** sources. You rejected the Pattern but found nothing to replace it with. That emptiness makes you vulnerable.

**DCS Integration:**
- **Evasion Modifier:** Desperate survivor profile (Dodge +1, Endure +0, Deflect +0)
- **Combat Style:** Hit-and-run survivor who never commits fully
- **Momentum Generation:** Reactive fighter—generates Momentum when wounded or cornered
- **Unique Role:** The Drow who fights like they're always outnumbered (because they are)

---

### Web-Born Drow (Chosen of the Spider)

> *"Vel'Zyren showed me my purpose before I could speak. I am her thread made flesh."*

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Charisma score increases by 2.

You are **chosen**. Born during a Mirror Eclipse, marked with Vel'Zyren's sign, raised in the inner sanctum where the Pattern is studied and perfected. You don't merely serve your goddess—you **embody** her will.

Where House Drow navigate politics and Escaped Drow flee judgment, you **are** the judgment. You are priest, enforcer, and prophet. Your faith is not cultural—it's **absolute**.

**Divine Purpose:** You know the *guidance* cantrip. Additionally, once per long rest, you can cast *augury* without material components. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells.

Vel'Zyren speaks to you more clearly than to others.

**Mirror-Blessed:** You can use your Shadow Step ability twice as many times (2 × proficiency bonus) before needing a long rest.

Your connection to shadow is **divine** rather than merely biological.

**Pattern Reading:** You have advantage on Insight checks to determine someone's position in social hierarchies or their ambitions.

You see the web's threads more clearly than others—who is climbing, who is falling, who is about to strike.

**Zealot's Focus:** When you deal damage to a creature that has attacked you or an ally since your last turn, you deal an extra 1d4 psychic damage.

Your wrath carries divine weight.

**Corruption Caveat:** You have **double vulnerability** to Corruption from **divine doubt** sources. If you ever truly question Vel'Zyren's wisdom, the corruption hits twice as hard. Your faith is your strength and your greatest weakness.

**DCS Integration:**
- **Evasion Modifier:** Divinely mobile profile (Dodge +1, Endure -1, Deflect +0)
- **Combat Style:** Holy assassin who treats killing as sacrament
- **Momentum Generation:** Faithful striker—generates Momentum through divine purpose
- **Unique Role:** The Drow who fights with religious certainty

---

### Shadowborn Drow (Deep Wanderer)

> *"The Underdark has places even Houses don't claim. I was born in one. The darkness there is... different."*

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Dexterity score increases by 1, and your Intelligence score increases by 1.

You come from the deepest reaches of the Underdark—the unmapped territories where even Drow cities don't reach. You were raised by outcasts, wanderers, or isolated communities that maintain only nominal connection to House politics and Vel'Zyren's Pattern.

You understand darkness in ways city-born Drow don't. The deep places taught you that survival matters more than hierarchy, that cooperation sometimes beats competition, and that the Underdark itself has horrors that make House politics seem petty.

**Deep Darkvision:** Your superior darkvision extends to 150 feet (instead of 120 feet).

You've adapted to places where even magical light rarely reaches.

**Underdark Survivor:** You have proficiency in the Survival skill and advantage on Survival checks made in the Underdark.

You know how to find food, water, and shelter in places that actively try to kill you.

**Stone Sense:** You have advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks to detect secret doors, hidden passages, and structural weaknesses in stone.

The deep places taught you to read stone like city Drow read politics.

**Wild Shadow Step:** When you use Shadow Step, you can choose to arrive prone and hidden (if cover is available), gaining advantage on the next attack roll you make before the end of your turn.

You learned to use teleportation not for grace but for **survival**.

**DCS Integration:**
- **Evasion Modifier:** Wilderness profile (Dodge +1, Endure -1, Deflect +0)
- **Combat Style:** Guerrilla fighter using terrain and ambush
- **Momentum Generation:** Environmental fighter—generates Momentum through superior positioning
- **Unique Role:** The Drow who treats every fight like hunting dangerous prey

---

### Dusk Drow (Twilight-Touched)

> *"I walk between. Neither day nor night claims me fully."*

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Dexterity score increases by 1, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

You are **rare**—Drow who have adapted to surface life without fully abandoning the darkness. Whether through gradual exposure, magical intervention, or unusual bloodline, your eyes can tolerate dim light and twilight without the crippling disadvantage that affects other Drow.

You represent the possibility that Drow and surface elves might one day reconcile. Or you represent a weakening of the bloodline. Opinions vary dramatically.

**Dusk-Adapted Eyes:** Your Sunlight Sensitivity only applies in **bright** sunlight.

You can operate normally in dim light, twilight, dawn, dusk, overcast conditions, and shade without penalty. This is an enormous tactical advantage compared to other Drow.

**Twilight Step:** You can use Shadow Step in dim light as well as darkness.

Your connection to shadow extends to the boundary between light and dark.

**Cultural Bridge:** You have advantage on Charisma (Persuasion) checks when interacting with surface elves or other Drow who have left the Underdark.

You understand both worlds and can translate between them.

**Reduced Darkvision:** Your superior darkvision is only 90 feet (instead of 120 feet).

This is the trade-off for light tolerance—your eyes are less sensitive in absolute darkness.

**DCS Integration:**
- **Evasion Modifier:** Adaptive profile (Dodge +1, Endure -1, Deflect +0)
- **Combat Style:** Flexible fighter who adapts to any lighting condition
- **Momentum Generation:** Opportunistic fighter—generates Momentum by exploiting environmental changes
- **Unique Role:** The Drow who can operate anywhere

## Playing a Drow

### Personality

Drow are not monolithic. A House Drow politician and a Shadowborn wanderer have little in common beyond shared ancestry. However, certain tendencies run through Drow psychology:

**Calculated Ambition:** You want more—more power, more respect, more security. But you want it **intelligently**.

Reckless ambition kills. Patient ambition succeeds.

**Tactical Cruelty:** Mercy is fine when it serves purpose. Cruelty is fine when it serves purpose.

You don't hurt people for pleasure (usually)—you hurt them when it's **useful**. This makes you more dangerous than sadists, who are predictable.

**Hierarchical Thinking:** You instinctively categorize people as above you (threats), below you (resources), or equal (rivals).

This isn't malicious—it's how you were taught to understand the world.

**Beauty as Standard:** You maintain appearance not from vanity but from **professional necessity**.

In Drow society, looking weak is dangerous. Looking impressive is armor.

**Pragmatic Loyalty:** You're loyal to those who benefit you and betray those who become liabilities.

This seems cold to surface dwellers, but you see it as honest. Surface folk just pretend their loyalty isn't transactional.

### Roleplaying Suggestions

**Assess Hierarchy:** When entering new situations, quickly determine who's in charge, who has real power (often different), and where you fit.

**Question Mercy:** When allies show mercy, ask why. Not judgmentally—genuinely. What does this serve? What does this cost?

**Appreciate Excellence:** Notice when things are done well. Drow respect competence regardless of source.

**Plan Contingencies:** Always have an exit strategy. Always have a backup plan. Trust is a luxury you can't always afford.

**Adapt to Light:** If you have Sunlight Sensitivity, mention struggling in bright conditions. Squinting, using shade, preferring dusk operations.

### Names

**Male Names:** Drizzt, Gromph, Jarlaxle, Zaknaefein, Rizzen, Pharaun, Berg'inyon, Valas, Nimor, Ryld, Halisstra, Danifae

**Female Names:** Malice, Briza, Maya, Vierna, Quenthel, Yvonnel, Triel, Sosdrielle, Liriel, Shakti, Eclavdra, Ginafae

**House Names:** Do'Urden, Baenre, Barrison Del'Armgo, Xorlarrin, Faen Tlabbar, Oblodra, Despana, Vandree

Drow names are often harsh-sounding to surface ears, with many apostrophes and hard consonants. House names carry tremendous weight—identifying yourself by house signals your position and alliances.

Many escaped Drow take surface names or drop their house name entirely.

## Drow in Dór-o Estel

The Drow presence in Dór-o Estel is **complicated**. Most surface dwellers have never seen a Drow in person—only heard stories of raids, assassinations, and horrors from the deep. Those stories are often **accurate**.

**After the Collapse of Ashkarra**, Drow houses sensed opportunity. Several launched major raids to the surface, capturing resources and slaves while surface nations were weakened. This earned your people a reputation that lingers.

However, a small but growing number of **Escaped Drow** have fled the Underdark. They seek redemption, freedom, or simply to survive. They are hunted by Retrieval Squads, mistrusted by surface dwellers, and represent a phenomenon Drow society claims is impossible: **defection**.

### Major Drow Cities

**Zil'Kathara (The First Web):** Largest Drow city, ruled by House Baenre for over 2,000 years.

**Vel'Shareth (The Mirror-City):** Center of Vel'Zyren worship, where the Pattern is studied most intensely.

**Graz'Zhendul (The Bleeding Market):** Trading hub where surface goods are exchanged for Underdark treasures (and slaves).

**Nar'Vhessek (The Severed House):** Abandoned city, now home to Escaped Drow attempting to build something new (constantly under siege).

### When Working with Other Races

**Surface Elves:** They see you as fallen. You see them as naive. Both are partially right.

**Dwarves:** Mutual respect for craftsmanship, mutual distrust for everything else. They defend well. You attack better.

**Humans:** Fascinatingly short-lived. They accomplish much but understand little. Easy to manipulate, surprisingly resilient.

**Orcs:** They understand hierarchy and strength. You could work together—if you could trust each other. (You can't.)

**Gnomes:** Clever enough to be useful, foolish enough to trust. Handle carefully.

## Integration with Mental Systems

### **Focus & Sanity:**
Your **standard mental attributes** mean you're as vulnerable as anyone else to mental trauma—but your **cultural conditioning** provides practical advantages:
- Trained to compartmentalize fear and use it tactically
- Experienced at maintaining focus under threat (or causing it)
- Familiar with psychological manipulation (defending and attacking)
- However, isolation and guilt hit you harder than expected

**Typical Drow Mental Strategy:**
- Use high discipline to maintain Focus during complex operations
- Frame horror as "another threat to manage"
- Rely on hierarchical thinking to reduce decision paralysis
- Be cautious around mirrors and reflections when Corrupted

### **Corruption Concerns:**
Your unique corruption vulnerability centers on **mirrors and divine connection**:
- Reflective surfaces become portals for corruption at Tier 2+
- Divine doubt triggers massive corruption spikes
- Vel'Zyren's displeasure can manifest as corruption effects
- Escaped Drow face corruption from guilt and purposelessness

### **Rally & Morale:**
Drow have situational advantages in morale systems:
- Advantage on Rally when fighting for position or under clear authority
- Disadvantage on Rally when fighting for ideals without personal benefit
- Your presence demoralizes enemies who believe your reputation
- Allies who trust you fight better; allies who don't trust you fight worse

**House Mission:**
- Political assassination disguised as adventuring
- Gathering intelligence on surface powers
- Seeking artifacts for your matron
- Exiled temporarily to prove worth through surface achievements

**Escaped:**

- Seeking allies powerful enough to protect you

- Running until you find somewhere—anywhere—safe
- Seeking magic to permanently sever Vel'Zyren's connection
- Hoping to find others like you (if any exist)

**Divine Mission:**
- Spreading Vel'Zyren's Pattern to surface world
- Investigating disruptions in the divine web
- Testing surface defenses for future conquest
- Seeking forbidden knowledge that even Houses don't possess
- Proving your worth to ascend in religious hierarchy

**Personal:**
- Young Drow seeking to prove themselves before returning home
- Exile trying to survive long enough to be forgiven
- Outcast who doesn't fit Drow society seeking purpose elsewhere
- Scholar studying surface cultures (potentially for invasion, potentially from curiosity)

- Return home in glory, position secured
- Destroy those who exiled or betrayed you

## Design Philosophy

The Drow lineage is designed to support:

1. **The Stealth Assassin** (classical Drow fantasy—Shadow Step + darkness magic)
2. **The Desperate Fugitive** (Escaped Drow surviving against impossible odds)
3. **The Political Infiltrator** (House Drow navigating surface society)
4. **The Divine Zealot** (Web-Born spreading Vel'Zyren's Pattern)
5. **The Lost Soul** (caught between worlds, belonging nowhere)

Your mechanical abilities (Shadow Step, Superior Darkvision, darkness magic) provide tangible advantages in stealth, infiltration, and darkness-based combat while Sunlight Sensitivity creates meaningful tactical challenges that force interesting decisions.

The subrace options allow you to lean into traditional Drow society (House Drow, Web-Born), embrace the wildest reaches (Shadowborn), tell survival stories (Escaped Drow), or bridge worlds (Dusk Drow)—while maintaining the core Drow identity of lethal elegance.

## Mechanical Considerations

### **Optimal Classes:**
Your abilities synergize excellently with:

- **Rogue:** Perfect synergy (Sneak Attack + Shadow Step + darkness = assassin perfection)
- **Ranger (Gloom Stalker):** Underdark specialist who dominates darkness
- **Warlock:** Your patron can't be worse than Vel'Zyren (probably)
- **Monk (Shadow):** Shadow Step stacks with ki abilities for extreme mobility
- **Fighter (Eldritch Knight):** Drow Magic + martial prowess + armor
- **Wizard (Bladesinger):** Intelligence builds get free utility spells and mobility

### **Strong But Less Traditional:**
- **Paladin (Oathbreaker/Conquest):** Dark knight who uses fear as weapon
- **Cleric:** Reformed Drow worshiping surface gods creates compelling tension
- **Bard:** Weaponize your people's reputation for psychological warfare
- **Barbarian:** Rage-fueled darkness user with hit-and-run tactics

### **Multiclass Synergies:**
- **Rogue/Fighter:** Precision + Action Surge for devastating alpha strikes
- **Ranger/Rogue:** Ultimate scout who sees everything, is seen by nothing
- **Warlock/Rogue:** Eldritch Blast + Sneak Attack + teleportation
- **Fighter/Wizard:** Gish builds with unmatched mobility

## Final Notes

### **Vel'Zyren's Influence:**
Even if you've rejected her, Vel'Zyren shaped your people's transformation from surface elves into Drow. Her Pattern runs through your blood, your culture, your very concept of self. You can flee the Underdark, but you cannot easily flee her web.

The threads still pull at you—in dreams, in moments of reflection, in the way you instinctively assess hierarchy and position. **Escaping physically is one thing. Escaping mentally is another.**

### **The Surface World:**
Most surface dwellers have never seen a Drow. Those who have survived encounters with raiders, assassins, or worse. **Your people earned their reputation.**

Playing a Drow means accepting that initial distrust is narratively appropriate and working through it creates compelling character development. You don't get to skip the "proving you're different" phase—that's part of your story.

### **Fellow Drow:**
If multiple players choose Drow, discuss:
- Same house or rival houses?
- Coordinated mission or coincidental meeting?
- How do you maintain operational security?
- What prevents betrayal? (Drow-style answer: "Because working together serves our goals better than betrayal—for now.")

### **Redemption vs. Survival:**
If you're an Escaped Drow, decide: Are you seeking **redemption** (proving you're good now) or **survival** (proving you deserve to exist despite your past)?

These are different stories with different challenges. Redemption requires confronting what you've done. Survival just requires staying ahead of the assassins.

### **The Mirror Question:**
Every Drow must eventually answer: When you look in a mirror, who do you see?

The person Vel'Zyren made you? The person you're trying to become? Something in between? Or something you don't recognize at all?
