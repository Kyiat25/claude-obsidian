# Sanity: The Mind's Delicate Armour

## A Warning from the Chronicler's Archives

*Listen carefully, brave soul.*

Not all wounds are visible. Some cut deeper than any blade, carving silent scars into the landscape of your mind. Sanity is your most vulnerable—and most crucial—defence against the horrors that lurk beyond the veil of perceived reality.

### The Essence of Sanity

Imagine your consciousness as a fragile lantern, flickering against the vast, unknowable darkness of cosmic horror. Sanity is not merely an attribute—it is the thin membrane separating reason from madness, hope from despair, reality from nightmare.

> *"In the vast theatre of existential terror, your mind is both the stage and the audience."*

---
## The Anatomy of Mental Resilience

### Your Mental Landscape

**Sanity** is a core attribute like Strength or Dexterity, representing a character’s ability to endure trauma, [[madness]], resist mental corruption, and maintain a stable grasp on reality.

 Starts between **8–20**, using rolled generation.
- Point buy = 10+spellcasting modifier + con modifier. (Charisma if does not have a spellcasting modifier)
- **Sanity Modifier** = (Focus Score - 10) ÷ 2 (rounded down)
- Used in **Sanity Checks**, **Sanity Saves**, and as a buffer against **Eldritch interactions and instability**, **fear and madness checks**, and **Corruption escalation**

Optionally you can throw a straight D20 for rolled generation if you want a more extreme/harsh system. If so and they equal less than 5 provide a feat associated with madness and if a 1 = feat plus Telepathic (but when used there is a chance no one understands or takes physic damage)

---
### The Mechanics of Mental Survival

#### Saving Your Consciousness

When reality itself becomes a weapon, you'll face saving throws that test the very fabric of your mind:

**Sanity Save Principles**:
- Roll against increasingly terrifying DCs
- Failure brings more than simple damage
- Each test chips away at your mental defences

**Confronting the Unthinkable**:
```
When Cosmic Horror Calls:
1. Encounter triggers SAN save
2. Roll d20 + SAN Modifier
3. Measure against encounter's madness DC
4. Consequences cascade beyond mere numbers
```

---
### The Descent into Madness

#### Temporary Fractures

Failing a sanity check isn't just a number—it's a journey into psychological darkness:
- Temporary losses manifest as:
  - [[Exhaustion]] levels
  - Phantom sensations
  - Momentary hallucinations
  - Existential tremors

#### Permanent Scars

Some horrors leave marks that never fully heal:
- Prolonged exposure
- Cosmic encounters
- Accumulated psychological trauma

Each scar tells a story of survival—or surrender.

## 🧠 **Sanity Score**

- Starts between **8–20**, using standard point buy or rolled generation.
- **Sanity Modifier** = (Sanity Score - 10) ÷ 2 (rounded down).
- Used in **Sanity Checks**, **Sanity Saves**, and as a threshold for **mental trauma**, **madness**, or **Eldritch effects**.

---
## 🎲 **When to Roll Sanity**

Sanity is tested when characters confront:

- Incomprehensible truths or glyphs.
- Direct exposure to **[[Eldritch Moons]]** or the **[[Null Reflection]]**.
- Visions, hallucinations, psychic attacks.
- Events that would shatter a typical mind (e.g., witnessing a god’s corpse, failing to save a loved one, or reliving traumatic memories).

---
## 📉 **Sanity Loss**

Sanity can be:

- **Temporarily reduced** (stress, fear, exposure). Recovers naturally.
- **Permanently reduced** (Eldritch contact, memory fractures, psychic damage). Requires greater healing or narrative intervention.

| **Effect Type** | **Recovery**                                                | **Source**                                                             |
| --------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Temporary Loss  | [[Long Rest]], Calm Emotions, Safe Haven, Sanity Aid        | Combat panic, glyph contact, fear-based effects                        |
| Permanent Loss  | [[Greater Restoration]], Dreamwalking, deep spiritual rites | Eldritch corruption, direct contact with Null beings, psychic collapse |

# ⚠️ **Sanity Thresholds & Effects**

|**Sanity Score**|**State**|**Effect**|
|---|---|---|
|13–20|Stable|No effect|
|9–12|Frayed|Disadvantage on Sanity saves vs. illusions or mental stress|
|5–8|Unraveling|Disadvantage on all Sanity checks and saves|
|1–4|Deranged|Gain 1 **Mental Trauma** (see below)|
|0|Broken|Immediate **Insanity**, **Narrative Control**, or **DM Fiat consequence**|

> **Note**: Dropping below a threshold immediately applies the associated condition.

---
## 🧩 **Mental Trauma Table (Roll 1d6)**

|d6|Trauma|Effect|
|---|---|---|
|1|**Delusions**|Believe falsehoods are real; Disadvantage on Insight|
|2|**Paranoia**|Disadvantage on Social checks involving trust|
|3|**Hallucinations**|DM may insert false sensory data|
|4|**Obsession**|Must repeat last action or pursue fixation|
|5|**Split Identity**|Personality fracture; roleplay change|
|6|**Recurring Visions**|Disadvantage on Focus saves in combat|

Each trauma is **persistent until resolved** via Sanity recovery and personal roleplay intervention.

---
## 🧩 **Interplay with Other Stats**

- **Focus**: Repeated Focus failures under pressure may trigger Sanity checks.
- **Corruption**: Increasing corruption may reduce maximum Sanity or trigger trauma.
- **Class Interaction**:
    - **Psions, Warlocks, Thaumaturges** may be at greater risk or have tools to buffer loss.
    - **Investigators** may gain bonuses when resisting madness due to training.

---
## 🧠 **At 0 Sanity: The Mind Breaks**

The character becomes **[[Mentally Broken]]**:

- Controlled by DM, or
- Suffers irreversible changes: possession, catatonia, mind-fracture, alignment shift, etc.
- Recovering from 0 requires both **magic** and **narrative healing**.

# 🔗 Sanity and Corruption

> "The deeper the crack, the louder the whispers."

Sanity doesn’t just defend against fear—it is the mind's last dam holding back **Eldritch corruption**.

|Sanity State|Corruption Effect|
|---|---|
|**Frayed** (SAN 9–12)|Failure on a Sanity save in a corrupted zone causes +1 Corruption.|
|**Unraveling** (SAN 5–8)|Every failed Sanity save causes +1 Corruption.|
|**Deranged** (SAN 1–4)|Eldritch exposure always causes Corruption, even passively.|
|**Broken** (SAN 0)|Immediate escalation to Terminal Corruption.|

---
## 🎲 Sanity Collapse & the Glyphic Soul

When Sanity reaches **0**, you are considered to have:

- Lost all resistance to Eldritch whispers
- Gained **Terminal Corruption** (20+)
- Become eligible for transformation into:
    - **Echo-Bearer**
    - **Glyphspawn**
    - **Mind-fractured narrative entity** under GM control

# 🧠 Focus vs. Sanity vs. Corruption – How They Interlock

| Attribute            | Role                                         | Mechanically Defends Against                      | Triggers                                           | Result of Failure                                    |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| **Focus**            | Mental clarity and resistance under pressure | Illusions, charm, psychic magic, magical collapse | Concentration disruption, Arcane Rifts, Wild Magic | Loss of spell control, confusion, spell feedback     |
| **Sanity**           | Stability of the mind vs. existential terror | Eldritch exposure, trauma, glyph contact          | Eldritch zones, cosmic horrors, narrative despair  | Madness, trauma, Corruption Score gain               |
| **Corruption Score** | Exposure to anti-Weave forces                | Grows from repeated failure of Sanity/Focus saves | Corruption sources (see doc), failed saves         | Tiers of corruption → mutations, madness, glyph loss |

---

## 🔁 Gameplay Loop Summary

1. **You enter a Dead Magic Zone**.
2. **Cast a spell** → trigger **Focus check** (to control surge or prevent feedback).
    - **Fail** = Spell chaos, possibly forced glyph recursion.
3. **Eldritch zone exposure** triggers **Sanity save**.
    - **Fail** = +1 Corruption Score.
4. **Corruption Score hits 5+** → enter **Minor Corruption Tier**.
5. Further exposure + trauma → **Sanity drops**, causing more CS gain → escalate to **Major or Eldritch Tiers**.
6. **Terminal Corruption (CS 20+)** triggers loss of control, mutation, glyph erasure, or transformation.

## 🛠 Optional Improvements

- **Let Focus Score buffer Sanity degradation** (e.g. Focus 16+ = ignore first +1 CS from spellcast failure).
- **Use Corruption Tiers as DC baselines** for Focus/Sanity saves in Eldritch zones:
    - Minor Corruption area: DC 12
    - Major: DC 15
    - Eldritch: DC 18+
- **Sanity Threshold drops** could **trigger Focus fatigue**:
    > “When your Sanity shifts from Frayed → Unravelling, you suffer Disadvantage on your next Focus Save.”
