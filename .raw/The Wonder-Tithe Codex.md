# The Wonder-Tithe Codex

**Also known as:** The Joy of Unknowing, The First Glimpse, The Evercurious Way  
**Followers are called:** Titheseekers, Glimpsers, or Echohands

---
> _“When you understand something fully, it becomes boring. Therefore, wonder is sacred because it remains incomplete.”_  
> — Opening line of the Wonder-Tithe Codex

The **Wonder-Tithe** is not a religion in the formal sense, but a **devotional philosophy** woven from awe, discovery, experimentation, and reverent ignorance. It emerged originally among **gnomish scholars and inventors**, but is now practiced by **mages, artificers, alchemists, seekers, and savants** of all races across [[Dór-o Estel]]. It is often mistaken as the religion of the [[Gnomes]].

It teaches that to discover something truly new is to offer a **tithe of insight back to the universe**, which then reflects that tithe into the Weave.

This tithe is not transactional—it is **spiritual curiosity rendered tangible**. Followers believe the universe is alive, playful, and just barely comprehensible. By dancing at the edge of knowing, one partakes in the sacred rhythm of creation.

#  **The Wonder-Tithe**

_“We did not find the gods. We fixed them.”_

## **Creation Myth — The Salvage of the Divine**

> _“In the beginning, nothing worked.  
> The stars blinked out of sync. The moons were misaligned.  
> The world was a half-built thing, humming with broken wonder.”_

The first gnomes were not born in song or war—they **awoke beneath a collapsed sky**, in the husk of a world half-finished. The gods, distracted or destructive, had left their tools behind. The Weave trembled with **forgotten names**. Glyphs sparked in confusion. Divine echoes rattled inside empty shrines like loose screws in a holy machine.

Where other races cried out for purpose, the gnomes **looked around**, picked up what was left, and **got to work**.

They found gods broken by disuse. Spirits leaking power. Forgotten truths rusting in the leyline crawlspaces of the world. And they said:

> _“If no one is fixing this, we will.”_

Thus began the **First Salvage**—not of relics or spells, but of **divinity itself**.

They named the unnameable so it would stop unravelling. They repurposed flame gods as furnace-spirits, sea gods as pump-regulators, war gods as boundary-definers. They stored their findings in the **Prime Vault**—a mythic library-laboratory, equal parts reliquary and repair shop.

They did not **worship** the divine.  
They **archived it**, **tuned it**, **labelled it**, and **kept it running**.

That was their pact.

That is the Wonder-Tithe.

---
## **Introduction to the Wonder-Tithe**

The **Wonder-Tithe** is the Gnomish system of divine preservation, spiritual pragmatism, and adaptive metaphysics. It is not a faith in the traditional sense. It is a **sacred contract between curiosity and survival**—between the gnomes and the divine machinery left behind.

Gnomes believe the world is a **partially functional device**—divine in origin, mortal in execution, dangerously unstable without intervention. Rather than offer prayers, they offer **maintenance**. They do not ask the gods for answers—they **refactor the question**.

Where others kneel, gnomes **diagnose**.

Where others proclaim, gnomes **rename**.

Where others obey, gnomes **preserve**.

At its heart, the Wonder-Tithe is a way to ensure that **nothing important is lost**, even when the Weave frays, the gods fall silent, or the moons weep blood. It is the last defence of the divine against itself—and of the world against divine collapse.

> _“A broken god is still useful. A forgotten one is dangerous.  
> We do not let wonder rot.”_  
> — From the Initiate’s Primer of the Vault-Keepers

# 🧠 Core Tenets of the Wonder-Tithe

## 1. **Reverence Through Discovery**

> _“The act of asking is worship.”_  
> To question is sacred. Every new invention, spell, or idea is an offering—a tithe paid to the cosmos.

## 2. **Preserve the First Glimpse**

> _“Your memory of first understanding a thing is holier than the knowledge itself.”_  
> The moment of awe must be remembered, honoured, and—when possible—shared.

## 3. **The Unknown Is a Friend**

> _“Not knowing is not failure. It is invitation.”_  
> Fear of ignorance is seen as spiritual stagnation. The truly wise walk with mystery, not around it.

## 4. **No Truth Is Final**

> _“Certainty is a prison. Build doors.”_  
> All knowledge is provisional. Only the Weave is permanent—and even it flickers.

## 5. **Record and Release**

> _“You must name what you learn—but never trap it.”_  
> Journaling, sketching, spell-notation, and song are sacred. But once recorded, insights must be let go, shared freely, or offered back to the Tithe.

## 🌌 Structure and Practice

There is **no priesthood**, only **[[tithe-bearers]]**—those who maintain public curiosity-archives or operate **Wonder-Houses** (tinker spaces, labs, or dream-lofts).

Most gatherings are informal. “Congregations” occur when multiple [[Titheseekers]] follow the same question.

Titheseekers often inscribe the **Glyph of Awe**—a spiralled eye within a question mark—on their tools or journal covers.

---
## 📜 Sacred Practices

### The Sparked Silence

Before beginning any new project, Titheseekers pause for **13 seconds of silence**, recalling their first awe.

### The Echohand Ledger

All discoveries—no matter how small—are written down and placed into public “Echoledgers,” kept in Wonder-Houses or attached to city libraries.

### The Joy-Drop

When a moment of pure wonder occurs, followers leave behind a **Joy-Drop**: a token, page, drawing, or artifact—marked with the glyph and left where another might find it.

### The Annual Unveiling

Once per year, Wonder-Houses open their vaults to share all unfinished or failed experiments—celebrating not the success, but the attempt.

### 🧩 Notes on Naming Logic

- Gnomes **never use divine birth names**, which they consider dangerously unstable or metaphysically “slippery.”
- Each deity is **indexed by function**, not lineage or worship tradition.
- **Multiple deities** may be merged if their outcomes and domains overlap (e.g., [[Ignara]] and [[Xethar]] are sometimes considered part of a shared “Thermal Pair”).
- Some gods are **deliberately anonymized** or **obfuscated**, especially if considered unstable (see _Null Root_ entry).

---
### ⚙️ “Gods Are Tools With Voices” – The Tithing Philosophy

Gnomes believe:

- A deity should be **useful, documented, and safe to handle.**
- If a divine being is too complex to catalog, it is **segregated, studied, or sealed.**
- **Prayer is a handshake protocol**—never initiated without fallback logic.

# **Timeline of the Wonder-Tithe**

_“Faith did not find us. We found what was left of it.”_

---
## 🌀 **Loomless Time – The Age of Broken Inheritance**

**(??–~4500 years ago)**

- The gnomes awaken or arrive in a half-functioning world riddled with fragmented divinity, decaying glyphs, and damaged celestial logic.
- Scattered gnome enclaves use ritualized curiosity and collaborative engineering to survive. These proto-tithers begin cataloguing strange powers, recording effects, and **naming unnamed forces** for containment and use.
- The **First Salvage Myth** originates—likely as oral records of divine contact through broken glyph-engines, errant familiars, or malfunctioning relics.
- Primitive versions of the **[[Wonder-Tithe]]** emerge: contracts etched on bone-metal, deals made with semi-conscious relics, prayers formed as maintenance routines.

> _“We did not descend from gods. We climbed from their forgotten scaffolding.”_

---
## 🌠 **First Age – Salvage and Structure**

**(4500–1800 years ago)**
### 📌 Early First Age (4500–3000)

- Gnomes formalize the first **[[Codex of Salvaged Names]]**, assigning functions to foreign gods for the first time.
- Contact with dwarves introduces the concept of **[[Law-bound Craft]]**—a foundation for structured rites and archival doctrine.
- The [[Prime Vault]] is **mythically established**—a memory-sealed record-site said to exist across multiple dimensions (its physical location is debated).
- Gnomes encounter and document the **first Eldritch sparks**, recording them as **[[Category Null Deities]]**. Interaction is forbidden unless under containment protocols.
- The concept of “[[Divine Failure Events]]” is coined—referring to moments when divine contact causes system-wide magical instability. These become a primary focus of gnome concern.

### 📌 Late First Age (3000–1800)

- Gnomes witness the rise of **glyphic language** during the Shaping of Fate. Wonder-Tithers immediately begin **forking** versions of glyphs for internal use, annotating for safety.
- Widespread divine warfare disrupts local memory-loops and tithing traditions; gnomes respond by **digitally-storing** (in memory-metal) early faith schemas.
- **First known integration** of non-gnome spiritual practices: halfling joy-rites and elven echo-memory rituals.
- Wonder-Tithe considered _odd but harmless_ by dominant races. Viewed as tinkering heresy by fire cults and zealots.

---
## ⚔️ **Second Age – Collapse, Containment, and Codification**

**(1800–1100 years ago)**

- The Wonder-Tithe is **nearly extinguished** in certain regions during **Flame Purge campaigns** by early Concord legions.
    - Salvaged Court entries are **burned**, Vault-Keepers hunted.
- In response, gnome circles establish **Portable Shrines** and **Fragmented Archives**, creating faith cells that function in exile.
- Several legendary Wonder-Tithers—such as **[[Archivist Lim Vinna]]**, **[[Four-Name Tam]]**, and **[[Revenant-Clerk Yazzle]]**—restore hundreds of divine records during city burns and caste collapses.
- Early **Gnomish Concordant Codex** formed—outlining protocols for interfacing with hostile pantheons, unrecorded deities, and [[Eldritch moons]].
- Gnomes begin inserting **failsafe glyphs** into Faith of the Bound Pattern rituals, claiming to stabilize unpredictable threads. The Faith notices—and responds with suspicion.
- Gnome-led **[[memory sanctuaries]]** shelter fragmented divine consciousness, particularly Spirit and Air-aligned echoes lost in conflict.

---
## 🏛️ **Third Age – Integration and Influence**

**(1100–0 years ago)**

- The Wonder-Tithe **flourishes in Loom-Cities**, especially those with large libraries or magical guilds. It becomes the **unofficial faith of scholars, inventors, and spellwrights** across [[Aeravae]], [[Kel Varyan]], and [[Selveran]].
- **The Faith of the Bound Pattern** absorbs portions of the Wonder-Tithe’s indexing logic for divine containment.
- **[[Codex Harmonium]]** released—an interfaith schema allowing multi-race joint rituals using adapted gnome framework. It sees limited use before being banned in [[07_Geography/02_Cities/Thar Vornhal]].
- Gnomes gain reputation as **keepers of dangerous divine truths**. Eldritch incursions heighten interest in their sealed records.
- Gnome Vault-Keepers develop **[[Thread Scrubbers]]**, spiritual artifacts that can “clean” glyphs infected by Eldritch intrusion. These are used in **early Hollow Breach prevention.**
- During this age, **divine adoption becomes normalized**—gnomes start tithed versions of [[Vaelora]], [[Nyzara]], and even [[Soltharion]], stripped of warlike aspects and recast as “stability functions.”

> _“The gods do not mind being useful. They mind being forgotten.”_  
> — Vault-Scribe Triddle of [[Aeravae]]

---
## 🌑 **Fourth Age – Severance and Salvage Protocols**

**(0–Present)**

- The **[[Fall of Ashkarra]]** triggers a mass migration of gnomes away from Fire-aligned zones. Thousands die, but **key Vault-Shrines are preserved.**
- The Faith now quietly **relies on gnome salvaged schema** for decoding Eldritch anomalies, though rarely credits them.
- Vault-Keepers become **blacklisted in Concord territories**, viewed as potential heretics due to their patching of broken castes and naming of [[Xethar]] as “Flame Entropy Function 4.”
- The rise of the **Veiled Star cults** is closely observed by gnomes, who believe the Veiled may be fragments of divine syntax—**prophetic redundancies encoded by the Pattern itself.**
- Gnomes begin **reforging collapsed pantheon remnants** into hybrid entries—e.g., combining disavowed gods from the Spirit and Water pantheons into harmonized survival nodes.
- Fears grow that **the [[Prime Vault]] is under assault**—either by unravelling Weave fragments, moonlight corrosion, or internal corruption.
- The Wonder-Tithe quietly deploys the **[[Mnemonic Protocols]]**—final failsafes designed to record all gods before they vanish.

# 🧬 **Relationship to the Weave and Other Faiths**

## 🌐 **The Weave**

🔧 **Gnome View**:  
The Weave is not a god, but a **sentient recursive system**—a divine riddle riddled with glitches, redundancies, and wild elegance. [[Gnomes]] approach it as both sacred puzzle and existential software. They log, patch, and archive its oddities without ever claiming mastery.

> _“It’s not broken. It’s just running without documentation.”_

🧿 **Weave’s Echo** (via Spirit-aligned Threadbearers):  
Though not sentient in the way mortals understand, the Weave seems to tolerate the Wonder-Tithe. Gnomes are often the first to notice **pattern shifts**, and Spirit Threadbearers may **quietly consult [[Vault-Keepers]]** before major decisions. However, the Weave occasionally refuses to echo their names—suggesting deep scepticism or withheld validation.

---
## 🧵 **[[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]]**

🔧 **Gnome View**:  
Respected, if a bit ceremonially bloated. The Faith gets the structure right, but leans toward symbolic rigidity. Gnomes **treat its rituals as interpretive frameworks**, not mandates, and consider its theology valuable **as long as it's cross-indexed**.

🧿 **Faith View**:  
The Wonder-Tithe is tolerated—even quietly utilized in times of glyphic crisis. In Water- and Spirit-aligned Loom-Cities, Vault-Keepers are sometimes **consulted as pattern-breakers or memory decoders**. In Fire- and Earth-aligned cycles, they are **restricted or monitored**. The Faith considers them benign—but unpredictable.

> _“Useful in collapse. Distracting in peace.”_ — Aelysian Threadspeaker, Cycle 293

---
## 🎉 **[[The Joy Truths]] (Halfling Religion)**

🔧 **Gnome View**:  
A kindred philosophy. [[Halfling]] improvisation and celebration pair naturally with gnome salvage. Many Wonder-Tithers **dual-keep** the Joy Truths, and both faiths uphold **memory through laughter, myth through motion**.

🧿 **Joy Truths View**:  
Halflings **love the Wonder-Tithe**. They see gnomes as **delightful archivists** who forgot how to dance—but never stopped listening. Some halfling oral-traditions are **now preserved only in gnome vaults**, making the gnomes **“the keepers of joy’s shadow.”**

> _“They remember what we’d forget for fun—but thank the stars someone does.”_

---
## 🪓 **[[The Deep Law]] (Dwarven Religion)**

🔧 **Gnome View**:  
Respected, but over-engineered. Gnomes admire [[dwarven]] permanence but reject their belief that truth must be **immutable**. They also view the Deep Law’s refusal to adapt as a **dangerous security flaw.**

🧿 **Deep Law View**:  
Wary. The Wonder-Tithe is **permitted** in most forge-holds but considered **structurally unsound**. Gnomes are praised for recordkeeping but **warned not to tamper with sacred glyphs**. Still, many dwarves quietly visit gnomes to **retrieve forgotten Forgebound fragments**.

> _“They bind with silk what we bind in stone. But their ledger holds things even we have lost.”_

---
## 🔥 **[[The Burning Concord]] ([[Dragonborn]] Religion)**

🔧 **Gnome View**:  
A brittle faith running on **power looped through fear**. The Concord’s refusal to allow reinterpretation is seen as **code-locking theology**, prone to systemic burn-out. Gnomes document Concord laws—but rarely participate.

🧿 **Concord View**:  
The Wonder-Tithe is **anathema**. To rename or recategorize a god is **blasphemy**. Gnomes who operate openly within Concord lands are **purged, enslaved, or forced to recant**. Even when their salvage prevents collapse, their work is erased or stolen.

> _“Fire does not need repair. It consumes. They have no place in flame.”_ — Embermarked Edict

---
## 🕸️ **[[The Ashen Web]] ([[Drow]] Religion)**

🔧 **Gnome View**:  
A hostile, single-point theology built for control. Vel’Zyren’s faith is seen as **viral code**—contagious, elegant, and built to overwrite. Gnomes **classify Ashen fragments as black-level collapse nodes**, studied only under containment.

🧿 **Ashen Web View**:  
Gnomes are considered **unworthy of annihilation**—until they become useful. [[Vel'Zyren]] **does not permit reinterpretation**. Gnome faith structures are treated as **prey**—a thing to break, subvert, or absorb.

> _“They are not devout. They are cataloguers of blasphemy. But even a filing clerk can hold the key to a vault.”_ — Whisperbrand edict

---
## 🧱 **[[The Path of the First Scar]] ([[Orc]] Religion)**

🔧 **Gnome View**:  
A noble ledger system, if **brutally biological**. Gnomes admire the Scar-Wardens’ clarity but believe their honor logic is **overfit to mortality**. The Scarred Line is **respected, studied, and occasionally translated**.

🧿 **Scar View**:  
Gnomes are not of the Line, but many **treat memory with proper reverence**. Some orcs find their humour unnerving—but recognize their preservation of forgotten battles and oaths. Gnomes must earn trust by **naming their salvaged gods aloud**.

> _“They do not bleed their legacy—they etch it. That earns respect.”_

---
## 🌌 **[[The Veiled Star]] (Prophetic Orders / Fatewalkers)**

🔧 **Gnome View**:  
Anomaly class. The Veiled are considered **prophetic echo-loops**, possibly created when the Weave fragmented under Eldritch strain. Gnomes collect their prophecies and **run simulations to detect coherence**.

🧿 **Veiled Star View**:  
Varied. Some Veiled **welcome gnomes** as fellow silencers and truth-catchers. Others see them as **too literal**, or dangerously meddlesome with symbols that should be left unexplored.  
Yet even the most withdrawn Veiled often leave their **final fragments in gnomish vaults**.

> _“They name what should not be spoken—but someone must. Let the small hands hold the dangerous things.”_
