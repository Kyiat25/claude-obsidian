# FEARS WEAPON MASTERY SYSTEM
*A comprehensive martial subsystem for tactical weapon combat*

---

## Overview

Weapon Mastery transforms weapon choice from a simple damage calculation into meaningful tactical decisions. Each weapon offers unique **Weapon Skills**—special techniques that create battlefield opportunities, disrupt enemy positioning, and synergize with the Dynamic Combat System.

This system:
- ✅ Gives martial classes tactical depth equal to spellcasters
- ✅ Integrates seamlessly with Momentum and DCS
- ✅ Makes Fighter the premier weapon master
- ✅ Provides universal martial progression without homogenization
- ✅ Rewards critical hits and skilled play with permanent growth

---

## Core Rules

### Gaining Weapon Mastery

#### Starting Mastery

Every martial or weapon-focused class begins with at least one mastered weapon. When choosing your starting mastery at character creation, you must select a weapon you are already proficient with.

| Class            | Starting Mastery                                   |
| ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| **Fighter**      | Two weapons of your choice                         |
| **Barbarian**    | One martial or simple weapon                       |
| **Paladin**      | One martial or simple weapon                       |
| **Ranger**       | One weapon (ranged or melee)                       |
| **Rogue**        | One finesse or ranged weapon                       |
| **Monk**         | One monk weapon (special category)                 |
| **Warden**       | One martial or simple weapon                       |
| **Reaper**       | One martial or simple weapon                       |
| **Bender**       | One monk weapon (special category)                 |
| **Inventor**     | One martial or simple weapon                       |
| **Investigator** | One weapon (ranged or melee)                       |
| **Tattooist**    | One martial or simple weapon                       |
| **Magus**        | One martial or simple weapon                       |
| **Bard**         | One martial or simple weapon                       |
| **Druid**        | One martial or simple weapon                       |
| **Apothecary**   | One martial or simple weapon                       |
| **Shaman**       | One simple weapon                                  |
| **Sangromancer** | One simple weapon                                  |
| **Witch**        | One simple weapon                                  |
| **Summoner**     | One simple weapon                                  |
| **Wizard**       | One simple weapon                                  |
| **Sorcerer**     | One simple weapon                                  |
| **Warlock**      | One simple weapon *(Hexblade: one martial weapon)* |

---

#### Progression Through Critical Hits

Characters unlock additional weapon masteries through combat excellence:

1. **Score the required number of critical hits** with that weapon
2. **Spend 1 week of downtime** training with it

The class does not need proficiency in the weapon to master it. However, if they do not and still manage to master it through critical attacks they gain proficiency in it. 

| Class Type                                                                                                  | Simple Weapon | Martial Weapon |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------- | -------------- |
| **Fighter**                                                                                                 | 1 crit        | 1 crit         |
| **Full Martials** (Barbarian, Ranger, Paladin, Monk, Rogue, Warden, Reaper, Bender, Inventor, Investigator) | 2 crits       | 3 crits        |
| **Half-Martials** (Magus, Tattooist, Bard, Druid, Apothecary, Shaman)                                       | 3 crits       | 4 crits        |
| **Non-Martials** (Sangromancer, Witch, Summoner, Wizard, Sorcerer, Warlock)                                 | 5 crits       | 5 crits        |

**There is no hard limit to total masteries learned.** Most characters naturally end with 3-6 mastered weapons over a campaign.

---

### Using Weapon Mastery

#### Once Per Turn Rule (Universal)

When you **hit a creature** with a weapon you have mastered, you may apply **one Weapon Skill** associated with that weapon **once per turn**.

You must declare the Weapon Skill **after confirming the hit but before rolling damage** (unless the skill specifies otherwise).

---

#### Fighter Exception: Multi-Mastery Rounds

**Fighters may use multiple Weapon Skills per round**, but only when tied to their action economy:

A Fighter may use:
- **1 mastery on their Attack Action**
- **1 mastery as a Bonus Action** (if the mastery has a BA version)
- **1 mastery as a Reaction** (if the mastery has a reaction variant)
- **1 mastery with Action Surge** (treating it as a second Attack Action)

**Maximum: 2-4 Weapon Skills in a single round.**

✅ **Never the same skill twice**  
✅ **Never without spending the appropriate action**

**Design Intent:** This preserves Fighter as the premier master-of-weapons class with tactical complexity equal to spellcasters, without creating infinite debuff loops or overshadowing control casters.

## Momentum Integration

### Amplified Effects at High Momentum

Many Weapon Skills gain **enhanced effects when you have +2 or +3 Momentum**.

**Examples:**
- **Hamstring** may impose Disadvantage at +3 Momentum
- **Bleed** deals increased ongoing damage
- **Break Flow** may drain Momentum from the target

**This rewards skilled, aggressive play.**

---

### Momentum Gain

Some Weapon Skills may provide **+1 Momentum** at GM discretion when:
- Used with advantage on the attack roll
- Executed immediately after another Weapon Skill in the same round
- Applied at a critical tactical moment that shifts the flow of combat

> This is a GM-facing guideline, not an automatic effect. Individual skills that grant Momentum will state so explicitly. Momentum cannot exceed +3 by any means.

> **Technique Point Note:** References to regaining Technique Points through Weapon Skills apply to Monks and Benders only.

---

### Monk & Bender Technique Point Integration

Monks and Benders share a unique mastery interaction:

**Flow Strike (Monk & Bender Only):**
- If used at **+3 Momentum**, regain **1 Technique Point**
- On any hit using Flow Strike, move **5 feet without provoking opportunity attacks**

**This solves Monk and Bender resource starvation while reinforcing flow-state martial artist identity.**

---

## DCS Integration

### Defensive Breakdown

Skills like **Shatter Guard** reduce DR directly, increasing party effectiveness against armored foes.

---

### Mobility & Positioning Disruption

Skills such as **Push**, **Pin**, or **Sweep** alter enemy placement, controlling battlefield flow and pressure.

---

### Boss Safety Valves

**Bosses cannot be permanently locked** by:
- Prone loops
- Movement-0 loops
- Momentum-drain loops

These effects:
- Apply **once per turn maximum**
- Cannot stack infinitely from multiple attackers
- Have built-in saving throw protections

## Weapon Skills Library

Each mastery has:
- **Action version** (standard on-hit trigger)
- **Fighter BA version** (if applicable)
- **Fighter Reaction version** (if applicable)

Skills are organized by weapon type.

---

### SWORDS

#### Greatsword / Heavy Blade

**Arcing Slash** *(Action)*  
On hit, deal half weapon damage to a second enemy within 5 feet.  
**At +3 Momentum:** Second target takes full damage.

**Fighter BA:** Prepare a follow-through arc; gain +2 damage on next Arcing Slash this turn.

---

**Grinding Halt** *(Reaction)*  
When you are subjected to a forced movement effect and must make a Strength saving throw to resist it, you may use your reaction to dig your greatsword into the ground. Roll 2d6 and add the result to the save. If successful and the effect was pushing you, you move 5 feet fewer than the effect would have caused.

**No BA version**  
**Fighter Reaction:** Standard use — triggers automatically when subjected to forced movement (no additional roll needed).

---

**Break Flow** *(Action)*  
Remove **1 Momentum** from target.
**At +3 Momentum:** Also deal extra **1d6 force damage**.

**No BA version**  
**Fighter Reaction:** When a creature gains Momentum from any source, you may Break Flow immediately (costs reaction).

---

#### Longsword

**Lock Blades** *(Action)*  
When a creature attacks you with a weapon, use your reaction to lock blades and parry. Make an attack roll with your longsword — you have advantage if wielding it two-handed. If your roll equals or exceeds their attack roll, their attack misses and you gain **+2 to your next Evasion roll** until the end of the attacker's turn.

**No BA version**  
**Fighter Reaction:** Standard use as reaction.

---

**Short Draw** *(Action)*  
As an attack, draw a sheathed one-handed sword and make an attack roll to strike with its pommel. This deals no damage, but the next attack roll against the target has advantage. You must have at least one hand free.

**Fighter BA:** Make a pommel strike with a sheathed backup weapon (does not replace Attack action).

---

#### Scimitar

**Bloody Wound** *(Action)*  
On hit, target takes **1d4 ongoing bleed damage** at the start of its turns (Constitution save ends, DC = 8 + proficiency + STR/DEX).  
**At +3 Momentum:** Bleed increases to **2d4** and the target loses **1 Momentum**.

**Fighter BA:** Make a shallow cut without an attack roll; apply **1d4 bleed** (save still applies).

---

**Short Draw** *(Action)*  
As per Longsword.

---

#### Rapier

**Main Gauche** *(Action)*  
When wielding both a rapier and a dagger, you may use your reaction after being targeted by an attack to add your **Proficiency Bonus** to your Evasion roll against that attack.

**No BA version**  
**Fighter Reaction:** Standard use as reaction.

---

**Lock Blades** *(Action)*  
When a creature attacks you with a weapon, use your reaction to lock blades and parry. Make an attack roll with your rapier. If your roll equals or exceeds their attack roll, their attack misses and you gain **+2 to your next Evasion roll** until the end of the attacker's turn.

**No BA version**  
**Fighter Reaction:** Standard use.

---

### AXES

#### Battleaxe

**Crushing Blow** *(Action)*  
On hit against an armored or naturally armored creature within 5 feet, the target's **DR is permanently reduced by 1** (minimum 0) until the armor is repaired.  
**At +3 Momentum:** DR reduction becomes **2** (minimum 0).

**Fighter BA:** Apply a glancing blow; DR reduced by **1** (minimum 0) until end of your next turn (not permanent).

---

**Cleave** *(Action)*  
On hit, deal half weapon damage to a second enemy within 5 feet.  
**At +3 Momentum:** Second target takes full damage.

**Fighter BA:** Prepare a follow-through; gain +2 damage on next Cleave.

---

**Hamstring** *(Action)*  
On hit, strike at the tendons of the target's leg. The target must make a **Constitution saving throw** (DC = 8 + proficiency + STR/DEX). On failure, its **movement speed is halved** until the end of its next turn.  
**At +2 Momentum:** The target also cannot take the Dash action until the end of its next turn.  
**At +3 Momentum:** The movement reduction becomes **three-quarters** instead of half, and the target has **disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws** until the end of its next turn.

**Fighter BA:** Quick strike at the leg; impose the speed-halved effect without dealing weapon damage (save still applies).  
**Fighter Reaction:** When a creature attempts to Disengage or move away from you, trigger Hamstring immediately (costs reaction).

---

#### Glaive

**Disarming Parry** *(Action)*  
When a creature attacks you with a weapon, use your reaction to catch their weapon in your glaive's hook and disarm them. Make an attack roll with your glaive. If result equals or exceeds their attack roll, their attack misses and they must succeed on a Strength saving throw or drop their weapon.

**No BA version**  
**Fighter Reaction:** Standard use.

---

**Trip** *(Action)*  
As an attack, sweep your opponent's legs. Make an attack roll against a Large or smaller creature. If hit, it must succeed on a Strength saving throw or fall prone.

**No BA or Reaction version** (control-heavy).

---

### POLEARMS

#### Spear

**Pin** *(Action)*  
Target must make a **Strength save** or be **pinned: speed = 0** until end of your next turn.  
**At +3 Momentum:** Target also loses **1 Momentum**, and this loss cannot be prevented by any effect.

**No BA version**  
**Fighter Reaction:** When an enemy enters your reach, Pin them immediately.

---

**Disarming Parry** *(Action)*  
When a creature attacks you with a weapon, use your reaction to catch their weapon in your trident's tines and disarm them. Make an attack roll. If result equals or exceeds their attack roll, their attack misses and they must succeed on a Strength saving throw or drop their weapon.

**No BA version**  
**Fighter Reaction:** Standard use.

---

#### Halberd

**Rebuff** *(Action)*  
On hit against target within 5 feet, push up to **10 feet** away from you.

**Fighter BA:** Use haft to push; shove **5 feet** without attack roll (Strength save applies).

---

**Trip** *(Action)*  
As per Glaive.

---

#### Lance

**Charge** *(Action)*  
If you move at least 20 feet straight toward a creature before hitting it with a lance attack, take an extra **1d12 piercing damage** and target must make a Strength saving throw. On failure, target falls prone.

**At +3 Momentum:** All damage including base weapon damage is rolled with advantage. 

**No BA or Reaction version.**

---

**Repel Charge** *(Action/Reaction)*  
As an action, set your lance against a charge. If a creature moves at least 20 feet straight toward you and attacks you, use your reaction to make an attack with your lance before the attacker makes their attack. If hit, it is an automatic critical hit and target must make a Strength saving throw. On failure, it falls prone and falls off its mount if mounted.

**Fighter Reaction:** Standard use.

---

### BLUDGEONS

#### Mace / Morningstar

**Bruising Blow** *(Action)*  
On hit, deal normal weapon damage and the target must make a **Constitution saving throw**. On failure, the target **cannot use their Dexterity modifier when making Dodge Evasion rolls** until the end of its next turn or until treated with a DC 10 Wisdom (Medicine) check.

**Fighter BA:** Quick strike; impose disadvantage on the target's next Dodge Evasion roll (no damage).

---

**Ribshatter** *(Action)*  
On hit against a humanoid target, deal normal weapon damage and the target must make a **Constitution saving throw**. On failure, it is **stunned** until the beginning of its next turn. If the damage dealt equals or exceeds the target's Constitution score, it is instead stunned until the end of its next turn.

**No BA or Reaction version** (stunning is powerful).

---

#### Maul

**Crushing Blow** *(Action)*  
On hit against armored or naturally armored creature, the target's **DR is permanently reduced by 1** (minimum 0) until the armor is repaired.  
**At +3 Momentum:** DR reduction becomes **2** (minimum 0).

**Fighter BA:** Glancing blow; DR –1 (minimum 0) until end of turn.

---

**Hurling Impact** *(Action)*  
On hit against Medium or smaller target, deal normal weapon damage and target must make a **Strength saving throw**. On failure, pushed up to **15 feet** away from you.

**No BA or Reaction version.**

---

#### War Pick

**Piercing Point** *(Action)*  
On hit, pinpoint a weak point in the opponent's armor. This attack **ignores all DR** from worn or natural armor — the target's armor provides no damage reduction against this strike. Deals normal weapon damage.  
**At +3 Momentum:** Attack crits on **19-20** this round.

**Fighter BA:** Load a piercing stance; the next hit also ignores cover bonuses to Evasion.

---

**Trip** *(Action)*  
As per Glaive.

---

#### Warhammer

**Bruising Blow** *(Action)*  
As per Mace.

---

**Sundering Strike** *(Action)*  
On hit against creature wielding a weapon (including natural weapons), weapon is **damaged**, and all attacks made with it have disadvantage. Another sundering strike **destroys** the weapon. No effect on magical weapons unless your warhammer is also magical.

**No BA or Reaction version.**

---

**Shatter Guard** *(Action)*  
On hit against an armored target, strike with precision at the weak points of their protection. The target's **DR is reduced by 2** until the start of your next turn. This reduction does not stack with Crushing Blow but applies simultaneously — use whichever reduction is greater.  
**At +2 Momentum:** The DR reduction increases to **3**.  
**At +3 Momentum:** The DR reduction increases to **4** and applies to the **next two attacks** that hit the target before the start of your next turn, not just the first.

**Fighter BA:** Focused strike at a joint or seam; reduce DR by **1** until the start of your next turn (no damage).  
**Fighter Reaction:** When an ally hits the same target you attacked this turn, apply Shatter Guard's DR reduction to that ally's damage roll.

> **Cross-Weapon Availability:** Both **Hamstring** and **Shatter Guard** are available to any weapon type they thematically suit, at GM discretion. Hamstring suits slashing and piercing weapons (swords, axes, spears, daggers). Shatter Guard suits bludgeoning and heavy weapons (hammers, mauls, warhammers, greataxes). Ranged weapons cannot use either skill.

---

### DAGGERS & SHORT BLADES

#### Dagger

**Bleed** *(Action)*  
As per Scimitar.

---

**Pinning Point** *(Action)*  
When you make a melee or ranged weapon attack with a dagger, attempt to pin a Large or smaller creature to a wall or surface by catching their clothing with the knife's point. Can be used against creatures without clothing at GM discretion. Make an attack roll; if hit, target must make a **Strength saving throw**. On failure, target's **speed reduced to 0** until dagger is removed. Target can make another Strength saving throw as an action on its turn.

**No BA or Reaction version.**

---

**Concealed Blade** *(Action)*  
Instead of making a Dexterity (Stealth) check to hide yourself, make a Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) check contested by active or passive Wisdom (Perception) to conceal your dagger. As an action, make a weapon attack with the concealed dagger against a creature that is surprised, or against a creature that has not yet taken a turn in the first round of combat. This attack has advantage.

**No BA or Reaction version.**

---

### RANGED WEAPONS

#### Longbow / Shortbow

**Pinning Shot** *(Action)*  
On hit, reduce target's movement by **half**.  
**At +3 Momentum:** Target loses its **Reaction** until their next turn.

**No BA version**  
**No Reaction version** (would be too oppressive).

---

**Trick Shot** *(Action)*  
As an action, make a single bow attack. Roll your attack twice (as if with disadvantage). If the lower of the two rolls still hits the target, the attack is automatically a critical hit.

**No BA or Reaction version.**

---

#### Crossbow

**Piercing Bolt** *(Action)*  
**Ignore DR** from armor, shields, or natural armor.  
**At +3 Momentum:** Attack crits on **19-20** this round.

**Fighter BA:** Load a piercing round; next hit ignores cover as well.

---

**Shrapnel Shot** *(Action, Heavy Crossbow)*  
As an action, fire a bolt into an unattended object or surface within range. This does not require a hit against a creature and does not use the standard on-hit trigger. The bolt deals double damage to the object. If the object is destroyed, it explodes into shrapnel — choose one effect on creatures within 5 feet of the object:
- Each creature must make a **Dexterity save** (DC = 8 + proficiency + DEX), taking **1d6 piercing damage** on failure
- One adjacent creature must make a **Constitution save** (same DC). On failure, it is **blinded** until the beginning of your next turn

**No BA or Reaction version.**

---

### MONK WEAPONS

#### Bō (Quarterstaff)

**Lunge** *(Action)*  
As an action, make a single melee attack roll with your bō against an enemy within **10 feet** of you. If hit, switch positions with the target as long as nothing physically prevents the target from moving.

**Fighter BA:** Prepare stance; next attack has +5 foot reach.

---

**Vault** *(Action)*  
You can use your bō to help you leap long distances. You can use an action to double the length of your long jump, allowing you to leap a number of feet up to twice your Strength score (this jump is part of your movement). If you land adjacent to a creature at the end of this jump, you can make a single attack with your bō as part of this action.

**No BA or Reaction version.**

---

#### Monk Unarmed Strike / Flow Strike

**Flow Strike** *(Action, Monk & Bender Only)*  
On hit, move **5 feet without provoking opportunity attacks**.  
**At +3 Momentum:** Regain **1 Technique Point**.

**No BA or Reaction version** (Monk-exclusive).

---

**Butterfly Kick** *(Action, Unarmed)*  
As an action, make a single melee attack roll with your unarmed strike. On hit, you may immediately make a **Dodge Evasion roll** against the next attack that targets you before the start of your next turn, without spending a reaction to do so.

**No BA or Reaction version.**

---

**Gentle Rebuke** *(Action, Unarmed)*  
Inspired by jūdō, this maneuver allows a monk to defend without attacking. Whenever you make an attack as a Reaction, you can make the attack a Gentle Rebuke. This attack does not deal damage, but you can choose to throw the target up to **10 feet** away, where it lands prone. This attack has no effect if the target is more than one size category larger than you.

**Fighter BA:** N/A  
**Fighter Reaction:** N/A (Monk-specific)

---

### FLAILS & WHIPS

#### Flail

**Chain Garrote** *(Action)*  
Attempt to grapple a creature by looping chain of your flail around its neck. While grappled in this way, creature **cannot speak, cannot breathe**, and has **disadvantage on attack rolls** against you. If you surprised the creature with this grapple, it cannot hold its breath and immediately begins suffocating. Creatures you cannot grapple or do not need to breathe are unaffected.

**No BA or Reaction version** (suffocation is extreme).

---

**Shield Snare** *(Action)*  
As an action while wielding a flail, make a single attack roll against a target carrying a shield or similar defensive item. This attack ignores any **DR or Evasion bonus granted by the shield** and deals normal weapon damage. If hit, the target must also make a **Strength saving throw**. On failure, its shield is pulled from its grip and lands at its feet.

**No BA or Reaction version.**

---

#### Whip

**Bullwhip** *(Action)*  
As part of your attack action, make a whip attack against a beast or target with an Intelligence score of **2 or lower**. The target must make a **Wisdom saving throw** or be **frightened** of you until the end of its next turn.

**No BA or Reaction version.**

---

**Snare** *(Action)*  
As part of your attack action, snap your whip around a creature or its weapon. If you target the creature, it must make a **Strength saving throw** or drop its weapon if it has one. If you target a weapon, the **creature wielding it** must make a **Strength saving throw** or become **restrained**. It may repeat this saving throw at the end of each of its turns. You cannot make whip attacks until the restrained condition ends.

**No BA or Reaction version.**

---

### HAMMERS

#### Light Hammer

**Dazing Blow** *(Action)*  
On hit with single ranged weapon attack, target must make a **Constitution saving throw** or be **incapacitated** until end of its next turn.

**No BA or Reaction version.**

---

**Sunder Joints** *(Action)*  
When you make a melee weapon attack against a creature in medium or heavy armor with a light hammer, you may choose to damage the joints of their armor instead of dealing damage to the creature. If hit, creature's **movement reduced by 10 feet** until end of its next turn. This reduction is not cumulative.

**No BA or Reaction version.**

## Weapon Mastery Feats

### Weapon Adept (Half-Feat)

**Prerequisites:** None

You have begun formal training in advanced weapon techniques.

- Increase **one ability score** of your choice by **1**, to a maximum of 20
- Choose one weapon you are proficient with. You gain **Weapon Mastery** with that weapon
- When using this weapon, you may apply its Weapon Skill **once per turn**, just like martial classes

**Design Intent:** Perfect for casters or half-martials wanting one tactical weapon trick. Keeps Fighter unique: Fighters still learn masteries faster and use more per round. Stat boost makes it viable early-game.

---

### Master of Arms (Full Feat)

**Prerequisites:** Proficiency with at least one martial or simple weapon

You have trained extensively in battlefield technique, unlocking deeper understanding of weapons.

- You gain **two Weapon Masteries** of your choice for weapons you are proficient with
- When using a mastered weapon, you may apply its Weapon Skill **once per turn**
- You count as having scored **one critical hit** toward future weapon-specific mastery progression for each weapon gained through this feat

**Design Intent:** Stronger than Weapon Adept but no stat boost. Ideal for Rogues, Rangers, Barbarians, Paladins, Monks, Benders, and Investigators. Still respects Fighter identity—Fighters get more masteries, use more per round, and crit-progression is faster.

## Class Integration Summary

### Fighter — Weapon Master Supreme

- **Multiple masteries per round** via action economy
- **Fastest mastery progression** (1 crit for any weapon type)
- **Largest number of learned masteries** (no practical cap)
- Fighter gameplay now mirrors spellcasters in breadth without overshadowing them in CC potency

**Typical Fighter Arsenal by Level 10:**
- 4-6 mastered weapons
- Can apply 2-4 skills per round (action, bonus, reaction, action surge)
- Tactical battlefield controller and damage dealer

---

### Barbarian

- **Momentum-friendly masteries** (build Momentum easily)
- Rage enhances **Bleed**, **Shatter Guard**, **Cleave**
- **Break Flow** gives them tempo disruption
- Recommended: Greatsword, Greataxe, Maul

---

### Paladin

- **One martial mastery** + Smite synergy
- **Break Flow** and **Hamstring** reinforce tank role
- **Shatter Guard** supports party DPS
- Recommended: Longsword, Warhammer, Lance (mounted)

---

### Ranger

- **Bow masteries** define battlefield control
- Synergy with **Reaction-based attacks**
- **Pinning Shot** creates lockdown zones
- Recommended: Longbow, Shortsword (melee backup)

---

### Rogue

- **Bleed** & **Hamstring** fit single-target pick role
- Provides debuffs without needing Sneak Attack setup
- **Pinning Point** creates escape opportunities
- Recommended: Rapier, Dagger, Shortbow

---

### Monk

- **Technique Point regeneration engine** through Flow Strike
- **Mobility-reinforcing masteries** reward aggressive positioning
- Fixes resource starvation at mid-levels
- Recommended: Bō, Unarmed Strike (Flow Strike), Shortsword

---

### Bender

- Functions identically to Monk for mastery purposes
- **Flow Strike** provides the same **Technique Point regeneration** at +3 Momentum
- **Mobility-based masteries** complement their fluid, redirect-focused combat style
- Recommended: Bō, Unarmed Strike (Flow Strike), Shortsword

---

### Warden

- **Full martial access** enables powerful elemental-themed weapon pairings
- **Shatter Guard** and **Break Flow** complement their Discipline-based battlefield control
- Mastery synergises with their Prime Element stance features
- Recommended: Spear, Halberd, Greataxe (varies by Discipline)

---

### Reaper

- **Full martial access** reinforces their role as executioners and psychological pressure dealers
- **Bleed** and **Hamstring** feed into their Soul Token generation loops
- **Break Flow** strips enemy Momentum before death-strike finishers
- Recommended: Scythe (reskinned Glaive), Dagger, Greatsword

---

### Inventor

- **Full martial access** despite their half-caster nature — engineering means knowing how weapons work
- **Shatter Guard** and **Piercing Point** complement gadget-based action combinations
- Mastery fills action economy gaps when devices are on cooldown
- Recommended: Any simple weapon or crossbow (varies by Specialisation)

---

### Investigator

- **Bow and melee masteries** define their flexible, adaptable combat approach
- Synergy with **Reaction-based attacks** rewards their read-the-room playstyle
- **Pinning Point** and **Hamstring** create control opportunities that complement their debuff toolkit
- Recommended: Light Crossbow, Rapier (melee backup)

---

### Tattooist

- Acts as **spellblade-lite** without overshadowing Magus
- Mastery enhances their reactive, ink-powered combat style
- Recommended: Rapier, Dagger

---

### Bard

- **Half-martial** — performance and movement inform weapon use, but not dedicated training
- **Break Flow** and **Hamstring** fit their disruption-focused, battlefield-slippery identity
- Mastery gives them a genuine combat contribution beyond Inspiration and spells
- Recommended: Rapier, Shortbow, Hand Crossbow

---

### Druid

- **Half-martial** — primal attunement grants instinctive weapon familiarity, not trained technique
- **Pin** and **Trip** complement their zone-control and terrain-manipulation identity
- Mastery is most relevant in non-Wild Shape situations
- Recommended: Spear, Quarterstaff, Shortbow

---

### Apothecary

- **Half-martial** — they know anatomy intimately, and that knowledge translates to precise, deliberate strikes
- **Hamstring** and **Bleed** reflect surgical precision over raw aggression
- Mastery serves as a reliable fallback when preparations are spent
- Recommended: Dagger, Shortsword, Light Crossbow

---

### Shaman

- **Half-martial** — spiritually-guided weapon use rather than trained martial discipline
- **Break Flow** and **Pin** complement their spirit-disruption abilities
- Mastery activates when spirit companions are unavailable
- Recommended: Spear, Quarterstaff, Shortbow

---

### Non-Martial Spellcasters

The following classes gain **one simple weapon mastery** at character creation and can earn additional simple weapon masteries through play, but require **5 critical hits** per mastery and can never master martial weapons through normal progression.

| Class | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| **Sangromancer** | Blood and ritual magic, not weapon discipline — Dagger fits thematically |
| **Witch** | Self-reliance in the wild, not combat training — simple weapons only |
| **Summoner** | Minimal personal combat investment — creatures fight for them |
| **Wizard** | Academic familiarity with simple weapons only |
| **Sorcerer** | Innate power, no martial discipline |
| **Warlock** | Pact-bound power; basic self-defence at best |
| **Warlock (Hexblade)** | **Exception:** Treat as Half-Martial. Gains one martial weapon mastery, follows 3/4 crit progression |
| **Cleric** | Access to weapons by divine tradition does not mean mastery of technique — no starting mastery |

> Non-martial classes may acquire one additional mastery via the **Weapon Adept** feat (one weapon of choice, with +1 stat boost), bypassing the 5-crit requirement for that single weapon.

---

## Balance Summary

### What This System Fixes

✅ **Martial/Caster Parity** — Martials now have tactical complexity equal to spellcasters  
✅ **Fighter Identity** — Fighter becomes hallmark martial with multi-phase turns  
✅ **Monk & Bender Sustainability** — Technique Point regeneration through Momentum creates fluid gameplay  
✅ **Rogue Viability** — Gains control/utility tools beyond Sneak Attack  
✅ **Universal Martial Depth** — All martials gain meaningful per-turn decisions  
✅ **Boss Engagement** — Martials can interact with elite enemies without magic  

### Power Ceiling Controls

🛡️ **Once-per-turn rule** prevents infinite debuff stacking (except Fighter's expanded use)  
🛡️ **Action economy gates** Fighter's multi-mastery (no free spam)  
🛡️ **Boss safety valves** prevent permanent lockdown loops  
🛡️ **Crit-based progression** rewards skill but limits rapid mastery acquisition  
🛡️ **No same-skill stacking** prevents Fighter nova abuse

## Final Verdict

**Weapon Mastery + Monk & Bender Technique Point Regeneration creates the best version of FEARS martial combat to date.**

**Fighter** becomes one of the best-designed classes in the system.  
**Monk and Bender** become fluid and reliable, not swingy.  
**All martials** gain tactical choice depth.  
**Casters** remain excellent but not dominant.  
**Bosses** remain challenging.  
**System cohesion** improves dramatically.

## Designer Notes

This system was built to solve specific design problems:
1. Martial classes lacked mechanical engines
2. Fighter had no unique identity beyond "attack more"
3. Monk and Bender suffered from resource starvation (Technique Points)
4. Rogues over-relied on Sneak Attack for everything
5. Casters overshadowed martials in tactical influence
6. New FEARS-original martials (Warden, Reaper, Inventor, Investigator) needed clear mastery identities

The solution preserves FEARS' tactical complexity while creating distinct martial identities that feel rewarding at every level.

Every weapon choice matters.  
Every critical hit represents growth.  
Every round offers meaningful decisions.

**This is martial excellence in FEARS.**
