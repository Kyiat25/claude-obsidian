# The Archive Within

> “What you remember is not always what you lived—but it is always what you become.”  
> — Thaumaturge Myral of the Broken Mirror

---
### 🔍 What is Memory?

**Memory** is a core mental skill in FEARS representing your ability to recall, retain, and reconstruct knowledge—both learned and experienced. It governs access to ancient truths, glyphic lore, ritual patterns, traumatic events, and the threadbare edges of forgotten histories.

Unlike static knowledge checks (like Arcana or History), Memory is **dynamic**, used to piece together fragments of thought under pressure, resist false memories, or interpret echoic imprints left on reality.

---

### 🎲 Memory Check Formula

**Memory Check** = `1d20 + Intelligence Modifier + Proficiency Bonus (if proficient in Memory)`

Proficiency in Memory Checks is typically granted by:

- Class features (e.g., **Investigator**, **Theurge**, **Thaumaturge**)
- Backgrounds that emphasize forgotten knowledge or trauma
- Special training or magical means (like glyph-binding rituals)

---

### 🧠 When to Roll Memory

|Situation|Memory Use|
|---|---|
|You try to recall a glyph seen during a vision|Memory Check (DC 12–18)|
|You piece together a fragmented prophecy or dream|Memory Check with Disadvantage if recent trauma occurred|
|You resist memory alteration (e.g., **Modify Memory**, Eldritch Echo)|Contested Memory vs. Spell DC|
|You attempt to rebuild forgotten lore or ritual|Memory Check (DC varies by depth, 15–25)|
|You relive trauma during a long rest|Memory Check (failure may reduce Sanity or impose a condition)|

---

### 🌀 Memory & Other Systems

Memory is **not Sanity**—it does not measure mental **stability** but instead the **clarity of cognition**. However, they do interact:

- **Low Sanity** = Disadvantage on Memory checks involving traumatic events or glyphic exposure.
- **High Corruption** = May seed **false memories** or replace truths with hallucinated alternatives.
- **Focus** = May be used to **pause** and stabilize a failing Memory roll via GM discretion (“thread of recall” moment).
- **Whisper Engine** = Memory governs whether a character can **reconstruct** or **decode** a Whispered glyph. You may be asked for a Memory Check before spending WP.

---

### 🧠 Memory Saving Throws?

Memory is not a saving throw ability by default but may be called on in special cases:

- Surviving **echo-based mind attacks** (Dream Echo, Eldritch Remnants)
- Withstanding **glyph recursion**
- Resisting **memory erasure or reversal**

GMs may ask for **Intelligence saves** with Memory proficiency applying when relevant.

---

### 🛠 Training & Advancement

To gain proficiency in Memory:

- Spend 4 downtime days in focused glyph-study, spirit meditation, or dream traversal.
- Be taught by an Echo-Keeper, Psion, or Glyph-Warden (GM approval).
- Survive a full **Eldritch Vision** and retain control (Sanity Save DC 18 → if successful, gain Memory as a learnable skill).
## 🧩 Key Differences

### 🔍 **Memory** is interpretive.

- You may _remember wrong_.
- You may _recall a false echo_.
- You may _rebuild_ rather than recite.

Unlike Arcana or History, **Memory is dynamic**—your **personal experience and trauma affect the result**.
