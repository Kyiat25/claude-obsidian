# Human
## Lineage

> *"We were not forged. We were not sung. We were not named. We were what looked back—when the gods first saw their flaws."*  
> — _Attributed to a nameless Human Thread-Scribe, 2nd Age_

You are Human. Not divine creation, not intentional design—**living contradiction**. Your ancestors emerged not from purpose but from **possibility**, when the gods looked upon their work and saw what they'd rather forget. Where elves remember too clearly and dwarves build too precisely, you **adapt**. Where others are bound by ancient tradition or racial destiny, you **change**.

You were not born to a single purpose. You were born to **become whatever you choose**, to write your own story in a world of predetermined threads. Your short lifespan—barely a spark compared to elven centuries—means you burn bright, fast, and fierce. You don't have time for the long view. You make history **now**.

## The Shard That Sang

According to the **Reflection Pattern**—the oldest human spiritual tradition—your people were born from divine mistake made sacred. Before the gods codified the Weave, before the Glyphs spun in rhythm, there existed the **Great Pattern Unbroken**: a vast tapestry of divine intent, flawless in symmetry, deafening in harmony.

But one strand refused to lie flat.

This was **the Thread of Reflection**—woven not with certainty, but with **possibility**. It turned sideways in the Weave, catching light and memory in strange ways. No god claimed it. No Glyph aligned to it. The Pattern ignored it.

Until the moment came when the gods, full of hubris, looked upon their work and asked: **"What flaw remains in our design?"**

They gathered at the Loom of Origins and cast their gaze into the depths of the Weave—and in doing so, they saw **themselves**. But not as they intended. They saw their doubts, their hesitations, their cruelties dressed as justice, their comforts mistaken for truth.

And in that moment of divine recoil, **the Mirror shattered**.

Twelve shards fell from the Loom into the Mortal Tangle below. From these shards—fractured, unclaimed, glowing with what should not be—rose the **first humans**.

### **The Race of the Shattered Mirror**

You were not made by a god, nor carved with purpose. You were born of **divine contradiction**—the reflections the gods would rather forget.

Each human is **a mirror**, but no two show the same truth. You change. You lie. You love. You break. You rebuild. And you **remember differently**—not through glyph, or law, or song, but through **choice**.

You did not need the gods to complete you. **You became what the gods had left undone.**

## Life Among the Many

You were raised knowing that nothing is fixed, that today's truth becomes tomorrow's memory, that **change is your birthright** and adaptability your greatest weapon. Human cultures span every extreme: some value honor above all, others treasure freedom; some build rigid hierarchies, others embrace chaos.

This is your strength. While elves take centuries to shift perspective and dwarves hold grudges for generations, you **evolve**. A human kingdom can transform completely in three generations. What your grandparents considered unthinkable, you make policy. What they built, you tear down or rebuild better.

**Legacy (vethris)** is not metaphorical. It is:
- Counted by deeds, changes enacted, problems solved, and futures altered
- Stored in stories, records, taught wisdom, and children raised
- Traded constantly—every generation redefines what matters

Your lifespan is roughly **80-100 years**—a heartbeat compared to other races, but you make it count. You measure time not in Ages but in **Generations** (roughly 25-year periods), and you measure worth not in perfection achieved but in **"Did I leave the world different than I found it?"**

Every major achievement is recorded in **Memory Stones**—carved markers or written records placed in villages, cities, and family homes, ensuring that even brief lives echo forward.

## Physical Characteristics

**Age:** You mature by age 18 and can live to 80-100 years. Some exceptional humans reach 110-120, but most don't see 90. This brevity creates urgency—you don't have time to waste.

**Size:** You stand between 5 to 6½ feet tall, with builds ranging from slight to powerful. Your size is Medium.

**Speed:** Your base walking speed is 30 feet. You're neither exceptionally fast nor slow—perfectly average.

**Build:** Humans show incredible physical diversity. You might be stocky or slender, muscular or lean, compact or rangy. Your hair comes in every shade, your skin in every tone, your features from every possible combination. This is your nature—**infinite variation within a single species**.

## Human Traits

As a Human, you possess the following lineage traits:

### **Adaptable**
You gain +1 to one ability score of your choice and proficiency in one skill of your choice.

This represents the core human gift: **you become what you need to be**. Where other races have fixed biological advantages, you have **flexibility**. You can specialize or generalize, focus or diversify, entirely based on your choices.

### **Feat** *(1st Level)*
You gain one feat of your choice at 1st level (in addition to any feats gained through your background or class).

Humans learn fast, adapt quickly, and develop specialized skills through sheer determination and necessity. While elves spend decades mastering a technique, you innovate workarounds. While dwarves perfect a craft over centuries, you create entirely new ones in a generation.

### **Quick Study**
When you fail an ability check, you gain advantage on your next ability check using the same ability score, as long as you attempt it within 1 hour. You learn from mistakes faster than other races.

### **Cultural Chameleon**
You can adopt and blend into new cultures with remarkable speed. After spending at least 1 week closely observing a culture or community, you have advantage on Charisma (Deception, Performance, or Persuasion) checks when attempting to pass as a member of that culture.

### **Languages**
You can speak, read, and write Common and one extra language of your choice.

**Common** is a human language—or more accurately, humans spread so thoroughly that their trade tongue **became** the common language. This linguistic flexibility reflects human nature: you're constantly communicating, trading, mixing, and creating new hybrid cultures.

## Mental Attributes & Psychology

**Focus Modifier:** +0  
**Sanity Modifier:** +0

Your mental baseline is exactly that—**baseline**. Humans have no inherent biological advantages in mental resilience. However, this neutrality is actually a strength: you're not locked into racial psychology. A human can develop iron focus or remain scattered; can cultivate serene sanity or embrace controlled chaos.

What you lack in biological mental advantage, you make up for in **learned flexibility**.

### **Cultural Mental Conditioning**
Human upbringing creates:

- **Urgency:** Short lives mean you can't afford to waste time
- **Pragmatism:** You solve problems now, worry about philosophy later
- **Innovation:** If the old way doesn't work, invent a new one
- **Emotional Range:** You experience the full spectrum quickly and intensely
- **Risk Tolerance:** With less to lose (shorter lives), you dare more

### **Corruption Vulnerability: The Unanchored**

As a Human, you have a unique relationship with Corruption. Your lack of strong ancestral or racial bonds means Eldritch forces can **manipulate your identity more easily**, but your adaptability also means you can **recover from corruption** in ways other races cannot.

**When you gain Corruption Score 10+ (Tier 2), you suffer:**
- Fractured Identity: Your sense of self becomes malleable and uncertain
- Memory Distortion: You begin misremembering key moments of your life
- Reflection Corruption: Mirrors and reflections sometimes show you as something else

**Corrupted Reflection Table (Corruption Tier 2+)**

When you complete a long rest at Corruption Score 10+, roll 1d6:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **False Memory:** You remember a pivotal moment differently. Make a DC 15 Wisdom save or believe the false version until your next long rest. |
| 2 | **Identity Fracture:** You can't remember why you chose your current path. You have disadvantage on saves against being frightened until your next long rest. |
| 3 | **Reflection Lie:** You see yourself in a mirror as something you're not (different race, age, or form). Take 1d4 psychic damage from the cognitive dissonance. |
| 4 | **Weight of Mortality:** You become acutely aware of how brief your life is. Make a DC 12 Sanity save or lose 1d4 Sanity. |
| 5 | **Eldritch Whisper:** Something offers you the secret to extending your life. Gain 1 Whisper Point but your next death save is made with disadvantage. |
| 6 | **Adaptability Holds:** Your human flexibility resists corruption this night. Reduce your Corruption Score by 1 (minimum 10). |

## Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Integration

### **Evasion Profile: Versatile Combatant**
- **Dodge Evasion:** +0 (baseline)
- **Endure Evasion:** +0 (baseline)
- **Deflect Evasion:** +0 (baseline)

**Tactical Note:** You have no natural advantages in evasion, but this means you're **infinitely moldable**. You can become a dodge-tank through class choice and feats, or an endurance monster through training, or a deflection specialist through equipment—or all three. Your lack of racial specialization means **every build is equally viable**.

### **Natural Damage Reduction:** +0
Your frame provides no inherent protection. Humans rely entirely on armor, training, and tactics for survival.

### **Combat Philosophy**
Humans fight with **innovative tactics and learned techniques**. You don't have dwarven toughness or elven grace—you have **adaptability**. You're the ones who invented shield walls, crossbows, combined arms tactics, guerrilla warfare, and siege engines. You solve combat problems through **creativity and cooperation**.

### **Breaking Point Mechanics**
Human Breaking Points are **highly variable**—depending on training, culture, and experience. However, you have **advantage on Rally checks when fighting for something you believe in**. Your brief lives make causes burn brighter.

## Languages

**Languages:** You can speak, read, and write Common and one extra language of your choice.

Humans are **linguistic sponges**. Where elves preserve ancient tongues unchanged for millennia and dwarves carve their language into stone, you **adapt and evolve language constantly**. Common itself is a creole, a trade tongue that absorbed words from every culture humans encountered.

Many humans are multilingual, picking up new languages out of necessity, curiosity, or opportunity. Some scholars believe humans' short lifespans actually **advantage** language acquisition—you're forced to learn quickly rather than relying on centuries of practice.

## Human Variants

While all humans share adaptability, different human cultures have developed distinct approaches to life. These are not biological differences—they're **cultural philosophies** that shape how you approach the world. Choose one of the following variants:

### **Cosmopolitan Human** *(Urban Versatility)*
You come from great cities where a dozen cultures mix daily. You've learned to navigate social complexity and urban environments with practiced ease.

**Skills:** You gain proficiency in one additional skill of your choice and one tool or instrument of your choice.

**Urban Savvy:** You have advantage on Intelligence (Investigation) and Wisdom (Insight) checks made in urban environments. You know how cities work: where to find information, who to ask, how systems connect.

**Network Builder:** During downtime in a city, you can make a DC 15 Charisma check. On a success, you establish a useful contact (informant, merchant, official) who can provide information or minor assistance once. You can maintain a number of such contacts equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum 1).

### **Frontier Human** *(Wilderness Tenacity)*
You come from the edges of civilization where survival requires resourcefulness and every day brings new challenges.

**Skills:** You gain proficiency in Survival and one additional skill of your choice.

**Frontier Resilience:** You have advantage on saving throws against exhaustion caused by environmental conditions (heat, cold, hunger, thirst).

**Make Do:** You can improvise tools and equipment in the wilderness. With 10 minutes and appropriate materials, you can create a functional (if crude) version of any common tool or piece of adventuring gear. It functions for 24 hours before breaking.

**Frontier Combat:** You gain proficiency with improvised weapons and they deal 1d6 damage in your hands (instead of 1d4).

### **Scholarly Human** *(Academic Excellence)*
You were raised in centers of learning—universities, monasteries, or great libraries—where knowledge is power and wisdom is cultivated.

**Skills:** You gain proficiency in three Knowledge skills of your choice from: Arcana, History, Nature, or Religion.

**Researcher:** When you spend at least 1 hour studying a topic using books, scrolls, or other reference materials, you can make an Intelligence check (DC 15) to recall specialized information about that topic, even if you wouldn't normally know it. You can use this ability once per long rest.

**Fast Learner:** You require half the normal time and gold to learn new languages and tool proficiencies during downtime.

**Archive Memory:** You can perfectly recall any text you've read within the past month, including diagrams and symbols.

### **Nomadic Human** *(Traveling Freedom)*
You come from wandering peoples—traders, herders, performers, or refugees—who never stay in one place long enough to call it home.

**Skills:** You gain proficiency in two of the following: Animal Handling, Performance, Persuasion, or Survival.

**Home Everywhere:** You take half the normal time to complete a long rest. You're accustomed to sleeping rough and making camp quickly.

**Cultural Navigator:** You have advantage on Charisma checks when first meeting members of new cultures or communities. Your nomadic background taught you to read social situations quickly.

**Wanderer's Luck:** Once per long rest, when you roll initiative, you can choose to swap your initiative result with any one willing ally's result. You've learned to coordinate movement through constant travel.

### **Warrior Culture Human** *(Martial Heritage)*
You come from societies where combat prowess defines status—whether honorable warrior clans, mercenary companies, or militant theocracies.

**Skills:** You gain proficiency in Athletics and one of: History, Intimidation, or Survival.

**Combat Training:** You gain proficiency with all martial weapons and medium armor.

**Warrior's Resolve:** When you're reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, you can make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw. On a success, you drop to 1 hit point instead. You can use this feature once per long rest.

**Battle Awareness:** You have advantage on initiative rolls. Your combat training emphasizes situational awareness and rapid response.

## Cultural Touchstones

### **The Reflection Pattern**
Many humans follow this ancient faith, even if they also practice the Faith of the Bound Pattern publicly. The Reflection Pattern teaches:

- Each soul carries echoes of ancestors
- Divinity is personal—your gods reflect your lineage
- Memory shapes the Weave—forgotten ancestors weaken reality
- Actions ripple across generations
- You are the mirror through which gods see themselves

Even humans who don't practice the Reflection Pattern are shaped by its concepts: **personal divine relationships, ancestral memory, and the power of choice**.

### **The Faith of the Bound Pattern**
Humans likely **originated or popularized** this unifying religion. It fits human nature perfectly:

- Multiple pantheons welcome (matches human theological flexibility)
- Structured but not rigid (provides order without crushing adaptation)
- Cycle-based (matches human generational thinking)
- Practical rather than mystical (humans value what works)

Many humans practice **both** faiths—the Bound Pattern publicly, Reflection privately. This "dual faith" is uniquely human.

### **The Seven Questions**
Traditional human philosophy centers on seven questions every person must answer:

1. **Who made you?** (Heritage and identity)
2. **What do you serve?** (Purpose and cause)
3. **What will you become?** (Aspiration and goal)
4. **What will you protect?** (Values and boundaries)
5. **What will you destroy?** (Enemies and obstacles)
6. **What will you leave behind?** (Legacy and impact)
7. **When you fall, who remembers?** (Community and meaning)

These aren't religious questions—they're **existential ones**. In a life of 80 years, you don't have time to avoid the hard questions.

## Playing a Human

### **Core Fantasy:**
You are the adaptable survivor, the innovative problem-solver, the person who **becomes whatever the situation requires**. You don't have ancient traditions or biological destiny—you have **choice**. Your brief life burns bright with urgency and possibility.

### **Mechanical Identity:**
- **Ultimate Flexibility:** Choose any ability score boost, any skill, any feat at level 1
- **Fast Developer:** Extra feat means faster specialization
- **Quick Learner:** Advantage on repeated ability checks, rapid cultural adaptation
- **No Racial Limits:** Every class, build, and concept works equally well
- **Customizable Variant:** Choose cultural approach that matches your concept

### **Roleplaying Hooks:**
- **The Proving:** Demonstrating human worth in a world of longer-lived races
- **The Legacy-Seeker:** Desperate to accomplish something meaningful before death
- **The Bridge-Builder:** Using human adaptability to unite diverse peoples
- **The Innovator:** Solving ancient problems with new thinking
- **The Rebel:** Rejecting racial destiny and forging individual path
- **The Scholar:** Compensating for short life by learning everything possible
- **The Remembered:** Ensuring your name survives your death

### **Party Dynamics:**
Other races often underestimate humans. This creates opportunities for:

**With Long-Lived Races (Elves, Dwarves):**
- You bring urgency they lack
- Your fresh perspective challenges their assumptions
- They may condescend ("you're so young"), then be shocked by your capability
- You force them to engage with the **present** rather than eternal patterns

**With Other Short-Lived Races (Halflings, Gnomes):**
- Shared understanding of living fast
- Mutual recognition of need to make every moment count
- Less patience for ancient grudges or centuries-old plans

**With Other Humans:**
- Incredibly diverse—human-to-human dynamics vary wildly based on cultures
- Shared language and generational thinking creates quick bonds
- But also potential for human-on-human prejudice (different kingdoms, religions, classes)

### **Common Character Arcs:**
- From aimless to purposeful (finding what's worth the brief life)
- From envying other races to embracing human strength
- From individual to legacy-minded (thinking beyond yourself)
- From derivative to innovative (creating rather than imitating)
- From traditional to revolutionary (breaking old patterns)
- From scattered to focused (making the limited time count)

## Mechanical Considerations

Your abilities synergize with **literally everything**. Humans are the ultimate "blank slate" race. The extra feat and flexible ability score mean:

- **Fighter:** Extra feat = Combat style flexibility
- **Wizard:** Intelligence boost + feat for Metamagic or similar
- **Rogue:** Dexterity boost + feat for mobility or skill expertise
- **Cleric:** Wisdom boost + feat for additional domain powers

Truly, **every class works perfectly** with humans because you customize to fit.

## Design Philosophy

The Human lineage is designed to support:

1. **Ultimate Flexibility** (choose everything, specialize or generalize)
2. **Fast Development** (extra feat accelerates character specialization)
3. **Adaptive Response** (mechanical bonuses to learning and adjustment)
4. **Variant Diversity** (five distinct cultural approaches)
5. **No Bad Builds** (every concept works because you customize to fit)

Your mechanical abilities (flexible ability score, free skill, extra feat, Quick Study) create a **character that becomes exactly what you design**. You're not locked into racial archetypes—you define yourself through choices.

The variant options allow you to lean into different human cultures—from urban cosmopolitans to frontier survivors to scholarly innovators to nomadic wanderers to warrior traditions—while maintaining the core human identity of **adaptable urgency**.

Every human carries awareness of limited time. Your character likely:
- Thinks in terms of "before I die, I must..."
- Measures accomplishments against lifespan
- Values present moment because it's fleeting  
- Understands urgency in ways eternal races can't

This isn't burden—it's **purpose**. You adventure because time is short and the world won't wait.

Modern Dór-o Estel recognizes human achievements:
- Dwarves respect human innovation (even if it's not built to last)
- Elves acknowledge human passion (even if it burns out quickly)
- Other races rely on human adaptability in crises
- The Faith of the Bound Pattern reflects human theology

### **The Reflection:**
Your people believe you are mirrors through which the gods see themselves. Every human life **redefines what divinity means**, every human choice **rewrites destiny**, every human death **echoes forward into the future**.

You adventure knowing that your brief life matters **precisely because** it's brief. You don't have time to waste on certainty or tradition. You must act, must change, must **become** what the world needs.

This is not pressure to fear—it is **certainty** that your choices matter beyond your years.
