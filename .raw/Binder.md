# The Binder

**Vessels of the Unfinished Dead**

> *"I do not command the dead. I let them borrow my hands, and I ask, politely, that they give them back."*

---

## Vessels of the Unfinished Dead

Before the Fracture of Accord, the Summoners of the old Wyrd tradition bent Thread-space to their will through precise, mathematical formulae — architects who commanded entities as a general commands troops. When the Fracture tore through the Thread-paths, it did not just kill thousands. It shattered the careful architecture that let those entities stay *entities* — separate, contained, obedient.

What it left behind, drifting in the wreckage of Thread-space, are **remnants**: fragments of the dead who never finished dying. Soldiers who fell mid-oath. Scholars who died mid-thought. Something older and stranger that was never quite alive to begin with, caught in the collapse regardless. They have no bodies left to hold them, no Thread-paths precise enough to give them independent form. They have only the hunger to finish what they started — and the binder's willingness to lend them a body to do it in.

You are not a commander. You are a **door**.

When you bind a remnant, you don't summon a servant — you invite a fragment of the dead to occupy the same space you do. For as long as the binding holds, its instincts bleed into your hands, its old wounds itch under your skin, its unfinished business becomes briefly, urgently yours. Your voice roughens with a dead soldier's battlefield rasp. Your eyes might mist over with a drowned scholar's cataracts. You do not control what a remnant was. You only control whether you let it in, and for how long.

This is not comfortable work. It was outlawed in most Loom-Cities within a decade of the Fracture, once it became clear what prolonged binding does to a person's sense of self. But comfort was never really the offer. Power was. And in a world still picking through the wreckage of what the old Summoners broke, there is no shortage of desperate people willing to pay a binder to let the dead finish what they started.

## The Binder's Essence

In FEARS, a Binder is a **shapeshifting resource manager** who trades the Summoner's separate-creature action economy for something faster and more personal: every remnant you bind changes what *you* can do, on your own turn, with your own body. There's no command action, no second initiative slot, no creature to protect. There is only you, wearing borrowed power, managing how much of yourself you're willing to lend out at once.

You excel when:
- **You need to adapt mid-fight** (Rebinding lets you swap your toolkit between remnants)
- **The fight is close and personal** (most Remnant abilities are self-contained — no summon to protect or reposition)
- **You lean into instability** (Bleed Points reward taking risks and absorbing punishment)
- **Your party needs a flexible answer** (a remnant catalog spanning damage, control, mobility, and support)

You struggle when:
- **You need raw single-target burst without setup** (your power is distributed across active remnants, not concentrated in one big number)
- **Your Focus or Sanity is already strained** (remnants press harder on a cracked mind)
- **You're forced to fight with remnants you didn't choose for the situation** (Rebinding has real limits)
- **The table wants a simple, low-tracking class** (multiple remnants, Bleed Points, and Trait signs are a lot to hold at once)

## Class Features

As a Binder, you gain the following class features:

### Hit Points
- **Hit Dice:** 1d8 per Binder level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 8 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per Binder level after 1st

### Proficiencies
- **Armor:** Light armor, medium armor
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons
- **Tools:** None
- **Saving Throws:** Charisma, Wisdom
- **Skills:** Choose two from Arcana, Deception, History, Insight, Intimidation, Religion, and Survival

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) a simple melee weapon or (b) a simple ranged weapon and 20 pieces of ammunition
- (a) leather armor or (b) studded leather armor
- 10 pieces of chalk or graveyard soil, and (a) a component pouch or (b) an arcane focus
- (a) a scholar's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
- A personal effect belonging to the first remnant you ever bound (your choice what it is, and how you got it)

---

## Quick Build

To quickly create a Binder, follow these steps:

1. **Ability Scores:** Make Charisma your highest score — it governs your Binding Save DC and how much of yourself you can lend without losing the thread. Constitution second, for the wear-and-tear of hosting the dead.
2. **Lineage and Heritage:** Any combination works; Heritages connected to death rites, mourning traditions, or old Wyrd-adjacent cultures are thematic but not required.
3. **Background:** Gravedigger, Haunted Survivor, Battlefield Medic, or any background touching death, grief, or the Fracture's aftermath.
4. **Combat Strategy:** Bind remnants that cover your gaps before combat, keep one Rebind in reserve for surprises, and don't be afraid to bank Bleed Points early for a big Push the Bind later.

## Core Class Features

### Remnant Binding *(1st Level)*

You have learned the Binding Rite: a 10-minute ritual of chalk or graveyard soil, spoken names, and old grief, which opens a door in your own soul just wide enough for a remnant to step through.

**The Binding Rite.** You can perform the Binding Rite during a short or long rest, or at any other time you have 10 uninterrupted minutes. Choose a number of remnants from the **Bound Remnants** list equal to your **Remnants Bound** (see the Binder table) — you cannot choose a remnant of a Tier higher than your **Remnant Tier**. These remnants remain bound until you perform the rite again or until you finish a long rest, at which point you may reselect.

**Binding Save DC and Attack.** Some Remnant abilities require a saving throw or attack roll.

> **Binding Save DC** = 8 + your Proficiency Bonus + your Charisma modifier
> **Binding Attack Modifier** = your Proficiency Bonus + your Charisma modifier

**Manifest Marks** *(see below)* appear whenever a remnant is bound, whether or not you're actively using its abilities.

> *Design Note — Why No Stat Block: A remnant is not a creature. It doesn't have its own HP, initiative, or turn. Its abilities run through your action economy, your hit points, and your saving throws. This is the whole point of the Binder's design: no external monster content is required to play one. Every remnant is fully self-contained on this document.*

---

### Manifest Marks *(1st Level)*

Every bound remnant leaves a visible sign on you for as long as it's bound — a physical tell that marks you as something other than fully yourself. Each Remnant entry describes its Mark. These marks have no mechanical effect by default, but they are not optional flavor: NPCs, guards, and anyone who knows what a binder looks like can potentially recognize you by them. Many Loom-Cities have laws against unregistered binding.

You choose whether your Marks are subtle (a faint discoloration, a slight limp) or overt (glowing eyes, a second shadow) when you first bind each remnant — this choice is cosmetic and does not change once made for that remnant, unless you retrain the remnant's flavor with your GM.

---

### Bleed Points *(1st Level)*

Hosting the dead is not free. Every time a remnant bleeds through further than intended, it costs you something — and gives you something in return. You have a pool of **Bleed Points (BP)**, maximum shown on the Binder table, which you can spend to push your remnants harder than they'd normally go.

**Gaining Bleed Points.** You gain 1 BP (max once per trigger, per round) when:
- You take damage from an attack or effect.
- An enemy within 30 feet of you drops to 0 hit points or is reduced to Bloodied by your attack.
- You fail a Focus or Sanity saving throw.

**Spending Bleed Points.**

**Push the Bind** *(1 BP)* — When you use an active ability from a bound remnant that deals damage or forces a saving throw, you can spend 1 BP to add one extra damage die of the same type, or to impose disadvantage on the triggered saving throw. You can do this only once per use of an ability.

**Steady the Crack** *(2 BP, reaction, when you take damage)* — You reduce the damage taken by 1d8 + your Binder level, as a bound remnant's old resilience briefly overlays your own.

**Force the Bind** *(3 BP, bonus action)* — You can treat one additional remnant — one you did not select during your last Binding Rite, of a Tier you can normally access — as bound for 1 minute. Its Manifest Mark appears immediately. At the end of the duration, or if you're reduced to 0 hit points first, the remnant tears free and you cannot use Force the Bind again until you finish a short or long rest.

You regain all spent Bleed Points when you finish a short or long rest.

---

### Rebinding *(2nd Level)*

Starting at 2nd level, you can use your action to perform an abbreviated version of the Binding Rite, releasing one currently bound remnant and binding a different remnant of the same or lower Tier in its place. Any ongoing effects created by the released remnant end immediately, and its Manifest Mark fades over the following minute.

Once you use this feature, you can't do so again until you finish a short or long rest. At 15th level, you can use Rebinding twice between rests.

---

### Class Reactions *(2nd Level)*

Hosting the dead sharpens your reflexes to threats you can't fully explain. At 2nd level, you gain access to **Class Reactions**, drawn from a pool of points equal to your **Proficiency Bonus**, which refreshes at the start of each short or long rest.

You gain access to two reactions at 2nd level, and a third at 7th level.

**Borrowed Reflex**
*Cost: 1 PB point. Trigger: You are targeted by an attack you can see.*

As a reaction, you may add your Charisma modifier to your Evasion roll against that attack, as a bound remnant's old battlefield instincts surface at the critical moment.

**Remnant's Warning**
*Cost: 1 PB point. Trigger: An ally within 30 feet is targeted by an attack they can't see coming (e.g., from stealth or ambush).*

As a reaction, you shout with a dead voice not your own. The target ally may immediately make an Evasion roll against the triggering attack, even if they otherwise couldn't, though they don't add any bonuses that require seeing the attacker.

**Grasp from Beyond** *(Available at 7th Level)*
*Cost: 1 PB point. Trigger: A creature within 15 feet of you attempts to move away from you or take the Disengage action.*

As a reaction, one bound remnant lashes out physically. The creature must succeed on a Strength saving throw (Binding Save DC) or its speed is reduced to 0 until the end of its current turn.

---

### Binding Order *(3rd Level)*

You've found — or founded — a tradition that shapes how you relate to the dead you host. Choose a **Binding Order**: the Chorus, the Severed, the Hollow Path, or the Cartographers. Your Order grants features at 3rd level and again at 6th, 10th, and 14th level. Full details follow the base class.

---

### Ability Score Improvement *(4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th Level)*

You may increase one ability score by 2, or two ability scores by 1 each. You cannot increase a score above 20 this way. Using the optional feats rule, you may instead take a feat for which you qualify.

---

### Second Remnant *(5th Level)*

Your Remnants Bound increases to 2, and your Remnant Tier increases to 2 (see the Binder table and the Bound Remnants list).

---

### Suppress/Reveal Signs *(7th Level)*

By 7th level, you've learned to fold your Manifest Marks away when discretion matters. As a bonus action, you can suppress all Manifest Marks from your currently bound remnants; they become fully invisible, though the remnants' abilities remain fully usable. As a bonus action, you can reveal them again. This costs you nothing but the action.

---

### Adamant Mind *(9th Level)*

Years of sharing your mind with the dead have taught you to guard what's still yours. You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed, frightened, or possessed, and against any effect that would read your thoughts or sense your emotions.

Additionally, whenever you succeed on such a save against an effect caused by a creature you can see, that creature takes psychic damage equal to your Binder level + your Charisma modifier, as the massed dead in your soul push back.

---

### Heroic Boon *(10th Level)*

Choose one of the following:

**Boon of the Steady Vessel.** Your Focus saving throws to resist a remnant's involuntary bleed-through (see individual remnants and GM discretion) are made with advantage.

**Boon of the Deep Well.** Your maximum Bleed Points increase by 2, and Steady the Crack now costs 1 BP instead of 2.

**Boon of the Open Door.** You can perform the Binding Rite in 1 minute instead of 10, once per long rest.

---

### Third Remnant *(11th Level)*

Your Remnants Bound increases to 3.

---

### Threadscarred *(18th Level)*

The line between you and the remnants you've hosted has worn thin enough that some of it never fully closes. Choose one currently bound remnant's Trait (see Bound Remnants below): you retain the benefit of that Trait at all times, even when that remnant is not currently bound, until you choose a different Trait to retain this way (which you may do at the end of a long rest).

---

### Fourth Remnant *(17th Level)*

Your Remnants Bound increases to 4, and your Remnant Tier increases to 5, granting access to the full Bound Remnants list.

---

### Unbound *(20th Level — Epic Boon)*

You have learned to hold the door open without losing yourself in the draft. Choose one:

**Epic Boon: Full Chorus.** You may bind one additional remnant beyond your normal maximum (5 total), and your Bleed Point maximum increases to 8.

**Epic Boon: The Last Door.** Once per long rest, as an action, you may bind every remnant on the Bound Remnants list simultaneously for 1 minute, gaining the benefit of every Trait and access to every active ability at once. At the end of the duration, you gain one level of Exhaustion and cannot perform the Binding Rite again until you finish a long rest.

## Bound Remnants

Remnants are presented by Tier. Unless an entry says otherwise, an active ability usable "as a bonus action" can be used once per turn, and any saving throw uses your Binding Save DC.

### Tier 1 Remnants *(Available at 1st Level)*

**The Unmarked Soldier**
*Manifestation: You wear a strip of cloth from a uniform belonging to no army anyone recognizes.*
*Mark: A fading bruise pattern across your knuckles that never quite heals.*
Trait: Once per turn when you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can deal an extra 1d6 damage of the weapon's type.
Active *(bonus action)*: You brace for the next hit. Until the start of your next turn, the first time you would take damage, reduce it by 1d8 + your Charisma modifier.

**The Drowned Scholar**
*Manifestation: A bowl of water left undisturbed overnight, in which a face was briefly visible at dawn.*
*Mark: Your eyes cloud faintly, as if cataracted, though your sight is unaffected.*
Trait: You have advantage on Intelligence (Investigation) and Wisdom (Insight) checks made to recall or interpret written information.
Active *(action)*: A creature within 30 feet must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or take 2d6 psychic damage and have disadvantage on the next saving throw it makes before the end of your next turn, as drowned memories crowd its mind.

**The Cornered Beast**
*Manifestation: Claw marks scratched into a doorframe by something that never escaped.*
*Mark: A low growl underlies your voice when you raise it.*
Trait: Your walking speed increases by 10 feet.
Active *(bonus action)*: You make a melee spell attack against a creature within 5 feet, dealing 1d8 slashing damage. If this attack hits a creature that has already taken damage this turn, it deals an extra 1d8 damage.

**The Quiet Arsonist**
*Manifestation: A matchbook, always full, that never seems to run out.*
*Mark: A faint smell of smoke follows you.*
Trait: You have resistance to fire damage.
Active *(bonus action)*: A creature within 30 feet must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw or take 2d6 fire damage. If the target is wearing flammable materials or standing in dry brush (GM's discretion), it has disadvantage on the save.

### Tier 2 Remnants *(Available at 5th Level)*

**The Broken Oathkeeper**
*Manifestation: A shattered signet ring, reforged badly, worn on a cord.*
*Mark: Your voice occasionally doubles, as if two people spoke the same word a half-beat apart.*
Trait: When an ally within 30 feet of you fails a saving throw, you can use your reaction to grant them a +2 bonus, potentially turning failure into success (declare before the outcome is known).
Active *(action)*: Choose a creature within 60 feet that can hear you. It must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be compelled to move 15 feet toward you or away from a location you designate on its next turn (your choice when you use this ability). This is a compulsion effect.

**The Unhealed Medic**
*Manifestation: A roll of bandages, always slightly bloodstained no matter how many times you replace it.*
*Mark: Faint surgical scars appear across your forearms.*
Trait: When you or an ally within 5 feet of you regains hit points, add your Charisma modifier to the amount regained (minimum +1). This applies once per instance of healing.
Active *(action)*: Touch a creature. It regains 2d8 + your Charisma modifier hit points. Once you use this active ability, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest, unless you spend 2 Bleed Points to use it again immediately.

**The Silent Watcher**
*Manifestation: A single unblinking painted eye on a scrap of canvas.*
*Mark: You do not blink as often as you should.*
Trait: You have advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks and can't be surprised while at least one remnant is bound to you.
Active *(action)*: You gain **truesight** out to 30 feet until the end of your next turn, piercing illusions, invisibility, and magical darkness.

**The Frostbitten Guide**
*Manifestation: A compass whose needle points toward the nearest source of extreme cold.*
*Mark: Your breath fogs even in warm air.*
Trait: You have resistance to cold damage, and difficult terrain caused by ice or snow doesn't cost you extra movement.
Active *(bonus action)*: A creature within 30 feet must succeed on a Constitution saving throw or take 2d8 cold damage and have its speed halved until the end of its next turn.

### Tier 3 Remnants *(Available at 9th Level)*

**The Unfinished Architect**
*Manifestation: A folded blueprint for a building that was never built, its ink still wet.*
*Mark: Faint chalk-dust residue on your fingertips that never fully wipes away.*
Trait: You can't be knocked prone against your will while standing on stable ground, as unseen scaffolding braces you.
Active *(action)*: You conjure a wall of hardened, translucent Thread-glass, 20 feet long, 10 feet high, and 1 foot thick, anywhere within 60 feet on a surface you can see. It has AC 5, 30 hit points per 10-foot section, and lasts for 10 minutes or until destroyed. Once you use this ability, you can't use it again until you finish a short rest, or by spending 3 Bleed Points.

**The Unsent Letter**
*Manifestation: An envelope, sealed, addressed to no one, that never opens.*
*Mark: Ink stains bloom faintly on your writing hand.*
Trait: You can communicate telepathically with any creature within 60 feet that can understand at least one language, though it can choose to block this connection.
Active *(action)*: Choose a creature within 60 feet. It must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or become **charmed** by you for 1 minute, believing you to be someone it once trusted deeply. The charm ends immediately if you or your allies do anything harmful to it.

**The Hungering Debt**
*Manifestation: A ledger page, torn out, listing a debt that can never be repaid.*
*Mark: Your shadow occasionally moves half a second out of sync with you.*
Trait: Once per turn when you deal damage to a Bloodied creature, you gain 1 Bleed Point (in addition to the normal Bleed Point trigger for reducing an enemy to Bloodied, which still applies only once).
Active *(bonus action)*: Mark a creature within 60 feet you can see. Until the end of your next turn, whenever it takes damage from any source, it takes an additional 1d6 necrotic damage, as the debt comes due. You can only have one creature marked this way at a time.

**The Patient Hunter**
*Manifestation: A single arrowhead, ancient, still sharp.*
*Mark: Your pupils narrow slightly in bright light.*
Trait: You have advantage on the first attack roll you make against a creature that hasn't acted yet in combat.
Active *(bonus action)*: Make a ranged spell attack against a creature within 90 feet, dealing 2d10 piercing damage on a hit. This attack ignores half cover and three-quarters cover.

### Tier 4 Remnants *(Available at 13th Level)*

**The Last Convocation**
*Manifestation: A circle of unlit candles that will not stay lit near you.*
*Mark: Faint whispering follows your speech, half a syllable behind.*
Trait: You have advantage on Charisma checks made to influence groups of three or more creatures at once.
Active *(action)*: You and up to five allies within 30 feet gain temporary hit points equal to 1d8 + your Binder level, and advantage on the next saving throw each of you makes before the end of your next turn.

**The Collapsing Star**
*Manifestation: A shard of stone, unnaturally warm, that hums faintly.*
*Mark: A faint corona flickers around you in dim light.*
Trait: You have resistance to radiant and necrotic damage.
Active *(action)*: Every creature of your choice within 20 feet of a point you can see within 60 feet must make a Constitution saving throw, taking 4d10 force damage on a failure, or half as much on a success. You can only use this ability once per short or long rest, or by spending 4 Bleed Points to use it again immediately.

**The Bound Executioner**
*Manifestation: A hood, always folded, that no light seems to fall on correctly.*
*Mark: Your shadow gains a faint secondary edge, as if cast by two light sources even in single light.*
Trait: When you score a critical hit, you gain 2 Bleed Points instead of the normal amount from that trigger.
Active *(bonus action)*: Make a melee spell attack against a Bloodied creature within 5 feet. On a hit, it takes 3d8 necrotic damage, and if this reduces it to 0 hit points, you regain 1d8 hit points as the remnant takes its due.

### Tier 5 Remnants *(Available at 17th Level)*

**The Unwritten Ending**
*Manifestation: A book with no final chapter, its last page perpetually blank.*
*Mark: Your reflection in still water lags a half-second behind your movements.*
Trait: Once per long rest, when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, you instead drop to 1 hit point.
Active *(action)*: Reroll any one saving throw, attack roll, or damage roll made by you or a creature within 60 feet within the last 6 seconds. You must use the new result. Once you use this ability, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest, or by spending 6 Bleed Points to use it again immediately.

**The Fracture's Herald**
*Manifestation: A splinter of Thread-glass that was present at the Fracture of Accord itself.*
*Mark: Reality visibly ripples around you when you take damage.*
Trait: When you fail a Focus or Sanity saving throw, you may immediately gain 2 Bleed Points instead of 1.
Active *(action)*: You tear a 15-foot radius rift at a point within 60 feet. Every creature in the area must make a Dexterity saving throw, taking 6d8 force damage on a failure or half on a success, and being knocked prone on a failure. Every creature within 60 feet of the rift (including you) makes a Sanity saving throw or gains 1 Whisper Point. This ability can only be used once per long rest.

## The Binding Orders

### The Chorus

The Chorus doesn't bind the dead one at a time and let each fade in turn — they collect them, layer them, and let the accumulated weight of many voices speak at once. A Chorus binder in full flow sounds like a crowd arguing inside a single throat.

**We Are Several** *(3rd Level)* — When you finish the Binding Rite, you can bind one additional remnant beyond your normal Remnants Bound, though it must be at least one Tier lower than your current maximum Remnant Tier. This remnant doesn't count against your total for the purposes of other features.

**Layered Voice** *(6th Level)* — Once per turn, when you use an active ability from one bound remnant, you can also apply the Trait (not the active ability) of a second bound remnant to the same action, if the GM agrees it makes sense to combine them.

**Shared Burden** *(10th Level)* — When you gain Bleed Points, an ally within 30 feet of your choice also gains a number of temporary hit points equal to the BP gained × 2, as the collective weight of your remnants briefly extends outward.

**Full Assembly** *(14th Level)* — Your We Are Several bonus remnant no longer needs to be a lower Tier than your maximum — it can be any remnant you have access to.

---

### The Severed

The Severed have made peace with a stranger arrangement: rather than let a remnant bleed into their own body, they let it step half a pace outside it — a spectral double, tethered by will alone, that fights alongside them without fully replacing them.

**Manifest Double** *(3rd Level)* — As a bonus action, you can manifest a spectral double of yourself in an unoccupied space within 5 feet, using your own ability scores and an Armor Class equal to 10 + your Dexterity modifier + your Charisma modifier. It has no hit points of its own — instead, any damage it would take is instead taken by you, halved (round down). It vanishes if you fall unconscious, dismiss it as a bonus action, or move more than 30 feet from it (in which case it instantly reappears at your side). On your turn, you may make one of your weapon or Remnant attacks through the double instead of yourself, as if you had made the attack from its space.

**Split Focus** *(6th Level)* — While your double is manifested, you can take the Disengage or Dodge action through it instead of yourself, without spending your own action.

**Twinned Strike** *(10th Level)* — Once per turn, when you hit with an attack made through your double, you can also make one weapon attack yourself as part of the same action, without expending an extra action.

**Trade Places** *(14th Level)* — As a bonus action, you can instantly swap positions with your double, provided it is within 30 feet. Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

---

### The Hollow Path

Where other binders lean into the remnants they host, the Hollow Path treats binding as a discipline of restraint — power held deliberately at arm's length, released only in precise, controlled bursts.

**Suppression as Strength** *(3rd Level)* — As a bonus action, you can suppress one bound remnant, gaining no benefit from its Trait or active ability, but gaining a +2 bonus to Evasion rolls and advantage on Focus saving throws until you resume normal use of that remnant (also a bonus action).

**Controlled Release** *(6th Level)* — When you resume normal use of a suppressed remnant, you immediately gain 1 Bleed Point, and the next active ability you use from that remnant this turn costs no action economy restriction it would otherwise have (it can be used even if you've already used your action or bonus action this turn, once).

**Vestigial Precision** *(10th Level)* — When an ability tied to a bound remnant calls for a saving throw, you can choose to grant yourself advantage on any single d20 roll tied to using that ability, once per turn. Once you use this ability, you must finish a short or long rest before using it again.

**Perfect Stillness** *(14th Level)* — While you have at least one remnant suppressed, you have resistance to psychic damage, and creatures have disadvantage on checks made to sense your emotional or mental state.

---

### The Cartographers

The Cartographers approach binding the way the old Summoners approached Thread-space: as something to be mapped, measured, and controlled with precision. They bind fewer remnants than most, but master each one completely.

**Precise Binding** *(3rd Level)* — Your Binding Save DC increases by 1. Additionally, when you fail a Focus saving throw to resist a remnant's involuntary bleed-through, you can spend 1 Bleed Point to succeed instead.

**Charted Rebind** *(6th Level)* — You can use your Rebinding feature without expending your daily use, once per long rest, provided the new remnant is the same Tier or lower than the one you're releasing.

**Annotated Grasp** *(10th Level)* — When you use an active ability from a bound remnant that would normally target one creature, you can instead target a second creature within 10 feet of the first, though the second target only takes half damage (if applicable) and has advantage on any saving throw.

**The Complete Map** *(14th Level)* — Once per long rest, when you perform the Binding Rite, you may treat it as though you had access to one Tier higher than normal for that Rite only, letting you bind a single remnant above your usual limit.

## Binder Last Stand Options

When a Binder's body finally fails, the remnants inside them don't leave quietly. Whether they burn out in one final blaze, tear loose all at once, or simply refuse to let their vessel go, a Binder's death is rarely the end of the story in the room.

### Rules for Last Stand

- **Trigger:** A Last Stand can be invoked when the character:
    1. Would be reduced to **0 hit points**,
    2. Rolls a **death saving throw**, or
    3. **Dies outright**.
- **Free Action:** Invoking a Last Stand is a free action and requires no reaction, consciousness, components, or any other functional capability beyond the player's choice.
- **Permanent Death:** Once a Last Stand is invoked, the character permanently dies after the effect resolves. Resurrection magic, *wish*, *true resurrection*, and all similar effects cannot return this character to life.

---

### 1. Every Door at Once

*You spent your whole career deciding how much of yourself to lend out. In the end, you stop deciding.*

**Effect:** Every remnant you have ever bound over your lifetime — not just your currently bound ones — tears loose simultaneously in a 30-foot radius centered on you. Every creature of your choice in the radius makes a Wisdom saving throw (Binding Save DC). On a failure, a creature is **frightened** for 1 minute and takes 6d8 psychic damage as the full weight of everyone you ever hosted passes through them; on a success, half damage and no fear effect. Allies in the radius instead gain temporary hit points equal to your Binder level × 3 and advantage on their next saving throw.

**Narrative Impact:** This is the Binder finally, fully opening the door — not for one remnant, but for every fragment of the dead they've ever carried. It's less a weapon than a release, and it should feel like one: overwhelming, cathartic, and final.

---

### 2. The Debt Comes Due

*Somewhere, the ledger has been keeping score the whole time. You never checked the balance. Now it's being collected.*

**Effect:** Choose one enemy creature within 60 feet that you have damaged at any point during this combat. That creature must succeed on a Constitution saving throw (Binding Save DC) or immediately drop to 0 hit points, its remaining vitality claimed as final payment. On a success, it instead takes damage equal to half its current hit points and is knocked prone.

**Narrative Impact:** A quiet, personal Last Stand — not a battlefield-wide event, but a single, specific reckoning with whoever the Binder decided owed them the most. It rewards a table that's tracked who's been hurting the Binder throughout the fight.

---

### 3. Unfinished Business

*The remnants never got to finish what they started. You're not going to finish it for them. But you can make sure someone does.*

**Effect:** Choose one ally within 30 feet. That ally can immediately take a full turn (movement, action, bonus action, and reaction) out of turn order, as your accumulated remnants' unfinished intentions pour into them instead of dissipating. For the duration of that turn only, the ally may use any one currently bound remnant's active ability of yours as if they were you (using your Binding Save DC and Attack Modifier).

**Narrative Impact:** The Binder's final act isn't about themselves at all — it's a handoff, a passing of unfinished work to someone still able to finish it. This Last Stand plays best when it sets up a dramatic ally turnaround rather than ending the fight outright.

---

## Roleplay Guidance: Playing a Binder

A Binder's core tension is a question every player should answer for their character before the first session: **how much of "you" is still in the room when a remnant is bound?** Some Binders treat their remnants as tools, fully partitioned from their own identity — cold, controlled, professional. Others feel the bleed-through as something closer to possession, and struggle visibly with where they end and the dead begin.

**Questions for character development:**

- How did you learn to bind — from a teacher, a book, an accident, a desperate necessity?
- Do you know where your remnants came from? Do you want to know?
- Is binding legal where you're from? If not, how do you hide it — or do you?
- Which of your bound remnants do you like the most? Which one scares you?
- Have you ever failed to release a remnant cleanly? What happened?

**On the ethics of binding:** Remnants are not fully people anymore, but they're not nothing, either — they're the unfinished remainder of someone who once was. Some Binders think of their remnants as roommates, owed courtesy and consideration. Others think of them as a resource, no different from a spell component, and don't lose sleep over it. Very few tables will agree on which view is correct — that's the point.

In Dór-o Estel, binding carries the weight of the Fracture's memory. Older generations who lived through it often regard Binders with the same wariness once reserved for the old Summoners — sometimes worse, since a Binder's power quite literally shows on their skin. Being recognized as a Binder in the wrong city can mean registration, monitoring, or arrest. In others, particularly Loom-Cities that lost people to the Fracture and never got proper burials, a Binder is viewed as something closer to a mourner-for-hire — someone who lets the unfinished dead finally finish something, even briefly, even secondhand.

---

## Party Synergies

Binders support their party through:

**Flexible Coverage:** Your Bound Remnants list spans damage, control, healing, and utility — a well-chosen loadout can plug whatever gap your party currently has.

**Adaptive Response:** Rebinding lets you retool between encounters (or, at higher levels, mid-fight) rather than being locked into a single build for the day.

**Resilience Under Pressure:** Bleed Points turn the punishment you take into resources you can spend, making you harder to shut down through sustained damage than most half-casters.

**Work well with:**
- **Frontliners:** Your remnants can patch their gaps (healing, control, mobility denial) without requiring you to abandon your own position.
- **Controllers:** Bleed Points generated from Bloodied enemies synergize with anyone who can quickly wear enemies down.
- **Other Binders (rare, but possible):** Multiple Binders in a party can specialize their Remnant loadouts to avoid overlap, effectively fielding a much wider toolkit collectively.

**Potential Friction:**
- The Chorus's We Are Several and the Cartographers' Precise Binding pull in opposite design directions (breadth vs. depth) — players choosing between Orders should know which itch they're trying to scratch.
- A table uncomfortable with body-horror-adjacent themes (visible Marks, bleed-through, possession-adjacent framing) should discuss tone with the Binder's player before session one.

## Multiclassing

**Prerequisites.** To multiclass into the Binder, you must have a **Charisma score of 13 or higher**.

**Proficiencies Gained.** Light armor, medium armor, simple weapons.

**Bleed Points.** Bleed Points do not combine with similar point-based resources from other classes. They remain a separate pool.

**Remnants Bound Restriction.** Your Remnants Bound and Remnant Tier are determined solely by your Binder levels, read off the Binder table as if your Binder levels were your total level.

**Binding Order Restrictions.** The Severed's Manifest Double uses your full character ability scores regardless of multiclass levels. The Chorus's We Are Several bonus remnant does not stack with similar "extra summon/companion" features from other classes.

**Recommended Synergies.** Charisma-based classes (Warlock, Sorcerer, Bard) pair naturally, sharing your primary ability score. Fighter or Barbarian levels give you a durable chassis to host remnants in melee. Rogue levels complement the Severed's double for battlefield-splitting tactics.

**Dip Warning.** A 1-level Binder dip grants one Tier 1 remnant and 2 Bleed Points — usable, but the class's flexibility (Rebinding, Binding Order features, higher Remnant Tiers) only comes online with sustained investment. A 3-level dip (to pick up a Binding Order) is the more common minimum for a multiclass build.

## Final Thoughts

The Binder replaces the old Summoner's architecture of command with something more intimate and considerably cheaper to run at the table: instead of controlling a separate creature with its own stat block, you *become*, briefly and partially, the remnant you've chosen to host. Every Bound Remnant is fully self-contained — no monster conversion, no CR lookup, no external content required to play the class from level 1.

Your choice of Binding Order shapes how you carry that power:
- **The Chorus** — more voices, more coverage, more risk of losing track of who's speaking
- **The Severed** — a second self, fighting beside you rather than through you
- **The Hollow Path** — discipline and restraint, power held until it's needed
- **The Cartographers** — precision over breadth, mastery of fewer doors held open

Whether you're a reluctant vessel who never wanted this power, a scholar treating the dead as a discipline to be mapped, or someone who opened the first door out of desperation and never quite closed it again, the Binder offers a class built entirely from what a single character can carry — nothing borrowed from outside these pages, nothing waiting on content that doesn't exist yet.

**You are not a commander. You are a door.** Every remnant you bind is a choice about how much of yourself you're willing to lend, and for how long. The dead are patient. They can wait for you to decide.
