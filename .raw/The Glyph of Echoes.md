# Glyph of Echoes - The Wheel of the Restorer 
> _"Even the ash of a dead world holds the memory of flame."_  
> — Attributed to the First Restorer

---
## 🧱 Layer

- **Layer:** Primal  
- **Tier Rank:** 1  
- **Domain(s):** Rebirth, Renewal, Memory, Restoration  
- **Opposing Glyph(s):** [[The Glyph of Endings]]  
- **Symbol (optional):** A spiraling loop, or a wheel with seven spokes)

---

## 🔷 Core Meaning

The **Glyph of Echoes** embodies the **law of cyclical return**. It ensures that **what is lost is never truly gone**—merely transformed.

It is not kindness. It is not fairness. It is **cosmic continuity**:  
- The breath after death  
- The spark after ruin  
- The future echo of a vanished past

Even **destruction must give way to renewal**, or the Pattern itself would stagnate.

### **The Unbroken Wheel**

The **Glyph of Echoes is not mercy—it is inevitability.**

- **The cycle must turn, for stagnation is another form of death.**
- **Memories must fade, but never fully vanish.**
- **What was must give rise to what will be.**

The gods who wield the **Glyph of Echoes** do not restore what was lost **as it was**, but **as it should be in the next turn of the cycle.**

- **The ashes of a world become the soil of the next.**
- **The bones of the dead feed the roots of the future.**
- **The end of an age is the beginning of a new legend.**

Even the Primal Powers **do not escape its pull.**

---
## 🌀 Narrative Role

- This glyph is **what resists the Maw**: not in defiance, but in purpose.  
- It is the **force that gives Endings meaning**, ensuring that **no death is final**, only foundational.
- Where **[[Astralor]] creates**, and **[[Aeloria]] observes**, [[the Restorer]] returns—with changes.

**Mythic Parallels:**
- The rise of mortal civilisations on ruins of forgotten empires  
- The reappearance of long-lost divine sparks in new forms  
- The strange truth that all Threads eventually loop

**Divine Figures** tied to this glyph include:
- The **Restorer** (name forbidden, often associated with the Wheel)  
- Whisper-honored spirits like **[[Nyzara]]**, who carry echoes forward  
- Possibly **Vaelora**, in her role as deep memory within the water

---

## ✨ Magical Influence

- **Necromancy** that restores, not animates  
- **Divination** through echoes or past imprints  
- **Illusion** that uses memory itself as power  
- **Restoration magic** at the highest tier (9th level revival spells, memory-seeing)

Echo-Glyphs can appear:
- As spectral spirals in healing rituals  
- In **Thread-mending ceremonies** performed by spirit-weavers  
- When [[leylines]] surge reawaken collapsed anchors

---
## ⚙️ Fate & Glyphic Mechanics

- **Echo-Glyphs** stabilise corrupted or fractured leylines by “remembering” their prior state.
- Spellcasters using this glyph may **retrace a previously cast glyph** under rare lunar alignments.
- Rituals tied to Echoes allow **repetition of past magical patterns**—with the risk of recursive corruption.
- Glyphbound objects marked by Echoes may **resist erasure**, **reincarnate upon destruction**, or **retain memory echoes** of past wielders.

> _“All threads fray. Some weave again.”_

---

## ⚠️ Eldritch Corruption

When corrupted, the Glyph of Echoes **does not forget—it repeats**:
- **The Broken Cycle** may cause **identities to overwrite**, **past to bleed into present**, or **entire cities to repeat actions** until collapse.
- Echo cults like **The Cyclebound Chorus** believe **nothing new is possible**, only **perfect repetition**.
- **False resurrections**, **memory loops**, and **spirit overgrowth** mark a glyph gone wrong.

Known corrupted sites:
- **The Mirror Graves of Cael’Tess**: tombs where the dead rise not as undead, but as they once were—again and again.
- **The Spiral Gate**: a ruin where all exit paths lead to the same threshold, looping forever.

---

## 🧠 Player Tie-ins

- Sorcerers with the **Echo-Touched Origin** can recall spells from past lives.  
- Warlocks of the **Cyclebound Wheel** speak with voices of themselves that have not yet lived.  
- Clerics of **Renewal** Domains may use this glyph to bless resurrection.  
- Bards and Archivists may manifest **memories too old to be theirs**.

Optional Mechanics:
- Echo Mark feat: Once per rest, repeat a spell you or an ally cast last round  
- Echo Binding: Touch a corpse and learn its last regret or undone action  
- Spirit Loop: On death, delay death by 1 round—repeating last action with +1 effect

## 🏛️ Cultural or Religious Significance

- Worshipped in quiet, half-remembered rites in **[[Aeravae]]**, where water remembers what is washed away.
- Inadvertly Honoured in **[[Kel Varyan]]** during the Rite of Rekindling, where old magical texts are burned and then rewritten from memory.
- Revered in **dwarven renewal rites**, where old tools are melted and reforged into weapons for the next generation.
- In **[[07_Geography/02_Cities/Thar Vornhal]]**, the **Chamber of Names Recalled** allows the recently dead to be ritually named again, in hopes their soul returns in the next cycle.
- In **ruins beneath [[Aeravae]]**, an ancient structure called the **Spiral Vault** replays dying visions in pools of slow water. It is not maintained, but never fades.

**Threadbearers of Spirit** often feel **tugged backward** before visions—sign of the glyph activating.
