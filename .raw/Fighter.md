# The Fighter

**Adaptable Masters of Combat**

> *"Everyone else has a plan. The Fighter has trained for the moment when the plan stops working."*

---

## Steel and Discipline

The gate was sealed. The ambush was perfect. The party had nowhere to go and no time to think — except for her. She had already mapped the exits, already gauged which of the six was the slowest, already decided which one she would hit first and how the others would respond. By the time the first blow landed, she had already won.

You are not the flashiest class. You do not transform, call on gods, or manipulate the laws of reality. What you do is fight — more effectively, more consistently, and over a wider range of circumstances than anyone else in the room. Your **Martial Actions** give you options. Your **Action Surge** gives you a second chance. Your **Weapon Mastery** means you are always the most dangerous person in the fight with whatever you're holding.

---

## The Fighter's Essence

In FEARS, a Fighter is a force multiplier — for yourself and for your party. Every combat round you have more choices than almost any other class: which Martial Action, whether to Surge, which weapon skill to apply, whether to Guard or Feint or Press. Your **Momentum** interaction is clean and reliable: Action Surge always gives you +1 regardless of whether the attacks hit.

You have the broadest weapon and armour proficiencies in the game. **Fighting Styles** let you specialise for your table's needs. **Multiattack** scaling (2 → 3 → 4 attacks) means you get more out of Momentum's stacking bonuses than any other martial class. At high levels, no one attacks more times per round.

Your **Last Stand** options embody three different Fighter identities: the unstoppable destroyer, the inspiring commander, and the tactical mastermind.

## Class Features

### Hit Points

- **Hit Dice:** 1d10 per Fighter level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 10 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d10 (or 6) + your Constitution modifier per Fighter level after 1st

### Proficiencies

- **Armour:** All armour and shields
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons, martial weapons
- **Tools:** None
- **Saving Throws:** Strength, Constitution
- **Skills:** Choose two from Acrobatics, Animal Handling, Athletics, Crafting, History, Insight, Intimidation, Perception, Resolve, and Survival

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) chain mail or (b) leather armour, longbow, and 20 arrows
- (a) a martial weapon and a shield or (b) two martial weapons
- (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) two handaxes
- (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) an explorer's pack

## Quick Build

To quickly create a Fighter, follow these steps:

1. **Ability Scores:** Make Strength or Dexterity your highest score, followed by Constitution.
2. **Lineage and Heritage:** Any combination works; Human, Hobgoblin, and Dwarf are thematic but not required.
3. **Background:** Soldier, Guard, or any background with Athletics or Intimidation.
4. **Combat Strategy:** Choose your Fighting Style, pick two Martial Actions suited to your weapon, and use Action Surge when it counts.

## Core Class Features

### Rush of Adrenaline (1st Level)

When damage would reduce your HP to less than half your hit point maximum, you can use your reaction to spend hit dice up to a number equal to your Proficiency Bonus, immediately rolling them. You regain HP equal to the total rolled + your Constitution modifier.

**Momentum Interaction:** When you use Rush of Adrenaline, your Momentum Score immediately resets to +1 (unless already higher). Your renewed vigour re-establishes your combat rhythm.

---

### Martial Action (1st Level)

Your tactical expertise allows you to take a bonus action on each of your turns to perform one Martial Action. You start knowing two Martial Actions of your choice. You gain additional Martial Actions at higher levels as shown in the Fighter table.

**Martial Actions:**
- **Aim:** Double your Proficiency Bonus for your next ranged weapon attack roll.
- **Guard:** Impose disadvantage on the next attack an enemy makes against you or an adjacent ally.
- **Quick Strike:** Make two additional attacks with a light melee weapon as a bonus action.
- **Wind Up:** Add extra damage equal to your Proficiency Bonus to your next attack with a heavy or versatile weapon.
- **Feint:** Grant yourself advantage on the next attack roll against a target within 5 feet.
- **Press the Advantage:** Move up to 10 feet without provoking opportunity attacks and make an additional melee attack after hitting a creature.
- **Suppressive Fire:** Impose disadvantage on a target's attack rolls until the start of your next turn.

---

### Weapon Mastery (1st Level and 3rd Level)

**At 1st level**, gain Weapon Mastery with one martial weapon and one simple weapon of your choice. When you hit with a mastered weapon, you may apply one Weapon Skill associated with that weapon once per turn.

**At 3rd level**, your mastery deepens. You gain Weapon Mastery with three additional weapons (four total). You may now use Weapon Skills multiple times per round, but only once per action type: one on your Attack Action, one as a Bonus Action (if the mastery has a BA version), one as a Reaction (if applicable), and one with Action Surge.

You cannot use the same Weapon Skill twice in one round. You unlock new masteries faster than any other class (1 critical hit per weapon), with no limit on total masteries.

---

### Action Surge (2nd Level)

On your turn, gain one additional action. Once used, you must finish a short or long rest before using it again.

**Momentum Interaction:** When you use Action Surge, immediately gain +1 Momentum regardless of whether your attacks land. The explosive burst of action shifts momentum in your favour.

At 13th level, usable twice per rest (once per turn). At 18th level, usable three times per rest.

---

### Fighting Style (2nd Level)

Choose one fighting style:

- **Archery:** +2 bonus to attack rolls with ranged weapons.
- **Blind Fighting:** Blindsight 10 feet.
- **Agile Defence:** Add Dexterity modifier to Evasion rolls regardless of armour type.
- **Armoured Defence:** Layer armour without penalties.
- **Dueling:** +2 bonus to damage rolls with a one-handed melee weapon and no other weapon or shield; roll Evasion with advantage once per turn.
- **Great Weapon Fighting:** Reroll 1s and 2s on damage dice for two-handed or versatile weapons.
- **Interception:** As a reaction, reduce damage to a nearby ally by 1d10 + PB.
- **Protection:** As a reaction, impose disadvantage on attacks against an adjacent ally (requires shield).
- **Shield Master:** Add shield's bonus to Evasion rolls.
- **Two-Weapon Fighting:** Add ability modifier to the damage of the second attack.
- **Unarmed Fighting:** Unarmed strikes deal 1d6 + STR bludgeoning; d8 when not wielding weapons or shields.

You may choose an additional Fighting Style at 13th and 18th level.

---

### Improvement (4th, 6th, 8th, 12th, 14th, 16th, 19th Level)

Choose one: increase one ability score by 2, increase two ability scores by 1 each, or increase one ability score by 1 and select a martial talent.

---

### Martial Versatility (4th Level)

Whenever you gain an Improvement, you may replace one Fighting Style or Martial Action with another from their respective lists.

---

### Multiattack (5th Level)

Make two attacks when you take the Attack action. Increases to three attacks at 9th level and four attacks at 17th level.

---

### Surgical Precision (5th Level)

Once per turn when you attempt a Called Shot, reduce the penalty to your attack roll by an amount equal to your Proficiency Bonus. At 5th level (PB +3), a Called Shot to the arm (normally −2) becomes a +1 bonus to hit.

---

### Battlefield Awareness (8th Level)

Gain +3 to initiative rolls. Opportunity attacks made against you have disadvantage. When an ally within 10 feet is targeted by an attack, you can use your reaction to impose disadvantage on that attack roll.

---

### Heroic Boon (10th Level)

Choose one:

**Boon of Indomitable Will:** Once per short rest, when you fail a saving throw against a condition, reroll and take the new result. Once per long rest, when reduced to 0 HP but not killed outright, drop to 1 HP instead.

**Boon of Tactical Superiority:** As a bonus action, grant an ally within 30 feet advantage on their next attack or saving throw. When you attack with advantage, deal an additional 2d6 damage.

---

### Master Combatant (18th Level)

When you use Action Surge, you take **two additional actions** instead of one. Once per round, as a reaction, you can make an attack or perform any action that normally requires an action (Dodge, Disengage, Help).

---

### Legendary Weaponmaster (18th Level)

All weapons you are proficient with are treated as mastered. You may use Weapon Skills from any weapon you wield. When you score a critical hit, you may apply two Weapon Skills instead of one (both from that weapon).

---

### Epic Boon (20th Level)

Choose one:

**Boon of the Warlord:** Once per long rest, as an action, grant all allies within 30 feet one additional attack action during their next turn for 1 minute. Allies within 30 feet gain +2 AC and saving throws while you are conscious.

**Boon of Unbreakable Valour:** Gain resistance to all damage while below half your maximum HP. Once per short rest, when an attack would reduce you to 0 HP, ignore the damage, drop to 1 HP, and immediately counterattack.

---

## Last Stand Options

The Fighter's final act reflects their martial identity: unstoppable offense, inspiring leadership, or tactical genius.

**Rules for Last Stand:** Invoked when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, roll a death saving throw, or die outright. A free action requiring no reaction or consciousness. Once resolved, the character permanently dies.

---

### 1. Die by the Sword

*They always used to call you a troublemaker. One thing's for sure: they won't call you a hypocrite.*

**Effect:**
- Move up to your speed as part of this action.
- Make **four times as many attacks** as you are allowed in a standard Attack Action.
- You are immune to any effect that stops your movement, prevents your attacks, or reduces your damage.

---

### 2. Finest Hour

*When at last you falter, your allies rise to your example.*

**Effect:**
- Allies within 100 feet at 0 HP are healed to 1 HP.
- All allies within 100 feet gain temporary HP equal to **4 × your Fighter level** for 5 minutes.
- Grant your Second Wind feature to all allies in range (using your Fighter level to determine HP regained; benefit lasts 5 minutes).

---

### 3. Warlord's Contingency

*Through the thunder and grit, you manage to shout your last words: "...Now!"*

**Effect:**
- Grant your Action Surge feature to all allies within 100 feet.
- When allies use your Action Surge, they also end one effect on themselves that would normally require a saving throw to end.
- Benefit lasts 5 minutes if unused.

| Last Stand Option | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Die by the Sword** | 4× normal attacks, immune to movement/attack/damage reduction effects. | Dealing massive damage. |
| **Finest Hour** | Down allies healed to 1 HP; all allies gain temp HP + Second Wind. | Reviving the party. |
| **Warlord's Contingency** | Allies gain Action Surge + remove one condition. | Turning the tide through action economy. |

## Class Reactions

Fighters have access to powerful reactions that counter enemy attacks and support allies. These reactions use your **Proficiency Bonus pool**, which refreshes on a short or long rest.

### Defensive Counter (2nd Level)

**Trigger:** A creature makes a melee attack against you.
**Effect:**
- **If they miss:** Make one melee weapon attack. On hit, deal weapon damage + PB + STR or DEX modifier.
- **If they hit:** Reduce damage by PB, then make one melee weapon attack.

---

### Guarded Riposte (3rd Level)

**Trigger:** A creature makes a melee attack against you.
**Effect:**
- **If they miss:** Make one weapon attack with advantage. On hit, add PB + ability modifier to damage. Target has disadvantage on its next attack.
- **If they hit:** Make one weapon attack. On hit, add PB to damage. Target has disadvantage on its next attack.

---

### Tactical Masterstroke (11th Level)

**Trigger:** An ally within 30 feet misses an attack.
**Effect:** Grant them a reroll with advantage. If they still miss, you may immediately make one weapon attack against the same target.
