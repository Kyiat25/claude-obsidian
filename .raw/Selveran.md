## 1. Overview & Founding History

### The Living City

_"Selveran is not built. It grows."_

Selveran stands unique among the Loom-Cities of Dór-o Estel—a civilization that has literally grown upward and downward from the legendary Split Seed of Vaelora. Unlike cities carved from stone or raised through conquest, Selveran is a **living symbiosis** between the ancient Vaeltrun trees and the people who dwell within, upon, and beneath them.

### The Myth of the Split Seed

Before Selveran was sky or soil, it was a place of profound sorrow. During the First Silence—one of the earliest Eldritch darknesses—an entire civilization was consumed, leaving only scarred earth and forgotten names. **Vaelora**, goddess of memory and balance, wept for the lost and planted her final tear-wrapped seed at the heart of the devastation.

This seed grew in both directions simultaneously:

- **Downward**: Its roots spread deep, creating clearings where the Rootbound Settlements would rise
- **Upward**: Its branches stretched toward the heavens, becoming the foundation for the Skywood Bastion

The myth teaches that this dual growth was blessed by **Aelys**, who whispered: _"Let it remember both earth and sky."_

### Historical Timeline

**Age of Planting (Mythic Era)**

- The Split Seed grows into the first Vaeltrun trees
- Early settlements form in the clearings
- The Verdant Spiral awakens as leylines converge

**Age of Harmony (Cycles 1-15)**

- Development of the Living Concord
- First spiral stairs carved into tree trunks
- Establishment of the Council of Threadways

**Age of Shaping (Cycles 16-28)**

- Mastery of ironwood crafting
- Expansion of the Skywood Bastion
- Development of mist-farming techniques

**Imperial Alignment (190 years ago)**

- Voluntary alliance with the Kel Varyan Empire
- Signing of the Oath of the Second Loom
- Current era of protected autonomy

### The Philosophy of Growth

Selverani culture is built on the principle that **growth requires both grounding and reaching**. This philosophy permeates every aspect of society:

- **Law must be rooted in tradition yet flexible enough to adapt**
- **Architecture must serve the tree as much as the inhabitant**
- **Progress means deepening understanding, not abandoning the past**

## 2. Geography & The Dual Structure

### The Vaeltrun Forest

Selveran occupies approximately 2,400 square kilometers of ancient forest, dominated by the colossal **Vaeltrun trees**—living giants that can reach 400 feet in height with trunks so massive they house entire districts. These trees are:

- **Semi-sentient**: They respond to glyph-songs and shaping rituals
- **Magically active**: Their sap conducts Weave energy
- **Incredibly long-lived**: Some date back to the original Split Seed
- **Cooperative**: They willingly allow hollowing and shaping when properly approached

### The Skywood Bastion

**Population**: ~156,000  
**Elevation**: 100-380 feet above ground  
**Structure**: Multi-tiered tree-city connected by aerial bridges

The Skywood Bastion exists at multiple levels within the Vaeltrun canopy:

#### Lower Canopy (100-180 feet)

- **Entry level accessible via spiral stairs**
- Mixed residential and commercial districts
- Primary connection points to Rootbound settlements
- **The Confluence Platform**: Main civic gathering space

#### Mid Canopy (180-280 feet)

- Specialized districts for crafting and education
- **Mistrise Farms**: Hydroponic growing platforms
- **The Threadways**: Major bridge networks
- Administrative buildings of the Council

#### Upper Canopy (280-380 feet)

- Sacred spaces and temples
- Advanced magical research facilities
- **The Windwatch**: Observation and communication towers
- Private residences of scholars and mystics

- **Living construction**: Branches trained to grow together
- **Glyph-lighting**: Illuminated paths for night travel

## 3. The Verdant Spiral World Anchor

### Location and Access

The Verdant Spiral sits at the heart of Selveran, embedded within **Vael'mir**, the First Listening Tree—the largest Vaeltrun in the forest. Unlike hidden World Anchors in other Loom-Cities, the Verdant Spiral is part shrine, part living monument, accessible to citizens through two main paths:

- **The Rootward Stair**: Ascending from Thornwall through ancient stone and living wood
- **The Threadspindle Walk**: Descending from the lower canopy through guided branch-ways

Both paths converge at the **Spiral Confluence**, a circular platform grown into Vael'mir's heart.

### The Living Anchor

The Verdant Spiral differs dramatically from other World Anchors:

#### Physical Structure

- **Glyph-grown ironwood and veinstone** braided into an ascending spiral
- **Self-regenerating**: Grows new rings each elemental cycle
- **The Echoleaf Sigil**: A floating glyph-knot that never touches the spiral but nourishes it
- **Memory chambers**: Hollow spaces that store shaped memories

#### Leyline Convergence

Three major energy flows meet beneath the Spiral:

1. **The Thread of Memory (Spirit)**: Flowing from ancient ruins to the southeast
2. **The Thread of Growth (Earth)**: Running north-south beneath the Rootbound
3. **The Whispering Bough (Air)**: A minor line that weaves between the others

### Functions and Significance

#### Weave Harmonization

- Balances competing Thread energies
- Prevents magical fractures between Skywood and Rootbound
- Maintains forest-wide glyph stability
- Anchors reality during Thread transitions

#### Cultural Center

- **Threadshift ceremonies**: Major civic rituals held here
- **Memory deposits**: Citizens can contribute shaped memories to the city's archive
- **Dispute resolution**: Final arbitration site for complex legal matters
- **Spiritual pilgrimage**: Destination for seekers from across the empire

#### The Knotted Root (Secret Chamber)

Deep beneath the Spiral lies a sealed chamber containing:

- Fragment of Vaelora's original memory
- Dormant Glyph of Union (potentially world-changing Primary Glyph)
- Access restricted to the most trusted Spiral Keepers
- Existence known only to current Threadbearers

The Council of Threadways embodies Selveran's core philosophy of balance through its unique **two-chamber structure** that requires cooperation between the **grounded wisdom of the Rootbound** and the **elevated vision of the Skywood**.

#### Meeting Location: Mir's Crown

- Ring-shaped chamber at the Spiral Confluence
- Half grown from ironwood, half from stone-vein root
- **The Echochime**: Central instrument that only sounds when both chambers speak in unison
- **Observation galleries**: Open to all citizens during public sessions

_"The wisdom of stone, the patience of roots."_

**Structure**:

**Powers**:

- Defense policy and settlement security
- Resource allocation and trade regulation
- Agricultural planning and glyph-harvest permissions
- Traditional law interpretation and precedent-setting

**Cultural Role**: Acts as the **moral memory of Selveran**, slow to change but deep in wisdom

The highest office in Selveran can only be held by **two people working in perfect accord**:

#### The Oathroot (Rootward Representative)

- **Current**: **Aldrich Stonebound** (Male Human, 54)
- Chosen by touching the Verdant Spiral and hearing memory-chimes
- Must maintain connection to traditional wisdom

**Current**: **Threadbearer Kaelen Vorthak** (Male Half-Elf, 67)

- Permanent guest seat in both chambers

### Governmental Processes

#### Daily Operations

- **Alternating sessions**: Chambers meet separately on different days
- **Joint sessions**: Held weekly for matters requiring unified action
- **Public forums**: Monthly open sessions for citizen concerns
- **Emergency protocols**: Rapid response procedures for crises

#### Legislative Process

- Bills must pass both chambers to become law
- **Two seals required**: Mark of the Root and Glyph of the Bough
- **Citizen review**: 30-day period for public comment on major legislation
- **Spiral blessing**: Important laws are formally blessed at the Verdant Spiral

#### Judicial System

- **Writ-Singers**: Neutral arbiters trained in both traditions
- **Trial by balance**: Complex cases heard by both a Rootwarden and Boughsinger
- **Appeals process**: Final appeals heard at the Spiral Confluence
- **Restorative justice**: Focus on healing community harmony over punishment

## 5. Demographics & Cultural Identity

### Population Breakdown

**Total Population**: 387,000

#### Racial Demographics

- **Elves (35% - 135,450)**: Primarily Wood Elves with significant High Elf and Sea Elf minorities
- **Humans (28% - 108,360)**: Mix of imperial citizens and local forest-adapted peoples
- **Gnomes (18% - 69,660)**: Predominantly Forest Gnomes specializing in glyph-craft
- **Halflings (14% - 54,180)**: Strong community presence, especially in agricultural roles
- **Orcs & Half-Orcs (3% - 11,610)**: Respected as Scar-Wardens and deep protectors
- **Dwarves (1.5% - 5,805)**: Mainly engineers and ironwood-shaping consultants
- **Other (0.5% - 1,935)**: Rare dragonborn visitors, drow refugees, and exotic travelers

#### Settlement Distribution

- **Skywood Bastion**: 40% of population (more diverse, younger average age)
- **Rootbound Settlements**: 60% of population (more traditional, established families)

### The Selverani Identity

#### Core Cultural Values

**Balance Above All**

- Extremes in any direction are viewed with suspicion
- Decision-making emphasizes finding the middle path
- Personal growth measured by achieving harmony between opposing forces

**Memory as Morality**

- Forgetting important lessons is considered as dangerous as committing crimes
- Family histories carefully maintained across generations
- Public memory-keepers hold positions of great respect

**Living Harmony**

- All things are believed to have breath and awareness
- Cooperation with natural forces preferred over domination
- Technology and magic developed in partnership with nature

**Consensus Through Spiraling**

- Truth is reached by circling inward through discussion
- Direct confrontation avoided in favor of gradual convergence
- Patience in reaching decisions valued over speed

#### Social Structure

**The Whole Tree Philosophy** Selverani don't see themselves as belonging to distinct classes but as **parts of a living organism**. Social mobility moves both vertically (between Rootbound and Skywood) and horizontally (between specializations).

**Family Structure**

- **Household trees**: Extended families often live in the same tree or settlement block
- **Cross-level families**: Common to have relatives in both Skywood and Rootbound
- **Adoption traditions**: Strong culture of incorporating outsiders into family units
- **Elder councils**: Family decisions made by rotating councils of adults

**Coming of Age**

- **The Split Blessing**: Young adults spend time in both levels before choosing their path
- **Craft adoption**: Teens apprentice across multiple specializations
- **Civic service**: All citizens contribute one year to community projects
- **Name-taking**: Adults choose their own names at age of majority

#### Cultural Practices

**Daily Rhythms**

- **Dawn breathing**: Morning meditation facing the rising sun
- **Midday greeting**: Acknowledge the trees you pass with a touch
- **Evening gratitude**: Share one thing learned with family/housemates
- **Night listening**: Quiet time to hear the whispers of the forest

**Social Customs**

- **Spiral walking**: Important conversations conducted while walking in gentle spirals
- **Gift threading**: Presents include threads from the giver's daily work
- **Memory meals**: Special dinners where stories of ancestors are shared
- **Harmony circles**: Group problem-solving through collaborative discussion

**Conflict Resolution**

- **The Weaving**: Disputes resolved by finding threads of connection between opposing views
- **Tree witness**: Important agreements made in the presence of a Vaeltrun tree
- **Memory binding**: Serious oaths sealed with shaped memories
- **Exile through forgetting**: Worst punishment is to be removed from community memory

### Generational Differences

#### The Deep Rooted (Born before Imperial Alignment)

- More traditional in religious practices
- Skeptical of rapid change
- Strong advocates for Rootbound rights
- Average age: 220+ (elves), 80+ (humans)

#### The Growing Branch (Born during early Imperial era)

- Comfortable with both traditions and innovation
- Form the backbone of current leadership
- Skilled at bridging cultural gaps
- Average age: 100-220 (elves), 40-80 (humans)

#### The New Leaves (Born in recent decades)

- Most comfortable with inter-level mobility
- More accepting of outsiders and new ideas
- Sometimes impatient with traditional deliberation
- Average age: Under 100 (elves), under 40 (humans)

## 6. The Living Architecture & Ironwood Crafting

### Philosophy of Cooperative Construction

_"We do not build upon the world. We ask the world to rise with us."_

Selveran's architecture represents the most advanced cooperation between civilization and nature found anywhere in Dór-o Estel. Every structure is **grown, shaped, and sung into being** through a combination of arbormancy, spirit-song rituals, and careful negotiation with the trees themselves.

### The Vaeltrun Partnership

#### Tree Consciousness Levels

The ancient Vaeltrun trees display varying levels of awareness:

**Awakened Trees** (5% of total)

- Fully conscious and communicative
- Can reshape their own structures intentionally
- Often serve as community centers or temples
- Require formal agreements for any modifications

**Dreaming Trees** (30% of total)

- Semi-conscious, respond to songs and emotional states
- Allow shaping when properly approached with rituals
- May shift their growth patterns based on inhabitant needs
- Show preference for certain types of residents

**Sleeping Trees** (60% of total)

- Unconscious but still responsive to glyph-guidance
- Can be shaped through patient arbormancy
- Provide stable platforms for most construction
- Require offerings and respect but no formal agreements

**Silent Trees** (5% of total)

- Non-responsive, possibly damaged or dying
- Used only for emergency construction
- Carefully monitored for signs of revival
- Often become memorial sites or contemplation spaces

### Ironwood Cultivation and Crafting

#### What is Ironwood?

**Ironwood** is not metal but specially cultivated wood with extraordinary properties:

- **Density**: Rivals steel in strength and durability
- **Flexibility**: Can be bent and shaped when growing, then hardens
- **Glyph-receptive**: Holds magical inscriptions as well as stone
- **Self-repairing**: Slowly heals from damage when properly maintained
- **Resonant**: Conducts sound and minor magical effects

#### The Growing Process

**Seedling Selection** (Year 1)

- Young branches identified through spirit-song divination
- Volunteers must be willing participants in the process
- Initial glyph-marking to guide growth direction
- Bonding ceremony between grower and branch

**Shaping Phase** (Years 2-7)

- Daily songs and glyph-applications guide growth
- Careful bending and training into desired forms
- Regular offerings of enriched soil and blessed water
- Gradual strengthening through elemental exposure

**Hardening Ritual** (Year 8)

- Final ceremony to lock the wood's shape
- All five Threads woven into the wood's fiber
- Community witness to validate the completion
- Naming ceremony for significant pieces

**Harvesting and Crafting** (Years 9+)

- Cut only with blessed tools during proper moon phases
- Shaped through traditional woodworking enhanced with glyphs
- Final finishing includes protective ward-singing
- Registration with the Arborwrights' Guild

#### Common Ironwood Applications

**Structural Elements**

- Bridge supports and decking
- Building frameworks and load-bearing beams
- Stairway construction and railings
- Foundation anchors and pylons

**Furniture and Tools**

- Multi-generational furniture pieces
- Specialized crafting tools and instruments
- Weapons and armor (when necessary)
- Artistic sculptures and decorative elements

**Sacred Objects**

- Temple fixtures and altars
- Ceremonial instruments and tools
- Memory storage vessels and archives
- Diplomatic gifts and treaty markers

### Metal Use and the Sacred Forge

#### The Scarcity Principle

True metal is rare in Selveran due to both philosophy and practicality:

- **Earth-reverence**: Mining seen as wounding the world
- **Tree-protection**: Forging heat must not endanger living wood
- **Harmony-maintenance**: Metal's rigid nature conflicts with organic architecture
- **Resource allocation**: Limited mining rights carefully regulated

#### Permitted Metal Applications

- **Bridge hinges and mechanisms**: Where flexibility is crucial
- **Ceremonial weapons**: For formal occasions and symbolic purposes
- **Glyph-cores**: Heart mechanisms for complex magical devices
- **Trade goods**: Items created specifically for export

#### The Sacred Forges of Redholt

**Location and Design**

- Built into natural stone formations separate from tree settlements
- **Hearthfire Chambers**: Special basalt growths that contain controlled heat
- **Wind-channeling**: Natural draft systems prevent smoke damage to trees
- **Water-cooling**: Blessed springs for rapid metal cooling

**The Fireless Plea** Before any forge is lit, smiths recite the Fireless Plea: _"Fire that warms, fire that shapes, fire that serves the greater growth,  
Be bound to stone, be bound to purpose, harm no root, scar no bark,  
We light only what we feed, we burn only what we offer,  
Trees need fire, but not cruelty."_

**Cast Iron Specialization**

- Most metalwork uses **cast iron** for its compatibility with sacred flame
- **Elaborate decorative work**: Compensation for material limitations through artistic excellence
- **Ritual cooling**: All metal cooled in blessed waters
- **Community blessing**: Major works blessed by both chambers of the Council

### Building Regulations and Traditions

#### The Arborwrights' Code

Inscribed on a living tablet grown into Thornwall's foundation, the Code governs all construction:

**Fundamental Prohibitions**

- No cutting of unwilling trees
- No blood sacrifice in shaping rituals
- No forging near root-wells or ley-springs
- No flame touching living bark

**Required Procedures**

- Tree consent through glyph-ripple communication
- Community approval for major projects
- Offering ceremonies before ground-breaking
- Completion blessings from both Chamber representatives

**Maintenance Responsibilities**

- Regular health checks for supporting trees
- Glyph renewal ceremonies each Thread-cycle
- Damage repair using approved methods only
- Expansion requests through proper channels

#### Sacred Construction Seasons

**Spring Growing** (Months 1-3)

- New projects begun during peak growth season
- Major tree-shaping work performed
- Seedling selection and bonding ceremonies
- Foundation preparations for Rootbound projects

**Summer Strengthening** (Months 4-6)

- Ironwood hardening rituals completed
- Metal-work performed during stable weather
- Bridge construction and repair projects
- Major architectural completions

**Autumn Harvesting** (Months 7-9)

- Finished ironwood pieces harvested
- Decorative work and artistic elements added
- Interior finishing and glyph-inscription
- Blessing ceremonies for completed projects

**Winter Resting** (Months 10-12)

- Planning and design work for future projects
- Tool maintenance and craft skill development
- Emergency repairs only
- Contemplation of architectural harmony

## 7. Religion & The Festival of Braided Flame

### The Multi-Faith Harmony

Selveran stands unique among Loom-Cities for its **religious pluralism within spiritual unity**. While the **Faith of the Bound Pattern** provides the overarching framework, each racial and cultural group maintains their traditional practices, creating a rich tapestry of belief that strengthens rather than divides the community.

### The Faith of the Bound Pattern in Selveran

#### Selverani Interpretation

Unlike the formal, institutional approach found in Kel Varyan, Selveran practices the Faith as:

- **Intimate and interpretive**: Personal connection emphasized over doctrine
- **Braided with tradition**: Local customs woven into standard practices
- **Community-centered**: Collective interpretation of sacred texts
- **Naturally harmonized**: Aligned with the rhythms of forest and tree

#### Key Tenets in Practice

- **Thread Balance**: Maintaining harmony between Spirit, Earth, and Air
- **Cycle Respect**: Following natural and magical rhythms
- **Memory Preservation**: Honoring past while growing toward future
- **Living Unity**: Seeing all existence as interconnected

#### Sacred Sites

- **The Verdant Spiral**: Primary anchor and pilgrimage destination
- **Branch Sanctuaries**: Small temples grown within awakened trees
- **Confluence Altars**: Worship spaces where different paths meet
- **Memorial Groves**: Sites honoring significant community memories

### Racial Religious Traditions

#### Elven Practices: The Woven Reverence

**Population**: ~135,450 practitioners

**Core Beliefs**

- Ancestral memory flows through living trees
- Seasonal cycles reflect spiritual truths
- Dreams connect past, present, and future
- Song and story preserve essential wisdom

**Sacred Practices**

- **Leaf-binding**: Prayers written on leaves and hung in sacred trees
- **Ancestral dream-rites**: Ceremonial sleeping to contact spirits
- **Seasonal remembrance chants**: Community songs marking natural cycles
- **Echo-dancing**: Movement rituals that honor departed elders

**Sacred Spaces**

- **Canopy Sanctums**: High-altitude temples in ancient tree crowns
- **Memory Pools**: Still water sites for reflection and spirit-contact
- **Song Circles**: Natural amphitheaters with perfect acoustics
- **Elder Trees**: Specific Vaeltrun designated as ancestors' homes

#### Human Traditions: The Hearth Faiths

**Population**: ~108,360 practitioners (diverse)

**Family Cults and Ancestor Rites**

- **Household Pantheons**: Gods chosen from various elemental pantheons
- **Ancestor Shrines**: Private worship spaces honoring family dead
- **Hearthfire Ceremonies**: Domestic rituals around cooking fires
- **Naming Traditions**: Children blessed by family patron deities

**Integration Practices**

- **Braided Cords**: Family god-names woven with Thread-symbols
- **Private Threads**: Personal spiritual practices within public harmony
- **Civic Service**: Religious duties expressed through community work

#### Gnomish Beliefs: The Borrowed Lights

## Conclusion: The Living City

Selveran stands as a **testament to the possibility of harmonious civilization**—a community that has grown rather than conquered, cooperated rather than dominated, and flourished by **embracing rather than rejecting** the **complexity and diversity** of the world around it.

From its **dual structure** of **Skywood Bastion and Rootbound Settlements** to its **revolutionary approach to religious pluralism**, from its **innovative agricultural techniques** to its **cooperative political system**, Selveran demonstrates that **civilization and nature**, **tradition and innovation**, **individual development and community welfare** need not be in conflict.

The city's **greatest achievement** may be its **Festival of Braided Flame**—a celebration that **honors every faith, every tradition, and every personal belief** within a **single magnificent ceremony**. This festival embodies Selveran's core truth: **strength comes not from uniformity, but from diversity woven into unity**.

Yet Selveran is not utopia. It faces **real challenges**: **generational tensions**, **environmental pressures**, **political complexities**, and **the constant work** of maintaining **harmony without stagnation**, **progress without destruction**. These challenges make it a **living, breathing community** rather than a static ideal.

For adventurers, scholars, diplomats, and seekers, Selveran offers **countless opportunities**: to **learn innovative techniques**, to **experience cultural harmony**, to **participate in environmental stewardship**, to **witness the daily miracle** of **cooperative civilization**.

Most importantly, Selveran serves as **proof that another way is possible**—that communities can grow **upward and downward**, **outward and inward**, **into the future and into the past** simultaneously, creating something **entirely new** while **honoring everything that came before**.

In Selveran, **every thread matters**, **every voice is heard**, and **every citizen participates** in the **ongoing song of community life**. It is not just a city—it is a **living demonstration** of **humanity's highest aspirations** made **real in wood and stone**, **law and custom**, **memory and hope**.

_"We are not two cities. We are one soul, grown in both directions."_
