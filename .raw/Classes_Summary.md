# Classes in the World

There are **24 playable classes** in FEARS, evenly split between the **Core 12** (D&D 5E chassis classes, rebuilt on FEARS mechanics) and the **FEARS-Original 12** (built from scratch for this system).

> **Note:** This replaces an earlier version of this summary, which was generated from an incomplete vault snapshot. It undercounted classes, omitted several entirely (Cleric, Druid, Bard, Apothecary, Bender, Reaper, Warden, and the Summoner/Binder replacement), and included one class — Thaumaturge — that has since been cut from the roster.

## Full Casters (9-Level or Full Spell-List Systems)
1. **Wizard** — Masters of Arcane Knowledge; rigid study and intellectual pursuit of magic
2. **Sorcerer** — Innate magic, raw and untamed; instinctive magical power
3. **Warlock** — Conduits of Forbidden Power; bound by eldritch pacts to otherworldly beings
4. **Cleric** — Faith Against the Void; divine power and support anchor
5. **Druid** — Voice of the World; shapeshifting nature caster
6. **Bard** — Reality's Storyteller; probability and outcome manipulation
7. **Witch** — Hexes and Primal Magic; curses that escalate over time
8. **Sangromancer** — Masters of Blood and Bone; casts by spending HP instead of spell slots (Sanguine Casting), but draws from a full spell list
9. **Shaman** — Spirit Mediator; Warlock-style pact slots powering totem and spirit magic
10. **Bender** — Element Made Motion; full spell-slot progression expressed through elemental combo sequencing rather than traditional casting

## Half-Casters (Martial + Magic Hybrids)
11. **Paladin** — Oath-driven warriors of smite; divine champions
12. **Ranger** — Track, hunt, and protect; martial hunters with primal magic
13. **Magus** — Blade and spell converge into a single action (Spellstrike)
14. **Inventor** — Masters of Magical Engineering; half-caster spell slots plus Specialisation Upgrades
15. **Tattooist** — Artists of Living Ink; half-caster whose Spellbanger magic is entirely separate from standard spellcasting
16. **Warden** — Nature's Front Line; single-level spell slots layered onto a retaliatory elemental tank

## Martial Classes (No Spellcasting)
17. **Barbarian** — Fury Made Flesh; grows stronger the more it's hurt
18. **Fighter** — Master of the Battlefield; highest decision density of any class
19. **Monk** — Perfect Motion; Ki-fueled mobility and disruption
20. **Rogue** — Precision and Opportunity; converts advantage into burst damage
21. **Reaper** — Harvester of the End; gains power from Soul Tokens generated by kills and morale breaks
22. **Investigator** — Truth is a Weapon; turns research and identified weaknesses into precision damage

## Specialized Classes (Non-Traditional Resource Systems)
23. **Apothecary** — Science of Survival; Reagent-driven Concoctions replace magic with predictable chemistry
24. **Binder** — Vessel of the Unfinished Dead; hosts fragments of the dead directly rather than summoning a separate creature, fueled by Bleed Points (replaces the retired Summoner)

## Summary Count
- **Full Casters:** 10 classes
- **Half-Casters:** 6 classes
- **Martial (Non-Caster):** 6 classes
- **Specialized:** 2 classes
- **Total: 24 classes**

## Key System Notes
All classes utilize:
- **Class Reactions** — Powered by a shared Proficiency Bonus pool that refreshes on short/long rest
- **Momentum System** — A dynamic combat mechanic representing advantage/rhythm
- **Dynamic Combat System (DCS)** — Includes Evasion, Damage Reduction, called shots, location targeting, and injury mechanics
- **Hit Dice** — Ranging from d6 (light casters) to d10 (heavy martial), d8 (hybrids)

## Notable Features by Class Type
- **Full Casters** use spell slots, prepared/known spells, or a fully self-contained alternate resource that stands in for slots (Sangromancer's Sanguine Casting, Bender's combo-triggered casting)
- **Half-Casters** blend partial spell-slot progression with martial or engineering features
- **Martial Classes** rely entirely on Actions, Bonus Actions, and Reactions with weapon proficiencies and class-specific resources (Soul Tokens, research/vulnerability tracking)
- **Specialized Classes** run on bespoke resource systems that intentionally sit outside the spell-slot framework entirely

**Unique class-specific resources at a glance:**
- Reaper: **Soul Tokens**
- Inventor: **Upgrades**
- Sangromancer: **Vitae (HP-as-resource)**
- Apothecary: **Reagents**
- Binder: **Bleed Points** (retires the former Summoner's Binding Points)
- Bender: **Combo sequencing** on top of standard spell slots

## Removed / Retired Classes (Do Not Reference)
The following were cut from the roster during development and should not appear in new content: **Shadowshaper, Runesmith, Psion, Tamer, Thaumaturge, Shadowbinder.** The Summoner has been replaced outright by the **Binder** (see above) rather than retired without a successor.
