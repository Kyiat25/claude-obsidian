# Tiefling
## Lineage

> *"We are not curses. We are the correction. Fire that walked out of the gods' shadow and dared to ask: 'What if they meant more than this?'"*  
> — **Sareth-Ka**, First Ashbinder

You are Tiefling. Not damned, not mistake—**untranslated**. Your people do not kneel before judgment but stand as proof that divine messages can be misread. Where others see curse, you see **incomplete divinity waiting for meaning**. Where they see infernal taint, you see the space where gods stuttered and created something they didn't intend—something that refuses to be erased.

You were not born carrying sin. You were born carrying **possibility**—to fall, to rise, to choose. Your ancestors survived not through apology but through **claiming their own meaning**, demonstrating that what is written in blood can be rewritten through action. Now you carry that legacy in your horns, your eyes, your certainty that **you define what you are, not what you were meant to be**.

---

## The Glyph That Broke the Sky

According to the **Ashen Testament**, tieflings were not created by devils or demons—they were born from **divine accident**. When the gods cast their voices across the Weave to shape reality, one glyph was left unfinished. An Eldritch scream shattered the breath. The glyph split across the sky like lightning made of sorrow.

Where it fell, it struck a dying child without name or family. She burned but did not die. She rose in ash, her body carved with the half-spoken glyph smoking from her skin. She named herself **Sareth-Ka**—"the scar the gods could not finish."

Tieflings are **untranslated glyphs**—divine messages broken in transmission, fragments of meaning that gods couldn't complete or control. Your horns, your eyes, your unnatural coloring—these aren't marks of damnation. They're **proof that even gods make errors**, and those errors can walk, choose, and burn brighter than intended creations.

The gods tried to speak you. They failed. So you learned to speak for yourself.

## Life Among the Flames

You were raised knowing that **judgment is mortal projection**, that every assumption about you is someone else's failure to understand, that your worth is measured not in what you were born with but in **what you choose to do with the power revelation brings**. Tiefling culture treats existence as defiant testimony, every breath a refusal to apologize.

Your lifespan is roughly **100-120 years**—longer than humans but shorter than elves. You measure time not in years but in **choices made and meanings claimed**, measuring worth not in age but in **how you transformed possibility into purpose**.

Every significant action is recorded in **Sin-Writs**—carved symbols on armor, skin, horns, or soul-bound relics. These aren't confessions. They're **testaments of legacy**, proof that you earned your power through action, not inheritance.

**Fire** is sacred among your people. Not destruction, but **revelation**—the flame that shows what was hidden, that transforms ash into proof something mattered, that burns away false judgment to reveal truth beneath.

## Physical Characteristics

**Age:** You mature at the same rate as humans, reaching adulthood by age 18. Most tieflings live to see 100, though some blessed or cursed by their nature see 120 or more.

**Size:** You stand between 5 to 6½ feet tall, with builds ranging from lithe to powerfully built. Your size is Medium.

**Speed:** Your base walking speed is 30 feet.

**Build:** Variable but distinctive. Your skin ranges from normal human tones to red, purple, blue, or even ashen gray. You have horns (shapes vary wildly: ram-like, swept back, curling, crystalline), a tail (prehensile or not, thick or whip-thin), and eyes without pupils—solid colors like gold, silver, red, or black. Your body carries the mark of broken divinity in every line.

## Tiefling Traits

As a Tiefling, you possess the following lineage traits:

### **Darkvision**
You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

Your eyes see clearly where gods cast no light—fitting for those born from what divinity forgot.

### **Hellish Resistance**
You have resistance to fire damage.

This isn't infernal heritage—it's the echo of Sareth-Ka's first burning. You carry flame's memory in your blood, and it recognizes you as kin rather than fuel.

### **Untranslated Glyph**
You know the *Thaumaturgy* cantrip. When you cast it, the effects always carry hints of flame—smoke curls, embers spark, shadows flicker with orange light, or heat shimmers in the air.

This represents the broken glyph carved into your existence—fragments of divine language trying to express themselves through you.

### **Defiant Legacy**
You have proficiency in one skill of your choice. Additionally, when you fail a saving throw against being frightened or charmed, you can use your reaction to reroll it, taking the second result.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, regaining all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

This is your defining trait—**you refuse to be what others expect**. Fear and charm are just more attempts to define you against your will.

> *"They told me I was cursed to betray them. They told me my nature would compel me to evil. So I saved them all, burned myself doing it, and told them: 'You don't know what my nature is. Neither do I. But I get to find out.'"*

### **Infernal Legacy**
You have an innate connection to magic that manifests differently based on your subrace. This isn't learned—it's the broken glyph trying to complete itself through you.

Your spellcasting ability for these spells is Charisma.

### **Ash-Marked**
Once per long rest, when you take fire damage, you can choose to absorb it instead. You take no damage from that source and gain temporary hit points equal to half the damage you would have taken. These temporary hit points last for 1 hour.

When you use this ability, ash briefly swirls around you and your eyes glow like embers.

## Mental Attributes & Psychology

**Focus Modifier:** +0  
**Sanity Modifier:** +0

Your mental baseline is human-standard, but your psychology is shaped by constant judgment and the need to define yourself against others' expectations.

### **Cultural Mental Conditioning**
Beyond your biology, Tiefling upbringing creates:

- **Defiant identity:** You reject imposed definitions reflexively
- **Moral flexibility:** You question absolute rules and divine certainty
- **Burnout resistance:** You're used to being tested; pressure is familiar
- **Power awareness:** You understand that how you use power reveals truth

### **Corruption Vulnerability: The Proving Wrong**

As a Tiefling, you have a unique relationship with Corruption. Your ability to resist false judgment provides some protection, but corruption that enters through **proving others right about you, accepting imposed definitions, or surrendering your agency to power** affects you catastrophically.

**When you gain Corruption Score 10+ (Tier 2), you suffer:**
- Definition-Loss: You struggle to remember who you chose to be
- Judgment-Weight: Others' expectations feel heavier, harder to resist
- Power-Hunger: Using power becomes addictive rather than revealing

**Broken Testament Table (Corruption Tier 2+)**

When you complete a long rest at Corruption Score 10+, roll 1d6:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **False Destiny:** You remember being told you'd become something terrible—and start believing it. Make a DC 15 Wisdom save or act on this belief unconsciously. |
| 2 | **Ash-Blindness:** Your fire no longer reveals truth. You cannot use Ash-Marked until your next long rest. |
| 3 | **Glyph Fracture:** The broken glyph stutters. Take 1d4 psychic damage and lose proficiency in one skill until your next long rest. |
| 4 | **Weight of Prophecy:** You feel crushing certainty that others were right about you. Make a DC 12 Sanity save or lose 1d4 Sanity. |
| 5 | **Eldritch Whisper:** Something offers to complete your glyph—at a price. Gain 1 Whisper Point but your next use of Defiant Legacy fails automatically. |
| 6 | **Testament Holds:** Your chosen meaning resists corruption this night. Reduce your Corruption Score by 1 (minimum 10). |

## Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Integration

### **Evasion Profile: Adaptive Survivor**
- **Dodge Evasion:** +1 (you've learned to not be where attacks expect)
- **Endure Evasion:** +0 (standard resilience)
- **Deflect Evasion:** +0 (you don't rely on deflection)

**Tactical Note:** You are designed for **flexible combat with reactive abilities**. Defiant Legacy provides re-rolls against mental effects, while Ash-Marked gives you occasional burst healing from fire sources. You adapt rather than specialize.

### **Natural Damage Reduction:** +0
You have no natural armor. Your protection comes from **intelligence, adaptation, and not accepting what seems inevitable** rather than physical toughness.

### **Combat Philosophy**
Tieflings fight with **creative adaptation and refusal to accept defeat**. You leverage Defiant Legacy to resist mental control, use Infernal Legacy for tactical magic, and rely on Hellish Resistance and Ash-Marked to turn fire against enemies. You fight like you live: **refusing to be defined by others' expectations**.

### **Breaking Point Mechanics**
Due to your constant experience with judgment and your cultural emphasis on self-definition, you have **advantage on Rally checks** when fighting against those who judged or persecuted you, but **standard checks otherwise**. Your defiance is sharpest when proven right.

## Languages

**Languages:** You can speak, read, and write Common and either Infernal or Abyssal (your choice).

**Infernal** is the language of law, contracts, and hierarchies—harsh consonants and precise grammar where even tone carries legal weight. Many tieflings learn it not because of demonic ancestry but because understanding the language of binding helps you avoid being bound.

**Abyssal** is the language of chaos, desire, and transformation—fluid sounds that shift meaning with context. Some tieflings prefer it because it resists being pinned down.

When Tieflings speak of sacred matters, many use **Ash-Tongue**—a dialect mixing Common, Infernal, and original words from Sareth-Ka's first testimony, designed for speaking truths that resist simple translation.

## Tiefling Subraces

While all Tieflings share the broken glyph, different communities have manifested different aspects of that fracture over generations. Choose one of the following subraces:

### **Ashbound Lineage** *(Testament-Keepers)*
You descend from tieflings who embraced the **Ashen Testament** most fully, who carry names of the forgotten and tend flames that refuse to die.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Charisma score increases by 2, and your Wisdom score increases by 1.

**Infernal Legacy (Ashbound):** 
- You know the *Produce Flame* cantrip
- When you reach 3rd level, you can cast *Hellish Rebuke* once per long rest as a 2nd-level spell
- When you reach 5th level, you can cast *Flame Blade* once per long rest

**Ash-Bearer:** You have proficiency in Insight or Religion (your choice). Additionally, when you witness someone die, you can perform a 1-minute ritual to preserve their name. You can recall these names perfectly and know one significant fact about each person whose name you carry.

**Sareth-Ka's Legacy:** Once per long rest, when you would take fire damage from an enemy's attack or spell, you can use your reaction to redirect that fire to a different creature within 30 feet (using the original attack roll or save DC). This is **not** revenge—it's correction.

### **Glyphscar Lineage** *(The Rewriting)*
You descend from tieflings who treat their broken glyph as **incomplete magic waiting to be mastered**, who seek to understand what the gods tried to say.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Charisma score increases by 2, and your Intelligence score increases by 1.

**Infernal Legacy (Glyphscar):**
- You know the *Mage Hand* cantrip
- When you reach 3rd level, you can cast *Comprehend Languages* once per long rest
- When you reach 5th level, you can cast *Suggestion* once per long rest

**Glyph-Reader:** You have proficiency in Arcana. Additionally, when you examine magical writing, glyphs, or spell effects, you can make an Intelligence (Arcana) check (DC 10 + spell level) to understand the spell's basic intent—even if you can't read the language or decipher the specific mechanics.

**Incomplete Incantation:** Once per short rest, when you see a spell being cast within 60 feet, you can use your reaction to attempt to "complete" it differently. Make a Charisma check contested by the caster's spellcasting ability check. On a success, you can change one aspect of the spell:
- Change damage type to fire
- Add or remove one target within range
- Increase or decrease range by 10 feet
- Force the caster to roll at disadvantage on spell attack rolls or reduce spell save DC by 2

### **Voidmarked Lineage** *(Edge-Walkers)*
You descend from tieflings touched by what the gods **truly** forgot—the spaces between Pattern and void, the silence where Eldritch Moons whisper.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Charisma score increases by 2, and your Constitution score increases by 1.

**Infernal Legacy (Voidmarked):**
- You know the *Minor Illusion* cantrip
- When you reach 3rd level, you can cast *Arms of Hadar* once per long rest
- When you reach 5th level, you can cast *Darkness* once per long rest

**Void-Sense:** You have advantage on Perception checks to notice hidden creatures, illusions, or anything trying to remain unseen. Additionally, you can sense when reality is "thin"—when Eldritch influence is near, you feel a cold pressure behind your eyes (GM discretion).

**Between Spaces:** Once per long rest, when you would be hit by an attack, you can use your reaction to briefly slip into the space between reality and void. The attack automatically misses, and you can move up to 10 feet without provoking opportunity attacks. You reappear wreathed in shadow-smoke. This is unsettling to witness.

**Corruption Note:** Voidmarked tieflings start with **Corruption Score 1** due to their connection to what gods forget. However, they have advantage on saves against gaining Corruption from **Eldritch sources**—they're already marked, making them partially immune to further marking.

### **Emberkin Lineage** *(The Integrated)*
You descend from tieflings who walked among other races long enough that your broken glyph began **trying to heal**, who proved that meaning can be found through connection rather than defiance alone.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Charisma score increases by 2, and your Dexterity score increases by 1.

**Infernal Legacy (Emberkin):**
- You know the *Friends* cantrip (but it doesn't cause hostile feelings afterward when used on sentient creatures—it's genuine, if fleeting, warmth)
- When you reach 3rd level, you can cast *Charm Person* once per long rest
- When you reach 5th level, you can cast *Lesser Restoration* once per long rest

**Bridge-Walker:** You have proficiency in Persuasion or Performance (your choice). Additionally, when you interact with someone who initially judges you negatively due to your tiefling nature, you have advantage on the first Charisma check you make to change their mind.

**Ember's Warmth:** Once per short rest, you can touch a creature and share your inner fire. They gain resistance to cold damage for 1 hour and advantage on the next saving throw they make against being frightened. When you use this ability, warmth visibly radiates from your touch, and your horns glow faintly.

## Naming Conventions

Tiefling names are **chosen, not given**—or if given, often **reclaimed or transformed**. Your name tells your story to those who understand the language of self-definition.

### **Structure:**
- **Birth Name:** The name you were given (often from your non-tiefling parent's culture)
- **Chosen Name:** A name you took upon claiming your identity
- **Testament Name:** A name earned through significant acts (optional)
- **Virtue Name:** Abstract concepts some tieflings adopt as identity markers

### **Examples:**

**Human-Style Birth Names:**

*Male-pattern:* Akmenos, Amnon, Barakas, Damakos, Ekemon, Iados, Kairon, Leucis, Melech, Mordai, Morthos, Pelaios, Skamos, Therai

*Female-pattern:* Akta, Anakis, Bryseis, Criella, Damaia, Ea, Kallista, Lerissa, Makaria, Nemeia, Orianna, Phelaia, Rieta

**Chosen Names (Self-Claimed):**

*Defiant:* Ashborn, Glyphbreaker, Flamewrit, Unbound, Rewritten, Defiance, Justice

*Transformative:* Phoenix, Cinder, Ember, Forge, Testament, Revelation

*Reclaimed:* Hope, Grace, Faith, Mercy, Truth, Art, Music

**Virtue Names (Concept-Claimed):**

Abstract concepts adopted as primary identity:
- Art (as in "the art of proving them wrong")
- Doubt (as in "healthy skepticism")
- Excellence (self-explanatory)
- Freedom (from imposed definitions)
- Justice (earned, not given)
- Mercy (chosen, not inherited)
- Poetry (meaning made beautiful)
- Sorrow (carried with dignity)
- Tempest (uncontrollable force)
- Vengeance (for what was assumed)

**Full Name Examples:**
- **Damaia "Ashborn" Grimwald** (kept birth name + earned chosen name + family name)
- **Art** (single virtue name, nothing else needed)
- **Kallista Testament** (birth name + significant act)
- **Ekemon called Phoenix, son of nothing** (birth name + chosen name + rejection of ancestry)

### **Name Practices:**
- Many tieflings go through multiple names as they discover who they are
- Virtue names are never used ironically—if you claim "Mercy," you better embody it
- Some tieflings keep their birth names as reminder of where they started
- Others burn their birth names in Ashen Flame ceremonies
- Testament names are witnessed by Ashbinders or community

## Cultural Touchstones

### **The Ashen Testament**
Many (though not all) Tieflings are raised within this framework:

- Sin is earned, not given—blood carries potential, not guilt
- The divine message is misread—you're untranslated, not damned
- Power reveals meaning—how you use it shows truth
- Ash carries truth—memory through transformation
- We walk without permission—legends are claimed, not granted

### **The Five Burnings**
These foundational truths guide Tiefling life:

1. **Burn Away False Judgment** - Refuse imposed definitions
2. **Burn Bright With Choice** - Let actions speak truth
3. **Burn Memory Into Ash** - Transform suffering into meaning
4. **Burn Your Own Path** - Walk without seeking permission
5. **Burn Until You Matter** - Exist loudly enough to be remembered

### **Spiritual Roles**
Tiefling spiritual leaders are witnesses and carriers:

- **Ashbinders:** Clerics who carry names of the forgotten dead
- **Sin-Scribes:** Those who record Testament writings
- **Ash-Walkers:** Wandering priests who tend forgotten pyres
- **Flame-Speakers:** Those who interpret what fire reveals

### **Rituals**
- **Sin-Writs:** Marking significant acts on body or belongings
- **Ash-Vigils:** Silent meditation beside flame
- **Scars of Choice:** Ritual acceptance of defining moments
- **Ashen Flame:** Communal burning of false judgments
- **Sin-Testament:** Once-in-lifetime record of all actions, read after death

You are the undefined soul, the misunderstood message, the living proof that potential transcends origin. You don't survive through fitting in or apologizing—you **burn away assumptions and claim meaning through action**, showing the world that what they fear is their failure to understand, not your nature.

### **Mechanical Identity:**
- **Flexible Caster:** Infernal Legacy provides utility magic at no resource cost

### **Common Character Arcs:**

## Design Philosophy

The Tiefling lineage is designed to support:

1. **The Defiant Hero** (resists imposed definitions, claims meaning through action)
2. **The Fire-Touched** (Hellish Resistance + Ash-Marked + fire magic options)
3. **The Social Wild Card** (Charisma focus + versatile skills)
4. **The Testament-Bearer** (rich theological framework for RP)
5. **The Adaptive Survivor** (reactive abilities + flexible magic)

Your mechanical abilities (Defiant Legacy, Hellish Resistance, Infernal Legacy, Ash-Marked) create a **flexible character who resists being pinned down**—just like you resist being defined by expectations. Unlike races with clear combat roles, you **adapt to situations and choose your own path** through each encounter.

The subrace options allow you to lean into different aspects of being untranslated—from ash-bearing remembrance (Ashbound) to seeking magical understanding (Glyphscar) to walking edges of reality (Voidmarked) to finding meaning through connection (Emberkin)—while maintaining the core Tiefling identity of self-definition through choice.

## Final Notes

### **The Weight of Potential:**
Every Tiefling carries the burden of possibility. Your character likely knows:
- Multiple stories of tieflings who became heroes or villains
- The feeling of being watched for signs you'll fulfill prophecy
- At least one Testament passage by heart
- The moment you chose who you'd be rather than accepting definition

This isn't burden—it's **freedom**. You define yourself through action.

### **Fear Across Cultures:**
Modern Dór-o Estel judges tieflings reflexively:
- Many assume infernal heritage means evil nature
- The Burning Concord brands tieflings as heretics
- Drow hunt Testament speakers
- Children are told tiefling stories to frighten them

But some recognize deeper truth:
- Spirit Threadbearers quietly respect Testament philosophy
- Goliaths acknowledge parallel focus on earned worth
- Halflings call tieflings "fire-hearted friends"
- Those who've witnessed tiefling heroes know assumptions are wrong

### **The Fire That Defines:**
Your people believe fire reveals truth—what burns away is false, what remains is real. You are that fire made flesh. Others fear what you might burn away about their certainties.

You adventure knowing this truth. Some burn to prove assumptions wrong. Some burn to understand what they are. Some burn to find others who understand.

But all tieflings carry the same certainty: **We are not your lesson. We are not your warning. We are fire that walked out of the gods' shadow and dared to exist on our own terms.**
