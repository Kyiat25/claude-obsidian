# Where Every Strike Tells a Story - The Dynamic Combat System - Version 2.0

_A Complete Guide for Players and Dungeon Masters_

---

## Preface: Combat Reimagined

_The blade whistles through the air towards Captain Thorne's exposed shoulder. In the old world, this would be simple—roll to hit, roll damage, move on. But here in the crucible of dynamic combat, every heartbeat offers choices. Will she twist away, gambling on her agility? Will she absorb the blow on her shield to strike back? Will she target her enemy's weapon arm to end this quickly? The dance of steel has never been so alive..._

The **Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Version 2.0** transforms D&D 5e combat from a numerical exchange into a living, breathing tactical experience. Gone are the days when Armour Class determined everything and missing an attack meant nothing happened. In DCS, every swing matters, every dodge counts, and every choice reshapes the battlefield.

This version addresses the complexity concerns of the original whilst maintaining the tactical depth that makes combat truly engaging. We've streamlined bookkeeping, made precision strikes genuinely appealing, and created clear pathways for both new and experienced players to master the system.

# Part I: The Player's Guide to Dynamic Combat

## Chapter 1: Core Principles - What's Changed and Why

### The Three Pillars of Dynamic Combat

**Pillar 1: Active Defence Through Evasion** _Defence is a choice, not a passive number._

Instead of static Armour Class, you actively roll to avoid attacks through **Evasion**—a dynamic system that puts agency in your hands. Every incoming attack becomes a moment of tension and meaningful decision-making.

**Pillar 2: Simplified Armour Protection**  
_Armour doesn't make you harder to hit—it makes you harder to kill._

Armour provides **Damage Reduction (DR)** that works in two ways: full protection for standard attacks, or location-specific protection for precision strikes. This creates a meaningful tactical choice without complex bookkeeping.

**Pillar 3: Combat Rhythm Through Momentum** _Combat has rhythm—ride it or be swept away._

**Momentum** captures the ebb and flow of battle advantage, creating natural story beats where success breeds success and tactical choices determine who controls the fight's rhythm.

### What Version 2.0 Fixed

**Simplified Bookkeeping**: One DR number for standard attacks, detailed only for precision strikes.

**Called Shots Actually Work**: Precision targeting now offers genuine benefits that outweigh the accuracy penalties.

**Clearer Learning Path**: New players can start with basic evasion and DR, adding complexity gradually.

**Maintained Tactical Depth**: All the strategic options remain, but they're more accessible and balanced.

## Chapter 2: Active Defence - The Evasion System

### 2.1 From Passive AC to Active Choice

In traditional D&D 5e, you wait passively to see if attacks hit your Armour Class. The Evasion system gives you agency in every defensive moment.

#### The Evasion Roll

When attacked, you choose how to defend:

```
Evasion Check = 1d20 + Defence Modifier + Proficiency Bonus (if applicable)
```

**Your Defence Options:**

**Dodge (Dexterity-based)**

- Agile avoidance through speed and reflexes
- Add proficiency if you have light armour, shield, or unarmoured defence training
- Philosophy: "I'll slip away from the blow"

**Brace (Strength-based)**

- Absorbing impacts through positioning and armour
- Add proficiency if you have heavy armour or shield training
- Philosophy: "I'll weather this storm"

**Take Hit (Automatic failure)**

- Deliberately fail to gain a free reaction opportunity
- Risk/reward: guaranteed damage for tactical advantage
- Philosophy: "I'll trade blow for blow"

### 2.2 Defence Outcomes

The interaction between attack rolls and your evasion creates five dramatic possibilities:

#### Perfect Defence (You roll Natural 20)

- **Effect**: Complete avoidance + immediate counterattack opportunity
- **Feel**: Time slows as you read the attack perfectly
- **Benefit**: No damage taken, gain one free attack as a reaction

#### Success (Your Evasion beats their Attack)

- **Effect**: Clean defence, no damage
- **Feel**: Skilled evasion or solid defence
- **Benefit**: No damage, gain momentum, special armour bonuses may apply

#### Glancing Blow (Your Evasion equals their Attack)

- **Effect**: Partial success—damage halved before DR application
- **Feel**: Almost dodged completely, but caught you slightly
- **Benefit**: Significantly reduced damage, momentum effects vary by armour

#### Full Hit (Your Evasion loses to their Attack)

- **Effect**: Attack lands normally
- **Feel**: Despite your efforts, the strike connects
- **Consequence**: Full damage after DR, lose momentum

#### Critical Hit (They roll Natural 20)

- **Effect**: Precision strike bypasses evasion entirely
- **Feel**: The blow is too perfectly timed to avoid
- **Impact**: Automatic hit, DR halved, significant momentum loss

### 2.3 Armour Types and Defence Identity

Your armour choice determines more than protection—it shapes your entire combat approach:

#### Light Armour: The Mobile Warrior

- **Defence Method**: Dodge (Dex + Proficiency)
- **Success Bonus**: Move 5 feet without provoking opportunity attacks after successful evasion
- **Philosophy**: Survive through mobility and positioning
- **Best For**: Rogues, rangers, mobile fighters, skirmishers

#### Medium Armour: The Balanced Fighter

- **Defence Method**: Dodge (Dex + Proficiency)
- **Stability Bonus**: No momentum loss on glancing blows (ties)
- **Philosophy**: Balance protection with tactical flexibility
- **Best For**: Clerics, druids, versatile fighters, tactical coordinators

#### Heavy Armour: The Immovable Guardian

- **Defence Method**: Brace (Str + Proficiency, requires heavy armour proficiency)
- **Protection Focus**: Highest DR values, designed to absorb punishment
- **Philosophy**: Control space through superior protection and positioning
- **Best For**: Paladins, defensive fighters, guardians, anchor points

### 2.4 Environmental Factors

Your evasion doesn't exist in a vacuum. The battlefield affects your defensive capabilities:

#### Evasion Modifiers

|Condition|Modifier|Why|
|---|---|---|
|**Half Cover**|+2|Partial protection aids defence|
|**High Ground**|+1|Positional advantage|
|**Difficult Terrain**|-1|Unstable footing|
|**Prone vs Melee**|Disadvantage|Vulnerable position|
|**Prone vs Ranged**|Advantage|Smaller target|
|**Fast Movement**|+1 to Dodge|Momentum assists evasion|

## Chapter 3: Simplified Damage Reduction

### 3.1 The Philosophy of Protection

Armour serves one primary purpose: **reducing the severity of wounds**. Version 2.0 streamlines this into a simple but tactically meaningful system.

### 3.2 How DR Works: Two Modes

**Standard Attacks**: Use your armour's **Total DR** regardless of where the blow lands. This represents the overall protective value of your equipment with simple, fast calculation.

**Called Shots**: Use only the **specific location's DR**. This represents the benefit of targeting weak points in armour coverage, but requires precision to achieve.

#### Armour DR Values

|Armour Type|Total DR|Head DR|Arms/Legs DR|Torso DR|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**Light**|5|1|1|2|
|**Medium**|10|2|2|4|
|**Heavy**|15|3|3|6|
|**Unarmoured**|0|0|0|0|

### 3.3 The Tactical Choice

This creates a meaningful decision for both attackers and defenders:

#### For Attackers:

**Standard Attacks**:

- ✓ Reliable to hit (no penalties)
- ✗ Full DR applies (maximum protection)
- ✓ Simple calculation
- **Best for**: Building momentum, guaranteed hits, chip damage

**Called Shots**:

- ✗ Harder to hit (location penalties)
- ✓ Reduced DR (bypass distributed protection)
- ✓ Special effects (disable, stun, disarm)
- **Best for**: High-damage weapons, tactical disruption, fighting heavily armoured foes

#### For Defenders:

**Against Standard Attacks**: Your full armour protection applies—you're getting maximum value from your equipment investment.

**Against Called Shots**: Specific locations matter. Unarmoured areas become genuinely vulnerable, but you can position to protect weak points.

### 3.4 Examples in Play

#### Standard Attack Example

_A bandit's longsword (1d8+3) strikes a fighter wearing plate mail:_

- **Damage Roll**: 1d8+3 = 9 damage
- **Standard Attack**: Uses Total DR = 15
- **Final Damage**: 9 - 15 = **0 damage** (DR absorbs all)

#### Called Shot Example

_The same attack targets the fighter's arm:_

- **Attack Penalty**: -2 to hit (arms)
- **Damage Roll**: 1d8+3 = 9 damage
- **Called Shot to Arm**: Uses Arm DR = 3
- **Final Damage**: 9 - 3 = **6 damage**
- **Trade-off**: -2 accuracy but bypassed 12 points of DR

#### Why Called Shots Now Work

Against heavy armour, called shots offer dramatic DR reduction:

- **Head shots**: 15→3 DR (save 12 damage) for -5 accuracy penalty
- **Limb shots**: 15→3 DR (save 12 damage) for -2 accuracy penalty
- **Torso shots**: 15→6 DR (save 9 damage) for no penalty

This makes precision strikes genuinely attractive tactical options rather than trap choices.

## Chapter 4: Momentum - The Heart of Combat

### 4.1 Understanding Combat Rhythm

Combat isn't just trading blows—it has psychological weight, momentum, and flow. A warrior landing perfect strikes feels confidence and tactical advantage building. A defender under pressure struggles with each action becoming harder than the last.

Momentum captures this reality, creating authentic combat rhythm where your performance affects your capabilities.

### 4.2 Momentum Ranges and Effects

#### Standard Range (-3 to +3)

| Score  | State           | Bonus | Special Effects                                                              |
| ------ | --------------- | ----- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **+3** | **Dominating**  | +3    | First attack has advantage; enemies have disadvantage on called shots vs you |
| **+2** | **Flowing**     | +2    | +5 feet movement speed                                                       |
| **+1** | **Confident**   | +1    | Minor edge in all actions                                                    |
| **0**  | **Neutral**     | 0     | Balanced state                                                               |
| **-1** | **Pressured**   | -1    | Slight disadvantage                                                          |
| **-2** | **Struggling**  | -2    | Enemies gain +5 feet movement when positioning around you                    |
| **-3** | **Overwhelmed** | -3    | First attack has disadvantage; attacks against you have advantage            |

#### Epic Ranges (High-Level Play)

Characters at levels 17+ may achieve +4, +5, or even higher momentum through exceptional performance, gaining additional benefits like extra reactions and heroic surges.

### 4.3 Gaining and Losing Momentum

#### Building Momentum (Positive Actions)

|Action|Momentum Change|Why|
|---|---|---|
|**Successful Attack**|+1|Building combat confidence|
|**Critical Hit**|+2|Exceptional performance|
|**Called Shot Success**|+2|Tactical precision (if save fails)|
|**Perfect Defence**|+1|Defensive mastery|
|**Drop Enemy to 0 HP**|+1|Decisive victory|

#### Losing Momentum (Setbacks)

|Event|Momentum Loss|Why|
|---|---|---|
|**Missed Attack**|-1|Lost rhythm|
|**Failed Saving Throw**|-1|Succumbing to effects|
|**Critical Hit Against You**|-2|Devastating strike|
|**Knocked Prone/Restrained**|-1|Loss of control|
|**Major Injury (50% HP)**|Reset to 0|Significant trauma|
|**Critical Injury (25% HP)**|-2|Severe disruption|

### 4.4 Momentum Application

Your momentum score modifies:

- **Attack rolls** (both weapon and spell attacks)
- **Evasion rolls** (both dodge and brace)
- **Initiative contests** (see Initiative system)
- **Some saving throws** (class-dependent)

#### Class-Specific Momentum Rules

Different classes interact with momentum in unique ways that reflect their combat identity:

**Barbarian: Rage-Fueled Flow**

- While raging, major injuries drop momentum to -1 instead of resetting to 0
- Gain +2 momentum when starting a turn at -2 or lower (fury builds)
- First critical hit per rage grants +3 momentum instead of +2

**Monk: Centred Balance**

- Missed attacks don't reduce momentum (disciplined training)
- Failed saves only reduce momentum on critical failures
- At +3 momentum, spend 1 Ki to maintain it until next turn

**Rogue: Precision Momentum**

- Sneak Attack hits grant +2 momentum instead of +1 (once per turn)
- Movement abilities don't reduce momentum (slippery)
- Never suffer disadvantage on first attack even at -3 momentum

**Fighter: Tactical Discipline**

- Major injuries drop to -1 instead of reset (trained resilience)
- Second Wind immediately resets momentum to +1 (recovery surge)
- Action Surge always grants +1 momentum regardless of results

## Chapter 5: Called Shots - Precision in Combat

### 5.1 The Art of Targeted Strikes

Called shots transform combat from mindless exchanges into tactical surgery. By targeting specific body parts, you can disable opponents, bypass heavy armour, or achieve dramatic effects beyond simple damage.

### 5.2 When to Use Called Shots

**Against Heavy Armour**: The DR bypass often outweighs accuracy penalties **For Tactical Control**: Special effects can change the battle's flow **High-Damage Weapons**: Big damage dice make the DR bypass more valuable **High Momentum**: Positive momentum bonuses offset accuracy penalties **Desperation Moves**: When standard attacks aren't working

### 5.3 Basic Called Shot Locations

#### Head (-5 penalty)

- **DR Bypass**: Heavy armour 15→3 (save 12 damage)
- **Special Effect**: Target stunned until end of next turn (Constitution save)
- **Best Against**: Casters, heavily armoured foes, when you need to disable
- **Risk**: Highest accuracy penalty, but highest reward

#### Arms (-2 penalty)

- **DR Bypass**: Heavy armour 15→3 (save 12 damage)
- **Special Effect**: Drop weapon OR -2 to attacks for 1 round (Strength save)
- **Best Against**: Weapon users, spellcasters (disrupts somatic components)
- **Balance**: Moderate penalty for significant tactical benefit

#### Legs (-2 penalty)

- **DR Bypass**: Heavy armour 15→3 (save 12 damage)
- **Special Effect**: Speed becomes 0 OR fall prone (Dexterity save)
- **Best Against**: Mobile enemies, flyers that need to land, pursuit situations
- **Tactical**: Control positioning and mobility

#### Torso (No penalty)

- **DR Bypass**: Heavy armour 15→6 (save 9 damage)
- **Special Effect**: None
- **Best For**: Reliable hits when you need guaranteed damage
- **Safe Choice**: No accuracy penalty, moderate DR bypass

### 5.4 Advanced Called Shot Locations

_Requires weapon proficiency to attempt_

#### Eye (-7 penalty)

- **Special Effect**: Blinded until end of next turn (Constitution save)
- **Extreme Risk**: Massive accuracy penalty
- **High Reward**: Blindness is devastating to most opponents

#### Hand (-4 penalty)

- **Special Effect**: Drop specific item OR fail somatic spellcasting (Dexterity save)
- **Precision Target**: Disrupt specific abilities or remove key items
- **Tactical Use**: Stop spellcasters, remove magical items

#### Throat (-6 penalty)

- **Special Effect**: Silenced for 1 turn (Intelligence save)
- **Shut Down**: Prevents verbal spells and calling for help
- **High Stakes**: Significant penalty but powerful disruption

### 5.5 Called Shot Resolution

#### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Declare Before Rolling**: "I'm targeting his sword arm"
2. **Apply Penalty**: -2 to your attack roll for arms
3. **Roll Attack**: Include all normal bonuses and the called shot penalty
4. **Defender Evades**: They roll evasion normally (called shots don't make evasion harder)
5. **Apply Damage**: Use location-specific DR instead of Total DR
6. **Resolve Special Effect**: Target makes appropriate save if damage > 0
7. **Gain Momentum**: +2 if special effect succeeds, +1 if it fails

#### Save DCs for Called Shot Effects

**DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your attack ability modifier**

### 5.6 Class Synergies

#### Rogue: Surgical Precision

- Reduce all called shot penalties by 2 when using Sneak Attack
- Critical hits with called shots automatically impose effects (no save)
- Against surprised targets, suffer no called shot penalties

#### Ranger: Hunter's Eye

- Reduce called shot penalties by 1 against marked targets (Hunter's Mark)
- No penalties for called shots against favoured enemies
- Colossus Slayer: Called shots that trigger this impose disadvantage on saves

#### Fighter: Tactical Mastery

- Battle Masters can spend superiority dice to reduce called shot penalties
- Precision Attack manoeuvre applies after seeing the roll
- Champions: 19-20 critical range applies to called shots

## Chapter 6: Reactions and Shields

### 6.1 Reactions: Turning Defence into Offence

Reactions in DCS go beyond simple opportunity attacks. They represent split-second tactical decisions that can change the flow of battle.

#### Standard Reaction Options

**Counterattack**

- **When**: After being hit by a melee attack
- **Effect**: Make immediate weapon attack with advantage
- **Risk/Reward**: Guaranteed damage taken for potential damage dealt

**Disarm**

- **When**: After being attacked with a weapon
- **Effect**: Contest your Athletics/Acrobatics vs their Athletics/Acrobatics
- **Success**: Their weapon lands within 5 feet
- **Tactical**: Remove threat, create opportunities for allies

**Weapon Trap**

- **When**: After being attacked with a weapon
- **Effect**: Contest to entangle their weapon
- **Success**: They can't use that weapon until freed (action to break free)
- **Control**: Neutralise dangerous weapons without disarming

#### Free Reaction Opportunities

**Perfect Defence (Natural 20 on Evasion)**

- Gain one free attack that doesn't use your reaction
- Represents flowing from defence into offence seamlessly

**Forgoing Evasion**

- Choose to automatically fail your evasion roll
- Gain a free reaction that doesn't use your normal reaction
- Limited uses per encounter (Proficiency Bonus ÷ 2, minimum 1)
- High-risk, high-reward tactical choice

### 6.2 Shields: Active Combat Tools

Shields aren't passive AC bonuses—they're active tools requiring skill and timing.

#### Shield Basics

- **DR Bonus**: +2 to Total DR and +2 to arm/torso location DR
- **Must be raised**: Need proper positioning to benefit
- **Reaction Arsenal**: Multiple active options available

#### Shield Reactions

**Shield Block**

- **When**: After being hit but before damage is rolled
- **Effect**: Add +5 total DR against this one attack
- **Use**: Reduce damage from powerful attacks
- **Resource**: Still uses your reaction

**Shield Deflect**

- **When**: As you're being attacked
- **Effect**: Contest your Athletics vs their attack roll
- **Success**: Attack negated completely
- **Failure**: Attack proceeds, reaction wasted
- **High Risk**: Complete success or complete failure

**Shield Bash**

- **When**: After being attacked in melee
- **Effect**: Contest Athletics vs their Athletics/Acrobatics
- **Success**: Deal 1d6+Str bludgeoning damage and push 5 feet
- **Tactical**: Create space, reposition enemies, deal damage

## Chapter 7: Initiative and Combat Flow

### 7.1 Dynamic Initiative System

Initiative in DCS reflects the natural ebb and flow of battle rather than maintaining rigid turn orders throughout combat.

#### Two-Phase System

**Round 1: Traditional Chaos**

- Everyone rolls 1d20 + Dexterity modifier as normal
- Represents unpredictable opening moments
- Preserves value of surprise tactics and high Dexterity

**Round 2+: Momentum-Driven Flow**

- Initiative determined by each side's average Momentum Score
- Higher average momentum acts first for the entire round
- Ties resolved by leadership contests (Focus modifier rolls)

#### Why This Works

**Narrative Logic**: Successful fighters build rhythm and advantage **Tactical Rewards**: Good performance leads to more opportunities  
**Comeback Potential**: Momentum can shift, allowing dramatic reversals **Team Coordination**: Winning side chooses their action order

### 7.2 How Momentum Initiative Works

#### Step 1: Calculate Side Averages

Add up each side's momentum scores and divide by number of active combatants

- **Routed/fleeing creatures**: Don't count (prevents death spirals)
- **Unconscious allies**: Count as 0 momentum

#### Step 2: Compare and Act

**Higher Average**: That side acts first, choosing their turn order **Lower Average**: That side acts second, responding to enemy actions

#### Step 3: Leadership Tiebreaker

**Equal Averages**: Each side's leader rolls 1d20 + Focus modifier **Leader**: Highest CR (monsters) or designated leader (PCs) **Winner**: Determines initiative for that round

### 7.3 Special Rules

**Legendary Dominance**: Once per combat, legendary creatures can act first regardless of momentum

**Auto-Rally**: When leaders become bloodied, they may attempt rallies that reset allied momentum

**Morale Collapse**: If all allies fail morale checks, their side automatically goes second until a successful rally

## Chapter 8: Character Creation and Class Considerations

### 8.1 Building for Dynamic Combat

#### Attribute Priorities

**Dexterity**: Always valuable for evasion, even more crucial now **Strength**: Vital for heavy armour users (Brace defence) **Constitution**: Health remains important, affects some momentum rules **Mental Stats**: Focus becomes relevant for initiative leadership

#### Armour Choice Impact

Your armour selection shapes your entire combat approach:

**Light Armour Characters**:

- Prioritise Dexterity for evasion
- Value mobility and positioning
- Consider feats that enhance movement
- Plan for hit-and-run tactics

**Medium Armour Characters**:

- Balance Dexterity and other stats
- Benefit from stability on ties
- Good for versatile builds
- Reliable middle-ground option

**Heavy Armour Characters**:

- Prioritise Strength for Brace defence
- Plan to absorb punishment
- Consider shield use and reactions
- Focus on area control

### 8.2 Class Considerations

#### Classes That Excel in DCS

**Rogue**: Sneak Attack bypasses DR, called shot bonuses, momentum efficiency **Monk**: Momentum resistance, mobility bonuses, reaction potential  
**Fighter**: Tactical reactions, weapon mastery, momentum discipline **Barbarian**: Momentum resilience, damage absorption, rage synergy

#### Classes That Need Adaptation

**Wizard**: Focus on battlefield control, leverage ranged advantage **Cleric**: Use support abilities, coordinate team momentum  
**Sorcerer**: Leverage spell flexibility, maintain distance **Warlock**: Exploit limited resources efficiently, use called shots with Eldritch Blast

#### Multiclassing Considerations

**Fighter Dips**: Valuable for any character wanting better reactions **Rogue Levels**: Sneak Attack's DR bypass is extremely valuable **Barbarian Rage**: Momentum resilience benefits many builds **Shield Proficiency**: Opens up entire reaction categories

## Chapter 9: Tactical Guidelines and Advanced Play

### 9.1 Beginning Player Guidelines

#### Start Simple

1. **Weeks 1-2**: Master evasion (dodge vs brace choice)
2. **Weeks 3-4**: Add basic momentum tracking
3. **Weeks 5-6**: Introduce simple called shots (limbs only)
4. **Week 7+**: Add advanced called shots and complex reactions

#### Core Concepts to Master First

**Evasion Choice**: When to dodge vs when to brace **Momentum Awareness**: How your performance affects future actions **DR Understanding**: Why called shots bypass protection **Reaction Timing**: When to use reactions vs save them

### 9.2 Intermediate Tactics

#### Momentum Management

**Build Early**: Use reliable attacks to establish positive momentum **Maintain Flow**: Avoid risky actions when momentum is positive **Break Negative Cycles**: Use defensive actions to reset when overwhelmed

#### Called Shot Strategy

**Target Selection**: Heavy armour = more bypass benefit **Timing**: Use when you have positive momentum for accuracy **Effect Priority**: Sometimes the special effect matters more than damage

#### Reaction Efficiency

**Save for Big Hits**: Don't waste shield blocks on minor damage **Coordinate**: Set up allies for better reactions **Resource Management**: Track uses per encounter carefully

### 9.3 Advanced Tactics

#### Team Coordination

**Momentum Synchronisation**: Coordinate to maintain team momentum **Initiative Control**: Position high-momentum allies strategically **Combined Arms**: Mix called shots with standard attacks

#### Environmental Exploitation

**Positioning**: Use terrain to enhance evasion **Cover Usage**: Combine with evasion for maximum defence **Hazard Tactics**: Force enemies into disadvantageous positions

#### Opponent Analysis

**Armour Reading**: Identify protection levels quickly **Momentum Assessment**: Target low-momentum enemies **Weakness Exploitation**: Use called shots against appropriate targets

# Part II: The Dungeon Master's Guide to Dynamic Combat

## Chapter 10: Implementation Strategy

### 10.1 Session Zero and Player Preparation

#### Essential Pre-Game Discussion

**Complexity Expectations**: Clarify that DCS is more tactically demanding than standard 5e **Learning Curve**: Set expectations for 4-6 sessions to achieve fluency **Table Commitment**: Ensure all players are willing to invest in learning **Modification Philosophy**: Establish that the system can be adjusted based on table needs

#### Character Creation Workshop

**Walk Through Examples**: Show how armour choice affects playstyle **Demonstrate Evasion**: Practice dodge vs brace decisions with sample scenarios **Explain Momentum**: Use simple examples to show how performance affects future actions **Preview Called Shots**: Show the risk/reward calculations without overwhelming detail

#### Preparation Materials

**Reference Cards**: Create quick-reference sheets for each player's class **Practice Scenarios**: Run simple combat encounters as tutorials **Momentum Tracking**: Establish how you'll track momentum (apps, tokens, sheets) **House Rules**: Discuss any modifications you plan to implement

### 10.2 Gradual Introduction Strategy

#### Phase 1: Foundation (Sessions 1-3)

**Introduce**: Evasion + Simplified DR only **Focus**: Active defence choices, basic armour protection **Skip**: Momentum, called shots, complex reactions **Goal**: Players comfortable with dodge/brace decisions

#### Phase 2: Rhythm (Sessions 4-6)

**Add**: Momentum tracking and basic effects **Include**: Simple reactions (counterattack, shield block) **Maintain**: Focus on core evasion mastery **Goal**: Understanding combat rhythm and flow

#### Phase 3: Precision (Sessions 7-9)

**Add**: Basic called shots (head, arms, legs, torso only) **Include**: Called shot special effects and saves **Expand**: More reaction options **Goal**: Tactical precision and target selection

#### Phase 4: Full System (Session 10+)

**Complete**: All advanced called shots, complex reactions **Master**: Full momentum rules, initiative system **Refine**: House rules based on table experience **Goal**: Complete system fluency and tactical mastery

### 10.3 Managing the Learning Curve

#### Common Player Challenges

**Analysis Paralysis**: Too many options slow decision-making

- **Solution**: Implement 30-second decision timers
- **Support**: Offer tactical advice during early sessions
- **Encourage**: Reward quick decisions even if suboptimal

**Bookkeeping Overwhelm**: Tracking multiple systems

- **Solution**: Provide tracking sheets and apps
- **Simplify**: Use average damage for minor enemies
- **Delegate**: Let players track their own momentum

**Tactical Intimidation**: Some players feel outmatched

- **Solution**: Pair experienced players with newcomers
- **Support**: Offer private coaching between sessions
- **Adapt**: Provide simpler options for less tactical players

#### Success Indicators

**Session 3**: Players choose evasion type without prompting **Session 6**: Momentum affects their tactical decisions  
**Session 9**: Called shots used appropriately in combat **Session 12**: Players coordinate team tactics naturally

## Chapter 11: Monster Adaptation and Encounter Design

### 11.1 Converting Existing Monsters

#### Quick Conversion Formula

**Step 1: Evasion DC**

```
Evasion DC = 8 + Dex modifier + Proficiency bonus
Alternative: Use (existing AC - 8) for quick conversion
```

**Step 2: Assign DR by Size**

|Size|Total DR|Head/Limbs|Torso|Examples|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**Small**|3|1|1|Goblin, Kobold|
|**Medium**|6|2|3|Orc, Bandit|
|**Large**|12|3|6|Troll, Owlbear|
|**Huge**|18|4|9|Adult Dragon|
|**Gargantuan**|24|5|12|Ancient Dragon|

**Step 3: Momentum Range**

|Creature Type|Range|Notes|
|---|---|---|
|**Beasts/Humanoids**|-2 to +2|Standard mortal limits|
|**Monstrosities**|-3 to +3|Chaotic and dangerous|
|**Dragons/Giants**|-5 to +5|Legendary presence|
|**Undead/Constructs**|-2 to +2|Limited emotional range|

#### Detailed Conversion Examples

**Goblin (CR 1/4) Conversion**:

- **Evasion DC**: 12 (8 + 2 Dex + 2 Prof)
- **DR**: Total 3, Head/Limbs 1, Torso 1
- **Momentum Range**: -2 to +2
- **Called Shot Vulnerabilities**: Head (stun), Arms (disarm), Legs (trip)
- **Reactions**: Basic counterattack only

**Troll (CR 5) Conversion**:

- **Evasion DC**: 12 (8 + 1 Dex + 3 Prof)
- **DR**: Total 12, Head/Limbs 3, Torso 6
- **Momentum Range**: -3 to +3
- **Special**: Regeneration affects momentum (gains +1 when regenerating)
- **Reactions**: Claw counterattack, intimidating roar

**Adult Red Dragon (CR 17) Conversion**:

- **Evasion DC**: 14 (8 + 0 Dex + 6 Prof)
- **DR**: Total 18, Head/Limbs 4, Torso 9
- **Momentum Range**: -5 to +5
- **Legendary**: Can override initiative once per combat
- **Reactions**: Wing buffet, tail sweep, frightful presence

### 11.2 Encounter Design Principles

#### CR Recalibration Guidelines

**General Rule**: Increase monster HP by 20-30% to account for:

- Active defence giving PCs more survival options
- Called shots potentially ending fights faster
- Momentum swings affecting encounter difficulty dynamically

#### Specific Adjustments by Encounter Type

**Single Powerful Enemy**: +30% HP, emphasise legendary actions **Multiple Weak Enemies**: +20% HP, use simplified reactions only **Mixed Groups**: +25% HP, vary
