## 🧠 Sanity in Combat

Sanity represents a combatant’s **mental resilience** against horror, confusion, or supernatural trauma. It is a **core attribute** in the FEARS system.

When exposed to mind-breaking effects, the unknown, or Eldritch encounters:

- Roll **Sanity Saves** (1d20 + Sanity Modifier + Proficiency)
- Failing can induce short-term Fracture, confusion, or forced actions

### 🌀 Sanity in Combat:

|Trigger Example|Save DC|On Failure|
|---|---|---|
|Witnessing eldritch transformation|10 + CR|Roll on Short-Term Madness table|
|Seeing an ally vaporized|15|Stunned for 1 round|
|Memory Glyph backlash|10 + Spell Level|Lose 1 Focus, gain 1 Whisper Point|
|Encountering Hollow-Eyed Entity|8–18 (varies)|Roll Fracture Save + gain temporary condition|

---
## 🔍 Fracture Save

Triggered after major Sanity loss, this is a **high-DC Constitution + Sanity check** to prevent total breakdown.

> DC = 13 + number of Whisper Points On fail: roll or apply a Fracture Effect (madness, hallucination, paralysis, etc.)

---
## 🔥 Focus in Combat

Focus represents a character’s **clarity, discipline, and responsiveness** in the chaos of combat. It is the primary **reaction and tactical currency**.

Each character has a **Focus Pool**, which can be:

- Spent to power Reactions, class abilities, or resist certain effects.
- Regained through resting, abilities, or regaining control of the fight.

### 💡 Gaining Focus:

| Condition               | Focus Gain |
| ----------------------- | ---------- |
| Dropping an enemy       | +1         |
| Critical Hit            | +1         |
| Regaining Momentum Tier | +1         |
| Passing a Fracture Save | +1         |
| Sanity Stabilization    | +1         |

> ✨ Max Focus = 3 unless modified by class, feats, or traits

---
## ⚙️ Sanity & Focus Interaction

- **Losing Sanity** can cause **Focus loss**, especially during eldritch or psychic events
- Certain abilities may **spend Focus to resist Fracture**
- Low Sanity = lower max Focus during long rests

### 🧩 Example:

A Cleric fails a Sanity Save while confronting a Howling Moon cultist. They lose 1 Focus and must roll a Fracture Save. They pass and gain back 1 Focus for resisting.

## 🧷 Optional Toggles

| Toggle Name          | Description                                                     |
| -------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Focus Fragility**  | Taking damage may cause Focus loss (DC 12 CON save to resist)   |
| **Momentum Synergy** | For every 3 Momentum, increase Focus max by +1                  |
| **Eldritch Sway**    | Sanity failures slowly convert into usable—but corrupting—Focus |
| **Mental Reserves**  | Focus = Sanity Modifier + 2 (instead of static cap)             |

| System              | Interaction                                                    |
