# The Ranger

**Masters of Wilderness and Precision**

> *"The druid asks the forest's permission. The Ranger has already mapped every angle of approach."*

---

## The Predator's Edge

She had been in this clearing before. She knew which stones shifted when stepped on, where the light came through the canopy at midday, exactly which approach path the bandit scouts would use. When they came, she was already where she needed to be. She didn't hunt them. She waited for them to walk into what she'd already prepared.

You are the Ranger. Not a warrior who happens to know wilderness survival, but someone who treats every environment — terrain, prey, timing — as a system of advantages waiting to be claimed. **Hunter's Mark** is not a buff. It is a relationship. **Claimed Ground** turns familiar locations into tactical extensions of your will. **Favoured Enemy** means the things you hunt most often have nowhere to hide.

In FEARS, the Ranger's Momentum interaction is reliable: successful hits against your Favoured Enemy always grant +2 Momentum. Claimed Ground grants +1 Momentum on your first hit each turn. Together, these create a class that accelerates into fights rather than recovering from them.

---

## The Ranger's Essence

You are the party's intelligence officer, scout, and precision striker. Your unique position is that you get better in fights you prepared for — and you prepare for almost every fight. **Predator's Insight** lets you read bloodied enemies before they cross the threshold. **Exploit Quarry** disables, exposes, or pins prey at exactly the right moment. At higher levels, **Terrain Mastery** and **Claimed Ground** mean you fight on your terms far more often than anyone expects.

You excel in prepared ambushes, single-target elimination, and extended wilderness travel. You struggle in urban environments you've never visited and against threats that ambush you first.

---

## Complexity Assessment: LOW (3/10) 

The Ranger tracks:  
• Hunter's Mark (scaling bonus damage, no concentration)
• Favoured Enemy types
• Exploit Quarry uses (WIS modifier per long rest)

Recommended for new players who want a martial feel with meaningful out-of-combat utility.

## Class Features

### Hit Points

- **Hit Dice:** 1d10 per Ranger level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 10 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d10 (or 6) + your Constitution modifier per Ranger level after 1st

### Proficiencies

- **Armour:** Light armour, medium armour, shields
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons, martial weapons
- **Tools:** Choose one: herbalist tools, navigator tools, or trapper tools
- **Saving Throws:** Strength, Dexterity
- **Skills:** Choose three from Animal Handling, Athletics, Crafting, Gathering, Insight, Investigation, Nature, Perception, Skinning, Stealth, and Survival

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) scale mail or (b) leather armour
- (a) two shortswords or (b) two simple weapons
- (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
- A longbow and a quiver of 20 arrows

---

## Quick Build

To quickly create a Ranger, follow these steps:

1. **Ability Scores:** Make Dexterity or Strength your highest score, followed by Wisdom.
2. **Lineage and Heritage:** Any combination works; Wood Elf, Halfling, and Human are thematic but not required.
3. **Background:** Outlander, Folk Hero, or any background with Nature or Survival.
4. **Combat Strategy:** Apply Hunter's Mark at the start of combat, use Exploit Quarry on priority targets, and use Stalker's Step to reposition for optimal attack angles.

## Core Class Features

### Explorer (1st Level)

- Gain either a climbing speed or swimming speed equal to your base movement.
- Advantage on checks to track creatures.
- Ignore movement penalties from nonmagical difficult terrain.

---

### Favoured Enemy (1st Level)

Choose one creature type as your Favoured Enemy (aberrations, beasts, dragons, undead, etc.).

- **Called Shot Benefits:** Reduce Called Shot penalties against your Favoured Enemy — Head becomes −2, Arms and Legs become 0.
- **Bonus Damage:** +2 weapon damage against Favoured Enemies.
- **Tracking:** Advantage on checks to track and recall information about your Favoured Enemy.
- **Synergy with Hunter's Mark:** Combine both features to eliminate Called Shot penalties entirely and increase bonus damage by one die step.
- **Favoured Enemy Agility:** Roll Evasion with advantage against Favoured Enemies.

Choose a new Favoured Enemy at 6th, 14th, and 19th level.

**Momentum Interaction:** When you successfully hit a creature designated as your Favoured Enemy, gain +2 Momentum instead of the normal +1.

---

### Hunter's Mark (1st Level)

Mark a creature within range as your quarry. While marked:

- **Bonus Damage:** 1d6 on weapon attacks or damaging spells. Increases to 1d8 at 6th level, 1d10 at 13th level, 1d12 at 17th level.
- **DR Reduction:** Reduce the marked target's DR by 2.
- **Bonus to Attack Rolls:** +1 at 1st level, +2 at 6th, +3 at 13th, +4 at 17th.
- **Called Shot Synergy:** Reduce penalties against marked targets — Head becomes −3, Arms and Legs become −1.
- **Evasion Penalty:** Marked targets roll Evasion with disadvantage against your attacks.
- **No Concentration Required:** Maintain one Hunter's Mark simultaneously without concentration.

---

### Weapon Mastery (1st Level)

Gain Weapon Mastery with one weapon of your choice. When you hit with that weapon, apply one Weapon Skill once per turn.

**Hunter's Synergy:** Weapon Skills that control enemy movement complement Hunter's Mark and Favoured Enemy well.

Unlock new masteries after scoring critical hits: 2 crits for simple weapons, 3 for martial weapons.

---

### Predator's Insight (2nd Level)

Against marked targets: when they become bloodied, immediately learn one of their bloodied effects (Frailty, Strength, Ability, or Death Throes). As a bonus action, make a Survival check (DC 12 + CR) to learn an additional effect.

Against Favoured Enemies: automatically know all four bloodied effects.

You have advantage on Survival checks to track bloodied creatures.

---

### Hunter's Preparation (2nd Level)

After at least 1 minute of uninterrupted preparation before combat (scouting, aiming, focusing breath), you gain advantage on your first attack roll in Round 1 and deal an extra +1 weapon damage die on that attack (or +2 dice against a Favoured Enemy). Once per short or long rest.

---

### Exploit Quarry (2nd Level/4th Level)

As a bonus action, choose a creature marked by Hunter's Mark or designated as your Favoured Enemy within 30 feet. Make a Survival or Perception check (DC = 8 + target's DEX modifier + their PB). On success, your next attack before the end of your next turn deals extra damage:

- 1d8 at 4th level, 2d8 at 8th level, 3d8 at 14th level, 4d8 at 20th level.

Additionally choose one effect: **Pinned Prey** (speed 0 until end of their next turn), **Expose Weak Point** (target's DR halved against all attacks until start of your next turn), **Disrupt Reaction** (target cannot take reactions until start of their next turn), or **Hamper Evasion** (disadvantage on their next Evasion roll against you or an ally).

At 6th level, you can target two creatures simultaneously. At 14th level, the target loses immunity to conditions imposed by this feature.

Uses equal to your Wisdom modifier per long rest.

---

### Fighting Style (2nd Level)

Choose one: Archery, Blind Fighting, Agile Defence, Dueling, Thrown Weapon Fighting, Two-Weapon Fighting, Beast Bond, Primal Strikes, or Forest Guardian.

---

### Ranger Subclass (3rd Level, and 7th, 11th, 15th Level)

Choose a Ranger tradition such as **Beast Master**, **Gloom Stalker**, or **Hunter**. Your subclass grants features at 3rd, 7th, 11th, and 15th levels.

---

### Multiattack (5th Level)

Make two attacks when you take the Attack action.

---

### Fleet Guard (5th Level)

If you move at least 10 feet during your turn, gain DR 1 until the start of your next turn. Stacks with armour DR.

---

### Empowered Mark (6th Level)

You can sense when your marked target is within 10% of becoming bloodied. You know immediately when they become bloodied. While they are within 60 feet, you always know their exact location and cannot be blinded or suffer disadvantage against them from invisibility.

When your marked target becomes bloodied: Hunter's Mark bonus damage increases by one die step; DR reduction from Hunter's Mark increases by 2 (total −4 DR); Called Shot penalties are eliminated entirely.

---

### Terrain Mastery (7th Level)

Choose one terrain type (arctic, coast, desert, forest, grassland, mountain, swamp, or Underdark). In your chosen terrain: ignore difficult terrain, advantage on Stealth checks, advantage on Survival checks, enemies have disadvantage on Perception checks to detect you while stationary. Choose additional terrain types at 11th and 15th level.

---

### Claimed Ground (7th Level)

After spending at least 10 minutes studying a location (observing sight lines, testing footing, noting approach routes), designate it as **Claimed Ground**. You can maintain a number of Claimed Ground locations equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 2). Claimed Ground is not magical.

**Combat Advantages in Claimed Ground:**
- **Prepared Ambush:** When combat begins within your Claimed Ground and you are not surprised, move up to half your speed as a free action before initiative is rolled.
- **Territorial Momentum:** The first time you hit on each of your turns while within Claimed Ground, gain +1 Momentum in addition to normal Momentum gain.
- **Immediate Mark:** When a hostile creature enters your Claimed Ground for the first time in an encounter, apply Hunter's Mark as a free reaction (no reaction pool cost).
- **Tactical Sight Lines:** You cannot be surprised. You always know the number of creatures within Claimed Ground even if you cannot see them.

Claimed Ground locations count as your chosen terrain type for all Terrain Mastery benefits.

---

### Stalker's Step (9th Level)

While in dim light, darkness, or obscured terrain, use a bonus action to become magically invisible until the start of your next turn (ends if you attack or cast). Usable a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest.

When a creature within 60 feet becomes bloodied, use Stalker's Step as a reaction: become invisible, immediately move up to half your speed without provoking, and your next attack against the bloodied creature has advantage.

---

### Heroic Boon (10th Level)

Choose one:

**Path of the Predator:** When you roll initiative, expend one use of Hunter's Mark to automatically mark a visible creature. When a marked creature drops to 0 HP, use your reaction to transfer Hunter's Mark to another creature within 60 feet. When you transfer your mark to a bloodied creature, automatically learn two of its bloodied effects.

**Path of the Sage:** Learn two cantrips and two ritual spells from the Primordial spell list (don't count against spells known). Learn the cantrip Guidance as a bonus action on yourself. When you cast a ritual spell while a bloodied creature is within 60 feet, learn one of its bloodied effects.

---

### Precision Kill (11th Level)

When you score a critical hit against your Favoured Quarry, gain +2 Momentum instead of the normal +1.

---

### Contested Ground (11th Level)

Your Tactical Sight Lines range extends to 120 feet. When a creature within Claimed Ground attempts to move to a position granting tactical advantage, use your reaction (1 Class Reaction use) to make one weapon attack. If the attack hits, their movement stops.

You may designate up to two allies as knowing your Claimed Ground; they gain Tactical Sight Lines while within that location.

---

### Keensense (14th Level)

Gain Keensense (30 feet). Detect invisible or hidden creatures within range. Bloodied creatures within Keensense range automatically fail Stealth checks against you. Advantage on attack rolls against bloodied creatures detected by Keensense.

---

### Strider (18th Level)

- Your movement never provokes opportunity attacks.
- While in natural terrain, Dash as a bonus action, ignoring all difficult terrain.
- Advantage on Survival, Animal Handling, Skinning, and Gathering.
- Advantage on checks to resist being grappled, restrained, or paralysed, or any effect reducing movement speed to 0.
- While in natural terrain, you and your party cannot become lost and travel at double speed.

---

### Sovereign Ground (18th Level)

Within a Claimed Ground location, gain tremorsense 30 feet. Invisible creatures cannot hide from you within your Claimed Ground.

Once per long rest, declare a Claimed Ground location your **Last Stand Ground** for one encounter. Within it: Hunter's Mark damage increases by one die step; creatures starting their turn within must make a WIS save (DC = 8 + PB + WIS) or be frightened of you until end of their next turn; once per encounter, when you would drop to 0 HP within Last Stand Ground, drop to 1 HP instead (reaction).

Maximum Claimed Ground locations increase to your Wisdom modifier + Proficiency Bonus.

---

### Epic Boon (20th Level)

Choose one:

**Foe Slayer:** Add your Wisdom modifier to attack and damage rolls against Hunter's Mark targets. Once per turn, declare an attack against a marked target as an unstoppable strike: bypass all DR and deal critical damage on a hit (mark consumed). When you reduce a marked creature to 0 HP, transfer Hunter's Mark to another visible creature within 60 feet without expending uses. Against bloodied marked targets, critical hits on 18–20. When you reduce a bloodied marked creature to 0 HP, make one additional attack against another creature in range (doesn't consume the mark).

**Nature's Wrath:** Maintain Hunter's Mark on up to three creatures simultaneously without concentration. When a marked creature drops to 0 HP, choose: 4d10 area damage (10 feet, choice of cold/fire/lightning/piercing) or 15 HP healing to you and all allies within 30 feet. When a marked creature attempts to move, use your reaction to force a STR save (DC = 8 + PB + WIS) or their movement becomes 0. All bloodied creatures within 60 feet have disadvantage on attacks against you and your allies.

## Last Stand Options

When the Ranger reaches their final hour, they embrace their role as hunters, guardians, and warriors of the wild.

**Rules for Last Stand:** Invoked when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, roll a death saving throw, or die outright. A free action requiring no reaction or consciousness. Once resolved, the character permanently dies.

---

### 1. Hunter's Reckoning

*Even as your body fails, your instincts sharpen to unnatural precision. The hunter becomes the predator one final time.*

**Effect:**
- Choose one target within 120 feet.
- For the next 5 minutes, your attacks and effects automatically hit this target. Any attack automatically crits.
- Your target gains no benefits from cover, invisibility, or resistance to your attacks.
- Make one ranged attack against this target as part of invoking this Last Stand.

---

### 2. Call of the Wild

*The spirits of the wild rise in answer to your call.*

**Effect:**
- Summon three creatures: Beasts (up to CR 4, e.g., Dire Wolf, Giant Eagle, Tiger), Elementals (up to CR 3), or Fey Creatures (up to CR 3).
- These creatures appear within 30 feet and remain for 5 minutes.
- They are immune to charm, fear, and magical control effects.

---

### 3. Ghost of the Vale

*Your body fades, but your presence lingers — a spectral guide ensuring your allies remain unseen.*

**Effect:**
- For the next 5 minutes, remain as a spectral guide:
  - Allies within 100 feet gain invisibility as long as they do not attack or cast spells.
  - Allies have advantage on Stealth checks and Dexterity saving throws.
- Enemies within 30 feet have disadvantage on Perception checks and ranged attack rolls.
- You move freely, passing through barriers, with a fly speed of 60 feet.

| Last Stand Option | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Hunter's Reckoning** | Auto-hit and auto-crit against one target for 5 minutes. | Eliminating a key threat. |
| **Call of the Wild** | Summon three powerful creatures immune to charm/fear. | Overwhelming enemy groups. |
| **Ghost of the Vale** | Allies gain invisibility; enemies have disadvantage on Perception. | Stealth, repositioning, escape. |

## Class Reactions

Rangers have access to powerful reactions that protect allies and punish threats to their marked prey. These reactions use your **Proficiency Bonus pool**, refreshing on short or long rest.

### Hunter's Reflex (2nd Level)

**Trigger:** A creature you've marked with Hunter's Mark makes an attack roll.
**Effect:** Impose disadvantage. If it still hits, reduce the damage by 1d6 + WIS modifier.

Against bloodied marked targets: damage reduction increases to 2d6 + WIS; if the attack still hits, the target must make a CON save (DC = 8 + PB + WIS) or have its Strength benefit suppressed until end of its next turn.

---

### Quarry Interpose (3rd Level)

**Trigger:** Your marked target attacks an ally within 30 feet.
**Effect:** Impose disadvantage. If it still hits, reduce the damage by 1d8 + PB.

Against bloodied marked targets: intercept the attack entirely by moving up to 10 feet (you become the target instead); if you do, gain resistance to the attack's damage.

---

### Hunter's Snare (11th Level)

**Trigger:** Your marked target moves within 30 feet of you.
**Effect:** Force a STR save (DC = 8 + PB + WIS). On fail: restrained until end of their next turn; take PBd6 piercing damage from spectral snares.

Against bloodied marked targets: save DC +2; on failed save, also grappled; damage increases to PBd8.
