# FEARS-Original Subclass Names & Flavour Text

This document lists **10 subclass names per FEARS-Original class**, each with a short flavour blurb. **Names and flavour only — no mechanics, features, or level gating.** Entries marked *(existing)* are already established elsewhere in the class files (whether fully detailed or currently referenced-only); entries without that tag are new additions proposed to round each class out to 10.

---

## Apothecary — *Alchemical Discipline*

1. **Plague Doctor** *(existing)* — Specialises in disease, toxins, and the razor-thin line between sickness and cure; where others see infection, they see a language to be spoken fluently, then weaponised.
2. **Mad Scientist** *(existing)* — Treats their own body as the final proving ground for every formula, undeterred by the failures that came before or the scars they've left behind.
3. **Field Medic** *(existing)* — Masters the chaos of battlefield triage, turning desperate seconds into saved lives through speed, precision, and unflinching calm.
4. **Toxinist** — Obsessed with venom in all its forms, cataloguing every creature's sting and every plant's poison until their reagent kit reads like a bestiary of quiet death.
5. **Anatomist** — Studies the body not to heal it but to understand exactly where it breaks, turning every Called Shot into a lesson in applied physiology.
6. **Vivisectionist** — Pursues knowledge with a coldness that unsettles even fellow Apothecaries, believing understanding justifies almost anything done in its pursuit.
7. **Elixir Sage** — Elevates the humble potion into high art, brewing draughts so refined that lesser concoctions seem crude by comparison.
8. **Battlefield Surgeon** — Brings the operating table to the front line, performing emergency procedures mid-combat that would make a hospital physician blanch.
9. **Venom Artisan** — Crafts poisons the way a jeweller crafts rings — each one a signature, each one unmistakably theirs.
10. **Cognitive Chemist** — Turns their expertise toward the mind rather than the body, brewing compounds that sharpen focus, induce clarity, or unravel an enemy's thoughts entirely.

## Bender — *Bender Discipline*

1. **Disciple of Ferocity** *(existing)* — Channels the elements as an extension of pure offence, every strike a small detonation of primal force.
2. **Disciple of Fortification** *(existing)* — Turns elemental power inward, wrapping themselves and their allies in barriers of stone, ice, or living flame.
3. **Disciple of Fusion** *(existing)* — Obsessed with the spaces between elements, chasing the volatile, unstable magic that exists only when two forces collide.
4. **Disciple of Invigoration** *(existing)* — Bends the elements to sustain rather than destroy, turning wind, water, earth, and fire into engines of endurance for their allies.
5. **Disciple of Precision** — Rejects raw power in favour of surgical control, landing every elemental strike exactly where it needs to go and nowhere else.
6. **Disciple of Momentum** — Believes stillness is the enemy of true bending, flowing from stance to stance in a combat rhythm that never fully stops.
7. **Disciple of Devastation** — Cares little for finesse, favouring overwhelming elemental force that levels whatever stands between them and their target.
8. **Disciple of Harmony** — Seeks balance among all four elements at once, refusing to let any single affinity dominate their style.
9. **Disciple of Entropy** — Draws power from decay and erosion — the slow rust of iron, the crumbling of stone, the rot beneath still water.
10. **Disciple of Transcendence** — Treats bending as a spiritual discipline as much as a martial one, chasing the moment where body and element become indistinguishable.

## Binder — *Binding Order*

1. **The Chorus** *(existing)* — Collects remnants the way others collect stories, letting many dead voices layer and argue within a single throat.
2. **The Severed** *(existing)* — Splits their sense of self deliberately, manifesting a second body to threaten two fronts at once.
3. **The Hollow Path** *(existing)* — Practices restraint above all, keeping bound remnants suppressed until the moment demands their release.
4. **The Cartographers** *(existing)* — Maps the architecture of binding itself, favouring precision and depth over breadth of remnants held.
5. **The Reliquary** — Treats each bound remnant as a sacred object to be catalogued, preserved, and honoured rather than merely used.
6. **The Vigil** — Binds the dead who died protecting something, and carries that unfinished duty forward as their own.
7. **The Undertow** — Draws on remnants who drowned, sank, or were lost beneath something vast — their power arrives slow, cold, and inescapable.
8. **The Threadbare** — Works with remnants so fragmented and faint that most binders couldn't hold them at all, specialising in the barely-there and the almost-gone.
9. **The Ossuary** — Focuses on remnants bound to bone rather than memory, drawing on the dead in the most literal, physical sense.
10. **The Unmoored** — Rejects the idea of a single tradition entirely, binding whatever remnant answers the call regardless of lineage or custom.

## Inventor — *Specialisation*

*(Already complete at 10 — listed here for reference only.)*

1. **Gadgetsmith** *(existing)* — Believes quantity is its own kind of quality, never caught without another trick already half-built.
2. **Golemsmith** *(existing)* — Pours their genius into a loyal construct companion, an extension of their will built from metal, stone, or stranger materials.
3. **Infusionsmith** *(existing)* — Tinkers with magic itself, laying enchantments into gear that outlast the moment of casting by hours, days, or longer.
4. **Potionsmith** *(existing)* — Turns alchemical knowledge into battlefield chemistry, brewing compounds as readily as others swing a blade.
5. **Thundersmith** *(existing)* — Channels destructive elemental force into bespoke weapons, treating lightning and thunder as raw material to be shaped.
6. **Warsmith** *(existing)* — Builds powered armour around themselves, becoming a walking testament to what engineering can do to a single body.
7. **Fleshsmith** *(existing)* — Turns inward, modifying their own biology with the same confidence others reserve for machines.
8. **Cursesmith** *(existing)* — Seizes dark power that most Inventors dare not touch, engineering solutions from forces that resist being engineered.
9. **Runesmith** *(existing)* — Channels long-lasting magical inscriptions, treating runes as components as reliable as any gear or spring.
10. **Relicsmith** *(existing)* — Harnesses divine force through engineered sacred tools, viewed with a mixture of scepticism and awe by their peers.

## Investigator — *Occult Specialization*

1. **Antiquarian** *(existing)* — Traces mystic lineages back through centuries of forgotten record, treating every artefact as a clue in a much older case.
2. **Archivist** *(existing)* — Maintains an obsessive personal library of the occult, cross-referencing every encounter against everything that came before.
3. **Detective** *(existing)* — Unravels supernatural conspiracies with the same cold logic others apply to mundane crimes, refusing to let the impossible go unexplained.
4. **Exterminator** *(existing)* — Hunts down otherworldly horrors with methodical precision, treating monster-slaying as a trade to be perfected rather than a calling to be feared.
5. **Inquisitor** *(existing)* — Interrogates suspects both mortal and otherwise, extracting truth from confession, coercion, or the careful reading of a lie.
6. **Medium** *(existing)* — Speaks with the dead directly, treating spirits as witnesses to be questioned rather than mysteries to be solved.
7. **Occultist** *(existing)* — Studies forbidden practice not to fight it but to understand it fully, walking closer to the line than most investigators are comfortable with.
8. **Cryptographer** — Reads meaning into patterns everyone else dismisses as coincidence, breaking codes both mundane and eldritch.
9. **Profiler** — Builds a psychological portrait of their quarry before ever seeing its face, predicting its next move from the shape of its past ones.
10. **Debunker** — Approaches every haunting and miracle with relentless scepticism — and is all the more dangerous for the rare cases where the supernatural turns out to be real.

## Magus — *War Tradition*

1. **Consuming** *(existing)* — Burns through magical energy at a ferocious rate, trading sustainability for overwhelming immediate output.
2. **Distorting** *(existing)* — Warps battlefield dynamics through disorienting, reality-bending applications of spell and steel together.
3. **Fabricating** *(existing)* — Builds temporary constructs and structures mid-combat, treating the battlefield itself as raw material.
4. **Protecting** *(existing)* — Turns the fusion of blade and spell toward defence, shielding allies as readily as striking down enemies.
5. **Piercing** — Specialises in strikes that bypass magical and physical defences alike, finding the gap in every ward.
6. **Binding** — Uses spellstrikes to restrict and restrain, turning a single well-placed hit into a lasting handicap for their foe.
7. **Channeling** — Focuses raw arcane current directly through their weapon, favouring sustained magical output over burst technique.
8. **Severing** — Targets the connections between caster and spell, specialising in disrupting concentration and unravelling ongoing magic.
9. **Tempering** — Hardens both body and blade against magical retaliation, the most resilient expression of the Magus's hybrid nature.
10. **Weaving** — Layers multiple minor enchantments into a single strike, favouring complexity and versatility over singular devastating effects.

## Reaper — *Reaper's Covenant*

1. **Grave Warden** *(existing)* — Protects the dying and ensures peaceful passage, treating Soul Harvest as a duty of care rather than conquest.
2. **Soul Drinker** *(existing)* — Consumes life force to sustain themselves, walking the line between necessary hunger and something closer to addiction.
3. **Death Knight** *(existing)* — Executes enemies with supernatural precision, treating the kill itself as an art form to be perfected.
4. **Bone Collector** — Keeps physical trophies from every harvested soul, building a macabre archive that grows more powerful the longer they carry it.
5. **Wraith Walker** — Moves between the world of the living and the edge of death so often that the line has begun to blur for them personally.
6. **Harvest Lord** — Commands the battlefield's dying with an almost aristocratic bearing, treating every fallen enemy as tribute owed.
7. **Deathwhisper** — Speaks to the souls they harvest before releasing their power, extracting final words, regrets, and secrets in the process.
8. **Plague Bearer** — Fuses soul harvesting with disease and decay, leaving battlefields that rot long after the fighting ends.
9. **Ashen Sentinel** — Stands watch over the newly dead with grim patience, harvesting only when a soul is truly ready to be taken.
10. **Void Reaper** — Draws on something beyond death itself, harvesting souls that flicker at the edge of comprehension and cost more than most Reapers are willing to pay.

## Sangromancer — *Sangromancer Pact*

1. **Pact of the Bloodreaver** *(existing)* — Wades into melee to harvest vitality directly from foes, thriving in the chaos of mutual attrition.
2. **Pact of the Crimson Sage** *(existing)* — Manipulates life force from afar, turning blood into a puppet's string and suffering into sustenance.
3. **Pact of the Marrow King** *(existing)* — Commands the bones of the dead, building legions from the corpses left behind by battle.
4. **Pact of the Hemomancer** — Masters the raw physics of blood itself — pressure, flow, and pulse — as a weapon distinct from flesh or bone.
5. **Pact of the Vein Warden** — Turns blood magic toward protection, guarding allies by routing danger through their own veins first.
6. **Pact of the Scarlet Oracle** — Reads the future in spilled blood, trading foresight for the steady toll Vitae Strain takes on body and mind.
7. **Pact of the Exsanguinator** — Specialises in draining enemies dry with ruthless efficiency, leaving little behind but husks.
8. **Pact of the Living Vessel** — Treats their own body as a canvas for blood magic's most extreme experiments, embracing transformation others would fear.
9. **Pact of the Blood Sovereign** — Rules over their own Vitae Strain like a monarch over a restless court, wielding the curse-boons with calculated authority.
10. **Pact of the Withering** — Channels slow, creeping blood magic that saps strength and vitality over time rather than in a single burst.

## Shaman — *Spirituality*

1. **Curse Binder** *(existing)* — Hosts spirits whose purpose is affliction, turning the Shaman's own body into a conduit for misfortune directed outward.
2. **Spirit Warrior** *(existing)* — Channels martial ancestor-spirits, fighting alongside echoes of warriors who died with their duty unfinished.
3. **Wild Heart** *(existing)* — Hosts primal, feral spirits tied to beasts and untamed nature, blurring the line between shaman and animal.
4. **Witch Doctor** *(existing)* — Blends spiritual hosting with folk healing and curse-craft, equally comfortable mending a wound or causing one.
5. **Ancestor Walker** — Hosts the spirits of their own bloodline specifically, carrying generations of lineage into every battle.
6. **Storm Caller** — Hosts spirits of weather and sky, unpredictable and volatile in the same way the elements they channel are.
7. **Bone Speaker** — Communes with spirits bound to remains and relics, drawing power from what's left after death rather than life itself.
8. **Dream Weaver** — Hosts spirits from the liminal space of sleep and prophecy, walking a line between waking combat and dream logic.
9. **Totem Warden** — Specialises not in hosting but in anchoring — building and maintaining Totems so stable that other Shamans seek their craftsmanship.
10. **Void Touched** — Hosts spirits from beyond ordinary cosmology, entities that unsettle even other spirit-hosts with their alien presence.

## Tattooist — *Tattooist Style*

1. **Neo Traditional** *(existing)* — Warrior-artists who apply volatile ink to weapons, weaving spell and strike together in a single fluid motion.
2. **Surrealism** *(existing)* — Pushes magical possibility to its limits, treating their own body as an experimental canvas and their spellbangers as raw material for the impossible.
3. **Blackwork** — Favours bold, heavy ink and equally bold, heavy effects — subtlety is not the point.
4. **Realism** — Renders tattoos with painstaking, lifelike precision, and their magic follows the same exacting standard.
5. **Tribal** — Draws on ancestral and cultural tattooing traditions, imbuing designs with inherited rather than invented meaning.
6. **Watercolor** — Works in soft, bleeding gradients of ink and magic alike, favouring effects that spread and shift rather than strike cleanly.
7. **Geometric** — Builds tattoos from precise mathematical patterns, their magic as structured and predictable as the designs themselves.
8. **Illustrative** — Tells stories through their ink, each tattoo a scene or character whose magic reflects its narrative.
9. **Biomechanical** — Fuses organic and mechanical imagery, tattoos that look like they're built as much as drawn.
10. **Minimalist** — Achieves maximum effect from the smallest possible design, proving that restraint can be as powerful as excess.

## Warden — *Discipline*

1. **Guardian** *(existing)* — Steel and stone. Fights at the front, stays at the front, and makes the front cost everything.
2. **Fanatic** *(existing)* — No weapon, no armour beyond leather — just bare hands, elemental fury, and the refusal to create distance.
3. **Sentinel** *(existing)* — Controls territory rather than chasing threats, making a claimed zone functionally impassable.
4. **Apex Predator** *(existing)* — Marks the most dangerous target on the field and dismantles it with focused, patient efficiency.
5. **Stormcaller** — Channels volatile, high-energy elements — lightning and wind — into an aggressive, mobile fighting style.
6. **Tidebreaker** — Bends water and current into both weapon and shield, unyielding as a shoreline against the tide.
7. **Ashbound** — Draws on fire tempered by loss, an elemental fury that burns cold with restraint until it doesn't.
8. **Stonewarden** — The most immovable of an already immovable class, anchoring themselves to earth itself.
9. **Rootbound** — Channels the slow, patient power of growing things, wearing enemies down the way roots wear down stone.
10. **Emberkeeper** — Tends a single elemental flame with quiet devotion, protecting it as fiercely as they protect their allies.

> **Note:** Warden already has all four *(existing)* Disciplines fully detailed with mechanics through 14th level. The six new names above are name-only additions on top of an already-complete class — confirm you actually want Warden expanded to 10 before any of these get written up.

## Witch — *Coven*

1. **Coven of the Hidden Moon** *(existing)* — Secretive gatherers of forbidden knowledge, binding dangerous spirits others fear to approach.
2. **Coven of the Verdant Wood** *(existing)* — Healers who bind nature-spirits exclusively, nurturing life rather than unmaking it.
3. **Coven of the Twilight Veil** *(existing)* — Walks the boundary between light and shadow, specialising in fate manipulation and liminal spirits.
4. **Coven of the Crimson Ember** *(existing)* — Channels passion and transformation through fire-spirits, embracing destruction as a path to rebirth.
5. **Coven of the Iron Thorn** — Binds spirits of old grudges and buried violence, their curses carrying a punishing, martial edge.
6. **Coven of the Drowned Bell** — Draws on spirits lost to water and silence, their magic arriving slow, muffled, and inescapable.
7. **Coven of the Salt Circle** — Practices protective and warding magic above all, binding spirits whose purpose is to keep things out — or in.
8. **Coven of the Weeping Willow** — Specialises in grief-bound spirits, turning sorrow into a slow, patient curse that never quite lets go.
9. **Coven of the Broken Mirror** — Binds spirits of illusion and false reflection, their hexes built on misdirection as much as misfortune.
10. **Coven of the Ashen Veil** — Works at the edge of life and death itself, binding spirits caught between one and the other.
