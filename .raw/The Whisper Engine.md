# The Mechanics of Player-Driven Fate

The **Whisper Engine** is a revolutionary **meta-narrative tool** in the **FEARS** System that blurs the line between player agency and storytelling mechanics. It allows players to subtly manipulate reality—shaping fate, altering probability, and rewriting events—at the risk of drawing the **world's attention** to their interference.

Unlike traditional luck mechanics, which still exist, the **Whisper Engine** isn’t just about rerolling dice—it’s about **player influence over the fabric of the game world itself**. The more you **bend reality**, the more **reality bends back**.

## **Core Concept: The Whisper Economy**

The **Whisper Engine** operates on [[Whisper Points]] (WP), a shared pool of narrative influence. Players and the GM interact with this pool dynamically, using **Whispers** to affect the game.

- Players can spend Whisper Points to alter events in their favour.
- There are different levels (Minor, Major, Critical ... and Forbidden)
- The GM can respond with [[Counter-Whispers]], reinforcing the world’s resistance to change.
- **Overusing Whispers can attract supernatural consequences, reality distortions, or even enemies who notice the players’ manipulation. Aberrations,**

You better hope **The Whispering Dark** doesn't notice you... or you are very good friends with **Timeless Keepers**.

# **The Whisper Engine: Lore**

The **Whisper Engine** isn’t just a game mechanic—it is an **intrinsic force of reality** within the world of FEARS, a metaphysical undercurrent woven into the **fabric of existence itself**. It represents the **tension between fate, free will, and cosmic forces**, allowing those attuned to it to bend reality—**but never without consequence**.

### **Origins of the Whisper Engine: A Fractured Reality**

The Whisper Engine exists because **reality is broken**—not in a way visible to the average person, but beneath the surface of the world, **where time, causality, and perception are fluid**. Long ago, something **happened**—a rift in the cosmic order, a war between forces beyond comprehension, or perhaps a single moment where fate itself was defied so catastrophically that **the world has never truly recovered**.

This event—known in esoteric circles as **The Shattering**, **The Forgotten Divergence**, or simply **The Whispering**—left **cracks** in the structure of reality. Most beings **cannot see** or **interact** with these fractures, but those who push against fate—the brave, the desperate, the reckless—**can slip between them, rewriting fragments of existence**.

If playing in [[Dór-o Estel]], this event would be known as "The Crack"

Those who use the Whisper Engine are not merely bending probability—they are **exploiting the wounds of reality itself**.

## **Why Some Can Whisper, and Others cannot**

Most beings are **locked into the flow of time and fate**, existing as mere passengers in reality’s current. But certain individuals—whether by **birth, training, madness, or exposure to eldritch forces**—can sense the fragility of existence and **push against it**.

Those who access the Whisper Engine often fall into one of these categories:

### **1. The Marked (Innate Whisperers)**

Some individuals are **born different**—marked by fate, touched by an unseen force, or perhaps remnants of a forgotten lineage that once **walked between possibilities**. These Whisperers don’t just act on instinct—they _feel_ the cracks in reality, their minds naturally drifting toward alternate possibilities.

- **Bloodlines of Forgotten Entities** → Descendants of beings who once manipulated time, fate, or reality.
- **Survivors of Temporal Anomalies** → Those who lived through paradoxes, such as cities that never existed or wars that never happened.
- **Dream-Touched and Riftborn** → People who experience memories of lives they never lived.

### **2. The Learned (Scholars of Fate)**

Some uncover the **existence of the Whisper Engine** through forbidden knowledge, deep meditation, or arcane experimentation. These individuals understand that reality is **not as solid as it seems**, and they train themselves to take advantage of its weaknesses.

- **[[Librarians of the Fractured Archive]]** → Scholars who record stories of events that were erased from history.
- **[[Mindwalkers of the Everfold]]** → Psions who walk the dreamscape, glimpsing pasts and futures that never were.
- **[[The Silent Archive]]** → The **greatest witnesses of fate**, the Silent Archive does not interfere just record all actions and events—but that does not mean they do not _understand_.
    - **Unlike others who seek to use the Whisper Engine, the Archive exists to observe it.**
        - Their Echo-Chroniclers record its effects in the **[[Vault of Echoes]]**, ensuring that every paradox, every fracture, and every lost timeline is accounted for
        - Whisperers do not bend reality themselves, but their knowledge is unparalleled.
        - Some believe that **[[the Timeless Keepers]]—**the enigmatic forces that seem to enforce the natural flow of fate—are linked to the Archive and that any Whisperer who abuses fate too recklessly will eventually be erased, leaving only an empty space where their record should have been.
        - Those who **push too far, who whisper too much, may find a Whisperer at their door**—not to stop them, but to _witness_ their final moments before reality swallows them whole
    - The Silent Archive's **existence challenges the fundamental morality of the Whisper Engine**. Are those who manipulate reality **rebels against a cosmic order**—or merely **correcting errors in an already broken system**?
    - The Whisperers **do not answer**, for they have already recorded the question and the answer both.

### **3. The Damned (Those Who Have Seen Too Much)**

Others do not choose to see the cracks in reality—they are **forced to**. Exposure to **eldritch knowledge, near-death experiences, or catastrophic failures of fate** can **shatter** a person’s ability to perceive time and causality normally, making them _aware_ of the Whisper Engine.

- **Survivors of Broken Timelines** → Those who should have died but didn’t, or those who remember pasts no one else does.
- **Bearers of the Voidbrand** → Marked by unknowable forces that exist _outside_ the laws of reality.
- **Victims of the Whispering Madness** → Those who have stared too deeply into the nature of existence and lost something of themselves.

## **The Cost of Whispers: Reality Bites Back**

**The Whisper Engine is not free.** Those who defy fate attract **attention**—from both the **forces that enforce reality** and the **things that lurk in the cracks**.

### **1. The Keepers of the Weave (The Enforcers)**

The world is **not without its defences**. Whether divine, arcane, or something deeper, there are **forces that resist change**—forces that do not appreciate mortals **tampering with fate**.

- **[[The Loom-Wardens]]** → A sect of divine agents who exist solely to repair the fabric of reality.
- **[[The Arcane Cartographers]]** → Wizards who map the "correct" flow of time and seek to erase deviations.
- **[[The Boundless Chorus]]** → Beings that _sing_ reality into alignment, subtly undoing the effects of whispers.

### **2. The Whispers from the Dark (The Predators)**

Some entities **thrive** in the instability of reality’s cracks. They _listen_ when a whisper is made, and in turn, they **whisper back**. To use the Whisper Engine too recklessly is to **invite something to notice you**.

- **[[The Eaters of Potential]]** → Beings that feast on discarded timelines, hunting those who defy their destined path.
- **[[The Forgotten Kings]]** → Rulers of empires that never were, seeking those who create paradoxes to **pull into their domain**.
- **[[The Echoing Ones]]** → Faceless figures who mimic those who have manipulated fate, gradually replacing them.

### **3. The Paradoxes (The Self-Created Doom)**

The more one **bends fate**, the more their **own history begins to unravel**. Overuse of the Whisper Engine can cause
- **[[Temporal Echoes]]** → The character begins remembering things they never did, living moments that have already passed.
- **[[Identity Shifts]]** → Other people recall the character differently—names, faces, and relationships subtly change.
- **[[Reality Collapse]]** → At worst, the character risks being **unwritten**—forgotten even by their closest allies.

## **Theological and Arcane Interpretations**

Because the Whisper Engine affects **the very nature of existence**, different factions, scholars, and faiths have **their own interpretations** of its origins.

### **1. The Divine View**

Many religions teach that **fate is sacred**, and that Whisperers are **heretics**, stealing from the gods’ domain.
- **Fateweaver Priests** → Believe that Whispers are **divine intervention**, and those who use them are simply fulfilling prophecy.
- **[[Cult of the Unwritten]]** → Worships an unnamed god who exists only in paradoxes and whispers.
- **[[The Silent Order]]** → Hunters dedicated to **destroying Whisperers** before they cause irreversible damage.

### **2. The Arcane Perspective**

Mages theorize that the Whisper Engine is **a remnant of a greater system**—one that **existed before the gods**.
- **[[The Everfold Theory]]** → The idea that **infinite realities exist**, and Whispers merely _nudge_ someone between them.
- **[[The Tapestry Hypothesis]]** → Belief that the universe is an **unfinished story**, and Whisperers are those who can _rewrite_ their chapters.
- **[[The Riftborn Conjecture]]** → Some wizards believe Whisperers **aren’t changing reality at all**—they are just remembering the "correct" version of events.

# 🎲 What Are Whisper Points?

Whisper Points are a **limited pool of metaphysical influence**, defined per character. They represent your capacity to twist reality before reality pushes back.

- Players can spend WP to:
    - Reroll failed checks
    - Add narrative flashbacks
    - Retcon missteps
    - Alter the environment
    - Force coincidences

**Each use must be declared within 1 round or 1 minute** of the triggering event unless explicitly using a **Flashback** action.
## 🔢 How Many Whisper Points Do Players Get?

Players begin play with:

**Base WP = 3 + Focus Modifier + ½ Sanity Modifier (rounded down)**

- This pool resets **only on a Long Rest**.
- Minimum WP is 1, even if modifiers are negative.
- WP **cannot be spent if you are Collapsed (Focus 0)** or **Broken (Sanity 0)**.
---
### ❗ Focus Limits

If a character’s Focus drops to **Fragmented or Splintered**, they may not spend more than **2 WP per scene** and must succeed on a **DC 12 Focus Save** to avoid a **Whisper Misfire** (GM determines effect).

---
## ⏳ Restoring Whisper Points

| Rest Type      | WP Recovered                              | Sanity Cost |
| -------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ----------- |
| **Short Rest** | 0                                         | 0           |
| **Long Rest**  | `1 + (Sanity Modifier ÷ 2, rounded down)` | 2 Sanity    |

> Even negative modifiers still restore at least 1 WP. Stable minds recover more fate.

---
## 🧠 What Can a Whisper Do?

The GM must approve all Whisper attempts. They must
- Stay grounded in plausible memory or retroactive logic
- Be believable within the character’s skills or background
- Not nullify another player’s agency
### Whisper Tiers:

|   |   |   |
|---|---|---|
|Tier|WP Cost|Description|
|Minor|1 WP|Adjust a roll, recall a forgotten item, minor coincidence|
|Standard|2 WP|Flashback prep, cause event delay, shift enemy tactics|
|Major|3 WP|Scene change, prevent death, bend timeline logic|
|Critical|4+ WP|Rewrite short-term reality, invert failure, alter major plot thread|

---
## 💥 Forcing Whispers (F-WP)

Players may **force** a Whisper even if they lack the required WP.

- Each F-WP causes **1d4 Sanity damage** (no save).
- F-WP cannot be prevented or reduced.
- A player may never Force more than 2 WP per scene.
- If Sanity drops below 6 during an F-WP, roll for **Memory Distortion**.
    

> F-WP can save lives—but it frays the mind.

---
## 🧠 Memory Checks & Distortion

When a character uses a Whisper to recall something they were never shown or to rewrite events, the GM may call for a **Memory Skill Check** (usually INT, WIS, or SAN-based).

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Memory Result|Outcome|
|Success|Whisper accepted as intended|
|Fail|Whisper altered, misremembered, or corrupted (GM decides)|

At **Sanity ≤ 5**, these failures become more common, and hallucinated or false events may be inserted.

---
## 🕷️ Corruption and the Whisper Engine

Whispering too boldly leaves a mark.

- Each **Forced Whisper** has a **10% chance of increasing Corruption Score (CS)**.
- In **Eldritch Zones**, spending 3+ WP or performing a Critical Whisper auto-triggers a **+1 CS**, unless the player passes a **DC 15 Sanity Save**.
- At **CS 10+**, Whispers may attract Whispering Entities, Glyph Echoes, or cause side effects
- At **CS 20**, all Whispers risk collapse, inversion, or transformation.
    

> Corruption is not just about what you touch. It’s about what you _change_.

---
## 🎲 Luck and the Whisper Engine

Fate rewards or mocks the bold.

- **Blessed Luck**: First Whisper per scene costs –1 WP.
- **Cursed Luck**: All Whispers cost +1 WP. Also triggers a **DC 13 Sanity Save** when used.
- If you **Whisper while Cursed and at Splintered Focus**, the GM may escalate to **Reality Rejection**—your Whisper is undone, and consequences spiral.

To **prevent abuse**, there are **limits** on WP accumulation:

- **Max WP is always 2x Base WP** _(to prevent excessive hoarding)_.
- **Players cannot regain WP more than once per scene/encounter.**
- **Overuse triggers Reality Rejection**, leading to supernatural attention.

# **Using Whispers**

## **Player Manipulation of Reality**

Players can use **three levels of Whispers**, each with a different cost and impact on the world.

| **Whisper Type**         | **Cost (Whisper Points)** | **Effect**                                                                                                         |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **[[Minor Whisper]]**    | 1 WP                      | Slightly shift the odds (reroll, add small detail, subtly adjust an NPC’s attitude).                               |
| **[[Major Whisper]]**    | 3 WP                      | Introduce a **meaningful** change (change battlefield terrain, force a clue discovery, shift an NPC’s motivation). |
| **[[Critical Whisper]]** | 5 WP                      | **Rewrite reality itself** (erase a recent event, force an enemy mistake, alter fate in a massive way).            |

Whisper Points can be used at **any time**, even retroactively, as long as they fit the **narrative logic**. (Decided by GM)

### **Examples of Player Whispers**

1. **Minor Whisper (1 WP)**
    - **“Wait, I checked that door for traps.”** (Retroactively applies a perception check before opening a trapped door.)
    - **“I must have overheard something useful in town.”** (Introduces a small, useful fact that helps in negotiations.)
    - **“The alley is just dark enough for me to hide.”** (Alters environmental conditions slightly to create a stealth advantage.)

2. **Major Whisper (3 WP)**
    - **“That chest isn’t locked after all.”** (Changes a locked chest into an unlocked one.)
    - **“The guard hesitates—maybe I remind them of someone.”** (Influences an NPC’s reaction in a meaningful way.)
    - **“A gust of wind makes the enemy archer miss.”** (Directly affects a combat roll or event.)
    
3. **Critical Whisper (5 WP)**
    - **“That attack should have missed me—I was never standing there.”** (Retroactively changes character positioning in combat.)
    - **“I know this city—I’ve been here before.”** (Grants knowledge the character was previously unaware of.)
    - **“The enemy’s gun misfires at the worst possible time.”** (Directly alters an opponent’s action.)
    - **“I didn’t actually die. I survived—just barely.”** (Prevents a character’s death at the cost of permanent consequences.)
## **The Game Master’s Response: Counter-Whispers**

While players can **alter reality**, the **GM can push back** using **[[Counter-Whispers]]**, ensuring that **actions have weight and consequences**.

| **Counter-Whisper Type**         | **GM Response**                                                                                                                                                                    |
| -------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **[[Reinforce Reality]] (1 WP)** | Slightly negates or reduces the effect of a Whisper (e.g., the enemy still notices something is off).                                                                              |
| **[[Reality Pushback]] (3 WP)**  | Introduces an unexpected complication in response to a Whisper (e.g., using a Whisper to avoid an injury results in an environmental hazard instead).                              |
| **[[Fate Bends Back]] (5 WP)**   | The Whisper has **far-reaching consequences**, causing an **unintended chain reaction** (e.g., if the players Whisper away a fatal injury, another misfortune befalls them later). |

**The GM doesn’t just use Counter-Whispers as punishment**—they **balance the tension** between **player agency and world stability**.

### **Examples of Counter-Whispers**

1. **Reinforce Reality (1 WP)**
    - **Player: “I know this guard from a past mission.”**
    - **GM: “You do, but they only barely remember you.”**
    
2. **Reality Pushback (3 WP)**
    - **Player: “I make an impossible leap across the chasm.”**
    - **GM: “You land… but the force cracks the floor beneath you.”**
    
3. **Fate Bends Back (5 WP)**
    - **Player: “I was never hit by that sword strike.”**
    - **GM: “You dodged—but the attack landed on your ally instead.”**

## **The Whisper Engine’s Danger: Reality Rejection**

The **more frequently** players **bend reality**, the more the **world resists**. If Whispers are overused, the **GM can escalate the danger** using **[[Reality Rejection Effects]]**.

|**Accumulated Whisper Use**|**Effect**|
|---|---|
|**5+ Whispers used**|Minor reality distortions (players see strange patterns, objects move subtly when unobserved).|
|**10+ Whispers used**|The world actively resists (NPCs experience déjà vu, time loops briefly, and spells behave unpredictably).|
|**15+ Whispers used**|A powerful force notices the disturbance (an entity begins hunting those who tamper with fate).|

**Potential Reality Rejection Consequences:**
- **Time Warps**: The party relives moments slightly altered from before.
- **Echoing NPCs**: Conversations repeat unnaturally as if the world is trying to reset.
- **Hostile Entities**: Beings attuned to fate (e.g., Reapers, Chronomancers, Eldritch Observers) **detect the manipulations** and intervene.

# **The Psychological Cost of Bending Reality**

The **Whisper Engine** is not just a system for altering fate—it is a fundamental force embedded in the **FEARS System's** reality, intrinsically tied to a character's **Sanity** and **Memory**. Those who wield it walk a dangerous path between control and collapse.

Whispering to reality isn't merely a mechanical effect; it **strains the mind** as it **grapples with paradox**, forcing a character to rationalize something that **should not be**. As a result, **Sanity deteriorates**, and **Memory distorts**, creating a world where one's **own past may no longer be trustworthy**.

By balancing **Whisper Points (WP), Sanity, and Skill Memory**, the system ensures that **manipulating reality has meaningful psychological and narrative consequences**.

## **Sanity as a Core Attribute**

**[[Sanity]] is a full attribute, just like Strength or Dexterity**, representing a character’s **ability to reconcile contradictions in reality and maintain mental stability.**

It determines
- **How well a character resists the stress of bending reality.**
- **How much forced reality manipulation (F-WP) they can endure before mental instability takes hold.**
- **How resistant they are to hallucinations, false memories, and paradox-induced mental degradation.**
- **Their ability to distinguish between what is real and what is an illusion.**

**Formula:**
- **Sanity Saving Throws**: **1d20 + Sanity Modifier + Proficiency (if applicable)**
- **Whisper Resistance Check** (resisting the urge to alter fate): **1d20 + Sanity Modifier vs. DC 10 + F-WP Used**
- **Memory Skill Check**: **1d20 + Intelligence + Proficiency (if applicable)**

## **Forcing Whisper Points (F-WP): The Toll of Reality Manipulation**

When a character **runs out of [[Whisper Points]]**, they can still **force reality to shift**, but doing so **directly impacts their [[Sanity**]].

This is called **[[Forcing Whisper Points]] (F-WP)**—the act of **breaking past natural limits to alter fate at any cost**.

|**F-WP Used**|**Sanity Cost**|**Side Effect**|
|---|---|---|
|**1 F-WP**|-1 Sanity|Minor mental strain (headache, nausea, déjà vu).|
|**2 F-WP**|-2 Sanity|Vivid hallucinations, a sense of being watched.|
|**3 F-WP**|-4 Sanity|Reality flickers, memory gaps form (**Memory Skill Check required**).|
|**4+ F-WP**|-6+ Sanity|Time distorts, paranoia sets in, _Reality Rejection_ possible.|

### **Consequences of Losing Sanity**

- A character **cannot reduce their Sanity below 0**. If they attempt to, they suffer a **Reality Fracture** (detailed below).
- If **Sanity drops below 50% of its maximum**, the character begins to experience **persistent mental instability**:
    - **Paranoia:** Constantly believes they are being followed or watched.
    - **Hallucinations:** Seeing people who aren’t there, hearing whispers, feeling things that don’t exist.
    - **Unstable Perception:** **Passive Perception is permanently reduced by 2** due to **doubt in their own senses**.

## **Memory Skill Check: The Fragility of Reality**

**[[Memory]]** plays a **critical role** in the Whisper Engine because **forcing reality to bend comes at the cost of certainty**.

Whenever a character **forces 3 or more F-WP in a single scene**, they must make a **Memory Skill Check** to determine whether they **retain clear recollection of what they altered.**

**Formula:** **Memory Check = 1d20 + Intelligence Modifier + Proficiency (if proficient in Memory Checks)** (_Difficulty increases based on the degree of forced reality shifts._)

| **Memory Check Result** | **Outcome**                                                                          |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **20+**                 | Full memory clarity—no additional cost.                                              |
| **15-19**               | Faint recollection—some details become unreliable.                                   |
| **10-14**               | Memory distortion—other people remember things differently.                          |
| **5-9**                 | Significant memory gaps—the character forgets key moments.                           |
| **Below 5**             | _[[Paradox Event]]_—the GM introduces a new false memory, replacing an actual event. |

### **How Memory Loss Affects Gameplay**

1. **Narrative Confusion** → Players may receive **conflicting information from NPCs**, leading to **uncertainty about past events**.
2. **False Memories** → The GM may introduce **events that never happened**, making the character **question their reality**.
3. **Paradox Events** → If Memory Checks fail too often, **reality itself begins rewriting the past** in ways beyond the character’s control.

## **Reality Fractures: The Ultimate Price of Whispering Too Much**

If a character **drops their [[Sanity]] to 0**, they suffer a **[[Reality Fracture]]**—a **permanent mental and narrative consequence** that fundamentally alters their perception of the world.

**Roll 1d6 on the Reality Fracture Table:**

|**Roll**|**Reality Fracture Effect**|
|---|---|
|**1**|The character loses **all memory of the last session** (must be retold by allies).|
|**2**|An **NPC from the past no longer recognizes them**, as if they never met.|
|**3**|The character gains a **permanent flaw**, such as paranoia or intrusive hallucinations.|
|**4**|They experience **random time skips**, blacking out and reappearing in different places.|
|**5**|A **mysterious entity** (perhaps an eldritch force or cosmic being) takes notice of their existence.|
|**6**|_Temporal Bleed_—a past event is **rewritten**, altering a key detail in the campaign (_GM’s choice_).|

**The only way to recover from a Reality Fracture is to restore Sanity**—but some effects **may never fully heal**.

## **Earning Whisper Points & Restoring Sanity**

To prevent overuse of Forced Whispers, players must **seek opportunities to restore WP and Sanity.**

| **Recovery Method**                                           | **Whisper Points Restored**               | **Sanity Restored** |
| ------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ------------------- |
| **[[Short Rest]] (Meditation, Reflection)**                   |                                           | **1 Sanity**        |
| **[[Long Rest]]**                                             | `1 + (Sanity Modifier ÷ 2, rounded down)` | **- 2 Sanity**      |
| **Roleplaying a Personal Revelation**                         | **2 WP**                                  | **3 Sanity**        |
| **Psychological Healing (Therapy, Clerical Aid, Mind Magic)** | **Full WP Refresh**                       | **1d6 Sanity**      |
| **Spiritual Awakening (Religious/Fate-Driven Moments)**       | **Full WP Refresh**                       | **Full Sanity**     |

## **Tying It All Together: The Risk & Reward of Reality Manipulation**

The **Whisper Engine** is **not just a mechanic**—it is a **dynamic force** that shapes the **FEARS System's narrative**.

- **Whisper Points** → Represent controlled reality shifts—**limited but powerful**.
- **[[Sanity]]** → The cost of overusing reality manipulation—**forcing WP drains mental stability**.
- **Memory Skill Checks** → Determine whether reality remains stable—**failure leads to altered pasts and paradoxes**.
- **Reality Fractures** → The ultimate price—**warping reality too much creates permanent distortions in the world**
