### The Critical Truth About Current Baseline

**This is Worse Than It Appears:**

The 58/100 reading represents **unstable equilibrium**, not stable stress:

```
STABLE STRESSED STATE (40-50):
- Damage accumulates slowly
- Can be managed indefinitely
- Major disasters rare
- Society can adapt gradually

UNSTABLE STRESSED STATE (51-65) ← CURRENT
- Damage accelerates if pressured
- Minor crises trigger major cascades
- Balance extremely fragile
- Any significant shock causes collapse

WOUNDED CRITICAL STATE (66-80):
- Constant reality glitches
- Major cascades from minor shocks
- Society barely functional
- Another 5-point damage triggers 81+

COLLAPSING STATE (81-100):
- ASHKARRA CONDITIONS
- All-out apocalypse
- Civilization survival unlikely
- Reality unraveling visible to all
```

**Why This Matters for Campaign Start:**

- Players arrive when world is **already on edge**
- Most campaign events will **accelerate** damage rather than start it
- System has **only 42 points tolerance** before catastrophe
- Even "winning" an arc might trigger civilization collapse if players aren't careful
- Reality Stability becomes **mechanical threat** as dangerous as any BBEG

## PART FOUR: DIVINE RESPONSE PROTOCOL

### When & How Gods Intervene Based on Stability Damage

#### CRITICAL PRINCIPLE: Gods Are Bound By Pact

**The Post-Fall Divine Accord (Established Year 0 4A):**

After the Ashkarra catastrophe, surviving gods made binding agreement:

```
DIVINE NON-INTERFERENCE PACT

To prevent another Ashkarra, the Divine Spark pantheons agree:

1. Direct divine intervention forbidden
2. Gods may grant powers to mortals (clerics/paladins)
3. Gods may manifest avatars in extreme circumstance
4. Gods may NOT:
   - Fight each other in mortal realm
   - Directly alter reality
   - Compel mortal actions
   - Interfere with Thread-system directly

ENFORCEMENT:
- Violation weakens violator's connection to Pattern
- Repeated violation = expulsion from Elemental Glyphs
- Expulsion = loss of divine status (becomes mortal)

EXCEPTION:
- If Reality Stability reaches 81/100 (Collapsing), Pact is void
- Gods may intervene freely to prevent second Ashkarra
- Violators forgiven if catastrophe is prevented
```

**Why This Matters:**

Gods are literally forbidden from helping until situation is dire. They WANT to help but can't without breaking pact. This creates dramatic tension: "Why won't the gods save us?" Answer: "They literally cannot, they're bound by ancient agreement."

---

### Divine Response Escalation Table

**Gods react in predictable sequence as Reality Stability worsens**

#### STAGE 1: PACT HOLDS (0-60 Damage)
**Divine Behavior:** Strictly Non-Interventionist

**What Gods Actually Do:**
- Clerics and paladins receive normal divine powers
- Granted spells work normally
- BUT no miracles, no manifestations, no direct help
- Gods respond to prayers with silence (intentional, not ignorance)
- Divine messengers DO NOT appear

**Visible Evidence:**
- Temples functioning normally
- Priests can still heal
- Blessing ceremonies work fine
- BUT divine guidance completely absent
- Prayers for "save us" go unanswered

**Player Perception:** 
> "The gods are abandoning us. Where are they?"

**Actual Truth:** 
> "Gods WANT to help but Pact forbids it. Their silence is agony to them."

**How PCs Learn This:**
- Can interrogate Threadbearers
- Can read pre-Fall archives
- Can find priests who know the truth
- Can negotiate with gods (requires specific actions)

**NPC Divine Servants' Behavior:**
- Many clerics lose faith (if gods won't help, why believe?)
- Some become desperate (pushing for violation of Pact)
- Some become accepting (understanding the burden on gods)
- Some become angry (directing rage at gods for binding themselves)

**What Actually Happens:**

- Unexplained healings in temples

```
just HAPPEN to thwart the apocalypse, well... that's mortal choice, isn't it?"

Gods are finding technical loopholes.
```

**NPC Divine Servants' Behavior:**

## PART FIVE: CAMPAIGN MECHANICS AT EACH STABILITY LEVEL

### How to Use This in Play

#### Session 1-2: Stability 58/100 (STRESSED)
**GM Approach:**

- Reality feels mostly normal
- Make damage *abstract* ("archive sites are unstable")
- Players don't see direct damage yet
- Establish that "Reality Stability" is trackable metric
- Make first measurement visible

**Example Session 1 Opening:**
```
CAPTAIN OF THE WATCH: "We've been monitoring the archive sites.
The one near Persimmon Street—it's become too unstable.
Reality seems to... waver there. Phantoms. Echoes.

The Kel Varyan Council is concerned. They're asking scholars
to determine: Is it getting worse?

So we're hiring: Track the Persimmon Street Archive Site.
Determine if it's stabilizing or destabilizing.

If it's getting worse, we need to know. Because if all the
archive sites are destabilizing simultaneously... well.

Last city that had reality instability was Ashkarra. 
It didn't end well."
```

**End of Session 1:**
```
TRACKER RESULTS:
Persimmon Street Archive: +2 damage (worse than 2 weeks ago)
Other sites: Similar pattern observed (some +1, some +3)

Average Reality Stability Trend: -1 per day

At this rate: 23 days until Stability = 35/100 (critical concern)
Before that: Some visible manifestations in Month 4-5

CURRENT READING: 58/100 (STRESSED)
```

---

#### Session 3-4: Stability 55/100 (STRESSED TRENDING CRITICAL)
**GM Approach:**

- Make damage **visible** (phantom manifestations occur)
- Players realize they're in arms race against Reality
- Show that their actions directly affect metric
- First "damage cascade" happens

**Example During Session 3:**
```
You're investigating the conspiracy when you realize:
```

## CONCLUSION: REALITY AS MECHANIC

**The Key Innovation:**

Instead of abstract "world damage," Reality Stability is **mechanical**, **measurable**, and **consequential**.

- It increases with arc conflicts
- It decreases with player action
- It reaches breaking points with visible effects
- It triggers divine response at thresholds
- It creates apocalypse conditions that are **preventable but difficult**

**Gods aren't absent. They're bound. And as world crumbles, those bonds break.**

**Players don't fight gods. They negotiate with gods while fighting time itself.**

**Reality Stability is the final BBEG.**
