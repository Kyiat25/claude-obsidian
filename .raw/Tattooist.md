# The Tattooist

**Artists of Living Ink**

> *"No other class writes power directly into the bodies of their allies. No other class turns the DCS's location system into a source of strength rather than vulnerability."*

---

## The Body as Canvas

Every tattoo she applied was a decision. Not a decoration — a decision. This one on the torso meant her fighter took less damage at that location. This one on the arm meant her ally's grip became iron when it mattered. The ink was never aesthetic. It was engineering, written directly into flesh.

You are the Tattooist. Where other enchanters imbue objects, you imbue bodies. Your **Spellbangers** are the smallest expression of this — fingertip tattoos storing spell energy you release with a touch. Your **Sketch Tattoo** system lets you create magic tattoos for yourself and your entire party. Your **Inscribed Ward** means your ink doesn't just look powerful — it absorbs impacts, adding DR to every location you've marked.

In FEARS, your Momentum engine is your craft itself. **Etched Flow** at 1st level: whenever you activate a tattoo, apply Bad Ink, or use Art Loan during combat, gain **+1 Momentum**. Your art creates rhythm.

**Weapon Mastery:** Tattooists do not gain Weapon Mastery. Your power flows through ink and design, not through martial perfection.

---

## The Tattooist's Essence

You are the party's durable enhancer. You make everyone else better in ways that persist between encounters and accumulate across a campaign. **Art Loan** at 7th level lets you share your personal tattoos with allies during combat. **Flash of Art** as a reaction protects allies when they need it.

Your **Tattooist Style** at 3rd level divides into **Neo Traditional** (warrior-artists who apply volatile ink to weapons and blend spell and strike) and **Surrealism** (arcane experimenters who treat their own bodies as extreme canvases).

---

## Complexity Assessment: MODERATE (5/10)   

The Tattooist tracks:       
• Spellbangers applied (slot-equivalent, per level/type) 
• Tattoo Designs Known (growing list)
• Attunement slots occupied by tattoos (own + allies)   
• Inscribed Ward DR bonuses per tattooed body location 
• Class Reaction pool (PB uses per short rest)     

Recommended for players who enjoy enhancing party members, long-term investment in body-location DR, and reactive support play.

## Class Features

### Hit Points

- **Hit Dice:** 1d8 per Tattooist level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 8 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per Tattooist level after 1st

### Proficiencies

- **Armour:** Light armour
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons
- **Tools:** Painter's supplies, tattooist's tools, one type of artisan's tools of your choice
- **Saving Throws:** Constitution, Charisma
- **Skills:** Choose two from Arcana, History, Insight, Investigation, Medicine, Nature, and Religion

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) a dagger or (b) any simple weapon
- (a) a diplomat's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
- An enchanted sketchbook, tattooist's tools, and a set of artisan's inks

---

## Quick Build

To quickly create a Tattooist, follow these steps:

1. **Ability Scores:** Make Constitution your highest score, followed by Charisma.
2. **Lineage:** Any combination works; Human, Tiefling, and Dragonborn are thematic.
3. **Background:** Entertainer, Guild Artisan, or any background with Arcana or Performance.
4. **Combat Strategy:** Apply Bad Ink to the party's primary striker before combat. Use your reaction (Flash of Art) proactively when allies face Called Shots at unprotected locations. Sketch Tattoo assignments should prioritise the three most-damaged body locations your party exposes.

## Core Class Features

### Spellcasting (1st Level)

You create **spellbangers** — small magic tattoos on your fingertips storing spell energy, sketched during a long rest. Constitution is your spellcasting ability.

- **Spell Save DC** = 8 + PB + CON modifier
- **Spell Attack Modifier** = PB + CON modifier

**Spellbangers Known:** Start with two 1st-level spells from the Bard, Sorcerer, Warlock, or Wizard spell lists. The Tattooist table shows how many spellbangers of each level you can have applied.

**Applied Spellbangers:** Spellbangers cast at the lowest level, but you can expend multiple spellbangers of the same spell to cast at a higher level.

**Spellbangers and Others:** Spellbangers function only for you — they cannot be transferred and become inert if removed from your skin.

**Casting from Spellbangers:** Uses the spell's normal casting time. No material components unless they have a gold cost. If the spell requires concentration, you must concentrate.

---

### Sketchy Imitation (1st Level)

When you witness a spell being cast against you or an ally, you can observe and recreate it through artistic interpretation. When hit by a spell from the Bard, Sorcerer, Warlock, or Wizard lists, make an Arcana check (DC = 10 + spell level). On success, add that spell to your known spellbangers for 24 hours; after your next long rest, you may permanently learn it (replacing one spell you know).

---

### Etched Flow (1st Level)

Whenever you activate a tattoo, apply Bad Ink, or use Art Loan during combat, gain **+1 Momentum**. Your living art creates combat rhythm through every act of application.

---

### Sketch Tattoo (2nd Level)

You create magic tattoos — larger, more complex works than your fingertip spellbangers.

**Tattoo Designs Known:** When you gain this feature, learn four magic tattoo designs of Common rarity. When you gain a Tattooist level, replace two known designs with new ones of a rarity you can create.

**Sketching:** During a short or long rest, spend 1 hour sketching one of your known designs into your enchanted sketchbook. A creature can attune to the tattoo immediately after you complete the sketch. Magic tattoos count toward normal attunement limits.

**Tattoo Rarity Limits:** Common at 2nd level; Uncommon at 6th level; Rare at 14th level.

**Material Costs:** Creating a magic tattoo requires magically imbued inks and components at approximately half the tattoo's market value.

**Body Location:** When you apply a magic tattoo, choose its body location: Head, Neck, Chest, Back, Arms, or Legs. This choice affects Inscribed Ward and Anatomical Precision.

**Copying a Tattoo:** Spend 1 hour studying a magic tattoo on a creature or in another tattooist's sketchbook to copy it (spending the material components). Copied designs don't count against your total. Higher rarity copies require 3 hours and a CON check (DC 15 + 2 per rarity tier above your limit); failure wastes the materials.

---

### Inscribed Ward (2nd Level)

While attuned to at least one magic tattoo at a specific body location, gain **+1 DR** at that location. Stacks with worn armour DR.

| Tattoo Placement | DCS Body Location | DR Bonus |
|---|---|---|
| Head | Head | Head DR |
| Neck | Head | Head DR |
| Chest | Torso | Torso DR |
| Back | Torso | Torso DR |
| Arms | Arms | Arm DR |
| Legs | Legs | Leg DR |

This bonus applies only to you. Deep Inscription at 14th level extends this to tattoos you create for others.

---

### Tattooist Style (3rd Level)

Choose your artistic and combat approach: **Neo Traditional** (warrior-artist, volatile weapon ink, spell-and-strike fusion) or **Surrealism** (arcane experimenter, extreme self-modification, spellbanger empowerment). Your Style grants features at 3rd, 5th, 9th, and 15th levels.

---

### Bad Ink (3rd Level)

As a bonus action, apply volatile magical ink to a weapon you hold or a willing creature's weapon within 5 feet. On the next hit with that weapon, deal additional **acid damage** per charge applied:

- **1d6** per charge at 3rd level; **1d8** at 5th; **1d10** at 10th; **1d12** at 15th.

The ink vanishes after the hit (or after 1 hour if unused). The weapon counts as magical while Bad Ink is applied.

Charges equal to your CON modifier, regaining all on a long rest.

**Momentum:** When Bad Ink triggers on a hit, the wielder gains **+1 Momentum**.

---

### Anatomical Precision (5th Level)

**Called Shot Awareness:** When an enemy makes a Called Shot targeting a body location where you have an active magic tattoo, you have advantage on your Evasion roll against that attack.

**Tattoo Exploitation:** When you make a Called Shot against a creature with a visible magical effect, tattoo, or enchantment on the targeted location, reduce the Called Shot penalty by 1.

**Ally Protection:** When an ally bearing one of your tattoos is targeted by a Called Shot at the tattooed location, that ally gains +1 to their Evasion roll.

---

### Trust the Process (6th Level)

You learn three additional magic tattoo designs of Uncommon rarity. Sketch a magic tattoo in **30 minutes** rather than 1 hour during a rest. During a short or long rest, change a tattoo's body location without re-sketching (the ink shifts across the skin).

---

### Art Loan (7th Level)

With tattooist's tools in hand, use an action to transfer one of your magic tattoos to a willing creature within 5 feet that has an available attunement slot. They benefit from the tattoo as if attuned, including DCS location benefits based on placement.

The tattoo returns to you after a short or long rest, disappearing from the recipient's skin and reappearing on yours. A creature can only benefit from one Art Loan at a time.

**Momentum:** Using Art Loan during combat triggers Etched Flow (+1 Momentum).

---

### Counterfeiter (11th Level)

During a long rest, apply one spellbanger of **6th or 7th level**.

When you use Art Loan, retain a **faded copy** of the transferred tattoo. The copy provides half the tattoo's stated benefits (rounded down) to you while the original is loaned out. It disappears when the original returns.

---

### Tattoo Mastery (11th Level)

Once per long rest, activate any one of your tattoos without expending the normal resource cost (ink charges, attunement activation, etc.). Your mastery over living ink allows you to manifest its power through sheer will.

---

### Master's Touch (13th Level)

Gain a **+1 bonus to all saving throws** for each magic tattoo you are currently attuned to (maximum +3). Your ink reinforces not just your body but your will.

---

### Master Tattooist (14th Level)

You can now create magic tattoos of **Rare** rarity. Learn two additional Rare tattoo designs.

**Deep Inscription:** Tattoos you create for others now grant the recipient **+1 DR at the tattooed body location** — the same benefit your Inscribed Ward provides to you.

---

### Inked Resilience (15th Level)

When you suffer an Injury, choose to have one of your active tattoos absorb the impact. If you do: that tattoo becomes inactive until your next long rest; reduce the Injury severity by one step (Critical → Major, Major → Minor, Minor → negated); ignore any Momentum penalty from that Injury.

Once per long rest; twice per long rest from 18th level.

---

### Living Gallery (17th Level)

While you have at least 3 magic tattoos applied to your body:

**Adaptive Resistance:** Resistance to one damage type of your choice. Change this type on a long rest.

**Sympathetic Network:** All allies currently bearing one of your tattoos (via Art Loan or crafting) gain **+1 to Evasion rolls** while within 60 feet of you.

**Gallery Activation:** Once per long rest as an action, activate all your tattoos simultaneously for 1 minute — +2 to all saving throws; Inscribed Ward DR bonuses double; tattoos shed bright light in a 20-foot radius.

---

### Genius Talent (18th Level)

While you have at least 3 magic tattoos, attune to **2 additional magic items** beyond normal attunement limits. Apply one spellbanger of **8th or 9th level** during a long rest. Replace two known tattoo designs during a long rest (rather than only when levelling up).

---

### Heroic Boon (10th Level)

Choose one:

**Boon of Artistic Mastery:** Sketch a magic tattoo in **15 minutes** during a rest (half of 30 minutes). Inscribed Ward DR bonus increases to **+2 per tattooed body location**.

**Boon of Living Canvas:** Once per short rest, command all your tattoos into a defensive configuration — advantage on Evasion rolls for 1 minute (tattoos glow and move across your skin). When you use Art Loan, retain a **full copy** (both you and recipient benefit fully for the duration).

---

### Epic Boon (20th Level)

Choose one:

**Boon of the Eternal Artist:** Your magic tattoos become permanent — cannot be removed, dispelled, or suppressed except by your choice or *wish*. Critical Injuries no longer suppress your tattoos. You no longer age. Once per day, cast any spell of 7th level or lower from any class list by temporarily manifesting it as a tattoo on your skin (fades after casting).

**Boon of the Grand Design:** All allies within 30 feet bearing one of your tattoos gain **+1 to attack rolls, damage rolls, and saving throws** for each magic tattoo currently applied to your own body (maximum +3). Once per long rest, spend 10 minutes to create a temporary legendary tattoo (any Very Rare or lower magic tattoo effect at maximum potency; lasts 24 hours; no attunement required).

## Last Stand Options

When a Tattooist's final moment arrives, their art refuses to fade quietly.

**Rules for Last Stand:** Invoked when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, roll a death saving throw, or die outright. A free action requiring no reaction or consciousness. Once resolved, the character permanently dies.

---

### 1. Ink of Defiance

*Your tattoos ignite into blazing sigils, erupting outward in a defiant explosion.*

**Effect:**
- 30-foot radius blast centred on yourself.
- All hostile creatures make a CON save (DC = 10 + PB + CON modifier).
- Failure: 10d10 acid + force damage; blinded until end of their next turn.
- Success: half damage, not blinded.
- All allies within the blast radius gain **+3 Momentum** as your final act galvanises them.

---

### 2. Last Stroke of the Artist

*You drag your finger across your skin, carving one final design. It glows, then detonates.*

**Effect:**
- Immediately cast any one spell you know through your spellbangers, ignoring components, spell level restrictions, or casting time.
- The spell is cast at its highest possible level.
- After the spell resolves, you die permanently even if the spell would heal or stabilise you.

---

### 3. Eternal Canvas

*Your tattoos animate, leaping from your flesh onto your companions. Your art lives through them.*

**Effect:**
- Choose up to three allies within 30 feet. Each receives one of your magic tattoos or active spellbangers of your choice.
- These tattoos function for them as if their own for 10 minutes.
- Each ally also gains **+2 to Evasion rolls and saving throws** as your protective designs wrap their skin.
- Each ally gains **+2 Momentum** as your legacy surges through them.
- When the effect ends, the tattoos fade, leaving faint scars in memory of your gift.

| Last Stand Option | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Ink of Defiance** | 30-ft acid/force explosion; blind enemies; +3 Momentum to allies. | Surrounded by enemies. |
| **Last Stroke of the Artist** | Any spell at max level, no components, no restrictions. | Climactic magical moment. |
| **Eternal Canvas** | Transfer tattoos to allies for 10 minutes; +2 Evasion and saves each. | Party survival and empowerment. |

## Class Reactions

Tattooists respond to threats with instinctive bursts of protective ink. These reactions use the **shared Class Reaction pool equal to your Proficiency Bonus**, refreshing on short or long rest.

### Inkguard (2nd Level)

**Trigger:** A creature you can see hits you with an attack.
**Effect:** Your tattoos animate and shift your body position. Gain a bonus to your Evasion equal to your Proficiency Bonus until end of the current turn. If this raises your Evasion above the attack roll, the attack retroactively misses.

*Cost: 1 Class Reaction use.*

---

### Ink Flare (3rd Level)

**Trigger:** A creature within 10 feet hits you with a melee attack.
**Effect:** Expend 1 spellbanger. The attacker makes a DEX save (DC = Spell Save DC) or take **3d6 acid damage** and have disadvantage on their next attack roll. On success: half damage, no disadvantage.

*Cost: 1 Class Reaction use + 1 spellbanger. Scaling: 4d6 at 11th level; 5d6 at 17th level.*

---

### Flash of Art (7th Level)

**Trigger:** A creature you can see targets an ally within 30 feet with an attack.
**Effect:** Your tattoos blaze with protective light. The targeted ally gains **+2 to Evasion** and **resistance to the triggering damage type** for that attack. If the attack still hits, reduce the damage by your **PB + CON modifier** (applied after resistance).

*Cost: 1 Class Reaction use. Scaling: at 15th level, ally also gains +2 to all saving throws until start of your next turn.*
