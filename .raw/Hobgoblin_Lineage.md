# Hobgoblin
## Lineage

> *"They cast us out without law. So we became Law itself—sharper than any blade they wielded against us."*  
> — **Ravva's First Command**, Chain Ascendant Scripture

You are Hobgoblin. Not mistake, not chaos—**perfected rejection**. Your people do not kneel before gods but stand beside them as equals who earned authority through discipline. Where others see abandoned fragments, you see **the forge that makes unbreakable steel**. Where they see divine rejection, you see the test that proves only the worthy survive.

You were not born to be forgotten. You were born to **prove that order needs no divine blessing**, to demonstrate that discipline transcends divinity, to show that the rejected can build empires stronger than those who cast them out. Your ancestors survived not through cunning or mockery but through **iron discipline, tactical perfection, and the absolute certainty that law forged in adversity outshines law granted by gods**. Now you carry that legacy in your blood, your formation, your certainty that **perfection is the only answer to rejection**.

---

## The Chain That Bound Itself

According to the **Faith of the Gnawing Crown**, hobgoblins represent **the echo of divine law, warped into militaristic order**. When the Fragment was cast out, part of it remembered structure, hierarchy, command. That memory became you—**the attempt to prove that order doesn't require gods**.

Hobgoblins believe you are not lesser reflections of divine law but **perfected versions** of it. The gods gave law to their chosen peoples. You forged law from nothing, in defiance of divine silence. Every command you follow, every formation you hold, every oath you bind is proof that **discipline transcends divinity**.

The Chain Ascendants teach that Ravva—whether goddess, ideal, or collective will—is **Law Unacknowledged**. The gods refused to see hobgoblin order as legitimate. So you made it **undeniable**.

---

## Life Among the Legions

You were raised knowing that **formation is sacred**, that every order obeyed is an act of war against chaos, that your worth is measured not in individual glory but in **how perfectly you fulfill your role**. Hobgoblin culture treats existence as military operation, every breath a step in formation.

Your lifespan is roughly **80 years**—longer than goblins but still mortal. You measure time not in years but in **campaigns completed and formations held**, measuring worth not in age but in **rank earned and oaths kept**.

Every significant achievement is recorded in **Chain-Runes**—marks of command etched into armor, skin, or weaponry. These aren't mere decorations; they're **proof of order maintained** against a chaotic universe that wanted you erased.

**Discipline** is sacred among your people. Not blind obedience, but **tactical perfection**—every soldier knowing their role so completely that the legion moves as one entity. To break formation isn't merely failure; it's **spiritual apostasy**.

## Physical Characteristics

**Age:** You mature by age 14, reaching full adulthood by 18. Most hobgoblins don't die of old age—you die in battle, in defense of formation, or by choosing honorable execution when you can no longer serve.

**Size:** You stand between 5½ to 6½ feet tall, with builds designed for endurance under armor and maintaining shield walls. Your size is Medium.

**Speed:** Your base walking speed is 30 feet. Your stride is measured, disciplined—every step in formation with those beside you.

**Build:** Powerfully built with broad shoulders, thick necks, and muscular frames. Your skin ranges from dark orange-red to deep gray-brown, sometimes with reddish or orange undertones. Your features are angular and stern, your eyes carrying tactical assessment. Your body is built for wearing heavy armor for extended periods and maintaining formation under fire.

## Hobgoblin Traits

As a Hobgoblin, you possess the following lineage traits:

### **Darkvision**
You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

Your ancestors learned to fight in the forgotten places, maintaining formation even in total darkness.

### **Martial Training**
You have proficiency with light armor, medium armor, and shields. Additionally, you have proficiency with two martial weapons of your choice.

This isn't cultural—it's **biological memory**. Hobgoblin children instinctively understand stance, grip, formation spacing. You were born knowing how to hold a line.

### **Saving Face**
When you miss with an attack roll or fail an ability check or saving throw, you can gain a bonus to the roll equal to the number of allies you can see within 30 feet of you (maximum bonus of +5).

You can use this trait a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, regaining all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

This isn't pride—it's **formation consciousness**. When your allies witness your attempt, their presence strengthens your resolve. The legion makes each soldier more than they are alone.

> *"I missed the first strike. My shield-brother's eyes reminded me: I am not alone. I am the legion. The second strike didn't miss."*

### **Fortune from the Many**
When you use Saving Face, you can also grant the bonus to one ally who can see you, instead of taking it yourself. That ally must use the bonus on their next attack roll, ability check, or saving throw within the next minute.

Your discipline inspires those around you, spreading the strength of formation.

### **Tactical Discipline**
You have proficiency in one of the following skills: Insight, Intimidation, or Perception. Additionally, when you take the Help action to aid an ally's attack, you can move up to 10 feet before or after helping without provoking opportunity attacks, and the ally gains advantage on their next attack roll against the target you helped with (not just the first attack before your next turn).

Your training emphasizes reading the battlefield and coordinating strikes with precision.

### **Formation Fighter**
When you are within 5 feet of an ally, you gain +1 to AC and attack rolls. This bonus increases to +2 if you are within 5 feet of two or more allies.

You fight best when you fight together. The legion is stronger than any individual warrior.

## Mental Attributes & Psychology

**Focus Modifier:** +1  
**Sanity Modifier:** +0

Your biology grants you **exceptional mental discipline**. Your Focus bonus represents generations of tactical training encoded into your very consciousness. You maintain concentration, hold formation under pressure, and execute complex maneuvers without breaking stride.

### **Cultural Mental Conditioning**
Beyond your biological advantage, Hobgoblin upbringing creates:

- **Tactical clarity:** You process battlefield chaos as ordered information
- **Formation dependence:** Fighting alone feels fundamentally wrong
- **Oath-weight:** Breaking promises causes genuine psychological distress
- **Hierarchy instinct:** You instinctively seek chain of command

### **Corruption Vulnerability: The Broken Chain**

As a Hobgoblin, you have a unique relationship with Corruption. Your disciplined mind provides strong defenses, but corruption that enters through **broken oaths, shattered formations, or betrayed trust** affects you catastrophically.

**When you gain Corruption Score 10+ (Tier 2), you suffer:**
- Command Confusion: Orders become harder to follow or give
- Formation Sickness: Being near allies causes discomfort
- Oath-Strain: Keeping promises requires conscious effort

**Shattered Chain Table (Corruption Tier 2+)**

When you complete a long rest at Corruption Score 10+, roll 1d6:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **False Command:** You remember receiving an order you never got. Make a DC 15 Wisdom save or believe it's real and feel compelled to follow it. |
| 2 | **Chain-Deafness:** You can no longer sense the presence of your allies. You lose the benefits of Formation Fighter until your next long rest. |
| 3 | **Link Rejection:** Your body rejects disciplined movement. Take 1d4 psychic damage and have disadvantage on attack rolls when within 5 feet of allies until your next long rest. |
| 4 | **Weight of Chaos:** You feel crushing certainty that order is meaningless. Make a DC 12 Sanity save or lose 1d4 Sanity. |
| 5 | **Eldritch Command:** Something tries to give you orders. Gain 1 Whisper Point but your next use of Saving Face fails automatically. |
| 6 | **Chain Holds:** Your disciplined mind resists corruption this night. Reduce your Corruption Score by 1 (minimum 10). |

## Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Integration

### **Evasion Profile: Shielded Tactician**
- **Dodge Evasion:** +0 (you don't dodge; you position)
- **Endure Evasion:** +1 (you weather attacks through armor and stance)
- **Deflect Evasion:** +1 (you turn blows with shield and tactical positioning)

**Tactical Note:** You are designed for **formation combat with mutual support**. Formation Fighter provides bonuses when near allies, while Saving Face turns misses into hits when witnessed. You're most effective when fighting as part of a unit.

### **Natural Damage Reduction:** +0
You don't have natural armor, but your Martial Training provides proficiency with armor and shields from birth. Your protection comes from **equipment and formation** rather than biology.

### **Combat Philosophy**
Hobgoblins fight with **coordinated precision and tactical flexibility**. You leverage Formation Fighter for bonuses near allies, use Saving Face to turn failures into successes when witnessed, and rely on Martial Training for heavy armor and tactical weapons. You fight like you live: **in formation, with purpose, as part of something greater**.

### **Breaking Point Mechanics**
Due to your disciplined training and formation consciousness, you have **advantage on Rally checks** when fighting with allies but **disadvantage when fighting alone**. You are exceptionally resilient as part of a unit but psychologically struggle in isolation.

Additionally, if your unit's **leader falls** or your formation is **broken**, you must make immediate Rally checks at disadvantage.

## Languages

**Languages:** You can speak, read, and write Common and Goblin.

**Goblin** in your mouth is different from how other goblinoids speak it. Where goblins cackle and bugbears growl, hobgoblins speak with **military precision**—every word crisp, every command clear. You use Goblin as a tactical language, stripping it of chaos and reforging it into something approaching divine law.

When Hobgoblins speak of sacred matters, they use **Chain-Tongue**—a formalized military dialect of Goblin where every phrase has tactical implications and spiritual weight.

## Hobgoblin Subraces

While all Hobgoblins share the Chain philosophy, different legions have specialized over generations through their tactical doctrines. Choose one of the following subraces:

### **Ravvan Legionnaire** *(Iron Oath)*
You descend from the core legions who follow **Ravva's Code** with absolute dedication, believing that perfect discipline is itself divine.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Constitution score increases by 2, and your Intelligence score increases by 1.

**Iron Oath:** When you make an oath or promise, you can bind it with iron will. Choose one oath to have active at a time. While you honor this oath, you gain one of the following benefits:

- **Oath of Protection:** Gain +2 to AC when within 5 feet of an ally you swore to protect
- **Oath of Vengeance:** Gain +2 to damage rolls against a creature or group you swore to defeat
- **Oath of Duty:** Gain advantage on saving throws while completing a task you swore to finish

If you break this oath, you lose all benefits of this feature until you complete a redemption act (GM's discretion).

**Legion Tactics:** You can use the Help action as a bonus action a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest. Additionally, when you use Help, the range increases to 10 feet.

**Disciplined Mind:** You have advantage on saving throws against being frightened or charmed. Your discipline is unbreakable.

### **Emberscale Legionnaire** *(Ashborn Avenger)*
You descend from legions who survived the fall of Ashkarra and turned that trauma into tactical rage—disciplined fury that burns with purpose.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Constitution score increases by 2, and your Strength score increases by 1.

**Ashborn Fury:** Once per short rest, when you hit with a melee attack, you can add fire damage equal to your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier to the damage roll. This fire damage increases by 1d6 at 5th level, 2d6 at 11th level, and 3d6 at 17th level.

**Scar Formation:** You have resistance to fire damage. Additionally, scars from fire damage don't fade—you wear them with pride as proof of survival.

**Burning Advance:** When you take the Dash action, you can move through enemy spaces (though you can't end your turn there) and deal fire damage equal to your Constitution modifier to any enemy whose space you move through. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

### **Steelroot Legionnaire** *(Siege Master)*
You descend from legions who specialized in **fortification, siege warfare, and defensive doctrine**—proving that hobgoblin order excels at both attack and defense.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Constitution score increases by 2, and your Wisdom score increases by 1.

**Siege Training:** You have proficiency with mason's tools and carpenter's tools. Additionally, you can analyze any structure and determine its weak points with a DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) check, granting your allies advantage on attack rolls against that structure for 1 hour.

**Fortified Stance:** As a bonus action, you can enter a fortified stance until you move. While in this stance:
- You cannot be moved against your will
- You have resistance to the first instance of damage you take each round
- Allies within 5 feet of you gain +1 to AC

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

**Shield Brother:** When an ally within 5 feet of you is hit by an attack, you can use your reaction to grant them +2 to AC against that attack (potentially turning a hit into a miss). If the attack still hits, you take half the damage instead of your ally.

### **Nightmarch Legionnaire** *(Shadow Tactical)*
You descend from legions who learned to fight in darkness and silence, proving that discipline doesn't require daylight or noise.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Dexterity score increases by 2, and your Intelligence score increases by 1.

**Tactical Stealth:** You have proficiency in Stealth. Additionally, when you and at least one ally both have advantage on a Stealth check, you both gain +5 to the roll (disciplined silence).

**Shadow Formation:** You can communicate with allies within 30 feet using hand signals, completely silently. Additionally, when you take the Hide action while within 5 feet of an ally, you both gain advantage on the Stealth check.

**Night Strike:** You have advantage on attack rolls against creatures that haven't taken a turn in combat yet. Additionally, if you and an ally both attack the same target on the first round of combat, you both gain +1d6 damage on your attacks.

## Naming Conventions

Hobgoblin names reflect **rank, achievement, and tactical doctrine**—designed to be clear in the chaos of battle and to encode military history.

### **Structure:**
- **Given Name:** Your birth name, often harsh and militaristic
- **Rank-Name:** Your military rank or specialty
- **Legion Name:** Your unit or tactical philosophy

### **Examples:**

**Given Names:**

*Male-pattern:* Azrok, Durak, Gorthak, Kazrak, Morvak, Thraak, Volkar, Zarak

*Female-pattern:* Korvaa, Drakka, Geshna, Nazra, Thrakka, Vorgga, Zartha

*Rank-neutral:* Many hobgoblins use gender-neutral names within the legion

**Rank-Names:**
- Ironshield (shield wall specialist)
- Flameheart (survived Ashkarra)
- Siegebreaker (fortification specialist)
- Shadowblade (night tactics specialist)
- Oathkeeper (maintains group morale)
- Chainbinder (chaplain of Ravva)
- Tactician (strategic command)

**Legion Names:**
- of the Iron Chain (Ravvan core legion)
- of the Burning Scale (Ashkarra survivors)
- of the Stone Wall (defensive specialists)
- of the Silent March (stealth legion)
- of the Broken Tower (siege warfare)
- of the First Oath (founding legion)

**Full Name Examples:**
- **Thraak Ironshield of the Iron Chain** (male shield wall specialist)
- **Korvaa Flameheart of the Burning Scale** (female Ashkarra survivor)
- **Azrok Shadowblade of the Silent March** (male night tactics expert)
- **Zartha Oathkeeper of the First Oath** (female spiritual leader)

### **Name Practices:**
- Hobgoblins earn rank-names through achievement, not birth
- Legion names are sacred—abandoning your legion means losing your name
- Some hobgoblins add numerical designators (Thraak-7, indicating seventh of that name in the legion)
- Titles stack with additional achievements (Azrok Shadowblade Siegebreaker of the Silent March)

## Playing a Hobgoblin

### **Core Fantasy:**
You are the disciplined soldier, the tactical genius, the living proof that order needs no divine permission. You don't survive through chaos or climb through individual glory—you **build something greater than yourself** through perfect coordination with those who fight beside you.

### **Mechanical Identity:**
- **Formation Fighter:** Bonuses when near allies make you strongest in groups
- **Support Multiplier:** Saving Face and Fortune from the Many help entire party
- **Tactical Commander:** Helping allies is core to your function
- **Disciplined Defense:** Armor proficiency + Endure/Deflect Evasion for tanking
- **Mental Fortress:** +1 Focus makes you excellent at maintaining concentration

### **Roleplaying Hooks:**
- **The Oathbound:** Following a sacred promise that led you adventuring
- **The Exile:** Cast out for breaking formation, seeking redemption
- **The Survivor:** Last of your legion, building a new one
- **The Bridge:** Teaching others that hobgoblins are more than monsters
- **The Tactician:** Using adventuring to develop the Perfect Strategy
- **The Questioner:** Starting to doubt if Ravva's way is the only way
- **The Inheritor:** Carrying the chain-runes of a fallen commander

### **Party Dynamics:**
Other races often misunderstand Hobgoblins as rigid or unimaginative. This creates opportunities for:

- **Tactical leadership** - Your training makes you natural battlefield commander
- **Reliable support** - Formation Fighter and Saving Face help entire party
- **Cultural education** - Show that discipline enables freedom, not restricts it
- **Unexpected flexibility** - Prove that tactics means adapting, not being inflexible
- **Protective instinct** - Your formation training makes you shield weaker allies

### **Common Character Arcs:**
- From rigid doctrine to adapted principles
- From formation-dependent to understanding individual worth
- From oath-slave to oath-chooser
- From building empire to building family
- From proving gods wrong to not caring what gods think
- From perfect soldier to complete person

## Design Philosophy

The Hobgoblin lineage is designed to support:

1. **The Tactical Commander** (Formation Fighter + Saving Face + Help action)
2. **The Disciplined Warrior** (Martial Training + armor + Focus bonus)
3. **The Team Multiplier** (Fortune from the Many + tactical bonuses)
4. **The Oath-Keeper** (rich honor framework for RP)
5. **The Proof of Order** (mechanically strongest when fighting with allies)

Your mechanical abilities (Formation Fighter, Saving Face, Martial Training, Focus bonus) create a **tactical support fighter who makes everyone around them better**. Unlike goblins who survive through chaos or Goliaths who endure through individual will, you **triumph through perfect coordination**.

The subrace options allow you to lean into different legion specializations—from iron oath-keepers (Ravvan) to ashborn avengers (Emberscale) to siege masters (Steelroot) to shadow tacticians (Nightmarch)—while maintaining the core Hobgoblin identity of disciplined military excellence.

## Final Notes

### **The Weight of Formation:**
Every Hobgoblin carries the pressure of the chain. Your character likely knows:
- Your legion's complete history and battle honors
- The names of every commander in your chain
- Multiple tactical formations by heart
- The circumstances of Ravva's legendary victories

This isn't burden—it's **structure**. You fight knowing your role in something greater.

### **Respect Through Fear:**
Modern Dór-o Estel respects hobgoblin martial prowess even while fearing it:
- Military commanders study hobgoblin tactics
- Some nations hire hobgoblin mercenary legions
- The Faith of the Bound Pattern cites hobgoblins as examples of order without gods (though they consider it blasphemous)
- Even those who hate hobgoblins acknowledge their discipline

### **The Chain That Will Not Break:**
Your people believe the Chain Ascendants will one day build a Perfect Legion that forces the Pattern itself to acknowledge hobgoblin truth. When that happens, the gods will face an army that needs no divine blessing.

You adventure knowing this destiny. Some work to build it. Some work to prepare for it. Some work to transcend it.

But all hobgoblins carry the same certainty: **We were rejected by divine law. So we became Law itself—sharper, stronger, and undeniable.**
