# The Investigator

**Supernatural Detectives of Arcane Precision**

> *"Every creature has a system. Every system has a failure point. Find it. Press it."*

---

## The Method

He didn't attack the vampire. He watched it. Forty seconds — that was all he needed. By then he knew which leg it favoured, how it reacted when flanked, and what it did when a strike landed on its left side. When he finally moved, he wasn't guessing anymore. He was executing a conclusion.

You are the Investigator. Where other classes ask how to fight something, you ask how to *unmake* it. **Exploit Vulnerability** turns observation into precision; your studied targets take more damage and suffer conditions tailored to their exact weaknesses. **Mythis and Legends** means you're never surprised by what bloodied enemies do — you studied them beforehand.

In FEARS, your Momentum engine is analytical. **Exploit Momentum** at 3rd level grants +1 Momentum whenever you designate a Quarry or successfully exploit a weakness — your tactical analysis creates combat rhythm as effectively as raw aggression.

---

## The Investigator's Essence

You are a burst striker, skill master, and supernatural specialist. **Finisher** at 10th level makes you devastating against bloodied targets — your study of *how* creatures fail grants you extra dice when striking below 50%. Against the supernatural specifically, you become increasingly resistsant and eventually capable of banishing entire categories of entities.

You struggle in chaotic fights where you cannot observe before acting. You excel in prepared encounters and against recurring enemy types you've had time to study.

---

## Complexity Assessment: HIGH (7/10) 

The Investigator tracks:  
• Ritual Level and Grimoire spells  
• Rushed Incantation uses (INT modifier, scaling)  
• Exploit Vulnerability uses (INT modifier per long rest) 
• Bloodied Analysis check results per studied creature
• Class Reaction pool (PB uses per short rest) 

Recommended for experienced players who enjoy analytical play, building information advantages, and burst damage against prepared targets.

## Class Features

### Hit Points

- **Hit Dice:** 1d8 per Investigator level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 8 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per Investigator level after 1st

### Proficiencies

- **Armour:** Light armour, medium armour
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons, hand crossbows, heavy crossbows, longswords, rapiers, shortswords
- **Tools:** One gaming set of your choice
- **Saving Throws:** Dexterity, Intelligence
- **Skills:** Choose three from Arcana, Athletics, Crafting, Deception, History, Insight, Intimidation, Investigation, Medicine, Memory, Nature, Perception, Persuasion, Religion, Resolve, Sleight of Hand, Stealth, and Streetwise

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- Leather armour
- A dagger and either (a) a longsword or (b) a rapier
- Either (a) a heavy crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) a hand crossbow and 20 bolts
- Either (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) a priest's pack
- A grimoire and a component pouch

---

## Quick Build

To quickly create an Investigator, follow these steps:

1. **Ability Scores:** Make Intelligence your highest score, followed by Dexterity.
2. **Lineage and Heritage:** Any combination works; Human, Gnome, and Half-Elf are thematic.
3. **Background:** Sage, Acolyte, or any background with Investigation or Arcana.
4. **Combat Strategy:** Study your primary target as a bonus action before attacking. Use your Exploit Vulnerability bonus damage and effect on the first major hit. Save Finisher for when the target crosses the bloodied threshold.

## Core Class Features

### Expertise (1st and 6th Level)

Choose two skill proficiencies. Double your Proficiency Bonus for ability checks using them. At 6th level, choose two more.

---

### Ritualist (1st Level)

You maintain a **Grimoire** of ritual spells. You start with three ritual spells of 1st level and add more as shown by your Ritual Level in the table. You can inscribe new rituals you encounter into your Grimoire by spending time and materials. Ritual spells can be cast without spell slots (they take 10 minutes to cast).

---

### Myths and Legends (2nd Level)

You have an almost preternatural ability to research creatures and forgotten lore. You have advantage on all checks to predict Bloodied effects from your Exploit Vulnerability feature, and automatically learn a target's Frailty when you complete the universal Research Bonus process. If you Study a creature already bloodied, immediately learn its Death Throes without a check.

---

### Rushed Incantation (2nd Level)

Cast any ritual spell in your Grimoire with a 1-action casting time as a **bonus action** — bypassing the normal 10-minute casting time. Material components worth 100 gp or less are not required. Uses equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1), scaling upward as shown in the table.

---

### Exploit Vulnerability (2nd Level)

As a **bonus action**, study a creature within 30 feet. Make an Investigation or Perception check (DC = 8 + target's DEX modifier + their PB). On success, your next attack against them before end of your next turn deals extra damage:

- 2d6 at 2nd level, 3d6 at 5th, 4d6 at 10th, 5d6 at 15th, 6d6 at 20th.

Additionally choose one effect: **Disrupt Evasion** (disadvantage on their next Evasion roll until end of your next turn), **Off-Balance** (DEX save or knocked prone/staggered, granting advantage on attacks until start of your next turn), or **Expose Weak Point** (DR halved against your attack and the next attack it receives before start of your next turn).

**Bloodied Analysis (Additional Check):** When you successfully study a creature, attempt to predict its bloodied effects with an Investigation or Medicine check:
- DC 12 + CR/2: learn one bloodied effect of your choice.
- DC 15 + CR/2: learn two effects.
- DC 18 + CR/2: learn all four effects.
- Natural 20: automatically learn all four effects with exact mechanics; gain advantage on your next attack against the creature.

At 6th level, study two creatures simultaneously. At 14th level, Exploit Vulnerability ignores target immunities to conditions this feature imposes.

Uses equal to your Intelligence modifier per long rest.

---

### Exploit Momentum (3rd Level)

When you designate a creature as your Quarry or successfully exploit a weakness, gain **+1 Momentum**. Your tactical analysis creates openings that enhance your combat effectiveness.

---

### Occult Specialisation (3rd Level, and 6th, 10th, 14th Level)

Choose an area of expertise: **Antiquarian**, **Archivist**, **Detective**, **Exterminator**, **Inquisitor**, **Medium**, or **Occultist**. Each grants unique abilities at levels 3, 6, 10, and 14.

---

### Exploit Weakness (5th Level)

Once per turn, when you hit a creature with a weapon attack, treat it as having vulnerability to the weapon's damage type. This applies only to the weapon's base damage roll.

**Bloodied Exploitation:** When you use this feature on a bloodied creature whose Frailty you have identified, choose one additional effect: **Compound Weakness** (maximise DR reduction or Evasion penalty of the Frailty), **Tactical Exposure** (all allies within 60 feet learn the Frailty; next ally to attack gains your Research Bonus benefits), or **Analytical Counter** (instead of bonus damage, counter one of the creature's bloodied Strength effects).

---

### Weapon Mastery (5th Level)

At 5th level, gain Weapon Mastery with one simple or finesse weapon. When you hit with that weapon, apply one Weapon Skill once per turn.

**DCS Synergy:** Weapon Skills that reduce enemy Evasion or expose body locations create openings for your Exploit Vulnerability bonus damage and Finisher.

---

### Forensic Called Shot (5th Level)

When you make a Called Shot against your designated Quarry, reduce the penalty to your attack roll by an amount equal to your Proficiency Bonus. At 5th level (PB +3), a Called Shot to the arm (normally −2) becomes a +1 bonus to hit.

---

### Forensic Upgrade (6th Level)

When you use Exploit Vulnerability, study up to two creatures simultaneously. Attempt Bloodied Analysis for both (separate checks). If you've completed the Research Bonus against either creature, the enhanced duration applies to each target independently.

---

### Opportunistic Piety (7th Level)

Choose one of three effects (once per short rest):

- **Banish:** Cast *banishment*.
- **Miracle Healing:** Heal a creature for twice your Investigator level.
- **Warding:** Trace a holy symbol dealing radiant damage to enemies attacking the warded creature.

---

### Supernatural Resolve (9th Level)

Gain resistance to necrotic and psychic damage. Immunity to being charmed or possessed.

At 15th level, also gain resistance to radiant and thunder damage.

---

### Finisher (10th Level)

Once per turn, when you hit a creature with a weapon attack that is bloodied (at or below 50% HP) and you apply Exploit Weakness, deal **4d10 bonus damage** (instead of the normal Exploit Weakness damage).

**Death Throes Denial:** When you reduce a bloodied creature to 0 HP with a Called Shot to a vital area, the creature must make a CON save (DC = 8 + INT modifier + PB) or its Death Throes don't trigger.

At 17th level, the bonus damage increases to 6d8.

---

### Heroic Boon (10th Level)

Choose one:

**Boon of the Occult Savant:** Double your Proficiency Bonus for INT checks involving creatures, spells, or magical effects when uncovering information. When you successfully identify creature weaknesses, allies gain +1d6 damage against that creature for 1 minute. Once per short rest, swap a spell in your Grimoire with another Investigator spell of equal or lower level.

**Boon of Paranormal Fortitude:** Resistance to psychic and necrotic damage; cannot be frightened or charmed. Allies within 10 feet cannot be surprised and gain a bonus to initiative rolls equal to your INT modifier.

---

### Foreseen Harm (11th Level)

Once per short rest, when you would suffer a Major Injury, downgrade it to a Minor Injury. Your analytical mind recognised the danger in time to minimise the impact.

---

### Enigma Arcane (12th Level)

Your grimoire expands with a higher-circle ritual. Select a new ritual from any spell list of a level equal to your Ritual Level. This does not count against your normal rituals. At 15th level and 18th level, select one additional spell (can be from any spell list; this spell can be cast once per long rest without a ritual, using your spell save DC).

---

### Esoteric Knowledge (13th Level)

Choose two additional skills to gain proficiency in. If already proficient in these skills, double your Proficiency Bonus for checks using them.

---

### Bloodied Mastery (14th Level)

When you attempt to predict bloodied effects, roll with advantage (stacks with Myths and Legends for near-certain success on lower DCs). Once per long rest, automatically succeed on a Bloodied effect prediction check, learning all four effects.

When using Exploit Vulnerability on a bloodied creature, choose two of the three Bloodied Exploitation options instead of one. Your Analytical Counter duration extends to 2 rounds. You and all allies gain immunity (not just advantage) to predicted Death Throes effects. Your Death Throes Denial save DC increases by +2.

---

### Exorcist (17th Level)

You can cast *protection from evil and good* at will.

---

### Bonus Grimoire Spell (19th Level)

Add one spell of your choice from any spell list to your Grimoire, regardless of its ritual status. This spell must be of a level you can cast using the Ritual Level column.

---

### Epic Boon (20th Level)

Choose one:

**Boon of the Eternal Hunter:** Choose three spells from the Investigator spell list — cast each once per long rest without components. When you use Exploit Weakness on a bloodied creature, they take an additional 8d10 damage (replacing Finisher damage). If you've identified the creature's Frailty, this damage ignores all DR.

**Boon of the Apex Hunter:** Advantage on all ability checks to track, identify, or recall information about creatures. Study a creature as a bonus action (instead of an action); learn one additional vulnerability, resistance, or immunity when you study. When you use Finisher on a bloodied creature, deal force damage (bypasses resistance) and increase the bonus by 4d10. Choose to trigger or suppress Death Throes when you reduce the target to 0 HP. Fiends, undead, and aberrations at 0 HP must make a WIS save (DC = your spell save DC) or be banished to their home plane for 1d4 rounds. Once per long rest, declare an attack as an Inevitable Strike — automatic hit, ignores resistances and immunities, deals maximum damage, cannot be prevented by reactions.

## Last Stand Options

An Investigator's Last Stand embodies their ability to exploit weaknesses, manipulate the battlefield, and ensure their knowledge endures.

**Rules for Last Stand:** Invoked when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, roll a death saving throw, or die outright. A free action requiring no reaction or consciousness. Once resolved, the character permanently dies.

---

### 1. Exploit Mortality

*Your final moments are spent unravelling every flaw, every weakness.*

**Effect:**
- Choose up to 3 creatures within 60 feet.
- Make an Investigation check (DC = 8 + target's DEX modifier + their PB) for each.
- On success: the target gains vulnerability to all damage types for 1 minute. Bloodied targets automatically fail and additionally suffer all Frailty effects at maximum.
- Make one weapon or spell attack against one of these creatures — on a hit, automatically crits and ignores all DR and resistances.

---

### 2. Revelation of the Veil

*As the veil lifts, you expose every secret of your foes to your allies.*

**Effect:**
- All allies within 60 feet gain Truesight (120 feet) and advantage on all attack rolls, saving throws, and skill checks for 1 minute.
- Enemies within 30 feet make a WIS saving throw (DC = 10 + PB + INT modifier) — on failure, disadvantage on all rolls and saves for 1 minute; on success, disadvantage on attack rolls until end of their next turn.

---

### 3. Inescapable Banishment

*You summon your final reserves of willpower and invoke ancient rites.*

**Effect:**
- All fiends, undead, and aberrations within 60 feet make a CHA saving throw (DC = 10 + PB + INT modifier).
- Failure: banished to their native plane for 1d4 days. Legendary Resistance cannot be used.
- Success: 4d10 radiant damage; frightened of you and your allies for 1 minute.

| Last Stand Option | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Exploit Mortality** | Up to 3 creatures gain vulnerability to all damage; one auto-crit. | Targeting bosses and high-value enemies. |
| **Revelation of the Veil** | Allies gain Truesight and advantage; enemies suffer disadvantage. | Empowering allies, crippling enemies. |
| **Inescapable Banishment** | Banish all fiends/undead/aberrations (ignores Legendary Resistance). | Supernatural campaign climax. |

## Class Reactions

Investigators have access to powerful reactions that exploit enemy weaknesses and disrupt dangerous actions. These reactions use your **Proficiency Bonus pool**, refreshing on short or long rest.

### Quick Analysis (2nd Level)

**Trigger:** A creature you can see makes an attack roll.
**Effect:** Force them to reroll with disadvantage. If it still hits, mark them with Exploit Weakness — +PB damage to the next hit against them.

Against bloodied attackers whose Strength effect you've identified: spend 2 reaction uses to also apply Analytical Counter as part of this reaction.

---

### Predictive Step (7th Level)

**Trigger:** Your designated Quarry makes an attack roll.
**Effect:** Impose disadvantage on that attack roll. Your analysis of their combat patterns allows you to predict and counter attacks before they land.

Uses equal to your Intelligence modifier per long rest (separate from the PB reaction pool).

---

### Exploit Interruption (5th Level)

**Trigger:** A creature you have Studied within 30 feet makes an attack roll, casts a spell, or moves.
**Effect:** Call a tactical counter — **Trip** (STR save or movement halved/stopped), **Expose** (target has disadvantage on next attack; your ally's next attack against them has advantage), or **Distract** (if casting, STR save or spell fails; slot not expended).

At 11th level, apply two different counters to the same trigger.

Against bloodied creatures: Trip + Movement Frailty reduces speed to 0 (no save); Expose + Evasion Frailty makes your next attack auto-hit on beating DC by 5; Distract + Focus Frailty automatically counters the spell.
