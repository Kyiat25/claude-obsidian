# A System for Crime and Reputation

The **Wanted Score & Disposition System** tracks how a character’s criminal actions shape their world. From local suspicion to international manhunts, this system defines escalating law enforcement responses, NPC attitudes, and access to services. Whether feared outlaw or redeemed hero, a character’s reputation is no longer just narrative—it's mechanical, impactful, and inescapable.

This system intertwines Wanted Scores, representing a character's criminal notoriety, with Disposition, reflecting NPC attitudes, to create a dynamic and reactive world.

# **Wanted Scores:**

A character's Wanted Score reflects the severity of their crimes and how widely they are sought by law enforcement. Scores range from 1 (Known Troublemaker) to 7+ (Enemy of the State).

### **Example:**

A character with a Wanted Score of 3 enters a tavern. The innkeeper, seeing the character's wanted poster, is initially Indifferent but wary. They agree to serve the character food and drink but charge a 30% markup. They refuse to offer lodging, fearing trouble with the authorities. If the character had a Wanted Score of 1, the innkeeper might not even recognize them or, if they did, might only charge a slightly higher price. If the character had a Wanted Score of 6, the innkeeper would likely refuse service entirely and might even alert the guards.
## **Notoriety System:**
### **Levels of Notoriety:**

**Local Whisper (Score 3+):** Recognized in local taverns and among certain circles. Grants advantage on Charisma ([[Streetwise]]) checks against common criminals but disadvantage on Charisma (Deception) checks against those who know their reputation.

**Urban Legend (Score 5+):** Known throughout the city or region. Stories of their exploits are exaggerated and embellished. Grants advantage on Charisma ([[Intimidation]] & [[Streetwise]]) checks against most NPCs but disadvantage on all Charisma checks when dealing with law enforcement or officials. Increased chance of encountering admirers or fanatics.

**Infamous (Score 7+):** Known throughout the land. Their name is synonymous with their crimes. Grants advantage on Charisma ([[Intimidation]] & [[Streetwise]]) checks against almost all NPCs but makes it nearly impossible to blend in or go unnoticed. Attracts unwanted attention from bounty hunters, powerful enemies, and even curious onlookers.

> PCs with a mid-to-high Wanted Score **and** Corruption are often seen as **walking catastrophes**. Bounty hunters may demand higher prices or refuse to engage entirely.

# **Disposition**

Disposition represents an NPC's attitude towards the character, ranging from Hostile to Helpful. A character's Wanted Score significantly impacts their initial disposition with NPCs.

- **Hostile:** Attacks on sight.
- **Suspicious:** Wary and distrustful. May attack if provoked.
- **Indifferent:** Neutral, unconcerned. May be swayed with bribes or favours.
- **Friendly:** Well-disposed, willing to offer limited aid.
- **Helpful:** Actively supportive, willing to go out of their way to assist.
## **Reputation and Disposition:**

A character's reputation, separate from their Wanted Score, can further modify NPC dispositions. A heroic reputation might improve disposition, while a reputation for cruelty might worsen it.
### **Example:**

A character with a Wanted Score of 4 (Lawbreaker) and a reputation for helping the poor enters a town. The town guard is initially Suspicious due to the Wanted Score. However, the character's positive reputation among the town's poor residents might shift the guard's disposition to Indifferent, making them less likely to immediately arrest the character. A local crime boss, impressed by the character's daring exploits, might offer them protection (Wanted Score becomes 4P), shifting their disposition to Friendly.

**Story Snippet:**

Elara, a rogue with a Wanted Score of 5 (Criminal), slipped through the shadowed alleys of Porthaven. Her reputation as a skilled thief preceded her, making even the rats wary. She sought the Whispering Wyvern, a tavern known for its shady clientele. Inside, the barkeep, a gruff man with a scarred face, eyed her with Indifferent suspicion. "Heard you're trouble," he grumbled. 

Elara flashed a pouch of gold. "I'm also resourceful," she replied. The barkeep's disposition shifted to Friendly. Gold, after all, spoke a universal language. He leaned in, "What brings a wanted woman like you to my humble establishment?" Elara smiled, "Information. And perhaps a bit of… opportunity."

## **Information Decay and Redemption:**

Wanted Scores aren't permanent. By avoiding further criminal activity and performing good deeds, characters can gradually improve their reputation and reduce their Wanted Score. The DM can establish specific requirements for redemption, such as completing quests, paying restitution, or publicly apologizing for past misdeeds. This allows for compelling character arcs and opportunities for redemption.
## **Wanted Score Decay:**

- **Time:** For every year a character avoids criminal activity, their Wanted Score decreases by 1 (minimum 1).

- **Good Deeds:** Performing significant good deeds (e.g., saving a town from a monster, rescuing hostages) can reduce a character's Wanted Score by 1-2 points, at the DM's discretion. The act must be public and demonstrably altruistic.

- **Making Amends:** Paying restitution for past crimes, returning stolen goods, or helping those harmed by their actions can reduce a character's Wanted Score, depending on the severity of the original crime and the sincerity of their efforts. Requires negotiation with authorities or affected parties.

- **Royal Pardon:** A character can seek a royal pardon to clear their name and reset their Wanted Score to 0. This is usually a difficult and complex process, requiring political manoeuvring, significant favours, or extraordinary circumstances.
