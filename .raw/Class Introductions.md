# The 24 Classes of Dór-o Estel
## Class Introductions for Player's Handbook

*Choose the path that calls to your soul. Each class represents a unique approach to power, a different answer to the question: "How will you leave your mark on this world?"*

---

## How to Use This Chapter

Each class entry includes:

- **What Makes This Class Special**: Core identity and unique appeal
- **How It Differs from 5E** (for core classes): Key changes and enhancements
- **Signature Mechanics**: The defining features that make this class unique
- **Playstyle**: How you'll actually feel playing this class
- **Complexity Rating**: Honest assessment of mechanical difficulty
- **Perfect For**: Player types who'll love this class

**Complexity Scale:**
- **Simple (★☆☆)**: Straightforward mechanics, ideal for new players
- **Moderate (★★☆)**: Balanced complexity with meaningful choices
- **Complex (★★★)**: Multiple systems to track, rewards system mastery

## Core Classes (Traditional Archetypes)

### 1. Barbarian - Primal Fury Incarnate
**Complexity: ★☆☆ Simple**

#### What Makes Barbarians Special

You don't just ignore pain—you transform it into fury. Where others would fall, you stand taller, your rage burning hotter with every wound until you become an unstoppable force of nature incarnate. The roar in your chest is older than civilization, a primal force that turns mortal limitations into legendary power.

#### How FEARS Barbarians Differ from 5E

**Enhanced Rage System:**
- Rage now interacts with the Momentum system, creating a flowing combat style
- **Resilient Flow**: You don't reset Momentum at 50% HP while raging—you maintain combat rhythm
- **Fury Threshold**: Gain +2 Momentum when at -2 or lower—pain fuels your tempo
- **Relentless Fury**: Your damage dice increase at 50% and 25% HP thresholds

**Dynamic Combat Integration:**
- Rage grants Damage Reduction (not just resistance)
- Works with the Evasion system rather than AC
- Class Reactions add tactical depth to pure fury

**Mental Attribute Interaction:**
- Rage affects Sanity temporarily but doesn't break concentration (you don't cast spells)
- Corruption manifests differently—your fury is viewed as either sacred or savage depending on culture

#### Signature Mechanics

**Rage**: Your defining feature, now enhanced with three DCS integrations:
1. **Momentum Maintenance** - Combat flow doesn't break when bloodied
2. **Fury Escalation** - Get deadlier as you take damage
3. **Tactical Reactions** - Furious Defiance (counterattack when hit), Bloodrush Reprisal (damage + knockback), Primal Shockwave (AoE on crits)

**Damage Escalation**: Unlike 5E's flat rage bonus, your damage grows as combat progresses and you take wounds. At 50% HP, your weapon dice increase by one step (d8→d10). At 25% HP, they increase again (d10→d12).

**Unarmored Defense**: Use Constitution instead of Dexterity—your sheer toughness is your armor.

#### Playstyle

**Combat Flow:**
- Enter rage and charge into melee
- Take hits to fuel your escalating damage
- Use Resilient Flow to maintain Momentum even when bloodied
- Explode with devastating force as combat intensifies
- Never fear being wounded—it makes you deadlier

**Tactical Decisions:**
- When to rage (limited uses)
- Whether to use Reckless Attack (advantage for you, advantage for enemies)
- Which Class Reaction to use from your Proficiency Bonus pool
- How to position for maximum impact

**Progression Arc:**
You start as a durable warrior who hits hard. By mid-levels, you're a Momentum engine who grows stronger as combat progresses. At high levels, you're nearly unkillable while raging, your critical hits devastating, and your ability to maintain combat flow legendary.

**Perfect For:**
- Players who want straightforward but powerful gameplay
- Those who enjoy being the party's frontline anchor
- Anyone who loves the fantasy of unstoppable berserker fury
- Players who appreciate risk/reward mechanics (Reckless Attack, damage escalation)

### 2. Bard - Arcane Performers & Masters of Inspiration
**Complexity: ★★☆ Moderate**

#### What Makes Bards Special

Your magic flows through song, story, and sheer force of personality. You weave the Weave itself with words and melodies, inspiring heroes to impossible feats or reducing tyrants to weeping fools. Where others see entertainment, you see fundamental truth: reality bends to a well-told tale.

#### How FEARS Bards Differ from 5E

**Enhanced Bardic Inspiration:**
- Scales naturally (d6→d8→d10→d12) rather than being level-locked
- New uses through **Bardic Performance**: Celebrate Life (healing), Cutting Words (reduce enemy rolls), Clarity of Thought (anti-charm/fear)
- More frequent refresh options

**Expanded Magical Secrets:**
- Access any spell list earlier
- More spells known to support versatility
- Can swap spells to adapt to campaign needs

**Class Reactions:**
- **Dissonant Echo**: Reduce damage + frighten attackers
- **Cutting Counter**: Negate enemy rolls and reposition allies
- Fueled by Proficiency Bonus pool (short rest refresh)

**Mental Systems:**
- Your performance directly affects ally Sanity and morale
- Rally checks benefit from your presence
- Joy-based corruption resistance (maintaining hope prevents corruption)

#### Signature Mechanics

**Bardic Inspiration**: Grant allies d6→d12 dice they can add to any roll. This is your core support mechanic, but in FEARS it's more versatile:
- Can be used on attack rolls, saves, ability checks
- Dice scale automatically with your level
- Multiple refresh opportunities per day

**Jack-of-All-Trades**: Add half proficiency to all unproficied checks—you're competent at everything.

**Expertise**: Double proficiency bonus in chosen skills—you're the master of knowledge, performance, and social interactions.

**Magical Secrets**: Learn spells from any class's list, creating unique spell combinations impossible for other classes.

#### Playstyle

**Combat Flow:**
- Use bonus action to grant Bardic Inspiration
- Cast control or support spells
- Make weapon attacks or cantrips as needed
- Use Class Reactions to protect allies
- Adapt spell selection to party needs

**Skill Specialist:**
- Handle most social encounters
- Solve knowledge-based problems
- Pick locks, disable traps, gather information
- Literally good at everything with Jack-of-All-Trades

**Support Flexibility:**
- Healing when needed
- Buffing allies
- Debuffing enemies
- Controlling battlefield
- Damage when appropriate

**Perfect For:**
- Players who enjoy being the party's utility Swiss Army knife
- Those who love social roleplay and performance
- Anyone who wants flexibility and adaptation
- Players who appreciate supporting others to shine

### 3. Cleric - Divine Conduits & Miracle Workers
**Complexity: ★★☆ Moderate**

#### What Makes Clerics Special

You are the mortal hand of divine will, channeling the power of gods who shaped the world before the Fracture. Your prayers are answered not with hope, but with tangible miracles. When you speak with divine authority, even death itself must pause and listen.

#### How FEARS Clerics Differ from 5E

**Enhanced Channel Divinity:**
- More uses per rest
- Domain-specific Channels more powerful
- Interaction with Corruption and Sanity systems
- Can channel to stabilize ally morale (Rally bonus)

**Divine Intervention:**
- Activates more frequently
- Has scaled effects based on level
- Can be used to resist Corruption
- Interacts with mental attribute systems

**Healing Integration:**
- Healing spells can also stabilize Sanity
- Can remove Injuries at higher levels
- Rally mechanic support through divine presence
- Mass healing options for party-wide trauma

**Class Reactions:**
- **Radiant Reprieve**: Reduce damage + blind attackers
- **Sanctified Intercession**: Reroll failed saves + heal

#### Signature Mechanics

**Channel Divinity**: Call upon divine power for unique effects:
- **Turn/Destroy Profane**: Core ability all Clerics share
- **Domain Channels**: Unique to your chosen domain (War, Life, Trickery, etc.)
- Uses scale with proficiency bonus

**Domain Powers**: Your choice of domain dramatically changes playstyle:
- **Life**: Healing powerhouse
- **War**: Frontline divine warrior
- **Trickery**: Sneaky divine magic
- **Light**: Radiant damage specialist
- Each domain has unique spell list and abilities

**Prepared Casting**: Change your entire spell list each long rest—ultimate flexibility.

**Destroy Undead**: Automatically destroy lower-level undead when you Turn them.

#### Playstyle

**Combat Flow:**
- Position to support allies with healing and buffs
- Use Channel Divinity at crucial moments
- Prepared spells adapt to known challenges
- Balance between healing and damage/control
- Use Class Reactions to protect wounded allies

**Domain Defines Role:**
- Life Clerics are primary healers
- War Clerics are frontline fighters
- Light Clerics are damage dealers
- Trickery Clerics are sneaky supporters
- Each plays completely differently

**Strategic Preparation:**
- Change prepared spells each day
- Scout ahead to learn what you'll face
- Adapt spell selection to party needs
- Always have the right tool for the job

**Perfect For:**
- Players who enjoy divine magic themes
- Those who want flexibility through prepared casting
- Anyone who likes being the party's anchor and healer
- Players who appreciate tactical spell selection

### 4. Druid - Nature's Guardians & Shapeshifters
**Complexity: ★★☆ Moderate**

#### What Makes Druids Special

The boundary between civilization and wilderness runs through your soul. You walk in the shapes of beast and bird, speaking languages older than words. The Hollowwood remembers you, the roots know your true name, and when you call upon nature's fury, even the stones answer.

#### How FEARS Druids Differ from 5E

**Enhanced Wild Shape:**
- Maintains more effectiveness at higher levels
- Some Circles allow casting while shaped
- Wild Shape HP pool separate from normal HP
- Can Wild Shape as reaction in some Circles
- Integrates with Momentum system

**Nature Magic Integration:**
- Prepared casting with full spell list access
- Wild Shape + spellcasting combos in certain Circles
- Environmental control through terrain manipulation
- Can stabilize natural areas corrupted by Eldritch influence

**Circle Powers:**
- Each Circle (subclass) dramatically changes playstyle
- Moon Circle: Combat shapes
- Land Circle: Terrain specialist
- Dreams Circle: Healing focus
- Wildfire Circle: Destructive force

**Mental Systems:**
- High connection to nature provides Sanity stability
- Corruption from Eldritch forces is existential threat
- Wild Shape can provide mental refuge from trauma

#### Signature Mechanics

**Wild Shape**: Transform into beasts:
- Use beast's HP pool (separate from your normal HP)
- Gain beast's physical abilities and movement
- Maintain mental stats
- Duration scales with level
- Return to normal form when beast HP reaches 0

**Circle Spells**: Each Circle grants automatic prepared spells that don't count against your limit.

**Nature Speech**: Speak with animals and plants, understanding the natural world.

**Timeless Body**: Age extremely slowly, living for centuries.

#### Playstyle

**Combat Flow (Moon Circle):**
- Wild Shape into combat form
- Fight as frontline beast
- Tank with Wild Shape HP pool
- Shift back when damaged, cast spells, shift again

**Support Flow (Land Circle):**
- Control battlefield with terrain spells
- Prepared casting provides utility
- Wild Shape for scouting and utility
- Environmental manipulation

**Versatility:**
- Scouting: Wild Shape into small creatures
- Combat: Transform into bears, dire wolves
- Utility: Prepared spells for any situation
- Social: Nature-themed charisma

**Perfect For:**
- Players who love transformation mechanics
- Those who enjoy nature themes and environmental magic
- Anyone who wants versatile prepared casting
- Players who appreciate tactical shape selection

### 5. Fighter - Martial Masters & Weapon Specialists
**Complexity: ★☆☆ Simple**

#### What Makes Fighters Special

While others rely on magic or divine favor, you've perfected the art of controlled violence. Your blade is an extension of your will, each movement precisely calculated, every strike exactly where it needs to be. In that moment of Action Surge, you move faster than thought itself, a blur of martial perfection.

#### How FEARS Fighters Differ from 5E

**Weapon Mastery System:**
- Fighters gain mastery with multiple weapons (start with 2)
- Weapon Skills provide tactical options (Shatter Guard, Pinning Point, Hamstring, etc.)
- Can unlock new masteries through critical hits + downtime training
- **Multi-Mastery Combat**: Fighters can use multiple Weapon Skills per round (1 on Attack Action, 1 as bonus action, 1 as reaction, 1 with Action Surge)

**Enhanced Action Surge:**
- More uses as you level
- Allows additional Weapon Skill use
- Creates legendary combat rounds

**More Fighting Styles:**
- Additional options beyond 5E
- Can learn multiple styles through progression
- Fighting Styles synergize with Weapon Mastery

**Class Reactions:**
- **Riposte**: Counterattack after successful dodge
- **Brace**: Intercept approaching enemies
- **Tactical Assessment**: Grant ally advantage
- Creates tactical depth every round

#### Signature Mechanics

**Action Surge**: Take an entire additional action in combat:
- Essentially two full turns in one round
- Multiple uses per rest at higher levels
- Allows for devastating nova rounds
- Can use additional Weapon Skill

**Fighting Style**: Choose specialized combat approach:
- Archery, Defense, Dueling, Great Weapon Fighting, etc.
- Defines your basic combat identity

**Extra Attacks**: Most attacks of any class:
- 2 attacks at 5th level
- 3 attacks at 11th level
- 4 attacks at 20th level
- Combine with Action Surge for 8 attacks in one turn

**Weapon Mastery**: Core Fighter identity:
- Start with 2 mastered weapons
- Learn more through critical hits
- Use multiple Skills per round (unique to Fighters)
- Create tactical combat flow

**Indomitable**: Reroll failed saving throw, because sometimes you just refuse to fail.

#### Playstyle

**Combat Flow:**
- Make multiple attacks per turn
- Apply Weapon Skills to control enemies
- Use Action Surge for devastating rounds
- Position tactically with reactions
- Build Momentum through consecutive hits

**Weapon Master:**
- Choose optimal weapons for situations
- Combine Weapon Skills for effects
- Shatter Guard → allies attack → Riposte reaction → Action Surge for more
- Create complex tactical turns

**Subclass Variety:**
- Battle Master: Tactical maneuvers
- Champion: Critical hit specialist
- Eldritch Knight: Spells + weapons
- Each dramatically different

**Perfect For:**
- New players wanting straightforward power
- Experienced players seeking tactical depth through Weapon Mastery
- Those who love martial combat and weapon expertise
- Players who enjoy being the reliable damage dealer

### 6. Monk - Ki-Powered Mystic Warriors
**Complexity: ★★☆ Moderate**

#### What Makes Monks Special

Your body is a temple, your discipline a weapon sharper than steel. Through meditation and training, you've learned to feel the flow of Ki—the vital energy connecting all living things. You catch arrows from the air, run up walls, and with a single touch, stop a giant's heart.

#### How FEARS Monks Differ from 5E

**Technique Points System:**
- Replaces Ki Points with clearer name
- Renamed for thematic consistency
- More generous point economy at higher levels

**Weapon Mastery Integration:**
- Monks gain Weapon Mastery with monk weapons
- **Flow Strike**: Weapon Skill that regenerates Ki when hitting
- Fixes the "Ki starvation" problem
- Creates sustainable combat loop

**Enhanced Mobility:**
- Movement speed bonuses stack with terrain features
- Step of the Wind more useful
- Reaction movement options

**Stunning Strike Clarity:**
- Clear DC calculations
- Scales more effectively
- Can be used with Weapon Skills

**Class Reactions:**
- **Deflect Missiles**: Catch projectiles, throw them back
- **Patient Defense**: Impose disadvantage on attacks
- **Slow Fall**: Reduce fall damage

#### Signature Mechanics

**Technique Points (Ki)**: Fuel your mystical abilities:
- Flurry of Blows: Extra unarmed strikes
- Patient Defense: Dodge as bonus action
- Step of the Wind: Disengage + jump boost
- Stunning Strike: Stun enemies
- **Regeneration**: Flow Strike Weapon Skill restores points when you hit

**Unarmored Defense**: AC = 10 + Dexterity + Wisdom—your discipline is your armor.

**Martial Arts**: Unarmed strikes use Dexterity and scale damage (d4→d10).

**Stunning Strike**: Spend 1 Technique Point when you hit, target makes save or is stunned.

**Flow Strike**: Core innovation—when you hit with mastered monk weapon, regain 1 Technique Point. Creates sustainable Ki economy.

#### Playstyle

**Combat Flow:**
- Make multiple attacks (Martial Arts bonus action)
- Spend Technique Points for extra attacks or utility
- Use Flow Strike to regenerate resources mid-combat
- Position with incredible mobility
- Stun key enemies with Stunning Strike

**Resource Management:**
- Unlike 5E monks, FEARS monks regenerate Ki in combat
- Flow Strike creates sustainable loop
- Still need to manage when to spend for Flurry vs Stun
- Short rest refills fully

**Mobility Master:**
- Run up walls
- Jump superhuman distances
- Move faster than anyone else
- Position perfectly every turn
- Catch arrows mid-flight

**Perfect For:**
- Players who love martial arts fantasy
- Those who enjoy high mobility and positioning
- Anyone who wants resource management without starvation
- Players who appreciate monk aesthetics

### 7. Paladin - Holy Warriors & Living Oaths
**Complexity: ★★☆ Moderate**

#### What Makes Paladins Special

You are oath made flesh, conviction forged into armor. When your blade falls, it carries the weight of divine judgment. Your very presence fortifies allies and burns the wicked. You don't just fight for righteousness—you *are* righteousness, and evil trembles before your aura of sacred purpose.

#### How FEARS Paladins Differ from 5E

**Enhanced Divine Smite:**
- Bypasses damage resistance to non-magical attacks
- Can be used with Weapon Skills
- Scales more dramatically at higher levels
- Interacts with DCS Momentum system

**Lay on Hands:**
- Can stabilize Sanity as well as HP
- Can remove Injuries
- Can boost morale (Rally mechanic support)
- More versatile healing pool

**Aura Buffs:**
- Affect mental attributes (Focus, Sanity)
- Protect against Corruption
- Provide morale stability in horror scenarios
- Radius scales with level

**Class Reactions:**
- **Divine Intervention**: Redirect attack to yourself to save ally
- **Shield of Faith**: Grant AC bonus as reaction
- **Retributive Strike**: Smite when ally is hit nearby

**Oath Powers:**
- Each Oath dramatically changes playstyle
- Oath spells don't count against prepared limit
- Channel Divinity unique to each Oath

#### Signature Mechanics

**Divine Smite**: Spend spell slot to add massive radiant damage:
- 2d8 + 1d8 per slot level above 1st
- Automatically max damage on crits
- Can be declared after hit confirmed
- Bypasses resistance to non-magical damage
- Core burst damage mechanic

**Lay on Hands**: Healing pool = Paladin level × 5:
- Heal 1 HP per point spent
- Remove poison/disease for 5 points
- Stabilize Sanity for 10 points (FEARS innovation)
- Remove one Injury for 15 points (FEARS innovation)

**Aura of Protection**: Everyone within 10 feet (30 feet at higher levels) adds your Charisma modifier to saves. Incredible party buff.

**Channel Divinity**: Oath-specific divine powers:
- Varies dramatically by Oath
- 1 use per short rest
- Defines Oath identity

#### Playstyle

**Combat Flow:**
- Fight as frontline warrior
- Use Divine Smite for burst damage on crits or key hits
- Position to maximize Aura benefits
- Use Lay on Hands between combats
- Protect allies with Class Reactions

**Burst Damage:**
- Hold spell slots for critical hits
- Divine Smite when it matters most
- One hit can drop major enemies
- Resource management is key

**Party Anchor:**
- Aura provides constant buff
- Lay on Hands emergency healing
- Presence stabilizes morale
- Divine protection against horror

**Perfect For:**
- Players who love holy warrior fantasy
- Those who enjoy protecting and buffing allies
- Anyone who wants burst damage with utility
- Players who appreciate oath roleplay

### 8. Ranger - Wilderness Experts & Precision Hunters
**Complexity: ★☆☆ Simple**

#### What Makes Rangers Special

The wilderness is not your home—you *are* part of it, as much as any tree or stream. You read stories in broken twigs and weather patterns, following trails invisible to others. Your quarry never knows you're hunting until your arrow finds its mark, and in your chosen terrain, you are apex predator.

#### How FEARS Rangers Differ from 5E

**Enhanced Hunter's Mark:**
- Free to cast (doesn't use spell slot)
- Scales damage automatically (d6→d8→d10→d12)
- Can mark as bonus action OR reaction
- Move mark as bonus action when target drops
- Core class feature, not just a spell

**Favored Enemy Revision:**
- Choose creature types you specialize against
- Reduces Called Shot penalties against favored enemies
- Bonus damage scales with level
- Multiple favored enemies by higher levels

**Improved Exploration:**
- Ignore difficult terrain
- Advantage on tracking
- Natural environment mastery
- Party movement speed bonus

**Class Reactions:**
- **Hunter's Reflex**: Disadvantage on marked target's attacks
- **Quarry Interpose**: Protect allies from marked prey
- **Hunter's Snare**: Restrain and damage moving targets

**Versatile Combat:**
- Works well with both melee and ranged
- Two-weapon fighting or bow specialist
- Companion options (certain subclasses)

#### Signature Mechanics

**Hunter's Mark**: Your core mechanic:
- Mark target as bonus action (free, no spell slot)
- Add 1d6→1d12 damage per hit (scales with level)
- Know exact location while marked
- Transfer mark to new target when current dies

**Favored Enemy**: Specialized knowledge of creature types:
- Reduced Called Shot penalties (DCS integration)
- Bonus to track and recall information
- Advantage on certain checks related to type
- Choose more types as you level

**Natural Explorer**: Master of environments:
- Ignore difficult terrain
- Can't become lost except by magic
- Forage while traveling
- Learn about dangers in area

**Multiattack**: 2 attacks per Attack action at 5th level, both benefit from Hunter's Mark.

#### Playstyle

**Combat Flow:**
- Mark primary target
- Make multiple attacks with bonus damage
- Transfer mark when target drops
- Position for optimal shots
- Use terrain to advantage

**Exploration Expert:**
- Track enemies effortlessly
- Navigate wilderness
- Forage for party
- Scout ahead safely

**Versatile Build:**
- Archery focus: Consistent ranged damage
- Two-weapon: Mobile melee striker
- Companion: Action economy advantage (some subclasses)

**Perfect For:**
- Players who love ranger/hunter fantasy
- Those who enjoy both combat and exploration
- Anyone who wants consistent damage
- Players who appreciate wilderness survival themes

### 9. Rogue - Masters of Precision & Skill Specialists
**Complexity: ★★☆ Moderate**

#### What Makes Rogues Special

You strike not with strength, but with precision—finding the gap between ribs, the weak point in armor, the moment when your enemy's guard drops. You're where you're not supposed to be, taking what you're not supposed to have, and gone before anyone realizes what happened.

#### How FEARS Rogues Differ from 5E

**Sneak Attack Enhancement:**
- Bypasses Damage Reduction completely (DCS integration)
- Can be used with Weapon Skills
- Scales to 10d6 at level 20
- Works with both melee and ranged

**Weapon Mastery Access:**
- Rogues gain Weapon Mastery with finesse/ranged weapons
- Hamstring, Pinning Point, etc. provide control without needing Sneak Attack
- Creates utility beyond damage
- One Weapon Skill per turn rule applies

**Enhanced Cunning Action:**
- Bonus action Dash, Disengage, or Hide
- Can be combined with Weapon Skills
- Creates incredible mobility

**More Expertise:**
- Start with 2 Expertise at 1st level
- Gain 2 more at 6th level
- Eventually 4 skills with doubled proficiency

**Class Reactions:**
- **Cunning Dodge**: Reduce damage and reposition
- **Shadow Slip**: Use stealth to avoid attacks
- **Killing Opportunity**: Capitalize on ally hits for extra damage

#### Signature Mechanics

**Sneak Attack**: Massive bonus damage once per turn:
- Need advantage OR ally within 5 feet of target
- **Bypasses DR completely** (FEARS innovation)
- Scales from 1d6 to 10d6
- Works with finesse or ranged weapons
- Can combine with Weapon Skills

**Expertise**: Double proficiency bonus in chosen skills:
- Start with 2 skills
- Eventually 4 skills
- Become unmatched in chosen areas
- Typically 10-12 higher than others

**Cunning Action**: Bonus action for Dash, Disengage, or Hide:
- Incredible mobility
- Hit and run tactics
- Position for Sneak Attack every turn

**Uncanny Dodge**: Use reaction to halve damage from one attack. Evasion: Take no damage from successful Dex saves (instead of half).

#### Playstyle

**Combat Flow:**
- Position for Sneak Attack advantage
- Use Cunning Action to engage or escape
- Apply one Weapon Skill per turn for control
- One massive damage hit per round
- Use reactions to avoid damage

**Skill Master:**
- Handle all skill challenges in specialty areas
- Expertise makes you the best at chosen skills
- Typically: Stealth, Sleight of Hand, Investigation, Perception
- Reliable Talent (higher level): Never roll below 10 on Expertise skills

**Hit and Run:**
- Engage with advantage
- Massive Sneak Attack damage
- Cunning Action to escape
- Hide for next advantage
- Repeat

**Perfect For:**
- Players who enjoy precision over brute force
- Those who love skill-based gameplay
- Anyone who wants to be the skill specialist
- Players who appreciate tactical positioning

### 10. Sorcerer - Innate Magic Users & Metamagic Masters
**Complexity: ★★☆ Moderate**

#### What Makes Sorcerers Special

Magic isn't something you learned—it's woven into your very being, burning in your blood and singing in your bones. You don't study spells; you reshape them with a thought, bending reality through sheer force of magical will. The Weave responds to you like a lover, intimate and immediate.

#### How FEARS Sorcerers Differ from 5E

**Enhanced Metamagic:**
- Learn more Metamagic options
- Sorcery Points more abundant
- New Metamagic options specific to FEARS
- Can combine multiple Metamagics (with cost increase)

**Bloodline Powers:**
- Draconic, Wild, Shadow, Divine, etc.
- Each fundamentally changes playstyle
- Passive benefits + active powers
- Thematic spell access

**Sorcery Point Conversion:**
- More flexible slot/point conversion
- Additional ways to gain points
- Point costs slightly adjusted

**Focus Integration:**
- Concentration uses Focus instead of Constitution
- Sorcery Points can stabilize Focus
- Wild Magic affects mental attributes
- Bloodline resilience to certain effects

#### Signature Mechanics

**Metamagic**: Modify spells on the fly by spending Sorcery Points:
- **Quickened Spell**: Cast as bonus action
- **Twinned Spell**: Target two creatures
- **Subtle Spell**: No verbal/somatic components
- **Empowered Spell**: Reroll damage dice
- **Extended Spell**: Double duration
- Choose 2 at 3rd level, learn more as you progress

**Sorcery Points**: Fuel Metamagic and create spell slots:
- Pool = Sorcerer level
- Spend to use Metamagic
- Convert to/from spell slots
- Defines your flexibility

**Bloodline**: Your magic's source:
- Draconic: Elemental resistance, AC bonus, powerful presence
- Wild: Chaotic surges, unpredictable power, reality bending
- Shadow: Darkness manipulation, stealth magic
- Each provides unique spells and abilities

#### Playstyle

**Combat Flow:**
- Choose spell for situation
- Apply Metamagic to optimize
- Quicken spell to cast two in one turn
- Twin important buffs or controls
- Manage Sorcery Points carefully

**Flexible Caster:**
- Fewer spells known than Wizard
- But can modify each spell multiple ways
- One spell becomes many different tools
- Adapt to any situation

**Resource Management:**
- Balance Sorcery Points vs spell slots
- When to use Metamagic
- Which Metamagic for which situation
- Short rest recovers some points

**Perfect For:**
- Players who love innate magic themes
- Those who enjoy flexible spellcasting
- Anyone who wants to modify spells creatively
- Players who appreciate resource management

### 11. Warlock - Pact-Bound Casters & Eldritch Servants
**Complexity: ★★☆ Moderate**

#### What Makes Warlocks Special

You've made a bargain with something powerful—something ancient, alien, or divine. It whispers secrets that mortals were never meant to know, and in exchange for your service, it grants you power that rewrites reality. Your pact is intimate and dangerous, a relationship that will define your fate.

#### How FEARS Warlocks Differ from 5E

**Enhanced Pact Magic:**
- Spell slots recharge on short rest (same as 5E)
- All slots at highest level (same as 5E)
- More Invocations available
- Patron spells don't count against spells known

**Invocation System:**
- More total Invocations learned
- New FEARS-specific Invocations
- Can swap Invocations on level up
- Greater customization

**Pact Boon Enhancement:**
- Pact of Blade: Better weapon options
- Pact of Tome: More ritual spells
- Pact of Chain: Stronger familiars
- New Pact options

**Patron Relationship:**
- Affects Sanity and Corruption
- Patron communication mechanics
- Quest hooks and story integration
- Mechanical benefits from patron relationship

**Mental Systems:**
- Patron contact affects Sanity
- Some Corruption baseline (depends on patron)
- Eldritch knowledge strains Focus
- Rally mechanics complicated by patron influence

#### Signature Mechanics

**Pact Magic**: Unique spell slot system:
- Few spell slots (2-4 total)
- All slots at your highest level
- Recharge on SHORT rest (not long)
- Can "nova" more frequently than other casters

**Eldritch Blast**: Your signature cantrip:
- Scales with character level (not class level)
- Invocations make it devastating
- Reliable damage every turn
- Often your primary attack

**Eldritch Invocations**: Customize your powers:
- Always-on abilities
- Enhance Eldritch Blast (Agonizing Blast, Repelling Blast, etc.)
- Gain special powers (Devil's Sight, Mask of Many Faces)
- Choose from huge list

**Pact Boon**: Your patron's gift:
- Pact of Blade: Summon magical weapon

## Choosing Your Class

With 24 options, choosing can feel overwhelming. Consider:

**Combat Role:**
- Tank: Barbarian, Fighter, Paladin, Monk
- Burst Damage: Rogue, Paladin, Magus, Sangromancer, Reaper
- Sustained Damage: Fighter, Ranger, Warlock
- Control: Wizard, Apothecary, Witch, Shadowshaper
- Support: Bard, Cleric, Shaman, Tattooist
- Healer: Cleric, Druid, Paladin

**Resource Style:**
- Long Rest Slots: Wizard, Sorcerer, Cleric, Druid, Witch
- Short Rest Slots: Warlock
- Point Pools: Monk, Apothecary, Sangromancer, Shadowshaper, Reaper
- Limited Daily Uses: Barbarian, Fighter
- Always Available: Fighter attacks, Rogue Sneak Attack

**Complexity:**
