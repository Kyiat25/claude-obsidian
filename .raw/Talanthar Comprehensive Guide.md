# THE GREENWOOD SOVEREIGNTY OF TALANTHAR
## A Comprehensive Study of the Last Unconquered Kingdom

*Year 487 of the Fourth Age*

---

## INTRODUCTION: THE FOREST THAT ENDURES

Talanthar, known in the elvish tongue as **Vael'Tharyn** (the Verdant Crown), stands as the last unconquered bastion of the Old Ways within Elarion. To the outside world, it is whispered of as The Forest Kingdom, The Court of Roots, or simply The Greenwood. To its people, however, Talanthar is not a kingdom at all, but a **living covenant** between mortals and the ageless spirit of the forest.

Nestled between the towering Skyspine Mountains and the misted hills of Corvessa's eastern frontier, Talanthar thrives within a labyrinth of river-carved gorges, luminous caverns, and colossal trees whose roots and canopies interlace to form both home and fortress. It is a realm where the boundaries between civilization and wilderness blur into something profound and ancient.

> *"Talanthar endures not because it stands against time, but because time itself flows through its roots."*  
> — Elvish Proverb

## PART I: GEOGRAPHY AND THE LIVING CITY

### The Heart of Talanthar

The Heart of Talanthar lies where two swift rivers — the **Vaelrun** and the **Thalaris** (also known as Aerendel and Myrn in some texts) — converge. Over eons, their waters have carved deep gorges and honeycombed caverns through the bedrock, creating the natural amphitheater that houses the city of Talanthar itself.

Above ground, the forest canopy forms living bridges and sun-dappled glades, while below, the underworld is a realm of phosphorescent fungi, subterranean streams, and crystal-lit forests. Vast pillars of stone support the caverns, with ancient bridges and carved crannogs linking them in every direction.

The forest canopy averages two hundred feet in height; in the oldest sectors it exceeds three hundred. Light filters in muted shafts, giving the entire region a perpetual dusk. The air is heavy with spores, dew, and a low electrical hum that sensitive augurs describe as a **living resonance**.

### Major Geographic Features

**The Vaelthir Crown**  
The upper terraces surrounding the World Tree, said to be the first of its kind in Elarion. Its roots feed both rivers and its leaves glow faintly at dusk. At the gorge's center, rising through rock and light, stands the Vaelthir Crown — trunk thicker than the Imperial Forum, branches that pierce the clouds and roots that lace the Under-Grove.

**Skyperch Cliffs**  
The ridgelines overlooking the gorge, where griffons and their riders make their aeries. Jagged limestone ridges home to the Skyguard Orders — aerial knights mounted on griffins and great eagles. Signal-fires and runestone perches allow instantaneous warnings across hundreds of miles. Legends tell that when the Corvessans advanced, griffins dropped molten resin and burning sap upon their ranks.

**Moonveil Glades**  
Misted hollows and shallow vales where witch covens and spirit seers dwell. Misty forest valleys where witch covens and moon-elves dwell. Here reality bends gently — paths repeat, moons double in reflection, and spirits dance in silver dew. Imperial observers are forbidden entry; none who disobeyed have returned whole.

**The Thornward Flats**  
A marshy plain between Corvessa and the forest edge, where countless invasions have died in the mud. Once a fertile plain of trade and migration, now it is a **cursed frontier**. Blackened trees, vine-choked ruins, and shallow marshes filled with broken helms and rusting banners. Monuments of rusting helms mark where Corvessa's finest Lances fell. Talanthar's druids allow no settlement here; the land itself is considered a living graveyard. Ghost-lights drift over pools that once held the bodies of knights and raptors alike.

From the Corvessan side, the horizon appears misted — yet scouts report the fog never crosses the border into Corvessan lands. It waits, like a living sentinel. Talanthar's side is eerily alive: green, bright, yet oppressive. Flowers bloom from skulls; roots form bridges over forgotten roads. The land itself has become a monument to balance restored through violence.

**The Underbloom**  
The vast caverns beneath the city, filled with glowing lichens, fungal orchards, and subterranean groves. These chambers range from narrow crawlways to vaults vast enough to contain entire villages. Bioluminescent flora — glowmoss, lich-orchids, and silver-cap fungi — illuminate the depths in soft hues of blue and green. Mushroom forests, pallid willows, and silver-rooted birches form subterranean biomes of their own. These places hum with quiet song — the breath of the forest's spirit moving through stone.

**The Halfling Joy-Wilds**  
Stretching southward of the main forest, a web of rolling meadows and tangled copses where wild halfling tribes roam. They live by hunt, feast, and song; their raptor-mounted warbands are beloved by Talanthar but feared by all others. A tavern may appear for a week on a certain glade and vanish the next, yet always seem to stand exactly where weary travelers most need it.

### The City of Talanthar (Vael'Tharyn)

Talanthar's capital is not a single city, but a **vertical labyrinth** of terraces, bridges, and caverns that span the cliffs where the rivers meet. The so-called city is less an urban construct than a **vertical ecosystem**. Homes are grown from living wood or carved into the stone itself. Lanternlight and glowberry vines cast a perpetual twilight hue upon the streets, while waterfalls cascade from level to level.

Terraces, bridges, and platforms spiral along the gorge walls; residences grow from living wood coaxed into shape by druidic art. Below, the river tunnels are lined with carved crannogs — half-stone, half-root — joined by bridges that sway yet never fall. Above, aerial walkways thread through the upper canopy, linking cliff-perched towers to colossal trees whose trunks serve as halls, archives, and temples.

At night, the entire gorge glows faintly — not by flame, but by the slow respiration of luminous vines. The effect is breathtaking, and profoundly disquieting: the sense that the city itself is awake.

#### Key Districts

**The High Crown**  
Sacred terraces surrounding the World Tree's roots. Home to the Archdruid, the Circle of the Moonweave, and the Council of Nine Groves. Upper terraces surrounding the base of the World Tree Vaelthir. Home to the Archdruidic conclave, sacred pools, and living monuments of bark and crystal. Access restricted; even most citizens enter only for ritual.

**The Silver Hollow**  
The underground district where trade, metalwork, and magic converge. Blacksmiths here forge using river-spirits' breath to temper enchanted alloys. Mid-level caverns housing forges and laboratories. Here Magi-smiths alloy imported metals with sapstone, producing weapons that hum with low vitality.

**The Bridge-Courts**  
Hanging walkways and suspended markets where griffon feathers, enchanted hides, and rare herbs are traded. Markets suspended between cliffs. Traders exchange herbs, pelts, and carved bone for smelted goods brought by dwarven or Imperial intermediaries.

**The Shadow Pools**  
Caverns of still water where the witches of Ishara and Sylthara hold rituals under bioluminescent trees. Lower caverns of dark water and fungal canopy where witch-covens commune with reflected spirits. The atmosphere is one of serene menace — beauty too still to be benign.

**Skyperch Aeries**  
Upper rim towers and landing platforms for griffon and elk-wing riders. Defensive patrols launch here, capable of reaching the forest edge within an hour.

Talanthar feels **alive** — its architecture grown, not built; its people dwelling in harmony with the ancient forest rather than above it.

#### Population and Structure

Reliable counts are impossible due to the shifting nature of the forest and the semi-nomadic habits of many communities. Estimates range from **eighty to one hundred thousand permanent residents within the gorge**, with perhaps **four times that number** (320,000-400,000) dispersed through outlying hamlets and groves.

Society divides into **Circles** rather than classes — Druids, Rangers, Magi, Witches, and Shamanic lineages — each with representation in the High Council of the Greenwood. No monarch presides; authority rotates with the seasons, granted by ritual consensus rather than decree.

### The Outlying Hamlets and Groves

The Greenwood beyond the gorge teems with small, semi-nomadic settlements — clusters of huts around standing stones, suspended dwellings in the boughs, or subterranean grotto-villages beside hidden streams. Paths between them shift like living veins: roads migrate, overgrowth conceals yesterday's trail, yet the locals navigate unerringly.

Notable settlement types include:

**Halfling Joy-Clans**  
Tribal bands of raptor-riders and elk-hunters. Their laughter precedes ambush or feast with equal likelihood. Feared and adored by the forest's other denizens.

**Druidic Groves**  
Each major Circle maintains a sacred clearing, marked by rune-stones that bloom when the moon is high. These groves act as both temple and council chamber.

**The Wander Inns**  
Rare wayhouses that appear to move. Travelers swear that taverns like The Resting Root stand in different locations along the same trail within weeks. The proprietors, unchanged, claim it is the road that moves, not they.

**The Ash-Marked Groves**  
Descendants of wood elf refugees from the Islands of Eternal Green (destroyed ~500-600 years ago on coastal Vashkora) maintain approximately **18 settlements** concentrated along borders and strategic approaches. While fully integrated into Talanthari society through the Oath of the Greenwood, these communities preserve distinct memorial traditions and specialized knowledge.

Distinguished by:
- **Astronomical observatories**: Maintaining Cycle-Singer traditions from Islands heritage
- **Memorial ceremonies**: Annual Seven Oases Remembrance honoring destroyed homeland
- **Enhanced vigilance**: Disproportionate representation in border defense and intelligence roles
- **Craft specializations**: Lens-making, astronomical instruments, specialized glasswork
- **Cultural synthesis**: Blended moon elf and wood elf traditions over 500+ years

**Notable Ash-Marked Settlements:**
- **Aeloren's Grove** (Southern interior, ~650 population): Cultural center and memorial site, hosts annual Seven Oases festival
- **Ashwatch** (Thornward frontier, ~800 population): Primary defensive position facing Corvessa
- **Skywatch Archive** (Eastern approaches, ~450 population): Intelligence post monitoring Kel Varyan movements
- **Rootremembrance** (Thornward frontier, ~600 population): Training center for anti-persecution tactics
- **Glasswork Haven** (Southern interior, ~500 population): Maintains lens-making and astronomical tool crafts

Population breakdown:
- **Core Ash-Marked settlements**: ~12,000-15,000 (18 communities)
- **Total Ash-Marked heritage** (anyone with Islands ancestry): ~45,000-55,000 (10-12% of Talanthar's population)
- **Integration**: Fully Talanthari citizens participating in all Circles; Ash-Marked identity is sub-cultural layer, not separate ethnicity

These hamlets form the connective tissue of Talanthar's society — simultaneously isolated and unified through the forest's unseen communication. Messengers claim the trees themselves carry whispers: news, warnings, and songs. Augur-crystals confirm minute shifts in aetheric resonance across miles whenever ritual drums sound within any grove.

## PART II: THE WORLD TREE VAELTHIR

### The Living Heart of the Greenwood

At the center of all things Talanthari stands **Vaelthir**, the World Tree — not merely a landmark, but a **living axis of the Weave** itself. This is no ordinary tree. Its trunk is thicker than the Imperial Forum, its branches pierce the clouds, and its roots descend so deep they lace through the Underbloom and beyond, perhaps touching the very bones of the world.

Every birth, death, and union within Talanthar is marked by blood offerings to the earth at Vaelthir's base, acknowledging life's cycle and the forest's price. The people say, **"The Tree remembers,"** and that when a drop of blood or tears falls upon its roots, the forest knows your name forever.

During the Corvessan Crusades, the bark absorbed the blood of thousands — the scar still gleams silver under moonlight, a perpetual reminder of those who sought to destroy it and failed.

### The Nature of Vaelthir

To the druids and witches of Talanthar, Vaelthir is not a god but a **memory of creation itself** — a living recording of all growth and loss since the dawn. It is older than the gods themselves, though not one of them. It does not demand worship, but it demands **recognition** — that all who shelter beneath its boughs acknowledge their place in the endless cycle of growth, death, and renewal.

The World Tree exhibits both botanical and arcane sentience. Imperial observers report that it pulses with a rhythm detectable by magical instruments, and that this pulse seems to synchronize with the heartbeats of those who approach with reverence. Those who approach with malice or greed find the forest itself turns against them.

Vaelthir's roots do not merely anchor — they **listen**. They taste the blood offered, feel the tears shed, and remember every oath spoken beneath its boughs. The forest's memory is not metaphorical; it is literal, biological, and **eternal**.

### The Covenant of Blood

Children's first blood is given to the forest so it "knows" them. This is not mere symbolism. From that moment forward, the forest recognizes them as one of its own. Paths open before them that remain closed to outsiders. Animals do not flee. The mists do not confuse. The roots do not trap.

But the covenant cuts both ways. Those who betray the forest — who take more than they give, who harm without cause, who forget the balance — find the forest turns upon them. Paths twist into mazes. Food spoils on the tongue. Water turns brackish. And if they persist, they vanish into the roots themselves, becoming part of the soil that feeds the next generation.

## PART III: CULTURE, SOCIETY, AND GOVERNMENT

### The Federation of Circles

Talanthar is less a monarchy and more a **federation of circles**: druidic orders, witch covens, ranger brotherhoods, and halfling joy-tribes. Each governs its own grove or valley, sending a representative to the **High Council of the Greenwood**, presided over by the Archdruid of Vaelthir.

This is governance by **consensus and omen**, not decree. Decisions are made through long councils where druids listen to the whispers of the forest, witches read the portents in water and moon, and the representatives of each grove speak for their people. When consensus cannot be reached, the Archdruid consults Vaelthir directly — a process that can take days of meditation and blood-offering.

### The Races of Talanthar

**Moon Elves**  
The eldest voices of the forest, keepers of memory and song. They dwell in the highest terraces and deepest grottoes, moving between worlds with an ease that unnerves outsiders. Their songs are polyphonic whispers that echo through the trees, beautiful, mournful, and unnervingly unified.

**Wood Elves**  
Including both indigenous populations and descendants of the Islands of Eternal Green refugees who fled Vashkoran persecution ~500-600 years ago. The "Ash-Marked" identity represents fully integrated Talanthari citizens who maintain additional memorial traditions. Significant intermarriage with moon elves over generations has created blended bloodlines while preserving distinct cultural knowledge.

**Halflings**  
The Joy-Wild clans who hunt from giant raptors and elk. They are the laughter of the Greenwood, the ones who feast after battle and turn sorrow into song. To Corvessans, they are "forest vermin." To Talanthar, they are **the Joy of the Greenwood** itself. They reportedly slew knights at the "Feast of Horns" with nothing but dinner forks and wild abandon.

**Beastfolk**  
Descendants of those who swore the Oath of the Greenwood in ages past, their bloodlines mingled with the spirits of the forest. They serve as scouts, shamans, and bridge-builders between the human and the wild.

**Humans**  
Fewer in number but no less devoted. Many are descendants of refugees from Corvessa's crusades, or those who fled the rigid order of the Pattern for the freedom of the forest. They serve as rangers, blacksmiths, and the interface between Talanthar and the outside world.

### Beliefs and Religion

The people of Talanthar do not worship Vaelthir as a god, but **venerate** it as both spirit and guardian. Temples to Ishara (nature and magic), Thamur (storms and strength), Sylthara (wisdom and prophecy), and Sarnock (death and cycles) stand beside the groves, representing balance between nature's fury and grace.

Witches, druids, and shamans act as intermediaries with lesser forest spirits. Magic in Talanthar is not studied from books but **learned from the land itself** — a conversation with the Weave rather than a command.

The core philosophical truth of Talanthar is simple:

> **Life is an endless song. Every leaf falls to feed the root; every death a verse that gives the next life its rhythm. To stop the cycle is the true heresy. To fix the Weave into a static design is to kill the song.**

### Traditions and Daily Life

**Blood Offerings**  
Children's first blood is given to the forest. Births and deaths are marked at Vaelthir's roots. Marriage ceremonies involve the binding of two saplings that will grow intertwined.

**Crops and Sustenance**  
Crops are modest: glowmoss rice, river millet, and shadowleaf herbs cultivated in flooded terraces or lighted caverns. Foraging, hunting, and fishing supplement what is grown. The people eat venison, river fish, glow-fruit, acorn bread, berry wines, and cultivate vast mushroom groves beneath the earth.

**Metalwork**  
Metal is rare and precious, traded from the dwarven holds or scavenged from the ruins of failed invasions. It is reforged by Magus-smiths in the Silver Hollow using techniques that blend traditional metallurgy with river-spirit breath and enchanted alloys.

**Festivals**  
Festivals follow the lunar cycles rather than solar seasons:

- **The Night of Still Waters**: When all magic and song fall silent to let the forest rest. Even the witches do not cast spells, and the druids do not call the spirits. It is a night of profound silence and meditation.

- **The Rising Veil**: When druids call the spirits to awaken the new season. Music, dancing, and the blessing of new growth marks this celebration.

- **The Feast of Memory**: An annual commemoration of all those who have returned to the roots, celebrated with storytelling and blood-offerings.

- **Seven Oases Remembrance** (Celestialdawn 15-23): Annual memorial honoring the Islands of Eternal Green, destroyed by Ashkarra's Burning Concord ~500-600 years ago. Primarily observed by Ash-Marked communities but increasingly adopted as general Talanthari commemoration of all victims of institutional knowledge suppression. Nine-day ceremony honors each destroyed oasis and reflects on Ashkarra's subsequent unmaking five days later. Features oral recitation of names, craft demonstrations (astronomy, lens-making, herbalism), and philosophical debates about whether Ashkarra's destruction was cosmic justice or parallel tragedy.

**Art and Music**  
Music is created with flutes of hollow vine and drums of bark. Songs echo through the trees in polyphonic whispers, voices overlapping like leaves in wind. Architecture flows organically with tree and cave. Crafts focus on living-wood shaping, bone-inlay, and rune-carving rather than the geometric precision of stone.

### Scholarship and Knowledge Preservation

Talanthar maintains one of Dór-o Estel's most comprehensive collections of "heretical" evidence regarding pre-divine civilizations, Thread cycle requirements, and cosmological patterns that challenge official theological narratives. This intellectual tradition combines:

**Indigenous Talanthari Scholarship:**
- Vaelthir-root memory reading
- Oral histories spanning millennia
- Forest-spirit communion for temporal information
- Anti-Pattern theological analysis

**Islands of Eternal Green Archives:**
- Astronomical records rescued by refugees
- Cycle-Singer mathematical models for Thread prediction
- Pre-divine archaeological evidence preserved in oral tradition
- Persecution warning systems and institutional pattern recognition

**Synthesis:**
Over 500+ years of integration, these traditions merged to create enhanced capabilities in:
- Long-term cosmic pattern prediction
- Identification of institutional knowledge suppression
- Understanding of pre-divine civilizations
- Evidence preservation despite external persecution

The **Eldermantle Archives** beneath the core city house rescued documents, while **Skywatch Archive** (Ash-Marked settlement) maintains astronomical instruments and observational records. **Scholar Verdanthil's teachings** at Aeloren's Grove represent the pinnacle of this cultural synthesis.

## PART IV: THE ETERNAL ENEMY — THE RELATIONSHIP WITH CORVESSA

### The Irreconcilable Divide

Of all the relationships that define Talanthar, none is more central, more visceral, and more enduring than its **eternal enmity** with the Kingdom of Corvessa. This is not merely a political rivalry or a border dispute. This is an **eschatological conflict** — a war not just over land, but over the very nature of reality itself.

What Corvessa calls "salvation," Talanthar calls "murder."  
What Talanthar calls "balance," Corvessa calls "blasphemy."  

No treaty can bridge such faiths because each is not merely a belief — it is a **definition of reality**. Thus the war cannot truly end; the enemy is not the other kingdom, but the **existence of the other worldview**.

### The Corvessan Worldview

To understand why Corvessa seeks to destroy Talanthar, one must understand the Faith of the Bound Pattern. Corvessa defines virtue as **structure**. The river is measured, the field furrowed, the bridge sanctified. To live rightly is to impose form upon the chaos of nature. Their Faith preaches that the Weave is a loom, each life a thread in divine design; deviation is heresy.

Art glorifies geometry: mosaics of symmetry, hymns in perfect metre, vineyards in ranked rows. Their knights see themselves as living extensions of law. Their symbol is **the Axe** — the tool that cuts down the forest, that brings order to wilderness, that shapes nature to human will.

Every Corvessan knight's axe is sworn to one day cut down the World Tree. To them, Vaelthir is not a guardian but a **false idol** demanding blood sacrifices, a corruption of the divine order that must be purged.

The Greenwood — growing, shifting, constantly rewriting itself — is **spiritual anarchy**. It denies the finality of death and mocks the perfection of divine architecture.

### The Talanthari Worldview

To Talanthar, Corvessa's faith is a **corruption of the Weave**, forcing scripture upon a living cycle. They mock Corvessa's Codex of the Pure Pattern as proof that the Bound Pattern is a cage, not a weave.

The druids and witches of Talanthar see life as an endless song, a cycle that cannot and must not be stopped. To fix the Weave into a static design is to **kill the song**. Death is not an ending but a returning. The forest feeds on what falls, and what falls feeds the forest. This is not cruelty but **balance**.

Corvessa's knights, to Talanthar, are **children playing with dead gods' bones** — predictable, arrogant fools who think shouting makes victory, who march in straight lines into forests that fight back. They are the Pattern's slaves, deaf to the song of life.

### Historical Memory: Centuries of Blood

Corvessa has launched **six major campaigns** into the Greenwood over the centuries (126, 298, 314, 403, 451, and 482 FA), all of which ended in failure or stalemate.

#### **The First Age: The Vanishing**
Before either polity consolidated, Corvessa's river-chiefs pushed eastward seeking timber and hunting grounds. These early expeditions vanished almost without trace. Only a single recovered journal survives — the Chronicle of Ser Tares the Younger — which records:

> *"The woods do not yield their paths; by noon the road is new, by dusk our names unmade."*

When the first fortified crossings appeared, the Greenwood responded not with armies but with **silence**: bridges collapsed overnight, crops failed, and horses refused to approach the treeline.

#### **The Crusades of the Pattern (312-360 FA)**
With the codification of the Faith of the Bound Pattern, the Corvessan Crown declared the Greenwood a spiritual infection requiring purgation. Three major crusades were launched:

**The First Ride of Purity (312-314 FA)**  
Ten thousand horse and levy crossed the Thornward Flats under banners of white flame. None returned save two heralds whose skin bore living vine scars that never healed.

**The Second Ride of Binding (333-334 FA)**  
A smaller, more disciplined force commanded by Saint-General Virel Marrow reached the Vaelrun ford before being encircled in mists that lasted six days. When the fog cleared, the army's standards hung from the trees upside-down, the men gone. Corvessa canonised Marrow as martyr; Talanthar remembers him as **The Weeping Steel**.

**The Third Ride of Order (359-360 FA)**  
Jointly sanctioned by the Kel Varyan Ecclesia before Corvessa became a client kingdom. This expedition was annihilated at what the elves call **The Battle of Singing Thorns**: vines reportedly erupted mid-charge, impaling cavalry. Surviving accounts describe music — the forest humming in time with the slaughter.

#### **The Breaking at Mossveil Gorge (298 FA)**
Still seen in Talanthar as their greatest triumph, in Corvessa as their greatest martyrdom. The details remain shrouded in legend and propaganda, but both sides agree: an entire Corvessan army marched into the gorge and never emerged.

#### **The Thornward Flats Disaster (482 FA) — The Fifth Failure**

The culmination of centuries of zeal came in the so-called **Holy Campaign of the River Lances**, an attempt to permanently subdue Talanthar with Imperial blessing. Nearly half of Corvessa's nobility rode that season — nine thousand lancers, a thousand clerics, and innumerable squires and servants. They expected triumph; they found annihilation.

Rain turned the Flats to mire, arrows fell like sleet, and **the forest itself advanced** — roots splitting armour, mud swallowing horses whole. Talanthar's response was surgical: rangers harried supply lines, witches inverted light, druids drowned the flanks with summoned floods. When retreat came, halfling joy-tribes hunted the survivors in what they call **The Great Chase**, each slain knight a trophy and an offering of laughter to the forest.

Legends tell that the halfling tribes sang the **"Iron Feast"** — captured knights were roasted in their own armour as offerings to the Old Wood. The moon elves tell tales of **"Silver Blood"** — the streams that ran red after the Lances fell.

Within five days the campaign ceased to exist. Corvessa's pride broke with its lances; its king aged ten years in mourning. Half of its knightly youth were dead.

Corvessa calls it the **Martyrdom at Thornward**, framing it as a test of faith rather than a defeat. Talanthar mocks it as **The Day of Laughing Shadows**, celebrating it annually with feasts and songs. Talanthari bards recite the Four Failures (and now the Fifth at Thornward) to laughter and applause in every tavern.

**Note on Ash-Marked Contributions:** While Talanthar would have won through the forest's inherent advantages, Ash-Marked communities contributed enhanced intelligence preparation. Their institutional pattern recognition predicted the campaign six months in advance, allowing optimal defensive positioning. Knowledge of Burning Concord military doctrine (preserved from refugee ancestors) informed counter-tactics against Pattern zealotry. Their expertise didn't determine victory but reduced Talanthari casualties and accelerated the campaign's collapse.

### The Cultural Mirror: How Each Sees the Other

| **Perspective** | **Corvessa's View of Talanthar** | **Talanthar's View of Corvessa** |
|---|---|---|
| **Faith** | Heretics of blood and root; their rituals mock divine geometry | The Pattern's slaves; deaf to the song of life |
| **Discipline** | Savages without law, ruled by whim of forest spirits | Children playing with dead gods' bones |
| **Art** | Twisting and asymmetrical, pleasing only to the mad | Soulless and frozen — art without breath |
| **Honor** | Treacherous ambushers, unworthy of knightly combat | Predictable fools who think shouting makes victory |
| **Fear** | The forest that eats men and remembers their names | The steel that forgets everything it kills |

Each society defines its virtues through the other's sins.

### Current State of the Conflict (487 FA)

**Corvessa: The Obsession Persists**  
The Grand Crusade has become a matter of politics as much as piety. Every monarch promises "preparation for the Final Cleansing" as a tool to rally nobles and divert unrest. The Faith avoids endorsing it yet tolerates the language to maintain influence.

Young knights train in "Forest Doctrine" — the art of fighting in terrain none have ever mastered — and every generation dreams of being the one to "fell the heretical tree." Behind the oaths and oratory, the truth remains: they do not seek conquest, but **atonement**. They wish to erase the reminder of failure that grows green beyond their reach.

Still, Corvessa's frontier nobles continue to send skirmishers, relic-hunters, and zealots into the woods, seeking to "redeem" ancestral failures or to find "proof" of Vaelthir's corruption. Each expedition vanishes, or returns half-mad, whispering of roots that drink blood and faces in the fog. The Faith does not sanction these missions — but neither does it forbid them. They are tolerated, even quietly celebrated. **The Crusade that never ends has simply become a cultural instinct.**

**Talanthar: Eternal Vigilance**  
Talanthar's high council remains patient but unyielding. They have seen the crusade come a dozen times before, and they know it will come again. Scouts maintain outposts hidden in the canopy and caves. The forest is their fortress; time their ally.

The High Council maintains a doctrine called **"Rooted Stillness"** — aggression is only sanctioned when the forest itself is threatened. However, this restraint should not be mistaken for weakness. When provoked, the Greenwood answers as one organism: rangers, beasts, spirits, and the land itself move in harmony.

The halfling tribes, still laughing, sharpen knives and polish trophies of gold and bone. The old magi whisper: **"The next charge will feed the roots anew."** They are emboldened, celebrating annual festivals of the Thornward victory, preparing for the "Sixth Failure" to be written in Corvessan blood if they try again.

### The "Two Burnings" Historical Memory

Talanthari historical consciousness encompasses not merely Corvessa's military crusades but also a broader pattern of civilization's hostility to uncomfortable truths. The integration of Islands refugees created what scholars call the **"Two Burnings" tradition**:

**The Thornward Burnings (Military Genocide):**
- Corvessa's repeated attempts to physically destroy Talanthar
- The crusades proving civilization will kill those who exist differently
- Military lesson: external powers threaten physical survival

**The Islands Burning (Knowledge Genocide):**  
- Ashkarra's systematic persecution of wood elf Cycle-Singers
- The destruction of astronomical evidence about Thread cycles and pre-divine civilizations
- Intellectual lesson: civilizations destroy those who preserve dangerous truths

**Combined Understanding:**
This dual memory creates comprehensive vigilance against:
- Military threats to Talanthari existence (Corvessa model)
- Theological/institutional threats to knowledge preservation (Ashkarra model)
- Any civilization claiming "divine mandate" to suppress evidence
- Both physical violence AND intellectual persecution as forms of genocide

The synthesis intensifies Talanthar's:
- Hostility to external authorities (both military and theological)
- Commitment to preserving uncomfortable truths
- Willingness to use preemptive force against perceived threats
- Support for refugees fleeing institutional persecution
- Deep suspicion of any power claiming to suppress knowledge "for the greater good"

**Practical Result:** Talanthar views Pattern-aligned civilizations as threatening on two fronts simultaneously—they will kill you for existing differently AND for knowing dangerous truths. This makes reconciliation with Corvessa or full Imperial integration impossible, as both represent the same fundamental threat the forest has resisted for centuries.

### The Geography of Division

**Fortifications and Watch Points**  
Corvessa maintains six active forts and dozens of ruined chapels along the border. The primary fortress is **Rivenscar**, built of black stone quarried from the first Crusade's ruins. Its walls bear the carved names of every fallen knight of the Pattern; new names are added yearly despite no new wars officially declared. The Faith Guard performs nightly vigils, ringing bells to "keep the forest awake."

In Talanthar, griffin towers perch upon cliffs overlooking these same valleys, their riders watching in silence. Neither side fires the first shot — both remember what happened when they did.

**Trade Corridors and Ghost-Roads**  
Between the outer hamlets of Talanthar and Corvessa's reed villages lie the **ghost-roads** — paths that alter course overnight. Some lead to the same tavern every traveller swears is elsewhere. Others end in pools reflecting stars not yet risen. These liminal routes act as neutral ground: smuggling trails, meeting places, sometimes graveyards for the lost.

### The Bitter Truth

To this day, any Corvessan entering the Thornward border uninvited finds paths twisting, food spoiling, and animals fleeing. Even those who mean no harm are treated with wary contempt. **No Talanthari forget that it was Corvessa who sought to cut the heart out of the world.**

**Talanthar remembers.** The Greenwood never forgets the ashes and prayers cast into its soil. In the deepest groves, the spirits of those slain during the Crusades still whisper in the bark.

There is **no diplomacy**. There is no standing embassy, no trade treaties, no peace accords. The Greenwood is considered enemy land. Corvessa and Talanthar are locked in a holy war without peace, their borders bristling with fortresses and groves. They do not talk. They do not trade legally. Every crossing of the Thornward Flats risks another bloodletting.

### The Imperial Perspective

The Kel Varyan Empire watches this eternal conflict with calculated detachment. The Empire notes this balance as both curse and blessing. Corvessa's zeal keeps its focus eastward, leaving its southern flank compliant and dependent. Talanthar's isolation ensures it will never become an aggressive regional power.

Thus, **the unending war that neither side can win has become the very mechanism that preserves the Imperial peace**. The Empire keeps Corvessa as a client buffer state but sees their fanaticism as dangerous. It tolerates Talanthar's independence, preferring to avoid direct entanglement.

Four years after Thornward, the Empire conducted its own limited incursion (486 FA) under guise of mapping. Two cohorts of the 14th Legion entered the eastern marches with orders of reconnaissance. They faced no open resistance — only ambush, misdirection, and weather turning hostile beyond natural measure. After forty-eight hours, command collapsed. When extraction came, fewer than half returned.

The Empire learned what Corvessa refused to accept: **discipline fails where the forest itself fights back.**

## PART V: MILITARY STRUCTURE

### The Forest as Fortress

Talanthar's military doctrine is simple: **never fight where the enemy is strong; always fight where the forest is alive**. The Greenwood itself is the first, last, and greatest weapon. Every tree, every root, every stream can become a tool of war.

### The Specialist Orders

Unlike Corvessa's massed cavalry charges, Talanthar employs **specialist small-unit orders** that excel at asymmetric warfare:

**Rangers and Scouts**  
Masters of camouflage, tracking, and ambush. They move through the forest like ghosts, appearing and disappearing at will. They know every path, every hollow, every secret of the Greenwood. 

**Ranger-Captain Ardhan Thornstep**, Commander of the Thornward Watch, is a veteran of three Corvessan invasions, famed for vanishing whole platoons without a sound.

**Warden Aelthir Ashborn** (Ash-Marked, age 180) commands the Thornward frontier watch-groves with particular focus on Pattern-knight counter-tactics. His aggressive defensive stance—advocating preemptive strikes against Corvessan border forts—creates occasional tension with the High Council's Rooted Stillness doctrine.

**Ranger-Captain Thornwatch** (Ash-Marked, age 67) serves as elite intelligence operative, specializing in institutional pattern recognition and long-term threat assessment. His expertise in predicting when "diplomatic missions" become reconnaissance for persecution campaigns makes him invaluable to border security.

**Griffin Riders and Skyguard**  
Aerial cavalry mounted on griffins and great eagles. They strike from above with devastating speed, dropping molten resin and burning sap upon enemy formations before vanishing into the clouds. Their aeries on the Skyperch Cliffs can scramble defenders to the border within an hour.

**Druids and Shamans**  
Combat mages who command the very elements of the forest. They can summon floods, grow barriers of thorns mid-battle, cause roots to erupt and impale cavalry, and bend light to create illusions that turn armies in circles. The forest **obeys** them because they are extensions of its will.

**Witches and Covens**  
Led by **Witch-Mother Selune Maraith**, Matriarch of the Moonveil covens, they maintain the moon-roads and veil Talanthar from scrying. In battle, they invert light, curse weapons to shatter, and whisper madness into the minds of invaders. They are responsible for the mists that claimed Saint-General Virel Marrow and his army.

**Halfling Joy-Wild Raiders**  
Mounted on giant raptors and elk, these are the terror of Corvessan nightmares. They do not fight with discipline but with **wild abandon and laughter**. **Halfling Chieftain Bran the Laughing Death** reportedly slew a dozen knights with a dinner fork at the "Feast of Horns." They hunt fleeing enemies like prey, turning retreat into massacre.

### Intelligence and Warning Systems

Ash-Marked communities contribute disproportionately to Talanthar's intelligence infrastructure through:

**Pattern Recognition:**
- Mathematical models predicting when civilizations become dangerous
- Signs that peaceful contact is becoming hostile infiltration  
- Identifying when "theological debate" becomes genocide preparation
- Institutional analysis understanding how bureaucracies suppress evidence

**Long-Term Vigilance:**
- Observational posts maintained for decades or centuries
- Multi-generational threat tracking
- Historical pattern correlation (comparing current events to past persecutions)
- Astronomical/celestial mechanics used for strategic timing

**Persecution Survival Techniques:**
- Methods for hiding archives during book-burning campaigns
- Maintaining knowledge through oral tradition when writing is threatened
- Evacuation protocols for scholars and children
- Recognizing escalation patterns before violence begins

**Burning Concord Doctrine:**
First-generation refugees brought direct knowledge of dragonborn military tactics, Embermarked priest authorities, and imperial logistical systems. Over 500 years, this was preserved, updated, and adapted, creating comprehensive understanding of Pattern-aligned military doctrine that proved valuable against Corvessa (similar theological foundations).

### Comparative Military Analysis

| **Aspect** | **Corvessa** | **Talanthar** |
|---|---|---|
| **Core Strength** | Heavy cavalry — River Lances are the finest in Elarion | Specialist small units in asymmetric terrain |
| **Doctrine** | Shock charges, frontal assaults, massed cavalry wedges | Small-unit tactics, ambush, skirmish, aerial superiority |
| **Weakness** | Predictable, inflexible in forests and broken ground | Cannot survive open plains warfare |
| **Magic Use** | Minimal "wild" magic; divine warlocks and theurges | Druids, witches, shamans — nature magic at its peak |
| **Terrain** | Dominates open plains and river valleys | Unconquerable in forest and broken terrain |

**The Result**: Stalemate through centuries. Corvessa cannot break into the Greenwood; Talanthar cannot survive a full plains campaign into the Ivora valley.

### The Doctrine of Rooted Stillness

Talanthar does not wage wars of conquest. It does not seek to expand beyond its borders or impose its will on others. The High Council's doctrine of **"Rooted Stillness"** means that aggression is only sanctioned when the forest itself is threatened.

But when threatened, **the Greenwood answers as one organism**. Every ranger, every beast, every spirit, and the land itself moves in harmony. Invasions are not defeated by superior numbers but by **superior adaptation** — the forest learning, adjusting, and responding to threats with terrifying efficiency.

**Internal Debate:** Warden Aelthir Ashborn and some Ash-Marked communities argue for expanded interpretation of "threatened"—suggesting preemptive action against Corvessan mobilizations rather than waiting for invasion. The High Council generally rejects this, maintaining that Rooted Stillness requires actual forest endangerment, not merely external hostility.

## PART VI: TRADE AND ECONOMY

### The Paradox of Isolation

Though officially hostile to Corvessa and wary of outsiders, Talanthar cannot survive in complete isolation. The forest provides much, but not everything. Metal must be imported. Crystal must be traded. Knowledge must be exchanged.

### What Talanthar Exports

**Medicinal Herbs and Healing Tinctures**  
Rare resins, shadowleaf herbs, and forest medicines that fetch fortunes in Corvessan and Imperial apothecaries. These are gathered by druids who know which plants to take and which to leave, maintaining the balance.

**Enchanted Wood and Moonwood**  
Living-wood carvings that continue to grow even after being shaped. Moonwood that glows faintly in darkness. These are coveted by Imperial collectors and nobles across Elarion.

**Griffon Feathers and Enchanted Hides**  
From the Skyguard's mounts and the hunts of the halfling tribes. These materials are used in the finest armors and cloaks.

**Leather and Bone Crafts**  
Intricate carvings and functional goods made from animals hunted sustainably within the forest.

**Specialized Instruments**  
Ash-Marked craftspeople produce high-quality lenses, astronomical instruments, and precision glasswork that command premium prices from Imperial scholars and navigators.

### What Talanthar Imports

**Metals and Tools**  
Iron, steel, bronze — all mined in Corvessa's western hills or the dwarven holds. These are traded through **Imperial intermediaries** and dwarven brokers, never directly with Corvessa.

**Salt and Preserved Goods**  
The forest provides much, but salt must come from the sea or mountain mines.

**Crystal and Gemstones**  
Used in magical workings and the illumination of the Underbloom.

**Knowledge and Books**  
Though Talanthar's oral traditions are strong, written knowledge from the outside world is valued, particularly magical texts and Imperial observations.

### The Hidden Trade

Though officially at war, Corvessa and Talanthar are **economically interlocked through intermediaries and necessity**:

**Smuggling through the Myrn Caves**  
Ex-legionnaires and dwarven traders run caravans of unmarked goods — iron, silk, even relics stolen from battlefields. Some whisper that Corvessan blood-mages secretly seek **Vaelthiri sap** for forbidden rites of regeneration.

**The Reed Fair**  
Every midsummer, a neutral market known as the Reed Fair assembles on the border at the River Velis. Talanthar sends witches and traders draped in living moss; Corvessa sends merchant-priests under white banners. Gold changes hands, goods are exchanged, and both sides pretend not to recognize each other.

**Neutral Inns and Moving Taverns**  
Three neutral inns operate within shifting forest borders:
- **The Silver Stag**: Appears during spring thaw; hosts treaties and assassinations alike
- **Mother Glinna's Cup**: Run by a halfling matron; safest passage for Imperial traders if they bring sweet pastries
- **The Red Root**: Serves both sides but demands a blood tithe from all guests — a single drop for the forest's memory

### Imperial Brokerage

The Kel Varyan Empire serves as the primary intermediary for "authorized" trade:
- **Metals & Tools**: Mined in Corvessa, smithed in Imperial ports, traded under neutral seal to Talanthar
- **Medicinals & Herbs**: Gathered in Talanthar, shipped through Imperial merchant guilds to Corvessa
- **Spirits & Vintages**: Corvessan wines exchanged for Greenwood meads
- **Art & Scripture**: Corvessan illuminated manuscripts traded for Vaelthiri living-wood carvings

## PART VIII: CURRENT STATE OF AFFAIRS (487 FA)

### The High Council's Dilemma

The High Council debates the renewal of the Covenant as Imperial agents prowl the borderlands. There are whispers that the Empire seeks to study Vaelthir's secrets, to adapt the forest's "self-sustaining magic systems" for their own purposes. Some druids argue for deeper isolation; others for cautious engagement.

Meanwhile, the forest itself seems restless. There are reports of groves where time flows unevenly, of spirits older than memory awakening, of the World Tree pulsing with unusual intensity. Some say the forest senses another great conflict approaching.

### Corvessa's Growing Desperation

King Aurel VII grows older and more desperate. The zealot youth orders are swelling with recruits, all dreaming of being the generation that finally fells the World Tree. The Faith officially counsels patience, but unofficially tolerates — even encourages — expeditions into the Greenwood.

Young knights train obsessively in "Forest Doctrine," studying Imperial records of the 486 FA skirmish, trying to learn what the legions learned too late. But the forest that defeated the legions is not the same forest they will face — it has learned, adapted, and remembered.

### The Imperial Calculus

The Empire watches and waits. It has learned that conquest of Talanthar is impossible, but **understanding** may be achievable. Imperial scholars seek to unlock the secrets of blood-bound communion, of sustainable symbiosis with living ecosystems, of magic that requires no external fuel.

But every attempt at direct study risks "moral and metaphysical contamination." Those who spend too long in the Greenwood begin to hear the forest's song. They return changed — speaking of cycles and balance, unable to return to the rigid certainties of Imperial doctrine.

The Empire's policy remains: **study, do not conquer. Observe, do not interfere. Profit while they bleed.**

### The Kethyr Flameheart Problem

Current events (487 FA) have created unprecedented internal tension within Talanthar. **Kethyr Flameheart**, ancient dragonborn survivor of Ashkarra's unmaking and leader of the persecution campaign against the Islands of Eternal Green, has launched a truth-revelation campaign exposing divine fraud and Thread violations through systematic temple destruction.

**The Impossible Moral Question:**

Ash-Marked communities face unique complexity:
- Kethyr personally led the persecution that killed their ancestors
- The evidence he reveals is identical to what Islands Cycle-Singers preserved
- His confession validates 500 years of Ash-Marked truth-telling
- But his methods (temple burning, targeted killings) repeat the cycle of violence

**Community Split (~40-30-30):**
- **40% Cautious Opposition**: Acknowledge truth but oppose methods
- **30% Active Opposition**: View as hypocrite and murderer regardless
- **30% Reluctant Support**: Believe truth revelation justifies costs

**High Council Position:**
- Officially opposes Kethyr's terrorism
- Respects Ash-Marked communities' complicated relationship
- Some Ash-Marked rangers have secretly joined Ashen Prophets
- Others actively hunt former community members who joined Kethyr
- Creates internal division unprecedented in Talanthari history

**Notable Positions:**
- Elder Sylthar: *Opposes Kethyr* ("The method is his truth")
- Warden Aelthir: *Reluctantly Supports* ("Truth from tainted sources")
- Scholar Verdanthil: *Torn* ("Can truth be poisoned by messenger?")
- Ranger-Captain Thornwatch: *Tactical Opposition* ("Creating his own prophecy")

This crisis forces Talanthar to confront central questions:
- Can truth serve justice when revealed by someone guilty of destroying it?
- Should descendants of victims support a perpetrator-turned-whistleblower?
- Does accepting truth from blood-soaked hands validate the bloodshed?
- What do communities who preserved truth through survival owe to someone who reveals it through destruction?

### The Question of the Sixth Failure

All of Talanthar knows the question: **When will Corvessa come again?**

The answer, whispered in every grove and sung in every tavern, is always the same:

**"They will come. The Pattern demands it. And when they come, the roots will drink deep once more."**

The forest waits. Patient. Eternal. Remembering every name carved into its bark, every drop of blood fed to its roots, every crusade that broke upon its shores.

Talanthar endures not because it stands against time, but because time itself flows through its roots.

## PART X: THE NATURE OF THE FOREST

### A Warning to Outsiders

Even to its own people, Talanthar's forest is not entirely safe. There are groves where the trees whisper in forgotten tongues, glades where time flows unevenly, and spirits older than memory. The forest protects its children, but it also **tests** them — demanding respect, blood, and balance.

Those who ignore its will vanish, becoming part of the roots and legends that sustain it.

The forest is not benevolent in any human sense. It is **indifferent** to individual survival but obsessed with collective balance. It will save a child who respects it and consume a king who does not. It judges not by rank or righteousness, but by **relationship** — whether one takes more than one gives, whether one listens or commands, whether one feeds the cycle or breaks it.

### The Liminal Spaces

Between the ordered world of stone and law and the wild chaos of the deep forest lie **liminal spaces** — places where reality bends:

**Ghost-Roads**  
Paths that change overnight, leading to different destinations depending on who walks them. Some roads lead to taverns that exist only on certain nights. Others lead to pools that reflect stars from other skies.

**Time-Glades**  
Clearings where time flows at different rates. A traveler might rest for an hour and emerge to find a week has passed outside. Or spend a day within and find only moments have elapsed in the world beyond.

**Memory-Groves**  
Places where the forest's memories become tangible. Walking through certain groves, one might see echoes of past battles, hear the songs of the dead, or witness moments from centuries past replaying like theater.

**Spirit-Crossings**  
Locations where the barrier between the material world and the realm of spirits grows thin. Here, druids commune with ancient powers, witches bargain with entities of pure concept, and the unwary may be claimed by forces beyond mortal understanding.

### The Price of Knowledge

Imperial scholars who have attempted to study the Greenwood describe a phenomenon they call **"forest-binding"** — the longer one spends within Talanthar, the more difficult it becomes to leave. Not physically, but psychologically. The rhythms of the forest begin to feel more natural than the rhythms of cities. The song becomes more compelling than speech. The cycle more true than linear time.

Some never leave. They are found years later, barely human, more spirit than flesh, having forgotten their names but remembered the names of every tree in their grove.

This is not corruption, the druids explain. This is **truth**. The forest does not lie. It simply shows you what you already were — and most cannot bear the revelation.

## CONCLUSION: THE MIRROR AND THE FOREST

Talanthar and Corvessa are twin reflections of the same flaw: **the need to make meaning from the chaos of existence**. Corvessa binds the world in pattern until it bleeds. Talanthar bleeds willingly to feed the world.

Between them stands the Vaelthir Tree — patient, ancient, and unbroken. It does not care for who wins or loses. It simply remembers.

Should the Empire ever seek dominion over the Greenwood, it must first decide whether it wishes to conquer a forest — or a **mirror**.

For Talanthar is not merely a place or a people. It is a question posed to all who seek to order the world: **What if the chaos is not the enemy? What if the cycle is not a flaw but the foundation? What if growth requires death, and death is not an ending but a feeding?**

These are questions Corvessa cannot ask without destroying itself. These are questions the Empire fears to answer.

And so the Greenwood endures, unconquered and unconquerable, not through strength of arms but through strength of **truth** — the truth that all things return to the root, all roots feed the tree, and the tree remembers **everything**.
