# The Inventor

**Create, Innovate, Dominate**

> *"Any sufficiently prepared Inventor is indistinguishable from magic."*

---

## The Engineer's Mind

The party faced a locked gate, a formation of armoured guards, and no time to think — except for him. In the forty seconds it took the Paladin to explain the situation, he had already catalogued three viable approaches, estimated the material requirements for each, and selected the one that required only what was already in his pack. By the time the Paladin finished talking, the gate was open.

You are the Inventor. Where a wizard memorises how magic speaks, you speak back — through engineering. Magic is not your art. Magic is your material. A spell is a component, the same as iron or leather, and the measure of a good Inventor is not how much magic they know but how cleverly they use what they have.

Your **Specialisation** defines your engineering discipline at 1st level. Your **Upgrades** let you customise your toolkit as you level. Your reactions — **Emergency Gadget**, **Infusion Burst**, and **Overclock** — turn your preparation into crisis response when your allies need it most.

---

## The Inventor's Essence

You are the party's problem-solver, utility provider, and equipment craftsperson. Your allies benefit from your infusions and gadgets long after the fight ends. Your tactical intelligence — knowing which body locations are unprotected, which enemy is reliant on a specific ability — often decides fights before damage becomes decisive.

You struggle if you haven't prepared for the current situation. You excel in any scenario you could see coming and any encounter where your gadgets, constructs, or infused equipment create compounding advantages.

## Class Features

### Hit Points

- **Hit Dice:** 1d8 per Inventor level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 8 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per Inventor level after 1st

### Proficiencies

- **Armour:** Light armour, medium armour
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons, hand crossbows, heavy crossbows
- **Tools:** Thieves' tools, and one other tool of your choice
- **Saving Throws:** Focus, Intelligence
- **Skills:** Choose three from Arcana, Crafting, Deception, Gathering, History, Investigation, Medicine, Nature, Religion, Sleight of Hand

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) any two simple weapons
- (a) scale mail, (b) leather armour, or (c) chain mail (if proficient)
- Thieves' tools and a dungeoneer's pack

## Quick Build

To quickly create an Inventor, follow these steps:

1. **Ability Scores:** Make Intelligence your highest score, followed by Constitution.
2. **Lineage:** Any combination works; Gnome, Human, and Dwarf are thematic.
3. **Background:** Guild Artisan, Sage, or any background with Crafting or Arcana.
4. **Combat Strategy:** Distribute infused items before combat. Use Emergency Gadget reactively during others' turns. Target your Called Shots at unprotected body locations for maximum DR bypass.

## Core Class Features

### Inventor Specialisation (1st Level)

Choose your engineering discipline at 1st level: **Gadgetsmith**, **Golemsmith**, **Infusionsmith**, **Potionsmith**, **Thundersmith**, **Warsmith**, **Fleshsmith**, **Cursesmith**, **Runesmith**, or **Relicsmith**. Your Specialisation grants features at 1st level and again at 3rd, 5th, and 14th levels. See the **Inventor Subclasses** document for detailed Specialisation features.

---

### Magic Item Analysis (1st Level)

You know *detect magic* and *identify* and can cast them as rituals without material components. This is practical assessment — you are reading engineering, not reciting formulae.

---

### Spellcasting (2nd Level)

At 2nd level, you gain the ability to cast Inventor spells. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability.

- **Spell Save DC** = 8 + PB + INT modifier
- **Spell Attack Modifier** = PB + INT modifier

You know spells from the Inventor spell list as shown in the table. You regain all spell slots on a long rest. You can use an arcane focus or any tool you are proficient with as your spellcasting focus.

---

### Arcane Retrofit (2nd Level)

During a long rest, perform a special ritual using any +1, +2, or +3 weapon (excluding artefacts and sentient weapons) to transfer its magical bonus into an Inventor weapon (created by a subclass feature or upgrade). The original weapon is destroyed. You can also convert armour with a magical AC bonus to a lighter armour type.

---

### Tool Expertise (2nd Level)

Your Proficiency Bonus is doubled for any ability check using tool proficiencies gained from this class.

---

### Specialisation Upgrade (3rd Level)

Choose an upgrade from your Specialisation list at 3rd level. Gain additional upgrades as shown in the Upgrades column. You cannot select the same upgrade more than once (unless its description says otherwise). When you gain a level, you may exchange one upgrade for another of the same or lower level requirement.

**Generic Upgrades** available to all Specialisations in place of any Specialisation upgrade: Shield Proficiency (gain proficiency with shields) and Tool Proficiency (gain proficiency with a tool of your choice).

---

### Engineered Guard (3rd Level)

While wearing armour you have modified or created using Inventor features (including any armour bearing your infusions, upgrades, or modifications), gain **DR 1** to all body locations. This DR stacks with base armour DR but does not stack with other Inventor features that explicitly grant DR.

---

### Weapon Mastery (5th Level)

At 5th level, gain Weapon Mastery with one simple weapon and one additional weapon granted by your Specialisation (if applicable).

After scoring critical hits, unlock new masteries through downtime training: 2 crits for simple weapons, 3 for martial weapons.

**DCS Synergy:** Inventor Weapon Skills frequently interact with the DCS — treat them as tactical levers. "Shatter Guard" reduces DR for your construct's next attack; "Hamstring" controls enemy movement so your deployed trap catches them.

---

### Arcane Reconstruction (6th Level)

You learn the *mending* cantrip (cast at will) and *cure wounds*. If you already know *cure wounds*, learn a different spell from the Inventor spell list instead. Constructs targeted by your *cure wounds* regain HP normally — biological repair and mechanical repair are the same discipline applied to different substrates.

---

### Cross-Disciplinary Knowledge (6th Level)

Your engineering perspective extends across Specialisation boundaries. Choose one of the following when you gain this feature:

- Any of the Gadgetsmith's Unrestricted Upgrades.
- An Infusionsmith's Animated Weapon, Blasting Rod, or Infused Weapon.
- A Potionsmith's Alchemical Reagent Pouch and Alchemical Fire or Acid.
- A Thundersmith's Stormforged Weapon (gain proficiency with that weapon and knowledge of how to make ammunition).

If a crafted item is lost or destroyed, recreate it during a long rest.

---

### Wondrous Item Proficiency (7th Level)

You can ignore all class restrictions on the use of magic items. Additionally, you can attune to up to **four magic items** at once.

---

### Improved Magical Crafting (10th Level)

Your expertise with magical construction has grown to allow you to craft or modify a wider range of magical items. During downtime, you can craft magical items of Uncommon rarity and below without requiring a special formula.

---

### Wondrous Item Recharge (10th Level)

During a short rest, you can recharge one magic item that has expended charges, rolling its recharge die and restoring that many charges (same as if the item had been recharged at dawn). Uses equal to your INT modifier per long rest.

---

### Heroic Boon (10th Level)

Choose one:

**Boon of the Master Tinkerer:** Once per short rest, spend 10 minutes to modify any magical item you are attuned to, temporarily granting it one additional property from a list of Inventor-approved modifications (determined with GM). This modification lasts until your next long rest.

**Boon of the Arcane Forge:** Once per long rest, you can spend 10 minutes to craft a single-use magical item of Uncommon rarity or lower from materials on hand. The item functions normally but crumbles to dust after one use.

---

### Study of Magic (11th Level)

Your analysis of magical construction allows you to adapt spells on the fly. When you cast a spell, choose one of the following modifications (no additional cost):

- Change the damage type to acid, fire, cold, lightning, or thunder.
- Increase or decrease the range by 30 feet.
- Change the area of effect shape (cone to cylinder, sphere to cube of similar size).

---

### Wondrous Item Mastery (17th Level)

You can now attune to up to **five magic items** simultaneously. Additionally, you can use any wondrous item as a bonus action instead of an action.

---

### Peerless Inventor (18th Level)

Your engineering reaches legendary status. Once per long rest, as an action, you can rapidly assemble a mechanical device of incredible complexity. This device functions as one of the following (your choice):

- An **Arcane Cannon** (deals 2d10 force damage in a 60-foot line or 20-foot cone; lasts 10 minutes; fires as a bonus action).
- A **Mechanical Ally** (use the Iron Golem stat block at half HP; lasts 1 hour; follows simple commands).
- An **Emergency Barrier** (creates a 60-foot wall of force-reinforced metal; lasts 10 minutes; 100 HP).

---

### Epic Boon (20th Level)

Choose one:

**Boon of Limitless Creation:** Once per short rest, produce any mundane or alchemical item worth up to 100 gp instantaneously. When you craft a magical item, reduce the time required by half and the cost by 25%. You can attune to up to six magic items simultaneously.

**Boon of Arcane Perfection:** You can ignore material components when casting Inventor spells, including expensive components. Once per long rest, cast any Inventor spell of 5th level or lower without expending a spell slot. All items you craft are treated as masterwork quality, adding +1 to their effectiveness.

## Last Stand Options

The Inventor's Last Stand reflects three different expressions of the same engineering identity: the final device, the legacy distribution, or the pre-built contingency.

**Rules for Last Stand:** Invoked when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, roll a death saving throw, or die outright. A free action requiring no reaction or consciousness. Once resolved, the character permanently dies.

---

### 1. The Last Gadget

*You built something for exactly this moment. You just didn't expect to need it today.*

**Effect:** Choose one option representing your masterwork device:

- **Null Field:** Create a 60-foot area suppressing all magic for 1 minute. Every creature in the area must make a CON save (DC = 10 + PB + INT) or take 6d10 lightning damage and be Staggered (cannot take reactions) until end of their next turn.
- **Golem Final Protocol:** Summon a construct with CR equal to your level that obeys simple commands for 1 minute.
- **Proximity Network:** Detonate a network of charges you've laid throughout the encounter — all creatures of your choice within 60 feet take 8d10 thunder damage (DEX save for half, DC = 10 + PB + INT).

---

### 2. Appreciation

*You spent a career creating things. Your final act: giving it all away.*

**Effect:**
- Teleport all your attunement items to allies of your choice (no attunement required; no limit on number attuned per ally).
- For the next 5 minutes, all your gadgets and infused items function at their maximum possible potency.
- Allies can use their bonus action to pass magic items between themselves within 60 feet.

---

### 3. Deadman's Switch

*You built the contingency years ago. The trigger was always "when I'm not there to stop it."*

**Effect:** Choose a number of allies equal to your INT modifier (minimum 2). Each receives one of the following (same or different): regain all expended spell slots; regain all expended class resource uses; gain temp HP equal to 3 × your Inventor level; immediately move up to their speed without provoking and gain advantage on their next attack or save.

**Additionally**, trigger one pre-built environmental modification:
- **Hazard Grid:** 60-foot area becomes difficult terrain; creatures of your choice take 2d8 thunder at start of each turn for 1 minute.
- **Barrier Network:** Force barriers materialise at positions of your choice (up to 120 feet of total connected length); solid and impassable to enemies, transparent to allies; last 1 minute.
- **Emergency Extraction:** All allies within 60 feet teleport to the nearest unoccupied space within 30 feet of a location you designated as a safe point in the last 24 hours.

| Last Stand Option | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| **The Last Gadget** | Final device: magic suppression, golem, or proximity detonation. | Maximum battlefield disruption. |
| **Appreciation** | Distribute all magic items; everything functions at maximum potency for 5 minutes. | Legacy transfer. |
| **Deadman's Switch** | Restore ally resources; trigger pre-built environmental modification. | Turning a losing fight. |

## Class Reactions

Inventors have access to powerful reactions that translate preparation into crisis response. These reactions use your **Proficiency Bonus pool**, refreshing on short or long rest, available from 7th level.

### Emergency Gadget (7th Level)

**Trigger:** You or an ally within 30 feet is hit by an attack or targeted by a spell.
**Effect:** Immediately deploy a stored gadget or invention from your Specialisation that normally requires an action to activate. The gadget activates as part of this reaction. Defensive effects (cover, obscurement, damage reduction, forced saves) apply before the triggering damage resolves.

---

### Infusion Burst (7th Level)

**Trigger:** You or an ally within 30 feet takes damage from an attack.
**Effect:** Reactively activate the stored power of an infused item you or that ally is carrying or wearing. Reduce the triggering damage by **2d6 + your INT modifier**, or trigger the infused item's offensive or utility effect instead.

*Scaling:* Damage reduction increases to **3d6 + INT** at 11th level and **4d6 + INT** at 15th level.

*DCS Integration:* When protecting an ally, if the attack would have reduced them below their Bloodied threshold, the attacker loses 1 Momentum.

---

### Overclock (7th Level)

**Trigger:** You take your Attack action and hit with an attack using an Inventor weapon or infused weapon.
**Effect:** Spend 1 reaction use to trigger an overclocked enhancement on that attack. Roll an additional weapon damage die and add it to the damage. Additionally, the target must succeed on a CON saving throw (DC = 8 + PB + INT modifier) or have their DR reduced by 2 until the start of your next turn — your strike overloaded their armour's energy absorption.
