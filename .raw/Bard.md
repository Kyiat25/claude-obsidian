# The Bard

**Masters of Reality's Symphony**

> *"You don't play music. You play the room. The music is just how you do it."*

---

## The Story That Changes Everything

The negotiation was failing. Three factions, each convinced the others were lying, none willing to move. He didn't stand up and make a speech. He told a story — the right story, at the right moment, about a man very much like the one in the room who had faced exactly this kind of impasse once before. By the time the story ended, the room was different. Not because anything had changed, except that now everyone in it understood something they hadn't before.

You are the Bard. Not because you play an instrument, though you might. Because you understand that every situation is a performance, and every performance can be shaped by someone who knows what they're doing. **Bardic Inspiration** doesn't just make your allies better — it shifts momentum at the exact moment it matters. **Cutting Words** doesn't just debuff an enemy — it makes them doubt themselves at the worst possible time.

In FEARS, your Momentum engine runs through your allies. Every Bardic Inspiration die granted immediately gives the recipient +1 Momentum — or more, if your Performance check exceeds the DC. **Cutting Rhythm** at 3rd level lets you actively suppress enemy Momentum generation as a reaction.

---

## The Bard's Essence

You are the party's force multiplier and its intelligence. **Magical Secrets** means your spell list at high levels is the widest of any class. **Expertise** means you dominate skills. **Bardic Performance** options give you moment-to-moment choices that shape the fight's emotional and tactical landscape simultaneously.

You struggle in pure attrition fights where your spell slots drain faster than your team can win. You excel in mixed encounters where intelligent use of inspiration and narrative control turns fights before they peak.

---

## Complexity Assessment: MODERATE (5/10)  

The Bard tracks:                                             
• Bardic Inspiration uses (CHA modifier per long rest)   
• Inspiration die size (d6 → d12)                            
• Spell slots (full caster progression)                      
• Performance check results (for Inspiration quality)
• Class Reaction pool (PB uses per short rest)    

Recommended for players who enjoy enabling others, flexible spell selection, and social/skill mastery.

## Class Features

### Hit Points

- **Hit Dice:** 1d8 per Bard level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 8 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per Bard level after 1st

### Proficiencies

- **Armour:** Light armour
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons, hand crossbows, longswords, rapiers, shortswords
- **Tools:** Three musical instruments of your choice
- **Saving Throws:** Dexterity, Charisma
- **Skills:** Choose any three

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) a rapier, (b) a longsword, or (c) any simple weapon
- (a) a diplomat's pack or (b) an entertainer's pack
- (a) a lute or (b) any other musical instrument
- Leather armour and a dagger

## Quick Build

To quickly create a Bard, follow these steps:

1. **Ability Scores:** Make Charisma your highest score, followed by Dexterity.
2. **Lineage and Heritage:** Any combination works; Half-Elf, Tiefling, and Human are thematic but not required.
3. **Background:** Entertainer, Charlatan, or any background with Performance or Persuasion.
4. **Combat Strategy:** Grant Bardic Inspiration at the start of combat, use Cutting Words to disrupt dangerous enemy attacks, and use your action for offensive spells or Bardic Performance effects each turn.

## Core Class Features

### Bardic Inspiration (1st Level)

As a bonus action, inspire a creature within 60 feet that can hear or see you. That creature gains a **Bardic Inspiration die** (d6, increasing at 5th, 10th, and 14th level), which they can add to one ability check, attack roll, or saving throw within the next 10 minutes.

When you grant Bardic Inspiration, make a **Performance check** (DC = 10 + recipient's level):

| Performance Result | Inspiration Die | Additional Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Fail DC** | Your base die | None |
| **Meet/Beat DC** | Your base die | Recipient gains +1 Momentum |
| **Beat DC by 5+** | Die increases one size | Recipient gains +1 Momentum |
| **Beat DC by 10+** | Die increases one size + Advantage on first use | Recipient gains +2 Momentum |
| **Natural 20** | Die increases two sizes + Advantage on first use | Recipient gains +3 Momentum; all allies within 10 feet gain your base die and +1 Momentum each |

**Natural 1:** Inspiration die granted at base size, but disadvantage on your next Performance check to grant Bardic Inspiration.

Uses equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum 1), regaining all uses after a long rest.

**Inspirational Momentum:** When you grant a Bardic Inspiration die to a creature, that creature immediately gains +1 Momentum (or more based on your Performance result). This occurs immediately when the die is granted, not when it is used.

---

### Spellcasting (1st Level)

Your magic emerges through performance and narrative. Charisma is your spellcasting ability.

- **Spell Save DC** = 8 + PB + Charisma modifier
- **Spell Attack Modifier** = PB + Charisma modifier

You learn cantrips and spells from the Bard spell list, plus any from Magical Secrets. Rituals can be cast without spell slots.

---

### Weapon Mastery (1st Level)

At 1st level, gain Weapon Mastery with **one simple or finesse weapon** of your choice. When you hit with that weapon, apply one Weapon Skill once per turn.

**Bardic Synergy:** Physical debuffs from skills combine with your Bardic Inspiration and spell effects for support plays that scale with party damage.

---

### Expertise (2nd and 6th Level)

Choose two skill or tool proficiencies. Double your Proficiency Bonus for checks using them. At 6th level, choose two more.

---

### Magical Inspiration (2nd Level)

When a creature with a Bardic Inspiration die casts a healing or damage-dealing spell, they can add the die's result to the spell's effect. The die is then expended.

---

### Bardic Performance (2nd and 6th Level)

You weave magic into your performance. Choose one effect per performance:

**Celebrate Life:** Allies can spend hit dice to heal, adding your Charisma modifier. They also gain advantage on death saves.

**Cutting Words:** Use your reaction to reduce a target's ability check, attack, or damage roll by the result of a Bardic Inspiration die.

At 6th level, add **Clarity of Thought:** Allies cannot be charmed or frightened and gain advantage on saves to end those effects.

---

### Bard Subclass (3rd Level, and 7th, 11th, 15th Level)

Choose a College such as **College of Lore**, **College of Valour**, or **College of Whispers**. Your College grants features at 3rd, 7th, 11th, and 15th levels.

---

### Font of Inspiration (5th Level)

Regain all Bardic Inspiration uses when you finish a short or long rest. Additionally, when you roll initiative with no Inspiration uses remaining, regain 1 use. Once per combat.

---

### Elusive Performer (7th Level, scaling)

Your performances teach you to flow like music, making you harder to pin down. Gain **+1 to Evasion rolls**. Increases to +2 at 11th level and +3 at 15th level.

---

### Magical Secrets (9th, 13th, and 18th Level)

Learn two spells from any class list at each of these levels. They count as Bard spells for you.

---

### Heroic Boon (10th Level)

Choose one:

**Persistent Inspiration:** If an Inspiration die is used and fails (doesn't help), it can be used again.

**Valiant Inspiration:** The recipient can add the Bardic Inspiration die to a damage roll or use it to reduce damage taken.

---

### Soothing Performance (10th Level)

Once per long rest, perform for at least 10 minutes during a short rest. Choose one creature who hears you throughout. That creature may downgrade one Injury by one tier: Major becomes Minor, Minor is healed completely. Must be uninterrupted; target must be conscious and able to hear you.

---

### Grand Performance (17th Level)

When you would grant Bardic Inspiration and have no uses remaining, declare a **Grand Performance** as a special action (does not require a use).

**Effect:**
- Automatically achieve a Natural 20 on the Performance check.
- The recipient receives an Inspiration die increased by two sizes with Advantage on first use.
- All allies within 10 feet of the recipient receive your standard Inspiration die.
- The target gains +3 Momentum; nearby allies gain +1 Momentum each.
- You gain +4 Momentum.

After using Grand Performance, you cannot regain Bardic Inspiration uses until you finish a long rest. Once per long rest.

---

### Epic Boon (20th Level)

Choose one:

**Curtain Call:** At the start of your turn, if you have no Bardic Inspiration dice remaining, regain a number of dice equal to your Proficiency Bonus. Once per long rest, perform a Masterpiece (action): for 1 minute, all allies within 60 feet gain advantage on all saving throws, add a 1d12 Bardic Inspiration die to all attack rolls and ability checks, and gain temporary HP equal to twice your Bard level.

**Crescendo of Magic:** Once per short rest, cast any spell you know or have prepared of 6th circle or lower without expending a spell slot, cast at its highest level. When you cast a spell affecting multiple allies, those allies gain temporary HP equal to your Charisma modifier + the spell's level.

## Last Stand Options

A Bard's Last Stand ensures their final act resonates through the battle — a forbidden spell, critical intelligence, or a rousing final performance.

**Rules for Last Stand:** Invoked when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, roll a death saving throw, or die outright. A free action requiring no reaction or consciousness. Once resolved, the character permanently dies.

---

### 1. Best Kept Secret

*You swore you would never use it so long as you lived. It seems you won't live much longer.*

**Effect:**
- Cast any spell from any class up to your highest known spell level.
- No material, verbal, or somatic components required. Does not consume spell slots.
- You may maintain concentration on the spell beyond death for the shorter of its duration or 5 minutes.

---

### 2. Friends on the Other Side

*After crossing over, you manage to persuade the gatekeeper to let you slip back just once.*

**Effect:**
- Choose a number of allies (up to your Charisma modifier) anywhere in the world.
- Reveal crucial information about a foe that they access in their next dream: a map of the foe's hideout, knowledge of a credible enemy the foe has made, or the foe's hidden motive or flaw that could be exploited.

---

### 3. Swan Song

*As your voice breaks, all the passion of a life well-lived can be heard in a question: tragedy or triumph?*

**Effect:**
- Perform a final song or performance.
- Allies who can hear you gain Bardic Inspiration dice equal to your Charisma modifier.
- These dice disappear after 5 minutes.
- When allies use these dice within 5 minutes, they **cancel all sources of disadvantage** on the roll.

| Last Stand Option | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Best Kept Secret** | Cast any spell without components; maintain concentration beyond death. | High-impact magical devastation. |
| **Friends on the Other Side** | Reveal critical intelligence to allies in a dream. | Long-term campaign edge. |
| **Swan Song** | Allies gain Bardic Inspiration (CHA modifier) that cancels disadvantage. | Rallying the party at a critical moment. |

## Class Reactions

Bards have access to reactions that disrupt enemy momentum and protect allies. These reactions use your **Proficiency Bonus pool**, refreshing on short or long rest. Note: some reactions also expend Bardic Inspiration.

### Dissonant Echo (2nd Level)

**Trigger:** A creature within 30 feet hits an ally.
**Effect:** Expend a Bardic Inspiration die. Subtract the result from the damage dealt. The attacker makes a WIS save (DC = 8 + PB + CHA) or be frightened of you until end of its next turn.

---

### Cutting Rhythm (3rd Level)

**Trigger:** An enemy creature you can see within 60 feet gains Momentum from a successful attack.
**Effect:** Expend one use of Bardic Inspiration to reduce the Momentum that creature gains by 1 (to a minimum of 0). Your cutting words disrupt the enemy's rhythm.

Uses equal to your Proficiency Bonus per short rest.

---

### Cutting Counter (7th Level)

**Trigger:** A creature within 60 feet makes an attack roll, ability check, or damage roll.
**Effect:** Expend a use of Bardic Inspiration and subtract the roll from the result. If the new result causes a failure, you and one ally within 30 feet may each move up to 10 feet without provoking opportunity attacks. At 15th level, both you and the ally also gain advantage on your next roll.
