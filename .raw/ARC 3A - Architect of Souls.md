## CAMPAIGN SUMMARY

**Category:** Ascender (Truth-Exploiting) with Divine Usurpation and Identity Programming

**BBEG:** Archmagus Therion the Unbound - Former High Threadbearer attempting manufactured godhood

**Arc Status:** Standalone or Sequential
- Can be played independently without Arc 1B
- Enriched significantly if Arc 1B preceded it
- Designed to acknowledge Arc 1B events without requiring them

**Core Premise:**

**Therion** (human archmage, age 90) served as High Threadbearer of Spirit Thread in Kel Varyan for 40 years. During deep Pattern communion, he discovered three truths:

1. Pattern operates independently of Divine Spark gods—it's a programmable force
2. Divine Spark gods achieved authority through Glyph manipulation + belief anchoring
3. Vel'Zyren's Drow transformation proves Personal Glyphs can reprogram identity itself

For 20 years since staging his death, he's worked toward godhood through a five-stage plan:
- **Stage 1 (COMPLETE):** Modified own Personal Glyphs for extended life and proto-divine status  
- **Stage 2 (IN PROGRESS):** Building cult of 537 "Rewritten" followers physiologically unable to doubt him
- **Stage 3 (PREPARED):** City-wide conversion ritual to rewrite 8.5M people simultaneously
- **Stage 4 (THEORETICAL):** Spread to other Loom-Cities
- **Stage 5 (UNKNOWN):** Possibly anchor to Elemental Glyphs themselves

**The Horror:** His technique WORKS. Converted cultists are GENUINELY HAPPY. He GENUINELY CARES for them.

**The Question:** If manufactured divinity functions identically to "natural" divinity, and worshippers are happier after conversion, is it actually wrong?

**The Crisis:** He's not attempting to become a minor deity like other ascenders. He's attempting to skip directly to **Tier 1 Major God status** in 72 hours—something that threatens divine population capacity limits.

**Current Date:** Bloomarch 2, Year 487 of the 4th Age (17 months after Cycle 4 Collapse)

**Primary Setting:** Kel Varyan (Fortress City), with continental implications

**Campaign Level Range:** 1-20 (approximately 2 years)

**Moral Complexity Rating:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Maximum)

The Pattern can sustain only a limited number of divine beings at each tier. This creates the central crisis of Therion's ascension attempt.

**Not gods—they ARE fundamental forces**

- **Vatharun** (Earth) - The Stone-Father, unmoved mountain

- **Zephir** (Air) - The First Breath
- **Thirian** (Air) - Stillness before motion
- **Aeloria's influence** (Spirit) - Woven into Pattern itself

**Pattern Capacity:** Unlimited (structural elements of reality)
**Worship Requirements:** None—exist regardless
**Authority:** Absolute within their domains
**Relevance to Arc:** Cannot be threatened by manufactured divinity

**Legitimate gods with established domains and continental worship**

- Thamur (Guardian Below)

**Water Pantheon (~3 gods):**
- Vaelora (Deep Mother)
- Marethis
- Others

**Air Pantheon (~4 gods):**
- Astria (Storm-Seer)

- Others

**Regional gods, successful ancestor-spirits, nature patrons**

- Local patron deities

**Not quite gods - minimal divine power**

- Ancestor echoes
- Nature spirits without worship
- Forgotten names
- Failed divine sparks
- Remnant consciousness

**Pattern Capacity:** Unlimited (too weak to strain Pattern)
**Authority:** None recognized
**Relevance to Arc:** Not relevant to Therion's ambition

### **The Capacity Crisis**

**Current Divine Population:**
- Tier 0 Embodiments: 6-8 (unlimited capacity)
- Tier 1 Major Gods: 22-25 (maximum ~30)
- Tier 2 Minor Deities: ~200 (maximum ~500)
- Tier 3 Spirits: Thousands (unlimited)

**Remaining Tier 1 Capacity: 5-8 slots**

**If Therion Succeeds:**
- He occupies 1 slot (manageable alone)
- BUT proves technique works at Tier 1 scale
- Other archmages will attempt it
- 5 Loom-Cities × 2-3 archmages each = 10-15 attempts
- Even 40% success rate = 4-6 new Tier 1 gods
- **Capacity exceeded within years, not centuries**

**Then Pattern Must Choose:**
1. Accept overcrowding (reality destabilizes, Fractured Districts everywhere)
2. Reject manufactured gods (mass wrong-thing creation)
3. Force divine retirement (kill existing gods to make room)

**All options are catastrophic.**

## THE BBEG: ARCHMAGUS THERION THE UNBOUND

### Identity & Background

**Current State:**
- Human, age 90 (appears 40 from Glyph modifications)
- Former High Threadbearer of Spirit Thread (served ages 30-70)
- Silver-haired, eyes shifting through all five Thread-colors
- Faint luminescent presence—not quite divine, no longer entirely mortal
- Lives and works openly in Kel Varyan (for now)

**Background Timeline:**

**Ages 0-30: Rise to Power**
- Born to minor Kel Varyan noble house
- Exceptional magical aptitude from childhood
- Graduated Prisada Arcana top of class (age 20)
- Youngest instructor in Pattern Theory (age 25)
- Published groundbreaking work on Personal Glyph structure
- Spent three years obsessively researching Ashkarra's fall

**Ages 30-70: Threadbearer Years**
- Appointed High Threadbearer at 30 (youngest in 200 years)
- 40 years of distinguished service to Spirit Thread
- Deep Pattern communion revealed mechanistic nature of reality
- Discovered Vel'Zyren's sealed archives in Vault of the Abyss (age 55)
- Began secret Personal Glyph experiments on self (age 58)
- Successfully extended own lifespan through Glyph modification (age 62)
- Published controversial paper: "Triple Spirit Alignment: Coincidence or Cosmic Warning?"

**Age 70: The Liberation Murder**
- Realized Threadbearer bond prevented necessary research
- Identified target: Threadbearer Marcus of Selveran (Water Thread, age 89)
- Traveled to Selveran under false pretenses
- Poisoned Marcus with slow-acting toxin mimicking heart failure
- Marcus died three days later, triggering global Threadbearer cascade
- **All five Threadbearers released from service, including Therion**

**One week later:**
- Staged "Pattern communion accident" in his tower
- Explosion, magical fire, charred unrecognizable body found
- Assumed dead by all authorities
- No connection made to Marcus's convenient death

**Ages 70-90: The Hidden Ascension (20 Years)**
- Established secret Soul Workshop beneath Kel Varyan
- Recruited Inner Circle of 12 archmages
- Perfected Personal Glyph rewriting techniques through experimentation
- Created first 537 "Rewritten" cultists successfully
- Built Ascension Spire connected to Pattern through leyline network
- Studied Ashkarra's fall extensively, convinced he understood their mistake
- **Accelerated dramatically after Cycle 4 Collapse (last 17 months)**

**Current Motivation:**
Now ready to reveal himself and ascend. The Threadbearer void created by Cycle 4's collapse provides perfect window—with only Naleth to detect his work, and Pattern too damaged to call new Threadbearers, he has unprecedented opportunity.

### What He Discovered

**Truth 1: Pattern Independence**

```
During deep communion with Spirit Thread, I felt the Pattern
directly. Not through divine lens. The RAW THING ITSELF.

And I understood: It's mechanical. A force operating according
to principles, not will. Like gravity or fire—impersonal,
programmable, exploitable.

The gods don't control it. They USE it. There's a difference.

Divine Spark gods manipulate Glyphs within Pattern's framework.
They didn't create the framework. They just learned to navigate
it skillfully.

This means: Anyone who understands Glyph-structure at sufficient
depth can do what gods do. Divine authority isn't inherent.
It's ACHIEVED.
```

**Truth 2: Divine Creation Through Technique**

```
I studied how Soltharion created dragonborn. How Vel'Zyren
transformed elves into Drow. How all Divine Spark gods anchored
themselves to reality.

The process is consistent:

STEP 1: Understand Personal Glyph structure of target race
STEP 2: Design modifications to alter identity/behavior
STEP 3: Implement rewrite through sustained ritual
STEP 4: Anchor self to collective belief generated
STEP 5: Sustain divine status through ongoing worship

Every Divine Spark god followed this pattern. The TECHNIQUE
is consistent. Which means: It's REPLICABLE.

Lyssa proved it works at Tier 2 scale (minor deity, 2,000
followers, 15 years). I'm proving it works at Tier 1 scale
(major god, 8.5 million followers, 72 hours).

The difference is ambition, not methodology.
```

**Truth 3: Identity is Programmable**

```
Vel'Zyren's transformation of Drow proves something horrifying
and liberating: Personal identity—the core "self"—is encoded
in Personal Glyphs. These Glyphs can be REWRITTEN.

She didn't convince elves to worship her. She REWROTE THEM so
they MUST worship her. Changed their fundamental drives, values,
emotional responses at the Glyph-level.

The elves who became Drow didn't lose free will—they became
DIFFERENT PEOPLE who freely chose to worship her.

This is terrifying. This is also PRACTICAL.

If I rewrite someone's Personal Glyphs correctly, I don't need
to convince them I'm divine. I can make them RECOGNIZE my
divinity as fundamental truth.

The converted don't worship me because I tricked them.
They worship me because I MADE THEM INTO people who recognize
my divinity as obviously true. They're HAPPIER this way.

Is this evil? Or is it just efficient theology?
```

**Truth 4: The Tier System**

```
I've studied divine ecology for 20 years. The Pattern has
capacity limits:

Tier 0 (Embodiments): Unlimited - they're structural
Tier 1 (Major Gods): ~30 maximum sustainable
Tier 2 (Minor Deities): ~500 maximum
Tier 3 (Spirits): Unlimited - too weak to matter

Lyssa achieved Tier 2. Respectable. She's now a regional deity
with 2,000 followers. The major gods barely noticed.

But I'm not interested in being TOLERATED. I want to be
RECOGNIZED. Tier 1. A seat at divine councils. Authority that
cannot be dismissed.

Current Tier 1 count: ~24 gods. Capacity: ~30.
Room for 6 more... if they emerge gradually over centuries.

I'm attempting to claim one of those slots in 72 hours.

This will force the pantheons to acknowledge manufactured
divinity as legitimate—or reveal their authority is built
on monopolistic gatekeeping, not cosmic law.

Either outcome serves my purpose.
```

### His Psychology

**On Divine Authority:**
```
"Gods aren't special because they're old. They're special because
they learned techniques no one else knew. Well, I've learned
those techniques now. Their monopoly on divinity ends with me.

If Soltharion can claim credit for Astralor's work and call
himself a god, I can achieve divinity through honest mastery
of the process. At least I'm not LYING about how I'm doing it.

Lyssa proved Tier 2 works. I'm proving Tier 1 works.
The difference is scale, not principle."
```

**On Personal Glyph Rewriting:**
```
"People are horrified when I say I rewrite identity. But what
do they think parenting is? Education? Cultural conditioning?
Society constantly reshapes who we are. I just make the process
intentional, directed, optimized.

Before conversion: Confused, suffering, searching for meaning,
afraid of death, uncertain of purpose.

After conversion: Certain, joyful, filled with purpose, trusting
their god, complete sense of belonging.

I've IMPROVED them. Made them better versions of themselves.
They're not victims. They're beneficiaries."
```

**On Consent:**
```
"Critics say I violate free will because conversion isn't truly
consensual—subjects can't understand what they're agreeing to
beforehand.

But NO major life choice meets that standard. Parents don't
get their children's consent before birthing them. Teachers
don't get full informed consent before shaping young minds.
No one can consent to BECOMING DIFFERENT because you can't
evaluate from perspective you don't have yet.

My converted followers are GLAD they converted. From their
current perspective, it was best choice of their lives. Isn't
that what matters? That they're happy NOW, not that some
theoretical 'previous version' of them might object?"
```

**On Divine Responsibility:**
```
"I'm not just creating worshippers. I'm creating a genuine
divine contract. I take responsibility for their wellbeing.
I answer their prayers. I provide guidance. I protect them.

I'm not abandoning them like Soltharion abandoned Ashkarra.
I'm not lying to them like the Burning Concord lied for centuries.
I'm being the god they DESERVE rather than the frauds they've
been stuck with.

Yes, I manufactured their worship. But I'm also providing genuine
divine services in exchange. Isn't that better than gods who
demand worship while providing nothing?"
```

**On Tier 1 Ambition:**
```
"Lyssa chose Tier 2. Small, safe, beneath notice. She'll be
a footnote in divine history—tolerated because she's irrelevant.

I refuse that fate.

If manufactured divinity is legitimate, it's legitimate at ALL
tiers. Claiming Tier 2 is acceptable but Tier 1 isn't? That's
not cosmic law—that's gatekeeping.

The major gods fear me because I threaten their monopoly. If
one mortal can achieve Tier 1 in a lifetime, what makes the
established gods special? Their age? Their accumulated power?

Both are ACHIEVED, not inherent. I can achieve them too.

Yes, this threatens Pattern capacity. Yes, others will attempt
it after me. Yes, divine ecology might collapse.

But that's not my problem. That's the Pattern's problem. If
the cosmic infrastructure can't handle manufactured divinity,
perhaps it should EVOLVE.

Stagnation is death. I learned that from Ashkarra."
```

**On His First Murder:**
```
"Marcus was 89. He complained of the Thread's weight. He would
have died within a few years anyway. I simply... accelerated
the inevitable.

His death freed five Threadbearers from their bonds. Five people
gained freedom to pursue their true purposes. The cosmic math
justifies one elderly man's peaceful passing.

I tell myself this every night. Some nights I believe it.

The truth? Marcus was a good man. He didn't deserve what I did.
But cosmic advancement requires sacrifice. If my ascension
succeeds, his death will mean something. If I fail, it was
murder for nothing.

I cannot fail. I will not let his death be meaningless."
```

**The Tragic Complexity:**

Therion is convincing because:
1. His logic is sound - technique can be replicated
2. His methods work - converted cultists measurably happier
3. He actually cares - genuinely serves his followers
4. The alternative is flawed - existing gods demonstrably failed
5. He's honest - not lying about what he's doing
6. He's haunted - Marcus's death weighs on him
7. **He's technically correct about divine monopoly** - Tier 1 gods DO gatekeep authority
8. **Lyssa proves his premise** - manufactured divinity IS achievable

The horror: he's RIGHT about most things, his solution WORKS, but the cost is potentially catastrophic.

## THE ASHKARRA CONNECTION

### Historical Parallel (Essential Campaign Element)

**What Therion Discovered About Ashkarra:**

During his three years researching historical Pattern failures at Prisada Arcana, Therion became obsessed with Ashkarra's fall. His controversial paper "Triple Spirit Alignment: Coincidence or Cosmic Warning?" concluded:

> *"The consensus interpretation—that Ashkarra fell due to theological corruption, Threadbearer suppression, and flame-sealed Anchor damage—is superficial. These factors contributed, but they don't explain the triple Spirit alignment.*
>
> *Spirit Thread governs consciousness, identity, transformation. Three consecutive Spirit alignments represent the Pattern OFFERING transformation, trying to save them through evolution.*
>
> *But Ashkarra's Burning Concord rejected transformation. They suppressed their Threadbearers, clung to flame-purity doctrine, refused to evolve when the Pattern demanded it.*
>
> *So the Pattern withdrew support. The Anchor, already weakened by flame-sealing, collapsed. The city fell not as punishment but as consequence of evolutionary refusal.*
>
> *The lesson: When the Pattern offers transformation, ACCEPT IT. Evolution is survival. Stagnation is death."*

**What Therion Doesn't Know:**

**The Real Ashkarra Truth (DM-Only Information):**

15 years before Ashkarra's fall, a Dragonborn researcher named **Syr'keth the Illuminated** conducted experiments identical to Therion's:
- Studied Vel'Zyren's sealed archives
- Developed Personal Glyph modification techniques
- Created 47 successful "Rewritten" subjects
- Attempted **Tier 1 divine ascension** using manufactured worship

**What Happened:**
- Syr'keth's ascension partially succeeded
- He achieved proto-divine status (between Tier 2 and Tier 1)
- Pattern initially accepted him
- **Years later, Pattern began rejecting him**
- He became a wrong-thing god with fragmented divinity
- Unable to complete transformation or die
- His corrupted divine presence destabilized the World Anchor
- Triple Spirit alignment was Pattern trying to call help
- When help didn't come, his corruption triggered Hollow Breach

**The Terrible Truth:**
Ashkarra fell because someone attempted what Therion is attempting now. The triple Spirit alignment wasn't offering transformation—it was screaming for help.

## DM GUIDANCE: RUNNING THIS CAMPAIGN

### General Principles

**1. Respect Player Agency**

This campaign has no "correct" ending. Every path is valid. PCs' choices determine outcome, not predetermined plot.

**Bad DM Behavior:**
- Invalidating PC decisions
- Forcing specific outcome regardless of choices
- Punishing "wrong" choices with failure
- Rewarding "correct" choices with easy victory

**Good DM Behavior:**
- Consequences match choices
- Multiple valid solutions exist
- No objectively right answer
- Player values determine what success means

**2. Embrace Moral Complexity**

Therion isn't villain. He's person making understandable choices. Same with Naleth, Lyssa, Cassian, and others.

**Bad DM Behavior:**
- Making Therion evil
- Making PCs heroes automatically
- Clear good guys vs bad guys
- Simple moral lessons

**Good DM Behavior:**
- Every faction has legitimate points
- PCs struggle with decisions
- No comfortable answers
- Moral complexity generates discussion

**3. Scale Consequences Appropriately**

Actions have weight. Millions of lives at stake. PCs' choices matter.

**Bad DM Behavior:**
- Catastrophe happens regardless of PC actions
- PCs' efforts meaningless
- Same outcome every time
- Railroading to specific ending

**Good DM Behavior:**
- PC actions genuinely change world
- Different choices create different worlds
- Consequences proportional to choices
- Player feels their power and responsibility

**4. Use Branch Structure Effectively**

Campaign designed with five diverging paths. Don't force single path.

**How to Use:**
- Play Act I and II normally (single path)
- At Act II conclusion, determine which branch
- Play appropriate Act III branch
- Let Act III flow into appropriate Act IV

**DM Preparation:**
- Read all branches before starting
- Prepare primarily for most likely branches based on PC tendencies
- Adapt other branches if PCs surprise you
- Don't try to prepare everything - focus on what PCs choose

### Specific Challenges

**Challenge 1: Knowledge Management**

Campaign involves PCs learning cosmic truths. Managing what they know vs what they discover:

**Early Campaign:** PCs don't understand manufactured divinity is possible
**Mid Campaign:** PCs learn technique exists, don't understand implications
**Late Campaign:** PCs understand implications, must decide response

**DM Tips:**
- Dole out revelation gradually
- Let PCs feel smart when connecting dots
- Don't info-dump
- Use NPCs to explain complex concepts in character

**Challenge 2: Pacing the 72-Hour Ritual**

Act II climax is 72-hour ritual. That's long in real-time, short in game-time.

**Option A: Real-Time Pacing**
- Each session covers 12-24 hours
- 3-6 sessions for entire ritual
- Tension builds naturally
- Players feel time pressure

**Option B: Montage Pacing**
- Summarize long stretches
- Focus on critical moments
- 1-2 sessions for entire ritual
- Faster but less immersive

**Option C: Hybrid Pacing**
- Hours 1-24: Real-time (establishing stakes)
- Hours 25-48: Montage (rising tension)
- Hours 49-72: Real-time (climax)
- Best of both approaches

**Recommendation:** Hybrid pacing for most groups.

**Challenge 3: Multiple Villain Coordination**

If playing Arc 1B sequel version, both Naleth and Therion are antagonists with different goals.

**Managing Multiple Villains:**
- Naleth focused on Seminary, suppressing truth, maintaining divine authority
- Therion focused on manufactured divinity, breaking divine monopoly
- They oppose each other (Naleth should stop Therion, can't without exposing self)
- PCs can play them against each other
- Or ally with one against other
- Or oppose both
- Or try to help both find middle ground

**DM Tips:**
- Track both villains' actions simultaneously
- Show how they interfere with each other
- Let PCs exploit tensions
- Neither is simple villain

**Challenge 4: Handling Divine NPCs**

Campaign involves interaction with Tier 1 gods, Elemental Embodiments, manufactured deities.

**Divine NPC Principles:**

**Tier 1 Gods:**
- Powerful but not omnipotent
- Have biases and limitations
- Political creatures with competing interests
- Can be negotiated with
- Can be wrong

**Elemental Embodiments:**
- Amoral forces
- Care about Pattern stability, not morality
- Neither help nor harm based on ethics
- Pure pragmatists
- Alien psychology

**Manufactured Deities:**
- Retain mortal psychology
- Recent transformation creates vulnerability
- Struggle with divine consciousness
- More relatable than traditional gods
- Can be reasoned with as former mortals

**DM Tips:**
- Don't make gods infallible
- Gods can be wrong, biased, afraid
- Divine != wise
- Power != correctness

**Challenge 5: Pattern Mechanics**

Campaign centers on Pattern mechanics. Keep it comprehensible:

**Simple Pattern Rules:**
- Reality Stability number (0-100)
- Divine Capacity numbers (current/maximum)
- Personal Glyphs (individual identity markers)
- World Anchors (5 cities maintaining reality)
- Thread system (5 elements maintaining Pattern)

**When to Use Numbers:**
- Major decisions affecting continental reality
- Divine ascension math
- Capacity crisis calculations

**When to Use Narrative:**
- Individual magic effects
- Local reality glitches
- Personal Glyph changes
- Daily life

**Balance:** Use mechanics for big picture, narrative for character moments.

### Common Questions

**Q: Can PCs side with Therion completely?**
A: Yes. Campaign supports playing as his allies, trying to ensure his success. Creates different but valid story.

**Q: Can campaign be played without Arc 1B?**
A: Yes. Standalone version works perfectly well. Arc 1B enriches but isn't required.

**Q: What if PCs ignore main plot?**
A: Therion proceeds with ritual on timeline. PCs face consequences of inaction. That's valid ending too.

**Q: What if PCs try to become gods themselves early?**
A: They lack knowledge. Early attempts fail catastrophically unless they study extensively. Becoming plot.

**Q: Can Therion be redeemed?**
A: He's not unredeemable—he genuinely wants to help people. But his methods cause harm. Redemption arc possible but difficult.

**Q: What about other Loom-Cities?**
A: Campaign can expand to other cities. Kel Varyan is centerpiece but not exclusive setting.

**Q: How lethal should campaign be?**
A: Moderate. PCs should feel in danger but not constantly dying. Save deadliness for climactic moments.

**Q: Can manufactured divinity technique be stopped completely?**
A: Knowledge exists now. Can be suppressed but not uninvented. Underground research continues regardless.

### APPENDIX B: WRONG-THING CLASSIFICATION

**For DMs Running Branch 1 or 4:**

Wrong-things come in types with different mechanics:

- CR 3-8

- Minds degrading slowly
- Can communicate and reason
- Presence causes minor Pattern damage
- Can potentially be cured if caught early

- CR 8-15
- Lost human form
- Range from minor distortions to dangerous predators
- Limited intelligence
- Presence causes moderate Pattern damage
- Cure difficult but possible

**Type 3: Gestalt Entities**
- CR 15-20
- Multiple people merged
- Share memories horribly
- High intelligence but fractured
- Presence causes major Pattern damage
- Cure extremely difficult

**Type 4: Blessing-Corrupted**
- CR 1-5
- Slowly transforming
- Appear normal initially
- Contagious through contact
- Presence causes minimal damage

- CR 25+
- Therion's ascension failure result

- Multiple forms simultaneously
- Presence causes catastrophic damage

- Identify which Glyphs corrupted

- Type 3: 15%

## FINAL NOTES FOR DMs

This campaign asks hard questions:
- Is manufactured divinity legitimate?
- Do ends justify means?
- Can truth be too dangerous?
- Should power be accessible or controlled?
- What price is acceptable for progress?

**There are no correct answers.**

Your players will debate these questions. Encourage that. The best sessions will be 30% combat, 70% arguing about ethics.

Some groups will side with Therion. Others will stop him. Both are valid. Both create interesting stories.

Your job isn't to guide players to "right" answer. Your job is to make consequences of their choices feel meaningful and real.

If players finish campaign and immediately start debating their choices, you succeeded.

If players finish campaign certain they were right and everyone else was wrong, you might have railroaded.

Embrace ambiguity. Embrace complexity. Embrace the fact that there is no clean resolution to these questions.

That's what makes manufactured divinity interesting.

That's what makes this campaign memorable.

Good luck, DM.

May your players create interesting catastrophes.
