# Osteomancy

_Style of Magic_

**"Bone remembers what flesh forgets."**

Osteomancy is the art of bone-bound magic—spells shaped through marrow, structure, and the whispering memory of skeletons. It is an ancient and somber style, often passed down through death rites, warrior legacies, or the hush of gravewind sermons. In places like Luyarnha, where the dead linger and the hunt never ends, Osteomancy is both a tradition and a weapon.

Osteomancers do not merely raise or animate bone; they understand it, speak to it, grow it, and shape it. Their magic blends necromancy, transmutation, and elemental force, but always channels through **the framework of death**. Every bone is a record. Every spell, a fracture or a forging.

---

## Key Themes

- **Bonecraft and Structure**: Magic manifests as bone formations, armor, claws, tendrils, or barriers.
- **Ancestral Memory**: Bones retain echoes of the dead; Osteomancy accesses their knowledge or pain.
- **Death as Material**: Treats bones as tools or components, not simply corpses or remains.
- **Controlled Decay**: Life and death in balance; some Osteomancers bind their own skeletons.

---

## Cultural Origins

### 💀 **Bonechanters of Luyarnha**

- Carry sacred boneflutes, summon ancestral spirits, and fight bone-for-bone against eldritch horrors.
- Use Osteomancy to reinforce limbs, graft armor, or animate skeletal watchdogs.

### ⚖️ **Mortarchs of the Pale Library**

- Ancient archivists who store spells in bone-scrolls and vertebrae.
- See bone as the final archive of truth.

### 🎭 **Grave-Sculptors of the Dying Coast**

- Create art and architecture from the skeletons of long-dead leviathans.
- Weave beauty from fossil, grief, and glyph.

## Osteomancy Casting Features

- **Bone Medium**: Any spell may be cast using a piece of bone from a relevant creature (not a mortal race) as a spellcasting focus. Grants + modifier of your spellcasting stat to spell attack or DC if appropriate. If piece of bone for relevant race grants +1 to spell attack or DC if appropriate
- **Marrow Echo**: Once per long rest, you may channel a bone's last memory to reroll a failed spell attack or force a creature to reroll a save.
- **Ossify**: You may cast a spell that deals force, bludgeoning, or necrotic damage as bone shards or marrow burst. If you do, creatures damaged by the spell must make a Strength save or be restrained until the end of their next turn.

---
## Echo Glyph Integration

Osteomancers often etch spells into bone, skull, or fossil. These echoes interact with their spellcasting and the Weave:

- **Glyph of the Ossuary**: Casting a necromancy spell on consecrated bones allows you to store it for later release.
- **Ancestral Crack**: When a spell kills a creature, a fragment of its bone carries part of that magic. You may reuse that bone once to cast the same spell without expending a slot.
- **Spinal Sequence**: Chain two spells together if both use bone as a material component or thematic medium. The second spell must be of 2nd level or lower.

## Social Perception

- **Revered Among the Deadlands**: In places plagued by death, Osteomancers are honoured as guardians and tombkeepers.
- **Feared in Cities**: Viewed as corpse-defilers or death cultists by more 'civilized' folk.
- **Tolerated by the Faith**: [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]] sees Osteomancy as acceptable only if used to preserve memory, not power.

---
## Optional Mechanics

- **Bone-Bound Focus ([[Feat]])**: Choose a creature type. You may use bones from that creature as a spellcasting focus and deal bonus damage to them.
- **Calcified Skin (Ritual or Mutation)**: Gain natural armour +1 and resistance to necrotic damage for 1 hour. Must graft bone to your flesh.
- **Fossil Library ([[Downtime]])**: Spend 1 week recording spells in bone tablets, readable only through touch and memory.

---
## Design Notes

Osteomancy is about **structure, legacy, and transformation**. It blurs the line between necromancy and artistry, using bone as a lens to see the shape of death and build from it. Unlike Blood Magic, which is emotional and sacrificial, Osteomancy is patient, tactile, and sculptural—a cathedral of pain turned inward, then outward.

It is the art of turning what is left behind into what protects or destroys.
