# 🌑 **Eldritch Moon Rise Procedure**

_“They do not rise. They awaken. They do not glow. They remember.”_

The **[[Eldritch Moons]]** are not celestial bodies obeying natural law — they are **Weave-wounds**, lunar overlays, and echoes of impossible orbits, pressing into mortal sight when the Pattern falters. Their presence shapes magic, warps minds, and stains the land long after they fade.

In the turning of the seasons, the Weave is **retied** — the Threads shift, balance resets, and the Pattern adjusts. This is the moment of risk, when a frayed stitch may loosen entirely and let the moons “open their eyes” upon the world.

---
## **When They Rise**

The Eldritch Moons appear when the **Weave strains under too many fractures** — when unresolved omens, unstable magic zones, and pantheon discord combine to thin reality’s cloth.

- Most often they manifest in **Deepveil** season, when prophecy and memory are most volatile, but they can bleed into the sky at any time if **Eldritch influence overtakes the seasonal balance**.
- Rising is not a celestial motion but a recognition — the moons are **eyes**, opening only when the world is weakened enough for them to see.

---
## **Trigger for Rolling**

At every **seasonal change**, the GM resolves the **Threads Turn** — the standard check that determines both **Pantheon Influence** and **[[Seasonal Mood]]** for the new quarter of the year.

**Step 1 – Roll Pantheon Influence & Seasonal Mood:**
- The GM makes the Threads Turn roll, which produces two results:
    1. **Pantheon Influence** (which elemental or Eldritch pantheon holds sway this season).
    2. **Seasonal Mood** (positive, neutral, chaotic, or negative tendencies in magic, weather, and societal stability).

**Step 2 – Identify Eldritch Triggers:**  
Immediately after the Threads Turn, check if **any** of the following conditions are met
1. **Pantheon = [[Eldritch Forces]]**
    - The season’s dominant influence is Eldritch. This alone is enough to risk an Eldritch Moon rise.
2. **Season Mood** = Chaotic
3. **Season Mechanics Escalation** _(see below)_
    - Any of the escalation conditions occur, representing pre-existing strain on the Weave.

**Step 3 – Initiate Moonrise Check:**
- If **any** of the above triggers are true, the GM must perform the **Eldritch Moon Appearance Roll**.
- This is done immediately after the Threads Turn resolution, before any other seasonal events are determined.

> **Lore Justification:**  
> In-world, seasonal changes are moments when the **Weave’s stitch is loosened** — the Pattern is retied by the Threads, but if Eldritch influence has seeped too deeply into the warp and weft, the moons “open their eyes” and press into mortal sight.

---
## **Season Mechanics Escalation**

Season Mechanics Escalation refers to the rare but dangerous convergence of factors that push a seasonal Thread beyond normal instability, forcing a check for an Eldritch Moon even if the Pantheon Influence isn’t Eldritch. These conditions represent the Weave being _already frayed_ before the Threads Turn completes.

**Escalation Triggers:**

1. **Omen Tripwire**
    - A single event so potent it resonates across the Weave. Examples include:
        - The fulfilment of a major prophecy or curse.
        - Sudden mass hallucinations affecting an entire settlement.
        - An unnatural celestial event (black sun, reversed constellations, bleeding stars).
    - Once triggered, the omen effect lingers for the remainder of the season and will always prompt an Eldritch Moon check at the next seasonal change.
        
2. **Zone Pressure**
    - Occurs when **three or more [[unstable magic zones]], ruptured leylines, or planar bleed sites** are simultaneously active.
    - These areas need not be geographically close; their combined strain affects the global Pattern.
    - The more zones present, the higher the chance of cascading moons if a rise is triggered.
        
3. **Dual Eldritch Seasonal Rolls**
    - If either Pantheon Influence **or** Seasonal Mood from the Threads Turn rolls as Eldritch, it creates a low-level risk.
    - If **both** results are Eldritch, treat it as a high-risk alignment:
        - Increase the starting cascade chance by **+1**.
        - If in [[Deepveil]] season, automatically trigger a Moon Appearance Roll without any further checks.

> **Lore Justification:**  
> Escalations are seen by scholars of the Faith of the Bound Pattern as **pre-fractures** — moments when the Pattern’s stitches are already splitting before the season’s turn is complete. Eldritch cults often interpret them as “the world’s pulse quickening” in anticipation of divine awakening.

---
## **Moon Appearance Roll**

Once the **Trigger for Rolling** confirms a moonrise check is needed, the GM performs the **Moon Appearance Roll** to determine:

1. **Which Eldritch Moon(s) appear**, and
2. Whether **multiple moons** rise in cascade.

---
### **Step 1 – Determine the First Moon**

- Roll **1d12** on the **Eldritch Moon Table**.
- The result is the first moon to appear in the season.
- If the rolled moon is already in the sky from a previous season, re-roll until a new moon is selected.

> **Lore:** The first moon to rise is considered the _primary wound_ — the tear in the Weave whose influence draws others to follow.

---
### **Step 2 – Cascade Check**

- After each moon is chosen, roll **1d6**:
    - **1–3** → No further moons rise; the event ends.
    - **4–5** → Another moon rises; roll again on the **Eldritch Moon Table** (re-roll duplicates).
    - **6** → Another moon rises, **and** the cascade roll is repeated after each new moon, adding **+1 to the cascade roll DC** each time.
        - This means if a 6 is rolled again, the next cascade triggers on a 5–6, and so on.
- Continue until no further moons rise or all twelve have appeared.
    

> **Lore:** Multiple moons in the sky are not separate events — they resonate with each other, multiplying their effects. Three or more visible moons vastly increase Hollow Breach risk.

---
### **Step 3 – Record Duration**

- The GM decides, or rolls, for how long the moon remains visible:
    - **Short-term**: Hours to days — often coincides with a single event or battle.
    - **Mid-term**: Weeks — a persistent influence through much of the season.
    - **Long-term**: Months to years — often leaves permanent scars on leylines and landscapes.
- Duration can be determined by lore context, random roll, or scenario needs.

> **Lore:** Moons fade only when the strain on the Weave eases — even if they linger past their “natural” duration, the scar remains long after they vanish.

## **Eclipse Severity**

**Calculation:**

- Add the **Severity values** of all **Eldritch Moons currently visible at the same time**.
- The result is the **Eclipse Severity** for as long as those moons remain together in the sky.
- Adjustments: If a moon sets mid-season, recalculate immediately.

**Tier Effects:**

| Total Severity | Name                       | Consequences                                                                                                                                 |
| -------------- | -------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **2–4**        | **Unnerving Disturbance**  | +1 to all [[Corruption]] DCs; minor environmental shifts tied to moon themes.                                                                |
| **5–7**        | **Regional Crisis**        | +2 Corruption DCs; +1 Omen event this season; instability in **one** Loom-City.                                                              |
| **8–10**       | **Widespread Chaos**       | +3 Corruption DCs; +2 Omen events; global travel/magic penalties due to planar interference.                                                 |
| **11–13**      | **Global Destabilisation** | Automatically set **[[Seasonal Mood]] = Chaotic** for next Threads Turn (overrides roll); permanent Weave scar forms somewhere in the world. |
| **14+**        | **Hollow Breach Risk**     | Global Chaotic ×3 seasons; monthly Moon Appearance Roll; if **any new moon rises**, immediately trigger a Hollow Breach (see summary below). |

# 🌑 **Eldritch Moon Set Procedure**

_“They do not fade. They close their eyes… and dream.”_

The disappearance of an Eldritch Moon is not like a natural lunar cycle. It is not dictated by orbital mechanics, but by the **Weave’s ability to heal**. A moon only “sets” when the wound in reality that allowed it to appear has been sutured, starved, or masked.

---
## **When They Set**

- Moons never simply _time out_. They fade only when the strain on the Weave is reduced below the threshold that sustains them.
- A moon may vanish suddenly during a decisive event — a ritual, the closing of a breach, the defeat of a major aberrant entity — or linger for months/years if instability persists.
- Even when they vanish, the land beneath often remains scarred: corrupted leylines, unstable glyphs, and memories that replay differently each time they are recalled.

---
## **Triggers for Setting**

At the end of each **season**, after resolving the Threads Turn for the next quarter, check for each active moon to see if it sets. A moon will only set if **any** of the following are true:

1. **Pantheon Influence = Non-Eldritch** _and_ **Seasonal Mood ≠ Chaotic**
    - The season’s dominant influence is stable and not chaotic, reducing the risk enough for the Weave to close the wound.
2. **Zone Stabilisation**
    - One or more unstable magic zones, ruptured leylines, or planar bleed sites feeding the moon’s influence are successfully sealed.
    - Requires either significant PC intervention or large-scale NPC ritual action.
3. **Breach Containment**
    - Any active Hollow Breach or major portal to the Null Reflection linked to this moon is closed or warded.
4. **Ritual Intervention**
    - A targeted ritual or divine act specifically intended to sever the moon’s influence succeeds. This may require unique components, sacrifices, or alignment of multiple Threads.
---
## **Set Roll**

If any of the above triggers apply, roll **1d6** for each active moon:

- **1–3** → The moon closes and fades from the sky.
- **4–6** → The moon remains, but its influence weakens. Roll again at the end of the next season with **+1** to the set roll (cumulative).
---
## **Lore Notes**

- Moons that remain for **four or more seasons** often become “permanent” — fixed eyes in the sky until the next major planar convergence or cataclysm forcibly removes them.
- The Faith of the Bound Pattern treats a moon’s fading as a _Weave reprieve_, and many Loom-Cities hold dawn vigils to mark the “closing of the eye.”
- Eldritch cults interpret a moon’s departure as a betrayal or retreat of their god, often leading to internal purges or desperate rituals to call it back.

# **Optional Escalation Rules** _(For high-risk campaigns)_

- If **Deepveil** season and **Pantheon = Eldritch**, add **+1** to the cascade roll for the first check.
- If the season is already under an **active Eldritch Moon** from the previous quarter, treat the first cascade roll as an automatic success (another moon appears).
- If the **Day of Twelve Shadows** alignment is ever repeated (all 12 moons within 58 days), trigger an **immediate Hollow Breach**:

### **Immediate Hollow Breach – In-Session GM Summary**

- **Weave Collapse:** The World Anchor of the nearest Loom-City shatters or fails, instantly destabilising all connected leylines. Magic becomes wildly unpredictable; certain glyphs fail entirely while others surge uncontrollably.
- **Planar Incursion:** Rifts to the Null Reflection and Umbral Veil tear open across the region. Horrors, aberrations, and corrupted echoes pour through — beginning in high-density areas and leyline nexuses.
- **Reality Warping:** Physics and time distort. Landmarks shift position, the sky fractures with static star-fields, and memory loops may trap mortals in repeating scenes.
- **Societal Collapse:** Civil and religious order fails within hours. Armies, priesthoods, and cults rush to seize or defend the breach site, often turning on each other.
- **Gods Constrained:** Even divine intervention is limited — the Pantheons cannot directly mend the tear without risking further unravelling of the Weave. Mortal champions must act.
