# 🧵 Dór-o Estel – World Timeline Overview

This timeline reflects the mythic, historical, and cosmological development of [[Dór-o Estel]], based on the records of Moonscribes, Bound Pattern Priests, Flame-Speakers, and Scar-Keepers.

---
## 🌑 First Age – *The Long Weaving* (~4500 to ~1800 years before present)
**Duration:** **~2700 years of mythic dawn, divine emergence, and primal shaping**

“Before stone was carved or fire was taught,  
Before the gods bore names or shaped tongues,  
The Weave was wild, and memory sang alone.  
Elves danced the first threads into light,  
Dwarves found law in silence,  
And the Earth hummed with buried promise.  
But deep beneath, something turned without name,  
Waiting to devour its own forgetting.”

- [[Elves]] awaken and begin weaving the Weave with spirit and song.
- [[Dwarves]] establish the **[[Deep Law]]** and begin stone-forged civilisation.
- [[Orcs]] form the foundations of **[[Scar-Law]]** and clan-based honour.
- [[Gnomes]] initially worship all gods, later shift to the **Borrowed Gods**.
- The **[[Fracture of Accord]]** ends the age: gods begin to fight, and mortals suspect a deeper truth in the Weave.
- First signs of the Eldritch begin stirring—deep and unbreathing.

“And thus the gods fractured, and silence followed stone.”

---

---
### 🧵 1. **Cosmic Foundations and the Living Weave**

- **The Weave itself** is not created but slowly **awakened**—an infinite loom of memory, rhythm, motion, and identity.
- **Threads begin to stir**, forming into mortals, echoes, places, and divine archetypes.
- **[[Primary Glyphs]] remain untouchable**, but **[[Secondary Glyphs]] begin to coalesce**, shaping early magic through instinct and emotion.
- **The Spirit Thread** is strongest in this era—**the gods are not yet dominant**, and memory binds the world more than law.

---
### 🧝‍♂️ 2. **The Elves and the Woven Reverence**

- **[[Elves]] are the first to awaken**—not born, but remembered by the Weave.
- They begin to **“sing the Pattern”** into places, laying down Spirit-bound songlines and echo-threaded names.
- **[[The Woven Reverence]]** emerges as the **earliest coherent faith**, centred around:
    - Attunement, harmony, memory.
    - No worship—only resonance.
- First contact with other races and gods leads to **discomfort**, as elves view later deities as “poorly tuned echoes.”

---
### ⛰️ 3. **The Dwarves and the Deep Law**

- [[Dwarves]] **emerge from the mountain silence**, shaped by **stone-song** and bound oaths.
- They establish  **[[The Deep Law]]**, a metaphysical system of legal song-runes, oaths, and sacred naming.
- The gods do not create dwarves; rather, dwarves shape their gods from **resonant law-forms** that prove worthy.
- The first **[[Song-Forge]]** is kindled—a blend of crafting, law, and memory that **births the early divine-bound constructs**.

---
### ⚔️ 4. **The Orcs and the Path of the First Scar**

- Orcs arise in clan fragments, tested by **harsh lands and divine silence**.
- They develop **[[The Path of the First Scar]]**—honour earned through trial, not lineage.
- Clans begin carving their **[[Founding Scars]]**, sacred trials that define their future oaths.
- Scar-Wardens emerge near the end of the Age, formalising cross-clan justice.
- Some early clans clash with Fire cults, planting the **first seeds of fire-resistance and trial-honour theology**.

---
### 🔥 5. **The Fire Pantheon and the Rise of Soltharion**

- [[Soltharion]], [[Pyrris]], and [[Ignara]] first rise as **living flames of will**, not yet structured into Concord.
- **Soltharion attempts to shape the Weave through flame**, claiming fire as the primal Thread.
- This results in the **[[First Firestorm]]**, which burns part of the Spirit-threaded lands.
- **[[Vaeldros]] and [[Thamur]] intervene**, halting Soltharion’s attempt to claim cosmic precedence.
- **The Elemental Accords** are forged (temporarily), recognising that no single Thread may dominate.

---
### 🌑 6. **The Exile of Vel’Zyren and Rise of the Drow**

- **[[Vel'Zyren]]**, once of [[The Spirit Pantheon]], turns to domination and cruel prophecy.
- She begins to “bind silence into chains,” demanding obedience from mortals and gods alike.
- **[[Gaeldros]] and [[Aelys]] lead the judgement**: Vel’Zyren is cast into the deep, severed from the Pattern.
- Her followers—**proto-[[Drow]]**—follow her willingly, becoming the **first race of exile**.
- The severing causes a **scar in the Weave** known as the **[[Hollow Line]]**, still feared in modern prophecy.

---
### 🌊 7. **The Verdant Accord and Rise of Druidism**

- **[[Ishara]] and [[Vaelora]]** awaken the **natural rhythm of the world**: seasons, root-cycles, tide-currents.
- The **first [[Druid]]** are born—not of race, but of place.
- **[[The Verdant Accord]]** emerges: the world has its own rites, independent of god or mortals.
- **[[07_Geography/04_World Trees/World Trees]] begin forming** in sacred groves—rooted in Spirit, Earth, and Water.
- Early Fire-aligned entities see the Accord as **primitive and chaotic**, initiating several minor clashes.

---
### 🌀 8. **Early Divine Fractures and Faith Schisms**

- The **gods begin to splinter** over domains, identity, and authority.
- **[[Thamur]]** warns the others: “We did not make the Weave—we only claim a place within it.”
- **[[Gaeldros]] and [[Soltharion]]** clash over **glyphic law vs. willful flame**.
- **[[Aelys]] and [[Nyzara]]** remain bound to memory and echoes, trying to preserve unity through the Spirit Thread.
- These divine tensions **foreshadow the Second Age’s Rebellions**.

---
### 🌘 9. **The Fracture of Accord (End of Age)**

> _“And thus the gods fractured, and silence followed stone.”_

- All pantheons break from unified purpose.
- Some mortals begin to realise the gods are **not creators**, but **weavers trapped in the same Pattern**.
- **Mage-cults, seers, and rogue prophets** begin probing the Weave without divine permission.
- The first **glyph-based spellcraft** is born—wild and uncontained.
- **Early Eldritch whispers** are heard beneath the earth and in dreams—**the Null Reflection stirs**
- The age ends with multiple elemental zones unraveling, sparking the need for a **Faith to bind the Pattern**.

## ⚔️ Second Age – *The Reckoning of Wills* (~1800 to ~1100 years before present)
**Duration:** ~700 years

_The gods once shaped us. But now we shape their worship.”_  
—Tarel Hoshin, Firebound Heretic of the Fourth Cycle

“The gods made temples, and mortals made war.  
From fire came empires, from sky came betrayal,  
And blood was traded for freedom beneath a cracked moon.  
Glyphs were bound to purpose, but none knew what they bound.  
The Drow vanished into shadow.  
The Halflings laughed into legend.  
The Eldritch stirred, watching from behind reflection.”

- [[Humans]] rise and begin forming cities and philosophies.
- Widespread rebellions break divine order; glyphs crack.
- The **[[The Whispering Dark]]** draws near.
- Mortals invent **[[glyph-binding]]** as structured magic.
- The **first Eldritch Moon appears**.
- [[Drow]] sever ties to surface faiths and form the **Ashen Web**.
- [[Halflings]] bind alliances through cunning and chaos.

“And when the moon cracked, the third story began.”

### 🌩️ 1. **Collapse of the Elemental Accord**

- The **pact that ended the First Age** dissolves entirely as the elemental pantheons fracture:
    - **Fire and Spirit** become bitter enemies.
    - **Earth and Water** sever alliance over law vs. flow.
- Divine intervention becomes more **contradictory**—leading mortals to **distrust prophecy** and pursue **glyphic alternatives**.
- **[[Thamur]] withdraws** from overt action, warning that the gods are **no longer unified enough** to prevent Weave instability.

---
### 🔥 2. **The Rise of the Burning Concord**

- **[[Soltharion]], [[Pyrris]], and [[Ignara]]** formalize a **caste-bound divine regime**: the **Burning Concord**.
- [[Dragonborn]] legions march across southern [[Kerythra]] and [[Ashkarra]], enforcing:
    - **Flame-worship**
    - **Strict caste law**
    - **Glyph-binding as divine gift**
- Non-[[Dragonborn]] peoples are **enslaved, converted, or destroyed**.
- Early **[[Tieflings]]** appear—glyph-marked mortals bound to Fire, seen as tools until they **begin developing autonomous doctrine** (foreshadowing [[The Ashen Testament]]).

---
### ⚔️ 3. **Rebellion of the Mortal Races**

- A massive, cross-racial uprising forms after centuries of **caste domination and divine silence**:
    - **[[Humans]]**—fractured in the First Age—unite under charismatic heroes and glyph-binders.
    - **[[Halflings]]** use irregular warfare and storytelling subversion to dismantle Fire camps.
    - **[[Elves]]** deploy **Woven Reverence seers** to unravel Firecast glyphs.
    - **[[Orcs]]**, under emerging Scar-Wardens, out-maneuver Dragonborn generals in spiritual and literal terrain.
    - **Gnomes** weaponise unstable invention and **accidentally invent arcane sabotage**.

---
### 🌑 4. **The Drow Resurface: Rise of the Ashen Web**

- In the chaos of the Concord’s expansion and the rebellion:
    - **[[Vel'Zyren]]’s followers launch brutal raids** from below.
    - They assassinate or subvert **religious leaders, Thread-Speakers, and Concord flame-bearers**.
- **[[The Ashen Web]]** becomes feared for:
    - Religious absolutism.
    - **Skinning of surface heretics** to create "Books of Silence".
    - Their complete **devotion to Vel’Zyren as the only true god**.
- [[Drow]] do **not join the rebellion**, nor the Concord—**they strike all surface powers equally**.

---
### 🧬 5. **Birth of Divergent Faiths and Heresies**

| Faith                  | Origin                                                                                                   | Response                                                                                                   |
| ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Ashen Testament**    | [[Tieflings]] begin questioning the morality of gods entirely. “Sin is earned, not given.”               | Hunted by Faith precursors, viewed as **dangerous skeptics**.                                              |
| **Wonder-Tithe Codex** | Gnomes develop a **philosophy of magical trade, experimentation, and divine irreverence**.               | Considered eccentric by most, heretical by Concord.                                                        |
| **Reflection Pattern** | Emerges among **humans** seeking emotional and ancestral stability amidst collapsing doctrine.           | Eventually absorbed/suppressed by early Faith movements.                                                   |
| **Veiled Star**        | Begins as an underground network of **Spirit-seers, Aasimar, Shadar-Kai** who warn of divine unraveling. | Survivors form the **first Pale Vows** after being nearly wiped out in the **Cleansing of Thread-Fallow**. |

---
### ⚖️ 6. **Sacred Atrocities and Divine Abandonment**

- **[[Soltharion Purity Wars]]** – Entire cities razed for rejecting caste hierarchy.
- **[[Cleansing of Thread-Fallow]]** – Spirit-seers slaughtered by Concord/Pattern allies after a prophecy fails. Begins the **Veiled Star diaspora**.
- **[[Joy-Reckonings of the South]]** – After halfling villages are burned, a coordinated **laughter-war** cripples three Concord outposts.
- **[[The Concord’s Blinding Fires]]** – Gnomish glyph-cults are **blinded with flame and re-glyph-bound**, a failed attempt to erase their philosophical deviation.
- **[[Orcish Trial-Wars]]** – Some Concord-aligned clans turn on their own; Scar-Wardens put down the traitors in ritual duels, inscribing **Scar-Law supremacy** in blood.

---
### 🌿 7. **Verdant Accord and the World Trees**

- As divine and magical conflict increases, **druidic orders withdraw** deeper into sacred territories.
- **[[07_Geography/04_World Trees/World Trees]]** flourish—seen as refuges and elemental anchors.
- At least one known World Tree **is destroyed by Fire zealots**, causing **natural and magical collapse across several regions**.
- Druids establish **“[[root sanctums]]”**—defended by toxic flora, primal spirits, and **sentient groves that attack intruders**.

---
### 🪓 8. **Legendary Figures and Heroic Age Myths**

- **[[Druvak Ash-Eyes]] (Orc)** – Founder of the Scar-Wardens. Said to have walked through fire to challenge a Concord general to single combat—then carved his name into a mountain.
- **[[Fibble Rootbrace ]](Halfling)** – Initiates the **Joy-Reckoning** tradition. Famous for destroying a fire fortress using fermented cheese and laughter-induced panic.
- **[[Tashara Flame-Singer]] (Human)** – Glyph-warrior and early Pattern precursor. Dies stopping an unstable fire-glyph from collapsing a leyline—**her death becomes foundational myth** for the Pattern’s moral limits.
- **[[Vaeryn of the Mirrors]] (Human)** – Prophet of the Reflection Pattern. Publicly executed, but **memory of her vision continues through family rites**.

---
### 🧵 9. **Closing of the Age – Shards of Unity**

- After a **century of rebellion, betrayal, and divine absence**, the survivors of all major races form **pre-Faith coalitions** to:
    - Halt magical destabilization.
    - Prevent total glyph-collapse.
    - **Stabilize the Weave** without full divine dependence.
- The seeds of **[[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]]** are planted:
    - Not as a conquering religion, but as **a truce and repair project**.
    - Backed by **Spirit-Thread prophets**, **Earth-led arbiters**, and **memory-binders**.
- The **Second Age ends** not with war, but **with a collective binding ritual**—a cosmic Oath to the Weave called the **Silent Accord**.

## 🌓 Third Age – *The Cracked Loom* (~1100 to 0 years before present)
**Duration:** **~1100 years of reckoning, rebuilding, and religious consolidation**

“[[Ashkarra]] burned in golden glory,  
A city of scales, law, and flame,  
But no fire can outshine the darkness it denies.  
[[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]] wove borders through chaos,  
And [[Loom-Cities]] rose atop sacred scars.  
Twelve moons wept blood across the sky—  
And the Anchor shattered.”

- The **[[Dragonborn Empire of Ashkarra]]** rises under the **[[Burning Concord]]**.
- The **[[Faith of the Bound Pattern]]** is founded to stabilize the Weave.
- [[Loom-Cities]] are built atop [[leylines]] nexuses.
- The **Cycle system** begins: Loom-Cities rotate elemental Threads to maintain Weave balance.
- **[[Eldritch Moons]] multiply**, memory becomes unreliable.
- Gnomes and Halflings ally with the Faith.
- Major event the **[[Fall of Ashkarra]]**, the appearance of all twelve Eldritch Moons, and the **[[First Hollow Breach]]**.
- Ends with the successful grafting of the newest world anchor and the roots of the world tree in Aevarea
“And in the unmaking of one city, the world relearned fear.”

> _“In the silence after ruin, the world was rewoven—not with trust, but necessity.”_  
> —Opening line of _The Loom of Memory_, Book III

---
### ⚔️ 1. **Aftermath of the Rebellions**

- The **Second Age ends** in fractured victory:
    - [[The Burning Concord]] is weakened but **still intact in [[Ashkarra]]**.
    - Survivors from all races are traumatized by divine betrayal, magical instability, and ideological fatigue.
- Divine interventions are rare. The gods seem **ashamed, distant, or resigned**.
- **The Weave begins to unravel in places**, especially where glyphic warfare and divine contradiction overlapped.

---
### 🧩 2. **Founding of the Faith of the Bound Pattern**

- A coalition of prophets, Threadbearers, and glyph-priests form a **global stabilizing institution**:
    - **[[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]]**.
- Its purpose is **not dominance**—but **survival**:
    - Bind the Threads.
    - Preserve memory and prophecy.
    - Prevent another Fracture or Null Reflection breach.
- Five core Threads (Fire, Water, Earth, Air, Spirit) become the stabilizing rotation for **Loom-Cities**.
- Faith centers are **politically neutral**, but hold immense **spiritual and administrative influence**.

---
### 🪓 3. **Systematic Suppression of Divergent Faiths**

|Suppressed Faith|Method of Erasure|
|---|---|
|**Wonder-Tithe**|Declared “improvisational instability.” Shrines absorbed or demolished. Many gnomes force-bound to Pattern oaths.|
|**Reflection Pattern**|Labeled “incomplete truth.” Public worship outlawed. Rituals absorbed into **funerary customs**.|
|**Ashen Testament**|Tieflings marked, relocated, and politically disenfranchised. Only in Emberpass do they hold sway.|
|**Veiled Star**|Pale Vows hunted as dangerous mystics. Survivors go underground or into exile.|
|**Ashen Web**|Faith and Earth-aligned cities begin ritual purges of known Drow enclaves. Failure results in occasional **Hollow Echoes** (resonance collapses).|
|**Joy-Wild**|Halflings technically exempted—but their festivals taxed, and sacred songs restricted in cities. **Retaliation causes ongoing unrest.**|

---
### 🏛️ 4. **Rise of the Loom-Cities**

- Cities aligned to each of the Threads form the **Loom-City Circle**, each hosting
    - A **[[World Anchor]]** to stabilize leylines.
    - An aligned **[[Threadbearer]]**.
    - Temples, forges, spirit-wells, storm-arches.
- Each Loom-City rotates through elemental Thread alignment every **Cycle**, shaping magic, memory, and governance.
- Cities like **[[Kel Varyan]], [[07_Geography/02_Cities/Thar Vornhal]], [[Veltharyn]], [[Aeravae]]**, and [[**Ashkarra**]] rise to prominence.

---
### 🧠 5. **Magical Reconstruction and Glyph Standardisation**

- Secondary Glyphs are finally **formalised**:
    - Spellcasting becomes structured and hierarchical.
    - [[Wild Magic zones]] are mapped and contained.
- Magical Colleges emerge from prior glyph cults and **integrate into Faith-controlled knowledge orders**.
- Magical records become sacred—stored in **Moonscribed Codices**, protected by **Echohands** and **Loomwardens**.

---
### 🧿 6. **Rise of the Eldritch Moons and Early Incursions**

- ~300 years into the Age, **the first Eldritch Moon** appears in the sky:
    - The **Howling Moon** triggers mass madness in northern regions.
- Over the next centuries, **twelve known [[Eldritch Moons]] manifest**, each bringing:
    - **Planar destabilisation**.
    - Echo-collapses.
    - Memory plagues or prophetic failures.
- The Faith begins hunting **Eldritch cults**, forming **[[Thread-Inquisitors]]**.
- **[[The Weave Unbroken]]** philosophy emerges: a whispered doctrine that claims **there are higher truths above even the Pattern**.

---
### 🛐 7. **Major Religious and Racial Flashpoints**

| --------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |

| **[[Flooding Trial of Kel Varyan]]**    | Water-seers unleash ritual flooding to **destroy fire-aligned glyph recorders**. Now commemorated yearly as a warning against magical arrogance.                                  |

---
### 🌓 8. **Final Events and Collapse of Ashkarra**

- **Ashkarra** enters a rare and dangerous **triple Spirit Thread alignment**.
- All twelve **[[Eldritch Moons]] appear in the sky** during the city’s Loom-Keeper coronation.
- The resulting event, known as **[[The Day of Twelve Shadows]]**, triggers:
    - **[[The First Hollow Breach]]**
    - **[[Collapse of the World Anchor]]**
    - **[[Weave rupture and reality collapse]]**
- The city becomes a cursed ruin. Faith doctrine now includes strict limits on **Thread repetition**—no city may hold the same alignment more than twice.

## 🧶 Fourth Age – *The Age of Binding Threads* (0 A4 to Present)
**Duration:** **487 years of instability, omens, and spiritual crisis**

_“The Threads are thinning. The Glyphs forget. The gods no longer answer in unison.”_  
—High Threadbearer Naelia before her disappearance, Cycle 4

“Now, each city spins its thread through time,  
Bound by cycles, watched by ghosts,  
Their faith held not in gods, but in rhythm.  
The Weave shudders. The Eldritch whisper.  
[[Ashkarra]] sleeps but is not silent.  
And the Pattern tests its own design.”

- The Faith becomes dominant in major cities and human empires.
- [[Scar-Wardens]] gain cross-racial authority to enforce honour and justice.
- **Twelve [[Eldritch Moons]]** continue to appear sporadically.
- **Cycle 4 collapsed in a single day**. Cycle 5 is currently active.

### 🧵 1. **The Collapse of Cycle 4 and Prophetic Failure**

- The **Fourth Cycle of the Faith’s leadership rotation** ends in **catastrophe**:
    - **Four Threadbearers die** in a single day.
    - The Weave flares and folds in major cities.
- [[Ashkarra]] had already fallen in Cycle 3 (see Third Age), but this marks the **first global loss of Threadbearer presence**.
- The **Fifth Cycle** begins under Spirit—**but without stability or consensus**.
- Many cities begin to **question the validity of the Thread system** for the first time in 2,500 years.

---
### 🌑 2. **Proliferation of Eldritch Influence**

- Since Ashkarra’s fall, **[[Eldritch Moons]] reappear with increasing frequency**.
- Cults aligned to the **Null Reflection**, **Umbral Veil**, and **Forgotten Names** spread within cities, especially where Threads repeat.
- **New Eldritch Moons** occasionally flash into view and vanish, triggering:
    - Mass dream-plagues.
    - Memory distortions.
    - Glyphic malfunctions.
- The Faith responds with **emergency purges**—often invoking **[[Gaeldros]]** or [[07_Geography/02_Cities/Thar Vornhal]]’s judicial forces.
- [[The Hollow Vault]] is whispered again. Some claim it is **leaking into the world**.

---
### ⚖️ 3. **Fragmentation of the Faith of the Bound Pattern**

| Crisis                           | Result                                                                                                       |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **The Third Thread Warning**     | No city may repeat the same Thread three times. Now part of sacred doctrine after [[Ashkarra]].              |
| **Rise of Unthreaded Movements** | Wonder-Tithe, Reflection Pattern, and Ashen Testament see **renewed growth** in private.                     |
| **Thread-Secession Movements**   | Some city factions seek to **create independent Looms**, severing from centralized control.                  |
| **Spirit-Thread Cities**         | Cities under Spirit alignment (e.g., [[Aeravae]]) begin **protecting heretical rites as “cultural memory”**. |

### 🔥 6. **Faith Atrocities and Tensions**

- **[[Blinding of Tithers in Thar Vornhal]]** – All Wonder-Tithe instructors are forced into silence or exile.
- **[[Echo Nulling in [[Veltharyn]]** – Spirit archives purged after Pale Vow writings found inside. Two archivists vanish.
- **[[Reflection Trial in Kel Varyan]]** – Mirror Mantle rituals disrupted by Earth-aligned inquisitors. Seven deaths. No punishment.
- **[[Flooding of Hollowmoor]]** – A city desecrated its sacred well. Verdant Accord retaliates with flood and fungus. Glyph-rain follows.
