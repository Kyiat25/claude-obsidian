# Shadar-kai
## Lineage

> *"We remember what must not return. And we sever what lingers too long."*  
> — **The Doctrine of Necessary Endings**

You are Shadar-kai. Not cursed, not damned—**transformed**. Your people do not fear death or flee from shadow. You **understand the Pattern's fraying edge** in ways other elves cannot, having touched the Shadowfell and returned changed. Where others see endings as tragedy, you see them as **necessary severance**, the cut that prevents greater unraveling.

You were not made to preserve. You were made to **release, to end, and to ensure that what must pass does so cleanly**. Your ancestors walked between life and death so many times that shadow became part of your essence, teaching you that sometimes the greatest mercy is letting go.

---

## The Shadow-Touched

According to the traditions preserved by your people, Shadar-kai are elves transformed by prolonged exposure to the **Shadowfell**—the realm of death, memory, and transition. Whether through accident, necessity, or deliberate choice, your ancestors crossed into shadow and stayed long enough that it marked them permanently.

> *"The Shadowfell does not curse. It reveals. It shows you what remains when light fades, when hope dies, when all decoration is stripped away. And if you can bear that truth, you emerge stronger—but changed."*

The **Raven Queen**, psychopomp goddess of death and fate, is often invoked by Shadar-kai, though not all worship her. She represents **death as transition**, endings as necessity, and the importance of accepting what cannot be held. Some say she personally transformed the first Shadar-kai, others that your people simply dwelled in her realm long enough to change.

Your people believe that **not all threads should be preserved**, that memory can become trap as easily as treasure, and that sometimes the Pattern requires **cutting** rather than mending. This makes you grim but not cruel, accepting but not passive.

You are the scissors that the Pattern uses when threads tangle too tightly to be saved.

---

## Life in Shadow

You were raised knowing that **everything ends**, that holding on too tightly causes suffering, that release is sometimes love. Even in communities far from the Shadowfell, you carry the weight of **necessary endings**.

**Severance (kal'seth)** is not metaphorical. It is:
- Understanding when to let go
- Knowing when mercy requires ending
- Accepting that preservation can become prison
- The courage to cut what must be cut

Your lifespan is roughly **750 years**, same as other elves. However, Shadar-kai measure time differently—not in years lived but in **transitions witnessed, endings accepted, and releases accomplished**. Some young Shadar-kai (under 200) feel ancient from experiencing profound loss, while some old ones (over 600) feel unfinished, still seeking the release they're meant to facilitate.

Every significant ending becomes part of your **Severance Record**—the personal history of what you've learned to let go, what you've helped others release, and what you're still holding that should pass.

## Physical Characteristics

**Age:** You mature at the same rate as other elves, reaching physical adulthood around 20 but not considered truly adult until you've experienced your first true loss (usually by 100). You can live 750+ years.

**Size:** You stand between 5½ to 6½ feet tall, with builds that range from wiry to ethereal. Your size is Medium.

**Speed:** Your base walking speed is 30 feet. You move like shadow itself—fluid, purposeful, inevitable.

**Build:** You resemble elves in basic structure, but the Shadowfell has marked you distinctly. Your skin takes on ashen tones (pale gray, bluish-gray, or charcoal), your eyes become dark and penetrating (black, deep purple, or silver), and your hair ranges from white to black with little variation. You appear perpetually at the edge of fading, as if reality's grip on you is weaker than on others.

**Appearance Notes:**
- Your features are sharper, more angular than other elves
- Colors seem muted around you, as if you drain vibrancy from the world
- Your shadow is darker and more substantial than normal
- In strong emotion, shadows seem to reach toward you or flee from you
- Many bear **Death-Marks**—scars or patterns from Shadowfell exposure that shift slowly over decades

## Shadar-kai Traits

As a Shadar-kai, you possess the following lineage traits:

### **Darkvision**
You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

Your Shadowfell heritage grants you exceptional vision in darkness.

### **Keen Senses**
You have proficiency in the Perception skill.

### **Fey Ancestry**
You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed, and magic can't put you to sleep.

Despite your shadow-touched nature, you retain elven resistance to mental manipulation.

### **Trance**
Elves don't need to sleep. Instead, you meditate deeply for 4 hours a day, remaining semiconscious. After resting in this way, you gain the same benefit that other races do from 8 hours of sleep.

However, unlike other elves who use Trance to relive memories, your Trance is different. You **sever** memories during Trance—processing, releasing, and letting go of what must pass. Some Shadar-kai report seeing those who have died during Trance, receiving their farewells.

### **Necrotic Resistance**
You have resistance to necrotic damage.

Your exposure to death and shadow has hardened you against decay and death magic.

### **Blessing of the Raven Queen**
As a bonus action, you can magically teleport up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space you can see. Once you use this trait, you can't do so again until you finish a long rest.

Starting at 3rd level, you also gain resistance to all damage when you teleport using this trait. The resistance lasts until the start of your next turn. During that time, you appear ghostly and translucent.

This represents your ability to briefly step through shadow, partially entering the Shadowfell to avoid harm.

### **Shadow Sight**
When in dim light or darkness, you can see detail as if in bright light up to 60 feet. Unlike normal darkvision, you perceive colors normally in darkness—the shadows reveal rather than obscure.

### **Severing Sense** *(Cultural Trait)*
You have advantage on Wisdom (Insight) checks to determine if a creature is undead, possessed, or magically compelled. You can sense when threads have been twisted or when something refuses to let go.

Additionally, you can sense when someone is actively dying within 30 feet (though not their exact location or identity).

## Mental Attributes & Psychology

**Focus Modifier:** +0  
**Sanity Modifier:** +1

Your mental baseline shows your Shadowfell heritage: your **Sanity receives a bonus** because Shadar-kai are psychologically conditioned by death and loss. You're raised from childhood to process grief, accept endings, and face mortality as **natural transition** rather than terror.

This isn't cultural optimism—it's deep psychological acceptance earned through proximity to the Shadowfell. The darkness that would break others' minds is familiar to you.

### **Psychological Identity**
Your mental landscape is defined by **acceptance and transition**:

- **Death-awareness:** You understand mortality viscerally
- **Release-focus:** You know when holding on causes more harm than letting go
- **Shadow-comfort:** Darkness doesn't frighten you—it reveals
- **Necessary cruelty:** You can make hard choices others flinch from

### **Corruption Vulnerability: The Lingering Shadow**

As a Shadar-kai, you have a unique relationship with Corruption. Your shadow-touched nature provides significant protection against death and darkness, but corruption that enters through **refusal to release, clinging to what must end, or denial of necessary severance** affects you catastrophically.

Your greatest weakness is **holding on too tightly** to anything—people, memories, life itself.

**When you gain Corruption Score 10+ (Tier 2), you suffer:**
- Shadow-Bind: Released memories return unbidden
- Death-Denial: You begin fearing endings you once accepted
- Severance-Failure: You cannot let go of what must pass

**Shadow-Caught Table (Corruption Tier 2+)**

When you complete a long rest at Corruption Score 10+, roll 1d6:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **Clinging Memory:** A memory you released during Trance returns and refuses to fade. Make a DC 15 Wisdom save or become obsessed with recovering what you once let go. |
| 2 | **Shadow-Locked:** Your ability to step through shadow falters. You cannot use Blessing of the Raven Queen until your next long rest. |
| 3 | **Death-Fear:** Your acceptance of endings fractures. Take 1d4 psychic damage and lose proficiency in one skill until your next long rest. |
| 4 | **Severance-Agony:** Letting go becomes physically painful. Make a DC 12 Sanity save or lose 1d4 Sanity. |
| 5 | **Eldritch Touch:** Something that should have passed finds you. Gain 1 Whisper Point but your next use of Severing Sense reveals something that terrifies you. |
| 6 | **Shadow Holds:** Your nature fights corruption. Reduce your Corruption Score by 1 (minimum 10). |

## Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Integration

### **Evasion Profile: Shadow Striker**
- **Dodge Evasion:** +2 (you move like shadow, hard to pin down)
- **Endure Evasion:** +0 (you're not built to tank damage)
- **Deflect Evasion:** +0 (you avoid rather than block)

**Tactical Note:** You are designed to **strike and fade**. Your teleportation ability lets you engage, attack, and escape with supernatural agility.

### **Natural Damage Reduction:** +0
You have no natural armor. Your protection comes from not being where attacks land.

### **Combat Philosophy**
Shadar-kai fight with **surgical precision and shadow-step mobility**. You identify priority targets, teleport into range, strike decisively, then fade before retaliation arrives. You're the assassin, the skirmisher, the one who appears where needed and vanishes when the work is done. Your combat style is **necessary violence**—no wasted motion, no unnecessary cruelty, just what must be done.

### **Breaking Point Mechanics**
Due to your death-acceptance, you have **advantage on saving throws against fear effects and death-related morale breaks**. However, you suffer **disadvantage on Rally checks** (you accept defeat as easily as victory) unless rallying to accomplish a necessary ending.

## Languages

**Languages:** You can speak, read, and write Common and Elvish.

You speak **Elvish** with a distinct Shadar-kai accent that other elves describe as "hollow" or "distant," as if you're speaking from far away. Some words you pronounce with Shadowfell inflection—sounds that seem to echo with finality.

Many Shadar-kai also learn **Sylvan** (for dealing with other fey-touched) or **Celestial/Infernal** (for understanding beings that bridge life and death).

## Shadar-kai Variants

While all Shadar-kai share shadow-touched nature, different responses to the Shadowfell have created distinct patterns. Choose one of the following variants:

### **Death's Herald** *(Raven Queen's Chosen)*
You serve as psychopomp, guiding souls to their proper endings.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Dexterity score increases by 2, and your Wisdom score increases by 1.

**Soul Guide:** You can cast *spare the dying* at will. Additionally, when a creature dies within 30 feet of you, you can use your reaction to grant them a peaceful death—they automatically stabilize at 0 HP instead of dying, or if already dead, they pass without becoming undead (DM's discretion on applicability).

**Reaper's Step:** When you use Blessing of the Raven Queen, you can choose to deal necrotic damage equal to your proficiency bonus to one creature within 5 feet of where you teleport to or from.

**Death's Insight:** You can always tell when someone you're speaking with has less than one week to live (though not how they'll die).

### **Shadow Blade** *(Umbral Assassin)*
You have mastered shadow as weapon, using darkness itself to strike.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Dexterity score increases by 2, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Shadow Weapon:** As a bonus action, you can create a blade of solidified shadow. It uses the statistics of a shortsword but deals necrotic damage instead of slashing. The blade disappears if you let go of it, and you can dismiss it at will. You can use Dexterity or Charisma as your modifier for attack and damage rolls with this weapon.

**Umbral Strike:** Once per turn when you hit a creature with a weapon attack while in dim light or darkness, you can deal an extra 1d6 necrotic damage. This increases to 2d6 at 6th level, 3d6 at 11th level, and 4d6 at 16th level.

**Shadow Blend:** When you use Blessing of the Raven Queen, you can become invisible until the start of your next turn if you end your movement in dim light or darkness.

### **Mourner** *(Keeper of Endings)*
You help others accept loss and process grief, serving as ritual guide for those who must let go.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Wisdom score increases by 2, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Shared Burden:** As an action, you can touch a willing creature and take one of their emotional burdens (grief, guilt, fear of loss) into yourself temporarily. For the next hour, they have advantage on saves against being frightened and on Wisdom saves. You have disadvantage on the same saves. Once you use this feature, you can't do so again until you finish a long rest.

**Release Blessing:** When you cast a spell that removes a condition or ends an effect, you can add your Wisdom modifier to any healing the target receives from any source until the end of their next turn.

**Severance Rite:** You can perform a 10-minute ritual over a creature's body to ensure they pass peacefully and cannot be raised as undead against their will. This is a sacred duty among Shadar-kai.

### **Gloom Weaver** *(Shadow Caster)*
You manipulate shadow and darkness as a caster would manipulate elements.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Intelligence score increases by 2, and your Dexterity score increases by 1.

**Shadow Magic:** You know the *minor illusion* cantrip. When you reach 3rd level, you can cast *darkness* once per long rest. When you reach 5th level, you can cast *shadow blade* once per long rest. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for these spells.

**Umbral Advantage:** When you cast a spell that creates darkness or shadows, you can see normally through the darkness you created.

**Shadow Step Enhancement:** When you use Blessing of the Raven Queen, you leave behind a shadow decoy in the space you left. It lasts until the start of your next turn and can be targeted by attacks. If attacked, it disappears in a burst of harmless shadow.

### **Shadowfell Survivor** *(Hardened by Death)*
You spent extensive time in the Shadowfell itself, and it made you resilient.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Constitution score increases by 2, and your Wisdom score increases by 1.

**Shadow Endurance:** You have resistance to necrotic damage and advantage on saving throws against exhaustion and effects that would reduce your maximum hit points.

**Death-Touched Resilience:** When you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, you can drop to 1 hit point instead. Once you use this feature, you can't do so again until you finish a long rest.

**Shadow Healing:** During a short rest spent in dim light or darkness, you can spend Hit Dice to heal as normal, but you regain the maximum value on each Hit Die instead of rolling.

**Shadowfell Memory:** You have advantage on checks to recall information about the Shadowfell, death rituals, undead, or psychopomps.

## Shadar-kai Names

Shadar-kai naming reflects both elven heritage and shadow-touched transformation.

**Birth Names** (Elven names retained):
- Traditional elven: Velethar, Shaundra, Kaelen, Mara, Theren, Elara
- Often keep their original elven name until a significant death changes them

**Shadow Names** (Taken after profound loss):
- Death-touched: Gravemist, Nightwhisper, Ashenwind, Duskwalker
- Severance titles: Last-Breath, Final-Mercy, Quiet-Ending, Gentle-Fall
- Simple and grim: Raven, Shade, Whisper, Silence, Sorrow, Shroud

**Title Names** (Earned through service):
- Death's Herald, Soul's Guide, Shadow's Edge, Memory's Keeper
- Ending-Maker, Thread-Cutter, Release-Bringer, Peace-Giver

**Typical Pattern:** Many use their birth name with close friends but introduce themselves to strangers with their shadow name:
- "Kaelen" to those who knew you before
- "Duskwalker" to those who meet you now
- "Last-Breath" to those who need your services

## Shadar-kai in Dór-o Estel

Shadar-kai occupy a somber position in society—necessary but unsettling, respected but feared, understood by few.

**After the Collapse of Ashkarra**, many Shadar-kai believe they failed in their role—they should have severed connections to corrupted leylines sooner, should have accepted the empire's ending before it dragged so many down with it. This has made some Shadar-kai more aggressive in their severance duties, others more hesitant, afraid of cutting too soon again.

The **Faith of the Bound Pattern** has conflicted views on Shadar-kai. You're valued during times of transition but distrusted for your comfort with endings. The Pattern teaches preservation; you teach release. These philosophies clash often.

When working with other races:

**Other Elves**: Complicated. Wood and High Elves see you as kin who went too far into darkness. You see them as those who can't accept necessary endings.

**Humans**: Fear and fascination. They're drawn to your death-acceptance but terrified by your comfort with mortality.

**Dwarves**: Surprising respect. They understand accepting what must end—their own traditions include "sealing away" corrupted sections of mines.

**Halflings**: They try to make you smile, to lighten your grimness. Some Shadar-kai find this annoying; others, oddly comforting.

**Aasimar**: Deep philosophical connection. Both marked by supernatural forces, both bearing burdens of purpose. Many friendships form between Aasimar and Shadar-kai.

**Tieflings**: Mutual understanding. Both changed by forces beyond control, both judged for your appearance.

**Clerics of Death Deities**: Professional respect. You're not clergy, but you serve similar functions.

**Necromancers**: Hostility. You exist to ensure proper endings; they exist to deny those endings. This creates fundamental conflict.

## Adventure Hooks

**The Refusing Dead**: Spirits refuse to pass, clinging to life long past their time. You must discover why and help them—or force them—to accept their ending.

**The Necessary Murder**: Someone must die to prevent greater catastrophe. The killing must be clean, merciful, and secret. Are you willing to be the blade?

**The Shadowfell Breach**: A tear to the Shadowfell opens, threatening to drain life from the region. You must enter your ancestral realm to seal it from within.

**The Clinging Memory**: A memory you thought you'd released during Trance returns, stronger than before. Following it leads to dark secrets someone wanted forgotten.

**The Raven Queen's Task**: You receive a vision from the Raven Queen herself—or something claiming to be her. The task requires you to end something you don't want to lose.

**The Failed Severance**: Years ago, you helped someone let go of something. Now it's returned, twisted and corrupted. You must finish what you started.

## Integration with Mental Systems

Your **+1 Sanity** is significant:
- Death doesn't terrify you
- Loss doesn't break you
- Horror is understood as part of existence
- Endings are accepted rather than fought

**Typical Shadar-kai Mental Strategy:**
- Process trauma through release, not retention
- Accept what cannot be changed
- Find peace in necessary endings
- Use shadow-comfort as psychological anchor

### **Corruption Concerns:**
Your unique corruption vulnerability centers on **failure to release**:
- Clinging to what should pass accelerates corruption
- Refusing necessary endings creates vulnerability
- Denial of death becomes toxic
- At Tier 2+, the Shadow-Caught table reflects inability to let go

### **Rally & Morale:**
Shadar-kai have unique morale mechanics:
- Advantage on saves against fear and death-related breaks
- Disadvantage on Rally checks (you accept defeat)
- **Exception**: Advantage on Rally checks when the goal is to accomplish a necessary ending
- Your presence reminds others that death is acceptable, which can be comforting or terrifying

## Design Philosophy

The Shadar-kai lineage is designed to support:

1. **The Shadow Assassin** (high Dodge Evasion + teleport create mobile strikers)
2. **The Death's Herald** (psychopomp support with death-focused abilities)
3. **The Grim Reaper** (combat specialist who brings necessary endings)
4. **The Mourner** (support character focused on helping others process loss)
5. **The Shadow Survivor** (tank build using shadow-healing and death resistance)

Your mechanical abilities (Blessing of the Raven Queen, Necrotic Resistance, Shadow Sight, high Sanity) create a **character who moves through shadow and accepts death as transition**. You're designed for mobility, death-themed abilities, and psychological resilience.

The variant options allow you to emphasize different aspects of shadow-touched nature—from the psychopomp Death's Herald to the assassin Shadow Blade to the support-focused Mourner—while maintaining the core Shadar-kai identity of necessary severance.

## Final Notes

### **The Weight of Endings:**
Every Shadar-kai carries death-awareness. Your character likely:
- Has experienced profound personal loss
- Knows several death rites by heart
- Can spot when someone is near death
- Struggles with attachment, knowing all things end

This isn't burden—it's **clarity**. You understand what others deny.

### **Respect Across Cultures:**
Modern Dór-o Estel has complex views on Shadar-kai:
- Death-clergy respect your service
- Common folk fear your grim acceptance
- Elves mourn what you've become
- The dying often request your presence

### **The Shadow That Releases:**
Your people believe that **holding on causes suffering**, that memory can become prison, and that sometimes the greatest love is letting go. The Shadowfell didn't curse you—it revealed a truth others refuse to see: everything ends, and that's sacred.

You adventure knowing that death will come—for you, for your companions, for everyone you meet. This doesn't depress you. It **clarifies**. Knowing all things end makes each moment precious, each choice meaningful, each connection valuable precisely because it won't last forever.
