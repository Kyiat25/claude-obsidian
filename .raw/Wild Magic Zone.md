# Wild Magic Zone

A **Wild Magic Zone** is a place where the flow of magic has gone wild and unpredictable. Here, spells might behave strangely—or explode in unexpected ways.

You step into the zone and immediately know: something is wrong. The air is charged and shifting—colours flicker like heat mirages, and sound warps in and out of pitch. Your magic slips through your grip like liquid lightning. Every spell you cast here might not do what you intend. It might do something else entirely. Chaos rules this place, and your power dances on a knife’s edge.

#### 💫 Core Mechanics:

- Every time you cast a spell or use a magical class ability, you’ll need to make a **Focus Save**.
- Fail the check? A chaotic magical effect occurs.
- Succeed? Your magic works normally—but it may still look or sound strange.

### 🎯 Mechanics Players Need to Know:
🌪️ **Concept:** Spellcasting is unpredictable and chaotic.

- **Focus Saving Throw:** (DC based on intensity, typically 15–20+)
    - Roll `1d20 + Focus modifier` when:
        - Casting a spell
        - Using a magical class ability
    - **Failure**: Trigger **Wild Magic Surge**.
    - **Critical Failure (Nat 1)**: Roll **2 surges**, they may combine.
    - **Success**: Spell succeeds with weird cosmetic effects.
    - **Critical Success (Nat 20)**: Gain **advantage** on saves and Focus rolls for 1 minute.
- **Cumulative Risk:**
    - Each failure adds +1 to the next zone DC until a success or you exit.
- **Concentration Risk:**
    - If you fail the Focus Save, make a **Concentration Check**, even for spells that normally don’t require it.
#### 🌀 Intensity:

- The stronger the zone, the harder it is to stay in control.
- Multiple failures may make things more dangerous over time.

#### 🎭 What You Experience:

- The air is thick with static. Colors shift erratically. Magic feels slippery or **alive**.
- Surges can cause radiant bursts, ice storms, time skips, or worse—often with unexpected consequences for everyone nearby.
