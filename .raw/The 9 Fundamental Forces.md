# The 9 Fundamental Forces: Primal Powers and Universal Regulators

> “In the first turning, Astralor struck the void with the Glyph of Will, and stars bled into form. But the Weave whispered back—not all that is forged remains whole.”

The cosmos of **Dór-o Estel** is governed by two **separate yet interwoven realities**—**The Primal Powers and The Universal Forces.** Each operates on a **different level of existence**, shaping reality in ways the other does not comprehend.

- **[The Primal Powers]** ([[Astralor]], [[Aeloria]], [[The Whispering Dark]], [[The Restorer]]) are **locked in their eternal struggle**, shaping the **cycle of existence** without awareness of the **Universal Forces**.
- **[The Universal Forces]** ([[The Keeper of Knowledge]], [[The Wildheart]], [[The Tide Between]], [[The Veiled Hand]], [[The Lantern Below]]) operate **beyond the sight of the Primal Powers**, ensuring that **reality’s deeper mysteries** continue to function.
- **[[The Gods]]** arise as **manifestations of these Universal Forces**, ensuring the laws they represent **have champions within creation**.
- **The Elements** serve as **the raw materials of all divine beings**, influencing their **domains, powers, and aspects within the pantheon.**

---

## **The Primal Powers**
**The Great Architects, Blind to the Universal Forces**

The **Primal Powers** are **fundamental cosmic forces** locked in **their eternal cycle**, unaware that **greater mechanics exist beneath them**.

### **1. [[Astralor]] (Creation & Order)**

- **Believes he alone is shaping the cosmos**, blind to the **Universal Forces’ hidden influence on his creation.**
- Sees the gods as **necessary tools** to maintain his work.
- Resets realities **without realising the Universal Forces preserve echoes of what was lost.**
- Is represented by [[Glyph of Will]]

### **2. [[Aeloria]] (Fate & Guidance)**

- **Weaves the destinies of all gods and mortals**, never aware that **her grand design is subtly altered by the Universal Forces.**
- The gods she selects as champions **are ultimately shaped by forces outside of her control.**
- Thinks **her manipulations are absolute**, but some gods **break free due to the Veiled Hand’s interference.**
- Is represented by [[Glyph of Threads]]

### **3. [[The Whispering Dark]] (Entropy & Unmaking)**

- **Consumes** all things, never questioning **why there is always something left behind to destroy.**
- Does not comprehend that **The Restorer is not the only force bringing things back—The Keeper of Knowledge ensures memory lingers, and The Tide Between ensures balance.**
- Sees only **finality**, unaware that **The Wildheart ensures that even destruction births new chaos.**
- Is represented by [[The Glyph of Endings]]

### **4. [[The Restorer]] (Rebirth & Cycles)**

- **Recycles the past**, not realising that **it is not just fate that shapes the returning echoes of lost worlds.**
- **Believes in inevitable renewal**, never noticing that **The Tide Between carefully balances the process.**
- Thinks everything **eventually returns**, but does not see **how The Veiled Hand’s illusions can change what is truly “real.”**
- Is represented by [[The Glyph of Echoes]]

---

## **The Universal Forces**
**The Silent Hand Beneath the Cosmos**

While the Primal Powers **fight their endless battle**, the **Universal Forces ensure the cosmos functions** by **influencing the gods who serve Astralor.**

### **1. [[The Keeper of Knowledge]] (Understanding & Secrets)**

- Ensures **divine knowledge exists, even when gods fall or fade.**
- **Whispers lost truths** into the minds of mortals, ensuring forgotten knowledge **eventually returns.**
- The reason **some gods are reborn with memories of previous existences.**

### **2. [[The Wildheart]] (Life, Chaos, & Instinct)**

- Ensures **nature and unpredictability exist, even in Astralor’s perfectly structured worlds.**
- Breathes **ferocity, wild magic, and primal energy into the divine pantheon.**
- The source of **gods of war, survival, and untamed lands.**

### **3. [[The Tide Between]] (Balance & Time)**

- Ensures that **the influence of the Primal Powers never becomes absolute.**
- Shapes the gods to **preserve balance**, ensuring **no force dominates completely.**
- The silent reason **the gods of death never fully destroy the cycle of souls.**

### **4. [[The Veiled Hand]] (Illusion & Perception)**

- Ensures that **some things can never be truly known**—even by the gods.
- Shapes **deities of deception, dreams, and forbidden truths.**
- The reason **some gods rise and fall in secrecy, erased from all memory or becoming more than they should be.**

### **5. [[The Lantern Below]] (Dream , Subcreation, and Prophecy)**
- **Burns in the deep subconscious of reality**, where **gods do not look**, and **Primal Forces cannot reach**.
- Governs **the space between knowing and unknowing**—where dreams become prophecy, and thoughts reshape what is real    
- The reason **prophets speak truths before they are known**, and why some visions reshape history.
- **Neither wholly safe nor wholly corrupted**, it is a **gateway between the Universal and the Eldritch**, glimpsed only when the Weave falters.
- The origin of **the Glyph of Dreaming**, and all magic that draws from sleep, vision, and unconscious will.


> _“When the gods sleep, the Lantern dreams. And in dreaming, it writes the glyphs they cannot read.”_
---

## **The Gods of Astralor**
**Shaped by the Universal Forces & the Elements**

The **gods who serve under Astralor** are not merely **fragments of his design**, but **products of the Universal Forces shaping them behind the scenes.**

### **1. Divine Beings Draw from the Universal Forces**

- Every **god of war** carries **The Wildheart’s ferocity**.
- Every **god of time** unknowingly serves **The Tide Between.**
- Every **trickster deity** is subtly manipulated by **The Veiled Hand.**

### **2. The Elements Shape Their Forms**

- Gods are **formed from the primal elements**, influencing their **domains and aspects.**
- A fire god’s **passion and destruction** reflects **the Fire Element.**
- A god of storms is a **fusion of Air and Water**, tied to The Wildheart’s chaos.

### **3. Some Gods Draw Directly from the Primal Powers**

- Some **exceptionally powerful gods** are **aspects of the Primal Powers themselves**, representing their will **in a more direct form.**
- These gods are **not shaped by the Universal Forces**, and **instead act as raw manifestations of the Primal Powers.**
- Example: A **god of destruction** might embody **The Whispering Dark’s will directly**, rather than a balance of cosmic forces.

---
## **The Eternal War That is Not a War**

The Primal Glyphs do not battle—they are **not opposed forces, but a cycle that endlessly turns.**

1. **Will shapes the world.**
2. **Threads guide its course.**
3. **Endings break what has been.**
4. **Echoes ensure nothing is truly lost.**

This is **the rhythm of existence itself.**

- **Astralor believes himself a master of creation**—but he is merely the wielder of a hammer that he did not forge.
- **Aeloria believes herself a master of fate**—but she merely watches the Loom that never ceases to spin.
- **The Whispering Dark believes himself the end of all things**—but the Maw never swallows without something rising from what is lost.
- **The Restorer believes himself a redeemer of all that is broken**—but the Wheel turns, with or without his guidance.

Thus, the cosmos continues, blind to the fact that **even the greatest gods are merely passengers upon the Wheel.**

And so, one question remains:

**Who placed the first spoke upon the Loom? Who carved the first mark upon the Forge? Who lit the first flame, who whispered the first fate, who struck the first blow, who spun the first thread?**

For if there was **a first**, then there may yet be **a last.**

And that… would be the final secret.

## **The Grand Illusion**
**The Great Powers Do Not See the Whole Truth**

_"The cosmos is not what it seems. The gods do not rule as they believe. The very fabric of existence is not forged by will alone, but by laws unseen, forces unspoken, and truths unknown even to the greatest of the divine."_

- **Astralor believes the gods are tools**—he does not realize they are **shaped by forces beyond his control.**
- **The Primal Powers think they are absolute**, yet **they are subtly influenced** by **laws they do not see.**
- **The Universal Forces are the hidden gears that turn reality**, ensuring that **existence does not spiral into chaos.**

The greatest deception of all is that **no force is truly supreme.** **Each bends to another, unknowingly shaped by something beyond its understanding.**

Even the **Primal Powers, in their vastness, are blind to the truth that they are but cogs in a grander machine.**

Some believe the Eldritch Beings do not simply oppose the fundamental forces—they seek to **rewrite or consume them.** The Mirror That Screams fractures the Glyph of Identity. The False Author mimics the Glyph of Threads but always collapses into chaos.

# **⚡ The 9 Great Glyphs of Creation – The Weave of Existence**

These **eight fundamental forces** define every **Creation World** that Astralor forges, forming the **core foundation of existence**. They are the **Primal Glyphs**, the **pillars upon which all reality rests**.

### **🔱 The Four Cosmic Forces – The Pillars of Reality**

The **first four Glyphs** are the foundation of all **creation, fate, entropy, and renewal**. They are the **pillars upon which existence is shaped, bound in an eternal cycle of building, breaking, and beginning again.**

#### **1. [[Glyph of Will]] – The Hammer of Astralor (Creation & Order)**

- Represents the **raw force of creation, the divine spark that brings form from void.**
- It is the reason **realms take shape instead of dissolving into chaos.**
- **Astralor embodies it**, but he does not command it—it is simply **the act of forging reality itself.**

#### **2. [[Glyph of Threads]] – The Loom of Aeloria (Fate & Subtle Guidance)**

- Represents the **invisible pattern that binds all things, ensuring destiny follows a woven path.**
- It ensures that **every action ripples through time, weaving fate into every life and world.**
- **Aeloria serves it**, but she does not control it—she **merely interprets the threads.**

#### **3. [[The Glyph of Endings]] – The Maw of the Whispering Dark (Entropy & Unmaking)**

- Represents **the force of destruction, entropy, and inevitable dissolution.**
- It does not kill—it **ensures nothing is eternal**, breaking down what has been made.
- **The Whispering Dark wields it**, but it does not belong to him—**he is merely its most devoted priest.**

#### **4. [[The Glyph of Echoes]] – The Wheel of the Restorer (Rebirth & Renewal)**

- Represents **the cycle of life, death, and rebirth.**
- Nothing is ever truly lost, **only reforged in a different shape, a new story.**
- **The Restorer embodies it**, ensuring that **what ends will always find its way back.**

---

### **🔮 The Four Universal Principles – The Hidden Hands of the Cosmos**

Beyond the cosmic cycle, there are **forces unseen, truths untold, mechanisms unknown even to the greatest gods.** These are the **Universal Glyphs**, the silent forces that shape all things.

#### **5. [[The Glyph of Understanding]] – The Archive of the Keeper of Knowledge (Truth & Secrets)**

- Ensures that **what has been learned cannot be fully erased.**
- Even forgotten knowledge **finds its way back**, whispered in dreams, carved in stone, buried in lost tomes.
- **The Keeper of Knowledge is its steward**, but it is not his will—**it simply ensures memory endures.**

#### **6. [[The Glyph of Life]] – The Pulse of the Wildheart (Instinct & Chaos)**

- Represents **growth, vitality, and untamed nature.**
- Ensures that **life flourishes, even when the gods seek to control it.**
- **The Wildheart embodies it**, but it is **not purely chaos—it is the very will of existence to survive and evolve.**

#### **7. [[The Glyph of Motion]] – The Balance of the Tide Between (Equilibrium & Time)**

- Ensures **progression, balance, and time itself.**
- Governs the **flow of cosmic energy**, ensuring **no force dominates too greatly or fades too quickly.**
- **The Tide Between manages it**, shaping the balance between **creation and entropy, fate and free will.**

#### **8. [[The Glyph of Shadows]] – The Illusions of the Veiled Hand (Deception & Unreality)**

- Represents **illusion, perception, and the shifting nature of what is real.**
- Governs **magic that bends the senses, conceals truths, and reshapes what can be seen.**
- **The Veiled Hand weaves it**, ensuring **reality is never quite what it appears to be.**

#### **9. [[The Glyph of  the Dreaming]] – The Gateway to The Lantern Below (Subconscious Creation & Prophecy)**

- Represents the **raw dreaming force beneath thought**—where **visions form**, **prophecy stirs**, and **reality is shaped by unspoken desire.**
- It governs **the intersection between truth and potential**, allowing mortals to glimpse futures, forge ideas into form, or reshape the world through **dream, longing, or madness**.
- **The Lantern Below tends this glyph**, though **even it may not understand its full shape.**
- It is the only glyph that **neither the Primal Powers nor the gods claim**, for **to understand it fully is to risk becoming what dreams should never be.**

> _“When the Glyph of Dreaming flickers, the world remembers futures that never happened—and forgets the ones that did.”_

The Nine Glyphs reflect differently across the cosmic layers. In the [[Umbral Veil]], the Glyph of Shadows eclipses Truth. In the [[Luminous Echo]], the Glyph of Life mutates into untamed wonder.


## Missing Glyphs
Beyond these Nine lie the Forbidden Glyphs—those etched in madness or silence. [[The Glyph of Severance]], it is said, can undo even the Glyph of Will. But to write it is to vanish
