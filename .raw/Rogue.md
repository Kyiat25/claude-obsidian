# The Rogue

**Masters of Precision and Possibility**

> *"Everyone fights. The Rogue chooses when, where, and whether the fight is worth having at all."*

---

## The Art of the Perfect Moment

She didn't need to be the strongest person in the room. She needed to be in the room at the right time, in the right position, with the right opening. That opening lasted less than a second. She took it.

You are not a warrior. You are a predator. You do not win fights by enduring — you win them by ending them before they begin, by positioning yourself where your strikes deal maximum damage and your survival requires minimum effort. **Sneak Attack** is the expression of that philosophy: not more hits, but one hit that matters. **Cunning Action** is how you maintain that edge: constant repositioning, constant control.

In FEARS, the Rogue's relationship with Momentum is elegant. Your Sneak Attack generates +2 Momentum instead of +1 — a single precision strike can swing a fight's rhythm. Your Cunning Action repositioning doesn't cost Momentum. Staying hidden costs you nothing. At 15th level, landing a Sneak Attack actively protects your Momentum from damage.

---

## The Rogue's Essence

You are the party's lockpick, scout, assassin, and escape plan — often simultaneously. **Expertise** means you dominate skills at a level no other class can match. **Reliable Talent** means at 9th level, your floor on proficient checks is effectively a 10. Your **Called Shot Synergy** means even your support actions threaten specific body locations without penalty.

You excel in encounters you can set up. You struggle in long frontal engagements where you cannot reposition. Play to your strengths: get hidden, get flanking, and make every Sneak Attack count.

---

## Complexity Assessment: MODERATE (5/10) 

The Rogue tracks:                                        
• Sneak Attack eligibility (once per turn)                   
• Cunning Action usage (bonus action economy)     
• Expertise skills (doubled proficiency bonus)               
• Class Reaction pool (PB uses per short rest)               
• Momentum interactions (especially post-Sneak Attack)       

Recommended for players who enjoy positioning, setting up plays, and high-impact moments over sustained pressure.

## Class Features

### Hit Points

- **Hit Dice:** 1d8 per Rogue level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 8 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per Rogue level after 1st

### Proficiencies

- **Armour:** Light armour
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons, martial weapons with the Finesse property
- **Tools:** Thieves' tools
- **Saving Throws:** Dexterity, Intelligence
- **Skills:** Choose four from Acrobatics, Athletics, Crafting, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Investigation, Memory, Perception, Performance, Persuasion, Sleight of Hand, Stealth, and Streetwise

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) a rapier or (b) a shortsword
- (a) a shortbow and 20 arrows or (b) a shortsword
- (a) a burglar's pack, (b) a dungeoneer's pack, or (c) an explorer's pack
- Leather armour, two daggers, and thieves' tools

---

## Quick Build

To quickly create a Rogue, follow these steps:

1. **Ability Scores:** Make Dexterity your highest score, then choose Intelligence (for Arcane Trickster or Investigation) or Charisma (for social builds).
2. **Lineage and Heritage:** Any combination works; Halfling, Drow, and Goblin are thematic but not required.
3. **Background:** Outcast, Criminal, or any background with Stealth or Deception.
4. **Combat Strategy:** Position for Sneak Attack each turn, use Cunning Action to reposition or hide, and save reactions for Uncanny Dodge when you cannot avoid a hit.

## Core Class Features

### Expertise (1st and 6th Level)

At 1st level, choose two skill proficiencies (or one skill and one tool). Double your Proficiency Bonus for ability checks using those proficiencies. At 6th level, choose two more.

---

### Sneak Attack (1st Level)

Once per turn, deal extra damage to a creature you hit with a Finesse or ranged weapon attack if you have advantage on the attack roll. The extra damage increases with level as shown in the table above.

You don't need advantage if another enemy of the target is within 5 feet of it, that enemy isn't incapacitated, and you don't have disadvantage on the attack roll.

**DCS Integration:**
- **DR Bypass:** Sneak Attack damage ignores the target's Damage Reduction.
- **Called Shot Synergy:** When making a Called Shot that qualifies for Sneak Attack, reduce the attack roll penalty by 2.
- **Critical Hits:** If Sneak Attack lands as a critical hit, double all Sneak Attack dice, and any Called Shot effect is automatically imposed without a saving throw.

**Momentum Interaction — Cunning Flow:** When you land a Sneak Attack, you gain +2 Momentum instead of the normal +1. Once per turn. The perfect exploitation of an enemy's vulnerability creates a significantly greater tactical advantage.

---

### Thieves' Cant (1st Level)

You understand the secret language of rogues. You can communicate through subtle signs, phrases, and jargon, and recognise hidden symbols that convey warnings or opportunities.

---

### Weapon Mastery (1st Level)

At 1st level, gain Weapon Mastery with **one Finesse or ranged weapon** of your choice. When you hit with that weapon, you may apply one Weapon Skill once per turn.

**Sneak Attack Synergy:** You can apply a Weapon Skill and Sneak Attack damage on the same hit. Rogues often focus on bow masteries that control enemy movement or finesse masteries that disable.

You may unlock new masteries after scoring critical hits: 2 crits for simple weapons, 3 for martial weapons.

---

### Cunning Action (2nd Level)

Use a bonus action to take the **Dash**, **Disengage**, or **Hide** action.

**Momentum Interaction — Slippery Escape:** When you use Cunning Action to Dash, Disengage, or Hide, you do not lose Momentum from these tactical movements. Standing up from prone also does not reduce your Momentum.

---

### Rogue Subclass (3rd Level, and 7th, 11th, 15th Level)

Choose a roguish archetype such as **Arcane Trickster**, **Assassin**, or **Thief**. Your subclass grants features at 3rd, 7th, 11th, and 15th levels.

---

### Uncanny Dodge (5th Level)

When an attacker you can see hits you, use your reaction to halve the attack's damage.

---

### Deadly Flourish (5th Level)

When you score a critical hit, choose one additional effect:

- **Disarm:** The target drops one held item.
- **Off-Balance:** Disadvantage on the target's next attack roll before the end of its next turn.
- **Hemorrhage:** The target takes additional 1d6 damage of the weapon's type at the start of its next turn.

This effect occurs after determining whether the critical hit causes an Injury — both can apply.

---

### Evasion (6th Level)

When subjected to an effect allowing a Dexterity saving throw for half damage, you instead take no damage on a success and only half damage on a failure.

---

### Reliable Talent (9th Level)

When making an ability check using a skill, tool, or vehicle with which you are proficient, treat a d20 roll of 9 or lower as a 10.

---

### Nerve-Steeled (9th Level)

The first **Minor Injury** you suffer between long rests does not impose its normal Momentum penalties. You still suffer the injury's other effects, but your combat rhythm is unaffected.

---

### Heroic Boon (10th Level)

Choose one:

**Deadly Precision:** When you deal Sneak Attack damage, reroll any damage die that shows a 1 or 2, taking the new result. When you score a critical hit, immediately use your reaction to make one additional weapon attack against the same target (can benefit from Sneak Attack if unused this turn).

**Shadow Dancer:** When you use Cunning Action to Dash, Disengage, or Hide, gain one of the following until the start of your next turn — Dash: movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks; Disengage: +2 bonus to AC; Hide: advantage on your next attack roll. When you use Uncanny Dodge, move up to 10 feet without provoking opportunity attacks as part of the same reaction.

---

### Sneak Attack Stability (11th Level)

When your Sneak Attack damage is applied to a hit, reduce any Momentum loss you would suffer before the start of your next turn by 1 (to a minimum of 0). Your perfect exploitation of vulnerability keeps you in the flow of combat.

---

### Precise Critical (13th and 17th Level)

You score a critical hit on a 19–20 with ranged or Finesse weapons. Roll one additional weapon damage die for critical hits. At 17th level, roll two additional dice instead.

---

### Perfect Shot (13th Level)

Once per long rest, make a Called Shot with no penalty to your attack roll. If this attack qualifies for Sneak Attack, you automatically deal maximum Sneak Attack damage (do not roll; use maximum values) in addition to rolling weapon damage normally.

---

### Keensense (14th Level)

Gain Keensense (10 feet) — detect invisible or hidden creatures nearby, provided you can hear.

---

### Shadow Step (16th Level)

As a bonus action, teleport up to 60 feet to an unoccupied space you can see that is in dim light or darkness. Your next attack before the end of your turn has advantage and deals an additional 2d6 damage. Usable a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest. Does not provoke opportunity attacks.

---

### Elusive (18th Level)

While not incapacitated, you impose disadvantage on all attack rolls made against you if you can see the attacker. When a creature misses you with a melee attack, use your reaction to either Riposte (make one melee attack against that creature) or Fade (Disengage and move up to half your speed without provoking attacks from the attacker).

---

### Epic Boon (20th Level)

Choose one:

**Stroke of Luck (Enhanced):** When you miss with an attack against a target qualifying for Sneak Attack, turn the miss into a critical hit. Alternatively, treat a failed ability check or saving throw as if you rolled a 20. Usable a number of times equal to half your Dexterity modifier per long rest.

**Master of Shadows:** As a bonus action, become invisible until the start of your next turn (ends on attack or spell). Usable a number of times equal to your Dexterity modifier per short or long rest. When you hit while you have advantage, deal maximum Sneak Attack damage instead of rolling (once per short rest). You cannot be tracked by nonmagical means; advantage on initiative rolls.

---

## Last Stand Options

When death comes for a Rogue, they make it a moment of mastery — a perfect strike, a cunning escape, or a haunting presence.

**Rules for Last Stand:** Invoked when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, roll a death saving throw, or die outright. A free action requiring no reaction or consciousness. Once resolved, the character permanently dies.

---

### 1. Shadow's Requiem

*Your foes blink, and you're gone — only to feel the cold steel of inevitability.*

**Effect:**
- Become invisible and move up to your speed.
- Make a melee attack against a number of enemies equal to your Dexterity modifier (minimum 1). Each attack automatically hits and counts as a critical hit.
- If targets are Surprised or Unaware, add your Sneak Attack damage to all attacks.
- You dissolve into shadow, leaving no trace of your body.

---

### 2. Escape Plan

*You were always three steps ahead. Even in death, they'll never catch you.*

**Effect:**
- Choose up to 5 willing allies within 60 feet.
- You and all chosen allies immediately teleport to any location where you've taken a long rest within the past week (or nearest safe location, DM discretion).
- All allies gain advantage on Dexterity saving throws and Stealth checks for 5 minutes.

---

### 3. Death's Whisper

*A single whisper in the chaos chills the blood of those who hear it.*

**Effect:**
- All enemies within 100 feet make a Wisdom saving throw (DC = 12 + PB + DEX modifier).
- On failure: frightened of your allies for 5 minutes; take psychic damage equal to your Sneak Attack damage at the end of each of their turns while within 30 feet of your allies.
- You become a haunting presence for 5 minutes: enemies cannot regain HP while within 30 feet of your spectral form.

| Last Stand Option | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Shadow's Requiem** | Invisible + automatic critical hits (with Sneak Attack) against multiple targets. | Eliminating enemies. |
| **Escape Plan** | Teleport up to 5 allies to a resting location; advantage on DEX saves/Stealth. | Saving the party. |
| **Death's Whisper** | Enemies frightened + psychic damage + no healing aura. | Disrupting enemy forces. |

## Class Reactions

Rogues have access to powerful reactions that enhance their survivability and capitalise on enemy mistakes. These reactions use your **Proficiency Bonus pool**, which refreshes on a short or long rest.

### Cunning Dodge (2nd Level)

**Trigger:** You are hit by an attack you can see.
**Effect:** Reduce the damage by 1d6 + DEX modifier, and move 10 feet without provoking opportunity attacks.

---

### Shadow Slip (3rd Level)

**Trigger:** A creature within 5 feet misses you with an attack.
**Effect:** Gain +1 Momentum as you capitalise on their mistake, or immediately take the Hide action and disappear into the chaos of combat.

---

### Shadow Reversal (3rd Level)

**Trigger:** You are targeted by an attack you can see.
**Effect:** Roll a DEX (Stealth) check contested by enemy Perception. On success, the attack misses and you may move 10 feet without provoking opportunity attacks.

---

### Killing Opportunity (11th Level)

**Trigger:** An ally within 30 feet hits a creature with an attack.
**Effect:** If that target is within your Sneak Attack range, make one weapon attack against it as part of the same reaction.
