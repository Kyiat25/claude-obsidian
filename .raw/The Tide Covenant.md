# **The Tide Covenant**
**_Triton Faith of the Three Currents_**

> _"We are not of the land. We are not of the sky. We are the voices between—where water meets wind, where tide meets memory, where depth meets dream."_  
> — Opening Hymn of the Tide-Singers

---

# 🌊 **Core Belief: The Three Currents Flow as One**

The Tritons believe the world is held together by **three sacred currents** that flow through all existence:

- **🌊 The Deep Current** (Water) — Memory, endurance, the weight of what was
- **🌀 The Wind Current** (Air) — Change, motion, the breath of what might be  
- **🕊️ The Soul Current** (Spirit) — Connection, wisdom, the thread of what endures

Unlike other races who see the elements as separate forces, Tritons experience them as **inseparable**—the ocean breathes with the wind, the wind carries memory, and memory flows like water.

> _"To separate the tide from the wind is to kill both. To forget the spirit is to drown in silence."_

---

# 🧭 **Foundational Truths**

## **1. The Ocean Remembers**

The sea is not empty. Every wave carries the echo of those who sailed it, every current holds the names of the drowned, every tide returns what was lost. **Memory is sacred**, and to forget is to betray the deep.

## **2. The Tide Is Fate, But the Wind Is Choice**

Fate pulls like the tide—inevitable, powerful, shaping all things. But within the tide's pull, the wind offers direction. **Tritons honor both**—accepting what must come while choosing how to meet it.

## **3. To Trade Is to Bind**

Commerce is not mere exchange—it is **covenant**. When Tritons trade, they bind themselves to the other party through spiritual obligation. A broken deal is a broken current, and the ocean punishes such fractures.

## **4. The Three Currents Must Flow**

Stagnation is death. A tide that does not turn becomes foul. A wind that does not move becomes suffocation. A memory that is not shared becomes madness. **Motion, exchange, and connection** are holy.

## **5. We Are the Voice Between**

Tritons do not belong fully to water, air, or spirit—they exist in the **liminal space** where these forces meet. This makes them **translators, mediators, and bridge-builders** between the elemental powers.

# 📿 **The Sacred Trinity: Gods of the Three Currents**

Unlike the rigid pantheons of land-dwellers, Tritons practice **fluid theology**—they recognize many gods but focus devotion on those aligned with the **Three Currents**.

## 🌊 **The Deep Current (Water Pantheon)**

### **[[Nereus]], the Tide-King** — _The Inevitable_
- **Domains:** Tides, Fate, The Ocean's Eternal Cycle
- **Role:** Primary water deity. Tritons see him as the heartbeat of the ocean—neither cruel nor kind, simply **inevitable**. 
- **Worship:** Before major voyages, Tritons speak their names to the tide, trusting Nereus to carry them home—or claim them if their time has come.

### **[[Marethis]], the River's Flow** — _The Guide_
- **Domains:** Rivers, Trade Routes, Safe Passage
- **Role:** Though Tritons are ocean-dwellers, they honor Marethis for guiding **freshwater trade routes** and ensuring merchant vessels reach port.
- **Worship:** Coins are cast into river mouths where salt meets fresh—a binding between ocean and land trade.

### **[[Vaelora]], the Deep Mother** — _The Keeper of Secrets_
- **Domains:** Secrets, Depth, What Should Stay Hidden
- **Role:** Revered with cautious respect. Tritons believe some knowledge belongs to the deep and should not be brought to the surface.
- **Worship:** Whispered prayers before diving to great depths. Offerings of silence—no speech for one full tide cycle.

### **[[Kaemor]], the Harbor's Keeper** — _The Threshold_
- **Domains:** Harbors, Safe Return, The Space Between Sea and Land
- **Role:** Vital to Triton merchants. Kaemor marks the boundary where ocean meets shore—a sacred threshold where deals are made.
- **Worship:** Every Triton trading vessel has a small shrine to Kaemor at its bow, refreshed with salt water at each port.

### **[[Selmira]], the Tidelit Lady** — _The Moon's Pull_
- **Domains:** Moon, Tides, Lunar Memory
- **Role:** Deeply sacred to Tritons, who track time by lunar tides. She ensures the ocean breathes—the rise and fall that sustains all life.
- **Worship:** Moonrise ceremonies on the water's surface. Tide-singers harmonize with the swell, believing their voices strengthen Selmira's pull.

---

## 🌀 **The Wind Current (Air Pantheon)**

### **[[Zephir]], the Trickster Wind** — _The Disruptor_
- **Domains:** Change, Rebellion, Unpredictability
- **Role:** Both feared and celebrated. Zephir brings the unexpected—sudden squalls that sink ships or favorable winds that save them.
- **Worship:** Tritons toast Zephir before risky ventures, knowing he respects audacity. Prayers are often jokes or riddles—he despises solemnity.

### **[[Thirian]], the Breath of Stillness** — _The Pause_
- **Domains:** Balance, Stillness, The Breath Between Waves
- **Role:** Honored during meditation and treaty negotiations. Thirian is the moment of decision—the held breath before diving.
- **Worship:** Silent vigils underwater, holding breath until the body demands air. The moment of breaking surface is considered sacred transition.

### **[[Orris]], the Trade Wind** — _The Merchant's Blessing_
- **Domains:** Trade Winds, Safe Navigation, Prosperity
- **Role:** Essential to Triton commerce. Orris fills sails and guides currents, ensuring goods reach their destination.
- **Worship:** Merchants leave wind-chimes hanging from ships. The more melodious the sound, the more favorable Orris's attention.

### **[[Aelis]], the Breath Between Worlds** — _The Dreamer_
- **Domains:** Prophecy, Madness, Divine Inspiration
- **Role:** Rarely invoked, deeply respected. Some Tritons experience **Aelis-Sight**—prophetic visions that come through the wind.
- **Worship:** Those touched by Aelis are marked with wind-spiral tattoos and serve as **Storm-Seers** (see below).

---

## 🕊️ **The Soul Current (Spirit Pantheon)**

### **[[Nyzara]], the Moon That Watches** — _The Mother of Memory_
- **Domains:** Protection, Memory, Death
- **Role:** Most beloved Spirit deity among Tritons. She watches over the drowned, preserving their names in moonlight on water.
- **Worship:** Funeral rites involve floating offerings under the full moon. Names of the dead are sung until dawn.

### **[[Aelys]], the Weave-Keeper** — _The Binder_
- **Domains:** Magic, Stability, Balance
- **Role:** Honored by Triton mages and those who maintain the **Coral Anchors** (living coral structures that stabilize ley lines).
- **Worship:** Coral-weavers meditate while grafting new growth, seeing their work as maintaining Aelys's great pattern.

### **[[Vaithis]], the Wandering Star** — _The Pathfinder_
- **Domains:** Destiny, Choice, Navigation
- **Role:** Essential to Triton navigators. Vaithis helps those lost at sea find their way—whether home or to their fate.
- **Worship:** Star-readings before voyages. Triton navigators tattoo constellation maps on their bodies as living charts.

### **[[Vaedoris]], the Oath of the Tide** — _The Contract_
- **Domains:** Oaths, Promises, Binding Agreements
- **Role:** **Most important deity for Triton traders.** Every deal is a spiritual contract witnessed by Vaedoris. Breaking one invites catastrophic consequences.
- **Worship:** Trade agreements are sealed with salt water and blood mixed in a shell. Both parties speak the oath to the tide.

# 🏛️ **Triton Society and the Mirror Chain**

## **The Coral Dominion: Triton Heartland**

The largest Triton civilization in the Mirror Chain, the **Coral Dominion** is a thalassocratic empire spanning thousands of miles of reef and ocean.

### **Population Breakdown:**
- **40% Tritons** — The dominant culture and political power
- **30% Sea Elves** — Skilled craftspeople and coral-weavers  
- **30% Humans** — Surface traders, air-breathing support roles

### **Government: The Council of Tides**
Power rotates monthly between **noble houses** according to the lunar cycle. Leadership is considered temporary—**the tide always turns**.

**Key Principles:**
- No house rules longer than one moon
- Major decisions require consensus during **the Still-Tide** (new moon)
- Disputes are resolved through **Tide-Duels** (ritual combat or debate at the turning of the tide)

---

## **Cultural Castes: The Three Depths**

Triton society is divided not by birth but by **depth of dwelling**—a practical and spiritual hierarchy:

### **🌊 The Deep-Dwelling (Elite)**
- **Depth:** Below 200 feet
- **Role:** Politicians, deep-sea traders, military commanders
- **Why Elite:** Only the strongest, most spiritually attuned Tritons can withstand the pressure of the deep for extended periods. It's considered proof of spiritual favor.

### **🌀 The Mid-Current (Majority)**
- **Depth:** 50-200 feet
- **Role:** Merchants, craftspeople, coral-farmers, the working class
- **Culture:** Most trade and daily life occurs here. The most diverse communities, including many sea elves and humans using water-breathing magic.

### **🕊️ The Surface-Singers (Honored Outsiders)**
- **Depth:** 0-50 feet and surface
- **Role:** Diplomats to air-breathers, wind-readers, those who navigate between worlds
- **Status:** Not lesser but **different**—they sacrifice deep-dwelling to serve as translators between ocean and sky.

## **Tide-Singers**
**The Voice of the Covenant**

- **Role:** Clergy who lead ceremonies, maintain oral histories, teach theology

**The Coral Architects**

- **Training:** 20+ years to master coral-weaving. Must attune to **Aelys** and **Marethis** simultaneously

  - Cities that heal themselves
  - Defensive reefs that grow to block harbors
  - Sacred shrines that sing in underwater currents
  - **Coral Anchors** that stabilize local ley lines

**Those Touched by Wind**

- **Role:** Prophets, weather-readers, interpreters of Aelis's visions
- **Marking:** Spiral tattoos that glow during storms. Considered blessed and cursed simultaneously
- **Powers:**
  - Predict weather with uncanny accuracy
  - Experience prophetic dreams

**The Trade Priests**

- **Role:** Witness and enforce commercial contracts. Serve as neutral arbiters in disputes

- **Rites:**
  - **Salt-Blood Binding:** Mixing salt water and blood to seal contracts
  - **Tide-Weight:** Measuring debts in shells that must be balanced before deals complete

## **Memory-Swimmers**
**The Keepers of the Drowned**

- **Role:** Preserve names and stories of those lost to the sea
- **Practice:** Swim in deep meditation, communing with Nyzara to recover memories
- **Sacred Duty:** Ensure no Triton is ever truly forgotten—even the drowned are remembered in **the Scroll of Tides** (written in bioluminescent ink on kelp parchment)

# 🌑 **Sacred Rituals and Rites**

## **The Rite of First Depth** (Coming of Age)

At age 15, young Tritons undergo a trial where they must:
1. **Dive to their limit** without magical aid
2. **Retrieve a token** from the sea floor (shell, coral, or stone)
3. **Return and speak their name** to the tide

The depth reached determines initial social standing. Those who reach **the Deep-Dwelling** threshold are celebrated. Those who cannot are not shamed—they simply find other paths.

## **The Moonfast Covenant** (Monthly Ritual)

On each new moon (**the Still-Tide**), all Tritons observe:
- **24 hours of silence**—no speech above water
- **Fasting from surface air**—remain submerged entirely
- **Memory-Song**—at midnight, Tide-Singers lead communal remembrance of ancestors

This ritual reinforces the **Three Currents**:
- Silence honors **the Wind** (Thirian's stillness)
- Submersion honors **the Water** (returning to the deep)
- Memory honors **the Spirit** (Nyzara's watch)

## **The Salt-Blood Oath** (Trade Ritual)

Before major contracts, parties perform:
1. **Mix salt water and blood** in a pearl shell
2. **Speak terms aloud** to an Oath-Keeper
3. **Pour the mixture** into the ocean as offering to Vaedoris
4. **Exchange tokens**—usually carved shells or coral

Breaking such an oath results in:
- Spiritual marking (a visible curse that repels water)
- Trade banishment from Coral Dominion ports
- In severe cases, **the Drowning Silence** (see Punishments below)

## **The Tide-Song Funeral** (Death Rites)

When a Triton dies, their body is:
1. **Wrapped in kelp** woven with pearls
2. **Weighted with carved stones** bearing their name and deeds
3. **Taken to deep water** during a full moon
4. **Released to the deep** while Tide-Singers perform the **Descent Hymn**

Cremation is considered **abomination**—fire destroys the spirit's connection to water.

The drowned's name is added to the **Scroll of Tides** and sung monthly during Moonfast.

## **The Storm-Rite of Aelis** (Prophetic Ceremony)

During violent storms, Storm-Seers may enter a trance state:
- They surface in the midst of the tempest
- Allow wind and rain to overwhelm their senses
- Speak prophecies in **the Storm-Tongue** (ancient, pre-cataclysm language)

Tide-Singers record these visions, though interpretation is difficult and dangerous—**Aelis's prophecies are half-mad by design**.

# ⚖️ **Laws and Punishments**

## **The Three Unforgivable Acts**

### **1. Breaking the Oath-Tide** (Contract Violation)
- **Punishment:** **The Drowning Silence**
  - The guilty is weighted with stones and submerged in a sacred pool
  - If they break free before drowning, they're pardoned (Vaedoris has released them)
  - If they drown, their name is stricken from all records

### **2. Memory-Murder** (Destroying Historical Records)
- **Punishment:** **The Forgetting**
  - All records of the guilty are erased
  - Their name is never spoken again
  - They are exiled, forbidden to enter any Triton settlement
  - Even their kin may not acknowledge them

### **3. Stilling the Current** (Preventing Trade or Blocking Ocean Passages)
- **Punishment:** **The Current's Mercy**
  - The guilty is bound and set adrift in a small boat
  - No water, no shelter
  - If they survive 7 days, they may plead their case
  - Most do not survive

# 🌊 **Triton Relationship with Other Races**

## **Sea Elves**
- **Status:** Honored partners, master coral-weavers
- **Tension:** Some sea elves chafe at Triton political dominance
- **Shared Ground:** Both revere **Selmira** and **Nyzara**, often worship together

## **Humans**
- **Status:** Necessary but limited
- **Role:** Surface traders, air-breathing support staff, diplomatic liaisons
- **Barrier:** Most humans cannot tolerate extended underwater living, even with magic
- **Cultural Clash:** Human linear time vs. Triton tidal cycles creates scheduling nightmares

## **Orcs (Coastal/Island)**
- **Status:** Respected trade partners
- **Common Ground:** Both cultures value **honor contracts** and **direct communication**
- **Difference:** Orcs see oaths as personal honor; Tritons see them as cosmic binding

## **Elves (Wood/High)**
- **Status:** Wary respect
- **Issue:** Elven long memory vs. Triton **cyclical memory** (tides erase and return)
- **Exception:** Moon Elves sometimes study with Triton Storm-Seers, both revering lunar cycles

## **Dwarves**
- **Status:** Confusing mutual fascination
- **Triton view:** Dwarves are "stone fish"—living in the deep but denying water
- **Dwarf view:** Tritons are "singing barnacles"—too focused on motion over permanence
- **Trade:** Rare but valuable—dwarven metal crafts for Triton coral and pearls

## **Halflings**
- **Status:** Delightfully baffling
- **Triton view:** Halflings laugh at fate, which is either blasphemy or enlightenment
- **Cultural Exchange:** Some Halfling traders have learned **Tide-Song** and are welcome in ports

# 📜 **Sacred Texts and Oral Traditions**

## **The Song-Memory** (Living Oral History)

Unlike written scripture, Triton theology is encoded in **melody**. The Song-Memory is:
- **Taught through apprenticeship**—Tide-Singers learn by singing with elders for decades
- **Adaptive**—new verses added as history unfolds
- **Variant**—different communities maintain slightly different versions (considered healthy diversity)
- **Tested**—accuracy verified when Tide-Singers from distant communities meet and their songs harmonize

## **The Scroll of Tides** (Written Record)

The **only** written Triton sacred text:
- **Material:** Kelp parchment written in bioluminescent ink
- **Content:** Names of every Triton who died at sea
- **Length:** Thousands of miles of continuous scroll, stored in deep ocean temples
- **Reading:** Performed publicly during Moonfast rituals
- **Growing:** New names added monthly by Memory-Swimmers

## **The Breathing Parables**

Short teaching stories used to educate young Tritons:

### _The Tide That Tried to Stay_
A tide refused to ebb, believing it could hold the ocean forever. It became stagnant, foul, and died. **Lesson:** Motion is life.

### _The Wind That Forgot the Water_
A wind blew so far from the ocean it forgot where it came from. It became dry, harsh, and cruel—until rain reminded it. **Lesson:** The three currents need each other.

### _The Shell That Held All Secrets_
A shell captured every secret of the deep. It became so heavy it sank beyond reach, taking its knowledge to the void. **Lesson:** Some knowledge must flow, not be hoarded.

# 🧬 **Triton Cultural Practices**

## **Name-Tides**

Tritons have **three names**:
1. **Birth-Name** (given by parents)
2. **Depth-Name** (earned during First Depth ritual)
3. **Trade-Name** (chosen when beginning commerce)

The **Depth-Name** is considered most true—it reflects what the ocean revealed about you.

**Example:**
- Birth-Name: Thalassia
- Depth-Name: Pearlcatcher (retrieved rare black pearl at 300 feet)
- Trade-Name: Swift-Current (known for fast delivery)

## **Tide-Marking** (Time Measurement)

Tritons don't track linear time but **tidal cycles**:
- **Flood-Tide** (morning)
- **High-Tide** (midday)
- **Ebb-Tide** (evening)
- **Low-Tide** (night)

**Longer periods:**
- **Moon-Tide** (lunar month)
- **Season-Tide** (3 moon-tides)
- **Great-Tide** (solar year)

This makes scheduling with surface-dwellers frustrating:
> _"Meet me in three flood-tides at the ebb-peak of the second moon-tide"_ means absolutely nothing to a human.

## **Current-Reading** (Meditation Practice)

Daily practice where Tritons:
1. Float motionless in a current
2. Feel the water's flow without resistance
3. Achieve **still-motion**—moving while not moving

This trains both physical skill (vital for navigation) and spiritual discipline (acceptance of fate while choosing direction).

## **Shell-Speech** (Visual Communication)

In depths where sound distorts, Tritons use:
- **Carved shells** worn on bodies, arranged in patterns
- **Bioluminescent signals** from trained jellyfish
- **Bubble-rings** blown in specific sequences

This creates a **secondary language** that surface-dwellers rarely learn.

## **The Tide-Feast** (Celebration)

Major celebrations involve:
- **Communal hunts** for large ocean predators
- **Offering portions** to the sea (feeding smaller fish, ensuring the cycle continues)
- **Kelp-wine** fermented in underwater grottos
- **Singing competitions** where best voice wins honor (and sometimes mates)
- **Pearl-counting** games where participants bet on currents' direction

# 🎵 **Music and Art**

## **Tide-Song** (Sacred Music)

The foundation of Triton culture:
- **Performed underwater**—sound travels differently, creating haunting harmonics
- **Multi-layered**—groups sing different parts, creating complex weaving melodies
- **Functional**—used for navigation, communication, and worship simultaneously
- **Improvisational**—while core melodies are traditional, variations are encouraged

## **Coral-Carving** (Visual Art)

Current-Weavers and artisans create:
- **Living sculptures** that grow and change over decades
- **Functional art**—beauty serves purpose (defensive reefs that are also masterpieces)
- **Memory-Stones**—coral formations carved with names and deeds
- **Light-Gardens**—arrangements of bioluminescent organisms creating living paintings

## **Current-Dancing** (Movement Art)

Performance art where dancers:
- Use ocean currents to create graceful, seemingly impossible movements
- Partner with marine life (dolphins, rays, sea turtles)
- Tell stories through motion—no words, only flow
- Considered highest art form—harder to master than Tide-Song

# 🌌 **Mythology and Legends**

## **The First Breath** (Creation Myth)

> In the beginning, there was only ocean—deep, dark, still. The water had no memory, no motion, no voice.

> Then the Wind came, curious about what lay beneath. It touched the water's surface and created the first wave. The ocean laughed for the first time.

> That laugh became the first Triton. Part water, part wind, part of the memory of that first touch. We are the conversation between ocean and sky, made flesh.

## **The Drowning of Ashkarra** (Historical Lesson)

Tritons tell their version of Ashkarra's fall differently than land-dwellers:

> _The fire-cities believed they ruled. They built towers that pierced the sky, trying to touch the stars without swimming through the ocean.

> They forgot: To reach above, you must first go deep.

> When their [[World Tree]] fell, the ocean rose—not in anger, but in correction. The tide simply returned what was always ours.

> Some cities sank. But we did not raid them. We preserved them. Deep in the grottos, their names are still sung. We remember what fire tried to forget._

**Lesson:** Hubris invites the tide. But the ocean remembers, even when fire does not.

## **The Storm-Pact of Zephir** (Why Wind Can Be Trusted)

A legend explaining Triton relationship with Air pantheon:

> Long ago, Zephir the Trickster and Nereus the Tide-King feuded over who controlled the surface. Zephir claimed the wind owned it; Nereus said the tide did.

> They agreed to a test: trap a single Triton at the surface during a storm. If she drowned, Nereus won. If she survived, Zephir won.

> But the Triton (named Calyssa Storm-Breath) did neither. She dove deep, then leapt high, riding both wind and wave. She laughed at them both and sang: _"You fools! We are already both!"_

> Since then, Zephir and Nereus have been allies. And Tritons honor both—we are the proof that wind and water belong together.

# ⚔️ **The Tide-Fanged Clans Relationship**

## **Pirate Problem**

The **Tide-Fanged Clans** (pirate confederacy) are Triton culture's greatest shame and greatest proof of freedom:

**Triton View:**
- **Shameful:** They reject Oath-Keeping, taking without proper contract
- **Respected:** They survive without support, proving Triton resilience
- **Complicated:** Many Tide-Fanged captains ARE Tritons who fled Deep-Dwelling politics

**Cultural Response:**
- Coral Dominion officially hunts them
- Unofficially, some Tritons slip supplies to Tide-Fanged ports
- Families of Tide-Fanged Tritons are **not** exiled—they're pitied but not punished

**Theology:**
The Tide-Fanged represent **the Severed Current**—those who broke from the cycle but still swim. They're proof that even broken oaths can't stop a Triton from moving.

# 🏺 **Artifacts and Sacred Objects**

## **The Pearl of First Tide**

Legendary artifact supposedly from the ocean's first wave:
- Grants the holder ability to breathe in **any** liquid
- Allows speaking all languages of Water pantheon deities
- Reportedly glows during crucial historical moments
- **Current Location:** Unknown (lost when Ashkarra fell)

## **The Conch of Calling**

Actual artifact possessed by the Coral Dominion:
- When blown, heard by **every Triton** within 100 miles underwater
- Used only for emergencies (war, natural disaster, divine omen)
- Blown by the ruling High Tide-Singer
- Last used during the vampire pirate incursion (15 years ago)

## **The Spiral Blade**

Ceremonial weapon used in Tide-Duels:
- Made of sharpened nacre (mother of pearl)
- Cannot cut flesh but stings like fire when it touches skin
- Dulls immediately if used with true intent to kill
- Victory determined by first to draw blood (even a pinprick)

## **The Kelp-Crown**

Symbol of Coral Dominion leadership:
- Woven from **never-dying kelp** blessed by Current-Weavers
- Each ruling house adds one strand during their moon-rule
- Crown now contains thousands of strands from centuries of rule
- Wearing it allows the bearer to **hear the Song-Memory** perfectly

# 📖 **Daily Life and Practicalities**

## **Diet**

- **Staples:** Raw fish, kelp salads, sea cucumbers, urchins
- **Delicacies:** Octopus, rare deep-fish, pearl-oysters (only during festivals)
- **Taboo:** Never eat dolphins or whales (sacred to Nereus)
- **Cooking:** Rare—most food eaten raw. Fire is **highly unnatural** underwater
- **Preparation:** Marinating in brine, fermenting in sealed shells

## **Architecture**

- **Deep-Dwelling:** Carved from underwater rock formations, reinforced with coral
- **Mid-Current:** Mix of natural caves and grown coral structures
- **Surface:** Floating platforms for interacting with air-breathers

**Key Features:**
- **Current-Ports:** Openings positioned to capture natural currents for ventilation
- **Glow-Gardens:** Bioluminescent organisms providing light
- **Sound-Chambers:** Designed to amplify Tide-Song for ceremonies
- **Pressure-Locks:** Magical barriers preventing compression damage when moving between depths

## **Fashion**

- **Materials:** Woven kelp, sharkskin, sea-silk (from certain mollusks)
- **Jewelry:** Shells, coral, pearls (black pearls indicate Deep-Dwelling status)
- **Practical:** All clothing must not create drag in water
- **Formal:** Elaborate shell-work arranged in patterns indicating clan/house
- **Nudity:** Not taboo—water provides natural modesty. Clothing is functional or decorative, not required

## **Marriage and Family**

- **Pair-Bonding:** Monogamy is common but not required
- **Ceremony:** Couples dive to their deepest shared depth and exchange pearls
- **Duration:** "Until the tide turns"—meaning until death or mutual agreement to part
- **Children:** Raised communally by extended families and pods
- **Inheritance:** Property and status pass through **depth-tested merit**, not automatic birthright

# 🎲 **For Game Masters: Running Triton NPCs**

## **Voice and Manner**

- **Speech Patterns:** Often insert tide/current metaphors naturally
  - "As the tide turns..." instead of "However..."
  - "Diving deep on that..." instead of "Let me think..."
  - "Surface thought" means "obvious" or "shallow understanding"
  
- **Body Language:** 
  - Constantly moving—standing still is uncomfortable
  - Eye contact more intense (used to peering through murky water)
  - Personal space is fluid—Tritons comfortable being very close or very distant

- **Negotiation Style:** Everything is give-and-take, like tide. Never refuse outright—redirect instead.

## **Cultural Conflicts**

- **Timing:** Tritons will miss deadlines not out of disrespect but because **tidal time** doesn't match **linear time**
- **Contracts:** Tritons take oaths with terrifying seriousness. Breaking a promise to them is relationship-ending
- **Fire:** Deep cultural aversion. Tritons will tolerate it but never comfortable around open flame
- **Memory:** Asking a Triton to forget something is deeply offensive—it insults Nyzara

## **Plot Hooks Involving Tritons**

1. **Broken Oath:** A human merchant broke a Salt-Blood Oath. The Oath-Keeper has declared **the Drowning Silence**. Can the party intervene?

2. **Storm-Seer Vision:** A Storm-Seer has prophesied disaster for a coastal city. Do the land-dwellers believe the "mad fish-person"?

3. **Pirate Triton:** A Tide-Fanged Triton captain seeks redemption. Can they make amends without being Drowned Silenced?

4. **Scroll of Tides:** A section of the ancient scroll has been stolen. Without those names, Memory-Swimmers can't complete Moonfast rituals.

5. **Coral Anchor Dying:** A Coral Anchor is mysteriously dying, destabilizing local ley lines. Current-Weavers need help investigating why.
