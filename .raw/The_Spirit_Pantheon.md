#  [[The Weave That Binds and Breaks]]

> _"Fate is not a cage, nor is it a road. It is a thread, woven through time, through choices, through gods and mortals alike. Some accept its pattern. Some break free. Some become the ones who weave it anew."_

The **Spirit Pantheon** is unlike the others. It does not rule **over fire’s ambition, earth’s endurance, water’s cycles, or air’s rebellion**—instead, it is **the force that connects all things, the cosmic foundation of magic, fate, and hidden knowledge**.

Spirit’s gods are **keepers of the Weave, recorders of destiny, seekers of lost truths, and whisperers of forbidden knowledge**. Some **preserve order**, ensuring that the Weave remains stable. Others **challenge its authority**, rewriting fate, revealing forgotten truths, and ensuring that not even gods hold absolute power over destiny.

> The gods of Spirit walk closer to mortals than any others.  
> They drift through dreams, names, echoes, and grief—**always near, always just beyond reach**.

This is no accident.

**[[Aeloria]]**, the forgotten Primal who removed herself from the Weave, **threaded the domain of Spirit into the world itself**—as her final act of presence. She made the Spirit gods **tangible**, **mirrored**, and **intertwined with mortal life**, so that she could **guide without being known**, **touch without being seen**.

The Spirit gods believe they are **shaping memory**, **echoing purpose**, or **preserving truth**.

But in truth?

> **They are threads on Aeloria’s hidden loom.**

# **🌌 The Greater Gods of the Spirit Pantheon – [[The Cosmic Weavers]]**

> _"The Weave stretches across all things—time, fate, magic, memory, the sky itself. These are not gods of war or rule, nor do they command fire or stone. They are the forces that hold all of existence together, the hands that weave the strands of destiny, and the minds that glimpse beyond the veil of understanding."_

The **Greater Gods of Spirit** are **not rulers nor conquerors—they are the weavers, the ones who record, structure, or defy the cosmic order**. Some are **preservers**, ensuring that fate and magic remain intact, while others **question or rewrite fate itself**.

These gods are deeply **tied to the stars, the moon, and the cycles of existence**, influencing **prophecy, magic, memory, and knowledge both known and forbidden**.

## **🌕 [[Nyzara]], The Moon That Watches** _(The Mother of the Weave, The Gentle Light in the Darkness)_

> _"The stars are distant. The sun is cruel. But the moon watches, and she never forgets."_

- **Domains:** Protection, Memory, The Celestial Guardian of the Weave
- **Symbol:** A full moon with an endless thread spiralling around it
- **Worshiped By:** Healers, storytellers, those who believe in fate’s guidance
- **Rivals:** **[[Vel'Zyren]] (Web-Spinner), [[Nytheris]] (Fate-Tearer), [[Xelthar]] (Whisper Beyond Time)**

### **Mythology & Influence**

- **Nyzara is the mother of the Weave, the first thread spun into the void.**
- **She does not command, nor does she dictate fate—she observes, records, and ensures that knowledge is not lost.**
- **The stars move beyond mortal reach, but the moon remains close—her light guiding those who seek understanding.**

**She is the gentle hand of Spirit, the counterbalance to fate’s cruelty, the force that ensures memories endure.**

_“In the dreams of Nyzara, some memories return unasked. She believes them to be mistakes. They are not. They are the work of the unknowable force [[the Restorer]]”_

- **She opposes those who seek to break or rewrite the Weave—those who do not respect the boundaries of magic.**

**Aelys is the guardian, ensuring that the Weave is never torn apart by reckless ambition.**

## 🌗 [[The Echoed Mother]], She Who Returns Them *(The Office of Rebirth, The Thread Rewoven Never Quite the Same)*

> *"I do not give back what was taken. Nothing can. I give the thread another weaving — and if you loved the first cloth, you will grieve even as you rejoice. That, too, is my gift."*

- **Divine Rank:** Greater God
- **Domains:** Rebirth, The Rewoven Thread, Return-Never-Quite-the-Same, The Memory Carried Across
- **Symbol:** A thread emerging from cloth, dyed a slightly different shade than it entered
- **Worshiped By:** Reincarnation-faiths, the bereaved who watch children for familiar gestures, midwives of the old rites
- **Allies:** **[[Nyzara]] (What the Thread Remembers), [[Ytheis]] (The Cycle), [[Restbringer]] (Who Delivers to Her Door), [[Aelys]] (see the conflation, below)**
- **Enemies:** **[[Astralis]] (Who Holds That What Is Released Is Gone), [[Morthius]] (Whose Rewriting Is Her Office Counterfeited), [[Varthos]] (Who Keeps What She Would Re-weave)**

### **Mythology & Influence**

- The final office of the death-cycle: what Varethis cuts and Restbringer carries, **the Echoed Mother weaves back in** — not restored, *rewoven*. Her doctrine's whole weight sits in the qualifier: never quite the same.
- She is why Spirit's faiths do not fear death and do not trust it either. Return is real; *sameness* is the lie. Grief and hope are, in her theology, the same emotion looked at from its two ends.
- The déjà-vu spirits ([[Ytheis]], [[Sytheris]]) orbit her office: an echo is a thread recognizing its previous weaving.

### 🌗 The Aelys Conflation *(⚑ Tier-1 ruling, written to the proposed resolution)*

Mortals persistently conflate the Echoed Mother with **[[Aelys]]**, the Weave-Keeper — two memory-goddesses, one name-shaped confusion. Per the proposed ruling (Spirit cross doc §5), this entry treats the conflation as **real Loomless sediment**: in the age before the Loom fixed identity, the keeping-function and the returning-function were one unsorted thread. Worship-pressure — thin and divided, as Spirit's always is — never finished separating them, and in places where the Weave runs weak, they **bleed back together**: an Aelys shrine answers a rebirth-prayer; a Mother-rite steadies failing magic. Her priestesses call these events *the Old Closeness* and consider them holy. Aelys's clergy call them filing errors and consider them embarrassing. **Both are right, which is the point.**

*If the ruling goes the other way (clean separation), delete this subsection and the Aelys ally-line; the rest of the entry stands.*

### **Worship & Beliefs**

- In the southern reincarnation-faiths, a newborn is shown three belongings of the family's dead; a grasp toward any of them is recorded, without conclusion, in the **book of echoes**. Drawing conclusions is the *family's* sin, forbidden by her rites — the Mother returns threads, not people, and naming a child as a returned soul is theft from both.
- Her midwife-priestesses dye one thread of every swaddling cloth off-shade. Ask why and they will say: *so the child knows, before it knows anything, that different is not wrong.*
- Her great feast is held at the year's turning — the calendar's own return-never-quite-the-same — and it is the only Spirit festival at which mourning clothes and birth-gifts are worn together.

## 🧵 [[Sulwen]], The First Spindle *(She Who Believes She Spins the Souls)*

> *"Every thread that has ever been, I drew from my spindle. Ask me how, and I will tell you honestly: I do not remember doing it. I only know that no soul has ever arrived without me — and none ever shall."*

- **Divine Rank:** Greater God
- **Domains:** The Origin of Souls (claimed), New Threads, The Moment Before the First Cry
- **Symbol:** A spindle wound with unspun light
- **Worshiped By:** Expectant parents, midwives (jointly with Earth's [[Lytha]] and [[Ethriss]]), every faith's nursery-rites — the broadest gentle worship in the setting
- **Allies:** **[[Lytha]] (The First Breath), [[Ethriss]] (The Pale Midwife), [[The Echoed Mother]] (a wary courtesy — see below), [[Nyzara]] (Who Remembers Every Thread's First Day)**
- **Rivals:** **Every pantheon's Sourcing doctrine (see the Dispute), [[Varethis]] (her opposite number, whom she has never once addressed)**

### 🔹 Role & Influence

- Sulwen holds the office the death-cycle implies and never names: **the source**. Varethis cuts, the Echoed Mother reweaves the returning — Sulwen claims the threads that have never been. Hers is the most attended and least witnessed work in creation: no oracle, seer, or god has ever *seen* her spin, and her faith holds this as proof of the work's sanctity rather than a question about it.
- Her correlation is perfect and has been forever: where Sulwen is invoked, souls arrive; souls have never arrived where something was not, by some name, invoking her. Her priesthood calls this the **Unbroken Attendance**. It has never failed. It is the entire argument.
- With the Echoed Mother she keeps a careful, ancient courtesy: the Mother returns used thread, Sulwen provides new, and neither has ever been able to say — though both have wondered, privately, for ages — exactly where the boundary between their offices lies, or who checked it last.

### 🌀 The Sourcing Dispute *(conflicting accounts — the disagreement is the canon)*

Sulwen's claim is universal; its acceptance is not. Every pantheon keeps its own Sourcing doctrine, and each treats Sulwen's cult as a sweet misunderstanding of *their* truth:

- **Earth:** souls condense in the deep places like ore, slowly, under weight — Sulwen merely *collects* what the mountain made.
- **Water:** they arrive on a tide from beneath the Black Abyss, and Sulwen stands at the waterline taking credit for the sea.
- **Fire:** each soul is struck like a spark from a strike no god has witnessed — Sulwen catches sparks; she does not strike them.
- **Air:** the first breath *is* the soul entering; Sulwen's spindle is a picture mortals drew of a wind.
- **Spirit (her own house):** the Weave frays *inward*, and a new thread is the fray, healed — a doctrine Sulwen's clergy consider technically compatible with her and everyone else considers evasive.

All five work liturgically. All five have miracles. No account is the record.

> [!truth] DM-Truth ⚑
> None of them spin the souls — including Sulwen. New threads emerge through the Weave itself, from [[Aeloria]]'s imperceptible weaving, a process no god can perceive any more than mortals can perceive the gods' own sourcing in belief. Sulwen is the mask worship built over the imperceptible: mortals needed the origin of their children to have a face, believed with the densest, most sustained tenderness in the setting, and a goddess condensed around the claim — **who therefore sincerely remembers an office she has never performed.** Her perfect correlation is real and means nothing: she attends every arrival because belief in her attends every arrival. She is the spine at its purest — a self-confirming partial truth so complete that even the god inside it cannot find the seam. The one crack: she does not remember doing the work, only having done it. She has never told anyone this. It is the loneliest sentence in her scripture, and it is not in her scripture.

# **🌌 The Lesser Gods of the Spirit Pantheon – [[The Weave’s Echoes]]**

> _"The Weave is not perfect. The Weave is not absolute. Fate is questioned, magic is flawed, and knowledge is dangerous. The Lesser Gods of Spirit are those who exist within its threads, shaping the gaps, the flaws, and the whispers that even the gods fear to hear."_

Unlike the **Greater Gods**, who establish **the grand cosmic laws of fate, magic, and knowledge**, the **Lesser Gods** are **the ones who challenge, refine, and break these rules from within**. Some **test prophecy, unraveling fate’s grip on mortals**. Others **represent the imperfections within magic itself, ensuring that power is never absolute**.

These gods are invoked **by those who defy, by those who seek knowledge best left unknown, and by those who understand that even the Weave has cracks.**

---

> _"There is always another way. You need only have the courage to seek it."_

- **Domains:** Cosmic Balance, The Weave’s Forked Roads, Fate’s Flexibility
- **Symbol:** A distant star flickering between two positions
- **Worshiped By:** Wanderers, those who seek to escape fate, scholars of fate’s paradoxes
- **Allies:** **[[Nytheris]] (Fate’s Rebellion), [[Telys]] (Dream Prophecy), [[Nyzara]] (Memory’s Guardian)**
- **Enemies:** **[[Astria]] (Fate’s Law – Air), [[Kaelor]] (Fate’s Bargains), [[Vel'Zyren]] (The Fate Manipulator)**

### **Mythology & Influence**

- **Vaithis ensures that no prophecy is unbreakable—not through destruction, but through choice.**
- **He does not defy fate, nor does he enforce it—he merely illuminates the paths that exist beyond what is foretold.**
- **Those who invoke him seek not to shatter destiny, but to reshape it—to find the door where none was thought to exist.**

✅ **Unlike Nytheris, who breaks fate, Vaithis helps mortals and gods find a way around it.**

## 🌒 The Death-Cycle Offices

*Spirit's death gods are one process with five offices: **[[Varethis]] (the cut) → [[Valthos]] (the threshold) → [[Restbringer]] (the passage) → [[The Echoed Mother]] (the return)**, with **[[Serelune]]** guarding the seam. Where the elemental pantheons each caught one phase of death, Spirit holds the machinery between the phases. ⚑ Framing per the one-cycle ruling (Spirit cross-relationships §7.2).*

---

## 🌒 [[Serelune]], The Veil at the Seam *(The Guardian of the Severing, She Who Keeps the Cut Clean)*

> *"Varethis cuts the thread. My work begins the instant his ends: to see that nothing — nothing — passes back through the cut."*

- **Divine Rank:** Lesser God
- **Domains:** The Boundary of Death, The Sealed Severance, The Watch Against Return
- **Symbol:** A cut thread, its two ends sealed with silver wax
- **Worshiped By:** Funerary priests, wards against the restless dead, those who fear what answers when the dead are called
- **Allies:** **[[Varethis]] (The One Who Unravels), [[Valthos]] (The Final Thread), [[Torvax]] (Earth's Weight of Ages), [[Astralis]] (The Silent Void)**
- **Enemies:** **[[Morthius]] and all who would rewrite endings, necromantic patrons of every stripe, [[Ytheis]] (whose cycles she distrusts on principle)**

### **Mythology & Influence**

- **Serelune is not death. She is death's integrity.** The cut Varethis makes would be meaningless if it could be unmade; she is the reason a severance *stays* severed.
- Where the elemental pantheons each caught one phase of death, Serelune holds the phase none of them noticed: **the seam itself** — the raw edge where a life used to be attached to the world.
- She is the least prayed-to and most relied-upon of the death offices. No one wants her attention; everyone wants her work done.
- **She is the office [[Morthius]]'s Glyph of Endings assaults directly.** Every rewritten death is a seam reopened, and her priesthood — small, scattered, humourless — is the first mortal institution to notice Arc 4A beginning, and the least equipped to explain what it is noticing.

### **Worship & Beliefs**

- Her rite is performed at every funeral in the Weave-faiths, in one gesture: the officiant ties, then **seals**, a thread — never merely knots it. A knot can be undone. Wax cannot be un-melted.
- Grave-wards bearing her cut-thread sign are carved *inside* tombs, facing the occupant. The protection is not for the living.
- In the river-cities it is held that speaking a dead name backward frays her seal, and the superstition is enforced with an earnestness that suggests somebody, once, proved it.

> [!truth] DM-Truth
> Serelune's seals hold against everything mortal necromancy can bring — and she has begun, in the last centuries, to find seams *already reopened* with her wax intact. She attributes it to Morthius's early workings. Some of it is. The rest is [[the Restorer]], which does not pass through the seam at all; it restores from the far side, where her office has no jurisdiction and her theology has no word. Like Nyzara's returned memories, she classifies the anomalies as her own failures. They are not.

## 🕯️ [[Restbringer]], The Quiet Road *(The Passage Between the Cut and the Return, The One Who Walks the Dead Home)*

> *"You will not remember the road. That is not a flaw in the road. That is the road's kindness."*

- **Divine Rank:** Lesser God
- **Domains:** The Passage of the Dead, Funerary Rites, The Walk Between, Rest Before Return
- **Symbol:** A lantern hung on a thread stretching into darkness
- **Worshiped By:** Mourners, hospice-keepers, funerary guilds, the dying who are ready
- **Allies:** **[[Nyzara]] (Memory's Keeper), [[Telys]] (The Dream That Speaks), [[Serelune]] (The Sealed Seam Behind), [[Veythos]] (Water's Drowned Ferryman — a working arrangement)**
- **Enemies:** **[[Morthius]] (who would make the road unnecessary), [[Vorendral]] (Earth's keeper of the unclaimed, whose kingdom is full of those the road missed), [[Xyvaris]] (whose broken bargains strand souls mid-passage)**

### **Mythology & Influence**

- Between Varethis's cut and the Echoed Mother's return lies a distance, and **Restbringer is the god of crossing it.** Not a judge, not a keeper — a *conductor*. The dead are not his; they are his passengers.
- The elemental faiths each believe their psychopomp carries the dead the whole way: Air's winds bear them, Water's ferryman rows them, Earth's weight settles them. Restbringer's priesthood teaches — quietly, without picking fights — that each of those carries the dead only **to the road**, and the road is his.
- His arrangement with Water's [[Veythos]] is the model: the Ferryman brings the drowned to a shore no living chart shows, and the Quiet Road begins at the waterline. Neither priesthood calls the other wrong. This makes them nearly unique.

### **Worship & Beliefs**

- The lantern-vigil: a light kept burning from death until burial, then extinguished with wet fingers — *handing the flame to the one who walks*. Practiced across the Weave-faiths; the number of nights varies by region and is defended locally with surprising heat.
- Hospice-keepers of the eastern valleys are ordained in his name and take the **Vow of the Unhurried**: never to speed a death, never to delay one.
- His only heresy is the shortcut. Cults promising a *faster* road for the beloved dead recur every few generations, and his priesthood treats them with a hostility it shows nothing else.

## 🪡 [[Omrith]], The Knotfinder *(Spirit — She Who Walks the Threads Looking for Flaws)*

> *"Aelys mends. Serelune seals. Varethis cuts. Splendid work, all of it — done exactly where I put the chalk mark. Ask any of them who told them where to look."*

- **Divine Rank:** Lesser God
- **Domains:** Inspection of the Weave, Knots Found in Fate, Fraying Found Before It Spreads, The Chalk Mark
- **Symbol:** A thread with a small white mark tied at its flaw
- **Worshiped By:** Diviners who audit rather than prophesy; curse-finders; and — by adopted analogy, not office — the weaver-guilds, who took her as patron uninvited and whom she has never troubled to correct
- **Allies:** **[[Aelys]] (Who Mends What She Marks), [[Serelune]] (Who Seals What She Flags), [[Varethis]] (Whose Cuts She Sometimes Recommends)**
- **Enemies:** **[[Nytheris]] (The Fate-Tearer, Her Whole Workload), [[Vel'Zyren]] (see below), [[Kyren]] (Who Reads Through the Frays She Reports — a professional horror)**

### 🔹 Role & Influence

- Fate's gods act on the Weave; **Omrith is the one who looks at it first.** She walks the threads with no power to mend, seal, or cut — only to *find*: the knot where two fates have tangled, the fray where the Weave thins toward what should not be looked at, the chalk mark that tells the menders where. Every other Weave-office is downstream of her eyes.
- Her seat is dual by nature: knots are **Fate's** business (a tangle is destiny gone wrong) and frays are **Death's** (a thinning is where endings leak), so she reports up both stairs and belongs wholly to neither — the only Spirit god the fate cluster and the death-cycle both claim, and both, on bad days, blame.
- **She regards [[Vel'Zyren]]'s web as the greatest knot ever tied** — deliberate, load-bearing, and unmarkable, because a knot that vast is indistinguishable from structure. Her scripture's one forbidden question is whether some knots are too old to chalk. The Drow, who know exactly one thing about Omrith, know that.

### 🔹 Worship & Beliefs

- Her diviners do not tell futures; they **audit** them — a Knotfinder reading answers only *"is something wrong,"* never *"what happens,"* and costs more, because it is the only reading whose answer can be checked.
- The weaver-guilds' adopted rite: the season's first bolt of cloth is inspected thread by thread in her name, and one deliberate flaw is left unmended — **Omrith's mark** — on the folk doctrine that a cloth with a found flaw is honest, and a cloth with no flaw found is merely uninspected. *(The guilds' larger belief — that loom-work touches the true Weave — is theirs, not hers, and is exactly the intuition [[Vaelith]] will one day industrialize. Omrith's silence on the matter will be remembered, in Arc 4B, as either innocence or complicity, depending who survives to tell it.)*

> [!truth] DM-Truth
> Omrith's eyes are genuine and her ledger does not balance. A small, steady fraction of the flaws she marks are *gone before the menders arrive* — mended by no office she can identify. Her doctrine long ago invented a category for this: **"self-healed,"** filed without further inquiry, because the alternative is a mender she cannot perceive. It is the same silent signature Serelune files as her own failures and Nyzara files as returned memories — [[Aeloria]] (and, rarer and stranger, [[the Restorer]]) passing through the inspection regime of gods who cannot know the inspector is also being inspected.

## [[Almerra]], The Lady of Counted Days *(Spirit — Time as Structure, the Calendar, the Named Year)*

> *"The year was always turning. I did not make it turn. I made it* mean. *Before me, time passed. After me, time was* kept."

- **Divine Rank:** Lesser God
- **Domains:** The Calendar, Named Days, The Counted Year, Appointments and Their Keeping
- **Symbol:** A cord of 360 knots, one dyed
- **Worshiped By:** Calendar-keepers, clerks, festival-wardens, everyone who has ever needed two people in the same place on the same day
- **Allies:** **[[Nyzara]] (Memory Needs Dates), [[Kaelor]] (An Oath Needs a Deadline), [[Selmira]] (Whose Tides She Counts)**
- **Rivals:** **[[Undren]] (below — the great quarrel), [[Orannis]] (Who Insists Time Is a Wheel), [[Zephir]] (Who Misses Appointments on Principle)**

### 🔹 Role & Influence

- Almerra is, openly, **younger than the thing she governs** — her own scripture's first line is *"The year was here before me,"* and no reader in her long history has taken the confession for what it is. She is a god condensed from mortal counting: the [[Verdant Loom]] calendar needed keeping, mortals believed the keeping was holy, and the belief was answered from inside itself.
- She governs time's *grid*, not its substance — the month-names, the festival-days, the idea that a debt can come due. Remove her and time still passes; it simply stops being *anyone's fault* when you are late.
- Her borders are contested at the two threshold-hours: **Fire claims the dawn** and **Spirit claims the dusk**, and Almerra rules neither — she merely records, with visible satisfaction, that both claims require her calendar to say when dawn and dusk *are*.

### 🔹 Worship & Beliefs

- Her rite is the **knot-cord**: households keep a year-cord and untie one knot each dusk. A skipped knot is confessed at the next festival — not as sin, but as a day that must be *accounted for somewhere*.
- Clerks touch her sign before setting any date in ink, on the doctrine that an unwitnessed deadline is merely a hope.

> [!truth] DM-Truth
> Almerra is the vault's cleanest specimen of a god of a mortal error — the "numbered calendar imposed on a random process," deified. She is entirely real, entirely functional, and her domain is a fiction that works, which under worship-pressure is indistinguishable from a truth. She half-knows. It does not trouble her: in her view, the gods of stone and sea also only *keep* what they did not make. She may be the most self-aware deity in the setting, purely because her illusion is small enough to see all of.

## 🛏️ [[Dovenna]], The Vigil at the Bedside *(Spirit — Not the God of Sleep, but Its Guardian)*

> *"I do not bring sleep. Sleep comes on its own, the way night does. I am the reason you can afford it."*

- **Divine Rank:** Lesser God
- **Domains:** The Protection of Sleepers, The Warding of Nightmares, The Defenceless Hours
- **Symbol:** An open eye above a closed one
- **Worshiped By:** Parents at cradles, soldiers on watch-rotation, healers of the sleepless, anyone who has ever guarded a sleeping person
- **Allies:** **[[Telys]] (a working friction — see below), [[Restbringer]] (Whose Road Sleep Rehearses), [[Serelune]] (Who Approves of Anything Guarded)**
- **Enemies:** **Nightmare-sendings of every origin, [[Nyxaris]] (The Maddening Breath, When It Comes By Night), [[Morgathis]] (Fear Has No Business in a Sleeping House)**

### 🔹 Role & Influence

- Sleep itself has no god — it comes like weather. **Dovenna is what stands over it.** Her office is the defenceless third of every mortal life: the body that cannot flee, the mind with its doors open. She wards the sleeper from without (the knife, the fire, the thing at the window) and from within (the nightmare, which her doctrine treats as a *trespasser in Telys's country*, not a resident).
- Her friction with [[Telys]] is old and functional: he sends the dreams; she stands at the door deciding what else gets in with them. Telys holds that a hard dream can carry a needed truth. Dovenna holds that truths can wait for daylight. Both are right; sleepers benefit from the argument.
- She and [[Restbringer]] keep the quietest doctrine in Spirit: sleep is the nightly rehearsal of his road — walked a little way in, and walked back out. Her vigil is why the walk back is safe.

### 🔹 Worship & Beliefs

- Her rite is the **watching candle**: not lit for light, but lit *at* the sleeper — a small delegated vigil. Blowing out another's watching candle is a hostility everyone understands and no law needs to name.
- Watch-standers, night-nurses, and cradle-side parents are hers without ordination; her clergy say the vigil *is* the prayer, and have never asked for more.

### [[Pell]], the Hush *(attendant spirit)*
A small spirit in Dovenna's train: the settling of a household toward sleep — the last door, the banked fire, the dropped voice. Pell has no rite and needs none; children in most lands know that whispering after the candle is lit is "keeping Pell waiting," and that Pell, though endlessly patient, remembers.

## 🕯️ [[Hethra]], Keeper of the Long Evening *(minor god — Spirit)*
- **Domains:** Old Age, Elders, The Last Working Years, Dignity at the Far End
- **Symbol:** A lamp trimmed low but burning clean
- Between vitality's gods and [[Varethis]]'s cut lay an unheld span; Hethra holds it — not dying, but *lasting*: the elder's craft-knowledge, the seat by the fire that is earned rather than offered, the right to be consulted. Her rite is the **handing-down**: tools, recipes, and grudges formally passed before the end, witnessed, so nothing dies untransferred. She and [[Dovenna]] share the late watches; she and [[Undren]] share an understanding; she and [[Nyzara]] share everything else.

---

## 🖋️ [[Tamsit]], The Recording Hand *(minor god — Spirit, adjacent to Nyzara)*
- **Domains:** Writing, Records, The Written-Down, Ink's Modest Honesty
- **Symbol:** A quill laid across a closed ledger
- The positive act of writing, finally held — junior to [[Nyzara]] (memory) and opposite [[Thamur]] (erasure), with one sharp doctrine that keeps him humble and keeps him hired: **a record is not the truth; a record is a witness** — it says only *someone wrote this then*, and that, honestly kept, is worth more than truth-claims. Scribes touch his sign before signing anything; his clergy audit archives the way Omrith audits threads; and his standing, cheerful feud with [[Wendrel]] — the written version against the living one — is the whole tension of the setting's historiography conducted between two minor gods over what both insist is a small professional difference. [[Aelys]]'s Tribunal-scribe office stands above him, uninvolved; Tamsit records mortals. That is plenty.

# **🌌 The Demigods & Spirits of the Spirit Pantheon – [[The Unseen Threads]]**

> _"Fate does not move in straight lines. Magic is not without flaw. Knowledge is not always a gift. Between the threads of the Weave, in the whispers of forgotten gods and the echoes of lost names, there are those who shape the world unseen."_

Unlike **the Greater and Lesser Gods of Spirit, who shape the Weave itself**, the **Demigods and Spirits** are those who **move between its threads.** Some **carry fate to mortals**, while others **unravel it in secret.** Some **preserve lost knowledge**, while others **ensure it remains buried.**

Many of these spirits **are not widely worshiped**, but they **hold deep significance in the myths of seers, mages, and those who walk the thin line between prophecy and madness.**

---

## **🌠 [[Elthir]], The Flickering Thread** _(The Messenger of Fate, The Star That Guides and Vanishes)_

> _"Fate does not always speak in clear words. Sometimes, it flickers, uncertain. Follow it at your own risk."_

- **Domains:** Fate’s Messengers, The Unstable Path, The Weave’s Hidden Patterns
- **Symbol:** A silver thread that fades in and out of sight
- **Worshiped By:** Oracles, those who seek divine messages, lost travelers
- **Allies:** **[[Vaithis]] (Wandering Star), [[Nytheris]] (Fate-Tearer), [[Astria]] (Fate’s Law – Air)**
- **Enemies:** **[[Aelys]] (Weave-Keeper), [[Keldros]] (The Unmoving Earth), [[Astralis]] (The Silent Void)**

### **Mythology & Influence**

- **Elthir is not a god—he is a messenger, a flickering thread that appears only when fate is uncertain.**
- **He is the reason oracles sometimes see contradictory visions.**
- **Some call him a guide, others a trickster—he does not deceive, but nor does he guarantee truth.**

✅ **His presence means fate is shifting, uncertain—sometimes for the better, sometimes for the worse.**

✅ **Librarians whisper her name when they find a book missing its last page. Prophets curse her when a vision ends before its meaning is clear.**

- **Domains:** Random Fate, The Chaos of the Weave, Unpredictable Destiny
- **Symbol:** A tangled knot that can never be fully unraveled
- **Worshiped By:** Tricksters, gamblers, those who believe fate is pure chaos
- **Allies:** **[[Zephir]] (Chaos in the Air), [[Nyxaris]] (Madness in the Wind), [[Ytheis]] (The Cycle of Fate)**
- **Enemies:** **[[Vaithis]] (Fate’s Guidance), [[Astria]] (Fate’s Law), [[Aelys]] (The Weave’s Order)**

### **Mythology & Influence**

- **Thyvael is the force of pure randomness in fate—the Weave’s unexpected, unpredictable chaos.**
- **Some destinies are not designed, manipulated, or destroyed—they simply happen.**
- **Those who worship him embrace the unknown, believing that destiny is a game of chance.**

✅ **Now, fate is not just structured, rewritten, or broken—it is sometimes utterly chaotic.**

## **🌫️ [[Sytheris]], The Lingering Choice** _(The Spirit of Missed Opportunities, The Paths That Could Have Been)_ 

> _"You have chosen. But do you wonder what could have been?"_

- **Domains:** Lost Destinies, Fate’s Regrets, The Weight of What-Ifs
- **Symbol:** A branching path that twists back into itself
- **Worshiped By:** Those haunted by past choices, those who believe they were meant for another life
- **Allies:** **[[Vaithis]] (Fate’s Guidance), [[Elyon]] (Shattered Paths), [[Telys]] (Dreams That Speak of Alternate Futures)**
- **Enemies:** **[[Nytheris]] (Fate’s Destruction), [[Ytheis]] (Fates That Repeat), [[Kaelor]] (Fate’s Oaths & Commitments)**

### **Mythology & Influence**

- **Sytheris governs the roads never taken, the choices that were made, and the ones that were abandoned.**
- **She does not reveal what might have been—but she ensures that those who wonder feel the weight of their uncertainty.**
- **It is said that those who dream of another life, those who feel they were meant for something different, have been touched by Sytheris’s presence.**
- **She does not change fate, nor does she break it—she simply ensures that mortals and gods alike never forget what they did not choose.**

✅ **Some revere her, hoping to glimpse the lives they lost. Others curse her, knowing they will never escape the question: “What if?”**

## 👁️ [[Kyren]], The Seer at the Fray *(The Half-Corrupted Prophet, Sight Through the Tear)*

> *"Xelthar sees too much and it broke him. I see through the place where seeing breaks. There is a difference. I can no longer tell you what it is."*

- **Divine Rank:** Lesser God / boundary case
- **Domains:** Sight Through Frayed Threads, Prophecy at the Tear's Edge, Knowledge From Where the Weave Ends
- **Symbol:** An eye whose iris is an unravelled thread
- **Worshiped By:** The most desperate oracles; seers who have exhausted every safe question; cults that should be watched
- **Allies:** **[[Xelthar]] (The Whisper Beyond Time), [[Zeythis]] (The Fractured Glyph), [[Tyralis]] (The Spell That Should Not Exist)**
- **Enemies:** **[[Aelys]] (Who Would Mend What He Sees Through), [[Oriveth]] (Who Would Seal It), [[Nyzara]] (Who Grieves Him)**

### **Mythology & Influence**

- Every Spirit seer looks *along* the threads. **Kyren looks through the gaps where threads have frayed** — and what can be seen through a tear in the Weave is not the future. It is whatever is on the other side.
- His prophecies are unlike Xelthar's maddening truths or Aelis's shattered futures: they are **accurate about things that have no business being knowable** — the position of a sealed door, the name under Thamur's erasure, the weather in a place that does not exist yet. Accuracy is what makes his cult persist and what makes it feared.
- Spirit's faiths dispute what he *is*. His remaining priests say a god paying sight's honest price. Aelys's clergy say a wound that has learned to talk. The oldest texts say both, in the same sentence, and were sealed for it.

### **Worship & Beliefs**

- His petitioners ask one question and pay in advance: a memory, surrendered before the answer, *chosen by him*. Those who return from his shrines are missing something and can never say what — which is, his cult notes serenely, the proof of payment.
- No temple; his sites are **places where the Weave has visibly frayed** — a valley where echoes precede sounds, a ruin that casts tomorrow's shadows. His faithful find them. No one teaches how.
- Oracles of every other tradition keep one Kyren-token — an unravelled thread in a sealed vial — *never to use, only to remember that the option exists*. Owning it is discouraged. Discarding it is worse.

> [!truth] DM-Truth
> Kyren is exhibit A for the gradient ruling (Spirit cross doc §7.5): a Spirit god **partway frayed into the Eldritch**, neither member nor casualty. His sight is genuine and its mechanism is exactly what his enemies fear — the "other side" of the tears he reads through is the same outside the [[Whispering Dark]] whispers from. He is not an agent of the Eldritch. He is what a god looks like *mid-process*, halted — and no one, including Kyren, knows what halted it, or whether it has resumed. If the hard-wall ruling is taken instead, reclassify him as fully Spirit with a stolen Eldritch instrument; his entries barely change, but his DM-truth inverts.

# [[When Only Echo Remains]]

>> **Spirit is not what stays behind—it is what is woven in without consent.**

When all else fades—fire, stone, breath—Spirit endures. Not as presence, but as **imprint**.

- A child dreaming a life never lived.
- A scar that remembers who made it.
- A voice that echoes a name no one taught them.

The Spirit gods walk the world thinking they are **guides**.  
They believe themselves **shapers of death, grief, memory, and return**.

But the Weave knows better.

> Their footsteps fall where **Aeloria once wove light into silence**.  
> Their hands echo what she placed long ago.  
> Their stories are not their own—they are hers, **forgotten by all but the Pattern**.

In the end, there is no spirit untouched.  
There is no silence unthreaded.

> And no echo that does not belong to Her.
