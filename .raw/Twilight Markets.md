# THE TWILIGHT MARKETS
## Underground Commerce Across Dór-o Estel

*Year 487 of the Fourth Age*  
*Comprehensive Player & GM Reference*

---

## OVERVIEW: WHERE DARKNESS DOES BUSINESS

The **Twilight Markets** exist in the liminal spaces between surface civilization and the Hollowdeep—neither truly above nor below, operating in the threshold zones where law loses its grip and necessity finds its price. Unlike common criminal enterprises built on violence and intimidation, the Twilight Markets function as a **commerce network first**, recognizing that dead customers don't return and burned bridges don't rebuild.

### The Merchant's Creed

*"Violence is expensive. Trade is eternal."*

This philosophy separates the Twilight Markets from thieves' guilds, assassination syndicates, and revolutionary cells. The Markets are **neutral ground** where enemies can conduct business without bloodshed, where information flows more freely than wine, and where the only absolute law is that contracts must be honored.

### Historical Origins

Established approximately 200 years ago when displaced Deep Gnomes, Duergar refugees, and surface criminals realized the threshold zones between civilization and the Hollowdeep offered unique opportunities. As Drow expansion intensified—corrupting leylines, raiding surface settlements, and transforming the upper Hollowdeep into a war zone—those fleeing the violence discovered these liminal spaces offered both sanctuary and profit.

What began as refugee camps and smuggler waystations evolved into permanent infrastructure. The early traders recognized three critical facts:

1. **Drow don't trade, they raid** - No negotiation possible with the corruption spreading from below
2. **Surface authorities criminalize survival** - Refugees need illegal services to exist
3. **Someone will fill the need** - Better organized commerce than random violence

The Twilight Markets have grown into a continent-spanning network serving three essential functions:
- **Refugee Processing:** Moving people away from Drow expansion zones
- **Forbidden Commerce:** Trading goods surface authorities deem too dangerous
- **Neutral Ground:** Providing space where traditional enemies can conduct necessary business

Each major city in Elarion hosts a Market, all loosely coordinated by the **Council of Veils**—a rotating leadership ensuring no single faction dominates and the flow of commerce never stops.

---

## THE COUNCIL OF VEILS
*Nine Voices, One Market*

The Council governs through **rotating seats** and **unanimous consensus**. No single individual or faction can dominate—every major policy decision requires all nine votes. This prevents the Markets from becoming tools of any particular interest while ensuring stability through shared investment in the system's survival.

Council members are called **"Veils"** because their true influence operates behind legitimate-appearing facades. Most maintain public identities as respectable merchants, scholars, or professionals while secretly coordinating the largest black market network in the known world.

### Seat Structure

Each seat represents a major faction within the Markets:
1. Surface Fences & Legitimate Fronts
2. Hollowdeep Refugees & Underground Trade
3. Restricted Magic & Banned Research
4. Biological Goods & Medical Services
5. Transit Services & Identity Changes
6. Violent Services Liaison
7. Forgery & Documentation
8. Information Brokerage
9. Neutral Arbitration (rotating)

### 1. THE SURFACE SEAT - "The Daywalker"

**Current Holder:** Marisol Vex  
**Race/Age:** Human, 45  
**Public Identity:** Owner of "Vex & Daughters Import/Export" (legitimate shipping company)  
**Specialty:** Legitimate-business fronts, customs corruption, overland smuggling

**Background:**  
Marisol inherited a struggling merchant company from her father and discovered that mixing legal and illegal cargo was far more profitable than honest trade. Over twenty years, she's built a network of "respectable" businesses across Elarion that serve as fronts for black market operations. Her ships carry both legitimate goods and contraband; her warehouses store both taxed imports and stolen art; her customs agents accept both legal fees and generous bribes.

She believes the Markets' survival depends on appearing respectable enough that authorities consider them "manageable problems" rather than existential threats. Her faction specializes in moving illegal goods through legal channels—the art of hiding stolen paintings among legitimate shipments, smuggling banned books inside crates of legal texts, and ensuring contraband always has plausible documentation.

**Philosophy:** *"We're not criminals. We're economic necessity with bad paperwork."*

**Faction Operations:**
- 47 legitimate businesses serving as fronts across Elarion
- ~200 customs officials receiving regular "consulting fees"
- Maritime smuggling network moving goods between coastal cities
- Document specialists who can make any cargo appear legal

**Personal Motivation:**  
Marisol genuinely believes black markets serve essential functions governments refuse to acknowledge. She's seen refugees starve waiting for legal asylum, patients die waiting for approved healers, and scholars lose decades of research to bureaucratic suppression. The Markets are her answer to systemic failure.

### 2. THE DEPTHS SEAT - "The Stone-Speaker"

**Current Holder:** Grindlethock "Grindle" Deepmantle  
**Race/Age:** Deep Gnome (Svirfneblin), 187  
**Public Identity:** None (operates entirely underground)  
**Specialty:** Hollowdeep navigation, refugee warning network, fungal trade

**Background:**  
Grindle's community was destroyed by Drow expansion 60 years ago. He escaped with 200 survivors through tunnels now crawling with corruption, losing half his people to Whisper-taint before reaching the surface approaches. He's spent six decades building an early-warning network to prevent others from suffering the same fate.

He represents the non-Drow inhabitants of the Hollowdeep—primarily Deep Gnomes (Svirfneblin), Duergar, and other refugees displaced by the ever-expanding Drow empire. His faction maintains maps of safe routes through the upper Hollowdeep layers, monitors corruption zone expansion, and provides early warning of Drow raids. The Twilight Markets serve as refugee processing points for those fleeing the violence below.

Grindle despises the Drow with cold, methodical hatred. He views the Markets as critical survival infrastructure—without them, refugees would have nowhere to go, no way to acquire false papers, no system for disappearing into surface society. He's personally guided over 10,000 refugees to safety.

**Philosophy:** *"The darkness below isn't empty. It's full of those running from worse darkness."*

**Faction Operations:**
- Network of scouts monitoring Drow movements in upper Hollowdeep
- Safe-route maps updated monthly as corruption spreads
- Fungal trade agreements with the Pale Choir (fungal civilization)
- Underground rail system for rapid refugee extraction
- ~50 guides capable of navigating Threshold Reaches

**Personal Motivation:**  
Grindle believes the Markets are the only organized resistance to Drow expansion that actually works. While surface militaries debate strategy and druids perform rituals, the Markets save lives daily. Every refugee successfully relocated is a small victory against the corruption below.

**Critical Intelligence:**  
Grindle's faction has identified that Drow raids on Markets aren't random—they're systematic attempts to destroy refugee infrastructure. The Drow don't want surface-dwellers organizing; they want chaos and scattered victims.

### 3. THE ARCANE SEAT - "The Bounded"

**Current Holder:** Sylthris Cinderveil  
**Race/Age:** Half-Elf, 78  
**Public Identity:** "Retired" researcher, occasional magical consultant  
**Specialty:** Banned glyphs, dangerous artifacts, illegal magical research

**Background:**  
Sylthris was once a respected researcher at Kel Varyan's Prisada Arcana until her experiments into memory-manipulation magic crossed ethical lines. When the Crown Court of Threads ordered her research destroyed and her memory modified to forget her discoveries, she fled with her notes. She spent the next fifteen years rebuilding her research in secret and realized that institutional suppression of knowledge creates far more danger than open study.

Now she supplies mages who can't—or won't—acquire materials through official channels. Her network includes banned research, forbidden glyphs, dangerous artifacts, and experimental magical techniques. She maintains that magic should be studied, not suppressed, regardless of moral qualms. Prohibition doesn't eliminate dangerous magic; it just ensures only criminals and fanatics have access.

**Philosophy:** *"Knowledge sealed away is knowledge lost. Someone will find it eventually. Better they buy it safely than discover it by accident."*

**Faction Operations:**
- Library of 2,000+ banned texts and suppressed research papers
- Network of 30+ rogue mages conducting forbidden experiments
- Artifact acquisition teams specializing in dangerous items
- Glyph-copying service (banned glyphs reproduced for sale)
- Memory-crystal archive containing suppressed discoveries

**Personal Motivation:**  
Sylthris believes the Faith's suppression of magical knowledge (Arc 1A: The Burning Library) is creating the exact conditions for catastrophic accidents. By making banned knowledge available to responsible researchers through controlled channels, she's actually preventing disasters. She's probably right, but authorities disagree violently.

**Connection to Campaign Arcs:**
- Possesses evidence related to Arc 1A (Burning Library suppressions)
- Sells Eldritch research materials (Arc 4C: Eldritch Rewriter)
- Supplies consciousness-manipulation techniques (Arc 3A: Ascending Throne)

### 4. THE FLESH SEAT - "The Merciful"

**Current Holder:** Doctor Alain Morrell  
**Race/Age:** Human, 52  
**Public Identity:** Occasionally treats patients in poor districts (pro bono)  
**Specialty:** Monster parts, biological materials, illegal healing services

**Background:**  
Morrell was a military surgeon in Corvessa's crusades against Talanthar. Over five campaigns, he watched hundreds die from infections, blood loss, and treatable injuries because healing magic was "reserved for nobility and officers." When he began treating common soldiers with illegal techniques and unauthorized magic, he was court-martialed. He deserted rather than face execution.

He now runs an underground medical network providing care to those who can't afford legal healers or can't risk official documentation: criminals, refugees, wanted fugitives, and the desperately poor. His monster-part trade funds medical charity—he sells griffin blood to wealthy collectors to afford healing magic for starving children. He's beloved by the poor and wanted in three nations.

**Philosophy:** *"Healing is not a privilege. The law disagrees. The law is wrong."*

**Faction Operations:**
- 15 underground clinics across Elarion providing free healing
- Monster-part acquisition network supplying alchemists and mages
- Biological goods trade (organs, blood, rare specimens)
- Medical supply smuggling bypassing official channels
- ~40 healers working underground (priests who lost faith, rogue druids, unlicensed surgeons)

**Personal Motivation:**  
Morrell fundamentally believes healthcare is a right, not a commodity. Every noble who pays 1,000 gold for vampire blood funds healing potions for a dozen slum children. Every wealthy mage who purchases rare organs for research pays for surgeries for victims who can't afford temples. The system is ugly but functional.

**Ethical Complexity:**  
Morrell's faction handles some of the Markets' most disturbing trades—organs, blood, monster parts, living specimens. He maintains strict ethical standards (no murder for organs, consent required for blood donations, humane treatment of captured monsters) but the nature of the business attracts less scrupulous operators. He struggles constantly with how much moral compromise is acceptable for greater good.

### 5. THE CHAINS SEAT - "The Liberator"

**Current Holder:** Kessa Ironbreaker  
**Race/Age:** Dwarf, 86  
**Public Identity:** None (operates in deep cover)  
**Specialty:** Refugee smuggling, identity changes, sanctuary placement

**Background:**  
Kessa was born into slavery in a distant nation and escaped at age 20. She spent the next 66 years ensuring others wouldn't suffer her fate. Her network specializes in moving people who need to disappear: escaped slaves, political refugees, witnesses fleeing retaliation, criminals seeking redemption, and anyone whose life depends on becoming someone else.

She absolutely refuses to move anyone against their will. Voluntary transit only. Her network includes forgers creating new identities, memory-mages modifying memories, and safe houses across the continent. She's moved over 30,000 people to new lives—some fleeing persecution, some escaping justice, all desperate for second chances.

**Philosophy:** *"Everyone deserves a second chance. And a third. And as many as it takes."*

**Faction Operations:**
- 200+ safe houses across Elarion and beyond
- Forger network creating complete identity packages
- Memory-modification specialists (controversial but effective)
- Underground railroad moving people between cities
- Sanctuary placement service matching refugees with communities
- ~100 guides operating various escape routes

**Personal Motivation:**  
Kessa believes in redemption. She's moved serial killers trying to reform, escaped slaves seeking freedom, political dissidents fleeing execution, and criminals wanting fresh starts. She doesn't judge—everyone gets help if they're sincere about changing. Her critics say she's enabled monsters. She responds that everyone was someone's monster at some point, and refusing second chances guarantees first mistakes become permanent.

**Ethical Dilemmas:**  
Kessa's faction has moved:
- War criminals seeking to avoid prosecution
- Abusers fleeing consequences
- Dangerous individuals who may reoffend
- People whose victims deserve justice

She maintains that denying escape to some means creating a system that can be used to deny escape to all. Better to save everyone than create tests that inevitably exclude the desperate.

### 6. THE BLADE SEAT - "The Introducer"

**Current Holder:** Varius the Blind  
**Race/Age:** Human, 61  
**Public Identity:** Retired mercenary running small security consulting firm  
**Specialty:** Connecting clients with assassins, mercenaries, bodyguards

**Background:**  
Varius spent 30 years as a successful mercenary before an assassination attempt left him blind and forced into retirement. During recovery, he realized his extensive network of violent professionals was more valuable than his sword arm had ever been. He became a broker—introducing people who need violence done to people who do violence professionally.

He maintains strict neutrality: clients get referrals, professionals get vetted customers, the Market takes no responsibility for outcomes. He doesn't employ killers; he doesn't broker specific contracts; he doesn't enforce completion. He makes introductions. What happens after they shake hands isn't his concern.

Lost his eyes when he broke neutrality once—took a contract out of friendship rather than business. The target survived; Varius didn't keep his vision. He learned neutrality's value the hard way.

**Philosophy:** *"I make introductions. What happens after they shake hands isn't my concern."*

**Faction Operations:**
- Network of 200+ verified violent professionals
- Vetting service ensuring competence and reliability
- Reputation system tracking completion rates and collateral damage
- Contract mediation (disputes between clients and hired help)
- Insurance service (guaranteeing professionals will show up)

**Personal Motivation:**  
Varius believes violence is inevitable. People will always need others killed, beaten, protected, or intimidated. Pretending otherwise doesn't stop violence—it just makes it chaotic and unprofessional. By creating organized systems, he reduces collateral damage, ensures fair payment, and prevents amateurs from botching jobs and creating bigger problems.

**Critical Distinction:**  
Varius doesn't broker assassinations—he introduces assassins to clients. The actual negotiation, payment, and contract happens outside Market spaces. This legal fiction keeps the Markets technically neutral while providing essential service.

**Types of Introductions:**
- Assassins (single target elimination)
- Mercenary companies (military operations)
- Bodyguards (protection services)
- Intimidators (non-lethal coercion)
- Kidnappers (abduction services)
- Torturers (information extraction)

All verified for competence. None verified for ethics.

### 7. THE INK SEAT - "The Perfectionist"

**Race/Age:** Unknown, Unknown  
**Public Identity:** Unknown (conducts all business through intermediaries)

The Scrivener's true identity is one of the Markets' greatest mysteries. They conduct all business through intermediaries and use voice-alteration magic for rare personal meetings. Their forgeries are legendary: travel papers that pass Imperial scrutiny, noble patents accepted by heraldic colleges, property deeds registered in government archives, religious documentation blessed by unwitting priests. The Scrivener's work isn't just good—it's perfect. Their documents become real through sheer quality.

**Personal Motivation:**  
Unknown. The Scrivener might be true believer in documentary perfection, displaced bureaucrat seeking revenge, or simply someone who discovered they're extraordinarily good at something lucrative. Theories range from philosophical to mercenary.

**Types of Forgeries:**

### 8. THE WHISPER SEAT - "The Collector"

**Current Holder:** Miranna Softvoice  
**Race/Age:** Halfling, 43  
**Public Identity:** Successful tavern owner and gossip columnist  
**Specialty:** Secrets, blackmail material, intelligence networks

**Background:**  
Miranna owns six taverns across Elarion, each staffed by people who understand that information is more valuable than gold. Barkeeps, servers, cleaning staff—all feed her a constant stream of overheard conversations, drunken confessions, and careless secrets. She also maintains networks of servants in noble houses, clerks in government offices, and informants in temples.

She knows everyone's secrets because people tell her—sometimes for coin, sometimes because they need someone to know, sometimes because they don't realize they're talking. Her information network is the largest and most comprehensive in Elarion. She never uses information to destroy people—only to facilitate deals, prevent conflicts, and occasionally correct dangerous imbalances of power.

**Philosophy:** *"Secrets kept are worthless. Secrets traded are currency."*

**Faction Operations:**
- 1,000+ active informants across Elarion
- Information brokerage matching buyers with sellers
- Blackmail material archive (used for protection, not extortion)
- Intelligence analysis service (connecting disparate information)
- Counter-intelligence (identifying spies and infiltrators)

**Personal Motivation:**  
Miranna believes information wants to circulate. Hoarding secrets creates dangerous power imbalances; trading them creates equilibrium. She's prevented three wars, exposed five major corruption schemes, and blackmailed 20+ nobles into better behavior through strategic information releases. She's probably saved more lives than any warrior.

**Ethical Framework:**  
Miranna won't sell information that will cause:
- Mass civilian casualties
- Exposure of innocents
- Destruction of critical infrastructure
- Assassinations of genuinely good leaders

She will sell information that causes:
- Embarrassment to the powerful
- Disruption of corruption
- Prevention of worse crimes
- Strategic political shifts

**Information Categories:**

**Public Secrets (50-200 gp):**  
Guard schedules, patrol routes, tax collector movements

**Private Secrets (500-5,000 gp):**  
Affair scandals, hidden wealth, illegal business dealings

**Dangerous Secrets (10,000+ gp):**  
Government conspiracies, military plans, revolutionary movements

**Existential Secrets (Price Negotiable):**  
Information related to BBEG campaign arcs, apocalyptic threats, cosmic truths

### 9. THE GREY SEAT - "The Arbiter"

**Current Holder:** Rotates Every 6 Months  
**Race/Age:** Variable  
**Public Identity:** None during tenure  
**Specialty:** Dispute resolution, contract enforcement, neutrality protection

**Background:**  
The Grey Seat has no permanent holder. It rotates among trusted senior Market operatives who've proven absolute neutrality over decades of service. When selected, they temporarily abandon their faction affiliations and personal interests to serve as the Market's conscience.

The Grey Arbiter moderates Council meetings, enforces the Three Absolutes (Market law), investigates violations of neutrality, and resolves disputes between factions. They have no faction interests to promote, no profit incentive to satisfy—only the Market's survival to protect.

**Philosophy:** *"The Market survives because no one owns it."*

**Responsibilities:**
- Moderate Council meetings (enforce consensus voting)
- Investigate neutrality violations
- Resolve inter-faction disputes
- Enforce the Three Absolutes
- Maintain emergency protocols
- Coordinate responses to existential threats (Drow raids, government suppression)

**Current Arbiter:** Theron Ashmark  
*Dragonborn, 89, Former Veltharyn Enforcer*

Theron served 40 years as neutral peacekeeper between Market factions. Selected as Arbiter for this rotation due to his successful mediation of the Corvessa-Kel Varyan smuggling dispute. Known for being absolutely incorruptible and phenomenally stubborn.

**The Three Absolutes (Market Law):**

1. **NO KILLING IN MARKET SPACES**  
Violence destroys trust. Trust is profit. Anyone who draws blood in Market territory is permanently banished. Enforcement is unanimous—all nine factions hunt violators together.

2. **CONTRACTS ARE BINDING**  
The Markets function on reputation. Breaking agreements destroys the system. Proven contract-breakers are banned, and all factions spread word of their betrayal.

3. **NEUTRALITY IS SACRED**  
Personal politics stay at the door. Corvessan crusaders and Talanthar rangers can trade in Markets without attacking each other. Enemies of the state can purchase goods without fearing arrest. The moment someone uses Markets to pursue personal vendettas, everyone loses.

**Violations and Punishment:**

**Minor Violations:**
- Heated arguments, insults, minor threats
- Punishment: Warning, temporary ban (1 month)

**Major Violations:**
- Drawn weapons, assault without injury, intimidation campaigns
- Punishment: Ban from specific Market (1 year), reputation damage

**Absolute Violations:**
- Murder in Market spaces, widespread betrayal, breaking neutrality systematically
- Punishment: Permanent banishment enforced by all factions simultaneously

When someone is banished, every Council member spreads word through their networks. Within weeks, the violator is blacklisted from every black market operation on the continent. They become unpersons in the shadow economy—unable to fence goods, hire services, or conduct illegal business anywhere.

## COUNCIL OPERATIONS

### Meeting Structure

**Schedule:** First new moon of each season (4 times per year)  
**Location:** Rotates between major Market sites  
**Duration:** Typically 2-3 days  
**Attendance:** All nine seats required; decisions postponed if anyone absent

**Agenda:**
1. Threat assessment (Drow raids, government suppression, rival organizations)
2. Policy decisions (require unanimous consensus)
3. Dispute resolution
4. Faction reports (revenue, operations, challenges)
5. Strategic planning
6. New business

**Decision Process:**
- **Unanimous Consensus Required:** All nine seats must agree for major policy
- **Majority Vote:** Minor operational decisions (7/9 seats)
- **Emergency Powers:** Grey Arbiter can make temporary unilateral decisions if Council cannot meet
- **Veto Power:** Any single seat can block policy changes

This structure prevents domination while enabling necessary coordination.

### Recent Decisions (Year 487 FA)

**PASSED - Drow Response Protocol:**  
All Markets implement enhanced security measures against Hollowdeep raids. Increase tribute to Grindle's faction for expanded early-warning network. Unanimous.

**PASSED - Price Standardization:**  
Establish baseline pricing across Markets to prevent inter-city competition from destabilizing network. 8/9 (Varius abstained—doesn't sell goods).

**FAILED - Slave Trade Ban:**  
Kessa Ironbreaker proposed banning all slave trade introductions. Failed 3/6 (Kessa, Morrell, Grey Arbiter supported; others argued prohibition drives trade elsewhere without stopping it). Remains contentious.

**PASSED - Information Quarantine:**  
Miranna proposed restricting sales of information related to Whisper-That-Was revelations (Arc 2C). Agreed evidence of divine manipulation is too destabilizing for open market. 7/9 (Sylthris and Miranna opposed—information wants freedom).

**PENDING - Protection Treaty:**  
Discussion of formal protection agreement with Talanthar in exchange for sanctuary if Markets are suppressed elsewhere. Requires more negotiation.

## MAJOR MARKET LOCATIONS

The Twilight Markets operate in threshold spaces—physically between surface and Hollowdeep, legally in grey zones where multiple jurisdictions create enforcement gaps. Each major city hosts a Market, all connected by underground passages making inter-city smuggling relatively safe for those who know the routes.

### KEL VARYAN - THE DELTA EXCHANGE
*"Where Three Waters Meet, Ten Thousand Secrets Flow"*

**Location:** The Flooded Veil district, accessible through "The Forgotten Cisterns"  
**Depth:** 0.5-0.8 miles below surface  
**Population:** ~1,200 permanent residents, 3,000+ daily visitors  
**Primary Trade:** Information, magical components, memory services, Imperial forgeries

The largest and most sophisticated Market, benefiting from Kel Varyan's position as continental trade hub. If you want something and have money, the Delta Exchange can find it.

#### The Layout

**The Drowned Bazaar:**  
Main marketplace occupies a series of partially flooded chambers where merchant stalls sit on floating platforms connected by rope bridges. Water level fluctuates with tides, creating constantly shifting geography that frustrates raiders and tax collectors. Bioluminescent fungi provide dim, blue-green lighting. Acoustic properties make conversations difficult to overhear—sound reflects off water in confusing patterns.

The Bazaar contains ~200 permanent stalls selling everything from banned books to vampire blood to forged noble patents. Vendors rent space monthly; waiting list for prime locations is years long. Most vendors are specialists—you don't sell potions and poisons from the same stall.

**The Three Sisters Vault:**  
Named for Kel Varyan's three converging rivers, this high-security storage facility offers:
- Secure deposit boxes (magical and mundane security)
- Magical stasis chambers (preserving perishable goods)
- Memory-crystal vaults (protected by psychic wards)
- Document storage (fireproof, waterproof, theft-proof)

Clients include noble houses storing embarrassing evidence, mages safeguarding dangerous research, and criminals hiding stolen goods. Access requires three separate keys held by three different Council representatives—unanimous consent needed to open any vault.

**Rental:** 50 gp/month (small box) to 5,000 gp/month (private vault chamber)

**The Scrivener's Workshop:**  
The Ink Seat's primary forgery operation occupies an entire wing. Twenty master forgers work constantly, producing documents so perfect they're accepted by Imperial bureaucracy without question. Security is extreme—only pre-approved clients are allowed entry, and all work areas are warded against scrying.

Services include:
- Travel papers (100-300 gp)
- Noble patents (2,000-5,000 gp)
- Property deeds (1,500-4,000 gp)
- Magical licenses (1,000-3,000 gp)
- Religious documentation (2,500-8,000 gp)
- Threadbearer credentials (15,000+ gp—extremely rare)

**The Grey Archives:**  
Miranna Softvoice's intelligence headquarters. Two hundred filing cabinets contain information on everyone who matters in Kel Varyan and most people who matter elsewhere. Clients can purchase information ranging from guard rotation schedules to noble house secrets to government conspiracy evidence.

**Pricing Scale:**
- Routine information (guard schedules, public secrets): 50-200 gp
- Sensitive information (affairs, illegal dealings): 500-2,000 gp
- Dangerous secrets (military plans, conspiracies): 5,000-20,000 gp
- Existential information (BBEG plans, cosmic threats): Price negotiable

Payment can be gold or favors. Favors are often more valuable.

**The Memory Market:**  
Legal in Kel Varyan but heavily regulated; black market operations offer unrestricted service. Memory traders can:
- Extract memories (800 gp)
- Implant memories (1,200 gp)
- Modify memories (1,500-5,000 gp depending on complexity)
- Erase memories (1,000 gp)
- Copy memories to crystals (500 gp)

Extremely controversial. Used for witness protection, trauma treatment, evidence suppression, and identity changes. Also used for darker purposes—implanting false memories, erasing inconvenient truths, or stealing information directly from minds.

**The Banned Library:**  
Sylthris Cinderveil maintains a secondary archive here containing:
- Copies of suppressed magical research
- Banned glyph documentation
- Forbidden theoretical texts
- Evidence of magical suppressions (related to Arc 1A: Burning Library)

Access by appointment only. Security includes:
- Memory wards preventing memorization of sensitive material
- Glyph locks requiring specific magical signatures
- Guardian constructs activated by theft attempts
- Kill-switches that destroy specific texts if removal is forced

**Reading fee:** 100-1,000 gp depending on text sensitivity  
**Copy purchase:** 500-10,000 gp depending on danger level

#### Specialty Services

**Threadbearer Contact Service:**  
Discrete communication with Kel Varyan's surviving Threadbearer (name withheld). Messages delivered through intermediaries. Used for:
- Clarification of Thread-system status
- Prophecy interpretation
- Water Thread magical consultations
- Information about other Threadbearers

**Service fee:** 500 gp per message (no guarantee of response)

**Imperial Bureaucracy Navigation:**  
Specialists who know how to navigate Kel Varyan's Byzantine government. Services include:
- Expedited permit approvals
- Legal loophole consultation
- Bribery coordination
- "Losing" inconvenient paperwork

**Fee:** 200-2,000 gp depending on complexity

**Delta Navigation:**  
The Convergence Delta is 3,000+ square miles of channels and islands. Getting lost is easy; drowning is common. Market guides offer:
- Safe passage through dangerous sections
- Secret routes avoiding authorities
- Underwater passage navigation (requires water-breathing magic)
- Island hideout rentals

**Guide fee:** 50 gp/day

#### Access Points

**PUBLIC - The Forgotten Cisterns:**  
Ancient water management infrastructure beneath Old Imperial District. Officially abandoned after flooding incident 80 years ago; actually maintained as primary Market entrance.

**Location:** Accessible through several legitimate buildings (taverns, warehouses, private homes) whose owners receive payments for looking the other way

**Entry Requirements:**
- Know correct knock sequence (changes monthly)
- Password (provided by Market contacts or informants)
- Not obviously being followed by authorities

Once inside cisterns, follow markings (subtle chalk signs visible only with darkvision or specific magical light) to reach the Drowned Bazaar. Journey takes 20-40 minutes through flooded tunnels. Water-breathing magic recommended but not required—air pockets exist throughout.

**VIP - Private Water-Gates:**  
High-value clients receive invitations to use private entrances from the Delta's countless waterways. Small boats collect pre-arranged customers from docks, transport them through the delta, and deliver them to hidden Market entrances. Completely discrete, no passwords required.

**Cost:** 100 gp per use (invitation only)

**EMERGENCY - Scholar's Descent:**  
Connection to Hollowdeep tunnels via the Grand Library's underground archives. Extremely dangerous—route passes near the Scribe Warrens (Arc 1A: Burning Library) where Velshara's agents operate. Only used when surface routes are compromised.

#### Security

**The Tide Guard:**  
~50 permanent enforcers ensuring Market law is followed. Ethnically diverse (humans, halflings, dwarves, half-elves), experienced fighters, absolutely loyal to Market neutrality rather than any Council faction.

**Commander:** Saren Tidecaller (half-elf, former Imperial Marine)

**Equipment:**
- Waterproof armor and weapons
- Tridents and nets (non-lethal control in flooded spaces)
- Underwater-breathing enchantments
- Truth-detection glyphs (identifying undercover authorities)

**Flood Defense System:**  
The Drowned Bazaar can be flooded completely within two minutes using emergency locks. This destroys merchandise but prevents raids—authorities must either drown or retreat. System has been triggered three times in Market history; each time successfully prevented capture.

**Memory Wards:**  
Glyphs throughout the market prevent:

## FINDING AND USING THE MARKETS

### First-Time Access

**METHOD 1 - Investigation:**
Intelligence ([[Streetwise]]) check:  
**DC = 15 + settlement size modifier**
- Hamlet/Thorp: +0
- Village: +2
- Town: +4
- City: +6
- Metropolis: +8

**Success:** Discover Market entrance and current password  
**Failure:** Cannot search again for 1d4 weeks + [[Wanted Score]]

**Consequences of Failure:**
- May attract attention from law enforcement (10% chance)
- Might encounter fake "markets" run by con artists (20% chance)
- Could stumble into rival criminal operations (15% chance)

---

**METHOD 2 - Criminal Contact:**
Characters with existing underworld connections can request introduction:
- **Background Feature** (Criminal, Charlatan, etc.): Automatic
- **Class Feature** (Rogue's Criminal Contact): Automatic
- **Charisma Check:** DC 15 Persuasion or [[Streetwise]]

**Success:** Proper introduction with vouching  
**Failure:** Refused (can try different contact)

---

**METHOD 3 - Reputation-Based Approach:**
Characters with [[Wanted Score]] 3+ may be approached by Market agents seeking to offer services. This is double-edged:
- **Advantage:** Easy access without searching
- **Disadvantage:** Increased risk of attracting unwanted attention

Markets actively recruit high-profile criminals because they spend money freely and need specialized services.

---

**METHOD 4 - Referral (Best Method):**
Existing customer vouches for character, providing:
- Current password
- Exact location of entrance
- Introduction to specific vendors
- Baseline trust

This is safest and most reliable method. Markets operate on trust networks—referrals bypass skepticism.

---

**DISCOVERY BY AUTHORITIES:**

Base chance per visit: **5%**

**Modifiers:**
- +5% per [[Wanted Score]] point
- +10% if character caused trouble in previous visit
- +10% if purchasing goods connected to recent high-profile crime
- -10% if character has political protection
- -20% if law enforcement is paid off (varies by city)
- -10% if using proper security protocols (disguises, countersurveillance)

**Consequences if Discovered:**
- Authorities raid Market within 2d6 days
- Character must flee immediately (combat or escape)
- [[Wanted Score]] increases by 1-2 points
- [[Black Market Reputation]] decreases (associated with disaster)
- May be banned from Market temporarily

**BETRAYAL:**

**Vendor Betrayal:**
- Vendors with Suspicious or worse [[Disposition]] may inform on customers (20% chance)
- Motivation: Reward money, coercion by authorities, personal grudges
- Warning signs: Nervous behavior, asking too many questions, insisting on specific meeting locations

**Customer Betrayal:**
- Rival criminals may ambush valuable customers (10% chance for high-value transactions)
- Motivation: Steal purchased goods, eliminate competition, ransom wealthy customers

**Informant Betrayal:**
- Undercover authorities posing as customers (5% chance in any transaction)
- More common in cities with active suppression campaigns

**Prevention:**
- Trust only vouched contacts
- Use [[Streetwise]] or Insight checks to detect deception (DC 15)
- Bring backup for large transactions
- Avoid discussing plans openly in Market spaces

---

**Defective Items:**
- 10% chance magical items are defective or cursed
- 15% chance "rare" components are common forgeries
- 20% chance information is outdated or deliberately false
- 25% chance biological goods are diseased or unstable

**Detection:**
- Arcana check DC 15 to identify magical defects
- Nature check DC 15 to verify biological quality
- Investigation check DC 15 to confirm information accuracy
- Insight check DC 15 to detect deliberate fraud

**Recourse:**
If defective goods are proven, Markets typically:
- Refund 50% of purchase price
- Ban vendor responsible
- Spread word of fraud through network

But proving defects can be difficult and dangerous.

**DROW THREAT:**

All Markets maintain constant vigilance against Drow raids from below.

**Warning Signs:**
- Increased Whisper-taint detection
- Fungal network reports unusual activity
- Refugees fleeing corruption zones
- Deep scouts disappearing

**Response Protocols:**
1. **Yellow Alert:** Increased patrols, restricted deep passage access
2. **Orange Alert:** Evacuation plans activated, security doubled
3. **Red Alert:** Immediate evacuation, emergency protocols

**If Raid Occurs:**
- Markets prioritize evacuating customers and refugees
- Valuable goods moved to secure locations
- Passages collapsed behind retreating defenders
- Council coordinates counter-attack if possible

Players caught in Market during Drow raid face combat encounter or desperate escape through collapsing passages.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

### Low-Level (Levels 1-4)

**"The First Transaction"**

**Setup:** Party needs restricted item for legitimate quest (healing potion for poisoned NPC, banned book containing key information, etc.). Normal channels fail—temples refuse service, libraries don't have the text, merchants can't legally sell it.

Someone suggests "asking around in the right places."

**Development:** Party investigates Markets, gains access, makes simple purchase. Transaction proceeds normally until law enforcement raids Market mid-deal. Party must choose:
- Fight authorities (increases [[Wanted Score]] dramatically)
- Flee with purchased item (moderate [[Wanted Score]] increase)
- Abandon item and escape (minimal consequences)
- Talk their way out (very difficult but possible)

**Complications:**
- Purchased item is actually stolen from important NPC
- Authority leading raid is sympathetic and offers deal
- Other customers assume party are undercover agents (violence)
- Market vendor had ulterior motive for helping party

**Resolution:** Party gains Market access but learns cost of illegal commerce. Future dealings become option for desperate times.

---

**"Message Running"**

**Setup:** Market contact offers decent payment (200 gp) to deliver sealed package to another city. Simple courier job, shouldn't attract attention, just needs discrete carrier.

**Truth:** Package contains stolen noble house documentation—evidence of corruption that multiple factions desperately want.

**Development:** During journey, party is ambushed by:
- Noble house agents trying to recover documents
- Rival criminals trying to steal valuable intel
- Authorities who've been tracking package

Party must decide:
- Complete delivery (get paid, but becomes entangled)
- Return package to noble house (possible reward or betrayal)
- Open package and read contents (information is dangerous)
- Destroy package (makes enemies of everyone)

**Complications:**
- Documents prove noble corruption but also reveal Market operations
- Multiple recipients claim to be rightful deliverv target
- Package is trapped with memory-extraction glyph
- Opening package marks reader with magical trace

**Resolution:** No perfect solution—every choice has consequences. Introduction to moral complexity of Market dealings.

---

**"The Fake Fence"**

**Setup:** Party encounters "black market" dealer offering incredible prices on magical items, stolen goods, and forbidden services. Seems too good to be true.

**Truth:** Undercover city guard running sting operation to catch criminals. "Dealer" is actually Guard Captain Theron Vastris's counterpart in different city—less corrupt, more zealous.

**Development:** If party makes purchase, they're arrested immediately. Must either:
- Escape custody (combat encounter, chase scene)
- Convince authorities they were investigating criminals (Deception DC 18 or Persuasion DC 20)
- Accept arrest and face trial (different adventure arc)
- Have connections intervene (costs favors, reputation)

**Complications:**
- Some prisoners are actual criminals who assume party are snitches
- Corrupt guard offers to release party for bribe (10x purchase price)
- Real Market agents are in prison—might help or hinder
- Trial reveals party's past deeds to public

**Resolution:** Teaches party to verify Market authenticity before conducting business. Introduction to [[Wanted Score]] system and legal consequences.

---

**"Refuge Request"**

**Setup:** Party encounters refugee fleeing persecution—escaped slave, religious heretic, deserter, or victim of false accusations. Desperate, terrified, begging for help.

Refugee asks for introduction to "people who can help them disappear."

**Development:** Party must decide:
- Help (requires Market introduction, costs money, increases [[Wanted Score]])
- Refuse (refugee faces capture, torture, possible death)
- Turn in refugee (reward from authorities, moral cost)
- Find alternative solution (creative problem-solving)

**Complications:**
- Refugee is actually criminal who deserves prosecution
- Refugee is victim but pursuing enemies are sympathetic
- Helping creates obligation to resist larger systemic injustice
- Refugee's family is still in danger

**Resolution:** Introduction to Kessa Ironbreaker's refugee network. Party learns Markets serve legitimate humanitarian functions alongside criminal operations. Forces moral complexity—helping refugees means supporting illegal infrastructure.

---

### Mid-Level (Levels 5-10)

**"The Drow Incursion"**

**Setup:** Market contacts hire party (5,000 gp) to investigate Hollowdeep passages beneath city. Drow raids are increasing in frequency and sophistication—Markets need intelligence on what's happening below.

**Discovery:** Drow are specifically targeting Markets to disrupt refugee networks. They're not random raids—they're systematic campaign to prevent organized resistance to their expansion.

**Development:** Party discovers Drow are:
- Mapping Market infrastructure
- Identifying key Council members
- Preparing coordinated multi-city assault
- Corrupting critical Hollowdeep passages

Party must choose:
- Warn Markets (saves lives, makes permanent enemies of Drow)
- Stay neutral (allows raids to continue, preserves deniability)
- Betray Markets to Drow (substantial reward, immense moral cost)
- Find diplomatic solution (nearly impossible but creative)

**Complications:**
- Some Council members want to negotiate with Drow
- Others want preemptive strike
- Surface authorities see this as criminals killing criminals
- Refugees will die if Markets fall

**Resolution:** Large-scale combat encounter defending Markets from Drow raid, OR negotiating impossible alliance between criminals and authorities, OR accepting that sometimes good people must work with bad systems to fight worse threats.

---

**"The Council Seat"**

**Setup:** One Council member has been murdered, breaking Market neutrality. Victim: Miranna Softvoice (Whisper Seat), found dead in her Grey Archives surrounded by scattered files.

Markets are paralyzed—all nine seats must vote for decisions, but one seat is empty. Council hires party (10,000 gp) to investigate quickly before system collapses.

**Discovery:** Miranna was secretly working with Kel Varyan authorities to expose certain Market operations (weapon smuggling, assassination brokerage) while protecting others (refugee processing, medical services).

**Killer:** One of three possible suspects depending on DM choice:
1. **The Scrivener** (protecting forgery operations)
2. **Varius the Blind** (protecting assassination network)
3. **Marisol Vex** (protecting weapon smuggling)

Each has legitimate reason and is simultaneously hero and villain depending on perspective.

**Development:** Investigation reveals:
- Miranna wasn't betraying Markets—she was trying to reform them
- She believed exposing worst operations would save Markets from complete suppression
- Killer acted to protect criminal infrastructure
- Both had valid points

Party must decide:
- Expose killer (destroys Council unity, might collapse Markets)
- Cover up murder (allows killer to escape justice)
- Reveal Miranna's plan (splits Council, causes civil war)
- Find compromise (incredibly difficult)

**Resolution:** No clean answer. Revealing truth might destroy organization that saves thousands of refugees annually. Covering it up means accepting murder of person trying to do right thing. Perfect moral complexity.

---

**"The Forbidden Archive"**

**Setup:** Party discovers Markets possess suppressed evidence related to major campaign arc:
- Arc 1A (Burning Library): Lists of destroyed books, names of executed scholars
- Arc 2C (Drow Revelator): Pre-Compact documents proving divine manipulation
- Arc 3A (Ascending Throne): Techniques for soul manipulation
- Arc 5A (Faithful Inquisitor): Evidence of Purity Cells torture

Information is revolutionary but purchasing it makes party targets.

**Price:** 20,000-50,000 gp depending on evidence type, OR major favor owed to Markets

**Development:** If party purchases:
- Information is accurate and devastating
- Multiple factions learn party possesses it
- Suppression attempts begin immediately
- Party must decide what to do with knowledge

**Pursuers Include:**
- Government agents trying to re-suppress evidence
- Faith Inquisitors eliminating witnesses
- BBEG agents preventing revelation
- Other factions wanting to control information

**Complications:**
- Evidence is incomplete—needs verification
- Multiple contradictory versions exist
- Revealing it might cause social collapse
- Keeping it secret perpetuates injustice

**Resolution:** Party must navigate between truth and stability, revelation and responsibility. Information wants to be free, but consequences are severe. Introduction to major campaign arc through criminal networks.

---

**"The False Identity Collapse"**

**Setup:** The Scrivener's forgery operation has been compromised—several customers are using "different" false identities that actually share same base documentation template.

Authorities discovered this and are systematically arresting everyone using that template. Race against time to warn all affected customers before capture.

**Affected Customers Include:**
- Innocent refugees fleeing persecution (deserve warning)
- Escaped prisoners who should be in jail (complicated)
- Undercover agents for various causes (political complexity)
- War criminals seeking escape (deserve capture)

Party has 48 hours to find and warn ~20 people scattered across city.

**Complications:**
- Some customers don't trust warnings (suspicious of trap)
- Authorities are tracking party's movements
- Rival criminals are hunting same people
- Each customer presents moral dilemma

**Development:** Party must decide for each customer:
- Warn them (save life, risk sheltering criminals)
- Don't warn (allow capture, accept moral cost)
- Warn selectively (impossible to know who deserves saving)
- Warn authorities of worst criminals (betrays others)

**Resolution:** Timed challenge with multiple moral checkpoints. Party saves some, loses others, must live with choices. No way to save everyone. Some rescued people are heroes; some are monsters. Perfect is enemy of good.

---

### High-Level (Levels 11-16+)

**"The Market War"**

**Setup:** Rival criminal organization attempts to destroy Twilight Markets through systematic betrayals, assassinations, and exposure. New organization: **The Guild of Chains**—violent, hierarchical, brutal.

They want to control all illegal commerce through fear rather than cooperation.

**Development:** Over several sessions:
- Council members are assassinated
- Markets are raided
- Customers are betrayed
- Infrastructure is destroyed

Guild offers ultimatum: surrender and serve, or die.

**Party Must Choose:**
1. **Defend Markets** - Fight Guild in criminal gang war
2. **Allow Collapse** - Watch Markets fall, eliminating useful but illegal infrastructure
3. **Broker Peace** - Nearly impossible negotiation between incompatible systems
4. **Replace Markets** - Create new system learning from both approaches

**Complications:**
- Surface authorities see this as criminals killing criminals (don't intervene)
- Some Market operations deserve destruction
- Guild is genuinely worse than Markets in every way
- Refugees will suffer most if Markets fall

**Resolution:** Large-scale faction warfare. Party coordinates defense across multiple cities, OR accepts that sometimes systems must fall and be rebuilt better, OR brokers impossible peace between cooperation and domination philosophies.

**Outcome:** Markets survive but changed, OR new organization emerges, OR Guild wins and everything becomes worse.

---

**"The Council Betrayal"**

**Setup:** One Council member is revealed to be working for major BBEG. Specific Council member depends on campaign arc:
- **Arc 1A:** The Scrivener (forging evidence for Velshara)
- **Arc 3A:** Doctor Morrell (providing subjects for Therion)
- **Arc 5A:** Brother Matthias (secretly still loyal to Faith)
- **Arc 6B:** Sylthris Cinderveil (supplying Malketh)

**Discovery:** Evidence emerges slowly—contradictions in decisions, unexplained intelligence leaks, suspicious timing.

**Party Must Decide:**
1. **Expose Immediately** - Destroys Council unity, stops BBEG support
2. **Use as Intelligence Source** - Monitor betrayer, learn BBEG plans
3. **Turn the Betrayer** - Attempt to flip them against BBEG
4. **Replace Quietly** - Assassinate and replace without causing panic

**Complications:**
- Betrayer is actually doing good work alongside bad
- Other Council members split on response
- Exposure might collapse entire Market system
- BBEG has leverage over betrayer (family hostage, magical compulsion)
- Betrayer genuinely believes they're helping

**Resolution:** Spy thriller investigating Council member while maintaining normal operations. Moral complexity: betrayer is simultaneously traitor and victim, criminal and hero depending on perspective. Resolution affects both Market stability and BBEG campaign arc.

---

**"The Drow Compromise"**

**Setup:** Drow offer Markets a deal through intermediary:
- Stop warning refugees about corruption zones
- Allow "certain goods" (kidnapped victims) to flow through Markets
- Provide intelligence on surface military operations

**In Exchange:**
- Drow stop raiding Market locations
- Trade access to deep Hollowdeep resources
- Protection from surface authority suppression

Council is split 4-4 with Grey Arbiter unable to decide. Party's recommendation will tip decision.

**Arguments FOR:**
- Saves Markets from destruction
- Allows refugee processing to continue
- Provides resources impossible to obtain otherwise
- Prevents immediate humanitarian crisis

**Arguments AGAINST:**
- Betrays victims being delivered to corruption
- Enables Drow expansion
- Compromises Market neutrality permanently
- Makes Markets complicit in atrocities

**Development:** Party investigates both sides, meets victims, witnesses corruption zones, studies strategic situation.

**Complications:**
- Time pressure (Drow demand answer in days)
- Some Council members threatening to split if vote goes wrong way
- Surface authorities preparing suppression campaign
- BBEG may be manipulating situation

**Resolution:** Party makes recommendation before full Council. Vote occurs. Either:
- Deal accepted: Markets survive but compromised
- Deal rejected: Markets remain neutral but face destruction
- Alternative found: Creative solution (nearly impossible)

**Consequences last for rest of campaign.** Major moral choice with no right answer.

---

**"The Imperial Reckoning"**

**Setup:** Kel Varyan Empire decides Markets have become too powerful and launches coordinated suppression across multiple cities simultaneously.

**Reason:** New Imperial Chancellor believes organized criminal infrastructure threatens state authority and must be eliminated.

**Development:** Empire strikes at:
- Delta Exchange (Kel Varyan)
- Penitent Exchange (Corvessa—with Faith cooperation)
- Ember Exchange (Veltharyn—with Forge-Council cooperation)
- Multiple minor locations

Simultaneous raids with military-level force.

**Party Must Choose:**
1. **Help Markets Resist** - Become wanted by Empire, defend criminal infrastructure
2. **Help Authorities** - Destroy useful resource, make criminal enemies, uphold law
3. **Evacuate Refugees** - Save innocents while allowing combat
4. **Stay Neutral** - Impossible—both sides demand choice

**Complications:**
- Markets contain both heroes and monsters
- Empire is legally right but morally complex
- Refugees will suffer regardless of outcome
- Some Markets deserve destruction
- Others provide essential services
- No clean separation between good and bad operations

**Resolution:** Multi-session military campaign across multiple cities. Large-scale combat, political maneuvering, moral complexity.

**Outcomes:**
- **Markets Win:** Empire withdraws, Markets more powerful but more visible
- **Empire Wins:** Markets destroyed, refugees scatter, underground becomes chaotic
- **Compromise:** Official Markets dissolved, underground networks continue informally
- **Pyrrhic Victory:** Both sides damaged, neither wins cleanly

**Campaign Impact:** Fundamental change to continental criminal infrastructure. Party's choice determines future of illegal commerce across Elarion.

## SYSTEM INTEGRATION

### With [[Wanted Score]] System

The Markets and [[Wanted Score]] are deeply interconnected:

**Market Access:**
- Higher [[Wanted Score]] makes Market access more difficult
- But Markets also provide services to reduce [[Wanted Score]]
- Creates feedback loop: crimes create need for Markets, Markets enable more crimes

**Price Scaling:**
- [[Wanted Score]] increases prices (risk premium)
- But high [[Wanted Score]] criminals need services desperately
- Markets profit from desperation while providing essential services

**Identity Services:**
Complete identity changes can effectively reset [[Wanted Score]] to 0:
- New documentation (legal identity)
- Memory modification (feel like new person)
- Sanctuary placement (new location)
- Physical alterations (appearance changes)

**Cost:** 5,000-8,000 gp + time investment + psychological adjustment

**Note:** Old crimes still occurred, just attributed to "different person." Witnesses can still recognize character if disguise fails.

---

### With [[Black Market Reputation]]

**Separate System** from [[Wanted Score]]:
- Can be notorious criminal (Wanted 6) with excellent Market reputation (+3)
- Can be respected citizen (Wanted 0) with terrible Market reputation (-2) if you betrayed them once

**Reputation Earned Through:**
- Honest dealings (completing contracts, paying debts)
- Protecting Market secrecy (not revealing locations/operations)
- Respecting neutrality (not using Markets to pursue personal vendettas)
- Performing favors for vendors
- Demonstrating reliability over time

**Reputation Lost Through:**
- Breaking contracts (even when legally justifiable)
- Scamming contacts (even when they deserve it)
- Bringing law enforcement (even accidentally)
- Betraying Market agents (even under duress)
- Violence in Market spaces (even in self-defense)

**Regional Variation:**
- Reputation is regional unless character achieves mythic status
- Can be respected in Kel Varyan (+2), banned in Corvessa (-2)
- Actions in one city don't immediately affect other cities
- But Council members communicate—major betrayals spread quickly

---

### With [[Disposition]] System

Markets provide clear framework for [[Disposition]]:

**Initial Disposition based on:**
1. [[Black Market Reputation]] (primary factor)
2. [[Wanted Score]] (secondary—too high creates wariness)
3. Referral quality (vouching matters immensely)
4. Character race/culture (some vendors have prejudices)

**Disposition Shifts:**
- Good: Fair negotiations, respectful behavior, honest dealing
- Bad: Aggressive haggling, disrespectful treatment, suspicious questions

**Special Cases:**
- **Hostile in Markets:** Rare because security removes hostile parties
- **Helpful in Markets:** Grants access to exclusive services, rare goods, protection

---

### With [[Hideouts]] System

Markets and [[Hideouts]] are complementary:

**Markets Provide:**
- Secure storage services (Three Sisters Vault in Kel Varyan)
- Dead-drop locations
- Supply sources for hideout equipment
- Contractor connections (guards, staff, specialists)

**Hideouts Serve Markets:**
- Safe houses for Market agents
- Storage for contraband
- Meeting locations for sensitive deals
- Emergency boltholes during raids

**Integrated Services:**
Some Markets ARE hideouts—dual-purpose spaces where vendors live and work. Others rent hideout space to customers (200-1,000 gp/month depending on quality).

**Warning Networks:**
Markets' information networks alert hideout owners of:
- Incoming raids (usually 2d6 days warning)
- Betrayals by staff
- Authority interest in locations
- Competitor surveillance

---

### With [[Corruption]] System

Markets have complex relationship with [[Corruption]]:

**High Corruption (CS 15+) Creates Problems:**
- Automatic Suspicious or Hostile disposition
- Many vendors refuse service (fear of taint)
- Prices increase dramatically (+50-100%)
- Limited access to biological/magical goods
- Expelled from Markets if visible corruption appears

**But Some Vendors Specialize in Corruption:**
- Doctor Morrell offers corruption-cleansing (1,500 gp, reduces CS by 1d4)
- Sylthris Cinderveil sells corruption-related research
- Whisperleaf studies Whisper-taint samples
- Ash-Memory Vaults contain corrupted artifacts

**Warning Systems:**
Markets maintain corruption detection:
- Drow-taint detection glyphs (300 gp)
- Regular screening of deep passages
- Immediate quarantine of corrupted individuals
- Connection to Grindle's early-warning network

**Ethical Complexity:**
Markets profit from corruption (selling cleansing services, tainted artifacts) while also fighting it (warning systems, refugee processing). As always, Markets embody contradictions.

---

### With [[Reputation]]/[[Fame]] Systems

Fame complicates Market access:

**Positive Fame:**
- Famous heroes may be refused service (too much attention)
- Or welcomed enthusiastically (some vendors admire heroism)
- Prices may increase (wealthy heroes expected to pay premium)
- Special requests (heroes asked to perform services for Markets)

**Negative Fame (Infamy):**
- Infamous villains receive priority treatment (respect for capability)
- Some vendors refuse service (don't want association)
- Prices vary wildly (some vendors charge premium, others give discounts to curry favor)
- Recruitment attempts (criminal factions want famous criminals)

**Regional Variation:**
- Hero in Corvessa might be criminal in Talanthar
- Markets treat each region separately
- Reputation in one area doesn't automatically transfer

---

### With [[Sanity]] System

Low [[Sanity]] affects Market interactions:

**Visible Symptoms (Sanity Score < 50):**
- Trigger Suspicious disposition automatically
- Vendors worry about unstable customers
- May be denied service (dangerous to serve)
- Prices increase (risk premium)

**Specific Trauma Effects:**
- **Paranoia:** Vendors seem threatening (false Hostile disposition)
- **Hallucinations:** See threats that aren't there (security incidents)
- **Dissociation:** Forget Market protocols (security breaches)
- **Compulsions:** Unwanted purchases, revealing secrets

**Market Response:**
- Doctor Morrell offers therapy services (500-2,000 gp)
- Some vendors exploit mentally ill customers
- Markets may ban extremely unstable individuals (danger to operations)

**Religious Cities (Corvessa, Thar Vornhal):**
Visible sanity deterioration seen as spiritual corruption—triggers immediate Hostile disposition or expulsion.

---

### With [[Focus]] System

[[Focus]] modulates market interactions:

**High Focus Benefits:**
- Advantage on negotiation checks
- Resistance to vendor manipulation
- Notice deception attempts automatically
- Navigate complex deals successfully

**Low Focus Problems:**
- Disadvantage on all market interactions
- Miss obvious red flags
- Make poor decisions under pressure
- Reveal too much information

**Specific Applications:**
- Maintaining disguise during transactions
- Resisting truth-detection glyphs
- Detecting undercover authorities
- Complex multi-party negotiations

---

### With [[Streetwise]] Skill

[[Streetwise]] is THE market skill:

**Finding Markets:**
- Primary skill for investigation checks
- Replaces general Investigation in urban settings

**Negotiation:**
- Can use Streetwise instead of Persuasion for price haggling
- Advantage when using Streetwise in criminal contexts

**Information Gathering:**
- Identify trustworthy vendors
- Recognize undercover authorities
- Assess market mood and risks
- Predict market responses

**Navigation:**
- Find best routes to markets
- Avoid surveillance
- Mask reputation temporarily
- Blend into criminal culture

---

### With [[Memory]] System

[[Memory]] affects market operations:

**Password Recall:**
- Markets change passwords monthly
- Memory check DC 12 to remember current password
- Failure means seeking new informant

**Vendor Recognition:**
- Remember past dealings (positive and negative)
- Recall who can be trusted
- Remember prices and negotiations

**Intelligence Use:**
- Purchased information must be remembered
- Memory modification affects what customers know
- Lost memories might contain crucial market contact information

**Memory-Altered Customers:**
- Some refugees have modified memories
- Former identity erased partially or completely
- May not remember crimes that warranted [[Wanted Score]]
- Creates interesting situations where records show crimes character doesn't remember committing

## DM GUIDANCE

### Running Market Scenes

**Atmosphere:**
Markets should feel simultaneously **dangerous and safe**:
- Violence is forbidden, but tension is constant
- Everyone is armed but nobody draws weapons
- Conversations are guarded but business flows smoothly
- Illegal commerce in polite environment

**Sensory Details:**

**Kel Varyan (Drowned Bazaar):**
- Sound of lapping water echoing off stone
- Bioluminescent fungi casting blue-green light
- Smell of salt, damp, and ancient stone
- Temperature cool but not cold
- Vendors speaking in hushed tones that echo strangely

**Corvessa (Heretic's Market):**
- Religious iconography surrounding illegal commerce (irony)
- Eternal flame glyphs casting flickering shadows
- Smell of old stone, candle wax, incense
- Acoustics designed for sermons creating strange echoes
- Atmosphere of reverent transgression

**Talanthar (Rootmarket):**
- Bioluminescent mushrooms providing soft illumination
- Thick spore-laden air (protective masks necessary)
- Living wood walls that occasionally pulse or shift
- Fungal music (low vibrations felt more than heard)
- Alien beauty of underground forest

**Veltharyn (Scorched Bazaar):**
- Intense heat radiating from walls
- Red-orange glow from visible magma flows
- Smell of sulfur, heated metal, volcanic rock
- Cooling glyphs creating temperature differentials
- Constant awareness of volcanic danger

---

### NPC Characterization

**Avoid "Evil Merchant" Stereotypes:**
- Vendors should have legitimate grievances
- Most are desperate people making difficult choices
- Many are heroes in their own stories
- Complexity over caricature

**Vendor Motivations:**

**Kessa Ironbreaker (Chains Seat):**
- Not profiting from refugee trade
- Genuinely believes in second chances
- Costs barely cover operation
- Will die protecting refugees

**Doctor Morrell (Flesh Seat):**
- Uses monster-part profits to fund free healing
- Violates medical ethics to save lives
- Every wealthy customer funds a dozen poor patients
- Genuinely believes healthcare is a right

**The Scrivener (Ink Seat):**
- Unknown motivation
- Perfect work suggests genuine pride in craft
- Or sociopathic perfectionism
- Or philosophical statement about reality

**Brother Matthias (Corvessa):**
- Traumatized by witnessing suppression
- Operating from genuine conviction
- Still spiritual despite lost faith
- Believes he's serving higher truth

---

### Moral Complexity

**Present Genuine Dilemmas:**

**Question 1: Is it ethical to buy healing services that save lives but fund criminal enterprises?**
- Markets enable healing for those who can't afford temples
- But also enable assassination, slavery, and suppression
- Supporting good operations requires supporting bad ones
- No way to separate them

**Question 2: Should you report slave traders even if it destroys refugee network?**
- Slave trade is objectively evil
- But Markets move 30,000+ refugees annually
- Destroying Markets means condemning refugees
- Tolerating Markets means enabling slavery

**Question 3: Do you purchase suppressed evidence even knowing it makes you targets?**
- Information proves government/Faith wrongdoing
- But possessing it endangers everyone you know
- Revealing it might cause social collapse
- Keeping it secret perpetuates injustice

**Question 4: Is protecting Market neutrality worth defending criminals?**
- Neutrality enables refugee processing and healing
- But also enables assassination and torture
- Perfect is enemy of good
- But accepting evil for good results corrupts everything

**No Right Answers:**
These questions should genuinely divide party. Players should argue. Characters should disagree. That's the point.

---

### Consequences

**Market Interactions Should Have Lasting Effects:**

**Purchases:**
- Create paper trails
- Establish obligations
- Make enemies
- Connect characters to criminal networks

**Vendors:**
- Remember how they were treated
- Hold grudges for years
- Reward loyalty equally
- Spread word through networks

**Information:**
- Purchased today becomes relevant months later
- Creates unexpected connections
- Opens new plot threads
- Changes how characters see world

**Enemies:**
- Made in Markets are dangerous
- Connected to vast criminal networks
- Patient and resourceful
- Strike when least expected

---

### Balancing Access

**Too Easy:**
If players can purchase anything instantly, tensions dissolve. **Make them earn it:**
- Build trust over multiple sessions
- Complete jobs for Market contacts
- Accumulate reputation points
- Accept that some items simply aren't available yet
- Price gates on most dangerous goods

**Too Hard:**
If Markets are too difficult to access, players may ignore them entirely. **Ensure:**
- Clear introduction opportunities in early game
- At least one sympathetic Market contact
- Prices that make sense for character level
- Options beyond just buying items (information, services, connections)
- Rewards for creative use of Market resources

**Right Balance:**
Markets should feel like resources that require investment to utilize fully:
- Early access: limited, expensive, risky
- Mid-level: reliable, reasonable prices, known vendors
- High-level: priority service, rare goods, protection

Trust is currency as much as gold.

---

### Drow Threat Integration

**Ongoing Background Threat:**

Drow raids should be constant pressure:
- Markets losing refugees and goods
- Hollowdeep passages becoming more dangerous
- Insurance costs rising (reflected in prices)
- Security increased (slows transactions)
- Evacuation drills (players experience tension)

**Escalation:**

**Early Campaign:** Occasional raids, minor damage
**Mid Campaign:** Systematic targeting, major losses
**Late Campaign:** Coordinated assault, existential threat

**Player Involvement:**

- Hired for security during high-risk periods
- Escort refugees through dangerous passages
- Scout corruption zone boundaries
- Defend Markets during raids
- Investigate Drow intelligence network

**Connection to Arc 2C (Drow Revelator):**

Markets provide unique intelligence about Drow operations:
- Refugee testimony about corruption zones
- Deep Gnome scouts mapping Drow expansion
- Captured Drow equipment and documents
- Pattern analysis of raid targets

This makes Markets valuable intelligence asset for players pursuing Drow-related campaign arcs.

- Refugees seeking escape from truth
- Criminals wanting reality modification
- People desperate for different existence

**How Malketh Recruits:**
- Offers "freedom from cosmic law"
- Targets Market customers overwhelmed by knowledge
- Provides escape through Glyph deletion
- Creates Liberation Colonies as alternative to Markets

**Market Response:**
- Some Council members intrigued
- Others horrified
- Debate about whether to ban Malketh access
- Refugees fleeing both Drow and Malketh

## FINAL NOTES

### The Markets Are Mirrors

The Twilight Markets represent **necessary evil**—infrastructure that enables both harm and help, crime and survival. They exist because:

**Prohibition Creates Black Markets:**
- Banning goods doesn't eliminate demand
- Only changes who profits
- Creates violence and chaos
- Better organized crime than random crime

**Markets Serve Unmet Needs:**
- Refugees need sanctuary
- Poor need healing
- Scholars need forbidden knowledge
- Criminals need services (for better or worse)

**Governments Create the Need:**
- Unjust laws create refugees
- Restricted healing creates underground medicine
- Suppression creates information black markets
- Persecution creates smuggling networks

### Players Should Feel Uncomfortable

**Using Markets Should Raise Questions:**
- Am I funding atrocities?
- Do my purchases enable evil?
- Is convenience worth moral compromise?
- Can I accept good coming from bad systems?

But also:
- Would these refugees die without Markets?
- Does prohibition actually work?
- Is perfect the enemy of good?
- Can I change system from inside?

### Markets Are Not Villains

They're not heroes either. They're **systems reflecting civilization's failures and necessities.**

**They Save Lives:**
- 30,000+ refugees relocated annually
- Free healing for tens of thousands
- Sanctuary for persecuted
- Truth preserved when governments suppress it

**They Enable Evil:**
- Assassination brokerage
- Slave trade facilitation
- Suppression technology
- Criminal infrastructure

**Both Are True Simultaneously:**

That's the point. The Markets hold mirror to society asking: *"If we weren't failing so many people, would criminal infrastructure be necessary?"*

The answer is uncomfortable.

### Use Them Wisely

The Twilight Markets are tools for:
- Moral complexity
- Ethical dilemmas
- Social commentary
- Character development
- Plot connections

They work best when players genuinely struggle with whether to use them. They work worst when treated as simple "evil merchants" or "convenient criminal service."

**Final Question for Players:**

*"If the Markets were destroyed tomorrow, would more people suffer or fewer?"*

There's no right answer. That's why the Markets exist.
