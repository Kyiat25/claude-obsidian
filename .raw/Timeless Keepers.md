# **Who Are the Timeless Keepers?**

The **Timeless Keepers** are enigmatic beings that exist **outside of the normal flow of time and fate**. Unlike gods, who demand worship, or cosmic entities, who manipulate mortals for their own designs, the Keepers **do not act unless absolutely necessary**.

They are the **last line of defence against reality's unravelling**. They do not interfere with small changes, nor do they care for the lives of individuals. But when someone **pushes fate too far**, bending the Whisper Engine beyond its limits, the Keepers arrive—not as executioners, but as **correctors**.

- Some scholars believe the **Timeless Keepers were created at the dawn of existence**, tasked with maintaining the balance of fate.
- Others whisper that they are **all that remains of those who were erased by the Whispering Dark**, doomed to ensure others do not share their fate.
- The Silent Archive keeps **records of their appearances**, but **never explanations**. No one has ever claimed to fully understand them.

### **Signs of a Timeless Keeper’s Presence**

When a **Timeless Keeper draws near**, the world begins to act **strangely**:
- **Time loses meaning.** Fires flicker too fast or too slow, conversations repeat themselves, and **people recall events that never happened**.
- **Shadows move unnaturally.** A person may turn a corner and see **themselves** walking past in the opposite direction.
- **The [[Silent Archive]] dispatches a Whisperer.** If a **Whisperer** is nearby, they will suddenly **stop recording and remain motionless**, as if awaiting judgment.
- **Clocks fail.** Any **timekeeping device shatters, malfunctions, or stops entirely**.
- **Reflections cease to match reality.** A mirror may show **an older version of a person** or **a future that should not be possible**.

Those **who recognize these signs** may still have **a chance to correct their path** before the Keeper arrives.

## **The Appearance of a Timeless Keeper**

No two Keepers appear the same.
- Some are **humanoid**, cloaked figures with **empty voids where faces should be**.
- Others manifest as **a presence in the air**, a distortion in reality that **warps the light around them**.
- A few have been described as **shifting between forms** as if they exist in multiple states at once.
- They **do not speak** with words—but rather through **thought, memory, and implication**.

When a **Timeless Keeper appears**, the world around them **pauses**. Conversations slow, movement becomes **distant and muffled**, and **the weight of inevitability presses upon all present**.

## **When Does a Timeless Keeper Intervene?**

### **They do not appear for minor infractions.**

They only act when a character has **pushed fate beyond its natural boundaries**, drawing [[The Whispering Dark]] too close.

|**Action Taken**|**Timeless Keeper’s Response**|
|---|---|
|**Ignoring multiple Soft & Hard Warnings**|The **Silent Archive’s Whisperers stop recording**.|
|**Using multiple Commanded Whispers in one session**|**Subtle anomalies begin appearing** (voices from nowhere, wrong memories).|
|**Reality Contradictions (Undoing death, rewriting history, etc.)**|A **Timeless Keeper manifests nearby**, observing but not yet intervening.|
|**Using a Forbidden Whisper**|The **Keeper fully manifests**, offering one final bargain before erasure.|

### **Their arrival is not immediate.**

They do not **hunt** people; they simply **arrive** when the threshold is crossed. The longer one **resists the signs**, the more severe the consequences.

# **The Bargain of the Timeless Keepers**

**If a Timeless Keeper speaks with you, you have one last chance to remain.**

[[The Whispering Dark]] _seeks_ to **erase those who disrupt fate**, but the Keepers do not erase—they **balance**. They offer a choice:

### **The Three Bargains of a Timeless Keeper**

1. **"Be Forgotten."**
    - **Your presence is allowed to remain, but you will never be remembered.**
    - No one will recall your name. Any written record of you **fades**.
    - Allies will trust you, but will **never know why**.
    
2. **"Lose What Was Never Meant to Be."**
    - **You may remain, but something must be taken in return.**
    - The **Keeper chooses** an aspect of your existence to **remove permanently** (a memory, a power, a skill, or even an NPC close to you).
    - It is **not an exchange**—it is a **correction**.
    
3. **"Offer Another in Your Place."**
    - **Reality must still take someone.**
    - The player **chooses another being** to be **erased in their place** (an NPC, an ally, or even a stranger).
    - This sacrifice **is not remembered**—the person simply ceases to exist.

**If a player refuses all three bargains, the Keeper does nothing. Because there is nothing left to do.**

The character is **unmade**, and the universe continues **without them**.

### **The Timeless Keepers & The Whispering Dark**
Many believe that [[The Whispering Dark]] is an **uncontrollable force of reality**, while the **Timeless Keepers are the only ones capable of resisting it**.

The **Silent Archive** suggests that the Keepers **may even be what remains of those who have been erased**, now acting as **the last barrier between existence and oblivion**.

**A theory from the Forbidden Codices of the Archive states:**
- The **Whispering Dark** is **not sentient**—it is simply a void, an inevitable force.
- The **Keepers exist as the final witnesses**, ensuring that **those who disappear do not go unnoticed**.
- They are not **gods**, nor are they **enemies**. They are **what remains when fate collapses**.

The **[[Silent Archive]]** suggests that the Keepers may even be **what remains of those who have been erased, now acting as the last barrier between existence and oblivion.**

_"The Whispering Dark is not sentient—it is simply a void, an inevitable force. The Keepers exist as the final witnesses, ensuring that those who disappear do not go unnoticed. They are not gods, nor are they enemies. They are what remains when fate collapses."_

### **The Keepers as the Last Witnesses of the Unwritten**

The **Timeless Keepers** are not **heroes, villains, or divine judges**.

They are **what remains when all else has faded**.
- If you see them, **you have bent fate too far.**
- If they speak, **you have one last chance.**
- If they leave without you, **you were never here.**

**Will you accept your fate, or will you bargain for one last chance to remain?**
