#  Glyph of Endings - The Maw of the Whispering Dark
> _"The first star was swallowed, and so began the cycle of loss."_  
> — Whispered in silence beneath the last eclipse

---
## 🧱 Layer

- **Layer:** Primal  
- **Tier Rank:** 1  
- **Domain(s):** Entropy, Unmaking, Oblivion, Return  
- **Opposing Glyph(s):** [[Glyph of Will]]  
- **Symbol (optional):** A black spiral within a hollow circle)

---

## 🔷 Core Meaning

The **Glyph of Endings** is the certainty that **nothing is eternal**. It is **the entropy beneath existence**, the force that ensures **all things created must one day return to formlessness**.

It is not a punishment, nor a judgment. It is the **balance to the Glyph of Will**, the unmaking that allows new creation to emerge.

- **Mountains crumble because of it.**
- **Stars burn out because of it.**
- **Gods fall because of it.**

This glyph is not active destruction—it is **inevitable conclusion**.

### **The Endless Hunger**  
  
The **Glyph of Endings does not destroy out of cruelty**—it does so **because it must.**  
  
- **Fire must consume, or it will smother.**  
- **Lands must erode, or they will remain barren.**  
- **Life must end, or nothing new can begin.**  
  
It does not hate. It does not choose. **It merely waits.**  
  
For all things are already claimed by the Maw—**it is only a question of when.**

🧩 **GM Tip:**  
The first sign of the Glyph of Endings may be **a name lost from memory**, even among close allies.  
Next, **light fades faster**. Then, **echoes cease**. Finally, **a player's thread cannot be seen in Loom-chambers.**

---

## 🌀 Narrative Role

- It is whispered in **funeral rites**, etched into **eroded ley-anchors**, and feared in **prophetic endings**.
- The **Whispering Dark** does not control this glyph—he is merely its **final servant**.
- **Corrupted cults** view it as the **true shape of divinity**: the hunger beneath all things.

This glyph’s presence heralds the **collapse of cities**, the **final echo of gods**, and the **return of Threads to silence**.

---

## ✨ Magical Influence

- **Necromancy**, **Void Magic**, and high-tier **Conjuration** rituals that erase or consume rely on its residue.
- Forbidden spells such as *[[Devour Thread]]*, *[[Last Ember]]*, or *[[Collapse Anchor]]* draw upon this glyph.
- It is present in **leyline decay zones**, areas where magic dies or refuses to spark.
- Threadbearers sense its presence as a **sudden silence in the Weave**.

---
## ⚠️ Eldritch Corruption

- When corrupted, the Glyph of Endings births **anti-patterns**: **Silence Pools**, **Woven Nulls**, or **Memory-Scars**.
- These sites **consume knowledge, light, or identity**.
- Cults aligned to **Nhal’Zereth** and the **Whispering Dark** seek to amplify it into a **Total Glyph Collapse**.
- **Ashkarra’s final moment** was not fire—it was the echo of this glyph overriding its Anchor.

### 🦴 **Corrupted Entities & Echoforms**

When the Glyph of Endings manifests through corrupted matter or memory, it does not create—it subtracts. The following are rare and dreadful echoforms shaped by its unraveling influence:

- **Echo-Wanes** — Half-there beings that erase names and memories as they pass. Even gods forget they exist after seeing them.
- **Unspooled Ones** — Once-mortal creatures whose Threads have been severed. They move like shadows dragged backward through time.
- **Nullborne Ash** — Residue from spellcasters overwhelmed by the glyph; no remains, no names, just the cold stain of entropy.

These entities are **not summoned**—they **occur**.

## 🏛️ Cultural or Religious Significance

### 🏛️ [[Whisper-Cult of Caldrith’s Fold]] 

In a ruined coastal village erased from most records, a threadless people known only in forbidden annals as the **Whisper-Cult of Caldrith’s Fold** honored the Glyph of Endings with **stone prayers**—unspoken, unwritten. Their high rite involved **watching the moon fall below the horizon in silence**, never recording who was present. They vanished during the Second Cycle. Their village leaves no ghosts.

### 🌓 **Divine Reverberations (Forgotten and Denied)**

Though no god claims the Glyph of Endings, its **silhouette appears in silence** behind even the greatest divine works.

- The **fires of Astralor** cool before it.
- The **laws of Gaeldros** bend around its presence.
- The **echoes of Aeloria** stop when it watches.
- **[[Tharamis]]** refuses to forge with materials taken from Ending-touched zones.
- **[[Ignara]]** teaches that flame can purify—but never undo—the mark of Endings.
- **[[Vaelora]]** (Water) leaves a bowl of still water in every temple, untouched, to honour “that which even tide cannot carry.”

Some say this glyph is not just the end of creation, but the **death of memory itself**—a force so old that even the Primal Ones flinch before naming it.

## 🏛️ Places of Worship & Legacy
Though no people knowingly revere the Glyph of Endings, its **echoes shape forgotten temples, funerary rites, and misremembered shrines** across Dór-o Estel. These places are **not holy**, but are **sacred in silence**, where reverence is offered **without knowledge**.

---
### 🕯️ **[[The Whisperfold Caverns]]** _(Western Tidespine)_

An underground complex where monks vow silence for life. They chant nothing, record nothing, and weave no text—only observing the erosion of time on walls, bones, and memory. They believe it brings clarity, not realising that **the glyph devours their names**.

> _“Those who enter with burdens forget them. Those who stay forget themselves.”_

---
### 🔲 [[The Chapel of the Black Thread]] _(Korram Dells)_

A roadside ruin kept immaculate by unknown hands. A single black thread runs from altar to ceiling. Those who sleep within awaken **refreshed, but missing**—a memory, a scar, a piece of themselves gone. Locals believe it’s a place of healing. It is not.

> _“It takes one pain to spare you three.”_ — Inscribed on the lintel

---
### ⛓️ **[[The Chain of 1000 Names]]** _(Ashkarra’s Outer Ring)_

Along the broken outskirts of [[Ashkarra]], rusted links stretch across a blasted causeway. Each was once engraved with a name. None remain legible. Pilgrims tie new links onto it, unaware they are **feeding the glyph**, one name at a time.

> _“To be forgotten is to be freed.”_ — Common saying among the Ashborn

---
### ⛩️ **[[The Shrine Without Return]]** _(Veiled Isle of Tarnosh)_

A coastal monument said to bless those who vanish. It marks where ships depart on one-way pilgrimages. No ship has returned. Families leave offerings, believing the shrine grants a better afterlife. In truth, it is a **mouth**, and it is always hungry.

> _“No footprints ever return from the tide’s edge.”_

## ⚙️ Fate & Glyphic Mechanics

- The Glyph of Endings **erodes magic** in active zones. Spells fizzle, enchantments fade early, and magical items lose attunement over time.
- When a Threadbearer enters a zone marked by Endings, **their Thread flickers or goes dark**—they may temporarily lose Weave access.
- **Glyphs of Endings cannot be cast or bound**—they are *revealed*, always at a cost. Casting a spell tied to Endings may:
  - Erase a memory
  - Shorten a lifespan
  - Create a Woven Null nearby
- Forbidden casting rituals (like *Collapse Anchor*) **risk anti-pattern collapse**, pulling the caster into historical voids.

> _“Where this glyph is seen, the Weave is not.”_
