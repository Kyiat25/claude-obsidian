 # Dwarf
## Lineage

> *"What is built must last. What lasts must be true."*  
> — The First Line of the Deep Law

You are dwarven. Not merely born, but **built**—forged from bedrock and pressure, tempered in the deep fires, carved with purpose into the bones of the world. Your ancestors did not kneel before gods but stood beside the First Builders, divine craftsmen who shaped reality itself. Now you carry that legacy in your blood, your bones, your unbreakable will to **endure**.

While others see stone as obstacle or resource, you understand it as **memory**. Every tunnel remembers who carved it. Every hall knows the names of those who built it. And when you place your hand on worked stone, you can feel the echoes of hands that shaped it centuries before yours.

---

## The Deep Law

According to the **Stone-Song Codex**, dwarves were not sculpted—they were **constructed**. The Forgebound took endurance from bedrock, will from molten veins, and strength from buried ore. When the first dwarves stood, they asked not "Why?" but "**What must be done?**"

This pragmatic foundation shapes everything about your people. Your philosophy is codified in the **Deep Law**—not mere legal codes, but the underlying truths of reality carved into stone, metal, and blood:

- **Stone Oath**: Endure what cannot be changed
- **Forge Oath**: Shape what lies beneath your hand
- **Clan Oath**: Stand with blood, or be forgotten
- **Silence Oath**: Do not speak of what must remain buried
- **Echo Oath**: Sing what was lost, even when none listen
- **Final Oath**: Return to stone with your name unbroken

You honor the Forgebound—divine artisans who built the world's foundations—but you don't worship them. You **acknowledge** their work and continue it. Gods may forget you, but **the mountains will remember**.

---

## Life Among the Stone

You were raised knowing that **deeds outlast words**, that quality matters more than speed, that what you build today must serve your great-grandchildren. Your lifespan—typically 350 to 450 years—gives you patience that shorter-lived races mistake for stubbornness. You've simply learned that things done correctly take time.

**Craft (vrokh)** is not merely work. It is:
- How you prove your worth to your clan
- How you honor those who taught you
- How you ensure your name survives your body
- How you serve the pattern the Forgebound began

Every masterwork is signed with your maker's mark. Every tool is maintained. Every oath is recorded in stone. Your culture doesn't build monuments to memory—**everything you build IS a monument**.

Your people measure time in **Stone-Cycles**—periods of roughly 100 years marked by significant geological or metallurgical events. You count not just in years but in **generations of work**, measuring worth not in what you've accomplished but in what **endures after you're gone**.

---

## Physical Characteristics

**Age:** You mature slowly, reaching physical adulthood by age 50 but not considered truly mature until 100—when you've lived long enough to understand patience. Few dwarves die of accident or violence—most work until age claims them, passing peacefully knowing their works endure.

**Size:** You stand between 4 to 5 feet tall, but built dense and powerful—150 to 250 pounds of compact muscle and thick bone. Your size is Medium, though your center of gravity is lower than most.

**Speed:** Your base walking speed is 25 feet. Your stride is measured but tireless. Where others sprint and rest, you march.

**Build:** Broad-shouldered, thick-limbed, built low to the ground. Your frame is optimized for stability—for working stone, carrying loads through cramped tunnels, standing firm when others are pushed back.

## Dwarf Traits

As a dwarf, you possess the following lineage traits:

### **Darkvision**
You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

Your people were made for the deep places where light does not reach.

### **Dwarven Resilience**
You have advantage on saving throws against poison, and you have resistance against poison damage.

Generations of working with toxic minerals, breathing forge fumes, and drinking questionable ale have made your constitution legendary.

> *"Dwarven medicine is measured in hammer-swings and whiskey. If it doesn't kill you, you're fine."*

### **Dwarven Endurance**
Your sturdy build provides inherent protection. Your base **Damage Reduction (DR)** is increased by **+1 for all body locations** (Torso, Head, Arms, Legs). This stacks with armor and class features.

Additionally, you have advantage on saving throws against being knocked prone or moved against your will. Once you plant your feet, **stone itself couldn't budge you**.

### **Stonecunning**
Whenever you make an Intelligence (History) check related to the origin of stonework, you are considered proficient in the History skill and add double your proficiency bonus to the check, instead of your normal proficiency bonus.

This represents your deep, almost mystical connection to worked stone—you can **read** it the way others read books.

### **Tool Proficiency**
You gain proficiency with one set of artisan's tools of your choice: smith's tools, brewer's supplies, mason's tools, or jeweler's tools.

Your people's reputation for craftsmanship isn't legendary by accident.

### **Languages**
You speak, read, and write Common and Dwarvish.

Dwarvish is full of hard consonants and guttural sounds, well-suited to echo through stone halls. Its runic script is angular and precise, designed to be carved rather than written—every letter a chisel-stroke, every word permanent.

## Mental Attributes & Psychology

**Focus Modifier:** +0  
**Sanity Modifier:** +1

Your biology grants you **exceptional mental resilience**. While your Focus is standard (dwarves can be distracted like anyone), your Sanity receives a bonus because dwarven biology—centuries of selective breeding for stubbornness and willpower—has made you naturally resistant to despair, doubt, and psychological manipulation.

You are taught to finish what you start, to stand firm when others break, to trust deeds over words. This isn't just cultural—it's bred into your very bones through generations of crafters who couldn't afford mental weakness when working with forces that could kill them.

### **Cultural Mental Conditioning**
Beyond your biological advantage, dwarven upbringing creates:

- **Unwavering focus:** You complete projects once begun, no matter the obstacles
- **Pragmatic outlook:** Problems are solvable; panic is wasteful
- **Long memory:** You remember slights and favors across decades
- **Clan pressure:** Representing your bloodline creates powerful motivation

### **Corruption Vulnerability: The Tainted Foundation**

As a dwarf, you have a unique relationship with Corruption. Your connection to stone and the Deep Law provides protection against mundane corruption, but corruption that enters through **geological sources**—deep earth, corrupted leylines, things sealed beneath mountains—affects you catastrophically.

**Corruption Resistance:** You have advantage on saving throws against gaining Corruption from **mundane** sources (cursed items, dark rituals performed by mortals).

**Corruption Vulnerability:** You have disadvantage on saves against Corruption from **geological** sources—Eldritch energies emerging from deep earth, corrupted leylines, or things sealed beneath mountains.

**When you gain Corruption Score 10+ (Tier 2), you suffer:**
- Stone-Deafness: Stonecunning no longer functions properly
- Foundation Crack: Your base DR from Dwarven Endurance is reduced by 1
- Oath-Weight: Keeping oaths and promises requires Wisdom saving throws

**Fractured Foundation Table (Corruption Tier 2+)**

When you complete a long rest at Corruption Score 10+, roll 1d6:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **False Memory**: The stone whispers lies about its history. You gain false knowledge about stonework or clan history. Make a DC 15 Wisdom save or believe it. |
| 2 | **Ancestral Static**: You dream of disappointed ancestors. You cannot benefit from Dwarven Resilience until your next long rest. |
| 3 | **Foundation Tremor**: Your connection to earth destabilizes. Take 1d4 psychic damage and have disadvantage on saves against being knocked prone until your next rest. |
| 4 | **Weight of Failure**: You feel crushing shame for works you haven't failed. Make a DC 12 Sanity save or lose 1d4 Sanity. |
| 5 | **Eldritch Resonance**: Something beneath the earth tries to reach you. Gain 1 Whisper Point but your next use of Stonecunning reveals disturbing information. |
| 6 | **Deep Law Holds**: Your ancestral connection to stone resists corruption this night. Reduce your Corruption Score by 1 (minimum 10). |

**When you reach Corruption Tier 3 (CS 15+):**
Your connection to the Deep Law begins to fray. Stone no longer speaks clearly to you. Your people will know something is wrong—they may not name it, but your presence feels **wrong** in ways dwarves instinctively recognize.

## Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Integration

### **Evasion Profile: Armored Fortress**
- **Dodge Evasion:** -1 (stocky build and dense frame reduce agility)
- **Endure Evasion:** +1 (dense bones and muscle withstand impacts)
- **Deflect Evasion:** +0 (standard defensive capability)

**Tactical Note:** Your **biology trades mobility for durability**. Your dense, compact frame is harder to maneuver quickly but absorbs impacts better. Your true defense comes from +2 Natural DR (thick skin, dense bone structure) rather than evasion. You don't dodge—you stand firm and let your body handle the impact.

### **Natural Damage Reduction:** +2
Your thick skin, dense bones, and stocky build provide substantial natural protection. This is biological, not armor—it stacks with any armor worn. Dwarves are simply **harder to injure** at the cellular level.

### **Combat Philosophy**
Dwarves fight with **methodical brutality**. You don't dance around enemies—you **stand your ground** and outlast them. High Endure Evasion + natural DR + advantage against forced movement makes you the ultimate immovable object. Enemies can't push you, can't knock you down, and slowly realize they can't **break** you.

### **Breaking Point Mechanics**
Due to your biological stubbornness and cultural conditioning, you have **advantage on Rally checks** and enemy forces must achieve higher casualty percentages to trigger your Breaking Point. Routing a dwarf formation is nearly impossible.

## Subraces

Dwarven biology varies significantly based on where your ancestors adapted to survive. Choose one of the following subraces:

---

### Hill Dwarf (Hearthborn)

> *"Before the hammer, before the fire, there was warmth. A house without memory is already ash."*

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Wisdom score increases by 1.

You come from surface-adjacent holds where family, memory, and community matter more than depths or fortifications. Hill dwarves are keepers of lore, brewers of finest ales, maintainers of ancestral records, and holders of the **Hearth-Stone Verses**. You understand that the greatest endurance is found not in stone alone, but in **kinship**.

Where Mountain Dwarves perfect craft and Dust Dwarves explore horizons, you perfect **continuity**. Your hold might not have the deepest mines or finest metalwork, but your genealogies go back further, your festivals are louder, and your children are raised knowing every ancestor's name.

**Dwarven Toughness:** Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and it increases by 1 again whenever you gain a level.

Your hardy constitution is legendary even among dwarves. You don't just endure—you **thrive**.

**Memory of Stone and Kin:** You have advantage on History checks related to stonework, clan genealogy, or dwarven traditions.

You can accurately recall details about bloodlines, oaths, and historical events going back generations. Your mind is a living archive.

**Narrative Anchor Affinity:** When you use a Narrative Anchor (see Unified Trauma System), you may heal an additional 1 Sanity on a successful save.

Your deep connection to family and memory strengthens your mind against horror. You remember who you are, who you come from, and that anchors you against the void.

**DCS Integration:**
- **Evasion Modifier:** Standard Hill Dwarf profile (Dodge -1, Endure +2, Deflect +0)
- **Combat Style:** Defensive tank who protects allies through sheer survivability
- **Momentum Generation:** Steady builder—each hit you take and survive generates Momentum for comebacks

---

### Mountain Dwarf (Forgeborn)

> *"The chisel must cut true. The line must be level. The ore must be pure."*

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 2.

You hail from the greatest forge-cities and deepest mine-holds where perfection is not a goal but a **requirement**. Mountain dwarves are master crafters, rigorous judges, and keepers of the **Iron-Vow Fragment**. You see weakness in the forge as failure in the field—and you tolerate neither.

Where Hill Dwarves value community and Dust Dwarves value discovery, you value **precision**. Every line must be true. Every measurement exact. Every flaw eliminated. This philosophy extends from your metalwork to your morality—you hold yourself and others to unforgiving standards.

**Dwarven Armor Training:** You have proficiency with light and medium armor.

Your body was shaped by working metal and stone, making armor feel like a second skin.

**Forge Resilience:** You have resistance to fire damage.

Centuries of your ancestors working forges have bred heat resistance into your bloodline. You can work comfortably in temperatures that would kill others.

**Master Craftsman:** When you make a check using artisan's tools you're proficient with, you add double your proficiency bonus.

Your people don't just make things—they make them **perfectly**.

**DCS Integration:**
- **Evasion Modifier:** Standard Mountain Dwarf profile (Dodge -1, Endure +2, Deflect +0)
- **Combat Style:** Heavy frontline fighter with superior armor and strength
- **Momentum Generation:** Aggressive builder—each successful hit generates Momentum for power attacks
- **Armor Synergy:** Your proficiencies + natural DR + Endure Evasion make you the tankiest standard race option

---

### Dust Dwarf (Wanderborn)

> *"The greatest forge is the horizon. The deepest mine is uncertainty."*

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Constitution score increases by 1, and your Wisdom score increases by 1.

You come from holds that sent explorers into deserts, wastelands, and frontiers—or you were born to wandering clans who follow mineral veins wherever they lead. Dust Dwarves are guides, desert survivors, and seekers of **new stone** rather than perfecting old traditions.

Where other dwarves dig deeper, you **spread wider**. You understand that the Deep Law applies even under open sky, that stone exists everywhere if you know how to read the land, that endurance means adapting rather than just resisting.

**Wasteland Resilience:** You have advantage on saving throws against exhaustion.

Your people learned to survive where water is scarce and shelter rare.

**Desert Navigator:** You have advantage on Wisdom (Survival) checks in arid, desert, or badlands environments.

The same skills that let you read stone strata apply to reading the land itself.

**Sunwalker:** Unlike most dwarves, you have no sensitivity to bright light and treat sunlight as comfortable illumination.

Your ancestors adapted to surface living. Some traditional holds view this as weakness; you understand it as **evolution**.

**DCS Integration:**
- **Evasion Modifier:** Adjusted profile (Dodge +0, Endure +1, Deflect +0)
- **Combat Style:** Mobile skirmisher who outlasts enemies through endurance
- **Momentum Generation:** Adaptive fighter—generates Momentum through clever positioning
- **Unique Role:** The dwarf who can keep pace with lighter races during long marches

---

### Duergar (Forgeless)

> *"They sealed the vaults to protect themselves. We stayed to guard what should not wake."*

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 1.

You come from holds that remained behind when the **Silence Chain** was enacted—dwarves who voluntarily sealed themselves in corrupted depths to serve as Living Locks against Eldritch forces. Generations in darkness, isolation, and constant vigilance have changed you.

Duergar are grim, practical, and unsentimentally loyal. Where other dwarves build for beauty or tradition, you build **to contain**. You understand that some stones should never be carved, some veins never mined, some doors never opened.

**Superior Darkvision:** Your darkvision has a radius of 120 feet.

Generations in absolute darkness have adapted your eyes to near-perfect night vision.

**Duergar Resilience:** You have advantage on saving throws against illusions and against being charmed or paralyzed.

Constant exposure to Eldritch influence has hardened your mind against manipulation.

**Duergar Magic:** When you reach 3rd level, you can cast the *enlarge/reduce* spell on yourself once with this trait, using only the spell's enlarge option. When you reach 5th level, you can cast the *invisibility* spell on yourself once with this trait. You don't need material components for either spell, and you can't cast them while you're in direct sunlight, although sunlight has no effect on them once cast. You regain the ability to cast these spells with this trait when you finish a long rest. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for these spells.

These are not gifts—they are **adaptations** your people developed to survive impossible conditions.

**Sunlight Sensitivity:** You have disadvantage on attack rolls and on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight when you, the target of your attack, or whatever you are trying to perceive is in direct sunlight.

This is the cost of Superior Darkvision—your eyes no longer tolerate brightness.

**DCS Integration:**
- **Evasion Modifier:** Standard profile (Dodge -1, Endure +2, Deflect +0)
- **Combat Style:** Ambush predator using magic for tactical advantage
- **Momentum Generation:** Burst fighter—generates large Momentum spikes through enlarge/reduce combos
- **Unique Role:** The dwarf built for dungeon-crawling and underground warfare
- **Corruption Interaction:** Duergar have advantage on saves against Corruption from **Eldritch** sources but disadvantage against corruption from **divine** sources (prolonged isolation has severed some spiritual connections)

---

### Forge-Cursed Dwarf (Flameheart)

> *"The fire wanted in. So we let it. Now it serves us—or we serve it. We're still deciding."*

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Constitution score increases by 2.

You descend from dwarves who worked too close to elemental fire for too many generations—or who made pacts with forge-spirits to survive impossible conditions. Now the flame lives **inside** you, burning in your veins, warming your bones, threatening to consume you if you ever stop moving.

Flame-Cursed dwarves are rarest and most feared of dwarven subraces. You are living furnaces, capable of enduring incredible punishment but always at risk of burning out—or burning through.

**Inner Flame:** You are immune to fire damage but vulnerable to cold damage.

The fire inside you protects against external heat but makes you catastrophically weak to freezing.

**Heat Vision:** You have darkvision out to 60 feet. Additionally, you can see heat signatures as easily as shapes—giving you advantage on Perception checks to detect living creatures in darkness, but disadvantage against undead or constructs (which often lack body heat).

**Pain as Fuel:** When you take damage that reduces you below half your hit point maximum (Bloodied state), you gain a **+2 bonus to all melee damage rolls** until the end of your next turn.

Your fury feeds the flame.

**Burning Focus:** When you spend a Hit Die during a short rest to regain hit points, you regain additional hit points equal to your Constitution modifier (minimum 1).

However, you have disadvantage on Stealth checks—your body radiates heat that can be seen in darkness.

**Sanity Cost:** Living with constant internal fire takes a toll. Your **Sanity baseline is reduced to +0** (instead of the normal dwarven +1).

Additionally, when you reach Bloodied state, you must make a DC 12 Wisdom saving throw or enter a brief rage (you must attack the nearest creature, friend or foe, for 1 round).

**DCS Integration:**
- **Evasion Modifier:** Adjusted profile (Dodge -1, Endure +3, Deflect +0)
- **Combat Style:** Berserker who becomes MORE dangerous as they take damage
- **Momentum Generation:** Explosive fighter—generates massive Momentum when Bloodied
- **Unique Role:** The dwarf who wins by refusing to fall even when they should be dead
- **Risk/Reward:** Highest damage potential but constant threat of losing control

## Playing a Dwarf

### Personality

Dwarves are not monolithic. A Hill Dwarf brewer and a Duergar exile have little in common beyond shared ancestry. However, certain tendencies run through dwarven psychology:

**Pragmatism:** You measure ideas by whether they **work**, not whether they're beautiful or philosophically satisfying.

An elf might contemplate a door's symbolic meaning; you check if it's properly hinged.

**Long Memory:** You remember. Slights from decades ago, favors owed, oaths made. This can manifest as loyalty or grudge-holding, depending on the dwarf.

**Judge by Deeds:** You trust actions over words. Promises mean nothing until kept. Reputation is earned through **consistent work** over years, not speeches.

**Blunt Honesty:** Most dwarves speak plainly, sometimes to the point of rudeness. Flowery language feels like lying with extra steps.

**Patience and Stubbornness:** When you decide something is right, you'll pursue it for decades. This can be admirable persistence or maddening inflexibility.

### Roleplaying Suggestions

**Respect Craft:** Comment on workmanship. Notice when buildings are poorly constructed, weapons are substandard, or food is prepared carelessly. Quality matters.

**Reference History:** Drop ancestors' names, cite precedent, mention events from a century ago as if they were yesterday. "Aye, that's how Grandfather Thorin lost his finger—same fool mistake."

**Physical Awareness:** You're built low and heavy. Mention ducking through doorways designed for humans, being unable to reach high shelves, or standing firm when others are knocked back.

**Oaths Matter:** When you swear to something, mean it. If you break your word, that's a story moment—something has gone terribly wrong.

**Community Ties:** Even exiled dwarves remember their clan. You might be traveling alone, but you'll mention your hold, cite their techniques, or wonder what they'd think of your choices.

Dwarves typically use their given name followed by their clan name (Thorin Strakeln) or their craft-name once earned (Rurik Ironhand). Hill Dwarves often include a lineage marker (Kathra, daughter of Baern). Dust Dwarves sometimes take descriptive names based on their journeys (Wandering Delg, Ash-Walker Torgga).

## Dwarves in Dór-o Estel

The dwarven holds of Dór-o Estel predate most surface civilizations. Your people remember when the first Loom-Cities were just villages, when the Faith of the Bound Pattern was still forming, when magic was wilder and the world younger.

**After the Collapse of Ashkarra**, dwarven holds across the world sealed their deepest tunnels, enacting the **Silence Chain** in mourning and shutting off connections to corrupted leylines. Many Living Locks were created—dwarves who voluntarily sealed themselves in isolated chambers to "contain what may echo."

The **Faith of the Bound Pattern** is respected by many dwarves, particularly followers of **Gaeldros** (law and oaths) and **Thamur** (sealing and containment), two of the High Forgebound who support the Faith. However, dwarves don't **worship** the Pattern—they see it as another truth to work with, like gravity or metallurgy.

### Major Dwarven Holds

**Kazad-Thumar (The First Anvil):** The oldest known hold, carved into the World's Spine mountain range. Home to the Archivists of Stone.

**Khazad-Gorim (The Iron Deep):** Largest forge-city, producing 60% of the world's masterwork weapons and armor.

**Baraz-Dûm (The Sunset Watch):** Westernmost hold, where Dust Dwarves maintain trade routes into desert kingdoms.

**Gundabad-Narag (The Sealed Gate):** The greatest concentration of Living Locks and Duergar, guarding what must not return.

### When Working with Other Races

**Elves:** You respect their long memory but mistrust their lack of permanent works. "They build for beauty. Their works fade."

**Humans:** Admirable ambition, pitiful patience. "They build too fast. They do not last."

**Halflings:** Surprising respect. "They do not build—but they do not pretend." A halfling who speaks truth is worth more than a boastful warrior.

**Orcs:** Ceremonial respect for endurance. "They understand trials. They lack precision."

**Gnomes:** Brilliant but reckless. "Gnomes invent quickly. Dwarves build perfectly."

## Integration with Mental Systems

### **Focus & Sanity:**
Your **+1 Sanity** is significant:
- Advantage on fear effects becomes even stronger with your natural resilience
- Breaking Points are harder to trigger
- Mental attacks are less effective
- Horror and trauma are processed pragmatically rather than emotionally

**Typical Dwarf Mental Strategy:**
- Use high Sanity to resist supernatural fear
- Frame problems as solvable engineering challenges
- Rely on clan memory and ancestral wisdom during stress
- Treat mental trauma like physical injury—something to recover from methodically

### **Corruption Concerns:**
Your unique corruption vulnerability centers on **geological sources**:
- Corrupted leylines beneath mountains affect you catastrophically
- Eldritch forces sealed in deep places can reach you more easily
- Things that should stay buried seem to **call** to you
- At Tier 2+, the Fractured Foundation table reflects your severing from stone

### **Rally & Morale:**
Dwarves have natural advantages in morale systems:
- Advantage on Rally checks (biological stubbornness)
- Higher Breaking Point thresholds required
- Your refusal to move or fall **inspires** allies
- Enemy forces become demoralized facing defenders who simply will not break

## Story Integration

### **Why Are You Adventuring?**

**Reclamation:**
- Your hold was abandoned generations ago; now your clan wants it back
- Leading an expedition to cleanse and reclaim ancestral halls
- Proving the hold can be defended again
- Recovering lost artifacts or knowledge

**Exile and Redemption:**
- You are Nameless—exiled for oath-breaking or failure
- Adventuring offers chances to earn deeds worthy of redemption
- Seeking the components for the Rite of Return
- Building a reputation separate from your shamed past

**Craft-Quest:**
- Seeking legendary materials for a masterwork
- Learning techniques from distant masters
- Proving your craft-skill through deed rather than years
- Creating something worthy of your maker's mark

**Guard Duty:**
- Serving as Living Lock escort
- Hunting those who disturb sealed places
- Investigating corruption in leylines or ancient vaults
- Ensuring what was buried stays buried

**Long-Term Goals:**
- Found a new hold through deed and discovery
- Achieve Masterwork status in your chosen craft
- Have your work placed in the Hall of Enduring Names
- Restore your clan's reputation after catastrophe
- Create something that will outlast you by centuries
- Become a Seal-Warden (requires specific path)
- Bridge understanding between dwarven tradition and changing world

## Design Philosophy

The Dwarf lineage is designed to support:

1. **The Immovable Tank** (highest base DR + Endure Evasion + anti-movement abilities)
2. **The Master Craftsman** (tool proficiencies + cultural respect for quality)
3. **The Long-Memory Sage** (Stonecunning + History bonuses + centuries of life)
4. **The Forge-Warrior** (Mountain Dwarf STR bonus + armor proficiencies)
5. **The Survivor** (Hill Dwarf HP bonus + mental resilience)

Your mechanical abilities (Dwarven Endurance, Resilience, Stonecunning, high Endure Evasion) create a **foundation that will not crack**. You're designed to stand in melee, outlast punishment, and create dramatic "they broke on our shields" moments.

The subrace options allow you to lean into different traditions—from family-focused Hill Dwarves to perfection-seeking Mountain Dwarves to grim Duergar guardians—while maintaining the core dwarven identity of enduring craftsmanship.

---

## Mechanical Considerations

### **Optimal Classes:**
Your abilities synergize excellently with:

- **Fighter:** High AC + DR + Endure Evasion = nearly unkillable warrior
- **Paladin:** Constitution + Sanity bonus + Lay on Hands creates durable holy defender
- **Cleric:** Wisdom-based casting + natural tankiness + Sanity for mental defense
- **Barbarian:** Damage reduction stacks beautifully with Rage resistance
- **Artificer/Inventor:** Tool proficiencies + crafting culture create natural inventors

### **Strong But Less Traditional:**
- **Rogue:** Unexpected but viable—Stonecunning creates "dungeon sage" rogues
- **Ranger:** Dust Dwarf wilderness abilities create excellent trackers
- **Wizard:** Mountain Dwarf armor proficiencies create "battle mage" builds
- **Monk:** Unlikely but interesting—the "stone monk" who won't be moved

### **Multiclass Synergies:**
- **Fighter/Paladin:** Ultimate tank with Smite damage and excellent saves
- **Cleric/Fighter:** Battle-priest who combines divine magic with durability
- **Barbarian/Fighter:** Stack DR + Rage for absurd damage reduction
- **Artificer/Fighter:** Master craftsman who fights in their own creations

## Final Notes

### **The Weight of Names:**
Every dwarf carries ancestral pressure. Your character likely knows:
- At least 3 generations of ancestors by name and deed
- The founding of their clan
- Multiple holds by reputation
- The names of legendary crafters in their field

This isn't burden—it's **scaffolding**. You stand on the shoulders of those who built before you.

### **Respect Across Cultures:**
Modern Dór-o Estel recognizes dwarven reliability:
- Elves acknowledge the parallel to their own memory-preservation
- Humans hire dwarven craftsmen for critical infrastructure
- Even the Faith of the Bound Pattern calls upon dwarven Seal-Wardens when containment must be absolute
- "Dwarven-made" is synonymous with "will outlast your grandchildren"

### **The Final Oath:**
Your people believe death is **returning to stone**. The worthy dead become part of the mountain, their essence joining the bedrock. Some even return as **Stone-Echoes**—geological formations that appear in times of need, guiding their descendants.

You adventure knowing the stone remembers, judges, and waits. This is not pressure to fear—it is **certainty** that your works matter beyond death.
