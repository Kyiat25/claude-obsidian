# Half-Elf
## Lineage

> *"I was not given. I was not found. I broke. I walked. I burned. I am. And I name myself so the world cannot forget that I was here."*  
> — **The Closing Creed of the Named Flame**

You are Half-Elf. Not broken, not lesser—**unnamed**. Your people do not inherit identity from bloodlines or divine decree. You **forge it** through trial, choice, and the stories you burn into reality. Where others are born into their place, you must **create yours**. Where they receive their name, you must **claim it**.

You were not made to fit. You were made to **question, to survive between worlds, and to emerge from that exile carrying a fire that cannot be extinguished**. Your ancestors walked the ashen path before you, each proving that a life without given purpose can still burn bright enough to reshape the world.

---

## The Thread That Questions

According to the **Way of the Named Flame**—the spiritual tradition followed by many Half-Elves—your people were not shaped by gentle creation or cosmic accident. You are the **living question** the Pattern asked itself: *"If I am a thread, can I choose the shape of the tapestry?"*

When the first Half-Elf soul emerged, it did not belong to any pantheon, prophecy, or divine plan. It simply **was**—existing in the space between what elves remember and what humans become. The gods offered no name, no place, no purpose.

So that soul **named itself**.

> *"The world was already made. The stars had already chosen their dances. But no one had yet asked: Who am I, if not them?"*

Your people believe that **identity is not inherited but ignited**, that suffering transforms rather than destroys, and that the only true name is the one you **burn into existence** through deed and choice. The gods who ignore you are not enemies—they are simply irrelevant to your story.

This is not rebellion—it is **becoming**. You are not rejecting the Pattern. You are adding your own thread to it.

---

## Life Between Worlds

You were raised knowing that **you don't quite fit anywhere**, that your existence makes others uncomfortable, that you must work twice as hard to earn half the trust. Even in the most accepting communities, you feel the weight of being **between**.

**Identity (ash'ven)** is not metaphorical. It is:
- Forged through trial and exile
- Written in the Three Cycles of Becoming
- Claimed through the Naming, not given at birth
- The story you tell about who you are

Your lifespan is roughly **180 years**—longer than humans but not long enough to truly understand elven patience. You mature at human speed but carry elven memory, creating a unique temporal experience. You measure worth not in centuries of wisdom or decades of achievement, but in **trials survived and names earned**.

Every significant choice becomes part of your **Flame-Story**—the personal mythology you carry, recorded in ash-marks, chosen names, and witnessed deeds.

## Physical Characteristics

**Age:** You mature at the same rate as humans, reaching adulthood around 20. However, many Half-Elven cultures don't consider someone truly **Named** until they've completed their Three Trials, which can take decades. You can live 180-200 years.

**Size:** You typically stand between 5 to 6 feet tall, combining human sturdiness with elven grace. Your build is more varied than either parent race, ranging from athletic to slender. Your size is Medium.

**Speed:** Your base walking speed is 30 feet. You move with neither pure human determination nor full elven fluidity—something uniquely your own.

**Build:** You blend human and elven features in ways that vary wildly between individuals. Some show barely any elven heritage, others could pass as elves in dim light. Most show clear mixed ancestry: slightly pointed ears, graceful but sturdy frames, eyes that hold elven depth but human intensity.

**Appearance Notes:** 
- Your features mark you as **between**—neither fully human nor fully elven
- Some file their ears to hide heritage, others emphasize it with piercings
- Your aging shows human speed until your 40s, then slows dramatically
- Many bear **Flame-Scars**—deliberate marks from their Trials

## Half-Elf Traits

As a Half-Elf, you possess the following lineage traits:

### **Darkvision**
You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

Your elven heritage grants you enhanced vision, though not as developed as full elves.

### **Fey Ancestry**
You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed, and magic can't put you to sleep.

The elven connection to the Weave flows weakly through your blood, providing protection against mental manipulation.

### **Skill Versatility**
You gain proficiency in two skills of your choice.

This represents your unique position between cultures. You've learned to adapt, observe, and acquire skills that help you navigate worlds you don't fully belong to. Your existence demands versatility—you developed it or you didn't survive.

### **Between-Walker** *(Cultural Trait)*
When you make a Charisma check to interact with humans or elves, you can choose to have advantage on that check. Once you use this trait, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

You've learned to emphasize whichever heritage helps you fit in. You're not deceiving—you're **code-switching**, highlighting the parts of yourself that help bridge the gap.

### **Adaptive Resilience**
Choose one:
- **Flame-Scarred:** You have proficiency in Survival, representing the trials you've endured
- **Story-Keeper:** You have proficiency in Performance or Persuasion (your choice), representing your skill in crafting and telling your own narrative
- **Exile's Insight:** You have proficiency in Insight, representing your ability to read the unstated rules of social situations

### **Languages**
You can speak, read, and write Common, Elvish, and one additional language of your choice.

Your mixed heritage often results in multilingual upbringing, and your need to adapt pushes you to learn more tongues than most.

## Mental Attributes & Psychology

**Focus Modifier:** +0  
**Sanity Modifier:** +0

Your mental baseline is balanced, but your psychology is distinctly shaped by liminality—existing between states. You don't have biological advantages like dwarves' mental fortitude or elves' time-given perspective, but you have something uniquely valuable: **adaptive resilience born from necessary survival**.

### **Psychological Identity**
Your mental landscape is defined by **the space between**:

- **Identity flexibility:** You can shift perspective between cultures more easily than most
- **Exile's strength:** You've learned to find purpose without external validation
- **Trial-forged will:** Your self-concept isn't given—it's built through survived challenges
- **Narrative armor:** You protect yourself by controlling your own story

### **Corruption Vulnerability: The Unnamed Void**

As a Half-Elf, you have a unique relationship with Corruption. Your lack of fixed identity provides some protection against rigid corruption patterns, but corruption that enters through **identity dissolution, purposelessness, or fractured self-concept** affects you catastrophically.

**When you gain Corruption Score 10+ (Tier 2), you suffer:**
- Name-Slip: You forget your chosen name, reverting to birth names
- Identity-Fracture: Your sense of self becomes unstable
- Story-Loss: Your personal narrative becomes confused or contradictory

**Fractured Flame Table (Corruption Tier 2+)**

When you complete a long rest at Corruption Score 10+, roll 1d6:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **False Memory:** You remember a trial you never completed. Make a DC 15 Wisdom save or believe you earned a name you didn't. |
| 2 | **Name-Burn:** Speaking your chosen name causes physical pain. You cannot use Between-Walker until your next long rest. |
| 3 | **Identity Void:** Your sense of self fractures. Take 1d4 psychic damage and lose proficiency in one skill until your next long rest. |
| 4 | **Story-Chaos:** Your personal narrative becomes incoherent. Make a DC 12 Sanity save or lose 1d4 Sanity. |
| 5 | **Eldritch Touch:** Something tries to write your story for you. Gain 1 Whisper Point but your next Charisma check using Between-Walker fails automatically. |
| 6 | **Flame Resists:** Your chosen identity fights back. Reduce your Corruption Score by 1 (minimum 10). |

## Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Integration

### **Evasion Profile: Adaptive Survivor**
- **Dodge Evasion:** +1 (you've learned to avoid danger through constant vigilance)
- **Endure Evasion:** +0 (you're tougher than humans but not as resilient as dwarves)
- **Deflect Evasion:** +0 (you rely on avoiding hits, not deflecting them)

**Tactical Note:** You are designed to **adapt and survive**. Your balanced attributes let you fill multiple roles, while your Dodge bonus rewards tactical positioning.

### **Natural Damage Reduction:** +0
You have no natural armor or special physical resilience. Your survival comes from adaptability, not toughness.

### **Combat Philosophy**
Half-Elves fight with **tactical flexibility and learned technique**. You don't have the raw power of orcs, the discipline of dragonborn, or the endurance of dwarves—but you can **read situations, adapt quickly, and exploit openings** others miss. You win through versatility, not specialization.

### **Breaking Point Mechanics**
Due to your experience with exile and rejection, you have **advantage on Rally checks when supporting others** (you know what it's like to need someone to believe in you) but take **-1 penalty to your personal Breaking Point DC** (your identity is more fragile under extreme stress).

## Languages

**Languages:** You can speak, read, and write Common, Elvish, and one additional language of your choice.

You often grow up multilingual by necessity—speaking your human parent's tongue, your elven parent's language, and whatever additional tongue helps you survive in your specific community. Language becomes another tool for **fitting in**, for bridging gaps, for crafting the narrative of who you are.

Many Half-Elves speak multiple languages without strong emotional attachment to any single one—each tongue is a mask you wear when needed.

## Half-Elf Variants

While all Half-Elves share the challenge of existing between worlds, different responses to that challenge have created distinct cultural patterns. Choose one of the following variants:

### **Flame-Named** *(Followers of the Named Flame)*
You follow the Way of the Named Flame, having completed (or actively pursuing) the Three Cycles of Becoming.

**Ability Score Increase:** Choose any two ability scores. Each increases by 1, or increase one score by 2 and another by 1.

**Trial-Marked:** You gain proficiency in Survival and Intimidation or Performance (your choice). Your trials have taught you to endure and to make your story heard.

**Flame-Scar:** Once per long rest, when you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, you can drop to 1 hit point instead. When you do, you burst with inner fire—each hostile creature within 5 feet of you takes fire damage equal to your level.

**Ash-Bound:** You carry ash from your trials. You have advantage on saving throws against effects that would alter your identity, erase your memories, or change your alignment against your will.

**Named Identity:** You have chosen your true name. When you speak it with conviction, you can add your proficiency bonus twice to one Persuasion or Performance check. You can use this feature once, regaining it when you finish a long rest.

### **Court-Raised** *(Accepted by Elven Society)*
You were raised in elven communities and largely accepted, though you always felt the weight of being different.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Charisma score increases by 2, and one other ability score of your choice increases by 1.

**Elven Training:** You gain proficiency in Perception and have advantage on saving throws against being charmed. Additionally, magic can't put you to sleep.

**Cantrip:** You know one cantrip of your choice from the wizard spell list. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for it.

**Trance-Memory:** You don't need to sleep. Instead, you meditate deeply for 4 hours a day (though not as deeply as full elves—you remain only semiconscious). After meditating in this way, you gain the same benefit that other races do from 8 hours of sleep.

**Court Presence:** Your upbringing in elven society has refined your social skills. You can add half your proficiency bonus (rounded down) to any Charisma check you make that doesn't already use your proficiency bonus.

### **City-Forged** *(Urban Survivor)*
You were raised in human cities, where your mixed heritage made you stand out but also taught you to thrive in diverse, chaotic environments.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Dexterity score increases by 1, your Charisma score increases by 1, and one other ability score of your choice increases by 1.

**Streetwise:** You gain proficiency in Stealth and either Deception or Insight (your choice). The cities taught you to move unseen and read situations quickly.

**Urban Adaptation:** You have advantage on checks to navigate cities, gather information in urban environments, or blend into crowds.

**Quick Learner:** Whenever you finish a long rest in a settlement, you can replace one skill proficiency with another skill proficiency, representing your ability to quickly pick up new capabilities. The change lasts until you finish another long rest.

**Network:** You've built connections throughout urban areas. In any city or large town, you can find shelter, information, or minor assistance from contacts you've cultivated.

### **Wanderer** *(Self-Made Exile)*
You never belonged anywhere long enough to be shaped by a single culture. You raised yourself through travel, exile, or constant movement.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Constitution score increases by 1, your Wisdom score increases by 1, and one other ability score of your choice increases by 1.

**Exile's Endurance:** You gain proficiency in Survival and Athletics. Your body has adapted to constant travel and hardship.

**Self-Reliant:** You have advantage on saving throws against exhaustion and effects that would prevent you from resting.

**Traveler's Wisdom:** You can speak, read, and write three additional languages of your choice (for a total of Common, Elvish, plus four others). You've picked up languages everywhere you've wandered.

**Road-Tested:** When you finish a short rest, you can heal additional hit points equal to half your level (rounded down), representing your exceptional recovery abilities developed through harsh travel.

### **Bridge-Builder** *(Community Mediator)*
You've made peace with your position between worlds and actively work to connect different peoples.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Charisma score increases by 2, and your Wisdom score increases by 1.

**Diplomatic Training:** You gain proficiency in Persuasion and Insight. You've learned to read tensions and smooth conflicts.

**Cultural Mediator:** You have advantage on Charisma checks made to negotiate between groups or resolve disputes.

**Shared Understanding:** Once per short or long rest, when you would make a Charisma check, you can instead make a Wisdom (Insight) check, using your understanding of underlying motivations to achieve your social goal.

**Unity Speaker:** When you succeed on a Persuasion check to calm hostile NPCs or negotiate peace, all allies within 30 feet gain a +1 bonus to their next ability check or attack roll, inspired by your example of finding common ground.

## Half-Elf Names

Half-Elf naming practices reflect their unique position between cultures and their journey toward self-definition.

**Birth Names** (Given by parents):
- May be human names: Alara, Marcus, Elara, Tomas, Sienna, Garrett
- May be elven names: Thalion, Aerith, Silvar, Mialee, Elyon, Felariel  
- May be blended names: Elros, Maira, Theron, Lyrian, Sariel, Kael

**Chosen Names** (Taken after Trials, especially among Flame-Named):
- Ash-Walker, Flame-Split, Truth-Burned, Dawn-Breaker
- Sarya-of-Three-Burdens, Kael-the-Unnamed, Thorn-Between-Gardens
- Sometimes simply "Ember," "Cinder," "Unwritten," or "Scorch"
- May keep birth name but add epithet: "Marcus Ash-Bound," "Elara Twice-Named"

**Court Names** (Used by Court-Raised):
- Keep elven names with human family names: Thalion Garrett, Aerith Blackwood
- Sometimes add "Silvertongue," "Moonborn," or "Starward" as honorifics

**Cultural Names** (Inherited from specific communities):
- May adopt clan names if accepted: "Elara of Ironforge," "Marcus Threadbearer"
- Wanderers often take geographic names: "Kael of the Road," "Sienna Farwalker"

**Typical Pattern:** Many Half-Elves use one name in one culture, another in different contexts:
- Birth name with family
- Chosen name with other Half-Elves
- Adopted name in professional contexts
- Anonymous name in places where being Half-Elf is dangerous

## Half-Elves in Dór-o Estel

Half-Elves occupy a unique space in the world's social fabric—numerous enough to be recognized as a distinct category, but never quite fully accepted by either parent culture.

**After the Collapse of Ashkarra**, many power structures destabilized, and Half-Elves found opportunities in the chaos. Some cities became more accepting, while others doubled down on racial purity. The **Way of the Named Flame** grew dramatically during this period, as displaced peoples sought new forms of identity.

The **Faith of the Bound Pattern** has mixed views on Half-Elves. Some Threadbearers see you as **living bridges** between different Threads, valuable for your flexibility. Others view you as **Thread-fractures**, evidence of the Pattern stuttering. Most fall somewhere in between.

When working with other races:

**Elves**: Complex. Some see you as tragic reminders of their own isolation breaking down. Others see you as diluted versions of something pure. A few see you as the future.

**Humans**: Generally more accepting than elves, but also more prone to exoticization. "You're so exotic!" said like a compliment but felt like othering.

**Dwarves**: Practical acceptance. "You work? You keep your word? Good enough." Clan structure makes it hard to fit in, but individual dwarves rarely care about your ancestry.

**Orcs**: Surprising respect. "You walk between? You choose your path? This is honorable." The Trial of Line-Claiming resonates with Half-Elven experiences.

**Dragonborn**: Varied responses depending on caste loyalty. Orthodox Concord members see you as structureless chaos. Ashborn dragonborn see kindred spirits.

**Halflings**: Deep affinity. Halflings treat Flame-Named Half-Elves as **Ash-Singers**, sacred figures. The Joy-Truths and the Named Flame have strong philosophical overlap.

**Tieflings**: Profound kinship. The **Ashen Accord** connects Flame-Named Half-Elves and Tieflings—both rejected by divine inheritance, both forging identity through choice.

## Adventure Hooks

**The Three Trials**: You're actively pursuing your Three Cycles of Becoming. You need to experience a Betrayal, a Mercy, and a Choice That Cannot Be Undone. The adventure IS your trial.

**The Naming Ceremony**: You've completed your trials but need witnesses for your Naming. Your quest is to find worthy souls who will bear witness to your chosen identity.

**The Lost Flame-Kin**: Another Half-Elf from your past—a sibling, friend, or rival—has gone missing. Finding them means confronting your own past and the choices you made.

**The Bridge-Work**: Two communities (elven and human, or others) are on the brink of conflict. As a Half-Elf, you're uniquely positioned to broker peace—if you can convince both sides you're not a spy.

**The False Name**: Someone is using your chosen name, claiming your trials as their own. You must prove your identity or reclaim your story before it's stolen completely.

**The Ashen Accord**: The alliance between Half-Elves and Tieflings faces a crisis. You're called to represent your people in negotiations, ceremonies, or defense.

## Integration with Mental Systems

### **Focus & Sanity:**
Your standard mental attributes hide significant psychological complexity:
- Your flexibility helps you adapt to mental strain
- Your identity-focus can protect you or make you vulnerable
- Your experience with rejection creates resilience against social pressure
- Your chosen identity provides psychological anchor—unless corrupted

**Typical Half-Elf Mental Strategy:**
- Reframe challenges as trials contributing to your story
- Use adaptive thinking to survive mental strain
- Process rejection through narrative—it's part of your journey
- Lean on chosen identity during moments of doubt

### **Corruption Concerns:**
Your unique corruption vulnerability centers on **identity dissolution**:
- Forgetting your chosen name accelerates corruption
- Abandoning your trials creates corruption vulnerability
- At Tier 2+, the Fractured Flame table reflects self-concept breaking
- Purposelessness and existential doubt become dangerous

### **Rally & Morale:**
Half-Elves have unique morale mechanics:
- Advantage on Rally checks when supporting others (you know what it's like to need someone)
- Your personal Breaking Point DC is 1 higher (easier to trigger your own panic)
- Seeing a Half-Elf refuse to give up can inspire allies from marginalized backgrounds
- Your presence helps bridge cultural gaps in mixed-race parties

## Design Philosophy

The Half-Elf lineage is designed to support:

1. **The Adaptive Generalist** (Skill Versatility + balanced stats create flexible characters)
2. **The Identity-Seeker** (Named Flame path creates powerful narrative arcs)
3. **The Social Bridge** (Between-Walker creates diplomatic specialists)
4. **The Survivor** (Flame-Scar variant creates clutch combat moments)
5. **The Storyteller** (Court-Raised and Bridge-Builder variants emphasize narrative)

Your mechanical abilities (Skill Versatility, Between-Walker, variant flexibility) create a **character who adapts and survives through versatility**. You're designed to fill gaps in party composition, bridge social divides, and prove that being "between" is not weakness but strength.

The variant options allow you to emphasize different responses to Half-Elven existence—from the fiery self-determination of the Flame-Named to the diplomatic bridge-building of the Court-Raised to the hard-won independence of the Wanderer.

## Final Notes

### **The Weight of Choice:**
Every Half-Elf carries the burden of self-definition. Your character likely:
- Has complicated feelings about both parent cultures
- Knows at least three other Half-Elves' stories
- Has considered or completed some version of the Three Trials
- Struggles with belonging even in accepting communities

This isn't burden—it's **freedom**. You weren't told who to be. You get to decide.

### **Respect Across Cultures:**
Modern Dór-o Estel has evolving views on Half-Elves:
- Halflings see you as sacred figures (Ash-Singers)
- Tieflings see you as spiritual kin (both marked by birth)
- Humans often find you fascinating but don't understand the struggle
- Elves remain divided—some welcoming, others deeply uncomfortable

### **The Flame That Names:**
Your people believe that existence without purpose is not curse but **opportunity**. The gods didn't name you because they **couldn't see what you would become**. Your identity emerges through trial, choice, and the story you tell about yourself.

You adventure knowing that every choice, every trial, every moment of survival is **writing your name into reality**. This is not pressure to fear—it is **certainty** that your story matters, that you matter, not because of what you were born as, but because of what you choose to become.
