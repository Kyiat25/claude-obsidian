# The Barbarian

**Primal Warriors of Fury and Might**

> *"Battle is not war against an enemy. It is a war against your own limits. The rage simply shows you where they were."*

---

## Fury Given Form

The battlefield shudders. Not from the thunder of boots or the clash of steel — from her. She has stopped moving. The warband that surrounded her a moment ago now watches her shoulders rise and fall with something that is not quite breathing. When the first soldier reaches her, he discovers what it means to strike a storm.

You are not a warrior who uses rage. You are rage that happens to be wearing a warrior. Every feature on this class is designed around that truth: you become more dangerous as the fight escalates, you punish everything that tries to hurt you, and when the situation looks most desperate, you hit harder than ever.

Your **Rage** is the engine. Your Momentum interactions mean you never lose your rhythm while it burns. Your **Relentless Fury** at 15th level means enemies who think they are winning by wounding you are actually feeding something that will destroy them.

---

## The Barbarian's Essence

In FEARS, a Barbarian is a living pressure system. You build, you escalate, and you punish aggression. **Reckless Attack** is a declaration: you are not afraid of attacks against you because you are more dangerous than anything trying to land them. **Brutal Critical** punishes hesitation. **Rage Stances** let you choose each fight's shape — fury for maximum damage, endurance for maximum survival, predator for surgical precision.

Your Momentum interactions are unique in the system. While Raging, a Major Injury doesn't break your rhythm — it drops you to −1 at worst. When you're struggling at −2, your next turn begins with a surge back to 0. At 11th level, Raging Momentum cannot fall below 0 at all. You are the class that momentum was designed to embody.

You excel when enemies cluster around you. You struggle when fights require patience, distance, or deception. Play to your strengths: get into the middle of it, stay there, and let the rage do the rest.

## Class Features

As a Barbarian, you gain the following class features.

### Hit Points

- **Hit Dice:** 1d12 per Barbarian level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 12 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d12 (or 7) + your Constitution modifier per Barbarian level after 1st

### Proficiencies

- **Armour:** Light armour, medium armour, shields
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons, martial weapons
- **Tools:** None
- **Saving Throws:** Strength, Constitution
- **Skills:** Choose two from Athletics, Intimidation, Nature, Perception, Skinning, Survival

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) a greataxe or (b) any martial melee weapon
- (a) two handaxes or (b) any simple weapon
- An explorer's pack and four javelins

---

## Quick Build

To quickly create a Barbarian, follow these steps:

1. **Ability Scores:** Make Strength your highest score, followed by Constitution.
2. **Lineage and Heritage:** Any combination works — Orc, Half-Orc, Goliath, and Dwarf are thematic but not required.
3. **Background:** Outlander, Soldier, or any background with Athletics or Survival.
4. **Combat Strategy:** Enter Rage immediately, choose your stance, move into the middle of the fight, and stay there.

## Core Class Features

### Rage (1st Level)

As a bonus action, you can enter a rage for 1 minute. While raging:

- Gain **advantage on Strength checks and Strength saving throws**.
- Add your **Rage Damage bonus** to melee weapon attacks using Strength.
- Gain **Damage Reduction** per attack — DR 1 at 1st level, DR 2 at 10th level, DR 3 + Constitution modifier (max 5) at 20th level.

**Momentum Interactions While Raging:**
- **Resilient Flow:** When you suffer a Major Injury (crossing the 50% HP threshold), your Momentum drops to −1 rather than resetting entirely.
- **Fury Threshold:** If you start your turn Raging with a Momentum Score of −2, you immediately gain +2 Momentum at the start of your turn.
- At 11th level, your Momentum Score cannot fall below 0 while Raging.

Your rage ends early if you are knocked unconscious or fail to attack or take damage since your last turn. You can rage a number of times as shown in the Barbarian table, regaining all uses after a long rest.

---

### Unarmoured Defence (1st Level)

While not wearing armour, you gain **+2 to Evasion Rolls** and may use your **Constitution modifier** instead of Dexterity for Evasion Rolls.

---

### Weapon Mastery (1st Level)

Your primal fury extends to your weapons. At 1st level, gain **Weapon Mastery** with **one weapon** of your choice (simple or martial). When you hit a creature with a mastered weapon, you may apply one Weapon Skill associated with that weapon once per turn.

**Rage Synergy:** Weapon Skills that deal extra damage (Bleed, Cleave) are particularly effective when combined with your Rage Damage bonus.

You may spend 1 week of downtime training after scoring the required number of critical hits to unlock new masteries: simple weapons require 2 critical hits, martial weapons require 3.

---

### Reckless Attack (2nd Level)

Gain advantage on melee attack rolls made with Strength during your turn. Until your next turn, all attack rolls against you also gain advantage.

---

### Danger Sense (2nd Level)

Gain advantage on **Evasion Rolls** against visible effects such as traps and spells.

---

### Primal Path (3rd Level)

Choose a subclass that reflects your fighting philosophy and spiritual heritage, such as **Path of the Berserker** or **Path of Wild Fury**. Your subclass grants features at 3rd, 6th, 11th, and 14th levels.

---

### Rage Stances (3rd Level)

When you enter a Rage, choose one of the following stances. You maintain this stance until your Rage ends.

**Fury:** Your melee weapon attacks deal +2 bonus damage. This bonus increases to +3 at 9th level and +4 at 16th level.

**Endurance:** You gain +1 DR for the duration of your Rage.

**Predator:** When you make a Called Shot while Raging, reduce the attack roll penalty by 1.

You may choose a different stance each time you enter Rage.

---

### Multiattack (5th Level)

Make two attacks whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.

---

### Fast Movement (5th Level)

Increase your speed by 10 feet. Upon rolling initiative, you may move up to half your speed as a free action without provoking opportunity attacks.

---

### Feral Instinct (6th Level)

Add your **Proficiency Bonus** to initiative rolls. If surprised, you can act normally on your first turn by using your reaction to enter Rage.

---

### Primal Awareness (6th Level)

You gain advantage on Survival checks and Nature checks to track creatures or forage. As a bonus action, detect the presence of hostile creatures within 30 feet (fiends, undead, beasts). This sense penetrates light cover but not full cover or walls.

---

### Primal Defiance (7th Level)

*Reaction.* When you would lose Momentum due to taking damage, you may use your reaction to unleash a primal roar. Reduce the Momentum loss by 1 (to a minimum of 0), and you gain +1 Momentum.

You can use this reaction a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per short rest.

---

### Brutal Critical (9th Level)

Your critical hits bypass **half the target's DR**. Add one extra damage die for critical hits, increasing to two dice at 13th level and three dice at 17th level.

---

### Heroic Boon (10th Level)

Choose one of the following:

**Instant Rage:** Automatically enter Rage when combat begins (no bonus action required).

**Stubborn Rage:** Your Rage does not end unless its duration expires or you choose to end it (unconsciousness still ends it).

---

### Enhanced Rage (11th Level)

While Raging, your Momentum Score cannot be reduced below 0. Your unbridled fury maintains a baseline combat rhythm that cannot be broken by enemy attacks or failed saves.

This interacts with Resilient Flow: even if a Major Injury would drop you to −1, Enhanced Rage keeps you at 0.

---

### Relentless Rage (14th Level)

When you drop to 0 hit points while Raging, make a **DC 10 Constitution saving throw**. On a success, you drop to 1 HP instead. The DC increases by 5 after each use and resets after a short or long rest.

---

### Relentless Fury (15th Level)

When your current hit points are **at or below 50% of your maximum**, the damage dice of your melee weapon attacks increase by one step (d6 → d8, d8 → d10). When your current hit points are **at or below 25%**, they increase by one additional step (d6 → d10, d8 → d12). This bonus stacks with Rage Damage and Brutal Critical. Dice revert to normal size when you exceed 50% HP.

---

### Unyielding Might (18th Level)

Treat any Strength or Constitution roll of **9 or lower as a 10**. When damaging objects or structures, add your Strength score as bonus damage.

---

### Epic Boon (20th Level)

Choose one of the following:

**Epic Boon: Titan's Wrath**
- While Raging, your melee attacks ignore all DR and you gain bonus damage equal to your Rage DR.
- Once per long rest, release a shockwave: all creatures of your choice within 30 feet make a Strength saving throw (DC = 8 + PB + STR modifier) or take **8d10 force damage** and be knocked prone. On a success, they take half damage and remain standing. This shockwave can also clear terrain, break barriers, or destabilise structures (GM discretion).

**Epic Boon: Primal Champion**
- Increase Strength and Constitution by 4 (maximum 24 each).
- Gain unlimited uses of Rage.
- You have advantage on checks to move, lift, or break obstacles. You automatically succeed on Strength checks with a DC of 15 or lower to perform feats of strength.

## Last Stand Options

When the Barbarian reaches their final hour, they channel the full force of their primal fury into one last act of legendary violence or sacrifice.

**Rules for Last Stand:** Invoked when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, roll a death saving throw, or die outright. A free action, requiring no reaction or consciousness. Once resolved, the character permanently dies and cannot be returned by any means.

---

### 1. Final Rage

*The scent of your own blood unlocks a clarity of purpose that your kind are rarely afforded. Kill and be killed.*

**Effect:**
- Enter a **Persistent Rage** for 1 minute.
- You **cannot have fewer than 1 hit point** during this time.
- You are **immune to all conditions**, including exhaustion.
- You may ignore any effect that reduces your damage or kills you outright without reducing you to 0 hit points.

---

### 2. Last Laugh

*They always used to call you a troublemaker. One thing's for sure: they won't call you a hypocrite.*

**Effect:**
- Move up to your speed as part of this action.
- Make a **critical hit** attack against one target within range.
- The target must make a **Constitution saving throw** (DC = 12 + PB + DEX modifier).
- On failure: the target begins **bleeding** for force damage equal to twice your character level at the end of each of their turns for 5 minutes.
- On success: the target takes only the critical hit damage.
- The saving throw **cannot be resisted by Legendary Resistance**.

---

### 3. Wrath of the World Totem

*Your ancestors join you in celebrating the fury of your brothers and sisters still among the living.*

**Effect:**
- Allies within **100 feet** gain the following for 5 minutes:
  - Resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage.
  - Add your Rage Damage bonus to all their attack and spell damage rolls.
  - Add your Constitution modifier to their Armour Class (minimum +1).

| Last Stand Option | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Final Rage** | Persistent Rage, cannot drop below 1 HP, immune to conditions. | Surrounded by enemies. |
| **Last Laugh** | Move + critical hit + bleed (ignores Legendary Resistance). | Against a single powerful target. |
| **Wrath of the World Totem** | Allies gain damage resistance, Rage bonus to damage, CON to AC. | Empowering the entire party. |

## Class Reactions

Barbarians have access to powerful reactions that punish aggression and sustain combat momentum. These reactions use your **Proficiency Bonus pool** (shared across all class reactions), which refreshes on a short or long rest.

- **Shared Pool:** Reaction uses equal to your Proficiency Bonus.
- **Refresh:** Short or long rest.
- **One per round:** You may take only 1 reaction each round.
- **Opportunity Attacks:** Do not spend a class reaction use.

---

### Furious Defiance (2nd Level)

**Trigger:** A creature within 5 feet hits you while Raging.
**Effect:** Reduce the damage by your Proficiency Bonus modifier, then immediately make a melee attack against the attacker.

---

### Bloodrush Reprisal (3rd Level)

**Trigger:** A creature within 5 feet hits you while Raging.
**Effect:** They must make a **CON save** (DC = 8 + PB + CON). On fail: 2d6 weapon-type damage and pushed 10 feet. On success: half damage, no push.
**Scaling:** +1d6 at levels 7, 11, 15, and 19.

---

### Primal Shockwave (11th Level)

**Trigger:** You score a critical hit.
**Effect:** Release a shockwave as part of the same reaction. All creatures of your choice within 15 feet make a **STR save** (DC = 8 + PB + STR) or be knocked prone and take **PB × d6 thunder damage**.
