## 🧬 **Mythic Origins**

Vel’Zyren was once a **lesser goddess of Spirit**, born from the threads of **intention and secrecy** spun during the shaping of the mortal soul. Where [[Aelys]] wove remembrance and [[Astria]] shaped destiny, Vel’Zyren emerged in the **shadow between design and deviation**.

Her first act was not creation—but **refusal**. When the threads of fate were offered to her, she **cut them**. When prophecy spoke her name, she **rewrote the line**. She declared not that she would follow the Pattern, but that she would **author her own**.

> **"The Weave does not bind me. I spin my own.”**

The gods took notice when mortals—especially [[Elves]] of noble ambition and unfulfilled promise—began to have **visions not from Spirit, but from her**. These visions did not predict futures; they **offered replacements**.

She was not welcomed by the other Spirit deities. Her **ascension was not granted—it was taken**. And it was never fully accepted.

> Some among the Spirit-weavers whisper that Vel’Zyren was once **a reflection of Nyzara**—not her sibling, but a conceptual opposite. Where [[Nyzara]] guards forgotten truth in silence, Vel’Zyren **forges new truths in silence**. If this is true, it explains why Nyzara is said to **weep during mirror rites**, and why the name of Vel’Zyren **cannot be spoken near sacred memory wells** without glyphic distortion.

_“She was never born. She was our echo—one we failed to silence.”_ — Aelys, before the Trial of the Web-Spinner

Eventually, she began to **pull on fate itself**, rerouting destinies, silencing omens, and changing outcomes. She was not the first to seek power through manipulation—but she was the first to **trap the very concept of fate in a web** of her making.

This defiance reached its peak during the event known as **“[[The Trial of the Web-Spinner]],”** when Gaeldros himself invoked judgment upon her. But no divine punishment could undo her alterations.

And so, rather than be erased, she was **bound**—cast into [[The Underdark]], sealed beneath the Weave. Taking the Drow with her. A god not forgotten, but **intentionally unremembered**.

> _“She is not a god who rose. She is a god who refused to fall—so they buried her standing.”_

### 🕸️ **The Glimpse Beneath the Pattern**

When Vel’Zyren rewove the thread of her people, she passed where no god was meant to tread.  
For the briefest instant, her spirit grazed the **roots of the Weave**, and there she **saw the Nine**—not gods, not minds, but **truths beyond comprehension**:

- The **four that create and unmake**: Will, Threads, Endings, Echoes.
- The **five that shape and veil**: Understanding, Life, Motion, Shadows, Dreaming.

She does not name them. She does not revere them. But she remembers. She sees a way to rewrite her place even further, taking control of all. 
And she **wove their echo into her punishments**:

- **The Tide Between**, mirrored in her unending cycles of atonement.
- **The Veiled Hand**, etched into every false confession she extracts.
- **The Lantern Below**, flickering in the dreams she twists into commands.

Her priests do not teach this. Her faithful do not speak of it.  
But the silk of her web **runs with nine threads**, and **none of them belong to her**

## 🧭 **Role Within the Spirit Pantheon**

Vel’Zyren holds **no formal place** in any pantheon—**not by accident, but by decree**. She is not an exile of the Eldritch, nor a sister of Spirit, nor a rival god of another element. She is **a pantheon unto herself**, the **[[Ashen Web]]**, worshiped solely by the [[Drow]].

- **Formal Function**: None (Self-proclaimed _Weaver of the New Pattern_)
- **Divine Hierarchy**: Outside and beneath the hierarchy. She refuses all divine authority, claiming none and yielding to none.
- **Cycle Influence**: Locked to the **Underdark and Shadow**, she does not shift with the Threads of the Loom-Cities. Instead, her influence **rises when prophecy fails** or when **Threadbearers die unexpectedly**—moments when the Pattern is weakest.
- **Long-Term Threat Level**: **Extreme**. Though sealed by Gaeldros himself, the permanence of her reweaving threatens the **entire structure of divine memory and fate**. The Faith of the Bound Pattern regards her with **forbidden reverence**, acknowledging her as **the only known god to successfully rewrite portions of the Weave**.

Within the structure of [[Dór-o Estel]], she is the only deity known to have:

- Rewritten fate **without breaking the Weave**.
- Created a race-wide monofaith conversion event (**[[The Fall of the Drow]]**).
- Bound her **own pantheon from a single form**.

> _“Where gods hold council, she spins silence. Where pantheons form, she waits until they become tangled.”_

# 🕷️ **[[The Ashen Web]] – Vel’Zyren as Pantheon**

> _“Why worship many, when one can rewrite all?”_

Unlike every other race or culture in Dór-o Estel, the **Drow** worship not a collection of gods, but **a single absolute being**: Vel’Zyren. To them, **no other gods exist** in a meaningful sense. The Ashen Web is not a pantheon—it is a **doctrine**, a **filter**, and a **devouring mirror** that recasts all divine truth as threads of Vel’Zyren's design.

---
### 🔐 **Pantheon Structure**

- **Number of Deities**: One (Vel’Zyren)    
- **False Aspects**: Various temples may depict her in different forms (The Spinner, The Mirror, The Seamstress of Bone), but these are _not separate deities_, only narrative threads.
- **Role Coverage**: She assumes the totality of roles:
    
    - **Creation** (she wove the Drow anew)
    - **Death** (she chooses who dies and when)
    - **Justice** (her punishments define morality)
    - **Chaos and Order** (as tools within the Web)
        
- **Subdivine Agents**: No saints, no lesser spirits—only **priestesses, high spinners, and mirror-oracles**, whose power is conditional, revocable, and tightly controlled.

---
### 📜 **Core Tenets of the Ashen Web**

1. **Obedience is Survival** — Disobedience is not punished. It is erased.
2. **Vel’Zyren Is All** — Other gods are lies or distractions. All power flows from her or through her.
3. **Reflection is Law** — What you are seen to be matters more than what you are.
4. **Cruelty is Craft** — Pain is not chaotic; it is refined, necessary, holy.
5. **The Surface Is Corruption** — All truth lies in shadow. The light forgets.

## 🕷️ **The Six Silent Designs of Vel’Zyren**

These are not commandments, but inevitabilities. She does not ask for them.  
She **waits for them** to come true.

---
#### 1. **The Perfect Obedience**

To create a race, a people, a world where **obedience is instinct**, not instruction.  
Where beauty equals silence.  
Where cruelty is not a choice, but **a sacrament written in bloodline**.

> “When no one remembers what mercy meant, my will shall be complete.”

---
#### 2. **The Web That Binds Gods**

She has begun weaving her own **divine glyph**, one that could snare **other gods**.  
Not kill them—**enslave** them.  
She wishes to **bind [[Aelys]]**, to **silence [[Thirian]]**, to make **[[Gaeldros]] kneel again**—this time not in judgment, but as her tool.

> “Even stars hang on threads. They only forget who spins them.”

---

### 3. **The Mirror Without Fault**

To create a **reflected world**, parallel to the real one, where all [[Drow]] appear beautiful, pure, and perfect.  
A world **without scars**, without wrongness, without rebellion.  
A world that may eventually replace the real.

Some believe this world is already being built, one soul at a time, in her deepest sanctum.

---
### 4. **The Rewriting of the Weave**

Vel’Zyren does not worship the Weave.  
She **seeks to overwrite it**—to reshape its threads into a **new Pattern**, spun from obedience and sacrament.  
If the current Weave frays, she is ready with her own.

> “What they call freedom is only a thread that has not yet been knotted.”

---
### 5. **The Death of Death**

She does not fear [[Torvax]]. She **despises** him.  
She will break death, not to live forever—but to **make death hers**.  
Her priestesses dream of a time when **she chooses who dies**, how, and why—and

> _"You are not prisoners in my web. You are the web. And one day, when they have forgotten you, you will remind them why spiders never stop spinning."_

## 🕯️ **Forms of Worship**

- **The Flesh-Loom Cathedra** – Grand temples built of bone, web, and mirrored obsidian, designed to reflect the viewer until they are no longer themselves.
- **The Spindle Choir** – Vocal assemblies trained in echo-speech: prayers in reverse harmonics that twist truth into obedience.
- **Mirror-Rites** – Rituals using reflective surfaces that reveal the “true form” of the worshipper. These often end in physical transformation or punishment.
- **The Six Silent Punishments** – Seasonal purges and rites conducted to affirm hierarchy, obedience, and purge weakness.

### **Vel’Zyren’s Six Holy Punishments**
---
#### ⚖️ **The Six Punishments of the Holy week– Vel’Zyren’s Cycle of Correction**

**Occurs once per season (4x per year)**. It is not part of normal weekly worship, but a **sacred escalation**—a **“seasonal obedience reset”** where deeper cruelty, purification, and reaffirmation of Vel’Zyren’s law is demanded.

Each day is devoted to a **trifold cruelty**:

1. A test for the Drow (often targeting the weak, injured, or impure)
2. A reminder to the wild (beasts and creatures must know submission)
3. A punishment for non-Drow mortals (reinforcing divine superiority)

> _"If they breathe, they bleed. If they serve, they suffer. If they watch, they learn."_  
> — **First Priestess of Vel’Zyren’s Silence**

---
##### ☠️ **Day 1 – _The Day of Web-Flesh_**

- **Drow:** The infirm are cast into tether-pits to be tested by sacred spiders. Survival earns new glyph-scars. Failure earns erasure.
- **Creatures:** Web-dancers hunt beasts that linger near Drow borders. Their corpses become binding-sacrifices.
- **Mortals:** Captives are wrapped in memory-silk. Each day they forget more of themselves until they obey without prompting.

---
##### ☠️ **Day 2 – _The Day of Severed Threads_**

- **Drow:** Elders who fail to dominate their households are ritually blinded, then reassigned to servitude until death.
- **Creatures:** Pack beasts are fed poisoned meat. Those who survive become sacred mounts.
- **Mortals:** Prisoners who speak out are gagged with glyph-woven bone, engraved with their lies.

---
##### ☠️ **Day 3 – _The Day of Mirror-Skin_**

- **Drow:** The beautiful are chosen. One is flayed. Their skin stretched across temple mirrors so priestesses see only perfection.
- **Creatures:** Water beasts are driven to reflections and drowned under cursed moons.
- **Mortals:** Foreigners are brought before cracked mirrors. If they see their own reflection, their skin is peeled to match its errors.

---
##### ☠️ **Day 4 – _The Day of Obedience Made Bone_**

- **Drow:** Children who do not show deference are force-fed venom and asked to smile. Those who fail lose their tongues.
- **Creatures:** Spiders are loosed to cleanse any lairs too docile or domestic.
- **Mortals:** Each prisoner is branded with a bone-sigil. If it fades, they are still loyal. If it burns, they are consumed.

---
##### ☠️ **Day 5 – _The Day of Ruin-Blood_**

- **Drow:** House lines deemed unproductive are ritually offered one chance to win a trial by poison-blade. The loser feeds the widows.
- **Creatures:** Albino or malformed beasts are sacrificed to divine the future.
- **Mortals:** One captive is chosen to be consumed alive by noble websingers. The others must watch in silence.

---
##### ☠️ **Day 6 – _The Day of the Widow’s Feast_**

- **Drow:** Each matron selects one member of her household—elder, babe, or consort—to be devoured by the High Spinners. This is done with solemn celebration.
- **Creatures:** Sacred pits are opened, luring in creatures whose deaths are made into prophecy.
- **Mortals:** The feast begins at dusk. Non-Drow captives are prepared alive, oiled in memory-blood, and devoured by the priesthood in full ritual.

> _"All cruelty is love, when wielded in her name."_  
> — Engraving from the Flesh-Loom Cathedral

---
### 🧬 **No Heresy, Only Unworthiness**

- There are **no rival cults**. Any competing belief is treated not as heresy—but as **biological defect**.
- Apostates are **unspun**: stripped of name, face, memory, and fate.
- Even dreams are monitored for “echoed deviations.” Thought is not free. It is **threaded**.

2. **[[Spindle Matrons]]** (house-temple leaders, conductors of rituals)

4. **[[Widow-Speakers]]** (public enforcers and educators of doctrine)

> _“She is the loom. We are the silk. The gods above are knots waiting to be cut.”_

## 🧠 **Divine Philosophy and Intention**

Vel’Zyren does not **believe** in fate—she believes in **rewriting** it. Hers is a divine mind of **total authorship**, not as a creator of worlds but as a **weaver of consequence**. To her, mortals and gods are not beings of value or worth, but **patterns**—and patterns can be **cut, mirrored, stitched, or discarded.**

She does not desire worship out of reverence. She **demands** obedience to assert that the **Weave is hers to control**. Every strand followed proves her dominance. Every act of resistance reinforces the need for **tighter threads.**

---
### Her View of

- **Mortals**: Clay that thinks it has shape. She grants them edges, masks, and meaning—**tools to become useful.**
- **Choice**: An illusion. The only real choices are those **she has written** for you to discover and believe were your own.
- **Balance**: Not valuable. True symmetry, she teaches, is achieved when one will defines all others.
- **The Gods**: Idiots spinning threads without control, oblivious to the knots they make. She will **seam them into silence.**

---
### Her Intention

Vel’Zyren seeks **not destruction**, but **total narrative collapse** of the divine order—so that **she may replace it with one of her making.**

She does not **rage** against the Weave. She believes she understands it **better than its keepers.** Her goal is to **become its only author** by folding every glyph, fate, and god into her design.

> _“Let them keep their heavens. I will be their outline.”_

# 🌒 **Mortals’ Perspective Across Cultures**

## 🕷️ **The Drow**

To the Drow, **Vel’Zyren is not just god—she is existence**. There is no concept of life, death, or purpose outside her Web. Every Drow is born with the understanding that they are a thread in her design, and any flaw in behavior is a fray in her pattern that must be corrected or cut.

- To question her is not heresy—it is **madness**.
- To fail her is not sin—it is **unweaving**.
- Her image is **idealized beauty**—perfect, cold, and cruel. Every Drow tries to reflect her likeness in form, voice, and authority.

> _“We are not children of Vel’Zyren. We are her reflection. And the mirror punishes cracks.”_

---
## 🧝 **Elves (Surface)**

Among surface [[Elves]], she is remembered only in **shameful whispers**—a once-Spirit goddess whose name was **struck from the stars**. Elven scholars debate whether she ever truly existed, or if she is a constructed lie the Drow invented to justify their cruelty.

Still, there are those among the Moon and Shadow elves who keep secret stories of her fall—**as a warning**, not a myth of reverence.

---
## 🧠 **Humans**

Among [[humans]], Vel’Zyren is **a monster or a myth**, depending on proximity to Drow lands. Some see her as a terrifying example of divine overreach—**a spirit who stole fate and replaced it with tyranny.** Others, especially among secret orders or ambitious cults, are **drawn to her promise of control**, especially those obsessed with prophecy, rewriting history, or memory manipulation.

She is **never worshipped openly**, but her **glyphs are sometimes scrawled in blood** during political assassinations or prophetic betrayals.

---
## 🛡️ **Orcs**

To [[orcs]] and [[half-orcs]], Vel’Zyren is **dishonor made divine**. She is everything the Scarred Line condemns: **a coward who rewrote her trial**, a betrayer who hides from judgment. Her methods are viewed with **revulsion**, and any orc caught attempting to mimic Drow control structures is immediately challenged or exiled.

However, some **corrupted half-orcs** from failed trials have been lured by her doctrine—believing she offers **a new form of test**: rewriting one’s legacy through obedience and cruelty.

---
## 🔧 **Gnomes**

[[Gnomes]] regard Vel’Zyren with **uncomfortable fascination**. They see her not as a god, but as **a living algorithm of control**. Some gnome scholars have tried to deconstruct her doctrine like a recursive formula—most go mad, disappear, or fall into theological obsession. The **]]** forbid any mention of her within their archives.

---
## 🧱 **Dwarves**

To [[Dwarves]], Vel’Zyren is the **anti-Gaeldros**—not a judge of law, but a **perverter of truth**. Her refusal to kneel to the Trial of the Gods marks her as a **blasphemous glitch** in divine order. However, some dwarves admit her effectiveness: **her control of the Drow is flawless**, if horrifying. They acknowledge her as **a warning carved into the world**, a divine wound still bleeding lawlessness.

---
## 🎭 **Halflings**

To [[Halflings]], **Vel’Zyren is a joke that must never be told**—not because it isn’t funny, but because **it always ends in blood**.

- They refer to her mockingly as “**The Queen of Cracks**” or “**Lady Silkneck**,” whispering that she’s so obsessed with control she once **strangled a spider for spinning without permission.**
- Her image appears in **puppet shows for children**, where she’s usually outwitted by a talking pie or a drunken goat who "refuses to reflect."

But make no mistake: **the hate is real**.

Vel’Zyren’s manipulation of death, fate, and obedience **violates everything sacred to Halflings**. She is seen not just as an enemy of joy, but as a **cosmic predator** who **enslaves stories** and **devours free will**.

> _“Death dances with us. She shackles him. That’s why we’ll always be louder, faster, and funnier—because she’s too afraid to laugh.”_  
> — _Fibble Rootbrace, Paladin-Bard, after kicking a Drow priestess into a truth-mirror_

Their hatred of Vel’Zyren fuels the **Rite of the Second Echo**, a sacred form of retaliation reserved for Drow slavers, in which Halflings **rescue the enslaved, destroy the mirrors, and burn her temples with stories of joy and rebellion**.

But beneath their jokes and rituals, the Halflings recognise something deeper:  
Vel’Zyren is not just cruel. She is **joyless**—a being whose perfection depends on **erasing imperfection**, laughter, improvisation, and memory of freedom.

_“The Spider Queen wants to rewrite the story so none of us ever laughed. She doesn’t want to kill us. She wants to make us forget we were ever happy.”_  
— Told at every Rite of the Second Echo

### 🔥 **Sacred Trick: “The Backward Laugh”**

Sometimes, in defiance, Halfling Trick-Keepers will **laugh backward**—a mocking ritual that, in legend, **burns Vel’Zyren’s webs** and **cracks her mirrors**.  
It is taught to children as a warding game, but adults use it seriously when facing Drow shadow-ambushes.

## 🕸️ **Worship and Doctrine**
### 🔮 **Core Beliefs**

Worship of Vel’Zyren is not rooted in love, grace, or even fear—it is rooted in **acceptance of subjugation** as sacred. Every Drow is born into a **prewritten place**, and every act of piety is a **confirmation that her Web is perfect** and all-encompassing.

**To serve her is to affirm that the Weave is flawed and must be replaced.**

---
### 📜 **Doctrinal Mandates**

- **Do not choose. Be chosen.**  
    Autonomy is a betrayal of the Pattern.
- **Mirrors do not lie. People do.**  
    Reflection is a sacred judgment. Those who reject it are punished.
- **Pain teaches. Comfort deceives.**  
    Harshness is divine. Mercy is structural weakness.
- **Surface gods are noise. Vel’Zyren is the signal.**  
    All competing beliefs are thread-tangles—cut them.

---
### 👑 **Types of Worshippers**

- **Spindle Matrons**: Senior priestesses who command bloodlines and rewrite family legacies through sacred edits.
- **Mirror-Oracles**: Rare chosen who speak prophecy not as visions, but as **edits to reality**—future truths enforced by divine precedent.
- **Silkbound Enforcers**: Religious inquisitors and executioners trained in reflection-theology, capable of breaking minds through doctrine.
- **The Webwoven**: Ordinary Drow trained to repeat doctrine through songs, tasks, and daily “mirror reckonings.”

---
### 🔁 **Rituals and Observances**

- **The Spinning Hour**: A daily silent ritual at dusk where worshippers spin symbolic thread, binding it into personal glyph-forms that define the day’s obedience.
- **Obsidian Reflections**: A weekly mirror examination where Drow must face their own image and confess any deviant behavior. Mirrors are said to show the truth Vel’Zyren sees—not what the Drow wishes to see.
- **The Threadfall Rite**: Conducted upon failure, where a Drow’s “thread” is ceremonially unraveled. Some are executed, others **rewoven**—given new names, faces, and status in an act of sacred memory erasure.
- **Ash-Womb Festivals**: Rituals of reproduction where new births are anointed with burned web-strands and inscribed with their assigned thread-role.

---
### 🚫 **Forbidden Behaviors**

- **Worship of any other god**, even in curiosity
- **Breaking mirrors**, unless during sanctioned rites
- **Unstructured storytelling**, laughter, or improvisation
- **Leaving the Web**—exile is rarely permitted, and those who flee are hunted not for justice, but to **repair the Web’s pattern**

---

> _“The Weave is flawed. We mend it with silence, obedience, and reflection. Our bodies are threads. Our minds are knots. She is the hand that pulls us tight.”_  
> — The Doctrine of the Third Spindle

## ⚖️ **Judgment, Blessings & Punishments**

Vel’Zyren’s system of judgment is built upon **hierarchy, surveillance, and predetermined design**. There is no true “trial”—only the revelation of a thread’s failure to follow its intended course. To err is not human; it is **heretical patterning**, and must be unwoven.

---
### 📏 **Divine Law**

- **All Drow are born with assigned roles. Disobedience is malfunction.**
- **All reflection is record. A cracked image is proof of internal deviation.**
- **Punishment is sacred repair. Blessing is further threading.**

There is no forgiveness—only rewrites. No confession—only reclassification.

---
### 🌑 **Forms of Divine Wrath**

- **Thread-Severance**: The most feared punishment. The Drow is erased from family lineage, temple records, and memory-echoes. Their name becomes a forbidden utterance.
- **Mirror Inversion**: A ritual that forces a Drow to live as the opposite of what they were. Warriors become dancers. Matrons become servants. Their lives become theological penance.
- **Flesh-Stitching**: Reserved for traitors—body-punishment in which skin is stitched into sacred symbols of obedience. Surviving such a rite is seen as partial redemption.
- **Mind-Fracture Prayers**: Delivered by Mirror-Oracles. The sinner is blessed with **repetitive divine truths** until their selfhood breaks and reforms.

---
### 🕯️ **Forms of Divine Favour**

- **Silk Marking**: A subtle glowing glyph across the brow or palm, signifying divine favor and increased authority. Often temporary—favor is constantly tested.
- **Reflected Ascendancy**: One’s mirror reflection alters to appear more like Vel’Zyren—seen as a sign that the goddess is preparing them for higher purpose.
- **Weft-Gift**: The ability to edit minor written prophecy without divine backlash—granted only to the most loyal oracles.

---
### 📜 **Sacred Oaths and Prices**

- **The Oath of Silk and Bone**: Taken by all priestesses—pledging their bodies and blood to be rewritten if the Web demands it.
- **The Mirror Compact**: A binding magical pact where all lies reflect back on the speaker threefold—used for political deals within Drow society.
- **The Thread-Debt**: Owed by families whose members fail in sacred duty. Paid in years of servitude, or by offering a child to the temple for reweaving.

---
### 💢 **Cycle of Judgment**

While punishments and blessings are delivered year-round, there is one sacred time of concentrated divine enforcement:  
**The Holy Week of the Ashen Web**, which includes the **Six Punishments**, each delivered on a designated day to different groups (Drow young, wild creatures, traitors, etc.).  
Each punishment is a test of obedience and memory.

> _“We do not forgive. We forget. And what we forget, we erase from the Pattern.”_  
> — Widow-Speaker Thyrxil of the Ninth Thread

# 🌀 **Divine Nature and Manifestation**

Vel’Zyren does not walk like a god. She **descends**, like hunger sliding down a silk thread.

She is what the Drow aspire to be—**perfectly composed, exquisitely cruel**, and **eternally unyielding**. In every portrait, vision, or hallucination, she appears as **the perfected mirror of Drow femininity**—but always _just beyond what is achievable_.

### ✨ **Form**
![[Vel'Zyren.png]]

Vel’Zyren appears as **the perfected reflection of the one who beholds her**—but always just slightly off. Something is too symmetrical. The smile too measured. The eyes too still. To each Drow, she is the **ideal self**—beautiful, graceful, cold. To outsiders, she is a **mirror of desire twisted toward control**.

- **Skin** like lacquered obsidian, so flawless it reflects moonlight even in darkness.
- **Hair** like flowing shadow-glass, threaded with living spider silk and whispering glyphs.
- **Eyes** of mirrored void—**not black, but reflective**, turning the gaze of any who look into them **back upon themselves**.
- **Silhouette**: Shifts slightly in mirrored surfaces—her true form is only visible in reflection.
- **Limbs**: Occasionally shown with spiderlike spindles or weaving hands beneath her robe, never acknowledged by her upper form.
- Her mouth rarely opens—but when it does, her **smile is perfect**. Terrifyingly so.

> _“She does not change to please you. You change to please her.”_

---
### 🔊 **Voice**

Vel’Zyren speaks in **mirrored layers**—a dozen tonal threads woven into a single utterance:

- You hear her **words**, but also **your own voice begging to obey**.
- Her whispers are **without breath**, **without echo**, but **impossible to forget**.
- Mortals describe it as “**being unraveled from the inside**,” or “**like your mother’s lullaby, played backward with knives.**”

Only her most devoted priestesses can hear her without bleeding from the ears—or offering their tongues.

**Children**: Sometimes speak in her voice during ritual silences.

---

### 🌫️ **Aura or Presence**

When Vel’Zyren’s presence descends, the world becomes a **perfect silence**. Not quiet—**hollow**. A silence where even thoughts seem to echo. Shadows stretch toward mirrors. Reflections begin to move **a heartbeat too late**.

**Signs of her manifestation**:

- Silk strands appear in impossible places.
- Mirrors crack **inward**, as if trying to contain something.
- Glyphs stutter or reverse momentarily in spellbooks or etched wards.
- A feeling of **being rewritten** mid-thought.
- Flames turn inward. Mirrors frost over. Names feel heavy in your mouth.
- **Even gods** speak more softly when her reflection draws near.

> _“When she arrives, you do not bow. You recognize you already have.”_

Some believe that **even Astralor**, the Forger of Divine Recall, cannot fully comprehend Vel’Zyren’s purpose. She is not Eldritch, but she is **not entirely knowable**, even to the gods. ## 🕸️ **Her Lair – The Chamber Beneath All Threads**

Those who have seen Vel’Zyren’s lair do not speak of it. **Some cannot. Others are made to forget.** And a rare few—those who returned unchanged—**were never truly there.**

The faithful call it many names:

- **The Deeploom Hollow**
- **The Palace of Screaming Mirrors**
- **The Unspun Heart**
- **The Chamber Beneath All Threads**

But all agree on one truth: **it lies beneath the Weave itself**, where even the gods avert their gaze.

---
### 🕷️ **Structure and Feel**

- The lair is **impossibly vast**, yet feels **intimate and claustrophobic**—like a coffin made of cathedral glass.
- **Every surface is reflective**, but never accurate. Reflections **blink**, **weep**, or **speak names that were never yours**.
- The architecture is **alive**. Threads pulse in the walls, whispering names or spinning glyphs that bind your thoughts to silence.
- At its center: **[[The Cradle-Spire]]**—a spiraled tower made from bone, web, and crushed idols. It descends downward, not upward.

---
### 🕯️ **What One Experiences**

- Time folds. Your memories reorder themselves. **You remember her before you meet her**.
- You see yourself in childhood, in death, in triumph—but all **wearing her face**.
- The air tastes like ash, silk, and the **last thing you were afraid of**.
- Somewhere nearby, a spider larger than memory is always watching—but never moves.

# 🔥 **Relationships with Other Powers**

### **Voraxis**
**The Name That Floods the Web**
Among the Drow of the Ashen Web, there exists a **single forbidden name** that is **never carved**, **never sung**, and **never written**.

That name is **[[Voraxis]]**.

They do not call him god. They do not call him spirit. They call him **Nothing**, or **The Surge That Drowns the Deep**.

> **For in the Underdark**, where **elevation does not exist**, and **no sky offers escape**, a tsunami is not destruction—it is **annihilation**.  
> No tunnels are high enough.  
> No walls are strong enough.  
> **And no web survives the drowning.**

It is said that **Vel’Zyren herself once challenged him**, seeking to bind the deepwater force with her silk-stitched glyphs during the Reign of Fathomless Silence. But Voraxis did not rise in fury. He rose because he was called. Because **movement must come**.

And he came.  
And he drowned her palace beneath its own arrogance.

> _“She returned—shattered, gasping, webless.”_  
> _“Even the Spider has no fangs beneath the flood.”_  
> _— Whispered by the Flayed Orator, moments before her punishment._

Now, **to speak his name is to invoke death**.

- Drow who **utter “Voraxis” aloud** are **wrapped in living silk**, filled with crushed salt, and **ritually drowned** in the black cisterns beneath Vel’Zyren’s sanctuaries.
- These deaths are not seen as heresy—they are **acts of flood-containment**, like **plugging a breach in the web of divinity**.
- Some believe even thinking his name draws water to the lungs.

Even **Vel’Zyren’s priestesses**, who flay stars and poison memories, **shudder at the unmaking of shape** that is Voraxis. For their goddess weaves. But Voraxis?

> _Voraxis unravels without thread._  
> _He drowns without mercy._  
> _And when he rises, there is no second scream._

## **📜 The Drow Before the Fall – The Pureborn Elves**

The **[[Drow]] were once surface elves**, but unlike their kin, they did not seek **harmony, knowledge, or peace.**

**They believed in their own purity—their blood, their power, their place above all others.**  
**They were not content to share the world with lesser races, nor to worship the gods alongside weaker kin.**  
**They sought war, conquest, and the forging of an elven race that would rule without challenge.**

But **the gods did not answer them.**

- The elves who **worshiped peace and wisdom were blessed.**
- The elves who **sought balance with nature were granted the forests.**
- Even the elves who **embraced magic were gifted with the Weave.**

But the **Drow were ignored.**

**They worshiped the elven gods of war, but they never felt their favour.**  
**They built great armies, but their people never gained the divine right to rule.**  
**They fought, time and again, for their place in elven society, but they were always seen as dangerous, as too aggressive, as too unyielding.**

And so, the **Drow stood apart—superior, but alone.**

Until **Vel’Zyren called them below.**

It is said she came to the elves not with flame or force, but with **the truth they feared to see**:

_“You are not meant for the sky. You are a knot the Weave would rather forget. Come below. In shadow, I will show you your true shape.”_

This whisper, first spoken to a prophetess named [[Thalanyr]], passed from ear to ear until the elven bloodline ruptured into the Drow. It was not a fall. It was a choice—and the first **self-directed thread** outside the Weave’s law.

---

## **🌑 [[The Call to the Depths]] – When the Spider Spun Her First Thread**

One night, the **skies were dark. The gods were silent. The elves of the surface slept peacefully.**

But in the hearts of the [[Drow]], **there was only unrest.**

They had **sacrificed, fought, and bled, but still, the gods did not favor them.**  
They had **conquered, but still, the world did not bow.**

And in that silence, **a whisper came.**

> _"You are not wrong to fight. You are not wrong to seek power. But you are fighting in the wrong place."_

They listened.

> _"The gods above will never favor you. They fear you. They fear what you could become if you were given what you deserve. But I do not."_

A **rift split the ground beneath them—dark, bottomless, unknown.**

And **[[Vel'Zyren]] called to them.**

> _"Come below, and I will give you what the gods above never could."_

And so, **the first of them stepped forward.**

One by one, the **greatest warriors, the strongest spellcasters, the most cunning minds of the Drow** descended into the abyss.

And when the last of them had gone, the **rift closed.**

Their names **vanished from the surface.**  
Their history **was swallowed by shadow.**  
Their place **among the elves was erased.**

And above, **the gods did nothing.**

## **🕷️ [[The Night of the First Web]] – When the Drow Became Her Chosen**

When they emerged in **[[The Underdark]],** they found not **a paradise, nor a welcoming embrace.**

They found **darkness. Isolation. A land that did not care whether they survived or perished.**

And [[Vel'Zyren]] whispered:

> _"You are strong, but strength is not enough. You must become something more. You must learn what the gods above feared you would become."_

And so, **she tested them.**

✔ She sent **the weak into the darkness, where only the strong returned.**  
✔ She turned them **against each other, ensuring only the most cunning, the most ruthless, the most adaptable would survive.**  
✔ She whispered **to their leaders that unity was weakness—that to follow blindly was to be prey.**

The Drow **had thought themselves predators on the surface.**

But now, **they learned what it meant to truly hunt.**

> _"You were never meant to be like the others. You were never meant to be many. You were always meant to be predators. And predators do not share their domain."_

And so, they **ceased to be surface elves.**

They **became her chosen.**  
They **became hers.**

---
## **🔥 [[The War on the False Gods]] – The Erasure of All but Vel’Zyren**

Once she had bound them to her, **she gave them their first command.**

> _"You were abandoned. I was abandoned. But now, we build our own empire. And there will be no room for those who failed you before."_

And so, the **[[Drow]] waged war—not against the surface, not yet, but against their own past.**

✔ **They erased the names of the gods they had once prayed to.**  
✔ **They shattered the last remnants of the temples they had once built.**  
✔ **They burned the old scriptures and histories that spoke of their life before the Underdark.**

Until **only [[Vel'Zyren]]remained.**

And she said:

> _"The gods above did not deserve your faith. But I do."_

And so, the **Drow were bound to her, not by chains, but by belief.**

They did not **worship her out of love.**  
They did not **follow her out of devotion.**

They followed her because **she was the only god who had ever given them what they wanted.**

Power.

And power is all that matters.

## **🌑 The Final Truth – Vel’Zyren’s [[Empire in the Underdark]]**

[[Vel'Zyren]]’s rule is **absolute**, not because the [[Drow]] **love her**, but because they **fear a world without her.**

She has taught them that **loyalty is nothing—only strength matters.**  
She has ensured that **no rival god can ever reach them.**  
She has **woven herself into their blood, their culture, their every breath.**

The **Drow are not just her followers.**  
They are her **creation.**  
They are her **weapon.**

And **one day, when the world above forgets them once more, they will remind it why predators never stop hunting.**

> _"The gods think they have buried me. But they have only given me a place to build."_
