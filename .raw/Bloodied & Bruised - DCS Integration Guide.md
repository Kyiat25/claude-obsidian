# Bloodied & Bruised: DCS Integration Guide

> _"At half strength, a creature reveals its true nature - the coward breaks, the warrior rages, and the ancient beast remembers why it has survived so long."_

---
## Overview

The Bloodied & Bruised system transforms the 50% HP threshold from a narrative checkpoint into a mechanical crisis point that integrates with every DCS system. When creatures cross this threshold, they don't just continue fighting - they undergo psychological and physiological transformations that fundamentally alter combat dynamics.

**This is the moment where survival instinct overrides tactics.**

### Integration with Dynamic Combat Systems

The Bloodied threshold triggers cascading effects across all DCS systems:

**Momentum System** → Resets to 0, forcing initiative recalculation  
**Breaking Points** → Leader bloodied triggers auto-Rally (morale crisis)  
**Focus & Sanity** → Mental attributes tested under extreme pressure  
**Called Shots** → New vulnerabilities exposed (Frailties)  
**Damage Reduction** → May increase (Strength) or decrease (Frailty)  
**Evasion** → Desperation affects defensive capability  
**Death Throes** → Explosive finale prepared when creature falls

---
## The Bloodied Condition

### When It Triggers

**A creature becomes BLOODIED at 50% HP or below.**

**Examples:**

- Goblin (7 HP) → Bloodied at 3-4 HP
- Troll (84 HP) → Bloodied at 42 HP
- Adult Red Dragon (256 HP) → Bloodied at 128 HP

**Critical Rule:** Bloodied effects trigger **once per encounter** when the threshold is first crossed. If a creature is healed above 50% and drops below again, effects don't re-trigger.

---
### The Four Simultaneous Effects

When a creature becomes bloodied, **all four** of these effects activate immediately:

|**Effect**|**Purpose**|**Player Opportunity**|
|---|---|---|
|**Frailty**|Exposes tactical weakness|Exploit the vulnerability|
|**Strength**|Grants desperation resilience|Adapt tactics to compensate|
|**Ability**|Once-per-combat power surge|Prepare countermeasures|
|**Death Throes**|On-death explosive finale|Position safely before kill|

## SYSTEM 1: Frailty (Tactical Weakness)

Frailties expose vulnerabilities that players can exploit to finish bloodied creatures.

### Common Frailty Types

**Defensive Frailties:**

- **-2 Evasion DC** (exposed wounds, compromised mobility)
- **-2 DR** (armor cracked, hide torn, scales chipped)
- **Disadvantage on saves** (specific type: Dex, Con, Wis, etc.)
- **Vulnerable to damage type** (fire, cold, bludgeoning, etc.)

**Offensive Frailties:**

- **Disadvantage on attacks** (wounded, distracted, panicked)
- **-2 to damage rolls** (weakened strikes)
- **Reduced range/reach** (wings damaged, legs injured)

**Mental Frailties:**

- **-2 Focus** (broken concentration, panic setting in)
- **-2 Sanity** (fear creeping in, doubt emerging)
- **Disadvantage on Rally attempts** (leader's voice wavers)

**Momentum Frailties:**

- **Cannot gain Momentum** (locked at current value until stabilized)
- **Momentum floor raised to -1** (can't recover fully)

---

### Frailty by Creature Type

- **Beasts:** Exposed flank, wounded leg (-2 Evasion, speed -10 ft)
- **Humanoids:** Broken armor, shield dropped (-2 DR torso, lose shield bonus)
- **Giants:** Frustrated rage (disadvantage on Dex saves, easier to avoid)
- **Dragons:** Damaged scales (wings -3 DR instead of -2, easier to ground)
- **Fiends:** Infernal wound (vulnerable to radiant damage)
- **Celestials:** Divine light dimming (-2 to healing/support abilities)
- **Undead (corporeal):** Brittle bones (critical hits reduce DR to 0 instead of half)
- **Undead (incorporeal):** Torn essence (-2 Evasion DC)
- **Constructs:** Malfunction (disadvantage on complex actions, may skip turn on 1-2)
- **Aberrations:** Reality anchor damaged (disadvantage on saves vs banishment/reality magic)

---
### DCS Integration: Frailty Effects

**With Momentum System:**

- Frailty often prevents Momentum gain
- Creates death spiral as creature becomes easier to hit
- Resets Momentum to 0 when triggered

**With Called Shots:**

- Frailties often reduce specific location DR
- Example: Dragon wings become DR 4 instead of 6 when bloodied
- Makes finishing called shots more effective

**With Breaking Points:**

- Leader Frailties visible to troops
- Troops more likely to fail morale if leader shows obvious weakness
- Adds +2 to Breaking Point DC if leader's Frailty is dramatic (bleeding, limping, etc.)

## SYSTEM 2: Strength (Desperation Resilience)

Strengths represent the creature fighting harder, moving faster, or becoming more dangerous when wounded.

### Common Strength Types

**Offensive Strengths:**

- **+2 to damage rolls** (fury, desperation, last stand)
- **Advantage on attacks** (berserker rage, nothing left to lose)
- **Extra attack on Multiattack** (desperate flurry)
- **Weapon Reach +5 ft** (reckless overextension)

**Defensive Strengths:**

- **+2 DR** (adrenaline dulls pain, thick hide contracts)
- **Resistance to damage type** (elemental fury protects, undead hardening)
- **+2 Evasion DC** (fight-or-flight reflexes, supernatural speed)
- **Regeneration** (1d6 HP per turn for 3 rounds)

**Movement Strengths:**

- **Speed +10 ft** (adrenaline surge, desperate flight readiness)
- **Ignore difficult terrain** (crashing through obstacles)
- **Climb/swim speed equals walk speed** (desperate escape options)

**Mental Strengths:**

- **+2 Focus** (clarity in crisis, battle focus)
- **+2 Sanity** (defiant resolve, "you can't break me")
- **Advantage on concentration checks** (magical desperation)

**Momentum Strengths:**

- **Gain +2 Momentum immediately** (surge of determination)
- **Momentum cap increased by +1** (can reach +4 instead of +3)
- **Rally attempts gain advantage** (inspiring last stand)

---

### Strength by Creature Type

- **Beasts:** Pack Tactics intensifies (+2 to hit when ally adjacent, was advantage before)
- **Humanoids:** Battle Cry (+2 to damage, allies gain +1 Momentum)
- **Giants:** Reckless Power (advantage on attacks, enemies have advantage back)
- **Dragons:** Elemental Aura intensifies (10 ft → 15 ft range, +1d6 damage)
- **Fiends:** Hellfire Weapons (+1d6 fire damage on all attacks)
- **Celestials:** Radiant Burst (heal allies 2d8 HP in 30 ft radius, once)
- **Undead (corporeal):** Undead Persistence (stay at 1 HP on killing blow, DC 15 Con save)
- **Undead (incorporeal):** Phase Shift (resistance to all damage for 2 rounds)
- **Constructs:** Overcharge Protocol (+3 damage, +2 speed, lasts 3 rounds then malfunction)
- **Aberrations:** Reality Distortion (attackers disadvantage to hit, madness aura)

---
### DCS Integration: Strength Effects

**With Momentum System:**

- Strengths often grant immediate Momentum boost
- May increase Momentum ceiling
- Can trigger "Bloodied Surge" where creature becomes more dangerous

**With Rally System:**

- Leader Strengths make Rally more likely to succeed
- Visible determination inspires troops (+2 to Rally DC)
- May grant advantage on Rally check

**With Focus/Sanity:**

- Mental Strengths shore up breaking morale
- +2 Focus makes concentration nearly unbreakable
- +2 Sanity prevents Breaking Point failures

## SYSTEM 3: Ability (Once-Per-Combat Power)

Bloodied Abilities are dramatic moves that trigger once when bloodied and cannot be used again this encounter.

### Ability Categories

**Aggressive Abilities:**

- **Desperate Strike:** Make extra attack with advantage, +2d6 damage
- **Multiattack Upgrade:** Gain extra attack in Multiattack for rest of combat
- **Area Attack:** Sweep/cleave hitting all adjacent enemies
- **Reckless Assault:** Advantage on all attacks, enemies have advantage vs you

**Defensive Abilities:**

- **Defensive Stance:** +3 Evasion DC, can't make attacks, lasts until you attack
- **Shell Up:** DR +4 all locations, speed 0, lasts 2 rounds
- **Evasive Maneuver:** Immediately move up to full speed without provoking
- **Mirror Image:** Create 1d4 illusory duplicates (as spell)

**Tactical Abilities:**

- **Battle Command:** All allies gain +1 Momentum and advantage on next attack
- **Tactical Retreat:** Disengage + move double speed
- **Reposition:** Swap places with willing ally within 30 ft
- **Called Shot Master:** Next called shot auto-succeeds (no save)

**Transformation Abilities:**

- **Rage Form:** Enter rage (as Barbarian), lasts 3 rounds
- **Shadow Meld:** Become invisible until you attack
- **Elemental Infusion:** Gain resistance + deal extra elemental damage
- **Size Change:** Enlarge or Reduce self (as spell)

**Summon/Support Abilities:**

- **Rally Cry:** Force all allies to reroll failed saves
- **Summon Reinforcements:** Call 1d4+1 minions (appear next round)
- **Inspiring Presence:** Allies gain temp HP equal to your Cha mod × 3
- **Shared Pain:** Link with ally, split damage between you

## SYSTEM 4: Death Throes (Explosive Finale)

Death Throes trigger when the creature is reduced to 0 HP, creating final consequences that reward careful positioning.

### Death Throes by Type

**Explosions:**

- **Elemental Explosion:** Creature explodes, damage matching type
    - 10 ft radius for Small/Medium
    - 15 ft radius for Large
    - 20 ft radius for Huge
    - 30 ft radius for Gargantuan
    - Damage: CR × 1d6 (Dex save DC 12 + CR/2 for half)

**Curses:**

- **Death Curse:** Killer cursed (disadvantage on saves until Greater Restoration)
- **Vengeful Spirit:** Haunts killer, disadvantage on rolls against creature's allies
- **Final Words:** Cryptic prophecy or information revealed

**Environmental:**

- **Timber:** Large+ creatures fall, crushing area
    - 10×10 ft for Large
    - 15×15 ft for Huge
    - 20×20 ft for Gargantuan
    - Dex save DC 12 + CR/3 or take 2d10 bludgeoning + prone

**Transformation:**

- **Spawn Creation:** Death creates 1d4 lesser creatures
- **Possession Attempt:** Spirit tries to possess nearest creature (Cha save)
- **Plague Release:** Disease or poison spreads (Con save)

**Support:**

- **Final Rally:** All allies gain +2 Momentum, advantage next attack
- **Dying Inspiration:** Allies heal 2d8 HP in 30 ft radius
- **Martyrdom:** One ally gains fallen creature's remaining HP as temp HP

**Destruction:**

- **Arcane Boom:** Magical explosion (force damage, 15 ft radius, 3d10)
- **Disintegration:** Leaves no body, equipment destroyed
- **Petrifying Gas:** Cloud lingers, Con save or petrified 1 round

---

### Death Throes Examples from PDF

**Angels (Deva, Planetar, Solar):**

- **Font of Celestial Light:** Burst of radiant energy heals allies 4d8, damages fiends/undead 4d8

**Balor:**

- **Taken from Stat Block:** Explodes in fire and shadow, 10 ft, 20d6 (Dex save half)

**Mephits (all types):**

- **Death Burst (Taken from Stat Block):** Each type has unique burst
    - Dust: Blinding cloud
    - Ice: Ice shards
    - Magma: Lava splash
    - Smoke: Choking cloud
    - Steam: Scalding burst

**Giants (all):**

- **Timber:** Massive body falls, creatures in 15 ft make Dex save DC 15 or take 3d10 bludgeoning + prone

**Elementals (all):**

- **Elemental Explosion:** Return to home plane violently
    - Air: Wind blast, pushed 20 ft
    - Earth: Rock shrapnel, 3d8 bludgeoning
    - Fire: Fire burst, 3d10 fire
    - Water: Water wave, knocked prone

**Undead (various):**

- **Death's Embrace:** Necrotic burst, 2d8 necrotic, 10 ft radius
- Used by: Banshee, Ghost, Specter, Wraith

**Gorgon:**

- **Petrifying Gas Cloud:** 15 ft radius, Con save DC 15 or petrified for 1 round

**Lich (with phylactery):**

- **Violent Soul Transfer:** Body explodes, 4d10 force, soul flees to phylactery

**Lich (no phylactery):**

- **Fearful Wail:** Final scream, Sanity save DC 18 or frightened + 1d6 Sanity damage

---

### DCS Integration: Death Throes Effects

**With Positioning:**

- Melee characters must weigh risk vs reward
- Ranged characters gain advantage by staying back
- Called shots to prevent Death Throes? (kill via decapitation = no explosion?)

**With Momentum:**

- Successful Death Throes damage may grant allies Momentum
- Players losing HP to Death Throes lose Momentum
- Creates final swing in close fights

**With Breaking Points:**

- Dramatic Death Throes may trigger early Breaking Points
- Example: Balor exploding might force morale check on demon allies at DC +2

## Auto Rally Attempt (Enemy Leaders)

**CRITICAL INTEGRATION POINT:** When enemy leaders become bloodied, they trigger an automatic Rally attempt.

### The Auto Rally Trigger

**Who:** Enemy leaders or highest CR creature (if no designated leader)

**When:** Immediately when bloodied threshold crossed

**Range:** 120 feet (all enemy allies within range affected)

**What:** Leader must attempt Rally check (no choice)

**Why:** Represents their cry of defiance or display of fury echoing across battlefield

---

### Auto Rally Mechanics

**Rally Check:** 1d20 + Focus modifier vs DC 15 (or current casualty tier DC if higher)

| **Roll Result** | **Outcome**                                                               |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Success**     | Enemy allies hold; restore +1 Momentum to all within range (max +3)       |
| **Failure**     | Enemy allies lose -1 Momentum; leader's cry rings hollow                  |
| **Natural 20**  | All enemy allies reset to 0 Momentum + advantage on next attack           |
| **Natural 1**   | All enemy allies lose -2 Momentum + leader cannot Rally again this combat |


**Legendary Enemy Leaders:** Auto-succeed this Rally once per combat (can choose when)

---

### Why This Matters

**Creates Dramatic Moment:**

- Everyone at table pays attention when leader bloodied
- Will the Rally succeed or fail?
- Changes entire combat flow

**Tactical Decision Point:**

- Do players focus-fire to kill leader immediately after bloodied?
- Do they spread damage to trigger more Frailties instead?
- Is leader's Focus high enough that Rally will likely succeed?

**Integration with Morale:**

- Successful Rally prevents cascading rout
- Failed Rally accelerates morale collapse
- Natural 1 can break entire enemy force

---

### Example: Orc Warlord

**Setup:**

- Orc Warlord (CR 4, Leader, Focus +2)
- 8 orc warriors (Focus +0, following leader)
- Party reduces Warlord from 60 HP to 28 HP (bloodied)

**Bloodied Effects Trigger:**

1. **Frailty:** -2 AC (exposed wounds)
2. **Strength:** +2 damage (rage)
3. **Ability:** Battle Cry (allies advantage next attack)
4. **Death Throes:** Explodes 10 ft for 2d6 on death (noted for later)

**Auto Rally Attempt:**

- Warlord rolls 1d20 + 2 (Focus) = 14 vs DC 15
- **Fails by 1**
- All 8 orc warriors lose -1 Momentum
- Warriors now at -1 Momentum (easier to hit, lower initiative)

**Player Response:**

- Rogue: "His troops are shaking! Focus the Warlord!"
- Wizard: "I'm staying 15 feet back - he explodes when he dies"
- Fighter: "I've got advantage hitting him now with his -2 AC"

**Next Round:**

- Party kills Warlord (Death Throes: 2d6 to anyone within 10 ft)
- All 8 orcs must make Breaking Point check DC 17 (leader death +2)
- Average orc has Sanity +0, rolls d20 vs 17
- 5 fail: Rout (flee toward exit)
- 3 succeed: Fight on at -2 Momentum (cumulative, were already -1)

**Result:** Combat effectively over, PCs can pursue or let them flee

## Integration with Full DCS Systems

### Momentum System Integration

**Bloodied Triggers Momentum Reset:**

- When bloodied, Momentum resets to 0 (not to current total)
- Represents psychological shock of major injury
- Forces Side Initiative recalculation next round

**Momentum Modifications:**

- Some Frailties lock Momentum at specific values
- Some Strengths boost Momentum by +2 immediately
- Death spiral vs surge depends on effects

**Example:**

- Dragon at +4 Momentum (dominating)
- Gets bloodied by critical hit
- Momentum resets to 0
- Gains Strength: +2 Momentum immediately
- Now at +2 Momentum (still dangerous but reduced)

---

### Breaking Points & Rally Integration

**Leader Bloodied = Auto Rally Attempt:**

- Covered in detail above
- This is THE critical integration point

**Bloodied Visible to Troops:**

- Enemies see leader's Frailty
- If dramatic (limping, bleeding, armor cracked): +2 to Breaking Point DC
- If Strength visible (rage, glowing with power): -2 to Breaking Point DC

**Multiple Leaders:**

- First bloodied triggers one Auto Rally
- Second bloodied triggers another (if different leader)
- Can create "Rally cascade" if multiple leaders

---

### Mental Attributes Integration

**Focus Effects:**

- High Focus leaders have better Rally attempts
- Low Focus leaders often fail Auto Rally
- Failed Rally at bloodied can cascade into rout

**Sanity Effects:**

- Bloodied creatures with low Sanity panic (Frailty likely mental)
- High Sanity creatures remain defiant (Strength likely mental)
- Affects how creatures react to being wounded

**Mental Frailties/Strengths:**

- -2 Focus = concentration vulnerable
- +2 Sanity = Rally advantage
- Creates psychological dimension to bloodied

---

### Called Shots Integration

**Bloodied Exposes Vulnerabilities:**

- Frailties often reduce location-specific DR
- Called shots become more effective after bloodied
- May create new called shot options

**Targeting Bloodied Locations:**

- If wing damaged at bloodied, called shot to wing easier
- If armor cracked at bloodied, torso DR reduced
- Players should ask "what changed when it got bloodied?"

**Tactical Sequencing:**

- Option 1: Called shot BEFORE bloodied to trigger it
- Option 2: Wait until bloodied, THEN called shot (easier)
- Risk/reward decision

---

### Injury System Integration

**Bloodied as Injury Threshold:**

- 50% HP = Major Injury tier
- Bloodied effects can overlap with injury rolls
- Some injuries trigger Frailties
- Some Strengths offset injury penalties

**Double Penalty Risks:**

- Getting bloodied + Major Injury same attack
- Could get -2 AC (Frailty) + -2 AC (injury)
- Creates dramatic death spirals

**Recovery Interaction:**

- Healing above 50% doesn't remove Frailty
- Bloodied effects last full encounter
- Injuries may last longer (need treatment)

## Designing Custom Bloodied Effects

### Step-by-Step Process

**1. Analyze Creature Nature**

- What is its fighting style?
- What are its natural defenses?
- What is its psychological makeup?
- How does it react to pain/fear?

**2. Design Frailty (Expose Weakness)**

- What breaks when wounded? (armor, speed, confidence)
- What becomes vulnerable? (specific location, damage type)
- What stops working? (ability, attack, defense)

**3. Design Strength (Show Resilience)**

- What compensates for injury? (rage, determination)
- What intensifies under pressure? (elemental aura, damage)
- What activates in crisis? (hidden power, transformation)

**4. Design Ability (Dramatic Move)**

- What desperate tactic would this use once?
- What hidden capability emerges?
- What changes its combat role?

**5. Design Death Throes (Final Act)**

- What happens when this dies dramatically?
- What would make the kill memorable?
- What consequences should the killer face?

---

### Balancing Guidelines

**Frailty + Strength Should Roughly Balance:**

- If Frailty is severe (-3 AC), Strength should be moderate (+1 damage)
- If Frailty is mild (-1 save), Strength can be significant (+3 damage)
- Creates dynamic without making creature too weak OR too strong

**Ability Should Be Once-Per-Combat Impactful:**

- Strong enough players notice and react
- Not so strong it feels unfair
- Thematic to creature type

**Death Throes Should Be Avoidable:**

- Positioning matters
- Saves offered when reasonable
- Damage scaled to CR (CR × 1d6 is good baseline)

---

### Example: Custom Bloodied Design

**Creature:** Adult Green Dragon (CR 15)

**Nature Analysis:**

- Cunning, patient, sadistic
- Poison breath, forest terrain specialist
- Manipulative and deceptive
- Arrogant but not reckless

**Custom Bloodied Effects:**

**Frailty: Wounded Pride**

- Disadvantage on Deception and Persuasion checks
- Rage makes it less subtle, more aggressive
- -2 Focus (harder to maintain concentration on spells)

**Strength: Toxic Blood**

- Anyone hitting with melee weapon takes 2d6 poison damage (Con save DC 18 half)
- Wounds seep acidic poison
- Resistance to poison damage

**Ability: Venomous Spite**

- Cast Cloudkill (5th level) without using action or spell slot
- Centered on self, but dragon immune
- Represents desperate area control

**Death Throes: Poison Nova**

- 30 ft radius poison cloud
- 6d6 poison damage (Con save DC 18 half)
- Cloud persists for 3 rounds (difficult terrain, lightly obscured)
- Represents body rupturing, releasing lifetime of poison

**Auto Rally:** Dragon roars in fury, all kobold servants rally with advantage

## Quick Reference: Bloodied Implementation

When creature becomes bloodied:

**☐ Announce:** "The [creature] is bloodied - crossing 50% HP!"

2. Strength activates (announce resilience)
3. Ability triggers (resolve immediately)
4. Death Throes noted (remind players later)

**☐ Momentum Reset:** Creature resets to 0 Momentum

**☐ Auto Rally Check** (if leader):

- Roll 1d20 + Focus vs DC 15 or current casualty DC
- Apply result to all allies within 120 ft
- Update allied Momentum accordingly

**☐ Update Initiative:** Recalculate Side Initiative for next round

**☐ Describe:** Narrate the transformation dramatically

### Player Quick Checklist

When enemy becomes bloodied:

**☐ Identify Changes:**

- What Frailty can we exploit?
- What Strength must we adapt to?
- What Ability just triggered?
- What Death Throes should we avoid?

**☐ Adjust Tactics:**

- Target exposed Frailty
- Counter or mitigate Strength
- Prepare for Death Throes (positioning)

**☐ Track Initiative:**

- Did their Momentum reset affect turn order?
- Should we act differently based on new initiative?

**☐ Consider Finishing:**

- Kill now while vulnerable?
- Or spread damage to trigger more bloodied states?

## Example Encounters

### Example 1: Goblin Boss Squad

**Enemies:**

- 1 Goblin Boss (Leader, HP 21, Focus 10, Sanity 12)
- 6 Goblin Warriors (HP 7 each, Focus 6, Sanity 9)

**Round 3:** Fighter crits Goblin Boss, reducing to 9 HP (bloodied at 10 HP)

**Bloodied Triggers:**

1. **Frailty:** -2 AC (11 → 9)
2. **Strength:** None (cowardly leader)
3. **Ability:** Redirect Attack (force one goblin to take hit meant for boss, once)
4. **Death Throes:** None (dies pathetically)

**Auto Rally:** Boss rolls 1d20 + 1 (Focus +1) = 8 vs DC 15 → **Fails**

- All 6 warriors lose -1 Momentum
- Boss's panicked voice betrays fear

**Result:** Warriors now at -1 Momentum, easier to hit, lower morale

**Round 4:** Wizard fireballs, kills Boss + 3 warriors

**Breaking Point:** Remaining 3 warriors roll vs DC 17 (leader death +2)

- Average roll with +0 Sanity
- All 3 fail → rout, fleeing combat

**Tactical Lesson:** Low Focus leader failed Rally, accelerating rout

---

### Example 2: Adult Red Dragon

**Enemy:** Adult Red Dragon (HP 256, Focus 21, Sanity 22)

**Round 6:** Party's combined attacks reduce dragon from 140 HP to 125 HP (bloodied at 128)

**Bloodied Triggers:**

1. **Frailty:** Wings vulnerable (DR 6 → 4, easier to ground)
2. **Strength:** Immunity to non-magical damage
3. **Ability Recharge:** Fire Breath recharges immediately
4. **Ability Recharge:** Legendary Actions recharge
5. **New Ability:** Fiery Wound (attacks leave burning wounds, 1d6 fire/round)
6. **New Ability:** Fling (can throw grappled creatures)
7. **Trigger:** Cornered (attacks all 4 adjacent PCs immediately)
8. **Death Throes:** Fire explosion 30 ft for 8d6 (noted)

**Auto Rally:** Dragon roars (1d20 + 7 = 19 vs DC 15) → **Success!**

- 12 kobold servants within 120 ft all gain +1 Momentum
- Kobolds rally, fighting with renewed vigor

**Dragon's Immediate Actions:**

- Cornered trigger: 4 claw attacks (one per adjacent PC)
- Legendary Action: Fire Breath immediately (just recharged)

**Party Response:**

- Wizard: "Fall back! It's MORE dangerous now!"
- Ranger: "Target the wings - they're vulnerable!"
- Paladin: "Spread out - 30 foot explosion when it dies!"

**Round 7-10:** Brutal fight, dragon at +3 Momentum

- Ranger called shots to wings
- Both wings damaged, dragon grounded
- Can't escape, fights to death
- Death Throes: 8d6 fire to anyone within 30 ft

**Tactical Lesson:** High Focus leader rallied successfully, prolonging fight

### Example 3: Undead Horde

**Enemies:**

- 1 Vampire Spawn (Leader, HP 82, Focus 16, Sanity 18)
- 8 Zombies (HP 22 each, no mental stats - mindless)

**Round 4:** Cleric reduces Vampire to 38 HP (bloodied at 41)

**Bloodied Triggers:**

1. **Frailty:** Chipped fangs (-2 to bite damage)
2. **Strength:** Undead persistence (stay at 1 HP on killing blow once, DC 15 Con)
3. **Ability:** Blood Frenzy (advantage on attacks, +2 damage vs wounded)
4. **Death Throes:** Necrotic burst 15 ft, 3d8 necrotic

**Auto Rally:** Vampire rolls 1d20 + 3 (Focus +3) = 11 vs DC 15 → **Fails**

- Zombies unaffected (mindless, immune to morale)
- No other intelligent undead to Rally

**Key Point:** Mindless undead DON'T use morale system

- Zombies continue fighting regardless
- Rally system irrelevant for them
- Only vampire cares about being bloodied

**Vampire's Response:**

- Blood Frenzy active, focuses wounded Wizard
- Advantage + extra damage makes it terrifying

**Tactical Lesson:** Some creatures ignore morale but still get bloodied effects

## Design Philosophy Summary

**Bloodied = Transformation Moment:**

- Not just "wounded," but fundamental shift
- Every creature reveals true nature under pressure
- Creates dramatic mid-combat turning point

**Four-Part System Creates Complexity:**

- Frailty gives players tactical opportunity
- Strength forces players to adapt
- Ability creates memorable moment
- Death Throes rewards careful play

**Integration Makes It Powerful:**

- Momentum reset changes initiative
- Auto Rally tests leader quality
- Mental attributes matter in crisis
- Called shots interact with Frailties

**DM Control Over Complexity:**

- Mooks: Generic effects only
- Regulars: Standard type-based effects
- Elites: Custom effects
- Bosses: Legendary Phase 2

**Player Engagement:**

- Clear telegraphing (announce bloodied)
- Tactical decisions (exploit or adapt)
- Positioning matters (Death Throes)
- Resource management (save burst for bloodied?)

## Final Notes

The Bloodied & Bruised system transforms the 50% HP threshold from an arbitrary number into a mechanical and narrative crisis point. When integrated with the full DCS:

✓ Momentum swings create initiative shifts  
✓ Focus tests determine Rally success  
✓ Sanity reveals morale resilience  
✓ Called shots exploit exposed Frailties  
✓ Death Throes punish poor positioning  
✓ Breaking Points cascade from failed Rallies

**Every wound matters. Every creature transforms. Every threshold becomes a tactical decision point.**

Use this system to make combat dynamic, memorable, and psychologically rich. Your players will never forget the moment the dragon became MORE dangerous at half health, or when the orc chieftain's failed Rally cry broke his army's courage.

**Give your monsters the dignity of desperate last stands and the tragedy of inevitable defeat.**
