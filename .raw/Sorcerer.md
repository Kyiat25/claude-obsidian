# The Sorcerer: Living Conduit of Raw Magic

Listen, adventurer, because this path is unlike any other. When you become a Sorcerer, you don't learn to wield magic. You discover that magic is what you are made of.

Where a wizard channels power through formulas scratched into a spellbook, you channel it through blood and breath. Where a cleric borrows divine fire, yours has never needed permission. The difference is not one of degree—it's one of nature. Magic doesn't flow _through_ you. It is _you_, and every spell you cast is a form of self-expression so primal that even the gods once took notice.

But your magic has a source. A bloodline. A chaos within. A shadow bond. A divine mark. **From the moment you become a Sorcerer, you choose which origin of power calls to you—and that choice is not a flavor text decision. It is your identity made mechanical.**

A Draconic Sorcerer plays the game fundamentally differently at level 1 than a Wild Magic Sorcerer or a Divine Soul. Your origin determines:

- What spells you have access to
- How your spell damage works
- Your defensive abilities
- Your resource economy
- Your role in the party
- Your action economy in combat

This is not a class with a generic core and flavor variations bolted on. This is a class where **your origin IS your class design.**

Your body is your arcane focus. You require no components that aren't made of gold. When hostile spells strike you, your nature metabolises the energy—converting raw magical punishment into sorcery points before the pain has even registered. At higher levels, you can throw yourself in front of spells meant for your allies, absorbing the impact and turning it to fuel. You feel magic the way others feel heat. By midlevel, you know what a spell is before it finishes being cast.

**Devour Spell** is not your signature feature—it's your birthright made mechanics. Where others see a threat, you see a meal.

---

> **The party wants a Sorcerer when:** They need someone defined by their origin, not their training. The enemy has spellcasters and you thrive in magical environments. Magic traps and cursed items litter the dungeon. You don't just survive magical encounters—your very nature transforms them into fuel. And because your origin was chosen at level 1, you've been built for your role since the beginning.

> **Weapon Mastery:** Sorcerers do not gain Weapon Mastery. Your power is entirely internal. Weapons are tools you carry, not extensions of your identity.

## Class Features

As a Sorcerer, you have the following class features.

### Hit Points

- **Hit Dice:** 1d6 per Sorcerer level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 6 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d6 (or 4) + your Constitution modifier per Sorcerer level after 1st

### Proficiencies

- **Armor:** None
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons
- **Tools:** None
- **Saving Throws:** Constitution, Charisma
- **Skills:** Choose two from Arcana, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Memory, Persuasion, Religion, and Resolve

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) any simple weapon
- (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
- Two daggers

> **No arcane focus or component pouch.** Your body is your focus. See **Innate Casting** below.

## Feature Descriptions

### Sorcerous Origin (1st Level)

At 1st level, you choose the source of your innate magical power. This choice defines your character from day one and grants features at 1st, 3rd, 6th, 10th, 14th, and 18th levels.

Your origin determines:

- Which spells you have access to (in addition to the standard Arcane list)
- Origin-specific resource mechanics
- Your defensive identity
- Your role in combat
- Your campaign contributions

**Choose one of the following origins:**

- **Draconic Bloodline** — Descended from dragons; your magic carries elemental fury
- **Wild Magic** — Born of chaos; your magic surges unpredictably but powerfully
- **Divine Soul** — Touched by the divine; you channel celestial or infernal power
- **Shadow Magic** — Bound to darkness; you move through shadow and command fell forces
- **Aberrant Mind** — Marked by the Far Realm; your magic operates through pure thought
- **Clockwork Soul** — Infused with cosmic precision; you manipulate magical balance itself
- **Any other published Sorcerous Origin** — See **Universal Conversion Rules** below

Your origin choice is **permanent** unless you work with your GM to change it (normally requiring narrative justification and downtime).

---

### Font of Magic (1st Level)

You draw from an internal wellspring of magic represented by **sorcery points**, which allow you to create a variety of magical effects.

**Sorcery Points:** You start with 2 sorcery points at 1st level, gaining more as shown in the progression table. You regain all expended sorcery points when you finish a long rest.

**Flexible Casting:**

- _Convert Spell Slots to Sorcery Points:_ As a bonus action, expend one Arcane spell slot to gain sorcery points equal to the slot's circle.
- _Create Spell Slots:_ As a bonus action, spend sorcery points to create a spell slot. Slots created this way vanish when you finish a long rest.

|Spell Slot Circle|Sorcery Point Cost|
|---|---|
|1st|2|
|2nd|3|
|3rd|5|
|4th|6|
|5th|7|

**Empowering Reserves:** Spend 2 sorcery points to add your Charisma modifier to one ability check.

**Imbuing Touch:** Spend 2 sorcery points to temporarily make one nonmagical item magical for 1 hour.

**Sorcerous Fortitude:** Spend any number of sorcery points to gain temporary hit points equal to twice the points spent.

**Reroll:** When you fail an ability check, spend 1 sorcery point to reroll the d20.

---

### Spellcasting (1st Level)

You are a conduit for Arcane power, capable of casting Arcane spells.

**Cantrips:** Choose three cantrips from the Arcane spell list. You gain additional cantrips as shown in the progression table.

**Spells Known:** At 1st level, choose two 1st-circle spells from the Arcane spell list. You learn additional spells as shown in the progression table. When you gain a Sorcerer level, you may replace one known spell with another from the Arcane list of a circle you can cast.

**Origin Spells:** Your Sorcerous Origin may grant additional spells known or allow you to cast certain spells without expending spell slots. See your origin description.

**Spell Save DC** = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier

**Spell Attack Modifier** = your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier

---

### Innate Casting (1st Level)

Because magic originates within you rather than being drawn from external sources, you require no intermediary between yourself and the weave.

**Your body is your spellcasting focus.** You never need an arcane focus, component pouch, or any external tool to cast your spells.

**You can cast any spell you know without material components** if those components have no listed gold piece cost.

---

### Arcane Metabolism (1st Level)

When you take damage from a spell cast by a creature you can see, you can use your reaction to absorb some of that magic, converting it to sorcery points.

**Automatic Absorption:** You automatically absorb magical damage equal to the spell's circle (minimum 1 sorcery point). This does not require a reaction; it happens passively.

Example: A 2nd-circle _scorching ray_ hits you for 15 damage. You take 15 damage. You gain 2 temporary sorcery points that last until your next long rest. These points can be spent like normal sorcery points.

**Arcane Metabolism and Damage Reduction:** If you have damage reduction or resistance against the spell's damage type, the RD/resistance applies first. Metabolism absorbs from what reaches you.

Example: A fire-resistant Draconic Sorcerer takes a _fireball_. Fire resistance halves the damage, then Metabolism absorbs the halved damage.

---

### Metamagic (2nd Level)

You gain the ability to twist your spells in subtle but powerful ways. At 2nd level, you know two of the following Metamagic options. You gain additional options at 6th, 11th, and 17th levels.

**Flexible Metamagic:** You can use multiple Metamagic options on a single spell if you have enough sorcery points.

#### Heightened Spell

Spend 3 sorcery points when you cast a spell that forces a creature to make a saving throw. The target makes the save at disadvantage.

#### Twinned Spell

Spend sorcery points equal to the spell's circle when you cast a spell that targets only one creature. Choose another creature in range of the spell. The spell targets both creatures.

#### Quickened Spell

Spend 2 sorcery points to cast a spell as a bonus action instead of an action.

#### Subtle Spell

Spend 1 sorcery point to cast a spell without verbal or somatic components.

#### Empowered Spell

Spend 1 sorcery point when you roll damage for a spell you cast. Reroll a number of those damage dice equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum 1). You must use the new rolls.

#### Careful Spell

Spend sorcery points equal to the spell's circle when you cast a spell that forces creatures to make saving throws. Choose a number of creatures equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum 1). Those creatures automatically succeed on their saving throws.

#### Distant Spell

Spend 1 sorcery point when you cast a spell with a range of 5 feet or greater. Double the spell's range. If the spell's range is self, it instead gains a range of 30 feet.

---

### Arcane Deflection (2nd Level)

When you take damage from a creature you can see within 30 feet of you, you can use your reaction to spend 1 sorcery point to deflect the magical energy.

The creature must make a Charisma saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier). On a failure, the damage is reduced by half. On a success, the damage is reduced by a quarter.

Additionally, you gain temporary sorcery points equal to the spell's circle (minimum 1).

---

### Volatile Aura (3rd Level)

Your magical essence reacts violently to impacts. When you take damage from any source, you can use your reaction and expend one use from your Class Reactions pool to trigger one of the following effects:

#### Elemental Retaliation

Magical or elemental energy erupts around you. All creatures of your choice within 5 feet of you must make a Dexterity saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier). On a failure, they take 1d6 damage (type dependent on your origin: fire for Draconic, lightning for Storm, cold for Clockwork, necrotic for Shadow, etc.). This damage increases to 2d6 at 5th level, 3d6 at 11th level, and 4d6 at 17th level.

#### Momentum Surge

Gain +2 Momentum as the damage focuses your will.

#### Arcane Echo

All creatures within 10 feet of you must make an Intelligence saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier). On a failure, they are **blinded** until the end of their next turn as arcane light sears their vision.

---

### Arcane Intercept (4th Level)

When a creature you can see within 30 feet of you casts a spell that targets another creature, you can use your reaction and a Class Reactions pool use to interfere with the spell.

**Intercept (Quick):** Spend 1 sorcery point. The spell targets you instead of its original target.

**Intercept (Full):** Spend 2 sorcery points. The spell is cancelled entirely. You gain temporary sorcery points equal to the spell's circle (minimum 1).

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus. You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

---

### Sorcerous Renewal (5th, 9th, 13th, 17th Levels)

At 5th level, you can recover sorcery points during a short rest. At the end of a short rest, you regain sorcery points equal to 1d4 + 1 (minimum 1). At 9th level, this becomes 2d4 + 1. At 13th level, 3d4 + 1. At 17th level, 4d4 + 1.

This is in addition to your normal long rest recovery.

---

### Metaflux Veil (7th Level)

When you take damage from a spell you can see, you can use your reaction and spend 1 sorcery point to manipulate the arcane energy.

**Deflect:** Reduce the damage taken by half (rounded down).

**Charge:** Reduce the damage taken by a quarter (rounded down) and gain temporary sorcery points equal to the spell's circle (minimum 1).

---

### Heroic Boon (10th Level)

You achieve a transformative milestone in your mastery of your origin. Choose one of the following Heroic Boons:

#### Arcane Fusion

Your metamagic becomes more efficient. Reduce the sorcery point cost of any Metamagic option by 1 (minimum cost 0). Additionally, you can now use two Metamagic options on the same spell without additional cost.

#### Spellsense

Your attunement to magic deepens. You can sense the presence of active magical auras within 60 feet of you. With 1 minute of concentration (no Focus cost), you can determine the school and circle of any spell affecting a target you can see within 60 feet.

#### Arcane Reflection

Your body reflects hostile magic. When you take damage from a spell, you gain +1 to your AC and saving throws until the end of your next turn.

---

### Devour Spell (14th Level)

When a creature you can see within 30 feet of you casts a spell that targets you or includes you in its area, you can use your reaction to attempt to absorb the entire spell.

Make a Charisma saving throw against the caster's spell save DC. On a success, the spell has no effect on you and you gain sorcery points equal to twice the spell's circle (minimum 2). On a failure, the spell affects you normally and you gain sorcery points equal to the spell's circle (minimum 1).

You can use this feature once, and you regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest. Alternatively, you can spend 4 sorcery points to use this feature again before your next long rest.

---

### Magical Shell (15th Level)

Your body reinforces itself against magical intrusion. You gain resistance to damage from spells. When you use Arcane Metabolism to absorb spell damage, the temporary sorcery points you gain are doubled.

---

### Epic Boon (20th Level)

You achieve the ultimate expression of your magical nature. Choose one of the following Epic Boons:

#### Arcane Conjunction

When you are targeted by a spell, you can use your reaction (no resource cost) to redirect it to a different creature within range of the spell. You gain this ability once, and you regain it when you finish a long rest.

Additionally, you can cast _counterspell_ without expending a spell slot a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum 1). You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

#### Boundless Sorcery

Your sorcery point pool becomes inexhaustible. When you finish a short or long rest, you regain your full sorcery point maximum. Additionally, the maximum number of sorcery points you can spend on a single action increases to your Charisma modifier (previously proficiency bonus).

#### Arcane Avatar

You fully embody your origin. Depending on your Sorcerous Origin:

- **Draconic:** You gain resistance to your dragon ancestor's damage type and can spend 5 sorcery points to gain advantage on spell attacks for 1 minute.
- **Wild Magic:** You can trigger up to two Wild Magic Surges per turn instead of one.
- **Divine Soul:** You can cast _spirit guardians_ at will without expending spell slots.
- **Shadow Magic:** You gain blindsight out to 60 feet in darkness and can cast _shadow step_ at will.
- **Other Origins:** Work with your GM to determine an appropriate capstone that reflects your origin's identity.

## Sorcerous Origins

### Draconic Bloodline

Your magic comes from draconic heritage. Perhaps an ancient dragon ancestor bred into your family line, or you were touched by a dragon in utero, or you carry the blessing—or curse—of a dragon's scale embedded in your heart. Whatever the origin, draconic power flows through your veins.

#### Draconic Ancestry (1st Level)

Choose a dragon type. Your magic carries that element. Your spells of your dragon's damage type gain bonus damage equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum 1).

|Dragon Type|Damage Type|
|---|---|
|Black, Copper|Acid|
|Blue, Bronze|Lightning|
|Brass, Gold, Red|Fire|
|Green|Poison|
|Silver, White|Cold|

#### Draconic Resilience (1st Level)

Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and again by 1 each time you gain a Sorcerer level.

Additionally, when you aren't wearing armour, your base AC equals **13 + your Dexterity modifier**. Magic reinforces your flesh like scales beneath skin.

#### Draconic Momentum (1st Level)

When you deal damage of your ancestor's element with a spell, gain **+1 Momentum**.

#### Elemental Affinity (3rd Level)

When you cast a spell that deals damage of your ancestor's element, add your Charisma modifier to one damage roll of that spell.

Additionally, spend 1 sorcery point to gain resistance to your ancestor's damage type for 1 hour.

#### Dragon Wings (6th Level)

As a bonus action, cause leathery wings to sprout from your back, granting you a flying speed of 60 feet. The wings last until you dismiss them as a bonus action. You cannot manifest wings while wearing armour unless the armour is specifically crafted to accommodate them.

#### Draconic Presence (10th Level)

As an action, spend 5 sorcery points to channel draconic presence into a 60-foot aura lasting 1 minute (concentration, using Focus). Each hostile creature in the aura when it forms, or that enters it for the first time, must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw against your spell save DC or be **charmed** (awe) or **frightened** (terror) of you—your choice—until the aura ends. Creatures that succeed are immune to this feature for 24 hours.

#### Draconic Ascendance (14th Level)

You gain immunity to your dragon ancestor's damage type. Additionally, when you cast a spell that deals damage of that type, you can spend up to 5 additional sorcery points. For each point spent, add 1d6 damage of that type to the spell.

#### Dragon Incarnate (18th Level)

As an action, you can spend 7 sorcery points to transform into a dragon-touched form for 10 minutes. Your size increases to Large, you gain a flying speed of 80 feet, and your Strength and Constitution increase by 2. Your spell save DC increases by 2. This transformation ends early if you are knocked unconscious.

### Wild Magic

Your magic is chaotic and unpredictable, tied to the raw forces of reality itself. You don't control your magic—you negotiate with it, guide it, sometimes ride it like a wild horse. This instability is not a flaw. It is your power.

#### Chaotic Heritage (1st Level)

Your magic surges unpredictably. Once per long rest, when you cast a spell of 1st circle or higher, roll a d10. On a 1, consult the **Wild Magic Surge Table** (see below). Otherwise, the spell resolves normally.

Additionally, you gain the **Tides of Chaos** feature: Once per long rest, you can gain advantage on one attack roll, ability check, or saving throw. When you use this feature, the DM rolls to determine if a Wild Magic Surge occurs on your next spell cast.

#### Bend Luck (1st Level)

You can spend 2 sorcery points as a reaction when another creature makes an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw to influence fate. Roll 1d4 and add the result to their roll, or subtract the result from their roll (your choice, after you see the roll but before the outcome is determined).

#### Tides of Chaos (1st Level)

See **Chaotic Heritage** above.

#### Controlled Chaos (3rd Level)

When you roll on the Wild Magic Surge table, you can spend 2 sorcery points to roll twice and choose which result occurs.

#### Chaotic Spell (6th Level)

When you cast a spell, you can spend up to 3 additional sorcery points. For each point spent, roll 1d10 on the Wild Magic Surge table (if you roll a 1).

#### Wild Surge Mastery (10th Level)

You gain control over your chaotic magic. When you cause a Wild Magic Surge to occur, you can spend 5 sorcery points to choose the result instead of rolling.

#### Cascading Chaos (14th Level)

When a Wild Magic Surge occurs, you gain +2 Momentum and can immediately cast a cantrip as a bonus action.

#### Avatar of Chaos (18th Level)

As an action, you can spend 7 sorcery points to enter a state of pure chaos for 10 minutes. During this time, a Wild Magic Surge is triggered on a roll of 1–3 instead of just 1. You gain +3 Momentum at the start of each of your turns. This state ends early if you are knocked unconscious.

### Other Sorcerous Origins

Any Sorcerous Origin published for D&D 5E is compatible using the universal conversions below. The Draconic Bloodline above serves as the worked conversion template.

**Suggested Origins:**

- **Divine Soul** — Access to the Divine spell list in addition to Arcane. Dramatically broadens spell options.
- **Shadow Magic** — Darkness affinity, Hound of Ill Omen, shadow walk. Strong stealth and control synergy.
- **Storm Sorcery** — Lightning and thunder, movement in storms, tempest channelling.
- **Aberrant Mind** — Psionic spells, telepathy. Pairs naturally with FEARS's mental attribute systems.
- **Clockwork Soul** — Magical cancellation and balance. Counterpoint to Arcane Metabolism by design.

---

### Universal Conversion Rules

When adapting a 5E Sorcerous Origin to FEARS, apply these changes:

|5E Element|FEARS Conversion|
|---|---|
|Bonus spell slots|Bonus sorcery points equal to the slot's circle, once per long rest|
|Concentration (Constitution saves)|Focus saves|
|Wild Magic Surge rolls|Wild Magic subclass only; never from base class reactions|
|Bonus Metamagic options|Added on top of standard Metamagic progression|
|Elemental Affinity-style damage bonus|Applies to damage you deal; no interaction with Metabolism|
|3rd-level origin feature|Move to 1st level if it defines the origin's identity|

### Momentum Integration by Origin Theme

If a published Origin has no built-in Momentum trigger, apply the relevant default:

|Origin Theme|Default Momentum Trigger|
|---|---|
|Elemental / Draconic|Deal damage of your element with a spell → +1 Momentum|
|Shadow / Necrotic|Reduce a creature to 0 HP with a spell → +1 Momentum|
|Wild / Chaos|A Wild Magic Surge triggers → +2 Momentum|
|Divine / Radiant|Restore HP to a creature with a spell → +1 Momentum (both you and target)|
|Psionic / Aberrant|A creature fails a saving throw against your spell → +1 Momentum|

## Playing the Sorcerer

### Your Level 1 Identity

Unlike other casters who begin as generic practitioners waiting for specialisation, **your Sorcerous Origin defines who you are at level 1.**

A Draconic Sorcerer plays completely differently from a Wild Magic Sorcerer, who plays differently from a Divine Soul.

- **Draconic:** You are a resilient elemental spellcaster. Your AC is good without armour. Your spells hit harder in your chosen element. Metabolism feeds on your own element, making you thrive in battles where elemental magic is prevalent.
- **Wild Magic:** You are chaos incarnate. Your spells are unpredictable. You guide fortune with Bend Luck and Tides of Chaos. You thrive when things go wrong because wild surges make you stronger.
- **Divine Soul:** You bridge the gap between Arcane and Divine. Your spell list is broader than other Sorcerers. You heal and damage with equal power.
- **Shadow Magic:** You move through darkness. You see in shadows. Your magic corrodes and weakens. You are a stealth spellcaster with control abilities.

These are not "flavors of the same thing." These are different classes that happen to share spell slots.

### Your Default Turn (Levels 2–10)

**Action:** Cast a spell. Check whether you should apply Metamagic. Most turns, Heightened Spell (force disadvantage) or Empowered Spell (reroll damage dice) are correct plays.

**Bonus Action:** If you used Quickened Spell on your action, cast a second spell. Otherwise, consider Imbuing Touch or another use of Font of Magic.

**Reaction:** Your reaction is your most important decision. Priority:

1. **Devour Spell** (14th level) — Absorb a powerful spell targeting you
2. **Arcane Intercept** — Pull a spell away from a vulnerable ally
3. **Arcane Deflection** — Reduce incoming damage and absorb points
4. **Volatile Aura** — Retaliate or surge Momentum when hit by non-spell damage
5. **Metaflux Veil** — Manipulate spell damage after seeing the result

### In Magical Encounters

Your Arcane Metabolism, Deflection, and Intercept features trigger constantly. You're converting hostile magic into fuel. A Wizard might try to avoid a fireball; you lean into it because it's feeding you sorcery points.

This makes Sorcerers the ultimate magical environment specialists. If your campaign has many spellcasting enemies, or magic-heavy dungeons, a Sorcerer thrives. If your campaign is martial-heavy, your absorption features are dormant, and you play like a standard (excellent) Arcane caster.

### Resource Awareness

|Resource|Refills On|Used For|
|---|---|---|
|Base Sorcery Points|Long rest (+ 1d4+ per short rest via Sorcerous Renewal)|Metamagic, spell slot creation, origin abilities|
|Temporary SP (Absorbed)|Lost on short/long rest|All SP costs|
|Class Reactions Pool|Short or long rest|Volatile Aura, Arcane Intercept, Arcane Deflection, Metaflux Veil|
|Devour Spell Use|Long rest (or 4 SP)|Proactive spell absorption|

Your resource economy is _rich_—you have multiple ways to generate sorcery points (Font of Magic, Sorcerous Renewal, Metabolism, Deflection, Intercept). This is intentional. Sorcerers should never feel resource-starved.
