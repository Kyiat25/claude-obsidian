# Triton
## Lineage

> *"We are not water. We are not air. We are not spirit. We are the bond between—the oath spoken where three currents meet. To break the flow is death. To break the bond is erasure. We are the Covenant. We are the Tide."*  
> — **The Binding Oath**, spoken at every Triton birth

You are Triton. Not merely a child of the ocean—you are its **voice**, its **memory**, its **living covenant**. Your people exist in the sacred space where Water, Air, and Spirit converge, bound by oaths that predate most gods. Where others see the ocean as backdrop or resource, you understand it as **witness, judge, and eternal record** of everything that ever was.

You were not born to conquer the elements. You were born to **bind them together**, to speak for what cannot speak for itself, to remember what should never be forgotten. Your ancestors made covenant with gods from three pantheons, creating a faith so complex it requires your entire people to maintain it. Now you carry that weight, and the ocean watches everything you do.

---

## The First Breath

According to **The First Breath** creation myth, Tritons were not created by gods—they were **the ocean becoming self-aware**. In the beginning, there was only ocean: deep, dark, still, without memory or motion or voice.

Then the Wind came, curious about what lay beneath. It touched the water's surface and created the first wave. The ocean **laughed** for the first time. That laugh became the first Triton—part water, part wind, part of the memory of that first touch.

Your people believe you are **the conversation between ocean and sky made flesh**. You are the breath between. You are the voice of the turning tide. This is why you bind Water and Air so naturally—it is your literal origin. Spirit came later, when the ocean developed **memory** of that first moment.

This makes you fundamentally different from other races. You don't worship the ocean or serve it—you **are** it, made manifest with consciousness and choice. The gods came after, agreeing to covenant with beings who already existed as living synthesis of three elements.

---

## Life Within the Tide

You were raised knowing that **every deal is a covenant, every price is a promise, every coin is a prayer**. Trade is not secular in Triton society—it is **sacred binding** witnessed by the ocean itself and enforced by divine will. Breaking an oath isn't just wrong; it's **cosmic fracture**, and the ocean itself turns against oath-breakers.

You learned to measure time not in years but in **tides and moon-cycles**, to navigate by **current-reading** rather than landmarks, to communicate through **shell-speech** when sound fails in deep water. Your childhood was spent learning to harmonize with the ocean's song, to read memories written in coral and kelp, to understand that **stillness is death** and motion is sacred.

Your lifespan is roughly **200 years**—longer than humans but shorter than elves. You measure time in **Moon-Tides** (lunar months of ~29.5 days), **Season-Tides** (three moon-tides), and **Great-Tides** (solar years). This makes scheduling with surface-dwellers... challenging.

Every major deed is recorded in the **Scroll of Tides**—thousands of miles of kelp parchment written in bioluminescent ink, preserving the name of every Triton who ever died at sea. As long as the Scroll exists, no Triton is truly forgotten.

## Physical Characteristics

**Age:** You mature at the same rate as humans, reaching adulthood around age 18, but live much longer—some Tritons reach 200 years or more. Your people age gracefully underwater but struggle with extended time on land.

**Size:** You stand between 5 to 6 feet tall, with lean, streamlined builds designed for swimming. Your size is Medium.

**Speed:** Your base walking speed is 30 feet. Your swimming speed is 30 feet. Unlike most races, you move equally well in water as on land.

**Build:** Lean and powerful, with webbed fingers and toes, skin tones ranging from deep blue to sea green to pearl white. Your hair moves constantly, even when there's no wind, as if still underwater. Your eyes are large and adapted for seeing in murky depths.

## Triton Traits

As a Triton, you possess the following lineage traits:

### **Amphibious**
You can breathe air and water. You are equally comfortable in both environments and suffer no penalties for extended time in either.

This is your most fundamental trait—you are the bridge between worlds, the voice between ocean and sky. You belong to both.

### **Darkvision**
You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

Your eyes are adapted for the deep ocean, where sunlight cannot penetrate. You see through murk and darkness that would blind surface-dwellers.

### **Guardian of the Depths**
You have resistance to cold damage. The crushing pressure and icy temperatures of the deep ocean are your natural home.

### **Emissary of the Sea**
You can communicate simple ideas with beasts that have a swimming speed. They can understand the meaning of your words, though you have no special ability to understand them in return.

This represents your connection to the ocean's creatures—they recognize you as kin, as part of the ecosystem, as voice of the currents.

### **Control Air and Water** *(Innate Magic)*
You know the *shape water* cantrip. When you reach 3rd level, you can cast the *fog cloud* spell once with this trait and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest. When you reach 5th level, you can cast the *gust of wind* spell once with this trait and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest.

Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells.

This represents your innate connection to Water and Air—the two elements that created your people. You don't learn these spells; you simply **are** them, as naturally as breathing.

### **Current-Reader**
You have proficiency in the Perception skill. Additionally, while underwater, you have advantage on Perception checks to detect movement or changes in water currents.

You were raised reading currents the way others read books. The ocean speaks to you through its motion.

### **Oath-Keeper's Intuition**
You can always tell when someone is lying to you about a promise or oath they've made. This doesn't work through magical deception, but natural lies about agreements trigger an instinctive awareness—a coldness in your chest, as if the ocean itself rejects the falsehood.

### **Languages**
You can speak, read, and write Common, Primordial (Aquan dialect), and one additional language of your choice. Aquan is the language of water elementals and the ancient tongue of the ocean itself.

## Mental Attributes & Psychology

**Focus Modifier:** +1  
**Sanity Modifier:** +0

Your biology grants you **enhanced mental discipline**. Growing up in an environment where three-dimensional navigation, pressure management, and constant current-reading are necessary for survival creates exceptional **spatial awareness and concentration**. Your Focus receives a bonus because Triton culture trains you from birth to maintain awareness across multiple simultaneous challenges.

However, your Sanity is standard. While your people have strong cultural frameworks for processing trauma (death is release to Nyzara, the tide always turns, etc.), you face unique psychological pressures: the weight of ancestral memory, the crushing expectations of oath-keeping, and the constant surveillance theology that nothing can be hidden from the ocean.

### **Cultural Mental Conditioning**
Beyond your biological advantage, Triton upbringing creates:

- **Oath-Weight:** Every promise carries spiritual pressure—you feel broken agreements physically
- **Memory-Burden:** Your people preserve everything; forgetting feels like betrayal
- **Flow-State:** Trained to find calm within motion rather than stillness
- **Depth-Pressure:** Physical pressure parallels psychological pressure in your culture

### **Corruption Vulnerability: The Stilled Current**

As a Triton, you have a unique relationship with Corruption. Your connection to the Three Currents and the covenant with multiple pantheons provides some protection, but corruption that enters through **stagnation, blocked flow, or broken oaths** affects you catastrophically.

**When you gain Corruption Score 10+ (Tier 2), you suffer:**
- Current-Deafness: Water stops "speaking" to you clearly
- Oath-Strain: Breaking promises becomes easier; keeping them harder
- Memory-Static: The Scroll of Tides becomes difficult to recall

**Fractured Covenant Table (Corruption Tier 2+)**

When you complete a long rest at Corruption Score 10+, roll 1d6:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **Still Water:** You feel stagnant. Your swimming speed is reduced by 10 feet until your next long rest. |
| 2 | **Forgotten Names:** You cannot remember the names of 1d4 ancestors. Make a DC 15 Wisdom save or lose proficiency in one skill until your next long rest. |
| 3 | **Covenant Fracture:** One of your innate spells (from Control Air and Water) fails when you try to cast it. It doesn't expend the use, but it simply doesn't work. |
| 4 | **Depth-Sickness:** The ocean feels wrong. Make a DC 12 Sanity save or lose 1d4 Sanity. While underwater, you feel watched by something that isn't the gods. |
| 5 | **Eldritch Tide:** Something whispers in the language of drowning. Gain 1 Whisper Point but your Current-Reader advantage becomes disadvantage until your next long rest. |
| 6 | **Covenant Holds:** The Three Currents flow strong tonight. Reduce your Corruption Score by 1 (minimum 10). You dream of Nereus's voice reminding you of your purpose. |

## Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Integration

### **Evasion Profile: Fluid Combatant**
- **Dodge Evasion:** +1 (graceful movement, trained in three-dimensional combat)
- **Endure Evasion:** +0 (lean build, standard resilience)
- **Deflect Evasion:** +1 (water-trained reflexes, current-reading creates prediction)

**Tactical Note:** Your biology is built for **fluid combat**. Growing up fighting in three dimensions (underwater combat includes up/down as viable directions) creates spatial awareness that translates to land combat. You don't tank hits—you **flow around them**, using prediction and positioning rather than raw toughness.

### **Natural Damage Reduction:** +0
Your lean build provides no inherent physical armor, but your combat training compensates through superior positioning.

### **Combat Philosophy**
Tritons fight with **fluid adaptation and tactical prediction**. You use Current-Reader to anticipate enemy movement (reading body language like currents), leverage three-dimensional thinking (even on land, you think in terms of vertical space), and maintain constant motion (stillness is death). You don't stand and trade blows—you **flow** through combat like water finding cracks.

### **Underwater Combat Dominance**
In aquatic environments, you gain:
- **Advantage on attack rolls** against creatures without swimming speeds
- **No penalties** for underwater combat (others have disadvantage)
- **Superior positioning** due to three-dimensional movement
- Ability to use **Control Air and Water** abilities for tactical advantage

### **Breaking Point Mechanics**
Due to your cultural conditioning around "the tide always turns" philosophy, you have **advantage on Rally checks when your side has numerical advantage** (because momentum flows your way). However, you struggle when flow reverses—**disadvantage on Rally checks when significantly outnumbered** (the tide turning against you triggers deep psychological response).

## Languages

**Languages:** You can speak, read, and write Common and Primordial (Aquan dialect). You gain one additional language of your choice.

**Aquan** is not merely a language—it's the **voice of water itself**. It sounds like whale-song mixed with rushing rivers, impossible for most air-breathers to pronounce correctly without magic. The language has extensive vocabulary for:
- Types of currents and water movement
- Depths, pressures, and ocean layers
- Oaths, contracts, and binding agreements
- Memory, preservation, and forgetting
- The relationship between water, air, and spirit

When Tritons speak of sacred matters (oaths, ancestors, the Covenant), they often switch to Aquan even if others don't understand, because some truths **can only be properly spoken in the ocean's tongue**.

**Shell-Speech:** Additionally, you know non-verbal underwater communication using carved shells, bioluminescent signals, and bubble-rings. This isn't a full language but conveys complex emotional states and simple messages when sound fails in deep water.

## Triton Subraces

While all Tritons share the Tide Covenant, different communities have specialized over generations based on their primary depth range and currents. Choose one of the following subraces:

### **Deep-Dwelling Lineage** *(The Pressure-Born)*
You descend from Tritons who dwell below 600 feet, where crushing pressure and complete darkness are home. Your people are the elite—nobles, military commanders, and senior priests who have proven their spiritual strength by enduring what should kill lesser beings.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 1, your Constitution score increases by 1, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Pressure Resistance:** You have advantage on saving throws against effects that would crush, compress, or physically restrain you. Additionally, you can withstand depths up to 1,200 feet without magical aid.

**Deep Current Communion:** You know the *prestidigitation* cantrip. When you reach 3rd level, you can cast *command* once with this trait (Nereus speaks through you with the weight of the deep). When you reach 5th level, you can cast *hold person* once with this trait (the pressure of your will becomes physical). You regain the ability to cast these spells when you finish a long rest. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells.

**Abyssal Vision:** Your darkvision has a range of 120 feet instead of 60 feet.

**Deep-Dwelling's Burden:** Your people carry the weight of leadership. You have proficiency in Intimidation or Persuasion (your choice).

### **Mid-Current Lineage** *(The Flow-Walkers)*
You descend from Tritons who dwell between 100-600 feet, where most Triton society thrives. Your people are merchants, artisans, soldiers, and farmers—the backbone of the Coral Dominion. You are the true keepers of the Covenant, maintaining trade and oaths that bind civilization together.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Dexterity score increases by 1, your Intelligence score increases by 1, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Trade-Current Blessing:** You have proficiency in Insight and one artisan's tool or gaming set of your choice. Additionally, when you make a Charisma check related to commerce, negotiation, or oath-keeping, you can add your proficiency bonus twice instead of once (if you're already proficient).

**Wind Current Affinity:** You know the *gust* cantrip. When you reach 3rd level, you can cast *expeditious retreat* once with this trait (the wind fills your movement). When you reach 5th level, you can cast *misty step* once with this trait (you flow like water). You regain the ability to cast these spells when you finish a long rest. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells.

**Current-Dancer:** Your people invented Current-Dancing, the highest art form of Triton culture. You have proficiency in Performance, and when you perform in or near water, you have advantage on Performance checks.

### **Surface-Singer Lineage** *(The Voice Between)*
You descend from Tritons who specialize in dwelling at 0-100 feet and on the surface world. Your people are diplomats, wind-readers, and air-breather liaisons—the bridge between ocean and land. You sacrifice deep-dwelling to serve as translators between worlds.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Constitution score increases by 1, your Wisdom score increases by 1, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Air-Walker's Adaptation:** You have advantage on saving throws against exhaustion caused by land travel or lack of water immersion. You can go up to 7 days without immersing yourself in water before suffering any penalties (most Tritons need immersion every 2-3 days).

**Soul Current Gift:** You know the *guidance* cantrip. When you reach 3rd level, you can cast *comprehend languages* once with this trait (the Voice Between understands all). When you reach 5th level, you can cast *calm emotions* once with this trait (you speak peace across cultures). You regain the ability to cast these spells when you finish a long rest. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for these spells.

**Diplomatic Training:** You have proficiency in two of the following skills: Persuasion, Deception, Insight, or Performance. Additionally, you can learn two additional languages of your choice.

**Surface-Swimmer:** You have learned to navigate the surface world. You don't have disadvantage on Perception checks while on land, and you can hold your breath indefinitely while in air (though you still need to breathe).

## Cultural Touchstones

### **The Tide Covenant**
All Tritons are raised within this faith. Even if you've traveled far from your people, its truths shaped you:

- **The Three Currents Are One Breath** - Water, Air, and Spirit must flow together
- **The Ocean Remembers—And Never Forgives** - Nothing is truly forgotten; all lies resurface eventually
- **The Tide Is Fate, But the Wind Is Choice** - You cannot stop the tide, but you can set your sail
- **To Trade Is to Bind—To Break Oath Is to Drown** - Every deal is sacred covenant, every broken promise is cosmic fracture
- **The Three Currents Must Flow—Stillness Is Death** - Stagnation is the only absolute sin
- **We Are the Voice Between** - You exist to speak for what cannot speak for itself

### **The Five Sacred Bindings**
These foundational truths define Triton existence:

1. **The ocean remembers everything** - Truth is sacred; lies will surface
2. **The tide is fate, wind is choice** - Accept inevitability while choosing how to meet it
3. **Every deal is covenant** - Commerce is sacred, contracts are spiritually binding
4. **Stillness is death** - Motion, exchange, and change are holy
5. **We speak for the silent** - You are mediator between Water, Air, and Spirit

### **The Three Unforgivable Acts**

**Breaking the Oath-Tide** (severe contract violation):
- Punishment: The Drowning Silence (weighted execution where innocent can break free)
- All contracts void, property forfeit, name stricken from records

**Memory-Murder** (destroying historical records):
- Punishment: The Forgetting (all records of you erased, permanent exile)
- The crime becomes the punishment—you're memory-murdered as you memory-murdered others

**Stilling the Current** (preventing ocean's flow):
- Punishment: The Current's Mercy (set adrift without supplies; survival = innocence)
- Building dams, creating dead zones, blocking harbors—all capital offenses

## Playing a Triton

### **Core Fantasy:**
You are the voice between worlds, the living covenant, the memory-keeper of everything the ocean has witnessed. You don't merely swim—you **are** the motion of water, the breath of wind, the memory of all who came before. Your oaths carry divine weight, your memories are eternal record, and your very existence bridges three elements that should be separate.

### **Mechanical Identity:**
- **Amphibious Specialist:** Equally effective in water and on land
- **Utility Caster:** Control Air and Water provides tactical options
- **Skilled Negotiator:** Oath-Keeper's Intuition + high Charisma
- **Mobile Combatant:** Moderate Dodge/Deflect Evasion, relies on positioning
- **Sensory Expert:** Current-Reader + darkvision for excellent awareness

### **Roleplaying Hooks:**
- **The Oath-Bound:** Carrying sacred obligation from home
- **The Exile:** Fled after breaking oath or being falsely accused

- **The Contract-Mediator:** Using Oath-Keeper training to resolve disputes
- **The Surface-Strange:** Surface-Singer uncomfortable in deep-dwelling society

### **Party Dynamics:**
Other races often misunderstand Tritons as overly formal or obsessed with contracts. This creates opportunities for:

- **Teaching through patience** - Explaining why promises matter spiritually
- **Cultural translation** - Bridging misunderstandings between party members
- **Surprising adaptability** - Showing you can thrive on land despite ocean-origin
- **Oath-keeping** - Demonstrating that your word is literally unbreakable
- **Memory-sharing** - Preserving party history and fallen comrades

### **Common Character Arcs:**
- From rigid oath-keeping to understanding flexible honor
- From deep-dwelling elitism to respecting surface-dwellers

- From oath-bearer to oath-breaker (and redemption)

## Design Philosophy

The Triton lineage is designed to support:

1. **The Diplomat** (Oath-Keeper's Intuition + high Charisma + trade focus)
2. **The Amphibious Warrior** (fluid combat + aquatic dominance)
3. **The Memory-Keeper** (Current-Reader + focus on history/preservation)
4. **The Elemental Controller** (Control Air and Water creates tactical flexibility)
5. **The Cultural Bridge** (Surface-Singer subrace specifically for land-adventuring)

Your mechanical abilities (Amphibious, Control Air and Water, Guardian of the Depths, Emissary of the Sea) create a **versatile character equally effective in multiple environments**. You're designed to shine underwater but remain competent on land, with utility magic that supplements any class choice.

The subrace options allow you to lean into different depth-castes—from pressure-born elite (Deep-Dwelling) to commerce-focused majority (Mid-Current) to diplomatic specialists (Surface-Singer)—while maintaining the core Triton identity of covenant-bound memory-keepers.

## Final Notes

### **The Weight of Memory:**
Every Triton carries ancestral pressure. Your character likely knows:
- Names and deeds of at least 5 generations of ancestors
- The founding oath of their trade-house or community
- Multiple sacred sites by reputation
- The Three Currents doctrine by heart
- Dozens of Tide-Songs encoding history

This isn't burden—it's **context**. You understand yourself as part of unbroken chain stretching back to the First Breath.

### **Respect Across Cultures:**
Modern Dór-o Estel recognizes Triton oath-keeping:
- Thread-Cities rely on Triton merchants for reliable trade
- Coastal cities fear Tide-Guard but respect Oath-Keepers
- Other ocean races (sea elves, merfolk) practice similar faiths
- Even land-dwellers know Triton word is binding

### **The Scroll of Tides:**
Your people believe death is **transition into memory**. The worthy dead are recorded in the Scroll, their names sung monthly, their deeds preserved forever. You adventure knowing that if you die honorably, your name will be added—and as long as the ocean remembers, you are not truly gone.

Some dead become **Ancestral Watchers** in Memory-Swimmer visions, some return as echoes in the tide, some are claimed by Nyzara the Moon That Watches. But none are forgotten.
