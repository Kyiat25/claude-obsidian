# Dead Magic Zone

A **Dead Magic Zone** is a cursed or collapsed area where **magic no longer works**. It’s a terrifying place for spellcasters—and a strategic risk for anyone.

The world falls silent. Not with peace—but with absence. The sky turns dull, the ground feels brittle, and the warmth of magic you’ve known your whole life is simply… gone. You reach for a spell, and nothing answers. Not even a flicker. The very Weave is absent here. Magic has been buried beneath stillness—and if you force it to rise, something might come looking for you.

#### 🛑 Effects:

- **All spells fail.** Even cantrips. Magical items stop working.
- **Magical creatures** may weaken or shut down entirely.
- **Portals, summoning, and teleportation** become impossible.

#### 💀 If You Try Anyway:

- Forcing magic in a Dead Magic Zone is possible—but dangerous. You may risk:
    - **Madness**
    - **Exhaustion**
    - **Magical backlash**
    - **Attracting predators** drawn to forced spells

### 🎯 Mechanics Players Need to Know:
> ❄️ **Concept:** All magic is suppressed. You can _try_ to force it, but consequences are severe.
- **Magic Suppression:**
    - Spells **Automatically fail**.
    - Magic items **do not function**.
    - Portals, teleportation, summoning, etc. **fail automatically**.
- **Forcing a Spell (at your own risk):**
    - **Sanity Saving Throw**:
        - DC scales by spell level:
            - Cantrip: DC 10
            - 1st–2nd: DC 12–14
            - 3rd–4th: DC 16–18 (also gain exhaustion)
            - 5th–6th: DC 20–22
            - 7th–9th: DC 24–28
    - **Exhaustion**: Forcing 3rd level+ spells causes 1–4 levels of exhaustion.
    - **[[Mark of the Rift]]**: Each forced spell makes you easier to track by magical predators.
- **Failure Effects**:
    - Short-term madness
    - Additional exhaustion
    - More Mark stacks = faster predators

#### 🎭 What You Experience:

- The world is **gray and silent**.
- Spells slip from your memory, magical items feel _mundane_, and your connection to the arcane goes **numb**.
- The feeling is one of loss, suppression, and creeping dread.
