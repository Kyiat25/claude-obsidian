# Attitudes That Shape the World

Not every threat begins with a blade, and not every ally wears a smile. In _[[Dór-o Estel]]_, the way creatures perceive you—whether with suspicion, warmth, or open hostility—can shape outcomes as sharply as any weapon. The **Disposition System** provides a flexible scale to determine how NPCs react to player characters during social interactions, exploration, and even combat. 

Whether you're negotiating with a wary merchant, calming a restless spirit, or swaying a noble council, Disposition guides the flow of trust, tension, and consequence. This scale is not fixed—actions, reputations, and circumstances will shift it over time, reflecting the living world’s response to who you are and what you’ve done.

Disposition represents a creature's general attitude and willingness to cooperate or engage in conflict. It serves as a valuable tool for roleplaying social interactions and influencing how creatures react to the player characters. The disposition scale ranges from actively hostile to willingly helpful:

# **Disposition Scale**

- **Hostile:** The creature is actively aggressive and seeks to harm or destroy the characters. It will attack on sight or at the earliest opportunity.
- **Suspicious:** The creature is wary and distrustful of the characters. It may observe them from a distance, question their motives, or demand proof of their good intentions. It will likely attack if provoked or if it perceives the characters as a threat.
- **Indifferent:** The creature is neutral towards the characters and has no particular interest in helping or harming them. It will generally ignore the characters unless they directly interact with it. It may be persuaded to offer assistance or information for a price or favour.
- **Friendly:** The creature is well-disposed towards the characters and willing to engage in conversation and offer limited assistance. It will not attack the characters unprovoked and may even defend them against minor threats.
- **Helpful:** The creature is actively supportive of the characters and willing to go out of its way to assist them. It will offer information, resources, and even direct aid in combat or other challenging situations.

## **Disposition of a Settlement**

- **[[Hamlet]]/[[Thorp]]:** Often start with Suspicious due to their isolation and wariness of outsiders.
- **[[Village]]:** Typically Indifferent, but can be Friendly if known for their hospitality or Suspicious if they've had recent trouble with outsiders.
- **[[Town]]:** Usually Indifferent, as they're accustomed to a flow of strangers. However, specific circumstances (e.g., recent crimes, an upcoming festival) can shift this.
- **[[City]]:** Generally Indifferent, treating most newcomers with anonymity. Specific districts or neighbourhoods might have different prevailing dispositions.
- **[[Metropolis]]:** Almost always Indifferent, as individuals are less likely to pay attention to strangers in a large, bustling city.

## **Using the Disposition Scale**

The DM uses the disposition scale to determine a creature's initial attitude towards the players. This attitude can change based on the characters' actions and interactions. The scale can be used in the following ways:

- **Social Interactions:** A creature's disposition influences how it responds to dialogue, requests, and offers. A hostile creature will likely reject any attempts at negotiation, while a helpful creature will be eager to assist.
- **Combat:** Disposition can influence a creature's targets and tactics in combat. A suspicious creature might focus on the most heavily armed character, while a friendly creature might prioritize protecting the characters.
- **Information Gathering:** A creature's disposition affects how willing it is to share information. An indifferent creature might require a bribe, while a helpful creature will freely offer what it knows.
- **Charmed Condition:** The disposition scale is directly integrated with the Charmed condition (see the Charmed condition for details). Charm effects shift a creature's disposition towards Helpful, influencing its actions and willingness to cooperate.
- **Moral Dilemmas:** The disposition scale can help the DM determine how a creature reacts to moral dilemmas or situations where its interests conflict with those of the characters.

### **Examples**

- **Hostile:** A group of goblins attacks the adventurers on sight, driven by their inherent aggression and territoriality.
- **Suspicious:** A shopkeeper eyes the adventurers warily, questioning their unfamiliar attire and asking about their business in town.
- **Indifferent:** A bartender serves the adventurers drinks but remains aloof, only engaging in conversation if directly addressed.
- **Friendly:** A local farmer offers the adventurers directions and warns them about dangerous creatures in the nearby forest.
- **Helpful:** A grateful villager, whose family was saved by the adventurers, offers them lodging and supplies for their journey.

# 🔗 System Integration

The **Disposition System** is a core pillar of social interaction in _[[Dór-o Estel]]_, and it interlocks with several other mechanics to create a dynamic and reactive world:

## 🛡️ **Wanted Score System**

A character’s **[[Wanted Score]]** directly influences the **starting Disposition** of NPCs and law enforcement:

- **Score 1–2**: Most NPCs are _Suspicious_ or _Indifferent_ in local areas.
- **Score 5+**: Most NPCs default to _Hostile_, while black market agents may start as _Friendly_.
- Disposition affects how authorities pursue or interrogate characters and can be modified by acts of redemption or notoriety.

## 🕳️ **Black Market Access**

[[Black market]] NPCs often begin with a **Suspicious** or **Indifferent** Disposition. Raising this to _Friendly_ or _Helpful_ through bribes, reputation, or past dealings grants:

- Access to rare or restricted goods.
- Better prices and safer interactions.
- Protection from betrayal or law enforcement stings.

## 🏠 **Hideouts and Safe Houses**

Disposition governs the **loyalty and reliability** of staff, informants, and local contacts of your [[Hideouts]]:

- A _Suspicious_ contact might leak your location for coin.
- A _Helpful_ one will defend your secrets or provide early warning.
- Betrayals, raids, or favors often stem from shifts in these relationships.

## 🌟 **Reputation System**

While **[[Reputation]]** tracks how the world _remembers_ you, **Disposition** shows how NPCs _feel about you now_. Together, they shape:

- How fast an NPC’s attitude shifts during interaction.
- Whether public admiration overrides a criminal past.
- Contextual behavior in moral dilemmas, negotiations, or faction encounters.

## 🧠 **Charmed**

The **[[Charmed]] condition** explicitly interacts with Disposition—often shifting it to _Friendly_ or _Helpful_. This creates mechanical clarity when players use magic to alter NPC attitudes and ensures the effect ties into the same emotional model used across social systems.

## 🧠 **Sanity** 
[[Sanity]] impacts Disposition Indirectly

- **Low Sanity** makes characters act erratically or dangerously, triggering **Suspicious or Hostile** reactions from NPCs—even if the character has no criminal record.
- Certain trauma effects (e.g. **Paranoia**, **Hallucinations**) affect how NPCs perceive the character in conversation or public spaces.
- In religious or tightly controlled cities (e.g., [[07_Geography/02_Cities/Thar Vornhal]]), visible Sanity deterioration may be seen as a spiritual corruption or a sign of Eldritch taint, forcing **Indifferent → Suspicious** shifts.
## 🎯 **Focus** 
[[Focus]] modulates Disposition Outcomes Under Pressure

- A high Focus score allows characters to stay composed during tense social or legal encounters (e.g., interrogations, bribes, disguises).
- Losing Focus can cause **narrative tells** (twitches, stuttering glyph residue, illusion bleed) that lower Disposition temporarily.
- In courtrooms, trials, or inquisitions, Focus can be used in place of Insight to detect manipulation or maintain control under magical duress.

## ☣️ **Corruption** 
[[Corruption]] destabilizes Disposition at High Levels

- Characters with **visible corruption** (e.g., mirrored eyes, glyph-ink blood) are often treated with fear or violence.
- **Terminal Corruption (CS 20+)** automatically shifts most NPCs to **Hostile**, except black market or Eldritch-aligned factions.
- Even minor corruption may seed rumours in tightly controlled cities, causing **price increases**, **service refusal**, or NPC avoidance.

## 🧩 **Memory** 
[[Memory]] influences Reputation and Disposition Context

- Memory governs what players remember about people, cities, and crimes. A failed Memory check might result in social faux pas or criminal misidentification.
- Successfully recalling past kindnesses or protocols improves **initial Disposition** in familiar settlements or among allies.
- Interacting with memory-altered or echo-bound NPCs requires Memory checks to **correctly respond**—affecting the flow of persuasion or intimidation.
  
## 🕵️ **Streetwise** 
The skill [[Streetwise]] improves Control Over Disposition Outcomes in Cities

- Allows PCs to read a crowd, neighbourhood, or guard’s mood and **predict or avoid Disposition pitfalls**.
- Used to **mask reputation**, delay Wanted Score effects, or **find the right merchant** who will still deal with them.
- Streetwise may shift **Suspicious → Indifferent** in hostile districts if players use it to blend in or speak the local slang.

### Example Use:

> In a town where the party is known criminals ([[Wanted Score]] 4), a successful Streetwise check lets them identify a neutral innkeeper who cares more about coin than crime—avoiding an automatic Hostile reaction.
