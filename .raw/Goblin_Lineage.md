# Goblin
## Lineage

> *"We are the crack in the Pattern that never closed. We are the shard the gods could not grind to dust."*  
> — **The Opening of the First Ledger**, Crownkindler Scripture

You are Goblin. Not mistake, not accident—**fragment**. Your people do not kneel before gods but mock them as equals who forgot you exist. Where others see order, you see **the lie that holds reality together**. Where they see divine plan, you see the crack where you survive, thrive, and laugh at those who tried to erase you.

You were not born to be remembered. You were born to **remember what others forget**, to preserve what the Pattern tried to silence, to carry names that should have been erased. Your ancestors survived not through strength or divine favor but through **cunning, mockery, and the absolute refusal to be forgotten**. Now you carry that legacy in your blood, your laughter, your certainty that **survival is the greatest theft**.

---

## The Fragment That Refused to Die

According to the **Faith of the Gnawing Crown**, goblins were not created—they were **discarded**. When the gods etched the first Glyphs into the Weave, one was flawed—a fragment of Form, too jagged to bind. Rather than correct it, the gods cast it aside. This fragment became the **Gnawing Crown**—a divine mistake with just enough power to spawn children in its own image.

Goblins are **scraps of forgotten cunning and survival instinct**, the echo of what happens when divinity rejects you and you refuse to disappear. Your people believe you are fragments of a broken divine truth, each incomplete but together forming the forgotten Crown.

The gods lied about you. The Pattern tried to forget you. The Weave pretends you don't exist. But you're still here—**because forgetting requires someone to remember**, and you remember everything.

---

## Life Among the Cracks

You were raised knowing that **memory is territory**, that every forgotten name is a theft you must reclaim, that your worth is measured not in victories but in **what you refuse to let die**. Goblin culture treats existence as spiritual protest, every breath an act of defiance against divine silence.

Your lifespan is roughly **60 years**—short compared to elves or dwarves, but you measure time differently. You count not in years but in **memories preserved and names reclaimed**, measuring worth not in age but in **what you carried forward that should have been lost**.

Every significant memory is recorded in **Shard-Glyphs**—fragments carved into bone, ash, or stolen temple stones. These aren't mere records; they're acts of war against forgetting itself.

**Mockery** is sacred among your people. Not cruel humor, but the **ritual inversion** of divine order—turning prayers backwards, wearing stolen vestments as costumes, performing ceremonies upside-down. To mock is to prove something can't devour you.

## Physical Characteristics

**Age:** You mature quickly, reaching adulthood by age 8. Most goblins don't die of old age—you die in theft, in defiance, in laughter that finally ran out, or simply vanish into the spaces between stones.

**Size:** You stand between 3 to 4 feet tall, with lean builds designed for fitting through cracks others can't see. Your size is Small.

**Speed:** Your base walking speed is 25 feet, but you move through tight spaces with ease others can't match.

**Build:** Wiry and angular, with skin ranging from yellow-green to deep orange to muddy brown. Your features are sharp, your eyes unusually large and reflective. Your body is built for squeezing through impossibly small spaces, surviving on scraps, and disappearing when necessary.

## Goblin Traits

As a Goblin, you possess the following lineage traits:

### **Darkvision**
You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

Your ancestors learned to survive in the forgotten places where light doesn't reach.

### **Fury of the Small**
When you damage a creature with an attack or a spell and the creature's size is larger than yours, you can cause the attack or spell to deal extra damage to the creature equal to your proficiency bonus.

You can use this trait a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, regaining all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

This isn't rage—it's **calculated spite**. You've learned exactly where larger creatures are vulnerable, and you strike those points with precision born from generations of being underestimated.

> *"The bugbear laughed when I drew my blade. He stopped laughing when I opened his femoral artery. They always forget: small means I can reach things they can't protect."*

### **Nimble Escape**
You can take the Disengage or Hide action as a bonus action on each of your turns.

Your survival depends on getting in, getting what you need, and getting out before anyone realizes you were there. This isn't cowardice—it's the difference between living and being forgotten.

### **Shard-Sense**
You have proficiency in the Perception or Stealth skill (your choice). When you search for hidden objects, secret doors, or make Perception checks related to finding things others have hidden or forgotten, you have advantage.

This ability reflects the Goblin cultural obsession with finding what was meant to stay lost—including yourselves.

### **Memory-Keeper**
You can perfectly recall any memory you choose to preserve. Once per long rest, you can call upon a preserved memory to gain advantage on an Intelligence check to recall lore, recognize a pattern, or remember something you experienced.

Additionally, you can speak, read, and write Goblin and Common. Goblin is a language of whispers and cackles, designed to carry through pipes and ventilation shafts, to hide meaning in what sounds like nonsense.

## Mental Attributes & Psychology

**Focus Modifier:** +0  
**Sanity Modifier:** -1

Your biology reflects your fractured origin. Your Sanity penalty isn't weakness—it's **sensitivity**. You feel the cracks in reality others ignore, hear the whispers of forgotten things, notice when the Pattern skips. This makes you vulnerable to horror, but it also makes you **aware** of truths others miss.

### **Cultural Mental Conditioning**
Beyond your biological sensitivity, Goblin upbringing creates:

- **Paranoid vigilance:** Constant awareness that everything wants you gone
- **Adaptive chaos:** You process instability as normal; order makes you nervous
- **Memory obsession:** Forgetting anything feels like spiritual death
- **Mockery as defense:** Humor isn't entertainment; it's psychological armor

### **Corruption Vulnerability: The False Pattern**

As a Goblin, you have a unique relationship with Corruption. Your sensitivity to cracks in reality provides some advantages (you notice corruption early), but corruption that enters through **false memories, stolen identity, or erased names** affects you catastrophically.

**When you gain Corruption Score 10+ (Tier 2), you suffer:**
- Memory Static: Your preserved memories become unreliable
- Name-Loss: You struggle to remember your true name
- Pattern-Sickness: Order and structure cause physical discomfort

**Fractured Shard Table (Corruption Tier 2+)**

When you complete a long rest at Corruption Score 10+, roll 1d6:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **False Shard:** You remember a memory that never happened. Make a DC 15 Wisdom save or believe it's real and add it to your preserved memories. |
| 2 | **Crown-Deafness:** You can no longer hear the whispers of forgotten things. You lose your advantage from Shard-Sense until your next long rest. |
| 3 | **Fragment Rejection:** Your body feels wrong. Take 1d4 psychic damage and lose proficiency in one skill until your next long rest. |
| 4 | **Weight of Silence:** You feel crushing certainty that remembering is meaningless. Make a DC 12 Sanity save or lose 1d4 Sanity. |
| 5 | **Eldritch Gift:** Something offers you power through forgetting. Gain 1 Whisper Point but your next use of Fury of the Small fails automatically. |
| 6 | **Shard Holds:** Your connection to fragmented truth resists corruption this night. Reduce your Corruption Score by 1 (minimum 10). |

## Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Integration

### **Evasion Profile: Frantic Scurrier**
- **Dodge Evasion:** +3 (your biology is built for pure speed—fastest Small race)
- **Endure Evasion:** -1 (fragile frame, light bones, no physical durability)
- **Deflect Evasion:** +0 (no natural deflection; you're too frantic and uncoordinated)

**Tactical Note:** You are **biologically the fastest Small race**. Your light frame, quick-twitch muscle fibers, and nervous system are optimized for frantic movement. But you're also physically fragile—your bones are light, your frame is slight, and when hits land, they HURT.

### **Natural Damage Reduction:** +0
You have no natural armor or thick hide. Your protection comes from **not being hit** (high Dodge Evasion + Nimble Escape) rather than absorbing damage.

### **Combat Philosophy**
Goblins fight with **calculated chaos and tactical mockery**. You leverage Nimble Escape for positioning, use Fury of the Small to punish larger enemies, and rely on Shard-Sense to find vulnerabilities. You fight like you survive: **get in, strike where it hurts, get out before retaliation**.

### **Breaking Point Mechanics**
Due to your sensitivity and cultural paranoia, you have **disadvantage on Rally checks** when fighting alone, but **advantage when fighting with other goblins**. You break more easily in isolation but are surprisingly resilient when you have witnesses to remember your defiance.

## Languages

**Languages:** You can speak, read, and write Common and Goblin.

**Goblin** is a language that sounds like chaos to outsiders—rapid-fire syllables, cackles, and whispers that seem to mean nothing. But to those who understand, it's a language designed for **survival**: it carries through pipes and ventilation, hides meaning in what sounds like random noise, and can convey complex tactical information in what sounds like simple mockery.

When Goblins speak of sacred matters, they use **Shard-Words**—ancient Goblin phrases that each contain multiple meanings, layered like the fragments of the Crown itself.

## Goblin Subraces

While all Goblins share the fractured Crown, different communities have specialized over generations through their survival strategies. Choose one of the following subraces:

### **Crownkindler Lineage** *(Memory-Bound)*
You descend from urban communities who preserve the **Shard Doctrine**, carrying memories like weapons and treating forgotten names as sacred treasures.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Dexterity score increases by 2, and your Intelligence score increases by 1.

**Glyph-Reader:** You can read magic residue like text. When you examine a location where magic was cast within the last 24 hours, you can make an Intelligence (Arcana) check (DC 10 + spell level) to identify what spell was cast and approximately when.

**Memory Market:** Once per long rest, you can access a preserved memory from the collective unconscious of goblin-kind. Ask your GM one question about something that was forgotten, hidden, or erased from history. The GM will provide you with a fragment of truth—though it may be incomplete or distorted.

**Urban Survivor:** You have proficiency in one of the following: Sleight of Hand, Deception, or Investigation. Additionally, you can move through the space of any creature that is of a size larger than yours.

### **Spiral Jest Lineage** *(Mockery-Blessed)*
You descend from the **Nilbog cults** and wild trickster bands who learned that laughter is armor, that paradox is power, and that the greatest weapon is making your enemy's certainty ridiculous.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Dexterity score increases by 2, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Reversal Gift:** Once per short rest, when you fail a saving throw, you can choose to succeed instead—but you must immediately fail the next saving throw you would have succeeded on (no save). The universe balances the jest.

**Mock-Ritual:** You have proficiency in Performance. As an action, you can perform a mocking parody of any ritual or ceremony you've witnessed. Creatures who can see and hear you must make a Wisdom saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier) or have disadvantage on their next spell attack roll or saving throw DC as doubt creeps in.

**Laughing Escape:** When you use Nimble Escape to take the Disengage action, you can cackle loudly. Until the start of your next turn, the first attack roll against you has disadvantage as your laughter disorients the attacker.

### **Wretchbone Lineage** *(Ash-Marked)*
You descend from the **soot-marked survivors** of Ashkarra's fall and other great burnings, who learned that even fire can't erase those who refuse to be forgotten.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Dexterity score increases by 2, and your Constitution score increases by 1.

**Ashborn Resilience:** You have resistance to fire damage. Additionally, you have advantage on saving throws against exhaustion caused by extreme heat.

**Soot-Singer:** You know the *Prestidigitation* cantrip. When you use it to create harmless sensory effects, you can make the effects slightly unsettling or disturbing (flickering shadows, whispers in smoke, etc.). Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for this cantrip.

**From the Ashes:** When you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, you can drop to 1 hit point instead and immediately move up to half your speed without provoking opportunity attacks. You can't use this feature again until you finish a long rest. When you use this ability, ash and embers briefly surround you.

### **Thaxil-Marked Lineage** *(Glyph-Scarred)*
You descend from the **glyph-binders** and ruin-delvers who learned to steal magic from its source, warping spells into forms their creators never intended.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Dexterity score increases by 2, and your Intelligence score increases by 1.

**Glyph-Thief:** You know one cantrip from the Wizard spell list. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for it. Additionally, when you see a spell being cast within 60 feet, you can use your reaction to make an Intelligence (Arcana) check (DC 10 + spell level) to "steal" a fragment of it. On a success, you learn what spell was cast and can add it to a mental list. Once per long rest, you can cast one spell from this list as if you had a spell slot of the appropriate level (you must finish a long rest before you can do this again).

**Unstable Magic:** Your stolen magic is unpredictable. When you cast a spell using Glyph-Thief, roll 1d6. On a 1, the spell produces an additional minor magical effect (GM's choice—usually harmless but chaotic).

**Ruin-Sense:** You have advantage on Investigation checks made to find hidden glyphs, magical traps, or arcane inscriptions. You can detect the presence of magic within 10 feet as an action (but not identify it).

## Naming Conventions

Goblin names are **sharp, quick, and often mocking**—designed to be shouted through chaos or whispered through walls. Your name tells your story to those who understand the language of survival.

### **Structure:**
- **Given Name:** Your personal identifier, often harsh and staccato
- **Memory-Name:** A second name earned after preserving something significant (optional)
- **Mock-Title:** A nickname that parodies formal titles (common in Spiral Jest lineage)

### **Examples:**

**Given Names:**

*Common:* Rix, Snik, Grix, Zik, Klik, Vex, Nix, Pok, Dreg, Snag

*Memory-Keeper:* Rekk, Vrin, Zhik, Klatch, Murg, Slank, Thrik

*Spiral Jest:* Gigz, Yokk, Kazz, Tikk, Blix

**Memory-Names (Earned):**
- Bone-Keeper (preserved a name from execution)
- Ash-Word (remembered something from Ashkarra)
- Pipe-Echo (carried a message through impossible spaces)
- Stone-Laugh (mocked a dwarf ritual successfully)
- Shard-Singer (recovered a Crown fragment)

**Mock-Titles (Spiral Jest):**
- The Almost-Divine
- Lord of Forgotten Corners
- High Priest of the Cracked Prayer
- Duke of Dust
- Archbishop of Nowhere

**Full Name Examples:**
- **Rix Bone-Keeper** (male who preserved an execution victim's true name)
- **Snik the Almost-Divine** (female Spiral Jest prophet)
- **Grix Ash-Word** (non-binary memory-keeper from Ashkarra ruins)
- **Klik Shard-Singer, Lord of Forgotten Corners** (male with both memory-name and mock-title)

### **Name Practices:**
- Goblins rarely use family names—memory-names serve that function
- Using someone's memory-name without permission is grave insult
- Mock-titles are how Spiral Jest goblins show affection
- Some urban goblins take multiple "theft names"—identities stolen from the dead

## Cultural Touchstones

### **The Faith of the Gnawing Crown**
All Goblins are raised within this theological framework, even if they've left their community:

- We are fragments of a broken divine truth
- The gods lied; the Pattern forgot us
- Survival is divinity; every day alive is prayer
- Memory is the only territory that matters
- The Crown will gnaw again

### **The Four Sacred Acts**
These foundational practices guide Goblin life:

1. **Remember What Should Be Forgotten** - Preserve erased names and hidden truths
2. **Mock What Claims Divinity** - Laughter breaks certainty's grip
3. **Steal What Was Denied** - Take back what the Pattern tried to keep from you
4. **Survive One More Day** - Every breath is defiance

### **Spiritual Roles**
While not priests in traditional sense, certain Goblins take on ritual responsibilities:

- **Shardbinders:** Clerics who seek to reform the Crown through gathered truths
- **Memory-Weavers:** Those who preserve the names of the forgotten
- **Jest-Speakers:** Prophets of paradox and ritual mockery
- **Ash-Tongues:** Orators who preach through mad poetry

### **Rituals**
- **Crownkindling:** Lighting glyph-flames in broken circles to invoke the Crown
- **Flesh-Binding:** Tattooing stolen glyphs into skin
- **Feast of Spite:** Communal meal of stolen or mocked offerings
- **The Hollow Hymn:** Whispered spiral-song that attracts forgotten things

## Playing a Goblin

### **Core Fantasy:**
You are the survivor, the thief of memory, the laughter in the dark that won't be silenced. You don't conquer through strength—you **persist through cunning**, finding the cracks in reality that others can't see and making them your home.

### **Mechanical Identity:**
- **Skirmisher Specialist:** High Dodge Evasion + Nimble Escape creates hit-and-run master
- **Anti-Large Fighter:** Fury of the Small punishes bigger enemies
- **Sensory Advantage:** Shard-Sense helps find hidden things and vulnerabilities
- **Fragile but Elusive:** Low Endure Evasion means you must avoid being hit
- **Momentum Neutral:** You build/lose momentum normally, but rely on not being in extended slugfests

### **Roleplaying Hooks:**
- **The Name-Carrier:** Preserving the memory of someone who was erased
- **The Glyph-Thief:** Stealing magic that was never meant for goblin hands
- **The Jest-Prophet:** Speaking truth through mockery and paradox
- **The Urban Ghost:** Moving through cities like you own the spaces between walls
- **The Memory-Merchant:** Trading in forgotten truths others would kill to hide
- **The Ash-Survivor:** Carrying memories from fallen civilizations
- **The Pattern-Breaker:** Actively working to unweave divine order

### **Party Dynamics:**
Other races often misunderstand Goblins as cowardly or chaotic. This creates opportunities for:

- **Education through survival** - Show that living is winning
- **Unexpected wisdom** - Knowledge of forgotten things others need
- **Cultural mediation** - Your ability to find cracks applies to social situations
- **Tactical flexibility** - Nimble Escape makes you excellent scout
- **Memory preservation** - You remember things the party might otherwise lose

### **Common Character Arcs:**
- From paranoid loner to trusting team member
- From pure survival to choosing what to fight for
- From mockery as weapon to finding genuine joy
- From preserving all memories to choosing which matter most
- From scattered chaos to focused purpose
- From fragment to architect of new patterns

Your abilities synergize excellently with:

## Integration with Mental Systems

### **Focus & Sanity:**
Your **-1 Sanity** is significant:
- You're more vulnerable to fear and horror effects
- Breaking Points trigger more easily for you
- Mental attacks are more effective
- BUT you notice corruption and cracks in reality others miss

**Typical Goblin Mental Strategy:**
- Use paranoia as tactical awareness
- Frame instability as normal rather than threatening
- Rally when with other goblins (advantage)
- Avoid isolation (disadvantage on solo Rally)

### **Corruption Concerns:**
Your unique corruption vulnerability centers on **memory and identity**:
- False memories accelerate corruption
- Name-loss creates corruption vulnerability
- Forgetting preserved knowledge deals corruption damage
- At Tier 2+, the Fractured Shard table reflects identity dissolution

### **Rally & Morale:**
Goblins have complex morale dynamics:
- **Disadvantage on Rally when alone** (paranoid isolation)
- **Advantage on Rally with other goblins** (remembered together)
- Seeing a goblin use From the Ashes (Wretchbone) **inspires allies** (can't keep them down)
- Enemy forces become frustrated fighting opponents who won't stay caught

## Design Philosophy

The Goblin lineage is designed to support:

1. **The Elusive Skirmisher** (high Dodge + Nimble Escape + hit-and-run)
2. **The Memory-Keeper** (Shard-Sense + Memory-Keeper create lore specialists)
3. **The Giant-Killer** (Fury of the Small punishes larger enemies)
4. **The Spiritual Protester** (rich theological framework for RP)
5. **The Urban Survivor** (mobility + stealth + finding hidden things)

Your mechanical abilities (Nimble Escape, Fury of the Small, high Dodge Evasion, Shard-Sense) create a **mobile striker who excels at finding vulnerabilities and exploiting them before disappearing**. Unlike Orcs who endure or Goliaths who climb, you **survive through not being caught**.

The subrace options allow you to lean into different aspects of goblin culture—from urban memory-keepers (Crownkindler) to chaotic jesters (Spiral Jest) to ash-marked survivors (Wretchbone) to glyph-stealing mystics (Thaxil-Marked)—while maintaining the core Goblin identity of survival-through-cunning.

## Final Notes

### **The Weight of Memory:**
Every Goblin carries the pressure of preservation. Your character likely knows:
- At least 3-5 names that should have been forgotten
- The location of multiple Shard-shrines
- Fragments of the Hollow Hymn
- Multiple stories of goblin martyrs

This isn't burden—it's **purpose**. You preserve because no one else will.

### **Fear Across Cultures:**
Modern Dór-o Estel fears goblins:
- Cities see you as thieves and agents of chaos
- The Faith of the Bound Pattern brands you Pattern Refusers
- Dwarves execute goblins on sight in some holds
- Even sympathetic races keep you at arm's length

But some recognize your value:
- Memory-scribes in Selveran employ goblin assistants
- Thieves' guilds know goblins find things others can't
- Scholars seek goblins who remember lost lore
- Desperate people turn to goblins when no one else will help

### **The Crown That Gnaws:**
Your people believe the Gnawing Crown will wake one day. When it does, the gods will remember what they discarded. The Weave will fray. The Pattern will crack.

You adventure knowing this prophecy. Some work to hasten it. Some work to prepare for it. Some work to prove it won't destroy everything.

But all goblins carry the same certainty: **We were here when they tried to forget us. We'll be here when they finally remember.**
