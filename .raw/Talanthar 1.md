The Greenwood Sovereignty of Talanthar

Overview

Talanthar, known in elvish as Vael’Tharyn (the Verdant Crown), stands as the last unconquered bastion of the Old Ways within Elarion. To the outside world, it is whispered of as The Forest Kingdom, The Court of Roots, or simply The Greenwood. To its people, however, Talanthar is not a kingdom at all, but a living covenant between mortals and the ageless spirit of the forest.

Nestled between the towering Skyspine Mountains and the misted hills of Corvessa’s eastern frontier, Talanthar thrives within a labyrinth of river-carved gorges, luminous caverns, and colossal trees whose roots and canopies interlace to form both home and fortress.


---

Geography

The Heart of Talanthar lies where two swift rivers — the Vaelrun and the Thalaris — converge. Over eons, their waters have carved deep gorges and honeycombed caverns through the bedrock, creating the natural amphitheater that houses the city of Talanthar itself.

Above ground, the forest canopy forms living bridges and sun-dappled glades, while below, the underworld is a realm of phosphorescent fungi, subterranean streams, and crystal-lit forests. Vast pillars of stone support the caverns, with ancient bridges and carved crannogs linking them in every direction.

Major Geographic Features

The Vaelthir Crown: The upper terraces surrounding the World Tree, said to be the first of its kind in Elarion. Its roots feed both rivers and its leaves glow faintly at dusk.

Skyperch Cliffs: The ridgelines overlooking the gorge, where griffons and their riders make their aeries.

Moonveil Glades: Misted hollows and shallow vales where witch covens and spirit seers dwell.

The Thornward Flats: A marshy plain between Corvessa and the forest edge, where countless invasions have died in the mud.

The Underbloom: The vast caverns beneath the city, filled with glowing lichens, fungal orchards, and subterranean groves.



---

The City of Talanthar

Talanthar’s capital is not a single city, but a vertical labyrinth of terraces, bridges, and caverns that span the cliffs where the rivers meet. Homes are grown from living wood or carved into the stone itself. Lanternlight and glowberry vines cast a perpetual twilight hue upon the streets, while waterfalls cascade from level to level.

Key Districts:

The High Crown: Sacred terraces surrounding the World Tree’s roots. Home to the Archdruid, the Circle of the Moonweave, and the Council of Nine Groves.

The Silver Hollow: The underground district where trade, metalwork, and magic converge. Blacksmiths here forge using river-spirits’ breath to temper enchanted alloys.

The Bridge-Courts: Hanging walkways and suspended markets where griffon feathers, enchanted hides, and rare herbs are traded.

The Shadow Pools: Caverns of still water where the witches of Ishara and Sylthara hold rituals under bioluminescent trees.


Talanthar feels alive — its architecture grown, not built; its people dwelling in harmony with the ancient forest rather than above it.


---

Culture & Society

Talanthar is less a monarchy and more a federation of circles: druidic orders, witch covens, ranger brotherhoods, and halfling joy-tribes. Each governs its own grove or valley, sending a representative to the High Council of the Greenwood, presided over by the Archdruid of Vaelthir.

Beliefs & Religion

The people venerate Vaelthir, the World Tree, as both spirit and guardian — a being older than the gods, though not one of them. Every birth, death, and union is marked by blood offerings to the earth, acknowledging life’s cycle and the forest’s price.

Temples to Ishara, Thamur, Sylthara, and Sarnock stand beside the groves, representing balance between nature’s fury and grace. Witches, druids, and shamans act as intermediaries with lesser forest spirits.

Traditions & Daily Life

Children’s first blood is given to the forest so it “knows” them.

Crops are modest: glowmoss rice, river millet, and shadowleaf herbs cultivated in flooded terraces or lighted caverns.

Metal is traded from the dwarven holds or scavenged from the ruins of failed invasions, reforged by Magus-smiths.

Halfling tribes hunt from giant raptors or elk, revered as the Joy of the Greenwood.


Festivals follow the lunar cycles: the Night of Still Waters, when all magic and song fall silent to let the forest rest, and the Rising Veil, when druids call the spirits to awaken the new season.


---

Relationships with Neighbours

Corvessa

Corvessa sees Talanthar as an affront — uncivilised, heretical, and wild. Talanthar views Corvessa’s order and faith as desecration. Centuries of war, raids, and failed crusades have proven the forest unconquerable. The infamous Thornward Flats Disaster (482 FA) shattered Corvessa’s pride, their knights broken by terrain, magic, and wild fury.

The Kel Varyan Empire

The Empire approached differently — with caution and curiosity. Their skirmish decades later ended in blood and lessons learned: that discipline fails where the forest itself fights back. Diplomacy followed, not conquest.

Trade & Outsiders

Talanthar trades rarely but pragmatically: medicinal herbs, enchanted wood, griffon feathers, and leather for metal and crystal. Travellers find few inns, but those who follow the forest’s signs are led to safe havens that shift in location but never vanish.

Key Dates in History

Pre-Imperial Age: The first druids awaken Vaelthir. Elves, halflings, and beastfolk swear the Oath of the Greenwood.

First Age of Crowns: Corvessan river-chiefs attempt first incursions — all vanish beyond the Thornward.

Third Age, Year 312: The First Crusade of the Pattern — crushed at the Veil of Roots.

Fourth Age, Year 487 (Present): Talanthar stands unconquered; the High Council debates renewal of the Covenant as Imperial agents prowl the borderlands.



---

The Nature of the Forest

Even to its own people, Talanthar’s forest is not entirely safe. There are groves where the trees whisper in forgotten tongues, glades where time flows unevenly, and spirits older than memory. The forest protects its children, but it also tests them — demanding respect, blood, and balance.

Those who ignore its will vanish, becoming part of the roots and legends that sustain it.
