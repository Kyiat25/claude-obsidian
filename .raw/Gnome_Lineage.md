# Gnome
## Lineage

> *"They told us we were too small to matter. So we invented fireworks, wrote treatises on the nature of laughter, and discovered seventeen ways to make cheese sentient. Then we asked: 'What were you saying?'"*  
> — **Quillix Wonderwhim**, First Archivist of the Impossible

You are Gnome. Not cursed to be small—**blessed to see small things**. Your people do not build monuments that pierce the sky but engines that **remake possibility**. Where others see the world as it is, you see the world as it **could be, should be, might be if you just twist this one thing sideways**.

You were not born to conquer or endure. You were born to **question, to tinker, to laugh at the cosmic joke and then improve the punchline**. Your ancestors didn't carve their names in stone—they wrote them in transformation, in the moment a machine first sparked to life, in the second before a discovery changed everything.

---

## The Wonder-Tithe Codex

According to the **Wonder-Tithe Codex**, gnomes were not created—they were **iterative improvements**. When the gods finished making the world, they sat back and said "This will do." And in that moment of divine satisfaction, the gnomes appeared and asked: "But have you considered...?"

Your people believe the universe is fundamentally **unfinished**, that reality is a first draft desperately in need of revision, and that curiosity is not merely virtue but **sacred obligation**. The Wonder-Tithe teaches that every gnome must pay their "tithe" to existence: **seven wonders witnessed, seven questions answered, seven impossibilities proven wrong**.

The gods may have built the world, but gnomes **optimize it**.

This is not blasphemy—it is **collaborative creation**. Gods provide the raw material; gnomes provide the imagination. Where other races worship through prayer, gnomes worship through **iteration**:

- **Quillix the Questionwright** (patron of impossible inquiries)
- **Vix'ara Sparkwhisper** (keeper of delightful failure)
- **Torble Geargrind** (saint of the thirty-seventh attempt)
- **Nimblewick the Necessary** (who proved gods could be wrong about cheese)

## Life Among the Curious

You were raised knowing that **standing still is death**, that the same answer twice means you've stopped learning, that the greatest tragedy is dying with an unanswered question still burning in your mind.

**Wonder (zhalix)** is not metaphorical. It is:
- Counted by inventions, discoveries, solved puzzles, and delightful accidents
- Stored in journals, schematics, prototypes, and the moment before "...oh no"
- Traded freely—knowledge hoarded is knowledge wasted

Your lifespan is roughly **350-500 years**—long enough to see the consequences of your curiosity, short enough to keep the pressure on. You measure time not in years but in **Iterations** (the cycles between major breakthrough and catastrophic failure), and you measure worth not in gold but in **"Have I made something that didn't exist before?"**

Every major discovery is recorded in **Wonder-Wheels**—rotating copper disks etched with breakthrough moments, preserved in universities and workshop-shrines across gnomish communities.

## Physical Characteristics

**Age:** You mature by age 40 (though emotional maturity is debatable at any age). Most gnomes live 350-500 years, during which they accumulate enough failed experiments to teach several universities.

**Size:** You stand between 3 to 4 feet tall, with builds ranging from wiry and quick to stocky and sturdy. Your size is Small.

**Speed:** Your base walking speed is 25 feet. What you lack in stride, you make up for in clever shortcuts.

**Build:** Compact and efficient, with dexterous fingers that can manipulate fine mechanisms and expressive faces that broadcast every emotion (whether you want them to or not). Your hair often comes in unusual colors—vibrant blues, forest greens, warm coppers—and has a tendency to stand on end when you're excited (which is often).

## Gnome Traits

As a Gnome, you possess the following lineage traits:

### **Darkvision**
You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

Many generations spent in workshops with poor lighting have adapted your vision to function in near-dark conditions.

### **Gnome Cunning**
You have advantage on all Intelligence and Wisdom saving throws against magic.

This is not merely biological—it reflects gnomish philosophy. Magic is **just another system to understand, optimize, and potentially improve**. When a spell targets your mind, your first instinct isn't fear—it's "How does this work? Can I make it better?"

> *"The lich tried to dominate my mind. I spent three rounds analyzing the spell structure and suggesting improvements. He got so frustrated he teleported away."*

### **Small Frame**
You count as one size smaller when determining your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag, or lift.

However, gnomish ingenuity has led to countless inventions to compensate: pulley systems, levitation charms, very persuasive friends.

### **Artificer's Insight**
You have proficiency in one of the following: Arcana, Investigation, or one set of artisan's tools of your choice.

Additionally, when you make an Intelligence check to understand how a magical or mechanical device works, you can add twice your proficiency bonus instead of your normal proficiency bonus.

This represents the gnomish gift for **reverse-engineering reality**.

### **Tinker** *(Variant Feature)*
You have proficiency with artisan's tools (tinker's tools). Using those tools, you can spend 1 hour and 10 gp worth of materials to construct a Tiny clockwork device (AC 5, 1 hp). The device ceases to function after 24 hours unless you spend 1 hour repairing it. You can use your action to dismantle the device, at which point you can reclaim the materials used to create it. You can have up to three such devices active at a time.

You can create the following devices:

- **Clockwork Toy:** A toy animal, monster, or person that moves 5 feet in a direction on each of your turns when wound up.
- **Fire Starter:** Produces a miniature flame for lighting candles, torches, or campfires.
- **Music Box:** Plays a single song at a moderate volume when opened.

At the GM's discretion, you may create other harmless devices with similar complexity.

### **Wonder-Speech**
You can speak, read, and write Common and Gnomish. Additionally, you know one extra language of your choice.

**Gnomish** is a rapid, complex language with seventeen tenses (including "definitely will happen," "probably won't happen," and "happened but I wish it hadn't"), extensive technical vocabulary, and a peculiar tendency to turn any conversation into a collaborative problem-solving session. Gnomes speaking about their projects literally cannot stay on topic—every explanation branches into fascinating tangents.

## Mental Attributes & Psychology

**Focus Modifier:** +1  
**Sanity Modifier:** +0

Your biology grants you **exceptional mental focus**. While your Sanity is standard, your Focus receives a bonus because gnomish brains are literally structured for sustained attention on complex tasks. You can hyperfocus for hours—and frequently do, forgetting to eat, sleep, or notice the building is on fire.

However, this comes with a trade-off: you're also **easily distracted by interesting new problems**. The same focus that lets you solve impossible equations means you might stop mid-combat to examine an unusual mechanism.

### **Cultural Mental Conditioning**
Beyond your biological advantage, gnomish upbringing creates:

- **Problem-Solving Compulsion:** You struggle to leave puzzles unsolved
- **Failure Normalization:** You view failure as "successful learning about what doesn't work"
- **Collaborative Thinking:** You instinctively seek second opinions and different perspectives
- **Temporal Flexibility:** You often lose track of time when focused

### **Corruption Vulnerability: The Static Mind**

As a Gnome, you have a unique relationship with Corruption. Your natural curiosity and mental agility provide excellent protection against many forms of mental corruption, but corruption that enters through **obsession, forbidden knowledge, or experiments gone wrong** affects you catastrophically.

**When you gain Corruption Score 10+ (Tier 2), you suffer:**
- Fractured Wonder: Your inventions begin to incorporate Eldritch elements without your conscious awareness
- Question-Weight: Answering questions becomes physically painful
- Curiosity Curse: You become compulsively drawn to study corrupted items and entities

**Tainted Innovation Table (Corruption Tier 2+)**

When you complete a long rest at Corruption Score 10+, roll 1d6:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **False Discovery:** You believe you've solved an impossible problem. Make a DC 15 Wisdom save or spend the next day pursuing this phantom breakthrough. |
| 2 | **Wonder-Static:** Your inventions begin whispering suggestions. You cannot benefit from Gnome Cunning against magical effects until your next long rest. |
| 3 | **Schematic Corruption:** Your notes and designs spontaneously develop Eldritch symbols. Take 1d4 psychic damage and lose proficiency in one tool until your next long rest. |
| 4 | **Weight of Knowing:** You feel crushed by terrible understanding you shouldn't possess. Make a DC 12 Sanity save or lose 1d4 Sanity. |
| 5 | **Eldritch Inspiration:** Something offers you a brilliant solution to a current problem. Gain 1 Whisper Point but your next Intelligence check fails automatically. |
| 6 | **Wonder Holds:** Your natural curiosity resists corruption this night. Reduce your Corruption Score by 1 (minimum 10). |

## Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Integration

### **Evasion Profile: Nimble Analyst**
- **Dodge Evasion:** +2 (you're small, quick, and excellent at getting out of the way)
- **Endure Evasion:** -1 (you're physically fragile)
- **Deflect Evasion:** +1 (you often carry shields, defensive gadgets, or have spells)

**Tactical Note:** You are designed to **avoid damage entirely** rather than tank it. Your high Dodge Evasion combined with Small size means you're genuinely difficult to hit, but when attacks land, they hurt.

### **Natural Damage Reduction:** +0
Your small frame provides no inherent protection. You rely on armor, cover, and not being where the attack lands.

### **Combat Philosophy**
Gnomes fight with **tactical precision and prepared contingencies**. You position carefully (high Dodge Evasion rewards good positioning), use cover effectively, and leverage prepared gadgets, spells, or environmental advantages. You don't stand in melee trading blows—you **control the battlefield** through clever preparation.

### **Breaking Point Mechanics**
Due to your curious nature and problem-solving focus, you have **advantage on Rally checks when you've observed an enemy's combat patterns**. After seeing an enemy attack three times, you can Rally with advantage as you've "figured out their system."

However, your Breaking Point is triggered more easily by **unanswered questions** or **unsolvable problems** than by casualties.

## Languages

**Languages:** You can speak, read, and write Common, Gnomish, and one extra language of your choice.

**Gnomish** is notorious for being:
- Extremely precise about technical matters
- Frustratingly vague about everything else
- Prone to recursive parenthetical statements
- Impossible to translate literally (every translation is an interpretation)

When Gnomes discuss serious matters, they often switch to Gnomish not for secrecy but for **precision**—some ideas require seventeen tenses and six grammatical moods to express correctly.

## Gnome Subraces

While all Gnomes share curiosity and ingenuity, different communities have specialized over generations through their particular focuses. Choose one of the following subraces:

### **Rock Gnome** *(Practical Inventors)*
You descend from gnomish communities that focused on **making things that actually work**. Rock gnomes are the engineers, the builders, the ones who don't just theorize but construct.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Intelligence score increases by 2, and your Constitution score increases by 1.

**Artificer's Lore:** Whenever you make an Intelligence (History) check related to magic items, alchemical objects, or technological devices, add twice your proficiency bonus instead of your normal proficiency bonus.

**Tinker:** You gain the Tinker trait (described above) if you didn't choose it as your variant feature. If you already have it, you can maintain up to **five** devices simultaneously (instead of three), and they last **48 hours** (instead of 24) before requiring repair.

**Stable Construction:** When you create magical or mechanical items during downtime, you require only **75% of the normal gold cost** and time (representing efficient use of materials and clever shortcuts).

### **Forest Gnome** *(Natural Illusionists)*
You come from communities that lived in deep forests, learning to blend nature and magic in ways that confound those who think "nature" and "artifice" are opposites.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Intelligence score increases by 2, and your Dexterity score increases by 1.

**Natural Illusionist:** You know the *minor illusion* cantrip. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for it.

**Speak with Small Beasts:** Through sounds and gestures, you can communicate simple ideas with Small or smaller beasts. Forest gnomes love animals and often keep squirrels, badgers, rabbits, moles, woodpeckers, and other creatures as beloved pets.

**Forest Camouflage:** You have advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks to hide in natural terrain that includes foliage, heavy rain, falling snow, mist, or other natural phenomena.

**Survival Tinker:** When you use your Tinker trait in natural environments, you can create devices using natural materials (wood, vines, stones) instead of requiring metal and purchased components.

### **Deep Gnome (Svirfneblin)** *(Underground Survivors)*
You are descended from gnomes who delved deep beneath the earth, learning to survive in hostile depths through paranoia, preparation, and grim determination.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Intelligence score increases by 2, and your Dexterity score increases by 1.

**Superior Darkvision:** Your darkvision has a range of 120 feet (instead of 60).

**Stone Camouflage:** You have advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks to hide in rocky terrain.

**Deep Gnome Resilience:** You have advantage on Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma saving throws against magic (this stacks with Gnome Cunning, granting you advantage on saving throws against magic twice—effectively, you'd need to roll a 1 to fail most magical saves).

**Languages:** You can speak, read, and write Common, Gnomish, and Undercommon.

**Sunlight Sensitivity:** You have disadvantage on attack rolls and Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight when you, the target of your attack, or whatever you are trying to perceive is in direct sunlight.

**Deep Engineering:** You have proficiency with mason's tools, and when working with stone or underground construction, you work at twice the normal speed.

### **Sparkwright Gnome** *(Arcane Experimenters)*
You come from the experimental magic academies where the motto is "If we haven't blown up the lab twice this month, we're not trying hard enough."

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Intelligence score increases by 2, and your Wisdom score increases by 1.

**Arcane Experimenter:** You know one cantrip of your choice from the wizard spell list. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for it.

**Magical Tinkering:** When you use your Tinker trait, you can create devices with minor magical effects:
- A device that glows as bright as a torch
- A device that repeatedly plays a recorded message (up to six seconds) when activated
- A device that produces a harmless sensory effect (smell, sound, or light pattern)

**Controlled Chaos:** Once per long rest, when you cast a spell that deals damage, you can choose to maximize one damage die instead of rolling it. However, roll 1d4—on a 1, the spell produces an additional unexpected (but harmless) effect at the GM's discretion.

**Spell Analysis:** When you observe a spell being cast, you can use your reaction to make an Intelligence (Arcana) check (DC 10 + spell level). On a success, you learn the spell's name, level, and school of magic.

## Naming Conventions

Gnome names are **aggressively whimsical** because gnomes believe names should make you smile. Many gnomes accumulate additional names throughout their lives based on achievements, mishaps, or characteristics.

### **Structure:**
- **Given Name:** Your personal identifier (usually melodic and cheerful)
- **Clan Name:** Your family designation (often relates to ancestral trades or discoveries)
- **Earned Epithet:** A nickname that develops over time (often embarrassing but worn with pride)

### **Examples:**

**Male Names:**

**Clan Names:**

**Earned Epithets:**
- Accidentally-Correct (solved a problem through a beautiful mistake)
- Enthusiastic-About-Bees (exactly what it sounds like)
- Probably-Fireproof (survived seventeen explosions)
- Technically-Succeeded (achieved the goal, destroyed the lab)
- Question-Collector (can't stop asking "why?")
- Optimization-Obsessed (made a device to optimize device-making)

### **Name Traditions:**
- Children receive given name and clan name at birth
- The **epithet** develops organically as you grow (usually by age 50-100)
- Some gnomes collect multiple epithets, using them like titles
- To reject your epithet is considered trying too hard to be serious (a gnomish sin)
- Many gnomes introduce themselves with their full name plus a brief explanation of how they earned their epithet

## Cultural Touchstones

### **The Wonder-Tithe Codex**
All Gnomes are raised within this philosophy. Even if you've rejected formal practice, its lessons shaped you:

- The universe is unfinished; improvement is sacred obligation
- Seven wonders must be paid: witnessed, answered, disproven
- Failure is data; repetition without variation is sin
- Share knowledge freely; hoarded wisdom is wasted wonder
- Question authority—especially divine authority
- If it works, find out why; if it doesn't, find out why not; if it shouldn't work but does, publish immediately

### **The Seven Obligations**
These foundational principles guide gnomish life:

1. **The Question-Tithe** - Ask one genuinely new question each week
2. **The Wonder-Witnessed** - Document seven impossible things you observe
3. **The Failure-Logged** - Record your mistakes so others can learn from them
4. **The Knowledge-Shared** - Teach freely what you discover
5. **The Iteration-Made** - Never settle for "good enough" when "better" is possible
6. **The Laughter-Kept** - Maintain joy even in failure
7. **The Pattern-Broken** - Prove at least one "impossibility" wrong in your lifetime

### **Wonder-Wheels**
The **Wonder-Wheels** are rotating copper disks mounted in gnomish communities, etched with breakthrough discoveries and catastrophic failures. They serve as both technical library and cautionary tale. When a gnome makes a significant discovery or causes a spectacular disaster, their name is added to the Wheel.

To have your name on a Wonder-Wheel is not merely honor—it is **proof you tried something new**.

## Playing a Gnome

### **Core Fantasy:**
You are the brilliant innovator, the relentless problem-solver, the person who looks at "impossible" and sees "I need more time to think about this." You don't dodge because you're cowardly—you dodge because **you've calculated exactly where the attack will land and you're not there**. Your size isn't limitation but **advantage**—you see details others miss, fit into spaces others can't, and approach problems from angles others never consider.

### **Mechanical Identity:**
- **Evasion Specialist:** High Dodge Evasion + Small size makes you difficult to hit
- **Mental Fortress:** +1 Focus + Gnome Cunning creates exceptional magical defense
- **Skill Monkey:** Artificer's Insight + extra language + subrace features
- **Tactical Combatant:** High Intelligence supports battlefield analysis and clever positioning
- **Support Master:** Tinker + magic items create utility options

### **Roleplaying Hooks:**
- **The Obsessed Inventor:** Currently working on your magnum opus (which keeps exploding)
- **The Question-Collector:** Keeping a journal of unanswered mysteries
- **The Accidental Disaster:** Your breakthroughs often come with collateral damage
- **The Collaborator:** Seeking other brilliant minds to workshop ideas
- **The Wonder-Seeker:** Adventuring specifically to witness impossible things
- **The Improvement Compulsion:** Cannot leave bad systems unoptimized
- **The Cheerful Pragmatist:** Approaching danger with curiosity instead of fear

### **Party Dynamics:**
Other races often find gnomes simultaneously delightful and infuriating. This creates opportunities for:

- **Comic Relief with Competence** - You're funny but also genuinely useful
- **Problem-Solving Montages** - Your inventions save the day in unexpected ways
- **Philosophical Contrast** - Challenge other characters' assumptions cheerfully
- **Expertise** - When the party needs to understand something technical, you shine
- **Optimistic Perseverance** - Your relentless positivity can inspire or annoy

### **Common Character Arcs:**
- From reckless experimentation to careful innovation
- From solo inventor to collaborative genius
- From theoretical knowledge to practical application
- From "more is more" to elegant simplicity
- From seeking approval to trusting your instincts
- From avoiding consequences to embracing responsibility

## Design Philosophy

The Gnome lineage is designed to support:

1. **The Brilliant Tactician** (high Focus + Gnome Cunning + Intelligence creates mental fortress)
2. **The Inventive Problem-Solver** (Tinker + Artificer's Insight create utility machine)
3. **The Evasive Combatant** (high Dodge Evasion + Small size creates hard-to-hit target)
4. **The Knowledge Seeker** (curiosity-driven character with mechanical support)
5. **The Support Specialist** (multiple ways to help allies through ingenuity)

Your mechanical abilities (Gnome Cunning, high Dodge Evasion, +1 Focus, Artificer's Insight) create a **mentally resilient, tactically clever character who survives through preparation and positioning**. You're designed to avoid damage, solve problems, and contribute through innovation rather than brute force.

The subrace options allow you to lean into different gnomish traditions—from practical inventors (Rock Gnome) to natural illusionists (Forest Gnome) to paranoid survivors (Deep Gnome) to arcane experimenters (Sparkwright)—while maintaining the core gnomish identity of curious innovation.

## Final Notes

### **The Weight of Wonder:**
Every Gnome carries questions. Your character likely:
- Maintains journals full of observations and theories
- Has at least three ongoing projects in various stages
- Knows multiple "impossible" questions they're working to answer
- Carries tools, components, or prototypes "just in case"

This isn't burden—it's **purpose**. You adventure because sitting still means questions go unanswered.

### **Respect Across Cultures:**
Modern Dór-o Estel recognizes Gnomish contributions:
- Dwarves respect gnomish engineering (even if it's "too fancy")
- Elves appreciate gnomish scholarship (even if it lacks reverence)
- Humans admire gnomish optimism (even if it seems naive)
- Even the Faith of the Bound Pattern employs gnomish inventors for temple mechanisms

### **The Wonder-Tithe:**
Your people believe life is measured in discoveries made and questions answered. The greatest gnomes are not those who accumulated wealth or power, but those who **changed what was considered possible**.

You adventure knowing that every mystery you solve, every invention you perfect, every "impossibility" you disprove **expands reality itself**. This is not pressure to fear—it is **certainty** that your curiosity matters beyond your lifespan.
