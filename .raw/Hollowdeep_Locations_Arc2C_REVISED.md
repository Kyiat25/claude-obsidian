## OVERVIEW: THE SEVEN DESCENDING LAYERS

The **Hollowdeep** is not merely an underground realm—it is a **metaphysical wound** in the fabric of Dór-o Estel, a vast network of chambers, tunnels, and impossible geometries where the **Weave itself has fractured and reformed into something alien**. It exists beneath every continent, connecting through ley-scarred passages that ignore conventional geography.

Unlike the surface, where the Five Threads (Earth, Fire, Water, Air, Spirit) maintain their elemental order, the Hollowdeep operates on **decayed Thread-logic**—magic filtered through stone, time, and trauma. Here, **echo-magic** and **memory-resonance** replace traditional spellcraft, and **thought itself becomes terrain**.

**Total Estimated Population:** 8-12 million sentient beings  
**Drow-Controlled Population:** ~5.5-6 million  
**Vertical Extent:** Surface to 42+ miles depth  
**Thread Alignment:** Corrupted Spirit (primary), distorted Fire and Earth (secondary)

# LAYER I: THE THRESHOLD REACHES
*The Contested Borderland*

**Depth:** 0.5-2 miles below surface  
**Character:** Frontier zone where surface ambition meets deep hunger

The Threshold Reaches serve as the **contested borderland** between the surface world and the true alien depths below. Unlike deeper layers, the Threshold still operates on relatively familiar principles—though those principles are strained, corrupted, and dangerous.

### Key Domains for Arc 2C

#### The Smugglers Highway

A network of hidden passages and neutral zones maintained by international criminal cartels. This is how **Whisper-That-Was's message** first reaches the surface.

**Major Hubs:**
- **Freeport Hollow** — Neutral trading post where drow "truth-bearers" make first contact
- **The Crossways Market** — Where identity-altering goods and forbidden knowledge change hands
- **Whisper's Rest** — Information broker stronghold; now infiltrated by those spreading revelations

**Campaign Use:** PCs first encounter escaped drow sharing Whisper's message at these locations. The Compact of Shadows (criminal governing body) is torn between profit from "truth-trade" and fear of drow retaliation.

#### The Bleeding Gates

**Location:** Throughout the Threshold, connecting to the Woundskin Layer below  
**Nature:** Permanent crimson portals that leak Drow magic upward  
**Current Status:** Activity increasing as Whisper's influence spreads

The Bleeding Gates are the primary passage points between the Threshold and the Drow domains. They glow with sickly red light and are heavily guarded on both sides—but Whisper's followers have begun using secondary gates.

**Campaign Use:** 
- **Act 1:** PCs investigate reports of strange lights at Threshold mining operations
- **Act 2:** PCs must navigate the Gates to descend into Drow territory
- **Defenses:** Drow War-Parties, trap-weavers, reflection-wards that reveal "true selves"

#### Vel'Thannis — The Elarion Espionage Node

**Location:** Beneath Kel Varyan and the Thorned Concord  
**Population:** ~45,000 Drow operatives and support staff  
**Function:** Intelligence gathering, surface infiltration, identity manipulation

The primary Drow outpost beneath Elarion, Vel'Thannis has operated for centuries as an espionage hub. Its agents have infiltrated noble houses, merchant guilds, and even the Faith of the Bound Pattern.

**Notable Locations:**
- **The Listening Webs** — Chamber where thousands of intelligence threads are processed
- **The Faceless Hall** — Identity-changing facilities for surface operatives
- **The Memory Vaults** — Storage for extracted surface memories

**Arc 2C Significance:** Vel'Thannis is experiencing a crisis. Some operatives have encountered Whisper's truth and can no longer maintain their surface identities. The split between loyalists and awakened is creating chaos in Drow intelligence operations.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Matron Zilv'rae of House Vel'thanar** — Traditionalist commander desperately containing the truth-contagion
- **Agent "Greycloak"** — Long-term surface operative now questioning everything
- **The Veiled Speaker** — Unknown figure secretly coordinating awakened operatives

# LAYER II: THE WOUNDSKIN LAYER
*Domain of the Drow Empire*

**Depth:** 2-5 miles below surface  
**Primary Location:** Beneath the Firegrass Vale of Kerythra  
**Thread Corruption:** Severe (95% leyline contamination)

The Woundskin is where the **true Hollowdeep begins**—a realm where familiar concepts of civilization still apply, but are twisted through the lens of exile, cruelty, and divine manipulation. This is the domain of the **Drow Empire of Vel'Zyren**, where beauty and horror intertwine in architectural nightmares of webbed marble and trap-laden grandeur.

## THE ASHEN WARRENS
*Heart of Drow Civilization*

**Population:** ~3.2 million Drow, plus ~800,000 enslaved surface-dwellers  
**Government:** Absolute matriarchal theocracy under the High Priestess of the Endless Thread  
**Thread Corruption:** Extreme—95% of local leylines carry Vel'Zyren's taint

### Ch'Zaan-Mir — "The Black Cradle"
*Capital of the Drow Dominion*

Ch'Zaan-Mir occupies a natural cavern system 12 miles across and 2 miles high at its central dome. The city is built in **inverted ziggurat style**—highest-status districts hang from the cavern ceiling on massive stalactites of worked obsidian, while lower castes dwell progressively closer to the cavern floor.

**Architecture:** Webbed marble spires spiraling impossibly upward, connected by bridges of crystallized spider silk and shadow-glass. Bioluminescent fungi and enslaved fire elementals provide perpetual twilight illumination.

#### District Structure

**The Hanging Thrones** (Ceiling Districts)
- **The Web of Vel'Zyren** — Primary temple complex dedicated to the Spider-Goddess
- **Matron's Spire** — Palace of the ruling Matron Mother
- **The Silken Courts** — Noble house compounds suspended on web-bridges
- **The Whisper Cathedra** — Training facility for Whisper-priests (now monitored for heresy)

**The Mid-Dark** (Wall Districts)
- **The Blade Markets** — Weapons, slaves, and poison trade
- **House Compounds** — Lesser noble families, each a self-contained fortress
- **The Breeding Warrens** — Population management facilities
- **Artisan Quarters** — Craftspeople serving noble houses

**The Floor-Bound** (Ground Districts)
- **The Slave Pens** — Holding areas for surface captives
- **The Fungal Farms** — Food production caverns
- **The Crawling Dead** — Undead labor quarters
- **The Maleless Warrens** — Where drow without house protection survive

#### Key Locations for Arc 2C

**The Flesh-Loom Cathedra**
- **Function:** Primary temple of Vel'Zyren
- **Features:** The Transformation Chambers where the original elven-to-drow conversion occurred
- **Current Status:** Heavily guarded; Whisper's message originated from knowledge stolen here
- **Defenses:** High Priestesses of the Endless Thread, mirror-magic wards, fate-editing defenses

**The Mirror Crypts**
- **Location:** Beneath the Flesh-Loom Cathedra
- **Function:** Where failed Drow are "rewoven"—their identities erased and replaced
- **Significance:** Contains the **Mirror of What Was**—an ancient scrying device that shows viewers their "true selves" before Vel'Zyren's manipulation
- **Current Status:** Sealed by order of the Matron Mothers after Whisper-That-Was accessed it

**The Compact Vault**
- **Location:** Unknown (rumored beneath Matron's Spire)
- **Contents:** Original documents of the divine agreement that created drow
- **Access:** Only Matron Mothers have ever entered
- **Significance:** Ultimate proof of how Vel'Zyren offered the same lie other gods told—she couldn't truly create, only transform

**The Silken Courts**
- **Function:** Where justice is dispensed through aesthetic torture
- **Current Crisis:** Some judges experiencing doubt—their punishments becoming less enthusiastic
- **Key Location:** The Hall of Reflected Judgment, where prisoners see their pre-Compact selves

### The Spire-Falls
*Contested Territories*

**Population:** ~450,000 (mixed rebels, exiles, rogue drow, surface refugees)  
**Government:** Shifting military control with temporary warlords  
**Nature:** Broken stalactite cities that were once ruled by Drow traitor houses

The Spire-falls are the closest thing to "free territory" within the Woundskin Layer. These hanging cities dangle from cavern ceilings like massive stone icicles, connected by rope bridges and zip-lines spanning chasms miles deep.

**Key Locations:**

**Vel'Kharis — The Hanging Garden**
- **Nature:** Prison-city where the beautiful are tortured until they become "perfect"
- **Arc 2C Significance:** Prisoners here have begun seeing their elven reflections in the torture mirrors
- **Current Status:** Quarantined by order of Ch'Zaan-Mir

**The Weeping Gallery**
- **Nature:** Waterfalls of acid tears from above
- **Phenomenon:** The waters have begun speaking—fragments of pre-transformation memories
- **Investigation:** High Priestesses have cordoned the area, claiming "contamination"

**Spider's Landing**
- **Function:** Neutral ground for negotiations between warring factions
- **Current Use:** Secret meeting place for those questioning Vel'Zyren's truth
- **Risk:** Heavily monitored by loyalist agents

---

### The Gloam-Tunnels
*Hunting Routes and Warpaths*

**Population:** ~200,000 (various exile races, tunnel communities)  
**Function:** Deep hunting routes and warpaths connecting to Threshold above and Murkmire below

The Gloam-Tunnels are carved by orcs, scar-wardens, halfling ghost-runners, and other exile races. They serve as highways for raid and invasion—and increasingly, for those fleeing Drow territories.

**Arc 2C Use:**
- Escape routes for awakened drow fleeing to the surface
- Hunting grounds where Retrieval Squads track escaped truth-bearers
- Connection points to the Smugglers Highway in the Threshold

# LAYER III: THE MURKMIRE DEEP
*Realm of Song and Spore*

**Depth:** 5-10 miles below surface  
**Character:** Bioluminescent, emotionally resonant, fungally alive

The Murkmire Deep is where the Hollowdeep becomes truly alien to surface dwellers. Here, **bioluminescence replaces natural light**, **emotion becomes ecology**, and **memory grows like moss** on the walls.

### Key Locations for Arc 2C

#### The Pale Choir Bloomfields

**Capital:** Hymmnos the Ever-Singing  
**Population:** ~650,000 fungal sapients and spore-touched humanoids  
**Government:** Choral memory collectives where decisions are sung into existence

**Arc 2C Significance:** The Pale Choir has begun singing songs that contain fragments of pre-Compact history. Their memory-storage methods predate Vel'Zyren's transformation—they remember what elves were before the descent.

**Key Location: The Harmony Archive**
- **Nature:** Fungal spore-library containing crystallized memories
- **Contents:** Songs predating the Drow transformation
- **Significance:** Potential evidence supporting Whisper's revelations
- **Access:** Requires emotional attunement; surface visitors often overwhelmed

#### The Hollowgroves

**Nature:** Underground forests of light-drinking trees and mind-sharing vines  
**Population:** ~380,000 plant-bonded peoples and tree-touched fey

**Arc 2C Significance:** The Hollowgroves connect to surface World Trees through deep root networks. Whisper's truth is spreading upward through these connections—manifesting as "identity doubt" in surface elf communities.

**Key Locations:**
- **The Memory Orchard** — Fruit grants visions of the past, now showing pre-transformation memories
- **The Whispering Dell** — Where the dead speak through rustling leaves; recently speaking of "who we were before"
- **The Sorrowsong Canopy** — Trees weeping musical sap; songs have changed to lamentations for lost identity

# LAYER IV: THE EMBERVAULT RIFT
*Domain of Scar and Flame*

**Depth:** 10-17 miles below surface  
**Character:** Volcanic, violent, Thread-scarred

The Embervault Rift is where the Hollowdeep becomes violent—a layer of magma channels, volcanic vents, and Thread-scalded zones where the Weave itself has been burned and twisted.

### Key Locations for Arc 2C

#### The Tharnak Bastions

**Capital:** Gorthak Dum — "The Cliff of Eternal Scars"  
**Population:** ~450,000 Trolls and scar-marked others  
**Government:** Regeneration-based hierarchy

**Arc 2C Significance:** Deep Trolls have their own creation myths, untouched by surface pantheons. Their oral traditions preserve accounts of the Drow descent from 1,800 years ago—they remember the elves who came down, and they remember the screaming as Vel'Zyren transformed them.

**Testimony Value:** Troll scar-histories provide external verification of Whisper's claims. The transformation wasn't natural adaptation—it was forced, agonizing, and took 105 years.

#### The Thread-Scalded Hollows

**Nature:** Zones where the Weave itself has blistered and burned  
**Phenomenon:** Dead magic, unstable spellcraft, living spell echoes

**Arc 2C Significance:** Some Thread-Scalded zones contain frozen moments from the Drow transformation—literal magical recordings of what happened. These "burn-memories" are terrifying to behold but impossible to deny.

# LAYER V: THE ECHOFOLD
*Where Thought Becomes Terrain*

**Depth:** 17-27 miles below surface  
**Character:** Psionic physics, fractured reality

The Echofold operates on psionic physics where thoughts have weight, memories become geography, and the act of observation changes what is observed.

### Key Locations for Arc 2C

#### The Echovault Archive

**Nature:** Semi-sentient library storing knowledge in crystallized thoughts  
**Population:** ~120,000 memory-keepers, thought-scribes, psychic archivists

**Arc 2C Significance:** The Echovault Archive predates the Drow transformation and contains thought-records from the elves who descended. These memories were supposed to be erased—but nothing is truly forgotten in the Echofold.

**Critical Evidence:**
- **Pre-Descent Memories** — Thought-crystals containing elven consciousness from before transformation
- **The Descent Record** — A complete psionic recording of the 105-year transformation
- **Vel'Zyren's Promise** — What she told them they would become vs. what they actually became

**Access:** The Archive requires visitors to offer memories in exchange for knowledge. It is choosy about who may access its deepest truths.

# LAYER VI: THE VAULTMARROW
*Graveyard of Ambition*

**Depth:** 27-42 miles below surface  
**Character:** Archaeological layers, ancient disasters

The Vaultmarrow contains ruins of ancient civilizations, divine experiments gone wrong, and wars fought with weapons that broke reality itself.

### Key Location for Arc 2C

#### The Ninth Coil Ruins

**Nature:** City-state that attempted to control time, folded inward through temporal dimensions  
**Current Status:** Recursive loop where it exists in multiple time periods simultaneously

**Arc 2C Significance:** The Ninth Coil contains temporal observation points that look backward through time. From here, one can literally watch Vel'Zyren's transformation of the elves as it happened—see the moment when free beings were remade into slaves who believed themselves chosen.

**Warning:** Accessing the Ninth Coil risks becoming temporally unstuck. Travelers may return to the surface before they left—or centuries after.

# TRUTH-MIRROR PHENOMENA

## The Nature of Truth-Mirrors

**Truth-Mirrors** are supernatural phenomena manifesting wherever Whisper-That-Was's influence grows strong. They are not physical objects but **reality distortions** that force viewers to confront evidence of divine manipulation.

### Manifestation Types

**Reflective Surfaces**
- Still water showing pre-Compact memories
- Polished stone revealing "original" forms
- Ice crystals projecting historical visions
- Metal surfaces displaying suppressed truths

**Acoustic Phenomena**
- Echoes speaking in forgotten languages
- Whispers revealing Compact details
- Songs preserving pre-transformation memories
- Silence that communicates meaning

**Emotional Resonance**
- Locations triggering identity doubt
- Areas where divine programming weakens
- Zones of existential clarity
- Spaces fostering questioning

---

### Known Truth-Mirror Sites

#### Within the Hollowdeep

**The Mirror of What Was** (Ch'Zaan-Mir)
- **Location:** Mirror Crypts beneath the Flesh-Loom Cathedra
- **Type:** Ancient scrying device predating the Compact
- **Effect:** Shows viewer's "true self" before Vel'Zyren's manipulation
- **Status:** Sealed by Matron Mothers
- **Arc 2C Objective:** Major evidence site requiring deep infiltration

**The Weeping Gallery Waters** (Spire-Falls)
- **Location:** Waterfall zone in contested territories
- **Type:** Acid tears that have begun carrying memory
- **Effect:** Contact triggers visions of pre-descent identity
- **Status:** Quarantined
- **Arc 2C Use:** Accessible evidence for mid-level parties

**The Harmony Archive Songs** (Murkmire Deep)
- **Location:** Pale Choir territory
- **Type:** Fungal spore-memories crystallized into music
- **Effect:** Listening triggers understanding of what elves were
- **Status:** Open to those who can survive emotional resonance
- **Arc 2C Use:** Alternative evidence path through non-Drow culture

#### Surface Manifestations

**The Questioning Pool** (Talanthar)
- **Location:** Sacred grove near World Tree
- **Effect:** Elves seeing drow reflections—realizing they are kin transformed
- **Current Status:** Causing theological crisis in elven community
- **Arc 2C Use:** Hook for elven PCs; evidence of truth spreading upward

**The Doubting Mirror** (Kel Varyan)
- **Location:** Noble district, unknown specific site
- **Effect:** Humans questioning lifespan limitations—seeing what they might have been
- **Current Status:** Multiple reported incidents, investigation ongoing
- **Arc 2C Use:** Urban mystery hook; evidence of broader species manipulation

**The Caste-Crack** (Veltharyn)
- **Location:** Lower-tier districts
- **Effect:** Dragonborn seeing pre-Compact ancestors—before caste existed
- **Current Status:** Causing lower-caste unrest and questioning
- **Arc 2C Use:** Connects to Ashkarra fall; evidence of multiple species manipulation

**The Wanderer's Rest** (Halfling Joy-Wild Territories)
- **Location:** Southern borderlands
- **Effect:** Halflings questioning wanderlust compulsion—was it always their nature?
- **Current Status:** Some clans settling permanently, others wandering more intensely
- **Arc 2C Use:** Shows truth affects even "untouched" races

## IDENTITY CRISIS CENTERS

### Communities Experiencing Existential Collapse

As Whisper's influence spreads, certain communities become **epicenters of identity crisis**—places where populations collectively begin questioning their divine-programmed natures.

#### Dragonborn Communities

**New Ashkarra** (Elarion refugee settlement)
- **Population:** ~31,000 dragonborn refugees
- **Crisis Level:** Severe
- **Manifestation:** Mass questioning of Compact that created their race
- **Symptoms:** Caste system breakdown, refusal of traditional roles, theological debates
- **Connection to Arc:** Ashkarra survivors remember the truth—they saw their empire fall because they were too certain. Now Whisper's message resonates.

**Veltharyn Lower Tiers** (Roothold tier)
- **Population:** ~115,000
- **Crisis Level:** Moderate
- **Manifestation:** Lower-caste dragonborn questioning caste system's divine origins
- **Symptoms:** Labor strikes, caste-mixing, demands for reform
- **Connection to Arc:** Veltharyn dragonborn are asking the same questions drow are—were we made this way, or did something make us this way?

#### Elven Communities

**Talanthar Sacred Groves**
- **Population:** ~280,000 in affected areas
- **Crisis Level:** Severe (for elves)
- **Manifestation:** Elves confronting that Drow are their transformed kin, not fallen strangers
- **Symptoms:** Calls for reconciliation, questioning of Woven Reverence, attempts to contact escaped drow
- **Connection to Arc:** Surface elves are the living evidence—if they can see themselves in drow, the truth becomes undeniable

#### Human Population Centers

**Kel Varyan Academic Districts**
- **Population:** ~200,000 in affected areas
- **Crisis Level:** Moderate
- **Manifestation:** Scholars questioning why humans live such short lives
- **Symptoms:** Obsession with life extension, resentment of longer-lived races
- **Connection to Arc:** Human doubt feeds the broader crisis—if gods manipulated drow, what did they do to humans?

## CAMPAIGN INTEGRATION

### Arc 2C Location Progression

**Act 1: Discovery (Levels 1-5)**

*Primary Locations:* Surface manifestations, Threshold Reaches

- Surface Truth-Mirror encounters trigger investigation
- First contact with escaped drow bearing Whisper's message
- Investigation of identity crisis centers
- Discovery of surfacing points and initial descent options
- **Key Sites:** The Questioning Pool, Freeport Hollow, Vel'Thannis observation

**Act 2: Descent (Levels 6-10)**

*Primary Locations:* The Bleeding Gates, Woundskin Layer outer territories

- Passage through the Bleeding Gates
- Navigation of Spire-Falls contested territories
- Contact with Drow resistance movement
- Recovery of preliminary Compact evidence
- **Key Sites:** Spider's Landing, The Weeping Gallery, Gloam-Tunnels escape routes

**Act 3: Revelation (Levels 11-15)**

*Primary Locations:* Ch'Zaan-Mir and the Ashen Warrens

- Infiltration of the Black Cradle capital
- Access to the Mirror Crypts and Transformation Chambers
- Confrontation with Whisper-That-Was's true nature
- Decision point: Suppress or spread the truth
- **Key Sites:** The Mirror of What Was, The Compact Vault, The Flesh-Loom Cathedra

**Act 4: Consequences (Levels 16-20)**

*Primary Locations:* Multiple continents, potentially Echofold or Vaultmarrow

- World responds to revealed/suppressed truth
- Identity crisis reaches global scale or is contained
- Final confrontation with forces defending the Compact
- Reshaping of divine-mortal relationship
- **Key Sites:** Echovault Archive (ultimate evidence), Ninth Coil Ruins (temporal proof), multiple surface crisis centers

## CANON INTEGRATION NOTES

### Alignment with Established Lore

**The Drow Transformation (The Fracturing):**
- Occurred ~1,800 years ago over 105 years
- Vel'Zyren offered the same lie other gods told—she couldn't truly create, only transform
- Physical and psychological rewriting of former elves
