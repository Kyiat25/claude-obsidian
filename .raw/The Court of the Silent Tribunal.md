# Where All Threads Are Judged

> *"The Weave does not forget. And when gods err, they are brought to the place where memory is law, silence is truth, and flame does not burn—it brands."*  
> —**[[Aelys]], the Eternal Scribe**

There exists a place not bound by geography, but by glyph, memory, and judgment. Known as **The Silent Tribunal**, it is not a hall, but a convergence—**where all divine law must eventually pass**, and where the **Five Threads** and even the Eldritch echoes are weighed against the Pattern of the Weave itself.

It is neither feared nor worshipped. It is simply **there**—beneath the echoes of law, above the bones of guilt, within the space between divine thought and mortal memory.

---

## 🔱 The Axis of Divine Judgment

The Silent Tribunal is served by five eternal figures, one from each elemental pantheon, each embodying a facet of divine law.

| Role                 | Name         | Pantheon | Function                                                                    |
| -------------------- | ------------ | -------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **⚖️ The Judge**     | [[Gaeldros]] | Earth    | Delivers final rulings. His word becomes leyline-law.                       |
| **✋ The Scribe**     | [[Aelys]]    | Spirit   | Records intention and outcome in mirrored glyphs—written into memory.       |
| **🔥 The Flame**     | [[Ignis]]    | Fire     | Brands the ruling into reality via the **Ember Throne**—inescapable memory. |
| **🌬 The Historian** | [[Astria]]   | Air      | Interprets omens and patterns; provides cosmic precedent and foresight.     |
| **🌊 The Messenger** | [[Selmira]]  | Water    | Delivers verdicts through tides, dreams, and cycles—ensures it is known.    |

No seat exists for the Eldritch. But they are judged all the same.

---

## 🔥 The Ember Throne

At the centre of all memory stands **[[the Ember Throne]]**, a burning paradox: it neither consumes nor destroys—it **sears truth into permanence**.

**[[Ignis]]**, the Eternal Flame, sits only during judgment. His fire is the last step. Once he brands an edict, it is unforgotten, even by those who wish to.

> *"You will not agree. But you will remember. That is enough."* —[[Ignis]], after the [[The Trial of Soltharion]]

---

## 📚 Confirmed Roles in Myth

### 🌍 **[[Gaeldros]], Pillar of Law**

His rulings define glyph-structure. It is said that ley lines tremble when he lifts his gavel.

### 🥊 **[[Aelys]], Eternal Scribe**

Records all Tribunal events not as they are said, but as they are **meant**. Her glyphs appear where truth was spoken.

### 🔥 **[[Ignis]], Memory's Flame**

Once judgment is passed, Ignis ignites it into all things—stone, breath, and mind.

### 🌬 **[[Astria]], Falcon of Fate**

Weaves the omens and constellations that contextualise a god’s actions before the court. Some claim she once foresaw her own failure.

### 🌊 **[[Selmira]], Tide-Bearer**

Her tides wash verdicts across the world. Cities that ignore her messengers often drown without storm or warning.

## 🔧 Notable Trials

### 🔹 **[[The Trial of Soltharion]]**

**Crime**: Tyranny, glyphic domination, godly overreach.  
**Judgment**: Banished to the Sun. His flames now power, but do not speak.  
**Legacy**: The Ember Throne glows still from that sentence.

### 🔹 **[[The Binding of the Web-Spinner]]**

**Crime**: Rewriting fate, spinning her own Weave, corrupting the Threads of the Pattern.  
**Judgment**: Exiled to [[The Underdark]], severed from all Threads but Earth.  
**Legacy**: [[Vel'Zyren]] now reigns as queen in exile, untouched by fate, feared by all.

> *"You will weave only for those trapped beneath the land. Your fate is your own. The gods will not claim you, and the Weave will not remember you."* —[[Gaeldros]]

Her trial marks the moment the Weave first failed to undo a divine act—a fracture that echoes in every loom-thread to this day.

### 🔹 **[[The Drowned Edict]]**

When the mortal city of [[Delmirath]] defied a Tribunal ruling, [[Selmira]] delivered judgment with a silent flood. Its bell still rings below the waves.

### 🔹 **The Forgotten Sentence**

Believed erased by **[[Nhal’Zereth]]**, the gods disagree whether the edict was ever real. Aelys still scratches blank glyphs onto stone, refusing to forget.

### 🔹 **The Trial of the Whispered Lie**

**Crime**: Twisting prophecy, creating a self-fulfilling calamity, elemental deceit of the Weave.  
**Defendants**: **[[Zephir]]**, god of chaos and sky-bound trickery; witnessed by **[[Astria]]** and invoked by **[[Thirian]]**.  
**Judgment**: Zephir was condemned to speak only in illusions and riddles—his true voice shattered across the high winds, never to be heard whole again.  
**Legacy**: The proto-city that would become [[Kel Varyan]] fell under false omens. Astria's prophecy unraveled; her sight now fractures time. [[Thirian]], having summoned judgment, was marked by silence—becoming the god of still breath, never again to move the wind himself.

> _"Truth is the only wind that leaves no trace."_ — Final Judgment of the Silent Tribunal

## ⚖ Divine Balance

No pantheon governs the Tribunal. Each thread speaks its part. Even when the gods disagree, the ruling, once passed, is **unbreakable**.

[[Eldritch Forces]] has never stood within it. But their echoes bleed into its walls. **Zhurr’Kael** has attempted to contradict its logic. **The Mirror That Screams** has reflected false trials. Still, the Tribunal endures.

> *"Truth does not shout. It waits."* —Gaeldros

---

## 📜 Cultural Legacy

- **Scar-Wardens** invoke the Tribunal in blood-edict ceremonies.
- **The Faith of the Bound Pattern** considers rulings here sacrosanct, beyond all prayer.
- **Elves and Dwarves** debate whether the Tribunal existed before the First Age.
- **Halflings** claim to have once disrupted a ruling by making all five judges laugh. This remains unverified.

---

## 🧵 Final Truth

The Tribunal is not a myth. It is a **loom-knot in the Pattern of the Weave**. When invoked, it does not arrive—but **reminds all that it was always there**.

You cannot escape it. You can only pray that when you are judged, **your Thread holds true.**
