# Blood Magic

_Style of Magic_

**"Blood remembers. Blood obeys. Blood endures."**

Blood Magic is the oldest taboo and the most primal offering: power drawn not from divine grace or arcane geometry, but from **life itself**. Where others shape the Weave with thought, speech, or sigil, the bloodmage does so by giving of the flesh—willingly or not. Practiced in secret by sanguine cults, battlefield surgeons, rebellious witches, and vampiric lineages, Blood Magic is feared as much as it is misunderstood.

In the world of **[[Dór-o Estel]]**, Blood Magic is seen as a style, not a school. It can flavor spells from any school, provided the spell is cast through bloodletting, sacrifice, or vitae manipulation. In Tolia and Kanrath, it is known as **Sanguimagia**, and practiced openly by the Crimson Orders. In other cultures, it survives only in whispers, scars, and black-market rites.

---

## Key Themes

- **Life-for-Power Exchange**: Blood Magic always costs something. Whether it's health, memory, or connection, the power comes at a physical price.
- **Biological Connection**: Many blood-based spells can influence or be resisted based on shared ancestry, living tissue, or bloodline traits.
- **Ritual Precision**: Blood Magic rewards preparation. Circles, incantations, and time can greatly enhance its effects.
- **Sentient Vitae**: Some advanced bloodmages believe blood holds memory, emotion, and even will. The most powerful can make it speak.

---

## Cultural Variants

### ✨ **Sanguimagia** (Tolia / Kanrath tradition)

- Performed with opera-like ritual and crimson performance.
- Automatons fueled by arcane vitae circuits.
- Emotion-infused blood used as spell catalysts.

### 🌑 **Crimson Creed** (Grim Hollow tradition)

- Emphasizes domination, undead creation, and necrotic rebirth.
- Often paired with corruption and mutation.

### 🩸 **Scar-Ward Rites** (Orc-blood offshoot)

- Mix of Blood and Scarred Magic.
- Magic recorded in bodily trauma and passed through ancestral scars.

---

## Blood Magic Casting Features

- **Blood Component**: The caster may replace a material or somatic component with a self-inflicted wound, taking damage equal to the spell's level.
- **Vital Resonance**: Gain advantage on saving throws or spell attacks if targeting a creature whose blood you have harvested (must have done so within the last 24 hours).
- **Ritual Bleeding**: Casting a spell as a 10-minute ritual allows the caster to draw on additional power. Reroll any 1, reduce the DC by your modifier of your spellcasting stat.

---

## Echo Glyph Integration

Blood Magic is rich in personal resonance, and frequently generates **Echo Glyphs** with lasting physical and emotional effects:

- **Echo of the Vein**: When casting a healing spell, it also reveals the target's physical trauma or bloodline curse.
- **Blood-Tied Anchor**: Bind a concentration spell to a vial of blood. If the target moves out of range, the spell persists as long as you hold the vial.
- **Crimson Memory**: When a creature dies under a Blood Magic effect, their final memory becomes available to the caster for 24 hours.

## Example Spells (Flavor Additions)

> These spells are modified or flavoured when cast via Blood Magic:

- **[[Shield]]** ([[Abjuration]]): The bloodmage splatters their own blood mid-air to form a crimson ward. Leaves a floating smear that fades slowly.
- **[[Mage Armour]]** ([[Abjuration]]): Fused bone-plate forms under the skin, costing 1 HP but increasing AC by an additional +1.
- **[[Inflict Wounds]]** ([[Necromancy]]): Leaves behind a pulsing red sigil inside the wound that glows in moonlight.
- **[[Suggestion]]** ([[Enchantment]]): Requires a drop of the target's blood to double the duration.

---

## Social Perception

- **Feared and Misunderstood**: In most civilized societies, Blood Magic is considered unclean or dangerous.
- **Ritually Sanctioned**: In Tolia and Cyherested, it is a sacred civic art.
- **Forbidden by the Faith**: The **Faith of the Bound Pattern** does not openly tolerate Blood Magic, considering it a Weave-deepening heresy.
- **Respected Among Orcs**: Blood sacrifice is a recognized form of oath-binding and truth-seeking.

---
## Optional Mechanics

- **Blood Sigil Tattoo ([[Feat]])**: Store one 1st-level spell in a blood-carved tattoo. Can be cast once per day as a reaction.
- **Crimson Pact (Boons)**: Exchange max HP for permanent magical benefits.
- **Blood Echo Surge (Crit effect)**: On a spell crit using Blood Magic, recover 1 spell slot of the same level (once per long rest).

---

## Design Notes

Blood Magic is not a new _school_, but a **style** that overlays onto existing spellcasting traditions. Any spellcaster can adopt the style through background, culture, or training. Mechanical options (like feats or subclass features) deepen the theme but are not required.

It is one of the most flexible, narrative-rich, and morally gray styles of magic in Dór-o Estel—offering immense power to those willing to pay in flesh, loyalty, or truth.
