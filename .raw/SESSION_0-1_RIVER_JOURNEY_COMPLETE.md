# OVERVIEW & CORE PRINCIPLES {#overview}

## The Concept

**The Hook:** Players begin as passengers on a trading barge traveling to Kel Varyan. News arrives: four of five Threadbearers are dead. All Imperial vessels are recalled immediately.

**The Reality:** This introduction is a **compressed model of the entire world**—forcing factions into proximity, making politics unavoidable, allowing every major campaign tension to enter play at whisper-level.

**The Truth:** Serana Tidecaller (Naleth Morvayn) is the sole surviving Threadbearer, using the crisis to consolidate power before new Threadbearers manifest.

## Design Principles

### 1. **The World Moves Without You**
- Crisis happened BEFORE players arrived
- They're discovering existing events, not triggering them
- Removes "chosen one" railroading
- Establishes world is alive and reactive

### 2. **Politics Through Proximity**
- Never explain what players can observe
- NPCs react naturally to each other
- Conflicts emerge through proximity
- Environmental details tell stories

### 3. **Violence Has Consequences**
- NPCs can die (and players mourn)
- Injuries require treatment
- Aftermath creates relationships
- Evidence creates questions

### 4. **Natural Party Cohesion**
- Don't force party together
- Make staying together advantageous:
  - Captain offers lodging
  - Shared secret (cargo)
  - Safety in numbers
  - Established relationships
  - Combined resources

### 5. **Mystery Without Withholding**
- Players should have questions, not frustrations
- Clear immediate objectives
- Multiple investigation paths visible
- Answers exist (not arbitrary secrets)

## Session Goals

**Primary:**
1. ✅ Establish world tone (living, complex, morally nuanced)
2. ✅ Introduce crisis (Threadbearer deaths)
3. ✅ Force journey to capital (recall order)
4. ✅ Create memorable NPCs (3-5 strong contacts)
5. ✅ Demonstrate consequences (violence, choices matter)

**Secondary:**
1. ✅ Plant investigation seeds
2. ✅ Show faction tensions
3. ✅ Reveal geography matters
4. ✅ Establish world independence

**Explicit Seeds (Choose 2-3 Max):**
- Captain's smuggling → underground economy
- Verik's heterodoxy → theological questions
- Noble's ambitions → political intrigue
- Scholar's research → academic mystery
- Tax collector's authority → investigation

# THE FOG AMBUSH (DAY 6) {#ambush}

## Encounter Design Philosophy

**This Should:**
1. ✅ Feel dangerous (real stakes)
2. ✅ Be winnable (balanced for Level 1)
3. ✅ Change relationships (NPCs react to actions)
4. ✅ Teach lessons (violence is messy)
5. ✅ Create mystery (evidence of larger world)

**This Should NOT:**
1. ❌ TPK the party
2. ❌ Feel random
3. ❌ Be meat grinder
4. ❌ Steal spotlight from PCs
5. ❌ Lack consequences

## Terrain & Environmental Rules

### The Barge Deck

**Difficult Terrain:** None normally, but slippery when wet (Acrobatics DC 10 or fall prone)

**Rails:** 3 feet high, climbable, provide half cover

**Water:** 10 feet to surface, cold, dark

**Falling Overboard:**
- Athletics DC 12 to grab rail
- Failure: in water, disadvantage on attacks, vulnerable
- Lurkers have advantage on attacks vs waterborne

**Fog:** Heavy obscurement beyond 20 feet

**Light:** Lanterns provide 20-foot bright, 20-foot dim

---

## Combat Pacing

**Round-by-Round Goal:**

**Round 1:** Chaos, positioning (where is everyone?)  
**Round 2:** First blood (someone drops)  
**Round 3:** Turning point (one side winning)  
**Round 4:** Escalation or retreat (reavers flee OR lurker arrives)  
**Round 5+:** Resolution

**If Combat Drags:**
- Reavers flee earlier
- Lurkers don't arrive
- NPCs land finishing blows
- Captain: "They're running! Let them go!"

**If Combat Too Fast:**
- +1 reaver reinforcement
- Lurker arrives immediately
- Environmental complication

**Target Time:** 30-45 minutes real-time

---

## Balance Controls

**If Too Easy:**
- Reavers fight smarter
- Spotter focuses fire better
- Reaver grabs hostage
- Lurker arrives earlier

**If Too Hard:**
- Reavers flee earlier
- Branik/Garek more effective
- Lurker arrives later/not at all
- Captain surrenders cargo

**If PC Drops to 0:**
- Reavers don't auto-kill (want hostages)
- NPC stabilizes (Garek, Verik, crew)
- Lurker targets unconscious reavers first

**If TPK Imminent:**
- Captain surrenders cargo
- Lurkers drive reavers away
- Reinforcements arrive
- **NEVER TPK in Session 0-1**

### The Registration System

**Two-Stage Process:**

**1. Captain Registration (Mandatory):**
- Name, writ number, manifest
- Cargo inspection (cursory, too many vessels)
- 7-day deadline to reach capital
- **Penalty for non-compliance:** Writ revoked, bounty after week

**2. Passenger Cataloguing (Tiered):**

**Imperial Clerk arrives with ledger:**

> "Passenger names? Origins?"
>
> (Records systematically)

**Treatment By Origin:**

| Origin | Process | Monitoring |
|--------|---------|------------|
| **Imperial** | Standard (name, profession, reason) | Report if official, free if common |
| **Corvessan** | Extra questions (Faith + Imperial) | Dual monitoring |
| **Talanthari** | Can refuse (sovereignty) | Captain pressures compliance |
| **Foreign** | Negotiated | Variable |

**Example Registration:**

**Imperial PC:**

> "Name, profession, purpose in capital?"
>
> (Records) "Free to conduct business."

**Corvessan PC:**

> "Name, estate, purpose?"
>
> (Faith Guard observes)
>
> "Regular communion? Faith chapel?"
>
> (Records extra details)

**Talanthari PC:**

> "Name and—"
>
> (PC can refuse)
>
> (Captain: "Please. My writ depends on compliance.")

### Geopolitical Complexity Visible

**Scene at Waystation:**

> Multiple barges being processed. Players watch:
>
> **High noble:** Clerk stands, bows, processes quickly
>
> **Warrior/Officer:** Efficient, respectful, weapons noted
>
> **Merchant:** Standard, guild checked, cargo noted
>
> **Common folk:** Rapid, minimal questions
>
> **Foreign:** Extra questions, sponsor verification
>
> **Talanthari refusing:** Standoff. Faith Guard glares. Imperial clerk frustrated. Captain pleading.

**This Shows Hierarchy Without Explaining It.**

# ARRIVAL AT KEL VARYAN {#arrival}

## The Docks Scene

**Two Days Later:**

> Kel Varyan's delta. Waterways choked with recalled vessels. Hours waiting for dock space.
>
> When you finally berth: chaos. Imperial soldiers directing traffic. Customs inspecting (perfunctorily—too many). Faith representatives observing. Everyone exhausted.

**The Inspection:**

> Customs inspector boards. Professional, rushed.
>
> **Inspector:** "Manifest?" (Captain provides)
>
> Glances at cargo, makes notes, doesn't dig deep.
>
> (To Brennan) "Imperial Customs. Report by tomorrow evening."
>
> (To captain) "Cargo cleared for now. Don't leave city limits. Writ suspended until crisis passes."
>
> (To everyone else) "You're processed. Proceed with calm and order. Welcome to Kel Varyan."
>
> She leaves.

## City in Crisis

**What Players See:**

> Beyond docks: organized chaos.
>
> Streets crowded. Checkpoints visible. Imperial soldiers everywhere. Faith Guard watchers. Merchants shouting. Refugees from recalled vessels seeking lodging.
>
> The Amethyst District (government center): locked down. Guards at every entrance. Strange lights from Ascension Spire.
>
> The Prisada Arcana (magic academy): under guard. Robed figures rushing.
>
> The Merchant Quarter: overflowing. Prices inflated. Opportunists everywhere.
>
> The Underworks: more active than usual. Shadows moving deliberately.

**The Atmosphere:** Urgent, tense, overwhelming, opportunistic

## Captain's Offer (Party Cohesion)

**The Natural Decision Point:**

> Captain clears throat.
>
> "I've got to stay with my boat—writ suspended. But I know people. Can get you places to stay, introductions if needed.
>
> (Looks at group)
>
> You planning to stick together? Safer that way. City's a mess. Easy to get lost... or disappeared.
>
> (Pauses)
>
> Also... Customs might have questions about the ambush. About what was in that crate that went overboard. Might be good if we all remember it the same way. Just a thought."

**Why Stay Together:**
1. **Captain offers lodging** (boarding house, 10 gold/week)
2. **Shared secret** (cargo irregularity)
3. **Safety in numbers** (crisis city)
4. **Established relationships** (proven in combat)
5. **Combined resources** (economy of scale)

**Freedom to Split:**
- Players CAN separate
- Consequences are real (harder alone)
- Choice is respected

**If They Stay:**

> "Good. I'll send word to my contact. Boarding house near Ivory Bridge. Clean rooms, reasonable rates, no questions.
>
> Meet me at the *Scale* tomorrow evening. I'll have information about work, connections, and what's really happening in this city."

## GM Core Reminders

**Trust the Design:**
- World feels complete because it IS complete
- Don't explain everything
- Let players discover
- Complexity emerges naturally

**Respect Player Agency:**
- Never force party cohesion
- Make advantages clear, let them choose
- Honor their decisions
- Show consequences (not punishments)

**Maintain Living World:**
- NPCs have goals independent of players
- Events progress with or without intervention
- Background details matter
- Nothing is just decoration

**Balance Spotlight:**
- NPCs support, don't dominate
- Players are protagonists
- Competent allies ≠ stealing thunder
- Let players shine

**Session 0-1 Success Metrics:**
- Players excited for Session 2?
- Questions they want answered?
- NPCs they remember fondly?
- Choices that mattered?
- World feels alive?

**If yes → Success. If no → Adjust.**
