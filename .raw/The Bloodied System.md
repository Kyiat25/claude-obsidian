# The Bloodied System

> *"Every creature has a breaking point. The trick is knowing what breaks — and what doesn't."*
> — Field notes of a [[War-Scribe of Thar Vornhal]], recovered post-mortem

---

## Overview

**This is an enemy and monster system.** Player characters do not use the Bloodied rules — when PCs cross 50% HP, the Injury System (Staggered threshold) applies instead. The Bloodied system applies only to the creatures and adversaries you face.

This asymmetry is intentional. PCs are heroes with agency. Enemies are creatures with breaking points. The Bloodied system makes those breaking points mechanical, predictable in structure if not in content, and tactically exploitable. Combat stops being pure attrition the moment you understand what an enemy becomes when it's dying — and whether killing it now is more dangerous than letting it bleed out.

---

## The 50% Threshold

An enemy becomes **Bloodied** the first time it drops to 50% HP or below in an encounter. This triggers the following sequence:

1. **Injury System first.** Roll on the Major Injury table (the Staggered threshold). Apply the result and reset the enemy's Momentum to 0. See the Injury System document for full tables.
2. **Bloodied effects second.** All four Bloodied effects — Frailty, Strength, Ability, and Death Throes — activate immediately after.

Both apply. The Injury roll represents physical trauma; the Bloodied effects represent the psychological and instinctive shift of a creature fighting for its life.

Bloodied effects trigger **once per encounter** when the threshold is first crossed. If an enemy is healed back above 50% HP and drops below again, the effects do not re-trigger — they are already active.

> **Players can see this coming.** Tracking enemy HP is legal. Planning around the Bloodied threshold — saving burst damage, pre-positioning for Death Throes, calling shots to force a specific injury at the crossing — is exactly the kind of tactical play this system rewards.

## The Four Effects

When an enemy becomes Bloodied, all four of the following effects activate simultaneously and persist for the rest of the encounter unless otherwise stated.

### Frailty — the exposed weakness

A Frailty is a tactical vulnerability that emerges when the creature is wounded and desperate. It is an opening.

Common Frailties include reduced Evasion or DR at a specific location, Disadvantage on certain saves, vulnerability to a damage type, a lower Momentum floor, or compromised senses. The specific effect depends on the creature.

*Example: A bloodied knight's visor is cracked — their head location DR drops from 3 to 1, and Called Shots to the head lose their −5 penalty.*

*Example: A bloodied owlbear has exposed its flank — it loses 2 Evasion when attacked from the side or rear.*

**What to do with a Frailty:** Exploit it immediately. Called Shots become more valuable when DR has dropped at a location. A Frailty that grants Disadvantage on Dexterity saves makes leg shots and sweep attacks more reliable. If you know the Frailty before it triggers — through an Investigator's analysis, a Ranger's mark, or a prior encounter — you can prepare for it.

---

### Strength — the desperation resilience

A Strength is what the creature gains when it stops holding anything back. The wolf in a corner is more dangerous than the wolf in open ground. The Strength represents that truth mechanically.

Common Strengths include bonus damage, resistance to a damage type, temporary hit points, a speed increase, advantage on attacks, Momentum immunity for a round, or a Momentum boost. Some creatures become harder to hit; others become harder to kill; others simply hit harder.

*Example: A bloodied troll's regeneration intensifies — it regains 2d6 HP at the start of its turn rather than its normal rate, and the effect is not suppressed by fire or acid.*

*Example: A bloodied bandit captain fights with reckless fury — they gain +3 to all damage rolls and their Called Shot penalties are halved.*

**What to do with a Strength:** Adapt. You cannot ignore a Strength the way you might try to ignore a Frailty. If the enemy is suddenly resistant to slashing, your rogue's daggers matter less. If they've gained temporary hit points, burst damage is less efficient. Recognising the Strength and adjusting is part of what the system asks of you.

---

### Ability — the once-per-combat move

A Bloodied Ability is a dramatic action that fires once when the threshold is crossed and cannot be used again this encounter. It represents a hidden capability, a desperate gambit, or the unleashing of something the creature had been holding in reserve.

Bloodied Abilities are resolved immediately when triggered — they interrupt the sequence if necessary. They may be offensive (a burst attack against all adjacent enemies), defensive (a sudden retreat behind cover), support-oriented (a rally cry that grants all allies Advantage on their next attack), or transformative (the creature shifts shape, enters a rage state, or activates a recharged ability).

*Example: A bloodied hobgoblin warlord immediately issues a Battle Command — all hobgoblin allies within 60 feet gain +2 Momentum and Advantage on their next attack roll, regardless of their current Momentum state.*

*Example: A bloodied vampire's Blood Frenzy triggers — until the end of combat, it gains Advantage on attacks against any creature that is not at full hit points.*

**What to do with a Bloodied Ability:** You can't stop it from triggering. What you can do is prepare countermeasures: a readied action to interrupt the follow-up, positioning that limits its radius, or saving a reaction specifically for this moment. Classes with the ability to predict Bloodied effects (see Class Hooks below) can neutralise an Ability before it becomes a crisis.

---

### Death Throes — the explosive finale

Death Throes trigger when the Bloodied creature is reduced to 0 HP. They are the creature's final act — and they punish poor positioning.

Common Death Throes include area damage explosions scaled to creature size, curses on the killing blow, environmental effects (a massive creature's body falls and crushes the area beneath it), spawn creation (the creature's death releases lesser creatures), or final rallies that affect nearby allies.

*Example: A bloodied fire elemental's Death Throes unleash an 15 ft. radius burst of fire (CR × 1d6 damage, Dex save DC 12 + CR÷2 for half).*

*Example: A bloodied lich without its phylactery lets out a final wail — all creatures within 30 feet must make a Sanity save (DC 18) or lose 1d6 Sanity and become Frightened until the end of their next turn.*

**Death Throes by creature size:**

| Creature size | Elemental explosion radius | Timber crush area |
|---|---|---|
| Small / Medium | 10 feet | — |
| Large | 15 feet | 10 × 10 feet |
| Huge | 20 feet | 15 × 15 feet |
| Gargantuan | 30 feet | 20 × 20 feet |

**Damage scaling:** Elemental explosions deal CR × 1d6 damage of the relevant type. Save DC = 12 + (CR ÷ 2).

**What to do with Death Throes:** Position before you kill. Once you know an enemy's Death Throes — from prior knowledge, a class feature, or the GM's announcement when the threshold is crossed — treat the kill as a positioning puzzle, not just a damage race. Melee fighters sometimes have to choose between the finishing blow and the safer distance. Ranged attackers gain a genuine advantage here.

> **Death Throes cannot be avoided simply by stepping back after the creature drops.** The effect triggers immediately when it reaches 0 HP. Pre-position before the killing blow.

## What Happens at the Threshold — Full Sequence

When an enemy crosses 50% HP mid-combat:

1. GM announces the creature is Bloodied (this is public information).
2. Roll on the **Major Injury table** (Staggered threshold). Apply the result to the creature. Momentum resets to 0.
3. All four Bloodied effects activate immediately — Frailty, Strength, Ability, Death Throes. The Ability resolves right now; the others persist.
4. If the creature is a leader (or the highest-CR creature on its side), it must immediately attempt an **Auto Rally check** (`1d20 + Focus modifier` vs DC 15 or current casualty tier DC if higher). See the Bloodied, Breaking Points & Rally document for full Rally rules.
5. Initiative is recalculated for the next round (the Momentum reset affects Side Initiative).

## Legendary Creatures — Phase 2

Legendary creatures do not simply gain the four standard Bloodied effects. Instead, they enter **Legendary Phase 2** — a qualitatively different combat state with new or enhanced abilities reflecting their desperate power.

Phase 2 replaces the standard Frailty/Strength/Ability/Death Throes structure with a custom set of phase properties defined in the creature's statblock. The Phase 2 transition should feel like a second encounter beginning within the first one. Positions that worked in Phase 1 may no longer work; abilities that recharged slowly may now recharge faster; new threats emerge.

Legendary leaders also auto-succeed on their Bloodied Rally check once per combat — the player-facing assumption should be that the first Rally will succeed, and the tactical goal is to force a second one or to isolate the leader from the troops it would Rally.

## Designing Bloodied Effects — A Reference for GMs

The four-part structure works for any creature if you think about it through four questions:

**Frailty:** What breaks when this creature is wounded? Where does the armour crack, the discipline falter, the body fail?

**Strength:** What does this creature stop holding back when survival is at stake? What gets worse before it gets better — for you?

**Ability:** What does this creature do once, desperately, that it would never waste at full health?

**Death Throes:** What does this creature's death mean for everything nearby? What consequence do you face for being the one to deliver the killing blow?

### Balancing guidelines

Frailty and Strength should roughly offset each other in mechanical weight. A severe Frailty (DR 0 at a location, or Disadvantage on all saves) warrants a meaningful Strength (resistance to a damage type, or guaranteed temp HP). A mild Frailty (−1 to a single save type) can pair with a significant Strength (+3 damage). The creature should feel more dangerous and more exploitable simultaneously.

The Ability should be impactful enough that players notice and respond, but not so overwhelming that it removes agency. A single powerful burst attack, a recharge, a buff to allies — these are appropriate. An Ability should create a moment, not end the fight.

Death Throes should always offer a save or be avoidable with positioning. Damage scaled to CR × 1d6 is the baseline. At high CR, Death Throes can be lethal if the party is bunched up — that is the point.

### Complexity scaling by creature role

| Creature role | Recommended approach |
|---|---|
| **Mooks** (low CR, disposable) | Use generic effects: −1 Evasion (Frailty), +1 damage (Strength), no Ability, no Death Throes or a trivial one |
| **Regulars** (mid-tier fighters) | Use creature-type effects from the tables below |
| **Elites** (recurring enemies, named NPCs) | Custom effects matching their established personality and combat style |
| **Bosses** (encounter-defining threats) | Legendary Phase 2 with full custom properties |

## Creature-Type Bloodied Effects — Quick Reference

The following represent typical Bloodied effects for each creature type. GMs should adapt these to the specific creature rather than applying them verbatim.

### Beasts

- **Frailty:** Exposed flank — Evasion −2, speed −10 feet (wounded leg or torn side)
- **Strength:** Pack instinct intensifies — +2 to hit when any ally is adjacent
- **Ability:** Feral Lunge — charge up to full speed and make an attack with Advantage; if it hits, knock the target prone
- **Death Throes:** Body crashes down — Dex save or 2d8 bludgeoning damage and knocked prone (Large or larger only)

### Humanoids

- **Frailty:** Armour cracked — Torso DR reduced by 2; dropped weapon or shield if Fractured Grip rolled on injury table
- **Strength:** Battle fury — +2 to all damage rolls; the fear of death sharpens the blade
- **Ability:** Last Stand Cry — all allies within 30 feet gain Advantage on their next attack roll
- **Death Throes:** None for mooks; leaders may have a Final Command that takes effect immediately (one ally gains their action)

### Giants

- **Frailty:** Frustrated and clumsy — Disadvantage on Dexterity saves; −2 to Called Shot penalties against them (easier to exploit their size)
- **Strength:** Simple but unstoppable — resistance to bludgeoning damage; forced movement effects no longer move them
- **Ability:** Ground Slam — all creatures within 15 feet make a Strength save (DC 14 + STR modifier) or fall prone
- **Death Throes:** Timber — body falls in a direction the GM determines; creatures in the area make a Dex save or take 3d10 bludgeoning damage and fall prone

### Dragons

- **Frailty:** Wings damaged — wing location DR reduced by 3; a successful Called Shot to the wings at this point grounds the dragon (removing flight)
- **Strength:** Elemental fury — the dragon's aura intensifies, expanding by 5 feet and adding 1d6 damage of the dragon's element to all attacks
- **Ability:** Breath Recharge — the dragon's breath weapon recharges immediately, regardless of its normal recharge condition
- **Death Throes:** Elemental explosion — 30-foot radius, CR × 1d6 elemental damage, Dex save for half

### Fiends

- **Frailty:** Infernal wound — vulnerable to radiant damage; the corrupted flesh cannot resist divine force
- **Strength:** Hellfire weapons — all attacks deal an additional 1d6 fire damage; the creature's desperation supercharges its infernal nature
- **Ability:** Infernal Rally — summons 1d4 imps or dretches (or equivalent) within 20 feet; or, if no summoning is appropriate, all nearby fiends gain Temporary HP equal to the creature's CR
- **Death Throes:** Hellfire eruption — 15-foot radius, 3d10 fire damage, Dex save for half; or a Death Curse on the killing blow (Disadvantage on saves until Greater Restoration)

### Undead (Corporeal)

- **Frailty:** Brittle bones — critical hits against this creature reduce its DR at the struck location to 0 until repaired; physical trauma is amplified
- **Strength:** Undead persistence — once per encounter, when reduced to 0 HP, remains at 1 HP instead (Constitution save DC 15; this save is already factored in, the GM rolls it secretly)
- **Ability:** Aura of Dread — all living creatures within 20 feet must make a Sanity save (DC 12 + CR÷2) or lose 1 Sanity and suffer −1 to attack rolls until the end of their next turn
- **Death Throes:** Necrotic burst — 10-foot radius, 2d8 necrotic damage, Constitution save for half

### Undead (Incorporeal)

- **Frailty:** Manifestation weakened — can be hit by non-magical attacks (if previously immune) or its incorporeality only works against attacks that miss by 5 or more rather than all non-magical attacks
- **Strength:** Haunt intensifies — its possession or fear abilities, if any, gain a +2 DC bonus; if it has no such abilities, it instead gains resistance to all damage except radiant, psychic, and force
- **Ability:** Final Wail — Sanity save (DC 14 + CHA modifier) or become Frightened for 1 minute (repeat save at end of each turn); if a creature fails by 5 or more, they also lose 1d4 Sanity
- **Death Throes:** Soul release — creatures within 15 feet make a Wisdom save (DC 12 + CHA modifier) or suffer one mental trauma condition (GM's choice appropriate to the creature)

### Constructs

- **Frailty:** Mechanical malfunction — the construct has Disadvantage on complex actions (spellcasting, anything requiring concentration, actions with multiple components); its precision falters
- **Strength:** Overcharge — +3 to all damage rolls and +10 feet of speed for 3 rounds; the damage cascade creates a brief surge of output before failure
- **Ability:** Emergency Protocol — the construct repairs itself for 2d8 HP and automatically stabilises its Momentum at 0 (neither gaining from the repair nor losing from the injury)
- **Death Throes:** Arcane explosion — 15-foot radius, 3d10 force damage, Dex save for half; magical debris litters the area (difficult terrain in a 10-foot radius)

### Aberrations

- **Frailty:** Psychic backlash — when the aberration takes damage, it involuntarily projects pain; all creatures within 10 feet must make a Sanity save (DC 10) or lose 1 Sanity
- **Strength:** Alien resilience — the creature becomes resistant to one damage type (GM chooses thematically); its alien physiology compensates in unexpected ways
- **Ability:** Reality Shudder — the aberration distorts local space; all creatures within 20 feet make an Intelligence save (DC 12 + INT modifier) or lose their reaction until the end of their next turn
- **Death Throes:** Psychic detonation — 20-foot radius, 4d8 psychic damage (Sanity save for half rather than Dex save); creatures that fail also lose 1d4 Sanity

### Fey

- **Frailty:** Glamour fading — Disadvantage on Charisma checks and saves; Advantage on saves to resist its charm and illusion effects for the remainder of the encounter
- **Strength:** Wild desperation — the creature can use the Disengage action as a bonus action, and difficult terrain no longer slows it
- **Ability:** Reality Slip — the fey teleports up to 60 feet to any location it can see; all ongoing conditions (grappled, restrained, marked) immediately end
- **Death Throes:** Fey dissolution — the creature vanishes but may return (at the GM's discretion, it survives at 1 HP in the Feywild); or it leaves a 10-foot radius of difficult terrain that lasts for 1 minute

### Celestials

- **Frailty:** Radiance dims — the celestial's aura effects are suppressed for 1 round after crossing the threshold; the wound is visible as a dark crack in its divine light
- **Strength:** Divine resolve — the celestial gains Advantage on all saving throws and its attacks deal an additional 1d8 radiant damage; it has stopped holding back
- **Ability:** Celestial Intercession — the celestial heals one ally within 60 feet for 3d10 hit points; or, if no allies remain, it grants itself the same healing
- **Death Throes:** Font of radiant light — all allies within 60 feet heal 4d8 HP; all undead and fiends within 30 feet take 4d8 radiant damage (Constitution save for half)

## DCS Integration

The Bloodied system does not stand apart from the Dynamic Combat System — it feeds directly into it.

**Momentum.** The Momentum reset to 0 at the Staggered threshold changes Side Initiative for the following round. A creature that was dictating the fight through high Momentum suddenly loses that advantage at the threshold, which often creates a window the party can exploit before the creature's Strength kicks in.

**Called Shots.** Frailties frequently reduce DR at specific locations or impose Disadvantage on saves — both of which make Called Shots more valuable. A Frailty that drops arm DR from 3 to 1 transforms a −2 penalty Called Shot into a near-guaranteed high-damage exchange. Recognising Frailties and pairing them with Called Shots is the core tactical loop of fighting experienced combatants.

**Injury System.** The Injury roll at the Staggered threshold (which fires before Bloodied effects) can itself compound the Frailty. A creature that rolls Fractured Grip on the Injury table and also has an arm Frailty from Bloodied is severely compromised at that location. These systems stacking is intentional.

**Breaking Points and Rally.** The Auto Rally that fires when a leader becomes Bloodied is directly connected to the morale system. A successful Rally at this moment can stabilise an entire force; a failure accelerates its collapse. The full Rally and Breaking Point rules are in the Bloodied, Breaking Points & Rally document.

**Evasion and DR.** Frailties that reduce Evasion or DR interact with the standard DCS attack sequence. A Frailty that imposes Disadvantage on Evasion rolls is mechanically equivalent to a significant hit bonus for every attacker for the rest of the fight — treat it accordingly.

## Class Hooks

Certain classes interact with the Bloodied system in ways that go beyond standard combat. These are abbreviated here; full details are in individual class entries.

**Investigator.** The Investigator's core loop is built around predicting and exploiting Bloodied effects. At level 2, Exploit Vulnerability allows them to identify one or more Bloodied effects before the threshold is crossed (Intelligence check, DC 12 + CR÷2 for one effect, scaling upward). At level 5, Bloodied Exploitation allows them to choose from compound effects when attacking a Bloodied creature whose Frailty they have identified — ignoring DR entirely, sharing the Frailty with all allies, or analytically countering the creature's Strength. At level 11, Finisher deals 4d10 bonus damage to Bloodied creatures and can deny Death Throes on a killing Called Shot. The Investigator is the party's Bloodied specialist.

**Monk.** Ki Sense (level 2) automatically detects when any creature within 30 feet becomes Bloodied, without requiring sight. This is mechanical awareness, not magical — it works through blindness and invisibility. Against Bloodied creatures, the Monk's Flurry of Blows gains a third strike, Stunning Strike costs no Technique Points, and Ki-Empowered Strikes deal bonus damage. At level 10+, the Monk's critical hit range expands against Bloodied targets.

**Ranger.** Hunter's Mark intensifies against Bloodied targets. The DR reduction from Hunter's Mark (equal to the Ranger's Wisdom modifier) becomes more significant when applied to a creature that already has a Frailty reducing a location's DR. The Path of the Predator heroic boon (level 10) allows automatic transfer of Hunter's Mark to a creature that just became Bloodied, and automatically reveals two of its Bloodied effects.

**Sangromancer.** The Sangromancer's Blood Resonance activates against Bloodied creatures, reducing the HP cost of their blood magic and increasing the healing they receive from hitting Bloodied enemies. At higher levels, a Sangromancer who is also Bloodied gains additional combat bonuses — the two desperate fighters mirror each other.

**Barbarian.** While Raging, the Barbarian's Momentum cannot drop below 0 from injury thresholds at level 11. This means the Barbarian maintains initiative advantage through the Staggered threshold — their Rage insulates them from the Momentum collapse that a standard Bloodied creature experiences, making them the party's most reliable sustained threat against enemies near the threshold.

## Player Quick Reference

**When an enemy becomes Bloodied, ask four questions:**

1. **Frailty** — What vulnerability just appeared? How do we exploit it?
2. **Strength** — What did they gain? How do we adapt?
3. **Ability** — What just triggered? What is the immediate response?
4. **Death Throes** — What happens when they die? Are we positioned safely?

**Before the threshold:**
- Track enemy HP and plan around the crossing
- Save burst damage if you want to cross the threshold and press immediately
- Pre-position for Death Throes if you know what they are

**After the threshold:**
- Pair Called Shots with revealed Frailties
- Do not stack up on the creature if its Death Throes are area damage
- Kill quickly if the Strength is severe; kite if you need to wait it out
- Interrupt or disrupt the Ability if your reaction economy allows it

### The four effects at a glance

| Effect | Triggers | Duration | Player response |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Frailty** | On becoming Bloodied | Rest of encounter | Exploit immediately — Called Shots, targeting the exposed location, abilities that target the relevant save |

| **Ability** | On becoming Bloodied | Once, immediately | Prepare countermeasure or react — readied action, reaction, positioning |

### Momentum at the Bloodied threshold

| Event | Momentum effect |
|---|---|
| Staggered threshold (Injury System) | Reset to 0 |
| Bloodied Strength (if Momentum type) | +2 MS immediately |
| Bloodied Frailty (if Momentum type) | Momentum floor raised to −1 |
| Auto Rally — Success | All allies within 120 ft gain +1 MS |
| Auto Rally — Failure | All allies within 120 ft lose −1 MS |
