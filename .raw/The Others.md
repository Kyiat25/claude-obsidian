# The Others
## *The Peoples Between Truth and Lie*

> *"The elves remember what was. Humans forget what wasn't. But we—we were never told a story complete enough to believe or reject. We found our own truths in the cracks."*  
> — **Moragh Ironvoice**, Dwarven Stone-Singer, speaking at the Grand Moot of Testimony

---

## The Cosmological Position (DM Only)

**What Actually Happened:**
- **Astralor** created all mortal races during this iteration of reality
- Each race emerged at different times during the Loomless Time and early First Age
- **Gods (Divine Spark beings) claimed creation credit** after races already existed
- **Elemental Embodiments** (Vatharun, Nereus, Soltharion, etc.) shaped the world but didn't create races
- **No mortal race fully comprehends** the 9 Fundamental Forces or Astralor's role

**The Spectrum of Understanding:**

**Closest to Truth:**
- **Elves** - Pattern-sense tells them gods lied, but can't explain alternative
- **Orcs** - Already assume gods are trials/enemies, not benevolent creators
- **Goblins** - Know they were "forgotten," closest to seeing no one was chosen
- **Wood Elf Druids** - Have living proof (trees, cycles) predating divine organization

**Middle Ground:**
- **Dwarves** - Focus on endurance over theology, instinctively skeptical of divine claims
- **Gnomes** - Treat reality as glitchy software, almost see the fundamental bugs
- **Tieflings** - Sense divine messages are misread, question inherited guilt
- **Halflings** - Preserve alternative truths through joy, but don't question deeply

**Furthest from Truth:**
- **Dragonborn** - Built entire identity on being "Soltharion's chosen"
- **Humans** - Too short-lived to verify claims, desperately need structure
- **Drow** - Believe Vel'Zyren's transformation was creation, deepest into the lie

**The Tragic Reality:**
Every race except elves has **short enough lifespans** or **fragmented enough cultural memory** that they cannot verify ancient claims. They must choose between:
- Accepting gods' creation story (comprehensible but false)
- Accepting incomprehensible cosmic truth (accurate but meaningless)
- Creating their own meaning (what most actually do)

## The Dwarves: Those Who Carve Permanence

### **Timeline:** ~12,000-10,000 years ago (mid-Loomless Time)

**When Dwarves Emerged:**

Unlike elves (ancient) or humans (recent), dwarves appeared during the **mid-Loomless Time** when reality was still stabilizing:
- Emerged in **mountain depths and subterranean spaces**
- First conscious awareness was of **stone, pressure, darkness, and endurance**
- Found themselves in a world of **molten heat and crushing weight**
- No memory of before—only the **now of being**

**What They Encountered:**

By the time dwarves developed consciousness, they found:
- **Elemental Embodiments** already present (especially Vatharun, Thamur, Rhoris)
- **Earth and Fire** as fundamental forces
- **Stone** as eternal, unchanging, reliable
- A world that **demanded you prove your worth to survive**

**The First Understanding:**

> *"We were not born. We were forged."*

The earliest dwarven consciousness developed around a central truth:
- They did not **arrive**—they **emerged** from stone itself
- The First Builders (Vatharun, Rhoris, Thamur, Orivak, Gaeldros) were there
- Whether these beings **made** them or simply **witnessed** them mattered less than: they were **here**, and they must **endure**

**Why This Mattered:**

Dwarves never developed a **creation theology** the way humans did:
- They didn't ask "Who made us?"
- They asked "What will last?"
- They didn't seek divine favor
- They sought **proof of permanence**

**The Deep Law Emerges:**

Over thousands of years, dwarven philosophy coalesced:

**Core Principles:**
1. **Stone remembers** - What is carved endures
2. **Oaths bind deeper than blood** - Spoken word backed by action
3. **What you build defines you** - Legacy over lineage
4. **The Deep Law remembers** - Reality has structure independent of gods
5. **A mountain does not envy the forest. It merely endures.**

**The Relationship with Elemental Embodiments:**

Dwarves understood the Embodiments differently than other races:
- **Vatharun** wasn't their creator—he **was** the mountain they lived in
- **Thamur** wasn't their forge-father—he **was** the primordial flame
- **Rhoris** wasn't their teacher—he **was** craftsmanship incarnate

To dwarves, these beings were:
- **Forces** to work with, not gods to worship
- **Materials** to shape, not masters to obey
- **Truths** to respect, not mysteries to solve

**The First Clans:**

Dwarven social structure developed around **craft and endurance**:
- Clans formed around **what they built** (forges, mines, halls)
- Identity tied to **what they created** (weapons, armor, stonework)
- Honor measured by **what survived** (buildings, tools, names)
- Shame tied to **what failed** (broken oaths, collapsed tunnels, tarnished works)

**Cultural Focus:**
- **Memory carved in stone** - nothing spoken lasts
- **Oath-binding** - your word shapes reality if backed by deed
- **Forge-Law** - what you craft reflects your soul
- **Controlled Expansion** - only claim what you can hold
- **Trial of 11 and 12** - when truth divides, steel decides

## The Dwarven Response to Divine Claims

### **Late First Age (~2,000 years ago)**

**When Gods Made Creation Claims:**

By the time Divine Spark gods organized into pantheons and claimed "We created all races," dwarves were already **~10,000 years old** with established culture:

**The Dwarven Response:**
> *"You claim you made us? Then why don't we remember you making us? And why does the stone remember before you?"*

**But They Didn't Fight It:**

Unlike elves, dwarves didn't **resist** the divine creation claim because:
- It **didn't matter** to their core philosophy
- What matters is **what endures**, not who claims credit
- Divine favor is **temporary**—stone is **eternal**
- Let gods claim what they want; **the mountain knows**

**The Pragmatic Compromise:**

Dwarves developed a relationship with divine claims that was:
- **Respectful** but **not worshipful**
- **Cooperative** but **not subservient**
- **Compatible** but **not dependent**

**Their Theological Position:**
> *"The gods are real and powerful. The Weave exists and structures reality. But we answer to stone and law, not Threads and echoes. We are not made to rule. We are made to endure. The gods may forget us. But the mountains will remember."*

**The Faith of the Bound Pattern:**

When the Faith emerged, dwarves:
- Saw it as **useful framework** for multi-racial cooperation
- Respected the **elemental organization** (especially Earth Thread)
- Never adopted it as **spiritual truth**
- Viewed Threadbearers as **administrators**, not prophets
- Participated in **civic rituals** without **religious belief**

**Critical Understanding:**
> *"The Faith of the Bound Pattern is theologically compatible but philosophically distinct from the Deep Law. Dwarves respect the Pattern as a tool of reality but revere the builders who left it behind, not the Threads themselves."*

## Dwarven View of Ashkarra's Fall

### **~150 years ago (Third Age ~950)**

**What Dwarves Saw:**

When Ashkarra fell, dwarves were among the **first to understand** what happened:
- Not divine punishment
- Not moral failure
- **Cosmic structural collapse** from violating fundamental law

**The Dwarven Analysis:**
> *"They tried to lock a Thread in place. The Pattern corrected them. This was not gods judging mortals. This was reality rejecting impossibility. Like trying to build a bridge from smoke—it doesn't matter how faithful you are. The structure fails."*

**Immediate Response:**

Under **Gaeldros's law**, dwarves:
- Shut **every Deep Hall** connected to Ashkarra's leyline with **Oath-Seals**
- Enacted **Trial of the Unmarked Flame** - all surviving dragonborn from Ashkarra judged
- Observed the **Silence Chain** - three days with no forging, no singing, no words
- Performed **Rite of Refusal** - Stone-Keepers sealed themselves in isolated chambers
- Amended the Deep Law with new warning:

> *"If even the Threads may fray, then carve deeper. Trust only what you build."*

**The Lesson Dwarves Learned:**

Ashkarra proved what dwarves always believed:
- **Divine favor doesn't prevent structural failure**
- **No amount of faith changes cosmic law**
- **What you believe matters less than what is real**
- **Stone endures; everything else is temporary**

**Why This Mattered:**

For dwarves, Ashkarra wasn't tragedy—it was **validation**:
> *"We told them. We showed them. Stone doesn't care about your faith. Mountains don't care about your gods. The law is carved deeper than divine will. They didn't listen. Now their city doesn't exist. The Deep Law stands. The mountains remain. We endure."*

## Dwarven View of Other Races

### **The Measuring of Worth**

> *"We measure a race by what they leave behind. If it cannot endure, it was never true. If it cannot be built, it was never worth carving."*

**Elves:**
> *"Elves build for beauty. Their works fade."*
- Respected for memory but mistrusted for lack of structural legacy
- Their magic sings, but it forgets
- Too enamored with cycles and aesthetics, which weakens foundations
- **Post-Ashkarra:** "They warned. We listened. They were right about structure."

**Humans:**
> *"Humans build too fast. They do not last."*
- Admired for ambition but pitied for impatience
- Capable of great acts but rarely lasting ones
- Human oaths are fragile, often broken for gain or convenience
- **Post-Ashkarra:** "They believed too easily. They built Ashkarra on faith, not stone."

**Orcs:**
> *"Orcs destroy. They leave no legacy."*
- Respected as force incarnate but dangerous, unstable
- Noted for honor but criticized for lack of preservation
- "To destroy is easy. To build what endures is sacred."
- **Post-Ashkarra:** "They never trusted the gods. They were wiser than they knew."

**Dragonborn:**
> *"Dragonborn remember the past, but never improve it."*
- Seen as ceremonial mimics—trapped by legacy and caste
- Rigid reverence noted, but faulted for stagnation
- "What you recite is not what you refine. Tradition without growth is gilded rust."
- **Post-Ashkarra:** "They forgot: even the chosen must follow cosmic law."

**Gnomes:**
> *"Gnomes invent quickly. Dwarves build perfectly."*
- Bright sparks full of promise and folly
- Appreciated for ingenuity but disdained for lack of testing and patience
- "A gnome builds a hundred things. A dwarf builds one that lasts a hundred years."
- **Post-Ashkarra:** "They archive what we build. This is valuable."

**Halflings:**
> *"Halflings do not build—but they do not pretend."*
- Given **surprising dwarven respect**
- Make no false claim to permanence; live truly, joyfully, remember well
- **Only race besides dwarves that understands death properly**—not as loss, but as legacy
- "They are worth half a Dwarf. That's more than can be said for the rest."
- **Post-Ashkarra:** "They laughed through the fall. We carved through it. Both are valid."

**Drow:**
> *"Drow steal. They are not worth the dirt they crawl through."*
- **Absolute dwarven contempt**
- Not for power—but for theft, lies, and desecration
- "Echo-thieves" who mimic the Deep Law in reverse
- Corrupt runes, deface oaths, dwell in hollowed stone without memory
- Even **Duergar** (darkened dwarves) are tolerated as wounded kin
- Drow are **void of kinship or worth**
- **Post-Ashkarra:** "They worship a goddess who tried to rewrite reality. She failed. Her children are living proof of that failure."

## The Halflings: Those Who Preserve Through Joy

### **Timeline:** ~8,000-6,000 years ago (Late Loomless Time)

**When Halflings Emerged:**

Halflings appeared relatively recently:
- Later than elves (~20,000+ years ago)
- Later than dwarves (~12,000 years ago)
- Around the same time as humans (~8,000 years ago)
- But unlike humans, they **already had community structure**

**What Made Them Different:**

When halflings first became conscious, they discovered:
- They were **small** in a world of larger races
- They were **vulnerable** to forces they couldn't fight
- They **couldn't build stone halls** like dwarves
- They **couldn't preserve written memory** like would come later

**So They Built Something Else:**

> *"The laugh must live longer than we do. That's the only law worth keeping."*

**The First Understanding:**

Early halflings developed around central truths:
- **Size doesn't determine survival**—cleverness does
- **Strength doesn't guarantee victory**—joy does
- **Stone doesn't preserve memory**—**story** does
- **You can't defeat everything**—but you can **outlast** it

**Why This Mattered:**

Halflings never tried to be:
- Warriors (too small)
- Builders (too weak)
- Scholars (too scattered)
- Rulers (too humble)

Instead they became:
- **Survivors** (too clever to kill completely)
- **Celebrants** (too joyful to break)
- **Storytellers** (too memorable to forget)
- **Avengers** (too patient to escape)

---

## The Joy Truths: Memory Through Laughter

**What Halflings Discovered:**

Over millennia, halflings realized something profound:
- Memory doesn't require **stone** (dwarves)
- Memory doesn't require **long life** (elves)
- Memory doesn't require **writing** (humans, later)
- Memory requires **joy** and **retelling**

**The Core Philosophy:**

> *"Joy is sacred and must outlive us. Memory is preserved through story and laughter, not stone or writing. Community and celebration are divine acts."*

**The Practices:**

**Flame-Singers:**
- Most honored storytellers—**never called leaders**
- Keep the long myths (The Laugh That Binds, Tale of Redtooth, Hollowed Cycle)
- Sing or speak stories at firelight gatherings
- Often elder wanderers with "a strange spoon or stone that helps them remember"
- **"You know it's a real Flame-Singer if they forget your name, but remember the riddle your gran told you while drunk."**

**Sparkmouths:**
- Children and fools who remember things **"exactly wrong in the right way"**
- Tell myths backwards, with added monsters, as riddles
- Their version always ends up **more right** than official ones

**Cultural Structure:**
- No formal hierarchy
- No written laws (until contact with other races)
- Everything preserved through **oral tradition and celebration**
- Festivals mark **memory**, not just time
- **Dual-keeping** with gnomes (who archive what halflings would forget for fun)

**The Echoed Mother:**

Halflings aligned with the **Spirit Pantheon**, especially:
- **The Echoed Mother** - who protects their souls
- **Vaedoris** - the Soulward Shield
- They don't worship as humans do
- They **celebrate with** these forces

## The Hidden Terror: When Halflings Hunt

### **The Compiled Warnings of the Free Races**

**The Truth Other Races Learned:**

Halflings preserve memory. And memory includes **grudges**.

When someone harms a halfling community, something happens:
- The laughter stops
- The songs change
- The stories begin
- **And the hunt starts**

**Every Race Has Learned This:**

**Humans:**
> *"Do not mistake size for softness. A Halfling smiling at your army means the ambush has already begun."*

**Elves:**
> *"Their joy hides memory sharper than any blade. Harm them, and their vengeance will echo longer than your bloodline."*

**Dwarves:**
> *"A Halfling is worth half a Dwarf—which makes them kin. Offend one, and we won't stop them."*

**Dragonborn:**
> *"Never raid a Halfling village. You will not survive what follows, and your bones will be used in a joke."*

**Orcs:**
> *"They don't fight fair. They fight like laughter with teeth."*

**Gnomes:**
> *"Trust them with your inventions. Don't trust them not to weaponize joy."*

**Spirit Faith:**
> *"Their joy is sacred and protected by the Echoed Mother. Harming a Halfling soul invites the Hunt beyond Death."*

**Drow:**
> *"If you see one, it is already too late. If it smiles, you are not the hunter."*

**Why Halflings Are Feared:**

When halflings seek vengeance:
- They don't declare war
- They don't challenge openly
- They **celebrate** your upcoming death
- Your food turns poisonous
- Your commanders go missing
- Your camp becomes a nightmare
- **Then they feast on what's left**

**The Philosophy:**
> *"We don't need to be warriors. We just need you to forget we're dangerous. And we're very good at making people forget."*

## Halflings and the Divine Lie

**When Gods Made Creation Claims:**

By the time gods claimed creation, halflings were ~6,000 years old:

**The Halfling Response:**
> *"Did you make us? Maybe! Do we care? Not really. You're invited to the feast either way."*

**Why They Didn't Resist:**

Halflings never cared about:
- Who created them
- Why they exist
- What divine plan includes them

They cared about:
- **Is there food?**
- **Are we laughing?**
- **Are our stories being told?**
- **Are we surviving?**

**The Pragmatic Acceptance:**

Halflings adopted the Joy Truths:
- Aligned with Spirit Pantheon (especially Echoed Mother)
- Participated in Faith of Bound Pattern where useful
- Never took theology seriously
- **"If the gods want worship, they can come to our feasts. Otherwise, we're busy."**

**Post-Ashkarra Understanding:**

When Ashkarra fell, halflings:
- **Took note** but didn't panic
- Added it to their **warning stories**
- Created new festivals about **"The City That Forgot to Laugh"**
- Used it as cautionary tale about **taking yourself too seriously**

> *"Ashkarra tried to lock the Pattern in place. We try to make sure joy never stays in one place too long. Guess which city still exists?"*

## The Sacred Bond: Halflings and Dwarves

### **The Myth of Stone & Laughfire**

Ancient legend speaks of:
- A time when both races were young
- Dwarves building too seriously
- Halflings laughing too recklessly
- Both nearly destroyed by what they lacked
- One dwarf and one halfling **saved both peoples** by teaching balance

**The Result:**

An unbreakable bond:
- Halflings carry **weapons of unmatched dwarven craftsmanship**
- **Only race permitted to modify or mock dwarven work** without insult
- Dwarves **will not intervene** if a Halfling takes vengeance
- Often **help** instead
- "A Halfling is worth half a Dwarf. That's more than can be said for the rest."

**Why This Matters:**

The bond proves:
- Opposite philosophies can coexist
- Stone and laughter both preserve
- Seriousness and joy both have value
- Different races can be **genuine kin** without theology

**Modern Practice:**

In dwarven holds:
- Halflings have **special guest-right**
- Their songs are **carved in dwarven halls**
- Their weapons are **forged by master smiths**
- Their vengeance is **backed by dwarven steel**

In halfling communities:
- Dwarves are **always welcome**
- Their stories are **preserved in song**
- Their works are **celebrated in feast**
- Their friendship is **never forgotten**

## The Orcs: Those Who Earn Through Trial

### **Timeline:** ~10,000 years ago (Mid-Loomless Time)

**When Orcs Emerged:**

Orcs appeared during the chaotic mid-Loomless Time:
- World still unstable
- Elements clashing
- Gods not yet organized
- **Everything was a test of survival**

**First Awareness:**

When orcs first became conscious, they found:
- A world that **wanted them dead**
- Elements that **tested them constantly**
- Other races that **feared or fought them**
- Gods that **tried to claim or control them**

**The First Response:**
> *"If everything wants to kill us, then everything is our enemy. And we will **earn** the right to exist by **surviving** what others cannot."*

**Why This Mattered:**

Orcs never had a period of:
- Divine favor
- Easy survival
- Natural acceptance
- Peaceful development

Everything was **earned through blood, scars, and endurance**.

---

## The Path of the First Scar

**The Core Philosophy:**

> *"We did not make the gods. But we made their scars. And in those scars, we placed our names."*

**What Orcs Believe:**

- All gods are **enemies first**
- Anything earned through worship must be **taken through struggle**
- **Honor is the true bloodline**—earned through trials, not birth
- Each god is a **trial** to be resisted, bested, or endured
- What other races call "blessing," orcs call "challenge"
- What others view as "guidance," orcs see as "tests to be beaten into submission"

**The Scarbound Names:**

Orcs don't use gods' true names—they use **Orc-earned titles**:

**Grak-Thul, The First Scar** (Soltharion, the Sun Tyrant):
> *"He tried to burn us into obedience. So we let his fire carve our truth."*
- Fire that teaches through pain
- Battlefield that remembers
- **Not worshiped as flame god, but as the scar left by resisting flame**

**Ugra-Mara, The Ash-Mother** (Ishara, the Verdant Queen):
> *"She tried to swallow us back into root and rot. But from ash, we became stronger."*
- Destroys but also tests
- From her wild storms, orcs learned to build with muscle, not stone

**Korrun, The Iron-Tongue** (Vaedoris/Echoed Mother):
> *"They spoke to us of memory and fate—but only after we screamed our names in blood."*
- Memory and honor earned, not given

**The Philosophy:**
> *"We do not kneel to gods. We test them. If they are worthy, we carry their names in our scars. If they are not, we forget them."*

## Orcish Culture: Honor as Currency

**The System:**

> *"Honor (zakkur) is not metaphorical. It is counted by deeds, titles, scars, and victories. It is stored in names, scars, stories, and the strength of one's lineage. It is traded only once: when a child inherits a parent's legacy."*

**Honor Rules:**

1. You may earn honor alone, but it belongs to your bloodline
2. To waste honor on petty pride is to steal from your descendants
3. To face a weak test when a harder one was available is dishonor
4. To lie about honor is worse than death—**it is erasure**
5. To defeat a greater foe is to mint new honor for your clan

**Challenge-Wealth:**

Every major orc clan tracks how many legendary tests:
- Their ancestors endured
- Their descendants have claimed
- Still await completion

**The highest honor is not to win—but to be known for enduring the test that should have broken you.**

> *"He did not win the duel—but he stood for five strikes after his chest was opened. His blood bought us a hundred years of respect."*

## The Seven Sacred Challenges

**The Founding Clans:**

Ancient orcs established seven clans through seven sacred challenges:

**I. Clan Graathurn - Challenge of Silence:**
- Endured seven days of torture without giving names of hiding kin
- Sacred test: Speak no words for seven days while training
- Founder **Graathurn the Stone-Throat** silenced a Weave-rip with single breath
- Echoed Mother herself appeared beside him in that silence

**II. Clan Marrak-Vorr - Challenge of Judgment:**
- Rendered impartial justice during war between three tribes
- Executed own cousin for oath-breaking
- Sacred test: Preside over blood-duel without bias, even when kin involved
- Judges trusted across all Orcish lands

**III. Clan Tharkuz - Challenge of the Sky-Hunt:**
- Tracked and slew cave wyvern across three mountain ranges without food
- Sacred test: Track and mark beast/foe without aid, return with story/scar/trophy
- Elite scouts and border-guards

**IV. Clan Velgra-Morn - Challenge of the Unseen Scar:**
- Took in dishonored warrior, let him earn redemption
- Sacred test: Accept and sponsor unclaimed soul through redemption trial
- Known as "Clan of the Returning Flame"
- Founder absorbed Eldritch corruption into own scar to spare others

**Modern Reality:**

Most orc clans are now **woven lineages**:
- Full orcs and half-orcs who passed trials
- Leaders often "Soft-Scarred" (half-orc) origin
- Purity irrelevant—**what matters is the scar you earned**

## Half-Orcs and the Trial of Line-Claiming

### **The Sacred Challenge**

**At Age 14:**

All half-orcs undergo **Trial of Line-Claiming** (Scarfire Choice):

**The Trial:**
- Overseen by two Line-Keepers and one Scar-Warden
- Must complete trial of endurance, wit, resolve tied to ancestral line's defining value
- Must speak aloud the name they seek and recite story of its weight
- Must accept one sacred wound symbolizing acceptance of legacy

**Outcomes:**

**If They Pass:**
- Name carved into clan's Ledger
- From that moment: **they are Orc** in every way
- Status never mentioned again unless they choose dual-epithet

**If They Fail:**
- **Not shamed**
- Marked with "soft-scar" across ribs (line not yet earned)
- Given **"The Farewell of Fire":**
  - Weapon forged from family's smithy
  - Armor with sigil of dormant line
  - Rations and coin for journey
  - Escort to nearest mixed-race city
- Can return anytime to attempt trial again

**The Philosophy:**
> *"We do not ask who your parents were. We ask what you scarred. We ask what you remember. We ask what you are willing to bear. If you carry it with pride, we will carry you with us."*

**Why This Matters:**

Half-orcs prove:
- Blood doesn't determine worth
- Trial determines identity
- **Anyone can be orc if they earn the scar**
- Honor transcends lineage

## Orcs and the Divine Lie

**When Gods Made Creation Claims:**

By the time gods claimed creation (~2,000 years ago), orcs were ~8,000 years old:

**The Orcish Response:**
> *"You claim you made us? Then explain why we had to fight you to exist. Explain why every god tried to conquer, silence, or twist us. If you made us, you made us wrong. And we corrected your mistake through blood."*

**Why Orcs Rejected Divine Claims:**

Orcs never experienced divine favor as:
- Gift
- Protection
- Guidance
- Love

They experienced it as:
- **Test**
- **Challenge**
- **Trial**
- **Enemy to overcome**

**The Theological Position:**

> *"All gods are enemies first. What other races call blessing, we call challenge. What they view as guidance, we see as tests to be beaten into submission. We don't worship—we **earn names** by surviving what gods send."*

**Post-Ashkarra Understanding:**

When Ashkarra fell, orcs:
- **Were not surprised**
- Saw it as proof gods don't protect
- Used it as teaching: **"Even divine favor doesn't save you from cosmic law"**
- Respected dragonborn survivors who questioned faith
- **"They learned what we always knew: the gods test you, they don't save you."**

## Orcish View of Other Races

**Elves:**
> *"Endurers, not beasts. The older families know we are line-bound like they are memory-bound."*
- Mutual respect for memory and lineage
- Scar-Wardens given access to ancestral libraries
- High Elves bow heads when Scar-Warden passes
- **Wound Treaty of Dawnmere** - peace brokered by Warden
- **"Both races believe a life not remembered is a life wasted—they simply scar it differently."**

**Dwarves:**
> *"Rivals in tradition, but equals in ancestral weight and enduring faith."*
- Stone-Singers sing names of great orcs during funerals
- Dwarves sharpen weapons, repair gear for Scar-Wardens
- Bond not warm but **deep**
- Honor to be named in orc's Ledger—and vice versa
- **"We may clash, but the stone remembers both names."**

**Halflings:**
> *"Honorable and terrifyingly serious, yet oddly comforting as protectors of truth."*
- Halflings deeply respect Scar-Wardens
- Offer food, shelter, volunteers for hunts
- View it as part of great tale
- Leave "stone of laughter" at orc graves
- **"Both believe in legacy; Orcs preserve through pain, Halflings through story."**

**Humans:**
> *"The hard mirror of their own ambition—unyielding, honor-bound, unwilling to compromise."*
- Feared in war, respected in law
- Some cities suspend civic courts when Scar-Warden arrives ("Day of Blade-Honor")
- Generals admire Scar-Law clarity vs political betrayal

**Dragonborn:**
> *"Honorbound warriors with poor understanding of divine order."*
- Mutual respect high, clashes frequent
- Scar-Wardens treated as honored guests (equivalent to Embermarked)
- At least three minor wars from Warden-vs-Sultan conflicts
- **"Both worship strength, lineage, and fire—but differ in how it should be wielded."**

**Drow:**
> *"Defilers of the Scar."*
- **Absolute enmity**
- Scar-Wardens not permitted in Drow territory
- All orcs treated as enemies or slaves
- **"Let them have their scars. We carve lineage into flesh that obeys."** (Drow view)
- No shared ground—Drow believe obedience is strength, orcs believe strength cannot be bent

**Gnomes:**
> *"Unfathomably structured, fascinating in ritual combat and spiritual memory."*
- View orcs as living myths
- Act as Honor-Librarians, copying Scar-Ledgers (with permission)
- Serve important role maintaining damaged Ledgers through magic
- Travel alongside Scar-Wardens to document rare trials

## The Gnomes: Those Who Archive the Glitch

### **Timeline:** ~7,000 years ago (Late Loomless Time)

**When Gnomes Emerged:**

Gnomes appeared relatively recently:
- After elves, dwarves, orcs
- Around same time as humans
- But with fundamentally different perspective

**First Awareness:**

When gnomes first became conscious, they noticed:
- **Reality had bugs**
- **Magic worked inconsistently**
- **Divine claims didn't match observable evidence**
- **Everything could be taken apart and examined**

**The First Response:**
> *"This is all running without documentation. We should probably keep logs."*

---

## The Wonder-Tithe Codex

**The Core Philosophy:**

> *"The Weave is full of broken Glyphs. Every spell, prophecy, or divine act should be questioned—not for disbelief, but for missing meaning. It's not broken. It's just running without documentation."*

**What Gnomes Discovered:**

Reality operates like:
- Software with bugs
- Hardware with loose connections
- Systems running on legacy code
- Processes that **work but no one knows why**

**The Response:**

Gnomes developed the **Wonder-Tithe Codex**:
- **Log everything** that seems wrong
- **Archive** all anomalies
- **Patch** what can be fixed
- **Document** what can't
- **Question** all divine claims
- **Test** all magical assertions

**The Practices:**

**Vault-Keepers:**
- Maintain archives of:
  - Pattern shifts
  - Glyphic failures
  - Divine contradictions
  - Magical anomalies
  - Historical inconsistencies
- Cross-reference everything
- Share findings with:
  - Other gnomes
  - Threadbearers (quietly)
  - Researchers (carefully)
  - Anyone who asks (eventually)

**The Philosophy:**
> *"We don't claim to fix reality. We just document where it's broken so someone else doesn't trip over the same bug twice."*

## Gnomes and Divine Claims

**When Gods Made Creation Claims:**

By the time gods claimed creation, gnomes were ~5,000 years old:

**The Gnome Response:**
> *"Interesting claim. Can you demonstrate that? What's your methodology? Can we replicate the results? Do you have documentation? Why do your claims contradict observable evidence from elvish archives? We'll just... make a note of this discrepancy."*

**Why Gnomes Were Skeptical:**

Gnomes had already observed:
- **Elven traditions predating divine organization**
- **Dwarven structures older than pantheons**
- **Magical systems working without divine intervention**
- **Pattern behavior inconsistent with divine control**
- **Tree rings, coral formations, geological evidence contradicting timelines**

**The Pragmatic Response:**

Gnomes didn't **fight** divine claims, they:
- **Documented** them
- **Cross-referenced** them
- **Noted inconsistencies**
- **Filed them** under "Unverified Claims - Divine Origin"
- **Moved on** to more interesting problems

**The Theological Position:**

> *"The Faith of the Bound Pattern gets the structure right, but leans toward symbolic rigidity. Gnomes treat its rituals as interpretive frameworks, not mandates, and consider its theology valuable as long as it's cross-indexed."*

**Post-Ashkarra Response:**

When Ashkarra fell, gnomes:
- **Documented everything**
- Created new filing category: "Catastrophic Pattern Failure - Case Study 1"
- **Analyzed** what caused collapse
- **Published findings** (carefully)
- **Noted** that Faith explanations were incomplete
- **Observed** that elven warnings were more accurate
- **Filed** under "Evidence Supporting Pre-Divine Weave Hypothesis"

> *"Ashkarra tried to lock a Thread. Pattern rejected it. Glyphic cascade failure. Unmaking event. Consistent with our models showing Pattern operates independently of divine will. See attached: 47 previous warnings from wood elf druids, all ignored. Additional note: divine intervention conspicuously absent during collapse. Conclusion: either gods cannot prevent such events, or chose not to. Either way, challenges creation authority claims."*

- **"They remember what we'd forget for fun—but thank the stars someone does."** (halfling view)

- Gnomes = "keepers of joy's shadow"

**With Dwarves:**
- **Wary respect**

- Dwarves praise recordkeeping but warn against tampering
- Many dwarves quietly visit gnomes to **retrieve forgotten Forgebound fragments**
- **"They bind with silk what we bind in stone. But their ledger holds things even we have lost."**

**With Orcs:**
- **Mutual fascination**
- Orcs view gnomes as living myths
- Gnomes study orcish ritual combat and spiritual memory
- Gnomes act as **Honor-Librarians**, copying Scar-Ledgers
- Travel with Scar-Wardens to document rare trials
- Both value **preservation of memory** in vastly different forms

**With Faith of Bound Pattern:**
- **Complicated**
- Tolerated, sometimes consulted during glyphic crises
- In Water/Spirit cities: consulted as pattern-breakers
- In Fire/Earth cycles: restricted or monitored
- **"Useful in collapse. Distracting in peace."**

## The Tieflings: Those Who Walk in Misread Fire

### **Timeline:** Varied - throughout ages

**What Tieflings Are:**

Unlike other races, tieflings are not:
- A separate creation
- An organized civilization
- A unified culture
- A chosen people

Tieflings are:
- **Touched by planar forces**
- **Marked by ancestral pacts**
- **Born from mortal-fiendish mixing**
- **Living questions** about sin, choice, and meaning

**The First Tieflings:**

No one knows when the first tiefling was born:
- Scattered throughout history
- Different origins in different places
- Some from pacts, some from curses, some from love
- All marked by **fire that burns differently**

**What They All Share:**

> *"We are not cursed. We are untranslated glyphs. What others fear is the power of meaning yet to be revealed."*

---

## The Ashen Testament

**The Core Belief:**

> *"Sin is not inherited—it is earned. Judgment is not divine—it is mortal projection. The gods are too vast, too alien, too encoded in paradox for their messages to be grasped by the masses. Tieflings are not cursed. They are untranslated glyphs."*

**What Tieflings Know:**

1. **The Divine Message Is Misread:**
   - Mortals misunderstand celestial intention
   - What is called "sin" may be misunderstood sacrifice, transformation, divine necessity
   - Gods are not malicious—they are **vast**
   - Tieflings are the space between meanings

2. **Sin Is Earned, Not Given:**
   - Being born tiefling doesn't grant sin
   - It offers potential—to fall, to rise, to choose
   - True sin is action
   - Blood opens the gate; **will** decides where it leads

3. **Power Reveals Meaning:**
   - Power is not evil
   - Power reveals the shape of your soul
   - Reflection of choice, not blood
   - Whether shows mercy or wrath is fire in which character forged

4. **The Ash Carries Truth:**
   - Ash is memory in final form
   - Proof something burned and meant something
   - Memory, guilt, rage, love—all sacred when transformed through fire

5. **We Walk Without Permission:**
   - Don't beg for entry
   - Don't ask for belonging
   - Burn own path
   - Legends are claimed, not granted

**The Practices:**

**Sin-Writs:**
- Mark great acts (noble or monstrous) on armor, skin, horns, soul-bound relics
- Not confessions—**testaments of legacy**
- Carved symbols, scorched journals, rune-branded blades

**Ash-Marks:**
- Wounds or burns commemorating defining moments
- **"What broke me shaped me; the flame witnessed."**
- Each mark tells story

**The Flame-Walk:**
- Pilgrimage where tieflings walk through fire
- Not to prove purity
- To prove **meaning**
- To show fire didn't consume them
- They **transformed it**

---

## Tieflings and Divine Claims

**When Gods Made Creation Claims:**

Tieflings weren't a unified people to hear it, but individually:

**The Tiefling Response:**
> *"You claim you created pure races, and I'm a mistake? Or you claim you created all races including me, and my fire is intentional? Either way, you're lying about something. I'll forge my own meaning, thank you."*

**Why Tieflings Rejected Divine Claims:**

Tieflings experienced:
- **Exclusion** from most faiths
- **Judgment** from most mortals
- **Claims** they were cursed/mistakes/aberrations
- **Contradictory** divine explanations

**The Response:**
> *"If the gods made me, they made me to ask questions you're afraid to answer. If they didn't make me, then all your creation claims are suspect. Either way, I'm keeping the fire."*

**Post-Ashkarra Understanding:**

When Ashkarra fell, tieflings:
- Saw it as **proof divine certainty kills**
- Used it to argue: **"The most faithful city fell. Maybe faith isn't the answer."**
- Created new philosophy: **"Even the gods' chosen burn. We've been burning all along."**
- Wrote it into Testament: **"Ashkarra was so certain. Two million souls paid for that certainty."**

> *"They were chosen by Soltharion. We are touched by fire no god claims. Their certainty unmade them. Our questions keep us alive. We'll take the fire that asks over the flame that demands."*

---

## Allied Faiths and Tiefling Solidarity

**The Way of the Named:**
- Shared by half-elves, tieflings, other outcasts
- Theology of **self-naming**
- Reject inherited identity
- Claim meaning through choice
- **"The flame is not divine. It is the self burning bright."**

**The Path of the Horizon-Soul (Goliaths):**
- Respect earned power and burden
- See tieflings as spiritual kin
- Both carry weight others don't acknowledge
- Both climb toward something

**The Joy Truths (Halflings):**
- See tieflings as "fire-hearted friends who walk beside death"
- Preserve tiefling tales in ballads
- Often with added laughter and impossible endings
- **"They carry darkness so we remember light. That's friendship."**

**The Deep Law (Dwarves):**
- Mixed response
- Some fear tieflings' relationship with flame
- Others honor tiefling who leaves proof of worthy labor
- Codex permits tiefling's name carved in stone—**if they die for the hold**
- **"The fire reveals character. If the character endures, carve the name."**

**Absolute Opposition:**

**The Ashen Web (Drow):**
- Total opposition
- Vel'Zyren's creed sees Testament as blasphemous rejection of hierarchy and blood-purity
- Drow assassinate tiefling leaders/poets who speak Creed in contested regions
- **"They claim fire without submission. Kill them."**

In being forgotten, they learned:

- **"The cracks are where truth leaks through"**

**Memory Markets:**

- **"We remember what you wanted forgotten"**

## Goblinoids and Divine Claims

**When Gods Made Creation Claims:**

By the time gods claimed creation, goblinoids were ~6,000 years old:

**The Goblinoid Response:**

- They correctly understand: **gods didn't create races**

**Post-Ashkarra Understanding:**

- **"The greatest city became the greatest Shard. We worship both."**

> *"They were chosen by fire. They fell in fire. We were forgotten by all gods. We endure. This is not irony. This is proof. The Pattern doesn't care about divine favor. Neither do we."*
