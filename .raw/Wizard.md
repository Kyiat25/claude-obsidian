# The Wizard

**Masters of Arcane Knowledge**

> *"The wizard doesn't command magic. The wizard understands it — and understanding is the more dangerous power."*

---

## The Architecture of the Weave

He had spent three days before the siege simply reading. Not planning — reading the notes from the last time this kind of creature had been fought, cross-referencing the environmental conditions, calculating which spells would interact with the terrain in ways the enemy couldn't anticipate. When the fight began, it lasted less than two minutes. He had already decided how it ended three days ago.

You are the Wizard. Not the most durable, not the most mobile, not the hardest hitter. The most prepared. Your **Spellbook** is a living document of accumulated understanding — every spell in it was studied, not given. Your **Arcane Recovery** is how you manage a long day without burning out. Your **Rote Spells** reflect the level of mastery achieved after years: spells so deeply understood they become extensions of thought.

In FEARS, Wizards gain their Momentum through **Arcane Flow** — when you cast a spell that successfully imposes a condition or reduces a creature to 0 HP, gain +1 Momentum. Your precision matters. Your spells that work build your rhythm. Your bond with **Echo Glyphs** is the deepest of any caster — you may bind more of them than anyone else, reflecting mastery of the Weave's layered resonances.

> **Weapon Mastery:** Wizards do not gain Weapon Mastery. Your tools are spells.

---

## The Wizard's Essence

You are the party's control engine, utility toolkit, and arcane intelligence. No class prepares as thoroughly. No class adapts their spell list as fluidly between adventuring days. No class binds as many Echo Glyphs.

You are fragile and short on hit points. Your power is your planning. When the encounter plays out as you foresaw, you are the most powerful class in the game. When it doesn't, you are still the best positioned to adapt — because you read the scenario before anyone else.

---

## Complexity Assessment: HIGH (8/10)    

The Wizard tracks:   
• Spellbook (all spells; can learn from any scroll/spellbook)
• Prepared spells (INT modifier + Wizard level)   
• Spell slots (full caster, long rest)  
• Arcane Recovery uses (once per day on short rest)  
• Echo Glyphs (more bindings than any other class)  
• Rote Spells (level-appropriate prepared cantrips) 

Recommended for players who enjoy preparation, tactical creativity, and playing the smartest person in the room.

## Class Features

### Hit Points

- **Hit Dice:** 1d6 per Wizard level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 6 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d6 (or 4) + your Constitution modifier per Wizard level after 1st

### Proficiencies

- **Armour:** None
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons
- **Tools:** None
- **Saving Throws:** Focus, Wisdom
- **Skills:** Choose two from Arcana, History, Gathering, Insight, Investigation, Medicine, Memory, Occult, Religion, and Resolve

### Starting Equipment

- (a) a quarterstaff or (b) a dagger
- (a) a component pouch or (b) an arcane focus
- (a) a scholar's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
- A spellbook with your known cantrips and 1st-circle spells

You begin with **4d4 × 10 gold pieces** (average 50 gp) for additional supplies or spell transcription.

> **Weapon Mastery:** Wizards do not gain Weapon Mastery.

---

## Quick Build

1. **Ability Scores:** Make Intelligence your highest score, followed by Constitution.
2. **Lineage:** Any combination; High Elf, Gnome, and Human are thematic.
3. **Background:** Sage, Scholar, or any background with Arcana or History.
4. **Combat Strategy:** Prepare a mix of control (crowd effects, battlefield reshaping) and utility. Save your highest-level slots for decisive moments. Use Arcane Recovery to extend your resource pool through long days.

## Core Class Features

### Spellbook (1st Level)

Your spellbook contains all the spells you know. At 1st level, it holds six 1st-circle Arcane spells of your choice. When you find a spell scroll or another wizard's spellbook, you can add those spells to your book (2 hours and 50 gp per spell level). You start with all cantrips you know in your book.

If you lose your spellbook, you can recreate it over a week by recalling spells from memory (recreating any spells you prepared that day at no cost; others require a DC 15 Intelligence check per spell level).

---

### Spellcasting (1st Level)

Intelligence is your spellcasting ability.

- **Spell Save DC** = 8 + PB + Intelligence modifier
- **Spell Attack Modifier** = PB + Intelligence modifier

You prepare a number of spells from your Spellbook equal to your **Intelligence modifier + Wizard level** (minimum 1). Preparing new spells requires 1 minute per spell circle and time equal to half the total circles prepared.

You regain all expended spell slots on a long rest. Ritual spells in your spellbook can be cast without spell slots (add 10 minutes to casting time).

---

### Arcane Recovery (1st Level)

Once per day when you finish a short rest, recover expended spell slots whose total circles equal **half your Wizard level** (rounded up, minimum 1). Slots recovered this way cannot be above 6th circle.

---

### Arcane Tradition (2nd Level, and 6th, 10th, 14th Level)

Choose a School of Arcane magic (Evocation, Abjuration, Illusion, Conjuration, Transmutation, Enchantment, Divination, Necromancy) or a thematic tradition. Your tradition grants features at 2nd, 6th, 10th, and 14th levels, plus advantages with spells from your school (such as half-cost transcription and free spells when you gain levels).

---

### Rote Spells (3rd Level)

You have memorised certain spells so thoroughly they require no preparation slot. Choose a number of 1st-circle spells from your spellbook equal to your **Intelligence modifier** (minimum 1). These spells are always prepared and do not count against your prepared spell total.

At 5th level, also choose one 2nd-circle Rote Spell. At 9th level, one 3rd-circle Rote Spell.

You can change your Rote Spells when you finish a long rest.

---

### Magic Sense (5th Level)

You have a persistent ability to read the arcane environment around you. You can detect active magical effects and enchanted objects within 30 feet as if permanently under a non-concentration *Detect Magic* effect. This sense cannot be suppressed except by an Antimagic Field.

When a creature within 60 feet casts a spell from the **Arcane spell list**, you automatically know what spell it is — no check required. For other traditions, make an Intelligence (Arcana) check (DC = 10 + the spell's circle).

**Momentum Interaction — Arcane Flow:** When you cast a Bender spell that successfully imposes a condition on an enemy or reduces an enemy to 0 HP, gain **+1 Momentum**. Your precise, calculated magic builds your combat rhythm.

---

### Arcane Echo Theory (9th Level)

Your mastery of the Weave allows you to bind more Echo Glyphs than any other caster. You may bind **a number of Echo Glyphs equal to your Intelligence modifier + your Proficiency Bonus** (rather than the standard limit). This represents spells so deeply understood that their Glyph resonances become natural extensions of your casting.

When you acquire a new spell, you may also attempt to learn its associated Echo Glyph during transcription (1 additional hour, DC 15 Intelligence (Arcana) check).

---

### Heroic Boon (10th Level)

Choose one:

**Focused Recall:** Once per long rest, as a bonus action, recover one expended spell slot of a level you could prepare (no higher than 5th circle). Your intense preparation means you always have one more thing in reserve.

**Arcane Surge:** Once per long rest, cast a spell you have prepared without expending a spell slot. The spell must be of a circle equal to or lower than half your Wizard level (rounded up).

---

### Spell Mastery (13th, 15th, and 18th Level)

You have achieved such mastery over certain spells that you can cast them with minimal effort.

**At 13th level:** Choose one 1st-circle spell in your spellbook. You can cast it at its lowest level without expending a spell slot. You can change this choice when you finish a long rest.

**At 15th level:** Choose one 2nd-circle spell in your spellbook. You can cast it at its lowest level without expending a spell slot. Changeable on long rest.

**At 18th level:** Choose one 3rd-circle spell in your spellbook. Castable at lowest level without a spell slot. Changeable on long rest.

These free castings use the spell's normal casting time and components.

---

### Epic Boon (20th Level)

Choose one:

**Arcane Mastery:** Add four Arcane spells from any circle to your spellbook (they don't need to be on the Wizard spell list). Prepare these spells freely — they don't count against your prepared spell total. Their spell slots are always available to you and don't expend from your normal pool (once per spell per long rest).

**Signature Spell:** Choose two spells of 3rd circle or lower. These spells are always prepared, cost no spell slots, and can be cast at their lowest level freely. Once per long rest per spell, you may cast either spell at a higher level by expending a slot normally.

## Last Stand Options

The Wizard's Last Stand reflects their mastery of arcane knowledge, whether unleashing catastrophic force, gifting power to allies, or rewriting the rules of the magical space around them.

**Rules for Last Stand:** Free action when reduced to 0 HP, rolling a death save, or dying outright. Character permanently dies once resolved.

---

### 1. The Final Theorem

*You complete the equation you've been working since the moment you first opened a spellbook.*

**Effect:**
- Cast any spell from your spellbook, regardless of whether it is prepared and regardless of its circle, without expending a spell slot or any components.
- The spell is cast as if using the highest possible spell slot (9th circle).
- If the spell requires concentration, you maintain it for up to 5 minutes after death.

---

### 2. Arcane Legacy

*Your knowledge does not die with you. It distributes.*

**Effect:**
- Choose a number of allies equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1) within 60 feet.
- Each ally receives a copy of your mental knowledge of any spells from your spellbook you choose to share. They can cast these spells once each within the next 24 hours as if they were prepared spells of their own class (using your spell save DC and attack modifier).
- The total circle levels distributed cannot exceed your Intelligence modifier × your Proficiency Bonus.

---

### 3. Null Field

*Magic is the study of rules. You are ending the rules.*

**Effect:**
- Create a **60-foot radius Null Field** centred on yourself for 5 minutes.
- Within the Null Field: all spells of 5th circle or lower are automatically counterspelled (no roll required); creatures must make an INT saving throw (DC = 8 + PB + INT modifier) to successfully cast any spell of 6th circle or higher; concentration spells cast by hostile creatures within the field end immediately.
- Your allies can still cast spells normally within the Null Field.
- When the Null Field ends, it collapses with a burst dealing **10d10 force damage** to all hostile creatures within the area (INT save for half).

| Last Stand | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| **The Final Theorem** | Cast any spellbook spell at 9th level without a slot; concentrate beyond death for 5 minutes. | One perfect, decisive spell. |
| **Arcane Legacy** | Distribute prepared spell knowledge to allies for 24 hours. | Long-form campaign legacy. |
| **Null Field** | 60-foot zone counterspells all spells ≤5th for 5 minutes; collapses for 10d10 force damage. | Shutting down enemy spellcasters. |

## Class Reactions

Wizards have access to reactions that leverage their deep arcane knowledge. These reactions use your **Proficiency Bonus pool**, refreshing on short or long rest.

### Arcane Deflection (2nd Level)

**Trigger:** You are hit by a spell or magical effect.
**Effect:** Halve the damage. You cannot cast levelled spells on your next turn after using this feature.

---

### Counterspell Instinct (5th Level)

**Trigger:** You see a creature within 60 feet begin casting a spell.
**Effect:** If you have *counterspell* prepared, you may cast it as a reaction, expending your reaction and a spell slot normally. This does not cost a Class Reaction pool use — your ability to identify spells via Magic Sense makes this reaction instantaneous.

---

### Arcane Rebound (10th Level)

**Trigger:** You succeed on a saving throw against a hostile spell that targets only you.
**Effect:** Spend 1 Class Reaction use. The caster must make a saving throw against their own spell's effects, as if they were the target, using their own save DC. On a failed save, they suffer the spell's effects.
