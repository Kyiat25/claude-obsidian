# Orc
## Lineage

> *"They gave us laws. We broke them. They offered peace. We spat it back. They called us beasts. So we made the name sacred."*  
> — **Tarn Griefscar**, First Warchanter of the Scarbound

You are Orc. Not beast, not savage—**tested**. Your people do not kneel before gods but stand beside them as equals earned through endurance. Where others see violence, you see sacred challenge. Where they see scars, you see **the Ledger of your Line**—each mark a word, each wound a truth that cannot be forgotten.

You were not born to be loved. You were born to **endure, to test, and to pass on honor greater than what you inherited**. Your ancestors carved their names into reality through trials that should have destroyed them. Now you carry that weight, and one day—if you are worthy—you will add your own scar to the chain.

---

## The Scarred Line

According to the **Ledger of the Scarred Line**, Orcs were not created by gods—they were **tested by them**. Every deity once tried to conquer, silence, or twist your people. What other races call blessings, Orcs call challenges. What they view as divine guidance, you see as trials to be beaten into submission.

Your people believe that **all gods are enemies first**, and anything earned through worship must first be taken through struggle. The names you give the gods are not their true names—they are **Orc-earned titles**, forged in conflict and paid for in blood:

- **Grak-Thul, The First Scar** (the fire god who tried to burn you into obedience)
- **Ugra-Mara, The Ash-Mother** (the earth goddess who tried to swallow you back into rot)
- **Korrun, The Iron-Tongue** (the spirit god who ignored you until you screamed your names in blood)
- **Varkhul, The Wound That Laughs** (the air god who mocked your pain—so you weaponized joy)

This is not blasphemy—it is **holy truth**. Gods are not above mortals. They are adversaries who must be tested, resisted, and understood through the scars they carve into the world.

## Life Among the Scars

You were raised knowing that **honor is the only true bloodline**, that glory must be counted and stored like gold, that the strongest clans are those with unspent honor where each generation is richer in name than the last.

**Honour (zakkur)** is not metaphorical. It is:
- Counted by deeds, titles, scars, and victories
- Stored in names, scars, stories, and the strength of your lineage
- Traded only once: when a child inherits a parent's legacy

Your lifespan is roughly **80 years**—short compared to elves or dwarves, but your people measure time differently. You count not in years but in **Cycles** (42-year periods marked by the Scar-Moon's eclipse), and you measure worth not in age but in **scars survived and trials endured**.

Every major deed is recorded in **Scar Scrolls**—etched hide or stone records of your clan's most enduring accomplishments, preserved in places of cold, stone, and silence.

---

## Physical Characteristics

**Age:** You mature quickly, reaching adulthood by age 14 (the age of the Trial of Line-Claiming for Half-Orcs). Few Orcs die of old age—most die in challenge, in defense of their Line, or by choice when they can no longer carry their name's weight.

**Size:** You stand between 6 to 7 feet tall, with powerful builds designed for endurance and strength. Your size is Medium.

**Speed:** Your base walking speed is 30 feet. Your body is built for relentless pursuit, not sprinting.

**Build:** Heavily muscled, with thick skin that bears scars proudly. Your body is a living record of your trials, each mark telling a story to those who can read the language of scars.

## Orc Traits

As an Orc, you possess the following lineage traits:

### **Darkvision**
You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

### **Relentless Endurance**
When you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, you can drop to 1 hit point instead. You can't use this feature again until you finish a long rest.

This is the signature trait of your people—**you do not fall easily**. Other races might collapse, but Orcs stand through wounds that should kill them, driven by the weight of the names they carry.

> *"He did not win the duel—but he stood for five strikes after his chest was opened. His blood bought us a hundred years of respect."*

### **Powerful Build**
You count as one size larger when determining your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag, or lift.

Your strength is not merely physical—it represents the weight of honor, legacy, and trials your people carry across generations.

### **Aggressive** *(Combat Trait)*
As a bonus action, you can move up to your speed toward an enemy of your choice that you can see or hear. You must end this move closer to the enemy than you started.

This represents the Orcish approach to challenges: **face them directly, without hesitation**. You do not retreat from tests—you close the distance.

### **Scar-Reader**
You have proficiency in the Insight skill. When you observe another creature's scars or wounds (including healed scars), you have advantage on Insight checks to understand their combat experience, pain tolerance, and whether they've faced significant challenges.

This ability reflects the Orcish cultural practice of reading history written in flesh.

### **Challenge-Tongue**
You can speak, read, and write Orcish. Additionally, when you speak your true name or the name of your ancestors in Orcish, you may add your proficiency bonus to Intimidation or Performance checks, as the weight of those names carries power.

## Mental Attributes & Psychology

**Focus Modifier:** +0  
**Sanity Modifier:** +1

Your biology grants you **exceptional mental resilience**. While your Focus is standard, your Sanity receives a bonus because Orcish culture trains you from birth to process fear, pain, and horror as **tactical information** rather than emotional overwhelm.

You are taught to face the Afterchallenge, to endure what should break lesser minds, to stand when standing should be impossible. This isn't cultural—it's bred into your very bones through generations of surviving trials that should have destroyed your people.

### **Cultural Mental Conditioning**
Beyond your biological advantage, Orcish upbringing creates:

- **Unwavering resolve:** You are trained to complete challenges once accepted
- **Pain as teacher:** Suffering is recontextualized as sacred rather than avoided
- **Death without fear:** You expect the Afterchallenge and prepare for it
- **Lineage pressure:** The weight of ancestors watching creates intense focus

### **Corruption Vulnerability: The Flame-False**

As an Orc, you have a unique relationship with Corruption. Your connection to the Scarred Line and the weight of ancestral memory provides some protection, but corruption that enters through **broken oaths, dishonored scars, or fractured lineages** affects you catastrophically.

**When you gain Corruption Score 10+ (Tier 2), you suffer:**
- Fractured Memory: Your Scar Scrolls become harder to recall accurately
- Name-Weight: Speaking your full name causes physical pain
- Oath-Strain: Keeping promises requires Wisdom saving throws

**Fractured Scar Table (Corruption Tier 2+)**

When you complete a long rest at Corruption Score 10+, roll 1d6:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **False Honor:** You remember a deed you never performed. Make a DC 15 Wisdom save or believe it's real and add it to your personal ledger. |
| 2 | **Ancestral Static:** You hear your ancestors screaming instead of guiding. You cannot benefit from Relentless Endurance until your next long rest. |
| 3 | **Scarline Fracture:** Your scars begin to fade or distort. Take 1d4 psychic damage and lose proficiency in one skill until your next long rest. |
| 4 | **Weight of Lies:** You feel the crushing weight of dishonor you haven't committed. Make a DC 12 Sanity save or lose 1d4 Sanity. |
| 5 | **Eldritch Touch:** Something tries to rewrite your ledger. Gain 1 Whisper Point but your next Intimidation check using Challenge-Tongue fails automatically. |
| 6 | **Scarline Holds:** Your ancestral connection resists corruption this night. Reduce your Corruption Score by 1 (minimum 10). |

## Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Integration

### **Evasion Profile: Enduring Juggernaut**
- **Dodge Evasion:** +0 (your large frame doesn't dodge easily)
- **Endure Evasion:** +2 (dense muscle, thick bones, biological pain tolerance)
- **Deflect Evasion:** +0 (you take hits head-on rather than deflecting)

**Tactical Note:** Your biology is built for **withstanding punishment**. Dense muscle tissue, thicker bones, higher pain threshold—you're naturally tougher than other races. This is innate, not trained. You don't dodge because your frame isn't built for it; you endure because your body can take what would kill others.

### **Natural Damage Reduction:** +1
Your thick hide and dense muscle provide inherent protection. This stacks with armor.

### **Combat Philosophy**
Orcs fight with **relentless aggression and calculated endurance**. You close distance rapidly (Aggressive), withstand punishment (Relentless Endurance + high Endure Evasion), and leverage strength (Powerful Build) to control the battlefield. You don't dance around enemies—you **run through them**.

### **Breaking Point Mechanics**
Due to your cultural conditioning and biological resilience, you have **advantage on Rally checks** and enemy forces must achieve higher casualty percentages to trigger your Breaking Point. You are exceptionally difficult to rout.

## Languages

**Languages:** You can speak, read, and write Common and Orcish.

**Orcish** is a guttural, powerful language where tone and volume carry as much meaning as words. Names spoken in Orcish carry weight—literally, in Orcish belief. The language has extensive vocabulary for:
- Honor, shame, and trial
- Types of scars and wounds
- Ancestral relationships and lineage
- Challenge and endurance

When Orcs speak of sacred matters, they often switch to Orcish even if others don't understand, because some truths **can only be properly spoken in the tongue of scars**.

## Orc Subraces

While all Orcs share the Scarred Line, different clans have specialized over generations through their founding challenges. Choose one of the following subraces:

### **Graathurn Lineage** *(Silent Endurers)*
You descend from the clan founded by **Graathurn the Stone-Throat**, who endured seven days of torture without speaking. His silence saved his clan, and your people carry that gift.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 2, and your Constitution score increases by 1.

**Stone Silence:** You have advantage on saving throws against effects that would force you to speak or compel you to reveal information (such as *command* or *suggestion* spells targeting speech).

**Enduring Flesh:** When you use your Relentless Endurance trait, you gain temporary hit points equal to your Constitution modifier (minimum 1).

**Graathurn's Legacy:** Once per long rest, you can enter a state of absolute focus by taking 1 minute of meditation. For the next hour, you have advantage on Constitution saving throws and checks.

### **Rhazgarn Lineage** *(Ash-Walkers)*
You descend from the clan founded by **Rhazgarn**, who carried a dying child through wildfire. Your people are protectors of the weak and keepers of the forge-flame.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 2, and your Wisdom score increases by 1.

**Born of Flame:** You have resistance to fire damage. This represents generations of forge-keepers and flame-walkers in your bloodline.

**Protector's Resolve:** When an ally within 5 feet of you is hit by an attack, you can use your reaction to impose disadvantage on that attack roll. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, regaining all uses when you finish a long rest.

**Ash-Walk Heritage:** You ignore difficult terrain created by ash, embers, or burned ground, and you have advantage on saving throws against effects caused by extreme heat (but not fire damage).

### **Korruk'tal Lineage** *(Puzzle-Crackers)*
You descend from **Korruk'tal**, who solved the riddle of an ancestral tomb. Your people value **intelligence as much as strength**, recognizing that wisdom is a weapon like any other.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 2, and your Intelligence score increases by 1.

**Riddle-Blood:** You have proficiency in one of the following skills: Arcana, History, or Investigation.

**Strategic Mind:** You can use your Intelligence modifier instead of your Strength modifier for attack and damage rolls with weapons that have the thrown property.

**Bone-Puzzle Insight:** Once per long rest, when you fail an Intelligence check, you can choose to reroll it, taking the second result.

### **Velgra-Morn Lineage** *(The Returning)*
You descend from **Velgra-Morn**, founder of the Clan of the Returning Flame, who specializes in redemption and second chances. Your people understand that strength includes mercy.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 2, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Redemption's Touch:** You gain proficiency in Medicine or Persuasion (your choice). When you stabilize a creature with a Medicine check, they regain 1 hit point.

**Second Flame:** When a creature you can see within 30 feet fails a death saving throw, you can use your reaction to allow them to reroll it. Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

**Forgiveness Rites:** You have advantage on Charisma checks made to persuade someone to give another creature a second chance or to accept redemption.

## Naming Conventions

Orc names carry **immense weight**. They are not casual labels but **ledger-marks**, **scarred truths**, and **measured worth**. Your name tells your story to those who understand the language of honor.

### **Structure:**
- **Given Name:** Your personal identifier, often harsh and direct
- **Earned Epithet:** A title reflecting your greatest trial or defining scar (earned after first major challenge)
- **Clan Name:** Your lineage designation (may include generation markers)

### **Examples:**

**Male Names:**
- Grath, Thok, Durn, Korak, Urgak, Varuk, Morrak, Druvak

**Female Names:**
- Yarga, Ugra, Morna, Rhaza, Velgra, Korva, Tharka, Grakka

**Earned Epithets:**
- Ash-Eyes (blinded by fire but survived)
- Stone-Throat (endured torture in silence)
- Iron-Tongue (spoke truth that should have killed them)
- Bone-Chain (carried ancestors' remains to safety)
- Gray-Scar (chose mercy as strength)
- Fire-Stood (withstood flame)
- Wound-Laughs (found joy in pain)

**Clan Names:**
- Graathurn, Rhazgarn, Korruk'tal, Velgra-Morn, Vorgrath, Durnhalm, Marrak-Vorr

**Full Name Examples:**
- **Thok Bone-Chain Graathurn** (male who completed the Trial of the Bone-Chain)
- **Ugra Fire-Stood Rhazgarn** (female who walked through flame)
- **Druvak Ash-Eyes** (male who founded a new clan through sacrifice)
- **Velgra Wound-Mother** (female who absorbed corruption to protect her line)

### **Name Earning:**
- Children receive only a given name and clan name at birth
- The **epithet** is earned during adolescence (around the Trial of Line-Claiming for Half-Orcs)
- Some Orcs earn multiple epithets over their lifetime—choosing which to use based on context
- To use an epithet you haven't earned is **grave dishonor**, potentially leading to exile or name-burning

## Cultural Touchstones

### **The Path of the First Scar**
All Orcs are raised within this faith. Even if you've left your clan or rejected tradition, its lessons shaped you:

- Every god has tried to kill us—so we made gods of the wounds they left
- Honor is the only true bloodline
- To waste honor on petty pride is to steal from your descendants
- The highest honor is not to win, but to endure the test that should have broken you
- Death is not the end—only the gate to the longest challenge

### **The Scarred Law**
These foundational truths guide Orcish life:

1. **Honor Is the First Inheritance** - A child is born with nothing but a name; that name must carry the Line's weight
2. **Worth of Deed Is Measured by Challenge** - Easy victories are empty; hardest battles buy deepest scars
3. **To Lie of Deed Is to Shatter the Line** - Falsify glory and your children inherit dust
4. **Each Scar Is a Word in the Ledger** - Wounds endured are stories earned
5. **Death Is Not the End—If the Line Endures** - Only those who leave nothing behind are truly gone

### **Scar-Wardens**
The **Scar-Wardens** are judges, executioners, memory-keepers, and death-guides. They belong to no clan, answering only to the truth of the Ledger and the weight of the Line. When a Scar-Warden arrives, their judgment is **final**. 

To attack a Scar-Warden is not merely crime—it is an attempt to break the memory of the Line itself.

## Playing an Orc

### **Core Fantasy:**
You are the immovable object, the trial that walks, the living ledger of ancestral honor. You don't dodge or dance—you **endure**. Your scars are not shame but scripture, each one a word in the sacred text of your people's survival.

### **Mechanical Identity:**
- **Tank Specialist:** High Endure Evasion + Relentless Endurance + natural DR
- **Aggressive Closer:** Use Aggressive to reach enemies quickly
- **Strength Primary:** Powerful Build enhances carrying capacity and grappling
- **Mental Fortress:** +1 Sanity makes you resistant to fear and horror effects
- **Last Stand Master:** Relentless Endurance creates dramatic "won't stay down" moments

### **Roleplaying Hooks:**
- **The Debt-Bearer:** Carrying honor-debt from a fallen parent or mentor
- **The Exile Seeker:** Searching for a clan member who was name-burned
- **The Trial-Walker:** Adventuring specifically to earn enough honor to start a new clan line
- **The Scar-Reader:** Using your ability to read others' combat history
- **The Bridge-Builder:** Trying to explain Orcish honor to those who don't understand
- **The Weight-Carrier:** Struggling under the pressure of a legendary ancestor's name
- **The Redemption-Giver:** Velgra-Morn lineage offering second chances

### **Party Dynamics:**
Other races often misunderstand Orcs as violent or savage. This creates opportunities for:

- **Education through action** - Show honor through deeds, not words
- **Quiet respect** - Let your scars and endurance speak for you
- **Cultural exchange** - Explain why you don't dodge, why scars matter, why challenge is sacred
- **Surprising wisdom** - Korruk'tal lineage can shock with tactical brilliance
- **Protective instinct** - Rhazgarn lineage naturally shields the weak

### **Common Character Arcs:**
- From honor-obsessed to understanding different forms of strength
- From solitary trial-walker to found family
- From shame-bearer to line-founder
- From violence as tool to violence as last resort
- From rigid tradition to adapted honor
- From name-hungry to name-earned

## Mechanical Considerations

### **Optimal Classes:**
Your abilities synergize excellently with:

- **Barbarian:** Relentless Endurance + Rage = nearly unkillable berserker
- **Fighter:** Powerful Build + Second Wind + Action Surge for extended combat
- **Paladin:** Strength focus + Sanity bonus + Lay on Hands creates durable holy warrior
- **Cleric:** Wisdom-based casting + high Sanity + natural tankiness
- **Monk (certain builds):** Wisdom synergy + Aggressive for mobility

### **Strong But Less Traditional:**
- **Bard:** Challenge-Tongue creates powerful intimidation-based bards
- **Wizard:** Korruk'tal lineage Intelligence bonus + "muscle wizard" concept
- **Rogue:** Scar-Reader + Sneak Attack creates unconventional rogues
- **Ranger:** Strength ranger builds work well with Orcish traits

### **Multiclass Synergies:**
- **Barbarian/Fighter:** Relentless Endurance means you keep raging longer
- **Fighter/Paladin:** Smite after Aggressive movement for devastating charges
- **Cleric/Fighter:** Battle-priest who won't stay down
- **Barbarian/Druid:** Primal warrior attuned to nature's trials

## Integration with Mental Systems

### **Focus & Sanity:**
Your **+1 Sanity** is significant:
- Advantage on fear effects becomes even stronger
- Breaking Points are harder to trigger
- Mental attacks are less effective
- Horror and trauma are processed as "just another trial"

**Typical Orc Mental Strategy:**
- Use high Sanity to resist battlefield panic
- Frame suffering as sacred rather than traumatic
- Relentless Endurance prevents Focus loss from hit point depletion
- Ancestral weight provides purpose during stress

### **Corruption Concerns:**
Your unique corruption vulnerability centers on **dishonored scars and broken oaths**:
- Lying about achievements accelerates corruption
- Breaking sworn oaths deals corruption damage
- Abandoning clan duties creates corruption vulnerability
- At Tier 2+, the Fractured Scar table reflects Line-breaking

### **Rally & Morale:**
Orcs have natural advantages in morale systems:
- Advantage on Rally checks (cultural conditioning)
- Higher Breaking Point thresholds required
- Seeing an Orc use Relentless Endurance **inspires allies** (potential Rally bonus)
- Enemy forces become demoralized facing troops that won't fall

## Design Philosophy

The Orc lineage is designed to support:

1. **The Unstoppable Tank** (highest Endure Evasion + Relentless Endurance)
2. **The Honor-Driven Warrior** (Challenge-Tongue + Scar-Reader create RP depth)
3. **The Cultural Bridge** (Sanity bonus + Scar-Reader help explain your people)
4. **The Surprising Sage** (Korruk'tal lineage for Intelligence-based Orcs)
5. **The Protector** (Rhazgarn lineage for defensive builds)

Your mechanical abilities (Relentless Endurance, Powerful Build, Aggressive, high Endure Evasion) create a **juggernaut that refuses to fall**. You're designed to be in melee, taking punishment, and creating dramatic "last stand" moments that inspire allies.

The subrace options allow you to lean into different clan traditions—from silent endurers (Graathurn) to strategic thinkers (Korruk'tal) to merciful redeemers (Velgra-Morn)—while maintaining the core Orcish identity of trial-tested honor.

## Final Notes

### **The Weight of Names:**
Every Orc carries ancestral pressure. Your character likely knows:
- At least 5 generations of ancestors by name and deed
- The founding challenge of their clan
- Multiple sacred sites by reputation
- The names of legendary Scar-Wardens

This isn't burden—it's **scaffolding**. You stand on the shoulders of those who endured before you.

### **Respect Across Cultures:**
Modern Dór-o Estel recognizes Orcish honor:
- Dwarves respect the parallel to their own craft-legacy
- Elves acknowledge the parallel to their memory-binding
- Humans admire the clarity compared to political betrayal
- Even the Faith of the Bound Pattern calls upon Scar-Wardens when justice must be absolute

### **The Afterchallenge:**
Your people believe death is **transition into the greatest test**. The worthy dead become Ancestral Watchers, testing newcomers and protecting their Line. Some even return as **Challenge-Echos**—spirit-bound reflections that appear in times of crisis.

You adventure knowing your ancestors watch, judge, and wait to test you when you fall. This is not pressure to fear—it is **certainty** that your deeds matter beyond death.
