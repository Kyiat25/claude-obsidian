# Scarred Magic

_Style of Magic_

**"Every mark is a memory. Every memory is a weapon."**

Scarred Magic is born of pain, honour, and ancestral defiance. Practiced almost exclusively among the [[orc]] clans and those who survive by trial, it is a magic that does not come from study or bloodline—but from **earned suffering**. Its spells are carved into the flesh, the soul, or the memory of the land, echoing through generations.

Where most magic flows like wind or light, Scarred Magic is **branded** into existence. Each scar is a lesson, a rite, or a burden willingly claimed. Those who practice it call themselves **Woundbinders**, **Painwrights**, or **Echo-Bloods**, and their bodies are both spellbooks and battlefield.

---
## Key Themes

- **Earned Power**: Spells are tied to physical wounds, emotional trauma, or legacy scars.
- **Living Spellbook**: Magic is remembered through pain. Each scar represents a spell or rite.
- **Defiant Tradition**: Rooted in survival, resistance, and familial duty.
- **Memory Anchoring**: Spells carry echoes of the caster’s ancestors, often manifesting as voices or visions.

---

## Cultural Roots

### 🩸 **Orcish Scar-Wardens**

- Receive new scars through trials of pain or combat.
- Each scar tells a true story, passed down and reforged.

### 🎨 **Stonecarvers of the Shattered Peaks**

- Blend scarification and tattoo-glyphs with chants of remembrance.
- Consider forgetting a spell a personal dishonour.

### 🕯️ **Ash-Walkers**

- Wanderers who bind ash, ink, and scar in traveling rites.
- Their scars can glow or bleed when specific spells activate.

---
## Scarred Casting Features

- **Pain Sigil**: You may suffer 1 HP per spell level to cast without verbal or somatic components.
- **Wound Echo**: Once per long rest, when reduced to half HP or lower, immediately cast a scarred spell without using a slot.
- **Brand the Glyph**: A creature you damage with a spell takes additional psychic damage equal to your proficiency bonus if the spell is scar-inscribed.

---
## Echo Glyph Integration

Scarred casters generate **pain-etched echoes** that remain even after healing:

- **Echo of the First Scar**: After casting a spell with HP cost, gain +2 DR until the start of your next turn.
- **Blood-Memory Rune**: A spell you cast that reduces an enemy to 0 HP leaves a faint glyph on your body. Once per day, you may recast it without using a slot.
- **Chain of Scars**: If you cast the same scarred spell two turns in a row, it cannot be counterspelled.

## Social Perception

- **Respected Among Orcs**: Scarred casters are honoured, their stories sung.
- **Feared by Outsiders**: Seen as masochistic or barbaric.
- **Overseen by the Faith**: The **Faith of the Bound Pattern** treats this magic as holy when bound to ancestral truth, but dangerous if misused.

---
## Optional Mechanics

- **Scarmark Memory ([[Feat]])**: Gain 1 extra known spell that may only be used when you take damage that round.
- **Blood-Trail Invocation**: While below half HP, deal +1 damage per spell level on spells that damage.
- **Legacy Scar ([[Downtime]])**: Craft a personal scar-rite that anchors a spell into your identity. Can be invoked even when silenced or stunned.

---
## Design Notes

Scarred Magic is deeply **personal** and **ancestral**. It asks the caster to **remember**, to **endure**, and to **earn**. Unlike Voidwrought Weaving (which shatters), or Blood Magic (which flows), Scarred Magic **brands**. It is a spellbook written in pain, a testament to survival, and a rite of defiant memory passed on through skin and soul.
