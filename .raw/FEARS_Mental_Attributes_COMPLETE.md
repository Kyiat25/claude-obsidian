## 1. CORE PHILOSOPHY

### 1.1 Design Intent

In FEARS, the mind is both weapon and fortress. Three mental attributes govern your character's interaction with magic, madness, and metaphysical horror:

- **Focus** = Mental clarity and precision under pressure
- **Sanity** = Psychological stability against existential terror
- **Corruption** = Accumulation of eldritch contamination

**These are not punitive mechanics.** They are:
- **Pressure gauges** that reward strategic play
- **Tactical resources** that create meaningful choices
- **Narrative drivers** that deepen roleplay
- **Warning systems** that telegraph danger before disaster

### 1.2 Core Design Principles

**For Players:**
- Clear thresholds indicate when you're in danger
- Multiple recovery paths exist
- You control your exposure to risk
- High risk yields high reward

**For GMs:**
- Easy to track and adjudicate
- Creates natural narrative tension
- Enables targeted threats and counterplay
- Scales with character progression

### 1.3 The Three-Pillar System

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                         │
│  FOCUS    →  Short-term mental control  │
│  SANITY   →  Long-term mental integrity │
│  CORRUPTION → Cumulative spiritual rot  │
│                                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**How They Interact:**
- **Focus failure** in dangerous situations → triggers Sanity checks
- **Sanity failure** in corrupted environments → gains Corruption
- **High Corruption** → makes Focus and Sanity harder to maintain
- **All three at critical levels** → catastrophic consequences

## 2. FOCUS: THE MIND'S BLADE

### 2.1 What Focus Represents

> *"Steel cuts flesh. Fire scars the world. But Focus? Focus cuts through chaos."*

**Focus** is mental precision—your ability to:
- Maintain concentration under pressure
- Control unstable magical forces
- Resist mental intrusion and illusion
- Execute complex spells in combat
- Operate glyphs safely

**Focus is active discipline.** Where Sanity is your defense against horror, Focus is the sword you wield in its face.

### 2.2 Focus Score Calculation

**Focus Score = Base + Modifiers (Range: 8-20)**

#### Starting Focus

**Rolled Generation:**
- Roll 3d6 for Focus (8-18 range)
- Heroic option: Roll 4d6, drop lowest (3-18 range)

**Point Buy:**
- Start at 10 (baseline human discipline)
- Add: **Primary ability modifier** (INT for Wizards, WIS for Clerics, CHA for Sorcerers, etc.)
- Add: **Constitution modifier** (physical endurance aids mental stamina)

**Formula: Focus = 10 + Primary Ability Modifier + Constitution Modifier**

#### Example Calculations

**Wizard (Intelligence 16, Constitution 14):**
- Base: 10
- INT modifier: +3
- CON modifier: +2
- **Total Focus: 15**

**Barbarian (Strength 18, Constitution 16, Wisdom 10):**
- Base: 10
- Primary (STR): +4
- CON modifier: +3
- **Total Focus: 17** (Barbarians have incredible mental fortitude)

**Sorcerer (Charisma 18, Constitution 10):**
- Base: 10
- CHA modifier: +4
- CON modifier: +0
- **Total Focus: 14**

### 2.3 Focus Modifier

**Focus Modifier = (Focus Score - 10) ÷ 2** (rounded down)

| Focus Score | Modifier |
|------------|----------|
| 8-9 | -1 |
| 10-11 | +0 |
| 12-13 | +1 |
| 14-15 | +2 |
| 16-17 | +3 |
| 18-19 | +4 |
| 20 | +5 |

### 2.4 Focus Thresholds

**Your Focus score determines your mental state:**

| Focus Score | State | Mechanical Effect |
|------------|------|-------------------|
| **13-20** | **Steady** | Normal operation |
| **9-12** | **Strained** | Disadvantage on Focus saves during active casting or chaos exposure |
| **5-8** | **Fragmenting** | Disadvantage on ALL Focus checks/saves; concentration breaks on any damage |
| **1-4** | **Splintered** | Auto-fail Wild Magic control; glyphs backlash on touch (DC 15 Sanity Save) |
| **0** | **Collapsed** | Cannot cast; must make DC 18 Sanity Save or gain 1d4 Corruption Score |

**Visual Indicators:**
- **Steady:** Calm, focused eyes; steady hands
- **Strained:** Furrowed brow; occasional tremor
- **Fragmenting:** Visible strain; shaking hands; sweat
- **Splintered:** Blank stare; incoherent muttering
- **Collapsed:** Total exhaustion; thousand-yard stare
