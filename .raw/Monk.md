# The Monk

**Masters of Inner Potential**

> *"The strike that ends the fight is not the fastest or the hardest. It is the one that arrives exactly when the enemy has nothing left to counter it."*

---

## Body as Instrument

No weapon. No armour. No gap between intention and action. She had been watching the fight since it began — not watching the blades, watching the spaces. The way a fighter's weight shifted a half-second before they committed. The moments between the attacks when they were most open. When she moved, it was into one of those spaces, and the rest was mathematics.

You are the Monk. Your power comes from understanding the body's rhythms — yours and your enemies'. **Ki Sense** tells you when a creature's vital energy destabilises before most would even know they were hurt. **Stunning Strike** exploits that destabilisation. **Flurry of Blows** means you are never waiting — you are always pressing.

In FEARS, the Monk has the cleanest Momentum engine of any class. **Balanced Rhythm** means missed attacks don't cost you your rhythm — you stay in flow even when individual strikes don't land. **Ki Flow** means maintaining +2 Momentum actively regenerates your Technique Points. You are perpetual motion.

---

## The Monk's Essence

You are a mobility and disruption specialist. You get in, you break the enemy's combat rhythm, and you use their disrupted state to press advantages no other class can. Against bloodied targets, your costs drop, your effects extend, and your damage surges.

You struggle with sustained ranged pressure and with fights where you cannot close the gap. You excel when you can choose your angle of engagement — and when you stay mobile.

---

## Complexity Assessment: MODERATE (5/10)        

 The Monk tracks:             
• Technique Points (regenerate on short rest)   
• Martial Arts die (damage and scaling)     
• Ki Sense range (passive awareness)    
• Momentum (Balanced Rhythm keeps it high)  
• Bloodied enemy interactions (reduced costs, enhanced effects)

Recommended for players who enjoy tactical positioning, resource-efficient combat, and fighting smartly.

## Class Features

### Hit Points

- **Hit Dice:** 1d8 per Monk level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 8 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per Monk level after 1st

### Proficiencies

- **Armour:** None
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons, shortswords (treated as monk weapons)
- **Tools:** One artisan's tool of your choice
- **Saving Throws:** Dexterity, Focus
- **Skills:** Choose two from Acrobatics, Athletics, Insight, Memory, Perception, Religion, Resolve, and Stealth

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) any simple weapon or (b) a shortsword
- (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
- 10 darts

---

## Quick Build

To quickly create a Monk, follow these steps:

1. **Ability Scores:** Make Dexterity your highest score, followed by Wisdom.
2. **Lineage and Heritage:** Any combination works; Human, Tiefling, and Elf are thematic but not required.
3. **Background:** Hermit, Far Traveller, or any background with Acrobatics or Insight.
4. **Combat Strategy:** Use Flurry of Blows to build pressure, Patient Defence when bloodied enemies attack, and Stunning Strike to break momentum at critical moments.

## Core Class Features

### Martial Arts (1st Level)

While unarmed or wielding only monk weapons and not wearing armour or a shield:

- **Unarmed Strike Damage:** Roll the Martial Arts die instead of normal damage.
- **Dexterity-Based Attacks:** Use Dexterity instead of Strength for attack and damage rolls with unarmed strikes and monk weapons.
- **Deflect Missiles:** As a reaction when hit by a ranged weapon attack, reduce the damage by 1d10 + DEX modifier + WIS modifier + Monk level. If reduced to 0, catch the missile and throw it back (proficiency bonus on attack roll, range 20/60, counts as monk weapon).
- **Bonus Attack:** When you take the Attack action with an unarmed strike or monk weapon, you may make one additional unarmed strike as a bonus action.

**Momentum Interaction — Balanced Rhythm:** You ignore the −1 Momentum penalty from missed attacks. Your flowing combat style maintains perfect rhythm even when strikes don't connect. Nat 1 crits still cost −2 Momentum. All other Momentum losses apply normally.

---

### Unarmoured Defence (1st Level)

While not wearing armour or wielding a shield, add your **Wisdom modifier** to all Evasion Rolls.

---

### Ki-Empowered Strikes (1st Level)

When you hit a bloodied creature (below 50% HP) with an unarmed strike:

- Deal bonus damage equal to your Martial Arts die.
- If the target has temporary hit points, reduce them by twice your Martial Arts die before dealing damage.
- Your strikes count as granting advantage for Sneak Attack purposes (if multiclassed).

At 10th level, attacks against bloodied creatures score critical hits on 19–20. At 17th level, on 18–20.

---

### Monk Techniques (2nd Level)

Your Technique Points fuel your manoeuvres. They equal your Monk level and replenish after a short or long rest (30 minutes of meditation).

**Technique Save DC:** 8 + PB + WIS modifier.

**Techniques Available:**
- **Flurry of Blows (1 TP):** After the Attack action, make two unarmed strikes as a bonus action. Against bloodied targets, make three strikes instead and reduce target speed by 10 feet per hit (cumulative). At 13th level, free once per short rest against bloodied targets.
- **Patient Defence (1 TP):** Take the Dodge action as a bonus action. Against bloodied targets, when they miss you due to Dodge disadvantage, spend 1 TP (no additional reaction) to make an unarmed strike with advantage — which can trigger Stunning Strike for free.
- **Step of the Wind (1 TP):** Take the Disengage or Dash action as a bonus action; double jump distance. Against bloodied targets, gain +15 feet movement and can teleport 15 feet through disrupted Ki flow.
- **Ki-Fueled Agility (1 TP):** Reroll a failed Evasion roll.

---

### Unarmoured Movement (2nd Level)

Your movement speed increases by 10 feet while not wearing armour or wielding a shield. This bonus increases as you gain levels as shown in the table.

---

### Ki Sense (2nd Level)

You can sense when any creature within 30 feet becomes bloodied — no action required, even through walls, darkness, and invisibility. You know their general direction and approximate distance. Range increases to 60 feet at 6th level, 90 feet at 11th level, and 120 feet at 17th level.

When you hit a creature with an unarmed strike, you learn whether it is above or below 50% HP.

---

### Ki Flow (2nd Level and 7th Level)

**2nd Level:** When you begin your turn at maximum Momentum (+3), immediately regain 1 Technique Point. Once per round.

**7th Level:** When you begin your turn at +2 or higher Momentum, regain 1 Technique Point. Once per turn.

---

### Monastery Subclass (3rd Level, and 7th, 11th, 15th Level)

Choose a subclass that reflects your martial and spiritual tradition, such as **Flickering Dark** or **Open Hand**. Your subclass grants features at 3rd, 7th, 11th, and 15th levels.

---

### Unarmoured Resilience (3rd Level)

While not wearing armour or wielding a shield, you gain DR 1. Increases to DR 2 at 9th level and DR 3 at 15th level.

This DR applies before calculating Injury thresholds and helps compensate for the vulnerability of an unarmoured combatant.

---

### Multiattack (5th Level)

When you take the Attack action, you can make two attacks instead of one.

---

### Stunning Strike (5th Level)

When you hit with an unarmed strike, spend 1 Technique Point to attempt to stun the target. The target makes a Constitution saving throw (DC = Technique Save DC) or is stunned until the end of your next turn.

**Against Bloodied Targets:**
- Costs 0 Technique Points (disrupted Ki makes them vulnerable).
- Save DC increases by +2.
- On a failed save, the target is stunned for 2 rounds total.
- Even on a successful save, the target has disadvantage on their next attack roll.

At 13th level, Stunning Strike against bloodied targets can affect creatures normally immune to stun (they save with advantage rather than being immune). At 17th level, bloodied targets below 25% HP make the save at disadvantage.

---

### Empowered Strikes (6th Level)

Your unarmed strikes count as magical for overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical damage. Against constructs and undead, add your Martial Arts die in force damage. Against bloodied creatures, your unarmed strikes deal an additional 1d10 force damage.

---

### Improved Flurry of Blows (6th Level)

When using Flurry of Blows, the second strike can critically hit on a 19–20.

---

### Perfect Motion (9th Level)

- **Fall Resistance:** Use your reaction to reduce falling damage by 5 × Monk level.
- **Fluid Movement:** Move along vertical surfaces and across liquids without falling during your turn.
- **Liquid Grace:** Once per short rest, ignore difficult terrain and move through enemy spaces without provoking opportunity attacks.

---

### Ki Flow Disruption (9th Level)

When a bloodied creature within your Ki Sense range moves, spend 1 Technique Point as a reaction to force a Constitution saving throw (DC = Technique Save DC). On failure, their movement speed becomes 0 for the rest of their turn. On success, their movement speed is halved. This works even against Dash or Disengage.

---

### Heroic Boon (10th Level)

Choose one:

**Purity of Body:** Immune to disease and poison. When you drop to 0 HP while bloodied creatures are within your Ki Sense range, immediately regain HP equal to your remaining Technique Points + (the number of bloodied creatures within range × 5). Once per long rest.

**Purity of Mind:** Advantage on Wisdom saving throws. As a bonus action, end the charmed or frightened condition on yourself. You can sense the emotional state of bloodied creatures within Ki Sense range — desperate, enraged, terrified, determined, or broken.

---

### Astral Teachings (13th Level)

Spend 2 Technique Points to gain temporary proficiency in one language, skill, tool, or weapon until incapacitated or this feature is used again. Spend 3 Technique Points to grant proficiency to an ally within 30 feet for 1 hour.

---

### Meridian Strike (13th Level)

**Cost:** 3 Technique Points. As part of an Attack action against a bloodied creature.

Make one unarmed strike with advantage. On hit, the target makes a Constitution save (DC = Technique Save DC + 2). On failure, choose two effects: Ki Seal (no legendary actions or reactions until end of your next turn), Meridian Lock (speed 0 until end of your next turn), Vital Disruption (disadvantage on attacks until end of your next turn), or Energy Drain (lose one recharged ability use). On a successful save, apply one effect of your choice.

If used on a bloodied, stunned target, the save automatically fails.

---

### Serene Focus (13th Level)

Once per short rest, when you suffer a Minor Injury, ignore all of its effects until the end of your next long rest. The injury remains and must still be healed normally.

---

### Diamond Soul (14th Level)

Gain proficiency in all saving throws. Spend 1 Technique Point to reroll a failed save, adding 1d4 to the result.

Against bloodied attackers, you automatically gain +1d4 to saving throws (no TP cost). If you succeed on a save against a bloodied creature's ability, you can reflect the effect back at them (they must make the same save).

---

### Deadeye Strike (15th Level)

Once per turn when you make a Called Shot, reduce the penalty by an amount equal to your Dexterity modifier (minimum reduction of 1).

---

### Timeless Self (17th Level)

You no longer age, require food or water, or suffer penalties from magical aging. Your ability scores and maximum HP cannot be reduced by any means short of a *wish* spell.

---

### Empty Body (18th Level)

Spend 4 Technique Points to become invisible for 1 minute and gain resistance to all damage except force. While invisible, your attacks deal an additional 1d10 force damage.

Against bloodied creatures while invisible: force damage increases to 2d10; they cannot sense you with magical detection; your Ki Sense range extends to 240 feet.

Alternatively, spend 8 Technique Points to cast *astral projection* (self only) without components.

---

### Epic Boon (20th Level)

Choose one:

**Boundless Technique:** Regain all Technique Points when you roll initiative. If you start a turn with no Technique Points, regain 2 automatically at +3 Momentum. If any creatures are already bloodied when initiative is rolled, gain +4 temporary Technique Points (can exceed maximum) usable only against bloodied creatures.

**Enlightened Mastery:** Once per turn, use a Technique without spending Technique Points. When you use Flurry of Blows, make three unarmed strikes instead of two. When you roll initiative with fewer than 4 TP remaining, regain TP equal to half your Monk level (rounded up). When you reduce a bloodied creature to 0 HP, immediately regain 2 Technique Points (can trigger multiple times per turn).

## Last Stand Options

A Monk's Last Stand reflects their mastery of Ki, discipline, and spiritual transcendence.

**Rules for Last Stand:** Invoked when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, roll a death saving throw, or die outright. A free action requiring no reaction or consciousness. Once resolved, the character permanently dies.

---

### 1. Empty Sun Technique

*Hand to hand, soul for soul — the forbidden strikes that expend your Ki for good.*

**Effect:**
- Move up to your speed.
- Deliver two critical hits to one target within melee range.
- Target makes a Wisdom saving throw (DC = 12 + PB + WIS modifier).
- On failure: placed under *imprisonment* (Slumber effect). Your dead body is the special component — cannot be removed or dispelled.
- This saving throw cannot be bypassed by Legendary Resistance.

---

### 2. Into the Mist

*You surrender your body in a self-immolating flourish that ferries your comrades to safety.*

**Effect:**
- Your physical form evaporates into steam, smoke, coloured powder, or shadow.
- Allies within 100 feet may teleport to any location where they took a long rest within the past week, or a short rest location within the week (requiring a DC 15 Constitution save).
- Enemies cannot interfere.

---

### 3. Transcendence

*You guide your friends and confound your foes, a spiritual essence that lingers before finally rising above.*

**Effect:**
- Remain as an invincible spectral image for 5 minutes after death.
- Enemies within 10 feet suffer disadvantage on all d20 rolls.
- Allies within 10 feet gain advantage on all d20 rolls.
- Gain a fly speed of 50 feet and can pass through physical and magical barriers.
- Communicate telepathically with any creature within 100 feet.

| Last Stand Option | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Empty Sun Technique** | Two critical hits; on failed save, target imprisoned forever. | Neutralising a powerful boss. |
| **Into the Mist** | Allies teleport to previous resting locations. | Saving the party from certain defeat. |
| **Transcendence** | Invincible spirit for 5 minutes; advantage/disadvantage aura. | Empowering allies in climactic fight. |

## Class Reactions

Monks have access to powerful reactions that leverage their flowing combat style. These reactions use your **Proficiency Bonus pool**, refreshing on short or long rest. Note: Monk reactions cost Focus rather than PB uses in some cases — see individual entries.

### Fluid Reversal (2nd Level)

**Trigger:** A creature within 5 feet makes a melee attack against you.
**Effect:** Spend 1 Focus. If they miss: unarmed strike with advantage; on hit, DEX save (DC = Technique Save DC) or knocked prone. If they hit: reduce damage by Martial Arts die + WIS modifier, then unarmed strike.

Against bloodied creatures: save DC +2; on failed save, target also stunned until start of your next turn; costs 0 Focus if attacker is bloodied.

---

### Flowing Reversal (3rd Level)

**Trigger:** A creature enters your reach or makes a melee attack against you.
**Effect:** Spend 1 Focus. Make an unarmed strike. On hit, STR save (DC = Technique Save DC) or knocked prone or pushed 10 feet (your choice). If triggered by entering reach, move up to 10 feet without provoking after the strike.

Against bloodied creatures: push 20 feet instead of 10; on failed save, speed reduced by 10 feet for 1 minute; disadvantage on the check to stand up.

---

### Perfect Counter (11th Level)

**Trigger:** You succeed on an Evasion roll vs a melee or ranged attack by 5 or more.
**Effect:** Spend 1 Focus. Teleport to an unoccupied space adjacent to the attacker and make two unarmed strikes with advantage. If both strikes hit, spend 1 additional Focus to stun the target until end of your next turn.

Against bloodied creatures: costs 0 Focus; automatically crit if target is below 25% HP; can use Stunning Strike after the strikes without additional TP cost.
