# Dragonborn
## Lineage

> *"We are not dragons. We are their shadow. We are not fire. We are its consequence."*  
> — **The Tablets of Flame**, First Inscription

You are Dragonborn. Not born of womb or mercy, but **branded into existence** by divine will. You are the last legacy of Soltharion, the Sun Tyrant—a race forged in flame and law, tempered by chaos, and bound to purpose eternal. Where others choose their path, yours was **written in fire** before you drew your first breath.

You were not made to be free. You were made to **endure, to serve, and to burn with purpose**. The caste marked upon your scales defines your role, your worth, your place in the divine structure. Now you carry that brand into a world that has moved beyond the empire that forged you, and one day—if you are strong enough—you will decide whether to honor the flame or forge something new.

---

## The Burning Concord

According to the **Doctrine of Burning Truth**, Dragonborn were not shaped by gentle hands or natural birth—they were **seared into being by Soltharion**, the Sun Tyrant, in his final act of defiance against oblivion. As the Weave began to bind even the gods, Soltharion refused annihilation by embedding his will directly into the Pattern itself.

> *"If I cannot reign, then let my flame reign for me."*

Into this crucible stepped **Xethar**, the Wildfire—flame without shape, destruction without doctrine. Together, their collision forged the **First Dragonborn**: not born but tempered, not blessed but branded.

Your people believe that **all purpose is divine command**, and anything outside the caste structure is chaos waiting to be purified. The gods who contributed to the Concord are not worshipped—they **obeyed, contributed, and stepped aside**:

- **Keldros, The Discipline-Forge** (gave discipline without ambition)
- **Vatharun, The Chain-Father** (gave duty above blood)
- **Aelys, The Memory-Keeper** (gave memory without revision)
- **Ignis, The Builder's Flame** (gave the flame that builds)

This is not blasphemy—it is **divine structure**. Gods are not masters but architects who completed their work and moved on.

## Life Within the Flame

You were raised knowing that **caste is destiny**, that order is sacred, that the flame tests all and the worthy endure. Even among Dragonborn who have left the Concord's direct control, the weight of the brand remains.

**Caste (zhar'tok)** is not metaphorical. It is:
- Marked by scale color and breath weapon type
- Encoded in the Scrolls of the Forged Line
- The measure of your divine purpose
- Your place in the eternal structure

Your lifespan is roughly **80-100 years**—longer than humans but shorter than elves. Your people measure time in **Flame-Cycles** (periods of 50 years marked by ritual purges and caste confirmations), and you measure worth not in age but in **duty fulfilled and structure upheld**.

Every significant action is recorded in **The Scrolls of the Forged Line**—flame-sealed lineage records maintained by the Embermarked, documenting every sanctioned life in the Concord.

## Physical Characteristics

**Age:** You mature quickly, reaching adulthood by age 15 (the age of caste confirmation). Most Dragonborn die in service to their caste's purpose, though those who live to old age (150-250) are venerated as Flame-Tested.

**Size:** You stand between 6 to 7 feet tall, with powerful draconic frames covered in metallic or chromatic scales. Your size is Medium.

**Speed:** Your base walking speed is 30 feet. Your body is built for endurance and presence, not speed.

**Build:** Powerfully built, with scale patterns that mark your draconic ancestry. Your body bears your caste's colors, and any deviation from your birth colors is considered a sign of divine testing or corruption.

## Dragonborn Traits

As a Dragonborn, you possess the following lineage traits:

### **Draconic Ancestry**
You have draconic ancestry, which manifests in your scale color and breath weapon. Choose one type of dragon from the Draconic Ancestry table. This choice determines your breath weapon and damage resistance.

**Draconic Ancestry Table:**

| Dragon | Damage Type | Breath Weapon |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Black | Acid | 5 by 30 ft. line (Dex. save) |
| Blue | Lightning | 5 by 30 ft. line (Dex. save) |
| Brass | Fire | 5 by 30 ft. line (Dex. save) |
| Bronze | Lightning | 5 by 30 ft. line (Dex. save) |
| Copper | Acid | 5 by 30 ft. line (Dex. save) |
| Gold | Fire | 15 ft. cone (Dex. save) |
| Green | Poison | 15 ft. cone (Con. save) |
| Red | Fire | 15 ft. cone (Dex. save) |
| Silver | Cold | 15 ft. cone (Con. save) |
| White | Cold | 15 ft. cone (Con. save) |

### **Breath Weapon**
You can use your action to exhale destructive energy. Your draconic ancestry determines the size, shape, and damage type of the exhalation.

When you use your breath weapon, each creature in the area of the exhalation must make a saving throw (DC = 8 + your Constitution modifier + your proficiency bonus). A creature takes 2d6 damage on a failed save, and half as much damage on a successful one. The damage increases to 3d6 at 6th level, 4d6 at 11th level, and 5d6 at 16th level.

After you use your breath weapon, you can't use it again until you complete a short or long rest.

### **Damage Resistance**
You have resistance to the damage type associated with your draconic ancestry.

### **Draconic Presence** *(Cultural Trait)*
You have proficiency in the Intimidation skill. When you speak with the weight of your caste's authority or invoke the Concord's divine structure, you may add your proficiency bonus twice to Intimidation checks (if you are already proficient).

This reflects the Dragonborn cultural training in commanding presence and the fear your people inspire.

### **Branded Purpose**
You were raised within a caste structure, even if you've since left it. Choose one:
- **Caste-Loyal:** You gain proficiency in one artisan's tool or one martial weapon, representing your training within your assigned caste.
- **Caste-Broken:** You gain proficiency in Deception or Survival (your choice), representing skills learned while escaping or surviving outside the structure.

## Mental Attributes & Psychology

**Focus Modifier:** +1  
**Sanity Modifier:** +0

Your biology grants you **exceptional mental discipline**. Your Focus receives a bonus because Dragonborn culture trains you from birth to maintain concentration, follow orders precisely, and execute duty without distraction. This isn't cultural—it's bred into your very essence through generations of structured purpose.

Your Sanity is standard, however. While you're trained to suppress fear and doubt, the rigidity of your upbringing can make you vulnerable to experiences that challenge the fundamental order you were taught.

### **Cultural Mental Conditioning**
Beyond your biological advantage, Dragonborn upbringing creates:

- **Unwavering discipline:** You are trained to complete assigned tasks perfectly
- **Order as foundation:** Chaos and uncertainty are recontextualized as tests
- **Purpose without question:** You expect your role to be defined, not chosen
- **Hierarchy as comfort:** Structure provides psychological stability

### **Corruption Vulnerability: The Fractured Brand**

As a Dragonborn, you have a unique relationship with Corruption. Your connection to divine structure provides some protection against chaos, but corruption that enters through **broken hierarchy, violated oaths, or shattered order** affects you catastrophically.

**When you gain Corruption Score 10+ (Tier 2), you suffer:**
- Brand-Flicker: Your scales begin to show discoloration or wrong colors
- Purpose-Strain: Following orders or maintaining discipline requires Wisdom saves
- Caste-Doubt: The certainty of your role becomes questioned, causing psychic pain

**Fractured Brand Table (Corruption Tier 2+)**

When you complete a long rest at Corruption Score 10+, roll 1d6:

| d6 | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **False Caste:** You remember orders you never received. Make a DC 15 Wisdom save or believe you belong to a different caste until your next long rest. |
| 2 | **Brand-Burn:** Your draconic ancestry rebels. You cannot use your breath weapon until your next long rest, and your scales ache constantly. |
| 3 | **Structure-Void:** The certainty of hierarchy crumbles. Take 1d4 psychic damage and lose proficiency in one skill until your next long rest. |
| 4 | **Weight of Chaos:** You feel reality becoming unbound. Make a DC 12 Sanity save or lose 1d4 Sanity. |
| 5 | **Eldritch Touch:** Something tries to rebrand you. Gain 1 Whisper Point but your next Intimidation check using Draconic Presence fails automatically. |
| 6 | **Flame Resists:** Your divine connection fights back. Reduce your Corruption Score by 1 (minimum 10). |

## Dynamic Combat System (DCS) Integration

### **Evasion Profile: Armored Enforcer**
- **Dodge Evasion:** +0 (you rely on armor and natural scales, not agility)
- **Endure Evasion:** +1 (your draconic body absorbs punishment)
- **Deflect Evasion:** +1 (your scales naturally turn aside glancing blows)

**Tactical Note:** You are designed to **hold position and project power**. Your breath weapon provides area control, while your natural resistance and discipline make you hard to break.

### **Natural Damage Reduction:** +2
Your draconic scales provide substantial natural protection. This stacks with armor worn over them.

**Special Note:** You gain an additional +2 DR against your associated damage type (from Damage Resistance), representing your scales' exceptional protection against that specific energy.

### **Combat Philosophy**
Dragonborn fight with **disciplined aggression and tactical control**. You hold key positions (high Endure/Deflect), use breath weapons to control space, and leverage natural resistance to tank specific damage types. You don't rush wildly—you **advance with purpose**.

### **Breaking Point Mechanics**
Due to your cultural conditioning and training in hierarchy, you have **advantage on Rally checks when being led by a superior** but **disadvantage on Rally checks when leaderless**. Your morale is tied to the chain of command.

## Languages

**Languages:** You can speak, read, and write Common and Draconic.

**Draconic** is an ancient, powerful language where tone and emphasis can literally reshape meaning. The language has extensive vocabulary for:
- Hierarchy, caste, and divine order
- Commands, obedience, and structure
- Fire, flame, and purification
- Ancestry and draconic lineages

When Dragonborn speak of sacred matters, they often use the formal registers of Draconic even if others don't understand, because some truths **must be spoken in the language of dragons**.

## Dragonborn Subraces

While all Dragonborn share the brand of the Concord, different castes and lineages have developed distinct traits over generations. Choose one of the following subraces:

### **Flameborn Lineage** *(Chromatic: Red, Gold, Brass)*
You descend from the warrior castes, bred for combat and conquest. Your fire burns hottest in battle.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength score increases by 2, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Warrior's Brand:** When you score a critical hit with a melee weapon attack, you can immediately use your breath weapon as part of the same action (if available). The breath weapon does not require a separate action.

**Furnace Heart:** Your breath weapon damage increases by 1 die (3d6 becomes 4d6, 4d6 becomes 5d6, etc.) when you are bloodied (at or below half your maximum hit points).

**Caste Memory:** You have proficiency in Athletics or Intimidation (your choice), representing your training in the warrior traditions.

### **Frostborn Lineage** *(Chromatic: White, Silver)*
You descend from the guardian castes, bred to hold territory and protect sacred sites. Your patience is as cold as your breath.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Constitution score increases by 2, and your Wisdom score increases by 1.

**Guardian's Stance:** You have advantage on saving throws against being moved against your will or knocked prone. When you choose to use the Dodge action, you also gain temporary hit points equal to your Constitution modifier.

**Frost Discipline:** Once per long rest, when you use your breath weapon, you can choose to freeze the affected area. The area becomes difficult terrain for 1 minute, and creatures that start their turn in the area take cold damage equal to your proficiency bonus.

**Caste Memory:** You have proficiency in Perception or Survival (your choice), representing your training in vigilance and territorial control.

### **Stormborn Lineage** *(Chromatic: Blue, Bronze)*
You descend from the scholar and administrator castes, bred for precision and control. Your lightning strikes with calculated purpose.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Intelligence score increases by 2, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Calculated Strike:** When you use your breath weapon, you can exclude a number of creatures in the area equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1). These creatures automatically succeed on their saving throw and take no damage.

**Storm Clarity:** Your Focus bonus increases to +2 (instead of +1). Additionally, you have advantage on concentration checks to maintain concentration on spells.

**Caste Memory:** You have proficiency in Arcana or History (your choice), representing your training in scholarly pursuits.

### **Venomborn Lineage** *(Chromatic: Green)*
You descend from the infiltrator and inquisitor castes, bred for subtlety and interrogation. Your poison flows with purpose.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Dexterity score increases by 2, and your Intelligence score increases by 1.

**Toxic Patience:** When you use your breath weapon and a creature fails its saving throw, that creature is also poisoned until the end of your next turn.

**Venom Discipline:** You have advantage on saving throws against poison, and you have proficiency in the Poisoner's Kit. You can apply poison to weapons as a bonus action.

**Caste Memory:** You have proficiency in Investigation or Insight (your choice), representing your training in uncovering truth.

### **Wyrmborn Lineage** *(Chromatic: Black, Copper)*
You descend from the craftsmen and builder castes, bred to create and maintain the empire's infrastructure. Your acid dissolves obstacles.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Constitution score increases by 2, and your Intelligence score increases by 1.

**Corrosive Touch:** When you use your breath weapon, you can choose to have it also deal acid damage to objects and structures. The affected area suffers double damage from your breath weapon.

**Builder's Resilience:** You gain proficiency with one set of artisan's tools of your choice. Additionally, when you spend Hit Dice during a short rest, you regain an additional hit point equal to your proficiency bonus per Hit Die spent.

**Caste Memory:** You have proficiency with one set of artisan's tools of your choice, representing your training in the crafting traditions.

### **Ashborn** *(The Branded-Out)*
You are casteless—marked with the **Shattered Flame**, cut from the Pattern. Whether through rebellion, failure, or birth, you exist outside the Concord's structure. This is both liberation and curse.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma score increases by 2 (your choice), and another score of your choice increases by 1.

**Unbound Flame:** You are not tied to a single breath weapon. When you finish a long rest, you can choose a different draconic ancestry from the table, changing your breath weapon and damage resistance until your next long rest.

**Caste-Breaker:** You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened, and you cannot be branded or marked by magical effects against your will.

**Scarred Freedom:** You have proficiency in two skills of your choice, representing the diverse survival skills learned outside the caste structure.

**Corruption Resistance:** You have advantage on saving throws against gaining Corruption from **hierarchy-based sources** (broken orders, violated structure), as you've already broken from that system. However, you have disadvantage on saves against Corruption from **isolation and purposelessness**.

**Social Stigma:** Within Dragonborn communities still tied to the Concord, you are marked as outcast. This can manifest as disadvantage on Charisma checks with caste-loyal Dragonborn, or as advantage when dealing with other outcasts and rebels.

## Dragonborn Names

Dragonborn names reflect caste, lineage, and achievement. Your name is not just identity—it is **record of your place in the divine structure**.

**Caste Names** (Earned at confirmation, age 15):
- **Flameborn:** Ashkar, Pyrthan, Ignak, Brazeth, Fyrmara
- **Frostborn:** Glacith, Rimthar, Borvan, Frostyn, Icelara
- **Stormborn:** Voltrak, Thundara, Azyrth, Stormak, Zephira
- **Venomborn:** Vexnar, Toxith, Venara, Corrath, Serpyra
- **Wyrmborn:** Scalth, Ironis, Copran, Alkareth, Metalyn

**Lineage Names** (Inherited from bloodline):
- Flame-lineages: Surathar, Igneus, Pyraxis, Embercrown
- Frost-lineages: Glaciax, Wintermaw, Rimeguard
- Storm-lineages: Voltaris, Azuresky, Thunderborn
- Venom-lineages: Viperscale, Toxiclaw, Serpentis
- Wyrm-lineages: Forgescale, Ironhide, Copperborn

**Ashborn Names** (Self-chosen after branding-out):
- Often reject caste names entirely
- May take names from other cultures
- Sometimes take ironic names ("Unbound," "Freescale," "Chaosborn")
- Might keep original name as defiance

**Typical Structure:** Caste-name + Lineage-name
- Examples: Ashkar Surathar, Glacith Wintermaw, Voltrak Azuresky

Ashborn often use only a single name, rejecting the two-name structure entirely.

## Dragonborn in Dór-o Estel

The Dragonborn empire of **Ashkarra** fell during the Collapse, scattering your people across the world. The Concord's rigid structure fractured, leaving Dragonborn to navigate a world that no longer revolves around caste law.

**After the Collapse**, Dragonborn communities responded differently:
- **Orthodox Enclaves** maintained the caste system in isolated communities
- **Adapted Communities** modified the Concord to fit new circumstances
- **Scattered Individuals** left the structure entirely, seeking new purpose
- **The Ashborn Movement** grew, offering an alternative to caste-defined existence

The **Faith of the Bound Pattern** is viewed with suspicion by orthodox Dragonborn, who see it as the antithesis of divine order. However, many scattered Dragonborn have found solace in the Pattern's embrace, trading rigid hierarchy for flexible purpose.

When working with other races:

**Dwarves**: Respected for their structure and craft-discipline. "They understand order, even if they lack proper hierarchy."

**Elves**: Distrusted for their focus on memory over obedience. "They remember, but do not act. What good is knowledge without purpose?"

**Humans**: Fascinating in their chaos. "They build empires that should collapse, yet somehow endure. Perhaps order can emerge from chaos."

**Orcs**: Strange kinship through trial-focus. "They test themselves as we test ourselves. They understand that strength must be proven."

**Halflings**: Complete bewilderment. "How can they function without structure? It should be impossible. Yet they survive by laughing at what should destroy them."

## Adventure Hooks

**The Brand Rebels**: You or someone you know has been marked Ashborn—cut from the caste system. Orthodox Dragonborn hunt for you, while other Ashborn see you as kin. Survive or build something new.

**Reclamation of Purpose**: The Concord's holy sites lie abandoned or corrupted. Your caste sends you to reclaim, cleanse, or investigate what was once sacred ground.

**The Doubled-Branded**: You bear two brands—your birth caste and another earned through impossible trial. This should not exist. Yet you do. Discover why.

**Corruption in the Structure**: The caste system itself is becoming tainted—Embermarked giving false orders, Scrolls rewriting themselves, the hierarchy inverting. Find the source before the Concord collapses from within.

**The Unburnt Scroll**: A Scroll of the Forged Line has survived from before the Collapse. It contains names and castes that no longer exist, including perhaps your own true lineage. Retrieve it before it's destroyed or corrupted.

## Integration with Mental Systems

### **Focus & Sanity:**
Your **+1 Focus** is significant:
- You maintain concentration more easily
- Complex orders are executed with precision
- Mental discipline under pressure is exceptional
- Chaos and disorder are managed through strict protocols

**Typical Dragonborn Mental Strategy:**
- Use high Focus to maintain concentration in combat
- Process fear through discipline protocols
- Breath weapon as tactical control, not emotional release
- Hierarchy as psychological anchor during stress

### **Corruption Concerns:**
Your unique corruption vulnerability centers on **broken structure and shattered order**:
- Violating caste duties accelerates corruption
- Abandoning your role creates corruption vulnerability
- At Tier 2+, the Fractured Brand table reflects divine structure breaking
- Chaos and disorder become existentially threatening

### **Rally & Morale:**
Dragonborn have conditional advantages in morale:
- Advantage on Rally when led by legitimate superior
- Disadvantage on Rally when leaderless or command structure broken
- Seeing a Dragonborn hold position through breath weapon inspire allies
- Enemy forces become demoralized by disciplined Dragonborn formations

## Design Philosophy

The Dragonborn lineage is designed to support:

1. **The Disciplined Commander** (high Focus + Draconic Presence create tactical leaders)
2. **The Branded Warrior** (breath weapon + natural DR create versatile combatants)
3. **The Purpose-Driven Guardian** (Fire Discipline + structure create reliable protectors)
4. **The Scholar of Order** (Stormborn for Intelligence-based Dragonborn)
5. **The Revolutionary** (Ashborn for those rejecting structure)

Your mechanical abilities (breath weapon, damage resistance, natural DR, high Focus) create a **disciplined combatant who controls the battlefield**. You're designed to hold key positions, project power through breath weapons, and maintain concentration under fire.

The subrace options allow you to lean into different caste traditions—from warriors (Flameborn) to guardians (Frostborn) to scholars (Stormborn)—while maintaining the core Dragonborn identity of purposeful structure. The Ashborn option allows for complete rejection of this paradigm.

## Final Notes

### **The Weight of the Brand:**
Every Dragonborn carries purpose-pressure. Your character likely knows:
- Their exact position in the caste hierarchy (or their rejection of it)
- The names of at least three Embermarked who shaped their community
- The location of multiple sacred sites
- The flame-prayers of their caste

This isn't burden—it's **foundation**. Unless you're Ashborn, in which case it's the weight you chose to cast off.

### **Respect Across Cultures:**
Modern Dór-o Estel has complex views of Dragonborn:
- Dwarves respect the structure but question the inflexibility
- Elves see echoes of their own lost empires
- Humans admire the discipline but fear the rigidity
- The Faith of the Bound Pattern sees potential converts or dangerous fundamentalists

### **The Flame That Tests:**
Your people believe that life is **divine trial through structure**. The worthy find their place, fulfill their purpose, and become part of the eternal Pattern that Soltharion carved. The unworthy are burned away, their ashes feeding the next generation's rise.

You adventure knowing the flame watches, judges, and waits to see if you will uphold the structure or forge something new. This is not pressure to fear—it is **certainty** that your purpose matters, whether given or chosen.
