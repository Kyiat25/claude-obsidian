# Dór-o Estel Timeline — Missing Events & Additions

Based on comparison between the uploaded timeline and project knowledge, here are events and details that should be added or expanded:

---

## 🌌 LOOMLESS TIME — Missing Events

### The War of Elemental Becoming
**When:** During the Loomless Time (before measurement)  
**What:** Each element claimed space, form, and identity through conflict. This war defined the era—Fire, Earth, Water, Air, and Spirit vying for dominance.  
**Significance:** The gods were formless, proud, and unknowing during this period.

### Soltharis Rises and the First Firestorm
**When:** Mid-Loomless Time  
**What:** Soltharion (also spelled Soltharis), god of flame and tyranny, rose to claim all change as his right. He attempted to shape the Weave through flame, claiming Fire as the primal Thread. This resulted in the **First Firestorm**, which burned part of the Spirit-threaded lands.  
**Response:** Vaeldros and Thamur intervened, halting Soltharion's attempt to claim cosmic precedence.

### The Elemental Accords
**When:** Late Loomless Time  
**What:** Following the First Firestorm, the Elemental Accords were forged (temporarily), recognizing that no single Thread may dominate.  
**Significance:** First attempt at divine balance; would eventually dissolve during the Second Age.

### The Dragonborn Compact
**When:** ~10,000 years ago (Mid-Loomless Time)  
**What:** Dragonborn emerged during the chaos of the Loomless Time. Soltharion found them before they found themselves:  
- They were naturally fire-resistant, physically powerful, instinctively hierarchical
- Soltharion offered them glory, purpose, and power in exchange for absolute loyalty
- They accepted, becoming the **first race to truly worship a god**
- This made them the **model** for what gods wanted from all races
- They began building the **First Divine Empire** dedicated to the Fire Pantheon  
**Significance:** Created the template for divine authority over mortals. Led to the Burning Concord religion.

### Emergence of Humans
**When:** ~8,000 years ago (Late Loomless Time)  
**What:** Unlike elves (who walked the world when it was still forming), humans arrived to find:  
- Elemental Embodiments already established
- Divine Spark gods already organizing into pantheons
- Elves with 12,000+ years of unbroken tradition
- Dragonborn already in service to Soltharion for 2,000+ years
- **The world already mapped, claimed, and explained**  
**Significance:** Humans encountered gods first and never knew anything else. They developed the **Reflection Pattern** as their first faith—focused on ancestors and personal pantheons rather than organized worship.

### The Sealing of Soltharis
**When:** End of Loomless Time / Beginning of First Age (~4,500 years ago)  
**What:** Pyrris and Vaeldros sealed Soltharis within the Sun—the **first divine punishment**. This marked the transition between ages.  
**Significance:** The Weave stabilized. Memory became possible. The First Age began.

## 🌑 FIRST AGE — Missing Events

### The Hollow Line
**When:** End of First Age (~1,800 years ago)  
**What:** When Vel'Zyren was judged and severed from all Threads by Gaeldros and Aelys, the severing caused a **scar in the Weave** known as the **Hollow Line**.  
**Significance:** Still feared in modern prophecy. A permanent wound in reality caused by divine punishment.

### The 105-Year Drow Transformation
**When:** ~1,800 to ~1,695 years ago  
**Detailed Timeline:**

| Years | Phase | Events |
|-------|-------|--------|
| 1-10 | The Call | Vel'Zyren whispered to ambitious elves; promised power for descent into the deep; rift opened in sacred groves |
| 10-15 | The Migration | Thousands of elves descended willingly; rift sealed behind them; wood elves witnessed the violation of sacred groves |
| 15-20 | The Trial | Gaeldros and other gods discovered Vel'Zyren's actions; divine trial convened; she was sealed in the Underdark with her "children" |
| 20-105 | The Transformation | Systematic remaking through Divine Spark manipulation; physical transformation (obsidian skin, white hair, glowing eyes); psychological reconditioning; memory erasure |

**Significance:** This was **forced transformation through Divine Spark manipulation**, not natural adaptation. Creates the Drow—victims of manipulation who became perpetrators of atrocities. Within living memory of oldest elves (1,800 years = "yesterday" in elven terms).

## 🔥 SECOND AGE — Missing Events

### The Unleashing of the Drow
**When:** Beginning of Second Age (~1,800 years ago)  
**What:** Marks the start of the Second Age. Vel'Zyren's followers launch brutal raids from below, assassinating religious leaders, Thread-Speakers, and Concord flame-bearers.  
**Significance:** Divine fracturing begins. The Drow do not join the rebellion OR the Concord—they strike all surface powers equally.

### Collapse of the Elemental Accord
**When:** Beginning of Second Age  
**What:** The pact that ended the First Age dissolves entirely:  
- Fire and Spirit become bitter enemies
- Earth and Water sever alliance over law vs. flow
- Divine intervention becomes contradictory
- **Thamur withdraws** from overt action, warning that the gods are no longer unified enough to prevent Weave instability  
**Significance:** Mortals begin to distrust prophecy and pursue glyphic alternatives.

### Rise of the Ashen Web
**When:** During the Concord's expansion and rebellion chaos  
**What:** Vel'Zyren's followers establish the Ashen Web, feared for:  
- Religious absolutism
- **Skinning of surface heretics** to create "Books of Silence"
- Complete devotion to Vel'Zyren as the **only** true god  
**Significance:** Drow become a third power distinct from both the Concord and the rebellion.

### Emergence of Tieflings
**When:** Second Age  
**What:** Glyph-marked mortals bound to Fire appear. Initially seen as tools until they begin developing autonomous doctrine (foreshadowing the Ashen Testament).

### Birth of Divergent Faiths
**When:** Second Age

| Faith | Origin |
|-------|--------|
| **Ashen Testament** | Tieflings questioning the morality of gods entirely. "Sin is earned, not given." |
| **Wonder-Tithe Codex** | Gnomes developing philosophy of magical trade, experimentation, and divine irreverence |
| **Reflection Pattern** | Humans seeking emotional and ancestral stability amid collapsing doctrine |
| **Veiled Star** | Underground network of Spirit-seers, Aasimar, Shadar-Kai warning of divine unraveling |

### The Cleansing of Thread-Fallow
**When:** Second Age  
**What:** The Veiled Star's followers were nearly wiped out in this purge. Survivors formed the **first Pale Vows** and went underground.

### Soltharion Purity Wars
**When:** Second Age  
**What:** Entire cities razed for rejecting caste hierarchy.

| Suppressed Faith | Method of Erasure |
|------------------|-------------------|
| **Wonder-Tithe** | Declared "improvisational instability." Shrines absorbed or demolished. Many gnomes force-bound to Pattern oaths |

### Faith Atrocities (Third Age)
- **The Vine-Storm of East Aethros**: Druidic orders retaliated after root sanctums were defiled. A Faith-aligned governor was choked to death by vines while delivering a sermon. The city was abandoned for 41 years.
- **Scar-Warden Reunification Trial**: Rogue orc clans challenged Faith supremacy. Scar-Wardens defeated them in public duels, affirming that Scar-Law may coexist with—but not bow to—the Pattern.
- **Halfling Joy-Reckonings**: Sparked by temple desecration. Halflings retaliated with precision raids and disappearances. Their Rite of the Joy-Reckoning enters urban legend.
- **Flooding Trial of Kel Varyan**: Water-seers unleashed ritual flooding to destroy fire-aligned glyph recorders. Now commemorated yearly as a warning against magical arrogance.

### Ashkarra's Three Spirit Alignments (Expanded)
Your timeline mentions these but project knowledge has more detail:

**~50 TA (First Spirit Alignment):**
- First alignment under Spirit Thread went as expected
- City leadership views this as proof of Ashkarra's special status

**~30 TA (Second Spirit Alignment):**
- Unprecedented second consecutive Spirit Thread
- First warnings from elven seers and Pattern scholars
- Sultan dismisses warnings: "The gods would not choose us twice unless they knew we could bear it"

**~10 TA (Third Spirit Alignment - Point of No Return):**
- Never before attempted in history
- Violates cosmic law established after reality stabilized
- Immediate spiritual crisis:
  - Weave stagnation — energy accumulating instead of flowing
  - Echo redundancy — every action creating multiple reflections
  - Memory overflow — past/present/future overlapping
  - Reality strain — physical laws becoming unreliable
- **The Ember Crucible begins failing:**
  - Sacred flame burning in reverse (consuming heat/light)
  - Spiral of Flame and Form inverting its rotation
  - Obsidian mirrors reflecting spiritual rather than physical forms
  - Temporal loops in judgments and ceremonies
  - Castes beginning to blur and shift

### Prophet Tyraen's Final Warning
**When:** Final years before Ashkarra's fall  
**What:** Prophet Tyraen of the Pool-Sky gave final prophecy:
> *"When the Thread weaves no longer, when echoes outnumber breath, when the moonlight comes in chorus, then shall flame forget its name."*

**Imperial Response:** Tyraen was mocked, imprisoned, then **disappeared**. Prophecy declared heretical.

## 🌒 FOURTH AGE — Missing Events

### The Unraveling Period (0-95 A4) — Expanded Detail
**What Actually Happened:**
- Global magical catastrophe — leylines snapped across continents
- Dragonborn Empire **immediately collapsed** into warring fragments
- Reality breach created the Shard Wastes — permanent zone of instability
- **150+ years of magical chaos** (the "Age of Shadows")
- Only elves have living memory of this period  
**Significance:** The timeline says "Unraveling Ends" at 95 A4, but project knowledge emphasizes this was **150+ years** of chaos, not 95. May need reconciliation.

### Aeravae Founding (Expanded)
**When:** Early Fourth Age (~Year 0-5 A4)  
**What the Records Say:** After Ashkarra's fall, the Faith urgently needed a new World Anchor. Surveys identified sacred cenotes and underground rivers.  
**What Actually Happened:** The "empty channels" were actually the **submerged root system of one of the last living World Trees**. When the Loom-Stone was lowered, it made contact with the **still-living heartwood** of the World Tree, creating an unprecedented **fusion**—a **hybrid Anchor** that was simultaneously natural and constructed, alive and ordered.  
**Who Participated:**
- Surviving Threadbearers from remaining Loom-Cities
- **Elven druids** — wood elves brought ancient grove-anchoring techniques
- **Sea elves** — surfaced for the first time in millennia to help
- High elf scholars — contributed glyph-stabilization research
- Human mages — Faith-aligned and Reflection practitioners alike
- Dwarves — brought Deep Law binding knowledge  
**Significance:** This proved the Weave could be stabilized without divine claims. Elven techniques were **necessary** for survival.

### Fourth Age Faith Atrocities
- **Blinding of Tithers in Thar Vornhal**: All Wonder-Tithe instructors forced into silence or exile
- **Echo Nulling in Veltharyn**: Spirit archives purged after Pale Vow writings found inside. Two archivists vanish.
- **Reflection Trial in Kel Varyan**: Mirror Mantle rituals disrupted by Earth-aligned inquisitors. Seven deaths. No punishment.
- **Flooding of Hollowmoor**: A city desecrated its sacred well. Verdant Accord retaliates with flood and fungus. Glyph-rain follows.

### The Echoed Weaving
**When:** Fourth Age (unclear exact year)  
**What:** A new Threadbearer emerges under the Spirit Thread, believed to have survived a test hidden by the Weave itself. Their presence is controversial and causes **spiritual dissonance** in cities.

### Death of Aethros the Binder
**When:** Fourth Age  
**What:** A famed Faith judge who committed **ritual exile into the Umbral Veil** rather than pass sentence on an unregistered Threadborn child. His glyph remains unsolvable.

### Scar-Moon Cycle 372
**When:** 372 A4  
**What:** Orc clans report a **dreaming god of silence and fire** challenging Scar-Law itself. Some Scar-Wardens call for a new test of the Bound Pattern.

### High Threadbearer Naelia's Disappearance
**When:** Cycle 4 (before 487 A4)  
**What:** High Threadbearer Naelia disappeared after stating: "The Threads are thinning. The Glyphs forget. The gods no longer answer in unison."

### The Thornward Flats Disaster
**When:** 482 A4 (5 years before present)  
**What:** Corvessa's holy host was destroyed attempting to invade Talanthar. The forest's wrath was sealed in song.  
**Significance:** Most recent major military defeat, demonstrating Talanthar's unconquerable nature.

### Imperial Border Skirmish
**When:** 486 A4 (1 year before present)  
**What:** The Kel Varyan Empire learned the same lesson Corvessa did—that Talanthar cannot be conquered through force.
