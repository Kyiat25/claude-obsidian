# FEARS Advanced Backgrounds - Complete System

## Analysis Methodology

**Selection Criteria:**

1. **Narrative Depth** - Backgrounds with compelling story hooks
2. **Mechanical Progression** - Clear advancement tied to gameplay
3. **Setting Integration** - Fit with Dór-o Estel's themes
4. **Versatility** - Useful across multiple class types
5. **Downtime Engagement** - Create meaningful non-combat activities

**Power Budget Per Rank:**

- Rank 1: 1-2 points (skill, tool, equipment, minor feature)
- Rank 2: 2-3 points (better holdings, expanded feature)
- Rank 3: 3-4 points (leadership, significant influence)
- Rank 4: 4-5 points (mastery, major impact)

## TIER 1: CORE BACKGROUNDS

_Essential backgrounds every campaign should include_

### 1. SOLDIER (Modified from PHB)

_You served in an organized military force, whether imperial legion, city guard, or mercenary company._

#### Rank 1: Recruit

You've completed basic training and seen your first campaigns.

**Skill Proficiency:** Athletics, Intimidation  
**Tool Proficiency:** One gaming set, vehicles (land)  
**Equipment:** Insignia of rank, trophy from fallen enemy, set of bone dice, common clothes, belt pouch with 10 gp

**Feature - Military Rank:** You have a rank from your military career. Soldiers of your organization recognize your authority and influence. You can invoke rank to gain temporary control of soldiers (if your rank is high enough) or requisition simple equipment for temporary use.

**Holdings:**

- Bunk in a garrison barracks
- Mess hall access and basic rations
- Contact: One veteran who served with you

**Progression:** Distinguish yourself in battle or complete a major military operation

---

#### Rank 2: Veteran

You've survived multiple campaigns and earned respect from your peers.

**Holdings:**

- Private quarters in garrison
- Access to armory (can borrow standard equipment)
- Small stipend (5 gp/month when not deployed)
- Network of 3-5 fellow soldiers who will aid you

**Feature Expansion - Tactical Mind:** When you spend 1 minute observing a fortification or defensive position, you can identify:

- Weak points in defenses
- Optimal approach routes
- Estimated garrison strength (±20%)

**Progression:** Lead a successful military operation or save your unit from disaster

---

#### Rank 3: Officer

You command troops and make strategic decisions.

**Holdings:**

- Officer's quarters
- Command over a squad (8-12 soldiers, use Guard statistics)
- Monthly stipend (20 gp)
- Access to military intelligence networks
- Right to requisition supplies for your squad

**Feature Expansion - Rally the Troops:** Once per long rest, you can spend 10 minutes delivering a rousing speech. All allies who hear it gain temporary hit points equal to your level + your Charisma modifier and advantage on their next saving throw against being frightened.

**Progression:** Win a major battle, develop a new military tactic adopted by your organization, or uncover and thwart enemy intelligence operation

---

#### Rank 4: Commander

You lead companies into battle and advise generals.

**Holdings:**

- Command suite with personal staff
- Command over a company (80-120 soldiers)
- Major stipend (100 gp/month)
- Influence with military leadership
- Access to restricted military documents and war councils

**Feature - Strategic Genius:** Once per long rest, you can call upon military resources:

- Request a squad of elite soldiers (Knights or Veterans) for one mission
- Gain intelligence reports on any known military force
- Requisition military-grade equipment (siege weapons, warhorses, etc.)

Additionally, your Rally the Troops feature can now affect up to 50 people and grants advantage on attack rolls for 1 minute.

- Network of 5-8 informants across the city

- Guards are -2 to passive Perception to spot you

**Progression:** Become indispensable to your organization or run a major criminal operation

---

## IMPLEMENTATION GUIDELINES

### Progression Timing

- **Rank 1 → 2:** 3-5 sessions, requires completing background-specific objective
- **Rank 2 → 3:** 5-8 sessions, requires significant achievement
- **Rank 3 → 4:** 8-12 sessions, requires legendary accomplishment

### Downtime Activities

Each background should have 2-3 specific downtime activities:

1. **Maintain Holdings** - Spend time keeping your position
2. **Expand Network** - Build new contacts
3. **Generate Income** - Use background to make money
4. **Advance Objectives** - Work toward next rank

### DM Integration

- **Story Hooks:** Each background provides built-in quest lines
- **Complications:** Holdings can be threatened, requiring player action
- **Resources:** Higher ranks give players agency to solve problems creatively
- **Power Balance:** The income and resources are significant but not game-breaking

### Background Features and Class Balance

- Background features should complement, not replace, class features
- Influence and resources are narrative tools, not combat power
- High-rank backgrounds might make some challenges trivial (that's intended)
- Players should feel their background matters as much as their class
