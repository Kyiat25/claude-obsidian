# Dór-o Estel Setting & Campaign — Map of Content

> A Map of Content (MOC) links into a cluster of related atomic notes. It is a navigation hub, not a container. Notes are not stored under this MOC; they live flat under `wiki/notes/` and are reached by following these links.

## Why this MOC exists

Split off from [[Dór-o Estel Cosmology MOC]] on 2026-07-14, once that cluster grew past ~370 pages and clearly outgrew a single navigation hub trying to serve two different needs at once: deep mythology/theology reference (which pantheon, which Eldritch god, which glyph layer) versus "what do I need to actually run this session" campaign material (which city, which faction, which villain, what's the current crisis). This MOC covers the second kind — geography, political factions, the Loom-Cities, and the campaign's structural BBEG framework. [[Dór-o Estel]] itself remains dual-listed under [[FEARS MOC]], [[Dór-o Estel Cosmology MOC]], and this MOC as the genuine bridge point between all three.

## Core notes

### The Loom-Cities
- [[Loom-Cities]] — the roster hub; five Thread-anchored cities, each holding one of the setting's five World Anchors
- [[Kel Varyan]] — Water Thread, the Amethyst Council + Senate of Salt and Scale, imperial capital
- [[Thar Vornhal]] — Earth Thread, the Stone Tribunal and the Gorge Wards, two cities in one
- [[Veltharyn]] — Fire Thread, the Sovereign Crucible carved into Mount Taz'karreth
- [[Selveran]] — Spirit Thread, the Living City grown from the Split Seed of Vaelora
- [[Aeravae]] — the hybrid Root-Anchor/World-Anchor Loom-City, built as Ashkarra's cooperative answer
- [[Ashkarra]] — the sixth, fallen Loom-City; the setting's central historical trauma, its physical ruins now part of [[Vashkora]]
- [[Threadbearers]] and the [[Loom-Wardens]] — the mortals and the secret organization bound to the Anchor system
- [[The Threadbearer Crisis]] — the live campaign hook: four of five Threadbearers assassinated, a three-way race over each new manifestation, and (per the production Session 0-1 module) a hidden architect in [[Kel Varyan]]'s own court

### Continents and regions
- [[Elarion]] — the most populous continent, imperial heartland, home to [[Kel Varyan]] and [[Veltharyn]]; the Eight Major Powers and the Threadbearer Crisis's continental impact
- [[Corvessa]], [[Talanthar]], [[Zhen-Lan]], [[Deepstone Cantons]], [[Lyrran]], [[Virelion]] — six of Elarion's Eight Major Powers, each with its own page; [[Marshlands]] (the twelve River-Keeper families) and [[Eight Guild Professions]] (the cross-continental craft-certification system) round out Elarion's cast
- [[Vashkora]] — Ashkarra's home continent, now an Anomalous Root Zone; [[Islands of Eternal Green]], a chain of World Tree oases destroyed five days before Ashkarra's own fall
- [[Mirror Chain]] — the archipelago continent hosting [[Selveran]]; [[The Hollowdeep]] — the setting's Underdark, seven descending layers from mundane mining concessions to pre-Weave Eldritch alienness
- [[Reedford]], [[Thorned Concord]] — named locations with their own dedicated pages; [[The Crimson Root Isles]] and [[Sylvenmere]] — hidden/destroyed World-Tree sanctuaries flagged as critical to multiple campaign arcs

### Political and economic factions
- [[Stone Ledger Compact]] — the trans-Loom "World-Bank" seated at [[Thar Vornhal]], governed by the Seven Debtwrights, deciding which civilizations survive collapse
- [[The Twilight Markets]] — a continent-spanning underworld commerce network run by the nine-seat Council of Veils, occupying the threshold between surface civilization and [[The Hollowdeep]]

### The BBEG campaign framework
- [[Campaign Arcs (The Great Deceivers and Truth-Seekers)]] — the master 6-category, 17-BBEG villain framework (Suppressors, Revealers, Ascenders, Rewriters, the Desperate Faithful, Cosmic Manipulators), all rooted in [[The Lie of Godhood]]'s "gods lied about creation" secret; includes a per-arc geographic placement table
- **10 arcs now have full dedicated pages** (2026-07-14, fifth sweep wave), each deep-read from its individual "COMPLETE CAMPAIGN GUIDE" source: [[Arc 1A - The Archivist Inquisition]], [[Arc 1B - The Threadbearer's Silence]] (resolves [[The Threadbearer Crisis]] in full), [[Arc 2A - The Prophet of Ashes]] (secretly an [[Xey'Tazun]] conspiracy), [[Arc 2B - The Elven Testimony]], [[Arc 2C - The Drow Revelator]], [[Arc 3A - The Architect of Souls]], [[Arc 3B - The Dragonborn Emperor]], [[Arc 4A - The Death-Rewriter]], [[Arc 4B - The Memory-Rewriter]] (supersedes an earlier, differently-conceived compact-summary version), [[Arc 4C - The Eldritch Rewriter]] (secretly a [[Zhurr'Kael]] conspiracy). Arcs 3C, 5A-5C, and 6A-6C remain compact-summary only.
- [[Session 0-1 - The River Journey]] — the actual ready-to-run opening adventure (Reedford → Kel Varyan by river barge)
- [[Reality Stability Score]] — a 0-100 campaign doom-clock tracking proximity to another Ashkarra-scale catastrophe
- [[The World That Still Holds]] — the "Memory Divide" theme: only elves personally remember [[The Unraveling]], everyone else must decide whether to believe them

### Recent events and practical reference
- [[The Burning of Sylvenmere]] — a recent-history massacre triggering a major, still-unfolding international crisis, directly enacting [[Campaign Arcs (The Great Deceivers and Truth-Seekers)|Arc 1A]]'s suppression logic
- [[Verdant Loom Calendar]] — the setting's official calendar; consulted by [[Eldritch Moon Rise Procedure]] and [[Seasonal Influencers]] (both under [[FEARS MOC]]) for seasonal random-event resolution

## Open questions / frontier

- [[The Threadbearer Crisis]]'s Serana-Tidecaller-is-Naleth-Spiralbound resolution comes from the production Session 0-1 module and the individual [[Arc 1B - The Threadbearer's Silence]] source, both agreeing on the identity but contradicting two earlier, more mythic-register sources' Kel-Varyan-vs-Aeravae ambiguity — treated as the best available answer, not a forced retcon. A second, unresolved layer: [[Arc 1B - The Threadbearer's Silence]] describes a lone insider murder, while the crisis's original framing describes an external coordinated Drow strike team — the two accounts of *how* the Threadbearers died aren't reconciled.
- Several named locations ([[Reedford]], [[Mirror Chain]]) and factions ([[The Twilight Markets]], [[Stone Ledger Compact]]) remain only partially read from very large source documents (2,000-3,900 lines each) even after the fifth sweep wave's deepening pass — full NPC rosters, location catalogs, and campaign-hook sections remain untranscribed, flagged in the manifest for a future session.
- Arcs 3C, 5A, 5B, 5C, 6A, 6B, and 6C still only exist as compact summaries on [[Campaign Arcs (The Great Deceivers and Truth-Seekers)]] — no individual `ARC` source files for these were found in `.raw/`, unlike the other 10 arcs.
- The two `Class_Lore_Compendiums_DorOEstel` files (24 classes × 1-2 pages of deep per-class mythology, distinct from the mechanical class pages under [[FEARS MOC]]) were confirmed genuinely valuable but deliberately deferred — a natural next-session target.
