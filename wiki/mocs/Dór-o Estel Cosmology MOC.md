# Dór-o Estel Cosmology — Map of Content

> A Map of Content (MOC) links into a cluster of related atomic notes. It is a navigation hub, not a container. Notes are not stored under this MOC; they live flat under `wiki/notes/` and are reached by following these links.

## Why this MOC exists

Split off from [[FEARS MOC]] on 2026-07-13, once the cosmology/pantheon/Eldritch/Vel'Zyren cluster grew past ~280 pages and clearly outgrew a single navigation hub shared with the ruleset. This MOC is DM-only setting lore for **Dór-o Estel**, the campaign setting — it is not FEARS-the-ruleset content, and (per [[Dór-o Estel]]'s own page) would remain true under a different ruleset entirely. Everything below is reachable from [[FEARS MOC]] via this page, and vice versa.

## Core notes

### The Primal Layer — four unaware forces, one hidden fifth
- [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]] — the founding source: [[The Weave]] as a five-Layer proving-ground cosmology, four unaware Primal Forces embodying the first Layer
- [[Astralor]] — the Forge; [[Aeloria]] — the Loom; [[The Whispering Dark]] — the Maw; [[The Restorer]] — the Wheel. Confirmed literal siblings (Astralor/Aeloria), "the Twins of Creation," per [[Twins of Creation]]
- **The knowledge-hierarchy contradiction — RESOLVED 2026-07-14**: who actually understands this cosmology? [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]] said Aeloria alone, secretly; [[Twins of Creation]] said the opposite (Aeloria doesn't even know [[The Restorer]] exists), backed 3-of-5 by [[Creation World]] and [[Creations Misunderstanding]]. Ruled in Twins of Creation's favor: [[The Restorer]] is the cosmology's most-aware Primal Force; Aeloria does not know it exists. Full comparison table on [[The Weave]], resolution also reflected on [[Aeloria]], [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]], [[The Restorer]], and [[The Whispering Dark]]
- [[Aeloria]]'s Great Silence — she erased herself from ALL memory, including [[Astralor]]'s specifically; the Spirit Pantheon is secretly her own hidden loom (`[!contradiction]` on [[Spirit Pantheon]])
- [[Divine Sparks]] — the accidental origin of the first gods, born from the collision of all four Primal Forces at once; deepened substantially by [[The Lie of Godhood]] (Mythalix vessel, four Spark origins, the Rite of Reckoning, the War of Ascendants) — which also frames [[Astralor]] as perceived by gods as "not a god, but the Law of Becoming"; **RESOLVED 2026-07-14** as an epistemology-vs-ontology split (Astralor is a real entity, but incomprehensible enough that gods only ever perceive the structural "Law" of him), not a contradiction
- [[The Lie of Godhood]] — the false-sovereignty thesis, plus a full 5-pantheon-plus-Eldritch-plus-Vel'Zyren "doctrine of godhood" comparison table (from `Pantheon_Perspectives_on_Godhood.md`): every pantheon internally consistent, every one wrong about something load-bearing

### The Glyph Layers — five laws of reality
- [[Primal Glyphs]] — the four immutable first-Law glyphs, one per Primal Force: [[Glyph of Will]], [[Glyph of Threads]], [[The Glyph of Endings]], [[The Glyph of Echoes]] (each now a full dedicated page)
- [[Universal Glyphs]] — second-Law regulators (Understanding, Life, Motion, Shadows, Dreaming)
- [[Elemental Glyphs]] — third-Law raw materials (Fire, Water, Earth, Air, Spirit)
- [[Form Glyphs]] — fourth-Law eternal blueprints of life (9 known forms; Eldritch corruption strain per glyph documented)
- [[Personal Glyphs]] — fifth-Law individual attunement (9 glyphs); shifts completely across [[Luminous Echo]] (Feywild) and [[Umbral Veil]] (Shadowfell)
- All five layers gained "Minor Variations of Primary Glyphs" sections from `Saga of the Glyphs.md` in the 2026-07-13 gap-closing wave

### World infrastructure
- [[Three Pillars of Magical Flow]] — [[Leylines]], [[Wells of Power]], [[Astral Conduits]]
- [[World Trees]] — true origin deliberately, permanently withheld even from the DM; **RESOLVED 2026-07-14**: [[World Anchors and Thread System]]'s "planted by Vatharun" is the mortal-comprehension tier of a tiered-knowledge hierarchy, not the DM-facing truth — mortals default to a single nameable deity because that's the largest act of creation they can conceive of. Some elder gods and Tree-bonded races (Wood Elves, Druids) understand the *consequences* of a Tree's destruction far better than mortals, without that implying they know the true origin either. Full "Layered Truth" tiered-knowledge framing and per-continent distribution table on the page itself
- [[Reflected Realms]] — the setting's Feywild/Shadowfell-equivalent planes (the Luminous Echo and the Umbral Veil), distinct from [[Luminous Echo]] and [[Umbral Veil]]'s own dedicated pages
- **The Loom-Cities (including [[Ashkarra]]), Threadbearers, continents/regions, factions, and the BBEG campaign framework moved to [[Dór-o Estel Setting & Campaign MOC]] on 2026-07-14** — split off once this MOC passed ~370 pages. Consult that MOC for [[Kel Varyan]], [[Elarion]], [[The Threadbearer Crisis]], [[Campaign Arcs (The Great Deceivers and Truth-Seekers)]], and every named location/faction; [[World Trees]] above remains here as the deeper cosmology behind the Loom-City Anchor system

### The Five Elemental Pantheons — full deity rosters
Every named deity across all five pantheons now has a dedicated page; each pantheon overview page carries hierarchy tables, origin myths, and its own "Perspective on godhood" section.

- [[Earth Pantheon]] — 36 deities, "The Lock That Holds the World" (+4 minor deities 2026-07-14: [[Frith]], [[Tovna]], [[Cairn]], [[Halix]])
- [[Fire Pantheon]] — 27 deities, "The First Lie"
- [[Water Pantheon]] — 65 deities, "the element that was always there" (+10 2026-07-14, including the long-red-linked [[Selmira]])
- [[Air Pantheon]] — 28 deities, "the sacred rebellion" (+1 2026-07-14: [[Wendrel]])
- [[Spirit Pantheon]] — 39 deities; secretly [[Aeloria]]'s own hidden loom (`[!contradiction]`) (+10 2026-07-14, completing the five-office death-cycle: [[The Echoed Mother]], [[Sulwen]], [[Serelune]], [[Restbringer]], [[Omrith]], [[Almerra]], [[Dovenna]], [[Hethra]], [[Tamsit]], [[Kyren]])
- **Two cross-pantheon naming collisions, both disambiguated rather than merged**: [[Ormara (Earth)]] vs. Water's own Ormara; [[Marethis (Air)]] vs. Water's Greater God Marethis
- **One unresolved cross-pantheon contradiction**: [[Xilthos]] is claimed almost identically by both Air and Fire under the same epithet ("the Whisper Before the Riot")

### Vel'Zyren cluster — the rival ascendant
- [[Vel'Zyren]] — the Web-Spinner; the only god in any pantheon to have successfully rewritten portions of the Weave without breaking reality ("I rewrote my own chains")
- [[The Trial of the Web-Spinner]] — her divine trial and binding
- [[The Fall of the Drow]] — the conversion event
- [[The Ashen Web]] — the resulting Drow monofaith
- [[The Court of the Silent Tribunal]] — the divine court where elemental crimes against the Weave are judged, including Vel'Zyren's own trial

### The Eldritch tier — cosmic disorder, three levels
- [[Guide to the Eldritch Forces]] — master campaign guide; three-tier hierarchy (Gods / Spirits / Moons), all spawned when [[The Whispering Dark]] first touched the Weave
- **9 Eldritch Gods** (8 core + [[Vezherak]] as a distinct Tether entity): [[Nhal'Zereth]], [[Ith'Quess]], [[Vael'Akir]], [[Xey'Tazun]], [[Ul'Naresh]], [[Zhurr'Kael]], [[Esh'Tarah]], [[Mirag'Nael]], [[Vezherak]]
- [[Eldritch Moons]] — 14 named moons (source-internal 12-vs-14 discrepancy, flagged not resolved), each with world/monster/madness effects plus glyph-interaction and divine-response mechanics
- [[Eldritch Spirits]] — 29 named entities across 5 categories (Echoes, Unfinished, Reflected, Dream-Born, Tethers)
- [[The Null Reflection]] — the anti-reality plane, distinct from [[Luminous Echo]] (Feywild) and [[Umbral Veil]] (Shadowfell)
- [[The Hollow Thread]] — the setting's designated apocalypse option; a corrupted leyline sealed beneath mountains by the [[Earth Pantheon]]

### Organized faiths — beyond the elemental pantheons (2026-07-14, 18 pages + 1 enrichment)
Practiced religions with their own doctrine, clergy, and rites, distinct from raw pantheon rosters. Several are race-specific and cross-referenced from the matching [[FEARS Lineages|lineage]] page.

- [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]] — the dominant, city-spanning religion of the Loom-Cities; treats all five elemental pantheons as equal Threads of one Weave, centered on [[Threadbearers]] and [[World Anchors and Thread System|World Anchors]]. A significant standing gap — referenced from 20+ existing pages but never itself written until this wave
- [[The Reflection Pattern]] — the older, pre-Faith Human religion; personal ancestral pantheons rather than one sanctioned doctrine
- [[The Woven Reverence]] — the Elven faith; attunement to the Weave rather than worship, predates the gods
- [[The Deep Law]] — the Dwarven faith; the Forgebound as makers who built and left, not gods to obey
- [[The Ashen Web]] — the Drow monofaith devoted entirely to [[Vel'Zyren]] (pre-existing page, enriched this wave with a fuller raw source covering rituals, divine identity, and the Drow's own origin account)
- [[The Ashen Testament]] — the Tiefling creed; sin is earned, not inherited, memory is the only afterlife
- [[The Way of the Named]] — the Half-Elf creed; identity forged through exile, trial, and a self-claimed name
- [[The Path of the First Scar]] — the Orcish faith; gods as adversaries earned through trial, honor as a literal currency of scars
- [[The Path of the Horizon Soul]] — the Goliath creed; divinity as endless ascent, gods as fellow climbers who never reached the summit
- [[The Joy Truths]] — the Halfling faith; joy and story as sacred, death as a companion rather than an enemy
- [[The Broken Crown and the Cracked Fang]] — the shared Goblin/Hobgoblin/Bugbear "Faith of the Gnawing Crown"; goblinoids as a discarded, jagged fragment of a divine Form Glyph
- [[The Wonder-Tithe Codex]] — the Gnomish devotional philosophy; divine salvage and archival curiosity, often mistaken for a Gnome pantheon-religion
- [[The Embers Echo]] — Kobold worldbuilding-texture religion; worship of "divine leftovers," not a documented playable lineage
- [[The Tide Covenant]] — the Triton faith; Water, Air, and Spirit bound together as identity rather than worship (merged from two raw sources, the original plus a fuller `_REWORKED` variant)
- [[The Deepwater Communion]] — the oldest oceanic faith, predating even the Tide Covenant and Verdant Accord; the ocean as one living, conscious being
- [[The Verdant Accord]] — the oldest primal druidic faith; balance through reciprocal transformation (growth through decay, healing through sacrifice)
- [[The Oath of the Veiled Star]] — prophecy-suppressing mystery faith of silence and unseen judgment; Aasimar- and Shadar-kai-aligned
- [[The Weave Unbroken]] — forbidden apocryphal cosmogony, the Song of the Five Layers, sung outside every sanctioned faith
- [[The Burning Concord]] — the Dragonborn caste-theocracy; divine law lived through mandatory branded caste, enforced by the Embermarked priesthood

### Cross-Pantheon Relationships — GM design references (2026-07-14, 7 pages)
A matched seven-node set analyzing how every pantheon relates to every other through a single "worship-pressure shapes the god" lens, closing on Vel'Zyren as the villain-prototype behind the setting's arc structure. **All flagged `doc-status: needs-rulings` — proposed GM design material, not settled canon**, distinct from the confirmed-canon pantheon pages above.

- [[Cross-Pantheon Relationships (Spirit)]] — Spirit as the medium every other pantheon's domain has a shadow-twin in
- [[Cross-Pantheon Relationships (Fire)]] — Fire as the usurper, universal reverence mistaken for fealty
- [[Cross-Pantheon Relationships (Water)]] — Water as the incumbent, patience as cosmic strategy
- [[Cross-Pantheon Relationships (Air)]] — Air as the dissenter, freedom that becomes the Eldritch's vector
- [[Cross-Pantheon Relationships (Earth)]] — Earth as the capstone, jailer of the apocalypse (the Hollow Vault)
- [[Cross-Relationships of the Eldritch]] — the Eldritch as a wound co-natal with creation, not an outside invader; the Seal/Forget/Cut containment triad
- [[Cross-Relationships of Vel'Zyren]] — the seventh node; her Six Silent Designs proposed as the source-pattern for the setting's arc-villains
