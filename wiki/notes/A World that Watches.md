# A World that Watches

Subtitled "Navigating Disposition, Reputation, and Mental Consequence." Introduces the social and psychological systems for **[[Dór-o Estel]]**, the setting these FEARS mechanics are demonstrated in. Frontmatter marks it `status: wip` — treat as an evolving design doc like [[Welcome to FEARS]], not a finalized ruleset.

## What it covers

- **Public standing**: [[Disposition]] (NPC starting attitude), driven by [[Wanted Score]] (criminal notoriety) and [[Reputation]] (heroic/cruel/notorious framing).
- **Underground systems**: [[Black Market]] (illicit goods and services) and [[Hideouts]] (secret bases, at risk from a high Wanted Score).
- **The Mental Triad**: [[Sanity]], [[Focus]], and [[Corruption]] — three interlocking mental-state tracks that also visibly alter how the world perceives the character.
- **Fate mechanic**: [[Luck]] — three natural 1s curses you, three natural 20s blesses you.
- **Knowledge skills**: [[Memory]], [[Occult]], [[Streetwise]] — each shifts NPC interactions or unlocks lore/location access.
- References an optional rule, **[[Whisper Engine]]**, previously only mentioned in passing inside [[Dynamic Action Economy]] — this source confirms it's a distinct, separately-documented optional subsystem.

## Connection to Welcome to FEARS

[[Translation Matrix]] (from [[Welcome to FEARS]]) states the 5E-to-FEARS conversion adds "Focus and Sanity attributes and streetwise skills" but gives no detail. This source is the first place those three actually get defined — [[Sanity]], [[Focus]], and [[Streetwise]] pages here should be treated as the canonical elaboration of that earlier one-line claim. No contradiction found; this fills a gap rather than conflicting.

## Editorial note

Same promotional, second-person voice as [[Welcome to FEARS]] ("your story is not told in isolation... it echoes"). No numeric resolution mechanics given for any system here except Luck's three-natural-1s/three-natural-20s trigger — Disposition, Wanted Score, and the Mental Triad are all described only in terms of narrative/behavioral effect, not dice math.
