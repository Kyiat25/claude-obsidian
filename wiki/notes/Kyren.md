# Kyren, The Seer at the Fray

*"Xelthar sees too much and it broke him. I see through the place where seeing breaks. There is a difference. I can no longer tell you what it is."*

**Domains**: sight through frayed threads, prophecy at the tear's edge, knowledge from where the Weave ends. **Symbol**: an eye whose iris is an unravelled thread. **Worshiped by**: the most desperate oracles; cults that should be watched.

Every Spirit seer looks *along* the threads; Kyren looks *through the gaps* where threads have frayed — and what can be seen through a tear in the Weave is not the future, it is whatever is on the other side. His prophecies are accurate about things that have no business being knowable (the position of a sealed door, a name under erasure, the weather in a place that does not exist yet). Petitioners ask one question and pay in advance: a memory, surrendered before the answer, chosen by him. His sites are places where the Weave has visibly frayed — no temple teaches how to find them; the faithful simply do.

> [!truth] DM-Truth
> Kyren is a Spirit god partway frayed into the Eldritch, neither member nor casualty. His sight is genuine, and its mechanism is exactly what his enemies fear — the "other side" of the tears he reads through is the same "outside" [[The Whispering Dark]] whispers from. He is not an agent of the Eldritch; he is what a god looks like mid-process, halted — and no one, including Kyren, knows what halted it, or whether it has resumed.
