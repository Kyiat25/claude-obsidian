# Frith, The Little Peace

**Domains**: peace as a condition — the truce kept, the quiet year, the war that didn't happen. **Symbol**: an unstrung bow hung on a wall.

Peace has a god, and the god is small — doctrinally, provably, permanently small, because peace is only prayed to in war, and prayers for peace are mostly prayers for victory wearing better clothes; the pressure never accumulates. His clergy (perhaps dozens, anywhere, ever) consider his smallness the whole theology: "the god of peace is small because peace is what you stop noticing." His name seals the guest-peace of the setting's frith/guest-right custom — kept daily by millions who have never once said his name as a prayer. Either the least or the most worshipped god alive, and his handful of priests find the ambiguity very funny.
