# Eldritch Scholar — Rank 1-4 (Student of Forbidden Knowledge)

*"You study things mortals were not meant to know."* Tier 3 (Dark Fantasy). Fulfills the "Eldritch Scholar" slot [[FEARS Advanced Backgrounds]] flagged as not-yet-developed after Batch 1. This background's whole progression is built on a corrupting-power tradeoff — every rank explicitly costs part of the character's humanity, unlike most other backgrounds where advancement is purely additive.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Curious Student | Forbidden Lore | Decipher a major forbidden text, survive an eldritch horror encounter, or discover a cosmic truth |
| 2 | Occult Researcher | Glimpse Beyond | Summon and survive an eldritch entity, prevent a catastrophe with forbidden knowledge, or trade away humanity for greater knowledge |
| 3 | Master of Forbidden Arts | Touch of the Void | Discover a reality-reshaping truth, prevent or cause an eldritch apocalypse, or transform into something more than human |
| 4 | Eldritch Prophet/Herald | Bridge Between Worlds | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Curious Student

**Skill Proficiency**: Arcana, Investigation. **Language**: two of choice (one should be ancient/dead). **Equipment**: a forbidden tome, charcoal sticks for transcribing runes, dark robes, 15 gp.

**Feature — Forbidden Lore**: advantage on Arcana checks related to aberrations, curses, or eldritch entities; identify eldritch symbols and sigils; know rumors of forbidden places and texts. **Cost**: your studies give you nightmares — once per week, CON save DC 10 or gain no benefit from your next long rest.

**Research Focus** (choose your obsession): The Outer Dark, Ancient precursors, The void between worlds, Forbidden rituals, Cursed artifacts, The true nature of reality.

**Holdings**: a small collection of disturbing texts; a mentor who guides (or misleads) you; access to a restricted library section.

## Rank 2: Occult Researcher

**Holdings**: a growing library of forbidden texts (worth 200 gp); income from wealthy patrons seeking knowledge (25 gp/month); a network of 5-8 fellow forbidden-knowledge scholars; protective wards on your study; a minor eldritch artifact or grimoire.

**Feature Expansion — Glimpse Beyond**: cast *detect magic* once per day; advantage on saves against mind-affecting magic from aberrations; understand eldritch writings, even unknown languages, with time and effort; suffer disturbing visions (the DM can give cryptic danger warnings). **Cost**: your appearance has subtly changed — unnatural eye color, strange dreams leak into reality around you.

## Rank 3: Master of Forbidden Arts

**Holdings**: an extensive forbidden-text library (worth 1,500 gp, some unique); significant income from desperate patrons (80 gp/month); a hidden sanctum warded against intrusion; several bound minor entities or familiars; students who study under you at great risk; a network of occult scholars; your research is referenced fearfully by others.

**Feature Expansion — Touch of the Void**: resistance to psychic damage; communicate with aberrations; once per week, invoke eldritch power (cast a warlock spell up to 3rd level, DM-determined); your presence unnerves animals and some people; perform rituals bordering on miracles or horrors. **Cost**: you don't age normally — ambiguous whether this is a blessing or a curse.

## Rank 4: Eldritch Prophet/Herald

**Holdings**: a legendary, priceless forbidden-knowledge collection; a strange income you don't need but comes anyway; a sanctum existing partially outside reality; a cult or student group (20-30); direct contact with entities beyond reality; your research forms new occult study branches; rulers and mages seek your counsel, fearfully.

**Feature — Bridge Between Worlds**: immune to madness and psychic damage; cast several warlock spells at will; once per month, ask an eldritch entity a question and receive a true but cryptic answer; reality bends around you in subtle ways; survive in the void, outer planes, or other impossible places; your existence prevents or invites eldritch incursions. **Cost**: you may not be entirely mortal anymore — time affects you strangely, your writings are sought and feared, some worship you and others want you dead, and upon death you might not stay dead, or might never have truly been alive.
