# Apprentice Spellcaster — Rank 1-4

*"You studied at a formal magical institution, learning the structured approach to arcane arts."* Batch 3, Magic-Focused category.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Junior Apprentice | Academic Access | Cast a difficult spell, complete a thesis on magical theory, or assist a master with important research |
| 2 | Advanced Apprentice | Scholarly Research | Publish magical research, discover a new spell variation, or defend your thesis before the magical council |
| 3 | Journeyman Mage | Magical Authority | Develop a new spell or technique, achieve a breakthrough in magical theory, or become a recognized school expert |
| 4 | Archmage/Master of the Arcane | Pinnacle of Magical Knowledge | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Junior Apprentice

**Skill Proficiency**: Arcana, History. **Tool Proficiency**: calligrapher's supplies. **Language**: one exotic language (Draconic, Celestial, Infernal, Primordial, Sylvan, or Abyssal). **Equipment**: a blank spellbook, calligrapher's supplies, common robes, academic insignia, 10 gp.

**Feature — Academic Access**: research spells and magical phenomena (advantage on Arcana checks in your library); borrow common spell scrolls for study (return within 1 week); attend lectures and demonstrations; seek advice from instructors.

**Holdings**: a shared dormitory room in the academy; access to a basic laboratory; a mentor or supervising instructor; a library card and restricted magical-text access.

## Rank 2: Advanced Apprentice

**Holdings**: a private study room; a small research stipend (15 gp/month); access to better laboratory equipment; permission to copy 2 spells/month from the academy library; a network of 5-8 fellow apprentices; access to uncommon spell scrolls.

**Feature Expansion — Scholarly Research**: identify spell effects automatically (DC 15 or lower); advantage on checks to understand new magical phenomena; spend downtime researching for temporary expertise in a topic; know other magical libraries and academies; recognize different magical traditions and schools.

## Rank 3: Journeyman Mage

**Holdings**: a personal tower room or sanctum; a good stipend or teaching income (60 gp/month); a complete laboratory with quality equipment; a personal library (worth 400 gp); 3-5 junior apprentices you instruct; access to restricted magical texts; you can create spell scrolls for the academy.

**Feature Expansion — Magical Authority**: identify any spell being cast; advantage on saves against spells you've studied; once per week, consult academy resources for a major magical problem; certify other spellcasters; your research is published in magical journals.

## Rank 4: Archmage/Master of the Arcane

**Holdings**: a personal tower or wing of the academy; excellent teaching/consulting income (200 gp/month); a comprehensive magical library (worth 3,000+ gp); a full laboratory with rare equipment; 15-20 apprentices and assistants; a voting seat on the magical council; your name on spells or techniques you developed.

**Feature — Pinnacle of Magical Knowledge**: cast *identify* at will without components; advantage on all Arcana checks (expertise if already proficient); once per month, contact other archmages for consultation; create unique magical items at DM discretion; your word on magical matters is authoritative. Young mages study your published works, governments consult you on magical threats, your research shapes magical practice, and you may serve as court wizard to royalty.
