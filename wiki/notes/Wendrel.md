# Wendrel, The Teller on the Wind

*"Tell it as you heard it, and say who you heard it from. That is the whole law. There is no second law. Stop asking about the other thing."*

**Domains**: storytelling, the kept versions, the tale carried onward — and, rarely, the telling that becomes true. **Symbol**: a speech-scroll unrolling into wind. **Worshiped by**: storytellers, wandering singers, grandmothers, liars of the better kind. **Enemies**: Earth's Thamur (the erasure of tales), [[Vel'Zyren]] (who would have one version).

His office is custody, not creation — the tale as it travels, teller to teller, version begetting version, held together by one commandment: tell it as you heard it, and say who you heard it from. His one heresy is the authoritative version; the day a story is corrected into a single truth, it stops being alive and his winds stop carrying it. He is Air's argument in miniature, and the reason the world's myths survive in competing accounts at all.

Very rarely — a handful of attested cases a century, ten claimed for every real one — a telling comes true (**the Truewind**). It cannot be invoked, aimed, earned, or repeated; tellers who chase it are the tradition's great cautionary fools. The attribution-chain ("as Maren told it, who had it from her father, who had it from a drowned man") is his sacrament: a tale with its chain intact is *witnessed*, and doctrine holds a witnessed tale is safer from the Truewind.

> [!truth] DM-Truth
> The Truewind is real, and Wendrel does not do it. A story intensely told and intensely believed is belief in miniature — worship-pressure at the smallest scale — and once in a great while a telling presses hard enough, in a thin enough place, that the Weave accommodates it: the same mechanism that condenses gods, misfiring at tale-size. Wendrel was almost certainly condensed *from* the phenomenon rather than being its author, and his clergy's containment-law is the most quietly important safety doctrine in mortal religion, maintained by people who believe it is about citation.
