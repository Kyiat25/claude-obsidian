# World Trees

Ancient natural stabilizers appearing at leyline connections, predating World Anchors by millennia, held sacred by multiple cultures. Cutting one down measurably stresses the Thread system. Per [[World Anchors and Thread System]]: 73-75 confirmed worldwide, 6-7 "Major Root Anchors," at least 5 known-corrupted, 7-9 lost to Loom-City expansion; for roughly 15,000 years ("the Loomless Time") they were the sole stabilization system in existence.

> [!note] Deliberately unexplained — by design, not by omission
> Unlike every other concept in this cosmology cluster, [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]] explicitly states World Trees' true origin is meant to stay a genuine mystery, **even to the DM**: "preserves cosmic wonder... prevents over-systematization... respects that some things transcend even divine knowledge." This is not a gap to be filled in by a future source — it's flagged as intentionally permanent. Don't resolve this the way other open threads in this cluster get resolved by later ingests; treat the ambiguity itself as canonical.

> [!contradiction] RESOLVED — A later source names a creator outright: Vatharun
> [[World Anchors and Thread System]] states as flat, unhedged background fact that World Trees were "planted by Vatharun (Earth Pantheon deity)" — no theory framing, no "some say," just an origin. This directly undercut the instruction above. Two ways to reconcile it were considered: (1) this is in-world *scholarly consensus*, itself possibly wrong, and the DM-facing mystery survives underneath it; or (2) the two sources simply weren't written consistently, same as the standing three-way Aeloria/Restorer/Whispering-Dark knowledge contradiction on [[The Weave]].
>
> **Ruled (2026-07-14):** reading (1). World Trees are woven into the cosmos at creation itself — genuinely older and stranger than any single deity's act of planting — and the DM-facing mystery from [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]] stands: **no one, not even the DM, definitively knows their true origin.** But comprehension of them is tiered, and "Vatharun planted them" is simply the mortal tier of that hierarchy: ordinary mortals can only conceive of creation at the scale of a single, nameable deity with a plausible domain (stone, growth, permanence), so Vatharun — Earth Pantheon, geologically-themed — is the natural, comprehensible answer they land on, true or not. It isn't a lie exactly; it's the largest explanation that fits inside a mortal mind. See the expanded tiered structure below, and "Who understands the stakes" for what sits above the mortal tier.

## Five competing theories, none canonical

1. **Astralor's Original Design** — his first stabilization attempt, planted before he created the gods, working better than later additions because closer to his original, uncorrupted intent.
2. **Aeloria's Manifestations** — physical embodiments of her stabilizing corrections, growing wherever her work is most needed; living representations of the Weave itself.
3. **The Restorer's Work** — recovered from previous failed Creation Worlds and brought forward as "what worked before," explaining why they seem older than this universe.
4. **Natural Weave Phenomena** — emerging spontaneously wherever reality needs stabilization, created by no one, simply a result of the Weave's own self-maintenance.
5. **Something Older** — remnants of a reality that predates even [[Astralor]] and [[Aeloria]] (the Twins of Creation), neither created nor maintained by them.

## The Layered Truth (a fuller, tiered version of the "five theories")

A separate, more systematized source frames the same unresolved-by-design mystery as **tiers of knowledge access** rather than five flat competing theories: mortals believe the gods planted the Trees (per the 2026-07-14 ruling above, specifically **Vatharun**, as the most comprehensible face of "a deity planted them"); the Faith teaches they're divine manifestations of the Earth Thread; scholars suspect they predate organized religion (druidic oral history running 15,000+ years, pre-divine cultivation sites); druids preserve the position that "the gods found the Trees, became bound to them, and convinced mortals — and themselves — that they had planted them"; the Woven Reverence secretly guards the same conclusion as its "First Truth," judged too destabilizing to speak aloud; and, known only to the primal forces themselves, the Trees actually manifested when **Aeloria** wove biological stabilization into the Pattern during the Weave's formation — the Earth Pantheon arose *after* the Trees existed, bonded to them, and now hold genuine (mistaken) divine memories of "planting" them. Even this deepest documented tier is not guaranteed to be the final word: per the ruling above, true DM-facing certainty about World Trees' ultimate origin is deliberately withheld — this "Aeloria wove them" account is the most complete answer any source commits to, not confirmation that nothing sits beneath it.

## Who understands the stakes, if not the origin

Understanding *why* World Trees exist and understanding *what destroying one actually costs* are two different axes, and comprehension of the second is tiered independently of the first. Most mortals grasp neither. Certain elder gods — plausibly [[The Restorer]], whose entire function is reclaiming what's erased and would recognize exactly what a Tree's loss of stored *experience* (not just information) permanently removes from the cycle — and long-lived races **culturally bonded to specific Trees**, most notably Wood Elves (see [[World Anchors and Thread System]]'s note on the Verdant Accord) and Druids, understand the consequences of a Tree's destruction with a clarity ordinary mortals and even most gods lack — without necessarily understanding the origin any better than a scholar does. Knowing the stakes and knowing the truth are not the same kind of knowledge.

## Distribution and what World Trees do that Anchors can't

Same canonical totals as [[World Anchors and Thread System]] (73-75 trees, 6-7 Major Root Anchors, 5+ confirmed corrupted), broken out by continent: [[Elarion]] has 1 major + 9 lesser trees (including the corrupted **Unspoken Root**, buried beneath a Loom-City); Kerythra has 1 major + 13 lesser (including the semi-corrupted **Splintering Crown**); [[Vashkora]] has 1 major + 9 lesser, several damaged by [[Ashkarra]]'s collapse (including the partially-inverted **Weeping Knot**); the Mirror Chain Isles have 8 lesser coral-rooted trees with tidal-memory and emotion-binding rites. Functionally, World Trees out-perform World Anchors in several ways mortal Anchors can't replicate: they store *experience*, not just information, in bark and root; they naturally harmonize all five Threads at once rather than locking to a single Thread; and corruption shows biological warning symptoms (wilting, wildlife behavior changes) years or decades ahead of failure — unlike Anchors, which "explode without warning when corrupted."

## The Earth Pantheon's stewardship — real skill, false memory

A fuller variant of the same "Layered Truth" source names specific Earth Pantheon deities and how each relates to the Trees they falsely believe they created — "simultaneously effective stewards... and theological frauds... while being innocent victims of their own mythology." **Vatharun** (the Stone-Father) may be too geologically alien to even distinguish "planting" from simply *being* the bedrock the Trees' roots run through — he communicates only through earthquakes and mineral deposits. **Neldros** (the Rootfather) is among the best stewards of the Trees — reverent, protective, genuinely knowledgeable — yet completely and honestly believes the divine-creation myth he perpetuates; learning the truth might shatter his identity. **Gaeldros** (the Oathkeeper) built the Deep Law's truth-detecting oath system on a Tree property (bark/sap resonance encoding truth) he discovered rather than created. **Ishara** (the Verdant Queen) is so merged with Tree vitality that she can't distinguish her own body from theirs — her presence is real, just not original. **Orruuk** (Root Pressure) guards the most ancient, temporally dangerous groves, where root-communion can trap souls in memory-loops.

## The Echo-Cenote: a case study in the stakes

[[Aeravae]]'s hybrid Root-Anchor/World-Anchor — see [[Aeravae]] for the full founding story, **resolved 2026-07-14**: the graft itself was a deliberate, suppressed multi-faction bargain (the Faith plus a broader coalition of older nature-bound traditions, not just wood elf druids), but the fusion's total, unprecedented success was the genuine accident nobody could have engineered — demonstrates concretely what World Trees can do that pure Anchors can't: it is, per this source, **the only Anchor in the network that has never failed or risked catastrophic failure**, combining the Tree's self-repair and corruption resistance with the Anchor's Thread-channeling and Threadbearer mechanics. The political question this raises deliberately goes unanswered in-setting: if grafting works this well, why hasn't it been done to the other four Anchors — and who would benefit if it were?

## Campaign use

Different in-world factions can hold different (and mutually exclusive) beliefs about World Trees without any of them being provably wrong. Discovering "the truth" could be a legitimate campaign goal — but the source explicitly allows for the answer to be genuinely unknowable, or for multiple answers to be simultaneously true. Framed as a deliberate reminder that "reality doesn't owe mortals complete understanding."
