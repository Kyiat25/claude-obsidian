# Loom-Wardens

A secret organization operating within and around the **Faith of the Bound Pattern** (see [[Pattern Keeper]]), manipulating theology, Thread policy, and individual fates in the belief that they serve some impersonal force — when in truth every one of their actions unknowingly serves [[Aeloria]] directly.

## Structure

**Public face**: some members are high-ranking Faith officials, others scholars, [[Threadbearers]], or seemingly uninvolved citizens. No visible hierarchy. Members communicate through coded messages and engineered "coincidences."

**Secret reality**: an ancient organization predating the Faith of the Bound Pattern itself, possibly tracing back to the original Threadbearers or earlier. Operates through cells that don't know about each other; whether any central leadership exists at all is unknown even within the organization.

**Membership**: recruited through mysterious means — often prophetic dreams or visions, or simply a felt "calling" to serve balance. Members don't understand what they're actually serving; many believe it's "the Weave" or "fate itself."

## What they actually do

- **Manipulate the Faith**: guide theological interpretation, influence which Threadbearers get formally "recognized," shape Thread-rotation policy, suppress extremist factions, and prevent the Faith from discovering the uncomfortable truths above their own understanding.
- **Fate-weaving**: identify individuals whose fate "needs correction," engineer coincidences toward necessary outcomes, and — per unconfirmed rumor within their own ranks — sometimes literally reweave a person's fate through unknown rituals.
- **Protect the system**: guard World Anchors against Eldritch corruption, monitor World Tree health, investigate threats to Thread stability.
- **Preserve knowledge**: maintain hidden libraries of forbidden/pre-Loom cosmology, and actively research what Threadbearers actually experience.

## Their own competing theories about what they serve

Three theories circulate within the organization, none of them correct:

1. **The Eternal Weave Theory** — the Weave itself is conscious and self-correcting; they serve the Pattern, not a person or god.
2. **The Hidden Hand Theory** — an unknown force beyond even the gods guides fate; they are its chosen instruments (names used internally: the Unseen Weaver, the Silent Guide, the Balance).
3. **The Pre-Divine Theory** — something older than the gods maintains reality, predating [[Astralor]]'s Creation World entirely; [[World Trees]] are read as its manifestations.

**The actual truth, known to none of them**: they serve [[Aeloria]] specifically. She guides them through subtle fate-adjustments they experience as intuition or calling, ensures they succeed often enough to stay motivated and fail often enough to stay unsuspicious, and — per the source — would erase herself from their awareness entirely if any of them ever learned her actual name.

## Relationship to Threadbearers

The Loom-Wardens study [[Threadbearers]] obsessively — sensing they touch something profound, trying to understand or replicate their perception, and possibly manipulating them (unknowingly, in Aeloria's service) without realizing every Threadbearer they've ever protected or tested was serving the exact same hidden purpose the Wardens themselves serve.

## Campaign implications (as stated in the source)

Could be secret allies, could be manipulating the party toward "fated" outcomes, may know more about an active Threadbearer crisis than they let on, may be internally divided into competing factions, and their morality is deliberately ambiguous — they genuinely believe they serve good, but their methods (fate manipulation, possibly sacrificing individuals for "the greater pattern") can be manipulative or cruel regardless of whether their underlying belief in cosmic necessity turns out to be justified.

> [!note] The "active Threadbearer crisis" referenced above now has a name
> [[World Anchors and Thread System]] confirms exactly the crisis this page's source anticipated: [[The Threadbearer Crisis]], in which four of five Threadbearers have been assassinated by Drow operatives. Given the Loom-Wardens' stated role guarding Anchors and monitoring Threadbearer recognition, they are a natural (if unconfirmed) faction to place on the "protective" side of that crisis's three-way race — or, given their own internal factionalism, potentially split across more than one side of it.
