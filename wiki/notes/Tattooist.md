# Tattooist — Power Written in Flesh

**Half-Caster tier**. "No other class writes power directly into the bodies of their allies. No other class turns the DCS's location system into a source of strength rather than vulnerability." A durable enhancer whose ink both empowers and protects specific body locations, directly plugging into the DCS's per-location DR system. Complexity self-rated MODERATE (5/10) by its own per-class deep-dive.

> "No other class writes power directly into the bodies of their allies. No other class turns the DCS's location system into a source of strength rather than vulnerability."

## Signature abilities

- **Magic Tattoos**
- **Spellbangers**
- **Anatomical Precision**

**Gameplay style**: enhancer, improving allies continuously. **Core loop**: apply → enhance → adjust → maintain. Effects persist between encounters and accumulate across a campaign, distinguishing it from turn-by-turn buffers.

## Class basics (from Tattooist.md deep-dive)

- **Hit Dice**: 1d8/level. HP at 1st: 8 + CON mod. HP at higher levels: 1d8 (or 5) + CON mod/level after 1st.
- **Proficiencies**: Armour — light. Weapons — simple. Tools — painter's supplies, tattooist's tools, one artisan's tool of choice. Saves — Constitution, Charisma. Skills — choose two from Arcana, History, Insight, Investigation, Medicine, Nature, Religion.
- **Starting Equipment**: (a) dagger or (b) any simple weapon; (a) diplomat's or (b) explorer's pack; an enchanted sketchbook, tattooist's tools, artisan's inks.
- **Quick Build**: Constitution highest, then Charisma. Any lineage (Human, Tiefling, Dragonborn thematic). Entertainer/Guild Artisan or an Arcana/Performance background. Apply Bad Ink to the party's striker pre-combat; use Flash of Art proactively against Called Shots at unprotected locations; prioritize Sketch Tattoo assignments on the party's most-exposed locations.

**Explicit statement, directly contradicting an existing table**: "Weapon Mastery: Tattooists do not gain Weapon Mastery. Your power flows through ink and design, not through martial perfection." See the [[Weapon Mastery]] contradiction below — this is stronger than a silent gap, since it's a direct denial.

## Core class features (full text)

### Spellcasting (1st level) — fifteenth confirmed DC formula, first Constitution-based

Casts via **spellbangers** — fingertip tattoos storing spell energy, sketched during a long rest. Constitution-based: **Spell Save DC = 8+PB+CON, Spell Attack = PB+CON** — fifteenth confirmation of the `8+PB+ability mod` formula, and the **first on Constitution** (previous confirmations spanned WIS/CHA/INT). Knows 2 first-level spellbangers from the Bard/Sorcerer/Warlock/Wizard lists; cast at their lowest applied level, or higher by expending multiple copies of the same spell. Spellbangers work only for the Tattooist — non-transferable, inert if removed from the skin. Normal casting time; no material components unless gold-costed; concentration still required if the spell needs it.

### Sketchy Imitation (1st level)

Being hit by a Bard/Sorcerer/Warlock/Wizard spell allows an Arcana check (DC = 10+spell level) to add it as a known spellbanger for 24 hours; after the next long rest, may permanently learn it (replacing a known spell).

### Etched Flow (1st level) — new Momentum gain source

Activating a tattoo, applying Bad Ink, or using Art Loan in combat grants **+1 Momentum**.

### Sketch Tattoo (2nd level)

Learn 4 Common-rarity tattoo designs; replace two on levelling. 1-hour sketch during a rest creates a tattoo in the sketchbook; attunable immediately, counts against normal attunement limits. Rarity caps: Common at 2nd, Uncommon at 6th, Rare at 14th. Requires materials at ~half market value. **Body Location** (Head, Neck, Chest, Back, Arms, Legs) chosen on application, feeding Inscribed Ward and Anatomical Precision. Copying an existing tattoo: 1 hour + materials (3 hours + CON check, DC 15+2/rarity tier above the Tattooist's limit, for higher-rarity copies; failure wastes materials); copies don't count against the known total.

### Inscribed Ward (2nd level) — new Damage Reduction class hook

While attuned to a tattoo at a body location, gain **+1 DR** there, stacking with armour DR. Mapped directly onto the DCS body-location system: Head/Neck→Head DR, Chest/Back→Torso DR, Arms→Arm DR, Legs→Leg DR. Self-only until Deep Inscription (14th) extends it to tattoos made for others.

### Tattooist Style (3rd level, and 5th, 9th, 15th)

Choose **Neo Traditional** (warrior-artist, volatile weapon ink, spell-and-strike fusion) or **Surrealism** (arcane experimenter, extreme self-modification, spellbanger empowerment). Features at 3rd/5th/9th/15th — not detailed further here.

### Bad Ink (3rd level) — second new Momentum gain source

Bonus action: apply volatile ink to a held or nearby willing creature's weapon. Next hit deals bonus acid damage per charge (1d6 at 3rd, 1d8/5th, 1d10/10th, 1d12/15th); vanishes after the hit or in 1 hour unused; the weapon counts as magical while inked. Charges = CON mod, full recovery on a long rest. **Triggering on a hit grants the wielder +1 Momentum** — distinct from (and additional to) Etched Flow's trigger on *applying* the ink.

### Anatomical Precision (5th level) — new Called Shot access route and defensive interactions

**Called Shot Awareness**: advantage on the Evasion roll against a Called Shot targeting a location bearing an active tattoo. **Tattoo Exploitation**: making a Called Shot against a creature with a visible magical effect/tattoo/enchantment at the target location reduces the penalty by 1. **Ally Protection**: an ally wearing one of the Tattooist's tattoos gets +1 Evasion against a Called Shot targeting that same location.

### Trust the Process (6th level)

3 more Uncommon designs; sketching drops to 30 minutes; a rest can relocate an existing tattoo without re-sketching.

### Art Loan (7th level)

Action, tattooist's tools in hand: transfer a magic tattoo to a willing creature within 5 ft with a free attunement slot — full benefit including DCS location bonuses. Returns after a rest. One Art Loan per recipient at a time. Triggers Etched Flow if used in combat.

### Counterfeiter (11th level)

Long rest: apply one 6th/7th-circle spellbanger. Using Art Loan retains a faded copy (half benefit, rounded down) for the Tattooist while the original is loaned; vanishes when it returns.

### Tattoo Mastery (11th level)

Once/long rest, activate any tattoo without its normal resource cost.

### Master's Touch (13th level)

+1 to all saving throws per currently-attuned magic tattoo (max +3).

### Master Tattooist (14th level)

Unlocks Rare-rarity tattoos; +2 Rare designs known. **Deep Inscription**: tattoos made for others now also grant +1 DR at the tattooed location, matching the Tattooist's own Inscribed Ward.

### Inked Resilience (15th level) — combined Injury/Momentum hook

Suffering an Injury: sacrifice an active tattoo (inactive until the next long rest) to downgrade the Injury a tier (Critical→Major, Major→Minor, Minor→negated) **and** ignore any Momentum penalty from that Injury — combining a tier-downgrade shape (like Paladin/Bard/Cleric) with a Momentum-penalty-ignore shape (like Rogue's Nerve-Steeled) in a single feature, the first hook to merge both. Once/long rest (twice from 18th).

### Living Gallery (17th level)

With 3+ tattoos applied: **Adaptive Resistance** (resistance to one chosen damage type, changeable on a long rest), **Sympathetic Network** (allies wearing a Tattooist tattoo get +1 Evasion within 60 ft), **Gallery Activation** (once/long rest, action, 1 minute: +2 all saves, Inscribed Ward DR doubles, 20 ft light aura).

### Genius Talent (18th level)

With 3+ tattoos: 2 extra attunement slots beyond normal limits; an 8th/9th-circle spellbanger per long rest; two design swaps per long rest instead of only on level-up.

### Heroic Boon (10th level)

Choose: **Boon of Artistic Mastery** (15-minute sketching; Inscribed Ward DR → +2/location) or **Boon of Living Canvas** (once/short rest, 1 minute of advantage on Evasion for all tattoos activated defensively; Art Loan retains a full copy, both parties benefit fully).

### Epic Boon (20th level)

Choose: **Boon of the Eternal Artist** (tattoos permanent, unremovable except by choice or *wish*; immune to Critical-Injury suppression; no aging; once/day, manifest and cast any spell ≤7th level from any class list as a temporary tattoo) or **Boon of the Grand Design** (allies within 30 ft wearing a tattoo get +1 attack/damage/saves per currently-applied Tattooist tattoo, max +3; once/long rest, 10 minutes to create a 24-hour temporary legendary tattoo at maximum potency, no attunement required).

## Last Stand (confirmed, 19th class of 20 ingested — standard permanent-death clause)

| Option | Effect |
|---|---|
| Ink of Defiance | 30 ft blast; hostiles CON save (DC 10+PB+CON) — fail: 10d10 acid+force, blinded until end of next turn; success: half, not blinded. Allies in the blast gain +3 Momentum |
| Last Stroke of the Artist | Cast any known spellbanger spell instantly, ignoring components/level/casting time, at its highest possible level; permanently dies after it resolves even if the spell would heal/stabilise |
| Eternal Canvas | Up to 3 allies within 30 ft each receive a tattoo/active spellbanger for 10 minutes; each gains +2 Evasion/saves and +2 Momentum; tattoos fade to scars when the effect ends |

Nineteenth class confirming [[Last Stand]] as universal (of 20 per-class files ingested — [[Sorcerer]] remains the sole exception with no Last Stand section at all); all three options reaffirm the standard permanent-death clause (no exception — Bender remains the lone confirmed exception). Ink of Defiance's DC (`10+PB+CON`) adds another data point to the `10+PB+ability` group.

## Class Reactions (confirmed, 20th ingest — spellbanger-layered)

Draws from the shared **Proficiency Bonus pool**, refreshing short/long rest. Ink Flare layers a spellbanger cost on top — a fourth class this session (after [[Magus]]'s spell slots, [[Reaper]]'s Soul Tokens, [[Sangromancer]]'s HP) to use this resource-layering shape, here with the spellbanger itself as the secondary cost.

| Reaction | Level | Trigger | Effect | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Inkguard | 2nd | A visible creature hits with an attack | +PB to Evasion until end of the current turn; if this raises Evasion above the attack roll, the attack retroactively misses | 1 use |
| Ink Flare | 3rd | A creature within 10 ft hits with a melee attack | Attacker DEX saves (DC = Spell Save DC) or takes 3d6 acid (scaling to 4d6/11th, 5d6/17th) + disadvantage on its next attack; success halves damage, no disadvantage | 1 use + 1 spellbanger |
| Flash of Art | 7th | A visible creature targets an ally within 30 ft with an attack | Ally gains +2 Evasion + resistance to the triggering damage type; if it still hits, reduce damage by PB+CON mod (after resistance). At 15th, also +2 to all saves until the Tattooist's next turn | 1 use |

Inkguard's retroactive hit-to-miss conversion is a genuinely novel Evasion interaction — no other class's reaction turns a confirmed hit into a miss after the fact rather than reducing/preventing damage.

## Mythology & Cultural Lore (from the Class Lore Compendium)

*Ink-Weavers: Those Who Write Magic on Living Canvas.*

Tattooists claim their art predates civilization — the first tattoos were survival tools, not decoration: protection-marks warding evil, strength-symbols channeling power, identity-glyphs preventing soul-theft. Kira the Illustrated, a human whose entire body carried 347 active functional spells, exemplifies the tradition's furthest extreme; her tattoos eventually gained semi-consciousness, her skin becoming a living spellbook that sometimes cast independently of her will. The Second Age's god-binding ink provoked the Ink-Purge, gods burning tattooed rebels alive to destroy their art with them, and Ashkarra's Fall killed thousands of tattooists whose living tattoo-libraries — the "Burning Archives" — died with their bearers. Thread instability now drives a Fourth Age renaissance, since ink written directly onto living flesh stays stable where stored spells fail.

**Source of Power (DM truth):** Tattoo magic accesses the [[Personal Glyphs]] — the fundamental force governing individual identity. Because the magic is written directly onto flesh, it becomes genuinely *part* of the bearer rather than a separate tool, making tattoo magic the most intimate form of magic in Dór-o Estel: the tattooist is literally editing their own existence.

**Legendary figures:** Kira the Illustrated, whose 347 active tattoos eventually became semi-conscious, her skin functioning as a living spellbook capable of casting independently.

*"Your power lives in books. In staves. In holy symbols you can lose. Mine? Mine is written on my skin. Permanent. Present. Personal... I am my magic. My magic is me. Inseparable. Eternal. True."* — The Tattooist's Truth
