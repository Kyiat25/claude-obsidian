# Reputation

One of the named inputs to [[Disposition]] — framed as the qualitative "how you're remembered" axis (heroic, cruel, notorious) distinct from [[Wanted Score]]'s purely criminal/legal tracking. `status: excluded`. A previously-untracked dedicated source now supplies the concrete scale [[A World that Watches]] left undefined.

## Reputation tiers

0 Unknown ("Wanderer," "No-One") → 1 Local (Hamlet/Village scope, e.g. "Woodswitch of Greerfield") → 2 Regional (Towns/minor cities, e.g. "Traitor of Ashkarra") → 3 National (Cities, nobles, clergy, e.g. "Queen's Foe") → 4 Global (Metropolis/Empire scope, e.g. "Living Legend"). Titles are **not always accurate** — earned truthfully, exaggerated, or completely misunderstood — and scale independently per region and per faction: the same character can be feared in one city and revered in another. An optional Fame Points rule lets a GM assign abstract points per public deed, promoting the character up a tier at set thresholds.

## Distinct from Disposition and Wanted Score

Explicit three-way distinction: Disposition tracks a specific NPC's current attitude (immediate/local, shaped by roleplay); [[Wanted Score]] tracks criminal heat and legal pursuit (legal/global); Reputation tracks public identity and cultural memory (narrative/zone-scoped). Fame grants Persuasion advantage with admirers and easier access to courts/temples; infamy brings Stealth/Deception disadvantage in known zones, harassment, and magical name-tracking. Relevant skills: History (know your own legend), Insight (gauge how it's being read), Deception (downplay it), Performance (shape the retelling), and [[Streetwise]] (track how it's spreading or distorting).
