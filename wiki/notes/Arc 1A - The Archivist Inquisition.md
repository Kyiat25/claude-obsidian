# Arc 1A — The Archivist Inquisition

The full deep-dive behind [[Campaign Arcs (The Great Deceivers and Truth-Seekers)|Category 1: The Suppressors]]'s flagship arc. *"She might be right. Every time faith has wavered, thousands have died."*

## High Archivist Velshara the Keeper

High Elf, 612 years old, Chief Archivist of the Prisada Arcana (Grand Library) in [[Kel Varyan]] for 325 years. Witnessed [[Ashkarra]]'s fall firsthand at age 125. Spent her first 200 years as a genuinely honest scholar, cross-referencing dendrochronology, dwarven architectural records, elven memory-songs, and oceanic coral formations across three continents — all independently converging on the same conclusion: mortals existed 15,000+ years before the gods claimed to have created them 10,000 years ago.

### The turning points

**The Cascade of Doubt (289 4A, 198 years before present)**: in [[Thar Vornhal]], Stone-Speaker **Thorin Deepdelver** — Velshara's colleague and friend — published rigorous proof that the Deep Anvil predates divine-creation myths by thousands of years. Within 30 days: riots across three cities, 847 dead, 2,300 injured, economic collapse. Thorin was publicly executed despite being correct. Velshara watched the Stone Tribunal burn its own historical records to preserve orthodoxy.

**The Theological Riots of 342 4A (145 years before present)**: a gnomish researcher in Kel Varyan published proof that organized magic predates pantheon formation. Same pattern, faster: riots across five cities, 1,200+ dead before military intervention.

**Her conclusion**: revelation → Faith condemnation → public violence → mass death → suppression anyway. Every time. So why not suppress *before* the deaths? She burned her own published research and began building the Archivist Inquisition the next day (Year 200 4A, 287 years before present). Her math: 127 targeted eliminations over 287 years versus 2,000+ deaths every decade truth leaks publicly. *"I do not suppress truth out of malice. I suppress it out of mathematics."*

## The Archivist Inquisition

**Public face**: the Prisada Arcana itself — 50,000 students, 847 faculty, Imperial funding, genuine ongoing legitimate scholarship. Nobody suspects the empire's most respected historian.

**The Inner Circle (5 conspirators, full knowledge)**:
- **Velshara** — mastermind, sole person who knows every conspirator's identity, most stressed during the current Threadbearer Crisis.
- **Master Archivist Korveth Stonescript** (Dwarf, 340) — recruited after witnessing the Cascade of Doubt; was Thorin Deepdelver's independent-discovery colleague, about to publish confirming evidence when Velshara intercepted him. Most internally conflicted (Deep Law demands truth); most likely to defect.
- **Senior Researcher Mirielle Bloodquill** (Half-Elf, 180) — has inherited elven Pattern-sense and can literally, physically feel the wrongness of every suppression and every murder as Weave-level pain. Joined hoping for "controlled disclosure," has instead helped kill 43 people in 150 years. Ready to defect the instant a real alternative appears.
- **Chief Acquisitions Officer Tharyn Voidward** (Human, 67) — former Imperial intelligence officer who brought military efficiency to the Burner network (83 of the 127 total kills are his, in just 40 years). Dying of magical corruption from handling Eldritch artifacts (5 years left) and growing reckless as death approaches.
- **Arcane Librarian Whisper-That-Knows** (Drow, 290) — escaped the Hollowdeep 300 years ago, recruited 80 years ago for memory-manipulation skills. **Secretly a double agent**, quietly gathering evidence of the entire conspiracy for an eventual mass revelation of her own.

## Campaign structure

"The Burning Library," a 1-20 campaign beginning with a series of researcher deaths across the continent that look like accidents. Current context: the campaign opens during the ongoing [[The Threadbearer Crisis|Threadbearer Crisis]] (18 days after four Threadbearers died), which makes Velshara more dangerous and more desperate than usual — she believes even a minor theological crisis during this instability could trigger continental collapse.
