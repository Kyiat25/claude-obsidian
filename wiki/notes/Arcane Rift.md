# Arcane Rift

`status: excluded`. A visible tear in reality's magical fabric — a shimmering, humming crack in space — that empowers spells of its associated school while weakening the opposing school. Rift-school spells cast one level higher, crit on 19-20, and gain advantage on concentration checks; opposing-school spells suffer disadvantage on spell attacks, can't be upcast, crit only on a natural 20, and lose concentration more easily. Dealing double or more a target's HP with a rift-boosted spell forces a [[Sanity]] (or WIS/INT) check — failure triggers a Wild Magic Surge.

## Enhanced Arcane Rift

A more volatile, dangerous evolution: rift-school spells cast **two** levels higher and crit on 18-20; the first turn after the rift forms grants 2 actions and double movement (spell/attack/move only); casters may maintain one extra concentration spell from the rift's school. Every cast requires a Sanity check (`DC 10 + spell level`) — failure triggers a Wild Magic Surge, a natural 1 triggers 2 Surges plus Blowback, and even a spell-critical-hit (18-20) has a 1-in-6 chance of school-based blowback. After 5 spells are cast near it, the rift may destabilize into a full [[Wild Magic Zone]], stabilize, or collapse entirely.
