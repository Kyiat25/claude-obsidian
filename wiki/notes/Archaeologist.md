# Archaeologist — Rank 1-4 (Explorer of Lost Places)

*"You seek lost civilizations, ancient treasures, and forgotten knowledge."* Tier 2 (Specialized). Fulfills the "Archaeologist" slot [[FEARS Advanced Backgrounds]] flagged as not-yet-developed after Batch 1.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ruin Delver | Historical Knowledge | Discover a significant artifact, map an important ruin, or publish recognized findings |
| 2 | Recognized Explorer | Ruin Sense | Discover a lost city or major site, recover a historically important artifact, or gain royal patronage |
| 3 | Master Archaeologist | Living History | Discover a world-changing artifact, solve a centuries-old historical mystery, or prove/disprove a major historical theory |
| 4 | Legendary Explorer | Master of Ages | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Ruin Delver

**Skill Proficiency**: History, Survival. **Tool Proficiency**: cartographer's tools or mason's tools. **Language**: one ancient language of choice. **Equipment**: a wooden case with maps and notes, a bullseye lantern, a miner's pick, traveler's clothes, 25 gp.

**Feature — Historical Knowledge**: advantage on History checks related to ancient civilizations; identify the age and origin of ruins or artifacts (DC 15 History check); know rumors of lost cities and treasure sites; can date architecture and construction methods.

**Specialty** (choose your focus): Pre-human civilizations, Ancient empires, Lost magic, Burial sites, Ancient religions, Precursor races.

**Holdings**: a collection of maps showing ruin locations; an academic contact interested in your discoveries; basic excavation tools.

## Rank 2: Recognized Explorer

**Holdings**: funding for expeditions (30 gp/month from a patron or university); an artifact collection (worth 200 gp); detailed maps of 5-8 major ruin sites; a team of 2-3 assistants/porters; contacts in academic circles; published papers on your discoveries.

**Feature Expansion — Ruin Sense**: identify traps common to ancient sites (advantage on Investigation); know structural weaknesses in old buildings; read ancient inscriptions for general meaning even in unknown languages; once per day, "feel" if something significant is nearby in ruins (DM provides a hint).

## Rank 3: Master Archaeologist

**Holdings**: major funding from multiple patrons (80 gp/month); an extensive artifact collection (worth 2,000 gp); a museum or university position; a team of 10-15 workers, guards, and scholars; access to excavation equipment and magical tools; published books on your discoveries; a network of contacts who report new sites to you.

**Feature Expansion — Living History**: automatic success on History checks (DC 20 or lower) related to ancient civilizations; activate some ancient devices and artifacts; once per week, use ancient knowledge to solve modern problems; your reputation opens doors in academic and noble circles.

## Rank 4: Legendary Explorer

**Holdings**: a university department or museum wing named after you; massive expedition funding (250 gp/month); a priceless artifact collection; expeditions numbering 30-50 people; your discoveries are taught in every school; multiple published books considered definitive; geographic features named after you.

**Feature — Master of Ages**: know the location of legendary lost sites even if others don't believe they exist; activate any ancient magic or technology given time to study; once per month, recover an artifact of legendary power from an ancient site; your historical knowledge affects policy; you can solve modern crises using ancient wisdom. Museums bid for your discoveries, nations fund your expeditions hoping for military/magical advantages, other archaeologists build careers on your discoveries, and you've recovered artifacts that shouldn't exist.
