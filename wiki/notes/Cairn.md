# Cairn, The Stone at the Crossing

**Domains**: land roads, crossroads, waymarks, the traveler's right of passage. **Symbol**: three stacked stones.

The road itself, finally held: every waystone-pile *is* Cairn, locally and entirely. Travelers add a stone in passing — the whole liturgy — and take direction from the pile's lean, which is Cairn answering. At crossroads he defers, by ancient courtesy, to [[Vaithis]]'s wanderers on *which* way, holding only that there be a way. Road-crimes against travelers fall under the setting's frith/guest-right custom's traveler-right: harm on the marked road scatters the nearest cairn, and every carter knows what a scattered cairn means about the miles ahead.
