# Creeping Tarlight

*"And lo, the moon did weep its own blood — not light, but tar, slow and thick and hungering."*

First seen 3rd Age, 421 TA, during an eclipse in Frostspire. A viscous mockery of lunar light.

## Nature

Appears when the Weave curdles, near corrupted leylines or failed sealing glyphs — oozing downward from [[The Null Reflection]]. A moon of stagnation and slow suffering.

## World effects

**The Tarfall**: thick black tar rains from the sky, glowing with glyphic rot (failed spell signatures); it cannot be cleaned magically; it extinguishes non-magical flames and halves light-source radius. **Travel impact**: speed halved on foot, flying creatures grounded or sluggish, 25% chance magical transport causes a surge.

**Monster effects**: beasts and monsters become gluebound, gaining resistance to slashing and temporary HP but disadvantage on Dex saves. Infected creatures rise 1d4 rounds after death as **Tar-Wrought** — bodies animated by rot-stained glyph threads, emitting low glyphic hums that disrupt concentration.

## Madness

Short-term: obsession with filth or purity. Long-term: discomfort with light. Indefinite: belief that everyone is melting.

## Campaign use

A symbol of decay for falling sanctuaries; tied to [[Vael'Akir]], [[Ith'Quess]], or [[Vezherak]]; a precursor to a Tether Spirit awakening.
