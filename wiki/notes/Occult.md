# Occult

One of three knowledge skills (with [[Memory]] and [[Streetwise]]). Lets a character interpret forbidden glyphs, rituals, and dream-visions; understand the motives/codes of cults, corrupted zones, or glyph-warped NPCs. Carries a stated social-fallout risk — using it can reveal you "know too much."

## Formula and design intent (from a dedicated `Occult.md` player-guide source)

**Occult Check** = `1d20 + (INT or WIS, player's choice by context) + Proficiency`. Distinguished explicitly from Arcana (how sanctioned magic works) and Religion (divine law) — Occult is "the rotted underside of both." Typical DCs 15-25; Eldritch-context checks may trigger a [[Sanity]] Save (DC 12-18, failure adds +1 Corruption or a hallucination), and composure near cursed glyphs may require a [[Focus]] Save. An optional rule lets a character who survives a glyph-bleed or dream-eclipse and passes a DC 18 Sanity Save permanently gain **Occult (Unstable)** proficiency — usable only while their Sanity is 12 or lower. Explicit GM guidance: Occult should never provide absolute truth, only warn, foreshadow, or reveal costs before powers.
