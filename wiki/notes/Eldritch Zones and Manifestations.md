# Eldritch Zones and Manifestations

> [!note] Source frontmatter reads `status: revision-needed`
> Filed as documented lore/rules content at `confidence: medium` rather than fully confirmed canon, consistent with how this vault treats other `revision-needed`-marked sources.

Ties Eldritch influence directly to [[Unstable Magic Zones]] — Dead Magic Zones, Arcane Rifts (including Enhanced), and Wild Magic Zones are framed as "the hidden infection" beneath many zone instabilities, not always immediate but often the result of echo, observation, or unintentional memory of a Glyph that never should have existed.

## Zone-by-zone Eldritch influence

**Dead Magic Zones** — most often caused by [[Nhal'Zereth]] leaking in when magic is overused, misaligned, or fatally distorted; these zones are consumed, as if the Weave itself unthreaded the memory of magic ever working there. Named corrupted beasts: **Null-Stags** (leave no tracks, no smell, no heat signature) and **Hollow Vultures** (eat magical creatures' memories instead of flesh).

**Arcane Rifts / Enhanced Arcane Rifts** — mutated echoes of Eldritch whispering into leyline seams, tied to [[Xey'Tazun]], [[Zhurr'Kael]], and a fourth named force ("Ul'Naresh" cross-reference aside, "Vael'Akir" appears here specifically tied to planar-summon corruption). Named corrupted beasts: **Glyphpuppets** (magical constructs replicating casters' behaviors before they're encountered) and **Fray-Beasts** (warp unpredictably mid-combat).

**Wild Magic Zones** — corrupted most often by [[Ul'Naresh]] (dream reversal) and [[Zhurr'Kael]] (anti-logic); these zones don't just surge, they contaminate, spreading instability across surrounding leyline threads and potentially mutating into self-aware magical fields. Named corrupted beasts: **Surgeborn** (elementals/familiars of twisted spell residue) and **Thought-Flayers** (distinct from mind flayers — steal spell slots, fill them with unstable energy).

## DM trigger tables

The source's core mechanical contribution: explicit trigger conditions for invoking each Eldritch God's subtle manifestation within a given zone type (e.g., forcing a 5th-level+ spell in a Dead Magic Zone gives a 50% chance [[Nhal'Zereth]] begins erasing the caster's memory of magic itself; three or more overkill spells in an Arcane Rift risks [[Xey'Tazun]] resetting the rift to an earlier state). Each zone gains an escalated state once triggered (Contaminated Dead Magic, an "Unanchored" Rift, a "Screaming Weave Field") with mechanical consequences layered on top of the zone's base rules.

## Sanity Corruption Score

A parallel mechanical track, not reconciled against the vault's existing [[Sanity]]/[[Corruption]] systems: +1 per forced spell in a Dead Zone, +1 per overkill spell in a Rift, +1 per failed Focus save in a Wild Zone, +2 for experiencing an Eldritch manifestation directly, +5 for knowingly invoking an Eldritch god. Thresholds run 5 (unsettling dreams) → 10 (glyph hallucinations) → 15 (seeing people who don't exist) → 20 (permanent madness or Personal Glyph replaced by an Eldritch echo).

> [!contradiction] A second Corruption-like track, not merged with the existing one
> This "Sanity Corruption Score" uses the same 5/10/15/20 threshold shape as [[Corruption]]'s established track from [[Four Threads of the Mind]], but is explicitly zone-triggered and Eldritch-specific rather than the general eldritch-exposure stat already documented. Treated the same way [[Sangromancer]]'s Vitae Strain was treated earlier in this vault — a deliberately parallel-but-distinct track, not folded into the existing Corruption score, flagged rather than merged.
