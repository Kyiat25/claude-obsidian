# The Null Reflection

*"It is not opposite. It is un."*

Not the Feywild ([[Luminous Echo]], untamed potential) and not the Shadowfell ([[Umbral Veil]], decay) — the Null Reflection is **anti-reality**: a mirror that rejects the image it reflects.

## Nature

- Time loops until it never began.
- Geometry folds without form.
- All leylines exist only where they do not.
- It **feeds on observation** — what is watched is warped.
- It is a source of anti-glyph energy, eroding Form and Universal Glyphs especially.

## Accessing it

**Entrances**: during certain Eldritch Moons ([[Slumbering Moon]], [[Blood Moon of Rebirth]]); through corrupted mirrors; via failed planar magic near Dead Magic Zones; during false awakenings; through the "Singing Lantern's Croon" sleep ritual.

**What happens there**: logic fails, identity becomes fluid, memory unravels — a visitor may not return, and if they do, they're changed.

## Inhabitants

Reflected Spirits (native to it), some Dream-Born spirits (visitors), echoes of forgotten things, fragments of erased gods, and — unsettlingly — one's own reflection, acting independently.

## Relationship to Esh'Tarah

The source explicitly floats that [[Esh'Tarah]] — the Eldritch God of identity collapse — may itself *be* the Null Reflection, or its body/realm, rather than merely inhabiting it. Not confirmed outright, flagged as a strong implication.

## Campaign use

**Low-level**: glimpses through corrupted mirrors, dreams that feel too real, NPCs who went there and came back wrong. **Mid-level**: brief excursions, rescue missions, recovering lost knowledge. **High-level**: extended exploration, confronting Esh'Tarah directly, understanding Eldritch nature at the cost of real risk of never returning.
