# Thread Merchant — Rank 1-4

*"You trade in the magical components that fuel spellcasting in Dór-o Estel."* Batch 3, Magic-Focused category. The first background source to give "Thread" and "Loom-Cities" (both first named in [[FEARS Advanced Backgrounds]] Batch 2's setting-integration notes) any concrete mechanical treatment — Threads are explicitly tradeable spell components here, not just a vague setting reference.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Thread Collector | Component Knowledge | Acquire a rare component, establish a relationship with a major buyer, or identify a valuable Thread source |
| 2 | Component Dealer | Thread Expertise | Establish a trade route to a Loom-City, acquire a legendary component, or broker a deal with a powerful mage |
| 3 | Master Thread Trader | Market Control | Monopolize Thread trade in a region, discover a new Thread source, or broker an international trade agreement |
| 4 | Thread Baron | Lord of Magical Trade | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Thread Collector

**Skill Proficiency**: Arcana, Persuasion. **Tool Proficiency**: jeweler's tools. **Equipment**: jeweler's tools, a merchant's scale, worthless sample Thread fragments, a ledger, traveler's clothes, 15 gp.

**Feature — Component Knowledge**: identify Thread fragments and Glyph materials by sight; know fair market value for common spell components; advantage on checks to locate component vendors; assess quality of magical materials.

**Holdings**: a small stall or shop space; a basic common-component inventory; a contact with local mages who purchase from you; supplier connections in your city.

## Rank 2: Component Dealer

**Holdings**: a proper shop with secured storage; moderate income (35 gp/month); inventory including uncommon components; a network of 8-10 suppliers and buyers; a license to trade in controlled magical materials; regular spellcaster customers; **contacts in Loom-Cities**.

**Feature Expansion — Thread Expertise**: identify any spell component and its uses; know the locations of major Thread sources in your region; accurately appraise rare magical materials; advantage on Persuasion checks negotiating component prices; once per week, locate a specific rare component through your network (1d4 days).

## Rank 3: Master Thread Trader

**Holdings**: multiple shops across cities; major income (120 gp/month); a warehouse of rare components; a network spanning multiple cities and **Loom-Cities**; 5-8 employees and agents; exclusive contracts with mage guilds; access to controlled and rare materials; relationships with Thread harvesters.

**Feature Expansion — Market Control**: acquire almost any spell component given time (1d6 days for rare, 2d6 for very rare); receive advance notice of Thread anomalies and wild magic zones; authenticate or verify any magical material; once per month, call in a favor from a major spellcaster you've supplied; your prices influence regional component markets.

## Rank 4: Thread Baron

**Holdings**: a continent-spanning trade empire; excellent income (400 gp/month); vaults containing legendary components; exclusive access to **Loom-City Thread wells**; an organization of 40+ traders, harvesters, and guards; political influence through control of magical supply; private Thread sources.

**Feature — Lord of Magical Trade**: acquire any non-artifact spell component given reasonable time; first access to newly discovered Thread sources; influence magical policy through economic pressure; once per month, grant a significant component gift to curry favor with a powerful organization; your supply affects what magic can be cast across regions. Archmages must negotiate with you for rare materials, governments fear your economic power, and you may have discovered a way to stabilize or cultivate Threads.

> [!note] What Threads actually are, now confirmed
> [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]] confirms Threads aren't just tradeable components — they're the elemental structure [[Astralor]] built reality from, and their rotation between locations is the result of three unwitting cosmic forces ([[Aeloria]], [[The Whispering Dark]], [[The Restorer]]) working at cross-purposes. A Thread Merchant character would have no idea their stock is, cosmically speaking, fought over by gods who don't know each other exist. See [[Threadbearers]] for the mortals who channel Threads directly rather than trading them.
