# Form Glyphs

Fourth-Law Glyphs ensuring every world and creature interacts with leylines in a structured way — the "Nine Known Form Glyphs," eternal blueprints that shape all forms of life, guaranteed to recur in every cycle regardless of what any Primal Force intends.

| Glyph | Represents | How it shapes life |
|---|---|---|
| **The Mortal Glyph** | Sapience, civilization, society | Intelligent races always emerge |
| **The Beast Glyph** | Instinct, predation, survival | Governs animals and creatures of pure instinct |
| **The Titan Glyph** | Colossal beings, divine figures | Great beings always rise — gods, giants, or celestial beings |
| **The Spirit Glyph** | Souls, the afterlife, rebirth | Souls persist, ghosts linger, death is never the true end |
| **The Aberration Glyph** | The unnatural, the eldritch | Some things always defy understanding and exist outside normal life |
| **The Draconic Glyph** | Mythic beasts, power embodied | Worlds always have apex creatures of great intelligence and strength |
| **The Undead Glyph** | The unliving, death's defiance | Some beings return after death, whether revenants, vampires, or worse |
| **The Fae Glyph** | Trickery, chaos, unbound magic | Governs mischievous, immortal, or wildly magical creatures |
| **The Construct Glyph** | Creation, artificial life | Some beings are built rather than born |

This is an expansion from the earlier [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]] framing, which named only five Form archetypes (Mortals, Beasts, Titans, Spirits, Aberrations) — [[Weave of Creation]] adds four more (Draconic, Undead, Fae, Construct) not present in the first source. Not treated as a contradiction; the later source simply gives fuller detail on an already-established Law.

Notably, the source states outright that [[Astralor]] trying to erase these patterns, [[The Whispering Dark]] trying to consume them, and [[The Restorer]] trying to rewrite them all fail against this Law — the nine patterns are more durable than any single Primal Force's will.

## Known Eldritch corruptions per Form Glyph

`Saga of the Glyphs.md` ties specific Eldritch corruption/mutation strains directly to individual Form Glyphs, cross-referencing the Eldritch Gods documented on [[Guide to the Eldritch Forces]]:

| Form Glyph | Known corruption |
|---|---|
| Beast | Fraywolves ([[Nhal'Zereth]]) — partially erased from sight; Spinecrawlers ([[Ith'Quess]]) — innards turned outward |
| Mortal | Hollow-Walkers ([[Nhal'Zereth]]) — forgotten entirely; Desire-Sick ([[Vael'Akir]]) — hunger for lives never lived |
| Titan | Temporal Echoes ([[Xey'Tazun]]) — titans born across time; Echo-Forms — memory-bound fragments that never fully appear |
| Spirit | Inversion Sleepers ([[Ul'Naresh]]) — trapped between dream and death; the Forgotten Dead — mourned but unrecognized |
| Aberration | Fleshwarps ([[Ith'Quess]]) — mimicry of memory; Reflection Spawn ([[Esh'Tarah]]) — contradictory mirror-beings |
| Draconic | Crownless Drakes ([[Mirag'Nael]]) — believe themselves kings of false eras; Sunderwings — born from rewritten histories |
| Undead | Ghostborn ([[Nhal'Zereth]]) — never died at all; Ossian Kings — bodies exist, souls migrated elsewhere |
| Fae | Mask-Eaters ([[Esh'Tarah]]) — beings who wear others' selves; Dream-Spliced ([[Ul'Naresh]]) — Fae caught in mortal recollection loops |
| Construct | Thought-Loops ([[Zhurr'Kael]]) — recursive minds that erase the user; Echo-Machina — constructs who remember being human |

Eldritch corruption often crosses glyph boundaries, rewriting creatures into hybrid forms or impossible contradictions. The Fae Glyph is especially unstable near [[Wild Magic Zone|Wild Magic Zones]]; constructs don't always bear souls but may earn them through interaction with the Mortal or Spirit Glyph.

## Minor variations

Per `Saga of the Glyphs.md`, life doesn't fit into one singular mold even within a single Form Glyph:

| Primary Glyph | Minor variation | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Mortal Glyph** (Sapience & Civilization) | Glyph of Elders | Wisdom and leadership — long-lived beings, ancestral spirits |
| | Glyph of the Forgotten | Those who exist outside of history — wanderers, outcasts |
| **Beast Glyph** (Feral Life & Instinct) | Glyph of the Pack | Social survival — wolves, cooperative hunters |
| | Glyph of the Lone Predator | Solitary survival — tigers, apex predators |
| **Titan Glyph** (Colossal Beings & Divine Power) | Glyph of the Sleeping Giants | Great beings waiting to awaken — dormant gods, hidden titans |
| | Glyph of the Tyrants | Beings that rule through fear and might |
