# Nyxthys, The Black Abyss

*"There are no flames at the bottom of the sea. Only silence. And only Water knows what waits there."*

**Domains**: the abyss itself, darkness beneath pressure, the floor of the world, the border no light crosses. **Symbol**: a perfect black circle on a field of deep blue. **Worshiped by**: no one, willingly — placated by trench-sailors, named in the oaths of deep-divers, invoked once and never twice.

Where other deep gods have offices — [[Vaelora]] keeps the abyss's secrets, its hunger and fear have their own names — Nyxthys is **the address**, the place all of them are *in*. He does not act, answer, hunt, or want; he is described only by what cannot happen inside him: light, sound, return. Deep-divers speak his name once, at the surface, before descending, never below, where he might mistake it for a knock. Trench-charts leave his waters uninked — not blank by ignorance, blank by manners.

> [!truth] DM-Truth
> Nyxthys is Water's Eldritch frontier, the counterpart to Spirit's fray and Fire's curdle. The abyss does not *become* corrupted, but it **borders** corruption — the wrongness in the deep-trenches sits at his floor. He is not corrupted; he is the wall, and the wall is not load-tested. Anything rising from the Eldritch through Water rises through him first, and no mortal theology has language for what it means if the Black Abyss has a far side.
