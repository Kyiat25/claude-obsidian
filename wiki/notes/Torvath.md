# Torvath, The Breaker of Banks

*"The sea you feared. The storm you watched. But it was the river — your river, the one your children drink from — that took the town."*

**Domains**: river floods, the breaking of banks, water's betrayal of its course. **Symbol**: a riverbank line, snapped in two. **Worshiped by**: feared by valley-towns and delta-folk, appeased by levee-builders. **Allies**: [[Voraxis]] (his salt-water twin).

Where Voraxis is the sea's erasure — a stranger from the horizon — Torvath is the river's: the water you were baptised in, turning on you. He is the pantheon's living argument against every canal, dam, and levee, each one a claim that the river has been bargained with; Torvath is the clause mortals didn't read. He doesn't rage annually — he waits generations, long enough for a town to forget why its old foundations sit so far up the hill. Delta-towns keep high-water stones, kissed once a year so he sees he is remembered and need not remind them himself.
