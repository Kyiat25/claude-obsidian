# The Burning of Sylvenmere

*"Sylvenmere: 12,000 years of peace. 6 hours of horror. Eternity of memory."*

A `status`-unspecified but heavily-detailed massacre event, dated **487 FA, Month 4, Day 23**, on the northern [[Kerythra]] coast. A recent, in-continuity atrocity directly connected to the "cosmological lie" theme documented on [[The Binding (The Short Memory and the Forged Faith)]] and [[The Lie of Godhood]]: an ancient wood-elf grove community was destroyed specifically because it possessed irrefutable, replicable physical evidence (dendrochronological tree-ring records dating back 12,400+ years) that predated the Faith of the Bound Pattern's divine-creation narrative.

## What happened

Sylvenmere was a remote, 12,000-year-old coastal grove of 600 peaceful inhabitants, isolated from geopolitics until Imperial trade expansion (484–486 FA) brought merchant contact, then a Corvessan Faith missionary who documented the grove's tree-ring evidence and filed a "theological threat assessment" urging suppression. Corvessan intelligence embedded operatives posing as friendly traders for months, mapping the grove and its defenses while the community — despite explicit warnings from a Star-Reader and their own weak forest-spirit guardian, the Root-Singer — dismissed the danger out of a 12,000-year tradition of trusting openness over suspicion. A coordinated naval assault with "blessed fire" killed 280–350 people (a ~70% casualty rate) over roughly six hours, burning 50+ ancient World Trees to stumps; 120–180 survivors fled to the Crimson Root Isles. All 56 attacking operatives escaped without casualties.

## The backfire

The attack's stated goal — destroying evidence and silencing witnesses — failed completely: redundant archives, seed-pod genetic continuity, and 167 consistent, mutually-corroborating survivor testimonies made the truth impossible to suppress, and Sylvenmere became an international cause célèbre rather than a forgotten atrocity.

## International reactions (condensed)

Orcish Scar-Wardens declared Corvessa "oath-broken" for the first time in 300 years; Halfling Joy-Wild clans began rare coordinated militant mobilization (~23,000 organizing); the druidic circle Talanthar shifted from defensive to offensive doctrine; twelve druidic Circles formed an unprecedented "Root-Pact" alliance; Kel Varyan faced embarrassment as its own trade routes had enabled the atrocity; and [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]]'s internal reform-vs-hardliner schism deepened openly. Corvessa itself faced escalating diplomatic isolation, economic pressure, and internal questioning of regime stability.

## Present status (six months after)

Survivors are rebuilding at the Crimson Root Isles; the Root-Singer spirit is dormant, its fragments possibly capable of regeneration over 200–300 years; nine ancient tree varieties are preserved for replanting across multiple future sites, ensuring "memory cannot die while seeds live."
