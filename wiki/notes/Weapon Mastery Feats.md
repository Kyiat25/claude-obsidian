# Weapon Mastery Feats

> **Scope note.** Third independent feat-system sighting, after [[Called Shot Feats]] (14) and [[Evasion Feats]] (4) — again completely disjoint. Three non-overlapping feat lists from three independent sources is strong evidence FEARS has one coherent, broad feat system rather than several unrelated one-off mechanics.

| Feat | Prerequisite | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Weapon Adept (half-feat) | none | +1 to one ability score (max 20); gain [[Weapon Mastery]] with one proficient weapon, usable once per turn like martial classes |
| Master of Arms (full feat) | proficiency with a martial or simple weapon | Gain two Weapon Masteries for weapons you're proficient with; counts as one prior crit toward each weapon's future progression |

Weapon Adept is explicitly designed for casters/half-martials wanting one tactical trick without encroaching on Fighter's identity. Master of Arms is aimed at Rogues, Rangers, Barbarians, Paladins, Monks, Benders, and Investigators wanting a head start — still weaker than Fighter's native progression speed and per-round mastery count.

Non-martial casters can also use Weapon Adept to bypass the 5-crit martial-weapon requirement for one chosen weapon.
