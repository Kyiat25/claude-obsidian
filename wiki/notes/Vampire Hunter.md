# Vampire Hunter — Rank 1-4 (Specialized Undead Slayer)

*"You dedicate your existence to destroying the vampire menace."* Tier 3 (Dark Fantasy). Fulfills the "Vampire Hunter" slot [[FEARS Advanced Backgrounds]] flagged as not-yet-developed after Batch 1. Narrower than [[Monster Hunter]]'s "choose any monster family" specialty system — this background is entirely and permanently vampire-specific.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Initiate Hunter | Vampire Lore | Destroy your first vampire spawn, save someone from becoming a vampire, or survive a vampire attack |
| 2 | Proven Hunter | Vampire Slayer | Destroy a vampire in its lair, prevent a vampire from creating more spawn, or save a settlement from vampire attacks |
| 3 | Master Vampire Hunter | Bane of the Undead | Destroy a vampire lord, prevent a vampire from completing a ritual, or eliminate an entire vampire coven |
| 4 | Grand Master Vampire Hunter | Vampire's Nightmare | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Initiate Hunter

**Skill Proficiency**: Investigation, Stealth. **Tool Proficiency**: woodcarver's tools, for stakes. **Equipment**: 10 wooden stakes, 3 vials of holy water, a silvered mirror, garlic cloves, a holy symbol, dark clothes, 15 gp.

**Feature — Vampire Lore**: advantage on Intelligence checks about vampires and vampire spawn; identify signs of vampire presence; know vampire weaknesses (sunlight, stakes, holy symbols, running water); identify vampire victims (bite marks, blood drain).

**Holdings**: a basic vampire hunting kit; a mentor, probably a traumatized veteran hunter; access to a church or hunting organization; knowledge of local vampire activity.

## Rank 2: Proven Hunter

**Holdings**: quality hunting equipment (blessed stakes, holy water, silvered weapons); income from the church or organization (30 gp/month); a network of 5-8 contacts reporting vampire activity; safe houses blessed against vampires; a collection of vampire fangs and evidence, as trophies; survivors you've saved who provide aid.

**Feature Expansion — Vampire Slayer**: advantage on attacks against vampires and vampire spawn; resistance to necrotic damage; identify vampire thralls and dominated victims; track vampires to their lairs; your presence makes vampires uneasy — they can sense you.

## Rank 3: Master Vampire Hunter

**Holdings**: an arsenal of vampire hunting equipment (blessed weapons, rare components); major income (100 gp/month); safe houses across the region; a team of 5-8 trained vampire hunters; a network providing intelligence on vampire activity; access to ancient vampire knowledge; church or organization backing.

**Feature Expansion — Bane of the Undead**: advantage on all saves against vampire abilities (charm, domination, etc.); sense the presence of vampires within 60 ft; once per week, track a specific vampire to its lair; your blessed weapons ignore vampire resistances; identify and free vampire thralls.

## Rank 4: Grand Master Vampire Hunter

**Holdings**: legendary vampire hunting equipment (ancient relics, holy artifacts); excellent income (250 gp/month); an organization of 30+ vampire hunters; a fortress-like headquarters, blessed and warded; an intelligence network spanning the continent; ancient texts on vampires that some vampire elders fear you possess; church and government support.

**Feature — Vampire's Nightmare**: immune to vampire charm and domination; sense vampires within 300 ft and identify their power level; once per month, organize a purge eliminating multiple vampires; your weapons harm even the most powerful vampire lords; vampires cannot regenerate damage you inflict. Vampire covens flee regions where you operate, elder vampires know your name and fear it, and some say you've become as dangerous as what you hunt.
