# Halfling

*"We do not fear death. We walk beside him. And when he finally catches up, we'll make sure the last joke is a good one."*

**Origin/culture**: Halfling life is governed by the Three Joy-Truths (see [[The Joy Truths]]): laughter lasts longer than breath; the story matters more than the sword; death is a companion, not a terror. Two traditions coexist — the settled Hearth-Echo Covenant (memory, hospitality, generational care) and the nomadic Way of the Joy-Wild (risk, survival, weaponized joy).

**Physical**: mature by 20, live ~150 years; Small, 2'8"–3'4", 25 ft. speed.

## Traits

- **Lucky**: reroll a natural 1 on attack/check/save, uses = proficiency bonus/long rest
- **Brave**: advantage vs. frightened
- **Halfling Nimbleness**: move through larger creatures' spaces
- **Naturally Stealthy**: can hide behind a creature only one size larger
- **Joy-Keeper**: Performance proficiency; grant an ally advantage on their next fear/despair save via a rest-time story or joke
- **Memory-Weaver**: 1/long rest, 10-min story about the dead grants a listener Inspiration
- **Languages**: Common + Halfling

**Mental**: Focus +0 / Sanity **+2** (the highest baseline Sanity modifier of any lineage documented so far — trauma processed through laughter rather than left to calcify).

**DCS Integration**: Dodge +1 / Endure +1 / Deflect +1; DR +0. Advantage on Rally, higher casualty threshold to break.

## Corruption: The Hollow Laugh

Corruption enters through suppressed emotion, denied grief, or forced silence. At Corruption 10+: Muted Joy, Story-Fracture, Silence-Weight. **Fractured Laughter table** (1d6): False Memory, Deadened Taste (hearth/feast comforts disabled), Stolen Laughter (1d4 psychic), Ancestral Static (Performance disabled), The Hollow (+1 Whisper Point, Joy-Keeper auto-fails), or Echo Holds (Corruption −1).

## The Drow exception

> [!note] A defining, absolute cultural exception
> The one thing Halflings take entirely seriously. An underground Halfling town, **Glintsing**, was silenced without warning by Drow — no battle, just absence: flutes snapped, laughter gone, no blood, only emptiness. The lone survivor's testimony: "They stole the sound. They unwrote our laughter." Halflings responded not with armies but with **the Second Echo** — silent hunting parties in blank ash masks who track Drow specifically, speak nothing during the hunt, and only laugh again after returning the stolen story exactly as it was, told aloud. Against every other enemy — dragons, demons, tyrants — Halflings joke, taunt, and laugh while fighting. Against Drow, the smile disappears entirely. See [[Drow (Lineage)]] and [[The Ashen Web]] for the other side of this dynamic; this is a significant, currently one-sided piece of inter-lineage lore that has no corresponding acknowledgment on the Drow side yet.
