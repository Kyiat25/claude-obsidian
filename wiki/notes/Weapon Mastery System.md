# Weapon Mastery System

"Weapon Mastery transforms weapon choice from a simple damage calculation into meaningful tactical decisions." The martial-class engine every prior combat source gestured at without defining — [[Called Shot]] mentioned "Weapon Mastery Skill" interactions, [[Combat Flow]] and [[Momentum]] both reference "Weapon Skill effects," and [[Fighter]]'s page has said "can use multiple Weapon Mastery Skills per round" since the very first Class_breakdown ingest — all without this system ever being detailed until now.

## Structure

- **[[Weapon Mastery]]** — the core rules: how mastery is gained (starting weapons per class, crit-based progression), the once-per-turn rule, Fighter's multi-mastery exception, Momentum integration, and the full class-by-class integration summary.
- **[[Weapon Skills]]** — the technique library itself: ~40 named skills organized by weapon type (Swords, Axes, Polearms, Bludgeons, Daggers, Ranged, Monk Weapons, Flails & Whips, Hammers), each with an Action version and, where applicable, Fighter Bonus Action / Reaction variants.
- **[[Weapon Mastery Feats]]** — Weapon Adept and Master of Arms, the third independent feat-system sighting after [[Called Shot Feats]] and [[Evasion Feats]].

## Resolves: Focus vs. Technique Point

This document uses **"Technique Point"** exclusively and repeatedly for Monk's (and, newly revealed, [[Bender]]'s) resource — never "Focus." Combined with [[Evasion]] and [[Damage Reduction]] independently saying the same thing, that's three sources against the one original [[Dynamic Combat System (DCS)]] summary mention of "Focus." Treated as resolved on [[Monk]]'s page now, with the original "Focus" wording preserved as a documented discrepancy rather than erased.

## New discovery: Bender shares Monk's Technique Point engine

**Flow Strike** is explicitly "Monk & Bender Only" — on hit, move 5 ft without provoking; at +3 Momentum, regain 1 Technique Point. No earlier source connected [[Bender]] to a Technique Point resource at all. Confirms the source's own framing: "Monks and Benders share a unique mastery interaction... solves Monk and Bender resource starvation while reinforcing flow-state martial artist identity."

## Contradiction: Summoner vs. Binder

The starting-mastery and non-martial-caster tables both list **Summoner** with a full weapon profile ("One simple weapon... Minimal personal combat investment — creatures fight for them"). **Binder is never mentioned anywhere in this document.** [[Classes_Summary]] states unambiguously that "The Summoner has been replaced outright by the Binder." This document's silence on Binder and continued treatment of Summoner as current is the same kind of version-mismatch signal as [[The Dynamic Combat System (Excluded Draft)]] — not marked excluded here, but likely an earlier snapshot predating the rename. Binder's Weapon Mastery profile remains genuinely unknown; not fabricated by analogy to Summoner's.

## Fighter's exact multi-mastery math

Confirms and sharpens [[Fighter]]'s existing "multiple Weapon Mastery Skills per round" note: 1 mastery on the Attack Action + 1 as a Bonus Action (if that skill has one) + 1 as a Reaction (if it has one) + 1 with Action Surge = **2-4 Weapon Skills in a single round**, never the same skill twice, never without spending the matching action.

## Design philosophy notes

- **Boss safety valves**: prone loops, movement-0 loops, and Momentum-drain loops from Weapon Skills apply once per turn max, don't stack from multiple attackers, and carry save protections — bosses can't be permanently locked down by martial control effects.
- Explicit design goals stated by the source: martial/caster parity, a distinct Fighter identity beyond "attack more," fixing Monk/Bender resource starvation, reducing Rogue's over-reliance on Sneak Attack, and giving the FEARS-original martials (Warden, Reaper, Inventor, Investigator) clear mastery identities.
