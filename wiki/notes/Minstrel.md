# Minstrel — Rank 1-4

*"You're a professional musician and storyteller, earning coin through performance."* Batch 3, Social/Performance category.

> [!note] Naming collision with the Bard class — flagged, not silently resolved
> The source names this background **"Minstrel / Bard"** — but a wiki page named `Bard.md` already exists as one of the 24 FEARS classes. Filed under "Minstrel" (the non-colliding half of the dual name) rather than disambiguating with a parenthetical, since "Minstrel" alone is a perfectly usable title on its own. Same underlying issue as [[Investigator (Background)]] vs. the Investigator class: two mechanically unrelated concepts sharing a name. This background is pure performance/fame progression with no spellcasting anywhere in its text — nothing like the [[Bard]] class's reality-manipulation magic. Also overlaps thematically with [[Entertainer]] (Batch 1) — both are performance-fame tracks; no source text distinguishes them, an unresolved redundancy between batches.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Street Performer | Wandering Entertainer | Perform at a noble event, create a popular song, or gain a patron's attention |
| 2 | Professional Minstrel | Master Performer | Write a song popular across the region, perform for royalty, or gain a wealthy patron |
| 3 | Renowned Performer | Master of the Stage | Perform at a royal coronation, create an era-defining masterwork, or become court minstrel to a king |
| 4 | Master of the Arts | Legend of Song | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Street Performer

**Skill Proficiency**: Performance, Persuasion. **Tool Proficiency**: three musical instruments. **Equipment**: three musical instruments, costume clothes, 5 sp in your hat, 10 gp.

**Feature — Wandering Entertainer**: earn a modest lifestyle through performances; know venues and taverns that hire performers; advantage on Performance checks for your specialty; gather rumors and gossip easily in taverns; other performers share information with you.

**Specialty** (choose your performance type): Singer, instrumentalist, storyteller, comedian, acrobat, dancer.

**Holdings**: a city reputation; regular performance spots; a network of 5-8 fellow performers; an instrument collection.

## Rank 2: Professional Minstrel

**Holdings**: a room at a favored inn or boarding house; income from performances (30 gp/month); quality instruments; regular engagements at taverns and halls; a small but growing reputation; contacts with venue owners.

**Feature Expansion — Master Performer**: earn a comfortable lifestyle through performances; advantage on Persuasion checks using your art; your performances can shift moods (calm an angry crowd, inspire fighters, etc.); know gossip from multiple cities; once per week, your performance creates an opportunity.

## Rank 3: Renowned Performer

**Holdings**: fine lodgings or a small house; major income (100 gp/month); legendary instruments (worth 500+ gp); bookings months in advance; 2-3 apprentices or backup performers; wealthy patrons; your songs are performed by others.

**Feature Expansion — Master of the Stage**: command performance fees of 50-100 gp/night; your performances are events people travel to see; advantage on all social checks when your reputation is known; gain audience with nobility through fame; once per month, your performance has major impact (inspire an army before battle, shift public opinion).

## Rank 4: Master of the Arts

**Holdings**: an estate or multiple homes; excellent income (300 gp/month); a priceless instrument collection; a troupe of 10-15 performers you lead; a permanent court appointment or multiple offers; schools teach your songs; nobility compete for your performances.

**Feature — Legend of Song**: your performances have a magical quality even without spellcasting (audiences moved to tears, inspired to action); once per month, use performance to achieve a near-miraculous effect (end conflict, inspire revolution); your songs are sung across the continent; you can gain audience with anyone, even kings; your word in song shapes history. Bards consider you the pinnacle of the art, songs about you are performed, and you may have created a new musical style or instrument.
