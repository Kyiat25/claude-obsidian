# The Threadbearer Crisis

> [!note] Live campaign situation, not historical background
> [[World Anchors and Thread System]] presents this as the **current** state of the setting — its closing timestamp reads "487-03-18 (Current campaign date)." Unlike [[Ashkarra]] or [[Aeravae]], which are settled history, this is an open, unresolved, currently-unfolding situation intended for direct campaign use.

## What happened

A coordinated Drow assassination campaign has killed **four of the five Threadbearers**, orchestrated in service of [[Vel'Zyren]]'s Ashen Web theology, which treats the Weave as prey and Threadbearer assassination as strategic warfare rather than random terrorism.

> [!contradiction] RESOLVED — Which Loom-City's Threadbearer is the sole survivor?
> [[World Anchors and Thread System]] states only **Kel Varyan**'s Threadbearer survives. [[World Anchors and the Loom-City System]] — an independent, near-duplicate draft of this same crisis — instead states "only Aeravae's hybrid system, more resistant to corruption through its living World Tree component, maintains full stability," implying Aeravae is the untouched Anchor. Both sources otherwise agree on every other detail (four dimmed Anchors, four dead Threadbearers, ongoing three-way race). **Ruled (2026-07-14) in favor of Kel Varyan**, confirmed by the user — already the reading backed by the most concrete, production-ready source (the actual Session 0-1 opening adventure): see below.

## Resolution: Serana Tidecaller is Naleth Spiralbound, and she personally murdered all four Threadbearers

Per `SESSION_0-1_RIVER_JOURNEY_COMPLETE.md` (a "ready to run" Session 0-1 opening adventure, the most concrete/production-level source touching this crisis) and the individual, "canonical version" [[Arc 1B - The Threadbearer's Silence|Arc 1B source]], the sole surviving Threadbearer is [[Kel Varyan]]'s own — publicly known as **Threadbearer Serana Tidecaller** (see [[Kel Varyan]]'s Notable Figures), who is secretly **Naleth Spiralbound** (Wood Elf, 187), the same figure named on [[Elarion]]'s page as the crisis's sole survivor. She is not merely consolidating power opportunistically — **she personally murdered all four other Threadbearers herself**, using her own Water Thread mastery to remotely stage each death as a natural accident, timed simultaneously to read as a Pattern event rather than coordinated murder. Her motive isn't hiding the cosmological truth (which she considers irrelevant) but stopping a specific dangerous pattern: Cycle 4's four Threadbearers were all reform-minded "questioners" who had begun comparing notes and connecting archaeological evidence, and she judged their doubt itself to be destabilizing the Pattern. She is now running a Seminary that trains the next Threadbearer generation into sincere theocratic belief, "not through violence, but through education." See [[Arc 1B - The Threadbearer's Silence]] for the full method and her four victims by name. This resolves the standing Kel Varyan/Aeravae survivor contradiction in Kel Varyan's favor — confirmed as this vault's ruling on 2026-07-14. A GM who prefers the Aeravae reading, or a Serana Tidecaller who is genuinely innocent, can still swap it out cleanly since [[Kel Varyan]]'s own page notes her only as a sympathetic public-facing NPC.

## Immediate consequences

Four World Anchors are now **dimmed** — not failed outright (per the FAQ on [[World Anchors and Thread System]], total simultaneous collapse is considered unlikely; new Threadbearers manifest fast enough to prevent it), but weakened and vulnerable. The real danger identified in-world is not sudden systemic collapse but the chaotic, contested transition period while new Bearers manifest.

## The three-way race

Per the Threadbearer death/succession mechanic (see [[Threadbearers]]), every surviving Threadbearer immediately senses a death and the call of the new Bearer, and is sacred-duty-bound to find them "or die trying." With four Bearers dead, four such calls are active at once, and three factions are converging on each new manifestation:

- **Protective factions** (Loom-City authorities, the Faith, the surviving Kel Varyan Bearer) racing to reach and secure new Threadbearers first.
- **Controlling factions** (political interests within each Loom-City) racing to influence or leverage a new Bearer before rivals can.
- **The Drow assassins**, finishing the job — actively hunting each new manifestation to prevent an Anchor from ever re-stabilizing.

## Confirmed connection to Arc 1B, and the standing Drow-assassination discrepancy

[[Arc 1B - The Threadbearer's Silence]] confirms Naleth's conspiracy is the actual hidden hand behind the four deaths, timed to read as a natural Pattern event. This sits in tension with the framing at the top of this page (a "coordinated Drow assassination campaign... orchestrated in service of Vel'Zyren's Ashen Web theology," per [[World Anchors and Thread System]] and [[World Anchors and the Loom-City System]]): those two sources describe an external Drow strike team, while the Arc 1B source describes a lone insider Threadbearer using her own Water Thread mastery, with no Drow involvement mentioned at all.

> [!note] Ruled (2026-07-14): kept deliberately unknown, not resolved
> Unlike the survivor-identity contradiction above, this one stays open by design rather than by oversight — the method behind the four deaths is genuinely unknown to *everyone* in-world at the current campaign moment, Naleth's own Seminary students and the surviving public included. A GM can run either the Drow-conspiracy version, the Naleth-solo version, or blend them (e.g. Naleth orchestrating the killings and *framing* the Drow, or a Drow strike team arriving to find the Threadbearers already dead and taking credit) — treat it as unresolved investigation material for PCs to actually uncover, not a documentation gap to close.

## Campaign use

This is presented as ready-made campaign-hook material: PCs could be protectors, hunted Threadbearers themselves, investigators tracing the Drow operation, or opportunists caught in the political scramble. It sits at the intersection of every major thread in this cosmology cluster — [[Loom-Cities]] politics, [[Threadbearers]] mechanics, and the Ashen Web's theological war on the Weave itself.
