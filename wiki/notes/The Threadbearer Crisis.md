# The Threadbearer Crisis

> [!note] Live campaign situation, not historical background
> [[World Anchors and Thread System]] presents this as the **current** state of the setting — its closing timestamp reads "487-03-18 (Current campaign date)." Unlike [[Ashkarra]] or [[Aeravae]], which are settled history, this is an open, unresolved, currently-unfolding situation intended for direct campaign use.

## What happened

A coordinated Drow assassination campaign has killed **four of the five Threadbearers**. Only the Threadbearer of [[Loom-Cities|Kel Varyan]] survives. The theological motive is explicit on [[World Anchors and Thread System]]'s theology comparison table: the Ashen Web (drow) treats the Weave as prey and their goddess Vel'Zyren as its predator, and treats Threadbearer assassination as strategic warfare, not random terrorism.

## Immediate consequences

Four World Anchors are now **dimmed** — not failed outright (per the FAQ on [[World Anchors and Thread System]], total simultaneous collapse is considered unlikely; new Threadbearers manifest fast enough to prevent it), but weakened and vulnerable. The real danger identified in-world is not sudden systemic collapse but the chaotic, contested transition period while new Bearers manifest.

## The three-way race

Per the Threadbearer death/succession mechanic (see [[Threadbearers]]), every surviving Threadbearer immediately senses a death and the call of the new Bearer, and is sacred-duty-bound to find them "or die trying." With four Bearers dead, four such calls are active at once, and three factions are converging on each new manifestation:

- **Protective factions** (Loom-City authorities, the Faith, the surviving Kel Varyan Bearer) racing to reach and secure new Threadbearers first.
- **Controlling factions** (political interests within each Loom-City) racing to influence or leverage a new Bearer before rivals can.
- **The Drow assassins**, finishing the job — actively hunting each new manifestation to prevent an Anchor from ever re-stabilizing.

## Campaign use

This is presented as ready-made campaign-hook material: PCs could be protectors, hunted Threadbearers themselves, investigators tracing the Drow operation, or opportunists caught in the political scramble. It sits at the intersection of every major thread in this cosmology cluster — [[Loom-Cities]] politics, [[Threadbearers]] mechanics, and the Ashen Web's theological war on the Weave itself.
