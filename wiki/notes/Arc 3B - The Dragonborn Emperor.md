# Arc 3B — The Dragonborn Emperor ("The God-Emperor Rises")

The full deep-dive behind [[Campaign Arcs (The Great Deceivers and Truth-Seekers)|Category 3: The Ascenders]]'s second arc.

## Emperor Vorthak the Eternal

Dragonborn, 400+ apparent age, the last legitimate Sultan of the Sovereign Flame — [[Ashkarra]]'s ruler at its height. Survived the Day of Twelve Shadows through forbidden Personal Glyph manipulation binding his life force directly to the Pattern. 582 years later, still consumed by two obsessions: understanding why Ashkarra fell (which led him to the proof that **Soltharion** stole credit for [[Astralor]]'s creation of the dragonborn), and ensuring it never happens again (which led him to conclude dragonborn need a god who actually cares).

His five-century plan is nearly complete: research and recovery, then establishing the **Secret Court** of loyal Ashkarra survivors; developing the **Blood Binding** ritual connecting all dragonborn; building the **Imperial Cult** and spreading evidence of Soltharion's fraud through [[The Burning Concord]]; and now, in its final century, the **Divine Throne** — the ascension ritual to formally replace Soltharion as the dragonborn's deity. *"I will become the god dragonborn SHOULD have worshipped — not through lies, but through genuine power."*

## Why he's dangerous to oppose cleanly

Designed to connect thematically with Arc 2A (Kethyr Flameheart, also an Ashkarra-grief-driven Eldritch pawn) and Arc 3A (manufactured divinity), while standing alone. Vorthak's core claim — that Soltharion abandoned dragonborn at Ashkarra and lied about creating them — is factually true per [[The Lie of Godhood]]; his proposed replacement, an emperor-god who would actually protect his people, may genuinely be an improvement, at the cost of a mass conversion ritual imposed on an entire race's religion without individual consent.
