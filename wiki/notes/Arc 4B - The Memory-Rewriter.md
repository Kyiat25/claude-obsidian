# Arc 4B — The Memory-Rewriter

The current, most-revised (v4.0) version of [[Campaign Arcs (The Great Deceivers and Truth-Seekers)|Category 4: The Rewriters]]'s second arc.

> [!contradiction] Supersedes an earlier, differently-conceived Arc 4B
> The compact summary on [[Campaign Arcs (The Great Deceivers and Truth-Seekers)]] (from `BBEG_Campaign_Arcs_Part2.md`) described Arc 4B as "The Reality Programmer," starring Gnome artificer **Kethris the Infinite** framing all reality as buggy software. This individual arc file — revised twice (v3.0 "FINAL," then v4.0 "REVISED," addressing "all critique points... Vel'Zyren integration, Archive-Keeper political systems") — instead centers an entirely different villain and target: **Vaelith Shadowmind** rewriting memory rather than reality's general source code. Treat this page as the current canonical Arc 4B; Kethris the Infinite's "Reality Programmer" concept appears to be an earlier draft direction that was replaced rather than kept as a parallel arc.

## Vaelith Shadowmind

High Elf memory-mage, 487 years old. *"Memory is the cruelest tyrant... memory, like death, is merely code. And code can be rewritten."* Working to manipulate the resonance patterns within **Chronicle Stones** — the physical anchors of the **Archive-Keepers**, mortals who maintain collective cultural memory — to enable selective forgetting of suffering. Her opposition voice, **Keeper Thyra of the Living Archive**, frames the counter-argument directly: *"To forget is to die without burial. Memory is not just record — it is identity, continuity, meaning."*

## Mechanism and stakes

Vaelith has already "healed" (memory-rewritten) three of the original Archive-Keepers and is corrupting historical records before the pattern is noticed. Her ritual requires the **Confluence of Echoes** — a resonance event during the anniversary of [[Ashkarra]]'s fall — and the strain of forcing it risks fracturing the Chronicle Stones outright, potentially erasing entire cultural histories rather than just individual trauma. The v4.0 revision folds in explicit [[Vel'Zyren]] integration and Archive-Keeper political-system detail not present in the earlier v3.0 draft — not read in full this pass.

## The moral trap

Vaelith's converted subjects experience genuine relief — real liberation from trauma, guilt, and grief. The question the arc is built around: is erasing suffering also erasing the self that suffered, and who gets to decide that trade for someone else?
