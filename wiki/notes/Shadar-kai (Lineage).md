# Shadar-kai

*"We remember what must not return. And we sever what lingers too long."*

**Origin myth**: elves transformed by prolonged exposure to the Shadowfell — accident, necessity, or deliberate choice, staying long enough that shadow became permanent. The Raven Queen, psychopomp goddess of death and fate, is often invoked though not universally worshipped; some say she personally transformed the first Shadar-kai. Core belief: not all threads should be preserved, memory can become trap as easily as treasure, and the Pattern sometimes requires cutting rather than mending — "the scissors that the Pattern uses when threads tangle too tightly to be saved." This is the standalone, fuller treatment — [[Elf (Lineage)]] also lists Shadar-kai as a brief subrace entry; treat this page as primary.

**Physical**: mature ~20 (not "adult" until first true loss, usually by 100), live 750+ years; Medium, 5½–6½ ft., ashen skin, dark eyes, muted colors nearby.

## Traits

- **Darkvision** 60 ft.
- **Keen Senses**: Perception proficiency
- **Fey Ancestry**: advantage vs. charm, immune to magical sleep
- **Trance**: 4-hr meditation = long rest, but spent *severing* memories rather than reliving them
- **Necrotic Resistance**
- **Blessing of the Raven Queen**: bonus action, teleport 30 ft., 1/long rest; from 3rd level, grants damage resistance until your next turn while ghostly
- **Shadow Sight**: full-color vision in dim/dark up to 60 ft.
- **Severing Sense** *(cultural)*: advantage on Insight to detect undead/possession/compulsion; sense a nearby creature actively dying within 30 ft.
- **Languages**: Common + Elvish (spoken with a "hollow" accent)

**Mental**: Focus +0 / Sanity +1 (death and loss processed as natural transition, not terror).

**DCS Integration**: Dodge +2 / Endure +0 / Deflect +0; DR +0. Advantage vs. fear/death-related morale breaks; disadvantage on ordinary Rally (accepts defeat as easily as victory) except when rallying toward a necessary ending.

## Corruption: The Lingering Shadow

The greatest weakness is holding on too tightly — corruption enters through refusal to release, clinging to what must end, or denial of necessary severance (the inverse of most lineages' vulnerabilities). At Corruption 10+: Shadow-Bind, Death-Denial, Severance-Failure. **Shadow-Caught table** (1d6): Clinging Memory, Shadow-Locked (Blessing of the Raven Queen disabled), Death-Fear (1d4 psychic + lose a skill), Severance-Agony (Sanity save or −1d4), Eldritch Touch (+1 Whisper Point, Severing Sense reveals something terrifying), or Shadow Holds (Corruption −1).
