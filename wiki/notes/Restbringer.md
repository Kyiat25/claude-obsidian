# Restbringer, The Quiet Road

*"You will not remember the road. That is not a flaw in the road. That is the road's kindness."*

**Domains**: the passage of the dead, funerary rites, the walk between, rest before return. **Symbol**: a lantern hung on a thread stretching into darkness. **Worshiped by**: mourners, hospice-keepers, funerary guilds, the dying who are ready.

Between Varethis's cut and [[The Echoed Mother]]'s return lies a distance, and Restbringer is the god of crossing it — not a judge or keeper, but a conductor; the dead are his passengers, not his possessions. Elemental faiths each believe their own psychopomp carries the dead the whole way (Air's winds, Water's ferryman, Earth's settling weight); Restbringer's priesthood teaches, without picking fights, that each of those carries the dead only *to the road*, which is his alone. His working arrangement with Water's ferryman Veythos is the model of interfaith cooperation this pantheon rarely manages elsewhere.

The lantern-vigil — a light kept burning from death until burial, then extinguished with wet fingers, "handing the flame to the one who walks" — is practiced across the Weave-faiths. His only heresy is the shortcut: cults promising a faster road for the beloved dead recur every few generations, and his priesthood meets them with a hostility it shows nothing else.
