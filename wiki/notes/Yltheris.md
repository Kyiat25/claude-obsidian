# Yltheris, The Dried River

*"You took me for granted. And now, I am gone."*

> [!note] Source-internal duplication
> The source file gives Yltheris two separate entries under two different epithets — "The Dried River" (drought, water scarcity) and "The Unanswered Rain" (rain that arrives too late to matter) — treated here as one deity with two aspects rather than two separate gods, since both share the name and the same underlying theme of water withheld past the point of use.

**Domains**: drought, water scarcity, rain that comes too late. **Symbol**: a cracked riverbed with a single droplet at its center. **Worshiped by**: farmers, desert wanderers. **Rivals**: Luthan, [[Istriel]]. **Allies**: Torvath, [[Marethis]].

Does not deny water — simply ensures those who forget its worth are reminded, and delivers it only once it is no longer needed. A god of consequence rather than cruelty: the punishment is timing, not denial.
