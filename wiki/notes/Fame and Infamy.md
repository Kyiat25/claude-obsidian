# Fame and Infamy

An optional module expanding [[Reputation]] with structured benefits, risks, and social effects based on how widely — and *why* — a character is known. `status: excluded`, not confirmed core canon. Fame is positive recognition; Infamy is notorious recognition; a character can hold both simultaneously across different regions or factions.

## Tiers (0-5)

0 Unknown (no effect) → 1 Recognized (minor discounts, mild suspicion) → 2 Talked About (rumor-level, strangers approach) → 3 Locally Iconic/Notorious (easier court/gang access, fame-based interruptions) → 4 Regionally Celebrated/Hated (songs, wanted posters, followers or fanatics emerge) → 5 Globally Revered/Feared (magical tracking, divine omens, impersonators, legacy trials). DMs track Fame Points to escalate; Fame decays without fresh deeds or witnesses, but Infamy lingers longer — only fading through redemption arcs, identity shifts, or "divine rewriting."

## Benefits and risks

Fame brings free lodging/gifts, audience with nobles and faction leaders, inspired NPC loyalty, and even divine interest (boons, visions, trials). Infamy brings being hunted by zealots/paladins/bounty guilds, public distrust, impersonators trading on the character's name or twisting it, and magical anchoring (scrying, geas, soul-marking) that specifically exploits a known name.

## NPC reaction table by type

Commoners, guards, clergy, criminals, nobles, and children each react distinctly to Fame vs. Infamy — e.g. clergy invite the famous to speak and offer blessings, but demand confession or curse the infamous; criminals discount to the famous but test, imitate, or fear the infamous.

## Campaign use

DMs are encouraged to attach short **Fame Tags** — one-line rumors or chants ("That's the one who tamed the storm!") — to a character rather than tracking numbers precisely, and to let players choose to embrace, manipulate, or actively burn down their own fame.
