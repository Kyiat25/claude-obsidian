# Luthan, The Rain-Giver

*"Too little, and you curse the sky. Too much, and you curse the sky. When I am doing my work well, you do not think of the sky at all."*

**Domains**: seasonal rain in measure, the balance of wet and dry, the farmer's calendar. **Symbol**: a grey cloud releasing exactly seven drops. **Worshiped by**: farmers, well-keepers, weather-priests.

The third position in Water's rain-argument — one rival's rain fails, another's drowns, a third surprises; Luthan is the rain that keeps its appointment. His triumphs are the years nothing happened, which his priests call "the invisible harvest." Where he turns away, mortals don't get drought or deluge, they get *unreliability*, which farmers hold to be crueller than either. His shrines are rain-gauges — a stone bowl and marked post — and worship consists of recording, on the belief that a measured rain is a promise witnessed. His portfolio runs directly through the still-unresolved naming collision between Water's own River-Warden [[Marethis]] and Air's Rain-Bearer [[Marethis (Air)]].
