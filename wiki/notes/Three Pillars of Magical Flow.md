# Three Pillars of Magical Flow

*"Magic is not a river, nor a lake, nor a road. It is all of these things at once."*

The magic of [[Dór-o Estel]] is structured around three primary arcane infrastructures, each with its own dedicated page: [[Leylines]] (the veins — energy flow and stability), [[Wells of Power]] (the reservoirs — stored power and concentration), and [[Astral Conduits]] (the highways — magical transport and dimensional links).

## The Earth Pantheon's view

Beneath all leylines lies the domain of Earth, whose gods "do not cast spells or shape storms — but prevent the Weave from breaking." [[Earth Pantheon|Vatharun]] is the Stone-Father, whose bones *are* the leylines — to harm one is to fracture reality itself. [[Earth Pantheon|Thamur]] ensures corrupted Wells are sealed before they awaken deeper horrors. [[Earth Pantheon|Gaeldros]] passes judgment on those who abuse or trespass through unanchored Conduits, binding even gods in law. The [[Earth Pantheon]] views the Three Pillars not as tools, but as **locks, weights, and boundaries** — each vital to keeping "the Nameless One Below" imprisoned, a clear reference to what [[The Hollow Thread]] names directly.

## Vel'Zyren's view

[[Vel'Zyren]] sees the Three Pillars as "the blocks of the web she requires to transcend again" — consistent with her broader ambition, documented in full on her own page, to rebuild the Weave's infrastructure under her own authorship.

## The Faith's view

The Faith of the Bound Pattern teaches all three Pillars are divine infrastructure — veins, wells, and gates set within the Weave itself, meant to serve the Pattern rather than defy it. [[Astral Conduits]] that pierce dimensional barriers are closely monitored by the Crown of Threads in Loom-Cities; unauthorized use is often viewed as heresy or a prelude to Eldritch breach.

## Connecting the Pillars to the Glyphic structure

The Three Pillars — reframed here as **Intent, Form, and Resonance** — express how mortals and magical structures interact with the Five Layers of Glyphs on a functional level:

| Pillar of flow | Glyphic root | What it represents |
|---|---|---|
| **Intent** | [[Personal Glyphs]] | The caster's will, identity, purpose, and control over magical expression |
| **Form** | [[Form Glyphs]] | The metaphysical blueprint shaping what magic creates |
| **Resonance** | [[Universal Glyphs]] | The harmonics between leylines and spellcasting — stability, corruption, or wild magic |

[[Primal Glyphs]] are explicitly *not* part of the Pillars but sit beneath them, defining the conditions under which flow is possible at all — the cosmic boundaries within which Intent, Form, and Resonance must operate.

## How the three interact

Leylines feed into Wells, allowing them to store and stabilize magic. Wells influence leylines, sometimes overloading or draining them. Astral Conduits pull energy from both to move magic across dimensions. When a leyline fractures, it may birth a new Well or an unstable Conduit; if a Conduit collapses, it can sever leyline connections across entire regions.

## Final comparison

| Feature | Leylines | Wells of Power | Astral Conduits |
|---|---|---|---|
| Function | Magic distribution network | Arcane energy reservoirs | Interdimensional magical highways |
| Movement | Constant, fixed paths | Static, concentrated locations | Ever-shifting, fluid corridors |
| Effect on spellcasters | Predictable magic boosts | Extreme bursts of power | Instant transport & magic redirection |
| Danger levels | Weak/broken lines cause instability | Overloaded Wells cause magical disasters | Collapsed Conduits cause reality fractures |
