# Selmira, The Tidelit Lady

*"I do not command the sea. I do not obey the moon. We move together, because we learned what happens when we do not."*

**Domains**: the moon-tide bond, cycles that must not break, moonlit navigation, the carrying of verdicts. **Symbol**: a crescent moon reflected whole on dark water. **Worshiped by**: night-sailors, midwives, tide-priests, calendar-keepers. **Allies**: [[Nereus]] (the Tide-King), bound to him by the ancient Pact of the Tides.

Not the ruler of the moon or the sea, but the covenant that keeps them moving together — a restraint learned the hard way in **The Breaking of the Tides**, when she pulled too far, Nereus pulled back, and the world nearly drowned between them. What they swore afterward *is* her office: the cycle, kept. She is also **Messenger of [[The Court of the Silent Tribunal]]** — when the five judges rule, it is her tides and turning cycles that carry the verdict into the world; a judgment no one has heard is not yet a judgment. When the city of Delmirath refused a Tribunal ruling, she delivered the Drowned Edict, a flood without storm or warning whose bell still rings below the waves.

## Whose is the moon?

Water's account holds the moon belongs to the tide — Selmira *is* its meaning. Spirit's account holds the moon is [[Nyzara]], the Moon That Watches — memory's lamp. Coastal syncretists say Nyzara is the moon's face and Selmira its hand; both priesthoods call this heresy, for opposite reasons.

> [!truth] DM-Truth
> There is no goddess "of" the moon. Worship-pressure carved two gods out of one phenomenon: dense coastal need anchored the *pull* into Selmira, scattered scholarly and mourner devotion anchored the *watching* into Nyzara. Neither was ever one being nor two rivals — two answers mortals gave to the same light, each real because each is believed. The hearth-goddess pattern (Kaldris/Hearthfire/Erona), replayed at Greater-God scale.
