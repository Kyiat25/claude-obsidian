# Goblin

*"We are the crack in the Pattern that never closed. We are the shard the gods could not grind to dust."*

**Origin myth**: per the Faith of the Gnawing Crown, goblins weren't created but *discarded* — a flawed Glyph fragment of Form, too jagged to bind, cast aside by the gods rather than corrected. This became the Gnawing Crown, a divine mistake with just enough power to spawn children in its image. Goblins survive through memory, mockery, and refusal to disappear — sibling lineage to [[Bugbear (Lineage)]] and [[Hobgoblin (Lineage)]]; see [[The Broken Crown and the Cracked Fang]] for the full faith.

**Physical**: mature by 8, live ~60 years; Small (3–4 ft.), 25 ft. speed, wiry, large reflective eyes.

## Traits

- **Darkvision** 60 ft.
- **Fury of the Small**: extra damage = proficiency bonus vs. larger creatures, uses = proficiency bonus/long rest
- **Nimble Escape**: Disengage or Hide as a bonus action every turn
- **Shard-Sense**: Perception or Stealth proficiency; advantage finding hidden/forgotten things
- **Memory-Keeper**: perfect recall of chosen memories, 1/long rest advantage on an INT check to recall lore
- **Languages**: Common + Goblin (a whisper-and-cackle language built to hide meaning)

**Mental**: Focus +0 / Sanity −1 — sensitivity to cracks in reality (aware of corruption early, but more vulnerable to horror).

**DCS Integration**: Dodge +3 (fastest Small race) / Endure −1 / Deflect +0; DR +0. Disadvantage on Rally alone, advantage with other goblins present.

## Corruption: The False Pattern

Corruption enters through false memories, stolen identity, or erased names. At Corruption 10+: Memory Static, Name-Loss, Pattern-Sickness. **Fractured Shard table** (1d6): False Shard, Crown-Deafness (Shard-Sense disabled), Fragment Rejection (1d4 psychic + lose a skill), Weight of Silence (Sanity save or −1d4), Eldritch Gift (+1 Whisper Point, Fury of the Small auto-fails), or Shard Holds (Corruption −1).

## Cultural touchstones

The Four Sacred Acts: remember what should be forgotten, mock what claims divinity, steal what was denied, survive one more day. Spiritual roles: Shardbinders, Memory-Weavers, Jest-Speakers, Ash-Tongues.
