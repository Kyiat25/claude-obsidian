# Kael'Thar, The Ash-Verdict

*"Flame does not ask. It decides."*

**Domains**: judgment, punishment, false purity. **Symbol**: a scorched blindfold over a burning scale. **Worshiped by**: fanatic inquisitors, divine enforcers, cults of confession. **Enemies**: [[Kaelith]] (estranged kin), [[Zerah]] (whose ambiguity he despises), [[Nyzara]] (he seeks to silence memory).

Once known as "the First Flame of Clarity" — a manifestation of fire's power to reveal truth by consuming lies, presiding over purgations where falsehood could not survive the pyre. As mortals invoked him more, he began burning faster than truth could flee: entire cities judged in a night, innocents declared guilty for surviving the flame. His fire could not doubt, so it could not stop. Some say his flame first woke [[The Hollow Thread|the Hollow Vault]] — a punishment so absolute it unstitched the Pattern itself.

**Myth — The Trial That Burned the Prophet**: a young prophet of [[Zerah]] cried out for the judgment to wait for the threads to align. Kael'Thar did not wait — he burned her and her village. That moment split fire's divine essence: [[Kaelith]] rose from the echo of regret and restraint; Kael'Thar fell, cast out from most temples, his name spoken only in curses or desperate need. Modern cults persist among inquisitorial sects, "Ash-Priests" in burned ruins, and Firebrands who carry guilt-brands for both the guilty and the innocent alike.
