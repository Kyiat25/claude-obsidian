# The Reflection Pattern

*"The gods do not stand above us. They look back through our eyes."*

The original spiritual framework of humanity — predates [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]] and survives quietly beneath and alongside it. Generational and relational rather than proselytizing: divinity begins in kinship, and each soul is shaped through its ancestors rather than a single sanctioned pantheon.

## Origin myth — The Shard That Sang

Before the gods sang their names, one strand of the Great Pattern Unbroken refused to lie flat — the Thread of Reflection, woven of possibility rather than certainty. When the gods gathered at the Loom of Origins and, in hubris, looked into the Weave to ask what flaw remained in their design, they saw themselves too clearly — their doubts, cruelties dressed as justice, comforts mistaken for truth — and the Mirror shattered. Twelve shards fell into the mortal world; from them rose the first humans, "the Race of the Shattered Mirror" — not made by a god, but born of divine contradiction, each one a mirror showing a different truth.

## Core beliefs

1. **All Spirits Reflect** — ancestors are living reflections of divine traits, not passive memories.
2. **The Pantheon Is Personal** — each family assembles its own reflected pantheon; no two are the same.
3. **The Guardian Watches** — one ancestor emerges each life as a Guardian Echo, often invoked instead of a god.
4. **To Forget Is to Break the Pattern** — forgotten ancestors fade and weaken a lineage's weave.
5. **The Mirror Shapes the Weave** — actions ripple forward and backward across ancestral lines.

## The personal pantheon

Each lineage keeps a shifting **Reflection Array**: 2–3 primary gods personal to the family, one Guardian Spirit (an honored ancestor), and one Forbidden Echo (a god or oath the family has sworn never to honor again). Fire gods (Ignara, Xethar) are reflected as hearth/defiance; Water gods (Vaelora, Selmira) as grief and restoration; Earth gods (Gaeldros, Thamur) as oaths and boundaries; Air gods (Thirian, Evara) as thought and crossroads; Spirit gods (Aelys, Nyzara) as soul-threads and return — nearly universal across Reflective lineages. Eldritch names are never spoken; families that suspect past corruption keep Silent Wards instead.

## Relationship to the Faith of the Bound Pattern

Tolerated as a "fragment-practice" rather than suppressed outright — the Faith recognizes ancestor-veneration as legitimate remembrance but treats invocation and personal pantheon-building as spiritually unstable. Historically ran from open persecution (Mirror Mantles burned, Echohands stripped of authority during the Faith's founding) through quiet survival in exile to formal, cautious tolerance today — after the Fourth Cycle's collapse and the loss of four Threadbearers, Echohands are now consulted rather than hunted, serving as witnesses in Spirit courts. Water- and Spirit-aligned cities have historically protected the practice; Earth- and Fire-aligned cities have criminalized or restricted it.
