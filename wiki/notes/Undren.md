# Undren, The Long Current

*"Orannis will tell you the water comes back. The rain, the river, the sea, the rain. He is right about the water. He is wrong about the hour it arrives in. That hour is mine, and you will not see it again."*

**Domains**: the flow of time, irreversibility, the hour passing, what cannot be stepped into twice. **Symbol**: a river drawn as a single line that never crosses itself. **Worshiped by**: the old, the dying, historians, anyone at a graveside or a departing ship. **Rivals**: [[Almerra]] (who would cage him in a grid), Orannis (the wheel against the line).

Time's substance where Almerra is its grid — the actual passing beneath the calendar. His doctrine is one sentence: what has flowed past has flowed past. The **Argument of Hours** is Water's own house divided — Orannis says time is a wheel, Undren says it's a current (the water returns, the hour does not), Almerra says it's a ledger (both needed her to have the argument on a date). All three are right about their portion; no account is the record. His rite is performed at departures: a cup of river-water poured back downstream of where it was drawn — the hour, returned, unreusable.
