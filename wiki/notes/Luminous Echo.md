# Luminous Echo (the Feywild)

The Feywild is not merely a mirror of the [[Creation World]] — it is its **boundless dream**, an ever-changing realm where possibility and emotion shape reality itself. Unlike the rigid laws of the Material Plane, the Feywild exists in a state of fluid creation, governed by will, whimsy, and the Weave's untamed essence.

Where the Luminous Echo is the Weave's dream, [[Umbral Veil]] is its shadow — its silence, its weight, its unspoken memory.

## Connection to leylines

Every Prime World leyline has a corresponding Feywild reflection — not identical, but an exaggeration of the leyline's potential rather than its fixed reality. When a Prime leyline surges, its Feywild counterpart doesn't just strengthen, it expands, bursts, reshapes the land: forests spontaneously bloom, mountains lift into the sky, rivers flow with liquid stardust. When a Prime leyline is disrupted, its Feywild counterpart fractures unpredictably into pockets of chaotic energy. Fey creatures instinctively attune to these reflections, altering reality through sheer belief.

## The Primal Glyphs in the Feywild

Each [[Primal Glyphs|Primal Glyph]] manifests differently here than on the Material Plane: the Glyph of Will becomes endless unrestrained creation; the Glyph of Threads makes fate fluid, rewritten by whim rather than structured by Aeloria's Loom; the Glyph of Endings makes true death rare, with echoes of past travelers manifesting as living illusions; the Glyph of Echoes reforges old stories and lost magic endlessly, making the Feywild a place of rebirth and recursion.

## The Laws of the Luminous Echo

1. **Law of Reflection** — everything in the Prime World casts an echo here, but not an exact one (a river may become a waterfall of liquid light).
2. **Law of Belief** — thoughts, emotions, and dreams have real power; sufficient belief can alter the Weave to make something real, for a time.
3. **Law of Exaggeration** — nothing remains small or subtle; every emotion and action is magnified far beyond its original intent.
4. **Law of Recurrence** — time loops and cycles through stories, myths, and past events; a traveler may have to relive and reshape a moment from their own past.

## The Fae

Populated by beings who exist as part of the Weave itself rather than separate from it. Archfey and powerful Fae Lords don't simply live in the Feywild — they *are* the Feywild, tied directly to its leylines. Lesser fae manipulate the Weave naturally through thought, music, and emotion. Mortal travelers are changed by prolonged exposure, gaining strange abilities, shifting memories, or forgetting their own names.

## The Unstable Heart

At the Feywild's center, where leylines converge in a chaotic storm of magic, lies the Unstable Heart — a realm of ever-shifting landscapes where dreams and nightmares become reality and time is meaningless. Said to be the source of all Wild Magic in the multiverse; many seek it for its infinite potential, but few return unchanged.
