# Krakenlight

*"The stars sank long ago. The sea just hasn't finished returning them."*

First seen Late 3rd Age, 201 TA, during a coastal flooding trial. An impossible reflection beneath the ocean surface.

## Nature

A shimmering shape with no celestial source, pulsing with an eerie lunar cadence — the moon of Nyxthys, the Black Abyss. When it rises, the sea does not rage; it listens.

## World effects

**Oceanic Dread**: the sea becomes still yet oppressive, waves stop crashing, tides stop moving — then the sea pulls, inhaling before it surges. **Maritime**: coastal waters recede then surge with alien timing, navigation suffers disadvantage, and divination about the sea fails unless cast within brine.

**Monster effects**: aquatic monsters gain advantage on stealth/ambush and innate darkvision, and feel a compulsion to drag enemies below the surface; deep aberrations rise nearer to coasts.

## Madness

Short-term: disorientation on land, constant rocking sensation. Long-term: an aquatic obsession, constant thirst, belief that one belongs in the ocean. Indefinite: the belief that the moon is beneath the sea and dry land is the lie.

## Campaign use

A harbor disappears overnight, only reflections remaining; a cult of fishermen speaks in glyph-riddles; a child is born with gills and glyphs on their palms.
