# Talanthar — The Greenwood Sovereignty

An ancient forest kingdom (~35,000 sq mi, population ~2.8 million, "difficult to count — integrated into forest") occupying the northeastern corridor of [[Elarion]], built around the World Tree **Vaelthir** at the gorge carved by the confluence of the Vaelrun and Thalaris rivers. Capital: **Talanthar** (Vael'Tharyn), a vertical labyrinth of terraces, bridges, and caverns spanning the gorge cliffs — "less an urban construct than a living ecosystem where architecture is grown, not built." Unconquered for centuries; has survived five major [[Corvessa]]n crusades through asymmetric warfare and total integration with the forest ecosystem.

## The Endless Song vs. the Bound Pattern

Talanthar's core philosophy, the **Endless Song**: *"Life is an endless song. Every leaf falls to feed the root; every death a verse that gives the next life its rhythm. To stop the cycle is the true heresy."* Vaelthir is not worshipped as a god but honored as living memory of creation itself. This stands in direct theological opposition to [[Corvessa]]'s Faith of the Bound Pattern, which frames life as a thread in a fixed divine design — making the Corvessa/Talanthar war **eschatological, not territorial**: they fight over the nature of reality, not land.

## The Greenwood is alive

The forest itself is described as three parts location, one part sentient entity — possessing forest-wide tremorsense (it can pinpoint and identify any creature by its footfalls), **bloodsense** (it recognizes individuals — and their bloodline — by blood spilled on its floor, sometimes punishing descendants for ancestors' old sins), and a form of memory that absorbs the knowledge of those who die beneath its canopy. It is quick to anger, slow to forgive, holds grudges across generations, and moves when unobserved. Predators strike only when "the forest hungers"; disease rarely spreads past a single host.

## Government: the High Council of the Greenwood

Governance by consensus and omen, not decree: each grove/valley governs itself through a local circle, sending a representative to the High Council, chaired by the **Archdruid of Vaelthir**. When consensus fails, the Archdruid communes directly with Vaelthir (days of meditation and blood-offering). Military doctrine is the **Doctrine of Rooted Stillness** — Talanthar never wages wars of conquest and only fights when the forest itself is directly threatened, though **Warden Aelthir Ashborn** and some Ash-Marked communities argue (against the Council's wishes) for preemptive strikes on Corvessan mobilizations.

**Notable figures**: **High Archdruid Liraen of the Nine Circles** (Moon Elf, converses with Vaelthir's spirit, current Council chair); **Speaker Halin Merebrook** (Halfling, diplomat of the Joy-Wild clans); **Witch-Mother Selune Maraith** (rumored immortal, maintains the moon-roads that veil Talanthar from scrying); **Ranger-Captain Ardhan Thornstep** (commander of the Thornward Watch, veteran of three Corvessan invasions); **Halfling Chieftain Bran the Laughing Death** (rides the raptor Redmaw); **Magus Kaelis Vaerion** (Keeper of the Eldermantle Archives).
