# Hedge Witch — Rank 1-4 (Folk Magic Practitioner)

*"You practice the old ways, folk magic passed down through generations."* Tier 3 (Dark Fantasy, though tonally gentler than most of this tier). Fulfills the "Hedge Witch" slot [[FEARS Advanced Backgrounds]] flagged as not-yet-developed after Batch 1.

> [!note] Naming proximity to the Witch class, not a mechanical link
> "Hedge Witch" shares no mechanics with the [[Witch]] class (no Witches Mark, no Coven, no Hex-anything) — this is folk herbalism/divination granting real Druid spellcasting by Rank 4, an entirely separate magic tradition. Flagged only because the name overlap could cause table confusion, not because the sources connect them.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cunning Woman/Man | Folk Remedies | Heal someone given up for dead, break a curse, or gain acceptance from a nature spirit |
| 2 | Village Witch | Old Ways | Protect your village from a supernatural threat, gain a nature spirit's blessing, or teach several successful apprentices |
| 3 | Renowned Cunning-Folk | Mistress/Master of the Old Ways | Save a region from blight or plague, gain recognition from druid circles, or perform a legendary miracle |
| 4 | Wise Woman/Man of the Realm | Living Bridge | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Cunning Woman/Man

**Skill Proficiency**: Medicine, Nature. **Tool Proficiency**: herbalism kit. **Language**: Sylvan or one regional language. **Equipment**: herbalism kit, a collection of herbs, clay pots, common clothes, 10 gp.

**Feature — Folk Remedies**: create herbal remedies for common ailments; know which plants are medicinal, poisonous, or magical; advantage on Medicine checks for natural treatments; country folk seek your healing; identify curses and minor magical effects.

**Specialty** (choose your tradition): Healing and midwifery, Weather reading and prediction, Protective charms and wards, Love potions and fertility magic, Curse breaking, Communing with nature spirits.

**Holdings**: a cottage or hut near the village; an herb garden; a reputation as healer; simple tools and supplies.

## Rank 2: Village Witch

**Holdings**: a well-appointed cottage with garden and workshop; income from healing and charms (20 gp/month); an extensive herb collection; 1-2 apprentices; a reputation across several villages; protected status (villagers defend you).

**Feature Expansion — Old Ways**: craft charms and minor magical items; know one Druid cantrip; perform divination rituals (similar to *augury*); advantage on Medicine checks (your remedies work better than others'); communicate with local nature spirits.

## Rank 3: Renowned Cunning-Folk

**Holdings**: a larger dwelling with extensive gardens and workshop; good income from clients seeking your aid (70 gp/month); a collection of rare herbs and magical components; 3-5 apprentices; a network of other hedge witches; protection from local authorities (they need you); your charms are sought after.

**Feature Expansion — Mistress/Master of the Old Ways**: cast several Druid spells, level determined by your rank; brew potions with real magical effects; once per week, perform a powerful ritual (remove curse, bless crops, predict the future); nature spirits treat you as an ally; your presence blesses the land (better harvests, healthier animals).

## Rank 4: Wise Woman/Man of the Realm

**Holdings**: a legendary dwelling (a pilgrimage site); excellent income from those seeking aid (200 gp/month); knowledge of every herb, plant, and natural remedy; many apprentices carrying on your traditions; a blessed garden where impossible plants grow; your charms and remedies are priceless; protected by nature itself.

**Feature — Living Bridge**: full spellcasting as a Druid of your level; speak with any plant or animal; once per month, perform miracle healing or blessing; nature spirits serve you willingly; your presence heals the land (blight recedes, illness fades). Rulers seek your counsel on natural matters, your remedies cure "incurable" diseases, stories say you can't die, and upon death you may become a nature spirit yourself.
