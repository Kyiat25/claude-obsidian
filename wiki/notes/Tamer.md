# Tamer

> [!contradiction] Retired per Classes_Summary
> [[Classes_Summary]] explicitly lists Tamer among six classes "cut from the roster during development" that "should not appear in new content." This page is preserved as a historical record of [[Welcome to FEARS]]'s original pitch, not as current FEARS canon. See [[FEARS Class Roster]] for the active 24-class list.

Beastmaster archetype built around the **Soul Bond** — a profound connection with an animal companion described as making tamer and beast "an unstoppable force with hearts beating as one."

No mechanical detail beyond this one-line pitch is given in the source.

## Mythology & Cultural Lore (from the Class Lore Compendium)

*Beast-Binders: Those Who Make Companions of the Wild.* Though retired from the current roster, the Class Lore Compendium preserves a full mythological treatment. Tamers claim to remember when mortals and beasts were peers rather than master and property, maintaining ancient partnership-traditions that civilization otherwise forgot. Revan Beast-Brother, a human who bonded with 47 companions simultaneously and whose consciousness fragmented into a living swarm-mind, represents the tradition's furthest extreme — his last fragment dissolved only when his final wolf-companion died at age 120. Ashkarra's Fall killed thousands of bonded pairs, companion and tamer dying together rather than accepting separation even in death.

**Source of Power (DM truth):** Tamer-bonds access the [[Universal Glyphs]] — the fundamental force governing relationship and connection itself. When a tamer and beast bond sufficiently, they create a genuinely new entity: neither fully tamer nor fully beast, but a hybrid consciousness. This makes tamers unwitting reality-hackers, proving that consciousness can bridge species barriers and merge separate minds into a greater whole.

**Legendary figures:** Revan Beast-Brother, who bonded with 47 companions simultaneously, his fragmented consciousness surviving as a swarm-mind until his last remaining wolf died of old age.
