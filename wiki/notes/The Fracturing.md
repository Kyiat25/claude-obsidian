# The Fracturing — The Breaking of Elvenkind

*"We were woven, not made. We walked the world when gods were still unnamed dreams. We split not once but many times — first by choice of place, later by rejection of lies. The oldest wounds are scars. The newest still bleeds."*

DM-only companion to [[The Binding (The Short Memory and the Forged Faith)]], giving the Elven side of the setting's central cosmological deception (see [[The Lie of Godhood]]).

## The cosmological truth

Astralor created all mortal races directly, weaving the Nine Fundamental Forces first; races were "cast outward" into existence, and Divine Spark gods emerged only *afterward*, finding vessels and then claiming they had created the races that already existed — a foundational lie mortals accept because gods are comprehensible where the Nine Fundamental Forces are not. This source draws a sharp distinction between two kinds of "gods": the **Elemental Embodiments** (Vatharun, Nereus, Soltharion, Veydras, Nyzara) who genuinely *are* their elements made conscious — not lying in the same way, but still permitting the broader creation-lie to persist because it benefits divine order — and the later **Divine Spark gods**, who gained power purely through worship and claimed domains within already-existing Elements.

## What elves alone know

Astralor gave elvenkind Pattern-sense: **Trance-Memory** (meditation touching ancestral echoes and fragments of the Pattern itself, sometimes predating the individual), **Pattern-Sensitivity** (an innate feeling for when something is cosmically "wrong"), and 750+ year lifespans providing enough cultural continuity to personally verify or refute claims about "ancient" history. [[The Woven Reverence]] is this Pattern-sense formalized into practice — not worship of beings, but attunement to existence itself, correctly sensing that elves "emerged when the Pattern required them." Crucially, elves intuit the gods' creation-claim is false *without* being able to comprehend or explain what actually created them, since Astralor's true nature is beyond mortal understanding entirely — they know the lie without being able to prove or replace it.

## Why "the newest wound still bleeds"

The title's structural claim, per the doc's outline: elvenkind's divisions run deeper than one schism — first splitting by choice of place during the Loomless Time (the proto-druidic First Listeners diverging by purpose), unified despite distance through the First Age, then torn by the God-Tide's divine consolidation and subsequent persecution — culminating in the [[The Ashen Web|Drow]] betrayal/departure, a wound this source frames as never having healed and as the specific reason elven subraces (Wood, High, Moon, Sea, Sun, Drow) remain permanently divided into the present age rather than reuniting.
