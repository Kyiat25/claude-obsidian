# Glyph of Threads — Aeloria's Loom, the Hand of Fate

*"Nothing is without pattern, no moment is without consequence. The Loom spins on."*

**Layer**: Primal, Tier 1. **Domains**: fate, guidance, causality, connection. **Opposing glyph**: possibly the [[Glyph of Severance]]. **Symbol**: a woven knot inscribed with concentric circles.

Represents the interconnectivity of all things — the framework by which destiny manifests, not as a lawgiver but as a weaver. It does not choose paths, it records them: why time moves forward, why history repeats but never quite the same way, why prophecy is both blessing and curse. The anchor of prophecy and the logic behind the [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]] — [[Aeloria]] spins the Threads not to enforce but to reveal what already is.

## Cultural significance

Central to the Faith of the Bound Pattern, symbolized not by a deity but by the "Silent Hand." In Loom-Cities and sanctums, Loom-Chambers are kept sacred — silent halls where fate is traced in string and ink, but no god is named. "We do not speak of the hand. We only follow the thread."

## Eldritch corruption risk

When broken, fates unravel and become self-conflicting; prophets go mad trying to read impossible futures; the past begins rewriting itself, causing paradoxes and self-erasing phenomena. Known corrupted forms include Thread-Eaters (beings who consume destiny), Snarlbinds (knots of self-cancelling lives), and Woven Nulls (mortals who never existed but still influence others). [[Ashkarra]]'s glyph is said to have resisted the unraveling even as its World Anchor collapsed — "the Third Thread of Ashkarra" is remembered as the deadly omen of repeating alignment that led to the city's doom.
