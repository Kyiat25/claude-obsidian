# Urchin — Rank 1-4

*"Nobody ever looks twice at a street kid. That's not a wound. That's the whole trick."*

> [!note] Original content — no raw source
> Flagged after Batch 1 as "adaptable but not yet written," Urchin never got an expanded treatment across any of the 3 source batches. Written here from scratch, deliberately structured around a different axis than [[Criminal]]. Criminal is an **enterprise** — it grows through hierarchy, specialty, and profit (crews, income, territory, a title like "Crime Lord"). Urchin has no enterprise at all: no boss, no cut, no product. Its axis is **social invisibility weaponized** — the fact that the powerful never learn to see you, and that this stops being a liability and starts being the most dangerous thing about you. There is no income table anywhere in this background — Urchins don't get rich, they get *unaccountable*.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Nobody | The Unseen | Slip past someone powerful specifically because they didn't register you as a threat, and use it |
| 2 | Someone Watching | Beneath Notice | Be underestimated by someone important, to their real cost |
| 3 | The Uncounted | Never on Any List | Do something that should have named you as the culprit, and go uninvestigated because no one thought to ask a street kid |
| 4 | The One Who Was Never There | You Were Never Here | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Nobody

**Skill Proficiency**: Sleight of Hand, Stealth. **Tool Proficiency**: none. **Equipment**: a small knife, ragged clothes that make you forgettable rather than notable, 2 gp.

**Feature — The Unseen**: guards, nobles, and anyone who considers themselves important instinctively don't register you as a threat or a witness. You have advantage on checks to go unnoticed around anyone who thinks they're above street-level trouble — and disadvantage on checks to be *taken seriously* when you need to be.

**Holdings**: no den, no savings, no fixed anything — you sleep where you're not noticed; a handful of other kids in the same situation who look out for each other on instinct, not organization.

## Rank 2: Someone Watching

**Holdings**: still nothing that could be taken from you — that's the point; a loose, unspoken understanding among the city's other overlooked people (beggars, servants, the elderly poor) that you're one of them, not a mark; no fixed shelter, but no shortage of places to disappear into either.

**Feature Expansion — Beneath Notice**: important people's guards, secrets, and plans are often discussed in front of you because you don't register as present — advantage on checks to overhear things you were never supposed to hear; once per week, learn one true thing a powerful person believes no "nobody" could possibly know.

## Rank 3: The Uncounted

**Holdings**: nothing in your name and nothing that could be — the point sharpens: you genuinely do not exist on any list, ledger, or watch anywhere in the city; a network of the truly overlooked (not organized, just aware of each other) numbering in the dozens; you've become someone other overlooked people quietly orient around without ever calling it a "den" or a "gang."

**Feature Expansion — Never on Any List**: when something goes wrong that a "nobody" couldn't possibly have caused, you are never even considered as a suspect — investigators, guards, and rivals alike structurally overlook you first; once per month, walk directly past a security measure meant to stop *someone*, because that someone was never imagined to look like you.

## Rank 4: The One Who Was Never There

**Holdings**: you own nothing, are recorded nowhere, and belong to no organization — and by now this is entirely by design, not poverty; hundreds of the city's overlooked people who'd close ranks around you without being asked; the unnerving fact that some of the most powerful people in the city have started to suspect *something* is working against them, without ever imagining what.

**Feature — You Were Never Here**: once per major event, you can be present for — even directly cause — something significant, and no investigation, divination, or interrogation will ever produce your name, because no one involved ever truly perceived you as a person who could matter; the city's underclass will alibi you unprompted, out of instinct rather than loyalty; you can walk through spaces built to keep "someone like you" out, because no one built them imagining you specifically. History will record what happened. It will never record who.
