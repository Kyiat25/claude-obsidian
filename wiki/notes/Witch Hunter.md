# Witch Hunter — Rank 1-4 (New, Dark Fantasy)

*"You hunt those who practice forbidden magic and dark arts."* Tier 4 (Niche, dark fantasy). Notable in-fiction tension worth flagging: nothing in this source addresses what happens when a Witch Hunter shares a party with a [[Witch]] player character — the background's flavor is squarely antagonistic to that class's whole premise, and the source doesn't reconcile it.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Initiate | Sense Corruption | Successfully expose and capture a witch or dark cultist |
| 2 | Hunter | Witch's Bane | Destroy a coven or cult, or survive an encounter with a powerful dark entity |
| 3 | Inquisitor | Cleansing Flame | Destroy a major coven, expose corruption in nobility, or survive facing an arch-demon |
| 4 | Grand Inquisitor | Scourge of Darkness | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Initiate

**Skill Proficiency**: Investigation, Religion. **Tool Proficiency**: poisoner's kit. **Equipment**: a silver holy symbol, a witch hunter's manual, iron shackles, 3 vials of holy water, dark common clothes, 15 gp.

**Feature — Sense Corruption**: advantage on Intelligence (Investigation) checks to find evidence of witchcraft; you can identify if someone is cursed (DC 15 Religion check); you know common hiding places for cultists and dark practitioners.

**Holdings**: a mentor in the witch hunter organization; access to the organization's basic equipment; a letter of authority giving some legal standing.

## Rank 2: Hunter

**Holdings**: income from the organization (25 gp/month); a network of 5-8 informants; access to silver weapons and holy items; legal authority to investigate and arrest suspects; safe houses in major cities.

**Feature Expansion — Witch's Bane**: advantage on saving throws against magic from cursed sources; you can identify magical effects related to curses, necromancy, or dark pacts; you know the weaknesses of common dark creatures (advantage on checks to recall information).

## Rank 3: Inquisitor

**Holdings**: command of 5-10 junior hunters; major organizational income (70 gp/month); access to restricted knowledge about dark magic; authority to conduct trials; a network across the region; a holy weapon or protective talisman from the organization.

**Feature Expansion — Cleansing Flame**: resistance to necrotic damage; you can perform rituals to break curses or destroy dark magic; once per week, call down divine wrath (similar to casting *flame strike*); your presence makes dark creatures uneasy.

## Rank 4: Grand Inquisitor

**Holdings**: command of 40+ witch hunters; a fortress or headquarters; excellent income (200 gp/month); a vast library of dark magic knowledge; political authority on magical matters; a network of spies and informants across the continent.

**Feature — Scourge of Darkness**: immune to being frightened; once per month, lead a purge that destroys a major dark magic operation; you can call upon military support from authorities; your word in trials is taken as truth; witches and cultists fear your name. You possess powerful holy relics, your organization has cells in every major city, rulers consult you on matters of dark magic, and your memoirs become textbooks for future hunters.
