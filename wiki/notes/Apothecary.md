# Apothecary — Science of Survival

A FEARS-specific class not mentioned in [[Welcome to FEARS]]; first appeared in [[Narrative Identity]], confirmed active by [[Classes_Summary]]. **Specialized tier**. "In a world where gods lie dead and magic itself corrupts, you turn to older truths." Complexity self-rated MODERATE (6/10) by its own per-class deep-dive.

## Signature abilities (from Classes_Summary / Class_breakdown)

- **Concoctions** — healing, toxins, utility
- **Reagent System** — resource-driven effects
- **Field Alchemy** — rapid, reactive support

**Gameplay style**: reactive support and control, stabilising allies and dismantling enemies through preparation. **Core loop**: prepare → apply → adapt → restock. Struggles in fights that don't allow time to use the kit; excels in extended encounter sequences where pre-prepared concoctions pay dividends.

## Class basics (from Apothecary.md deep-dive)

- **Hit Dice**: 1d8/level. HP at 1st: 8 + CON mod. HP at higher levels: 1d8 (or 5) + CON mod/level after 1st.
- **Proficiencies**: Armour — light. Weapons — simple, blowgun, hand crossbow. Tools — alchemist's supplies, herbalism kit, poisoner's kit. Saves — Intelligence, Constitution. Skills — Medicine, plus choose two from Arcana, Investigation, Nature, Perception, Sleight of Hand, Survival.
- **Starting Equipment**: (a) light crossbow + 20 bolts or (b) any simple weapon; (a) leather armour or (b) scale mail; (a) scholar's pack or (b) explorer's pack; alchemist's supplies, herbalism kit, 10 empty vials; a plague doctor's mask (counts as arcane focus for Apothecary features).
- **Quick Build**: Intelligence highest, then Constitution. Any lineage works (Gnome, Human, Dwarf thematic). Sage/Guild Artisan or a Medicine/Nature background. Prepare a healing/offensive concoction mix each morning; use Emergency Injection reactively; deploy offensive concoctions with your action; use Called Shots with Anatomical Precision for disruption.

## Core class features (full text)

### Field Alchemy (1st level)

Use Intelligence instead of Wisdom for Medicine checks. Gain Medicine proficiency (or Expertise if already proficient). During a short rest, spend 1 Reagent Point to grant a creature +1d4 HP per Hit Die spent (scaling to 1d6 at 5th, 1d8 at 11th, 1d10 at 17th). Identify poisons/diseases/chemical substances via INT (Medicine) or INT (Nature).

**Injury Treatment**: the class best equipped to treat, prevent, and downgrade Injuries mechanically — each Discipline interacts with [[Injury System]] differently (see Field Medic's Trauma Specialist below).

**Chemical Rush** (confirmed [[Momentum]] gain source): successfully healing a bloodied ally or applying a beneficial concoction in combat grants +1 Momentum.

### Reagent System (1st level)

A pool of **Reagent Points** (max per the table), fully regained on a long rest; a short rest (10 minutes gathering materials) regains 1d4, once per short rest. **Concoction Slots** are prepared each morning from known **Formulas** (count per table); using one costs an action (unless stated) and sometimes Reagent Points. On leveling, one known formula may be swapped.

### Medical Expertise (2nd level)

Stabilising a dying creature with a healer's kit also restores 1 HP. Action + 1 Reagent Point: end one disease or neutralise one poison on a creature within 5 ft. Advantage on saves against poison and disease.

### Toxin Resistance (2nd level)

Resistance to poison damage, immunity to poisoned. Resistance to acid damage at 10th. Immunity to poison damage at 15th.

### Alchemical Disciplines (3rd level, and 6th, 10th, 14th, 18th)

Choose a specialisation: **Plague Doctor**, **Mad Scientist**, or **Field Medic**. Features at 3rd/6th/10th/14th/18th. First sighting of Apothecary subclasses in this wiki — no prior source to cross-check against.

- **Plague Doctor (3rd)**: toxic concoctions ignore poison resistance; poison-immune creatures take half damage instead. Diagnose any disease/poison via 1-minute examination.
- **Mad Scientist (3rd)**: during a long rest, create a mutagen granting one of +2 to an ability score (max 20), 60 ft darkvision, +10 ft movement, or +2 DR natural armour. One mutagen active at a time.
- **Field Medic (3rd)**: administer concoctions to adjacent allies as a bonus action instead of an action. Healing a creature at 0 HP also grants temp HP = INT mod.
- **Field Medic (6th) — Trauma Specialist**: spend time + Reagent Points to remove an Injury outright — 1 minute + 2 RP for Minor, 10 minutes + 4 RP for Major, 1 hour + 8 RP for Critical. A third distinct [[Injury System]] class hook, and the first that **removes** an Injury outright rather than downgrading a tier (Paladin's Sanctified Intercession downgrades Critical→Major; Bard's Soothing Performance downgrades Major→Minor/heals Minor) — a stronger effect gated behind real time and resource cost rather than being free.

### Anatomical Precision (4th level) — a new Called Shot variant

Gain proficiency in Called Shots. Making a Called Shot with a **thrown concoction** targets a body part with concoction-specific effects distinct from [[Called Shot]]'s standard penalty/save tables:

| Location | Effect |
|---|---|
| Head | Target has disadvantage on mental saves for 1 round |
| Torso | Target takes +1d6 damage from the concoction |
| Arms | Target has disadvantage on attack rolls for 1 round |
| Legs | Target's speed reduced by 10 ft for 1 round |

Once per turn on a concoction hit, the target's DR is reduced by 1 against the Apothecary's next concoction until the end of the Apothecary's next turn.

### Unstable Concoctions (5th level) — new Corruption-gain source

Using a concoction may be made "unstable": roll a d20. On 1-2, roll a mishap; otherwise the concoction's numerical effects increase 50%.

| d6 | Mishap |
|---|---|
| 1 | Affects the Apothecary instead of the target |
| 2 | Explodes: 2d6 fire to all within 5 ft |
| 3 | Gain 1 [[Corruption]] Score |
| 4 | Toxic fumes: all within 10 ft CON save or poisoned |
| 5 | Works, but 1d6 psychic damage and lose 1 Focus |
| 6 | Wild magic surge — see [[Unstable Magic Zones]] |

### Enhanced Reagents (6th level)

Reroll 1s on concoction effect dice (keep the new result). Concoctions costing 2+ Reagent Points cost 1 less (minimum 1).

### Emergency Protocol (7th level) — new Corruption-gain source

At combat start, if at least half Reagent Points remain, gain temp HP = Apothecary level. Once per short rest, as a reaction when about to drop to 0 HP: drop to 1 HP instead, gain resistance to all damage until the start of the next turn, and gain 1 [[Corruption]] Score.

### Advanced Formulas (9th level)

Learn two formulas that don't count against the known-formulas limit. Can concentrate on two concoction effects simultaneously.

### Heroic Boon (10th level)

Choose: **Boon of Endless Experimentation** (regain 1d4 Reagent Points at combat start; prepare 2 additional concoctions/day; learn 3 additional formulas) or **Boon of Chemical Immunity** (immune to acid and poison damage; can't be diseased, petrified, or paralysed; concoctions affect normally-immune creatures).

### Chemical Burn (11th level) — new Corruption-gain source

Bonus action: inject combat drugs. 1 minute: +2 attack rolls and saves, +10 ft speed, an extra bonus action/turn (concoctions only), immunity to frightened/charmed. On ending: 1 level of exhaustion and 1d4 [[Corruption]] Score. Once per long rest.

### Metamorphic Infusion (13th level) — new Corruption-gain source

During a long rest, spend 8 Reagent Points + 100 gp rare materials to permanently alter a willing creature: +1 to an ability score (max 22), a new sense (darkvision, 10 ft tremorsense), resistance to one damage type, or a minor physical adaptation. Once per creature per month. Each infusion risks 1d4 [[Corruption]] Score **for the subject**, not the Apothecary — the first sighting of Corruption risk being transferable to someone other than the actor.

### Master Alchemist (15th level)

Concoction ranges double; area effects +5 ft radius. Create two concoctions with a single action (full cost each). A critical thrown-concoction hit doesn't consume the concoction.

### Volatile Mastery (17th level)

Unstable Concoction mishaps only trigger on a natural 1 (not 1-2). May trigger a "controlled mishap" for double effect, choosing the mishap result. Immunity to own area-effect concoctions.

### Epic Boon (20th level)

Choose: **Boon of the Philosopher's Stone** (once/day transmute up to 1 cubic ft of material; healing concoctions also remove all conditions/diseases/poisons; no aging, immune to magical aging; halved Corruption gains; once/long rest create 1d6 vials of Universal Solvent) or **Boon of the Living Laboratory** (choose a damage-type immunity each turn; regain 5 HP/turn at 1+ HP; regain 1 Reagent Point/turn in combat; taking piercing/slashing damage deals 2d6 acid to all within 5 ft, no save).

## Corruption-gain pattern (cross-cutting note)

Apothecary is the most Corruption-heavy class documented so far: four independent mechanical Corruption-gain triggers (Unstable Concoction mishap roll of 3, Emergency Protocol's emergency-stimulant reaction, Chemical Burn's crash, Metamorphic Infusion's risk to the subject) — a real mechanical link between "chemistry as a substitute for magic" and the same metaphysical-rot system magic itself risks, not previously demonstrated by any non-caster class. See [[Corruption]].

## Class Reactions (confirmed universal mechanic, largest set seen so far — 7 reactions)

All draw on the shared PB pool (refresh short/long rest, 1/round cap, OA-exempt) confirmed by [[Class Reactions]); most also cost Reagent Points on top of PB uses, extending the Bard's resource-stacking pattern:

- **Emergency Injection** (1st) — trigger: ally within 5 ft takes damage; 1 PB + 1 RP: ally gains 1d6+INT temp HP; if bloodied, also resistance to the triggering damage type until the Apothecary's next turn.
- **Volatile Defence** (2nd) — trigger: a creature enters a space within 5 ft; 1 PB: chemical cloud, CON save (DC 8+PB+INT) or poisoned + movement 0 until the Apothecary's next turn.
- **Caustic Retaliation** (3rd) — trigger: hit by a melee attack; 1 PB + 1 RP: attacker takes 2d6 acid; their weapon loses 1 DR penetration (cumulative, max −3) until repaired.
- **Adrenaline Surge** (5th) — trigger: an ally within 30 ft fails a save; 2 PB + 2 RP: they reroll with advantage; if still failed, they gain +2 Momentum.
- **Smoke Bomb Escape** (7th) — trigger: the Apothecary or an ally within 30 ft is targeted; 2 PB: 10 ft smoke cloud (heavily obscured until the Apothecary's next turn); the target may move half speed without provoking.
- **Panacea Protocol** (10th) — trigger: an ally within 30 ft drops to 0 HP; 3 PB + 4 RP: they regain 3d8+INT HP and end one condition; the Apothecary gains +2 Momentum.
- **Philosopher's Reversal** (15th) — trigger: the Apothecary or an ally within 30 ft would gain a condition (except exhaustion); 3 PB + 3 RP: negate it and reflect onto the source (within 60 ft) — CON save (DC 8+PB+INT) or they suffer it instead.
