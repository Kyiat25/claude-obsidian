# Blood Moon of Rebirth

*"You are not the first version of yourself. You never were."*

First seen End 3rd Age, on **the Day of Twelve Shadows** — the Fall of [[Ashkarra]]. The moon whose rising marked the single worst Eldritch event on record.

## Nature

The Weave's grip on death weakens. Mythically, the Glyph of Passing falters; the Restbringer and Echoed Mother are silenced for three nights; a "Time of the Returning" where even sealed graves weep.

## Mechanics

Takes three nights to fully crest; no other Eldritch Moon can appear while it holds; it grows deeper red each night. At its apex: the **Rebirth Surge** — over a 1-hour period, any creature dead within the last 30 days has a 50% resurrection chance (unless their soul was destroyed or consumed by [[Nhal'Zereth]] or [[Vael'Akir]]). Resurrection check: roll d6, 3+ succeeds and retains identity/alignment; each prior death increases the DC by +1.

**On failure**: the creature returns as a **Hallowed-Husk** (void of memory, drawn to Eldritch forces) or a **Bestial Echo** (no longer a person, tethered to [[The Null Reflection]]). Those who return whole bear the **Brand of Red Return**, a spiraling glyph of blood and unmaking that cannot be hidden or removed.

## Interpretations

Spirit seers call it "The Soulquake"; Earth priests lock tomb-vaults and silence dead bells; Weave-Wardens call it "The Third Moon That Shouldn't Be."

## Campaign use

Reintroduce dead NPCs — changed; create cults planning deaths timed to the moon's rise for resurrection; Tether/Dream-Born spirits return wearing flesh not theirs.

## The Ashkarra Convergence

This moon's most infamous rising is inseparable from Ashkarra's fall: all twelve Eldritch Moons rose within 58 days, culminating in this moon's alignment on Ashkarra's Spirit Thread festival — the third consecutive Spirit alignment (Thread stagnation), resulting in the First Hollow Breach, the World Anchor's implosion, and the complete unmaking of the Loom-City of Ashkarra. This proved, definitively, that no Thread may hold the same alignment more than twice. See [[Ashkarra]] and [[The Hollow Thread]] for the full cascading-failure account.
