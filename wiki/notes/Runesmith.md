# Runesmith

> [!contradiction] Retired per Classes_Summary
> [[Classes_Summary]] explicitly lists Runesmith among six classes "cut from the roster during development" that "should not appear in new content." This page is preserved as a historical record of [[Welcome to FEARS]]'s original pitch, not as current FEARS canon. See [[FEARS Class Roster]] for the active 24-class list.

"Master of inscribing power into reality itself," commanding the First Language. Resource: **Rune Points**, spent to carve symbols in ancient tongues (Draconic, Abyssal, Celestial, Infernal) via **Runic Cores** and **Personal Symbols**. Every casting risks **Backfire** — "reality lashes back at such presumption."

Backfire is thematically the closest archetype-level echo of [[Unstable Magic Zones]]' volatility. No mechanical detail beyond this one-line pitch is given in the source.

## Mythology & Cultural Lore (from the Class Lore Compendium)

*Symbol-Forgers: Those Who Write Reality in Stone.* Though retired from the current 24-class roster, the Class Lore Compendium preserves a full mythological treatment worth recording. Runesmiths claim the first among them discovered that carving a symbol with sufficient intent makes reality conform to it — predating formal glyphic theory entirely ("did runesmiths discover glyphs first, or did glyphs inspire runesmithing?" remains an in-world open argument). Dwarven Stone-Law formalized the tradition in the First Age; the Second Age's god-binding runes (which briefly trapped lesser deities) triggered a divine Rune-Purge that killed thousands of practitioners. At Ashkarra's Fall, the city's own defensive runes contradicted each other when all Eldritch Moons aligned, and the resulting reality-collapse contributed directly to the city's destruction.

**Source of Power (DM truth):** Runes access the [[Form Glyphs]] — reality's self-description. A runesmith carving a symbol with sufficient skill and conviction is editing reality's source code directly; the universe reads the inscription and updates itself. This makes runesmithing the most permanent form of magic in Dór-o Estel: carved reality doesn't fade the way cast spells do.

**Legendary figures:** Thalgrim Rune-Breaker, the dwarf who developed anti-runes capable of canceling any other runic power, making him arguably the most dangerous mortal alive in his age.

*"Wizards cast spells that fade. Clerics channel gods that fail. We carve truth into stone that outlasts civilizations... This is command written in language reality cannot ignore. We are writing the world."* — The Carver's Creed
