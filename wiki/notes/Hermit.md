# Hermit — Rank 1-4 (Reclusive Mystic)

*"You withdrew from society to pursue enlightenment, knowledge, or redemption."* Tier 2 (Specialized). Fulfills the "Hermit" slot [[FEARS Advanced Backgrounds]] flagged as not-yet-developed after Batch 1 (that earlier note suggested it "could adapt to Wilderness Guide or Mystic" — this version leans fully into the mystic/oracle framing rather than wilderness).

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Seeker | Discovery | Share your discovery with someone worthy, complete a spiritual journey, or help someone using your wisdom |
| 2 | Wise One | Sage Wisdom | Your counsel prevents a disaster, a pilgrim spreads word of your wisdom, or you perform a miracle of insight |
| 3 | Oracle | Prophetic Vision | Make a prediction that saves a nation, guide a hero on their quest, or achieve a legendary spiritual breakthrough |
| 4 | Enlightened Master | Transcendent Wisdom | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Seeker

**Skill Proficiency**: Medicine, Religion. **Tool Proficiency**: herbalism kit. **Language**: one of choice. **Equipment**: herbalism kit, a scroll case full of notes, a winter blanket, common clothes, 5 gp.

**Feature — Discovery**: during your isolation, you uncovered something significant (a great truth about the cosmos, nature, or gods; a terrible secret; a powerful prophecy; the location of something lost; or a new perspective granting wisdom) — worked out with the GM.

**Life of Seclusion** (choose why you withdrew): Seeking enlightenment, Hiding from something, Serving penance, Protecting a secret, Communing with nature/spirits, Avoiding persecution.

**Holdings**: a remote dwelling (cave, hut, abandoned tower); knowledge others seek; self-sufficiency (you can survive without civilization).

## Rank 2: Wise One

**Holdings**: an improved dwelling; a small garden or food source; a collection of books or scrolls (worth 100 gp); 3-5 people who've sought your counsel; a reputation in nearby settlements as a wise hermit.

**Feature Expansion — Sage Wisdom**: advantage on Insight checks; answer philosophical or spiritual questions (campaign-mystery insights for players); people seek your counsel on difficult decisions; once per week, provide cryptic but accurate guidance; your presence calms animals and people.

## Rank 3: Oracle

**Holdings**: a well-appointed hermitage with a shrine or meditation space; a significant knowledge collection (worth 500 gp); a pilgrim's trail leading to your dwelling; 20-30 people consider you their spiritual guide; gifts from grateful seekers (equivalent to 50 gp/month); students who maintain your hermitage.

**Feature Expansion — Prophetic Vision**: once per week, receive a vision or insight about future events (DM provides a cryptic clue); enter deep meditation to seek answers (similar to *augury* or *divination*); your predictions often come true; leaders consult you before major decisions; advantage on Insight to see through deceptions.

## Rank 4: Enlightened Master

**Holdings**: a legendary hermitage (a pilgrimage site); a monastery or school built around your teachings; 100+ disciples and students; your sayings are collected and studied; offerings and gifts (worth 200 gp/month, which you may give away); protected status (harming you is taboo).

**Feature — Transcendent Wisdom**: automatic success on Wisdom checks (DC 20 or lower); once per month, achieve perfect clarity about any question (the DM must answer one question truthfully); your presence brings peace (creatures near you have disadvantage on attacks unless they save); perceive truth and falsehood automatically; your counsel shapes the course of history. Rulers journey to seek your wisdom, your teachings become a philosophy or religion, miracles are attributed to you, and after death you're venerated as a saint or enlightened master.
