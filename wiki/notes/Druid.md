# Druid — Voice of the World

**Full Caster tier**. "The druid doesn't protect the forest. The forest protects itself. The druid is how it asks." Control, transformation, and accumulated memory — beast forms give tactical flexibility no pure caster can access, and a growing network of Nature-Remembered locations means the Druid fights better in places they've fought before. Complexity self-rated MODERATE (6/10) by its own per-class deep-dive — noticeably higher than [[Cleric]]'s 4/10, driven by Beast Form's own HP pool, Evasion, and DR tracked alongside normal spellcasting.

> "The druid doesn't protect the forest. The forest protects itself. The druid is how it asks."

## Signature abilities

- **Wild Shape** — become beasts
- **Nature Magic** — control environment
- **Primordial Bond** — sustain and resilience

**Gameplay style**: adaptive, shifting between roles depending on need. **Core loop**: transform → act → adapt → repeat.

## Class basics (from Druid.md deep-dive)

- **Hit Dice**: 1d8/level. HP at 1st: 8 + CON mod. HP at higher levels: 1d8 (or 5) + CON mod/level after 1st.
- **Proficiencies**: Armour — light, medium, shields. Weapons — simple. Tools — herbalist tools. Saves — Intelligence, Wisdom. Skills — choose two from Animal Handling, Arcana, Gathering, Insight, Medicine, Nature, Perception, Religion, Skinning, Survival.
- **Starting Equipment**: (a) shield or (b) any simple weapon; (a) scimitar or (b) any simple melee weapon; leather armour, an explorer's pack, and a druidic focus.
- **Quick Build**: Wisdom highest, then Constitution. Any lineage (Wood Elf, Human, Half-Elf thematic). Outlander/Hermit or a Nature/Animal Handling background. Open fights in familiar locations when possible; transform for front-line work; use Draw Power to recover slots; use Nature's Snare to control space.

## Core class features (full text)

### Druidic (1st level)

Knows Druidic, the secret language of druids — can leave hidden messages in bark, stone, and grass only other druids can find.

### Natural Anchor (1st level) — new Sanity interaction

In natural environments: advantage on Sanity saves against fear, horror, and supernatural dread. After each long rest in a natural environment, recover 1 additional [[Sanity]] point. First class-specific Sanity-recovery hook seen in this cluster.

### Nature's Gift (1st level)

Bonus action: heal a creature within 5 ft, rolling PB d4s (d6s at 5th level) and restoring that many HP. Uses = PB per long rest. Once per long rest, also remove one minor condition (poisoned, charmed, frightened, blinded).

### Spellcasting (1st level) — fifth confirmed caster DC formula, and a Focus-check confirmation

Wisdom is the spellcasting ability. **Spell Save DC = 8 + PB + Wisdom modifier. Spell Attack Modifier = PB + Wisdom modifier.** Fifth independent confirmation of the `8 + PB + ability mod` formula, after [[Bard]], [[Bender]], [[Binder]], and [[Cleric]]. Prepares spells = WIS mod + Druid level, from the Primordial spell list. Ritual spells castable without slots. **Concentration uses a Focus check (`1d20 + WIS`) instead of Constitution** — the first exact formula given for this mechanic, after [[Bender]]'s unformulated "Focus check" and [[Binder]]'s "Focus saves." Not stated to be identical to those (Bender/Binder never gave a roll formula), but it's the strongest evidence yet that FEARS replaces CON-based concentration with a Focus-based roll universally, not just for Druids.

### Wild Shape (2nd level) — full DCS integration

Transform into Beast Form (action) or use Draw Power (bonus action). Uses per short rest scale with level (table above).

**Beast Form**: transform into a memorised Beast up to the CR cap in the Improved Beast Form progression (table below). Duration = hours equal to PB.

**Beast Form HP by size**:

| Beast Size | Beast Form HP |
|---|---|
| Tiny | 2 + PB |
| Small | 8 + PB |
| Medium | 14 + PB |
| Large | 22 + PB |
| Huge | 34 + PB |
| Gargantuan | 50 + PB |

**Beast Form Evasion**: `1d20 + Beast's DEX modifier + PB`. Small/Tiny forms roll with advantage — see [[Evasion]] for the class-hook cross-link.

**Beast Form DR**: `(Beast's AC − 10) + Beast's CON modifier + Beast's STR modifier (Large or larger only)`. Negative modifiers treated as 0. **Called Shots reduce Beast Form DR by 2** — a Beast-Form-specific DR penalty distinct from any general Called Shot rule. See [[Damage Reduction]] for the baseline formula, kept separate from the Circle of the Moon subclass bonus already on that page.

**Momentum**: entering Beast Form during combat **resets** Momentum to +1 (not a gain or loss — the first "reset to a nonzero value" Momentum interaction seen in this cluster). Natural attacks count as weapon attacks for Momentum purposes.

**Reference table** (sample beasts): Wolf (Medium, AC 13, CON +1, Flat DR 4), Black Bear (Medium, AC 11, CON +2, Flat DR 3), Brown Bear (Large, AC 11, CON +3, STR +4, Flat DR 8), Dire Wolf (Large, AC 14, CON +2, STR +3, Flat DR 9), Giant Crocodile (Huge, AC 14, CON +4, STR +6, Flat DR 14), Mammoth (Huge, AC 13, CON +5, STR +7, Flat DR 15).

**Primal Dissolution**: staying in Beast Form beyond normal duration requires a **Sanity save (DC 12)** to revert voluntarily. Failure: retry once per minute; each subsequent failure causes 1 Sanity degradation. New fixed-DC Sanity save, distinct from the confirmed `1d20+SAN+PROF` roll formula (this is the target DC for that roll, not a new formula).

**Draw Power**: expend 1 Wild Shape use + 1 Focus to recover one expended spell slot of level ≤ PB. Cannot use while in Beast Form. A direct class-specific draw against the [[Focus]] pool (max 3), on top of its existing Reaction/tactical-currency role.

### Wild Shape Combat Modes (2nd level)

Choose one when transforming: **Predator Form** (reduce Called Shot penalties by 1 in Beast Form), **Guardian Form** (+1 DR in Beast Form, stacks with Beast Form DR), **Tracker Form** (advantage on Evasion rolls in Beast Form).

### Nature's Snare (2nd level, reaction)

When a hostile creature enters or ends its turn within 10 ft: STR save (DC = 8+PB+WIS) or restrained until end of the Druid's next turn. Area becomes difficult terrain until the Druid's next turn regardless. Once per round — its own self-limiting budget, not the shared pool (ruled: explained by Druid's Wild-Shape-centered resource identity — see [[Class Reactions]]).

### Druid Subclass (2nd level, and 7th, 11th, 15th)

Choose a Circle (Circle of the Leaf, Circle of the Shifter, Circle of the Moon named as examples). Features at 2nd/7th/11th/15th, plus always-prepared Circle Spells.

### Improved Beast Form (5th level and beyond)

| Level | Max Beast CR |
|---|---|
| 2nd | 1/4 |
| 5th | 1/2 |
| 9th | 1 |
| 14th | 3 |
| 17th | 4 |
| 20th | See Nature's Boundless Form |

### Bestial Reflex (6th level, reaction)

While in Beast Form: when targeted by a melee or ranged attack, gain advantage on the Evasion roll; if the attack is evaded, move up to 5 ft without provoking. Uses = PB per short rest — its own independent budget, directly gated on Wild Shape being active (ruled: explained by that dependency, not an unexplained pool bypass — see [[Class Reactions]]).

### Call of the Wild (6th level)

Communicate meaningfully with any Beast- or Animal-tagged creature regardless of current form.

### Nature's Remembrance (6th level) — new Momentum gain source

Casting a Primordial spell of 2nd level or higher, using Wild Shape, or spending 10+ minutes attuning at a location may earn that location a **Nature-Remembrance Mark** (tracked with the GM, up to 3 per location). In a Nature-Remembered area: **+1 Momentum** when casting a spell or using Wild Shape (once per turn); once per short rest, reroll a failed Concentration check there and use either result.

### Heroic Boon (10th level)

Choose: **Rite of the Kingdom** (Beasts/Animal-tagged creatures understand the Druid's speech; summoned/charmed creatures +2 damage rolls; advantage on CHA checks with Beasts; once/long rest, cast a Beast-summoning/charming spell without a slot) or **Rite of the Shaper** (regain 1 Wild Shape use if none remain when rolling initiative, once/long rest; Beast Form entry becomes a bonus action; once/long rest, enter Beast Form at max HP + WIS mod × 2).

### Primal Shift (10th level) — second new Momentum gain source

Entering Beast Form during an active combat encounter (not before initiative) grants **+1 Momentum** immediately — separate from and additional to Wild Shape's Momentum reset-to-+1 above.

### Nature's Command (11th level)

Action, uses = PB per long rest. **Command Beasts** (WIS-mod creatures within 60 ft WIS save vs Spell Save DC or charmed 1 min), **Summon Vines** (20 ft/30 ft-at-15th radius difficult terrain, STR save or restrained 1 min), or **Elemental Surge** (conjure a 1-minute elemental spirit within 30 ft, bonus-action commands: Fire 2d8 burst, Water 2d8 heal, Air push+prone, Earth difficult terrain).

### Deepening Memory (11th level)

Nature-Remembered areas with 2+ marks gain **Grounding Presence** (advantage on Sanity saves/fear from unnatural sources, self + allies within 30 ft) and **Primordial Reading** (once/long rest/location, sensory impression of the most significant magical event there in the past 30 days).

### Primordial Bond (14th level)

**Flexible Nature Magic**: on finishing a long rest, choose one unprepared Primordial spell and cast it once without a slot. **Reactive Disruption** (reaction): when a creature within 30 ft begins casting, contested WIS (Insight) check vs their spellcasting ability check — success negates the spell (slot not expended). Uses = WIS mod per long rest — again its own independent budget (ruled: consistent with Druid's Wild-Shape-centered resource design rather than a shared-pool contradiction — see [[Class Reactions]]).

### Nature's Grace (17th level)

Resistance to poison damage; immunity to poisoned. Resistance to cold damage. Natural 20 on a saving throw recovers one expended spell slot (7th level or lower) or one Wild Shape use.

### Primordial Memory (17th level)

Locations with 3 Nature-Remembrance Marks become **Primordially Remembered**: the 60 ft area is difficult terrain for hostiles (non-magical, undispellable); Tremorsense 30 ft while there; always aware of direction/distance/intrusion by a magically-present creature at up to 3 such locations since the last long rest.

### Epic Boon (20th level)

Choose: **Nature's Eternal Voice** (once/long rest cast any Primordial spell ≤8th without a slot; once/short rest maximise damage dice for a Primordial spell ≤5th; two spells ≤6th always prepared, each castable once/long rest without a slot; ignore V/S/free-material components) or **Nature's Boundless Form** (unlimited Wild Shape; transform into CR ≤5 Beasts without a charge; regain WIS-mod HP at the start of each turn in Beast Form; once/long rest cast *shapechange* without a slot; action to end charmed/frightened/poisoned/paralysed/disease on self or one ally within 30 ft, WIS-mod uses/long rest).

## Last Stand (confirmed universal, 7th class — standard permanent-death clause)

| Option | Effect |
|---|---|
| Final Form | Immediately enter Wild Shape as any Beast, Plant, or Elemental with CR up to character level; lose all damage vulnerabilities; immune to hostile abjuration magic. Lasts up to 5 minutes |
| Ley Waste | Choose one Primordial cantrip/spell up to one level below highest slot; at the end of each turn for 1 minute, cast it without a slot/components (no concentration). Maintains one instance up to 4 additional minutes after death |
| Uncivilize | 5 minutes: all hostile creatures within 1000 ft have speed halved and can't teleport/plane-travel; all allies within 1000 ft gain *Freedom of Movement* |

Seventh class confirming [[Last Stand]] as universal; all three options reaffirm the standard permanent-death clause (no exception, same as Barbarian/Bard/Apothecary/Binder/Cleric — Bender remains the lone exception).

## Class Reactions — a genuine break from the shared-pool pattern

Druid names three reaction-triggered features — **Nature's Snare** (2nd, once per round, no pool reference), **Bestial Reflex** (6th, PB uses per short rest, Beast Form only), **Reactive Disruption** (14th, WIS-mod uses per long rest) — but unlike every other class confirmed so far, **none of them draw from a shared PB-sized reaction pool**. Each has its own independent, differently-scaled budget (round-capped, PB/short rest, WIS-mod/long rest). This is a step beyond [[Cleric]]'s single pool-bypassing exception (Sacred Interposition) — Druid has no shared pool to bypass at all. Not treated as a contradiction of [[Class Reactions]] as a framework (the other six classes still confirm it), but flagged as the first per-class deep-dive where the "universal shared pool" default doesn't appear to apply to any of the class's reactions.
