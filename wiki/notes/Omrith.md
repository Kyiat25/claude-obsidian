# Omrith, The Knotfinder

*"Aelys mends. Serelune seals. Varethis cuts. Splendid work, all of it — done exactly where I put the chalk mark. Ask any of them who told them where to look."*

**Domains**: inspection of the Weave, knots found in fate, fraying found before it spreads. **Symbol**: a thread with a small white mark tied at its flaw. **Worshiped by**: diviners who audit rather than prophesy; curse-finders; the weaver-guilds (an adopted patronage she has never corrected).

Fate's gods act on the Weave; Omrith is the one who looks at it first — with no power to mend, seal, or cut, only to find. Knots are Fate's business, frays are Death's, so she reports up both stairs and belongs wholly to neither — the only Spirit god both clusters claim. She regards [[Vel'Zyren]]'s web as the greatest knot ever tied: deliberate, load-bearing, and unmarkable, because a knot that vast is indistinguishable from structure.

Her diviners don't tell futures — they *audit* them, answering only "is something wrong," never "what happens." The weaver-guilds' adopted rite leaves one deliberate flaw unmended in the season's first bolt of cloth — Omrith's mark — on the doctrine that a cloth with a found flaw is honest, and a cloth with no flaw found is merely uninspected.

> [!truth] DM-Truth
> Omrith's ledger doesn't balance: a small, steady fraction of the flaws she marks are gone before the menders arrive, mended by no office she can identify. Her doctrine files these "self-healed." It is the same silent signature Serelune files as her own failures and Nyzara files as returned memories — [[Aeloria]] (and, rarer, [[The Restorer]]) passing through the inspection regime of gods who cannot know the inspector is also being inspected.
