# Aeravae

The Loom-City built as a direct answer to [[Ashkarra]]'s fall — and, at present, the site of the crisis in [[The Threadbearer Crisis]].

## The Echo-Cenote

Where every other Loom-City built its World Anchor by clearing ground for one, Aeravae's Anchor — the **Echo-Cenote** — was deliberately grafted directly into a living, newly-planted World Tree. It is the only known hybrid Anchor/World Tree system in existence: a mortal-crafted construct sharing structure with a naturally-occurring one, built at the seam between two traditions that, per [[World Anchors and Thread System]], had turned out to be interdependent rather than substitutable.

## Why cooperation, why here

Ashkarra's near-collapse of the entire Anchor system was the forcing event. Building Aeravae required cooperation across factions that otherwise rarely align: the Faith of the Bound Pattern, wood elves, sea elves, high elves, humans, and dwarves all contributed to its founding. The result is presented in the source as proof that cooperation across traditions outperforms any single tradition's purity — a explicit thematic counterpoint to Ashkarra's mono-culture (Burning Concord) collapse.

## Government and culture

Matrilineal Monarchy — the only Loom-City with this government form. Faith influence is cooperative rather than dominant, reflecting its founding: no single tradition (Faith orthodoxy, Woven Reverence, Deep Law) holds primacy the way Kel Varyan or Selveran skew toward their respective dominant traditions.

## Current relevance

Per the FAQ on [[World Anchors and Thread System]], Aeravae-style grafted hybrids are explicitly named as "the most likely future" for the Anchor system generally, given that pure Tree-only and pure Anchor-only approaches have each already failed or proven insufficient once. Aeravae is therefore not just a historical curiosity but the template the setting is quietly pointing toward for what comes next.
