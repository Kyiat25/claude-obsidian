# The Others — The Peoples Between Truth and Lie

*"The elves remember what was. Humans forget what wasn't. But we — we were never told a story complete enough to believe or reject. We found our own truths in the cracks."* — Moragh Ironvoice, Dwarven Stone-Singer

DM-only companion to [[The Fracturing]] and [[The Binding (The Short Memory and the Forged Faith)]], placing every remaining race on a spectrum of how close it comes to sensing the setting's central creation-lie (full framing on [[The Lie of Godhood]]).

## The spectrum

**Closest to the truth**: Elves (Pattern-sense, can't explain the alternative — see [[The Fracturing]]), Orcs (already assume gods are trials/adversaries rather than benevolent creators — see [[The Path of the First Scar]]), Goblins (know they were "forgotten," closest to intuiting no race was ever truly chosen — see [[The Broken Crown and the Cracked Fang]]), Wood Elf druids (living proof via trees and cycles that predate divine organization).

**Middle ground**: Dwarves (endurance over theology, instinctively skeptical of divine claims — see [[The Deep Law]]), Gnomes (treat reality as "glitchy software," almost see the fundamental bugs — see [[The Wonder-Tithe Codex]]), Tieflings (sense divine messages are misread, question inherited guilt — see [[The Ashen Testament]]), Halflings (preserve alternative truths through joy without probing them deeply — see [[The Joy Truths]]).

**Furthest from the truth**: Dragonborn (built their entire identity on being "Soltharion's chosen" — see [[The Burning Concord]]), Humans (too short-lived to verify any ancient claim, desperately need structure — see [[The Reflection Pattern]]), Drow (believe Vel'Zyren's transformation of them *was* creation — the deepest immersion in the lie of any race — see [[The Ashen Web]]).

## The tragic reality

Every race except elves has either short enough lifespans or fragmented enough cultural memory to be unable to verify ancient claims, leaving each with only three real options: accept the gods' creation story (comprehensible but false), accept the incomprehensible cosmic truth (accurate but meaningless to daily life), or — what most actually do — create their own meaning in the gap between the two. This framing is the thesis binding together every individual race-religion page already in this vault: each faith documented under [[Dór-o Estel Cosmology MOC]]'s "Organized faiths" section is, per this source, one race's answer to exactly this unresolvable choice.
