# Ship Captain — Rank 1-4 (Naval Commander)

*"You command a vessel and crew, whether merchant, naval, or pirate."* Tier 1 (Essential). Fulfills the "Ship Captain" slot [[FEARS Advanced Backgrounds]] flagged as not-yet-developed after Batch 1. Distinct from [[Pirate]], which covers the outlaw side of seafaring specifically.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | First Mate | Sea Legs | Command the ship during the captain's absence, save the ship from disaster, or prove your navigation in a storm |
| 2 | Captain | Master Sailor | Win a naval battle, complete a historic voyage, or attract famous crew members |
| 3 | Fleet Commander | Naval Authority | Win a major naval engagement, establish a new trade route, or become admiral of a nation's navy |
| 4 | Admiral/Merchant Prince of the Seas | Master of the Seas | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: First Mate

**Skill Proficiency**: Athletics, Perception. **Tool Proficiency**: navigator's tools, water vehicles. **Equipment**: navigator's tools, 50 ft of silk rope, a belaying pin (club), a lucky charm, traveler's clothes, 10 gp.

**Feature — Sea Legs**: advantage on checks to avoid being knocked prone on a ship; climb ship rigging at full speed; predict weather at sea (advantage on Survival checks); always know which way is north at sea.

**Specialty** (choose your vessel type): Merchant ship, Naval warship, Pirate vessel, Fishing boat, Smuggler's ship.

**Holdings**: quarters on ship; the trust of the captain; knowledge of 2-3 safe harbors; 5-10 crew members who respect you.

## Rank 2: Captain

**Holdings**: your own ship (3,000-10,000 gp depending on type); a crew of 20-40 sailors; income from shipping/trade/prizes (40 gp/month); contacts in 5-7 port cities; letters of marque or trading licenses, if applicable; a personal cabin with maps and charts.

**Feature Expansion — Master Sailor**: perform complex maneuvers automatically; advantage on all checks related to ship operations; estimate a ship's cargo and value by observation; know the reputation of most captains in your region; your crew has +1 to morale saves.

## Rank 3: Fleet Commander

**Holdings**: a fleet of 3-5 ships; a crew totaling 100-200 sailors; major income from operations (150 gp/month); a warehouse or company headquarters in a major port; a network of contacts across many ports; 5-8 trusted captains under your command; shares in a trading company or privateering operation.

**Feature Expansion — Naval Authority**: requisition supplies and repairs in any friendly port; your fleet can be split for multiple operations; once per week, call the fleet together for a major operation; an intelligence network among sailors across seas; you can organize blockades, escorts, or trade convoys.

## Rank 4: Admiral/Merchant Prince of the Seas

**Holdings**: a fleet of 12+ ships; a private port or major stake in a port city; massive income (500 gp/month); a crew of 500+ across your fleet; you own or control a shipping company; political influence in maritime matters; a legendary flagship (artifact-level ship).

**Feature — Master of the Seas**: organize naval operations affecting entire regions; once per month, mobilize your fleet for a major action; your presence in a port brings prosperity; governments seek your aid in naval matters; pirate lords or enemy fleets think twice before engaging you. Sea charts and routes are named after you, young sailors dream of serving under you, your flagship is known across all seas, you can affect maritime law and custom through your influence, and some ports grant you unofficial authority.
