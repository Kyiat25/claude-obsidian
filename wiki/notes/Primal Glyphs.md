# Primal Glyphs

The immutable, first-Law Glyphs — the core laws of reality, existing before even the gods, embodied one each by the four Primal Forces. Also called the Eternal Pillars of Existence: "before there was time, there was the Hammer, the Loom, the Maw, and the Wheel." Not beings, minds, or wills, but the bones of reality itself — forces so powerful that not even [[Astralor]] nor [[Aeloria]] can control them.

| Glyph | Bearer | Cosmic influence | Dedicated page |
|---|---|---|---|
| **[[Glyph of Will]]** | [[Astralor]] | Astralor's Forge, the First Hammer — governs the force of creation itself; leylines emerge through it | [[Glyph of Will]] |
| **[[Glyph of Threads]]** | [[Aeloria]] | Aeloria's Loom, the Hand of Fate — dictates how leylines and fate weave through reality | [[Glyph of Threads]] |
| **[[The Glyph of Endings]]** | [[The Whispering Dark]] | The Maw of the Whispering Dark — determines the decay of leylines and concepts, ensuring entropy | [[The Glyph of Endings]] |
| **[[The Glyph of Echoes]]** | [[The Restorer]] | The Restorer's Wheel, the Cycle Unbroken — ensures leylines and lost things never fully vanish | [[The Glyph of Echoes]] |

These glyphs are immutable — mortals and deities can manipulate leylines, but not rewrite the fundamental laws the Primal Glyphs express. [[Weave and Magic]] and [[Weave of Creation]] both restate this exact four-glyph structure with no material difference from [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]]'s original framing. Each glyph now has its own dedicated page (linked above) with full domain, symbol, eldritch-corruption, and worship detail — this hub page catalogs the shared cosmic-law framing that spans all four.

## The Eternal War That Is Not a War

The four Primal Glyphs don't battle — they're a cycle that endlessly turns: Will shapes the world, Threads guide its course, Endings break what has been, Echoes ensure nothing is truly lost. Per `Saga of the Glyphs.md`, even the Primal Forces who bear them don't understand what they wield: Astralor believes himself a master of creation but is merely the wielder of a hammer he did not forge; Aeloria believes herself a master of fate but merely watches a Loom that never ceases to spin; The Whispering Dark believes himself the end of all things, but the Maw never swallows without something rising from what is lost; The Restorer believes himself a redeemer, but the Wheel turns with or without his guidance. The cosmos continues, blind to the fact that even the greatest gods are merely passengers upon the Wheel. Closing question, left deliberately unanswered: *"Who carved the First Glyph?"* — not even Astralor knows.

## Minor variations of the Primal Glyphs

Per `Saga of the Glyphs.md`, minor variations aren't a separate glyph tier — they're subtleties within a Primary Glyph, letting the same fundamental concept manifest differently by context, wielder, and intent, without changing the glyph's core truth.

| Primary Glyph | Minor variation | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Glyph of Will** (Creation & Order) | Glyph of Divine Will | Creation with divine purpose — holy miracles, celestial shaping |
| | Glyph of Mortal Will | Creation through personal effort — invention, art, mortal ambition |
| **The Glyph of Endings** (Entropy & Unmaking) | Glyph of Decay | Slow, natural breakdown — aging, erosion, rather than total destruction |
| | Glyph of Ruin | Sudden, violent catastrophe — world-ending disasters |
| **The Glyph of Echoes** (Rebirth & Renewal) | Glyph of Ancestral Echoes | Past lives and legacies influencing the present |
| | Glyph of Forgotten Echoes | Ancient magic, whispers of lost civilizations, memories hidden in time |
