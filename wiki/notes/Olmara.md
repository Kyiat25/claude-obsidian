# Olmara, The Sleeping Stone

*"Not all stones are yours. Not all gems are meant to shine. Some dreams are buried for a reason."*

**Domain**: living stones, sentient gems, forbidden mining. **Symbol**: a faceted gem with a closed eye at its center. **Feared/respected by**: deep miners, dwarves who speak to the stone, halfling wanderers, gnomish crystal-scribes.

A semi-eldritch divine who does not speak or answer prayers — some believe she sleeps beneath the deepest mines, others believe she *is* the mine. Has no temples, but miners leave offerings at dangerous depths; when a gem too perfect is found, tradition says to leave it and walk away without speaking.

**Myth — The Stonecutter of Khor-Kazan**: a dwarven stonecutter named Tharan Gravetongue struck an unmapped, pulsing gem while carving a royal tomb deep in Mount Kalvorak, and cut it free anyway. It began whispering — not words, "stone thinking" — each night more. When he tried to return it, the mine had already collapsed. He vanished a week later; his chisel was found fused to the now-dull gem, sealed forever in the Silent Gallery of Kaz-Veldur. Feared by [[Zorvakar]], who desires what she guards; [[Thamur]] and [[Ormara (Earth)]] understand her silence and keep their distance. **Aspect of the Lock**: a passive defense, one of the oldest sentiences of stone, dreaming around the thing that stirs beneath — if she wakes, something ancient and wrong may be released as well.
