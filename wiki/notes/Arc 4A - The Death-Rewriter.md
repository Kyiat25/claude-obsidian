# Arc 4A — The Death-Rewriter

The full deep-dive behind [[Campaign Arcs (The Great Deceivers and Truth-Seekers)|Category 4: The Rewriters]]'s flagship arc, and — like Arc 2A — a hidden bridge into the Eldritch tier.

## Morthius the Unending

Human lich, 600 years old (died at 32 in Year -113 A4, originally from Kaelithar on the western continent), operating from the Sepulchre of Silent Names in the Ashenvale Mountains. His phylactery is his own rewritten Personal Glyph, making him unusually hard to destroy by conventional means. Believes death is not sacred, only universal — *"and what is universal can be rewritten"* — and is working to rewrite what this source calls **the Maw** (the "Glyph of Endings") to make death optional for everyone, not just the crude workaround he settled for personally.

## The mechanism and the stakes

With four of the five [[Threadbearers]] dead (see [[The Threadbearer Crisis]]), Morthius is quietly corrupting their inexperienced replacements before anyone notices the pattern, working toward the **Synchronous Bloom** — a once-every-500-years event when all five World Trees flower simultaneously — as the trigger window for his ritual. The ritual's stress threatens to crack **the Hollow Vault's seal**, potentially releasing the First Eldritch Corruption and tying this arc directly into [[The Hollow Thread]] as a live campaign-ending risk, not just a metaphor.

## The moral trap

His converted followers are genuinely happy and physiologically incapable of doubt. The source states plainly there is no predetermined correct answer: stopping him preserves a system that genuinely kills everyone eventually; letting him succeed risks a transformation nobody can predict the shape of, and either way "victory is not guaranteed to feel like victory."
