# Glowering Moon

*"It does not judge. It observes. It remembers. And then it waits."*

First seen 3rd Age, 1087 TA, during a Faith council deadlock. An unblinking eye in the sky.

## Nature

Watches intensely, endlessly, unyieldingly — the first cosmic awareness the Weave ever encountered. The Spirit Pantheon refuses to name it; Earth buries glyphs during its rise.

## World effects

**Total Perceptual Overexposure**: passive perception +5 for all creatures, players see too much; dreams become first-person visions of others' actions; secret doors, illusions, and lies have a 50% chance of spontaneous reveal. **Suppressed Privacy**: written messages broadcast to nearby minds, whispered conversations become audible at random, secrets leak — people say things they didn't mean.

**Monster effects**: creatures with legendary actions or telepathy can perceive multiple targets simultaneously; hunting happens by sight alone, bypassing stealth, illusion, and barriers; affected creatures stare and move unnaturally slowly, always toward the watcher.

## Madness

Short-term: feeling watched from behind every mirror, glyph, or eye. Long-term: saying others' thoughts aloud before they think them. Indefinite: belief that you are the one watching — the world performs for you.

## Glyph interactions and resistance

Divination Glyphs become overcharged, pulling information that should not be retrievable — scrying shows false and true images overlapping like double exposure; detect thoughts pulls surface thoughts from anyone nearby, not just the target; illusion and invisibility fail 50% of the time outside sealed or sacred space. The Fate-Weaver coils her threads, cutting even her faithful off from future knowledge; Seralyne, Blade of Threads, sharpens her magic against being seen, becoming unreachable; the Restbringer closes the veil harder than ever, and resurrection spells fail as souls flee the sight of the moon.

## Campaign use

A prophet dies mid-vision, screaming they're watched from inside their own tongue; an NPC writes journal entries in your voice; a reflection refuses to mirror movements.
