# The Thorned Concord — the Nine Hidden Valleys of the Briar-Lords

A deliberately isolated highland kingdom (~35,000 sq mi, population ~850,000) in remote northwestern [[Elarion]], nine river-carved valleys hidden by permanent mist, dense forest, and rolling Scottish/Norwegian-style terrain rather than Alpine peaks. Outsiders assume they're a mysterious fey-touched, druidic culture like [[Talanthar]] — they are in fact **hyper-practical mountain farmers who weaponized horticulture**. Over 800 years, the Concord has cultivated the **Thorn-Maze**: giant briars 40-60 feet tall with sword-length thorns, grown in deliberately planned maze patterns integrated into the natural forest until nearly invisible from outside — agricultural infrastructure that doubles as living fortification, built through selective breeding rather than magic.

## Three forest philosophies, contrasted

Talanthar: *"The forest lives through us."* The Marshlands: *"We live within the balance."* The Thorned Concord: *"We grow barriers that kill invaders and produce the finest cider in [[Elarion]]."*

## The Nine Valleys

Val Kartheris ("the First Valley"), Val Duracen ("the Stone Valley"), Val Rosarin ("the Apple Valley"), Val Sageris ("the Archer's Valley"), Val Petras ("the Slinger's Valley"), Val Miresin ("the Wine Valley"), Val Tarnos ("the Guardian Valley"), Val Ornos ("the Scholar's Valley"), and Val Sorin ("the Hidden Valley"). No major trade routes pass through the region, and the valleys have historically only been found by accident or long deliberate search.

## Val Kartheris, the political heart

The First Valley (House Kartheris, "the Thornkeepers," ~120,000) hosts the capital **Thornhaven** (~18,000, the Concord Hall where all Nine Houses meet, 800 years of thorn-cultivation archives). Current **High Thornkeeper** (a rotating leadership seat) is **Lady Mirela Kartheris** (58), a patient diplomat who deliberately keeps the Concord off the radar of major powers — most years no outsiders visit at all, and the Empire has only a vague notion the Concord exists "somewhere in the northwest." She's quietly worried about the wider Thread crisis but unsure how to help without revealing the Concord's location.
