# The Islands of Eternal Green

A chain of seven major oases (~800km of western Vashkora coastline, historical population ~12,000 wood elves plus ~3,000 scholars/refugees), sustained by ancient aquifers fed by Emberline Rise glacial melt filtered through volcanic rock — verdant "green jewels set in the harsh coastal landscape." Five of its World Trees were 12,000+ years old, sacred to wood elf refugees as living Cycle-Memory keepers whose growth rings recorded pre-divine cosmic alignments, climate shifts, and Thread cycles — an archive tradition comparable to [[Sylvenmere]]'s and the [[The Crimson Root Isles|Crimson Root Isles']].

## The Archive Tradition

Over millennia the wood elf communities here developed four specialized roles: **Cycle-Singers** (priests interpreting tree-ring patterns and maintaining records), **Root-Speakers** (druids in direct tree communion), **Memory-Weavers** (scholars correlating tree data with astronomy), and **Refuge-Keepers** (guardians of the oases and archives). For ~200 years after the Burning Concord's conquest, they practiced careful resistance — hidden duplicate records, traditions taught in coded forms, forbidden mathematics camouflaged as legitimate astronomy, and copies smuggled to distant archives (some reaching [[Talanthar]]) before Kethyr's final purge.

## Destruction

**Destroyed**, Third Age, five days before [[Ashkarra]]'s own unmaking — systematically burned by **High Embermarked Priest Kethyr Flameheart** and imperial forces of the Burning Concord (which had conquered the territory ~200 years earlier), using combined magical and mundane fire across all seven groves. The site remains salted earth and a corrupted aquifer, preserved now only as a memorial.
