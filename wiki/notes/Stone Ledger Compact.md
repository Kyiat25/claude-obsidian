# The Stone Ledger Compact — the World-Bank of Dór-o Estel

*"Gold may pass. Crowns may fall. Stone remembers what blood forgets."* — inscription above the Third Vault Gate

The single most powerful non-divine institution in the setting, seated at [[Thar Vornhal]] since the late First Age (predating the Faith's formalization) and recognized across every [[Loom-Cities|Loom-City]] and Imperial state. It does not rule territory, command armies, or speak prophecy — it **decides which civilizations are allowed to survive collapse**. Every Loom-City, empire, and major trade coalition is bound, directly or indirectly, to contracts held in its vaults, with debts denominated not only in gold but in land, labor, memory, or time. Founding principle: *"Stability precedes morality"* — civilizations fall not because they are evil but because they lose the capacity to absorb shock, and the Compact exists to preserve continuity even when justice, truth, or mercy must fail. Distinct from, but likely functionally overlapping with, the currency-and-credit system documented on [[Deepstone Cantons]] and [[Lyrran]] — not yet reconciled with those pages.

## The Weight of Seven — the Debtwrights

A governing council of seven, each holding domain over a different lever of civilizational survival, each with a public doctrine and a private compromise:

- **Arkhavel Stone-Limit**, Warden of Thresholds — determines how far a city may fall before intervention becomes mandatory, infamous for delaying aid to the last viable moment. Secretly believes one Loom-City must be allowed to fall completely to reset the whole system, and is waiting for the right candidate.
- **Maerith of the Silent Column**, Keeper of Indenture — oversees all Compact-recognized labor bonds and inherited debt, views freedom itself as a destabilizing luxury. Her own family has been indentured for six generations under her own rulings.
- **Kelros Vant, the Grain Hand**, Arbiter of Sustenance — controls when food shortages become real, has started wars without issuing a single command, believes hunger teaches obedience better than fear. Once secretly violated Compact doctrine to prevent a famine, then erased the evidence.
- **Sister Dhalienne of the Measured Veil**, Liaison to Faith & Anchors — a former priestess who authored the first Anchor-backed debt frameworks, treating World Anchors as financial instruments while speaking the language of mercy. One World Anchor is already fully mortgaged and nearing failure.
- **Borrik Deep-Writ**, Keeper of the Ledger Strata — missing a hand, replaced with rune-scarred iron; physically inscribes debt into stone and treats the Ledger itself as a living structure. Knows of false entries within the Ledger and chooses to maintain balance rather than correct them.
- **Tessera Quill-No-More**, Arbiter of Defaults — signs the documents that end civilizations, burning seals into stone rather than writing them; believes mercy compounds suffering. Privately running out of cities that can be allowed to collapse without triggering a chain reaction.
- **The Seat of Burden**, Final Balance — occupant unknown, rotational, possibly non-mortal; rarely speaks but overrides every other ruling and may be bound directly to the Earth Thread. Rumored to be an institutional consciousness embedded in stone rather than a person. Cannot be directly leveraged — only the other six Debtwrights can be influenced.

## Campaign use

Framed explicitly as a "how players confront the bank" institution: it **cannot** be defeated by assassination, theft, arson, or moral exposure alone — only by forcing contradictions. Valid victory conditions per the source: **collapse the assumption** (prove a city or Anchor can stabilize without Compact-imposed suffering — this threatens the Compact more than open rebellion); **break synchronization** (trigger two simultaneous collapses, or an unexpected stabilization, since the Compact is designed to manage only one failure at a time); **steal legitimacy** (get a Loom-City to reject Compact arbitration and survive one full Thread cycle, fracturing its authority globally); or **force an Anchor choice** into public view (reveal that two Anchors cannot both be saved, and that the Compact has already secretly chosen one). Each Debtwright's private secret is built as a specific, usable leverage point for players investigating or negotiating with the Compact rather than fighting it outright.
