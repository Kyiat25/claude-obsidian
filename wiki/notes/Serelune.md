# Serelune, The Veil at the Seam

*"Varethis cuts the thread. My work begins the instant his ends: to see that nothing — nothing — passes back through the cut."*

**Domains**: the boundary of death, the sealed severance, the watch against return. **Symbol**: a cut thread, its two ends sealed with silver wax. **Worshiped by**: funerary priests, wards against the restless dead. Also revered among Elves as one of the Woven Constellation's Threads (see [[The Woven Reverence]], where she appears as Serelyne, the Pale Blade, guardian of severance and sacred sacrifice).

Not death itself, but death's integrity — the reason a severance *stays* severed. Where the elemental pantheons each caught one phase of death, Serelune holds the phase none of them noticed: the raw seam where a life used to be attached to the world. The least prayed-to and most relied-upon of the death offices; her funerary rite ties, then *seals*, a thread — never merely knots it, since a knot can be undone but wax cannot be un-melted.

> [!truth] DM-Truth
> Serelune's seals hold against everything mortal necromancy can bring, but she has begun finding seams already reopened with her wax intact. She attributes it to Morthius. Some of it is; the rest is [[The Restorer]], which restores from a side of the seam her office and theology have no jurisdiction over or word for. Like Nyzara's returned memories, she classifies the anomalies as her own failures. They are not.
