# Eldritch Moons

Not celestial bodies — Weave-wounds, lunar overlays, echoes of impossible orbits, manifestations of [[The Null Reflection]] pressing close when the Weave frays. The sky doesn't change; understanding of it does. No pantheon claims them, no ward has truly banished them, and speaking of them can snag threads of fate.

## When they rise and set

**Rising**: each seasonal change triggers a Thread-turn roll; moons manifest when the Weave strains under too many fractures. Most common during "Deepveil season" (when prophecy and memory are most volatile), but can appear anytime Eldritch influence overtakes seasonal balance.

**Setting**: fade when strain on the Weave eases, fractured leylines are restored, elemental balance reasserts, and the Null Reflection loosens its hold. The scar remains — zones of instability persist even after a moon sets.

> [!contradiction] Fourteen full profiles, twelve chronological entries
> The source's own "Detailed Moon Profiles" section gives full write-ups to **fourteen** moons — the twelve above, plus [[Weeping Waxing]] and [[False Dawn]] — but neither of those two appears in the chronological "Twelve Eldritch Moons" table. Both are documented on their own pages regardless, flagged as a source-internal inconsistency rather than silently folded into (or excluded from) the twelve.

A closely-related second source, `Eldritch Moons.md`, restates all fourteen moons with deeper mechanical detail — full "World Effects," "Monster Effects," and "Madness" tables (already reflected on each moon's page from the first source) plus two subsections the first source lacked: **Glyph Interactions** (how each moon warps a specific Glyph category) and **Divine & Mythic Response** (how named Pantheon figures react). Both have now been folded into all 14 individual moon pages under a "Glyph interactions and resistance" heading.

## The Ashkarra Convergence

The single most infamous Eldritch event on record. All twelve moons rose within 58 days, culminating in an alignment on Ashkarra's Spirit Thread festival — the third consecutive Spirit alignment, a violation of the cosmic law that no Thread may hold the same alignment more than twice. The result: the First Hollow Breach, the World Anchor's total implosion, and the complete unmaking of the Loom-City of Ashkarra. This event is called **the Day of Twelve Shadows**. See [[Ashkarra]] and [[The Hollow Thread]] for the fuller cascading-failure account.

## Multiple Moon Protocol

Multiple moons magnify each other exponentially, not additively: two moons double effects with unpredictable overlap; three moons make reality seriously unstable with high Hollow Breach risk; twelve moons at once (Ashkarra) is confirmed apocalypse.

## Pantheon reaction patterns

Earth seals, restricts, and contains. Spirit observes, records, and contains. Fire uses the opportunity or suppresses the threat. Water waits and endures. Air's response is variable by specific god — no unified reaction exists.
