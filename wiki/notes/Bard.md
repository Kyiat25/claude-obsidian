# Bard — Reality's Storyteller

**Full Caster tier**. "The world is a story—and you decide how it ends." Manipulates probability and outcomes. Complexity self-rated MODERATE (5/10) by its own per-class deep-dive.

> [!note] Naming collision with the Minstrel background — flagged, not silently resolved
> [[FEARS Advanced Backgrounds]] Batch 3 introduces a "Minstrel / Bard" *background* ([[Minstrel]]) — a mundane performance-and-fame progression track with no spellcasting anywhere in its text, mechanically unrelated to this class's reality-manipulation magic. Filed under "Minstrel" to avoid a filename collision, same pattern as [[Investigator (Background)]] vs. the Investigator class.

> "You don't play music. You play the room. The music is just how you do it."

## Signature abilities

- **Inspiration**
- **Performance Magic**
- **Outcome Control**

**Gameplay style**: support and control, making allies succeed and enemies fail.
**Core loop**: inspire → influence → reshape outcomes → repeat. Struggles in pure attrition fights where spell slots drain faster than the party can win; excels in mixed encounters where inspiration and narrative control turn fights before they peak.

## Class basics (from Bard.md deep-dive)

- **Hit Dice**: 1d8/level. HP at 1st: 8 + CON mod. HP at higher levels: 1d8 (or 5) + CON mod/level after 1st.
- **Proficiencies**: Armour — light. Weapons — simple, hand crossbows, longswords, rapiers, shortswords. Tools — three musical instruments of choice. Saves — Dexterity, Charisma. Skills — choose any three.
- **Starting Equipment**: (a) rapier, (b) longsword, or (c) any simple weapon; (a) diplomat's pack or (b) entertainer's pack; (a) a lute or (b) any other musical instrument; leather armour and a dagger.
- **Quick Build**: Charisma highest, then Dexterity. Any lineage works (Half-Elf, Tiefling, Human thematic but not required). Entertainer/Charlatan or any Performance/Persuasion background. Grant Bardic Inspiration at combat start, use Cutting Words to disrupt dangerous enemy attacks, spend your action on offensive spells or Bardic Performance each turn.

## Core class features (full text)

### Bardic Inspiration (1st level)

Bonus action, inspire a creature within 60 ft that can hear or see you. That creature gains a Bardic Inspiration die (d6, scaling at 5th/d8, 9th/d10, 14th/d12) usable on one ability check, attack roll, or saving throw within 10 minutes. Granting it rolls a **Performance check** (DC = 10 + recipient's level):

| Performance result | Inspiration die | Additional effect |
|---|---|---|
| Fail DC | base die | none |
| Meet/beat DC | base die | recipient gains +1 Momentum |
| Beat by 5+ | die +1 size | recipient gains +1 Momentum |
| Beat by 10+ | die +1 size + advantage on first use | recipient gains +2 Momentum |
| Natural 20 | die +2 sizes + advantage on first use | recipient gains +3 Momentum; all allies within 10 ft gain the base die + 1 Momentum each |
| Natural 1 | base die | disadvantage on the Bard's next Performance check to grant Inspiration |

Uses equal to Charisma modifier (minimum 1), all regained on a long rest.

**Inspirational Momentum** (confirmed [[Momentum]] gain source, not previously listed on that page): the Momentum gain triggers immediately when the die is granted, not when it's used.

### Spellcasting (1st level) — first explicit caster DC formula in this wiki

Charisma is the spellcasting ability. **Spell Save DC = 8 + PB + Charisma modifier. Spell Attack Modifier = PB + Charisma modifier.** [[Momentum]]'s "Caster Spell Save DC floor" note referenced Spell Save DCs being calculated without ever giving the base formula — this is the first source to state one outright. Confirmed for Bard, and independently confirmed again with the exact same `8 + PB + ability mod` shape by [[Bender]]'s deep-dive — now treated as the confirmed universal caster formula rather than just a reasonable assumption. Learns cantrips/spells from the Bard list plus Magical Secrets; rituals castable without spell slots.

### Weapon Mastery (1st level)

One simple/finesse weapon at 1st level — consistent with [[Weapon Mastery]]'s "most others start with one" rule. **Bardic Synergy**: physical debuffs from Weapon Skills combine with Bardic Inspiration and spell effects for support plays that scale with party damage. This source doesn't restate Bard's specific crit-progression numbers; [[Weapon Mastery]] already has them (Half-Martial: 3 crits simple / 4 crits martial).

### Expertise (2nd and 6th level)

Choose two skill or tool proficiencies; double Proficiency Bonus for checks using them. Two more at 6th level.

### Magical Inspiration (2nd level)

A creature holding a Bardic Inspiration die that casts a healing or damage-dealing spell may add the die's result to the spell's effect. The die is then expended.

### Bardic Performance (2nd and 6th level)

Choose one effect per performance: **Celebrate Life** (allies spend hit dice to heal, adding Charisma modifier; advantage on death saves), **Cutting Words** (reaction, reduce a target's ability check/attack/damage roll by a Bardic Inspiration die's result). At 6th level, add **Clarity of Thought** (allies can't be charmed or frightened, advantage on saves to end those effects). This confirms and generalizes the existing page's note that "Cutting Words reduces enemy [[Enemy Rally]] checks" — that was a specific application of this general "reduce any roll" rule, not a separate mechanic.

### Bard Subclass (3rd level, and 7th, 11th, 15th)

Choose a College — see the subclass note below for the source's example list versus what's already documented. Features at 3rd, 7th, 11th, 15th level.

### Font of Inspiration (5th level)

Regain all Bardic Inspiration uses on finishing a short or long rest. Additionally, rolling initiative with no uses remaining regains 1 use, once per combat.

### Elusive Performer (7th level, scaling)

+1 to Evasion rolls, increasing to +2 at 11th and +3 at 15th.

### Magical Secrets (9th, 13th, 18th level)

Learn two spells from any class list at each of these levels; they count as Bard spells.

### Heroic Boon (10th level)

Choose: **Persistent Inspiration** (an Inspiration die used and failing to help can be used again) or **Valiant Inspiration** (the recipient can add the die to a damage roll, or use it to reduce damage taken).

### Soothing Performance (10th level)

Once per long rest, perform for at least 10 minutes during a short rest for one creature who hears it throughout: downgrade one Injury by a tier (Major becomes Minor; Minor is healed completely). Must be uninterrupted; target must be conscious and able to hear. A second, distinct [[Injury System]] class hook alongside Paladin's Sanctified Intercession (Critical→Major) — different tiers, no overlap, same design pattern of classes softening the injury track.

### Grand Performance (17th level)

When granting Bardic Inspiration with no uses remaining, may declare a Grand Performance as a special action (no use required): automatically achieve a Natural 20 on the Performance check; the recipient's die is +2 sizes with advantage on first use; all allies within 10 ft of the recipient get the standard die; the target gains +3 Momentum, nearby allies +1 each, and the Bard gains +4 Momentum. Locks out Inspiration regen until a long rest. Once per long rest.

### Epic Boon (20th level)

Choose: **Curtain Call** (at the start of your turn, if no Inspiration dice remain, regain a number equal to PB; once/long rest, perform a Masterpiece — 1 minute, all allies within 60 ft get advantage on saves, a 1d12 Inspiration die on all attack rolls and ability checks, and temp HP equal to 2× Bard level) or **Crescendo of Magic** (once/short rest, cast any known/prepared spell of 6th circle or lower without a slot, at its highest level; spells affecting multiple allies grant them temp HP = CHA mod + spell level).

## Bard Subclass (College) — not a contradiction, an incomplete list

The per-class deep-dive names **College of Lore**, **College of Valour**, and **College of Whispers** as illustrative examples ("such as") of a Bard Subclass. The existing page already documented **College of Swords** (removes Called Shot penalties during a Blade Flourish, per [[Called Shot Subclass Features]]) — not in this source's example list. Same non-contradiction pattern as [[Barbarian]]'s Primal Path list: the source's own "such as" phrasing means this isn't exhaustive, so College of Swords is treated as a fifth, simply-unmentioned College rather than a naming conflict. Full subclass features for any College remain undocumented.

## Last Stand (confirmed universal mechanic)

Invoked when the Bard would be reduced to 0 HP, rolls a death save, or dies outright — a free action requiring no reaction or consciousness. Once resolved, the character permanently dies. Three named options:

| Option | Effect | Best use |
|---|---|---|
| Best Kept Secret | Cast any spell from any class up to highest known level; no material/verbal/somatic components, no spell slot cost; may maintain concentration beyond death for the shorter of the spell's duration or 5 minutes | High-impact magical devastation |
| Friends on the Other Side | Reveal crucial intelligence about a foe (map of their hideout, a credible enemy they've made, a hidden motive/flaw) to up to CHA-modifier allies anywhere, delivered via their next dream | Long-term campaign edge |
| Swan Song | Allies who can hear the performance gain Bardic Inspiration dice equal to CHA modifier (disappear after 5 minutes); using these dice within that window cancels all sources of disadvantage on the roll | Rallying the party at a critical moment |

Confirms [[Last Stand]] as a universal per-class mechanic (previously flagged as possibly Barbarian-specific). Full framework and cross-class table on [[Last Stand]].

## Class Reactions (confirmed universal mechanic)

Bards draw on the same shared, PB-sized reaction pool as [[Barbarian]] (refresh short/long rest, 1/round cap, opportunity-attack exempt) — confirming [[Class Reactions]] as universal. All three of Bard's reactions additionally expend a Bardic Inspiration die/use on top of the PB pool cost — a resource-stacking pattern worth watching for on other Inspiration/resource-based casters:

- **Dissonant Echo** (2nd) — trigger: a creature within 30 ft hits an ally; expend an Inspiration die, subtract from the damage dealt; attacker WIS saves (DC 8+PB+CHA) or is frightened of the Bard until end of its next turn.
- **Cutting Rhythm** (3rd) — trigger: an enemy within 60 ft gains Momentum from a successful attack; expend an Inspiration use to reduce that Momentum gain by 1 (min 0) — the mechanic this source's own narrative introduction previewed.
- **Cutting Counter** (7th) — trigger: a creature within 60 ft makes an attack roll, ability check, or damage roll; expend an Inspiration die, subtract from the result; if it now fails, the Bard and one ally within 30 ft may each move 10 ft without provoking opportunity attacks. At 15th level, both also gain advantage on their next roll.

Full framework and cross-class table on [[Class Reactions]].

## Enemy-morale hook (from Bloodied Breaking Points Rally)

**Cutting Words** reduces enemy [[Enemy Rally]] checks; **Vicious Mockery** targets enemy [[Sanity]] — direct mechanical teeth for "making... enemies fail" above, extended to the morale layer specifically.

## Called Shot hook

College of Swords removes Called Shot penalties during a Blade Flourish — see [[Called Shot Subclass Features]] and the subclass note above.

## Initiative hook (from Initiative System)

**Jack of All Trades** adds half Proficiency Bonus to a [[Seize the Moment]] check; **Bardic Inspiration** helps allies capitalize on the +1 first-attack bonus from a successful attempt.
