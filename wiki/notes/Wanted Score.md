# Wanted Score

A measurable notoriety mechanic that rises with unlawful acts, from petty theft to regicide. Affects NPC suspicion, law-enforcement response (fines, pursuit, bounty hunters, executions), access to services, and ability to move freely between cities.

## Full tier table (from a dedicated `Wanted Score.md` player-guide source)

**1-2 "Known Troublemaker"** (local/Hamlet-Village scope): increased scrutiny, minor fines; merchants charge 10-20% markup or demand payment upfront; 10-20% identification chance locally; reduces by 1 after 6 clean months. **3-4 "Lawbreaker/Wanted Alive"** (regional/Town): active pursuit, amateur bounty hunters, 25-50% markup, innkeepers may refuse lodging; 30-40% regional identification chance. **5-6 "Criminal/Wanted Dead or Alive"** (national/City): wanted posters, professional bounty hunters, most legitimate services refuse outright, black market prices 75-100%+ inflated; 50-60% national identification chance. **7+ "Enemy of the State"** (international/Metropolis): international manhunt, execution likely, no legitimate services anywhere, 80-90% identification chance even in remote areas — reducible only by royal pardon, divine intervention, or world-altering heroism. A special **"Score + P" (Protected)** state layers on top of any score when a patron shields the character within their sphere of influence, reducing active pursuit and improving disposition among the protector's allies.

## Notoriety tiers (separate from raw score)

**Local Whisper** (Score 3+): advantage on Streetwise vs. common criminals, disadvantage on Deception against those who know the reputation. **Urban Legend** (Score 5+): advantage on Intimidation/Streetwise against most NPCs, disadvantage on all Charisma checks with law enforcement, draws admirers/fanatics. **Infamous** (Score 7+): advantage on Intimidation/Streetwise against almost everyone, but blending in becomes nearly impossible.

## Decay and redemption

Score decreases by 1 per year of avoided criminal activity (minimum 1); significant, public, demonstrably altruistic good deeds can reduce it 1-2 points at GM discretion; restitution/returning stolen goods can reduce it depending on severity and sincerity; a royal pardon can reset it to 0 entirely, but requires major political maneuvering or extraordinary circumstances.

## Key threshold

- **Score of 7+** = "Enemy of the State" — often no safe harbor remains.

Fame from a high score isn't purely negative — some NPCs admire boldness even as others fear or hunt the character. A higher score also directly raises the risk of a [[Hideouts|hideout]] being discovered.

## Background tie-in

[[Bounty Hunter]] is the natural other side of this mechanic — a character built to track down whoever this score is climbing for. No source text explicitly links the two, but the table interaction (a Bounty Hunter PC pursuing a Wanted-Score-flagged target) is immediate.
