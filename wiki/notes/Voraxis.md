# Voraxis, The Tidal Wrath

**Domains**: tsunamis, the unstoppable wave, the sea's fury. Among the Drow of the Ashen Web there exists a single forbidden name never carved, sung, or written — Voraxis. They don't call him god or spirit; they call him Nothing, or The Surge That Drowns the Deep. In the Underdark, where elevation doesn't exist and no sky offers escape, a tsunami isn't destruction — it's annihilation. No tunnels are high enough, no walls strong enough, and no web survives the drowning.

## The Flood of Xeyl'Har

[[Vel'Zyren]] once challenged him during the Reign of Fathomless Silence, seeking to bind him with silk-stitched glyphs. Voraxis didn't rise in fury — he rose because he was called, because movement must come. He drowned her palace beneath its own arrogance, obliterating thousands of Drow, spiders, and priestesses. She returned shattered, gasping, webless. Now, Drow who utter his name aloud are wrapped in living silk, filled with crushed salt, and ritually drowned — not as heresy, but as an act of "flood-containment," plugging a breach in the web of divinity. Even Vel'Zyren's own priestesses shudder at the thought of him: she weaves, but Voraxis unravels without thread and drowns without mercy.

## Vel'Zyren's dread

Per [[Vel'Zyren]]'s own campaign material, Voraxis is her single greatest weakness — her binding limits her manifestation on the surface, but even in the Underdark there is no high ground and no escape from rising water. The Water Gambit (a possible campaign hook) involves the Water Pantheon offering an alliance to flood sections of the Underdark against the Drow, at a price Voraxis himself would demand.
