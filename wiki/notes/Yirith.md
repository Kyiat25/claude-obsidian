# Yirith, The Wandering Fool

*"I never know where I'm going, and that's why I always arrive somewhere important."*

**Domain**: trickery of roads, lost purpose, accidental destiny. **Symbol**: a jester's foot stepping into a swirling chasm. **Worshiped by**: fools, bards, halfling wanderers, chaos-sorcerers, "lost" souls.

A trickster god of misdirection — leads people astray not to harm, but to give them stories they were never meant to have. Myth: a tyrant demanded a road to his enemy's capital; Yirith showed him one, and he arrived only at his own childhood home, face to face with the boy he used to be.

**Myth — The Song No One Should Sing**: the halfling bard Pimble Two-Shoes learned a strange melody from a stranger in patchwork robes at a nameless crossroads. Singing it once flickered the lights; twice, the innkeeper forgot her own name; a third time, the crossroads itself vanished. Pimble was found weeks later, still humming the tune, unable to remember where he'd been. Secretly works around [[Gaeldros]], whose law is too rigid for him; playful rival of [[Vel'Zyren]], who weaves purpose while Yirith unravels it into chaos. **Aspect of the Lock**: ensures not all seekers reach what they seek, sometimes protecting the world from those who would break the seal.
