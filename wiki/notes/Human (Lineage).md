# Human

*"We were not forged. We were not sung. We were not named. We were what looked back — when the gods first saw their flaws."*

**Origin myth — The Shard That Sang**: per the Reflection Pattern (the oldest human faith), humans were born when the gods, gathering at the Loom of Origins to ask "what flaw remains in our design," saw themselves too clearly and the Mirror shattered — twelve fallen shards became the first humans, "the Race of the Shattered Mirror." Not made by a god, but born of divine contradiction.

**Physical**: matures by 18, lives 80–100 years (110–120 exceptional); Medium size, 30 ft. speed, wildly variable build.

## Traits

- **Adaptable**: +1 to one ability score of choice, proficiency in one skill of choice
- **Feat** (1st level): one bonus feat, on top of background/class feats
- **Quick Study**: fail an ability check → advantage on the next check with that same ability, within 1 hour
- **Cultural Chameleon**: after 1 week observing a culture, advantage on Deception/Performance/Persuasion to pass as a member of it
- **Languages**: Common + one extra of choice

**Mental**: Focus +0 / Sanity +0 — deliberately neutral, no biological ceiling either direction.

**DCS Integration**: no innate Evasion or DR bonuses (+0/+0/+0, DR +0) — every build is equally viable since nothing is wasted on unused racial specialization. Advantage on Rally checks when fighting for a belief.

## Corruption: The Unanchored

Lacking racial/ancestral anchoring makes Humans easier for Eldritch forces to manipulate identity-wise, but their adaptability also lets them recover from Corruption other races can't. At Corruption 10+ (Tier 2): Fractured Identity, Memory Distortion, Reflection Corruption. **Corrupted Reflection table** (1d6, rolled at Corruption 10+ on long rest): False Memory, Identity Fracture, Reflection Lie (1d4 psychic), Weight of Mortality (Sanity save or lose 1d4), Eldritch Whisper (gain 1 Whisper Point, next death save at disadvantage), or Adaptability Holds (Corruption −1).

## Cultural touchstones

Split faith is common — **the Reflection Pattern** privately (personal divine relationships, ancestral memory, choice reshapes destiny) and **the Faith of the Bound Pattern** publicly (structured, cycle-based, theologically flexible — humans likely originated or popularized it). The "Seven Questions" (who made you / what do you serve / what will you become / protect / destroy / leave behind / who remembers) frame human philosophy as existential rather than religious, fitting a life too short to avoid hard questions.
