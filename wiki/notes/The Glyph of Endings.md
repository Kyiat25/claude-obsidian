# The Glyph of Endings — The Maw of the Whispering Dark

*"The first star was swallowed, and so began the cycle of loss."*

**Layer**: Primal, Tier 1. **Domains**: entropy, unmaking, oblivion, return. **Opposing glyph**: [[Glyph of Will]]. **Symbol**: a black spiral within a hollow circle.

The certainty that nothing is eternal — not punishment or judgment, but the balance to the Glyph of Will, the unmaking that allows new creation to emerge. Mountains crumble, stars burn out, gods fall because of it; not active destruction, but inevitable conclusion. [[The Whispering Dark]] does not control this glyph — it is merely its final servant.

## Corrupted entities

When manifesting through corrupted matter or memory, this glyph subtracts rather than creates: **Echo-Wanes** (half-there beings that erase names and memories as they pass — even gods forget they exist after seeing them), **Unspooled Ones** (once-mortal creatures whose Threads have been severed, dragged backward through time), **Nullborne Ash** (residue from spellcasters overwhelmed by the glyph — no remains, no names, just the cold stain of entropy).

## Ashkarra

[[Ashkarra]]'s final moment was not fire — it was the echo of this glyph overriding its World Anchor entirely.

## Places of worship (unknowing)

No people knowingly revere it, but its echoes shape forgotten temples and funerary rites — the Whisperfold Caverns (monks who chant nothing, believing silence brings clarity, unaware the glyph devours their names), the Chapel of the Black Thread (sleepers wake refreshed but missing a memory or piece of themselves), the Chain of 1000 Names at [[Ashkarra]]'s outer ring (pilgrims tie new links unaware they feed the glyph one name at a time), the Shrine Without Return (blesses those who vanish — in truth, a mouth, always hungry).
