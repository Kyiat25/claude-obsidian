# Whisper Points

A narrative-currency resource described as "moments of destiny crystallized into mechanical power." Framed as the player's connection to the story itself, not a combat stat.

## What they let you do

- **Reshape Reality** — alter minor details of past events or current circumstances
- **Amplify Actions** — turn an ordinary success into an extraordinary one
- **Defy Death** — pull back from the brink when a character would otherwise die
- **Weave Narrative** — introduce story elements that benefit your character or party

## How they're earned

"Exceptional roleplay, critical moments of character development, and actions that resonate with your character's core identity" — no numeric award table given for ongoing awards. One concrete combat instance now confirmed: [[Sanity & Focus in Combat]]'s Memory Glyph backlash trigger grants +1 Whisper Point (alongside a Focus loss). A second: [[Binder]]'s Tier 5 remnant The Fracture's Herald grants everyone near its rift ability (including the Binder) 1 Whisper Point on a failed Sanity save — consistent with [[Fracture Whispers]]'s existing "failing a Sanity Save" gain trigger.

## Starting pool formula (from FEARS Character Framework)

`3 + Focus Mod + ½ Sanity Mod` — the first numeric grant rule given for this resource. Presumably a starting/refresh pool rather than a per-earn amount, since roleplay-triggered awards are still described as GM-adjudicated. Whether "½ Sanity Mod" rounds up or down is not stated.

## Relation to other systems

The [[Dynamic Action Economy]] page describes a **Whisper Engine** that "tracks and amplifies player agency" — see [[Whisper Engine]] for how [[Fracture Whispers]] resolves this relationship.

## Dual identity: narrative currency and instability meter (from Fracture Whispers)

[[Fracture Whispers]] describes Whisper Points as "a mechanical measure of how close a character is to mental instability or revelation," with its own gain table (failing a Sanity Save, viewing an Eldritch Moon, using Eldritch Magic/Glyphs, recalling a traumatic memory) and a hard cap: `1 + Sanity Modifier` (minimum 1). Reaching that cap forces an automatic [[Fracture Save]].

**Editorial synthesis, not a verbatim source statement**: no source explicitly says "these are the same pool," but the contextual evidence points that way rather than toward two coincidentally-named resources: [[Fracture Whispers]]'s own text states "Fractures lead to... the acquisition of Whisper Points," and its Integration Hooks table says the [[Whisper Engine]] is "tied directly" to Whisper Points, "Fracture is the primary danger of building Whisper." Read together, this describes one coherent push-your-luck design: the same Whisper Points that can be spent to Reshape Reality, Amplify Actions, Defy Death, or Weave Narrative also silently accumulate risk, and banking too many forces a Fracture Save. Treated here as the working interpretation, not a fully source-confirmed fact — flagged so a future source can correct it if it turns out to describe two genuinely separate resources.
