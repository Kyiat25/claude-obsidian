# Corruption

One-third of the **Mental Triad** ([[Sanity]], [[Focus]], [[Corruption]]): metaphysical rot from Eldritch contact, broken glyphs, or madness. Grows with repeated failures or forbidden acts; alters body and soul until the character "becomes something else." The only Mental Triad stat described as visibly changing a character's appearance, making it the most direct mental-state -> [[Disposition]] link of the three.

Formalized as **Corruption Score (CS)** on the [[FEARS Character Sheet]] — confirms this is a tracked number, not just a narrative label. One of the (unspecified) inputs to the [[Mental State]] tracker. **Confirmed as cascading from [[Focus]] loss** by [[Four Threads of the Mind]].

## Thresholds (from Four Threads of the Mind)

The first numeric thresholds given for Corruption:

| Score | Effect |
|---|---|
| 5 | Character begins to change |
| 10 | Glyphs whisper back |
| 15 | No longer fully mortal |
| 20 | Becomes something else — Echo-Bearer, Glyphspawn, or worse |

Being **Blessed** ([[Luck]]) while Corrupted is described as drawing [[The Weave]]'s attention ("the Weave may mark you") — effect unspecified.

## Possible connection

Thematically adjacent to [[Unstable Magic Zones]]' Dead/Wild Magic Zone collapse and [[Runesmith]]'s Backfire — all three describe magic or the mind "lashing back." Not stated as mechanically linked in either source; flagged as a pattern worth checking if a mechanics-crunch source is ingested later.

## First mechanical gain triggers (from Apothecary)

[[Apothecary]]'s per-class deep-dive is the first source to give concrete, mechanical Corruption Score gain triggers rather than the vague "repeated failures or forbidden acts": an Unstable Concoction mishap roll of 3 (1 CS), the Emergency Protocol reaction's emergency stimulant (1 CS), Chemical Burn's post-use crash (1d4 CS), and Metamorphic Infusion (1d4 CS — notably risked by the **recipient** of the infusion, not the Apothecary casting it, the first sighting of Corruption risk being transferable to someone else). A real, non-caster mechanical link between "chemistry as a magic substitute" and the same metaphysical-rot system magic itself risks.

## Second class, and a terminology variant (from Bender)

[[Bender]] adds two more mechanical Corruption triggers: activating Primordial Avatar (1 point) and an ally permanently accepting a Primordial Sacrifice transformation (3 points, via [[Last Stand]]). Uses "Corruption Point" where this page's formalized term is "Corruption Score (CS)" — treated as terminology drift, not a distinct resource, the same handling given to Focus/Technique Point naming variance elsewhere in this cluster.

## A rougher, approximate threshold (from Cleric)

[[Cleric]]'s Turn the Profane Expanded targets creatures with "high Corruption scores (typically 8+)" as eligible for turning — a different, rougher threshold than [[Four Threads of the Mind]]'s formal 5/10/15/20 table. Described as approximate ("typically") and used for GM-adjudicated targeting eligibility rather than a character-facing consequence tier, so not treated as contradicting the existing thresholds — more likely a separate, looser rule of thumb for the same underlying stat.

## Third and fourth mechanical gain triggers, and a threshold-consistent detail layer (from Reaper)

[[Reaper]] adds two more concrete gain triggers: harvesting souls from innocent (non-hostile) creatures gives 1 Corruption per 5 souls; harvesting from humanoids the Reaper personally killed gives 1 per 10 — both passive, volume-based accumulation rather than single-event spikes like Apothecary's or Bender's. Reaper's own "Death's Taint" progression (visual changes 1-4, animal hostility 5-9, undead-detection quirks 10-14, undead speech/necrotic resistance 15-19, partial-undead state 20+) maps closely onto [[Four Threads of the Mind]]'s existing 5/10/15/20 thresholds rather than contradicting them — read as flavor/mechanical detail filling the bands between those thresholds, not a competing scale. Soul Drinkers can spend 5 Soul Tokens on a long rest to reduce Corruption by 1 — the first confirmed Corruption-reduction mechanic tied to a class resource.

## A deliberately parallel, explicitly distinct resource (from Sangromancer)

[[Sangromancer]]'s **Vitae Strain** is structurally similar to Corruption — a six-threshold accumulation track (5/10/15/20/25/30) with permanent Boon-and-Curse consequences at each level, reducible only through drastic means — but the source states outright that it is **not** the same stat: "Vitae Strain measures only what the sanguine arts have done to you, not what eldritch exposure has done to your soul." Treated as a deliberately parallel, mechanically-inspired-by-but-distinct-from Corruption system rather than a variant terminology case like [[Bender]]'s "Corruption Point." Worth watching whether any other class introduces its own similarly-shaped-but-separate degradation track.
