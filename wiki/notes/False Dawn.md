# False Dawn

*"It rises not to end the night, but to lie about the morning."*

> [!note] Not in the "Twelve Eldritch Moons" chronological table
> Like [[Weeping Waxing]], this moon receives a full profile but does not appear in the earlier chronological "Twelve Eldritch Moons" list — see [[Eldritch Moons]] for the full discrepancy note.

The cruelest moon — not for what it destroys, but for what it makes mortals believe.

## Nature

Appears as a premature sunrise with soft amber-gold light, feeling warm, hopeful, and healing. Beneath that light, the Weave lies.

## World effects

**Illusory Comfort**: the world appears healed — wounds vanish, structures mend, the dead bloom — but none of it is real or permanent, and no one knows until it's too late. **When the real sun rises**, everything restored by the False Dawn either vanishes, mutates, or becomes hostile. **Reality Reversal**: failed spells suddenly succeed, lost items are found in one's pack, dead friends return — but with inverted memories.

## Madness

Short-term: confusion between past and future. Long-term: emotional detachment, belief that this is a dream. Indefinite: worship of False Dawn as a benevolent god.

## Campaign use

A PC's dead loved one returns and no one believes they were ever gone; a village rejoices at healing rains that turn to blood at sunrise; a new sun-god is worshipped and briefly, genuinely answers prayers.
