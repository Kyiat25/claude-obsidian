# Rhaldris, The Fleet-Lord

*"Aelthys sinks fleets. I decide which one."*

**Domains**: naval warfare, fleet strategy, the reading of currents in battle, victory at sea. **Symbol**: two ship-prows crossing on a chart. **Worshiped by**: admirals, marines, corsair-captains, war-navigators.

War refracted through Water's nature: not the charge but the current, not the blow but the position — doctrine holds the sea fights on the side that has *read* it. He is Water's answer to Fire and Air's own war-craft gods, each certain the decisive element is their own; naval chaplains of Rhaldris and storm-callers of the rival tradition serve the same fleets and despise each other professionally. His one law: the sea owes you nothing for winning — a victorious fleet that fails to give the sea its due sails home into the storm-goddess Aelthys. Before an engagement, southern captains pour a chart's worth of ink into the water, giving the sea the plan so it may choose to keep the secret.
