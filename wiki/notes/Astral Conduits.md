# Astral Conduits

Not physical structures like [[Leylines]] or [[Wells of Power]], but pathways in the Weave itself — invisible currents of power connecting worlds, planes, and lost civilizations. The third of the [[Three Pillars of Magical Flow]], and the only one that's *always in motion*, shifting as the Weave breathes.

## Function

Function like rivers in the Astral Sea, carrying raw magical energy across dimensions. Some are naturally occurring; others were forged by forgotten civilizations. They allow rapid movement of magic — teleportation, soul travel, the return of forgotten knowledge — but don't store magic like Wells or distribute it like leylines; they *move* it.

## Types

| Type | Function | Effect on magic |
|---|---|---|
| **Starborn Conduits** | Links between celestial bodies | Divine magic, celestial travel, memory recall from past cycles |
| **Ley Convergence Paths** | Where leylines intersect | Instant spellcasting, increased ritual power |
| **Whispering Roads** | Corridors influenced by The Whispering Dark | Warps reality, creating echoes of the past, phantoms, corruption |
| **Dreamstrands** | Woven by the Veiled Hand | Travel through dreams, time distortions, illusion-based teleportation |

## Named great Conduits

- **The Silver Lattice** — a vast network running through the heavens, once used by gods to send messages across the cosmos.
- **The Broken Path** — a shattered conduit where time itself has fractured; spells cast within shift unpredictably between past and future.
- **The Whispering Roads** — lead to lost memories, letting travelers step into moments of history long forgotten.
- **The Tides Between Worlds** — a shifting conduit allowing safe travel between planes, but only when celestial bodies align.

## Eldritch corruption

Astral Conduits exposed to [[Esh'Tarah]] or [[Xey'Tazun]] may fracture time and reflection, splitting casters across timelines or identities. The Faith designates such zones as **Threadbreaks**, dispatching elite Threadbearers or Scar-Wardens to seal them or die trying — events marking, per the source, "the slow unbinding of the Pattern," a prophecy whispered only during the Day of Twelve Shadows (see [[Ashkarra]]).
