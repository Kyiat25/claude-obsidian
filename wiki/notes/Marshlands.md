# The Marshlands — the River-Keepers

Not a major continental power but the scattered blood-kin family clans (~1 million total, twelve families) who control the estuary delta of [[Elarion]] where the Ivora (from [[Corvessa]]), the Thalaris (from [[Talanthar]]'s gorges), the Westwater, Silverrun, and Darkflow converge into an ever-shifting maze of open marsh, mangrove tangle, floating islands, and hidden channels. Outsiders who know only **Velcran** (the sanitized Imperial-Corvessan estuary port) think that's the whole of Marshlands civilization — the true Marshlands extend inland through channels that shift every season, navigable only to those who know the marks. The marsh-folk call themselves **River-Keepers**: they keep the rivers safe, for a price (5-12% passage tax on cargo). Homes are **crannogs** — artificial wooden platforms anchored to the swamp bottom, part of the marsh ecology rather than imposed on it, rising and falling with tide and flood.

## Blood-magic as daily life

Unlike the hidden Crimson Veil societies elsewhere, the Marshlands practice blood-magic openly and see it as honest — "the cost is ours to bear," in explicit philosophical contrast to Talanthar's forest-symbiosis ("the forest gives us power; we sacrifice to honor the exchange"). Everyday uses include blood-glyphs that seal wounds or preserve food, family-identifying ritual scarification, blood-sealed binding oaths, and blood-marks for channel navigation. Darker practices exist at the edges: bloodfeud tracking/cursing magic, memory-extraction from those who've consumed family blood, controversial soul-bonding, and **forced-bloodsharing**, which is the Marshlands' single most serious crime.

## The Twelve Families

**Wavebreak** (~85K, northern navigation), **Deepwater** (~72K, poison-craft, dangerous routes — prophecy-dependent, hit hard by [[The Threadbearer Crisis]]), **Marshroot** (~78K, food/fishing), **Tideborn** (~70K, tide-reading), **Shadowwater** (~65K, creature-hunting), **Brightstone** (~62K, ancient stone-circle passages), **Silverrun** (~58K, agriculture), **Rushwood** (~55K, herbs/poisons), **Fogmist** (~50K, smuggling and hidden routes), **Moonlight** (~48K, prophecy — also destabilized by the Crisis), **Blacktide** (~52K, ocean sailing), and **Willowmere** (~43K, mediation, the most peaceable).

## Strategic invisibility

Per a comparative-context supplement, the Marshlands control what should be one of Elarion's most valuable strategic positions — the estuary where its major rivers converge before the sea — yet remain diplomatically isolated by deliberate choice, not weakness: the family economy is self-sufficient and doesn't want external trade, non-family outsiders can only navigate the channels with a guide, and **Dimension Gates** provide rapid defensive mobilization without ever revealing true family numbers to outsiders. They could blockade Velcran or dominate river trade; they choose strategic invisibility instead.
