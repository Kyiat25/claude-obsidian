# Fence (Black Market Dealer) — Rank 1-4

*"You buy and sell stolen and illegal goods."* Batch 3, Criminal Specialist category. Source dual-names this "Fence / Black Market Dealer"; filed under the first name. The most direct mechanical tie yet to the existing [[Black Market]] hub page — this background is entirely built around the fencing mechanic that page only gestured at.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Small-Time Fence | Black Market Contact | Move a particularly valuable stolen item, establish a relationship with the thieves' guild, or avoid capture |
| 2 | Established Fence | Black Market Expert | Fence a legendary item, establish a network in a second city, or become preferred fence for a major guild |
| 3 | Black Market Baron | Master of Stolen Goods | Fence crown jewels, establish an international trafficking network, or become indispensable to the underworld |
| 4 | Lord of the Black Market | Master of Vice | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Small-Time Fence

**Skill Proficiency**: Deception, Insight. **Tool Proficiency**: forgery kit. **Equipment**: forgery kit, a merchant's scale, a coded ledger, common clothes, 15 gp.

**Feature — Black Market Contact**: find buyers for stolen items at 40% normal value; advantage on checks to appraise stolen goods; know black market locations in your city; identify stolen goods and estimate the original owner; thieves know you as a reliable buyer.

**Holdings**: a hidden stash location; 2-3 thieves who sell to you regularly; a small stolen-goods inventory; a contact with a larger fence.

## Rank 2: Established Fence

**Holdings**: a legitimate business front (shop, warehouse, etc.); income from fencing (40 gp/month); a larger inventory and stash; a network of 10-15 thieves and burglars; fence items up to 500 gp value; a corrupt guard on payroll; safe houses for hiding goods.

**Feature Expansion — Black Market Expert**: find buyers at 50% normal value; identify the value and origin of any item; know how to "clean" stolen goods; once per week, find a buyer for a difficult-to-move item; advantage on Deception checks conducting illegal business.

## Rank 3: Black Market Baron

**Holdings**: multiple front businesses; major income (120 gp/month); warehouses of stolen goods; a network of 30+ thieves and burglars; fence items worth thousands; several corrupt officials on payroll; international smuggling connections; you can acquire illegal items on request.

**Feature Expansion — Master of Stolen Goods**: find buyers at 60% normal value; fence magical items, at risk; once per month, move an "impossible" item (artifact-level theft, royal regalia); identify if any item in the city is stolen and who took it; advantage on all illegal-trade checks.

## Rank 4: Lord of the Black Market

**Holdings**: a legitimate business empire (worth 50,000+ gp); excellent income (400 gp/month); control of illegal trade across multiple cities; an organization of 100+ criminals; you can move anything anywhere; your own officials and guards; international connections.

**Feature — Master of Vice**: find buyers for any stolen item at 70% normal value; fence legendary magical items; once per year, arrange sale of an artifact-level item; control prices in the black market; your network can locate any item in the world, given time. Thieves' guilds negotiate with you, authorities know of you but can't touch you, and you may be wealthier than nobility.
