# The Woven Reverence (The Weaving)

*"We were not made. We arrived when the Pattern required us. And when it changes again, we shall return."*

The elven faith — not worship but attunement. Elves believe they were woven into the Weave before the gods understood the threads they now claim to command; they do not serve gods, they resonate with them, and they do not pray, they remember.

## Core tenets

1. **Existence Is a Thread** — all things are threads in the greater Pattern; the elven soul seeks harmony, never pulling too hard.
2. **Memory Is the True Divine** — forgetting is spiritual decay; stories and names are spiritual reinforcement, not mere culture.
3. **Change Is Woven, Not Forced** — true change comes from attunement, not imposition.

## The Three Loompaths

Keepers of the Pure Pattern (preservation, strict memory, aligned with Aelys/Gaeldros), Walkers of Divergent Threads (innovation through harmony, aligned with Vaithis/Nyzara), and Spinners of Flameleaf (radicals who believe the Pattern is a controlling lie — some edge toward Eldritch influence).

## The Woven Constellation (divine echoes, not gods to serve)

Seven archetypal Threads grouped by function rather than element: **Anchor Threads** (Aelys the Weave-Keeper, Nyzara the Moon That Watches, Vaelora the Deep Mother — preserve the Pattern), **Divergent Threads** (Vaithis the Wandering Star, Pyrris the Ember-Speaker — allow change and choice), **Renewal Thread** (Ishara the Verdant Queen — rebirth), and the **Severing Thread** (Serelyne the Pale Blade — protects the Pattern through necessary loss, exile, and sacrifice).

## Regional variants by elven subculture

Each elven people emphasizes different Threads: Wood Elves (Wildwoven — Ishara, Nyzara, Serelyne; reject curated cities), High Elves (Songwrights — Aelys, Pyrris, Vaithis; treat magic and memory as arcane architecture), Moon Elves (Dreamweavers — Nyzara, Vaelora, Vaithis; near-deify Nyzara as "the Silver Weaver"), Sun Elves (Canticle Guardians — Aelys, Serelyne, Ishara; the most rigid, law-bound tradition), Eladrin (Seasonal Harmonists — Pyrris, Vaithis, Ishara; emotions as sacred notes), [[Sea Elf (Lineage)|Sea Elves]] (Driftwoven — Vaelora, Nyzara, Vaithis; see the Pattern as tidal, ride it rather than walk it — overlaps with [[The Deepwater Communion]]), and [[Shadar-kai (Lineage)|Shadar-kai]] (Shadow-Bound — Serelyne, Nyzara, Vaelora; honor the Frayed and Severed as necessary sacrifices).

## Practices

The First Song (sung in fragments at every major ritual, believed to have brought elves into being), the Moonfast Vigil (silent night under a full moon), Threadcasting (divination via wind-blown silk or water), and Name-Weaving (true names are found through resonance, often decades after birth, not given).

## The Cycle of Renewal

Every 333 years the Weave shifts, and each grove or line-family must ritually give up a song, tradition, memory, or sacred object — guided by **Shatter-Weavers**, whose role is sacred destruction in service of harmony.

## Half-Elves

Regarded as "resonant anomalies" — not fully woven, not shunned but never truly attuned. Some philosophers believe they carry a hidden Divergent Thread the Weave never expected, and a prophetic verse suggests a Half-Elf may one day "rebind the forgotten chord." Compare [[The Way of the Named]], the separate Half-Elf-specific faith built on self-naming through trial, which treats this same liminality as a strength to be ignited rather than a strain to be resolved.

## Relationship to Vel'Zyren

Long ago a voice tore itself from the Spirit Pattern in refusal rather than harmony — Vel'Zyren, once sister to Aelys. Her name is never sung among the Woven Ones; she is referred to only as the Fraywalker or the Loom-Burned Queen. See [[The Ashen Web]] for her worshippers' own account, which frames this same rupture as chosen ascension rather than exile.

## History (condensed)

Loomless Time (proto-reverence, pure listening) → First Age (rites crystallize, "Woven Reverence" named by human scholars) → Second Age (Flameleaf schism forms in response to Fire-cult destruction of grove-memory) → Third Age (the Faith of the Bound Pattern fells World Trees for World Anchors, making the Woven Reverence their protector and eventual reluctant ally in refounding [[Aeravae]] after the [[Fall of Ashkarra]]) → Fourth Age (present: a recognized council voice within the Faith's Spirit Sanctum, though still claiming no priesthood or authority).
