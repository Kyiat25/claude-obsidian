# Enemy Rally

Enemy-only Focus-based action to restore troop morale (see [[Bloodied Breaking Points Rally]]'s status note). Two triggers: the automatic version at [[Bloodied and Bruised]] threshold, and this deliberate version.

## Normal Rally (deliberate)

A free action, once per round, when allies fail [[Breaking Points]] or are wavering. Requires: designated leader or highest-CR enemy, allies within 120 ft, and the leader must not be incapacitated, routing, at 0 Focus, or below 0 Sanity.

**Check**: `1d20 + Focus modifier` vs. the same DC as the failed Breaking Point.

| Roll | Result |
|---|---|
| Success | Allies reroll failed Breaking Point with Advantage; those who now succeed reset to 0 Momentum |
| Failure | Allies lose −1 Momentum instead |
| Nat 20 | All allies in range reset to 0 Momentum, gain Advantage on next attack, immune to next Breaking Point |
| Nat 1 | Allies locked at −2 Momentum; leader cannot Rally again this combat |

## Limits

Once per round (normal); Legendary auto-succeed once per combat; cannot Rally at 0 Focus, below 0 Sanity, routing, unconscious, or dead; a Nat 1 forfeits further Rallies for the rest of combat.

## Legendary and Epic tiers

- **Legendary leaders**: may auto-succeed any Rally once per combat (including the Auto Rally from [[Bloodied and Bruised]]); grant allies within 120 ft +2 to Breaking Point saves ("Death-Defying Presence"); enter Legendary Phase 2 instead of standard Bloodied effects.
- **Epic-tier (CR 17-20)**: can reach +4/+5 Momentum; Focus 18+ leaders may Rally as a bonus action; Sanity 20+ leaders are immune to casualty-triggered Breaking Points.

## Player counterplay

Interrupting a Rally (stun, silence, forced movement), damaging the leader beforehand to lower Focus, isolating the leader beyond 120 ft, or killing the leader mid-Rally are all named tactical options. [[Called Shot]]s targeting a leader's head/throat/hand can specifically cripple their ability to Rally before the kill.
