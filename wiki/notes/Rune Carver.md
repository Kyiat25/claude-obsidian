# Rune Carver (Enchanter) — Rank 1-4

*"You inscribe magical symbols that imbue objects with lasting power."* Batch 3, Magic-Focused category. Source dual-names this "Rune Carver / Enchanter"; filed under the first name.

> [!note] Distinct from Runesmith — a retired class of the same rune-adjacent theme
> [[Runesmith]] is a *retired* FEARS class (per [[Classes_Summary]], not to be used in new content). This background is unrelated — it's live content from Batch 3, not a revival of the retired class — but the naming proximity (both "rune"-themed) is worth flagging for table clarity.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Apprentice Rune Carver | Rune Sight | Inscribe a functional magic item, identify a dangerous rune trap, or learn a rare runic tradition |
| 2 | Journeyman Enchanter | Skilled Inscription | Enchant a significant item, develop a new runic pattern, or recover lost runic knowledge from ruins |
| 3 | Master Rune Carver | Runic Mastery | Create a rare magical item, rediscover a lost runic tradition, or develop a revolutionary technique |
| 4 | Grandmaster Enchanter | Legendary Inscription | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Apprentice Rune Carver

**Skill Proficiency**: Arcana, Investigation. **Tool Proficiency**: jeweler's tools, woodcarver's tools. **Equipment**: jeweler's or woodcarver's tools, a magnifying glass, 5 pieces of chalk, a fine chisel, 15 gp.

**Feature — Rune Sight**: detect the presence of magical runes or glyphs (inscribed magic only); advantage on Investigation checks to find hidden runes; identify the school of magic in a rune; know how to safely approach inscribed magical objects.

**Holdings**: a small carving workspace; basic inscription tools; a collection of rune templates; a mentor or guild master.

## Rank 2: Journeyman Enchanter

**Holdings**: a personal workshop with quality tools; income from commissions (30 gp/month); a collection of magical inks and materials; a library of runic alphabets and patterns; 3-5 regular clients; a network of other enchanters.

**Feature Expansion — Skilled Inscription**: create common magical items through inscription (standard crafting rules, DM approval); inscribe protective runes (advantage on first save against one specific magic type per inscription); temporarily enhance weapons/armor (+1, 24 hours, once per long rest); read ancient runic scripts; safely disarm magical rune traps.

## Rank 3: Master Rune Carver

**Holdings**: a renowned workshop (worth 1,500 gp); major income (100 gp/month); an extensive runic-lore library; rare inscription materials (worth 1,000 gp); 5-8 apprentices; commissions from nobility; your signature runic style is recognized.

**Feature Expansion — Runic Mastery**: create uncommon magical items through inscription; inscribe multiple protective runes on one item; once per week, create a temporary rune of power (functions as a specific spell, DM approval); identify and safely dispel any inscribed magic; modify existing enchantments.

## Rank 4: Grandmaster Enchanter

**Holdings**: multiple workshops across cities; excellent income (300 gp/month); a complete collection of known runic traditions; access to legendary inscription materials; an organization of 25+ enchanters; royal commissions and patents; museum-quality examples of your work.

**Feature — Legendary Inscription**: create rare magical items through inscription; inscribe complex multi-effect enchantments; once per month, create a temporary artifact-level rune (1 week duration); sense all inscribed magic within 120 ft; inscribe spells into objects for later casting. Kings commission you to enchant regalia and weapons, your technique becomes standard practice, ancient runic sites reveal secrets to you, and you may have discovered a link between **Threads and runic magic** — some items you created centuries ago still function perfectly.
