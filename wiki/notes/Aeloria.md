# Aeloria

One of four **Primal Forces** shaping the [[Creation World]] (see [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]]), embodying **the Loom** and the **[[Glyph of Threads]]**. [[Twins of Creation]] confirms she and [[Astralor]] are literally siblings — "her brother" — called **the Twins of Creation**, the foundation mortals and gods alike believe is the entire source of existence. She is **the Keeper of Fate**: the Weaver, the Unseen Hand. Where Astralor builds the framework, she ensures it can stand — she doesn't create laws but ensures they make sense, doesn't choose destinies but ensures they have weight, doesn't command the gods but ensures their power doesn't unravel reality.

> [!contradiction] RESOLVED — How much does Aeloria actually know?
> [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]] states, under a section literally titled "ONLY SHE KNOWS EVERYTHING," that Aeloria knows the complete cosmic structure, [[The Whispering Dark]]'s nature and function, and [[The Restorer]]'s existence (if not fully its capabilities). [[Twins of Creation]] directly contradicts this: "She does not know that The Restorer exists," and "She does not know that The Whispering Dark does more than merely consume." It goes further — she "assumes that all outcomes are shaped by her and her brother alone," and questions but never resolves why things that "should have been erased" sometimes return (unknowingly witnessing the Restorer's actual work). [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]] gave a third, still different framing — symmetric ignorance across all four Primal Forces, Aeloria included. [[Creation World]] independently backs [[Twins of Creation]]'s specific claim: she does not know [[The Restorer]] exists, and merely questions (never resolves) what she witnesses when erased things reappear.
>
> **Ruled (2026-07-14):** [[Twins of Creation]]'s account is adopted as this vault's canon, backed 3-of-4 by [[Creation World]] and [[Creations Misunderstanding]]. Aeloria does **not** know [[The Restorer]] exists, does not understand [[The Whispering Dark]]'s true nature (believes it merely consumes), and assumes she and [[Astralor]] alone shape all outcomes — she is not the cosmology's most-aware force; [[The Restorer]] is. [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]]'s "ONLY SHE KNOWS EVERYTHING" framing and the "symmetric ignorance" framing from [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]] are both non-canonical on this specific point going forward; left in place below as historical record of the disagreement rather than deleted. See [[Twins of Creation]] and [[The Weave]] for the full comparison table, also updated with this ruling.

## Domain

Fate must be woven before it can be lived — Aeloria's Loom tests patterns, trials, and destinies, ensuring time itself can hold together. Without this refinement, reality would unravel. What the earlier source frames as passive testing, the later source reframes as active, ongoing labor: she is constantly, deliberately correcting flaws in Astralor's creation that he never notices.

## What she actually does, in secret

None of the following is contradicted by [[Twins of Creation]] — that source only disputes what she *knows about the other two forces*, not her own stabilizing function:

- **Stabilizes Astralor's flawed creations** without his knowledge — his designs "work" largely because of her invisible correction work. [[Twins of Creation]] confirms this independently in different language: she "takes what is raw and refines it," ensuring Astralor's contradictions don't collapse the structure.
- **Guides Thread rotation** by subtly adjusting probability so no single Thread dominates one location long enough to destabilize reality (see [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]]'s Thread-rotation explanation and the Ashkarra Catastrophe case study).
- **Directs the [[Loom-Wardens]]** — an entire secret organization serves her unknowingly, experiencing her influence as intuition, calling, or divine favor, never her actual guidance.
- **Touches [[Threadbearers]]** directly — they sense her stabilizing corrections as "fate," one of three cosmic layers they unknowingly perceive at once.
- Would **erase herself from the awareness** of anyone who learned her true name, per [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]] — not directly addressed by [[Twins of Creation]] either way.

By contrast, [[Twins of Creation]] specifically says she does **not** realize a third or fourth force could be responsible for effects she witnesses — when she sees things "that should have been erased" reappearing (unknowingly, the Restorer's work), she questions it without ever suspecting another force exists. She believes she and her brother are the only two hands shaping the cycle.

## The Great Silence: erasing herself from all memory, not just the Restorer's

A separate, later-ingested source deepens her "unknown even to Astralor" framing into something far more deliberate than mere oversight. At the beginning of time, per this account, Aeloria made a choice no other being has ever made: she **removed herself from history entirely**, erasing her name from all divine records and ensuring not even the gods speak of her — creating a veil so absolute that even [[Astralor]], her own twin, does not know she exists at all. This is explicitly framed as the ultimate act of control, not fear: "if no one knows her, no one can act against her... if no one knows her, then her will is absolute." [[Astralor]]'s own account leaves ambiguous whether he ever knew and was made to forget, or never knew at all.

> [!contradiction] How total is Astralor's ignorance of Aeloria specifically?
> Every cosmology source already ingested in this cluster ([[Twins of Creation]], [[Creation World]], [[Creations Misunderstanding]], etc.) treats Astralor and Aeloria as siblings who both know of each other and are jointly unaware only of [[The Whispering Dark]] and [[The Restorer]]. This source goes much further: Astralor does not know Aeloria exists at all, full stop — not merely unaware of her methods or her stabilizing work, but unaware she is a person. This is a different, stronger claim than the "twins jointly blind to the other two forces" framing used everywhere else, and sits alongside [[The Lie of Godhood]]'s separate contradiction (Astralor as an impersonal Law rather than a person) as a third distinct account of what Astralor is and knows. Not resolved — flagged the same as the standing three-way knowledge-hierarchy conflict on [[The Weave]].

## The Loom-Wardens and the Spirit Pantheon, reconciled

This source calls the Loom-Wardens the closest beings to uncovering her existence — they know fate doesn't sustain itself alone, call her "the Hidden Hand" or "the Eternal Weave," but never learn her name, and if they ever did, "Aeloria would vanish from them," since the moment she is known she is compromised. The [[Spirit Pantheon]]'s own source material goes further still, stating she "threaded the domain of Spirit into the world itself" as her final act of visible presence — meaning every Spirit deity, not just the Loom-Wardens, is one of her unknowing threads. Treated as compatible framings of the same secret at two different scales (one small organization, one entire pantheon) rather than a contradiction — the Loom-Wardens would simply be a further, tighter-knit unknowing cell within the broader unknowing pantheon.

## Divine Sparks

Per [[Creation World]], Aeloria unintentionally helped create **[[Divine Sparks]]** — the origin material of the first gods — by binding fate-threads around things that had no place in the pattern. Neither she, [[Astralor]], [[The Whispering Dark]], nor [[The Restorer]] intended this or is aware of it; it's presented as a pure accident of all four forces acting simultaneously.

## Naming note

"Loom" and "Threads" as Aeloria's domain vocabulary connects directly to the **Loom-Cities** and **Thread**-component material referenced in [[FEARS Advanced Backgrounds]] (Batch 2/3) and confirmed further by [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]]'s own citation of `World Anchors and the Loom-City System.md` (still uningested) as background material. The [[Loom-Wardens]]' very name is now confirmed to derive from her domain specifically — not a coincidence, and no longer just a suspected connection.
