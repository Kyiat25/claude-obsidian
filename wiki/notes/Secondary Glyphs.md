# Secondary Glyphs

*"Primary Glyphs shape the cosmos. Secondary Glyphs let mortals survive in it."* Where the [[Primal Glyphs|Primary Glyphs]] are immutable cosmic laws mortals cannot directly wield, Secondary Glyphs are the **mortal-scale framework** that tames raw Weave power into something learnable, castable, and repeatable — the underlying grammar of every spell's mechanical shape (spell slots, range, casting time, duration, saving throws). Framed as "echoes" of the Primary Glyphs, close enough to the true language of reality to be spoken safely without warping the caster's soul.

## The 23 Secondary Glyphs, by category

**Spell Access & Scaling (7)**: Glyph of Ritual (slow casting without spell slots), Glyph of Pact (externally-granted magic — Warlocks, divine contracts), Glyph of Focus (concentration), Glyph of Sustaining Magic (non-concentration duration), and three power tiers — Glyph of Empowerment (levels 1-3), Glyph of Greater Power (4-6), Glyph of Grand Weaving (7-8), Glyph of Apex (level 9, reality-altering magic).

**Spell Ranges & Targeting (8)**: Glyph of Melee, Glyph of Touch, Glyph of Close Reach (10-15 ft), Glyph of Short Range (30 ft), Glyph of Medium Range (60 ft), Glyph of Long Range (120+ ft), Glyph of Sight (line-of-sight targeting), Glyph of Area Influence (zone effects).

**Spell Duration & Persistence (3)**: Glyph of Instant Casting, Glyph of Lingering Effects (residual influence, e.g. Wall of Fire), Glyph of Temporal Persistence (delayed/unfolding triggers, e.g. Contingency).

**Casting Time & Speed (2)**: Glyph of Swift Weaving (reaction/bonus-action casting), Glyph of Grand Invocation (slow, powerful magic like Resurrection).

**Saving Throws & Resistances (3)**: Glyph of Willbreaking (forced saves), Glyph of Unstoppable Force (bypasses resistance/immunity), Glyph of Adaptive Resilience (dynamic resistance that shifts with repeated exposure to the same spell type).

## Why they exist

Not all casters access magic the same way (ritual vs. pact vs. raw concentration), not all spells hit the same way (single target vs. area, melee vs. long range), and not all magical defenses are static — Secondary Glyphs are the shared vocabulary that lets wildly different casting traditions all still resolve through the same underlying rules. They're also explicitly framed as the reason Wild Magic doesn't happen by default: Secondary Glyphs constrain raw Weave power into safe, structured expression, and it's specifically when a Glyph fails or is imperfectly bound that a **Shattered Glyph Event** and true chaos magic results.
