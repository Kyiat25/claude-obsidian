# Thar Vornhal — The Stone Tribunal & the Gorge Wards

Earth-Thread [[Loom-Cities|Loom-City]], population ~4.2 million, in central [[Elarion]]. Not one city but two occupying the same space: the **Upper City** (carved into three connected mountain peaks — Deepdelver, Ironheart, and Truthhammer, tiers 1-9, ~2.5 million, dwarf-dominated) and the **Gorge Wards** (a chaotic sprawl of hanging platforms, gantries, and cliff-carved warehouses along the river gorge below, ~1.7 million, human-majority). Overall racial split: 63% Human, 12% Dwarf, 9% Gnome, 16% others — but dwarves hold 6 of 7 Council seats, the judiciary, and the World Anchor despite being a numeric minority, creating deep and enduring dwarf/human tension ("We are the many who serve the few" vs. "It is not numbers that grant wisdom, but connection to stone").

Where other Loom-Cities anchor power, memory, or prophecy, Thar Vornhal is **the load-bearing city of the Loom** — its Deep Anvil resonance stabilizes leylines across thousands of miles of the Estelwater corridor. Civic motto, carved in six-foot Threadgold letters above the Stone Tribunal: **"BURDEN IS NECESSARY."**

## Founding: the Threefold Sacrifice

~2,847 years ago, three dwarven kings — **Tharn Deepdelver**, **Vorn Ironheart**, and **Hal Truthhammer** — descended into an Eldritch breach beneath the mountains and bound their souls into the stone to seal it, becoming the foundation of the anchor point rather than simply dying to close it. The breach isn't sealed so much as **held**, eternally, by the three kings' unending will; the Deep Anvil (the chamber where they died) became the Earth Thread World Anchor. The Gorge Wards were a later, contested human addition — engineers reshaped the river the breach's sealing had diverted through the mountain into a functioning trade gorge, over dwarven objection that has never fully healed. What isn't public record: the river's strange "memory" comes from flowing past the sealed wound, and the **Vein-Labyrinths** beneath the city are fractures radiating from the breach itself, growing deeper every year.

## Government: the Council of Seven Hammers

A judicial oligarchy of 7 High Judges (6 dwarf, 1 human), operating under [[The Deep Law]] — precedent and restoration over punishment. Current Hammers: **Aldric Deepdelver** (First Hammer, Stone-Speaker Supreme, 487, traditionalist); **Thora Ironforge** (Second, military justice, pragmatist); **Marcus Stonebridge** (Third, the sole human seat, commercial law, reformist); **Grimhilde Truthhammer** (Fourth, criminal justice, descendant of Hal Truthhammer, secretly meeting with organized crime to explore reducing violence); **Barek Earthshaper** (Fifth, infrastructure, systems-obsessed); **Venna Silverbraid** (Sixth, civil law, quietly the most powerful Hammer via favor networks); **Torunn Deepcaller** (Seventh, Thread law, the lone voice warning the seal is weakening). The semi-autonomous **Dock Council**, led by Dockmaster **Kestra Cliffborn**, governs the mostly-human Gorge Wards.

## Current crisis: the Succession Deadlock

Threadbearer **Moira Stonehand** died 18 days ago after a 43-year term; the Council of Seven Hammers has deadlocked along Traditional/Reform/Moderate blocs and cannot reach the 5-vote supermajority needed to install a successor among candidates Aldric Deepdelver, reformist **Mira Truthseeker** (29, youngest Stone-Speaker ever certified), **Gareth Stonewhisper** (the only human Stone-Speaker in 200 years), or dark-horse Torunn Deepcaller. With the World Anchor unattended, the seal beneath the city is weakening, Stone-Speakers report discordant echoes, and the Vein-Labyrinths are more active than in centuries — and the Council remains paralyzed by politics while ~4.2 million people live above what the three kings died to contain.

## Notable figures beyond the Council

**Commander Thrain Ironshield** (Glyphmarked Captain-General) — 12,000 troops trying to police a city of 4.2 million, openly warning the Council he's under-resourced. **Watch-Captain Moira Blackstone** (Deep Watch Commander) — has led 63 Vein-Labyrinth expeditions, lost people on 57 of them, convinced the depths are stirring. **Garrick "Three-Fingers" Riverstone** (Pilot's Guild Master) — keeps secret logs of the river's "memory currents," worth a fortune, refuses to sell. **Merida Swift** (Water-Watcher Speaker) — senses river-memory shifts hours in advance, but her Water Thread abilities are fading now that the cycle has moved on.
