# The Glyph of Echoes — The Wheel of the Restorer

*"Even the ash of a dead world holds the memory of flame."*

**Layer**: Primal, Tier 1. **Domains**: rebirth, renewal, memory, restoration. **Opposing glyph**: [[The Glyph of Endings]]. **Symbol**: a spiraling loop, or a wheel with seven spokes.

Embodies the law of cyclical return — what is lost is never truly gone, merely transformed. Not kindness or fairness, but cosmic continuity: the breath after death, the spark after ruin, the future echo of a vanished past. What resists [[The Glyph of Endings|the Maw]] is not defiance but purpose — the ashes of a world become the soil of the next.

## Divine figures

The Restorer (name forbidden, often associated with the Wheel); whisper-honored spirits like [[Nyzara]], who carry echoes forward.

## Eldritch corruption

When corrupted, the glyph does not forget — it repeats: identities overwrite, past bleeds into present, entire cities repeat actions until collapse. Echo cults like the Cyclebound Chorus believe nothing new is possible, only perfect repetition.

## Places of worship

Honored in quiet rites in [[Aeravae]], where water remembers what is washed away; the Chamber of Names Recalled in Thar Vornhal allows the recently dead to be ritually named again, hoping their soul returns in the next cycle; an ancient structure beneath Aeravae, the Spiral Vault, replays dying visions in pools of slow water, unmaintained but never fading.
