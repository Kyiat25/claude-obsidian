# Styles of Magic

A family of optional, thematically-flavored casting traditions (alongside [[Glyph Binding]]) that re-skin how a caster's magic works mechanically without changing which spells they know — each with its own culture, cost, and aesthetic. Mostly `status: testing` or `excluded`, not confirmed core canon.

## Eldritch Carving

*"To carve the unknowable is to welcome pain, prophecy, and transformation."* A forbidden style: practitioners (Apostles, Jaegers, Martyrs) inscribe the Words of the [[Guide to the Eldritch Forces|Great Ones]] directly into living flesh rather than casting through gesture or formula. Carvings can be **Drawn** (weakest), **Tattooed** (stronger), or **Scarified** (strongest) — deeper inscription means more power and exponentially higher risk of madness or mutation. Originated in the bloodstained rituals of the Hollow Scourge; some carvings are fragments of failed containment glyphs originally meant to bind [[Vezherak]], and unconsciously perpetuate his doctrine of recursive sealing.

## Osteomancy

*"Bone remembers what flesh forgets."* Bone-bound magic shaped through marrow and skeletal memory, blending necromancy, transmutation, and elemental force but always channeled through the framework of death. Osteomancers don't just raise or animate bone — they understand it, speak to it, grow it, shape it, treating each bone as a record and each spell as a fracture or a forging. Prominent in Luyarnha, where the dead linger.

## Voidwrought Weaving

*"The stars are not silent. They whisper. They pull. They remember."* Cosmic-pressure magic learned through exposure rather than study — a mind brushing against stars that shouldn't be, or truths beyond the horizon of comprehension. Practiced by those touched by [[Eldritch Spirits|Eldritch energies]], haunted by the Null Reflection, or mutated by the Eldritch Moons. Distorts causality: spells may resolve before they're cast, or linger as echoes in time. Sanity is both cost and compass — the more lost, the clearer the stars become.

## Scarred Magic

*"Every mark is a memory. Every memory is a weapon."* Practiced almost exclusively among orc clans: magic earned through suffering rather than study or bloodline, branded rather than flowing like wind or light. Practitioners — Woundbinders, Painwrights, Echo-Bloods — carry spells as scars, each one a lesson, rite, or willingly-claimed burden, sometimes manifesting as ancestral voices or visions.

## Blood Magic

*"Blood remembers. Blood obeys. Blood endures."* The oldest taboo: power drawn from life itself rather than divine grace or arcane geometry, always costing health, memory, or connection. A style rather than a school — it can flavor spells from any tradition, provided they're cast through bloodletting, sacrifice, or vitae manipulation. Practiced openly as **Sanguimagia** by the Crimson Orders in Tolia and Kanrath; elsewhere it survives only in whispers, scars, and black-market rites. Effects can be influenced or resisted based on shared ancestry or bloodline traits.
