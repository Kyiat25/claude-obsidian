# Half-Orc

*"Born of two paths, but forged in one. Blood is not enough. Scar is not enough. Only choice is enough."*

**Origin/culture**: Orcish tradition holds mixed blood is not dishonor but demands clarity of purpose. At age 14, all Half-Orcs undergo the **Trial of Line-Claiming** ("the Scarfire Choice"), overseen by two Line-Keepers and a Scar-Warden — a trial of endurance, wit, and resolve tied to the ancestral line's defining value, followed by a sacred wound accepting the legacy. Passing means full, permanent Orc status (heritage never mentioned again unless you choose); failing carries no shame — you receive a soft-scar and a full-honor escort called the **Farewell of Fire**, then live as an Honored Watcher among any Orcish clan. Many Half-Orcs never take the trial at all — an equally honored path. See [[The Path of the First Scar]] for the fuller Orcish faith this trial belongs to, and [[Orc (Lineage)]] for the parent culture.

**Physical**: mature by 14, live ~75 years (shorter than humans, longer than full Orcs); Medium, 5½–6½ ft.

## Traits

- **Darkvision** 60 ft.
- **Relentless Endurance**: drop to 1 HP instead of 0, 1/long rest
- **Savage Attacks**: reroll one extra weapon damage die on a critical hit
- **Bridge-Walker**: 2 skills from Intimidation/Insight/Persuasion/Survival
- **Dual Heritage**: advantage vs. frightened
- **Languages**: Common + Orcish (often spoken with a recognized "two-tongued" accent)

**Mental**: Focus +0 / Sanity +0 — no biological Sanity bonus (unlike full Orcs), but Dual Heritage grants fear resistance specifically.

**DCS Integration**: Dodge +1 / Endure +1 / Deflect +0; DR +0 (toughness via Relentless Endurance, not hide). A "surge fighter" — waits for the crit moment (Savage Attacks) with Relentless Endurance as backup.

## Corruption: The Split Thread

A distinct vulnerability from full Orcs — corruption enters through identity crisis, belonging denial, or self-hatred. At Corruption 10+: Identity Fracture (confusion over which culture's values to follow), Belonging Void, Worth Erosion. **Split Thread table** (1d6): False Belonging (disadvantage on Bridge-Walker), Heritage Rejection (Relentless Endurance disabled), Dual Silence (disadvantage on CHA checks), Identity Void (Sanity save or −1d4), Whisper of Unbelonging (+1 Whisper Point, Savage Attacks' extra die fails), or Bridge Holds (Corruption −1).

## Backgrounds (not mechanical subraces — narrative framing only)

Clan-Raised (deep Scar-Law fluency, constant pressure to prove worth), Human-Raised (heritage as abstract concept, seeks connection), Wanderer (self-taught in both traditions), Dual Household (natural cultural translator).

## Claiming vs. not Claiming

Both paths carry equal dignity per the source. Claimed Half-Orcs are Orc, full stop, with all clan rights; un-Claimed Half-Orcs remain honored guests in any Orcish territory, protected by guest-right, and may fight in a Warden's name if called. Dishonoring the clan while un-Claimed is judged *more* harshly than for full-blooded Orcs — marked "Split-Blood Failed," one of the gravest entries in Orcish record-keeping.
