# Fisher (Dockworker) — Rank 1-4

*"You make your living from the sea and docks."* Batch 3, Working Class category. Source dual-names this "Fisher / Dockworker"; filed under the first name. Working-class maritime labor, distinct from [[Ship Captain]]'s command-track progression — no overlap stated, though the two could plausibly intersect (a Fisher's crew serving under a Ship Captain, for instance).

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Dockhand | Dockside Worker | Prevent a dock accident, earn a docker gang's respect, or prove your worth on a voyage |
| 2 | Skilled Fisher | Master of Nets | Survive a major storm, discover a profitable fishing ground, or save someone from drowning |
| 3 | Fleet Captain | Sea's Bounty | Establish a fishing monopoly, discover new fishing grounds, or weather a hurricane without losses |
| 4 | Master of the Fishing Trade | Lord of the Catch | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Dockhand

**Skill Proficiency**: Athletics, Perception. **Tool Proficiency**: water vehicles. **Equipment**: fishing tackle, 50 ft of rope, a net, thick clothes, 10 gp.

**Feature — Dockside Worker**: tie complex knots automatically; advantage on Athletics checks involving ropes, swimming, or lifting; know the layout of docks and ships; find work on docks or fishing boats easily; recognize different vessel and cargo types.

**Holdings**: a bunk in a dockside boarding house; modest daily wages; a network of fellow dockworkers; knowledge of ship schedules.

## Rank 2: Skilled Fisher

**Holdings**: a small fishing boat (worth 200 gp); income from fishing (25 gp/month); fishing equipment; regular customers who buy your catch; 1-2 crew members; knowledge of good fishing spots.

**Feature Expansion — Master of Nets**: advantage on Survival checks related to fishing or reading water; predict weather at sea 24 hours in advance; know the best fishing times and locations; once per week, catch a legendary fish or valuable haul; navigate coastal waters expertly.

## Rank 3: Fleet Captain

**Holdings**: 3-5 fishing boats; good income (70 gp/month); crews totaling 15-20 fishermen; a warehouse for storing catch; contracts with restaurants and markets; a reputation for quality catch; knowledge of secret fishing grounds.

**Feature Expansion — Sea's Bounty**: your vessels always return with a good catch barring disaster; advantage on all fishing/water-navigation checks; predict fish migration and seasonal patterns; once per month, your fleet brings in an extraordinary haul (2-3x normal value); organize fishing expeditions to distant waters.

## Rank 4: Master of the Fishing Trade

**Holdings**: a fleet of 15+ vessels; excellent income (200 gp/month); hundreds of employees; multiple warehouses and processing facilities; contracts with cities for regular supply; you control fishing-market prices; your methods are industry standard.

**Feature — Lord of the Catch**: your fleet always finds fish, whether by supernatural luck or skill; once per year, catch a creature of legend; organize major fishing operations affecting regional food supply; your techniques preserve stocks (sustainable fishing); storms seem to avoid your fleet. Cities depend on your catch, other fishers follow your predictions, and you may have an alliance with sea creatures or spirits.
