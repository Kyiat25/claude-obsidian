# Kel Varyan — Seat of the Amethyst Crown

Water-Thread [[Loom-Cities|Loom-City]] and imperial capital, population ~8.5 million (45% Human, 25% Gnome, 20% Elf, 10% others — Halfling, Dwarf, Half-Orc, Dragonborn, Tiefling). A 3,000-year-old coastal city, originally named **Deveros**, in [[Elarion]].

> [!note] Two accounts of this city
> An earlier, thinner gazetteer entry (`.raw/Dór-o Estel.md`) described Kel Varyan as governed by a "Prophetic Bureaucracy" of Tide-Readers reading wave patterns, split into Upper/Lower twin cities. The deep-dive campaign source below instead describes a Senate-and-Council imperial government with a named Crownbinder lineage. Treat the material below as the more detailed and current canon; the wave-reading detail may still exist as color for minor civic ritual, but the governing structure is the Amethyst Council/Senate system.

## Founding history

Legend holds that the water spirit **Varyssa of the Endless Mirror** sang the "First Glyph of Return" over the delta, and the settler-prophet **Loxis the Threadtouched** planted the first anchor-glyph at Hastellian Rise. About 2,800 years ago, the **War of the Shattered Coast** saw the warrior-seer **Alveria the Sky-Spear** — wielding a spear blessed by Selmira — defeat the blue dragon **Ivora the Tidelocked** in a 24-day battle; the River Ivora and the Amethyst Crown myth both trace to this fight. The **Senate of Reflection** arose from 24 resurrected fallen warriors of that war, beginning the Crownbinder tradition. About 1,100 years ago, Crownbinder **Vireon the Just** tried to sever the city's loyalty to the Weave during the **Flooding Trial**; the Weave surged and flooded the city, exposing its hidden World Anchor and formally inducting Kel Varyan into the Faith of the Bound Pattern as a Loom-City.

## Geography

Six core districts: **Hastellian Rise** (Crown District — the Amethyst Crown Palace and Prisada Arcana), **Prisada Arcana** (Scholarly Quarter — Deveros Arcane University, the Moonscribe Vault), **Consentiarus** (Administrative Heart — seat of the Senate of Salt and Scale), **Caeli Tower Heights** (Sky District, air travel), **Solenta Minor** (Industrial Quarter — the Flame-Forges of Cindralia), and **Gardens of Song** (Memorial District). Outside the core: the **Tidelit Docks** and the **Flooded Veil**, a rumored criminal hideout.

## The Crowned Glyph (World Anchor)

Housed in the Crownhold Chamber, "The Crowned Glyph" is a levitating amethyst crystal ring performing Weave Stabilization, Memory Anchoring, Reality Binding (against Eldritch corruption), and Legal Foundation for magical contracts. Communing with it exacts a **Memory Price** — the High Crownbinder ages rapidly with each session in exchange for insight.

## Government: the Amethyst Council and the Senate of Salt and Scale

**The Amethyst Council** (executive, 5 permanent seats): the **High Crownbinder** (wielder of the Amethyst Crown, supreme judicial/magical authority, serves until death or abdication); the **Threadbearer of the Current Cycle** (rotating by active Thread, currently Water-aligned, liaison to other Loom-Cities); the **Moonscribe Archivist** (keeper of the Moonscribed Codex, interprets omens); the **Tideforged Admiral** (commands naval forces and overseas territories); and the **Voice of the Senate** (elected, 3-year term).

**The Senate of Salt and Scale** (legislative, 108 seats): 36 Noble Houses, 36 Guild Representatives, 24 Regional Delegates, 12 Faith Representatives (3 per major pantheon). Sessions run every sixteen days on the half-moon cycle, with special sessions at the Lunar Reflectorium during major celestial events.

**Imperial territories**: continental — Zhen-Lan (tribute-paying ally), Myrrhaleon (philosophical satellite), the Lyrran Coast Towns; maritime — [[Selveran]] (autonomous Loom-City in the Mirror Isles) and the Outer Island Settlements, both dependent on an Admiralty Council and Fleet Communications network that leaves them vulnerable to communication delays and strategic isolation.

### Current leadership

- **High Crownbinder Lysander Memorialis** (67, 23 years in office) — rose from a scribe family via merit; modernized the legal code, established memory-theft protections; visibly aging from Anchor communion, quietly grooming a successor.
- **Threadbearer Serana Tidecaller** (52) — prophetic Water Thread spiritual leader, mediates interfaith disputes. Per [[The Threadbearer Crisis]]'s Session 0-1 opening-adventure source, she is secretly the sole surviving Threadbearer of the ongoing crisis and its actual hidden architect — using it to consolidate power before rival Threadbearers can manifest. Not disclosed to the public, and not necessarily true at every table.
- **Admiral Marina Stormwright** (48) — career naval officer, modernized the imperial fleet and its magical-warfare tactics.
- **Grand Moonscribe Varallis the Keeper** (78) — guards the Vault of the Abyss, leading scholarly authority on the fall of [[Ashkarra]] and Eldritch threats.
- **Echo-Keeper Mineth Spiritvoice** — spiritual leader of the Spirit Well of Echoes.

## Culture and religion

Citizens keep **echo-journals** as living diaries/legal documents; forgetting is treated as spiritual harm. All major acts are recorded in glyphic form, and disputes are settled through memory projection and Weave testimony ("echo-truth" — every action ripples forward). The Faith of the Bound Pattern is the state religion, integrated into governance, with a Water Thread emphasis on memory, prophecy, and cyclical change; the older **Water Pantheon** (Selmira the Tide-Speaker; Nereus the Deep Current) remains devoutly worshipped alongside it.

## Mysteries and campaign hooks

- **The Krakenlight Resonance** — locations across the city show corruption from Krakenlight, one of the twelve Eldritch Moons: independent shadows, wrong reflections, memories that don't belong to their holders.
- **The Missing Moonscribes** — three Vault of the Abyss researchers studying historical memory preservation have vanished without trace over six months.
- **The Anchor's Pulse Anomaly** — the Crowned Glyph has begun pulsing in unprecedented patterns; the Amethyst Crown glows unexpectedly, and officials report strange dreams.
- **The Succession Question** — Lysander Memorialis's rapid aging is forcing succession politics into the open.
- **The Selveran Autonomy Movement** — [[Selveran]] is agitating for full self-governance as a Loom-City, straining imperial unity.
- **The Foreign Trade Conspiracy** and **The Memory Merchant Ring** — economic espionage and large-scale memory trafficking, both suspected of high-level protection.
