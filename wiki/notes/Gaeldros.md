# Gaeldros, The Stone of Judgment

*"Fate is not law. Prophecy is not judgment. Even gods must kneel before the law. Even fate must answer to justice. Even the stars must answer to stone when the Pattern begins to fray."*

**Domain**: strength, divine law, the one who judges gods. **Symbol**: a stone tablet with an unbroken chain carved into it. **Worshiped by**: judges, oathkeepers, those who enforce divine law. **Rivals**: [[Vel'Zyren]] (defier of fate), those who seek to break divine law. **Allies**: [[Vatharun]] (stability), [[Orivak]] (leylines).

The Judge of [[The Court of the Silent Tribunal]] — his word becomes leyline-law. Presided over [[The Trial of the Web-Spinner]], where he issued the Edict Unbound (a divine law with no precedent) and delivered the ruling that bound [[Vel'Zyren]] beneath the Underdark. That trial revealed the gods' limits: even Gaeldros could not undo, erase, or kill her — only contain her. When Vel'Zyren sank her threads too deep into stone-law during [[The Fractured Pillar]] event, Gaeldros declared her an enemy of the Pattern itself, and the resulting divine confrontation nearly shattered the Pillar of Gaeldros, a symbolic anchor of divine law — reforged afterward, but with one crack deliberately left unsealed as a reminder that even stone can fail.
