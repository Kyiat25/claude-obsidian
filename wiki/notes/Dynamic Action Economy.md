# Dynamic Action Economy

FEARS' replacement for the rigid 5E turn structure (move / action / bonus action). Pitched as removing the "wait your turn" constraint in favor of coordinated, simultaneous planning between party members.

## Components

- **Fluid Action System** — actions aren't confined to the immediate scene; framed as rippling through the whole campaign.
- **Initiative Reimagined** — players actively plan and combine actions together rather than acting in strict sequence ("the battlefield becomes a canvas for coordinated heroism").
- **[[Whisper Engine]]** — tracks and amplifies player agency; converts decisions into "world-altering" moments. [[A World that Watches]] confirms this is documented as a standalone optional rule elsewhere, not exclusively bundled in this action economy — see its page for the open question on how it relates to [[Whisper Points]].
- **Narrative Momentum** — generated by actions; lets players reshape past events through later strategic choices.

## What's not specified

No turn-order procedure is actually described — "no longer do you wait your turn" is asserted without replacement mechanics for resolving simultaneity or conflicts between players acting "together."
