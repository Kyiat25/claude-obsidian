# Scout — Rank 1-4 (Wilderness Expert)

*"You know the wilds better than civilization, reading the land like others read books."* Tier 1 (Essential). Fulfills the "Scout/Ranger" slot [[FEARS Advanced Backgrounds]] flagged as not-yet-developed after Batch 1.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Trailblazer | Wayfinder | Map uncharted territory, guide a party through dangerous wilderness without casualties, or discover a significant natural landmark |
| 2 | Expert Guide | Wilderness Mastery | Guide an important expedition, map a dangerous region, or save a settlement by predicting a natural disaster |
| 3 | Master Scout | One With The Land | Lead a historic expedition, discover a new route that changes trade, or write a standard cartography text |
| 4 | Legendary Pathfinder | Master of the Wilderness | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Trailblazer

**Skill Proficiency**: Nature, Survival. **Tool Proficiency**: cartographer's tools. **Language**: one of choice. **Equipment**: cartographer's tools, hunting trap, traveler's clothes, 10 gp.

**Feature — Wayfinder**: excellent memory for geography — recall terrain/settlement/feature layouts you've been near; find food and water for yourself and up to 5 people/day in most terrain; travel at a fast pace without a passive Perception penalty.

**Specialty** (choose your familiar terrain): Arctic, Coast, Desert, Forest, Grassland, Mountain, Swamp, Underdark.

**Holdings**: a reliable map of your home region; a contact with local hunters or trappers; a hidden campsite known only to you.

## Rank 2: Expert Guide

**Holdings**: detailed maps of multiple regions; income from guiding services (20 gp/month); a network of 5-8 wilderness contacts (rangers, druids, hermits); supply caches at 3-4 hidden locations; reputation in frontier settlements.

**Feature Expansion — Wilderness Mastery**: navigate by stars/sun automatically; advantage on Survival checks in your specialty terrain; predict weather 24 hours in advance; know hidden paths and shortcuts; your party doesn't get lost while you're guiding.

## Rank 3: Master Scout

**Holdings**: a comprehensive map collection (worth 500 gp); major income from expeditions and cartography (70 gp/month); a network of 15-20 contacts across regions; 3-5 junior scouts under you; supply caches in 10+ strategic locations; your maps are sold and copied by others.

**Feature Expansion — One With The Land**: find water and shelter even in barren terrain; advantage on initiative in wilderness; identify all plants, animals, and natural hazards in your regions; once per week, call on wilderness contacts for major assistance; your party gains +2 AC when you choose the campsite.

## Rank 4: Legendary Pathfinder

**Holdings**: a definitive, authoritative atlas of the wilderness; excellent income from consulting and map sales (200 gp/month); a network of 40+ wilderness contacts across the continent; a school of scouts you founded/lead; your name on geographic features; expeditions seek your consultation.

**Feature — Master of the Wilderness**: automatic success on Survival checks (DC 20 or lower); navigate any terrain as if it were your specialty; weather seems to favor you; once per month, lead an expedition through "impossible" terrain; wild animals regard you as neutral. Rulers consult you before military campaigns through wilderness, your maps are worth 10x normal value, settlements along your discovered routes are named after you, and you can predict natural disasters with uncanny accuracy.
