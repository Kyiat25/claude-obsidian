# Sanguine Eclipse

*"It is not an absence of light. It is the presence of something far worse."*

First seen 3rd Age, 58 TA, during a military purge in Ashkarra. A celestial erasure — the moon consumed by a red-black corona.

## Nature

Known as "The Bleeding Eye" or "The Hush Above" — the moment the Veiled Hand looked away, and [[Ul'Naresh]], The Dream Inverted, looked back.

## World effects

**Dimming of Witness**: all light casts red-tinted shadows that bleed outward; sound carries poorly and is muffled; no birds sing, animals fall silent, and words echo in reverse. **Eclipse mechanics**: lasts 1d6 hours; passive perception drops -5; magic cannot be counterspelled once begun (it becomes inevitable); divine casters have a 25% chance of receiving no response from their gods.

**Monster effects**: undead and aberrations coordinate in silence, operating in unison; creatures freeze in place, then violently awaken at the eclipse's conclusion.

## Madness

Short-term: feeling watched, not now but retroactively. Long-term: hallucinations of forgotten or failed people. Indefinite: the belief that one already died and the world is a delusion.

## Campaign use

A battlefield where the dead can't be raised, shadows lingering; a PC's familiar returns, speaking backwards and knowing too much; a city watches the moon vanish, half its population subtly rewritten.
