# The Burning Concord

*"We are not dragons. We are their shadow. We are not fire. We are its consequence."*

Not a faith in the traditional sense but a divine structure of flame, caste, and obedience into which every Dragonborn is branded at birth. Where others worship, the Concord commands. See [[Dragonborn (Lineage)]] for the matching lineage page.

## Origin myth

As the Weave began to bind even the gods, **Soltharion**, the Sun Tyrant, refused to be unraveled and poured the remnants of his divine fire directly into the Pattern — not to create mortals gently, but to forge "a lineage that would burn with purpose." **Xethar**, the Wildfire, an uncontrolled force of destruction without doctrine, joined the crucible not as rival but as counterbalance — instinct to Soltharion's order. From their collision, the first Dragonborn were **branded into existence**, not born: "You will be the foundation. You will be the fire that never falters."

## The Five Embers of Truth (core tenets)

The Flame Creates, Not Chance (caste is divine designation, not birthright to question); The Flame Decides, Not Desire (ambition and love are distractions — only the Embermarked interpret the flame's will); The Flame Purifies, Not Pity (failure is cleansed by fire, not forgiven); The Flame Orders, Not Ambition (function is rewarded, not personal greatness); The Flame Endures, Not the Flesh (legacy lives in obedience fulfilled, not in name or body).

## The Nine Flames (mandatory caste system)

Every hatchling is caste-branded within days of birth by a flame-glyph reading, from **Sovereign Flame** (the Sultan, forged through the Trial of the Crownfire) down through **Embermarked** (the priesthood), **Scaled Fang** (warrior nobility), **Furnacebound** (artisans/engineers), **Ashen Scale** (soldiers), **Cinderkin** (laborers), **Coinbrand** (merchants, tolerated but distrusted), **Smokehands** (the "dirty" caste — butchers, gravekeepers, sanitation), to **Ashborn** (the casteless, cut from the Flame entirely, watched, purged, or enslaved). Social mobility exists only through divinely-witnessed Flame Trials or irreversible Sultan's Decree.

## The Embermarked priesthood

The true power behind the throne — more feared than the Sultan himself. Organized into the High Ember (supreme voice of flame law, can override the Sultan on spiritual matters), the Circle of Seared Law (nine priests overseeing societal domains from unions to death), the Flamebranders (approve every mating union for genetic and ideological "purity"), the Ashseers (inquisitors who determine every hatchling's caste and monitor for "Smoke-Tainted" deviation), the Firebinders (ritual enforcers), and the Burnt-Scribes (record every sanctioned life in the Scrolls of the Forged Line — removal from the registry is spiritual execution).

## Enforcement

Violation of the Doctrine of Burning Truth (the unalterable Five-Tablet scripture governing birth, obedience, cleansing, union, and renewal) is punished by Ash Rite (ritual incineration), Caste Erasure, or Smokebrand. Even the Sultan is not exempt — a secret rite, the Emberveil, embeds an Ash Clause allowing the Embermarked to depose a Sultan who betrays the Concord.

## Relationship to other faiths

Officially declared a "divergent relic" by [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]], tolerated only in ruins and historical enclaves — Threadbearers do not recognize Embermarked authority. Among the pantheons: Fire once honored Soltharion's vision but the Concord now stands apart even from that; Earth (Gaeldros) acknowledges its structure but enforces boundaries; Spirit (Aelys, Nyzara) rejects its denial of memory and free choice outright; the Ashborn caste specifically shows recurring signs of Eldritch corruption, a standing concern for outside observers.
