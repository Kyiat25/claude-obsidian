# Bounty Hunter — Rank 1-4 (New, Grim Hollow-inspired)

*"You track down criminals, monsters, and fugitives for coin."* Tier 3 (Grim Hollow-inspired dark fantasy). The natural counterpart to [[Criminal]] and [[Wanted Score]] from the other side of the law — no explicit cross-reference exists in the source, but a Bounty Hunter character mechanically pursuing a [[Wanted Score]]-flagged target is an obvious table interaction.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Tracker | Hunter's Mark (feature) | Bring in a bounty that's eluded others, or capture a dangerous criminal alive |
| 2 | Licensed Hunter | Professional Tracker | Capture a high-value target, or solve a case that's baffled authorities |
| 3 | Renowned Hunter | Never Escape | Capture a notorious criminal, or bring in a supernatural creature alive |
| 4 | Master Hunter | Apex Predator | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Tracker

**Skill Proficiency**: Investigation, Survival. **Tool Proficiency**: one set of artisan's tools related to tracking (cartographer's tools, leatherworker's tools, etc.). **Equipment**: manacles, a wanted poster, a personal hunter's-mark symbol, traveler's clothes, 10 gp.

**Feature — Hunter's Mark** (background feature, distinct from any class ability of the same name): advantage on Wisdom (Survival) checks to track humanoids; advantage on Intelligence checks to gather information about a specific target; you know how to access bounty boards and contract postings.

**Holdings**: a contact who posts bounties; a small collection of wanted posters; a relationship with the local constabulary (they tolerate you).

## Rank 2: Licensed Hunter

**Holdings**: a license proving you're a legitimate bounty hunter; income from regular bounties (20 gp/month average); a network of 5-8 informants; access to guard stations and jails (to deliver bounties); better bounty rates (10% more than standard).

**Feature Expansion — Professional Tracker**: you can determine how long ago someone passed (up to 1 week); you know common hiding places and escape routes in cities; you can identify if someone is using a false identity (DC 15 Insight check).

## Rank 3: Renowned Hunter

**Holdings**: multiple simultaneous contracts; major bounty income (80 gp/month); a network of 15-20 informants across the region; access to restricted bounty postings (high-value targets); contacts with nobles who hire you privately.

**Feature Expansion — Never Escape**: once you've studied a target (1 hour examining evidence), gain advantage on all checks to find them; you can requisition aid from local authorities; you know if the target has left the region within 48 hours.

## Rank 4: Master Hunter

**Holdings**: fame across the continent; excellent income from high-value contracts (300 gp/month); a network of 40+ informants and contacts; a private investigator's office; influence with law enforcement organizations.

**Feature — Apex Predator**: you can track anything, supernatural or otherwise; once per month, call in favors from authorities (magical tracking, scrying, military aid); you automatically know if someone is lying about your quarry; you can negotiate releases or pardons for captured targets, if you choose. You can also charge 1,000+ gp for difficult bounties, some criminals surrender upon hearing you're after them, and your name is used to frighten criminals ("The Hunter's coming for you").
