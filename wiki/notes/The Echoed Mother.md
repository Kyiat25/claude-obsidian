# The Echoed Mother, She Who Returns Them

*"I do not give back what was taken. Nothing can. I give the thread another weaving — and if you loved the first cloth, you will grieve even as you rejoice. That, too, is my gift."*

**Domains**: rebirth, the rewoven thread, return-never-quite-the-same. **Symbol**: a thread emerging from cloth, dyed a slightly different shade than it entered. **Worshiped by**: reincarnation-faiths, the bereaved who watch children for familiar gestures. **Allies**: [[Nyzara]] (what the thread remembers), [[Restbringer]] (who delivers to her door), [[Aelys]] (see the conflation, below).

The final office of the five-part death-cycle — what Varethis cuts and Restbringer carries, the Echoed Mother weaves back in, never restored, only *rewoven*. Her doctrine's whole weight sits in that qualifier: return is real, but sameness is the lie. Grief and hope are, in her theology, the same emotion viewed from its two ends. Mortals persistently conflate her with **Aelys**, the Weave-Keeper — two memory-goddesses, one name-shaped confusion the Weave itself never finished sorting out.

Southern reincarnation-faiths show a newborn three belongings of the family's dead; a grasp toward any is recorded without conclusion in the **book of echoes** — drawing conclusions is forbidden, since she returns threads, not people. Midwife-priestesses dye one thread of every swaddling cloth off-shade, so the child knows before it knows anything that "different" is not "wrong."
