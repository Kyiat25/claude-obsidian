# Pirate — Rank 1-4 (Seaborne Raider)

*"You take what you want on the high seas, living by your own law."* Tier 2 (Specialized). Fulfills the "Pirate" slot [[FEARS Advanced Backgrounds]] flagged as not-yet-developed after Batch 1. The outlaw counterpart to [[Ship Captain]] — a character progressing this track could plausibly become the legitimate-vs-illegitimate version of the exact same career.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Deck Hand | Sea Raider | Distinguish yourself in a raid, save the ship in crisis, or earn the captain's trust |
| 2 | Pirate Officer | Sea Wolf | Lead a successful major raid, survive a naval battle against warships, or take a fortune-worth prize |
| 3 | Pirate Captain | Terror of the Seas | Build a pirate fleet, take a legendary prize, or establish your own pirate haven |
| 4 | Pirate Lord | King/Queen of Pirates | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Deck Hand

**Skill Proficiency**: Athletics, Intimidation. **Tool Proficiency**: navigator's tools or water vehicles. **Equipment**: a cutlass or boarding axe, 50 ft of hemp rope, a lucky charm, common clothes, 10 gp.

**Feature — Sea Raider**: identify pirate vessels and flags; know pirate havens and free ports; understand pirate codes and customs; fence stolen goods at pirate ports (50% of value).

**Specialty** (choose your style): Corsair (sanctioned by nation), Freebooter (independent), Privateer (licensed raider), Smuggler (illegal cargo), Slaver (dark trade), Treasure hunter.

**Holdings**: a hammock on the ship; a share of booty; a crew who (mostly) respect you.

## Rank 2: Pirate Officer

**Holdings**: a private cabin; a larger booty share (equivalent to 30 gp/month average); authority over a section of the crew (10-15 pirates); quality weapons and equipment; a reputation in pirate havens; contacts in 3-4 pirate ports.

**Feature Expansion — Sea Wolf**: advantage on Intimidation checks against merchant crews; identify valuable cargo by sight; know the best trade routes to raid; your crew provides backup in port fights; you can arrange for ships to be attacked or protected.

## Rank 3: Pirate Captain

**Holdings**: your own pirate ship (fast and heavily armed); a crew of 40-80 pirates (use Bandit or Thug statistics); a major booty share (100+ gp/month average); a safe haven in a pirate port (your own dock/warehouse); contacts across pirate networks; a reputation that makes merchants flee; a flag/personal colors feared at sea.

**Feature Expansion — Terror of the Seas**: merchant ships often surrender without a fight when they see your flag; organize raids with multiple ships; once per week, fence a major haul for 60% value through your network; arrange "accidents" for rivals; pirate captains seek alliance with or avoid your waters.

## Rank 4: Pirate Lord

**Holdings**: a fleet of 5-10 ships; a pirate haven under your control (a fortified port); massive piracy wealth (400+ gp/month); a crew of 300+ pirates across your fleet; political influence (some nations secretly negotiate with you); your own code of laws in your haven; a network spanning multiple seas.

**Feature — King/Queen of Pirates**: organize massive raids or blockades; once per month, affect maritime trade across entire regions; navies think twice before engaging your fleet; some nations pay you tribute to leave their ships alone; your haven is a nation unto itself. Legendary treasures are associated with you, nations debate whether to fight or negotiate, other pirates swear loyalty or stay far away, and your flag may continue to sail under a successor after death.
