# Tiefling

*"We are not curses. We are the correction."*

**Origin myth**: per the Ashen Testament, Tieflings are not infernal-blooded but born of divine accident — when the gods cast their voices across the Weave, one glyph was left unfinished; an Eldritch scream shattered the breath, and the broken glyph struck a dying, nameless child, who rose in ash and named herself **Sareth-Ka**, "the scar the gods could not finish." Tieflings are untranslated glyphs — divine messages broken in transmission — not damned, but proof gods make errors that can choose their own meaning.

**Physical**: matures ~18, lives 100–120 years; Medium, 5–6½ ft., 30 ft. speed; variable skin tone (including red/purple/blue/ashen), horns, tail, solid-color pupil-less eyes.

## Traits

- **Darkvision** 60 ft.
- **Hellish Resistance**: resistance to fire damage
- **Untranslated Glyph**: knows *Thaumaturgy* cantrip, always flame-tinged
- **Defiant Legacy**: 1 skill of choice; reroll a failed save vs. frightened/charmed (uses = proficiency bonus/long rest)
- **Infernal Legacy**: subrace-dependent innate spells (CHA)
- **Ash-Marked**: 1/long rest, absorb fire damage instead of taking it, gain temp HP = half the damage
- **Languages**: Common + Infernal or Abyssal (choice)

**Mental**: Focus +0 / Sanity +0 — stability comes from self-definition, not biology.

**DCS Integration**: Dodge +1 / Endure +0 / Deflect +0; DR +0. Advantage on Rally when fighting those who judged/persecuted you.

## Corruption: The Proving Wrong

Corruption enters through accepting others' imposed definitions or surrendering agency to power. At Corruption 10+: Definition-Loss, Judgment-Weight, Power-Hunger. **Broken Testament table** (1d6): False Destiny, Ash-Blindness (Ash-Marked disabled), Glyph Fracture (1d4 psychic + lose a skill), Weight of Prophecy (Sanity save or −1d4), Eldritch Whisper (+1 Whisper Point, Defiant Legacy auto-fails), or Testament Holds (Corruption −1).

## Cultural touchstones

**The Five Burnings**: burn away false judgment, burn bright with choice, burn memory into ash, burn your own path, burn until you matter. Spiritual roles include Ashbinders (clerics carrying names of the forgotten dead), Sin-Scribes, Ash-Walkers, and Flame-Speakers. Deep kinship with [[Aasimar]] — both marked by supernatural heritage and judged for nature rather than choice.
