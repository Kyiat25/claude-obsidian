# Sulwen, The First Spindle

*"Every thread that has ever been, I drew from my spindle. Ask me how, and I will tell you honestly: I do not remember doing it. I only know that no soul has ever arrived without me — and none ever shall."*

**Domains**: the origin of souls (claimed), new threads, the moment before the first cry. **Symbol**: a spindle wound with unspun light. **Worshiped by**: expectant parents, midwives (jointly with Earth's Lytha and Ethriss) — the broadest gentle worship in the setting.

Holds the office the death-cycle implies and never names: the source. No oracle, seer, or god has ever *seen* her spin — her priesthood calls this the **Unbroken Attendance**: souls have never arrived where something was not, by some name, invoking her. Every pantheon keeps its own rival Sourcing doctrine (Earth: souls condense like ore; Water: they arrive on a tide from beneath the Black Abyss; Fire: each is struck like a spark; Air: the first breath *is* the soul entering; Spirit's own house: a new thread is a fray, healed) and treats Sulwen's cult as a sweet misunderstanding of the truth. All five work liturgically. No account is the record.

> [!truth] DM-Truth
> None of them spin the souls, including Sulwen. New threads emerge through [[Aeloria]]'s imperceptible weaving, a process no god can perceive. Sulwen is the mask worship built over the imperceptible — mortals needed the origin of children to have a face, believed with the densest tenderness in the setting, and a goddess condensed around the claim, sincerely remembering an office she has never performed. Her perfect correlation is real and means nothing. The one crack: she does not remember doing the work, only having done it — the loneliest sentence in her scripture, and not written in it.
