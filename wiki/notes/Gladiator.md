# Gladiator — Rank 1-4 (Arena Champion)

*"You fight for glory in the arena, where skill and showmanship matter equally."* Tier 2 (Specialized). Explicitly described in-source as "different from [[Pit Fighter]]" — Pit Fighter is Grim Hollow-inspired underground/illegal fighting (Tier 3, dark fantasy), while Gladiator is legitimate, spectacle-driven arena combat with public fame rather than criminal-underworld framing. Fulfills the "Gladiator" slot [[FEARS Advanced Backgrounds]] flagged as not-yet-developed after Batch 1.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Novice Fighter | Arena Presence | Win your first major bout, develop a signature move/persona, or defeat a veteran fighter |
| 2 | Proven Gladiator | Crowd Favorite | Win a championship tournament, defeat a famous opponent, or develop a legendary rivalry |
| 3 | Arena Champion | Legendary Combatant | Defeat an undefeated champion, fight in the capital's Grand Arena, or become the most famous gladiator of your generation |
| 4 | Arena Legend | Living Legend | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Novice Fighter

**Skill Proficiency**: Athletics, Performance. **Tool Proficiency**: disguise kit, for arena personas. **Equipment**: an arena weapon of choice, costume/armor for arena fighting, a symbol of your stable, 10 gp.

**Feature — Arena Presence**: know how to work a crowd (gain advantage or impose disadvantage on enemies through showmanship); use Performance instead of Intimidation in the arena; free training and equipment from your arena stable; recognition in arena districts.

**Fighting Style** (choose or roll d8): Beast fighter, Chariot racer, Duelist, Mounted combatant, Net fighter (retiarius), Team fighter, Unarmed brawler, Weapon specialist.

**Holdings**: a bunk in the gladiator barracks; arena stable membership; training partners; a small fanbase (10-20 people).

## Rank 2: Proven Gladiator

**Holdings**: a private room in better quarters; income from fights and prizes (25 gp/month); a growing fanbase (100+ fans); quality weapons and armor; training from veteran fighters; invitations to private matches.

**Feature Expansion — Crowd Favorite**: earn 50-100 gp per major fight; advantage on Performance checks in the arena; fans provide gifts and information; your fights draw larger crowds; you can challenge or be challenged by other gladiators.

## Rank 3: Arena Champion

**Holdings**: luxury quarters or your own residence; major income from fights, endorsements, and prizes (120 gp/month); a massive fanbase (1,000+); a signature weapon and armor, possibly magical; a retinue of trainers and handlers; sponsorships from wealthy patrons.

**Feature Expansion — Legendary Combatant**: demand 500-1,000 gp per fight; your matches are major events (nobility attends); once per week, invoke your fame for advantage on Charisma checks; mentor other gladiators; your techniques are studied and copied.

## Rank 4: Arena Legend

**Holdings**: a mansion or estate; massive income (400 gp/month); your own gladiatorial school or stable; thousands of devoted fans; political influence through popularity; your life story is performed as entertainment.

**Feature — Living Legend**: demand any price for appearances; rulers invite you to fight in their arenas; your endorsement is worth more than most people earn in a lifetime; once per month, organize a fight that becomes a cultural event affecting public opinion; you can retire wealthy or continue fighting on your terms. Statues of you stand in major arenas, your signature moves are named after you, gladiatorial schools teach your techniques, some fans worship you like a god, and your autobiography becomes a bestseller.
