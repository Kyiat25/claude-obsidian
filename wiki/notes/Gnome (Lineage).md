# Gnome

*"They told us we were too small to matter. So we invented fireworks, wrote treatises on the nature of laughter, and discovered seventeen ways to make cheese sentient. Then we asked: 'What were you saying?'"*

**Origin myth**: per [[The Wonder-Tithe Codex]], gnomes are not creations but *iterative improvements* — when the gods finished the world and declared "this will do," gnomes appeared and asked "but have you considered...?" The universe is a first draft; curiosity is sacred obligation. Every gnome owes a tithe: seven wonders witnessed, seven questions answered, seven impossibilities proven wrong.

**Physical**: mature by 40, live 350–500 years; Small, 3–4 ft., 25 ft. speed, vibrant unusual hair colors.

## Traits

- **Darkvision** 60 ft.
- **Gnome Cunning**: advantage on INT/WIS saves vs. magic
- **Small Frame**: counts one size smaller for carrying capacity
- **Artificer's Insight**: Arcana/Investigation/tool proficiency; double proficiency bonus understanding magical/mechanical devices
- **Tinker** (variant feature): craft up to 3 Tiny clockwork devices (Clockwork Toy/Fire Starter/Music Box)
- **Languages**: Common + Gnomish + one extra

**Mental**: Focus +1 (hyperfocus, but easily distracted by new problems) / Sanity +0.

**DCS Integration**: Dodge +2 / Endure −1 / Deflect +1; DR +0. Advantage on Rally after observing an enemy's pattern 3+ times; Breaking Point triggered more by unanswered questions than casualties.

## Corruption: The Static Mind

Corruption enters through obsession, forbidden knowledge, or experiments gone wrong. At Corruption 10+: Fractured Wonder (inventions incorporate Eldritch elements unconsciously), Question-Weight, Curiosity Curse (drawn to study corrupted items). **Tainted Innovation table** (1d6): False Discovery, Wonder-Static (Gnome Cunning disabled), Schematic Corruption (1d4 psychic + lose a tool proficiency), Weight of Knowing (Sanity save or −1d4), Eldritch Inspiration (+1 Whisper Point, next INT check auto-fails), or Wonder Holds (Corruption −1).
