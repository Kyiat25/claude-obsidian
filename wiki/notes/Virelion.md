# Virelion — the Nine Valleys of the Condottieri-Princes

A Knight Order Confederation of nine independent fortress-city "Vales" (population ~1.4 million) in the highland frontier of [[Elarion]], run on a Renaissance-city-state model. Not "civilized people who fight" nor "barbarians who read" but **warriors for whom warfare and wisdom are inseparable** — the defining image is a frontier outrider quoting Stoic philosophy while skinning game and watching for undead raiders. Guiding philosophy: *"We think, then we act. Reality sorts the unprepared,"* contrasted explicitly against [[Corvessa]] ("We pray, then we charge — God sorts the worthy") and [[Talanthar]] ("We listen, then we strike — the forest sorts the arrogant"). Economy is built on mercenary contracting and specialized battle-magic.

## Radical meritocracy

Virelion doesn't care about ancestry, only capability, which has made it the most racially diverse power on the continent: 28% Human, 22% Orc/Half-Orc (many freed slaves or genocide refugees, disproportionately the heaviest shock troops and fiercely loyal to the one society that judges them on deeds), 15% Goliath (a 75-year influx of natural mountain-warfare specialists), 12% Elf/Half-Elf, 10% Dwarf, 9% Halfling (elite light-cavalry raiders from the "Emerald Scatter Wildhalflings," treated with a respect rare elsewhere), 2.5% Gnome, 1% Dragonborn exiles (fleeing rigid caste systems elsewhere — intensely loyal once accepted), and a small remainder of Tieflings, Genasi, and other refugees.

## The Nine Fortress-Cities

Val Karren ("the Iron Vale"), Val Auros ("the Golden Vale"), Val Drake ("the Beast Vale"), Val Corvinus ("the Scholar's Vale"), Val Stern ("the Stone Vale"), Val Falken ("the Swift Vale"), Val Medici ("the Merchant Vale"), Val Blackwood ("the Shadow Vale"), and Val Argent ("the Silver Vale") — each an independent city-state under its own condottieri-prince, unified only loosely.

## Val Karren, "the Iron Vale," in more detail

House Karren ("the Ironbound," ~200,000) rules from **Eisentor** ("Iron Gate"), a seven-level fortress carved directly into a mountain face. The **Seventeen Halls** are memorial galleries to each undead incursion the Vale has repelled, each carrying weapons, tactical maps annotated with what went wrong, portraits of the fallen with their final words, and a lesson carved in marble. The **Academy of Steel**, the Vale's premier military university, requires Philosophy of Warfare and Ethics of Command alongside siege mathematics — its 40,000-volume library is half military history, half classical philosophy, and its students debate Marcus Aurelius between pike-formation drills. Every forged blade in the **Forge Quarter** is signed and dated by its maker; a flawed weapon is treated as dishonor.

## Diplomacy: pragmatism that infuriates Corvessa

Virelion respects Talanthar's tactically brilliant asymmetric warfare and Kel Varyan's accumulated statecraft and magical scholarship, trading and studying with both — "New Money with Old World taste" in the Imperial assessment. This ideological flexibility drives Corvessa's crusaders to distraction, since Virelion refuses to treat either the Empire or the "forest-heretics" as an enemy: as the Virelion saying goes, "ideology is expensive, and Virelion can't afford it."
