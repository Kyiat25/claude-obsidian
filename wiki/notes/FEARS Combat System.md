# FEARS Combat System

Replaces 5E's Armor Class with an **Evasion + Damage Reduction** model, described as a "philosophical shift in how combat flows" rather than a pure numeric substitution.

## Components

- **Active Defense** — players choose each hit as evade-or-take, trading safety for tactical advantage. [[FEARS Character Framework]] confirms taking a hit unlocks a **free Reaction** (counterattack, disarm, bash, or block) that varies by class.
- **Counterattack Opportunities** — deliberately taking damage can open a window for a stronger response (see Reactions above).
- **Damage Reduction (DR)** — armor/abilities reduce incoming damage rather than negating hits outright. Tracked **per body part** (head/torso/arms/legs), not as one armor number. Pairs with **[[Called Shot]]**, which targets a specific body part for tactical effect.
- **Momentum** — a combat-advantage tracker ranging **-2 to +3**, shifting with every strike/dodge, modifying attack and evasion rolls and able to grant advantage/disadvantage.

## Formula (from FEARS Character Framework)

- **Evasion (EV) roll**: `1d20 + DEX + PROF` — replaces the AC check; this is the first concrete dice mechanic given for the system.
- Conversion note: an existing 5E character tracks Evasion instead of AC using the same roll.
