# Sailor — Rank 1-4

*"Ships that carry you don't sink. Ask anyone who's sailed with you — they'll tell you why they're still alive to say so."*

> [!note] Original content — no raw source
> Flagged after Batch 1 as "adaptable but not yet written," Sailor never got an expanded treatment across any of the 3 source batches. Written here from scratch, deliberately structured around a different axis than its nearest cousins: [[Ship Captain]] progresses through **ownership and command** (bigger ships, bigger crews, a fleet); [[Fisher]] progresses through **commercial scale** (bigger catches, bigger contracts, a trade empire). Sailor progresses through neither — no vessel ever belongs to you, and there's no income table climbing toward wealth. Instead this background tracks **debts owed to you by people who survived something with you**, and a superstitious reputation among sailors that eventually becomes almost supernatural. You'll never be richer at Rank 4 than Rank 1 — you'll just never be turned away, and never truly alone at sea.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Survivor | A Debt at Sea | Survive a disaster that killed others aboard, and be the reason someone else survived it too |
| 2 | Marked by the Sea | Sailors Talk | Be the one steady presence during a second disaster, so the story starts spreading between ports |
| 3 | Ill-Luck's Exception | Ships Don't Sink With You Aboard | Survive something that should have killed the entire crew, with witnesses who'll swear to it in any port |
| 4 | The Unsinkable | Fortune Favors This Ship | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Survivor

**Skill Proficiency**: Athletics, Perception. **Tool Proficiency**: navigator's tools, water vehicles. **Equipment**: a belaying pin, a scrap of wreckage you kept as a memento, common clothes, 5 gp.

**Feature — A Debt at Sea**: one person is alive because of something you did during a maritime disaster (storm, wreck, attack, mutiny — your choice with the GM). They owe you their life, and they know it. You can call on that debt once, for nearly anything within their power to give.

**Holdings**: no permanent berth or savings — you go where ships need an extra hand; the one survivor who owes you; a scar or story that opens conversation in any dockside tavern.

## Rank 2: Marked by the Sea

**Holdings**: still no fixed berth or savings of note — sailors who've heard your story will vouch for you sight-unseen; 2-3 people across different ships who now owe you a debt from disasters since Rank 1; a growing reputation that precedes you into any port before you arrive.

**Feature Expansion — Sailors Talk**: sailors are deeply superstitious, and word travels fast — crews who've heard of you treat you as good luck, not just a hand; advantage on checks to be believed or trusted by sailors who don't yet know you personally; once per week, learn what's being said about you (and by extension, what's happening) in a port you haven't visited yet.

## Rank 3: Ill-Luck's Exception

**Holdings**: a name now spoken in at least a dozen ports without your ever having visited some of them; 5+ people across multiple crews and cities who owe you their lives; captains competing quietly to have you aboard for the worst voyages, since bad things seem to happen to *other* ships when you're not on theirs.

**Feature Expansion — Ships Don't Sink With You Aboard**: once per month, a crew you're sailing with gains advantage on saves against a single catastrophic event (storm, sea monster, curse) — sailors attribute it to your presence, not skill; you're never asked to pay for passage, food, or drink in a port where your story is known; you can sense when a voyage is about to go wrong, hours before anyone else notices.

## Rank 4: The Unsinkable

**Holdings**: still nothing you own — you've never needed to; more debts owed to you than you could ever call in, scattered across every sea you've sailed; captains who refuse to sail into genuine danger without you, even at the cost of delaying a voyage to find you first; a name whispered as a ward against disaster wherever sailors gather.

**Feature — Fortune Favors This Ship**: once per voyage, a ship you're aboard automatically survives one disaster that would otherwise sink it — no roll, just survival, at a cost or complication the GM determines; every debt anyone has ever owed you is honored instantly and without question, anywhere on the water; sailors who've never met you still refuse to leave port if you're not accounted for on a dangerous run. Songs about you are sung in ports you've never set foot in, ships that claim you once sailed with them command higher fares, and some sailors swear you can't drown even if you tried.
