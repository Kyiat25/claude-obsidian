# Pattern Keeper — Rank 1-4

*"You maintain the Faith of the Bound Pattern, preserving the divine weave that once bound reality."* Batch 3, FEARS-Specific category. The first background to give the **"Faith of the Bound Pattern"** — first named only in passing in [[FEARS Advanced Backgrounds]] Batch 2's setting-integration notes — any concrete mechanical or theological detail. Confirms the gods are dead (matching [[Acolyte]]'s "though the gods are dead" line) and reframes the Pattern as the actual object of religious maintenance: a "divine weave that once bound reality," damaged sites of which can be studied, stabilized, and restored.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Novice Keeper | Pattern Sight | Stabilize a damaged Pattern site, learn ancient Pattern lore, or perceive a major Pattern disruption |
| 2 | Keeper of the Weave | Pattern Weaver | Prevent a Pattern catastrophe, recover lost Pattern knowledge, or successfully weave a complex stabilization |
| 3 | Master Keeper | Pattern Master | Restore a major Pattern site, prevent a reality tear, or discover a forgotten aspect of the Pattern |
| 4 | Archkeeper of the Pattern | Master of Divine Weave | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Novice Keeper

**Skill Proficiency**: Insight, Religion. **Tool Proficiency**: weaver's tools. **Language**: one exotic language. **Equipment**: weaver's tools, a pattern-design holy symbol, a book of Pattern prayers, pattern-embroidered robes, 10 gp.

**Feature — Pattern Sight**: sense locations where the Pattern is strong or damaged; advantage on Religion checks related to the Bound Pattern; identify Thread types and Glyphs; know rituals to stabilize weak Pattern points; understand the relationship between gods and the Pattern.

**Holdings**: a cell in a Pattern temple; access to Pattern archives; a mentor keeper; you can study damaged Pattern sites.

## Rank 2: Keeper of the Weave

**Holdings**: private temple quarters; a stipend (20 gp/month); authority to perform Pattern maintenance rituals; access to restricted Pattern texts; you can visit [[Unstable Magic Zones|wild magic zones]] for study; a network of 5-8 fellow keepers.

**Feature Expansion — Pattern Weaver**: perform rituals temporarily stabilizing wild magic zones (1 hour); advantage on saves in wild or dead magic zones; sense disturbances in the Pattern (someone using powerful magic nearby); once per week, smooth disrupted magic (disadvantage on one wild magic surge within 60 ft); identify areas where gods died.

## Rank 3: Master Keeper

**Holdings**: a wing of a Pattern temple; a good stipend (70 gp/month); authority over Pattern sites in the region; 5-8 junior keepers under you; access to the deepest Pattern archives; you perform major rituals; consulted on Thread and Glyph matters.

**Feature Expansion — Pattern Master**: permanently stabilize small wild magic zones; sense major Pattern events across the continent; once per month, perform a ritual affecting local magic (suppress or enhance); advantage on all checks related to Threads, Glyphs, or the Pattern; identify how a god died by examining the Pattern.

## Rank 4: Archkeeper of the Pattern

**Holdings**: a lead Pattern temple or archive; an excellent stipend (150 gp/month); authority over all Pattern sites in the nation; 30+ keepers in your order; access to Pattern secrets only archkeepers know; you perform legendary rituals; consulted by rulers on reality itself.

**Feature — Master of Divine Weave**: permanently stabilize medium wild magic zones via a week-long ritual; sense Pattern disturbances across the entire world; once per year, temporarily restore a small Pattern portion to the godly era (pre-death conditions, 1 day); see threads of fate and destiny connecting people; may be able to communicate with the Pattern itself. Wizards seek your knowledge of magic's foundation, rulers fear your power over reality, and you may know why the gods died — some say you can reweave the Pattern, forbidden knowledge.
