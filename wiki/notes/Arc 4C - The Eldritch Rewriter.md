# Arc 4C — The Eldritch Rewriter

The full deep-dive (Revised Edition v2.0) behind [[Campaign Arcs (The Great Deceivers and Truth-Seekers)|Category 4: The Rewriters]]'s third arc. *"What if the comfortable lie is genuinely better than the incomprehensible truth?"*

## Thalanis, the Void-Scholar

A brilliant wizard who believes he has discovered how to "upgrade" mortal consciousness to perceive reality the way the Eldritch gods do — three dimensions and linear causality reframed as mere "bad design," not cosmic law. His converted "Adapted" followers report perfect happiness and expanded awareness.

**The actual horror**: Thalanis is infected with corruption from **[[Zhurr'Kael]]**, the Eldritch God of paradox and self-consuming logic — and cannot perceive this, because the corruption has compromised the exact cognitive tools he'd need to evaluate himself. What he calls mathematics are Zhurr'Kael thought-loops ("one plus one is fire; two plus two is hunger"): logic eating itself, not genuine insight. His followers' "happiness" is a thought-loop masquerading as enlightenment, not real transcendence.

## The dilemma

The arc is built to be unresolvable by design: players cannot fully evaluate a transformation whose entire premise is that mortal cognition is inadequate to judge it. Stopping Thalanis may deny thousands a genuine (if incomprehensible) improvement; letting him continue risks catastrophic, unbounded reality damage neither he nor the players can predict. Central tensions the source names explicitly: evaluation vs. action, consent vs. protection, preservation vs. transcendence, and whether stopping visibly happy people makes the party the villain.
