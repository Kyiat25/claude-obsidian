# Esh'Tarah, The Mirror That Screams

*"You think you are yourself. Let me show you the cracks."*

**Domain**: the self revealed as construction, easily deconstructed. **Symbol**: a cracked mirror with no reflection. **Truth**: you are not you — just a pattern that could be otherwise. **Mythic tie**: [[The Null Reflection]] itself may be Esh'Tarah's realm or body. **Fatal effect**: others come to see you as a stranger wearing your skin.

## How it bleeds into reality

- **Water Pantheon**: [[Water Pantheon|Vaelora]]'s sunken cities reflect different versions of themselves; ripples show alternate realities; the ocean becomes a hall of mirrors.
- **Air Pantheon**: [[Air Pantheon|Zephir]]'s illusions take on lives of their own; reflections disobey; movement happens before its source moves.

## Corruptions

- **Beasts**: stop reacting to their reflections, begin trusting mirror-selves more than instinct — mimicking reflection instead of nature.
- **Monsters**: reflect wrong (eyes on the back of heads, gestures reversed) — slay the reflection and the monster itself dies, its body following the mirror-image.
- **Mortals — Sundered Identity**: friends describe them differently; memories diverge between people; family insists they were always someone else; multiple contradictory identities simultaneously true.

## Preferred manifestation zones

Wild Magic Zones, Dead Magic Zones, anywhere with many mirrors, sites of identity magic.

## Warning signs

Reflections moving independently; people not recognizing each other; mirrors showing wrong images; identity becoming fluid.

## Campaign tiers

| Tier (Levels) | Scale |
|---|---|
| 1 (1-4) | Cursed mirror showing a false self |
| 2 (5-10) | A town where everyone's confused about identities |
| 3 (11-16) | Reflection invasion — mirror-selves replacing originals |
| 4 (17-20) | The concept of self itself begins to unravel |

## Role in the Hollow Thread

Esh'Tarah's specialization is identity theft: replacing guardians of [[The Hollow Thread]]'s seal with corrupted copies, so they continue their duties while unknowingly serving Eldritch goals — the explicit worst case is guardians opening the seal themselves, believing they're protecting it.
