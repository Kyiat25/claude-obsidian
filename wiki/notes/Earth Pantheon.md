# Earth Pantheon

*"Others burn, drown, soar, or weave. We hold. We endure. We remember. We wait."* — [[Thamur]], The Guardian Below

The primary defense against [[The Hollow Thread]], and (per [[Guide to the Eldritch Forces]]'s Pantheon Vulnerabilities) the **lowest**-vulnerability pantheon — stone resists unbeing best, though it can still be unmade.

## Cosmic role: The Lock That Holds the World

Earth is not merely stone, soil, and growth — beneath its crust lies something unknowable and ancient, [[The Nameless One Below]] (this cluster's own name for what [[The Hollow Thread]] calls the Hollow Vault). The Earth gods' great purpose is not dominion but **containment**. The [[Leylines]] are [[Vatharun]]'s bones; the Weave is anchored by them; if Earth fails, magic fails; if Earth breaks, the gods fall.

**The Myth of the First Stone**: before the gods shaped the world, before seas rose or sky was torn from earth, something else already was — not alive, not dead, simply *was*. While Fire raged, Water rose, Air howled, and Spirit wove, Earth remained still, because something waited beneath it. When the gods dug too deep and disturbed it, [[Vatharun]] chose to hold: raising mountains to weigh it down, splitting continents so no single force could reach it, driving the leylines deep to wrap it in unbreakable power. For ages the Lock held — until the leylines cracked (perhaps during Fire and Water's first war, perhaps when Spirit rewove fate once too often, perhaps when [[Vel'Zyren]] first tried to rewrite the Weave), and something beneath began to stir.

> [!note] The Nameless One Below and the Hollow Thread/Hollow Vault are the same referent
> This source's "Nameless One Below" and "the Lock" describe, from Earth's own in-world mythic register, exactly what [[The Hollow Thread]] documents in DM-facing mechanical/campaign terms: a corrupted leyline sealed by [[Vatharun]], [[Thamur]], [[Orivak]], and [[Gaeldros]] using the same four-phase ritual (folding mountains, erasing the name, binding runes, passing an unbreakable edict). Treated as one myth told in two registers, not two separate secrets.

## Minor spirits and demigods (roster only)

The source names dozens more spirits and demigods with only a one-line domain/role each (no individual myth or featured writeup) — catalogued here rather than as separate thin pages:

**Underdark spirits**: Ithanis (the Eyes in the Dark, watches Vel'Zyren's prison), Othrum (the Mouth Beneath, bottomless pits), Velkith (the Stalker in the Veins), Glysha (the Bloom That Devours, Underdark flora), Raknor (the Whispering Chasm, false paths).

**Ruins & wealth spirits**: Eldros (the Last Guardian, preserved ruins), Zerethis (the Whispering Gold, cursed hoards).

**Civilisation demigods**: Tyveris (the Architect of the Endless City), Naedra (the Silent Grove, untamed sacred land), Ethriss (the Pale Midwife, birth/death in childbirth), Lazrek (the Forager's Friend), Solmara (the Keeper of the Last Flame, opposes [[Karthas]]), Ruthan (the Architect's Hand) and Dreska (the Bone of the Earth) — co-builders of an unbreakable bridge, Ythra (the Alchemist's Daughter), Narveth (the Watcher on the Border), Rashka (the Bone of the Earth, mining).

**Fire-within-earth spirits**: Yvrak (the Mountain's Heart, magma), Krymora (the Veins of the World, lava flow), Torvek (the Maker's Ember).

**Underground water spirits**: Velmira (the Black Current), Rylka (the Wellspring That Whispers), Dronis (the Keeper of the Dry).

**Echo & voice spirits**: Erynth (the Voice That Lingers), Vakros (the Silent Song), Thryss (the Cry That Will Not End).

**Poisoned water spirits**: Nyxora (the Tainted Spring), Ulveth (the River That Forgets), Rathis (the Weeping Stone).

**Living stone spirits**: Grythas (the Stone That Remembers), Borith (the Shattered Soul).

**Path spirits**: Vekros (the Road That Remembers).

**Birth/time twin spirits**: Lytha (the First Breath) and Veyrith (the Child That Ages Backward) — a mother bore two sons, one born dead and mourned, one who aged in reverse until he vanished as she died of old age.

**Newly ingested, with full dedicated pages (2026-07-14, from a fuller draft source)**: [[Frith]] (peace as a deliberately small condition), [[Tovna]] (herds, flocks, and the herd-bond), [[Cairn]] (spirit of the dry roads and waystones), and [[Halix]] (salt — a rare *deliberately* shared spirit jointly held with [[Water Pantheon]]).

## The Nameless One Below

Not a god, not a spirit — something older, unnamed even by the gods, who do not act because it does not need to; it is already winning. Myth: a god once tried to see what lay beneath the deepest caverns, and when he returned he no longer knew his own name. Belief: it was here before the gods and will be here after them. Signs: leylines cracking, standing stones humming without reason, echoes speaking in unknown tongues.

## The Fractured Pillar

The only recorded instance of the Earth Pantheon acting in outright divine aggression: during the First Age, [[Vel'Zyren]] sank her threads too deep into stone-carved oaths, claiming law must bend to fate. [[Gaeldros]] issued the Edict Unbound in response; [[Vatharun]] moved for the second time in the world's history; [[Thamur]] opened the Silent Gate at [[The Hollow Thread|the Hollow Vault]] to show Vel'Zyren what she risked. The combined effort cracked the Pillar of Gaeldros itself — divine oaths briefly lost their bindings, prophecies collapsed, law became uncertain. It was not Vel'Zyren who broke the world in that moment; it was Earth's own refusal to yield. The Pillar was reforged afterward, but one crack was deliberately left unsealed as a reminder that even stone can fail.

## Strategy against the Eldritch

Physical containment, sealing and forgetting, and killing those who know too much — extreme, but per [[Guide to the Eldritch Forces]], effective.

## Perspective on godhood: "We are the foundation that holds"

Earth understands godhood as burden, not prize — [[Vatharun]]: "I did not choose the mountain. The mountain chose me." They believe Divine Sparks are obligations that bind gods to maintain cosmic order, and that their role as "the pantheon that holds reality together" is sacred duty. They don't know their sealing of [[Vel'Zyren]] and [[The Hollow Thread|the Hollow Vault]] created new cosmic problems, that World Trees predate them, or that their entire identity unknowingly serves [[Aeloria]]'s corrections rather than their own divine mandate. Full comparison across all five pantheons plus the Eldritch and Vel'Zyren on [[The Lie of Godhood]].
