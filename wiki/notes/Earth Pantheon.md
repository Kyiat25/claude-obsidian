# Earth Pantheon

The primary defense against [[The Hollow Thread]], and (per [[Guide to the Eldritch Forces]]'s Pantheon Vulnerabilities) the **lowest**-vulnerability pantheon — stone resists unbeing best, though it can still be unmade.

## Named deities

**Vatharun, the Stone-Father** — the First Mountain, Earth's foundation god; his bones are literally the [[Leylines]], such that harming one fractures reality itself. Named as the deity who **planted the World Trees** in [[World Trees]]'s standing contradiction. Physically sealed [[The Hollow Thread]]'s vault by folding mountains over it — moving for only the second time in recorded history — and swore never to move again; if forced to move by a cosmic threat, the seal weakens.

**Thamur, the Guardian Below** — chief architect of the Hollow Thread's seal. Erased the vault's true location from reality itself — not hidden, *removed*, from written records, spoken language, thought, memory, divine knowledge, and the Weave's own structure. Kills anyone who gets too close to the truth, not from cruelty but necessity; must keep forgetting the location himself to keep the seal strong.

**Gaeldros, the Stone of Judgment** — passes divine sentence on the Hollow Thread ("this leyline shall not be destroyed... it must be endured, and forgotten"). Issued the Edict Unbound, a law with no precedent, binding even gods; also passes judgment on those who abuse or trespass through unanchored [[Astral Conduits]]. His judgments can be undermined by those who don't recognize his authority, such as [[Vel'Zyren]].

**Orivak, the Rune-Bound Sentinel** — maintains the quarantine network around the Hollow Vault, binding nearby leylines with anti-collapse runes that reinforce the identity of surrounding leylines rather than stopping magic outright. Cannot enter the deepest seal layers without risking corruption himself.

## Strategy against the Eldritch

Physical containment, sealing and forgetting, and killing those who know too much — extreme, but per the source, effective.
