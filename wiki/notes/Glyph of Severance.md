# Glyph of Severance

Also called **The Unmaker's Mark** or the **Glyph of Unbeing** — a Glyph erased from existence, distinct from and far more dangerous than [[The Whispering Dark]]'s ordinary Glyph of Endings.

## Two names, one concept

[[Weave and Magic]] introduces it as the **Glyph of Severance**: unlike the Glyph of Endings (which ensures the ordinary cycle of death and rebirth), Severance removes things *entirely* from reality. It was so feared that even the gods erased it, though whispers persist that a fragment still remains somewhere beyond time.

[[Guide to the Eldritch Forces]] independently describes a **Glyph of Unbeing**: a glyph that is not written but *removed*, existing only through what it disallows — space, memory, time. It unthreads magic retroactively, erasing spells before they're cast. The gods themselves debate whether it was born from [[The Whispering Dark]] or is sentient in its own right.

These are treated here as the same concept under two source-specific names rather than two separate Glyphs, given the identical function (total erasure beyond the ordinary Endings cycle) and identical origin ambiguity.

## Where it surfaces

- **[[The Hollow Thread]]** is described as the direct physical consequence of this Glyph's removal — the leyline punched through when [[The Whispering Dark]] consumed a Glyph too contradictory to survive.
- **[[Shattered Moon]]** is the memory of the moment the Glyph of Severance first touched the sky, marking the first leyline implosion.
- Royalty erased by this Glyph is the mythic root of the [[Yellow Crown]] Eldritch Moon.
