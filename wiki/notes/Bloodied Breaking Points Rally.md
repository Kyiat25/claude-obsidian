# Bloodied Breaking Points Rally

The enemy-morale layer of combat. Confirms directly what [[Combat Flow]] already stated about its own 50%-HP sequencing note: Bloodied effects trigger after the Injury roll and apply to **enemies and monsters only**, never player characters. This document is the full elaboration of that one sentence.

## Three pillars

1. **[[Bloodied and Bruised]]** — at 50% HP, an enemy gains all four of Frailty/Strength/Ability/Death Throes, and an enemy leader (or highest-CR enemy) must attempt an automatic Rally check.
2. **[[Breaking Points]]** — Sanity-based morale checks triggered by leader death or 25/50/75% casualty thresholds; failure means rout.
3. **[[Enemy Rally]]** — a Focus-based leader action (once per round) that can reroll failed Breaking Points with Advantage and restore Momentum.

## Design intent

"This system makes those moments matter mechanically. It transforms battles from HP attrition into psychological warfare where killing the right target at the right moment can shatter an entire force." Makes enemy Focus and Sanity scores combat-relevant (previously, the Mental Triad had only been discussed for PCs — see [[Sanity]] and [[Focus]]) and makes enemy leaders explicit high-value targets.

## Optional: PC Breaking Points

A clearly-labeled optional variant extends Breaking Points to PCs (party leader falls, 50% party incapacitated, catastrophic events), with materially softer consequences — PCs never auto-rout, they choose retreat vs. helping an ally, and any PC with the highest Focus can attempt Rally. Recommendation given: **use enemy-only as the default.** Not adopted as the vault's assumed ruleset — noted as available but off by default, same treatment given to other "Optional Rule" callouts across this cluster (e.g. [[Damage Reduction]]'s Armor Degradation).
