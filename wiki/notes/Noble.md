# Noble — Rank 1-4 (Born to Power)

*"You were born into privilege, with wealth, status, and expectations."* Tier 2 (Specialized). Fulfills the "Noble" slot [[FEARS Advanced Backgrounds]] flagged as not-yet-developed after Batch 1. The only background in either batch to explicitly state a character might not start at Rank 1 — see below.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Untitled Noble | Position of Privilege | Distinguish yourself politically, militarily, or socially; earn your own title; or increase family standing |
| 2 | Landed Noble | Noble Authority | Increase your lands, form profitable alliances through marriage or treaty, or perform a service granting higher title |
| 3 | High Noble | Political Power | Become advisor to the crown, lead your faction to dominance, or be granted ducal rank |
| 4 | Royal Noble/Duke | Power of the Realm | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Untitled Noble

**Skill Proficiency**: History, Persuasion. **Tool Proficiency**: one gaming set. **Language**: one of choice. **Equipment**: fine clothes, a signet ring, a scroll of pedigree, 25 gp.

**Feature — Position of Privilege**: common folk and merchants treat you respectfully; you can secure audience with local officials; noble establishments extend comfort and service; your word carries more weight than a commoner's.

**Holdings**: a room in the family estate; an allowance from family (20 gp/month); access to family resources; a position in the line of succession, probably distant.

> [!note] Starting rank varies by family standing
> The source explicitly notes: "depending on your family's standing, you might start at Rank 2 or even Rank 3." No other background in either batch makes this kind of explicit table-facing note about skipping starting ranks — worth applying by GM judgment for a specific campaign's noble houses, not a universal default.

## Rank 2: Landed Noble

**Holdings**: a manor house with a small estate; income from lands (50 gp/month); a staff of 10-15 servants; a title (Baron/Baroness, Lord/Lady, etc.); a seat on the regional council; you can levy taxes and administer justice in your domain.

**Feature Expansion — Noble Authority**: command men-at-arms (a small military force); legal authority over your lands; petition higher nobility; invitations to court functions; your favor is sought by merchants and artisans.

## Rank 3: High Noble

**Holdings**: a castle or large estate; significant lands generating major income (150 gp/month); a staff of 50+; a military force of 100+ soldiers; a higher title (Count/Countess, Viscount/Viscountess, etc.); a seat on the national council or parliament; political alliances with other houses; control over several minor nobles.

**Feature Expansion — Political Power**: propose laws and policies; your vote matters in succession and war; arrange marriages and alliances; host grand events that affect politics; once per month, call upon your political influence for a major favor.

## Rank 4: Royal Noble/Duke

**Holdings**: multiple castles and vast estates; massive income from lands (500+ gp/month); a private army of 500+ soldiers; a ducal or princely title; you're among the closest advisors to the crown; multiple noble houses swear fealty to you; political power affecting national policy; possibly heir to the throne.

**Feature — Power of the Realm**: raise armies; your word becomes law in your domain; once per month, leverage your power for an action affecting the entire nation; make and break lesser nobles; rulers must consider your opinion. Your bloodline shapes succession for generations, treaties and laws bear your seal, cities are named after your family, and even the crown negotiates with you rather than commands.
