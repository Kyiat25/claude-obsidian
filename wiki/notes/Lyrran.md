# The Lyrran Trading Cities — the Bankers of Civilization

Twelve independent merchant republics (population ~2.7 million) along ~2,400 miles of [[Elarion]]'s western coastline, coordinated through a Trade Council with no fixed capital (**Velantros** hosts it). If the [[Deepstone Cantons]] are the vault of civilization, Lyrran is its circulation system: over 40 banking houses with 200+ offices issue letters of credit backed by Deepstone gold reserves (the **Deepstone Compact**), making money "weightless" — a merchant can deposit gold in Velantros and withdraw silver in [[Kel Varyan]] without a single coin physically moving. Three of the [[Eight Guild Professions|Eight Great Guilds]] (Leatherworker, Tailor, Woodworker) keep their primary headquarters here. The last power foolish enough to attack Lyrran — a pirate confederation in Year 298 4A — was embargoed by every trading nation simultaneously and mutinied from lack of pay within eight months.

## Coastal zones

The **Northern Reach** (temperate — Caelra, Thornport; shipbuilding and lumber), the **Golden Coast** (Mediterranean-equivalent — Velantros, Portmara, Azuris; banking headquarters and guild halls), the **Spice Approaches** (subtropical — Sulani, Goldreach, Coralmouth; international trade and foreign exchange), and the **Island Networks** (warehouse stations and safe harbors, some independent, some contested).

## How a letter of credit actually works

Originated Year 89, 4A, solving the problem that physically shipping coin (10,000 silver marks weighs ~100 kg) invited robbery, shipwreck, and theft. A merchant deposits silver at a Velantros banking house, receives a letter (minus a 2.5% fee), travels with only paper, and presents it at an affiliated house in another city, which verifies signature/seal/secret marks and pays out; the two houses settle net balances quarterly through a Deepstone clearinghouse. ~40 independent, family-owned, fiercely competitive banking houses cooperate through this shared verification system to make letters universally acceptable across the network.

## Notable figures

**Trade-Prince Aldric Velmont** (71, head of House Velmont, de facto Trade Council leader — "Trust is our only product"), **Lady Serena Goldwater** (134, half-elf, most aggressive expansion strategy, pushing controversial new financial instruments), **Master Theodric Tidemark** (58, House Tidemark insurance, runs the most comprehensive maritime risk-intelligence network on the continent), **Guildmistress Yara Silkweaver** (89, Tailor Guild chair, scrambling for alternatives as [[Zhen-Lan]]'s dream-silk supply falters), and **the Information Broker** — an unseen, possibly-plural underworld figure who sells intelligence to every faction.
