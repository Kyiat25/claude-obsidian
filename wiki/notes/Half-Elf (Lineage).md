# Half-Elf

*"I was not given. I was not found. I broke. I walked. I burned. I am. And I name myself so the world cannot forget that I was here."*

**Origin myth**: per the Way of the Named Flame (see [[The Way of the Named]]), Half-Elves are the Pattern's own question to itself — "if I am a thread, can I choose the shape of the tapestry?" No pantheon claimed the first Half-Elf soul, so it named itself. Identity is not inherited but ignited; suffering transforms rather than destroys; the only true name is one burned into existence through deed and choice.

**Physical**: mature ~20 (not "Named" until completing Three Trials, which can take decades), live 180–200 years; Medium, 5–6 ft.

## Traits

- **Darkvision** 60 ft.
- **Fey Ancestry**: advantage vs. charm, immune to magical sleep
- **Skill Versatility**: 2 skills of choice
- **Between-Walker** *(cultural)*: 1/short-or-long rest, advantage on a CHA check interacting with humans or elves
- **Adaptive Resilience**: choose Flame-Scarred (Survival), Story-Keeper (Performance/Persuasion), or Exile's Insight (Insight)
- **Languages**: Common + Elvish + one extra

**Mental**: Focus +0 / Sanity +0 — resilience is earned through survived trials, not biological.

**DCS Integration**: Dodge +1 / Endure +0 / Deflect +0; DR +0. Advantage on Rally when supporting others; personal Breaking Point DC is 1 lower (more fragile under extreme stress).

## Corruption: The Unnamed Void

Corruption enters through identity dissolution, purposelessness, or fractured self-concept. At Corruption 10+: Name-Slip (revert to birth name), Identity-Fracture, Story-Loss. **Fractured Flame table** (1d6): False Memory, Name-Burn (Between-Walker disabled), Identity Void (1d4 psychic + lose a skill), Story-Chaos (Sanity save or −1d4), Eldritch Touch (+1 Whisper Point, Between-Walker auto-fails), or Flame Resists (Corruption −1).

## Cultural touchstones

Deep kinship with [[Tiefling (Lineage)]] via the **Ashen Accord** — both rejected by divine inheritance, both forge identity through choice rather than birthright. Halflings treat Flame-Named Half-Elves as sacred "Ash-Singers."
