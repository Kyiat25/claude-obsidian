# The Tide Covenant

*"We are not water. We are not air. We are not spirit. We are the bond between—the oath spoken where three currents meet. To break the flow is death. To break the bond is erasure. We are the Covenant. We are the Tide."*

The Triton faith — and, per its own doctrine, not truly worship but identity. Tritons claim permanent covenant with three elemental forces at once (Water, Air, Spirit) rather than honoring them as separate domains; the ocean is living proof the three can be one, since it moves (Water), carries wind (Air), and remembers every name spoken to it (Spirit). See [[Triton (Lineage)]] for the matching elemental-origin creation myth (the First Breath, born when Wind touched Water).

## The Five Sacred Bindings (core truths)

1. **The Ocean Remembers—And Never Forgives** — the ocean literally records everything; lies spoken at sea eventually resurface. Creates a surveillance theology where privacy is viewed with suspicion.
2. **The Tide Is Fate, But the Wind Is Choice** — bound determinism: fate (the tide) is inevitable, but how one meets it (the wind) is chosen and is where honor lives.
3. **To Trade Is to Bind—To Break Oath Is to Drown** — the faith's defining and most distinctive truth: commerce is sacred, not secular. Every deal invokes Vaedoris, god of oaths, and breaking one is cosmic fracture, not mere civil failure.
4. **The Three Currents Must Flow** — stagnation is death; motion, exchange, and connection are holy.
5. **We Are the Voice Between** — Tritons belong fully to no single element, existing in the liminal space between them, making them natural translators and mediators.

## The Sacred Trinity of gods

Devotion spans three pantheons rather than one: the **Deep Current** (Water — Nereus the Tide-King as fate incarnate, Marethis for trade rivers, Vaelora for guarded secrets, Kaemor of harbors, Selmira for lunar tides), the **Wind Current** (Air — Zephir the trickster disruptor, Thirian for stillness/pause, Orris for trade winds, Aelis for prophecy and madness), and the **Soul Current** (Spirit — Nyzara, most beloved, watching over the drowned; Aelys for the Coral Anchors that stabilize ley lines; Vaithis for navigation/destiny; and **Vaedoris**, god of oaths, the single most important deity to Triton traders).

## Society: the Coral Dominion and the Three Depths

The largest Triton civilization, ruled by a monthly-rotating **Council of Tides** (no house rules longer than one moon — "the tide always turns"). Society divides by depth rather than birth: **Deep-Dwelling** (elite — politicians, commanders, spiritually proven by pressure endurance), **Mid-Current** (majority — merchants and craftspeople), and **Surface-Singers** (honored diplomats to air-breathers, not lesser but different). This maps directly onto the three subrace castes in [[Triton (Lineage)]].

## Spiritual roles

Tide-Singers (clergy, oral historians, oath witnesses), Current-Weavers (coral architects, 20+ years training), Storm-Seers (prophets marked by glowing spiral tattoos, respected but isolated — "their gift often brings madness"), Oath-Keepers (trade priests backed by Vaedoris, enforce the Salt-Blood Binding), and Memory-Swimmers (recover the names of the drowned into the Scroll of Tides).

## Law and punishment

Three unforgivable acts, each with an escalating punishment: Breaking the Oath-Tide (the Drowning Silence — submerged weighted, freeing oneself means pardon), Memory-Murder / destroying records (the Forgetting — total erasure from all record and kinship), and Stilling the Current / blocking trade or passage (the Current's Mercy — set adrift with no water or shelter for seven days).

## Relationship to other peoples

Honored partnership with Sea Elves (shared reverence for Selmira and Nyzara, some political tension over Triton dominance); necessary but limited relations with Humans (linear time clashes badly with tidal reckoning); respected trade partnership with coastal Orcs over shared honor-contract values; wary respect for Elves, mutual fascination with Dwarves ("stone fish" vs "singing barnacles"), and delighted bafflement with Halflings.
