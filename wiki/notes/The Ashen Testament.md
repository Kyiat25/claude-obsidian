# The Ashen Testament

*"We are not your lesson. We are not your warning. We are fire that walked out of the gods' shadow and dared to ask: 'What if they meant more than this?'"*

A creed and quiet rebellion, not a religion of apology — the Tiefling answer to being born under whispers of damnation.

## Origin myth

One divine glyph was left unfinished when the gods spoke the Weave's laws; an Eldritch Moon's rising shattered their breath mid-utterance, and the broken glyph struck a dying, nameless child, who rose in ash rather than dying. She named herself **Sareth-Ka** — "the scar the gods could not finish" — and became the First Ashbinder, gathering others burned by the same divine failure and etching their names in bone and fire rather than worshipping the gods who forgot them.

## Core beliefs

1. **The Divine Message Is Misread** — what's called "sin" may be misunderstood sacrifice or necessity; the gods are vast, not malicious.
2. **Sin Is Earned, Not Given** — being born Tiefling grants potential, not guilt.
3. **Power Reveals Meaning** — power reflects choice, not blood.
4. **The Ash Carries Truth** — ash is memory in its final form.
5. **We Walk Without Permission** — legends are claimed, not granted.

## The Five Sin-Flames

A codified moral code separating rightful fury from selfish wrath, studied and named before decisive action: the Flame of Spite (wounding without meaning), Vanity (power to be seen), Forgetting (erasing others' grief), Silence (preventing others' pain from being spoken), and Uncarried Ash (using power without accepting its weight).

## Death and memory

Death is not an end but a fracture in the Pattern — the Testament rejects other faiths' afterlife doctrines entirely: no heaven, no punishment, "only remembrance or forgetting." The **Rite of the Ash-Walk** (performed by an **Ashbinder**) gathers ash from the dead's last fire, burns a piece of the body with it into a Grief-Coal, reads their Sin-Testament aloud, and sings their name three times — forward, backward, and in silence — so the soul "walks beside the living" rather than unravelling. Those who die unwitnessed become **Ash-Wraiths**, unmoored echoes drawn to fire and memory, redeemable only if someone speaks the name they never received.

## Divine concept: The Echoed Grief

The Testament has no gods of its own but speaks of the Echoed Grief — a being whispered into existence by the world's collective mourning, appearing as a woman of ash and silence. Not worshipped, only felt and remembered.

## Relationship to other faiths

Deep kinship with [[The Way of the Named]] (shared emphasis on earned identity, the "Ashen Accord") and Halfling joy-traditions; tolerated but restricted by the Faith of the Bound Pattern; hostile relationship with the Burning Concord (branding of Tieflings at birth in some Concord cities); total opposition from [[The Ashen Web]], which assassinates Tiefling leaders who speak the Testament's creed aloud in Drow territory.
