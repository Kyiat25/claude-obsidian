# World Anchors and the Thread System: A Comprehensive Guide

> [!note] DM-only, marked canonical — but written from the mortal/scholarly register
> Unlike [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]], [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]], and [[Twins of Creation]] — all mythic-register, DM-secret-truth documents — this source reads as an in-world scholarly compilation ("compiled from historical records, theological texts, druidic oral traditions, Faith doctrine, and firsthand accounts," closing line). It's still `audience: dm-only` and `status: canonical`, but its content is closer to what [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]] called "Tier One: what everyone experiences" and "Tier Two: what scholars and mystics suspect" than "Tier Three: the cosmic truth." This is very likely the exact "World Anchors document" that source cited as its own baseline.

## The Weave, restated from the mortal side

Confirms the Five Threads (Fire, Water, Earth, Air, Spirit) weave together to form the Weave, and gives concrete, severe stakes for Weave collapse: magic failing outright, fate becoming self-contradictory, memory fracturing, physical laws wavering, societal and economic collapse, and weakened planar boundaries inviting Eldritch incursion. Confirms — from the mortal side — that **no one knows why Threads rotate**, listing the same open question [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]] answers only for the DM: natural limit, safety mechanism, Anchor design flaw, or unintended consequence of losing World Trees. This is consistent, not contradictory — mortals are supposed to not know the real answer.

### The critical limit, confirmed

A Thread aligning twice consecutively to the same Anchor is safe and has happened multiple times. Three consecutive alignments has happened **exactly once** — [[Ashkarra]]'s Spirit Thread triple alignment — with catastrophic results.

## World Trees vs. World Anchors

| Aspect | World Trees | World Anchors |
|---|---|---|
| Origin | Natural, planted by Vatharun (Earth Pantheon) | Mortal-crafted constructs |
| Maintenance | Self-sustaining | No upkeep needed, but no self-repair either |
| Thread interaction | Naturally harmonize all Threads | Require Threadbearer attunement for a single Thread |
| Stability | Extremely stable, corrupt slowly | Can be corrupted quickly |
| Memory | Encode ancestral/regional memory biologically | No inherent memory storage |
| Failure state | Corruption → warning signs → slow decay | Corruption → explosion (see [[Ashkarra]]) |

> [!contradiction] RESOLVED — Does anyone know why World Trees exist, or is it a deliberate permanent mystery?
> [[World Trees]] (from [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]]) is explicit that their true origin should stay unknown **even to the DM** — five competing theories, no canonical answer, framed as a design choice to preserve wonder. This source instead states flatly, as settled background fact with no ambiguity or competing theory offered: World Trees are "ancient trees of immense size **planted by Vatharun** (Earth Pantheon deity)." No hedge, no "some say," no alternate theory — just an origin, directly undercutting the earlier source's explicit instruction.
>
> **Ruled (2026-07-14):** this document's flat "planted by Vatharun" is the mortal tier of a tiered-comprehension hierarchy, not the DM-facing truth — see [[World Trees]] for the full ruling. World Trees are woven into the cosmos at its creation; true DM-facing certainty about their origin remains deliberately withheld, per [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]]'s original instruction. Mortals land on Vatharun specifically because he's the largest, most comprehensible act-of-creation a mortal mind can conceive of — a single, nameable, geologically-themed deity — not because it's actually true. This source's own note below, on Wood Elves' and Druids' cultural bond to the Trees, is kept and expanded on [[World Trees]]'s "Who understands the stakes" section: some elder gods and long-lived, Tree-bonded races understand the *consequences* of a Tree's destruction far better than ordinary mortals, without that implying they understand the origin any better either.

World Trees: 73-75 confirmed worldwide, 6-7 "Major Root Anchors," at least 5 known-corrupted, 7-9 lost to Loom-City expansion. For roughly 15,000 years (the "Loomless Time") they were the *only* stabilization system, requiring no ritual or maintenance — defended culturally by Wood Elves (Verdant Accord), Druids, Orcs (ancestral judges), Halflings (tied to joy/grief/death rites), and Dwarves (oath-vaults, Stone-Song resonance).

### The Faith's theological problem with World Trees

World Trees prove the Weave predates the gods, that reality can be stabilized without divine authority, and that elven druidic tradition was right about fundamental cosmology — all deeply inconvenient for the Faith of the Bound Pattern, which downplays Tree importance publicly, folds some Tree veneration into Earth Pantheon worship, and frames Trees as "divine gifts" rather than pre-divine phenomena. Internally, the Faith is split between clergy who see Trees as complementary pre-Faith sacred law and those who see them as dangerous pre-Pattern relics.

## World Anchors: mortal imitations

Physical constructs (not living entities), created ~1,100 years ago alongside the founding of the Faith of the Bound Pattern and the Loom-City system, in response to deforestation-driven Tree loss. Critically, **World Anchors were discovered too late to be true replacements** — they work *better* when World Trees still exist nearby, and losing too many Trees stresses the Anchors that were meant to replace them. The system turned out to be interdependent, not substitutive. Anchor rituals (ceremonies, offerings) are explicitly **traditional, not mechanically necessary** — the Anchor functions identically with or without them; cultures maintain them for political/psychological/community reasons only.

## The five Loom-Cities and beyond

Full detail on its own hub page: [[Loom-Cities]]. Five cities (Kel Varyan, Veltharyn, Thar Vornhal, Aeravae, Selveran), each with a distinctly-themed World Anchor, government type, and degree of Faith influence — plus three regions explicitly outside the system (Corvessa, Talanthar, the Underdark/Drow), each with a strong, distinct relationship to the Thread system covered on the same hub page.

## Ashkarra and Aeravae

[[Ashkarra]] — the only World Anchor to ever completely fail, permanently destroyed via triple Spirit Thread alignment, its city lost, its Weave-shockwave nearly collapsing the entire system. [[Aeravae]] — the unprecedented cooperative response: a World Anchor deliberately *grafted into* a living, newly-planted World Tree rather than built by clearing one, the only known hybrid Anchor/Tree system in existence, proof that cooperation across every faction (Faith, wood elves, sea elves, high elves, humans, dwarves) outperforms any single tradition's purity. Both get dedicated pages given how much narrative weight they carry across every cosmology source ingested so far.

## Threadbearers, mechanically and culturally

Full detail folded into the existing [[Threadbearers]] page, substantially expanded by this source: the manifestation event ("the Beaming"), the born-anywhere/no-bloodline/no-divine-selection rule, the death-and-transfer mechanic (every surviving Threadbearer immediately senses a death and the new Bearer's call, and is sacred-duty-bound to find them "or die trying"), and sharply different cultural treatment depending on where a Threadbearer is born — political figures in Loom-Cities, "living saints" driving religious zealotry in Corvessa specifically.

## The current crisis, in brief

Full detail on its own page: [[The Threadbearer Crisis]]. Four of five Threadbearers assassinated in a coordinated Drow operation; only Kel Varyan's Bearer survives; four World Anchors are dimmed and vulnerable; new Threadbearers are manifesting into an active three-way race between protective factions, controlling factions, and the Drow assassins finishing the job. This is presented as the **current, active campaign situation**, not historical background — the source's own closing timestamp reads "487-03-18 (Current campaign date)."

## Six theological interpretations, compared

| Faith/Tradition | Core view of the Thread system | On World Anchors | On Threadbearers |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Faith of the Bound Pattern** (dominant) | Five Threads are divine manifestations requiring mortal stewardship | Sacred instruments of the gods' will | Chosen vessels blessed by the Weave |
| **Woven Reverence** (elven) | The Weave/Pattern predates the gods entirely | Inferior mortal constructs, necessary only due to past mistakes | Natural manifestations, not divine chosen — respect the person, question the institution |
| **Deep Law** (dwarven) | Laws and oaths bind reality as much as magic does | Acceptable if properly constructed; care about function over doctrine | Respect the office, judge the person by competence |
| **Burning Concord** (dragonborn) | Pre-Ashkarra: Fire Thread supremacy and divine caste; post-Ashkarra: fractured shame | N/A (Ashkarra was their city) | N/A — traumatic aftermath split the faith into loyalists, reformists, exiles, nihilists |
| **Joy-Wild** (halfling) | The Pattern is something to dance with, not control | "If you're going to have them, at least make them beautiful" | Sympathy for their burden; told as stories about people, not icons |
| **The Ashen Web** (drow) | The Weave is prey, Vel'Zyren (their Spider Goddess) is predator | Targets for destruction | High-value assassination targets — strategic warfare, not random terrorism |

## Practical Q&A worth preserving

The source closes with a DM-facing FAQ worth keeping intact rather than summarizing away: whether the Weave can exist unstabilized (fundamentally unlikely, untested by choice), whether a Tree-only system could return (theoretically yes, practically blocked by urbanization and Faith resistance — "most likely future: more grafted hybrids like Aeravae"), what happens if all Threadbearers die at once (Anchors dim but don't fail outright; new Bearers manifest fast enough that total collapse is unlikely — the real danger is the chaotic transition), why more Anchors aren't simply built (leyline nexus scarcity, over-stabilization risk, political rivalry, cost, an unknown Threadbearer cap, and general fear of tampering further with a barely-working system), and why Thread rotation can't be controlled (nobody understands the mechanism — "we're passengers, not drivers").
