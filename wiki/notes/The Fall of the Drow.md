# The Fall of the Drow

## Before the Fall: the Pureborn Elves

The proto-Drow were once surface elves, but unlike their kin they believed in their own purity — blood, power, and place above all others — and were unwilling to share the world with "lesser races" or worship gods alongside weaker kin. They sought war, conquest, and an elven empire that would rule unchallenged. The gods did not answer them: elves who worshiped peace were blessed, elves who sought balance were granted forests, elves who embraced magic were gifted the Weave — but the warrior-elves were ignored, growing bitter and abandoned, waiting for a deity who would appreciate their strength.

## The whispering campaign

[[Vel'Zyren]] came not with force but with a truth they feared to hear: *"You are not meant for the sky. You are a knot the Weave would rather forget. Come below. In shadow, I will show you your true shape."* The first whisper went to a prophetess named Thalanyr, who heard during meditation that the gods feared what the warrior-elves could become — and that Vel'Zyren alone would give them purpose. The message spread ear to ear, personally tailored to each listener, as dreams of the depths and promises of recognition.

## The Night of the First Web

The ground split beneath warrior-elf enclaves during the darkest moon — dark, bottomless, only invitation. One by one, the greatest warriors, strongest spellcasters, and most cunning minds stepped forward and descended; when the last had gone, the rift closed. Their names vanished from surface records, their history swallowed by shadow, their place among elves erased. The gods did nothing.

## The 105-year descent

The journey to the Underdark took an entire elven generation of transformation, not corruption but evolution: skin darkened to absorb shadow, eyes adapted to absolute darkness, bodies grew leaner and more predatory. Surface memories faded, compassion eroded, mercy became weakness. Connection to the surface gods severed completely — only Vel'Zyren's voice remained, and the Glyph of Binding Will inscribed itself onto their souls.

Vel'Zyren did not welcome them warmly. She sent the weak into darkness (only the strong returned), turned them against each other (ensuring only the most cunning survived), and taught that unity was weakness and trust was vulnerability. *"You thought yourselves predators on the surface. Now learn what it truly means to hunt."*

## Arrival and the War on the False Gods

They emerged not into paradise but into darkness, isolation, and hostility — a land that didn't care if they lived or died. Their first command from Vel'Zyren was to erase every trace of their old faith: the names of surface gods struck from memory, old temples shattered, scriptures burned. Only Vel'Zyren remained, and the Drow were bound to her not by chains but by belief — the only god who had ever given them what they wanted: power.

## The Moment of the Crack

At the moment of complete transformation, every Drow — regardless of location, even those not yet descended — experienced Vel'Zyren's presence entering their minds simultaneously: perfect, terrifying unity, the Glyph of Binding Will activating, individuality subsumed into her Pattern. The Drow became not slaves but extensions of her will, a race-wide hivemind she can call upon, and — as long as she exists — a people who can never truly be destroyed, rebuildable from a single survivor.

See [[The Ashen Web]] for the totalizing religion this transformation produced.
