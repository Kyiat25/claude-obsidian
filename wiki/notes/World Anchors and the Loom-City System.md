# World Anchors and the Loom-City System

> [!note] Near-duplicate of World Anchors and Thread System, not a distinct new source
> This source covers almost exactly the same ground as the already-ingested [[World Anchors and Thread System]] — the founding of the Loom-City system 1,100 years ago, the five cities, [[Ashkarra]]'s fall, [[Aeravae]]'s cooperative founding, Threadbearer mechanics, and the current assassination crisis — in near-identical prose in places. Treated as a genuine second draft/parallel telling rather than excluded or merged silently, per this cluster's established pattern (see [[Creation World]] vs. [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]] for the prior instance of this same relationship).

## What's identical to World Anchors and Thread System

The founding narrative (deforestation destroying World Trees, the Faith orchestrating World Anchor construction as a supplement), the mechanical differences between World Trees and Anchors (living vs. inert, self-repairing vs. exploding when corrupted), the five Loom-Cities and their thematic Anchors, Ashkarra's fall as the catastrophic proof of the "no Thread twice consecutively" limit, Aeravae's Echo-Cenote as the unique grafted hybrid, and the broad strokes of Threadbearer manifestation (the Beaming, no bloodline requirement, the death/succession sacred duty).

## What's genuinely new here

**The Day of Twelve Shadows, named explicitly**: Ashkarra fell "during the Day of Twelve Shadows — when all twelve Eldritch Moons aligned for the first and only time in recorded history." This directly ties the Thread-mechanical Ashkarra catastrophe to the separately-ingested [[Eldritch Moons]] material, which independently confirms the same event from the opposite direction (see [[Blood Moon of Rebirth]] and [[Ashkarra]]'s own updated page).

**The wood elf druids' rebuke**: when they arrived to help found Aeravae, they delivered "a devastating truth: 'We told you. The Weave existed before the gods. We can anchor it without a divine claim. We've been doing it for 15,000 years. You just never listened.'" A sharper, more directly quoted version of the Faith-vs-Woven-Reverence tension already documented on [[World Anchors and Thread System]].

**A historical precedent for the current crisis**: this source states the current Threadbearer mass-assassination crisis "has happened once before, during the Second Age," and that it caused massive political chaos, widespread war, mutual accusations, and generations-long collapse of inter-city trust — but did not cause Weave collapse either time. No equivalent precedent is mentioned in [[World Anchors and Thread System]].

> [!contradiction] Which Loom-City's Threadbearer survives?
> This source states "only Aeravae's hybrid system, more resistant to corruption through its living World Tree component, maintains full stability" — implying Aeravae is the sole untouched Anchor. [[World Anchors and Thread System]] instead states only Kel Varyan's Threadbearer survives. Documented on [[The Threadbearer Crisis]] and [[Ashkarra]], not resolved here.

## Maintenance and mystery, restated

Confirms World Anchors require no actual maintenance to function — rituals are traditional and psychological, not mechanical — and restates the still-unresolved question of why Threads must rotate at all, with the same four candidate explanations (natural limit, safety mechanism, Anchor design flaw, unintended consequence of World Tree loss) as [[World Anchors and Thread System]].
