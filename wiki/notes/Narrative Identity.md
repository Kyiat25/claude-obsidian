# Narrative Identity

## Status of this content — read this first

Unlike the other four FEARS sources, this document has **no frontmatter, no in-fiction narrator, and no rulebook framing**. It reads as a direct design consultation — the closing line is literally "If you want, next I can turn this into a blank worksheet you can fill for all 24 classes, or I can do six sample classes fully." This is a **proposal being pitched to the FEARS designer**, not settled, published game content. `confidence: low` reflects that: everything below may or may not have been adopted into actual FEARS rules. Treat named currencies and classes here as *candidate* content until a rulebook-style source confirms them, the way [[Welcome to FEARS]], [[A World that Watches]], [[FEARS Character Framework]], and [[Four Threads of the Mind]] have for everything else in this cluster.

## The core pitch

FEARS already has strong **mechanical identity** (what buttons you press) and **thematic identity** (flavor and vibe). The document proposes a third layer, **narrative identity** — the recurring story tension, world reaction, and consequences a class generates, independent of combat. Full framework: [[Narrative Identity Framework]].

## New classes revealed

Four FEARS-specific classes appear here for the first time, with enough detail to warrant their own pages: [[Warden]], [[Reaper]], [[Bender]], [[Apothecary]]. None of the [[FEARS MOC|original 12 archetypes]] from [[Welcome to FEARS]] are redefined, but **6 of them — Magus, Psion, Runesmith, Shadowbinder, Tamer, Thaumaturge — are not mentioned anywhere in this document**, a gap worth noting rather than assuming means anything.

The document also confirms FEARS includes standard 5E classes (Fighter, Rogue, Barbarian, Ranger, Monk, Paladin, Bard, Cleric, Wizard, Sorcerer, Warlock), recommended to stay mechanically lighter than the setting-specific classes by design, plus one passing mention of a Druid. No dedicated pages created for these — they get essentially zero FEARS-specific content here beyond being named.

## Worked examples

Two classes get a full six-part narrative identity write-up: [[Witch]] (currency: [[Spirit Debt]]) and [[Warden]] (currency: [[Territory Bond]]).

## Class count discrepancy

The document's own framing implies ~24 total classes ("all 24 classes"). Counting every class actually named across this document and the prior four sources gives 27 (12 original + 4 new here + 11 standard 5E imports). Not reconciled — flagged as an editorial note rather than forced into agreement.
