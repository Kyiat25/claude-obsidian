# Ashkarra

The Loom-City that no longer exists. First named as a brief case study in [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]] (the Ashkarra Catastrophe); [[World Anchors and Thread System]] confirms it as a real city with a real fall, and elevates it to the setting's central historical trauma.

## What happened

Roughly 487 years before the current campaign date (Third Age), Ashkarra's World Anchor suffered the only recorded **triple consecutive Spirit Thread alignment** in the setting's history — the one documented case exceeding the known-safe limit of two consecutive alignments to the same Anchor. The result was total: the Anchor was permanently destroyed, the city was lost, and the resulting Weave-shockwave nearly collapsed the entire Anchor system setting-wide.

Ashkarra was the seat of the **Burning Concord** — dragonborn, Fire Thread supremacists, organized around a divine caste system. The catastrophe shattered that faith as thoroughly as it shattered the city: the Burning Concord fractured afterward into loyalists (who maintain pre-Ashkarra doctrine), reformists, exiles, and nihilists, a schism that persists into the current era.

## The Day of Twelve Shadows

Per [[Guide to the Eldritch Forces]] and [[The Hollow Thread]], Ashkarra's fall was simultaneously an Eldritch catastrophe, not just a Thread-mechanical one. **All twelve [[Eldritch Moons]] rose within 58 days**, culminating in an alignment on Ashkarra's Spirit Thread festival — this convergence is called **the Day of Twelve Shadows**, and its climactic moon was the [[Blood Moon of Rebirth]]. The result went beyond a destroyed World Anchor: it triggered the **First Hollow Breach**, the first-ever crack in [[The Hollow Thread]]'s containment seal, nearly precipitating the setting's true apocalypse rather than a regional one. [[Veiled Star]] frames Ashkarra's Third Thread failure specifically as prophecy becoming unbearable — "what should have spoken then, but chose not to."

Per [[The Hollow Thread]]'s own account, this was a perfect storm of compounding factors, not Thread stagnation alone: geographic proximity to the (unknown, unmarked) Hollow Vault region, the World Tree severed to build Ashkarra's own Anchor (removing natural containment), and the Burning Concord's divine hubris in dismissing every warning. The Earth gods' emergency response — reinforcing the seal and letting Ashkarra fall completely — reset local Weave conditions and averted total catastrophe "by inches." Since Ashkarra, Eldritch Moons rise roughly three times more frequently than before, and the containment seal has never fully recovered.

## The lead-up (per a fuller narrative source, `status: excluded/template` — treat as evocative color, not fully confirmed)

A more detailed account frames the fall as escalating hubris across a century: at peak power, Ashkarra's imperial leaders began dismissing [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]]'s warnings and the [[The Burning Concord|Burning Concord]] declared itself superior to other faiths. The first Spirit Thread alignment (50 years before the fall) brought such prosperity that authorities declared it "divinely approved" for extended use — directly violating the law that Threads must rotate. A second consecutive alignment (30 years before) produced early warning signs — prophetic contradictions, spontaneously-changing sacred inscriptions, brief backward time-flow — met with suppression rather than correction; other Loom-Cities' demands for rotation were dismissed as jealousy. The third, unprecedented alignment (10 years before) triggered Weave stagnation, "echo redundancy," and reality strain, with the city's **Ember Crucible** World Anchor beginning to fail: its sacred flame burning in reverse, its glyphic Spiral of Flame and Form inverting. In the final three years, names became fluid, caste lines blurred, and a final prophet, **Tyraen of the Pool-Sky**, was imprisoned and disappeared after prophesying "when the Thread weaves no longer... then shall flame forget its name."

## The Day of Twelve Shadows, hour by hour (same source)

Morning: twelve Eldritch Moons appeared in the sky during the Spirit Festival; reflections stopped working, a lake began boiling into crystal glass. Noon: the Ember Crucible reached critical failure — flame reversal, glyph collapse, and Anchor implosion, its last inscription reading "Ashkarra" written backward in bleeding script. Afternoon: not destruction but **unwriting** — the city ceased to exist in any comprehensible way; most citizens simply ceased to exist, some became twisted Eldritch echoes trapped in temporal loops, and a handful of survivors escaped but were erased from all records and memory. Immediate aftermath: the Dragonborn Empire collapsed into warring fragments, the breach created the permanently unstable **Shard Wastes**, and a 150+ year "Age of Shadows" of magical chaos began. This source's stated thesis: Ashkarra fell from spiritual pride and violation of cosmic law, not external attack — reality itself "corrected" the error by unmaking the city entirely.

## The city itself, before the fall

"The Ember Crown of the Desert" stood in the heart of [[Vashkora]] for over two millennia, built on stepped plateaus between the **Saphyran Lake** (40 miles long, famous for its "Double Dawn" mirror-sunrise; now cracked into crystalline fragments with pools of temporal distortion) and the **Oasis of Threads**, a sacred spring within the Forbidden Temple of Flame and Oath (now sealed behind a phasing glyph-barrier, guarded by undying flame spirits). The **Shardspine Desert**, a 100-mile ring of shifting, ley-fluctuating dunes, defended its approaches — it is now the permanently unstable **Shard Wastes**. Architecture blended Indus Valley grid-planning, Egyptian monumentalism, and Byzantine dome-and-mosaic sophistication in white marble and desert-gold sandstone, under the governing philosophy "the Eternal Flame Made Stone."

Society ran on the **Nine-Caste System**, still the template for Dragonborn social order today: the **Sultan** (Sovereign Flame, absolute divine authority), the **Embermarked** priesthood (interpreters of divine will), the **Scaled Fang** (war-noble governors), **Furnacebound** craftsmen, **Ashen Scale** professional soldiers, **Cinderkin** domestic servants, **Coinbrand** merchants, **Smokehands** laborers, and the casteless **Ashborn** — exiles with no legal protection, treated as living warnings against defying divine order.

## Why it matters beyond its own story

Ashkarra is the reason the current Anchor system has any redundancy discourse at all — it is cited in-world as the reason more Anchors aren't casually built (see the FAQ on [[World Anchors and Thread System]]: fear of repeating exactly this failure is one of the explicit reasons expansion is politically and practically resisted). It is also the direct cause of [[Aeravae]]'s founding: the near-collapse it caused is what forced unprecedented multi-faction cooperation, producing the only hybrid Anchor/World Tree system in existence.

> [!contradiction] RESOLVED — Which Loom-City's Threadbearer survived the current assassination crisis?
> [[World Anchors and Thread System]] states that only **Kel Varyan**'s Threadbearer survives the ongoing Drow assassination wave (see [[The Threadbearer Crisis]]). [[World Anchors and the Loom-City System]] instead states "only Aeravae's hybrid system, more resistant to corruption through its living World Tree component, maintains full stability" — implying Aeravae, not Kel Varyan, is the sole untouched Anchor. Both sources otherwise describe the same crisis (four dimmed Anchors, four dead Threadbearers). **Ruled (2026-07-14) in favor of Kel Varyan** — see [[The Threadbearer Crisis]] for the full resolution (Serana Tidecaller is secretly Naleth Spiralbound, the survivor).
