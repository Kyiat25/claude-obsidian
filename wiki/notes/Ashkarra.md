# Ashkarra

The Loom-City that no longer exists. First named as a brief case study in [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]] (the Ashkarra Catastrophe); [[World Anchors and Thread System]] confirms it as a real city with a real fall, and elevates it to the setting's central historical trauma.

## What happened

Roughly 487 years before the current campaign date (Third Age), Ashkarra's World Anchor suffered the only recorded **triple consecutive Spirit Thread alignment** in the setting's history — the one documented case exceeding the known-safe limit of two consecutive alignments to the same Anchor. The result was total: the Anchor was permanently destroyed, the city was lost, and the resulting Weave-shockwave nearly collapsed the entire Anchor system setting-wide.

Ashkarra was the seat of the **Burning Concord** — dragonborn, Fire Thread supremacists, organized around a divine caste system. The catastrophe shattered that faith as thoroughly as it shattered the city: the Burning Concord fractured afterward into loyalists (who maintain pre-Ashkarra doctrine), reformists, exiles, and nihilists, a schism that persists into the current era.

## The Day of Twelve Shadows

Per [[Guide to the Eldritch Forces]] and [[The Hollow Thread]], Ashkarra's fall was simultaneously an Eldritch catastrophe, not just a Thread-mechanical one. **All twelve [[Eldritch Moons]] rose within 58 days**, culminating in an alignment on Ashkarra's Spirit Thread festival — this convergence is called **the Day of Twelve Shadows**, and its climactic moon was the [[Blood Moon of Rebirth]]. The result went beyond a destroyed World Anchor: it triggered the **First Hollow Breach**, the first-ever crack in [[The Hollow Thread]]'s containment seal, nearly precipitating the setting's true apocalypse rather than a regional one. [[Veiled Star]] frames Ashkarra's Third Thread failure specifically as prophecy becoming unbearable — "what should have spoken then, but chose not to."

Per [[The Hollow Thread]]'s own account, this was a perfect storm of compounding factors, not Thread stagnation alone: geographic proximity to the (unknown, unmarked) Hollow Vault region, the World Tree severed to build Ashkarra's own Anchor (removing natural containment), and the Burning Concord's divine hubris in dismissing every warning. The Earth gods' emergency response — reinforcing the seal and letting Ashkarra fall completely — reset local Weave conditions and averted total catastrophe "by inches." Since Ashkarra, Eldritch Moons rise roughly three times more frequently than before, and the containment seal has never fully recovered.

## Why it matters beyond its own story

Ashkarra is the reason the current Anchor system has any redundancy discourse at all — it is cited in-world as the reason more Anchors aren't casually built (see the FAQ on [[World Anchors and Thread System]]: fear of repeating exactly this failure is one of the explicit reasons expansion is politically and practically resisted). It is also the direct cause of [[Aeravae]]'s founding: the near-collapse it caused is what forced unprecedented multi-faction cooperation, producing the only hybrid Anchor/World Tree system in existence.

> [!contradiction] Which Loom-City's Threadbearer survived the current assassination crisis?
> [[World Anchors and Thread System]] states that only **Kel Varyan**'s Threadbearer survives the ongoing Drow assassination wave (see [[The Threadbearer Crisis]]). [[World Anchors and the Loom-City System]] instead states "only Aeravae's hybrid system, more resistant to corruption through its living World Tree component, maintains full stability" — implying Aeravae, not Kel Varyan, is the sole untouched Anchor. Both sources otherwise describe the same crisis (four dimmed Anchors, four dead Threadbearers). Flagged as unresolved on [[The Threadbearer Crisis]] rather than silently picking one — a GM should choose whichever reading suits their table.
