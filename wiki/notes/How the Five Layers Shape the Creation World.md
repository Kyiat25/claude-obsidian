# How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World

> [!note] DM-only source
> This file's own frontmatter marks it `audience: dm-only`. Treated here as secret/behind-the-screen lore — the wiki page inherits that flag rather than dropping it, since this is exactly the kind of content that shouldn't leak to players via a shared vault.

Mythic-register cosmology essay explaining how **the Weave** (see [[Weave and Magic]], not yet ingested) produces the **[[Creation World]]** (not yet ingested) through five interlocking "Layers" — each described as a Law. [[The Weave]] already had a thin wiki page (address `c-000044`) built from [[Four Threads of the Mind]] and [[A World that Watches]]; this source is the first to actually structure it rather than gesture at it. Checking the raw `.raw/` sources directly (not yet reflected in most of these classes' wiki pages) confirms "the Weave" is invoked by name across the FEARS class cluster: [[Wizard]]'s subtitle is "Master of the Weave," [[Sorcerer]]'s own text says casters need "no intermediary between yourself and the weave," [[Sangromancer]]'s intro contrasts itself against "a wizard draws from the Weave," and [[Narrative Identity]]'s per-class design template literally asks "how do people, factions, places, spirits, institutions, or the Weave react to this class?" — meaning the Weave is baked into the class-design framework itself, not incidental flavor.

## The core claim: reality is tested, not made

The Creation World is explicitly a **proving ground**, not a finished setting — "the place where all things begin," where new realities are trialed before being committed to permanence. The five Layers don't act independently; the source's whole thesis is that they're bound together and inseparable, though (see below) none of the entities involved actually realize this.

## The Five Layers

### First Law — Primal Glyphs set the cycle

Four **Primal Forces**, none of whom know the Creation World exists or watch it directly — the world simply follows their cycle, "unshaken and inevitable":

| Primal Force | Domain | Glyph | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **[[Astralor]]** | The Forge | [[Glyph of Will]] | Forges realities, tests laws of existence before they become permanent |
| **[[Aeloria]]** | The Loom | [[Glyph of Threads]] | Weaves fate before it can be lived — tests patterns, trials, destinies |
| **[[The Whispering Dark]]** | The Maw | The Glyph of Endings | Culls what shouldn't be born — erases contradictions, flaws, unsustainable concepts |
| **[[The Restorer]]** | The Wheel | The Glyph of Echoes | Nothing erased is truly gone — gathers and reforges lost fragments into future cycles |

### Second Law — Universal Glyphs define the hidden weave

Five Glyphs that don't shape the Creation World directly but keep whatever emerges from it coherent: **Glyph of Understanding** (records lessons across cycles so mistakes don't repeat), **Glyph of Life** (ensures some spark of existence always survives testing), **Glyph of Motion** (prevents stagnation, keeps even discarded worlds shifting), **Glyph of Shadows** (conceals forbidden truths even from the gods), **Glyph of the Dreaming** (lets thought take shape before it's fixed into permanent reality).

### Third Law — Elemental Glyphs shape the canvas

The classical five elements, framed here as still-fluid testing material rather than fixed physics: **Fire** (destruction/rebirth), **Water** (change, non-stagnation), **Earth** (weight, permanence, foundation), **Air** (knowledge, thought, prophecy), **Spirit** (memory, breath, soul — "the thread of identity through the storm of becoming"). Explicitly given as the reason magic and physics behave inconsistently across different cycles/settings — the elements are still being refined, not yet locked in.

### Fourth Law — Form Glyphs shape life's return

Five life-pattern archetypes guaranteed to recur in every cycle regardless of what any Primal Force intends: **Mortals**, **Beasts**, **Titans** ("gods, giants, or forces beyond comprehension"), **Spirits** (ensuring death is never truly final), **Aberrations** (ensuring something always lurks beyond understanding). Notably, the source states outright that even [[Astralor]] trying to erase concepts, [[The Whispering Dark]] trying to consume them, and [[The Restorer]] trying to rewrite them all fail against this Law — these five patterns are more durable than any single Primal Force's will.

### Fifth Law — Personal Glyphs determine who wields fate

Once the world, laws, elements, and life-patterns are set, individual souls are marked with their own **Personal Glyphs** — these don't create power, shape destiny, or define fate directly; they determine *how* a given individual channels power and interacts with the fate they're given. Framed as the final ingredient that makes a world "truly alive."

## The Final Truth: none of them know they're connected

The source's explicit dramatic irony, stated as its capstone: the Primal Forces believe they control creation, the Universal Glyphs believe they're hidden architects, the Elements believe they're mere tools, the Form Glyphs believe they're simply inevitable, and the Personal Glyphs believe they only define the individual. **None of them realize all five Layers are one design.** This reads as a deliberate, structural narrative hook — a truth the DM knows and the setting's own cosmic entities do not.

## Open thread: the Severance Glyph

Closing line teases a tenth, forbidden Glyph — the **Severance Glyph** — waiting "beyond the cycle, where no god dares look," explicitly set up as the thing that eventually "breaks the cycle." No further detail given in this source. Flagged as an open narrative hook, not a documented mechanic.

## Relationship to Creation World

[[Creation World]] (ingested later) reads as the likely narrative root this page's Primal Force table draws from — same four forces, same forge/loom framing, same "none of them realize they're not alone" capstone — but with no Layer/Glyph taxonomy of its own. It also introduces [[Divine Sparks]], a concept absent from this page entirely.
