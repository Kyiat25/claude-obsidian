# The Unraveling — A Complete Chronicle

*"We adapted to hell. That's what humans don't understand. We didn't overcome — we adapted. We learned to live with acceptable losses. Thousands daily was acceptable."* — Warden Celeborn, 723 years old

The 95-year period between [[Ashkarra]]'s fall (Year -95 A4) and [[Aeravae]]'s completion (Year 0 A4), during which the world had only four World Anchors supporting five Thread systems — a stated mathematical impossibility. See [[The Ages of Dór-o Estel]] for the condensed Phase summary this page expands on.

## The five phases (condensed)

**Phase 1 — Absolute Chaos**: roughly a quarter of the global population dies as magic fails unpredictably and governments collapse. **Phase 2 — The Thirty Years of Failed Experiments**: three catastrophic attempts to build a replacement Anchor, totaling ~110,000 deaths and permanent landscape scars. **Phase 3 — The Dark Stabilization** (~35 years): the world accepts survival on four overloaded, dimmed Anchors; magical rationing becomes mandatory and life expectancy drops. **Phase 4 — Desperate Cooperation**: a forcing prophetic vision drives the Faith, druids, and every faction into unprecedented joint planning. **Phase 5 — The Construction of Aeravae**: built in five years as a hybrid World Anchor/World Tree, activating at Year 0 and ending the crisis.

## Living memory vs. official history

The chronicle's sharpest device: humans remember the Unraveling as a brief, tidy "difficult adjustment period" resolved by international cooperation. Long-lived elven witnesses remember it as ninety-five years of watching the world die — children aging to death in hours, Tree-fragments "screaming themselves to death," and thousands of daily deaths becoming normalized background mortality. This gap produces genuine present-day political disagreement: humans treat the triple-Thread prohibition as probably excessive and Aeravae's 487 years of stability as proof of security; elves treat any Thread repetition as an active Ashkarra-level risk and 487 years as barely tested.

## Direct parallel to the current crisis

The chronicle explicitly frames the ongoing [[The Threadbearer Crisis]] as a structural repeat: four Threadbearers now support five Thread systems, the same "one stabilization point short of functional" mathematics that caused the original Unraveling — except Threadbearers, unlike Anchors, are people who cannot be repaired, only replaced, and none are currently manifesting. Favorable differences from last time: Aeravae still functions (unlike Ashkarra's total destruction), a cooperation-infrastructure precedent already exists, and elven living witnesses remain to advise. Unfavorable differences: the current crisis stems from deliberate assassination rather than accident, no replacement Threadbearers are appearing, political fragmentation is worse than pre-Unraveling levels, and resources from Aeravae's construction have not fully recovered. Per Warden Celeborn's assessment: "we're watching the Unraveling begin again. Just faster."
