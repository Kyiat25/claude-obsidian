# The Twilight Markets

`status: canonical`. *"Violence is expensive. Trade is eternal."* A continent-spanning underground commerce network occupying the liminal threshold zones between surface civilization and [[The Hollowdeep]] — neither above nor below, where law loses its grip. Unlike thieves' guilds or assassination syndicates, the Markets function as **commerce first**: neutral ground where traditional enemies can trade without bloodshed, and the only absolute law is that contracts must be honored. A distinct, richer institution from the generic [[Black Market]] mechanic — this is its specific in-fiction implementation.

## Origins

Established ~200 years ago when displaced Deep Gnomes, Duergar refugees, and surface criminals recognized that the threshold zones between civilization and the Hollowdeep offered both sanctuary and profit as Drow expansion intensified below. Founding logic: Drow don't trade, they raid, so no negotiation is possible with the corruption spreading upward; surface authorities criminalize survival itself for refugees; and someone was always going to fill that need better through organized commerce than random violence. What began as refugee waystations became permanent infrastructure serving three functions: refugee processing (moving people out of Drow expansion zones), forbidden commerce, and neutral ground for necessary business between enemies. Every major city in [[Elarion]] hosts a Market, loosely coordinated continent-wide.

## The Council of Veils

Nine rotating seats, governed by unanimous consensus so no single faction can dominate the network. Members are called "Veils" because their real influence hides behind legitimate public identities — respectable merchants, scholars, professionals. Seats: Surface Fences & Legitimate Fronts, Hollowdeep Refugees & Underground Trade, Restricted Magic & Banned Research, Biological Goods & Medical Services, Transit Services & Identity Changes, Violent Services Liaison, Forgery & Documentation, Information Brokerage, and a rotating Neutral Arbitration seat.

**The Surface Seat, "the Daywalker"** — currently **Marisol Vex** (Human, 45), who runs the legitimate shipping front "Vex & Daughters Import/Export," operates 47 legitimate-business fronts and ~200 bribed customs officials across Elarion, and specializes in moving contraband through legal-looking paperwork. *"We're not criminals. We're economic necessity with bad paperwork."*

The full nine-seat roster: Marisol Vex (Surface), **Grindlethock "Grindle" Deepmantle** (Depths — Hollowdeep refugees and underground trade), **Sylthris Cinderveil** (Arcane — restricted magic and banned research), **Doctor Alain Morrell** (Flesh — biological goods and medical services), **Kessa Ironbreaker** (Chains — transit services and identity changes), **Varius the Blind** (Blade — violent services liaison), **the Scrivener** (Ink — forgery and documentation), **Miranna Softvoice** (Whisper — information brokerage), and **Theron Ashmark** (the current, rotating Grey Seat — neutral arbitration).

## Market Masters (city-level operations leaders)

**Kel Varyan — Thalia "Three-Tongue" Morden** (Human, 38, disgraced former Imperial translator caught selling state secrets): coordinates the Delta Exchange's information brokerage, speaks 7 languages, and — as a deliberate anti-surveillance quirk — speaks in three languages simultaneously mid-sentence. Currently offering 5,000 gp to anyone who can translate a set of pre-Compact ancient elvish religious texts.

**Corvessa — Brother Matthias the Fallen** (Human, 51, ex-Faith priest): lost his faith completely after personally witnessing [[Arc 1A - The Archivist Inquisition|Velshara's Burners]] destroy an irreplaceable historical library. Now runs the Penitent Exchange, still conducting confession sessions (therapeutic, not religious) with missionary zeal for the opposite cause — preserving forbidden knowledge. Possesses a complete list of every book the Faith has burned in the last 200 years, priced at 50,000 gp or a scholar's rescue.

**Talanthar — Whisperleaf** (Firbolg, 143, exiled druid): exiled from mainstream Greenwood society for studying death-magic during the Corvessan crusades; runs the Rootmarket's forbidden-research section.
