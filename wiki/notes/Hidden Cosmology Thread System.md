# Hidden Cosmology: What's Really Happening

> [!note] DM-only, marked canonical
> Unlike [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]] (also `dm-only` but marked `status: complete`), this source's own frontmatter marks it `status: canonical` — the strongest provenance marker seen on any cosmology source so far. Treat this as the authoritative DM-facing truth where it overlaps with or refines the earlier source.

A structured "who knows what" reveal document, built around a six-tier knowledge hierarchy, that dramatically expands and partially reframes [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]]'s cosmology.

## The knowledge hierarchy (six tiers, ascending)

| Tier | Knows | Doesn't know |
|---|---|---|
| **Mortals** (players, NPCs, most clerics) | The Five Threads weave reality; the Weave is fundamental; World Trees/Anchors stabilize it; Threadbearers manage Thread flow; the Faith of the Bound Pattern interprets it all | That [[Astralor]] and [[Aeloria]] exist at all, or that their own understanding is incomplete |
| **Gods** (elemental pantheons) | [[Astralor]] is the Overgod who created them; they exist within his Creation World; previous Creation Worlds failed; something constrains even their power | That [[Aeloria]] exists, that Astralor's creations are flawed, that the Weave *is* Aeloria's corrections |
| **Eldritch Entities** (the Eight Being-Level Eldritch Gods) | More than the elemental gods, but still incomplete — sense something is "wrong"/"incomplete" about existence, perceive paradoxes, know previous Creation Worlds left residue | The full truth about the Twins, Aeloria's existence, that [[The Whispering Dark]] is a fundamental force they may worship without knowing what it is |
| **[[Loom-Wardens]]** (secret organization) | Fate doesn't sustain itself; something corrects and guides the Weave (they call it "the Hidden Hand" or "the Eternal Weave"); the Faith is incomplete; reality needs active maintenance | Aeloria's name or nature, that they're her unknowing agents, the full cosmological structure |
| **[[Astralor]]** (the Overgod) | He creates and refines universes; this is his latest Creation World; previous attempts failed; [[The Whispering Dark]] exists and consumes | That [[Aeloria]] exists, that his creations only work *because* she stabilizes them, that [[The Restorer]] exists, that he's working within patterns he didn't create |
| **[[Aeloria]]** (the Keeper of Fate) | ~~**Nearly everything** — the complete cosmic structure, her own stabilizing role, the Whispering Dark's nature and function, the Restorer's existence, why the Weave works as it does, how to stay hidden from everyone including Astralor~~ **Superseded 2026-07-14** — see the ruling below; she does not know the Restorer exists | Her own stabilizing role and how to stay hidden from Astralor (confirmed elsewhere); ~~the Restorer's full capabilities~~ **the Restorer's existence itself**, whether the Whispering Dark does more than consume, whether anything exists beyond even her own awareness |

> [!contradiction] RESOLVED — Does "none of them know" still hold, now that Aeloria knows almost everything?
> [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]]'s "Final Truth" states flatly that none of the five Layers — including, by implication, all four Primal Forces equally — realize they're bound together into one design. This source instead gives Aeloria a starkly different epistemic position: she alone possesses "the complete cosmic structure," including full awareness of Astralor, the Whispering Dark, and the Restorer, and deliberately conceals this from everyone. That's not "none of them know" — it's "one of them knows almost everything, and hides it." Flagged as unresolved at the time — this source is marked `canonical` where the competing sources are merely `complete`, but that status difference wasn't treated as an automatic tie-breaker.
>
> **Update from a third source**: [[Twins of Creation]] (also `complete`, ingested afterward) directly contradicts *this* source's central claim instead — it states plainly that Aeloria does **not** know the Restorer exists and doesn't understand the Whispering Dark's true nature, and elevates the Restorer, not Aeloria, to the position of greatest cosmic awareness. [[Creation World]] and [[Creations Misunderstanding]] independently back Twins of Creation's specific claim.
>
> **Ruled (2026-07-14):** [[Twins of Creation]]'s account is adopted as canon — Aeloria is *not* this cosmology's most-aware force; the table above (this source's own "Nearly everything" row) is superseded on that point. This source's `canonical` status wasn't ultimately the deciding factor; the 3-of-4 source count for Twins of Creation's reading was. See [[Aeloria]], [[Twins of Creation]], and [[The Weave]] for the resolved comparison table.

## Astralor and Aeloria are "the Twins of Creation"

This source explicitly names Astralor and Aeloria together as **the Twins of Creation** — "the source of everything," unknown to mortals. [[Twins of Creation]], ingested afterward, confirms this directly and elaborates it into a full source of its own — including a direct contradiction of this source's central "Aeloria knows everything" claim (see the contradiction callout above).

## What Threadbearers are actually sensing

[[Threadbearers]] (mortal Thread-channelers, see dedicated page) unknowingly touch three layers of reality simultaneously: Aeloria's stabilizing corrections (experienced as "fate"), Astralor's raw elemental structure (experienced as elemental power), and the gaps left by the Whispering Dark's consumption and the Restorer's returns (experienced as paradox and prophetic uncertainty). The source calls this "the tragic irony" — Threadbearers are the mortals closest to cosmic truth, and are powerful precisely *because* they don't understand what they're sensing; full self-awareness might break their abilities entirely, or make them targets.

## Thread rotation, explained

Previously an unexplained in-world mystery ("nobody knows why Threads must rotate"). This source resolves it as the uncoordinated interaction of three forces: [[Aeloria]] adjusts probability to prevent any one Thread dominating a location too long; [[The Whispering Dark]] consumes the "excess" energy that sustained single-Thread alignment accumulates, creating pressure for change; [[The Restorer]] returns previously successful rotation patterns so the system isn't fully random. None of the three coordinate with each other — the appearance of a coherent pattern is emergent, not designed, which is explicitly why no single god or mortal theology can explain it.

### Worked example: the Ashkarra Catastrophe

A specific historical catastrophe used as a case study — three consecutive Spirit Thread alignments at the same location, where the three forces went from complementary to actively working against each other: Aeloria could no longer stabilize the saturation, the Dark's consumption couldn't keep pace with the excess, and the Restorer had no genuine past success to draw from, so it "returned" a previous failure state instead. The source's own conclusion: triple alignment isn't catastrophic merely from Thread saturation — it's catastrophic because the three cosmic forces became **desynchronized and worked against each other**.

## The Loom-Wardens

A secret organization operating within and around the Faith of the Bound Pattern, manipulating fate and theology while genuinely believing they serve something else entirely (the Weave, "the Hidden Hand," or forces predating the gods). In truth, every one of their actions unknowingly serves Aeloria. Detailed on its own page: [[Loom-Wardens]].

## World Trees: a deliberate, permanent mystery

Ancient leyline stabilizers predating World Anchors by millennia — and the source is explicit that their true origin is **meant to stay unknown, even to the DM**. Five competing theories are given (Astralor's first design, Aeloria's living manifestations, the Restorer's recovered work, spontaneous Weave phenomena, or something older than the Twins themselves) with no canonical answer. Detailed on its own page: [[World Trees]].

## DM usage guidance: the three-tier reveal system

The source closes with explicit guidance on what to reveal and when, structured as three tiers of in-fiction knowledge (matching, but not identical to, the six-tier hierarchy above): what everyone experiences (Thread system, World Anchors, Faith theology), what scholars/mystics suspect (something guides fate beyond the gods), and the cosmic truth (this document, DM-only). Explicit instruction: never fully reveal Aeloria even if players discover her — "reality should always have unexplored depths."
