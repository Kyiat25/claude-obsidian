# Arc 3A — The Architect of Souls ("The Ascending Throne")

The full deep-dive behind [[Campaign Arcs (The Great Deceivers and Truth-Seekers)|Category 3: The Ascenders]]'s flagship arc, v3.0 "pantheon-integrated." Designed to stand alone or run as a sequel to Arc 1B.

## Archmagus Therion the Unbound

Human, 90, former High Threadbearer of Spirit Thread in [[Kel Varyan]] for 40 years, who during deep Pattern communion concluded: the Pattern is an impersonal, programmable force, not divine will; Divine Spark gods achieved authority by hacking Glyph structure and anchoring belief (Soltharion rewrote dragonborn identity and claimed credit; [[Vel'Zyren]] rewrote elven identity into the Drow); and if gods can do it, so can he. He staged his own death 20 years ago and has pursued a five-stage plan since: (1, complete) modify his own Personal Glyphs for extended life and proto-divine status; (2, in progress) build a cult of 537 "Rewritten" followers physiologically incapable of doubting him; (3, prepared) a city-wide ritual to convert Kel Varyan's 8.5 million people simultaneously; (4, theoretical) spread to the other Loom-Cities; (5, unknown) possibly anchor to the Elemental Glyphs themselves.

## Proof of concept: Archmagus Lyssa the Transcendent

A separate ascender who successfully became a genuine Tier 2 minor deity after 15 years, now ruling as a regional deity over ~2,000 worshippers. Her success is Therion's evidence the whole premise works — he intends to skip past her cautious Tier 2 approach and go directly for Tier 1 Major God status within 72 hours, a pace that threatens the setting's divine population capacity limits.

## The horror the arc runs on

Therion's technique genuinely works. His converted cultists are genuinely, measurably happier. He genuinely cares for them. The question the arc is built to not answer cleanly: if manufactured divinity functions identically to "natural" divinity and makes people happier, is it actually wrong to stop him?
