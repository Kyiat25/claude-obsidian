# Umbral Veil (the Shadowfell)

The Shadowfell is not merely a dark mirror of the [[Creation World]] — it is its **fading memory**, the weight of all things lost. Unlike the ever-changing [[Luminous Echo]], the Shadowfell is a realm where time slows, echoes linger, and entropy binds reality in stillness. Not destruction — **stagnation**, where things persist long after they should have faded into nothingness.

## Connection to leylines

When a Prime World leyline weakens, its Shadowfell counterpart grows stronger, draining energy into entropy. If a leyline is completely severed, its energy lingers here as residual echoes of what once was — landscapes turn gray, time slows, sound and sensation mute. The leylines here are frayed, brittle, and cold, pulling inward and consuming vitality rather than surging outward like their Feywild counterparts.

## The Primal Glyphs in the Shadowfell

The Glyph of Endings dominates here, ensuring all things eventually succumb to stagnation. The Glyph of Threads doesn't weave new fates — it ensnares the past, binding lost moments into endless cycles. The Glyph of Echoes ensures nothing is truly forgotten: memories, lost souls, and forgotten spells all linger in the Veil's silence. The Glyph of Will, which represents creation in the Prime World, here becomes a force of endurance — things persist indefinitely, refusing to fade, rather than being made anew.

## The Laws of the Umbral Veil

1. **Law of Fracture** — all things break; every truth here is a half-truth, reflected until meaning thins into void.
2. **Law of Silence** — names, sounds, and thoughts lose weight; words decay unless engraved with memory.
3. **Law of Reversal** — cause follows effect, choices precede will; destinies undo themselves retroactively.
4. **Law of Recurrence (Inverted)** — what dies here may rise again, but only as a loop, a fragment, or a memory mistaking itself for flesh.

## The Hollow

At the Shadowfell's heart lies The Hollow, where all leylines collapse into pure stagnation and even memories fade. Magic falters, crumbles, or drains the caster's own essence; entire decades pass in the Prime World while only days elapse within it; those who stay too long risk losing their sense of self entirely, becoming just another wandering shade.

## As the graveyard of the Weave

Taverns remain full of spectral patrons reliving the same night over and over, unaware ages have passed. Ruined castles flicker with echoes of their former glory, replaying great battles long lost to history. Once-powerful mages repeat their incantations endlessly, minds unable to escape the moment their spells unraveled. Not undeath, but an endless, dreamlike half-existence — what was refuses to be forgotten, yet can never truly move forward.
