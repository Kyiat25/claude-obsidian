# The Hollowdeep — the Living Underdark

*"It is not a basement beneath the world. It is another world entirely, one that dreams in stone and bleeds in shadow."* — Keth'mira the Threadseeker, Last Testament before the Silent Descent

Not merely an underground realm but a **metaphysical wound** in the Weave of [[Dór-o Estel]], a network of chambers and impossible geometries existing beneath every continent, connected through ley-scarred passages that ignore surface geography. Where the surface's Five Threads maintain elemental order, the Hollowdeep runs on **decayed Thread-logic** — echo-magic and memory-resonance where thought itself becomes terrain. It is alive as an ecosystem of interconnected awareness: the stones remember, the shadows hunger.

## The Seven Descending Layers

**I. The Threshold Reaches** (0.5-2 miles down) — the contested borderland where surface races seek fortune: Goblin mining cartels (~280,000, extracting mythril and thread-crystals), the **Smugglers Highway** (a neutral criminal-cartel-run trade network moving contraband and refugees), and the ruined vaults of fallen surface civilizations.

**II. The Woundskin Layer** and **III. The Murkmire Deep** — intermediate transition zones (not deeply catalogued this pass).

**IV. The Embervault Rift** — a volcanic transition layer (not deeply catalogued this pass).

**V. The Echofold** (17-27 miles down) — psionic physics where thoughts have weight and memories become geography; home to the semi-sentient **Echovault Archive** (trades knowledge for visitors' memories), the psionic Gnome **Mind-Key Spiral** enclaves (who navigate by systematically forgetting), and the **Unspun Hollows** (anti-magic zones where the Thread cannot exist, inhabited by beings adapted to exist without any magic at all).

**VI. The Vaultmarrow** (27-42 miles down) — the archaeological graveyard layer: the **Ninth Coil Ruins** (a city that tried to control time and folded inward into a recursive temporal loop), the **Mycelium Accord Grounds** (a diplomatic meeting between Fungal and Dwarven civilizations that ended with the two sides literally growing into each other), and the **Blackglass Wastes** (a crater of emotion-reflecting obsidian glass, its weather triggered by feeling rather than temperature).

**VII. The Nameless Dominion** (42+ miles down) — the deepest, most alien layer, predating the Weave itself, home to Eldritch entities that were never conquered by the Threads but woven *around*. Contains the **Inverted Cathedral** (where staircases erase memory with each step and visitors must shed their entire selfhood to exist there), the **Black Spiral Root** (a vertical pit-city under a dead World Anchor, its walls lined with neural coral storing ancestral trauma), the **Sea of Still Eyes** (a lake reflecting guilt instead of light, home to whale-like Thought Lords called the Blind That Sing), and the **Bleeding Glyph Hive** (a wall of runes that grow, hunt, and breed).

[[Selveran]] secretly harbors ~50 drow refugees who fled the Hollowdeep and renounced their former culture.

## Two Vaultmarrow ruins in more detail

The **Ninth Coil Ruins** exist in multiple time periods simultaneously — visitors may meet past, present, and future versions of the same trapped inhabitants, most unaware they're looping. Its still-partially-functional Chronocraft Engines are studied by multiple [[Campaign Arcs (The Great Deceivers and Truth-Seekers)|campaign-arc BBEGs]] as a source of time-manipulation technique (most directly [[Arc 4A - The Death-Rewriter]] and [[Arc 4C - The Eldritch Rewriter]]).

The **Mycelium Accord Grounds** hold the Diplomatic Archives of the failed fungal-dwarven alliance — records proving peaceful underground coexistence was once possible, now suppressed by the current Drow regime — and living Hybrid Consciousness beings, merged stone-and-spore minds who remember both civilizations across thousands of years and can serve as unique information sources (or allies) on underground history predating Drow rule, relevant to [[Arc 2C - The Drow Revelator]].
