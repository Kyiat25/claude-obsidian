# The Hollow Thread: The First Corrupted Leyline

> *"It was not a prison. It was a patch. And it is fraying."* — Thamur, The Guardian Below
> *"The Weave was rewritten once. It can be rewritten again."* — [[Vel'Zyren]], The Web-Spinner

## What it is

A leyline that doesn't carry magic — it carries **unbeing**. Where normal leylines flow with arcane energy, this one bleeds backwards, rotting all connections it touches with retroactive erasure. Corrupted by [[The Whispering Dark]] to the point it no longer follows the laws of the Weave: instead of carrying energy, connecting leylines, and supporting reality's structure, it erases magical energy retroactively, unravels other leylines it connects to, and undermines the structure of reality itself.

**The terrible truth**: it is not a broken leyline — it is a leyline that *remembers nonexistence*. It exists in a state of anti-existence: present but rejecting presence, flowing but denying flow, connected but unraveling connection. "The wound where reality remembers what it's like to not be."

## The origin wound

In the Age Before Ages, when [[Astralor]] forged reality and [[Aeloria]] wove fate into its structure, the Weave was imperfect but stable. One Glyph was woven too tightly — contradictory in its essence, expressing something reality could not sustain (its exact nature is lost, possibly deliberately erased). [[The Whispering Dark]] consumed this contradictory Glyph, intending mercy — removing the flaw cleanly. It didn't. The void left behind was so profound it punched through the layer of reality where leylines flow. The struck leyline didn't disappear; it inverted, carrying not energy but absence, not connection but severance, not existence but the memory of nonexistence.

**Why it can't simply be destroyed**: per Gaeldros's Divine Sentence, it's already destroyed (existing in a state of having been erased); destroying it would validate its nature, teaching other leylines how to "remember" nonexistence; its death would spread like infection through every connected leyline; and the Weave has no framework for deleting deletion. The only option: containment — not destruction, not healing, not transformation, but burial so deep even the Weave forgets where to look.

## The Hollow Vault: where it's sealed

**Location**: unknown, even to the gods who sealed it — deliberately. [[Earth Pantheon|Thamur]] sealed it with a name erased from the world, making it impossible to speak, think, or navigate toward. What's known: deep beneath Earth's crust, under mountains [[Earth Pantheon|Vatharun]] folded over it, wrapped in stone-runes ([[Earth Pantheon|Orivak]]'s work) resisting conceptual collapse, bound by a law with no precedent ([[Earth Pantheon|Gaeldros]]'s Edict Unbound). Even the Spirit Pantheon has lost its coordinates. Suspected locations: beneath the ruins of [[Ashkarra]], under an ancient pre-continental mountain range, or somewhere that "used to be somewhere else" before geography was rewritten.

### The four-phase sealing

1. **Isolation** ([[Earth Pantheon|Vatharun]]) — folded mountains over the site, physically severing the leyline's surface connection.
2. **Runic Binding** ([[Earth Pantheon|Orivak]]) — anti-collapse runes forming a quarantine network that slows infection by reinforcing the identity of surrounding leylines.
3. **Legal Imprisonment** ([[Earth Pantheon|Gaeldros]]) — the Edict Unbound, a law stating the sealed thing "shall not be unsealed... that which is named shall have no name... that which exists shall not be acknowledged to exist" — a deliberate legal paradox.
4. **Memory Erasure** ([[Earth Pantheon|Thamur]]) — removed the location's true name from written records, spoken language, thought, memory, divine knowledge, and the Weave's own structure. Even those who sealed it can no longer remember where "there" is.

### The Vault's four layers

1. **The Forgetting Zone** — memory becomes unreliable; thoughts about the Vault slide off consciousness.
2. **The Reality Scar** — physical space becomes unreliable, passages lead nowhere/everywhere, time flows wrong; Eldritch influence leaks upward here.
3. **The Seal Proper** — mountains folded over themselves, impossibly dense stone, anti-light runes; no mortal has reached this layer and returned sane.
4. **The Thread Itself** — unknown, unverifiable; those who claim to have reached it retroactively never went.

### Bleed-through symptoms (even sealed)

Geological (mountains that forget their own names, appearing/disappearing caves, seismic events with no epicenter), magical (moving dead magic zones, spells that retroactively never happened, flickering leyline junctions), mental (miners forgetting their names, dreams of impossible spaces), and Eldritch (more frequent Eldritch Moons, easier Hollow Breach formation, more frequent Tether Spirit manifestation).

## Why it must never be freed: the cascading collapse

If unsealed, reality doesn't explode — it **forgets itself**, escalating over a fixed timeline:

| Stage | Scale | Key effect |
|---|---|---|
| Hour One | 100-mile radius | Leylines unravel retroactively; magic becomes unstable, not absent |
| Day One | ~500-mile radius | Regions vanish from maps with no memory they existed; people forget their names mid-sentence |
| Week One | Continental | The Five Elemental Glyphs (Fire/Earth/Water/Air/Spirit) begin misremembering their purpose — Spirit's corruption is worst, since memory and identity themselves become unreliable |
| Month One | Global | Personal Glyphs collapse first (loss of self), then Form Glyphs (biology stops making sense), then Elemental Glyphs, then Universal Glyphs fracture one by one |
| Month Two | Planar | [[The Null Reflection]] merges with reality; even the Eldritch Gods, having achieved their goal, dissolve into the nothing they sought; [[Vel'Zyren]] becomes lost in a reality she can no longer reshape |
| Month Three | Total | Existence stops — not with collapse, but by retroactively never having started; the universe is forgotten, even by itself |

Even the True Powers are stymied at this endpoint: [[Astralor]] cannot forge what doesn't exist to be forged upon; [[Aeloria]] cannot weave fate in a universe with no causality; [[The Restorer]] cannot reclaim erasure this complete.

## Those who contain it

**Knowing Guardians** — full profiles on [[Earth Pantheon]] (Thamur, Orivak, Gaeldros, Vatharun) and [[Spirit Pantheon]] (Aelys, Nyzara); plus the Deep Law (dwarven religion, unknowingly avoids certain depths through tradition alone) and Hidden Guardians (the Silent Monks of Thamur, certain Threadbearers unknowingly positioned to reinforce the sealing network).

**Unknowing Guardians** — the Faith of the Bound Pattern (Thread Rotation and World Anchor maintenance reinforce containment without the Faith knowing why), the Woven Reverence (World Tree protection provides natural stabilization), the Burning Concord (Ashkarra's destruction, while catastrophic, actually removed a destabilizing element and reset local Weave conditions), and ordinary mortals (superstitious avoidance of "cursed" regions is itself a defense — collective ignorance prevents the mass-seeking that would guarantee discovery).

## Those who seek to release it

**The Eldritch Gods** want total unmaking — the Hollow Thread has already done the hard work of remembering nonexistence, so releasing it lets reality do the rest. [[Nhal'Zereth]], [[Xey'Tazun]], [[Ul'Naresh]], [[Ith'Quess]], and [[Esh'Tarah]] each contribute a distinct method (eroding memory of the seal, breaking its causality, planting false location-memories, undermining its physical anchors, or replacing its guardians with corrupted copies). Three named mortal cult types pursue release for different reasons: the Hollow Seekers (archaeological expeditions), the Void Prophets (dream-walkers following Ul'Naresh's visions), and the Un-weavers (deliberate, methodical saboteurs — the most dangerous).

**[[Vel'Zyren]]** wants something different: not destruction but ownership, a controlled breach to erase Aeloria's current Weave and rebuild it under her own authorship. Full profile on her own page.

**Why neither has succeeded yet**: the seal is genuinely excellent (multiple god-level entities cooperating), the location is truly unknown even to Eldritch entities, guardians are vigilant and lethal toward investigators, the Eldritch prefer incremental long-game weakening over direct assault, and different Eldritch entities have slightly different goals that occasionally interfere with each other.

## The cascading failure scenario: how Ashkarra nearly broke everything

[[Ashkarra]]'s fall was a near-apocalypse, not just a city's destruction — a perfect storm of geographic coincidence (built near the Hollow Vault region, its leyline network connected), Thread stagnation (the confirmed third consecutive Spirit alignment), World Tree severing (Ashkarra's Tree was cut to build its World Anchor, removing natural containment), and divine hubris (the Dragonborn Empire dismissed all warnings). The hidden truth: the Hollow Thread sensed the opportunity through the stagnation, containment weakened from the compounding factors, the Eldritch Moons that manifested were symptoms rather than causes, the seal cracked (not broke) in the First Hollow Breach, and Ashkarra was retroactively unmade in certain ways rather than simply destroyed. The Earth gods' emergency response — reinforcing the seal and letting Ashkarra fall completely — reset local Weave conditions and averted catastrophe by inches.

### The Grand Equation (as given verbatim)

```
Containment Strength = (Earth Gods × Spirit Gods × Faith Practices × World Anchors × World Trees) ÷ Eldritch Pressure

When: Thread stagnation ÷2 · World Anchor falls ÷1.5 · World Tree severed ÷1.3 · Eldritch Moon rises ÷1.1/moon · Hollow Breach forms ÷1.2 · Vel'Zyren gains power ÷1.4
If total < 1.0 = Seal fails
```

Currently hovering around 1.3-1.5 (stable but declining); it was near 0.9 (near-failure) immediately post-Ashkarra. Active sabotage could push it below 1.0 within decades — without intervention, the timeline runs from continued fraying (next 50 years) to seal failure becoming inevitable (500+ years).

## Everything connects back to containment

Per the source's own "Grand Web of Containment" section: the Five Elemental Threads, the Faith of the Bound Pattern's Test of Threads and Threadbearer system, the five [[Loom-Cities|World Anchors]] (forming an unwitting containment ring around the Hollow Vault region), and [[World Trees]] (an organic backup system, pressure-valving excess Weave energy) all turn out to serve containment whether their custodians know it or not. Even pantheon conflicts are framed as a feature, not a bug — no single element dominating prevents the Thread stagnation that weakens the seal.

## Campaign integration

**Revelation tiers**: Tier 1 (symptoms — strange geology, forgetting), Tier 2 (pattern recognition — Faith concerned but not panicking), Tier 3 (learning something is sealed beneath Earth, meeting Thamur's guardians), Tier 4 (the full truth and a genuine choice). **Endgame options, all with real costs and no "correct" answer**: preserve the seal (accept the necessary evil of killing investigators), find an alternative (immense sacrifice, uncertain), attempt a controlled release (astronomically risky), or accept a partial breach (sacrifice a region to relieve pressure on the whole).

## The terrible irony

The seal's greatest protection is that almost no one knows it exists. If everyone knew, everyone would seek it — curiosity, power-hunger, academic interest all becoming Eldritch or Vel'Zyren pawns, making accidental discovery probable and risking the very mass panic that could destabilize the seal. So the gods keep the secret and let mortals believe they're arbitrary tyrants, that the Faith is dogmatic without reason, that some places are simply cursed. **Ignorance is survival** — but ignorance also means warnings get dismissed as superstition and practices get abandoned as outdated, until eventually ignorance fails and everyone learns what the gods have been hiding.
