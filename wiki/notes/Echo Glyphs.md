# Echo Glyphs

`status: testing`. *"All glyphs speak. Some echo."* The third and most personal tier of glyphic magic, completing the taxonomy alongside [[Primal Glyphs|Primary Glyphs]] (eternal cosmic truths) and [[Secondary Glyphs]] (the structured mortal framework): a mutable, memory-bound reflection of magic — "a whisper of how a spell *wants* to be cast," shaped by place, lineage, will, or trauma rather than taught in academies. Different cultures name the same phenomenon differently: **Thread Glyphs** (Faith of the Bound Pattern), **Mnemonic Edits** (gnomish spirit-scholars), **Glyph-Mutations** (those who study Eldritch taint), **Legacy Echoes** (ancestral/shamanic traditions), **Void Whispers** (when sourced from corrupted leylines).

## Mechanics

An Echo Glyph is a spell-specific modification — a small rune, phrase, or mental resonance bound to one known spell, changing *how* it behaves without altering its core identity: adjusted casting time or range, changed elemental flavor, modified targets/duration/save mechanics, or new trade-offs (bonus damage for shortened range). One Echo Glyph per spell; active Echo Glyphs limited to `level + Proficiency Bonus` (minimum 1); bound during a long rest, spirit rite, or ritual reflection at a glyph anchor/leyline nexus/memory pool; no spell-slot cost unless stated; only one Echo Glyph affects a given casting, though it can combine with Metamagic or other effects. A source-internal [[Unstable Magic Zones|Spell Critical]] outcome, "Echo Glyph Surge," is referenced as a related consequence elsewhere in this cluster.
