# The Ember's Echo (Kobold religion)

*"We were not born. We were struck."*

Kobolds follow no formal pantheon, worshipping instead **shards of greater truths** — fragments and forgotten sparks of other gods' creation, centered on the **Broken Flame**, sacred precisely because it is cracked and unfinished. Not related mechanically to the FEARS playable-lineage roster (Kobolds are not a documented player lineage in this wiki), but relevant as worldbuilding texture and a possible NPC/monster-culture faith.

## Origin myth

A forgotten strike of the fire-god Rhoris fell deep into the world's crust, igniting a hollow obsidian seam and giving rise to kobolds — "the burnt memory of divine intent, crawling from the stone like a whispered apology." Not dragons, not beasts: leftover divinity.

## The Four Sacred Figures

Kobolds re-cast fragments of other pantheons' gods as their own reflected figures, echoing the Gnomish Wonder-Tithe Codex's re-indexing practice but independently arrived at: Grelthak the Gnawed (reflects the Dwarven god Grathis — cunning, traps), Veltraza the Ash-Womb (reflects Dalvora — brood-care, rebirth), Scarn the Ember-Drowned (reflects Thamur — sacrifice, silence), and Chiktchik (reflects Nyzara — lore, mimicry, joyful madness — and is viewed with rising unease as possibly Eldritch Moon-born in the current age).

## Practice

Ember Carving (a maturity rite — a glowing glyph carved into tunnel stone), the Ashbirth Ceremony (ash smeared on a new clutch's heads, retelling Veltraza's myth), the Chitterfast Reckoning (a lying/storytelling contest whose winner becomes Echo-Speaker), and Flame-Hollow Oaths (a shared ember carried in separate lanterns until a deal is fulfilled).

## Cultural stance

Fire is sacred but treated cautiously as identity rather than domination; dragons are envied and resented as "the arrogant siblings who flew away"; tunnels are holy, each one an echo of Veltraza's womb; sacrifice in defense of the warren is the highest virtue.

## Interfaith relations

Warmest with the Spirit Pantheon (especially Nyzara) and with Halflings (mutual fondness, shared storytelling culture); reserved tolerance from Dwarves and Gnomes; hostility from the Burning Concord (historical enslavement of kobolds) and from Drow, who hunt or sacrifice kobold followers. Tolerant but dismissive treatment from [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]], which regards kobold belief as folkloric rather than a spiritual threat.
