# Glyph Binding

`status: testing`. *"What is carved into the world cannot be unmade by mere speech."* An optional style of magic: inscribing, embedding, or forging glyphs directly into reality — flesh, metal, stone, or memory — as a living interaction with the Weave rather than fixed-formula rune magic. The practitioner becomes a shaper of magical continuity, not merely a spellcaster; the body or tools become living glyphic conduits. Practiced across cultures — Valikan Forgebinders storm/fire-sealed axes and gauntlets triggering on rage or war-chant, Dwarven Anvil-Glyphics treating inscription as spiritual debt to the Forge-Spirits — and regarded by [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]] as sacred, but potentially dangerous if misused.

## Key themes

Permanent magic (glyphs persist and channel/store magic over time, rather than being cast once and gone), tangible spellcraft (magic is burned, carved, or sung into the world rather than merely spoken), bound triggers (spells tied to conditions, oaths, time delays, or emotional states), and personal inscription (the mage's body becomes the spellbook).

## Optional mechanics

**Glyph-Scribe** (feat) — inscribe one cantrip or 1st-level spell onto an item once per day, usable until triggered. **Living Glyph** (mutation/boon) — a tattooed glyph shifts location on the body, changing which resistances or spell effects it grants. **Soul-Scribed Tome** (downtime project) — a personal glyphic grimoire binding spells to emotion or ancestry. A level-gated spell list re-flavors standard spells as glyphic effects (e.g. Glyph of Warding at 3rd, Contingency and Forbiddance at 6th+ as world-glyph barriers).
