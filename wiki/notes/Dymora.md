# Dymora, The Mire-Keeper

*"The river carries. The sea takes. I keep. Ask anything that has ever stepped into me whether there is a difference."*

**Domains**: swamps and mires, the slow hold, shelter in the unwanted places, preservation by bog. **Symbol**: a footprint filling slowly with dark water. **Worshiped by**: marsh-folk, bog-iron gatherers, fugitives, herbalists of the wet country.

The dry world files her with the rot-spirits; marsh-folk file her with the guardians — the same mire that swallows a tax-collector's horse also hides a village from an invading army. She keeps *everything* that enters, uncorrupted, for a thousand years: leather, oath-tokens, the honoured dead of the marsh clans, kept better than any tomb. Marsh clans give their honoured dead to the bog rather than the ground, with a wooden token naming them, "so she knows they are lent, not lost." Draining works begin, where they dare, with a spoken apology into the last pool — and the fen-folk keep count of the works that skipped it.
