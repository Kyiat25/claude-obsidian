# Elemental Glyphs

Third-Law Glyphs shaping how leylines and magic physically manifest in the world — the classical five elements, framed as still-fluid testing material rather than fixed physics.

| Glyph | Cosmic influence |
|---|---|
| **Glyph of Fire** | Governs ley surges, magical overloads, unstable magical storms |
| **Glyph of Water** | Ensures leylines remain adaptable, shifting location when necessary |
| **Glyph of Earth** | Dictates leyline stability, preventing collapse from entropy |
| **Glyph of Air** | Governs teleportation and the communication of magic across distances |
| **Glyph of Spirit** | Binds memory, prophecy, and ancestral echoes to leylines — differs from the others in shaping *meaning*, not just matter |

Explains why magic and physics behave inconsistently across different cycles/settings: the elements are still being refined, not yet locked in. These correspond directly to the Five Threads of the Weave documented elsewhere in this cluster (Fire/Water/Earth/Air/Spirit). [[The Hollow Thread]]'s cascading-failure timeline describes exactly these five glyphs "misremembering their purpose" in sequence during a Week One infection scenario, with Spirit's corruption framed as the worst since it governs memory and identity themselves.
