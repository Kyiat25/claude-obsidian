# The Mirror Chain — "The Shattered Continent"

A vast equatorial archipelago (6.2 million km², population ~9 million) roughly 1,500 miles west-southwest of [[Elarion]]'s southern coast, spanning ~2,500 miles east-west along a volcanic arc. Hundreds to possibly thousands of islands, from continent-scale landmasses to unmapped rocks, named for volcanic islands that come in mirrored pairs across narrow straits, their still waters producing disorienting perfect reflections. Some scholars believe it was once a single landmass shattered by ancient cataclysm. Home to [[Selveran]], the Spirit Thread Loom-City (called here "Selveran Skygrove," population figures for it vary between sources — see [[Selveran]] for the primary treatment).

## Major powers

**The Coral Dominion** (~2.8 million, Triton/Sea Elf/Human thalassocracy) — cities grown from living coral that expand and self-heal, governed by a Council of Tides, controls most inter-island trade and holds the Mirror Chain's strongest navy. **The Tide-Fanged Clans** (~700,000, pirate confederacy of dispossessed sailors and exiles) — "pirates with principles" who never attack fishing vessels and honor ransoms, electing an Admiral-Supreme annually, in ongoing conflict with the Coral Dominion. **Tamal-Khera**, "the Dust-Sung Archive" (~500,000, Human/Earth Genasi/Spirit-touched) — a true desert island despite being surrounded by ocean, where oral contracts are said to bind souls.

## Government: no unified authority

A patchwork of competing systems: the Coral Dominion's rotating noble-house thalassocracy; Selveran's fey-elven vertical council, balanced by Spirit Thread obligations and the Memory Grove keepers' unofficial influence; the Tide-Fanged Clans' pirate confederacy (annual Admiral-Supreme elections); Tamal-Khera's oral confederation (Word-Walkers and Clay Priests, laws literally sand-song-bound onto participants); **Rak'Atlal**, theocratic pyramid city-states ruled by Bone-chanter priest-kings, loosely confederated for mutual defense but frequently at internal war; and assorted merchant-republic oligarchies and tribal confederacies on the smaller islands. Selveran and Tamal-Khera both function as neutral arbitration ground for inter-island disputes; full-scale wars are rare since they're too disruptive to trade.
