# The Way of the Named (Flame)

*"We are not the chosen. We are the choosers. We are not born. We become. The first name spoken was not a prayer. It was a truth."*

Followed by many Half-Elves across Dór-o Estel — not a religion of gods, but of self-made myth, forged from exile, trial, and the deliberate claiming of a name.

## Origin myth

When the Weave was young and the Pattern already set, a nameless thread trembled and asked: "if I am a thread, can I choose the shape of the tapestry?" The gods did not answer, but the Weave "shivered, as if amused" and wove the question's answer in beside the Pattern rather than rejecting it. From that first self-claimed Name came the Named — souls belonging to no pantheon's domain, chosen into being by their own breath and echo. Aelys wept and said "even the Pattern must grow"; Soltharion burned with scorn; the Eldritch listened with curiosity and, for once, did not devour.

## The Three Cycles of Becoming

1. **The Departure (First Break)** — a purposeful separation from family, homeland, or comfort; the kindling of the flame.
2. **The Trial (Ashen Path)** — three unchosen, recognized-in-life events: a Betrayal, a Mercy, and an unundoable Choice, each leaving a metaphysical Flame-Scar.
3. **The Naming (Return With Fire)** — after the third trial, a Chosen Name is publicly declared and witnessed — only then is a Half-Elf considered a true Named Flame.

## Death: the Three Embers

Upon death, a follower's soul becomes a **Trail-Star** (Name truly earned — becomes a guiding light for others' Trials), an **Ember-Lingerer** (Naming incomplete or cut short — lingers near ash-pits, seeking recognition), or **Cold Ash** (lived under false names or avoided Trial — simply forgotten, not punished).

## Relationship to the gods

Does not deny the gods but claims none, and is claimed by none — believing the gods "skipped a thread" and Half-Elves were born in the pause between a divine breath and its intention. Aelys, Thirian, Selmira, Vaelora, and Nyzara are acknowledged as sympathetic "Reflected Flames" (not patrons); Gaeldros, Vel'Zyren, Soltharion, Marethis, and Ishara are rejected outright for enforcing inherited law, obedience, or ancestry against the faith's core value of self-naming. Notably rejects all Fire gods as authority figures, since "the flame is not divine, it is the self burning bright" — fire in this faith is internal, not granted.

## Relationship to other faiths

Deep kinship with [[The Ashen Testament]] (Tieflings, "Ash-Bound Kin," the **Ashen Accord**) and [[The Path of the Horizon Soul]] ("Those Who Burn Skyward"); tolerated but watched by [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]]; friction with [[The Burning Concord]]; forbidden entirely under [[The Ashen Web]], where practitioners are executed. In dialogue with [[The Weave Unbroken]]'s Five Layers, the faith claims Half-Elves are "the missing tenth Pattern" — deliberately absent from the Form Glyphs' nine categories, and sacred for that absence.
