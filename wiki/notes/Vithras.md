# Vithras, The Icebound Warden

*"The drowned belong to Tzhor. The frozen belong to me. Do not ask which of us the ice-drowned belong to. We have not finished asking each other."*

**Domains**: death by cold, souls held in ice, the preserved dead, the unfinished passage. **Symbol**: a hand beneath clear ice, palm open. **Worshiped by**: arctic burial-keepers, ice-fishers, the kin of those lost on frozen crossings.

Where the drowned are kept by Tzhor, Vithras holds the frozen — the difference is doctrine. The drowned are gone into the deep; the frozen are still here, visible, preserved, waiting. Every spring thaw is a theological event: what melts, moves on. Ice-coast dead are interred in glacier-niches, facing outward, so they can watch for the thaw that will be theirs. Those who find a preserved body in the ice must leave it and mark the place — taking it is theft from Vithras, and the thief's own crossing will find thin ice.
