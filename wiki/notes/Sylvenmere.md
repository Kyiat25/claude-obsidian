# Sylvenmere — the Singing Grove of Memory

*"The Sacred Forest That Trusted Too Much."* A ~12,000-year-old grove sanctuary on the northern [[Kerythra]] coast, 40 miles south of the Imperial trading post "The Hook," home to 50+ ancient World Trees whose growth rings preserve dendrochronological proof that civilization predates the gods. Population ~500-600 before [[The Burning of Sylvenmere|the 487 FA attack]] that this page's companion event page documents. This page covers what Sylvenmere *was*.

## The Three Circles

Governed simply, by contrast with [[Talanthar]]'s complex political system, through the **Circle of Roots** — seven elders: **Elder Loralyth Songkeeper** (Wood Elf, 687, First Speaker and chief Cycle-Singer, died defending the trees), **Thornwhisper the Ancient** (Wood Elf, 1,124, Master Druid with direct tree-consciousness communion, evacuated successfully), **Scholar Mareth Brightwater** (Human, 68, a former Faith scholar who'd fled persecution decades earlier, captured and executed as a martyr), **Joybinder Mira Softfoot** (Halfling, 83, whose relentless optimism "typifies the community's fatal flaw," survived to lead refugee integration), **Root-Speaker Kethran Deeproot** (Wood Elf, 445, the first to voice suspicion of the new traders — ignored — died on the evacuation boat), **Healer Selwyn Gentletouch** (Half-Elf, 127, stayed too long treating the wounded, barely survived), and **Star-Reader Vynnia Nightsky** (Wood Elf, 312, prophesied "ships bring fire," dismissed as too abstract — survived to become a key witness).

## The tragic irony

Where [[Talanthar]] spent centuries building paranoid defenses against theological persecution, Sylvenmere met Imperial traders with open friendship and freely shared its sacred knowledge — never understanding that every helpful answer was intelligence for its future destroyers. Multigenerational stability (many families had lived there for centuries) bred a 12,000-year tradition of trusting openness over suspicion that proved fatal once Imperial trade expansion (484-486 FA) brought the grove to outside attention.

## The Nine Ancients

Among the 50+ World Trees (250-350 ft tall, semi-sentient, root-interconnected via mycelial network), nine named elders stood out as the grove's oldest living evidence: **Skyfather** (14,200 years, tallest at 387 ft, rings holding pre-Fracturing climate data), **Rootmother** (13,800 years, widest trunk at 47 ft diameter, a sacred hollow-base chamber used for the grove's most important ceremonies), and **the Twins, Starsong & Moonsong** (12,600 years, intertwined roots growing in perfect synchronization, their combined canopy forming a natural cathedral used for weddings). Each tree's genetic and growth-ring evidence independently supported the same conclusion Velshara and Kethyr each separately verified elsewhere: natural evolution and germination, not divine creation.
