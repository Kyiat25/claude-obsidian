# The Reflected Realms of the Weave

*"Every leyline casts a shadow. Every spell, a dream."* Not other planes in the traditional sense but **living reflections of the Prime World**, shaped by its ley lines, glyphs, and the breath of the Weave — unbound, reactive, and volatile where the Prime World is structured and cyclical. They represent what magic might become without balance.

## The Luminous Echo (the Feywild equivalent)

The Weave's dream-state: untethered potential, emotion-bound creation, recursive story. Time loops, memories gain mass, emotions shape reality via Personal Glyph resonance, and ley lines exist as mirrored exaggerations of their Prime counterparts. Contains **the Unstable Heart**, the nexus of Wild Magic. Governed by the Law of Reflection, Law of Belief, Law of Exaggeration, and Law of Recurrence — "fate is rewritten through whim, and nothing is ever truly lost, only transformed."

## The Umbral Veil (the Shadowfell equivalent)

*"This is not death. This is what memory becomes when it is unloved."* The Weave's grief: a record of what could not be, what should not remain, what the Weave would forget if it could. Forms where ley lines collapse and Personal Glyphs fray without being fully erased. Time collapses inward, magic decays into repeating glyph-fragments, memories persist independently as echo-beings and dreamless specters, and **the Hollow** — a dead glyph well where Severance eats reality — lies at its heart. Governed by the (fraying, not binding) Law of Fracture, Law of Silence, Law of Reversal, and an inverted Law of Recurrence.
