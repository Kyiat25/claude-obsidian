# Water Pantheon

*"The sea does not burn. The river does not kneel. The tide does not conquer—it returns. And in the end, all things return to the water."* — [[Nereus]], The Tide-King

Per [[Guide to the Eldritch Forces]]'s Pantheon Vulnerabilities, a **high**-vulnerability pantheon — its depths hold secrets better forgotten.

## The truth of Water: the element that was always there

Unlike Fire, which claims to be first, Water never needed to be remembered — it flowed before all things and will flow after all things are gone. It is patient rather than aggressive: it does not need to fight for control, because in the end, Water always wins. When [[Soltharion]] tried to boil the oceans, Water simply waited out his hunger, then returned. Every empire Fire builds by conquest is eventually erased by Water; every city drowned by Water is still remembered, while every city built by flame is remembered only by its ashes.

## The Ninth Wave (epilogue)

*"What does water want?" The tide does not answer. It returns.*

The gods of Water do not speak in commandments — they whisper, drift, and remember. Where other pantheons are defined by power, Water's gods are defined by what they carry: [[Vaelora]] remembers what others try to forget; [[Nereus]] rules because someone must, though even he bows to deeper currents; [[Selmira]] sings for the drowned as testimony, not mourning; [[Marethis]] forgets strategically, hiding truths for a time not yet ready; [[Voraxis]] reminds the world that rage does not require cruelty; Nyxthys proves silence is not absence; [[Tzhor]] speaks only when no other justice can be found; [[Jorvethis]] waits out of certainty, not mercy; [[Varuthis]] is the riptide of choices once dismissed. Together they are called the Ninth Wave — the wave that does not crash, but rises again after the world has stopped counting. When the Weave falters, it is Water that bends before it breaks.

## Myth: the Pact of the Tides

Water doesn't conquer like Fire, rule like Earth, or scatter like Air — it *returns*. This is why the Moon and the Tides are bound forever: [[Selmira]] moves with the sea rather than commanding it. Once, in "the Breaking of the Tides," Selmira pulled the tides too hard and they stopped returning, drying rivers and cracking the land; [[Nereus]] pulled back in response and nearly drowned the world in retaliation. Both stopped short of catastrophe and agreed never to try ruling one another again — the sea cannot rise without the Moon, and the Moon is nothing without the sea.

## Strategy against the Eldritch

Patience — drowning corruption slowly, waiting for Eldritch forces to exhaust themselves. "Time defeats all things."

## Perspective on godhood: "We reflect what was and will be"

Water sees godhood as memory and transformation — [[Vaelora]]: "some stories sink so deep they become solid — these are the Sparks. We do not claim them. We become them." They don't know Sparks predate the concept of story, emerging instead from failed Creation Worlds. [[Lyrithis]] believes retroactively-unmade drownings are natural tidal law rather than [[The Restorer]]'s intervention; [[Rymora]]'s prophetic song-verses are glimpses of [[Aeloria]]'s Weave, mistaken for divine inspiration. Full comparison across all five pantheons plus the Eldritch and [[Vel'Zyren]] on [[The Lie of Godhood]].
