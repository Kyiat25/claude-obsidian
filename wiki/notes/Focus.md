# Focus

One-third of the **Mental Triad** ([[Sanity]], [[Focus]], [[Corruption]]): mental discipline and resistance under magical or social pressure. Helps a character concentrate during glyph overload, interrogation, or illusion effects. Losing Focus causes spell feedback, false memories, or Corruption spikes — the one Mental Triad stat with a direct mechanical link into [[Corruption]] gain, **confirmed** by [[Four Threads of the Mind]]: "lose Focus, and you may gain Corruption."

Abbreviated **FOC** on the [[FEARS Character Sheet]]. Its modifier feeds the [[Whisper Points]] starting-pool formula (`3 + Focus Mod + ½ Sanity Mod`), and it's one of the (unspecified) inputs to the [[Mental State]] tracker.

## Focus Save and Burned out (from Four Threads of the Mind)

Every spell cast in chaos tests a **Focus Save**. At **0 Focus**, a character is [[Burned out]] — cannot cast or concentrate. No DC or roll formula given for the save itself; [[Sanity & Focus in Combat]] doesn't resolve this gap either — it defines Focus purely as a spendable pool, not a saving throw. [[Bender]]'s deep-dive adds a related but not confirmed-identical data point: Bender concentration checks use a "Focus check" instead of Constitution — not stated to be the same mechanic as this still-undefined Focus Save, so not assumed identical. [[Binder]] adds a third data point: "Focus saves to resist a remnant's involuntary bleed-through" are named directly (and can be granted advantage via a Heroic Boon) — genuinely closer to the "Focus Save" terminology than Bender's "Focus check," but still not explicitly tied to the same DC-less mechanic from [[Four Threads of the Mind]]. **[[Druid]]** supplies the first exact formula for any of these: concentration uses a Focus check of `1d20 + WIS` instead of Constitution — the strongest evidence yet that FEARS replaces CON-based concentration with a Focus roll universally (matching Bender's unformulated "Focus check" by name), though still not explicitly reconciled with the DC-less Focus Save from [[Four Threads of the Mind]].

## Focus Pool (confirmed, from Sanity & Focus in Combat)

Beyond the Focus *stat* (mental-triad attribute) above, Focus is also FEARS's primary reaction/tactical **currency**, spent to power Reactions, class abilities, or resist certain effects. **Max Focus = 3**, unless modified by class/feat/trait — the first concrete cap given for this pool. Gained by: dropping an enemy (+1), Critical Hit (+1), regaining a Momentum tier (+1), passing a [[Fracture Save]] (+1), Sanity Stabilization (+1). An optional toggle (Momentum Synergy) raises the cap by +1 per 3 [[Momentum]]. Full detail on [[Sanity & Focus in Combat]]. [[Fracture Whispers]]'s Fracture Table can also drain this pool entirely (Weep Unseen: lose all Focus, can't regain it for 1 minute) — the clearest confirmed example of a failed Fracture Save costing Focus, not just Sanity. **[[Druid]]**'s Draw Power spends 1 Focus (alongside 1 Wild Shape use) to recover an expended spell slot — a direct class-specific draw against this pool, on top of its existing Reaction/tactical-currency uses.

## Provenance note

Same gap-fill relationship as [[Sanity]]: named without definition in [[Translation Matrix]]'s summary of [[Welcome to FEARS]], first actually defined here in [[A World that Watches]].
