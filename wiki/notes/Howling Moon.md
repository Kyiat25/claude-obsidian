# Howling Moon

*"The wind does not carry sound. It is sound. And it remembers howls from before voices were born."*

First seen 2nd Age, 1765 SA, over the Western Plains as a plague of dream-voices. Historically the first harbinger of the Eldritch Beckoning Cycle.

## Nature

Not only visible but audible — its arrival is marked by a chorus of howls, beastial, human, neither. Awakens buried blood-memory of shape, hunger, and wild form. A time of "Wyrd Howls."

## World effects

Every night a phantom howl rolls across the land, not from beasts but from the Weave itself. All beasts stay on edge, unpredictable; beast-like races experience blood-surge visions.

**Monster effects**: beasts/monstrosities gain advantage on perception and can't be surprised; stealth-reliant monsters turn aggressive instead; lycanthropes automatically shift, even through wards.

**Wild Resonance**: creatures with bestial or primal Glyphs must make a DC 13 Wisdom save at dusk — failure means losing control for 1d4 hours and acting on primal instinct; success still leaves the pull felt, with disadvantage on resisting bloodlust.

## Madness

Short-term: heightened hearing, phantom howls, hunting sounds. Long-term: identity fracture, believing oneself a beast in disguise. Indefinite: belief that civilization is a lie and one's true form is feral and divine.

## Campaign use

A druidic enclave experiences spontaneous shapeshifts; a PC with shifter/lycanthropic ancestry awakens a forgotten form; a Glyphlock prison holding a Tether Spirit of the Hunt begins cracking.
