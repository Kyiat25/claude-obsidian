# The World That Still Holds

*"Every morning, scholars wake and check the leylines. Every evening, Threadbearers test the Anchors. Every day, the world does not end. This is not certainty — it is diligence. This is not stability — it is held breath."* — Master Archivist Elendra of [[Thar Vornhal]] (Elf, 647)

A player-facing framing of the Fourth Age's central campaign tension: **the Memory Divide** — only long-lived elves personally remember [[The Unraveling]], the 95-year period of engineered chaos following [[Ashkarra]]'s fall, and everyone else must decide whether to believe them. 487 years have passed; to most humans it's distant myth, to elves who lived it, "yesterday's nightmare that could return tomorrow."

## Five positions on the Divide

- **The Rememberers** (elves 600-750+): lived through 95 years when reality could fail without warning, still check the sky from habit, speak of the Unraveling in present tense. *"I'm the reason you exist to doubt me!"*
- **The Unnaturally Enduring** (ancient dragonborn, liches, others who cheated death): remember too, but their testimony is suspect — what did they trade to survive that long?
- **The Inheritors** (dwarves): raised on great-grandparents' stories and sealed emergency vaults "for when the sky breaks again," caught between ancestral warning and creeping doubt about whether they're hoarding for nothing.
- **The Skeptics** (humans, halflings, most others): view elven warnings as fear-mongering that conveniently justifies elves keeping disproportionate influence over the current system.
- **The Revisionists** (young human scholars): reinterpret elven survivor accounts as traumatic exaggeration — "sky cracking like pottery" read as metaphor, not literal history — and argue modern knowledge could succeed where the ancestors who built the failed Anchors could not.

## The system under visible strain

Three Threadbearers assassinated in the last two years ([[The Threadbearer Crisis]]) read as unprecedented catastrophe to elves and "unfortunate coincidence" to humans; Wild Magic zones expand by inches yearly, obsessively documented by elves and dismissed by humans building closer anyway; the Ashkarra breach itself is disputed as widening or simply as old news. Each of the five [[Loom-Cities]] holds a different position on the Divide — Aeravae's strong elven influence maintains strict protocol against younger-generation pushback, while Kel Varyan (human-dominated) treats elven warnings as merely "advisory."

## Campaign use

This framing gives every faction in the setting a reason to disagree about how urgently to respond to [[The Threadbearer Crisis]] and the wider [[Campaign Arcs (The Great Deceivers and Truth-Seekers)|Suppressor/Revealer/Ascender arc structure]] — not because they disagree about facts, but because they disagree about whether 500-year-old elven trauma should still be allowed to run the world. It also gives elven PCs and NPCs a structurally-justified, sympathetic reason to sound paranoid and controlling to everyone else at the table.
