# Forester (Game Warden) — Rank 1-4

*"You protect and manage specific lands, enforcing law in the wilderness."* Batch 3, Druidic/Nature category. Source dual-names this "Forester / Game Warden"; filed under the first name. A lawful-authority nature background, distinct from [[Scout]] (pure wilderness expertise, no legal authority) and [[City Watch]] (urban law enforcement).

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Junior Warden | Warden Authority | Catch a poacher network, prevent illegal logging, or save a noble on a hunt |
| 2 | Game Warden | Forest Law | Stop a major poaching ring, manage the forest through a crisis, or expand protected territory |
| 3 | Master Forester | Land Steward | Protect the forest from a major threat, expand protected lands significantly, or be appointed chief warden |
| 4 | Chief Warden/Lord Forester | Guardian of the Wild | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Junior Warden

**Skill Proficiency**: Nature, Survival. **Tool Proficiency**: woodcarver's tools. **Equipment**: woodcarver's tools, a hunting trap, a badge of office, warm clothing, 10 gp.

**Feature — Warden Authority**: question travelers in your territory; investigate poaching or illegal logging; levy minor fines for violations; know the boundaries of protected lands; other rangers and wardens recognize your position.

**Holdings**: a small ranger station or cabin; an assigned patrol territory; basic rations and equipment; a senior warden as supervisor.

## Rank 2: Game Warden

**Holdings**: a warden's lodge; a salary from a noble or government (25 gp/month); a horse for patrols; quality equipment (weapons, traps, etc.); authority over a specific forest territory; a network of 5-8 local hunters and foresters who report to you.

**Feature Expansion — Forest Law**: arrest violators in your territory; advantage on tracking and investigation in your forest; know all settlements and camps in your territory; requisition supplies from local communities; once per week, call on local militia for a major violator.

## Rank 3: Master Forester

**Holdings**: a master lodge with staff; a good salary (70 gp/month); multiple territories under your management; 5-8 junior wardens reporting to you; authority to make land-use decisions; you can grant or deny hunting/logging permits; influence with a noble or governing body.

**Feature Expansion — Land Steward**: read forest health (know when disease, blight, or corruption affects trees); advantage on all Nature/Survival checks in managed lands; organize large-scale conservation or harvest operations; once per month, request military support for major enforcement; your decisions shape land use and preservation.

## Rank 4: Chief Warden/Lord Forester

**Holdings**: a grand lodge or forest headquarters; an excellent salary (200 gp/month); authority over all wardens in the region (30+); influence equal to nobility; you can create new protected areas; you manage millions of acres; you answer only to the king/queen on forestry matters.

**Feature — Guardian of the Wild**: automatic success on Nature checks (DC 20 or lower) in your lands; magically track anyone in your territory across the entire region; once per month, declare the forest closed, enforced by all rangers; animals in your territory act as informants; sense major threats to the forest from anywhere in the region. Nobility must get your approval for forest use, your conservation practices become law, and legends say the forest itself obeys you.
