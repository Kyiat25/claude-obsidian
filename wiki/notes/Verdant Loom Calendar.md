# Verdant Loom Calendar

*"The Weave draws quiet. In frost, we remember."* — Aelys

The official calendar of [[Dór-o Estel]], interweaving elemental Threads, seasonal cycles, leyline resonance, and World Tree mythic echoes; maintained by [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]] and governs civic life, ritual magic, and metaphysical rhythm across all [[Loom-Cities]]. The setting's deified calendar-goddess [[Almerra]] is this calendar's in-fiction personification.

## Structure

Twelve 30-day months, plus a 6-day intercalary month, **Auroratide**, at year's end — total 366 days. The current campaign date is Celestialdawn 18, Year 487 of the Fourth Age.

## The six-day week (Thread Days)

Threnday (Weave), Emberday (Fire), Stonehold (Earth), Windfell (Air), Tidecall (Water), Veilrest (Spirit). Casting aligned to a day's Thread carries small narrative advantage; cities schedule trials, births, and executions accordingly. Auroratide's days are Threadless — no divine guidance, no Weave anchor — and ritual failure, madness onset, and silent omens are common during them.

## The five mythic seasons

**Verdant Rise** (Earth, starts Blossomaril 1) — renewal, memory planting, soil rites. **Scorchpeak** (Fire, Sunfirelia 1) — forging season, war blessings, sacred trial fires. **Twilight Tithe** (Air, Harvestfall 1) — offering, tribute, migration of wisdom. **Deepveil** (Water, Frostspire 1) — stillness, remembrance, moonlit prophecy; the setting's most dangerous prophetic season, when memory-echoes resurface, Eldritch Moons flicker even before rising, and many Hollow Breaches trace their origin to Deepveil rituals gone wrong. **Threads Turn** (Weave/Threadless, Auroratide 1) — the Loom unravels and fate fractures; the canonical GM cue for Loom-Keeper changes, city Thread realignments, first whispers of a new Eldritch Moon, or player-affecting fate rewrites, since Threadless time is unmoored and ideal for surreal or retroactive-omen sequences.

## Random event tables (GM tool)

A d20 universal monthly-event table (wild magic surges, political scandals, archaeological discoveries challenging official history, prophecy panics) and a d12-style Thread-specific event table (Earth: geological shifts; Fire: forge-miracles or riots; Air: storms revealing hidden locations, uncontrollable secrets; Water: floods, dreams becoming real, memories resurfacing; Spirit: public ghost manifestations, prophecy proving catastrophically true or false) are provided for randomizing seasonal happenings during play.
