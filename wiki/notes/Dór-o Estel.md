# Dór-o Estel

The campaign setting used to demonstrate FEARS' social and mental-state systems in [[A World that Watches]]. Cosmology is setting-level, not ruleset-level — this content would remain true under a different ruleset entirely (see the earlier note on [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]] et al. for the deeper mythic layer). This page was substantially expanded 2026-07-14 from a comprehensive "world bible" source covering physical geography, the Thread/glyph magic system, demographics, and the full Loom-City roster.

## World overview

A terrestrial plane where reality is woven from **Threads** — metaphysical energy lines originating from the bones of **Vatharun**, the Earth Overgod. ~510 million km² total, 147.9 million km² landmass (29% coverage), population ~125–130 million across seven major continents plus island chains. At the world's southern pole lies **the Hollow Root**, a ~2,000km spiralling depression where the universe first expanded outward from Vatharun's divine breath; leylines — the fossilized bones of the Stone-Father — radiate outward from it, carrying [[The Weave]]. The Weave pulses in sync with the **Twelve Eldritch Moons**, whose combined influence creates **Thread Cycles** roughly every 73 days.

## The Five Elemental Threads

| Thread | Domain | Peak | Color/sensation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Earth** | Stability, memory, permanence | Dawn, winter | Amber; weight, solidity |
| **Fire** | Change, passion, destruction | Noon, summer | Scarlet; heat, urgency |
| **Water** | Flow, adaptation, cleansing | Twilight, autumn | Azure; coolness, fluidity |
| **Air** | Freedom, communication, movement | Midnight, spring | Silver-white; lightness, liberation |
| **Spirit** | Connection, life, transcendence | Constant, fluctuates with emotion | Iridescent; recognition, belonging |

## The Glyph system (magic's architecture)

Magic operates through **Secondary Glyphs** — learned frameworks that filter Thread energy safely, preventing the backlash of direct manipulation. Layered: **Primary Glyphs** (forbidden, direct Thread manipulation, causes Thread-burn, researching them is a capital heresy in most Loom-Cities), **Secondary Glyphs** (standard academy/guild magic, categorized by school), **Echo Glyphs** (memory/trauma-shaped spell variants learned through critical hits or enemy nat-1 saves, don't use spell slots), **Tertiary Glyphs** (folk magic — hearth-wards, blessings, rarely recognized as "real" magic by scholars), and **Wild Glyphs** (dangerous, spontaneous phenomena from Thread instability, criminalized in most nations).

## Magic instability zones

The Weave can tear, overload, or fall silent — collectively **Unstable/Wild Magic Zones**, escalating in severity: **Wild Magic Zones** (spells behave erratically — "cast a flame, birth a flower"), **Arcane Rifts** (the Weave focuses rather than breaks, amplifying one school while destabilizing its opposite), **Enhanced Arcane Rifts** (awakened, alien-logic zones that may collapse into worse), and **Dead Magic Zones** (the most feared — Threads fall utterly silent, enchanted items turn mundane, magic-born creatures fall inert). These zones form near leyline intersections, ancient ruins, or ritual-abuse sites, and often serve as harbingers of larger imbalance or Eldritch corruption.

## The Hollowdeep (the Underdark equivalent)

A six-layer subterranean realm beneath all continents, deepening from the **Woundskin Layer** (0–500m — Drow city-states like the Ashen Warrens, contested Spire-Falls warzones) through the **Murkmire Deep** (bioluminescent fungal civilizations), the **Embervault Rift** (magma-adapted Deep Troll and fire-ascetic communities, plus Dead Magic Zones), the **Echofold** (thought made tangible — living memory-libraries, psionic gnome research), the **Vaultmarrow** (time-folded ruins and psychically-scarred wastes from ancient cosmic conflicts), down to the **Nameless Dominion** (8000m+ — Eldritch civilizations where geometry and logic fail, home to a dead World Anchor called the Black Spiral Root).

## Notable wonders (selected)

The **River Vael** ("the Glyphlight Current," Elarion) glows with visible Thread-energy during alignments; the **Hollow Basin of Ashkarra** ("the Wound That Bleeds Memory") is the 300km impact crater of the First Hollow Breach, where floating memory shards drift and display scenes from the destroyed city; the **Threadfall Delta** (Kerythra) is a natural World Anchor whose braided rivers form shifting glyph patterns readable as Thread-weather forecasts; the **Hollowwood** is a continent-spanning sentient forest with collective consciousness.

## International diplomacy

Cross-continental institutions include the **Confluence Council** (dispute resolution and multi-continental coordination, no enforcement power), the **Thread-Stability Monitoring Consortium** (predicts Thread-storms, coordinates magical emergency response — actively grappling with **Cosmic Threat Preparedness** for a potential Second Hollow Breach), and the **Cultural Preservation Society**. Diplomatic protocol includes **Thread-Sworn Negotiations** (agreements bound with Thread-energy that punishes violators) and **Neutral-Ground Protocols** observed in the bridge regions.

## The present moment (canonical current date)

Per a shorter DM-overview draft source: the world is in the **Fourth Age, 8th Cycle**, canonical date **487-03-18** (6th day of the 8th Cycle, month of Celestialdawn), with the Weave currently on its **third consecutive Spirit Thread alignment** — "the Third Thread." Full calendar structure (12 months + the intercalary Auroratide, six-day weeks) lives on [[Verdant Loom Calendar]]. Current-era tensions per that source: declining trust in the Weave after multiple recent Cycle failures, Spirit Thread dominance causing prophetic instability and memory fractures, more frequent Eldritch Moons, strained inter-city politics over Threadbearer legitimacy, and growing Drow/Underdark aggression.

> [!contradiction] RESOLVED — Two draft sources disagree on continental geography
> The comprehensive world-bible source above (used for the bulk of this page) describes [[Iriveth Orre]] as an ice/aurora continent ("Crown of Ice and Song," ice plateaus and glacial fjords) housing [[Aeravae]]. A separate, shorter DM-overview draft instead describes Iriveth Orre as a jungle/aquifer island-continent ("aquifer-caverns, sacred springs").
>
> **Ruled (2026-07-14):** both are true of different parts of the same continent. Iriveth Orre is overwhelmingly the ice/aurora continent described in the main account — "Crown of Ice and Song" is the dominant identity for most of its landmass. But it has one or two peninsulas that break from that climate entirely, warm and water-rich, matching the jungle/aquifer-cavern account — and **[[Aeravae]] is built on one of these peninsulas**, not on the ice sheet proper. This also resolves a quieter tension in [[Aeravae]]'s own description: a city of interconnected canals, floating islands, and Submersion Districts for aquatic races reads far more naturally as sitting on a warm aquifer peninsula than on a glacial ice plateau.
