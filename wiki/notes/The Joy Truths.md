# The Joy Truths (The Joyful Chase)

*"We do not worship the gods, but we walk beside them."*

Not a temple-bound faith or rigid doctrine but a living philosophy woven into laughter, memory, and story. Halflings believe they were made when a god realized the world lacked joy; their central organizing idea is **the Chase** — life as pursuit of joy in every form (feasting, hunting, storytelling, taming beasts), with **the Greatest Joy** as a halfling nirvana-equivalent, reached only once the Chase itself no longer needs to continue.

## The Three Truths

1. **Laughter Lasts Longer Than Breath** — joy is the truest immortality; core ritual is the **Laughing Feast**, honoring the dead with the loudest, most ridiculous jokes rememberable.
2. **The Story Matters More Than the Sword** — survival and worth are measured in how a life is told, not how it is fought; core ritual is the **Great Story Circle**.
3. **We Do Not Fear Death. We Walk Beside Him** — death is a companion to be mocked, tempted, and led on wild chases, never ignored; core ritual is the **Rite of the Second Echo** (see also [[Halfling (Lineage)]] for its darker, Drow-specific wartime variant).

Every halfling gift carries a cost — passion into obsession, indulgence into addiction, wanderlust into unraveling. Those who suppress their own joy and desires shatter into **the Hollowed**, a recognized spiritual failure state.

## Two traditions, one fire

**The Hearth-Echo Covenant** (settled communities — joy through memory, hospitality, and generational care) and **The Way of the Joy-Wild** (wandering clans and survivors — joy weaponized against despair) never war with each other, treating themselves as two flames of the same fire — "a flame may dance or sit in the hearth; it is still fire."

### The Hearth-Echo Covenant's Divine Court (the Circle at the Table)

Non-hierarchical gods with a seat at every shared feast: **Hearthmother Thalara** (home, cooking, protection), **Old Father Lanton** (planting, paths, elder wisdom), **Tolla-of-Tales** (stories, memory, trickery), **Cousin Bright** (joy, song, mischief), and **The Quiet One** (grief, ancestry, forgotten names — honored via the Empty Plate left at every feast).

### The Way of the Joy-Wild's Primal Figures

Invoked in action, not worshipped in temples: **Redtooth** (the Hunter-Joy, risk and feast), **Wyrdsmile** (the Trickster-Joy, wit and reversal), **Grandma Ash** (the End-Joy, comforter of the dying), **First Fang** (the Child-Joy, first kills and fearless hunger), and **The Guest Who Was God** (the Stranger-Joy, luck and judgment — refusing hospitality to a stranger risks insulting this figure directly).

## Rituals of the Joy-Wild

The Blood-Feast (after a first hunt or kill), the Fire-Tale (a dying halfling's last story, or the best story told about them if death was sudden), the Joy-Test (a survival ordeal undertaken willingly in crisis, whispered into the desperate by First Fang), the Hollow-Song (a mournful warning-tune for those who denied their joy), and the Echo-Gift (hospitality received must be echoed forward, never merely repaid).

## Names for the gods of other pantheons

Halflings give every pantheon's deities their own folk-names and find the tallfolk's insistence on "correct" divine names baffling — "the gods don't care what you call them. They care if you laugh while bleeding."

## Relationship to other faiths

Deep, genuine kinship with [[The Deep Law]] via the historic Stone & Laughfire Pact (dwarves gained songs, halflings gained steel) — Grin-Wardens hide rude lyrics inside borrowed dwarven stone-blessing songs out of affection. Quiet admiration for [[The Woven Reverence]]'s memory rites, tempered by teasing about elven joylessness. Baffled distaste for [[The Burning Concord]]'s obedience-over-dance philosophy. Mutual respect with [[The Path of the First Scar]] as fellow survivors who turned near-annihilation into either honor or jokes. Tolerant but unconverted coexistence with [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]]. And, uniquely among all races, **no fear of any death god across any pantheon** — every one is treated as an old, fondly regarded, frequently-late dinner guest.

## The Drow exception

The one sustained exception to Halfling joy-in-all-things: the Glintsing massacre by [[The Ashen Web]]'s Drow, which gave rise to the grim, silent **Rite of the Second Echo** war-tradition and figures like the Grin-Wardens and Echo-Hunters — see [[Halfling (Lineage)]] for the fuller account.

## History (condensed)

First Age (informal way of living; early Joy-Walkers like Old Nellie Rootdrinker) → Second Age (the Stone & Laughfire Pact with dwarves; the Hearth-Echo Covenant and Way of the Joy-Wild formalize as distinct expressions; the first Joy-Reckoning — a halfling revenge tradition — is born from an imperial massacre) → Third Age (adaptation rather than resistance to the rising Faith of the Bound Pattern; the Rite of the Second Echo is first performed after Drow slaughter) → Fourth Age (present: halflings recognized as "keepers of fragile joy," essential to the Pattern's resilience against unraveling, not merely charming bystanders).
