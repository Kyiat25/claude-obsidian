# Wizard — Master of the Weave

> [!note] "The Weave" now has cosmological structure
> This class's own subtitle names [[The Weave]] directly. [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]] is the first source to actually structure what the Weave is — a five-layer cosmology with four unaware Primal Forces — rather than leave it as unexplained flavor. Worth reading alongside this page.

**Full Caster tier**. "The wizard doesn't command magic. The wizard understands it — and understanding is the more dangerous power." Wins encounters before they begin through preparation rather than raw power; fragile, but the best-positioned class to adapt when a plan goes wrong because it read the scenario first. Complexity self-rated HIGH (8/10) by its own per-class deep-dive. The twenty-fourth and final per-class deep-dive ingested this cluster.

> "Not the most durable, not the most mobile, not the hardest hitter. The most prepared."

## Signature abilities

- **Spellbook** — vast flexibility
- **Prepared Casting** — always has the right tool
- **Arcane Mastery** — control and manipulation

**Gameplay style**: strategic caster, solving encounters with the right spell at the right time. **Core loop**: prepare → identify solution → cast → adapt. Fragile and low-HP; power comes from planning, not durability.

## Class basics (from Wizard.md deep-dive)

- **Hit Dice**: 1d6/level. HP at 1st: 6 + CON mod. HP at higher levels: 1d6 (or 4) + CON mod/level after 1st.
- **Proficiencies**: Armour — none. Weapons — simple. Tools — none. Saves — Focus, Wisdom (a fourth confirmed Focus-as-saving-throw class, after [[Inventor]], [[Monk]], [[Shaman]]). Skills — choose two from Arcana, History, Gathering, Insight, Investigation, Medicine, Memory, Occult, Religion, Resolve.
- **Starting Equipment**: (a) quarterstaff or (b) dagger; (a) component pouch or (b) arcane focus; (a) scholar's or (b) explorer's pack; a spellbook with known cantrips + 1st-circle spells. Starts with 4d4×10 gp (avg. 50gp) for supplies/transcription.
- **Quick Build**: Intelligence highest, then Constitution. Any lineage (High Elf, Gnome, Human thematic). Sage/Scholar or an Arcana/History background. Prepare a control/utility mix; save top-level slots for decisive moments; use Arcane Recovery to extend a long day.

**Explicit statement, twice**: "Wizards do not gain Weapon Mastery. Your tools are spells." Stated both in the flavor introduction and again under Starting Equipment — the strongest possible confirmation of [[Weapon Mastery]]'s existing Non-Martials listing, matching the shape of [[Sorcerer]]'s identical explicit denial rather than a silent gap.

## Core class features (full text)

### Spellbook (1st level)

Holds all known spells; starts with six 1st-circle Arcane spells + all known cantrips. New spells from scrolls/other spellbooks: 2 hours + 50gp/spell level. Lost spellbook: reconstruct over a week (prepared-that-day spells free; others need a DC 15 INT check per spell level).

### Spellcasting (1st level) — nineteenth confirmed DC formula

Intelligence-based: **Spell Save DC = 8+PB+INT, Spell Attack = PB+INT** — nineteenth confirmation of the `8+PB+ability mod` formula, matching every prior confirmed pattern. Prepares INT-mod + Wizard-level spells (min 1); 1 minute/circle to prepare, total time = half the summed circles. Full slot recovery on a long rest; ritual-tagged spellbook spells cast slot-free (+10 min casting time).

### Arcane Recovery (1st level)

Once per day on finishing a short rest: recover slots totaling half Wizard level (round up, min 1), capped at 6th circle.

### Arcane Tradition (2nd level, and 6th, 10th, 14th)

Choose a School (Evocation, Abjuration, Illusion, Conjuration, Transmutation, Enchantment, Divination, Necromancy) or a thematic tradition. Features at 2nd/6th/10th/14th plus in-school perks (half-cost transcription, free spells on levelling) — not detailed further here (Bladesinger's Called Shot hook is documented separately via [[Called Shot Subclass Features]], and isn't named among this generic document's School list).

### Rote Spells (3rd level, scaling)

INT-mod (min 1) 1st-circle spells always prepared, free of the prepared-spell count. +1 2nd-circle Rote at 5th, +1 3rd-circle at 9th. Swappable on a long rest.

### Magic Sense (5th level) — new Momentum gain source, with a notable copy-paste error

Persistent, unsuppressible-except-by-Antimagic-Field *Detect Magic* within 30 ft. Automatically identifies any Arcane-list spell cast within 60 ft (Arcana check, DC 10+circle, for other traditions).

> [!contradiction] "Arcane Flow" names the wrong class in its own feature text
> The class's flavor introduction correctly describes this trigger generically: "when you cast a spell that successfully imposes a condition or reduces a creature to 0 HP, gain +1 Momentum." But the **Magic Sense** feature write-up itself says: "When you cast a **Bender** spell that successfully imposes a condition on an enemy or reduces an enemy to 0 HP, gain +1 Momentum" — Wizard doesn't cast "Bender spells" (Bender is an unrelated class with its own spell list); this reads as a copy-paste artifact from another class's document, left uncorrected. Treated as an authoring error, not a genuine cross-class interaction — the intro's generic "a spell" wording is taken as authoritative for the actual trigger condition.

### Arcane Echo Theory (9th level)

Binds Echo Glyphs up to INT mod + PB (more than any other caster). New spells can attempt an Echo Glyph as part of transcription (+1 hour, DC 15 Arcana).

### Heroic Boon (10th level)

Choose: **Focused Recall** (once/long rest, bonus action, recover one expended slot ≤5th circle) or **Arcane Surge** (once/long rest, cast a prepared spell ≤half Wizard level, rounded up, without a slot).

### Spell Mastery (13th level, and 15th, 18th)

Choose one 1st-circle (13th), one 2nd-circle (15th), one 3rd-circle (18th) spellbook spell — each castable at its lowest level without a slot, swappable on a long rest, using normal casting time/components.

### Epic Boon (20th level)

Choose: **Arcane Mastery** (add 4 any-circle Arcane spells to the spellbook, even off-list; freely prepared, don't count against the limit, each usable once/long rest without expending the normal slot pool) or **Signature Spell** (two spells ≤3rd circle always prepared, free at lowest level; once/long rest each, castable at a higher level by spending a slot normally).

## Last Stand (confirmed, 23rd class confirming of 24 ingested — standard permanent-death clause, and a second 8-base DC data point)

| Option | Effect |
|---|---|
| The Final Theorem | Cast any spellbook spell (prepared or not, any circle) with no slot/components, as if using a 9th-circle slot; concentration (if required) persists up to 5 minutes after death |
| Arcane Legacy | INT-mod (min 1) allies within 60 ft each receive shared spell knowledge, castable once each within 24 hours using the Wizard's DC/attack mod; total shared circles capped at INT mod × PB |
| Null Field | 60 ft Null Field, 5 minutes: spells ≤5th circle auto-counterspelled inside it; casting ≥6th circle requires an INT save (DC = 8+PB+INT); hostile concentration spells inside end immediately; allies cast normally. Collapses at the end for 10d10 force damage to hostiles in the area (INT save for half) |

Twenty-third class confirming [[Last Stand]] as universal (of 24 per-class files ingested — [[Sorcerer]] remains the sole exception); all three options reaffirm the standard permanent-death clause (no exception — Bender remains the lone confirmed exception). Null Field's DC (`8+PB+INT`) is the **second confirmed instance of the plain `8+PB+ability` Last Stand base**, after [[Reaper]] — no longer a one-off, now a real (if minority) recurring pattern alongside the `10` and `12` bases.

## Class Reactions (confirmed, 24th class confirming — self-explained pool exception)

Draws from the shared **Proficiency Bonus pool**, refreshing short/long rest — but Counterspell Instinct is an explicit, stated exception.

> [!note] Already explained by the source itself — no ruling needed
> Unlike every other pool exception this session, the deep-dive ties Counterspell Instinct directly to a specific named feature (Magic Sense) rather than stating it as a bare rule. The source explains its own exception.

| Reaction | Level | Trigger | Effect | Pool cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Arcane Deflection | 2nd | Hit by a spell or magical effect | Halve the damage; can't cast levelled spells next turn | 1 use |
| Counterspell Instinct | 5th | A visible creature within 60 ft begins casting | If *counterspell* is prepared, cast it as a reaction (spell slot spent normally) | **Explicitly not a pool use** — Magic Sense makes the reaction instantaneous |
| Arcane Rebound | 10th | The Wizard succeeds a save against a hostile spell targeting only them | The caster must save against their own spell (using their own DC) or suffer its effects | 1 use |

Counterspell Instinct joins [[Cleric]]'s Sacred Interposition, [[Investigator]]'s Predictive Step, and [[Monk]]'s total departure as confirmed partial/total exceptions to the shared-pool default — the fourth distinct exception shape this cluster, and the only one explained in-text by a specific class feature (Magic Sense) rather than left as a bare rules statement.
