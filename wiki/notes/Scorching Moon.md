# Scorching Moon

*"It does not burn to illuminate. It burns to reveal what should have stayed buried."*

First seen 2nd Age, 1688 SA, during the Great Fire of Thar Vornhal. A paradox: a moon that radiates no visible fire.

## Nature

Makes the air boil with intention — it doesn't glow, it seethes, a pulsing blistered eye that doesn't blink. Known as "The Flame Without Law" or "Ash Memory."

## World effects

All natural heat increases with no source; campfires and torches go cold even as the world itself burns; metal armor becomes painful (DC 12 Con save per hour or gain 1 exhaustion). Grasslands scorch but never catch flame; stone surfaces develop heat glyphs that glow red and burn anyone who looks at them.

**Monster effects**: fire-resistant creatures become more aggressive, absorbing rather than resisting; fire-vulnerable creatures must flee daylight; elementals mutate into ember-wrapped aberrations.

## Madness

Short-term: the sensation of being watched from within one's own skin. Long-term: an obsession with purging "the impure." Indefinite: belief the world is always burning and comfort is illusion.

## Campaign use

A desert city visible only as a mirage, everything burning with no heat; a forge-master possessed by a burning script; a Fire Glyph Codex reveals a 13th forbidden fire-glyph.
