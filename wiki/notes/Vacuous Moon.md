# Vacuous Moon

*"There is no light. There is no reflection. Only the sense that something is watching, and always was."*

First seen 2nd Age, 1641 SA, over the Endless Steppe. Not seen — felt.

## Nature

No visible orb; the world simply forgets what light does. Possibly an impossible reflection of [[Nhal'Zereth]], or what remains of a god erased by the Glyph of Unbeing.

## World effects

**Total Obfuscation**: no natural light functions even in daylight; Darkvision ceases, replaced by strange flickers; magical light works at half radius and may whisper. **Disorientation**: navigation is impossible without physical markers, compasses and direction glyphs mislead or loop, settlements disappear from maps.

**Monster effects**: creatures relying on blindsight, tremorsense, or echolocation suffer frenzy or paralysis; psychic beasts repeat phrases no one spoke; intelligent monsters stop vocalizing, communicating instead via gaze or hallucination. Staring at reflective surfaces for 1+ minute risks a possession attempt by a Reflected Spirit (Vacuous Possession).

## Madness

Short-term: discomfort with illumination. Long-term: phobia of faces, especially one's own. Indefinite: belief a shadow not one's own must be obeyed.

## Glyph interactions and resistance

Illusion Glyphs become self-referential, looping or spawning identical decoy casters that act semi-independently; Spirit Glyphs tied to truth, memory, or rest flicker or cause nightmares; Universal Glyphs begin unraveling local definitions of up, real, and next. The Fate-Weaver's threads are cut off entirely, making prophecy and augury fail; the Earth Pantheon's sealing glyphs hold but resonate with low-frequency vibrations like a distant breath against stone; Ceymarn, the Whisper-Vigil, stands vigil at shadowgates trying to contain what stirs beneath reflection.

## Campaign use

Infiltration of divine sites; assassinations where the target won't be remembered; corruption from [[Nhal'Zereth]], Esh'Tarah, or Mirag'Nael.
