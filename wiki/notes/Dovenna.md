# Dovenna, The Vigil at the Bedside

*"I do not bring sleep. Sleep comes on its own, the way night does. I am the reason you can afford it."*

**Domains**: the protection of sleepers, the warding of nightmares, the defenceless hours. **Symbol**: an open eye above a closed one. **Worshiped by**: parents at cradles, soldiers on watch-rotation, anyone who has ever guarded a sleeping person.

Sleep itself has no god — it comes like weather. Dovenna is what stands over it: the body that cannot flee, the mind with its doors open. Wards the sleeper from without (knife, fire, the thing at the window) and from within (the nightmare, treated as a trespasser in the dream-god's country, not a resident). She and Restbringer keep the quietest doctrine in the pantheon — sleep is the nightly rehearsal of his road, walked a little way in and walked back out; her vigil is why the walk back is safe. Her rite is the watching candle, lit not for light but *at* the sleeper — a small delegated vigil. Blowing out another's watching candle is a hostility no law needs to name.

Attended by **Pell, the Hush**, a small spirit in her train governing a household's settling toward sleep — the last door, the banked fire, the dropped voice. Children in most lands know that whispering after the candle is lit is "keeping Pell waiting."
