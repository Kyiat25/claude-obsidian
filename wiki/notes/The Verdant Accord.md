# The Verdant Accord

*"Nothing escapes the Cycle. What grows must wither. What dies must feed."*

The oldest primal faith of Dór-o Estel, predating [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]] and rooted in the First Age. A druidic belief system with no unified clergy — instead a web of **Circles**, each interpreting the Weave's living cycles differently. Sees balance not as peace but as reciprocal transformation: growth through decay, healing through sacrifice, creation through destruction.

## Core truths

1. **All Things Root and Rot** — death is the currency of renewal, not an ending.
2. **Balance Is Not Stillness** — true balance requires storms, plagues, floods; druids are stormcallers, not peacekeepers.
3. **The Weave Is Wound in Root** — the Weave runs through trees, soil, and stone as much as celestial glyphs; every forest is a spell.
4. **The Pact Was Made in Silence** — older than worship, older than priesthood; the sacred pact was felt, not spoken.

## The Circles

- **Wild Reclaiming** — civilization must periodically be devoured by wilderness; releases plagues and beasts on over-civilized land.
- **Crimson Root** *(dark sect)* — blood is the most potent fertilizer; practices Fleshbloom sacrifice rites at the **Hollowbark Sepulcher**, producing forbidden relics called Fleshblooms used to forge soul-piercing Blossom-Blades.
- **Withered Bloom** — decay as sacred transition; preserves memory through rot and the "Mulching of Names."
- **Ashseed Flame** — formed after the First Hollow Breach; fire is renewal, burning away corruption — in constant tension with the Burning Concord over who "owns" sacred fire.
- **Verdant Keepers** — the moderate, most publicly acceptable face; seasonal bloom-rites and Equinox restorations.

## Death and the Three Foldings

Death is a phase of the Cycle, not a punishment: **the Unrooting** (death itself, body laid on soil), **the Feeding** (return of body/blood to the world — boneplanting, sky burial, joyfeasting), and **the Echoing** (memory becomes seed — a Grave-Sapling bloomed, a name sung in season). Deaths that resist this cycle (Eldritch-caused, or erased from memory) become **Cycle-Starved** — a metaphysical wound the Crimson Root sometimes "reclaims" through Bloodsap groves.

## Relationship to other faiths

Closest kinship with the Earth Pantheon (Thamur, Ishara, Gaeldros) and deep reverence for Spirit gods Aelys ("the Leaf That Remembers") and Nyzara; mutual affection with Halfling joy-traditions; grudging respect from Dwarves; elder-kin recognition from [[The Woven Reverence]] despite discomfort with Accord blood rites; absolute rejection from the Burning Concord, which hunts Accord members on sight; cryptic mutual indifference with the Ashen Web (shared fungal/death lore, different tone). [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]] treats the Accord as a suspicious wild remnant — publicly denouncing the Crimson Root while secretly studying its regenerative rites.

## Auroratide

During the 137-year World Tree blooming event (called the "Sigh of Threads" by elves), the Accord practices its own parallel rite — **the Root-Unbinding** — including Petal-Seeding, the sacrificial Hollow Breath rite, and (in Crimson Root's most secretive practice) the **Verdant Hemorrhage**, which produces a single Fleshbloom petal once every 137 years.
