# Dalvora, The Sower of Stone

*"The land does not resist you. You simply do not know how to work with it."*

**Domain**: agriculture, civilization's growth, the land that feeds. **Symbol**: a plow embedded in stone, wheat growing around it. **Worshiped by**: farmers, city-builders, those who cultivate the land. **Rivals**: [[Ishara]] (wild growth), [[Karthas]] (the burier of empires). **Allies**: [[Tirvas]] (harvest).

**Myth — The Harvest That Burned**: the golden kingdom of Kaereth's Bounty declared it had dominion over the land, burning wildflowers and paving sacred groves. Dalvora withdrew her blessing entirely — crops failed for seven cycles, rivers turned to silt, the king's line was buried in famine. Only one field survived: the one a humble farmer-priest had sown beside the last uncut tree. Her lesson: "You do not command the land by naming it. You earn its voice by listening." City architects and rural planners still offer sacrifices before expansion, lest she withdraw her growth and let [[Ishara]] reclaim the ruins.
