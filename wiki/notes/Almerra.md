# Almerra, The Lady of Counted Days

*"The year was always turning. I did not make it turn. I made it* mean. *Before me, time passed. After me, time was* kept."

**Domains**: the calendar, named days, the counted year, appointments and their keeping. **Symbol**: a cord of 360 knots, one dyed. **Worshiped by**: calendar-keepers, clerks, festival-wardens, everyone who has ever needed two people in the same place on the same day.

Openly younger than the thing she governs — her own scripture's first line, "the year was here before me," is never read for what it is. Governs time's grid, not its substance: the month-names, the festival-days, the idea that a debt can come due. Her borders are contested at the two threshold-hours (Fire claims dawn, Spirit claims dusk), and Almerra rules neither, merely records — with visible satisfaction — that both claims require her calendar to say when dawn and dusk *are*. Households keep a year-cord, untying one knot each dusk; a skipped knot is confessed at the next festival not as sin, but as a day that must be accounted for somewhere.

> [!truth] DM-Truth
> Almerra is the vault's cleanest specimen of a deified mortal error — the numbered calendar imposed on a random process. She is entirely real, entirely functional, and half-knows it; in her own view the gods of stone and sea also only *keep* what they did not make. Possibly the most self-aware deity in the setting, purely because her illusion is small enough to see all of.
