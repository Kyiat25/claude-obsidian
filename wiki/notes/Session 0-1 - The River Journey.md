# Session 0-1: The River Journey

The campaign's ready-to-run opening adventure (3-3.5 hours, level 1). PCs begin as passengers aboard the trading barge ***The Weighted Scale***, traveling the Ivora River from [[Reedford]] (Lower Ivora Flats, [[Corvessa]]) to [[Kel Varyan]], each with a different mundane reason for the trip — spy, merchant, mercenary, refugee. Six days is the advertised journey time between the border castle and the capital.

## Design intent

*"The world was already moving when you stepped into it."* The opening is built as a **compressed model of the entire world**: forces are placed in unavoidable physical proximity (a Corvessan tax collector, a heretical prophet, nobles, a smuggling captain, contraband both legal and illegal) so that politics, faction tension, and looming war between [[Corvessa]] and [[Talanthar]] surface through observation rather than exposition. Core design principles: the world moves without the players (they discover an already-unfolding crisis, not trigger one); politics through proximity, never explained; violence has lasting consequences (NPCs can die and are mourned); party cohesion is earned through shared secrets and safety in numbers, never forced.

## Structure

Days 1-5 are social/investigative river transit (war preparations visible on the banks, canal transit, a fog stretch); Day 6 is **The Fog Ambush** (river reavers, then river-lurker monsters, a two-phase combat encounter with environmental hazard rules); Day 7 is **The News Arrives** — word reaches the barge that four of the five Threadbearers are dead, and as an Imperial-writ vessel, *The Weighted Scale* is recalled to Kel Varyan immediately, tying the whole opening into [[The Threadbearer Crisis]].

## Key NPCs

**Captain Harren Voss** (52, human, Imperial-writ holder and discreet smuggler) — runs a legitimate-cover/illegal-cargo double life to make his margins; suspects the Threadbearer deaths are assassination, not accident; offers the party lodging, underworld introductions, and future smuggling work in exchange for corroborating his story to Imperial Customs after the ambush. **Brennan Holt**, an Imperial tax collector, is another named key passenger (among others not fully detailed in this pass).

## Connection to the wider crisis

Per the same source, the "sole surviving Threadbearer" the news reports is [[Kel Varyan]]'s own — publicly **Serana Tidecaller**, secretly **Naleth the Preserver** of [[Campaign Arcs (The Great Deceivers and Truth-Seekers)|Arc 1B]] — see [[The Threadbearer Crisis]] for the full resolution of this identity and the standing Kel Varyan/Aeravae survivor contradiction.

## Earlier design draft: an alternate, deliberately ambiguous framing

`Session 1.md` and `Introduction session 0 - 1.md` are earlier GM/AI brainstorm conversations that produced this final module. One draft's read-aloud hook frames the surviving Threadbearer's status as genuinely contested rumor rather than settled fact: *"Some say the Emperor is protecting them. Others say they're a prisoner. A few whisper they're not even human anymore."* Useful as an alternate opening beat for a GM who wants to delay confirming the Serana Tidecaller/Naleth Spiralbound reveal past Session 0-1.
