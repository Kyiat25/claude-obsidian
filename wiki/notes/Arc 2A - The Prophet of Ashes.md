# Arc 2A — The Prophet of Ashes ("Let It Burn — Canonical Version with Eldritch Conspiracy")

The full deep-dive behind [[Campaign Arcs (The Great Deceivers and Truth-Seekers)|Category 2: The Revealers]]'s flagship arc — and, per its own subtitle, secretly an [[Xey'Tazun|Eldritch God]] conspiracy underneath its truth-revelation surface.

## Embermarked Kethyr Flameheart

A former high priest of the Burning Concord who personally led the raid on the [[Islands of Eternal Green]] (burning the ancient World Trees that proved pre-divine civilization) and executed the Cycle-Singer who warned him five days before [[Ashkarra]]'s fall. His entire family — wife Sylara Emberheart, son Korvus, daughter Velithra, 20+ extended relatives — was unmade in the Day of Twelve Shadows.

In the crater aftermath, grieving and half-mad, he called out for answers and power — and **Xey'Tazun**, one of the nine Eldritch Gods, answered with a warlock pact: centuries of unnaturally extended life "to prevent future catastrophes." Over the following 500+ years he systematically verified every piece of pre-divine evidence he had once destroyed, concluded the gods' fraud was total, and radicalized into revealing the truth at any cost — now ~600-700 years old, eldritch-touched (temporal shimmer, harmonic voice, eyes reflecting Wyrmglass Flats crystal patterns), leading the **Ashen Prophets** in burning Faith temples and forcing revelation across the continent.

## The twist Kethyr doesn't know

His guiding "visions" aren't insight or divine correction — they're Xey'Tazun's manipulation. The Earth Pantheon's containment of an entity referred to as **the Nameless One Below** doesn't rely on physical seals; it relies on certainty, maintained oaths, and theological infrastructure. Every Faith temple Kethyr burns, every divine authority he undermines, every ancient theological certainty he shatters — all of it weakens that containment a little more. Kethyr, driven by genuine guilt and righteous fury, is unknowingly doing Xey'Tazun's work of unraveling "the Lock That Holds the World." This directly ties Arc 2A into the Eldritch tier and likely into [[The Hollow Thread]] as the setting's designated apocalypse option.

## Campaign structure

Five geographic phases: the Wyrmglass Flats (origin point) → [[Veltharyn]] (the new Fire Anchor) → the Dragonborn Diaspora → the major Loom-Cities (continental spread) → a climactic Phase 5, simultaneous global revelation. The evidence Kethyr actually possesses — dendrochronological proof, dwarven structural analyses, cross-racial testimonies, historical persecution records — is real and can't be dismissed, but carries a subtle "temporal inconsistency" that hints at its Eldritch tampering to sufficiently perceptive investigators.
