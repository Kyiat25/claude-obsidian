# The Weave Unbroken (The Song of the Five Layers)

*"Before the gods, before the stars, before even time itself, there was the Weave... within it, five great truths were spun—five layers, bound yet distinct, turning in harmony, forever entwined."*

Not a religion with worshippers, but a **forbidden apocryphal text** — first whispered in the 1053rd year of the Third Age during the Vein-Split Eve, when two Eldritch Moons (the Glowering Moon and the Krakenlight) hung in the sky together for the first time. Unsanctioned by any Threadbearer, unbound by any temple, it is nonetheless remembered by "the lost, the drowned, the burned," and inscribed beneath the moons by one **Mirevin Quenhal, the Mirror-Tongue**.

## The Five Layers (a full cosmogonic taxonomy)

1. **The Primal Glyphs** — the Hammer (forging), the Loom (fate), the Maw (endings), the Wheel (return): Will, Threads, Endings, Echoes. The first laws carved into the cosmos.
2. **The Universal Glyphs** — Understanding, Wildheart, Motion, the Veiled Hand (deception), the Lantern (thought shaping reality): unseen forces that keep the Weave from stagnating.
3. **The Elemental Glyphs** — Fire, Water, Earth, Air, Spirit: not gods or laws, but the raw material and foundation of creation.
4. **The Form Glyphs (the Nine Patterns)** — Mortals, Beasts, Titans, Spirits, Aberrations, Dragons, Undead, Fae, Constructs: the recurring shapes life takes so that "life would always rise."
5. **The Personal Glyphs** — Power, Knowledge, Flow, Passion, Shadows, Creation, Destruction, Spirit, Mind: the last layer, granting individual souls will and choice rather than pre-written fate.

## The Final Lie / The Final Truth

The text ends in two contradictory codas. **The Weave Unbroken** frames the Five Layers as a harmonious, self-sufficient cycle where "no layer exists without the others... no fate is absolute," closing with an invitation to look within oneself for the First Glyph. **The Weave Unbound**, immediately following, reframes the same five layers as tearing, unnaming, burning, fracturing, and screaming — recasting the first coda as a comforting lie and revealing (per this apocrypha) that the First Glyph lies not within the self but *beneath* it, "waiting," and that it "remembers what the gods have forgotten." This second coda is described in-text as a **leak** — a moment when the Weave itself split and truth spilled through, not a sanctioned revelation.

## Reception

The Threadbearers deny the song; the cities erase it. Only "the Moons remember," and a scattered following called the **Spiral Refrain** still listens and repeats it. Framed in-setting as dangerous precisely because it is unfalsifiable and pre-dates every sanctioned pantheon's origin account — a GM tool for undermining player confidence in any single "true" cosmology.
