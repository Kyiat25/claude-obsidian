# Outlander — Rank 1-4

*"Civilization has a thousand rules for things the wild solved in one motion. You've never fully learned theirs. You've never needed to."*

> [!note] Original content — no raw source
> Flagged after Batch 1 as "adaptable but not yet written," Outlander never got an expanded treatment across any of the 3 source batches. Written here from scratch, deliberately structured around a different axis than [[Scout]]. Scout is a **profession** — a career of maps, clients, consulting fees, and a guild-like progression toward "the definitive atlas" and paid expertise. Outlander has no profession, no clients, and — unlike every other background in this system — **no income table at any rank**. You don't sell your knowledge of the wild; you simply are of it, and civilization has to come to you when it needs something the wild alone provides. The tension this background tracks isn't expertise, it's **belonging**: the further you rise, the more you're needed by a world you were never fully raised to understand.

## Rank progression

| 1 | Stranger to Walls | Unlettered, Unlost | Navigate a situation using wild instinct where civilized training would have failed |

## Rank 1: Stranger to Walls

**Skill Proficiency**: Athletics, Survival. **Tool Proficiency**: none. **Equipment**: a staff, a trophy from an animal you killed with your bare hands or a crude weapon, hide clothing that marks you as an outsider in any city, no coin at all.

**Feature — Unlettered, Unlost**: you've never needed maps, money, or permission to move through the world, and it shows — you cannot be lost in wilderness, ever, but you also don't understand currency, contracts, or civic law well enough to navigate them without help. You and companions can always find food and water for up to 6 in the wild.

**Holdings**: none — you own nothing and owe nothing; a bond to the place and people who raised you, who do not understand why you'd ever want to leave.

## Rank 2: Neither Here Nor There

**Holdings**: still none in any city's terms — no coin, no property, no contracts; a strained but real connection to your birth clan or tribe, who see you as changed; a handful of civilized people who've started to seek you out specifically because you don't think or negotiate like they expect.

**Feature Expansion — Two Tongues, One Truth**: you're learning to move between both worlds without fully belonging to either — advantage on checks to communicate plainly and honestly across a cultural or trust gap civilization usually can't bridge; once per week, cut through a dispute or negotiation by simply refusing to play by its unspoken rules, disorienting everyone involved into honesty.

## Rank 3: Called When Needed

**Holdings**: no wealth and no fixed home, by choice as much as circumstance; a reputation among both frontier settlements and wild-dwelling peoples as someone who shows up exactly when needed and asks for nothing in return; contacts among druids, rangers, and others who move between worlds, though you were never trained by any of them.

**Feature Expansion — What the Wild Remembers**: when civilization and the wild come into real conflict, you are the one people think to send for — not hired, *sent for*, because coin was never the point; once per month, resolve a dispute or crisis neither side could have solved alone, simply by being someone both sides trust without fully understanding; advantage on all checks surviving or navigating any wilderness, however unfamiliar.

## Rank 4: Between Both Worlds

**Holdings**: nothing that could be taken, sold, or inherited — your worth was never measured that way; hundreds across both the wild and civilized world who'd answer if you called, though you rarely do; the strange, quiet authority of someone both sides need and neither side can claim.

**Feature — The Wild Answers**: you can move through any wilderness as though born to it, automatic success on Survival checks (DC 20 or lower); once per month, be present at exactly the moment civilization and the wild would otherwise collide catastrophically, and simply by being there, prevent it; nature itself seems to make way for you, and rulers who'd never admit it still send for you when nothing else has worked. You have never owned anything of value. You have never needed to. Songs are sung about you on both sides of the treeline, and neither side agrees on whether you belong to them.
