# The Wonder-Tithe Codex

*"When you understand something fully, it becomes boring. Therefore, wonder is sacred because it remains incomplete."*

Not a religion in the formal sense but a **devotional philosophy** — reverent curiosity, experimentation, and archival maintenance of divinity, practiced primarily (but not exclusively) by Gnomes. Followers are called Titheseekers, Glimpsers, or Echohands. Often mistaken by outsiders as "the religion of the Gnomes," though it is open to any race.

## Creation myth — The Salvage of the Divine

The first gnomes awoke beneath a collapsed sky, in a half-finished world the gods had left broken and abandoned mid-construction — stars out of sync, moons misaligned, divine echoes rattling in empty shrines "like loose screws in a holy machine." Rather than worship, they **salvaged**: naming the unnameable so it would stop unravelling, repurposing broken gods as furnace-spirits, pump-regulators, and boundary-definers, and archiving their findings in the mythic **Prime Vault**. "We did not find the gods. We fixed them."

## Core tenets

1. **Reverence Through Discovery** — the act of asking is worship.
2. **Preserve the First Glimpse** — the memory of first understanding a thing is holier than the knowledge itself.
3. **The Unknown Is a Friend** — not knowing is invitation, not failure.
4. **No Truth Is Final** — certainty is a prison.
5. **Record and Release** — name what you learn, but never trap it; once recorded, insight must be shared or released.

## The Salvaged Court

Gnomes never use gods' birth names, which they consider "dangerously unstable." Instead, deities from every pantheon are re-indexed by *function*, stripped of worship tradition and warlike aspects, and reassigned utilitarian roles: Astria becomes the Spark-Mother (invention/ignition), Thamur the Seal-Keeper (containment), Aelys the Echo-Archivist (memory), Nereus the Drift-Master (flow regulation), Xethar the Ember Judge (overheat/punishment subroutines), and so on. One entry, the **Null Root**, is a deliberately unnamed Earth/Eldritch crossfunction placeholder — "never invoked aloud."

## Practice

No priesthood — only tithe-bearers maintaining public curiosity-archives ("Echoledgers") in Wonder-Houses. Practices include the **Sparked Silence** (13 seconds recalling first awe before any new project), the **Joy-Drop** (leaving a token of wonder for a stranger to find), and the **Annual Unveiling** (Wonder-Houses open their vaults to celebrate failed experiments, not just successes).

## Relationship to the Weave and other faiths

Gnomes treat the Weave as "a sentient recursive system... running without documentation," to be logged and patched rather than mastered. Relations with other faiths vary sharply by how rigidly those faiths police reinterpretation:

- **[[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]]**: respected but "ceremonially bloated"; tolerated in turn, consulted as pattern-breakers in crisis.
- **[[The Joy Truths]]** (Halfling): a kindred philosophy — mutual affection, some halfling oral traditions now survive only in gnome vaults.
- **[[The Deep Law]]** (Dwarf): respected but seen as over-engineered and dangerously inflexible; tolerated with caution.
- **[[The Burning Concord]]** (Dragonborn): anathema to the Concord — gnomes who rename gods openly in Concord lands are purged or enslaved.
- **[[The Ashen Web]]** (Drow): hostile on both sides — gnomes classify Ashen fragments as "black-level collapse nodes"; Vel'Zyren's faith treats gnomes as prey, useful only until they aren't.
- **[[The Path of the First Scar]]** (Orc): mutual respect once trust is earned by naming salvaged gods aloud.

## Timeline (condensed)

Loomless Time (proto-tithing, divine scavenging) → First Age (Codex of Salvaged Names formalized, Prime Vault mythically established) → Second Age (near-extinguished by Concord Flame Purge campaigns, survives via Portable Shrines) → Third Age (flourishes in Loom-Cities as the unofficial faith of scholars and inventors) → Fourth Age (present: quiet emergency-containment role, deploying "Mnemonic Protocols" to record gods before they vanish, credited rarely despite being relied upon).
