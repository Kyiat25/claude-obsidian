# Shattered Moon

*"The sky broke before anything else. It just never stopped screaming."*

First seen 2nd Age, 1724 SA, during the Siege of Vaernhold. Not a lunar phase but a memory of celestial catastrophe.

## Nature

Appears as a fragmented halo of broken shards orbiting a void; the shards gleam with shearing resonance. It is the memory of the first leyline implosion — the moment the Glyph of Severance first touched the sky.

## World effects

Physical structures creak, fracture, and collapse; non-magical buildings have a 10% chance per day of structural failure; spell durations shatter early unless concentration is held at disadvantage. Arcane glyphs become visible to the naked eye, and failed spellcasting releases a Weave Shard (10-ft radius force/psychic damage).

**Monster effects**: constructs, elementals, and summoned creatures become unstable; summon durations are halved; constructs break down, transmitting glyph-noise. Form-shifters risk partial transformation lock, becoming misshapen and multi-staged.

## Madness

Short-term: hallucinations of cracking and splintering. Long-term: belief the world is a dream being broken. Indefinite: phobia of containment, compulsively breaking things.

## Glyph interactions and resistance

Universal Glyphs of Balance, Time, and Arcane Flow destabilize — time-glyphs repeat or skip, form-glyphs cause polymorph failures or doubled body parts; Personal Glyphs visibly vibrate when cast, as if struggling to stay bound; Shatter, Dispel Magic, and Counterspell all function at +1 spell level but risk backlash on repeated same-day use. [[Gaeldros]], the Stone of Judgment, becomes volatile, his bones said to groan under pressure trying to keep the land from unraveling; the Fate-Weaver cannot maintain her Thread of Prediction, returning only fragmented riddles; Ceymarn, the Whisper-Vigil, is overwhelmed, sometimes seen sealing cracks in empty air while weeping threads of light.

## Campaign use

A wizard's tower collapses, debris humming with half-finished spells; the sky cracks (not visibly, but divination reveals it); a new shard-language appears.
