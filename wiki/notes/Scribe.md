# Scribe (Clerk) — Rank 1-4

*"You're a professional writer and keeper of records."* Batch 3, Urban Specialist category. Source dual-names this "Scribe / Clerk"; filed under the first name.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Junior Clerk | Professional Scribe | Uncover important information in records, prevent fraud through careful notation, or be trusted with sensitive documents |
| 2 | Senior Clerk | Records Master | Expose major corruption through record analysis, manage a critical government function, or create a widely-adopted system |
| 3 | Chief Clerk | Master of Information | Become secretary to a ruler, create a recordkeeping system for a realm, or uncover a conspiracy through documents |
| 4 | Grand Archivist | Master of Records | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Junior Clerk

**Skill Proficiency**: History, Investigation. **Tool Proficiency**: calligrapher's supplies. **Language**: one of choice. **Equipment**: calligrapher's supplies, a 1 oz ink bottle, 20 sheets of parchment, a small knife, 10 gp.

**Feature — Professional Scribe**: write quickly and legibly (double normal writing speed); copy documents accurately; advantage on checks to forge handwriting or documents; read difficult handwriting or faded text; know where to find public records in your city.

**Holdings**: a desk in a government office or business; access to archives and records; basic writing supplies; a supervisor or senior clerk.

## Rank 2: Senior Clerk

**Holdings**: a private office or desk; a salary (20 gp/month); a complete set of quality writing tools; access to restricted records; authority to certify documents; 2-3 junior clerks assisting you.

**Feature Expansion — Records Master**: find specific archive information in 1/4 normal time; advantage on Investigation checks related to documents; identify forged documents automatically (DC 15 or lower); know legal and bureaucratic procedures; once per week, "find" a document that went missing.

## Rank 3: Chief Clerk

**Holdings**: a private office with staff; a good salary (60 gp/month); an archive or library under your control; 8-12 clerks working under you; authority over documentation standards; access to all non-classified information; your signature on official documents carries weight.

**Feature Expansion — Master of Information**: locate any public information in your city within 24 hours; certify or verify any document's authenticity; advantage on all Intelligence checks related to records, laws, or bureaucracy; once per month, declare a document "lost" or "found"; your organizational systems are models of efficiency.

## Rank 4: Grand Archivist

**Holdings**: a grand archive or national library; an excellent salary (150 gp/month); authority over all government records; 30+ scribes and clerks; access to classified information; your word on documentation is law; historical documents under your protection.

**Feature — Master of Records**: locate any recorded information in your realm, given time; automatic success on records/documentation checks (DC 20 or lower); once per month, uncover a "lost" historical document that proves crucial; identify any forgery instantly; your archives are the authoritative source for history and law. Rulers consult you on historical precedent, lawyers cite your archives, and scholars must get your permission to access certain documents.
