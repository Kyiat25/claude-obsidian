# Fracture Scout — Rank 1-4

*"You explore and map wild magic zones where reality has broken."* Batch 3, FEARS-Specific category. The cartography-and-survival counterpart to [[Pattern Keeper]]'s religious-maintenance approach to the same [[Unstable Magic Zones|wild magic zones]] — Fracture Scouts map and survive them; Pattern Keepers ritually stabilize them. The two backgrounds explicitly reference each other's field at Rank 4 ("Pattern Keepers consult you on zones").

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Zone Observer | Wild Magic Awareness | Successfully map a dangerous zone, survive a wild magic catastrophe, or discover a new zone |
| 2 | Fracture Mapper | Reality Navigation | Map a previously unknown zone, survive a reality tear, or discover safe paths through a hazardous area |
| 3 | Master Scout | Fracture Master | Map a legendary zone, discover a stable route through a major fracture, or survive a zone no one has returned from |
| 4 | Archscout of the Fractures | Master of Broken Space | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Zone Observer

**Skill Proficiency**: Arcana, Survival. **Tool Proficiency**: cartographer's tools. **Equipment**: cartographer's tools, a reality anchor charm (minor protection), a journal of observations, protective clothing, 50 ft of rope, 15 gp.

**Feature — Wild Magic Awareness**: advantage on Arcana checks related to wild magic; identify an approaching wild magic zone within 1 mile; know basic safety protocols for wild magic exposure; estimate a zone's danger level by observation; advantage on saves against wild magic effects, first save only.

**Holdings**: a small tent with minor reality anchors; basic protective equipment; maps of known wild magic zones; a contact with a mage guild or research organization; danger pay for observations.

## Rank 2: Fracture Mapper

**Holdings**: quality reality anchor equipment; danger pay (35 gp/month); complete cartography tools; protective gear and antidotes; a collection of safely-stored wild magic samples; a network of 5-7 other scouts; mage guilds purchase your maps.

**Feature Expansion — Reality Navigation**: navigate through wild magic zones without getting lost, usually; advantage on all saves in wild magic zones; predict some wild magic surges (Arcana check); know which zones are currently active/dormant; once per week, guide a party safely through a dangerous zone.

## Rank 3: Master Scout

**Holdings**: advanced reality anchoring equipment; major danger pay (100 gp/month); dozens of explored wild magic zones; 3-5 junior scouts training under you; contracts with multiple organizations; your maps are authoritative; protective equipment few others possess.

**Feature Expansion — Fracture Master**: resistance to damage from wild magic effects; sense safe paths through zones automatically; advantage on all checks in wild magic zones; once per month, navigate through a zone thought impassable; identify the cause and age of wild magic zones; predict when zones will expand or shift.

## Rank 4: Archscout of the Fractures

**Holdings**: a personal reality anchor network; excellent pay (250 gp/month); you've explored every known zone and discovered new ones; an organization of 20+ scouts; governments contract you for dangerous expeditions; your knowledge shapes magical theory; equipment includes relics from the pre-death era.

**Feature — Master of Broken Space**: immune to the negative effects of wild magic zones; navigate any wild magic zone safely, automatic success; once per year, establish a safe route through an "impossible" zone; sense reality tears before they form; may be able to slightly stabilize zones through presence alone. Wizards seek your knowledge of wild magic, Pattern Keepers consult you on zones, and you may have visited zones so broken you saw other realities — some say you've seen where gods died.
