# Cross-Pantheon Relationships of the Spirit Pantheon

*"The others are threads. Spirit is the loom the threads run through. You do not befriend the loom. You are woven on it."*

A GM-facing design reference (`doc-status: needs-rulings` — a working draft with several `⚑`-flagged proposals awaiting a table-owner decision, not settled canon). First of a matched seven-part set analyzing how each pantheon, plus the Eldritch and [[Vel'Zyren]], relates to the others through a single lens: **worship-pressure shapes the god**.

## Why Spirit relates differently

Spirit is the least tangible pantheon — the medium the other four are written on, not an element you can touch. This produces three consequences: it is the **least anchored**, so its gods drift, merge, and fray more than any others; as the medium rather than a material, almost every Spirit domain (memory, fate, death, forgetting, oaths, magic itself) has a **shadow-twin claimed by another pantheon through an element**; and its outermost gods shade toward the Eldritch, since Spirit handles the same raw substrate the Whispering Dark corrupts.

**The proposed spine**: Spirit's textile language (Weave, Thread, Pattern) is simultaneously the truest description available from inside the setting *and* the central misunderstanding every villain exploits — Spirit is the pantheon that most believes its own metaphor, because its metaphor is its domain.

## The contested domains (axes with other pantheons)

- **Memory — Spirit ↔ Water**: [[Nyzara]] weaves the past into time, retrievable; Water's [[Vaelora]] holds it drowned, unrecoverable but incorruptible. Proposed as a rivalry disguised as kinship, not simple cooperation.
- **Death — Spirit ↔ all four**: Spirit holds a proposed whole death-cycle (sever → threshold → passage → rebirth, [[Serelune]] guarding the seam) while each element grasps only one phase (Earth: the cut/burial; Water: retention; Air: release; Fire: judgment) — see [[The Echoed Mother]], [[Restbringer]] for the two offices without prior pages.
- **Fate & Prophecy — Spirit ↔ Air**: fate as cuttable thread (Spirit) vs. fate as fixed omen-law (Air's Astria) — hard-coded in canon as an enmity (Valthos names Astria his enemy).
- **Forgetting — Spirit ↔ Earth**: erasure into the void (Astralis) vs. burial under stone (Mourndrel) — with major stakes for whether a "forgotten" memory is truly gone or merely sealed and recoverable.
- **Oaths — Spirit ↔ Earth ↔ Water**: a three-way division of labor (thread of obligation vs. stone-carved vs. tide-bound) rather than rivalry.
- **Magic itself — Spirit ↔ everyone**: [[Aelys]] maintains the Weave every pantheon's magic runs through — not a rivalry but total dependency, making her the keystone and the single point of failure the Eldritch threat to her endangers all magic through.
- **The Eldritch edge**: Spirit's corrupted/frayed gods ([[Kyren]], among others) are proposed as a *gradient* into the Eldritch rather than a hard wall — Spirit borders the slag because it deals in the same raw thread the slag is made of.

## Special cases

**[[Vel'Zyren]], the Defector** — not a rival but an apostate who tried to replace the medium itself (the Web for the Weave); feared by her Water nemeses in the one place her control fails. **The Aelys/Echoed Mother conflation** — proposed to be canonized as a real Loomless near-merger (the memory-function and rebirth-function hadn't yet separated into two threads) rather than resolved as an error, per this doc's Tier-1 ruling recommendation.
