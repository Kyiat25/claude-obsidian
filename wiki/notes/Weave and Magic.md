# Weave and Magic

A source that restates and lightly extends the Five Layers cosmology from [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]], adding the Triadic Reflection framing and naming the forbidden [[Glyph of Severance]] for the first time in this cluster.

## The Five Layers, restated

Confirms the same five-layer structure already documented on [[Primal Glyphs]], [[Universal Glyphs]], [[Elemental Glyphs]], [[Form Glyphs]], and [[Personal Glyphs]] — each of those pages is built primarily from this source's content plus [[Weave of Creation]]'s parallel (and slightly more detailed) version.

## The Triadic Reflection

Every magical structure reflects differently across the three layers of reality: the Material Plane, [[Luminous Echo]] (Feywild), and [[Umbral Veil]] (Shadowfell). In the Luminous Echo, magic clings to emotion, memory, and resonance. In the Umbral Veil, spells warp with grief, silence, and decay. In the Material Plane, balance is maintained, but the scars of the other two layers bleed through.

## Leylines and magical storms

[[Leylines]] are described here as the veins of the world's magic — stable leylines create predictable magical zones, unstable leylines lead to chaos, and when leylines rupture entirely, **Magical Storms** occur, erupting magical energy uncontrollably and warping reality.

## The Lost Glyph of Severance

Introduces [[Glyph of Severance]] — the Unmaker's Mark, a Glyph erased from existence for being too dangerous, distinct from the Glyph of Endings' ordinary death-and-rebirth cycle. Full page cross-references its later appearances across [[Guide to the Eldritch Forces]] and [[The Hollow Thread]].

## The Hidden Architects

Restates the "none of them fully understand" Final Truth already established: [[Astralor]] believes he forges reality but is merely using tools shaped by the Universal Glyphs; [[Aeloria]] believes she weaves fate but is only interpreting part of a larger pattern; [[The Whispering Dark]] thinks it consumes all but doesn't realize [[The Restorer]] ensures nothing is truly lost.
