# Reedford — the Last Good Dock

A transshipment river-town (~8,500 permanent population, ~13,000 at peak season) on the Ivora River, ~280 miles upstream from Velcran, on the geographic and cultural border where [[Corvessa]]'s Faith-saturated hierarchy shades into Imperial pragmatism. It exists purely because the river forces cargo to change hands there: below Reedford the Ivora is wide and navigable by deep-drafted estuary barges; above it the channel narrows and only flat-bottomed skiffs can pass. Everyone in town — lord, priest, pilot, stevedore, beggar — lives on their relationship to what flows through the warehouses.

## Lord Cassin Valdrek

House Valdrek is only three generations noble — founder Boren Valdrek was a river merchant elevated to the lordship for supplying the retreating army after the Thornward Flats disaster (472 FA), which the old cavalry houses have never quite forgiven. Cassin (49) is a pragmatic "functional lord" who taxes river tolls, transshipment, warehousing, and quay fees rather than producing anything himself, and quietly tolerates — even profits from — the grey economy running through his warehouses' overnight storage. His son **Aldren** (26) has embraced Faith zealotry his father finds "exhausting and privately worrying," and is unknowingly acting as Inquisitor Cors's inside source against his own family.

## The Sanctum of the Measured Flow

Reedford's Water Thread temple is run by **Brother-Warden Sethos Annyr** (54, nineteen years in post), a pragmatic priest whose doctrine — "the Ivora does not ask what it carries" — conveniently absolves the town's grey trade. He has kept eighteen pages of secret notes on "the river's second voice": the blessing-water has tasted wrong for six months, and since [[The Threadbearer Crisis|the Threadbearer assassinations]] eighteen days ago, the wrongness has stopped being subtle. His opposite number, **Inquisitor Delvane Cors** (38, Fraywatcher), is a sincere true believer — not a villain, but doctrinally correct about the grey economy's heresy and doctrinally blind to everything else, the first Fraywatcher in nineteen years to look at Reedford closely enough to threaten Sethos's careful equilibrium.

## Institutions

The **Wardbreakers** (guild charter — from House Valdrek directly, not the Crown or Faith, four generations old, led by Guild Master **Renn Ashford**) handle the three threat categories Corvessan cavalry can't: Greenwood Bleed (displaced Talanthar wildlife, most of their work), Thornward Residue (spiritual corruption lingering from six cavalry campaigns since 312 FA against the killing ground fifteen years on), and less-natural Thread-instability phenomena. Funded by a cargo levy that falls mainly on merchants, not the farmers who need the protection most — a structural gap nobody is solving. Ashford's theurge-trained cleric operates at the edge of Faith sanction, which Inquisitor Cors is building a case around. The grey economy runs through three supply channels coordinated in part by river-pilot **Joss**, who maintains secret Talanthari trade connections that Lord Valdrek quietly takes a finder's fee from. Imperial customs interests are represented by Factor **Harun Belessi** (52, eleven years in post) — whose reports to Kel Varyan have recently begun receiving unusually specific, fast-turnaround attention from unfamiliar contacts, unknowingly making him a data source for [[The Threadbearer Crisis|Naleth Spiralbound's]] consolidation of power through the Imperial administrative apparatus; the party's names will already be in his files before they arrive in town.
