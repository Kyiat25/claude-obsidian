# Divine Sparks

Pure fragments of unresolved divinity — not energy, but "the glyph of potential divinity": a crystallization of identity, power, memory, and will with no form until claimed. Introduced in [[Creation World]] as the origin of the first gods; [[The Lie of Godhood]] independently confirms and substantially deepens the concept with mechanical/campaign detail Creation World never gives.

## How they came to exist

None of the four Primal Forces intended to create them. Each contributed unknowingly:

- **[[Astralor]]** forged them when iterations of perfection collapsed into unresolved creation.
- **[[Aeloria]]** bound them into thread when she wove fate into things that had no place.
- **[[The Whispering Dark]]** rejected them, yet could not unmake them fully.
- **[[The Restorer]]** pulled them from the void, refusing to let their stories end.

Together, entirely by accident, the four forces produced the raw material of godhood. The source frames this as proof that "some truths resist removal" — ideas and stories that refuse to vanish even under a cosmology explicitly built to test, trim, devour, and revise everything that exists.

## Becoming a god

Whoever or whatever first finds a Divine Spark doesn't recognize what they've found. The source names three kinds of first-touchers: creatures suddenly given voice and vision, echoes of past failed creations clinging to new structure, and mortals who "should never have existed" in the first place. On contact, the toucher changes, and the Weave — described as "hungry for anchors" — writes them into the Pattern. This is how the first gods came to be: some ascended in silence, some tore holes in the world, some screamed against the pattern forming around them. All were "caught, unknowingly, in the trap of divinity."

## Origins, per The Lie of Godhood

Where [[Creation World]] describes one accidental collision, [[The Lie of Godhood]] catalogs four distinct Spark origins: **Primal Sparks** (born from Weave convulsions — raw, dangerous, reality-warping), **Living Sparks** (bound inside mythical creatures such as the White Stag or the Eternal Wolf — nature's own answer to divinity), **Residual Sparks** (left behind when gods die, often soaked in memory and danger), and **False Sparks** (twisted by Eldritch forces — specifically named whispers from [[Xey'Tazun]] or [[Mirag'Nael]] infecting divine fragments). Some mortals are born already carrying a Spark — called **mythbreakers**, whose very birth disrupts prophecy and Weave-stability.

## The Mythalix

To touch a Spark without a **Mythalix** — a soul-forged vessel attuned to both [[Form Glyphs]] and [[Personal Glyphs]] — is fatal. A Mythalix is not an object but a crafted echo shaped to fit the ascendant's deepest pattern. Without one, the Spark burns, splinters, or draws the attention of higher forces; with one, the Spark may root, but only if the bearer survives the **Rite of Reckoning**. Even then ascension isn't instant — the Spark must be nurtured through belief, stabilized by purpose, and protected from erasure, or it may fade, fracture, or be claimed by another.

## The War of Ascendants

Seeking a Divine Spark means seeking war: against rival ascendants who hunt Sparks like prey, against divine enforcers (the Knights of the Final Thread, Vaedoris of the Spirit Court) who purge unlicensed gods, and against the Pattern itself, which rejects unworthy deities through fatestorms, memory collapse, or prophetic backlash. The path to godhood is one-way and irrevocable.

## The Glyph of Sovereignty

A glyph believed by kings and demigods to represent rulership — it grants burden, not control. It binds the divine to the structure; it is a shackle, not a crown. The first who wore it is said to have screamed for a hundred years, their voice still echoing in forgotten leylines; no god has ever removed it once bound.

> [!contradiction] Is Astralor a Primal Force, or "not a god" — the Law of Becoming?
> Every other cosmology source in this cluster ([[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]], [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]], [[Twins of Creation]], [[Creation World]], [[Creations Misunderstanding]]) treats [[Astralor]] as one of four Primal Forces and [[Aeloria]]'s literal sibling — a person, however cosmic. [[The Lie of Godhood]] instead states flatly that the Divine Rank System governing ascension "was not created by the gods... shaped by the glyphic Will of Astralor, one of the Universal Forces — **not a god, but the Law of Becoming**. It is a pattern, not a person." It adds that no god knows of Astralor before ascending, and doesn't understand it even afterward — only feeling "the inescapable weight of the structure into which they are drawn." This is a direct reframing, not a minor detail: every other source's Astralor has personality, memory, and a sibling; this source's Astralor is an impersonal cosmic law. Flagged on [[Astralor]]'s own page as well — not resolved, treated the same as the standing three-way knowledge-hierarchy contradiction on [[The Weave]].

## Open questions

- Whether any currently-worshipped deity in the setting (including any Earth Pantheon figures like Vatharun, named in [[World Trees]]'s contradiction note) originated from a Divine Spark specifically, or through some other means — not addressed by this source or any other ingested so far.
- Whether Divine Sparks still form today, or whether the phenomenon was a one-time consequence of the earliest cycles of the Creation World. The source frames it in origin-story terms ("the first gods") without stating whether the process is ongoing.
- Whether becoming a god via Divine Spark is experienced as a blessing, a curse, or something genuinely ambiguous — the three outcome examples (silent ascension, world-tearing, screaming against the pattern) suggest it varies wildly and isn't a controlled or benevolent process.
