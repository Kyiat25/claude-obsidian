# Corvessa — The River Kingdom of Faith and Steel

Feudal monarchy on [[Elarion]], client kingdom of [[Kel Varyan]] (semi-autonomous, bound by tribute and treaty). ~72,000 sq mi along the 900-mile Ivora River, population ~3.2 million (First Estate ~1,200 River Lords controlling 60% of arable land; Second Estate ~45,000 Knightly Houses; Third Estate ~160,000 Faith clergy; Fourth Estate ~2.5 million commoners; ~80,000 unestated mercenaries/traders). Economy: wine, timber (goldwood/ironwood), paper. Corvessa "sanctifies its defeats" — a kingdom whose entire national identity is organized around an unwinnable holy war.

## The Pattern Perverted

Corvessa follows the Faith of the Bound Pattern, but where the mainstream Faith teaches balance among the Five Threads, Corvessa has turned it into crusader doctrine: civilization-as-worship, deviation-as-heresy, the axe as the sacred weapon (it both wages war and prunes sacred groves), martyrdom as proof of faith, and — above all — the belief that [[Talanthar]]'s World Tree **Vaelthir must fall**. The *Prophecy of the Felled Root* (Book of the Pattern's Triumph, 17:12-24) promises a final seven-day battle in which the "Hooves of the Faithful" ride down the World Tree and raise a "Spire of a Thousand Threads" in its place. Three factions interpret it differently: the **Literalists** (dominant, believe it's imminent and every campaign might be the one), the **Cyclicists** (a generations-long spiritual process), and the persecuted **Reformists** (read it as a metaphor for internal corruption, not a literal war).

## Crown and estates

**High Crown Selvan III of House Viraelis** rules — caught between an Empire growing impatient with Corvessa's failures and a nobility demanding the Crusade continue regardless of cost; his father died of shame after the catastrophic **Thornward Flats** disaster (482 FA, the latest of five "Great Failures" against Talanthar), while his own son burns to avenge it. Military and religious authority are fused: the **River Lances** (elite heavy cavalry, commanded by an ordained Thread-Warden), the **Faith Guard** (clergy-soldier infantry — Paladins, War Clerics, Zealot Barbarians), the **Augurates** (battle-mage diviners, mostly Corvessa-unique Theurges), and the **Sanctified Blades** (Inquisitor Rogue and Blade of Radiance assassins serving the Fraywatchers).

## Geography

The Ivora River defines the kingdom: the **Upper Ivora Hills** (vineyards, oldest noble houses), the **Middle Crossing Lands** (fortified bridges doubling as chapel-fortresses, market towns), the **Lower Ivora Flats** (breadbasket floodplains, near the killing ground of Thornward Flats), and the **Western Marches** (goldwood/ironwood forestry, the jointly-administered charter port of Velcran — a source of noble resentment toward Imperial merchants). Bordered northeast by the hostile Greenwood/Talanthar frontier, south by Imperial trade corridors, west by hill country and the sea.

## Doctrine versus reality

Corvessa's cavalry doctrine (concentrate overwhelming force, break enemy lines with shock charge, hold ground) has crushed rebel levies and steppe raiders, but has failed against Talanthar **five times** — the terrain doesn't allow massed cavalry maneuver, campaigns are plagued by unseasonable rains, and Talanthar simply refuses to fight the way Corvessa expects (no massed formations, only ambush and misdirection). Corvessa's response each time has been to intensify rather than adapt — more knights, more consecrated weapons, more faith. The First Failure (312-314 FA), "the First Ride of Purity," sent ten thousand horse and levy into the Greenwood; only two heralds returned, their skin bearing living vine scars that never healed.
