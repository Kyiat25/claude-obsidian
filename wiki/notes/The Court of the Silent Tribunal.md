# The Court of the Silent Tribunal

*"The Weave does not forget. And when gods err, they are brought to the place where memory is law, silence is truth, and flame does not burn—it brands."* — [[Aelys]], the Eternal Scribe

A place not bound by geography but by glyph, memory, and judgment — a convergence where all divine law must eventually pass, and where even Eldritch echoes are weighed against the Pattern of the Weave itself. Neither feared nor worshipped; simply there, beneath the echoes of law, above the bones of guilt, within the space between divine thought and mortal memory.

## The Axis of Divine Judgment

Served by five eternal figures, one from each elemental pantheon:

| Role | Name | Pantheon | Function |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Judge** | [[Gaeldros]] | Earth | Delivers final rulings — his word becomes leyline-law |
| **The Scribe** | [[Aelys]] | Spirit | Records intention and outcome in mirrored glyphs, written into memory |
| **The Flame** | Ignis | Fire | Brands the ruling into reality via the Ember Throne — inescapable memory |
| **The Historian** | [[Astria]] | Air | Interprets omens and patterns, provides cosmic precedent and foresight |
| **The Messenger** | Selmira | Water | Delivers verdicts through tides, dreams, and cycles, ensuring they are known |

No seat exists for the Eldritch — but they are judged all the same.

## The Ember Throne

At the centre of all memory stands the Ember Throne, a burning paradox that neither consumes nor destroys — it sears truth into permanence. Ignis, the Eternal Flame, sits only during judgment; his fire is the last step, and once he brands an edict, it is unforgotten, even by those who wish to forget it. "You will not agree. But you will remember. That is enough."

## Notable trials

**The Trial of Soltharion**: crime of tyranny, glyphic domination, godly overreach. Judgment: banished to the Sun, his flames now power but do not speak. See also [[Soltharion]]'s own myth of the Ember Throne Challenged.

**The Trial of the Web-Spinner**: crime of rewriting fate, spinning her own Weave, corrupting the Threads of the Pattern. Judgment: exile to the Underdark, severed from all Threads but Earth. Full account on [[The Trial of the Web-Spinner]] and [[Vel'Zyren]]'s own page — this trial marked the moment the Weave first failed to undo a divine act, a fracture echoing in every loom-thread since.

**The Drowned Edict**: when the mortal city of Delmirath defied a Tribunal ruling, Selmira delivered judgment with a silent flood; its bell still rings below the waves.

**The Forgotten Sentence**: believed erased by [[Nhal'Zereth]] — the gods disagree whether the edict was ever real. Aelys still scratches blank glyphs onto stone, refusing to forget.

**The Trial of the Whispered Lie**: crime of twisting prophecy, elemental deceit of the Weave. Defendant [[Zephir]], witnessed by [[Astria]], invoked by [[Thirian]]. Judgment: Zephir condemned to speak only in illusions and riddles. Full account on [[Zephir]]'s and [[Astria]]'s own pages.

## Divine balance

No pantheon governs the Tribunal — each Thread speaks its part, and even when the gods disagree, a ruling once passed is unbreakable. The Eldritch have never stood within it, but their echoes bleed into its walls; Zhurr'Kael has attempted to contradict its logic, and Esh'Tarah (the Mirror That Screams) has reflected false trials. Still, the Tribunal endures.
