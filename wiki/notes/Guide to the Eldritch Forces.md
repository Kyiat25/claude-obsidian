# Guide to the Eldritch Forces: The Gods of Unbeing and Their Cosmic Wounds

> *"They are not gods. They are errors. Echoes of what should never have been. And yet, they are here."* — Thamur, Guardian Below

This is the master campaign guide for the Eldritch cluster — a large, `status: complete` source spanning three tiers of threat, the plane where they originate, pantheon-by-pantheon vulnerability, and full campaign-integration material.

## The cosmic hierarchy of unbeing

Unlike the Five Pantheons (elemental and spiritual order), the Eldritch forces represent cosmic disorder — not chaos, but **anti-existence**, operating on three distinct levels:

| Tier | Type | Origin | Campaign threat level |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Eldritch Gods** | Anti-creation forces | Spawned when [[The Whispering Dark]] touched the Weave | Apocalyptic — can unmake reality itself |
| **Eldritch Spirits** | Sentient symptoms | Manifestations of infection or failed creation | Regional to World-level — highly variable |
| **Eldritch Moons** | Cosmic manifestations | Weave-wounds appearing when reality fractures | Variable — from omen to cataclysm |

## The Whispering Dark, reframed

The primordial force that "should not exist" — not evil, not destructive, but anti-existence itself. When it first brushed against the Weave, reality cracked, and through those cracks the Eldritch Gods bled into being. Not a god in any traditional sense; cannot be fought directly, only its symptoms; no pantheon claims it because acknowledging it gives it form; even speaking of it can invite its attention. This significantly deepens [[The Whispering Dark]]'s existing characterization from the core cosmology cluster — see that page for the cross-reference.

## The Eldritch Spirits

Not deities, but sentient symptoms of the Eldritch infection — fragments of greater gods, independent echoes of forgotten truths, unrecorded and unintended but terrifying. Full roster and tier system on [[Eldritch Spirits]].

## The Eldritch Moons

Weave-wounds, not celestial bodies — lunar overlays, echoes of impossible orbits, manifestations of [[The Null Reflection]] pressing close when the Weave frays. Full chronology and 14 detailed profiles (against a stated "Twelve," a source-internal discrepancy) on [[Eldritch Moons]].

## Campaign integration: the four-tier escalation

| Tier (Levels) | Focus | Example threat |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (1-4) | Local corruption — mystery/survival | Single Eldritch Spirit or minor moon effect |
| 2 (5-10) | Regional crisis — investigation/prevention | Major spirit, single dangerous moon, early god influence |
| 3 (11-16) | World-level threat — containment/sacrifice | Multiple spirits, moon convergence, direct god influence |
| 4 (17-20) | Apocalypse prevention — cosmic stakes | Moon convergence, Hollow Vault breach, direct god manifestation |

### Eldritch corruption exposure stages

Witness → Contact → Infection → Transformation → Vessel, each stage escalating from reversible (rest/cleansing) to effectively permanent (becoming a servant of the Eldritch force itself). Natural resistance comes from a strong Personal Glyph (sense of self), clear purpose, strong emotional bonds, or divine protection.

### The most important rule (as the source states it)

The Eldritch are not villains with motivations to understand or deals to make — "they simply are," pressed against reality like water against a dam. They cannot be defeated traditionally; only contained. Victory is temporary; survival is victory. This framing governs every Eldritch-adjacent page in this cluster: don't write them as antagonists with goals, write them as forces with a nature.

## The Ultimate Stakes: the Hollow Vault connection

This document's own endgame material converges entirely on [[The Hollow Thread]] — the Eldritch Gods' collective, if uncoordinated, goal is to see the Hollow Vault unsealed and the First Corrupted Leyline released. Four campaign endgame options are offered with no "correct" answer: preserve the seal (accept necessary evil), find an alternative (immense sacrifice, uncertain), controlled release (incredibly dangerous), or embrace the unmaking (everything ends). See [[The Hollow Thread]] for the full cascading-failure timeline and faction breakdown.
