# Yellow Crown

*"It is not worn. It is recognised. And once seen, it never stops seeing you."*

First seen 3rd Age, 654 TA, during a coronation marred by assassination. Not a celestial object but a glowing ring that hovers, rotating imperceptibly.

## Nature

A shadow of forgotten divinity — royalty erased by the Glyph of Unbeing. Possibly [[Nhal'Zereth]]'s own coronation, or the crown [[Vezherak]] will one day wear.

## World effects

**Crown of Perception**: symbols of authority appear where none exist; leaders feel a pull, watched for their "worthiness." Authority figures must make a DC 15 Wisdom save at moonrise; failure grants the Mark of the Yellow Crown, a glowing ring visible in shadow or in the eyes. **The Test**: the Marked gain advantage on Intimidation and Insight, and slowly speak as if commanding even when unnecessary. **Obedience Spiral**: NPCs become hyper-suggestible toward marked individuals, compulsion effects gain +2 DC, and defying a marked individual requires a daily save or paranoia develops.

## Madness

Short-term: a desire to be recognized, fear of being overlooked. Long-term: belief one is destined to rule, hostility toward rivals. Indefinite: accepting that "the Crown wears you," not the other way around.

## Campaign use

A forgotten noble house reclaims a city overnight; a mage binds a summoning to the Crown so the summoned creatures believe they're serving a king; a PC receives loyalty oaths from strangers.
