# Orc

*"They gave us laws. We broke them. They offered peace. We spat it back. They called us beasts. So we made the name sacred."*

**Origin myth**: per the Ledger of the Scarred Line (see [[The Path of the First Scar]]), Orcs were not created by gods but *tested* by them — every deity that tried to conquer, silence, or twist Orcish people had that trial turned into a title earned in blood: Grak-Thul the First Scar (fire), Ugra-Mara the Ash-Mother (earth), Korrun the Iron-Tongue (spirit), Varkhul the Wound That Laughs (air). All gods are adversaries first; anything earned through worship must first be taken through struggle. Parent lineage to [[Half-Orc (Lineage)]] and its Trial of Line-Claiming.

**Physical**: mature by 14, live ~80 years; Medium, 6–7 ft.

## Traits

- **Darkvision** 60 ft.
- **Relentless Endurance**: drop to 1 HP instead of 0, 1/long rest
- **Powerful Build**: counts one size larger for carrying capacity
- **Aggressive**: bonus action move toward a visible/audible enemy, must end closer
- **Scar-Reader**: Insight proficiency; advantage reading others' combat history from scars
- **Challenge-Tongue**: speaking a true name in Orcish adds proficiency bonus to Intimidation/Performance
- **Languages**: Common + Orcish

**Mental**: Focus +0 / Sanity +1 (fear/pain reframed as tactical information, not overwhelm).

**DCS Integration**: Dodge +0 / Endure +2 / Deflect +0; DR +1. Advantage on Rally, higher casualty threshold to break — the highest-Endure, most durable "enduring juggernaut" profile documented among the lineages read so far.

## Corruption: The Flame-False

Corruption enters through broken oaths, dishonored scars, or fractured lineages. At Corruption 10+: Fractured Memory, Name-Weight (speaking your name hurts), Oath-Strain. **Fractured Scar table** (1d6): False Honor, Ancestral Static (Relentless Endurance disabled), Scarline Fracture (1d4 psychic + lose a skill), Weight of Lies (Sanity save or −1d4), Eldritch Touch (+1 Whisper Point, Challenge-Tongue auto-fails), or Scarline Holds (Corruption −1).

## Cultural touchstones

Scar-Wardens — judges, executioners, memory-keepers, and death-guides belonging to no clan, whose judgment is final; attacking one is treated as an attempt to break the Line's memory itself. The Path teaches death is not an end but "the gate to the longest challenge."
