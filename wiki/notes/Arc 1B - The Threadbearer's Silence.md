# Arc 1B — The Threadbearer's Silence ("The Fraying Threads")

The canonical, production-updated version of [[Campaign Arcs (The Great Deceivers and Truth-Seekers)|Category 1: The Suppressors]]'s second arc, and the fullest available account of who is actually behind [[The Threadbearer Crisis]].

## Naleth Spiralbound

Wood elf, 187 years old — the sole surviving Threadbearer (Water Thread), publicly "the Miraculous Survivor": serene, maternal, working 18-hour days maintaining the Vault of the Abyss, training temporary Thread-monitors, and founding a Threadbearer Seminary, all while every Loom-City treats her survival as proof of divine favor.

> [!note] Naming variance across sources
> An earlier, less-detailed compact summary (`BBEG_Campaign_Arcs_Part1.md`) named this character "Grand Threadbearer Naleth the Preserver," a 400-year-old Moon Elf. This individual, "canonical version" source instead gives Wood Elf, 187, "Naleth Spiralbound" — matching the name used on [[Elarion]]'s page ("Naleth Spiralbound (Spirit Thread) survived"). Treat this page's details as the more current/specific canon, and [[Kel Varyan]]'s "Serana Tidecaller" as a third alias layer for the same underlying figure per [[The Threadbearer Crisis]]'s resolution.

Privately: a brilliant theologian and ruthless pragmatist who has spent 80 years studying Thread mechanics and concluded that democratic Thread oversight is a category error — *"you don't vote on whether gravity works."* Cycle 4's four Threadbearers were all reform-minded "questioners" who had begun comparing notes, studying wood elf druids, and connecting dangerous archaeological evidence; Naleth personally murdered all four to stop the pattern before it destabilized the Thread-system further, not primarily to hide the cosmological truth (she considers the truth irrelevant) but to protect *function* and *stability*.

## How she killed four Threadbearers simultaneously

Using her own Water Thread mastery (which allows remote manipulation of blood and fluids in any body) plus years of preparation and trusted relationships with all four victims: **Korvus** (Earth, [[Thar Vornhal]]) died in an engineered "accidental" structural collapse via resonance manipulation; **Sylara** (Fire, [[Veltharyn]]) died of induced blood crystallization disguised as cardiac strain from communion; **Aethon** (Spirit, [[Aeravae]]) was rendered unconscious and pushed to a fatal fall during meditation; **Velithra** (Spirit, Selveran) drowned in her own root-sanctum's moisture, read by wood elves as a peaceful "merging with the World Tree." No physical evidence, no witnesses, and the simultaneous timing itself suggested a natural Pattern event rather than coordinated murder.

## The Seminary

Officially training the next generation of Threadbearers; actually a psychological-conditioning program. Naleth selects children (10-14) with genuine Thread-sensitivity, deep pre-existing faith, functional intelligence, *weak* critical-thinking habits, and minimal exposure to heretical ideas — then runs a 6-year curriculum that fuses Thread theory with Faith theology from lesson one, presents democratic oversight exclusively through case studies of its failures, and produces graduates who sincerely believe theocratic Thread governance is correct, not because they were forced to believe it but because they were educated into it. *"That's how you make permanent change — through education, not through violence."*

## Why she's a genuinely hard BBEG to oppose

She may be partially right: democratic decision-making did correlate with the instability that preceded Cycle 4's collapse, and she is, in fact, the only living World Anchor expert — exposing her could itself trigger Pattern collapse. The moral question the arc poses directly: can four murders be justified by preventing thousands of deaths from Ashkarra-style instability, and is a theocracy that actually works better than a democracy that doesn't?
