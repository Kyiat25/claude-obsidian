# The Deep Law

*"What is built must last. What lasts must be true."*

Dwarves do not kneel before gods. They honor the **First Builders (the Forgebound)** — divine architects who carved the bones of the world, then left the tools behind. Dwarves do not seek divine favor; they seek to continue the work. No temples, no priests, no prayers, no sacrifices, no birth myths — only Hall-Shrines, Stone-Keepers, Oathchants, and Craft-Offerings.

## Core tenets

The Builders Came Before Us; The World Is a Forge (life is construction, not chaos); To Shape Is to Know (you understand only what you can make, break, and remake); The Deep Law Remembers (stone does not forget, metal does not lie); The Vault Must Hold (not all secrets are meant for sunlight).

## The High Forgebound (six eternal architects)

Vatharun the Stone-Father (stability, ley lines as his bones), Thamur the Guardian Below (seals and containment — his greatest legacy is **the Lock**, sealing a corrupted thread of the Weave beneath the world), Gaeldros the Stone of Judgment (law and oaths — supports [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]]'s Earth Thread), Rhoris the Forge-Architect (craft and engineering), Dalvora the Sower of Stone (agriculture, underground civilization), and Karthas the Buryer of Empires (entropy — teaches that nothing lasts forever, not even stone).

## The Six Oaths

Stone Oath (endure what cannot be changed), Forge Oath (shape what lies beneath your hand), Clan Oath (stand with blood, or be forgotten), Silence Oath (do not speak of what must remain buried), Echo Oath (sing what was lost, even when none listen), Final Oath (return to stone with your name unbroken).

## Key legends

The First Forge and the Stone That Sang (creation myth — Vatharun's hammer-strike cracked the world, Rhoris caught the resulting flame); The Lock Beneath All Things (Thamur sealed a loose, corrupting thread of the Weave beneath the earth — why dwarves build downward); The Day the Anvil Spoke Back (a city that forged a weapon purely to boast was buried overnight by Karthas — "if your work cannot outlive your name, it is not worth carving"); The Great Hammer That Waits (Kromdûl, an unbroken hammer offered to and refused by the gods, sealed away for a day of need that has not yet come).

## Subrace ritual traditions

Each dwarven subrace carries its own fragment of the shared Stone-Song Codex: Hill Dwarves (Hearth-Stone Verses — kinship and memory), Mountain Dwarves (Iron-Vow — precision and craft perfectionism), Duergar (Ash-Tongue Stone — bitterness at perceived divine abandonment), Gearforged (Clickstone Invocation — mechanical immortality as proof of the Deep Law), Dust Dwarves (Wind-Buried Line — nomadic survival memory), Frost Dwarves (Ice-Root Verse — cold as the truer test of endurance), and Embergut Dwarves (Flamecore Oath — purification through inner fire).

## Relationship to the Weave and the Faith

The Deep Law does not personify or venerate the Weave — it is treated as material to be shaped and contained, not a god to obey (see the Lock myth). Theologically compatible but philosophically independent from [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]]: Gaeldros and Thamur support the Faith's Earth Thread, but dwarves see the Threads as cooperative materials, not sacred authority.

## Views on other races

A blunt, endurance-based hierarchy: Elves build for beauty but their works fade; Humans build too fast to last; Orcs destroy and leave no legacy; Dragonborn recite the past but never improve it; Gnomes invent quickly but lack patience (compare [[The Wonder-Tithe Codex]]); Halflings, surprisingly, earn genuine respect for making no false claim to permanence; Drow are despised outright as "echo-thieves" who corrupt runes and desecrate memory. "We measure a race by what they leave behind."

## History (condensed)

First Age (the Forgebound carve the world's foundations, the Lock is sealed, Kromdûl offered and refused) → Second Age (the cautionary Shattering of Ul'Drathen; Dust Dwarves emerge from exile) → Third Age (Gearforging begins; the Rite of Refusal — voluntary self-sealing as a "Living Lock" — is canonized) → Fourth Age (the Fall of Ashkarra triggers a global Silence Chain mourning-rite and mass emergency Sealings; the Deep Law is amended: "if even the Threads may fray, then carve deeper — trust only what you build").
