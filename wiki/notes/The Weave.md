# The Weave

The cosmological force underlying fate and magic in the FEARS setting. Referenced obliquely and without definition in earlier sources — [[A World that Watches]]'s Luck section ("modify Disposition randomly... but the Weave smiles anyway") and [[Unstable Magic Zones]]'s "arcane fabric... frayed or broken" both gesture at the same underlying idea without naming it. [[Four Threads of the Mind]] is the first source to name and elaborate it directly: "The Weave watches. The Weave waits." It governs [[Luck]] ("only if the dice are watching") and appears to react to player state — being Blessed by Luck while Corrupted is described as drawing the Weave's attention ("the Weave may mark you"), though the effect of being "marked" is not specified.

## Structure now known: the Five Layers (from How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World)

[[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]] is the first source to actually structure the Weave rather than gesture at it. It describes five interlocking "Layers": Primal Glyphs (embodied by four Primal Forces — [[Astralor]], [[Aeloria]], [[The Whispering Dark]], [[The Restorer]]), Universal Glyphs, Elemental Glyphs, Form Glyphs, and Personal Glyphs, all shaping a "Creation World" proving-ground where new realities are tested before becoming permanent. Its own "Final Truth" is that none of the five Layers — including the four Primal Forces — realize they're secretly one connected design.

> [!contradiction] RESOLVED — Does the Weave watch, or is it blind? And who, if anyone, actually knows?
> [[Four Threads of the Mind]] personifies the Weave with apparent will and awareness — "The Weave watches. The Weave waits," and it "may mark you" for being Blessed while Corrupted. Three DM-only cosmology sources since then had each given a *different* answer to who (if anyone) actually has that awareness:
>
> | Source | Who knows the most | Framing |
> |---|---|---|
> | [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]] | No one | Symmetric ignorance across all 4 Primal Forces / 5 Layers |
> | [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]] | [[Aeloria]] | Deliberate, near-total, self-concealed omniscience — **superseded, see ruling below** |
> | [[Twins of Creation]] | [[The Restorer]] | **Adopted as canon** — Aeloria and Astralor both blind to 2 of 4 forces; Whispering Dark aware of the Restorer but unable to explain it |
>
> These couldn't all be true as stated — the largest contradiction in this cosmology cluster, three internally-coherent, mutually-incompatible answers to the same question, from three sources of comparable authority (two marked `complete`, one marked `canonical`). Read as deliberately unreliable in-universe mythology — different tellings from different vantage points — right up until it was resolved below.
>
> [[Creation World]] — the likely narrative root [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]] partially systematizes — added a fourth data point in Twins of Creation's favor: it independently tells the same specific detail, that Aeloria does not know [[The Restorer]] exists and only ever questions, never resolves, what she witnesses. [[Creations Misunderstanding]] made it a fifth: an independent essay stating the exact same pairing outright (Aeloria unaware of the Restorer; the Whispering Dark aware but unable to communicate it). That gave **3-of-5 sources** (Creation World, Twins of Creation, Creations Misunderstanding) agreeing on this specific point, against 1 (Hidden Cosmology Thread System) claiming Aeloria secretly knows almost everything, with How the Five Layers' "no one knows" framing compatible with either reading.
>
> **Ruled (2026-07-14):** [[Twins of Creation]]'s account is adopted as this vault's canon on the strength of that 3-of-5 majority. The Restorer is the cosmology's most-aware Primal Force; Aeloria does not know the Restorer exists and does not understand the Whispering Dark's true nature; [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]]'s "Aeloria knows almost everything" framing is superseded. [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]]'s "no one knows" framing remains compatible as a broader statement (none of the four Primal Forces grasp the full Five-Layer design) — it just no longer implies the four are *symmetrically* ignorant of each other specifically. All disagreeing source text is left in place on each page as historical record rather than deleted.

## Structure now known: three layers of cosmological detail

Three DM-only sources now build on, and partly contradict, each other:

- [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]] — first source to structure the Weave at all: five interlocking "Layers" (Primal Glyphs embodied by four Primal Forces — [[Astralor]], [[Aeloria]], [[The Whispering Dark]], [[The Restorer]] — plus Universal, Elemental, Form, and Personal Glyphs), all shaping a "Creation World" proving-ground. Its "Final Truth": none of the five Layers realize they're one connected design.
- [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]] — marked `canonical` — reframes [[Astralor]] and [[Aeloria]] as **the Twins of Creation**, gives Aeloria uniquely elevated, deliberately-concealed awareness of nearly everything, explains Thread rotation as the three Forces' (Aeloria/Whispering Dark/Restorer) uncoordinated interaction, and introduces [[Threadbearers]] (mortals who unknowingly touch all three Forces at once) and the [[Loom-Wardens]] (a secret organization unknowingly serving Aeloria).
- [[Twins of Creation]] — confirms Astralor and Aeloria are literal siblings, gives both their first real personalities, but **directly contradicts** the previous source's central claim: Aeloria does not know the Restorer exists and doesn't understand the Whispering Dark's true nature. Elevates the Restorer instead to "the only force that sees the entire pattern," with the Whispering Dark occupying a strange aware-but-silenced middle position.
- [[Creation World]] — the narrative root text [[How the Five Layers Shape the Creation World]] appears to systematize; independently corroborates Twins of Creation's specific claim about Aeloria's ignorance of the Restorer, and introduces [[Divine Sparks]] — the accidental, all-four-forces-at-once origin of the first gods, a wholly new concept not present in any other source in this cluster.
- [[Creations Misunderstanding]] — the fifth cosmology source, states the same knowledge hierarchy as Twins of Creation and Creation World in essay form, with no new contradiction but the sharpest, most explicit statement yet of exactly who knows what.

## Open questions

- Is the Weave a deity, a physical force, a narrative personification, or literally the game's dice-fate system given a name? Three cosmology sources now disagree on who (if anyone) is behind it — see the contradiction above. Not resolved.
- "Shattered Loom" ([[Archivists of the Shattered Loom]]) plausibly ties into Weave/loom imagery — **confirmed as almost certainly correct**: [[Aeloria]] embodies "the Loom" directly, and [[Hidden Cosmology Thread System]] independently ties Loom vocabulary to the [[Loom-Wardens]] and Loom-Cities. Still not a proven identical reference, but the coincidence explanation no longer holds up.
- Relationship to [[Unstable Magic Zones]]' "arcane fabric" — same substance under two names, or two related-but-distinct concepts? Not addressed by any of the three cosmology sources yet.
