# The Oath of the Veiled Star

*"To speak too soon is to lie. To act too late is to abandon. We listen to the echo between."*

Known to outsiders as the Silent Faith or Pale Path — a mystery-bound faith of paradox built on silence rather than temples or miracles, most aligned with [[Aasimar (Lineage)|Aasimar]], [[Shadar-kai (Lineage)|Shadar-kai]], star-marked Humans, and fate-touched Elves. Teaches that prophecy shattered and the gods no longer speak clearly, and that judgment must be delivered without acclaim.

## Origin myth

Before flame, glyph, or god, a radiance stirred in the hush before the Weave's song — the **Veiled Star**, which refused to declare, create, or command, choosing instead to never be forgotten by folding inward and hiding its truth. From its shrouded glow came the **Star-Marked** (Aasimar, Shadar-kai, and others), not born to rule but to intervene when memory fails or prophecy screams too loud.

## Core tenets

1. **Truth Arrives Veiled** — prophecy shattered before the gods learned to speak it whole; prophets gather fragments, they don't proclaim.
2. **Silence Is Sacred** — words are flawed containers; the first truth broke the moment it was spoken.
3. **You Are the Star Made Flesh** — followers are living paradoxes, their nature carved through patience and unsung mercy, not inherited blood.
4. **Judgment Must Be Unseen** — open justice breeds spectacle; the Veiled act without acclaim.
5. **What Is Forgotten Is Not Gone** — even erased glyphs hum beneath the world; every action echoes regardless of silencing.

## Sects

- **The Order of the Unspeaking Word** — records and interprets prophecies too dangerous to speak aloud, via dream-mapping and glyphic weeping.
- **The Watchers Below** — sunken-sanctuary oracles who read the Pattern through subsurface memory ("where the Weave has pooled, not stretched").
- **The Pale Vow** — delivers death to those who cannot be judged and mercy to the unremembered; hooded executioners who use a single Oathblade once, per victim, then shatter it.

## The Eldritch Moons doctrine

Central and distinctive theology: the Eldritch Moons are not lies but **"true things revealed too soon, too fully, or in the wrong shape"** — overexposed truths rather than corruptions. Prophecy during a moonrise is void or unclean; the sacred rite of **Silence Quelling** binds practitioners' mouths, ears, and eyes for 12 hours during any Moon's rise to prevent "infected knowledge" reception. The Faith's greatest scriptural fear is a whispered **Thirteenth Moon** that would force the Veiled to finally speak aloud.

## Relationship to pantheons and other faiths

Reverent alignment with the Spirit Pantheon (Aelys as a "soul-mirror," Nyzara as patron of silent memory); mystic resonance with Water; respectful distance from Air; guarded tolerance from Earth; open hostility from Fire and the Burning Concord, which hunts Pale Vow agents; existential antagonism with the Eldritch proper, whom the Veiled regard as "screaming stars." [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]] grants it peripheral recognition — welcomed during Spirit/Water Cycles, mistrusted during Eldritch Moon events. Total opposition from [[The Ashen Web]], which purges any Veiled cult found in Drow territory without trial.

## Cosmological status

Explicitly framed as pre-divine and outside the Weave's Thread/Glyph structure entirely — older than worship itself, one of the few forces the Eldritch are said to fear, though not one of the setting's four named Primal Forces (Aeloria, Astralor, the Whispering Dark, the Restorer).
