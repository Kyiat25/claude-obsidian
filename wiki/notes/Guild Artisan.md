# Guild Artisan — Rank 1-4 (Modified from PHB)

*"You're a skilled craftsperson and member of a guild."* Tier 2 (Specialized).

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Apprentice | Guild Membership | Create a masterwork item, complete a major commission, or invent a new technique |
| 2 | Journeyman | Master Craftsman | Create an exceptional masterwork, train 3+ successful apprentices, or open a famous shop |
| 3 | Master Craftsman | Guild Authority | Become guild master, revolutionize your craft, or build a crafting empire |
| 4 | Guild Master | Master of the Craft | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Apprentice

**Skill Proficiency**: Insight, Persuasion. **Tool Proficiency**: one type of artisan's tools. **Language Proficiency**: one of choice. **Equipment**: artisan's tools, letter of introduction from the guild, traveler's clothes, 15 gp.

**Feature — Guild Membership**: the guild provides lodging and meals (modest lifestyle); aid if arrested (unless the crime was against the guild); funeral and dependent support if you die; access to guild contacts in other cities.

**Guild Business** (choose or roll d20): Alchemists/apothecaries, Armorers/smiths, Brewers/distillers, Calligraphers/scribes, Carpenters/roofers, Cartographers/surveyors, Cobblers/shoemakers, Cooks/bakers, Glassblowers/glaziers, Jewelers/gemcutters, Leatherworkers/tanners, Masons/stonecutters, Painters/sign-makers, Potters/tile-makers, Shipwrights/sailmakers, Smiths/metalworkers, Tinkers/pewterers, Wagon makers/wheelwrights, Weavers/dyers, Woodcarvers/coopers.

**Holdings**: shared workshop space; basic tools and materials; guild connections in your city.

## Rank 2: Journeyman

**Holdings**: a small personal workshop; modest commission income (15 gp/month); 1-2 apprentices; guild contacts in 3-5 cities; access to the guild's bulk material purchasing.

**Feature Expansion — Master Craftsman**: crafting time reduced by 25%; your items sell for 10% more than standard; you can identify and appraise crafted items with advantage.

## Rank 3: Master Craftsman

**Holdings**: a successful shop or large workshop; significant commission income (60 gp/month); 3-6 skilled workers/apprentices; guild contacts across the region; a seat on the guild council (minor political influence).

**Feature Expansion — Guild Authority**: you can authenticate items in your specialty (detect forgeries); you're invited to judge competitions and certify masterworks; you can train specialists (create NPCs with expertise in your craft); access to rare materials through guild connections.

## Rank 4: Guild Master

**Holdings**: a guild hall or master workshop; major income from guild operations (250 gp/month); a network of 50+ guild members across multiple cities; political influence in trade matters; control over quality standards in your trade.

**Feature — Master of the Craft**: you set prices and standards for your entire trade in the region; you can blacklist competitors (they can't work in guild cities); access to the finest materials and most skilled workers; you can organize guild-wide actions (strikes, boycotts, etc.). Items you create are considered masterworks — worth 50% more than standard, magical items require 25% less time and gold, and you can create unique signature pieces that become legendary.
