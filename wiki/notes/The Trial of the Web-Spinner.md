# The Trial of the Web-Spinner

The moment the gods discovered they were not truly omnipotent — one of the most significant divine conflicts in this cosmology's history, held at [[The Court of the Silent Tribunal]].

## The charges

Trapping the concept of fate in her web; altering the destinies of entire bloodlines; silencing omens from other gods; creating a forbidden glyph (the Glyph of Binding Will); attempting to rewrite stone-law (Gaeldros's own domain); corrupting the Weave's structure through unauthorized edits.

**Presiding judge**: [[Gaeldros]]. **Witnesses**: [[Aelys]], [[Thirian]], [[Astria]], [[Nyzara]]. **Prosecutors**: the Spirit Pantheon collectively. **Defender**: [[Vel'Zyren]], representing herself. **Duration**: unknown — time behaved strangely throughout.

## The evidence and the defense

[[Aelys]] presented fractured leylines showing her edits, prophecies rewritten mid-fulfillment, glyphs that changed meaning retroactively. [[Astria]] testified her own prophecies had been twisted backwards. [[Gaeldros]] showed stone-law she'd attempted to overwrite, and proof she'd tried to rewrite the terms of her own trial.

Vel'Zyren did not deny the charges. Instead: *"I did not break the Weave. I corrected it. Your Pattern is flawed—full of loose threads and contradictions. I simply tightened what was fraying."* Her defense's shocking core claims: the Weave genuinely has errors the gods cannot fix within their own rules; she acted outside those rules and could therefore edit what they could not; her changes improved the Pattern's stability; and the gods feared her not because she was wrong, but because she might be right.

## The judgment

The gods tried, in order, to erase her from existence (failed — her edits were permanent), undo her changes (failed — the Weave kept her rewrites), strip her divinity (failed — she had rewritten her own divine nature), and kill her (failed — she had edited death's claim on her). [[Gaeldros]]'s ruling: *"You have proven that you cannot be unmade by the laws you rejected. Therefore, you shall be bound by the one law you cannot rewrite—stone itself. You will weave only for those trapped beneath the land. Your fate is your own. The gods will not claim you, and the Weave will not remember you."*

## The binding

[[Vatharun]] moved for only the second time in recorded history, folding mountains over her prison and creating the Underdark as her domain. [[Thamur]] opened the Silent Gate as a warning to any who would defy cosmic law, erasing her name from sacred memory. [[Orivak]] bound the nearby leylines, preventing her from touching surface magic directly. The effort nearly shattered the Pillar of Gaeldros itself — see [[Earth Pantheon]]'s "Fractured Pillar" myth for the fuller account of this same event from the Earth Pantheon's own perspective.

## What the trial revealed

The gods are not omnipotent — they could not undo what she had done, only contain her. The Weave genuinely has flaws the gods cannot fix within their own rules, meaning someone willing to break rules can accomplish what gods cannot. Binding is not victory: she remains alive and powerful, still grants divine magic to the Drow, and waits rather than accepts defeat. Some Spirit gods secretly agreed with aspects of her argument, and the pantheons nearly fractured over her fate — the first crack in divine unity, and proof that divine law can be questioned at all.
