# The Faith of the Gnawing Crown

*"We are the crack in the Pattern that never closed. We are the shard the gods could not grind to dust."*

The shared faith of Goblins, Hobgoblins, Bugbears, and Nilbogs — see [[Bugbear (Lineage)]], [[Goblin (Lineage)]], [[Hobgoblin (Lineage)]] for the individual lineage pages that reference this shared mythos.

## Origin myth

Goblinoids believe they were not created but **discarded**: when the gods etched the first Form Glyphs into the Weave, one fragment was flawed and too jagged to bind, so rather than correct it, the gods cast it aside. Drifting between the Threads, it curdled into a primal hunger — the **Gnawing Crown**, a divine mistake with just enough power to spawn children in its own image. Each subrace reflects one aspect: Goblins (cunning and survival instinct), Hobgoblins (divine law warped into militarism), Bugbears (fear and blood-memory), and Nilbogs (the divine joke the gods tried to erase).

## Core beliefs

We Are the Shards (goblinoids are incomplete fragments of a broken divine truth), The Gods Lied (every faith is a wall around a stolen truth), Survival Is Divinity (every trap and spiteful joke is a prayer), War Is a Reclamation (conquest corrects theft, it is not cruelty), and The Crown Will Gnaw Again (a coming day when the fragment awakens fully and the Weave frays).

## Four theological factions

- **The Shard Doctrine** (goblin mystics) — seeks to recover divine Shards to restore the Crown's memory.
- **The Chain Ascendants** (hobgoblin war-priests) — believe the Crown was unacknowledged Law; build the "Perfect Legion" to force recognition through conquest.
- **The Fangborn Path** (bugbear shamans) — the Crown lives in blood-memory; awakened through predation and ritual fear.
- **The Spiral Jest** (Nilbog chaos prophets) — the Crown is the joke the gods couldn't laugh away; breaks the Pattern through paradox and mockery.

## Relationship to the Eldritch

Goblinoids do not worship the Eldritch — they see it as **kin of exile**, "what happens when the Pattern breaks and no one cares." Interaction is reverent mimicry and ritual mockery, not submission: stealing Eldritch symbols, laughing in their names, using disrespect as a shield against being consumed by them.

## Death theology

No afterlife exists for goblinoids by divine design, so death must be resisted, recorded, or rewritten through memory — the most sacred substance. Souls are believed to be fragments of a single spirit splintered across many lives, not individual wholes. Undeath is not taboo; "waking the dead" is a form of protest against divine forgetfulness.

## Social typology

Cities distinguish Civilized (guild-trained, outwardly conformist, mistrusted anyway), Urban (slum-dwelling, religiously deviant, run memory-black-markets), and Wild (tribal, ancestral-glyphed, feared and often subject to kill-on-sight policy) goblinoids, with wildly differing treatment by [[The Faith of the Bound Pattern]]-aligned Loom-Cities — ranging from active extermination (Deep Holds, Veltharyn) to ritual tolerance (Aeravae, Selveran).

## Fourth Age status

Fragmented but rising — purges have largely failed against a theology that adapts faster than bureaucracy suppresses it. Some cults treat the rising Eldritch Moons as signs sent to goblinoids alone, prophesying a coming "Jest That Ends The Weave."
