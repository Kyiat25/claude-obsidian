# Mirag'Nael, The False Author

*"The truth was written. I simply replaced it. Temporarily."*

**Domain**: short-term reality editing without cosmic permission. **Symbol**: a quill writing over a shattered glyph, vanishing as it's drawn. **Truth**: can rewrite events, but reality forcibly reasserts itself. **Restriction**: cannot defy the 8 Great Glyphs permanently. **Dangerous power**: rewrites must collapse within time or recognition.

> [!note] Feared even by other Eldritch gods
> Mirag'Nael is explicitly called out as uniquely feared within its own pantheon, because rewriting reality creates direct conflict with fundamental cosmic forces — including, implicitly, [[Aeloria]]'s fate-weaving domain.

## How it bleeds into reality

- **Shadows (the Veiled Hand)**: perception becomes malleable; truth hides in plain sight; what is seen can be edited.
- **Threads (Aeloria's Fate)**: fate-weaving is corrupted; destinies are temporarily rewritten; futures that never were.

## Corruptions

- **Beasts — Narrabeasts**: retell reality as they experience it, rewriting the memories of observers, changing the past through speaking.
- **Monsters**: living lies made flesh, existing by consensus and dying by contradiction — truth and fiction blur.
- **Mortals**: speak rewritten events with certainty, then realize moments later they're false; can **cast false spells** that seem real for 1-3 rounds before backlash; memory becomes an unreliable narrator.

## Preferred manifestation zones

Wild Magic Zones, Arcane Rifts, Glyph Forges, sites of historical revision.

## Warning signs

Historical records changing; people remembering events differently; reality feeling unstable; truth becoming negotiable.

## Campaign tiers

| Tier (Levels) | Scale |
