# Veltharyn — The Sovereign Crucible

Fire-Thread [[Loom-Cities|Loom-City]], "the City that Never Yielded," carved into seven ascending tiers of **Mount Taz'karreth**, a still-active volcano rising 4,847m. Population ~680,000 (750,000+ during high-cycle festivals).

> [!note] Two accounts of this city
> An earlier gazetteer entry (`.raw/Dór-o Estel.md`) described a 3.8-million-population city under a Forge-Caste System and a "Council of Seven Flames." The deep-dive campaign source below instead describes a Stonebound Assembly of Twelve Seats and a much smaller population. Treat the material below as the more detailed and current canon.

## Founding: the Wyrm-Dawn Pact

Before the city existed, the mountain was home to **Korth'thalar the Forge-Heart**, a primordial mountain-god. During the First Weaving, the Wyrm-Dawn Pact between Dragonborn flame-clans and Dwarven deep-miners bound Korth'thalar's essence into the mountain's core, creating the **Heartforge World Anchor** — a willing sacrifice that left the stone pulsing with a divine heartbeat and crowned the peak with the **Anvil-Sun**, a perpetual forge-fire. Since [[Ashkarra]]'s fall eight cycles ago, Veltharyn is the **sole remaining Fire Thread anchor**, and blackened World Tree roots recovered from Ashkarra's destruction are now integrated into the Heartforge chamber as a standing warning against Thread corruption.

## The seven tiers

**Roothold** (foundation, ~115,000, built around the Cradle Mines — residents called "Deep-Born" develop darkvision and heat resistance) → **Emberward** (working-class residential, ~135,000) → **Forgeheart** (industrial/civic heart, ~140,000, home to the Tri-Crowned Square and the Grand Flame-Forges) → **Lawspire** (judicial/administrative, ~105,000, the Hall of Deep Law descends thirteen levels, and the Sacred Descent leads to the Heartforge) → **Crafthold** (elite artisans, ~85,000) → **Skyreach** (noble district, ~65,000) → **Crownpeak** (sacred summit, ~15,000, accessible only to the highest religious authorities). A separate Four Quarters framing overlays the tiers: the Outside (surface farms/trade towns), Topside (high tiers), the Heart (civic spine), and the Underside (mines and the vast subterranean **Undercrucible**).

## Government: the Stonebound Assembly

Twelve seats: seven hereditary Dwarven seats fixed by tradition (Forge, Deep, Builder's, Rooted, Anvil, Lore, Oath), four elected Civic seats (Outer, Topside, Heart, Underside), and the Threadbearer's Seat. Votes are hammered into molten metal in a central forge-pit; unanimous decisions become eternal plaques, split decisions are recorded on metal that eventually cracks — "symbolizing the need for future revision." Four factions compete: the Traditionalist Coalition (the seven Dwarven seats), the Adaptive Alliance (elected seats and younger members), the Flame Purists (Dragonborn-dominated, pushes Fire Thread supremacy over the other Loom-Cities), and the Pattern Harmonists (cross-racial, pro-cooperation).

### Notable leadership

- **Threadbearer Azareth Flamewright** (Dragonborn, 127) — Twelfth Seat, negotiated the Hearthspirit Accord with Kel Varyan, currently managing the spiritual strain of being the sole Fire Thread anchor.
- **Judge Thrain Oathbinder** (Dwarf, 203) — Oath Seat, Chief Justice, presided over more cases than any judge in the city's recorded history.
- **Keeper Silvana Threadmind** (Elf, 156, originally from [[Selveran]]) — Heart Seat, civil administrator known for cross-cultural coordination.
- **Master Thorma Ironbound** (Dwarf, 178) — Forge Seat, pioneer of Memory-Metal techniques.
- **Vareth "Copperlung" Sootwhisper** (Half-Orc, 67) — reformed former leader in the Hollow Concord (the city's criminal underworld), now runs legitimate mining operations and rehabilitation programs for other ex-criminals.
