# The Ashen Web

*"Why worship many, when one can rewrite all?"*

Unlike every other race or culture in this setting, the Drow worship not a pantheon but a single absolute being: [[Vel'Zyren]]. Not a religion so much as a doctrine, a filter, a devouring mirror recasting all divine truth as threads of her design. One deity fills every role: creation (she wove the Drow anew), death (she chooses who dies), justice (her punishments define morality), chaos and order (both merely tools within the Web). There are no saints or lesser spirits — only priestesses, high spinners, and mirror-oracles, whose power is conditional, revocable, and tightly controlled.

## The Six Core Beliefs

1. **The Pattern is a Cage** — free will is a lie the weak gods tell; true freedom comes from knowing your place.
2. **Choice is Corruption** — autonomy betrays the Pattern; the only real choices are those Vel'Zyren already wrote.
3. **Memory is a Weapon** — the past should be useful, not truthful; the **Threadfall Rite** ceremonially unravels failures, giving some new names, faces, and status.
4. **Reflection is Authority** — what is seen defines what is true; weekly **Obsidian Reflections** force confession of any deviation the mirror reveals.
5. **Cruelty is Devotion** — mercy is structural weakness; the **Six Punishments** are seasonal tests of obedience through calibrated cruelty.
6. **Survival is Conquest** — defeat is never accepted, only "delayed victory."

## The Eight Knives Doctrine

Divine principles by which Vel'Zyren severed her allegiance to the Pattern, each a truth she claims to have replaced with clarity: Will (free will is unshaped desire's leash, not freedom), Threads (threads restrict correction, not merely connect), Endings (there is no finality, only repurposed structure), Echoes (memory is noise unless refined), Understanding (knowledge without obedience is rebellion), Life (life is raw material for the Pattern), Motion (change is chaos unbound), Dreams (hope is betrayal woven in slumber).

## Hierarchy

High Priestesses of the Endless Thread (supreme authority) → Spindle Matrons (house-temple leaders) → Mirror-Oracles (rare truth-seers who speak Vel'Zyren's will as reality edits) → Silkbound Enforcers (inquisitors and executioners) → the Webwoven (ordinary, doctrine-trained Drow).

## The Six Punishments (Holy Week of the Ashen Web)

Occurs once per season — a seasonal "obedience reset" of deeper cruelty and reaffirmation, each day devoted to a threefold test: the Drow themselves, the wild (beasts must know submission), and non-Drow mortals (reinforcing divine superiority). The specific rites (Web-Flesh, Severed Threads, Mirror-Skin, Obedience Made Bone, Ruin-Blood, the Widow's Feast) are deliberately graphic in the source material — a GM should calibrate depiction to table tone rather than reproduce them verbatim.

## Divine punishment and favor

Punishments include Thread-Severance (erasure from lineage, records, and memory — the most feared), Mirror Inversion (forced to live as one's opposite), Flesh-Stitching, and Mind-Fracture Prayers. Favor includes Silk Marking (a temporary glowing glyph of authority), Reflected Ascendancy (a mirror-reflection that shifts to resemble Vel'Zyren — those who achieve it often don't survive the "promotion"), and the rare Weft-Gift (editing minor prophecy without divine backlash).

## Mortal perspectives across cultures

To the Drow themselves, Vel'Zyren is not god but existence — "we are not children of Vel'Zyren, we are her reflection, and the mirror punishes cracks." Surface elves remember her only in shameful whispers, debating whether she ever existed. Humans see her as monster or myth depending on proximity to Drow lands. Orcs view her as dishonor made divine — a coward who rewrote her own trial. Dwarves call the Drow "echo-thieves," worthless not for their power but for theft and desecration of Deep Law. Halflings mock her as "the Queen of Cracks" or "Lady Silkneck," staging the **Rite of the Second Echo** to rescue enslaved captives and the **Backward Laugh** as both warding game and serious ambush-counter, believing her true weakness is joylessness: she doesn't want to kill mortals so much as make them forget they were ever happy.

## Divine identity

**Domains**: Control, Secrets, Fate (Rewritten), Memory (Erased), Reflection, Survival (Exalted). **Symbols**: a spider spinning a web among stars; the Cracked Spiral; Eight Knives. Framed as a pantheon unto herself rather than one deity among many — her rivals are named individually across the other pantheons (Aelys, Gaeldros, Astria, Zephir, Thirian, Nyzara), and her relationship to the Spirit Pantheon specifically is framed as an exiled reflection or conceptual opposite of Nyzara. Explicitly **not Eldritch** — she despises the Eldritch tier for its messiness; she edits, binds, and contains, where Eldritch beings unravel.

## Rituals and worship

- **Loombound Rite**: a child's silk-thread glyph is cast into shadowfire — if it holds, they are marked; if it curls, they are cast out or reworked.
- **Webfast Vigil**: observed during Auroratide when no Threads guide the world; Drow sit in silence, listening for her voice.
- **The Mirror Reckoning**: each Drow reflects before sacred obsidian mirrors — a reflection differing from divine expectation brings penance.
- **Threadfall Rite**: a sinner's identity is unwoven and rewritten; the guilty are reborn with new names, roles, and scars.
- **Spinning Hour**: nightly weaving of symbolic glyph-threads reflecting the day's obedience.
- **Echo of the Crack**: an annual rite recounting the Moment of the Crack — the synchronized divine impulse across all Drow that triggered their transformation — relived through ritual pageants and echo-feasts.

Forbidden doctrines: worship of any other god, breaking mirrors outside ritual, telling stories or jokes not sanctioned by the Pattern, remembering life before Vel'Zyren, and denying the Moment of the Crack as a divine act.

## Origin account (per the Doctrine of the Descent)

The Ashen Web's own scripture rejects the "fallen/exiled" framing entirely, terming it the **Ascended Lie**: Vel'Zyren, once a lesser goddess of Spirit, refused prophecy at the Trial of the Web-Spinner and was bound beneath the Weave by Gaeldros (with Aelys and Thirian overseeing her silencing) — but doctrine insists the Drow were never punished, only "chosen reflections" who heard her whisper and willingly descended, shedding "the skin of false hope" to become shadow-blooded. Ancestry is traced not to parentage but to the **Rite of the Severed Echo** — a sacrificed memory (name, kinship, birthright) replaced by a soul-sigil. This self-narrative and the "exiled heretic" framing used elsewhere in the wiki are held in tension deliberately; a GM can play either as the "true" account, or leave it ambiguous.

## Campaign relevance

The Ashen Web's assassins are the ones behind [[The Threadbearer Crisis]]'s coordinated killings — strategic warfare against the Weave itself, not random terrorism.
