# Converting Monsters to DCS

*"A dragon is not merely HP and AC — it is fury and fear, scales and strategy, ancient wisdom and terrible pride."* A GM guide for converting standard D&D 5E monster stat blocks into full [[Dynamic Combat System (DCS)]] combatants, rather than leaving them as flat "attack until dead" stat pools.

## Why convert

Standard 5E monsters are static: fixed AC, a draining HP pool, no psychological dimension — a goblin fights identically whether leading a winning charge or watching its warband collapse. DCS conversion adds: **[[Evasion]]** replacing static AC with contested defense rolls; **[[Damage Reduction]]** as a separate physical-toughness layer, so heavy armor makes hits less effective rather than less frequent; **[[Momentum]]** making monsters more dangerous while winning and more vulnerable while faltering; **Sanity and Focus** enabling real morale — cowardly goblins break easily, dragon tyrants hold firm, celestials never waver; **[[Called Shot]]** giving every monster genuine exploitable weak points (a troll's head disrupts regeneration, a dragon's wings ground it, an orc captain's weapon arm disarms them); **[[Bloodied and Bruised|Bloodied states]]** at 50% HP triggering desperation, exposed frailties, or once-per-combat abilities; and the **[[Injury System]]** giving monsters visible, mechanically-weakening, cascading wounds instead of a flat HP countdown.

## Scope

Provides both **Quick Conversion Rules** (fast on-the-fly formulas for improvised encounters) and a **Standard Conversion System** (detailed guidelines for important encounters and boss fights), covering Evasion/DR mapping from existing 5E stat blocks, Called Shot vulnerability assignment, Bloodied-threshold transformation design, and monster-specific mental-attribute assignment (which creature types get meaningful Sanity/Focus pools vs. which are exempt, e.g. mindless undead or constructs).
