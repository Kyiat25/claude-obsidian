# Caltheris, The Dying Spark

*"A fire that burns too bright will consume itself. But oh, how beautiful it will be before it fades."*

**Domains**: passion, art, inspiration. **Symbol**: a flickering ember, on the verge of going out. **Worshiped by**: artists, poets, dreamers, tragic lovers. **Enemies**: [[Soltharion]] (control), [[Vaeldros]] (judgment).

The Elven connection to Fire — not war or conquest, but the passion of fleeting beauty; embodies inspiration in the moment of divine creation before it burns away. Elves believe he was once part of the High Flame but couldn't endure its rigid order and faded. His priests build no temples, only masterpieces, music, and poetry that burn bright but cannot last.

**Myth — The Flame That Inspired the First Elves**: before Elves learned [[The Weave]]'s threads, Fire was their first teacher, showing them how to shape and create beauty; the first Elven blades were forged in flame, the first Elven stories traced by embers and sparks. But Fire's gift proved fleeting — paintings faded, sculptures cracked, greatest works turned to ash — so Elves turned to the Weave's more enduring patterns instead. Caltheris still whispers in Elven art and music, the fire that was almost enough, the light that nearly guided them but never truly did.
