# Four Threads of the Mind

Subtitled "The Core of FEARS," framed as "A Whispered Primer from the [[Archivists of the Shattered Loom]]." The **first source in this cluster marked `status: complete`** rather than `wip`/`revision-needed` — treat this as more authoritative than [[Welcome to FEARS]], [[A World that Watches]], or [[FEARS Character Framework]] if any of them conflict with it.

## What it resolves from earlier sources

- **Corruption thresholds**: 5 / 10 / 15 / 20, each with a named consequence. [[Corruption]]'s prior page had no numbers at all — this is the first source to give any.
- **Confirms the Focus→Corruption mechanical link** that [[Focus]]'s page had already inferred from [[A World that Watches]] ("losing Focus causes... corruption spikes") — this source states it directly as a cascade rule.
- **Confirms the Sanity→Focus cascade**: "fail a Sanity save, and your Focus may fracture" — new information, not previously stated anywhere.
- **[[The Weave]]** — the cosmological force behind fate and magic, referenced obliquely in [[A World that Watches]] ("the Weave smiles anyway") and [[Unstable Magic Zones]]'s "arcane fabric," now named and elaborated directly.

## What's genuinely new

- **[[Burned out]]** — the terminal state at 0 Focus (cannot cast or concentrate).
- **[[Broken]]** — the terminal state at 0 Sanity ("soul no longer entirely yours"). **Open question, not silently resolved**: [[FEARS Character Framework]]'s [[Mental State]] tracker also ends in a stage called "Broken" (Stable → Frayed → Deranged → Broken). This source doesn't say whether hitting 0 Sanity *is* reaching Mental State's Broken stage, or a separate, coincidentally-named terminal condition specific to Sanity alone. Flagged on both pages and on [[FEARS MOC]].
- **Focus Save** and **Sanity Save** — named trigger mechanics (Focus Save when casting in chaos; Sanity Save on exposure to Eldritch zones, glyphic corruption, or otherworldly truths). Neither has a stated DC or roll formula.
- **[[Archivists of the Shattered Loom]]** — a second in-fiction narrator/authorial voice, distinct from [[Daldir Lunadreamer]].

## Editorial note

The source links to a "Short Rest" concept in the Luck section ("three natural 1s before a Short Rest"). Not given its own page here — it's an unelaborated generic TTRPG term borrowed from 5E, not a FEARS-specific mechanic, and creating a page for it would be a thin filler page with nothing to say beyond the definition every reader already knows.
