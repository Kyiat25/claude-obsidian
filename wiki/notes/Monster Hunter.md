# Monster Hunter — Rank 1-4 (Slayer of Supernatural Beasts)

*"You track and kill the monsters that prey on civilization."* Tier 3 (Dark Fantasy). Fulfills the "Monster Hunter" slot [[FEARS Advanced Backgrounds]] flagged as not-yet-developed after Batch 1. Broader in scope than [[Vampire Hunter]] (which specializes in one monster type exclusively) — this background lets the player pick any monster family as their specialty.

## Rank progression

| Rank | Title | Feature | Progression trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Novice Hunter | Monster Lore | Kill your first major monster, save a settlement from a creature attack, or survive an encounter with a legendary beast |
| 2 | Professional Hunter | Slayer's Knowledge | Kill a notorious monster, save a major settlement, or discover a weakness of an "unkillable" creature |
| 3 | Master Monster Slayer | Apex Predator | Slay a legendary monster, write the definitive hunting guide for a creature type, or save a region from a monster plague |
| 4 | Legendary Monster Hunter | Slayer of Legends | — (capstone) |

## Rank 1: Novice Hunter

**Skill Proficiency**: Investigation, Survival. **Tool Proficiency**: poisoner's kit or leatherworker's tools. **Equipment**: a hunter's kit (silvered weapon, monster identification guide, 3 vials of antitoxin, manacles), dark traveler's clothes, 15 gp.

**Feature — Monster Lore**: advantage on Intelligence checks to recall monster information; identify monsters by tracks, leavings, or attack patterns; know where to sell monster parts; understand basic monster weaknesses.

**Specialty** (choose your focus): Undead (vampires, ghouls, wraiths), Lycanthropes, Demons and devils, Dragons and drakes, Aberrations, Fey creatures.

**Holdings**: basic hunting equipment; a contact who posts bounties; knowledge of local monster activity.

## Rank 2: Professional Hunter

**Holdings**: quality hunting equipment (silvered weapons, magical traps); income from bounties (30 gp/month average); a network of 5-8 contacts (other hunters, scholars, alchemists); a collection of monster parts and trophies; a reputation in frontier settlements; basic alchemical supplies for potions.

**Feature Expansion — Slayer's Knowledge**: advantage on attacks against your specialty monsters; craft basic potions and oils (antitoxins, oils of slipperiness); know advanced weaknesses and habits of monsters; track monsters across any terrain; your reputation makes common folk seek you out.

## Rank 3: Master Monster Slayer

**Holdings**: extensive hunting equipment (multiple silvered/magical weapons); major income from high-value contracts (100 gp/month); a workshop for alchemical preparations; a rare monster-parts collection (worth 500+ gp); a network of 15-20 contacts across regions; 2-3 junior hunters who work with you; your name is known among monster hunting circles.

**Feature Expansion — Apex Predator**: resistance to a damage type common to your specialty (necrotic for undead hunters, etc.); craft powerful monster-hunting potions and weapons; once per week, track and locate a specific monster within the region; know how to exploit any monster's weakness; advantage on saves against your specialty monsters' abilities.

## Rank 4: Legendary Monster Hunter

**Holdings**: an arsenal of specialized weapons and equipment; excellent income (250+ gp/month); a museum-worthy trophy and parts collection; a school or organization of hunters you founded; international reputation; your methods are studied and taught; access to restricted information on the most dangerous creatures.

**Feature — Slayer of Legends**: advantage on all attack rolls and saves against monsters; craft preparations that harm even damage-immune creatures; once per month, hunt down and confront any monster (you know where it is); low-intelligence monsters flee your presence; identify any monster and know its complete capabilities. Rulers hire you for impossible hunts, your contracts command 1,000+ gp for legendary creatures, books are written about your hunts, and your weapons become legendary heirlooms.
