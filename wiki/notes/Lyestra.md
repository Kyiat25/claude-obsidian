# Lyestra, The Unmoved Mirror

*"Othrys is water that has forgotten how to move. I am water that has chosen not to."*

**Domains**: still water, reflection, the contemplative pause, truth seen on the surface. **Symbol**: an unrippled pool reflecting a single star. **Worshiped by**: monks, diviners who scry in bowls, mourners seeking quiet, judges before verdicts.

Embodies Water's internal dispute over stillness: one rival treats stillness as death, where nothing grows; Lyestra treats it as clarity, where things are finally seen. She is Water's mirror to Air's [[Thirian]] — he is the breath held before action, she the surface held before truth. Scrying doctrine holds a vision requires her permission; a rippled bowl is a refused question. Her shrines are basins under open sky, filled once and never topped up — when the water is gone, the shrine has said what it will say. Casting a stone into a still pool without word or need is a small, universally recognized blasphemy.
