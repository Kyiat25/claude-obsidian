# Vaeldros, The Ash-Warden

*"Some flames must be extinguished."*

**Domains**: war, grave, judgment. **Symbol**: a silver sword with black flames. **Worshiped by**: executioners, inquisitors, divine judges. **Enemies**: [[Xethar]], Pyrris, [[Zerah]].

Ensures fire remains pure — burns heresy before it spreads, purges knowledge threatening Fire's divine rule, ensures no rebellion rises without being snuffed out. His followers are knights, magistrates, and inquisitors who believe law is absolute; his priests carry branding irons. Already established elsewhere in this cluster (see [[Guide to the Eldritch Forces]]) as fearing [[Nhal'Zereth]] above all other Eldritch forces, with purging flames left backwards by [[Ith'Quess]]'s corruption.

**Eldritch Breach — "The Flesh That Would Not Burn"**: at the Glyph-Forge ruins of Durnahal, Vaeldros once unleashed flame to cleanse an inverted leyline already touched by [[Ith'Quess]]. Instead of purging it, the fire reversed — flesh melted inward, bone melted upward. Survivors are said to still wander as unconsumed, permanently smouldering "Ash-Born Husks." Mythic truth drawn from the incident: fire may test all things, but some flesh refuses its shape.
